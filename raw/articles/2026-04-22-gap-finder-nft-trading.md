## Advanced Trading Strategies and Techniques

The wiki lacks documentation of specific trading strategies that have become standard practice among professional NFT traders. **Arbitrage trading** across NFT marketplaces represents a fundamental strategy enabled by price discrepancies between venues, though it exists in multiple forms[37]. **Spatial arbitrage**, also known as cross-exchange arbitrage, involves buying NFTs at lower prices on one marketplace and selling at higher prices on another, capturing the spread minus transaction fees[37]. **Triangular arbitrage** applies to NFT collections where price discrepancies emerge between three different marketplaces simultaneously, enabling traders to cycle through purchases and sales to accumulate additional positions of the original asset[37]. **Statistical arbitrage** (StatArb) represents a quantitative, data-driven approach using mathematical models, machine learning algorithms, and historical data to identify relationships and price movements between correlated NFT collections, executing trades when prices deviate beyond statistical norms[37].

**Wash trading** represents a critical concept traders must understand both as a risk (avoiding markets with extensive wash trading that obscures true price discovery) and potentially as a compliance issue[23]. The research on wash trading detection describes algorithmic approaches that flag transactions as wash trades when a wallet repurchases an NFT token within 30 days of initially selling it, helping identify market manipulation and potential regulatory concerns[23].

**NFT floor price mechanics** require explanation as traders often misunderstand how floor prices are calculated and what they represent. The floor price is simply the lowest listing price within a collection on secondary markets, representing the minimum entry cost but not an average valuation. Understanding that floor prices fluctuate based on active sell-side listing dynamics, rather than representing some fundamental asset value, enables traders to identify arbitrage opportunities when floor prices disconnect from collection fundamentals.

**Rarity-based trading strategies** emerge from the toolkit of professional traders who use rarity scores to identify undervalued assets or premium traits selling below statistical value. Understanding how rarity calculators weight individual traits versus overall collection rarity enables traders to spot statistical anomalies where particular assets trade at prices that deviate significantly from their rarity profiles[7].
