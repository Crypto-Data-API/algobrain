# Arkham Intelligence

## Overview

On-chain intelligence platform focused on deanonymizing blockchain activity and connecting wallets to real-world entities. Arkham combines entity labeling, cross-chain tracking, and visual relationship graphs to reveal who is behind on-chain transactions. Their standout feature is the entity page -- a comprehensive profile showing all known wallets, balances, transaction history, and counterparty relationships for funds, exchanges, whales, and individuals. Arkham also runs a unique Intel Exchange: a bounty marketplace where users pay (in ARKM tokens) for information identifying unknown wallets.

## Pricing

- **Free tier**: generous -- entity search, basic wallet tracking, portfolio monitoring, alerts
- **Paid plans**: advanced analytics, deeper historical data, API access, priority alerts
- **ARKM token**: used for bounties on the Intel Exchange and premium platform features
- Exact paid pricing varies; Arkham monetizes partly through the token economy

## What You Get (vs Free)

- Free tier is actually usable: basic entity search, wallet tracking, and alerts at no cost
- Paid upgrades unlock full API access, advanced cross-chain analytics, and deeper historical data
- Visual entity graphs showing fund/whale interconnections and fund-of-fund relationships
- Real-time transfer alerts for tracked wallets with customizable thresholds
- Portfolio-level tracking: aggregate all known wallets for an entity into a single view
- Cross-chain support spanning Ethereum, Bitcoin, Solana, Polygon, Arbitrum, and more

## Alpha Edge

- **Deanonymize on-chain flows**: Arkham's entity labeling reveals when major funds (Wintermute, Jump, Galaxy Digital) are moving assets. A large transfer from a labeled fund wallet to an exchange signals potential selling
- **Front-run large transfers**: real-time alerts when whale wallets initiate large transfers to exchanges (sell signal) or withdraw from exchanges (accumulation signal)
- **Track VC and fund portfolios**: see the full on-chain portfolio of known crypto funds -- what they hold, when they buy, when they sell
- **Intel Exchange alpha**: the bounty marketplace creates a crowdsourced intelligence network. Users identify unknown wallets for rewards, continuously expanding the label database
- **Government and legal tracking**: Arkham tracks wallets linked to seized funds, sanctioned entities, and protocol exploits

## Key Features

- **Entity Pages**: comprehensive profiles for funds, exchanges, whales, and protocols with all known wallets
- **Visualizer**: interactive graph showing wallet relationships, fund flows, and counterparty connections
- **Intel Exchange**: bounty marketplace for wallet identification (pay ARKM to learn who owns an address)
- **Alerts**: real-time notifications for wallet movements, large transfers, and entity activity
- **Cross-Chain Tracking**: unified view across multiple blockchains for a single entity
- **Sniper**: track specific token movements across all labeled wallets

## Who Uses It

- Crypto traders monitoring whale and fund movements for trading signals
- On-chain investigators and security researchers tracking hacks and exploits
- Compliance teams and legal investigators tracing illicit fund flows
- Journalists and researchers investigating crypto market manipulation
- DeFi protocols monitoring large holder behavior and potential risks
- Anyone who wants Bloomberg Terminal-level entity intelligence for [[crypto-trading]]

Arkham competes directly with [[nansen]] on wallet labeling but differentiates with the Intel Exchange bounty system, stronger visualization tools, and a more generous free tier.
