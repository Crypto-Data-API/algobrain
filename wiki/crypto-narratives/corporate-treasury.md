Public companies that put crypto (mostly BTC, later ETH/SOL) on their balance sheet move prices through three repeatable patterns. The initial "we're becoming a treasury company" announcement reliably rockets the company's own stock while only mildly nudging the coin; sustained accumulation funded at a premium to net asset value (mNAV) acts as a steady demand pump; and when the coin falls and that premium collapses toward or below 1.0, the same leveraged holders become forced sellers — a reflexive "death-spiral" risk. The category's directional bias is therefore **mixed**: structurally bullish on the way up, structurally bearish on the way down.

## How it moves price

The core driver is **reflexivity around the mNAV premium** — the ratio of a treasury company's market cap to the market value of the crypto it holds.

- When mNAV > 1, a company can issue equity/convertibles/preferreds above net asset value and buy more coin. Each buy is accretive (raises crypto-per-share), validating the premium and enabling the next raise. The other side of the trade is dilutive equity buyers who pay a premium for leveraged exposure, and coin sellers who receive a persistent structural bid.
- When mNAV falls toward or below 1, that machine stops. Accretive issuance ends, leverage and covenants create pressure to monetize, and selling drops more firms below NAV — forcing more selling.

The asymmetry that defines the category: a treasury-adoption **announcement** moves the *stock* violently (it re-rates a sleepy small-cap into a high-beta crypto proxy) but barely moves the *coin* (the first purchase is small versus coin market cap and often already executed). A disclosed corporate **sale** is likewise a weak per-event coin catalyst — the real bearish tail is systemic: a whole cohort holding a large share of supply being forced out together while leveraged longs in the treasury stocks unwind simultaneously.

## Treasury-Adoption Announcement (company pivots to crypto reserve)

**Mechanism.** A public company announces it is adopting BTC/ETH as a primary treasury reserve asset, copying the MicroStrategy playbook. The other side is equity investors who suddenly re-rate the stock as a leveraged, tax-advantaged crypto proxy (and short-sellers who get squeezed). The stock pops violently because the announcement converts a small-cap into a high-beta vehicle that can trade at a large premium to its holdings (mNAV > 1). The coin moves much less — sentiment/adoption-driven, not flow-driven.

**Directional bias:** bullish (on the announcing stock; mild-bullish-to-neutral on the coin).

**Magnitude / lag / duration / recurrence.** +20% to +4000%+ on the announcing stock (size-dependent); BTC/ETH typically -2% to +3% same day. Lag is immediate (same session, often pre-market). The stock pop lasts intraday-to-days, narrative read-across can persist weeks, coin effect <24h. Very frequent in 2024–2025 — dozens of copycats clustering around each high-profile entrant.

**Affected scope:** the announcing company stock, BTC, ETH, other treasury-stock peers (MSTR, SMLR, MARA, SBET).

**Leading signals:** 8-K / press release announcing treasury policy adoption; board approval of crypto-as-reserve; a convertible-note or ATM equity raise filed days before; a beaten-down small-cap with a crypto-aligned chairman; a sudden short-interest spike then squeeze.

## Sustained Accumulation / mNAV-Premium Flywheel (ongoing buys funded at a premium)

**Mechanism.** Once established, a treasury company runs a reflexive flywheel: because its stock trades at a premium to its holdings (mNAV > 1), it issues equity/convertibles/preferreds above NAV and buys more coin. Each buy raises crypto-per-share, validating the premium and enabling the next raise — net new dry powder flowing into the coin. The other side is dilutive stock buyers and coin sellers who get a persistent structural bid. Per buy the coin barely moves, but in aggregate the cohort is a large, price-insensitive, sustained buyer. DATCos deployed an estimated ~$42.7B into crypto in 2025 ($22.6B in Q3 alone, per cited estimates).

**Directional bias:** bullish.

**Magnitude / lag / duration / recurrence.** Individually negligible (<1–2% per buy); the cohort contributes meaningfully to multi-month uptrends. Lag is diffuse — it builds over weeks/quarters rather than per-event. Persists as long as mNAV > ~1 and capital markets stay open (multi-quarter). Continuous through the 2024–2025 bull phase, with weekly purchase 8-Ks from MSTR and peers.

**Affected scope:** BTC, ETH, SOL, the treasury-stock cohort.

**Leading signals:** mNAV premium > 1 (especially > 2); fresh ATM/convertible/preferred raise filings; weekly Monday MSTR purchase 8-Ks; rising "BTC Yield"/coin-per-share metric; a growing count of new treasury entrants (crowding); exchange outflows to corporate custody addresses.

## Distribution / Forced Selling / mNAV-Discount Unwind (the flywheel in reverse)

**Mechanism.** The flywheel runs backward. When the coin falls and the stock's premium collapses to or below NAV (mNAV ≤ 1), accretive issuance stops and pressure builds to monetize — an outright corporate sale (Tesla 2022) or, for over-leveraged firms with covenants, forced liquidation to defend mNAV / service debt. Selling pushes the coin down, dropping more firms below 1 and forcing more sales — a "death spiral." Direct corporate sales tend to produce only a modest, sentiment-driven dip (the sale is often already done); the real bearish tail is systemic — a cohort holding a large share of supply being forced out together, amplified by leveraged longs in the treasury stocks unwinding at the same time.

**Directional bias:** bearish.

**Magnitude / lag / duration / recurrence.** -2% to -5% coin on a single disclosed corporate sale; -10% to -25% coin during a full premium-collapse/forced-selling regime. Sentiment dip is immediate; systemic forced-selling plays out over days-to-weeks. Single-sale dips last hours-to-days; mNAV-unwind regimes persist weeks-to-months. Outright sales are rare (Strategy held through 2023–2025); systemic discount-unwind episodes clustered in late 2025.

**Affected scope:** BTC, ETH, the treasury-stock cohort (MSTR, MARA, SBET, BMNR, Metaplanet), broad crypto.

**Leading signals:** mNAV falling toward/below 1.0; CEO comments about "conditions to sell"; a treasury stock down >50% YoY while the coin is down less (premium gone); debt/preferred coverage stress and covenant headlines; treasury-stock share-buyback announcements (cash drain); "one in three treasuries below NAV" breadth; rising correlation of treasury stocks crashing together.
