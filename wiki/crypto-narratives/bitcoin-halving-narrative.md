The Bitcoin halving cycle is the market's oldest and most reliable crypto narrative: roughly every four years (every 210,000 blocks) Bitcoin's mining reward is cut in half, halving the rate of new BTC supply hitting the market. Historically this has anchored a repeatable ~4-year rhythm — a pre-halving rally into the event, a few months of post-halving chop, then a 12-18 month bull run that tops out, followed by a deep bear market. The big caveat is diminishing returns: the halving-day-to-peak multiple has fallen from roughly 90x+ (2012) to ~30x (2016) to ~8x (2020) to only ~2x (2024) as Bitcoin's market cap has grown and spot-ETF flows have reshaped how price forms.

> **Cycle-breaker (4th halving).** The playbook may be over. **2025 became the first-ever RED post-halving calendar year** — BTC closed ~−6% (~$87,700, *below* its January open), unprecedented vs the hugely-green 2012/2016/2020 post-halving years — with only **~+31% at the 1-year mark** (vs ~+300%/+567% in prior cycles). BTC's S&P/Nasdaq correlation rose sharply (it now trades as a macro/rates asset) and **ETF flows displaced miner-selling** as the dominant supply-side signal, structurally weakening the halving supply-shock thesis. The next halving (~2028) is already being floated as a "buy the dip" narrative — treat the 4-year cycle as a *fading* prior, not a law.

## How it moves price

The mechanical core is supply restriction. Each halving instantly cuts the BTC issued per block (50 → 25 → 12.5 → 6.25 → 3.125), so the daily flow of new coins that miners must sell to cover electricity and hardware drops sharply. If demand holds steady or rises, a smaller marginal buy-side flow now clears the market at higher prices. On top of that mechanical effect sits a reflexive, behavioral one: rising price draws media attention and retail/institutional FOMO, which lifts price further, which draws more attention — until the marginal buyer is exhausted and leverage is maxed, at which point the same loop runs violently in reverse.

Who is on the other side: in the bull leg, short sellers and skeptics who fade the rally early, plus miners and early holders who sell into strength too soon. In the bear leg, the losers are over-levered longs and late entrants who bought near the top; the winners are patient accumulators buying the capitulation low and long-term holders who distributed into the prior strength. Since the 2024 cycle, US spot Bitcoin ETFs have become a structural new counterparty, absorbing miner sell-pressure and front-running the scarcity story so aggressively that BTC made a new all-time high *before* the halving for the first time ever.

## Post-Halving Bull Cycle (the 12-18 month parabolic run)

**Mechanism:** The halving cuts new BTC issuance in half overnight. Daily new supply that miners must sell drops sharply while demand is steady or rising, so a smaller marginal buy-side flow clears the market at higher prices. The move is reflexive — rising price draws attention and FOMO, which lifts price further. The top forms when the marginal buyer is exhausted and leverage is maxed; late entrants are left holding the bag into the subsequent bear market. On the other side: short sellers and skeptics who fade too early, and miners/early holders who sell into strength too soon.

**Directional bias:** bullish.

**Magnitude / lag / duration / recurrence:** Halving-day-to-peak of ~+9000% (2012), ~+2900% (2016), ~+700% (2020), ~+95% (2024) — diminishing each cycle. The parabolic phase typically begins 3-6 months after the halving and runs 12-18 months from halving to cycle top. Recurs once per ~4-year cycle; 4 occurrences 2012-2024.

**Affected scope:** BTC, large-cap alts, entire crypto market.

**Leading signals:**
- Halving event occurs (block height a multiple of 210,000)
- Stock-to-flow ratio doubles
- Miner reserves declining
- BTC reclaims prior cycle ATH
- Rising spot ETF net inflows (2024+ cycle)
- Funding rates turning persistently positive

## Pre-Halving Rally / Buy-the-Rumor

**Mechanism:** In the 6-12 months ahead of a halving, the upcoming supply cut is a well-telegraphed, calendar-certain event. Traders front-run the expected post-halving scarcity narrative, accumulating into the event ("buy the rumor"). Demand front-loads while issuance is still at the old, higher rate, lifting price into the halving. On the other side: late sellers and those who wait for the halving day itself, who find the easy gains already priced. In 2024 this front-running (amplified by the ETF launch) was so strong that BTC set a new ATH *before* the halving for the first time ever — a structural shift in the pattern.

**Directional bias:** bullish.

**Magnitude / lag / duration / recurrence:** Roughly +100% to +180% in the ~12 months leading into the halving. Builds over 6-12 months before the halving date; duration 6-12 months pre-event. Recurs once per ~4-year cycle, ahead of each halving.

**Affected scope:** BTC, large-cap alts.

**Leading signals:**
- Halving date within 12 months
- Rising Google search interest in "bitcoin halving"
- Accelerating spot ETF inflows (2024+)
- BTC trending above its 200-day moving average
- Improving funding / open-interest

## Post-Cycle Bear Market (the deflationary back half of the 4-year cycle)

**Mechanism:** After the post-halving bull tops out (~12-18 months post-halving), the reflexive process runs in reverse. Late buyers who entered on leverage near the top are underwater; as price falls, forced liquidations and capitulation cascade, and the FOMO that drove the run becomes fear. Long-term holders who accumulated cheap distribute into the prior strength, becoming the supply overhang. The bear typically erases 70-85% of the peak before the next pre-halving rally rebuilds a base. Losers: top-buyers and over-levered longs. Winners: patient accumulators buying the capitulation low. This is the bearish mirror image that completes the ~4-year cycle.

**Directional bias:** bearish.

**Magnitude / lag / duration / recurrence:** -70% to -85% peak-to-trough. Begins immediately after the cycle top (~18mo post-halving) and runs ~12 months into the cycle low — roughly the second half of the 4-year cycle. Recurs once per ~4-year cycle, following each bull-market top.

**Affected scope:** BTC, large-cap alts, entire crypto market.

**Leading signals:**
- BTC loses its 200-day moving average after a parabolic run
- Funding rates flip persistently negative
- Exchange net-inflows spike (holders moving to sell)
- Failure to make new highs for >8 weeks after a blowoff top
- Long-term-holder distribution rising
