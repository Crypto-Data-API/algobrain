## Fred's GFC Trades (preserved note)

Like the [[crash-of-1987]], the GFC reinforced the lesson that severe market downturns, while painful, are the single greatest wealth-building opportunities for prepared investors who maintain [[cash-as-asset|cash reserves]]. The lesson is structural: markets overshoot during panics because forced selling (margin calls, fund redemptions, risk-model-driven deleveraging) drives prices well below intrinsic value, creating asymmetric opportunities for those with liquidity and conviction.
