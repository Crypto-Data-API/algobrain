# Market History

Historical events, market cycles, notable trades, and the evolution of financial markets.

Markets are cyclical, and history provides the most valuable dataset for understanding what drives booms, busts, and regime changes. The [[gfc|2008 Global Financial Crisis]] reshaped global regulation and central bank policy. The [[ftx-collapse]] demonstrated that counterparty risk in crypto is existential. The [[gamestop-short-squeeze]] showed that retail coordination can overwhelm institutional positioning.

Studying these events is not just academic -- the same patterns of **leverage, complacency, crowding, and panic** repeat in every market cycle. The recurring template is visible across four decades of volatility shocks: a crowded strategy grows large enough that its own forced unwind becomes the market move it was meant to hedge against.

## The Modern Volatility-Shock Series

A four-decade chain of crashes that share the same crowded-deleveraging DNA. Each is a reference event in the [[long-vol-vs-short-vol]] framework:

- [[black-monday|Black Monday (1987)]] -- DJIA -22.6% in a day; portfolio-insurance feedback loop; birth of the equity volatility skew
- [[long-term-capital-management|LTCM Collapse (1998)]] -- relative-value leverage blow-up; correlations went to 1.0 in stress
- [[gfc|Global Financial Crisis (2007-2009)]] -- SPX -57% over 17 months; subprime / Lehman / AIG
- [[volmageddon|Volmageddon (2018)]] -- inverse-VIX ETP unwind
- [[covid-crash|COVID Crash (2020)]] -- fastest 30%+ drawdown in history; the long-vol monetization event
- [[vix-august-2024-spike|August 2024 VIX Spike]] -- yen-carry + 0-DTE dealer-gamma unwind
