# Bull Markets

Major bull runs, rallies, and periods of sustained market growth.

A **bull market** is conventionally defined as a rise of **20% or more** from a recent low, typically accompanied by broad economic optimism, rising corporate earnings, expanding valuations, and increasing retail participation. Bull markets are where most long-term wealth is created — and where late-stage **euphoria** sows the seeds of the next [[bear-markets-overview|bear market]]. The dominant dynamics are **[[momentum]] feedback loops**, **multiple expansion** (rising P/E ratios), FOMO-driven retail inflows, and **sector rotation**.

## Anatomy of a Bull Market

| Phase | Behaviour |
|---|---|
| **Accumulation** | Begins amid disbelief at the bear-market low; "smart money" buys while sentiment is still bleak |
| **Public participation** | Earnings recover, breadth broadens, trend becomes obvious; the longest, most profitable phase |
| **Euphoria / blow-off** | Valuations stretch, leverage ([[margin]] debt) builds, speculative narratives dominate, retail piles in at the top; breadth narrows even as indices make new highs |

The euphoria phase is recognisable by behavioural tells: parabolic price action in story stocks, IPO/token-launch frenzy, dismissal of valuation as "old thinking," and record [[margin]] balances. These are late-cycle signals, not timing tools — bubbles run further and longer than reason suggests (see [[poseidon-bubble]]).

## Catalogue of Major Bull Markets

| Bull market | Approx. gain | Duration | Driver |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Post-WWII boom (1949-1968)** | S&P 500 ~+1,100% | ~19 years | Reconstruction, consumer expansion, demographics |
| **1982-1987** | S&P 500 ~+230% | ~5 years | Disinflation (Volcker), falling rates; ended in [[1987-stock-market-crash\|Black Monday 1987]] |
| **1990s tech rally (1990-2000)** | S&P 500 ~+400%, Nasdaq ~+1,000% | ~10 years | Internet, productivity boom; ended in dot-com bust |
| **2009-2020** | S&P 500 ~+400% | ~11 years | Longest US bull on record; ZIRP, QE, tech megacaps; ended by [[covid-crash\|COVID]] |
| **Post-COVID recovery (2020-2021)** | S&P 500 ~+114% off the low | ~2 years | Fiscal + monetary stimulus, reopening, retail/meme surge |
| **2023-2025 AI bull** | S&P 500 / Nasdaq new highs | ongoing as of 2026 | AI capex cycle, megacap earnings, rate-cut expectations |
| **Crypto bull cycles (2013, 2017, 2021)** | BTC +1,000% to +10,000% per cycle | ~1 year each | Halving-driven supply, narrative cycles, retail inflows |

Crypto bull cycles are shorter but far more explosive than equity bulls, historically clustering around Bitcoin's [[bitcoin-halving|halving]] events and ending in 80%+ drawdowns. Each cycle has had a dominant narrative (2017 ICOs, 2021 DeFi/NFTs/L1s), echoing the equity-market pattern where "in every boom a dominant theme emerges that lets valuations disengage from reality" (see [[poseidon-bubble]]).

## Trading the Bull

- **Ride the trend** — [[trend-following]] and [[momentum]] strategies do their best work in sustained bulls; cutting winners early is the dominant mistake.
- **Respect breadth divergences** — when fewer stocks make new highs while the index does, leadership is narrowing, a late-cycle warning.
- **Mind valuation and leverage as risk gauges, not timing signals** — stretched P/E and record [[margin]] debt raise the *fragility* of the market without telling you *when* it breaks.
- **Plan the exit before euphoria** — the time to build [[cash-as-asset|cash reserves]] and hedges is during the euphoria phase, not after the top; see [[bear-markets-overview]] and [[market-crashes]].
- **Beware late-cycle FOMO** — the largest retail inflows reliably arrive near tops; doing the opposite of the crowd's late-stage behaviour is the edge.
