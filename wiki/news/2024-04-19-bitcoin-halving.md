# Fourth Bitcoin Halving

## What Happened

On April 19, 2024, the fourth [[bitcoin]] halving occurred at block 840,000. The block reward was cut from 6.25 BTC to 3.125 BTC per block, reducing new daily BTC issuance from approximately 900 BTC to 450 BTC. This programmatic supply reduction happens every 210,000 blocks (roughly every four years) and is hardcoded into Bitcoin's protocol. At the time of the halving, BTC was trading at approximately $63,000. Notably, this was the first halving where BTC had already surpassed its previous cycle ATH beforehand, a dynamic unique to the ETF-driven demand era.

## Market Impact

- **BTC price at halving**: ~$63,000
- **Immediate reaction**: Dipped approximately 5% before stabilizing and recovering
- **Daily new supply**: Reduced from ~900 BTC/day to ~450 BTC/day
- **Annual inflation rate**: Dropped below 1% for the first time, making BTC scarcer than gold by this metric
- **Miner revenue**: Block subsidy halved, but Ordinals/inscriptions and high transaction fees partially offset the loss
- **Mining stocks**: Mixed reactions — efficient miners with low costs rallied, marginal operators sold off

## Why It Matters

Every previous halving has been followed by a massive bull run within 12-18 months. The 2024 halving was unique because BTC had already set a new ATH before it, driven by ETF demand from the [[2024-01-10-bitcoin-spot-etf-approval]]. The combination of halved supply and sustained institutional demand through ETFs created an unprecedented supply squeeze. With issuance dropping to 450 BTC/day while ETFs alone were often absorbing 1,000-5,000+ BTC/day, basic supply-demand dynamics pointed strongly upward.

## Aftermath

After a consolidation period through Q2 and Q3 2024, the halving's impact became evident in Q4 when BTC surged past its March ATH and eventually broke [[2024-12-bitcoin-100k]]. Ordinals and inscriptions, which had gained popularity throughout 2023-2024, provided supplemental fee income for miners, helping the ecosystem adjust to the reduced block subsidy. Less efficient mining operations were forced to shut down or consolidate, while major players like Marathon and Riot expanded through acquisitions of distressed assets.

## Trading Lessons

- **"Sell the halving" was a trap**: The initial 5% dip shook out weak hands, but the macro trajectory was up
- **Historical patterns rhymed**: The 12-18 month post-halving bull run pattern held again
- **Supply math compounds with demand catalysts**: Halving + ETF inflows was a double supply shock
- **Miners adapt or die**: Ordinals fees and operational efficiency separated winners from losers
- **Patience is required**: The biggest post-halving gains came 6-8 months later, not immediately
- **Each halving is different**: Previous halvings happened at much lower prices without ETF demand — context matters for applying historical patterns
- **Watch miner capitulation as a signal**: Post-halving miner stress created a temporary sell-side overhang before clearing
- **Ordinals changed the fee equation**: Transaction fee revenue from inscriptions meant the halving was less devastating to miners than feared
