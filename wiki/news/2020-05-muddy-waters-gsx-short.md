# Muddy Waters Short on GSX Techedu (May 2020)

On **May 18, 2020**, Muddy Waters Research published a 28-page report alleging that GSX Techedu (NYSE: GSX, later renamed **Gaotu Techedu** in 2021) — a Chinese online K-12 tutoring company — had fabricated as much as **70% of its reported revenue**. The report was the first of three Muddy Waters publications on GSX in 2020 and remained a high-conviction Muddy Waters short until the stock collapsed in March 2021 as part of the [[archegos-blowup-2021|Archegos cascade]] — making GSX one of the largest single-name profit drivers for Muddy Waters in 2021.

## Background

GSX Techedu IPO'd on NYSE in **June 2019** at $10.50/share. The company reported explosive growth: 2019 revenue of RMB 2.1B (+432% YoY), 2020 revenue of RMB 7.1B (+239% YoY). Stock peaked at **$149.05 on January 27, 2021** — a 14x gain from IPO, against a fundamental backdrop that competing analysts (J Capital, Citron) considered impossible.

Co-founded by **Larry Chen Xiangdong** (former New Oriental executive); listed in NY despite being a fully Chinese-domiciled education business — typical of the post-2014 China-ADR class via VIE structures.

## The Muddy Waters Reports

**Carson Block / Muddy Waters Research** published three GSX reports in 2020:

| Date | Title | Key Allegation |
|------|-------|----------------|
| May 18, 2020 | *Down with the GSX-ness* | 70% of paid users are bots; revenue inflated 70%+ |
| Jun 30, 2020 | *Smoking Gun Evidence of Massive GSX Fraud* | Class-attendance data shows ~80% of "students" are non-existent |
| Sep 2, 2020 | *Last but Not Least* | Insider connections to undisclosed related-party transactions |

The reports drew on:
- Web-traffic / class-attendance scraping showing ~80% of recorded students never logged in.
- Cross-referencing GSX-cited live-class concurrent counts with technical infrastructure constraints.
- Insider whistleblower accounts.
- Bot-network forensic analysis of "user" accounts.

**Citron Research** (Andrew Left) and **J Capital Research** (Anne Stevenson-Yang) followed with parallel reports alleging similar revenue fabrication. SEC opened an inquiry in late 2020.

## The Stock's Path

Despite the public short reports, GSX rallied massively through 2020-early 2021:
- May 18, 2020 (first MW report): close $32.95.
- Aug 2020: $98.
- Nov 2020: $112.
- Jan 27, 2021: peak $149.05.

The pump was reportedly driven by — among others — **Bill Hwang's Archegos** building a major undisclosed position via [[total-return-swap|TRS]] across multiple prime brokers. Other concentrated holders included Tiger Global and various China-focused tech funds.

Muddy Waters and other shorts experienced ~3x mark-to-market losses on the position from the May 2020 report through Jan 2021 peak — a textbook "right thesis, wrong timing" episode that would have ended many short funds.

## The Crash (March 2021)

The [[archegos-blowup-2021|Archegos cascade]] forced Goldman Sachs and Morgan Stanley to dump their GSX TRS hedge positions on **Friday March 26, 2021**:

- Pre-cascade close (Mar 25): ~$93.
- Friday open block: ~$60.
- Friday close: ~$33.
- April 5: $13.
- Net: **-91% from January peak in 10 weeks**.

Then the China regulatory hammer hit: **July 2021** Beijing's "Double Reduction" policy effectively banned for-profit K-12 tutoring overnight. GSX/Gaotu's core business was destroyed by regulation; stock dropped to ~$2 by August 2021.

## Muddy Waters' P&L

Carson Block / Muddy Waters never disclosed exact numbers but the trade is understood to have been one of their largest 2021 contributors. Estimated based on disclosed peak short notional (~$50-100M) and the realised path (~85% drawdown from average short entry):
- Approximate P&L: **$200M-$500M** on the GSX short alone.

Made GSX one of Muddy Waters' top-3 lifetime trades alongside Sino-Forest (2011) and Luckin Coffee (2020).

## How Other Players Profited

1. **Citron / Andrew Left** — also short, similar P&L profile.
2. **J Capital / Anne Stevenson-Yang** — similar.
3. **Generalist short-only funds** (Kerrisdale, Spruce Point) — moderate gains.
4. **Volatility funds** — long GSX put options into the cascade printed.
5. **CDS-on-China-ADR-basket strategies** (uncommon but executed by some EM funds).

## Consequences and Lessons

1. **Right thesis ≠ profitable thesis without survival.** Muddy Waters held GSX short through a 4x mark-to-market drawdown. Many funds would have been forced to cover.
2. **Short-only funds need either deep balance-sheet or short-dated structures (puts) to survive bubble extensions.**
3. **The catalyst that finally vindicates the short is often unrelated to the thesis.** Muddy Waters was right that GSX was a fraud; what crashed it first was a forced-seller cascade, and what *finished* it was Beijing regulation. Neither was in the original report.
4. **China-ADR fraud + concentrated US hedge-fund ownership = recurring pattern.** Luckin, GSX, and the broader 2020-2022 China-ADR delisting wave followed this template.
