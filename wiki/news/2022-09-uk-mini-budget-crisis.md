# UK Mini-Budget Crisis (September 2022)

On **Friday, September 23, 2022**, UK Chancellor **Kwasi Kwarteng** delivered a "Growth Plan" mini-budget under Prime Minister **Liz Truss** featuring £45B of unfunded tax cuts — the largest UK tax giveaway since 1972. Markets reacted with extreme violence: GBP/USD crashed to an all-time low of $1.0327 within 72 hours; 30-year gilt yields rose 100+ bp in three sessions; **Liability-Driven Investment (LDI)** strategies used by UK pension funds came within hours of forced collateral defaults that would have triggered systemic collapse.

The Bank of England intervened on September 28 with emergency gilt buying. Truss's premiership effectively ended within weeks; she resigned October 20, 2022 — the shortest tenure in British history at 49 days.

## Background

Liz Truss became Prime Minister September 6, 2022, succeeding Boris Johnson. She and Chancellor Kwarteng campaigned on a "growth-through-tax-cuts" platform. The September 23 mini-budget delivered:

- Cancel planned corporation-tax rise from 19% to 25%.
- Cut top income-tax rate from 45% to 40%.
- Reverse 1.25% NI rise.
- Stamp-duty cut.
- £150B energy-price guarantee (already announced).
- All without independent OBR forecast or fiscal-credibility framework.

**Total unfunded tax cuts: ~£45B/year.**

Markets assessed: UK already running large twin deficits (current account + fiscal), inflation 10%+, BoE in tightening cycle. Mini-budget would expand fiscal stimulus into an inflationary economy — requiring more BoE tightening, more bond supply, weaker GBP.

GBP fell ~9% in 72 hours. 30Y gilt yields rose 130 bp in 4 sessions — historically a multi-month move compressed into days.

## The LDI Mechanism (Why It Nearly Broke the System)

UK defined-benefit pension funds use **Liability-Driven Investment (LDI)** to match their long-dated liabilities. In LDI structures:

1. Pension fund posts collateral (typically gilts) to a derivatives counterparty.
2. Counterparty provides interest-rate swap exposure that hedges the pension's liabilities.
3. When gilt yields rise, the value of the gilt collateral falls, but the value of the liability hedge also falls (offsetting from pension's perspective).
4. **However:** the swap MTM creates a margin call on the pension fund.

When 30-year gilt yields spiked 100+ bp in 4 days, LDI structures faced **simultaneous double pressure**:

- Gilt collateral lost ~20-30% of value.
- Swap counterparties demanded additional margin.
- Funds had to **sell gilts to raise cash** — pushing yields up further.
- Self-reinforcing fire sale: each margin call triggered more selling, which raised yields, which triggered more margin calls.

By September 28 morning, **dozens of UK pension funds were within hours of default** on margin calls. Some funds reportedly faced complete insolvency.

## BoE Intervention

At **11:00 BST on September 28**, the BoE announced emergency gilt purchases up to £65B (£5B/day for 13 days) targeting long-dated gilts to "restore orderly market conditions."

Yields collapsed instantly. The intervention:
- Was contradictory to QT (BoE was selling gilts under quantitative tightening days earlier).
- Was financial-stability driven, not monetary policy.
- Stabilized markets within hours.
- Set a precedent: BoE acts as backstop for UK pension system.

## Triangular Aspects

The crisis created multiple cross-rate dislocations:

**Triangle 1: GBP-USD-EUR.** GBP fell against USD by 5-9%, against EUR by 3-5%. Cross-rate via USD vs direct quote diverged by 30-100 bp during peak Sunday-Monday Asia session when London markets closed.

**Triangle 2: GBP-Gilt-USD.** A short-GBP / short-Gilt / long-Treasury triple was the canonical macro trade. GBP and Gilts moving in lockstep is the textbook "fiscal-crisis" signal.

**Triangle 3: GBP-CDS-Treasury.** UK sovereign CDS widened from 25 bp to 50 bp; gap to gilt yield rise indicated market pricing of redenomination/restructuring risk (wildly inappropriate but reflected panic).

## Winners

**Crispin Odey / Odey Asset Management** — Reportedly Odey had short gilt positions and short GBP via various vehicles for months ahead of the mini-budget. Estimated profits: **£250M+** for the firm in September. Odey had publicly criticized UK fiscal trajectory for years.

**Hedge funds short UK rates / GBP** — Numerous macro funds (Brevan Howard, Caxton, Bridgewater) had medium-conviction UK shorts; gains in the £100-500M range.

**Sovereign CDS specialists** — Boutique CDS desks profited as UK CDS spreads widened.

**Inflation-linked vs nominal arbs** — UK 10Y breakeven inflation widened materially; long inflation-linked / short nominal-gilt structures profited.

## Losers

**UK pension funds with leveraged LDI** — Estimated £125-200B in losses across the system; many funds saw 20-30% collateral-value drawdowns.

**UK retail savers** — Mortgage-rate spike triggered ~30% increase in monthly payments for new variable-rate borrowers.

**Liz Truss's premiership** — 49 days, ending October 20, 2022. Replaced by Rishi Sunak (October 25).

**Kwasi Kwarteng** — Fired October 14, 2022.

## Lessons for Traders

1. **Fiscal credibility is repriced violently.** Markets give 0 days of grace when a credible-currency country abandons fiscal orthodoxy.
2. **Pension-system feedback loops are systemic.** The LDI cascade was a 2022 echo of LTCM 1998.
3. **Central-bank credibility constraints.** BoE was forced to do QE while announcing QT — protocol-broken signal.
4. **GBP is structurally vulnerable.** Twin deficits + inflation + tightening = peg-break-style move even without an explicit peg.
5. **The triangle confirms the directional view.** Cross-rate consistency between GBP, Gilts, and CDS during the move validated the macro thesis.

## Aftermath

- Sunak/Hunt reversed almost all mini-budget measures by October 17.
- BoE QE (financial-stability) extended to October 14.
- LDI structures mandated to hold larger liquidity buffers (regulator response 2023+).
- BoE balance-sheet QT resumed in November 2022.
- UK fiscal-credibility took 12+ months to restore.
- Truss became cautionary tale; "lettuce won" (live-stream by *The Star*).
