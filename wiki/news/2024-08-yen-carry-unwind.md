# Yen Carry Unwind (August 5, 2024)

The **yen carry unwind of early August 2024** was a global deleveraging event in which a sudden strengthening of the [[japanese-yen|Japanese yen]] forced the rapid liquidation of yen-funded [[carry-trade|carry trades]] across equities, FX, and crypto. It peaked on **August 5, 2024**, when the Nikkei 225 fell **-12.4%** in a single session — its worst day since Black Monday 1987 — and dragged risk assets worldwide lower, producing the [[2024-08-vix-spike|August 2024 VIX spike]] to roughly 65. The shock was driven not by an economic crisis but by a positioning unwind: years of cheap [[japanese-yen|JPY]] borrowing had funded leveraged longs everywhere, and a modest [[bank-of-japan|Bank of Japan]] policy shift was enough to trigger a self-reinforcing cascade.

## The Carry Trade Setup

For more than a decade the yen was the world's premier **funding currency**. With BoJ policy rates pinned near or below zero, traders and institutions borrowed in yen at almost no cost and bought higher-yielding assets — US Treasuries, dollar money markets, the Mexican peso, US tech equities, even crypto. The trade earns the interest-rate differential (the "carry") so long as the yen stays weak or stable. Estimates of total yen-funded carry exposure at the peak ranged widely, commonly cited in the **$500 billion to over $1 trillion** range across all channels.

The risk in any carry trade is asymmetric: small, steady gains from the rate differential punctuated by sudden, large losses when the funding currency appreciates. Yen appreciation simultaneously (a) raises the cost of repaying the yen loan and (b) triggers margin calls on the levered asset side — forcing buyers to sell exactly when liquidity is worst.

## Triggers

| Date | Catalyst |
|------|----------|
| **Jul 11, 2024** | Suspected BoJ/MoF FX intervention pushes USDJPY off ~161, its weakest since 1986 |
| **Jul 31, 2024** | BoJ raises policy rate to ~0.25% and signals balance-sheet tapering — more hawkish than expected |
| **Aug 1, 2024** | Soft US ISM manufacturing; Fed seen behind the curve on cuts |
| **Aug 2, 2024** | Weak July US payrolls (114K vs ~175K; unemployment 4.3%) triggers "Sahm Rule" recession fears, narrowing the US–Japan rate gap that underpins the carry |
| **Aug 5, 2024** | Cascade peaks: Nikkei 225 -12.4%; USDJPY falls toward ~141.7 from ~150s; global risk assets sell off |
| **Aug 7, 2024** | BoJ Deputy Governor Uchida walks back hawkishness, pledging no hikes during market instability — calming the unwind |

The combination of a hawkish BoJ (yen stronger) and a dovish-forcing US payroll miss (rate differential narrowing) attacked the carry trade from both ends at once.

## The Cascade Mechanics

1. **Yen appreciation** raised repayment costs and breached stop-losses on short-JPY positions.
2. **Forced JPY buying** to cover those shorts pushed the yen up further — a reflexive loop.
3. **Margin calls** on the leveraged asset side (Nikkei, US tech, crypto) forced simultaneous selling.
4. **Cross-asset contagion**: the same leveraged books spanned FX, equities, and crypto, so liquidation in one bled into all. [[bitcoin|Bitcoin]] fell sharply alongside equities, confirming the move was about funding/leverage rather than any single market's fundamentals.
5. **Volatility feedback**: equity dealers caught short gamma amplified the equity leg, producing the [[2024-08-vix-spike|VIX spike to ~65]].

## Aftermath and Recovery

The unwind was violent but brief. After the BoJ's August 7 walk-back, the yen stabilized, margin pressure eased, and risk assets staged a sharp V-shaped recovery — the S&P 500 recouped its losses within about two weeks and the Nikkei recovered most of the drop. Like the [[2024-08-vix-spike|VIX spike]], the episode left little fundamental damage but served as a stark reminder of how much hidden leverage the yen carry trade injects into global markets.

## Trading Lessons

- **Funding-currency risk is systemic, not local.** A BoJ decision became a global equity and crypto event because the yen funds leverage everywhere.
- **Carry is short volatility.** Steady carry income masks a fat left tail; position sizing must survive a multi-sigma funding-currency move.
- **Correlations go to one in a deleveraging.** Diversification across asset classes fails when the same levered hands hold all of them.
- **Central-bank reaction functions matter.** The BoJ's rapid verbal intervention (Aug 7) was the proximate circuit-breaker — policy walk-backs can end positioning unwinds as fast as policy shifts start them.
