# Ethereum Spot ETFs Begin Trading

## What Happened

On July 23, 2024, spot [[ethereum]] ETFs began trading on U.S. exchanges after a two-step approval process. The SEC had approved the 19b-4 filings in May 2024 (a surprise to many who expected denial), followed by S-1 effectiveness in July. Products included BlackRock's ETHA, Fidelity's FETH, Bitwise's ETHW, 21Shares' CETH, VanEck's ETHV, Invesco Galaxy's QETH, Franklin Templeton's EZET, and the Grayscale Ethereum Trust (ETHE) conversion. ETH was trading at approximately $3,400 at launch. The May approval had caught markets off guard, as prediction markets had placed the probability of approval below 25% just days before the decision.

## Market Impact

- **ETH price at launch**: ~$3,400
- **Initial inflows**: Significantly lower than BTC ETFs on a comparable basis
- **Grayscale ETHE**: Experienced substantial outflows mirroring the GBTC pattern, as investors rotated to lower-fee alternatives
- **Net flows**: Turned negative in early weeks due to ETHE redemptions overwhelming new product inflows
- **ETH/BTC ratio**: Continued declining, as ETH ETF flows disappointed relative to BTC ETF success
- **Competing L1s**: Solana and other L1 tokens were largely unaffected

## Why It Matters

The Ethereum ETF approval was significant as a regulatory milestone — the SEC had previously suggested ETH might be a security. Approval of a spot ETF effectively confirmed ETH's commodity status. However, the market reaction demonstrated diminishing returns: BTC ETFs were a paradigm shift, while ETH ETFs were an incremental follow-on. The staking yield exclusion (ETH ETFs could not stake) reduced their appeal compared to holding ETH directly.

## Aftermath

ETH ETF flows remained muted compared to BTC ETFs through the rest of 2024. The "buy the rumor, sell the news" dynamic played out clearly — ETH rallied on the May approval surprise but faded through the actual launch. ETH eventually benefited from the broader Q4 crypto rally driven by the [[2024-11-05-trump-election-crypto-rally]], but the ETH/BTC ratio continued to underperform throughout the year. The experience tempered expectations for future altcoin ETF products and highlighted that each asset requires its own demand thesis beyond the ETF wrapper itself.

## Trading Lessons

- **Second-mover disadvantage**: ETH ETFs lacked the novelty factor that made BTC ETFs historic — market had already priced in the ETF paradigm
- **Grayscale outflow pattern repeats**: The ETHE-to-low-fee rotation mirrored GBTC exactly — a predictable headwind
- **"Buy the rumor, sell the news"**: Classic pattern played out cleanly; the May surprise approval was the better entry than the July launch
- **No staking = reduced appeal**: Excluding staking yield made ETH ETFs structurally less attractive than direct ownership
- **Manage expectations across assets**: Success of one ETF product does not guarantee equal success for the next
- **Prediction markets can misprice**: The sub-25% approval odds before the May surprise showed how wrong consensus can be
- **Regulatory milestones have lasting value even if price disappoints**: ETH's commodity status confirmation had long-term structural significance
- **Compare flow dynamics across assets**: ETH ETF net flows provided a real-time relative-value signal versus BTC ETFs
