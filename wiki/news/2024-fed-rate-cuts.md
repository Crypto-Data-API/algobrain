# Federal Reserve Begins Rate Cutting Cycle

## What Happened

On September 18, 2024, the Federal Reserve cut the federal funds rate by 50 basis points, from 5.25-5.50% to 4.75-5.00%, marking the first rate cut since March 2020. This ended the most aggressive hiking cycle since the early 1980s, during which the Fed had raised rates from near zero to 5.25-5.50% between March 2022 and July 2023. The Fed followed with 25bps cuts in November and December, bringing the rate to 4.25-4.50% by year end. The 50bps opening move was larger than many economists expected, with Fed Chair Powell citing confidence that inflation was on a sustainable path back to 2%.

## Market Impact

- **S&P 500**: Rallied to new all-time highs, finishing 2024 up ~23%
- **Nasdaq**: Outperformed on growth stock rotation, led by 2024-nvidia-ai-boom and the AI sector
- **Bitcoin**: Benefited from looser financial conditions; BTC thrives in expanding liquidity environments
- **Bonds**: Treasury yields fell; TLT and long-duration bonds rallied
- **US Dollar (DXY)**: Weakened as rate differential narrowed versus other currencies
- **Housing**: Mortgage rates began declining from 8%+ peaks, thawing the frozen housing market
- **Gold**: Rallied to new all-time highs above $2,700/oz

## Why It Matters

The rate cutting cycle confirmed the "soft landing" narrative — the Fed had tamed inflation from 9%+ to near its 2% target without triggering a recession. This was historically rare and extremely bullish for risk assets. The 50bps opening cut signaled the Fed was confident enough to move aggressively. Looser monetary policy expanded the liquidity environment that drives both traditional risk assets and crypto markets. The pivot validated those who had been positioning for cuts throughout 2024.

## Aftermath

The combination of rate cuts and the [[2024-11-05-trump-election-crypto-rally]] created a powerful tailwind for risk assets into year end. Lower rates reduced the opportunity cost of holding non-yielding assets like [[bitcoin]] and gold, both of which hit new all-time highs. Equity markets broadened beyond mega-cap tech as lower rates benefited rate-sensitive sectors like utilities, REITs, and small caps. The housing market began a slow thaw as mortgage rates declined from their 8%+ peaks.

## Trading Lessons

- **"Don't fight the Fed" works both ways**: The hiking cycle crushed risk assets in 2022; the cutting cycle supercharged them in 2024
- **The first cut size matters**: A 50bps cut signaled urgency and confidence simultaneously — it set the tone for aggressive easing
- **Crypto is a liquidity sponge**: BTC's correlation to global liquidity conditions was reinforced; rate cuts = more liquidity = higher BTC
- **Rate-sensitive sectors lead on pivots**: REITs, small caps, and housing-related stocks outperformed once cuts began
- **Position before consensus**: By the time cuts are announced, much of the move has already happened — the time to buy is during the debate
- **The soft landing trade was real**: Those who bet on no recession and rate cuts were rewarded across every asset class
- **Watch the dot plot**: Fed forward guidance through the dot plot moved markets as much as the actual cuts
- **Global rate cycles matter**: Other central banks (ECB, BOE) also cut, creating a synchronized global easing wave
