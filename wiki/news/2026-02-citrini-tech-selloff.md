In **February 2026**, [[citrini-research]] published its **"2028 Global Intelligence Crisis"** scenario — positing 10%+ unemployment and ~40% equity decline if superintelligent AI deployment outpaces labor reabsorption. The publication triggered a broad sell-off in service sectors and is widely regarded as the first organized AI tail-risk scenario to gain institutional distribution (Source: [[2026-04-22-gap-finder-possible-ai-fueled-global-job-loss-reces]]).

## What happened

- Citrini Research published the "2028 Global Intelligence Crisis" scenario in early February 2026.
- The scenario quantified an explicit AI tail risk: 10%+ unemployment, ~40% equity decline.
- The market sold off broadly, with service sectors hit hardest:
  - Finance
  - Real estate
  - Media
  - Medical services
  - Legal services
- These service sectors fell **alongside software** — breaking the previous narrative in which only software companies were considered AI-displaceable.
- The move was led by the [[technology]] complex and concentrated in [[nasdaq|Nasdaq]]-listed names, where AI-displacement exposure and rich valuations overlap most directly — the [[nasdaq|Nasdaq-100]] was the epicentre before the selloff broadened into the white-collar service basket.
- Energy rotated **+12% YTD** as tech weakened, illustrating an active sector reallocation rather than a simple risk-off move.

## Why it mattered

- **First named, distributed AI tail-risk scenario**: the institutional desk community had been talking about AI risk in vague terms; Citrini gave them a quantified scenario they could plug into VaR and stress tests.
- **Broadened the displacement basket**: the move proved the market believes AI displacement extends well beyond software into core white-collar service industries.
- **Established energy as the AI hedge**: energy outperformance during the selloff cemented [[ai-sector-rotation-energy-hedge]] as a working hedge for AI-displacement equity exposure, supported by AI data center power demand (see [[ai-data-center-power-demand]]).
- **Set precedent for follow-on research**: through 2026, several major sell-side firms published their own variants of the framework, all tracing back to Citrini's distribution.

## Trading implications

- **Short-basket construction**: the sectors that fell on Citrini's scenario (finance, real estate, media, medical, legal, software) now form the natural short-basket for AI-displacement themes.
- **Energy as hedge**: energy + utilities pair well with AI-displacement shorts; the +12% YTD rotation demonstrated reflexivity.
- **Tail-risk overlay**: VIX hedging and scenario PnL stress now reference the Citrini scenario explicitly.
- **Event risk on Citrini publications**: subsequent Citrini scenario updates should be treated as event risk for short-vol books.
