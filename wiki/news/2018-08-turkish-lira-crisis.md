# Turkish Lira Crisis (August 2018)

On **Friday, August 10, 2018**, the Turkish lira (TRY) collapsed **20% in a single day** against the US dollar, capping a multi-month decline that took USD/TRY from ~3.80 (January 2018) to ~7.20 (August peak) — a **47% loss for lira holders in 8 months**. The proximate trigger was Trump's tariffs on Turkish steel and aluminum (announced in retaliation for the detention of US pastor Andrew Brunson). The deeper cause was 5+ years of Erdoğan's heterodox monetary policy, current account deficit financed by short-term USD flows, and political pressure on the central bank against rate hikes.

Macro funds short TRY made meaningful profits; long-Turkey bond funds and European banks with Turkish exposure took losses; Turkish retail and corporates with USD-denominated debt were severely impaired.

## Background

Turkish economic structure (2013-2018):
- High GDP growth (~5-7% per year) financed by **short-term USD borrowing** (current account deficit ~5-6% of GDP).
- Inflation persistently 8-15%.
- Erdoğan publicly espoused unorthodox view that *high* interest rates *cause* inflation — pressured central bank to keep rates too low.
- Turkish corporates accumulated $300B+ in USD-denominated debt.
- Erdoğan re-elected June 2018 with expanded executive powers.

The carry-trade was attractive: TRY rates 17.75% (mid-2018) vs USD 2%. ~15% APR if you ignored FX risk. Foreign holdings of Turkish lira bonds peaked ~$30B in 2017.

## The Trigger

**August 10, 2018:** Trump tweets:
> "I have just authorized a doubling of Tariffs on Steel and Aluminum with respect to Turkey... Their currency, the Turkish Lira, slides rapidly downward against our very strong Dollar! Relations with Turkey are not good at this time!"

Trump's tariffs were retaliation for Turkey's detention of **Pastor Andrew Brunson** on terrorism charges since 2016. The narrow trigger was symbolic but markets read it as confirmation that Turkey-US relations had broken down.

## Triangular Aspects

The lira crisis offered classic EM-triangular trades:

**Triangle 1: TRY-USD-EUR.** Turkish bank exposure was heavily European (Spain's BBVA via Garanti, Italy's UniCredit via Yapı Kredi, BNP Paribas via TEB). EUR weakness on Turkish-bank fears created TRY/EUR vs TRY/USD vs EUR/USD inconsistencies.

**Triangle 2: TRY-USD-Russian ruble.** Both EMs were under sanctions pressure. Cross-rate dislocations between TRY/RUB and the USD-implied alternative offered 50-150 bp spreads.

**Triangle 3: TRY spot vs TRY NDF vs TRY swap.** Turkish Central Bank attempted to support TRY by squeezing offshore swap markets — leading to massive divergence between onshore TRY rates (artificially low) and offshore swap-implied rates (40-1000% briefly). Specialist FX arbs profited from the fragmentation.

**Triangle 4: TRY-Turkish 5Y CDS-Turkish equity.** Turkish bank stocks (Garanti, Akbank, Yapı Kredi) collapsed; CDS widened from 200 to 600+ bp; lira fell. Cross-asset triangle for those modeling Turkish systemic risk.

## Winners

**Macro funds short TRY** — Multiple, including Caxton, Brevan Howard, Tudor, several Asian-based EM funds. Estimated $100-500M cumulative profits.

**Vol funds long TRY puts** — Implied vol on TRY went from 12% to 60%+; OTM puts had multi-x returns.

**Short-EU-bank specialists** — BBVA -25% on Turkish-exposure fears; specialist short funds profited.

**Turkish-debt-restructuring specialists** — Boutique distressed funds bought Turkish corporate USD bonds at 50-70¢ during peak panic; recovered to 80-90¢ within 12 months.

## Losers

**Turkish corporates with USD debt** — $300B+ outstanding; lira collapse increased domestic-currency burden by 50%+. Many ultimately required restructuring.

**Turkish retail with mortgages** — TRY mortgage rates spiked; defaults rose.

**Long-only EM bond funds** — PIMCO, Templeton, JPMorgan EM debt funds took 5-15% drawdowns.

**European banks with Turkish subsidiaries** — BBVA Garanti (Spain), UniCredit Yapı Kredi (Italy), BNP TEB (France) all took mark-to-market losses; Spanish/Italian sovereign CDS widened on contagion.

**Carry-trade investors** — Long TRY bond positions wiped out 12+ months of carry in days.

## Aftermath

- **September 13, 2018:** Turkish CB hiked rates 6.25% (to 24%) — finally orthodox response.
- **October 12, 2018:** Pastor Brunson released; US-Turkey relations partial reset.
- **2019-2020:** Lira stabilized at 5.50-6.00 vs USD.
- **2021:** New crisis as Erdoğan again pressed central bank to cut rates despite high inflation.
- **2022:** TRY reached 18.50; "lira-protected deposit" scheme bailed out savers.
- **2023:** Erdoğan re-elected; finally appointed orthodox finance minister Mehmet Şimşek.
- **2024:** TRY around 32; rate hikes restored to 50%; gradual disinflation.

## Lessons for Traders

1. **Political interference with central banks is a leading indicator.** Erdoğan's anti-rate-hike statements predicted the crisis 2-3 years in advance.
2. **Carry trades concentrate political risk.** 15% APR yield came with 47% peak FX loss — Sharpe was negative once realized.
3. **External political triggers can crystallize structural fragility.** Trump's tariff was symbolic; the lira was already in fragile structural state.
4. **Central-bank swap-line manipulation is a recurring EM signal.** Turkey's TRY swap squeeze was a classic "deny the bears" maneuver — and a clear marker of dysfunction.
5. **European-bank exposure to EM is the contagion vector.** Spanish/Italian banks lost on Turkey, then 2022-2023 on Russia, then 2024 on China.
