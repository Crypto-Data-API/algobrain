# Bitcoin Bull Run and Multiple All-Time Highs in 2025

## What Happened

Bitcoin continued its post-halving, post-ETF, post-Trump bull run throughout 2025, printing multiple new all-time highs. The convergence of three powerful catalysts — the April 2024 halving reducing supply issuance, spot ETF inflows providing persistent demand, and the Trump administration's pro-crypto executive order — created the most structurally bullish environment in Bitcoin's history. Institutional adoption accelerated dramatically: sovereign wealth funds disclosed BTC positions, corporate treasuries expanded beyond MicroStrategy's pioneering model, and pension funds gained exposure through regulated ETF wrappers. ETF assets under management grew massively, making [[bitcoin]] a macro asset traded alongside gold, treasuries, and oil.

## Market Impact

- **BTC price**: Multiple new all-time highs through the year, driven by structural demand
- **Spot ETF AUM**: Grew to record levels as institutional inflows accelerated
- **MicroStrategy (MSTR)**: Continued aggressive BTC accumulation, stock became a leveraged BTC proxy
- **Mining equities**: Rallied as BTC price outpaced post-halving revenue compression
- **BTC dominance**: Remained elevated as institutional capital preferred Bitcoin over altcoins
- **Gold correlation**: Strengthened, reinforcing the "digital gold" narrative
- **Altcoins**: Eventually caught a bid in later quarters as profits rotated down the risk curve

## Why It Matters

The 2025 bull cycle was fundamentally different from previous cycles. Instead of being driven primarily by retail speculation and leverage, it was underpinned by structural institutional demand through ETFs and corporate treasuries. This changed the character of BTC volatility — drawdowns were shallower and recoveries faster because ETF inflows provided a buy-the-dip mechanism absent in prior cycles. The "digital gold" narrative, long dismissed by traditional finance, gained credibility as BTC and gold correlations strengthened during periods of macro uncertainty. [[dollar-cost-averaging]] strategies were vindicated as consistent buyers through the cycle outperformed those attempting to time entries.

## Aftermath

Bitcoin's 2025 performance cemented its status as a permanent allocation in institutional portfolios. Financial advisors began recommending 1-5% BTC allocations as standard practice. The success of the bull run fueled the [[2025-defi-renaissance]] as capital rotated into the broader crypto ecosystem. However, the cycle also attracted excessive leverage and speculative behavior in altcoin markets, setting the stage for inevitable corrections. By early 2026, BTC traded around $69K — below its highs but well above pre-cycle levels — as the Iran conflict and tariff uncertainty created macro headwinds.

## Trading Lessons

- **Structural demand changes cycle dynamics**: ETF inflows created a fundamentally different buyer profile than prior cycles
- **[[dollar-cost-averaging]] outperforms timing**: Consistent accumulation through the cycle beat attempts to catch exact bottoms
- **Halving supply reduction is a slow burn**: The effect compounded over months, not days — patience was required
- **Narrative convergence amplifies moves**: Halving + ETFs + government policy created a triple tailwind
- **Correlation shifts matter**: BTC's growing gold correlation meant traditional macro frameworks became more applicable
- **Corporate treasury adoption is a leading indicator**: Track 13F filings and corporate announcements for demand signals
- **Cycle character evolves**: Shallower drawdowns and faster recoveries reflected institutional buy-the-dip behavior absent in prior cycles
