Wealth building is the long-term process of creating financial independence through [[compounding]], regular investment, and debt management. Fred McNaught summarises his core principle as: "lower consumption + higher investment = more wealth." He emphasises that wealth creation is not about earning a high income but about consistently investing a meaningful portion of income over decades. Fred's own approach combines a disciplined [[savings-rate]] with a [[two-portfolio-strategy]] that separates growth and income objectives, allowing [[compounding]] to work across both portfolios simultaneously.

Key wealth-building principles include starting early (to maximise the [[compounding]] runway), minimising fees and taxes (see [[tax-efficiency]]), maintaining an appropriate [[asset-allocation]], and avoiding the behavioural mistakes that destroy compounding -- panic selling during drawdowns, chasing performance, and excessive trading. The most commonly cited heuristic is the "rule of 72": dividing 72 by the annual return gives the approximate number of years to double an investment. At 7% annual returns, wealth doubles roughly every 10 years.
