Savings rate is the ratio of money saved to total income, typically expressed as a percentage. A higher savings rate provides more capital for investment and accelerates [[wealth-building]] through [[compounding]]. Fred McNaught emphasises his mother's disciplined savings as the foundation of his family's wealth, demonstrating that consistent saving over decades matters more than investment sophistication. Fred considers the savings rate the single most controllable variable in wealth creation, since market returns are uncertain but the amount you set aside is entirely within your control.

The financial independence community popularised a rough rule of thumb: at a 50% savings rate with reasonable investment returns, financial independence can be reached in approximately 17 years; at 25%, it takes roughly 32 years. The relationship between savings rate and time-to-independence is non-linear -- each incremental percentage point saved early in the journey has an outsized impact compared to marginal increases later. Automating savings (pay yourself first) and tracking the rate monthly are simple but effective behavioural tools for maintaining discipline.
