Work-life balance is the equilibrium between professional commitments and personal life. Fred McNaught discusses the "10-year sacrifice" principle: work intensely hard in your early career to maximise income and savings, then let [[compounding]] do the heavy lifting for the remaining decades. He argues that front-loading effort creates a larger capital base earlier, which compounds dramatically over a 30-40 year investment horizon. This approach trades short-term work-life balance for long-term financial freedom, though Fred acknowledges it requires discipline and a supportive personal environment.
