# Centralized Finance (CeFi) vs Decentralized Finance (DeFi)

## Overview

CeFi and [[defi]] represent two competing models for financial services. CeFi -- platforms like [[binance]], Coinbase, and traditional banks -- relies on trusted intermediaries that custody funds, verify identities, and manage operations. DeFi uses smart contracts on blockchains to provide permissionless financial services without intermediaries. The tension between these models shapes crypto markets, regulatory debates, and how traders access yield, lending, and trading.

## Comparison Table

| Dimension | CeFi | DeFi |
|---|---|---|
| **Custody** | Institution holds funds | Self-custody (your keys) |
| **KYC/Identity** | Required | Not required (permissionless) |
| **Typical Yields** | 1-5% (savings, staking) | 3-20%+ (variable, higher risk) |
| **Primary Risk** | Counterparty / bankruptcy | Smart contract bugs / exploits |
| **Regulation** | Heavy, growing | Light, evolving |
| **Transparency** | Opaque (trust the company) | Fully on-chain (verifiable) |
| **Composability** | Siloed products | "Money legos" (protocols compose) |
| **Insurance** | FDIC (banks), some SIPC | Limited (Nexus Mutual, etc.) |
| **Accessibility** | Account + identity required | Wallet + internet only |
| **User Experience** | Polished, familiar | Complex, requires crypto literacy |
| **Speed of Innovation** | Slow (compliance-gated) | Fast (permissionless deployment) |

## Key Differences

**Custody and Trust** is the philosophical divide. CeFi requires trusting an institution with your assets. When that trust is well-placed (regulated banks, major exchanges), the experience is smooth. When it is not, the consequences are catastrophic -- Celsius, FTX, and Voyager demonstrated that CeFi counterparty risk can vaporize billions overnight. [[defi]] eliminates custodial risk but replaces it with smart contract risk and user responsibility.

**Transparency** is objectively better in DeFi. Every transaction, every pool balance, and every protocol parameter is verifiable on-chain. CeFi platforms can misrepresent reserves, commingle funds, or hide insolvency -- as several did spectacularly in 2022. DeFi's transparency does not eliminate risk, but it makes hidden fraud structurally harder.

**Composability** is DeFi's most powerful innovation. Protocols can build on top of each other without permission: deposit collateral in Aave, receive aTokens, stake those in a yield farm, use the LP token as collateral elsewhere. This "money legos" architecture enables financial products that would take years to build in CeFi. It also creates systemic risk through cascading dependencies.

**Regulation and Compliance** favor CeFi for institutional adoption. Banks, funds, and corporations need regulatory clarity, compliance frameworks, and legal recourse. DeFi's permissionless nature is simultaneously its greatest feature and its biggest barrier to institutional capital.

**User Experience** still favors CeFi significantly. Creating a [[binance]] account and buying crypto takes minutes. Navigating DeFi -- managing wallets, signing transactions, understanding gas fees, bridging between chains -- requires substantial technical knowledge and introduces real risk of user error.

## When to Use Each

**Choose CeFi when** you need regulatory compliance, institutional-grade custody, fiat on/off ramps, familiar UX, and insurance protections. Best for beginners, institutional traders, and anyone who values simplicity and legal protections over maximum yield.

**Choose [[defi]] when** you want self-custody, permissionless access, higher yield opportunities, on-chain transparency, and composable financial products. Best for crypto-native users comfortable with wallet management and smart contract risk.

**Use both when** you bridge between worlds: hold reserves on regulated CeFi platforms for fiat access and stability, while deploying capital into DeFi for yield opportunities and novel financial instruments.

## Verdict

Neither model is universally superior. CeFi offers familiarity, regulation, and ease of use but demands trust in fallible institutions. [[defi]] offers transparency, composability, and permissionless innovation but requires technical sophistication and exposes users to smart contract risk. The 2022 CeFi blowups strengthened the case for DeFi's transparency, while DeFi's own exploits remind users that "trustless" does not mean "riskless." Most active traders use both.
