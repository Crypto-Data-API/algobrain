## Overview

[[crypto-markets]] and [[forex]] are both global, decentralized markets for trading currencies, but they operate at vastly different scales and with different risk profiles. Forex is the largest financial market on Earth at $7.5 trillion daily volume, dominated by institutional players trading major currency pairs. Crypto trades ~$150 billion daily, driven by retail speculation and a growing institutional presence. Both attract technical traders, but the structural differences in volatility, leverage, and regulation create very different trading environments.

## Key Differences

**Volatility is the most obvious divide.** [[forex]] majors like EUR/USD might move 0.5-1% on a big day. [[bitcoin]] can move 5-10% on a normal day, and altcoins can move 20-50%. This means crypto offers larger profit opportunities per trade but also demands much tighter [[risk-management]] and smaller [[position-sizing]] relative to account size.

**Leverage compensates for forex's lower volatility.** Forex brokers offer 50-500x [[leverage]] specifically because currency pairs move so little. A 0.5% move at 100x leverage is a 50% account gain or loss. Crypto already has extreme volatility, so using high leverage on crypto is exponentially riskier. A 5% move at 100x = [[liquidation]]. This is why experienced traders use far less leverage in crypto than in forex.

**Liquidity depth is not comparable.** The EUR/USD pair alone trades over $1 trillion daily. The entire crypto market trades ~$150B. This means [[slippage]] on large orders is virtually nonexistent in forex majors but can be significant in crypto, especially for altcoins. However, for typical retail position sizes, both markets offer adequate [[liquidity]].

**Fundamental analysis is completely different.** Forex traders study central bank policy, interest rate differentials, employment data, and inflation reports. Crypto traders analyze on-chain metrics, token unlocks, protocol revenue, developer activity, and social media sentiment. The intellectual frameworks barely overlap, though [[macro-economics]] increasingly influences both.

**Regulation creates a trust gap.** Forex brokers are regulated by well-known agencies (CFTC, FCA, ASIC) with segregated client funds and compensation schemes. Crypto exchanges operate in a regulatory gray zone where funds can be frozen, exchanges can collapse (FTX), and recourse is limited. This gap is narrowing but remains significant.

## When to Use Each

**Choose [[crypto-markets]] when:**
- You want 24/7 market access including weekends
- You thrive on [[volatility]] and larger daily ranges
- You want exposure to a new, high-growth asset class
- You want [[self-custody]] options and decentralized trading
- You are comfortable with less regulatory protection

**Choose [[forex]] when:**
- You want the deepest [[liquidity]] in the world with minimal [[slippage]]
- You prefer lower [[volatility]] with higher [[leverage]]
- You want well-regulated brokers with segregated funds
- You are a macro trader focused on central bank policy
- You want tighter spreads and more predictable cost structures

**Consider trading both when:**
- You want market access across different sessions and time zones
- You apply [[technical-analysis]] methods that work in any liquid market
- You want to diversify trading income across uncorrelated strategies
- You use forex for macro directional trades and crypto for higher-volatility setups
- You want to develop skills across multiple market structures

## Verdict

Crypto and forex attract similar personality types (technical traders, leverage users, global market participants) but offer very different experiences. Forex is the mature, stable, regulated choice with razor-thin spreads and deep liquidity. Crypto is the volatile, innovative, 24/7 frontier with larger moves and higher risk. Many traders eventually trade both, applying [[technical-analysis]] skills across markets. The critical adjustment when switching between them is leverage and [[position-sizing]]: what works in forex volatility will destroy you in crypto volatility.
