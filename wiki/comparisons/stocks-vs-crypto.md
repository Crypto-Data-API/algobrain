## Overview

Stocks and crypto represent two fundamentally different market structures. stock-markets are centuries-old, heavily regulated, and backed by company earnings and assets. [[crypto-markets]] are barely a decade old, lightly regulated, and driven by network adoption, speculation, and tokenomics. Understanding the structural differences between these asset classes is critical for anyone allocating capital across both, which increasingly describes the modern retail and institutional trader.

## Comparison Table

| Dimension | Stocks (stock-markets) | Crypto ([[crypto-markets]]) |
|---|---|---|
| **Trading Hours** | 9:30 AM - 4:00 PM ET, Mon-Fri (with pre/after hours) | 24/7/365, never closes |
| **Regulation** | Heavy: SEC, FINRA, exchanges, auditors | Light: evolving; SEC, CFTC jurisdictional confusion |
| **Volatility** | s-and-p-500 avg ~15% annual std dev | [[bitcoin]] avg ~60-80% annual std dev; altcoins much higher |
| **Fundamentals** | Earnings, revenue, P/E ratios, dividends | Tokenomics, TVL, active addresses, adoption metrics |
| **Leverage** | 2x margin (Reg T); 4x day trading; futures higher | Up to 125x on [[perpetual-futures]] exchanges |
| **Custody** | Broker holds shares; SIPC insured up to $500K | Exchange custody or [[self-custody]] via wallets |
| **Trading Fees** | $0 commissions at most brokers (PFOF model) | 0.01-0.5% per trade depending on exchange |
| **Tax Treatment** | LTCG/STCG; wash sale rules apply | LTCG/STCG; no wash sale rule (as of 2025 in US) |
| **Market Maturity** | 200+ years; deep institutional infrastructure | ~15 years; rapidly maturing but still early |
| **Institutional Adoption** | Universal: pensions, endowments, sovereign funds | Growing: [[btc-etf]], corporate treasuries, hedge funds |
| **Liquidity** | Extremely deep for large caps ($500B+ daily US equities) | Deep for BTC/ETH; thin for most altcoins |
| **Information Asymmetry** | Insider trading laws; mandatory disclosures (10-K, 10-Q) | Minimal disclosure; whales and insiders trade freely |

## Key Differences

**24/7 markets change everything.** [[crypto-markets]] never close. There is no overnight gap risk, but there is also no break. Major price moves happen on weekends, holidays, and at 3 AM. This creates both opportunities (reacting to news faster) and risks (you cannot step away without [[stop-loss]] orders or risk management automation).

**Volatility is structurally higher in crypto.** A 5% daily move in the s-and-p-500 makes headlines and triggers circuit breakers. A 5% daily move in [[bitcoin]] is a normal Tuesday. Altcoins routinely move 20-50% in a day. This [[volatility]] cuts both ways: larger gains and larger drawdowns. [[position-sizing]] matters enormously.

**Leverage access is dramatically different.** US stock traders get 2x margin under Reg T and 4x intraday under the pattern day trading rule. Crypto traders on [[binance]] or [[hyperliquid]] can access 50-125x [[leverage]]. This is why crypto produces both spectacular gains and total account wipeouts far more frequently.

**Fundamental analysis diverges.** Stock analysts examine earnings, cash flow, management, and competitive moats using decades of established frameworks like value-investing. Crypto analysis is more nebulous: protocol revenue, total value locked, token emissions schedules, developer activity, and narrative momentum. The information gap favors insiders in crypto far more than in regulated equities.

**Tax treatment has a notable difference.** The wash sale rule prevents stock traders from selling at a loss and immediately rebuying to claim the tax deduction. Crypto was historically exempt from this rule, allowing tax-loss-harvesting strategies that stock traders cannot use. Check current rules as this is changing.

## When to Use Each

**Focus on stocks when:**
- You want mature [[regulation]] and investor protections
- You prefer fundamental analysis with audited financial data
- You need SIPC-insured custody and stable broker infrastructure
- You want dividends and long-term compounding via [[index-funds]]

**Focus on crypto when:**
- You want 24/7 market access and global liquidity
- You are comfortable with higher [[volatility]] and risk
- You want exposure to decentralized technology and Web3
- You need access to high [[leverage]] for small-account trading
- You value [[self-custody]] and permissionless markets

**Consider both when:**
- You want a diversified portfolio across traditional and emerging assets
- You can size each allocation appropriately for its risk profile
- You understand that crypto [[correlation]] to stocks increases during macro stress events
- You want to apply [[technical-analysis]] across multiple market structures
- You are building long-term wealth and want exposure to different growth drivers

## Verdict

Stocks and crypto are complementary, not competing. Stocks offer stability, deep regulation, fundamental transparency, and centuries of track record. Crypto offers asymmetric upside, 24/7 access, permissionless innovation, and a new asset class uncorrelated (sometimes) to traditional markets. A diversified portfolio increasingly includes both. The key is sizing crypto exposure appropriately for its [[volatility]]: most advisors suggest 1-10% of total portfolio in crypto, adjusted for individual risk tolerance and conviction.
