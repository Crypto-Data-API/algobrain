# Scalping vs Position Trading

## Overview

[[scalping]] and position trading sit at opposite ends of the trading timeframe spectrum. Scalpers hold trades for seconds to minutes, extracting tiny profits from rapid-fire transactions. Position traders hold for weeks to months, capturing major trend moves and sometimes earning [[carry-trade]] income along the way. These styles demand entirely different skills, tools, temperaments, and capital structures. Choosing between them is less about which is "better" and more about which matches your personality and resources.

## Comparison Table

| Dimension | Scalping | Position Trading |
|-----------|----------|-----------------|
| **Holding Period** | Seconds to minutes | Weeks to months |
| **Trades Per Day** | 50-200+ | 0-1 (may go days without a trade) |
| **Profit Per Trade** | 1-10 ticks / 0.01-0.1% | 5-30% or more |
| **Capital Required** | Moderate ($25K+ for US stocks, lower for futures) | Large (to withstand drawdowns over time) |
| **Tools Needed** | Level 2, DOM, fast execution, low-latency connection | Charts, fundamental data, economic calendar |
| **Commission Impact** | Critical (can eat all profits) | Negligible per trade |
| **Stress Level** | Extreme (constant rapid decisions) | Low (infrequent decisions, wide stops) |
| **Screen Time** | 100% of trading session | Minutes per day for review |
| **Lifestyle** | Full-time, intense, exhausting | Part-time compatible, low maintenance |
| **Edge Source** | Microstructure, speed, tape reading | Macroeconomic analysis, trend identification |
| **Retail Success Rate** | Very low (hardest style for retail) | Higher (time and trends work in your favor) |

## Key Differences

**Speed vs patience.** Scalping is the fastest form of trading, requiring split-second decisions and flawless execution. Position trading is the slowest, requiring weeks of patience as a trade develops. A scalper might make more trades before lunch than a position trader makes in a quarter.

**Edge and competition.** Scalpers compete directly with high-frequency trading firms and market makers who have faster technology and lower costs. The edge for retail scalpers has eroded significantly. Position traders compete on analysis and patience -- areas where retail traders can match or exceed institutional players because they have no mandate to be fully invested.

**Profit mechanics.** Scalping profits come from accumulating many tiny gains. A single bad trade can erase hours of work. Position trading profits come from a few large winners that more than compensate for smaller losses. The math is fundamentally different and attracts fundamentally different personalities.

**Physical and mental toll.** Scalping is physically demanding: hours of intense focus, rapid mouse clicks, and constant adrenaline. Many scalpers burn out within a few years. Position trading is mentally demanding in a different way -- enduring drawdowns over weeks requires emotional discipline -- but it is physically sustainable over a career.

**Transaction costs.** With 100+ trades per day, commissions and slippage are the scalper's biggest enemy. Even pennies per share multiply into significant costs. Position traders may pay a few commissions per month, making transaction costs almost irrelevant to their returns.

## When to Use Each

**Choose scalping when:**
- You thrive in fast-paced, high-pressure environments
- You have access to professional-grade execution tools and low commissions
- You are trading liquid instruments (ES futures, major forex pairs)
- You can dedicate full market hours to active trading
- You have a proven edge in order flow or microstructure

**Choose position trading when:**
- You prefer low-maintenance, low-stress trading
- You have a larger capital base that can absorb multi-week drawdowns
- You want trading compatible with a career or other pursuits
- You are better at analysis than real-time execution
- You want time and compounding working in your favor

## Verdict

Position trading is objectively more suitable for the vast majority of retail traders. The lower stress, lower transaction costs, reduced competition with algorithms, and lifestyle compatibility make it the rational choice. Scalping, while potentially lucrative, has the lowest retail success rate of any trading style because the edge is thin, the competition is fierce, and the psychological demands are extreme. Unless you have genuine edge in microstructure and execution infrastructure, position trading will serve you far better over a career.
