## Overview

[[robinhood]] and Interactive Brokers (IBKR) represent opposite ends of the brokerage spectrum. Robinhood democratized stock trading with a beautiful mobile app and zero commissions, targeting beginners. IBKR built the most powerful professional trading platform available to retail, offering access to 150+ markets in 33 countries. The tradeoff is stark: simplicity vs power, free vs cheap-but-feature-rich, beginner-friendly vs professional-grade.

## Key Differences

**Execution quality is the hidden cost of free.** [[robinhood]] makes money through payment for order flow (PFOF), selling your orders to market makers like Citadel Securities. The SEC has scrutinized this practice because it can result in worse fill prices. On a large order, the execution difference might cost more than IBKR's commissions would have. IBKR Pro's SmartRouting actively seeks the best price across exchanges, potentially saving more on large trades than the commission costs.

**Platform depth is incomparable.** Robinhood's interface is designed for simplicity: a clean mobile app where you can buy stocks in seconds. IBKR's Trader Workstation (TWS) is a full professional terminal with customizable layouts, over 100 [[order-types]], real-time risk analytics, portfolio margining, and algorithmic order execution. The learning curve for TWS is steep, but it provides capabilities that Robinhood cannot match.

**Margin rates create a massive gap for leveraged traders.** Robinhood charges 12-13% on margin (8% with Gold subscription). IBKR charges 5.8-6.8%. On a $50,000 margin balance, that is a difference of $2,000-3,500 per year. For anyone using [[margin]] regularly, IBKR pays for itself immediately.

**Global market access opens entirely different strategies.** Robinhood offers only US-listed securities. IBKR gives access to 150+ exchanges in 33 countries, including international stocks, futures, [[forex]], and bonds. For traders who want to buy European equities, trade Nikkei futures, or access [[forex]] pairs, there is no comparison.

**Options capability separates beginners from professionals.** Robinhood offers basic [[options]] trading: single legs and simple spreads. IBKR supports every options strategy imaginable, portfolio margin (lower requirements for hedged positions), and direct access to options exchanges. Professional options traders cannot use Robinhood for complex multi-leg strategies.

## When to Use Each

**Choose [[robinhood]] when:**
- You are a beginner buying your first stocks or [[index-funds]]
- You want the simplest possible mobile trading experience
- You trade small positions where execution quality matters less
- You want easy access to crypto alongside stocks
- You value design and simplicity over features

**Choose Interactive Brokers when:**
- You are an active trader who needs professional tools
- You trade [[options]] with complex multi-leg strategies
- You need access to international [[stock-markets]] and [[forex]]
- You use [[margin]] and want the lowest rates available
- You want superior [[order-types]] and execution quality
- You manage a large portfolio and need real-time risk analytics

**The graduation path is common:**
- Start on [[robinhood]] to learn the basics: buying stocks, understanding [[order-types]], building habits
- Notice limitations: poor fills, lack of international access, basic charting
- Open an IBKR account for serious trading while keeping Robinhood for casual positions
- Eventually consolidate to IBKR once you need portfolio margin, global access, and professional tools

## Verdict

These brokers serve fundamentally different users. [[robinhood]] is the gateway drug: it gets people into markets with zero friction and zero cost. Interactive Brokers is the professional platform: once you outgrow Robinhood's limitations, IBKR is where serious traders graduate. The common path is to start on Robinhood, learn the basics, realize you need better tools and execution, and migrate to IBKR. There is no shame in using Robinhood for simple buy-and-hold investing, but if you are actively trading [[options]], using [[margin]], or want global access, IBKR is objectively the better platform.
