# GLP-1 Megatrend → LLY/NVO Long, Staples & Dialysis Short

## Headline

Per [[eli-lilly]], LLY is a Pharmaceuticals leader with 5y avg EBITDA
margin **29.4%** and ROE **70.8%** — the cleanest "quality compounder" in
healthcare per the Fred framework. Mounjaro and Zepbound (tirzepatide)
plus Novo's Ozempic and Wegovy (semaglutide) have created a
multi-hundred-billion-dollar obesity-pharmaceuticals category essentially
from scratch in three years. Late-stage pipeline (LLY's oral orforglipron
and triple-agonist retatrutide; NVO's amycretin) extends the moat. The
basket has **zero healthcare exposure today** — this is a major structural
gap, and the GLP-1 megatrend is the highest-conviction healthcare theme.

**Long LLY + NVO calls; short staples (KO, MDLZ) and dialysis (DVA) as
the read-across short legs that benefit from the *same* thesis playing
out (obesity prevalence falls → less calorie consumption → fewer
ESRD patients).**

## Thesis

Four reinforcing inputs:

1. **Demand exceeds supply for the third year running.** LLY and NVO both
   have years of capacity buildouts ahead. Until supply normalizes, every
   incremental dose sold drops to operating margin at >70% incremental
   contribution. Per [[eli-lilly]], LLY operating margin expanded from
   19.2% (2023) to 28.2% (2024) — that's the demand-supply imbalance
   monetizing.
2. **Pipeline expands TAM.** Once-monthly injectables, oral GLP-1s, and
   triple-agonist combinations (GLP-1 + GIP + glucagon) extend addressable
   patients from "willing to inject weekly" to "anyone with a pulse."
   Each pipeline asset adds tens of billions of TAM. Phase 3 readouts on
   orforglipron and retatrutide are clean, dated catalysts inside the
   180-day window.
3. **Demand-destruction in adjacent categories is already empirically
   visible.** Walmart announced GLP-1 patients reduce grocery spending
   ~10%. Aircraft seat-belt-extender demand has fallen. Per [[resmed]],
   sleep-apnea device demand softened on GLP-1 weight loss (RMD has
   already lapped this). The read-across short basket — sugary drinks
   (KO), packaged snacks (MDLZ), salty staples (KMB indirectly), and
   dialysis (DVA, where ESRD prevalence tracks Type 2 diabetes
   prevalence) — has multi-quarter earnings headwinds that consensus
   under-prices.
4. **Coverage expansion is the asymmetric kicker.** CMS has begun
   covering GLP-1s for obesity indications selectively. Each expansion
   adds 5-10M addressable patients. Private insurers follow Medicare.

## Risks

- **Compounding-pharmacy ruling.** A court ruling that materially
  expands the FDA's permission for compounding pharmacies to produce
  copycat semaglutide/tirzepatide would break the price-power thesis.
  Active legal docket — watch closely.
- **Safety signal.** Any cardiovascular, thyroid, gastric, or
  pancreatitis safety signal in a long-term GLP-1 trial would be
  catastrophic for the long basket. The trade carries genuine
  binary-event risk.
- **Valuation premium.** LLY at 81x trailing P/E (per [[eli-lilly]] at
  source date) is priced for perfection. A modest miss compresses the
  multiple meaningfully.
- **Short-side beta.** KO/MDLZ are defensives; in a sharp risk-off, they
  outperform even if their fundamental thesis is right. Size accordingly
  and consider buying SPY puts as additional offset rather than
  concentrating short basket risk.
- **Already crowded.** GLP-1 has been the consensus 2024-2025 long. Check
  IV-rank on LLY/NVO; if 30-day IV is in top quartile, defer entry or
  use call verticals to manage vega.
- **Disruptive new mechanism.** Gene therapy for obesity, hypothalamic
  modulation, novel pathways could obsolete GLP-1s. Multi-year tail risk;
  not material in 180-day window.
