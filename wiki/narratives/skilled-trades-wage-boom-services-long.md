# Skilled Trades Wage Boom → Long Electrical / Mechanical Services

## Headline

The [[ai-layoff-trap]] hits cognitive labor; the [[skilled-trades-wage-boom]] is its inverse — the trades that AI cannot displace are seeing wage acceleration and multi-year backlog visibility from the data-center + reshoring buildout. PWR, MTZ, FIX are the public pure-plays. Distinct from [[ai-layoff-trap-capex-beneficiaries]] (which focuses on power-generation and electrical-equipment OEMs like VRT, CEG, ETN); this narrative is the **labor-services layer** — the humans who actually install the capex.

## Thesis

Three reinforcing forces drive this basket through 2026:

1. **AI-capex labor demand** — every $1B of data-center capex requires ~3,000–5,000 skilled-trades hours. PWR (Quanta Services) is the dominant electrical-services contractor for grid + data center; backlog has grown ~30%+ YoY through 2025. MTZ (MasTec) covers communications + power infrastructure. FIX (Comfort Systems) is the HVAC/mechanical layer that AI data centers absolutely require for cooling.
2. **Wage premium acceleration** — per [[skillit-construction-wages|Skillit]] and BLS data, electrician/HVAC/mechanical wages are running 8–12% YoY vs ~3–4% for the broader workforce. The wage premium is structural (apprenticeship pipeline lag) and translates directly to pricing power for the contractor margins.
3. **Reshoring + grid modernization compounding** — beyond AI, the IRA Section 48 / CHIPS Act / grid-modernization spending stack creates concurrent demand on the same labor pool. Backlog visibility extends 2–4 years for the primary names.

The basket complements (does not duplicate) the [[ai-layoff-trap-capex-beneficiaries]] capex narrative: that captures the *equipment OEM* layer (VRT cooling, CEG generation, ETN switchgear); this captures the *services / installation* layer.

## Why now

- **Q2 2026 earnings (June–August)** is the next discrete catalyst. The pattern from prior prints: backlog growth + book-to-bill > 1.0 → multiple expansion. PWR especially has been printing cleanly.
- **Hyperscaler capex guidance has not been cut** — MSFT/GOOGL/META/AMZN remain elevated through FY26. As long as that holds, services basket has visibility.
- **Wage data continues accelerating** — Skillit's monthly skilled-trades wage prints have not yet rolled over. Real-time confirmation of the structural premium.
- **180-day horizon** covers two earnings cycles, at least two hyperscaler capex updates, and one or two major contract-award announcement cycles.

## Risks

- **Hyperscaler capex peak** — if MSFT/GOOGL guides FY27 capex flat-to-down, the entire long basket de-rates. This is the cleanest macro invalidator. Monitor each quarterly capex update.
- **Wage data plateau** — if Skillit / BLS skilled-trades wage prints flatten for 2+ months, the structural premium thesis weakens (still works on backlog, but margin tailwind compresses).
- **Single-name execution risk** — PWR/MTZ have lumpy contract awards; missing a major bid (e.g., a hyperscaler reassigning vendor) could whiplash the stock 10–15%. Mitigation: basket structure across 3 primary + 4 secondary names.
- **Recession dominates** — broader economic downturn pulls forward project cancellations even with capex commitments. SPY put hedge mitigates.
- **Crowded trade** — PWR especially is well-known consensus long. Watch IV-rank and managed-money positioning. Don't pay 80th-percentile vega.
- **Pigouvian tax** — political probability of [[pigouvian-automation-tax]] rising could slow AI capex broadly, hurting the basket. Tail risk.
