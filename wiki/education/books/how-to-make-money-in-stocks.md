**How to Make Money in Stocks** by [[william-oneil|William O'Neil]] (4th edition, 2009; first published 1988) is the canonical text for the [[canslim|CAN SLIM]] growth-investing system — a rules-based methodology that fuses [[fundamental-analysis]] and [[technical-analysis]] to identify high-growth stocks at optimal entry points. O'Neil, founder of *Investor's Business Daily* and the popularizer of the [[cup-with-handle]] chart pattern, derived the system from an exhaustive historical study of every major stock-market winner since the 1880s, identifying the characteristics they shared *before* their big advances.

## Core Thesis

The biggest stock-market winners are *not* cheap, contrarian, value plays. They are leading companies with accelerating earnings, new products or services, tight share structures, and heavy institutional accumulation — and they are bought at the precise moment they break out of a sound consolidation pattern, in an uptrending market. O'Neil's central, counter-intuitive claim is that **what looks too high and risky to the majority usually goes higher, and what looks low and cheap usually goes lower.** Discipline, not prediction, is the source of edge: a mechanical 7–8% stop loss combined with rigorous selection lets winners run while capping every loss, producing an asymmetric payoff over many trades.

## The CAN SLIM System

Each letter is a screen that a candidate must pass before purchase (see [[canslim]] for the full strategy page):

| Letter | Criterion | O'Neil's threshold / guidance |
|--------|-----------|-------------------------------|
| **C** | Current quarterly earnings | EPS up ≥ 25% YoY (the best show 50–100%+ and acceleration); strong sales too |
| **A** | Annual earnings growth | 3–5 years of consistent annual EPS growth (≥ 25%/yr), high ROE (≥ 17%) |
| **N** | New products, management, or price highs | A catalyst — new product/service, new mgmt, industry change — plus stock making *new* highs out of a base |
| **S** | Supply and demand | Tight share float; volume surge (≥ 40–50% above average) on the breakout day |
| **L** | Leader or laggard | Buy the #1 or #2 leader in a strong group; [[relative-strength]] (RS) rating ≥ 80 |
| **I** | Institutional sponsorship | Increasing ownership by quality mutual/pension/hedge funds — the "smart money" buying power |
| **M** | Market direction | Trade *with* the general market; ~3 of 4 stocks follow the market; use a "follow-through day" to confirm new rallies |

## Chapter / Section Themes

- **The CAN SLIM framework** — one chapter (or several) per letter, each with annotated charts of historical winners.
- **Reading charts and recognizing base patterns** — the [[cup-with-handle]], flat base, double-bottom, ascending base, and saucer; what valid vs. faulty bases look like; pivot points and proper buy points.
- **When to sell and cut losses** — the 7–8% stop-loss rule, profit-taking discipline (often take gains at 20–25%), and sell signals (climax tops, breaking key moving averages, churning).
- **Picking the best market themes and groups** — group/industry leadership, sector rotation, and avoiding laggards in the same group as a leader.
- **Money management and psychology** — concentration over diversification for the active investor, pyramiding into winners, avoiding common emotional mistakes.
- **Market timing** — using the general-market averages, distribution days, and follow-through days to get invested or move to cash.
- **Models of greatest winners** — a historical gallery (e.g., names spanning a century of market history) illustrating that the CAN SLIM template repeats across eras.

## Key Concepts and Takeaways

| Concept | Takeaway |
|---------|----------|
| Complete system | All seven CAN SLIM criteria must be met — no cherry-picking; discipline over discretion. |
| Earnings acceleration | Current quarterly EPS ≥ 25% is the floor; acceleration signals expanding advantage ([[earnings-momentum]]). |
| Proper bases | Buy breakouts from tight, well-formed bases (5–26+ weeks); never chase extended stocks. |
| Relative strength | RS rating ≥ 80 — buy leaders, not laggards ([[relative-strength]]). |
| Volume confirmation | Breakout-day volume ≥ 40–50% above average confirms institutional demand. |
| Stop loss | Cut all losses at 7–8%, mechanically, no exceptions — the system's risk cornerstone ([[risk-management]]). |
| Institutional power | Only sustained institutional buying can drive a stock from $30 to $300; individuals can't. |
| Tight action | Tight, orderly consolidation = supply drying up; loose/wide/volatile bases = distribution. |
| Market timing | Three of four stocks follow the market; "M" is the most important variable. |
| Historical repeatability | Winners share the same pre-advance template because human/institutional behavior is constant. |

## Criticisms and Limitations

- **Possible backtest overfitting.** Critics argue CAN SLIM's thresholds were reverse-engineered from a hand-picked sample of past winners (survivorship-flavored), so the rules may fit history better than the future.
- **Cyclicality.** As a growth/[[momentum]] system, CAN SLIM tends to outperform in trending bull markets and struggle badly in choppy, mean-reverting, or sharply rotating regimes (e.g., value-led periods).
- **Whipsaw cost of tight stops.** The 7–8% stop is disciplined but can be repeatedly triggered in volatile, sideways markets, producing a string of small losses (death by a thousand cuts).
- **Subjectivity in chart reading.** Despite the quantitative screens, identifying a "proper" base and a clean pivot retains a discretionary, pattern-recognition element that is hard to fully systematize.
- **Data/tooling dependence.** Full implementation leans on IBD's proprietary EPS/RS ratings and screens (now MarketSmith), creating an ecosystem dependency.
- **Independent academic validation is mixed.** The American Association of Individual Investors (AAII) has tracked a CAN SLIM screen with strong long-run results, but peer-reviewed replication is limited and methodology-sensitive.

## Who Should Read This

Growth-oriented stock traders who want a clearly defined, rules-based system; technical analysts who want to fold fundamental criteria into their process; and anyone frustrated by vague advice — every CAN SLIM decision has a number attached. Best suited to intermediate traders comfortable with chart patterns who want a systematic framework that marries technicals and fundamentals. Pair it with [[breakout-strategies]] and [[risk-management]] for a complete operating playbook.

## How It Applies to AI Trading

CAN SLIM is one of the most naturally automatable discretionary systems because nearly every criterion has a quantifiable threshold. An [[machine-learning|ML]]/[[artificial-intelligence|AI]] pipeline can screen for 25%+ quarterly EPS growth, RS ≥ 80, rising institutional ownership, and breakout volume from structured data; base detection (cup-with-handle, flat base, double bottom) is a classic time-series / computer-vision pattern-recognition problem; the 7–8% stop maps directly to algorithmic [[risk-management]]; and the "M" criterion maps to [[market-regime|regime-detection]] models classifying the tape as uptrend, downtrend, or transition. A fully automated CAN SLIM agent could scan the entire universe daily, rank by a combined fundamental-technical score, and execute breakout entries with predefined risk controls.

## Rating

**8/10** — The most clearly defined, practically actionable stock-picking system in print, with every rule backed by historical data spanning over a century. The 7–8% stop-loss discipline alone justifies the book. Marked down for likely backtest overfitting and the cyclicality of any pure growth/momentum approach. As a disciplined framework that unites fundamentals and technicals, it has few peers.
