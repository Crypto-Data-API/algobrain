# Financial Engineering and Risk Management — Columbia University on Coursera

Columbia University's Financial Engineering and Risk Management specialization is the most rigorous free course available for understanding derivatives, risk, and quantitative finance theory. It is more mathematical than most trading courses, but the knowledge is essential if you work with options, structured products, or risk management systems.

## What It Covers

**Derivatives Pricing** — Black-Scholes, binomial trees, risk-neutral valuation. Understanding how options are priced gives you an edge in [[options-greeks]] and volatility trading. You learn not just the formulas but the intuition behind no-arbitrage pricing.

**Monte Carlo Simulation** — Generating price paths, pricing exotic options, variance reduction techniques. Monte Carlo is foundational for any quant — it appears everywhere from risk modeling to [[backtesting-checklist]] validation.

**Term Structure Models** — Yield curves, interest rate models (Vasicek, CIR, Hull-White). Relevant for fixed income trading and understanding how macro rates affect equity markets.

**Portfolio Optimization** — Mean-variance optimization, efficient frontier, factor models. Connects directly to [[factor-investing]] and [[risk-budgeting]]. Understanding Markowitz is prerequisite knowledge for any portfolio-level AI system.

## Why Take This Course

- **Free to audit** — full content access without payment
- Taught by actual Columbia financial engineering faculty
- The theory here underpins what ML models are trying to learn implicitly
- Essential background for anyone building options or volatility strategies
- Understanding risk-neutral pricing prevents costly modeling errors

## Who This Is For

This course is ideal for traders who want to go beyond pattern recognition into understanding market structure and pricing theory. If you are building ML models for options pricing or volatility prediction, the concepts here are non-negotiable.

## Prerequisites

Comfortable with calculus, linear algebra, and basic probability. This is a graduate-level course and does not simplify the math. If the math feels heavy, consider starting with [[andrew-ng-ml-course]] or [[ml-for-trading-specialization]] first.

## Pairing With AI Trading

The theoretical frameworks from this course — factor models, stochastic processes, risk-neutral pricing — become the feature engineering and evaluation frameworks for your ML models. Theory without ML is slow; ML without theory is dangerous. Combine both.
