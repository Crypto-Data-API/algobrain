# ML for Trading Specialization — Georgia Tech on Coursera

The Machine Learning for Trading Specialization from Georgia Tech is the single best free structured course for anyone wanting to apply AI to financial markets. It consists of three courses that build on each other, taking you from data manipulation through ML prediction to reinforcement learning for portfolio management.

## The Three Courses

**1. Machine Learning for Trading** — Introduction to market data, technical indicators, and basic ML models for stock prediction. Heavy use of Python, pandas, and scikit-learn. You build indicator-based strategies and evaluate them with proper backtesting methodology.

**2. Computational Investing** — Deeper dive into portfolio construction, CAPM, factor models, and event-driven strategies. Teaches you to think about markets quantitatively rather than just applying ML blindly. Covers market microstructure basics.

**3. Reinforcement Learning and Trading** — The crown jewel. Applies RL techniques to portfolio optimization and order execution. Connects directly to concepts in [[deep-reinforcement-learning]] and [[rl-portfolio-management]]. Q-learning, policy gradient methods, and environment design for financial applications.

## Why This Course Stands Out

- **Free to audit** on Coursera (no certificate without payment, but full content access)
- Taught by Tucker Balch, who ran a quant fund and co-founded Lucena Research
- Python-first approach using pandas, NumPy, and scikit-learn throughout
- Assignments involve real market data and actual strategy implementation
- Bridges the gap between generic ML courses and financial application

## Prerequisites

Basic Python programming and some familiarity with statistics. If you have never touched ML, consider taking [[andrew-ng-ml-course]] first for the theoretical foundation, then come here for the financial application.

## What Comes Next

After completing this specialization, you will have the foundation to explore [[factor-investing]], build strategies on [[quantconnect-bootcamp]], or dive deeper into [[deep-reinforcement-learning]] for more sophisticated approaches. Pair with [[python-quant-stack]] to expand your toolkit beyond what the course covers.

## Limitations

The course material is a few years old. Some libraries and API calls may need updating. The RL section, while excellent conceptually, uses older RL frameworks — consider supplementing with Stable-Baselines3 for modern implementations.
