# Professional Certifications — CFA, CMT, FRM

These three certifications represent the traditional finance education path. While none of them teach AI or programming, the domain knowledge they cover is foundational for building intelligent trading systems. Understanding what these certifications teach helps you decide which knowledge gaps to fill.

## CFA — Chartered Financial Analyst

The gold standard of investment management credentials. Three levels, each requiring 300+ hours of study and a pass rate around 40-50%.

**What it covers**: Financial reporting, equity valuation, fixed income, derivatives, portfolio management, economics, quantitative methods, ethics, alternative investments.

**Relevance to AI trading**: The CFA curriculum covers factor models, portfolio theory, and risk measurement — all foundations for [[factor-investing]] and [[risk-budgeting]]. Understanding how traditional analysts value securities helps you build better fundamental features for ML models. The quantitative methods section teaches statistics that overlap with financial ML prerequisites.

**Is it worth it?** For career credibility in traditional finance, absolutely. For building trading systems, the knowledge is valuable but you can learn the relevant portions faster through [[financial-engineering-coursera]] and targeted reading.

## CMT — Chartered Market Technician

The technical analysis certification. Two levels focused entirely on price and volume analysis.

**What it covers**: Classical chart patterns, indicators, intermarket analysis, behavioral finance, system design and testing, risk management.

**Relevance to AI trading**: CMT knowledge helps you understand what discretionary traders see in charts — useful for building features that capture the same patterns algorithmically. Connects to [[cnn-chart-recognition]] and technical indicator-based strategies.

**Is it worth it?** Only if you want credibility in the technical analysis community. The knowledge itself is available in standard TA textbooks.

## FRM — Financial Risk Manager

The premier risk management certification. Two parts covering market risk, credit risk, operational risk, and quantitative analysis.

**What it covers**: Value at Risk (VaR), stress testing, Monte Carlo methods, credit risk models, Basel regulations, risk-adjusted performance measures.

**Relevance to AI trading**: Risk management is where most algo traders fail. The FRM curriculum teaches the frameworks behind [[risk-budgeting]], position sizing, and portfolio-level risk control. Understanding VaR, expected shortfall, and stress testing is non-negotiable for managing real capital.

**Is it worth it?** The risk knowledge is essential; the certification itself matters mainly for institutional roles.

## The AI Trader's Take

You do not need any of these certifications to build profitable trading systems. But you do need the knowledge they represent — especially risk management (FRM material) and factor theory (CFA material). Use the course pages in this wiki to learn the same concepts faster and cheaper, then read the [[academic-papers-quant-finance]] that underpin the curriculum.
