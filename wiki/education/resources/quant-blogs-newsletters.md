# Best Blogs and Newsletters for AI Trading Education

These free online resources provide ongoing education in quantitative and AI-driven trading. Unlike courses that you complete once, blogs and newsletters keep you current with new techniques, research, and market developments.

## Systematic Trading and Quant Strategy

**QuantStart (quantstart.com)** — Comprehensive tutorials on systematic trading, from beginner to advanced. Covers backtesting infrastructure, strategy development, execution systems, and ML applications. Particularly strong on the engineering side — how to actually build a trading system, not just the math.

**Quantocracy (quantocracy.com)** — The quant blog aggregator. Curates posts from dozens of quant blogs into a single feed. The best way to discover new authors and stay current across the entire quant blogging ecosystem. Check weekly for new content.

**Robot Wealth (robotwealth.com)** — Practical systematic trading education by Kris Longmore and team. Focuses on realistic expectations, proper research methodology, and the gap between academic research and profitable trading. Excellent for avoiding common pitfalls.

## Factor Investing and Academic Research

**Alpha Architect (alphaarchitect.com)** — Bridge between academic finance research and practical investing. Covers factor investing, momentum, value, and quantitative strategy evaluation. Connects directly to [[factor-investing]] concepts. Their research summaries make dense papers accessible.

**Marcos Lopez de Prado's Research Page** — Papers and presentations from the author of "Advances in Financial Machine Learning." Covers meta-labeling, triple barrier method, fractional differentiation, and other techniques central to modern financial ML. Dense but essential.

## Algo Trading Education

**QuantInsti (quantinsti.com/blog)** — Blog from an algo trading education company. Free articles on Python for trading, strategy development, machine learning applications, and market microstructure. Good supplementary reading alongside [[ml-for-trading-specialization]].

**Ernest Chan's Blog (epchan.blogspot.com)** — Written by the author of "Quantitative Trading" and "Algorithmic Trading." Covers mean reversion, cointegration, Kelly criterion, and practical strategy development. More traditional quant approach but the risk management insights are timeless.

## How to Use These Resources

- **Subscribe via RSS or email** to your top 3-4 sources
- **Quantocracy first** — it aggregates many others, saving you time
- **Read actively** — replicate code examples, test claims against your own data
- **Cross-reference** — when multiple independent sources converge on a technique, it is more likely to be genuine
- **Pair with practice** — reading without implementing is entertainment, not education. Use [[python-quant-stack]] to test what you read.
