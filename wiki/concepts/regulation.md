# Financial Regulation

Financial regulation is the framework of laws, rules, and enforcement mechanisms that govern how financial markets, institutions, and participants operate. Regulations exist to protect investors, maintain market integrity, prevent systemic risk, and combat financial crime. For traders, regulation determines what you can trade, where you can trade it, what leverage is available, what disclosures are required, and what happens when things go wrong.

## Why Regulation Matters for Traders

- **Access**: regulatory status determines whether a platform can legally serve you in your jurisdiction
- **Leverage limits**: regulated brokers face caps (e.g., ESMA limits retail forex to 30:1 in the EU)
- **Custody and protection**: regulated brokers must segregate client funds; unregulated platforms may not
- **Tax reporting**: regulated exchanges report trades to tax authorities; crypto DEXs generally do not
- **Recourse**: if a regulated broker fails, deposit insurance or compensation schemes may apply (e.g., SIPC in the US, FSCS in the UK)

## Regulation and Crypto

Crypto exists in a regulatory grey zone in most jurisdictions. Key dimensions:

- **Exchange licensing**: some jurisdictions require crypto exchanges to register as VASPs ([[vasp-regulation]]) or obtain specific licenses
- **Securities classification**: whether a token is a security (subject to [[sec|SEC]] rules) or a commodity (subject to [[cftc|CFTC]] rules) determines the regulatory regime
- **Stablecoins**: increasingly regulated as money-market equivalents ([[stablecoin-regulation]])
- **DeFi**: decentralized protocols generally operate outside regulatory frameworks, though enforcement actions are increasing
- **Tax**: most jurisdictions treat crypto as taxable property ([[cryptocurrency-tax-australia]])

The [[cex-vs-dex]] comparison highlights the practical implications: CEXs operate under regulation with KYC; DEXs like [[hyperliquid]] and [[asterdex]] operate without KYC but face geo-restrictions and potential future enforcement.

## Regulation by Asset Class

| Asset | Primary US Regulator | Key Rules |
|-------|---------------------|-----------|
| Stocks | SEC | Registration, disclosure, insider trading |
| Options | SEC + OCC | Position limits, margin rules, suitability |
| Futures | CFTC | Margin, position limits, speculative limits |
| Forex | CFTC + NFA | 50:1 max leverage, registration |
| Crypto (US) | Unclear (SEC vs CFTC turf war) | Evolving; enforcement-driven |
| CFDs | Not legal for US retail | Regulated by FCA/ESMA/ASIC elsewhere |
