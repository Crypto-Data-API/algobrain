# Fundamental Analysis

Fundamental analysis is the method of evaluating an asset's intrinsic value by examining underlying economic, financial, and qualitative factors.

## Overview

While technical analysis studies price and volume charts, fundamental analysis asks: "What is this asset actually worth?" It involves analyzing financial statements, economic data, industry conditions, and competitive dynamics to determine whether an asset is overvalued, undervalued, or fairly priced (Source: [[book-security-analysis]]). The gap between intrinsic value and market price creates trading or investment opportunities. [[benjamin-graham]] formalized this approach in the 1930s, and Philip Fisher later expanded it to include qualitative factors like management quality and competitive positioning (Source: [[book-common-stocks-and-uncommon-profits]]).

## Key Details

### Equity Fundamentals

- **Price-to-Earnings (P/E)**: Market price per share divided by earnings per share. Measures how much investors pay per dollar of earnings.
- **Discounted Cash Flow (DCF)**: Projects future cash flows and discounts them to present value. The gold standard of intrinsic valuation.
- **Earnings growth**: Revenue and profit trends drive long-term stock prices. Earnings surprises (beating or missing expectations) cause significant price moves.
- **Balance sheet analysis**: Debt levels, cash reserves, and asset quality indicate financial health and risk.

### Macro Fundamentals

- **[[interest-rates]]**: Central bank policy rates influence asset valuations across the board. Higher rates reduce the present value of future cash flows.
- **Inflation**: Erodes purchasing power and impacts corporate margins. Central bank responses to inflation drive rate expectations.
- **GDP growth**: Broad economic growth supports corporate earnings and risk appetite.
- **Employment data**: Payroll reports, unemployment rates, and wage growth signal economic health.

### Crypto Fundamentals

- **On-chain metrics**: Active addresses, transaction volume, TVL (total value locked), developer activity.
- **Tokenomics**: Supply schedule, inflation rate, burn mechanisms, staking yields.

## Trading Relevance

Fundamental analysis is most effective for medium- to long-term positioning (Source: [[book-the-intelligent-investor]]). Short-term traders may use it to identify catalysts (earnings dates, economic releases) that trigger [[volatility]]. Combining fundamental conviction with technical timing and [[sentiment]] analysis creates a more complete framework.
