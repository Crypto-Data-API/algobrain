# HSR Review (Hart-Scott-Rodino Antitrust Review)

The **Hart-Scott-Rodino Antitrust Improvements Act of 1976** requires parties to large mergers and acquisitions to notify the U.S. **Federal Trade Commission (FTC)** and **Department of Justice Antitrust Division (DOJ)** before closing, and observe a statutory waiting period during which the agencies may investigate. HSR is the gating regulatory milestone for nearly all material U.S. M&A and a primary risk factor in [[merger-arbitrage]] and [[regulatory-approval-arbitrage]].

## Statutory Framework

**Filing thresholds (2024 — adjusted annually for GDP):**
- Size-of-transaction: >$119.5M.
- Size-of-person: at least $23.9M and $239M tested against transaction value.

**Waiting period:**
- Standard: **30 calendar days** (15 days for cash tender offers and bankruptcy 363 sales).
- Pre-2024 reform: 30 days for all transaction types.
- Early termination request: agencies may grant before 30 days (suspended Feb 2021 under Khan; resumed Feb 2025).

**Second Request:**
- During the standard waiting period, either FTC or DOJ may issue a "Second Request" — a comprehensive document subpoena that *re-starts* the clock.
- Compliance typically 6-12 months.
- After substantial compliance, parties have 30 additional days; agency must sue or close.

## Process Timeline

| Stage | Typical Duration | Trader-Relevant Signal |
|-------|------------------|------------------------|
| Pre-announcement filing prep | 30-60 days | Often invisible |
| Announcement → Filing | 5-15 days | Public deal announcement |
| Initial waiting period | 30 days | Spread compresses if no SR |
| Second Request issued (if any) | At day 30 | **Major spread blowout signal** |
| SR compliance | 6-12 months | Earnings, financing strain |
| Substantial compliance | 30 days | Final pre-decision window |
| Outcome | Days-weeks | Close, consent decree, or DOJ/FTC suit |

## Outcomes by Statistical Frequency

| Outcome | Approximate % (modern, 2018-2024) |
|---------|-----------------------------------|
| Cleared (no SR) | 95-97% |
| SR issued, closed without challenge | 1-2% |
| SR issued, closed with consent decree | 0.5-1% |
| Challenged in federal court | 0.3-0.7% |
| Deal abandoned | 0.5-1% |

The base rate is overwhelmingly clearance — but the long tail kills risk-arbs unprepared for it.

## What Triggers a Second Request

Empirical drivers (from FTC / DOJ guidance and academic studies):

1. **Industry concentration:** HHI > 2500 post-deal or HHI delta > 200 in concentrated markets.
2. **Vertical foreclosure concerns:** acquirer can deny inputs or distribution to competitors.
3. **Killer-acquisition pattern:** large incumbent buying nascent competitor.
4. **Industry political salience:** healthcare, big tech, retail, defense — under enhanced 2021-2024 scrutiny.
5. **Past agency loss:** if FTC/DOJ recently lost in this industry, they're more likely to challenge again.

## Lina Khan / Jonathan Kanter Era (2021-2025)

The Biden administration appointed **Lina Khan** (FTC, June 2021) and **Jonathan Kanter** (DOJ Antitrust, Nov 2021), both academic-progressive antitrust theorists. Their tenure produced:

- Most-active enforcement era since 1970s.
- Notable challenges: Microsoft-Activision (2023), Kroger-Albertsons (2022-24), Illumina-GRAIL (2021-23), Meta-Within (2022).
- Many wins for arbs who priced high block probability and shorted; many losses for arbs who took the historical 95%+ clearance base rate.
- Spread volatility on second-request announcements increased markedly.

Trump-2 administration (Jan 2025+) signaled return to more permissive antitrust posture.

## What Risk Arbs Watch

- **Filing date** — start of clock.
- **30-day "no SR" milestone** — spread compression if no second request.
- **Second Request issuance** — spread blowout; reassess fundamentals.
- **Substantial compliance certification** — proximity to decision.
- **DOJ/FTC press releases** — pre-announce outcomes via leaks.
- **"Pull-and-refile"** — sometimes parties withdraw and refile to restart the clock; a signal of negotiation.

## Strategic Implications for Arbs

| Phase | Typical Spread Move | Action |
|-------|---------------------|--------|
| Pre-announcement | N/A | Position early on rumor |
| Announcement → 30 days | Compresses 50-70% of total | Initial position |
| Second Request issued | Blow out 200-500 bp | Reassess; double down or exit |
| Compliance period | Slow grind tighter or wider | Hold |
| Outcome announcement | Resolves | Close position |
