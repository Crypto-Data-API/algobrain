The **Geopolitical / Policy Shock regime** is basket #12 of the fourteen-basket [[crypto-market-regime-taxonomy]] — the recognition that policy and geopolitics produce their own *forensic* regimes with distinct, measurable signatures, not just background noise. A pro-crypto executive order triggers an immediate, euphoric [[open-interest|OI]] build and basis spike; a tariff escalation triggers a macro cascade where crypto sells off in lockstep with equities. The window runs hours to weeks and trades in **both directions**, with the catalyst's policy character — friendly versus hostile — setting the sign of the move (Source: [[2026-06-03-cryptodataapi-14-basket-regime-framework]]). The slower backdrop these shocks land against is the [[geopolitical-risk-premium]] embedded in the tape, while the durable rulebook is captured by the [[regulatory-risk-map]] (both forward links — pages to build).

What makes this basket distinct from a generic headline is that policy shocks have *legible* market plumbing. The euphoric leg of a pro-crypto order is visible in funding and basis well before price tops; the cascade leg of a tariff shock is visible as a sudden spike in crypto-equity correlation. The edge is reading which kind of policy shock you are in and trading its known signature rather than guessing the headline.

## Sub-Regimes

Each policy-shock type carries its own signal, directional bias, and what-to-trade posture (Source: [[2026-06-03-cryptodataapi-14-basket-regime-framework]]).

### Pro-Crypto Policy (Euphoric)
- **Signal:** A pro-crypto executive order, favourable legislation, or a constructive regulatory ruling lands; immediate OI build, euphoric positioning, and a spike in [[basis-carry-regime|basis]] follow within hours.
- **Bias:** Long the euphoria — the policy tailwind is real, but the positioning gets crowded fast.
- **What to trade:** Long BTC/ETH and the highest-beta policy beneficiaries (exchange tokens, US-regulated names) into the euphoric build; moderate-to-high leverage early, then trim. Watch the [[basis-carry-regime|crowded basis]] and elevated funding as the live tell for the unwind — once basis and funding spike to extremes, the euphoric long is the fuel for the next flush, so flip from chasing to defending.

### Trade War / Tariff Escalation (Risk-off cascade)
- **Signal:** Tariff announcement, trade-war escalation, or retaliatory measures; crypto begins correlating tightly with equities as the risk-off cascade develops.
- **Bias:** De-risk and short *with* the macro, not against it.
- **What to trade:** Short BTC/ETH alongside the equity sell-off and cut alt exposure; this is a beta trade, not a coin-specific story. See [[crypto-macro-correlation-regime]] — during the cascade, crypto trades like a leveraged Nasdaq, so size the short off the macro move and respect that correlation can snap back violently on a de-escalation headline.

### Central Bank Rate Signals (Risk on/off)
- **Signal:** Fed (or major central bank) tone shift — a dovish pivot, a hawkish hold, or a tightening surprise; reads off the rate path and forward guidance.
- **Bias:** Risk-on on a Fed pivot, risk-off on tightening — directional with the macro signal.
- **What to trade:** A Fed pivot = BTC risk-on rally → long majors and high-beta. Tightening = risk-off across all assets → de-risk or short majors. Trade the macro direction the rate signal implies rather than fading it; these moves are persistent because they re-rate the whole risk complex, not just crypto.

### Country Ban / Adoption (Fade within days)
- **Signal:** A single country announces a ban (or, conversely, national-level adoption); a sharp single-day move follows on the headline.
- **Bias:** Fade the overreaction — these jurisdiction-level shocks typically revert within days.
- **What to trade:** Fade the initial spike (short the adoption pop, buy the ban dip) once the panic exhausts; small-to-moderate leverage given the headline can re-fire. Hold the fade only for the days-long reversion window — most country-level bans prove porous and most single-country adoptions are too small to durably re-rate global liquidity.

## Policy Regimes as States

Beyond discrete shocks, the broader regulatory backdrop is itself a slow-moving *state* worth labelling — friendly, hostile, or transitional — distinct from the sharp catalysts above (Source: [[2026-06-03-cryptodataapi-14-basket-regime-framework]]):

- **Friendly regime** — a constructive policy posture (clear rules, ETF approvals, supportive executive tone) sets a multi-month bullish tone; dips get bought and pro-crypto shocks land on receptive positioning.
- **Hostile regime** — an enforcement-led, restrictive backdrop caps rallies and amplifies negative shocks; even good news struggles to hold.
- **Transitional regime** — the rulebook is actively being rewritten (pending legislation, a shifting SEC/CFTC posture); two-way volatility is elevated and conviction should be lower.

The durable map of *which* jurisdictions sit where is the job of the [[regulatory-risk-map]] (forward link). The distinction matters: a discrete shock is traded as a window, but the prevailing policy regime sets the *bias and size* you bring to every other basket.

## Detection Signals

Read these together to identify the policy-shock state (Source: [[2026-06-03-cryptodataapi-14-basket-regime-framework]]):

- **Executive orders / legislation** — pro- or anti-crypto orders, bills, and signed law; the cleanest trigger for the euphoric or hostile sub-states.
- **SEC / CFTC posture** — enforcement actions, settlements, ETF decisions, and rule proposals that define the friendly/hostile axis.
- **Tariff and trade-war headlines** — the trigger for the risk-off cascade sub-state; watch the escalation/de-escalation cadence.
- **Central-bank calendar and tone** — FOMC dates and the dovish/hawkish read that flips risk-on/risk-off.
- **Country-level ban / adoption news** — jurisdiction headlines that produce sharp, fade-able single-day moves.

These overlap the scheduled macro prints catalogued in [[event-catalyst-regime]] (the regulatory-news and macro-print sub-states are shared), and they transmit into price through [[crypto-macro-correlation-regime|equity correlation]] whenever the shock is macro in character.

## Relationship to Other Regimes

The policy-shock basket is best read as a *shock layer* overlapping its neighbours:

- **[[event-catalyst-regime]]** — regulatory headlines and macro prints appear in both; a policy event large enough to set a multi-week tone graduates from a discrete catalyst into a full policy-shock regime.
- **[[security-black-swan-regime]]** — a severe hostile shock (a coordinated ban, a punitive enforcement wave) can tip into systemic, black-swan territory.
- **[[crypto-macro-correlation-regime]]** — the transmission mechanism: tariff and rate shocks reach crypto *through* the equity-correlation channel, so a policy shock often *is* a correlation regime shift.
- **[[derivatives-native-regime]]** — pro-crypto policy euphoria can ignite a [[derivatives-native-regime|leverage build]] (OI and basis spike) that later cascades when the crowded positioning unwinds, chaining a policy shock into a derivatives-native flush.

## Pitfalls

- **Trading a ban headline as permanent.** Most country-level bans fade within days and prove porous in practice; chasing the panic instead of fading the overreaction is the classic error.
- **Ignoring that pro-crypto euphoria builds fragility.** The euphoric OI and basis build *is* the setup for the next flush — treating the policy tailwind as risk-free, rather than watching the crowded basis, is how the long turns into the liquidation fuel.
- **Assuming policy is one-directional.** Policy cuts both ways within a single news cycle (a friendly order, then a hostile enforcement action); positioning for a single direction ignores the two-way nature of the regime.
- **Mis-reading central-bank tone.** Over-reacting to a hawkish word in an otherwise dovish statement (or vice versa) and getting the risk-on/risk-off sign wrong on the largest, most persistent driver in the basket.
