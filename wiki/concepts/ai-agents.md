# AI Agents

**AI agents** in crypto are autonomous programs — often powered by large language models (LLMs) or reinforcement-learning models — that can independently execute on-chain actions: trading, managing DeFi positions, executing governance votes, interacting with protocols, or creating content. They represent two distinct things that are frequently conflated: (1) **infrastructure agents** — genuine software that autonomously manages assets or services on-chain; and (2) **AI agent narrative tokens** — crypto tokens whose price is driven by the "AI agent" narrative rather than by deployed autonomous functionality. Both matter to crypto traders, for different reasons.

## How It Works

### Infrastructure AI agents

A functional AI trading agent typically combines:

1. **Perception layer:** Reads on-chain state (wallet balances, pool liquidity, oracle prices), off-chain feeds (news, social, market data), and potentially real-world sensors.
2. **Reasoning layer:** An LLM or RL model evaluates the perceived state and decides on an action — trade, rebalance, bridge, stake, or wait.
3. **Execution layer:** Submits signed transactions on-chain, typically via a permissioned wallet or a smart contract with delegated authority.
4. **Memory and learning:** Stores prior actions and outcomes; may update a policy or prompt template based on performance.

**Agent frameworks:** The most widely used frameworks in 2024–25 are ElizaOS (formerly eliza by ai16z), Virtuals Protocol, and Fetch.ai's Agentverse. These provide scaffolding for building agents that interact with Ethereum, Solana, and other chains.

**Trust and security model:** An AI agent controlling an on-chain wallet is a significant security surface. Prompt injection (adversarial inputs designed to cause the agent to make unwanted transactions), key management, and permission scoping are the dominant engineering challenges.

### AI agent narrative tokens

In late 2023 and through 2024, a speculative category of tokens emerged whose primary value proposition was association with AI agents: VIRTUAL (Virtuals Protocol), AI16Z (ai16z/ElizaOS), AIXBT (an AI-driven market commentary agent), and HYPE-adjacent tokens. These tokens are more closely related to [[memecoins]] in their trading dynamics — driven by narrative velocity, social media virality, and the overall AI × crypto narrative cycle — than by agent deployment metrics.

## Concrete Examples

- **AIXBT (launched Nov 2024):** An AI agent (powered by ElizaOS) that posts real-time market analysis to X/Twitter. Gained 150,000 followers within weeks. The associated AIXBT token reached a market cap of ~$500M in early 2025 — largely on narrative and social engagement metrics, not on any trading revenue generated by the agent itself.
- **ai16z / ElizaOS (Nov 2024):** AI16Z is a DAO whose treasury is managed (ostensibly) by an AI agent. The AI16Z token was a top-performing token in Q4 2024 as the agent framework became the most-forked AI agent toolkit. Token price driven almost entirely by narrative and developer adoption of the open-source ElizaOS framework.
- **Fetch.ai (FET, 2019–):** One of the oldest AI agent projects. FET powers a decentralised network of agents for supply-chain, mobility, and financial applications. Merged into a "Super AI" token (ASI Alliance) in 2024 with SingularityNET (AGIX) and Ocean Protocol (OCEAN).
- **Prediction market AI agents:** AI agents trading prediction markets (see [[ai-prediction-markets]]) represent a concrete functional use case — agents that monitor news feeds, update probability estimates, and execute trades on [[polymarket]] or [[kalshi]] with sub-second latency.
- **DeFi position managers:** Agents that rebalance liquidity positions on Uniswap V3, adjust borrow ratios on Aave to avoid liquidation, or harvest and compound yield-farming rewards autonomously. These are more "automated bots" than sophisticated AI, but are increasingly marketed as "agents."

## Trading Relevance

AI agents matter to AlgoBrain strategies in two distinct ways:

**As market infrastructure (using agents):**

- **[[ai-agent-strategies]]:** The AlgoBrain strategy catalog includes AI-agent-driven trading strategies — momentum detection via LLM reasoning, on-chain monitoring, and cross-venue execution. This is the *use* of AI agents as trading infrastructure.
- **[[prediction-market-strategies]]:** AI agents as prediction market participants. Agents that read news and adjust Polymarket positions faster than human traders represent growing competition on the informational edge; human traders' advantage shifts from news speed to calibration quality.
- **[[on-chain-flow-trading]]:** AI agent wallet activity is increasingly detectable on-chain (large coordinated bot clusters, known agent frameworks' wallets). Tracking agent wallet flows is an emerging signal.

**As a narrative investment (trading agent tokens):**

- AI agent tokens trade like high-beta narrative tokens — large up moves during AI narrative cycles, severe drawdowns in bear markets (AI16Z fell ~85% from peak within months). They belong in the [[multi-strategy-crypto-portfolio]] memecoin/convexity sleeve with hard caps (≤3-5% NAV).
- **Narrative timing:** AI agent tokens are driven by developer adoption metrics (GitHub stars, ElizaOS forks, Virtuals agent deployments) and macro AI sentiment (ChatGPT news, OpenAI releases, GPU availability). These are different drivers from most crypto tokens and require dedicated signal tracking.
- **[[vampire-attack-arbitrage]] analogue:** New AI agent frameworks frequently launch with token incentives for early developers and users. The emission-capture logic from vampire-attack-arbitrage applies — identify the bootstrap window, participate (deploy agents, earn tokens), exit before the taper.
