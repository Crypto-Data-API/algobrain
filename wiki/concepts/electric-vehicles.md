**Electric vehicles (EVs)** are automobiles powered by electric motors drawing on rechargeable battery packs rather than internal-combustion engines. As a *trading theme*, "EV" spans a vertical supply chain — automakers, battery cell makers, charging networks, power semiconductors, and the raw [[commodities-overview|commodities]] (lithium, nickel, cobalt, copper, graphite) — and is one of the most-traded structural-growth narratives in equities. The thesis is a long-horizon demand-substitution story, but the sector trades with very high beta, sharp boom-bust cycles, and heavy sensitivity to policy, rates, and commodity prices.

## The investable supply chain

EV exposure is not a single asset — it is a chain of distinct, loosely correlated layers:

- **Automakers / pure-plays.** [[tesla|Tesla (TSLA)]], BYD, Rivian, Li Auto, NIO, plus legacy OEMs (Ford, GM, VW) with EV divisions. Pure-plays trade as high-multiple [[growth-stocks|growth stocks]]; legacy OEMs trade on the cannibalization of their ICE franchise.
- **Battery cells.** CATL, LG Energy Solution, Panasonic, Samsung SDI — the highest-capex, most commoditized layer.
- **Battery materials / mining.** [[lithium|Lithium]], nickel, cobalt, graphite, copper. The most volatile layer; lithium prices roughly 10x'd into 2022 then collapsed ~80%+ into 2024, devastating miner equities even as EV unit sales kept rising. A canonical example of a great end-market with a brutal upstream cycle.
- **Power semiconductors.** Silicon-carbide (SiC) and gallium-nitride devices for inverters — e.g. [[on-semiconductor]], [[navitas-semiconductor]], STMicro, Infineon. EVs use far more power-semi content per vehicle than ICE cars.
- **Charging infrastructure.** ChargePoint, EVgo, Tesla Supercharger network — capital-intensive, policy-dependent, slow to profitability.

## Why it trades the way it does

- **Long-duration growth, hence rate-sensitive.** EV valuations discount cash flows far into the future, so they are highly sensitive to the discount rate. The 2022 rate-hiking cycle compressed EV multiples brutally; the sector behaves like a leveraged bet on falling real yields. See [[growth-stocks]].
- **Policy-driven.** Subsidies, emissions mandates (EU CO2 targets, China NEV credits, US IRA tax credits), and tariffs (EU/US duties on Chinese EVs) are first-order price drivers. Subsidy rollbacks produce step-changes in demand and in stock prices.
- **Commodity-coupled.** Upstream miner equities track lithium/nickel spot prices more than EV unit growth, creating a [[pairs-trading|pairs/relative-value]] opportunity between the demand story (automakers) and the supply cycle (miners).
- **Crowded thematic flows.** Thematic [[etf|ETFs]] (e.g. LIT for lithium/battery, DRIV, KARS) concentrate retail and momentum flows, amplifying both rallies and the [[crowding-risk|unwind]].

## Trading relevance

- **Thematic / [[sector-rotation|sector-rotation]] expression.** Trade the theme via baskets/ETFs to diffuse single-name blow-up risk, or via the highest-beta pure-play for maximum leverage to the narrative.
- **Supply-chain relative value.** Long the layer with pricing power, short the commoditized layer — e.g. long power-semis or a profitable automaker, short money-losing chargers or oversupplied lithium miners.
- **Event trading.** Quarterly delivery numbers (especially [[tesla|Tesla]] deliveries), subsidy/tariff announcements, and battery-tech milestones are scheduled volatility events suited to [[earnings-options-strategies|options]] structures.
- **Commodity overlay.** Lithium and copper exposure can be taken directly via [[commodities-overview|commodities]] rather than through equity miners, isolating the price view from company-specific risk.

## Key risks

- Demand-growth disappointment vs. lofty embedded expectations (multiple compression).
- Chinese oversupply and price wars compressing margins industry-wide.
- Commodity-cycle whipsaw decoupling miner equities from end-demand.
- Policy reversal (subsidy cuts) and rate shocks hitting long-duration valuations simultaneously.
