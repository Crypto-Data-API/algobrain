**HODL** is crypto-native slang for a long-term buy-and-hold strategy: accumulating an asset (originally [[bitcoin|Bitcoin]]) and refusing to sell through volatility, drawdowns, and the temptation to trade. The term originated from a now-famous typo of "hold," and was later backronymed to "Hold On for Dear Life." It functions both as a trading approach and as a behavioural-finance meme that encourages investors to suppress panic-selling.

## Origin

The term comes from a December 18, 2013 post on the Bitcointalk forum titled "I AM HODLING," written during a sharp BTC sell-off (Bitcoin had fallen from roughly $1,100 toward the $400s). The author, admittedly drunk, argued he was a bad short-term trader and would simply hold rather than try to time the market. The misspelled "HODL" went viral and became the canonical crypto term for conviction holding.

## The Underlying Strategy

HODL is the crypto framing of [[buy-and-hold]]: take a long-term directional view, accept high interim [[volatility]], and avoid the transaction costs, taxes, and behavioural errors that come from frequent trading. It is often combined with [[dollar-cost-averaging]] (buying fixed amounts on a schedule regardless of price) to smooth entry and remove timing decisions.

The thesis rests on three claims, each debatable:

1. **Long-run appreciation** -- the held asset trends up over multi-year horizons despite drawdowns of 70-85% in past bear markets.
2. **Timing is hard** -- most retail traders underperform by selling near lows and buying near highs; removing the decision improves outcomes.
3. **Cost and tax efficiency** -- holding avoids spreads, fees, and short-term capital-gains events.

## Behavioural Dimension

HODL is as much a psychological commitment device as a strategy. It directly counters the [[disposition-effect]] (the tendency to sell winners too early and hold losers too long) by pre-committing to hold both. In practice the behavioural failure modes cut the other way: holders can ride a fundamentally broken asset (a dead altcoin) to zero under the banner of "diamond hands," conflating disciplined conviction with refusal to update on new information.

## Trading Relevance

For an allocator, HODL is the passive baseline that any active crypto strategy must beat after costs and risk -- analogous to how [[index-investing]] benchmarks active equity managers. Its drawdown profile is brutal: Bitcoin has had multiple 80%+ peak-to-trough declines, so a pure HODL position requires position sizing small enough to survive that path without forced selling. It is also the relevant benchmark for evaluating [[impermanent-loss|liquidity provision]] and yield strategies -- an LP or staker must out-earn simply HODLing the underlying tokens. The strategy works only if the holder can genuinely tolerate the volatility; size it as risk capital, not as a position requiring margin or near-term liquidity.
