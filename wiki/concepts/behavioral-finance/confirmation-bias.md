Confirmation bias is the tendency to search for, interpret, favor, and recall information that confirms one's existing beliefs while giving disproportionately less attention to information that contradicts them. In trading, it is one of the most damaging [[cognitive-biases]] because it systematically distorts the information processing that underlies every trade decision.

## How Confirmation Bias Operates

### Selective Information Seeking

After forming a view (e.g., "this stock is going up"), traders actively seek information that supports it:
- Reading only bullish analysis after going long
- Following social media accounts that share the same directional view
- Searching for positive earnings revisions while ignoring deteriorating fundamentals
- Gravitating toward trading communities that confirm the thesis

### Selective Interpretation

Even when exposed to neutral or contradictory information, biased traders interpret it favorably:
- A stock's earnings miss is reframed as "already priced in" or "one-time event"
- A negative analyst report is dismissed because "analysts are always wrong"
- Deteriorating technical signals are explained away as "shakeout before the real move"

### Selective Memory

Traders remember information that confirmed their trades and forget or minimize information that contradicted them. This distorts the feedback loop that should improve trading skill over time, because the lessons from losses are filtered through a self-serving lens.

## Trading Impact

| Situation | How Confirmation Bias Hurts |
|---|---|
| Pre-trade research | Only gathering supporting evidence for a predetermined view |
| Position management | Ignoring stop-loss signals and warning signs while holding losers |
| Post-trade review | Attributing wins to skill and losses to bad luck |
| Strategy evaluation | Cherry-picking backtest parameters that confirm a strategy works |
| Market analysis | Fitting narrative to price action rather than updating views objectively |

### Echo Chamber Effect

Modern trading communities amplify confirmation bias through echo chambers:
- Subreddits and Discord servers organized around specific tickers create self-reinforcing bullish (or bearish) consensus
- Algorithm-driven social media feeds surface content that matches previous engagement, creating information bubbles
- Traders who share dissenting views are downvoted, muted, or banned, removing the corrective signal

This dynamic was visible in the [[meme-stocks]] phenomenon, where communities like WallStreetBets created intense confirmation bias around specific holdings.

## Interaction with Other Biases

- **[[overconfidence-bias]]** — confirmation bias feeds overconfidence by selectively exposing the trader to confirming evidence
- **[[anchoring-bias]]** — once anchored to a price or view, confirmation bias reinforces the anchor by filtering out disconfirming information
- **[[disposition-effect]]** — confirmation bias supports holding losers ("I just need to find the right analysis to confirm this will recover")
- **[[narrative-fallacy]]** — the tendency to construct coherent stories from random data, then seek confirming evidence for the story

## Counter-Strategies

1. **Actively seek disconfirming evidence** — before entering a trade, deliberately search for the best argument against it. If you can't find a strong bear case for your long, you haven't looked hard enough
2. **Pre-trade checklists** — define entry/exit criteria before looking at a specific instrument. Check the criteria objectively rather than fitting the data to a desired conclusion
3. **Red team exercise** — write the opposing thesis as convincingly as possible before committing capital
4. **Quantitative rules** — mechanical trading rules override subjective judgment and are immune to confirmation bias in execution (though not in design)
5. **Diverse information sources** — deliberately follow analysts and traders with opposing views
6. **[[trading-journal]]** — record pre-trade thesis and expected scenarios. Review later to see whether you honored your own criteria or rationalized deviations
