Swan Bitcoin is a US-based, Bitcoin-only financial-services company founded in 2019 by Cory Klippsten and Yan Pritzker. It is built around **automated recurring purchases ([[dollar-cost-averaging|DCA]])** of [[bitcoin]] with an explicit emphasis on long-term accumulation and [[self-custody]], deliberately avoiding the multi-asset, high-churn model of general crypto exchanges. Its positioning is openly "Bitcoin-maximalist" and educational rather than trading-oriented.

## Business Model

- **Recurring DCA buys** — users set automatic daily/weekly/monthly buys, the core product and the practical embodiment of the [[dca-strategy]].
- **Self-custody first** — Swan encourages withdrawal to user-controlled hardware wallets rather than leaving balances on-platform, contrasting with the custodial-default model of most exchanges.
- **Concierge / private-client and IRA products** — larger-balance services and Bitcoin retirement accounts for US investors.
- **Education** — Swan publishes research, a podcast, and explainer content, functioning partly as a Bitcoin on-ramp and advocacy brand.

Swan does not list altcoins and has been publicly critical of multi-coin platforms, a differentiator it markets as a feature (reduced conflict of interest, no token-listing incentives).

## Trading Relevance

- **DCA as strategy, not speculation.** Swan exists because [[dollar-cost-averaging]] into a volatile asset removes timing risk and behavioural error — relevant to the [[dca-strategy]] and [[dca-technical-hybrid]] wiki entries as a real-world implementation channel.
- **Counterparty and custody risk.** Swan's self-custody emphasis is a direct response to exchange-failure events (FTX, Celsius); the page is a useful reference point when evaluating [[exchanges|exchange]] vs self-custody trade-offs.
- **On-ramp economics.** Fee structure (spread/commission on recurring buys) matters for net DCA returns versus a [[bitcoin-etf|spot Bitcoin ETF]] or a low-fee exchange — a cost comparison any systematic accumulator should run.
- **Not a trading venue.** Swan offers no leverage, derivatives, or active-trading tooling; it is irrelevant to short-term or [[algorithmic-trading|algorithmic]] strategies and is best understood as a long-horizon savings rail.
