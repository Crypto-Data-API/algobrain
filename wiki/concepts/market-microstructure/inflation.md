# Inflation

**Inflation** is the sustained increase in the general price level of goods and services over time, eroding purchasing power. It is typically measured by the Consumer Price Index (CPI).

Central banks like the [[federal-reserve]] use [[interest-rates]] as the primary tool to control inflation. Rising inflation tends to be bearish for [[bonds]] and can create [[volatility]] across all asset classes.

## Types of Inflation

- **Demand-pull inflation** -- occurs when aggregate demand outpaces supply, typically during strong economic growth. Too much money chasing too few goods.
- **Cost-push inflation** -- driven by rising input costs (energy, raw materials, wages) that producers pass through to consumers. Supply chain disruptions and tariffs are common triggers.
- **Monetary inflation** -- caused by excessive growth in the money supply relative to economic output. Quantitative easing and deficit spending can contribute to monetary inflation over time.

## Measurement

- **Consumer Price Index (CPI)** -- the most widely reported inflation gauge, measuring price changes in a basket of consumer goods and services. Core CPI excludes volatile food and energy prices.
- **Personal Consumption Expenditures (PCE)** -- the [[federal-reserve]]'s preferred inflation measure, with a broader scope than CPI and a methodology that accounts for consumer substitution behavior. The Fed targets 2% annual PCE inflation.
- **Producer Price Index (PPI)** -- measures price changes from the producers' perspective, often serving as a leading indicator of future consumer inflation.

## Impact on Markets

- **Rising rates hurt growth stocks** -- when inflation forces central banks to raise [[interest-rates]], the present value of future earnings declines, disproportionately affecting high-growth and long-duration equities.
- **Commodities and gold benefit** -- real assets tend to hold value or appreciate during inflationary periods, making them popular inflation hedges.
- **Bonds suffer** -- rising inflation erodes the real value of fixed coupon payments, pushing bond prices down and yields up.
- **TIPS and I-Bonds** -- Treasury Inflation-Protected Securities adjust their principal for CPI changes, providing direct inflation protection.

Inflation remains a central concern for traders and investors, with tariff-driven cost pressures and fiscal policy adding complexity to the outlook.
