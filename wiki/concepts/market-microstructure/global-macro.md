# Global Macro

Global macro is a trading and investment strategy that takes positions across asset classes -- [[forex]], [[bond-market|bonds]], equities, and [[commodities]] -- based on analysis of macroeconomic trends, [[central-bank]] policy, geopolitical events, and capital flows. It is a top-down approach that focuses on the biggest forces moving markets rather than individual security analysis.

## Core Approach

Global macro traders analyze variables such as:

- **[[interest-rates]]** and [[central-bank]] policy (Fed, ECB, BOJ, BOE)
- **Inflation** and employment data
- **Currency dynamics** and balance of payments
- **Government fiscal policy** and sovereign debt levels
- **Geopolitical risk** and trade policy
- **Capital flows** between countries and asset classes

Positions are expressed through whatever instruments offer the best risk/reward: currencies, government bonds, equity indices, [[commodities]], or [[derivatives]].

## Notable Practitioners

- **[[george-soros]]**: Pioneered discretionary macro with the [[quantum-fund]]. His [[black-wednesday]] trade is the most famous macro trade in history.
- **Ray Dalio**: Founded Bridgewater Associates, the world's largest [[hedge-funds|hedge fund]], using a systematic macro approach.
- **Stanley Druckenmiller**: Managed money for Soros and independently, with one of the strongest long-term macro track records.
- **Paul Tudor Jones**: Combined macro analysis with technical timing.

## Discretionary vs. Systematic

Discretionary macro relies on the trader's judgment and experience to synthesize information and size positions. Systematic macro uses quantitative models to process data and generate signals. Many modern macro funds blend both approaches.

## Why It Matters for Traders

Understanding [[macroeconomics]] is essential even for traders who focus on individual assets, because macro forces ([[quantitative-easing]], rate decisions, [[currency-dynamics|currency moves]]) drive the underlying environment in which all assets trade. As Soros's career demonstrates, macro analysis can identify the most asymmetric opportunities in markets.
