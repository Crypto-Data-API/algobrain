See [[when-to-retire-a-strategy]] — kill criteria are the pre-defined, numerical
conditions that automatically retire (or pause) a strategy: e.g. drawdown > 20%,
rolling 6-month Sharpe < 0, or a rolling win-rate below its backtested floor.
Setting them in advance removes discretion from the decision to pull a losing
strategy. See also [[position-sizing]] and [[risk-management]].
