Auto-Deleveraging (ADL) is the forced closure of *profitable* counterparty positions on a perpetual-futures venue when the [[insurance-fund]] cannot absorb the bankruptcy losses generated by liquidations on the other side of the book. Unlike ordinary [[liquidation-risk|liquidation]], which only hits losing traders, ADL reaches across to the winners and clips their P&L — meaning a "neutral" hedge can be torn open without warning during stress events. ADL is the single largest gap between idealized perp backtests and live execution, and was the proximate cause of billions in surprise losses during the [[2025-10-crypto-liquidation-cascade|October 10–11 2025 cascade]] (~$20B in liquidations across venues).

## How ADL Works

When a trader is liquidated below the bankruptcy price (the price at which their margin is fully consumed), the resulting deficit must be paid by *someone*. The standard waterfall is:

1. **Liquidation engine** attempts to close the position at or above bankruptcy price. Any surplus flows to the [[insurance-fund]].
2. **Insurance fund** absorbs the deficit if the close happens below bankruptcy. The fund is built up from prior liquidation surpluses.
3. **Auto-Deleveraging** triggers when the insurance fund is insufficient (or, on some venues, when it falls below a configured floor). The exchange ranks all opposing-side traders by an **ADL queue score** and force-closes them in order until the bankruptcy is covered.

The ADL queue is typically computed as:

```
ADL score = unrealized_PnL_pct  ×  effective_leverage
```

The most profitable, highest-leveraged accounts on the *winning* side are deleveraged first. Closes happen at the bankruptcy price of the liquidated account — usually well off the mark — so the ADL'd trader takes a meaningful haircut versus where they could have closed voluntarily.

## When Platforms Invoke It

ADL is normally rare on functioning venues because the insurance fund is sized for routine liquidations. It activates when:

- A **flash move** liquidates a large cluster of positions faster than the order book can absorb (no bids deep enough at bankruptcy price).
- The **insurance fund is depleted** or has fallen below the platform's configured trigger level.
- A **single oversized account** blows up such that its bankruptcy alone exceeds available reserves (a "whale wipeout").
- **Oracle deviations** create mismatches between mark price and true liquidity, so the venue cannot close at the mark.

## Why It Broke "Neutral" Hedges in October 10–11 2025

The October 2025 crash is the canonical case. A roughly $20B liquidation cascade swept through major crypto perp venues over ~24 hours after a sharp BTC and ETH drawdown. Several venues' insurance funds were drawn down within hours, and ADL queues started firing.

Sophisticated market makers and basis traders were running what they believed to be **delta-neutral books**: long perp on Venue A hedged short perp on Venue B, or long spot hedged short perp. ADL on the short-perp leg force-closed the hedge at a stale bankruptcy print just as the spot leg was rallying back — instantly converting a flat book into a directional long, often at the worst possible mark. Strategies that had backtested to Sharpe > 2 with capped drawdowns showed -10% to -30% single-day P&L because the backtest had no concept of "your hedge gets ripped off by the exchange."

The lesson: in a perp backtest, **liquidation is not a one-sided event**. Both sides of every trade are exposed to ADL probability proportional to their queue rank.

## ADL Across Major Venues

The mechanics differ meaningfully and matter for backtest assumptions:

| Venue | Insurance fund first? | ADL formula | Notification | Notes |
|-------|----------------------|-------------|--------------|-------|
| [[binance]] | Yes (large fund) | PnL% × leverage, top of queue first | Account-level "ADL light" indicator (5 bars) | Historically rare on majors; activated repeatedly Oct 2025 |
| [[bybit]] | Yes | PnL% × leverage | ADL indicator in UI | Aggressive ADL during Feb 2025 stress; UTA accounts cross-margined |
| OKX | Yes | PnL% × leverage, tiered | Indicator + email | Operates a separate "risk reserve" fund per instrument |
| [[hyperliquid]] | HLP vault absorbs first (community LP) | Profitable longs/shorts socialized via HLP P&L | None per-account; HLP P&L visible | "ADL" effectively socialized through vault drawdown rather than per-trader force-close |
| dYdX v4 | Insurance fund + validator-driven liquidation | Validator-coordinated; deleveraging by socialized losses if fund empty | On-chain event | Fully on-chain; deleveraging logic in protocol |
| GMX V2 | Liquidations go to GLP/GM pool (LPs are the counterparty) | LPs absorb losses; no per-account ADL | Pool drawdown | Different model — LP pool *is* the insurance |

The takeaway: any backtest that uses one venue's liquidation model and assumes it transfers is wrong. Hyperliquid's HLP loss-socialization, GMX's LP-counterparty model, and Binance's queued ADL produce very different P&L distributions for the *same* strategy.

## Implications for Backtesting

ADL is the **most critical gap in retail and even most institutional perp backtests**. Standard backtest assumptions that break under ADL:

1. **"Liquidations only hit losing positions."** False. Profitable positions are exposed proportional to their PnL% × leverage rank.
2. **"My hedge protects me."** False if both legs are on perp venues — ADL on the winning leg destroys the hedge.
3. **"Drawdown is bounded by stop distance."** False. ADL closes happen at bankruptcy price, often far past any stop.
4. **"Funding rate is the only carry cost."** False. Implicit ADL probability is a hidden tax on profitable carry trades.
5. **"Insurance fund will absorb anything reasonable."** False on smaller venues, and false anywhere during a true cascade.

If a backtest does not produce the same P&L distribution after injecting plausible ADL events, it is overstating Sharpe and understating tail risk — typically by a large margin in cascade-prone strategies (basis arb, [[funding-rate]] carry, market-making).

## How to Model ADL in Backtests

A defensible ADL model has three components:

1. **Trigger probability** as a function of stress signals:
   - Funding-rate dislocation (|funding| > 99th-percentile)
   - Open-interest concentration on one side
   - Realized-volatility spike (>3σ over 1h window)
   - Insurance-fund drawdown rate (where data is published)
   - Venue-wide liquidation flow in the prior 30–60 minutes (CoinGlass)

2. **Queue rank** for the simulated position:
   - Compute simulated PnL% × leverage at each historical timestamp
   - Estimate venue-wide quantile of the score (synthetic or empirical)
   - Top-decile positions face high ADL conditional probability during a triggered event

3. **Close price**:
   - ADL closes at *bankruptcy price* of the liquidated counterparty, not mark
   - Approximate as mark × (1 ± expected_overshoot), where overshoot is calibrated from October 2025, May 2022 LUNA, and similar events (typically 2–8% beyond the move)

A simple Monte Carlo wrapper:

```python
def apply_adl(positions, stress_score, queue_quantile):
    # stress_score in [0,1]; queue_quantile in [0,1] (1 = most exposed)
    p_adl = base_rate * stress_score * queue_quantile
    if uniform() < p_adl:
        close_price = bankruptcy_price * (1 - overshoot_sample())
        return force_close(positions, close_price)
    return positions
```

This can be calibrated against historical ADL bars published by Binance/Bybit and against HLP drawdowns on [[hyperliquid]].

## Mitigations for Traders

- **Lower leverage.** ADL queue rank scales linearly with leverage; halving leverage roughly halves ADL probability conditional on a trigger.
- **Trim winners during stress.** When funding flips violently or OI skews extreme, voluntarily realize PnL — this drops queue rank below the cut.
- **Diversify across venues.** ADL is venue-local; splitting a position across [[binance]], [[bybit]], OKX, and [[hyperliquid]] reduces single-venue queue exposure.
- **Watch the ADL indicator.** Most CEX perp UIs publish a 1–5 bar ADL exposure light. Treat 4–5 bars as a hard signal to rebalance.
- **Hedge with spot, not perp, where possible.** Spot legs are immune to ADL.
- **Avoid running synthetic-neutral positions through cascades.** The "free" carry of a perp-vs-perp basis trade evaporates the moment ADL fires.
- **Monitor insurance fund metrics.** Several venues publish IF size; a sharp drawdown is a leading indicator of queue activation.
