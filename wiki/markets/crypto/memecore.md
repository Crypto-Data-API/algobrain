# MemeCore

**MemeCore** (M) is a self-described "Meme 2.0" Layer 1 blockchain (with [[proof-of-meme]] / "PoM" branding) whose token listed in July 2025 and ran from $0.05 to a $4.82 ATH within ~nine months, printing a top-30 market cap. For traders it is primarily a **cautionary case study in float games**: on-chain investigators (including ZachXBT) allege ~90–99% insider supply control, and its multi-billion-dollar valuation rests on daily volume that is often under $30M — **$8.1M on 2026-06-20, a Vol/MC of just 0.21%**. Treat the printed market cap as structurally unreliable. As a [[meme-coin|meme]]-branded but float-driven asset, M is the textbook [[market-manipulation]] / [[wash-trading]] screening template (see [[meme-l1]] peers).

## Overview

MemeCore is the first Layer 1 blockchain, built for Meme 2.0 — a new paradigm where meme coins transcend speculation to become engines of culture, value, and community coordination. In this new era, memes are not just content — they are currency, governance, and creative expression.

Mission
To structure and sustain the meme viral economy, empowering anyone to launch tokens, earn from cultural contributions, and build openly in a decentralized, meme-native environment.

Vision
MemeCore envisions a world where meme coins are vehicles of shared ownership and viral innovation. Every social post, meme remix, and on-chain action becomes a part of an economic feedback loop — fueling a participatory ecosystem where culture is capital.

*(The above is the project's own framing. The market-structure reality below matters more for trading.)*

## Protocol / Technology

MemeCore markets itself as a **meme-native Layer 1** rather than a single token, with a distinctive **"Proof of Meme" ([[proof-of-meme|PoM]])** consensus/branding narrative:

- **Meme L1 thesis** — an EVM-compatible chain purpose-built for launching, trading and "earning from" meme assets, positioning M as the gas/infrastructure token of a meme economy rather than a single joke coin. This is the same structural pitch as other **meme L1s** (see [[meme-l1]] peers).
- **"Proof of Meme" (PoM)** — the project's framing for a consensus/reward layer that purports to reward "cultural contribution" (social posts, meme remixes, on-chain virality) alongside ordinary block production. In practice the marketing-forward consensus story is far less important to traders than the supply distribution.
- **Multi-venue token** — the M token exists on its own L1 and is also deployed on [[bnb|BNB Chain]] (`0x22b1458e780f8fa71e2f84502cee8b5a3cc731fa`), which complicates clean float accounting.
- **Verification caveat** — much of the technical "PoM" framing is project-sourced and unaudited by third parties; for trading, the dominant fact is **who holds the unlocked vs locked supply**, not the consensus design.

> *Engineering claims here are the project's own; treat them as marketing until independently verified. The market-structure section is the operative analysis.*

## Tokenomics & Supply

The tokenomics — not the technology — are the entire story, and they are the central red flag:

| **Max Supply** | 10,000,000,000 M |
| **Market Cap** | $3.80B |
| **Fully Diluted Valuation** | $15.57B |
| **Market Cap / FDV Ratio** | **0.24** |

- **Massive dilution overhang.** Only ~13.1% of the 10B max supply is "circulating"; MC/FDV = 0.24 means **~76% of fully-diluted value sits in not-yet-circulating tokens** ($15.57B FDV vs $3.80B MC). Future unlocks are a structural headwind dwarfing most top-30 names.
- **Insider concentration ~90–99.6%.** On-chain probes (ZachXBT; ~99.6%) and others (~90%) estimate that the float is overwhelmingly insider-controlled — meaning the small "circulating" number is itself misleading and the printed market cap is largely notional.
- **Liquidity vs valuation mismatch.** ~$8.1M/day volume on a ~$3.80B cap (Vol/MC 0.21%) is the defining quantitative tell of a thin, insider-managed float: a multi-billion-dollar "valuation" that could not absorb even modest real selling.
- **No meaningful burn/sink.** Unlike supply-capped memes such as [[pepe|PEPE]] (MC/FDV ≈ 1.0, fully circulating), M's value rests on a large, controlled, and largely locked supply — the opposite of a clean float.

The huge MC/FDV gap (0.24) underscores the dilution overhang. Tracker-dependent figures still warrant skepticism.

## Market Structure & Derivatives

- **Why it matters**: M is the textbook example of a **low-float, high-FDV, insider-controlled listing** sustaining an inflated printed market cap — the same pattern as RAVE/SIREN-style implosions. Useful as a screening template: **Vol/MC < 1%** (M is ~0.21%), **MC/FDV gap > 3×** (M is ~4×), single-venue price leadership, and a concentrated holder base are the tells. See [[market-manipulation]] and [[wash-trading]].
- **Where it trades**: Kraken (M/USD), Bitget (M/USDT), Binance Alpha, HTX, BingX, HashKey. **No deep major-venue perp depth.** Unlike liquid memes such as [[pepe|PEPE]] — which has very active, retail-driven [[perpetual-futures|perp]] markets on [[hyperliquid]] and CEXs with volatile funding — M lacks meaningful [[perpetual-futures|perpetual]] liquidity, and **borrow for shorting is scarce and expensive**. That asymmetry is *precisely how the float game persists*: there is no cheap, liquid way to short the obvious overvaluation, so the inflated cap can sustain far longer than fundamentals justify.
- **Liquidity reality**: with ~$8.1M traded against a ~$3.80B printed cap, realistic exit liquidity is that of a single-digit-millions-per-day token — any attempt to exit a meaningful position would move the price hard.

## Valuation Framework

There is no fundamental valuation anchor; the printed market cap is the *output of a thin, controlled float*, not a market-clearing price:

- **Printed cap is notional.** MC = price × circulating supply, but ~90–99.6% insider control means very few tokens actually trade — the "valuation" reflects the marginal price of a tiny free float, not what the whole supply could fetch.
- **FDV exposes the overhang.** $15.57B FDV vs $3.80B MC (MC/FDV 0.24) implies large future dilution; on any normalized free-float basis the asset is dramatically overvalued.
- **Comparative tell.** Healthy memes like [[pepe|PEPE]] sit at MC/FDV ≈ 1.0 with high turnover; M's 0.24 MC/FDV and 0.21% Vol/MC are the inverse — the quantitative signature of a managed cap. Value M on its *liquidity and holder distribution*, not its headline rank.

## Trading Playbook

- **Default stance: avoid / monitor.** For most books the correct position is none; M is a study object, not a core trade.
- **If traded at all**: treat as **[[event-driven]] only** — exchange listing/delisting announcements, unlock/insider-flow alerts (ZachXBT-style on-chain monitoring), and short-horizon momentum scalps with **hard stops**. Size to exit liquidity of a ~$8–30M/day token, never the ~$3.8B printed cap.
- **Short side is a trap.** The fundamental overvaluation is "obvious," but scarce/expensive borrow and thin perp depth make shorting operationally hazardous — crowded, hard-to-exit, and squeeze-prone. The float game survives on exactly this difficulty.
- **Kill signal to watch**: any large insider wallet movement to exchanges (repeating the 2025-07-03 Kraken-deposit pattern) historically precedes drawdowns — a primary early-warning tell.

## Risks

- **Insider supply control (~90–99.6%)** — the dominant risk; a handful of wallets can move price and exit liquidity at will. The small "circulating" float is itself misleading.
- **Extreme illiquidity vs valuation** — ~$8.1M/day against a ~$3.80B printed cap (Vol/MC ~0.21%); real exit liquidity is a fraction of the headline rank, so any sizable seller crashes the price.
- **Massive dilution overhang** — MC/FDV 0.24; ~$15.57B FDV vs ~$3.80B MC means large future unlocks loom over the price.
- **Manipulation / pump-and-dump allegations** — ZachXBT and others have characterized the move as bearing signs of a coordinated pump-and-dump and criticized listing due diligence ([[market-manipulation]], [[wash-trading]]).
- **Hard-to-hedge** — scarce/expensive borrow and thin [[perpetual-futures|perp]] depth mean the obvious overvaluation cannot be cheaply shorted; the float game persists precisely because of this asymmetry.
- **Extreme-fear backdrop (2026-06-20)** — an Established Bear Market (Fear & Greed = 23) is structurally hostile to a thin, speculative, insider-controlled asset; a single insider-to-exchange flow could trigger a cascade.
- **Tracker unreliability** — historical CMC vs CoinGecko valuation disagreements (billions apart) mean even the basic numbers should be treated skeptically.
