# InfiniFi USD

**InfiniFi USD** (ticker **IUSD**; native chain [[ethereum|Ethereum]]) is a yield-bearing, USD-pegged [[stablecoins|stablecoin]] issued by **InfiniFi Labs**. It targets a **1:1 peg to the US dollar** while passing on yield to holders; the yield model is best understood as a **maturity-laddered "fixed vs liquid" stablecoin banking** design (holders choose between an instantly liquid balance and locked, higher-yielding tranches). As of the snapshot below it trades on peg at ~$0.9988, but its price history includes a **severe past de-peg** (ATL $0.2431) that materially shapes its risk profile.

## Architecture — How InfiniFi Works

InfiniFi USD is a **yield-bearing stablecoin** on [[ethereum|Ethereum]] that conceptually splits a dollar deposit into a *liquid* leg and a *locked/term* leg, in the spirit of on-chain "stablecoin banking." The publicly described model works roughly as follows; precise live parameters should be confirmed against the project docs (docs.infinifi.xyz):

- **Mint:** A user deposits a base dollar stablecoin (e.g. [[usdc|USDC]]) and receives IUSD 1:1. IUSD is the liquid, instantly redeemable claim.
- **Liquid vs locked tranches:** Holders can keep IUSD liquid (lower yield) or **lock** it into fixed-term positions that earn a higher rate. The locked capital is what funds the riskier/illiquid yield strategies, while the liquid buffer is kept in safe, redeemable assets so day-to-day redemptions clear. This **maturity-laddering** is the core design idea — it lets the protocol chase term yield without making every holder illiquid.
- **Redeem:** Liquid IUSD redeems toward $1 against the protocol's liquid reserve; locked positions return at maturity. As with any term product, exiting a *locked* position early may not be possible at par.

### Yield source & distribution

Yield is generated by deploying the locked/term capital into on-chain strategies (lending, money-market-style allocations, and partner protocols) and distributing the proceeds to lockers, with the liquid tier earning a thinner spread. This is **strategy yield**, not a guaranteed deposit rate — returns rise and fall with DeFi rates and carry the underlying strategies' credit and smart-contract risk. The precise collateral set and live allocations are **not fully attested** in the data available to this wiki, so the backing should be treated as **partially unverified** until a transparent reserve report is ingested.

## Tokenomics & Supply

- **Circulating / total supply:** ~77.93M IUSD (fully circulating; no large unvested overhang surfaced in the snapshot).
- IUSD is **mint-on-deposit / burn-on-redeem**, so supply tracks deposits rather than a fixed cap.
- The split between liquid IUSD and locked-term positions determines how much of the float is instantly redeemable at any moment — a key solvency-liquidity tradeoff for holders to monitor.

## Narrative / Category & Catalysts

IUSD sits in the **yield-bearing / "on-chain dollar banking"** narrative — the fast-growing category (USDe, sUSDS, USDY, and term-yield designs) that competes with bare fiat coins by paying a rate. Its distinctive angle is the **liquid-vs-locked maturity ladder**, which is a credible way to offer term yield without trapping every holder. Catalysts: rising or falling DeFi base rates (which set the achievable spread), a transparent reserve attestation (which would materially de-risk the page from "partially unverified"), CEX/DEX listings to fix the liquidity problem, and integrations that put IUSD to work as collateral. In the current **bottoming/accumulation** crypto regime (Fear & Greed 21, Extreme Fear, as of 2026-06-22), risk appetite for thin, opaque yield dollars is low — a headwind for adoption.

## How / where it trades

Liquidity is **extremely thin**: reported 24h volume is only **~$50** against a ~$78M market cap, with a 24h range of $0.9985–$1.00. There is effectively **no active secondary market**, so the quoted ~$1 price is redemption-/last-print-dependent rather than a tradeable level. Realistic exit at par relies on the protocol's own redemption path. The CoinGecko snapshot surfaces no deep named exchange order books, and there is no evidence of deep Curve/Uniswap pools — composability is minimal at this size.

## Risks

- **De-peg risk (historical, severe):** ATL of $0.243 shows the peg has broken hard before; thin liquidity makes future dislocations plausible.
- **Liquidity risk (acute):** ~$50/day volume — exiting any meaningful size at par may be impossible without protocol redemption.
- **Backing transparency risk:** Collateral and yield source are undisclosed in available data — a material unknown.
- **Yield-strategy risk:** As a yield-bearing dollar, returns depend on an underlying strategy that can lose value, especially in the current "Established Bear Market" (Fear & Greed 23).
- **Issuer / smart-contract risk:** Single-issuer (InfiniFi Labs) model with limited public information.
- **Regulatory risk:** Yield-bearing stablecoins face uncertain and tightening regulatory treatment.

## Trading / Usage Playbook

- **Treat the secondary "price" as informational, not executable** — at ~$50/day volume there is no liquid market; plan to enter and exit through the protocol's own mint/redeem path, not a DEX.
- **Distinguish liquid IUSD from locked tranches** before committing capital: locked positions earn more but cannot be exited at par before maturity.
- **Demand the reserve attestation.** Until backing is transparently verified, size positions as if the yield strategy could impair principal.
- **Respect the de-peg history.** An asset that printed $0.243 and $1.38 in one week is not a cash equivalent; do not use it as a stable store of value for funds you cannot afford to lock or lose.
- **Watch the liquid-buffer ratio** as the key solvency-liquidity signal — a thin liquid tier against heavy locked capital raises run risk.
