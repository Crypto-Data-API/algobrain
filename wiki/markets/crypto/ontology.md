# Ontology

**Ontology** (ticker **ONT**) is a **Layer 1 public blockchain focused on decentralized identity and trust**. Launched in 2018 by Chinese company OnChain — led by Da HongFei and Erik Zhang, the same founders behind NEO — Ontology was built as a "distributed trust collaboration platform" that lets businesses adopt blockchain (including decentralized digital identity, or DID) without overhauling their existing systems. Its dual-token model uses **ONT** for staking/governance and **ONG** as the gas/utility token. ONT is ranked **#487** by market capitalization as of this snapshot.

ONT's full 1B supply is in circulation (market cap equals FDV, ratio ~1.00), so there is **no ONT unlock overhang** — a positive for holders. Ontology uses a **dual-token model**: ONT is staked for consensus and governance, and holding/staking ONT generates **ONG**, the gas token used to pay for transactions. ONG accrues to ONT holders over time, which is how the network's emissions effectively reach participants. (See [[staking]].)

## Technology & Consensus

Ontology runs its own **Layer 1** chain using a **VBFT** consensus that combines Proof-of-Stake, Verifiable Random Function (VRF), and Byzantine Fault Tolerance, aligning it broadly with the [[proof-of-stake]] family. The architecture is built around a **distributed identity framework (ONT ID)** and a distributed data-exchange protocol, supporting both public and permissioned/business deployments and interoperability across chains. The dual-token (ONT/ONG) design separates staking/governance from gas costs. (See [[layer-1]], [[proof-of-stake]].)

## Use Case, Narrative & Category

Ontology's category is **decentralized identity (DID) and trust infrastructure**. Its core products — ONT ID (self-sovereign identity) and a reputation/data-exchange layer — target enterprises and Web3 applications needing verifiable, privacy-respecting identity. CoinGecko category tags include Smart Contract Platform, Masternodes, and Proof of Stake (PoS). The narrative is a long-running bet on **decentralized identity** adoption; as a 2018-era project from the NEO founders, it carries strong name recognition but must compete in a crowded DID landscape.

## Valuation Framing (qualitative)

ONT is effectively a **fully-diluted legacy L1** (MC = FDV, no unlock overhang), so its ~$46M cap is a relatively "clean" read on what the market assigns to the brand, the [[proof-of-stake|VBFT]] chain, and the still-unrealized DID thesis. Against that, the relevant comparison is to other DID/identity tokens and to the broad small-cap L1 cohort: ONT trades like a low-float legacy name whose price is driven by sentiment and CEX liquidity rather than fundamentals or token unlocks. Multi-cycle failure to convert the identity narrative into measurable on-chain usage is the key reason it sits ~99.6% below its 2018 ATH. A durable re-rating would require either real DID adoption or a renewed identity narrative — this is framing, not a price target.

## Risks

- **Extreme long-term drawdown**: ONT is ~99.6% below its 2018 ATH and has not recovered across multiple cycles.
- **Narrative dependence**: The decentralized-identity thesis has been slow to convert into measurable on-chain usage; DID is highly competitive.
- **Geographic/regulatory exposure**: Its "Made in China" origin and association with the China-linked NEO/OnChain ecosystem can add regulatory and perception risk.
- **Inflationary gas token**: ONG emissions add ongoing supply to the dual-token system.
- **Macro / regime risk**: Extreme Fear (index 23) and an Established Bear Market weigh on small-cap tokens.
- **Legacy project risk**: As an older project, ONT competes against newer L1s and identity solutions for relevance and developer mindshare.
