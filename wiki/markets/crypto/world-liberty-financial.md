# World Liberty Financial

**World Liberty Financial** (WLFI) is the governance token of the Trump family-affiliated [[defi|DeFi]] and stablecoin venture launched in September 2024, issuer of the **[[usd1-wlfi|USD1]] [[stablecoins|stablecoin]]**. For traders it is the purest large-cap expression of the "Trump-affiliated crypto" basket: a politically driven asset whose price responds to US political news, regulatory decisions touching the venture, and its own contentious governance (token unlocks, wallet blacklists, the [[justin-sun|Justin Sun]] dispute).

## Protocol / Product

World Liberty Financial was launched in **September 2024** with Donald Trump styled "Chief Crypto Advocate" and his sons as ambassadors; a Trump family business entity is entitled to a large share of token-sale revenues. WLFI is the **governance token** of the venture; its flagship product is the **[[usd1-wlfi|USD1]] dollar stablecoin**.

### Components

- **WLFI governance token** — votes on treasury deployment and protocol decisions; the politically affiliated, cash-flow-less asset.
- **USD1 stablecoin** — a dollar-pegged [[stablecoins|stablecoin]] issued March 2025, custodied by BitGo. Used in mid-2025 for a **$2B Abu Dhabi (MGX) investment into Binance**, and launched natively on the Stripe-backed **Tempo L1** mainnet on 8 May 2026. USD1 also runs on Ethereum, Solana, BNB Chain, and [[tron|Tron]].
- **DeFi/treasury** — a multi-billion-dollar treasury whose deployment is subject to governance votes (a recurring supply/risk event).

### Trump affiliation

The Trump family connection is the entire investment thesis and the entire risk: WLFI's value rests on political affiliation rather than protocol cash flows. The venture's revenue-share structure with a Trump family entity, and Trump's role as "Chief Crypto Advocate," tie WLFI's fortunes to US political and regulatory developments.

## Valuation Framework

WLFI is **not valuable on protocol cash flows** — it is valued on political/affiliation premium and the USD1 business option. Useful lenses:

- **Political-premium proxy** — WLFI is effectively a leveraged bet on the Trump administration's pro-crypto posture; its premium expands/contracts with political and regulatory news.
- **USD1 franchise value** — the stablecoin is the only real business; USD1 supply growth (and a potential OCC-chartered trust bank) is the fundamental upside that could eventually justify part of the FDV.
- **Supply-adjusted discount** — with 68% locked, any cash-flow or franchise valuation must be heavily discounted for dilution; FDV ($5.87B) vs market cap ($1.87B) shows how much "future supply" the price already implies.
- **Basket relative** — trades with [[official-trump|TRUMP]] and [[crypto-com-chain|CRO]]; relative-value within the Trump basket expresses views on which affiliation premium is rich/cheap.

## Trading Playbook

- **Political/regulatory event trade** — WLFI moves on US political news, regulatory decisions touching the venture, and Trump-family developments. Position around OCC trust-charter decisions, election-cycle headlines, and policy announcements.
- **Trump-affiliated basket** — anchor of the basket with [[official-trump|TRUMP]] and [[crypto-com-chain|CRO]]; all three co-move on political news, making the basket a natural election-cycle/regulatory-headline vehicle.
- **Unlock-overhang awareness** — each treasury unlock vote is a supply event on a 0.32 MC/FDV token; fade rallies into unlocks unless a political catalyst overrides.
- **Capitulation-vs-catalyst battleground** — at ~14% above ATL with persistent negative sentiment, WLFI is a battleground between bear-market capitulation and an asymmetric political/regulatory catalyst; the [[hyperliquid|Hyperliquid]] perp is the cleanest way to express either side with defined risk.
- **Counterparty caveat** — the team has blacklisted wallets before (see History); large holders carry a unique custody/freeze risk.

## History & Controversies

- **Sep 2024** — World Liberty Financial launched; Trump as "Chief Crypto Advocate," sons as ambassadors; Trump family entity entitled to a large share of token-sale revenue.
- **2024–2025** — WLFI token sales, including **~$75M of purchases by [[tron|Tron]] founder [[justin-sun|Justin Sun]]**.
- **Mar 2025** — **USD1** stablecoin issued (BitGo custody); used mid-2025 in the **$2B Abu Dhabi (MGX) → Binance** investment.
- **1 Sep 2025** — WLFI became transferable/tradable on Binance, Kraken, Upbit, Bitget, KuCoin, Crypto.com, and Hyperliquid; **all-time high $0.3313 on listing day**, then a persistent downtrend.
- **Sep 2025 — wallet blacklist controversy** — the project **blacklisted Justin Sun's wallets**, freezing billions of WLFI; the basis of his 2026 lawsuit.
- **Dec 2025 — treasury unlock vote** — governance proposal to deploy ~5% of a ~$2.4B treasury (~$120M) to grow USD1 supply; closely contested vote (an example of unlock-driven governance risk).
- **Jan 2026 — OCC trust-bank application** — WLTC Holdings LLC applied for a **national trust bank charter** ("World Liberty Trust Company") to issue/custody USD1 under federal oversight (Business Wire, 7 Jan 2026).
- **Late Jan 2026 — Abu Dhabi stake** — interests linked to Sheikh Tahnoun bin Zayed Al Nahyan reportedly agreed to buy **49% of WLFI (the company) for ~$500M**.
- **Apr 2026 — Justin Sun lawsuit** — Sun sued WLF alleging extortion, frozen wallets, and denied voting rights tied to his blacklisted tokens; same month, **$25M fresh USD1 minted / $3M burned** in a single operation amid borrowing controversy.
- **2 May 2026** — **new all-time low $0.0516**.
- **8 May 2026** — **USD1 launched natively on the Stripe-backed Tempo L1** mainnet.
- **20 Jun 2026** — **$0.0587**, rank #46, ~82% below ATH, ~14% above ATL, in an established bear market.

### Controversies (stated factually)

- **Wallet blacklisting** — the team froze Justin Sun's wallets (billions of WLFI), a unique counterparty/custody risk and the basis of active litigation.
- **Conflict-of-interest concerns** — a sitting US president's family profiting from a crypto venture has drawn extensive political and ethical scrutiny; reported as documented controversy, not a verdict.
- **Governance/unlock disputes** — closely contested treasury-unlock votes and the large locked supply have fueled criticism of insider control.

## Risks

- **Political/governance risk** — value rests on political affiliation, not cash flows; a shift in the Trump family's political position or pro-crypto policy would directly impair WLFI.
- **Concentration / insider-control risk** — ~68% of supply locked/non-circulating; treasury and unlock decisions are insider-influenced governance events; FDV implies large pending dilution.
- **Counterparty/custody risk (unique)** — the team has **blacklisted wallets and frozen tokens**; large holders face a freeze/seizure risk uncommon in major tokens.
- **Regulatory risk** — the OCC trust-charter and the conflict-of-interest scrutiny cut both ways; an adverse regulatory or legal outcome (incl. the Justin Sun litigation) is a tail risk.
- **No cash-flow valuation** — WLFI itself produces no revenue; only USD1 has a business model, and that value is heavily diluted at the token level.
- **Bear-market regime** — Fear & Greed 23; price near ATL with persistent negative sentiment makes WLFI a high-volatility capitulation-vs-catalyst battleground.
