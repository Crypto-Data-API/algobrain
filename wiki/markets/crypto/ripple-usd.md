# Ripple USD

**Ripple USD** (ticker **RLUSD**) is the NYDFS-regulated, fiat-backed USD [[stablecoin]] issued by **Standard Custody & Trust Company**, a wholly owned subsidiary of [[ripple|Ripple Labs]], launched in December 2024 on both the [[xrp|XRP Ledger]] and [[ethereum|Ethereum]]. For traders it is one of the fastest-growing regulated stablecoins (~$1.6B and closing on $2B by mid-2026), a direct beneficiary of the [[genius-act|GENIUS Act]] regime, and — importantly — a complicating factor for the XRP thesis, since RLUSD competes with XRP's bridge-asset use case.

## Overview

RLUSD is a USD-denominated stablecoin created with trust, liquidity, and compliance at its core; each RLUSD is backed by at least an equivalent amount of US dollars and cash equivalents. It is purpose-built for cross-border payments and institutional settlement, leveraging Ripple's decade of banking relationships and global license portfolio. RLUSD is positioned as the "compliance-first" challenger to [[usdc|USDC]] in institutional flows, rather than competing with [[usdt|USDT]]'s offshore retail dominance.

---

## 2025–2026 Developments

- **GENIUS Act (July 2025)** — the US federal payment-stablecoin framework enacted in July 2025 played directly to RLUSD's regulated design; Ripple also **applied for a US national bank charter** with the OCC in July 2025 (the OCC conditionally granted national trust charters to Circle, Paxos and three other nonbanks in December 2025).
- **Institutional integrations (2025–2026)** — BlackRock adopted RLUSD as a **redemption mechanism for its BUIDL tokenized fund**; LMAX Group made RLUSD a core collateral asset for banks, brokers and buy-side institutions; Deutsche Bank integrated Ripple's payment infrastructure; SBI began a Japan rollout in Q1 2026; Aave's Horizon RWA market launched with Ripple as a partner.
- **Record usage** — RLUSD transfer volume hit **$18.4B in Q1 2026**, the highest quarter on record, with >55% of activity in March 2026.
- **Market cap milestones** — crossed $1.25B (~15 months post-launch), $1.6B by spring 2026, ~$1.67B by June 2026; on track for $2B.
- **XRP tension** — analyst commentary through 2026 ("RLUSD's growth isn't helping XRP") highlights that RLUSD absorbing payment/settlement flows undercuts the legacy XRP bridge-asset narrative.

## Mechanism & Backing

RLUSD is a **fiat-reserve payment stablecoin** — the most conservative backing model among the tokens in this cluster, comparable to [[usdc|USDC]]:

- **Reserves** — each RLUSD is backed ≥1:1 by US dollar deposits, short-term US Treasuries, and cash equivalents held by Standard Custody & Trust under a **NYDFS limited-purpose trust charter** (New York's bank-grade regime). Reserves are segregated and bankruptcy-remote.
- **Attestations** — monthly third-party attestations of reserve composition and adequacy; this is an attestation regime, distinct from a full GAAP audit.
- **Mint / redeem** — institutional participants mint and redeem 1:1 against USD; RLUSD is purpose-built for cross-border payments and institutional settlement, leveraging Ripple's banking-license portfolio.
- **No native yield** — under the GENIUS Act, payment stablecoins cannot pay issuer interest; RLUSD yield must come from money-market wrappers or DeFi deployment ([[stablecoin-yields]]).

**KYC / regulatory.** RLUSD is positioned as the "compliance-first" challenger to USDC in institutional flows. Ripple applied for a **US national bank charter** with the OCC in July 2025; the OCC conditionally granted national trust charters to Circle, Paxos, and others in December 2025. RLUSD's NYDFS oversight and GENIUS alignment make it a regulated-stablecoin bellwether.

## Risks

- **Substitution risk to XRP** — RLUSD absorbing payment/settlement flows undercuts the legacy [[xrp|XRP]] bridge-asset narrative; bullish for Ripple-the-company, ambiguous-to-bearish for XRP-the-token.
- **Reserve / custodial risk** — concentration with Standard Custody and the underlying Treasuries; attestations are not full audits.
- **Regulatory / charter risk** — pending OCC national-bank-charter application; evolving GENIUS-Act implementation could reshape permissible features.
- **Competitive risk** — crowded regulated-stablecoin field (USDC, PYUSD, USDtb); RLUSD must keep winning institutional integrations to sustain its growth trajectory.
- **Smart-contract / dual-chain risk** — separate deployments on the XRP Ledger and Ethereum each carry their own contract and bridge surface.
