# Ronin

**Ronin** (ticker **RON**) is the native token of the **Ronin chain**, a gaming-focused [[ethereum]] sidechain built by Sky Mavis to scale **[[axie-infinity|Axie Infinity]]** after Ethereum gas fees made play-to-earn uneconomic. RON pays gas, secures the network via staking, and is used across the Ronin gaming ecosystem. The chain is best known both as the original GameFi flagship and as the site of one of crypto's largest exploits — the **March 2022 ~$625M Ronin bridge hack**.

## Platform & Chain Information

**Native Chain:** Ronin — an EVM-compatible Ethereum **sidechain** built by Sky Mavis. It runs a delegated/authority validator set (originally proof-of-authority, later opened to staking) and historically bridged assets to and from [[ethereum]] via the Ronin bridge. CoinGecko also tags it as an "L1" and "Gaming Blockchain"; functionally it is a gaming-optimised sidechain rather than a fully independent base layer.

---

## Use Case, Narrative & Category

Ronin is the canonical **gaming sidechain** and the home of **[[axie-infinity|Axie Infinity]]**, the breakout play-to-earn title of the 2021 cycle. Its purpose is to give games near-zero gas fees and fast finality that [[ethereum]] mainnet could not offer during peak demand. Key context:

- **Built by Sky Mavis**, the studio behind Axie Infinity; RON launched in 2021 as the chain's gas and staking token.
- **GameFi / Play-to-Earn** is the core narrative — at its peak Axie drove a large share of all on-chain gaming activity, particularly in the Philippines and Southeast Asia, where "scholarship" models let players earn in-game tokens (SLP/AXS).
- Post-hack, Ronin opened up beyond Axie to host **third-party games** and partnered with studios to broaden the ecosystem; it has also moved toward a more decentralized, staked validator set.
- Backers include **Andreessen Horowitz (a16z)** and the former Binance Labs (now YZi Labs).

## Notable History

### The 2022 Ronin Bridge Hack (~$625M)

On **2022-03-23**, attackers compromised the **Ronin bridge**, draining **173,600 ETH and 25.5M USDC** — worth roughly **$625M** at the time — in one of the largest crypto exploits ever recorded. Key facts:

- The Ronin bridge used a **9-validator multisig**, requiring **5 of 9** signatures to approve withdrawals. The attacker gained control of **5 validator keys**: four from Sky Mavis nodes plus one from the **Axie DAO**, which had earlier whitelisted Sky Mavis to sign on its behalf during a period of high transaction load — and that delegation was never revoked.
- The breach reportedly traced back to a **social-engineering / spear-phishing attack** on a Sky Mavis employee, giving the attacker access to the infrastructure.
- The exploit occurred on 2022-03-23 but was **not discovered until 2022-03-29**, six days later, when a user reported being unable to withdraw funds — highlighting a monitoring failure.
- The **US Treasury/FBI later attributed the attack to the Lazarus Group**, a North Korea-linked state-sponsored hacking group.
- **Sky Mavis raised $150M** (led by Binance) to help **reimburse affected users**, reopened the bridge in mid-2022 with an expanded validator set and stricter security, and committed to making users whole.

The hack permanently reshaped the chain's risk profile and is a canonical case study in **bridge security** and **validator-key custody** failures (see [[cross-chain-bridge]] and [[smart-contract-risk]]).

## Valuation Framing (qualitative)

At ~$48M market cap RON is priced ~98.6% below its 2024 peak — a distressed GameFi micro-cap valuation. The bull case is that Ronin is still the most credible **dedicated gaming chain** with real, branded distribution ([[axie-infinity|Axie Infinity]]), an a16z/YZi-Labs backer set, and a post-hack push to host third-party games that could re-rate it if a new web3-gaming cycle arrives. The bear case is heavier: single-ecosystem dependence on a fading flagship, a ~0.77 MC/FDV dilution overhang, a comparatively centralized validator set, and an indelible bridge-hack reputation. It trades almost entirely as a high-beta GameFi sector option, prone to sharp squeezes (e.g. the +7.5% snapshot day) precisely because the float and book are thin.

## Risks

- **Bridge / security history** — the 2022 exploit demonstrated severe custody and validator-key risk; while security has since been hardened, the chain carries reputational and structural bridge risk (see [[cross-chain-bridge]]).
- **Single-ecosystem dependence** — RON's fundamentals are tightly coupled to Axie Infinity and a still-maturing roster of third-party games; play-to-earn demand has fallen dramatically from 2021 levels.
- **Centralization** — a comparatively small validator set (vs. large L1s) concentrates trust, a contributing factor in the original hack.
- **GameFi sector weakness** — the play-to-earn thesis remains out of favour, weighing on token demand and emissions absorption.
- **Dilution overhang** — with MC/FDV ~0.77, ~23% of supply is still to unlock; FDV sits ~29% above the live cap, adding sell-side weight as incentives/staking rewards vest.
- **Thin liquidity & extreme-fear regime** — ~$6M daily volume plus the **Fear & Greed 23 / "Established Bear Market"** backdrop (2026-06-20) make sharp moves likely in both directions (cf. the +7.5% snapshot day). See [[market-regime]] and [[crypto-fear-and-greed-index]].
