# BitMart

**BitMart** (ticker **BMX**, on [[ethereum|Ethereum]]) is the native [[exchange-tokens|exchange utility token]] of BitMart, a global [[centralized-exchange|centralized cryptocurrency exchange]]. BMX is an ERC-20 token, first issued as **BMC** in December 2017 and renamed **BMX** in January 2018 after the original ticker clashed with another project. As a classic CEX token, BMX is used for trading-fee discounts and exchange loyalty programs, and its value is tied to BitMart's trading activity and credibility.

BMX is an ERC-20 exchange token with a capped max supply (~881.68M). The standard CEX-token model applies: holders receive fee discounts and access to exchange perks, and exchanges typically support such tokens with periodic buyback-and-burn programs funded from exchange revenue to create deflationary pressure. The **~0.53 market-cap/FDV ratio** means roughly half of max supply is not yet circulating — meaningfully more dilution overhang than fully-circulating peers like [[coinex-token|CET]] or [[safepal|SFP]] (~1.00 Mcap/FDV). If those reserve/emission tokens reach the market faster than burns retire supply, the float roughly doubles versus today.

## Valuation Framing

Like all [[exchange-tokens]], BMX is best read as a **claim on BitMart's franchise** rather than a cash-flow asset:

- **Volume proxy** — a ~$106M token cap implies the market ascribes modest but non-trivial value to BitMart's exchange business. The relatively deep ~$9.7M/day token turnover suggests more genuine two-sided interest than peers like [[coinex-token|CET]].
- **Burn coverage** — the buyback-and-burn is revenue-funded; its credibility scales with BitMart's actual fee income. With ~47% of supply still uncirculated, the burn has to outpace future emissions to be net-deflationary.
- **Relative strength** — BMX's modest drawdown (-49.7% vs ATH) versus 87–94% for the AI peers in this cohort marks it as a lower-beta, franchise-anchored name rather than a narrative trade.

Qualitative framing only — see valuation.

## Notable History

- **Dec 2017:** Token launched as **BMC**.
- **Jan 2018:** Renamed to **BMX** due to a ticker collision (the contract/explorer may still show "BMC").
- **2021 BitMart hack:** BitMart suffered a major exchange security breach in December 2021 (~$150M in reported losses), a reminder of the counterparty/security risk inherent to exchange-token issuers.
- **2024-06-05:** BMX printed its $0.619 ATH; it has since retraced ~50%, holding up far better than most peers through the 2025–2026 downturn.

## Risks

- **Exchange counterparty & security risk:** BMX value depends on BitMart's solvency, security, and reputation; BitMart's 2021 hack underscores this exposure.
- **Regulatory risk:** Global CEXs face shifting licensing and enforcement regimes that can restrict listings, fiat rails, and jurisdictions.
- **Venue concentration:** Limited external listings concentrate liquidity around the issuing exchange.
- **Supply overhang:** ~47% of max supply not yet circulating; future emissions/unlocks can pressure price.
- **Regime risk:** Despite recent relative strength, an [[crypto-fear-and-greed-index|extreme-fear]] (F&G 23) bear market keeps drawdown risk elevated for all exchange tokens.
