# Sentient

**Sentient** (ticker **SENT**) is an Ethereum-based [[artificial-intelligence|AI]] token backing an open, community-built AGI (artificial general intelligence) platform. Its core product, "THE GRID," is pitched as a decentralized network of agents, models, datasets, tools, and compute intended as an open-source counterweight to closed labs such as OpenAI, [[anthropic|Anthropic]], and Google. It sits in the AI / decentralized-compute narrative on the [[ethereum|Ethereum]] ecosystem.

## Architecture / How it works

Sentient's thesis is that AGI built behind closed doors concentrates power in a handful of corporations. Its answer is **THE GRID** — described as an open network of intelligence where independent contributors supply specialized **agents, models, datasets, tools, and compute** that **compose** to deliver results. Rather than one monolithic model, the design is a coordination layer that routes a query/task across many specialised components and assembles the outputs.

The conceptual pieces, as self-reported by the project:

1. **Contributor network** — independent parties supply the five resource types (agents, models, datasets, tools, compute), each tracked so contribution can be rewarded.
2. **Composition / orchestration** — components combine to answer a task, so the "intelligence" is emergent across the network rather than housed in a single proprietary model.
3. **Open-source framing** — the project leans on community and open-source positioning ("open AGI vs. closed labs") rather than a single guarded model, and markets partnerships across "100+" collaborators spanning agents, models, data, and compute.
4. **Token-coordinated incentives** — SENT is meant to coordinate and reward contributors across the network, placing Sentient in the broad **decentralized AI / [[depin|DePIN]]-adjacent** infrastructure category.

**Verification caveat:** building competitive AGI infrastructure is extremely capital- and talent-intensive, and decentralized coordination adds difficulty. Delivery of a usable, adopted network is unproven in the wiki's ingested data; treat THE GRID as the project's stated design and the "100+ collaborators" as self-reported marketing rather than verified, at-scale infrastructure.

## Trading Profile

### Venues & liquidity

SENT is tradable on [[binance|Binance]] — **spot plus a USD-margined perpetual** (funding, open interest, and liquidations available). It is **not listed on Hyperliquid**, so Binance is the primary leveraged venue and effectively the reference market for derivatives price discovery, funding, and OI/liquidation flow. Concentrating leveraged activity on a single CEX perp means funding and liquidation dynamics are Binance-driven: crowded positioning shows up as funding skew and cascades on that one book, and there is no cross-venue perp to arb it against. Combined with SENT's mid-cap size and moderate turnover (spot liquidity is adequate on tier-1 books but thin on smaller pairs), this argues for conservative sizing on leverage, wider stops around unlock/news windows, and scaling entries/exits into deeper spot liquidity rather than the perp alone. Broad tier-1 spot coverage (Binance/Kraken/Upbit/Bitget/KuCoin/Crypto.com) supports the cash leg of spot-perp structures.
