# THORChain Yield

**TCY** is a yield/treasury token in the [[thorchain]] ecosystem, created to resolve the protocol's bad debt from its lending and Savers products (collectively "THORFi"). Under the resolution, **10% of all THORChain revenue, in perpetuity, is directed to TCY**; holders must stake their TCY to claim a pro-rata share, which is paid out in [[thorchain|RUNE]]. It ranks **#915** by market capitalization.

## Origin: Resolving THORFi Debt

TCY exists because of a debt restructuring, not a token sale. **THORFi** combined THORChain's Lending and Savers features; in January 2025 those positions defaulted, leaving roughly **$210 million** owed to users. Rather than absorb a total loss, THORChain converted **each $1 of debt into 1 TCY**, minting ~210 million TCY and distributing them to creditors. This swapped an unrecoverable liability for a claim on the protocol's future revenue, aligning former creditors with the network's recovery. Fixed supply is therefore 210,000,000 TCY (circulating ≈ total ≈ max; MC/FDV ≈ 1.00).

## Mechanism: Real Revenue, No Inflation

THORChain has **no block rewards** — all revenue comes from real swap/protocol fees paid by users. The TCY arrangement permanently routes **10% of that real revenue** to staked TCY, paid in RUNE. Because the yield is funded by genuine usage rather than token emissions, it is non-inflationary: TCY's distributions rise and fall with THORChain's actual trading volume and fee income. Holders must **stake** TCY to receive their pro-rata RUNE; unstaked tokens earn nothing.

## Token Role & Value Accrual

TCY is effectively a **revenue-share / treasury token**: a fixed-supply claim on a 10% perpetual cut of THORChain fees. Its fundamental value is tied to THORChain's volume — higher cross-chain swap activity means more RUNE flowing to stakers. It is not a governance token for a separate protocol; its worth derives entirely from THORChain's revenue trajectory.

Because supply is fixed at 210M and the yield is funded by **real fees, not emissions**, TCY can be valued like a perpetual claim on a fee stream — roughly, the present value of (10% × THORChain annual fee revenue), discounted for RUNE price risk and the staking requirement. This makes it one of the cleaner "real yield" instruments in DeFi conceptually: there is no inflation diluting the claim, MC/FDV is ≈1.00 (no unlock overhang), and distributions scale directly with usage. The flip side is that the claim has **no floor** — if THORChain volume collapses, the fee stream and thus TCY's intrinsic value approach zero.

### Why TCY exists at all (the alignment trade)

The conversion of $210M of THORFi debt into 210M TCY was a deliberate **alignment mechanism**: instead of socializing the loss or letting creditors dump claims, it turned former creditors into long-term revenue participants whose payoff depends on THORChain thriving. That converts an adversarial creditor base into a stakeholder base — but it also means TCY's price discovery began from a distressed-debt starting point, which partly explains its steep drawdown from the 2025 ATH.

## Competitive Position

TCY is a bespoke instrument unique to THORChain rather than a competitor in a broad category. Its closest analogues are other "real yield" / fee-sharing tokens, but its distinctive feature is its origin as a debt-resolution vehicle.

The key differentiator: TCY pairs a **non-inflationary, fixed-supply** claim with a **distressed-origin** discount, and its prospects rise or fall directly with [[thorchain]]'s competitiveness as a decentralized cross-chain swap network.

## How & Where It Trades

- **Venues & liquidity:** TCY trades very thinly — sub-$15K reported daily volume. There are no major centralized-exchange pairs surfaced in the data; it is effectively a THORChain-ecosystem asset. Marks are unreliable and slippage is high, so it behaves more like an illiquid claim than a freely traded token.
- **Derivatives:** No perpetual-futures market exists for TCY; there is no funding-rate or open-interest signal. Directional exposure to THORChain is more liquidly expressed through [[thorchain|RUNE]] itself.
- **Yield capture requires staking:** Holding TCY on an exchange or in a wallet earns nothing — the 10% fee share accrues only to **staked** TCY, paid in RUNE. This is a structural reason passive holders should not expect the "10% of revenue" headline to benefit them automatically.

## Narrative, Category & Catalysts

TCY sits in the **real-yield / fee-share** category, with a unique distressed-debt-recovery angle. Its narrative is fundamentally "owning a piece of THORChain's cross-chain swap business."

**Catalysts (positive):** rising THORChain swap volume (e.g. renewed Bitcoin/native-asset cross-chain demand), growth in supported chains/assets, and RUNE appreciation (which raises the USD value of distributions). **Catalysts (negative):** declining THORChain volume, any recurrence of protocol/solvency stress (the THORFi default is the cautionary precedent), and RUNE drawdowns that depress the value of yield paid out.

## Trading Playbook (bear / Extreme-Fear regime)

In the 2026-06-24 backdrop (Fear & Greed 22, established bear market, BTC ~18% below its 200-day MA), TCY is a **high-beta, illiquid bet on THORChain usage**:

- **It is a usage proxy, not a hedge.** In risk-off regimes, cross-chain swap volume tends to fall, which directly shrinks the fee stream TCY is claim on. Expect TCY to underperform, not protect, in a downturn.
- **Liquidity is the binding constraint.** Sub-$15K daily volume means any position is hard to exit near mark; size accordingly and assume material slippage.
- **Yield only if staked.** If the thesis is to harvest the 10% revenue share, the tokens must be staked (yield paid in RUNE) — and that introduces RUNE price risk and an unstaking delay. Unstaked TCY is a pure price bet.
- **RUNE as the more liquid expression.** Traders who simply want directional THORChain exposure can use [[thorchain|RUNE]], which is far more liquid than TCY and avoids the staking-claim mechanics.

## Risks

- **Dependence on THORChain revenue** — the yield is 10% of real fees; if THORChain volume falls, distributions shrink and TCY's value erodes.
- **Protocol/solvency history** — TCY itself stems from a ~$210M default; THORChain's history of lending/Savers stress is a cautionary signal about protocol risk.
- **Staking requirement** — holders earn nothing unless they stake; unstaked or passive holders capture no yield.
- **RUNE exposure** — distributions are paid in RUNE, so TCY holders inherit RUNE price risk on their yield.
- **Illiquidity** — TCY trades very thinly (sub-$15K daily volume reported), so entry/exit can be costly and price marks unreliable.
- **Drawdown volatility** — TCY is well below its 2025 ATH (~$0.34) and can move sharply on THORChain news.

## Overview

10% of all revenue on THORChain, in perpetuity, goes towards TCY tokens.  TCY token holders have to stake their tokens in order to access their pro rata share of the revenue.  The revenue is paid out in RUNE tokens (the main token of THORChain).

There are no block rewards on THORChain.  All the revenue is real, paid by real users.  Therefore, the revenue going towards TCY tokens is real and not inflationary.

TCY was created to resolve THORChain's $210 million debt created by THORFi.  THORFi was the combination of Lending and Savers features.  It was defaulted on in January 2025.

Each $1 of debt was converted into 1 TCY, resulting in 210 million TCY tokens outstanding.  This way, creditors received tokens instead of a total loss.  Giving them a piece of the network's future revenue, aligning their interests with the protocol, and helping stabilize the network.
