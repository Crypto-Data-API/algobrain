# World of Women

World of Women (WoW) is a collection of 10,000 diverse female portrait [[nft|NFTs]] on [[ethereum]] by artist Yam Karkai, launched in July 2021 at a mint price of 0.07 ETH. The project became a prominent symbol of female representation in the male-dominated NFT and crypto space, attracting high-profile advocates including actress Reese Witherspoon and signing with entertainment manager Guy Oseary (known for managing Madonna and U2). While culturally significant for advancing gender diversity in web3, the project's price trajectory also demonstrated that narrative and representation alone cannot sustain market value.

> **Subject note:** World of Women is an **ERC-721 NFT collection**, not a fungible token. It has no ticker, market cap, or token price — it is valued by **floor price in ETH** on NFT marketplaces. There is no WoW coin; any fungible "WoW"-named token would be unrelated.

NFT/collectible markets are highly sentiment-driven and have been weak. No current WoW floor price is asserted here — see disclaimer below.*

## History

### Launch and Cultural Moment (July 2021)

World of Women launched in July 2021, featuring 10,000 algorithmically generated portraits of women with diverse skin tones, hairstyles, backgrounds, and accessories. Artist Yam Karkai's colorful, bold art style and the project's explicit mission of celebrating female representation resonated at a time when the NFT space was overwhelmingly male-dominated in both creators and collectors. The collection minted at 0.07 ETH and gained traction rapidly as a cultural statement as much as a collectible.

### Celebrity and Industry Endorsements (2021-2022)

World of Women attracted attention from prominent women in entertainment and media:

- **Reese Witherspoon** became one of the most visible celebrity collectors, publicly advocating for the project and using WoW NFTs as profile pictures
- **Guy Oseary**, the entertainment manager behind Madonna and U2, signed on to represent World of Women, bringing mainstream entertainment industry connections
- Partnerships were established with **The Sandbox** (metaverse gaming), **IMDb**, and other media brands
- The project was featured in mainstream media coverage as an example of diversity in the NFT space

### Galaxy Companion Collection (2022)

World of Women launched the "World of Women Galaxy" companion collection in 2022, expanding the ecosystem with a new set of characters. The companion collection followed the industry trend of derivative launches, aiming to broaden the community and create additional entry points at lower price levels.

### Market Decline

Despite the cultural impact, celebrity endorsements, and media partnerships, the World of Women floor price declined over 95% from its peak. The sharp decline tracked the broader NFT market downturn but was exacerbated by the project's reliance on narrative momentum rather than utility, technical innovation, or revenue-generating business models.

## Collection mechanics & provenance

World of Women is a generative PFP collection: 10,000 unique tokens algorithmically composed from layered traits (skin tones, hairstyles, backgrounds, accessories, expressions), with rarity driven by trait frequency. Like other ERC-721 PFP collections, value within the set is **stratified by rarity** — common pieces cluster at the floor while rare-trait and "grail" IDs command large multiples in thin, infrequent sales.

- **Provenance and IP:** Created and art-directed by Yam Karkai. Unlike collections that later sold their IP (e.g., Moonbirds/PROOF to Yuga), WoW retained its founding-team identity, with Guy Oseary's representation bridging it to mainstream entertainment.
- **Ecosystem expansion:** The **World of Women Galaxy** companion collection (2022) added a larger, lower-priced character set — the standard "second-collection" playbook used to widen the holder base and create cheaper entry points.
- **Holder rights:** WoW granted holders broad commercial/IP usage rights to their owned token (a model popularized by BAYC), an attempt to let collectors build derivative brands. As with most PFP projects, those rights proved valuable for only a handful of holders.

### Royalties

WoW, like most 2021-era collections, was structured to earn **creator royalties** (a percentage of each secondary sale) to fund operations. The 2023–2024 "royalty wars" — in which marketplaces such as Blur and (optionally) [[opensea|OpenSea]] made royalties optional to win volume — eroded this revenue stream industry-wide. **Royalty erosion** is therefore a structural risk for WoW's treasury and its ability to fund the entertainment/media initiatives that underpin its narrative (see Risks).

## How & where it trades

As an Ethereum ERC-721 collection, WoW trades on NFT marketplaces — primarily [[opensea|OpenSea]] and Blur, with secondary presence on aggregators — priced in **ETH**, not USD. There is no centralized-exchange listing because there is no fungible token.

- **Liquidity structure:** NFT liquidity is fundamentally different from token liquidity — it is "floor-bid" driven. A handful of resting bids near the floor set the marginal clearing price; in a weak market, bids thin out and the realizable floor can be well below the displayed "lowest ask."
- **Blur dynamics:** Blur's bidding pools and incentive programs concentrate liquidity for blue-chips but largely bypass faded collections, so WoW's effective depth in a bear tape is shallow.
- **Settlement:** sales settle in ETH on-chain; with ETH ~$1,737 (2026-06-22 macro snapshot), USD-denominated floor value is also exposed to ETH's own drawdowns — a double sensitivity for holders.

## Cultural Significance

World of Women holds a distinctive place in NFT history for its cultural impact, even as its market performance disappointed:

- **Gender representation**: The project was the most visible effort to address the extreme gender imbalance in NFT creation and collecting, bringing women's voices and imagery to a space dominated by male creators and collectors
- **Celebrity advocacy**: Reese Witherspoon's involvement demonstrated how mainstream celebrities could amplify NFT projects, though it also showed the limits of celebrity endorsement for sustaining market value
- **Narrative vs. value**: WoW became a case study in the disconnect between cultural significance and market price. The project proved that a compelling social narrative can drive initial interest but cannot substitute for sustainable utility, revenue, or technical innovation
- **Diversity as marketing**: The project raised legitimate awareness about inclusion in web3, but also prompted criticism that "representation" was being used primarily as a marketing angle rather than a foundation for lasting structural change
- **Entertainment industry engagement**: The Guy Oseary signing and IMDb partnership represented early attempts to bridge NFTs and traditional entertainment, predating similar moves by larger projects

## Narrative / category & catalysts

WoW sits in the **culture/representation PFP** category — a narrative-first corner of the NFT market. Its catalysts are almost entirely attention-based: a renewed mainstream-entertainment partnership (via Guy Oseary), a high-profile collector re-engaging, a successful media/IP project converting the brand into recurring revenue, or a broad NFT-market risk-on rotation. In the current Bottoming/Accumulation macro tape with Extreme Fear (F&G 21) and weak collectible demand, none of these catalysts is active, and floor recovery is unlikely without a fresh, tangible IP development.

## Risks

- **Sentiment reflexivity** — WoW's value is tied to cultural narrative and attention, not utility or cash flow; floor price is highly sentiment-driven and has already fallen >95% from its 2022 peak.
- **Thin / floor-bid liquidity** — faded blue-chips clear on a handful of resting bids; realizable value can sit well below the displayed lowest ask, and large lots cannot be exited without moving the floor.
- **Royalty erosion** — optional-royalty marketplaces have gutted the creator-royalty revenue that funds the team's media/entertainment ambitions, weakening the path back to utility.
- **Cycle dependence** — collectible markets are among the most fad-driven assets; in a bear NFT cycle, narrative-only collections underperform utility/blue-chip names.
- **ETH-denomination risk** — floor is priced in ETH; an ETH drawdown compounds USD losses for holders even if the ETH floor holds.
- **Celebrity-correlation risk** — celebrity endorsement amplified the launch but has not sustained the floor; loss of high-profile advocacy removes a key attention driver.

## Trading playbook

WoW is a faded narrative blue-chip; in the current Extreme-Fear/Bottoming tape it is a deep-value-or-dead-money proposition:

- **Floor vs grail:** common-floor WoW pieces trade on raw sentiment and are dead money absent a brand catalyst; rare-trait IDs are illiquid lottery tickets. Neither is a liquid trade in this tape.
- **Bid the bid, not the ask:** in thin NFT markets, place patient bids near where real liquidity rests rather than lifting the lowest ask; assume wide effective spreads.
- **Catalyst-gated:** only re-engage on a concrete, dated IP/entertainment development — not on representation narrative alone, which history shows does not sustain the floor.
- **Macro overlay:** with collectibles weak and F&G at 21, treat any rally as sentiment-driven and likely to round-trip; manage exits accordingly.

This is not investment advice; NFT floors are illiquid and highly speculative.
