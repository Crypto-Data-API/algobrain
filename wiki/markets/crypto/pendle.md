# Pendle

**Pendle** (PENDLE) is a [[defi|DeFi]] protocol that enables the **tokenization and trading of future yield**. It splits yield-bearing assets into two components -- a **Principal Token (PT)** representing the underlying asset at maturity and a **Yield Token (YT)** representing the future yield stream -- allowing traders to speculate on, hedge, or lock in fixed rates on DeFi yields. Deployed primarily on [[ethereum]] and [[arbitrum]] (plus BNB Chain, [[optimism]], Base, Sonic and others), Pendle's novel time-decay AMM brings yield-curve trading -- a concept borrowed from traditional fixed-income markets -- on-chain.

## Overview

Pendle is a protocol that enables the tokenization and trading of future yield. With the creation of a novel AMM that supports assets with time decay, Pendle gives users more control over future yield by providing optionality and opportunities for its utilization.

---

## How PT/YT Tokenization Works

### The Split

When a user deposits a yield-bearing asset (e.g., stETH from [[lido]], aUSDC from [[aave]], GLP from [[gmx]]) into Pendle, it is split into:

- **PT (Principal Token)**: Redeemable 1:1 for the underlying asset at maturity. Before maturity, PT trades at a discount to the underlying -- this discount IS the implied fixed yield rate. Buying PT = locking in a fixed yield
- **YT (Yield Token)**: Entitles the holder to all yield generated by the underlying asset from now until maturity. YT price decays toward zero as maturity approaches (time decay, similar to options theta). Buying YT = going long on yield rates

### Example

Deposit 1 stETH (yielding ~3.5% APR) with 6-month maturity into Pendle:
- Receive **1 PT-stETH** (tradeable, worth ~0.983 ETH today, redeemable for 1 stETH at maturity in 6 months = ~3.5% annualized fixed yield)
- Receive **1 YT-stETH** (tradeable, entitles holder to all stETH rebasing yield for 6 months; if stETH yield increases, YT becomes more valuable; if yield decreases, YT loses value)

### The Pendle AMM

Pendle's AMM is specifically designed for assets with time decay (like YT tokens and PT tokens approaching maturity). It uses a modified invariant that accounts for the convergence of PT price toward the underlying asset at maturity, providing low-slippage trading throughout the token lifecycle.

## Trading Strategies

### 1. Fixed-Rate Yield Locking via PT (Low difficulty)

- **Mechanism**: Buy PT tokens at a discount to lock in a guaranteed fixed yield at maturity. This is equivalent to buying a zero-coupon bond in traditional finance
- **Example**: Buy PT-stETH at 0.965 ETH when implied yield is 7% annualized. At maturity, redeem for 1 stETH regardless of what actual stETH yield was during the period. If stETH yield dropped to 2% during that time, the PT buyer earned 7% vs. the 2% they would have received by simply holding stETH
- **APY**: Depends on the implied yield at time of purchase (typically 3-15% for major assets; can spike to 20%+ for newer or more volatile yield sources)
- **Capital requirement**: $1,000+ (Pendle is live on [[arbitrum]] and other L2s with low gas costs)
- **Difficulty**: Beginner-intermediate -- requires understanding maturity dates and implied yields
- **Risk**: Opportunity cost if actual yields exceed the fixed rate you locked in; smart contract risk; underlying asset risk (e.g., if stETH depegs, PT-stETH is still denominated in stETH)
- **Best for**: Risk-averse DeFi users who want predictable, fixed-rate returns

### 2. Yield Speculation via YT (High difficulty)

- **Mechanism**: Buy YT tokens to speculate that the underlying asset's yield will increase (or at least stay above the implied rate). YT provides leveraged exposure to yield changes because the entire future yield stream is compressed into a token that costs a fraction of the underlying
- **Example**: Buy YT-stETH for 0.035 ETH (3.5% of 1 stETH). If stETH yield rises from 3.5% to 5%, the YT becomes significantly more valuable because it entitles the holder to more yield than what was priced in at purchase. Conversely, if yield drops to 2%, the YT loses value
- **Leverage**: YT provides 10-30x leveraged exposure to yield changes (you pay ~3-5% of the underlying to capture 100% of the yield stream)
- **Capital requirement**: $500+ (YTs are cheap relative to the notional exposure)
- **Difficulty**: Advanced -- requires yield curve analysis, understanding of time decay, and active management
- **Risk**: YT decays to zero at maturity regardless of yield. If yields drop or stay flat, YT holders lose their entire investment. This is analogous to buying options that expire worthless
- **Best for**: Traders with conviction on yield direction who understand options-like payoff structures

### 3. Yield Curve Trading (Expert difficulty)

- **Mechanism**: Trade the spread between PT implied yields at different maturities (the DeFi yield curve). If you expect short-term yields to rise relative to long-term yields (curve steepening), buy short-dated YT and sell long-dated PT (or vice versa for flattening)
- **Example**: stETH 3-month implied yield = 4%, stETH 12-month implied yield = 6%. If you believe the curve will flatten (short-term yields will rise toward long-term), buy 3-month YT-stETH and sell 12-month YT-stETH
- **Capital requirement**: $10,000+ (need sufficient capital to express the spread trade meaningfully)
- **Difficulty**: Expert -- requires fixed-income analytical skills applied to DeFi yield markets
- **Risk**: Both legs can move against you; time decay on both YT positions; liquidity risk on less-traded maturities
- **Unique value**: This strategy has no equivalent elsewhere in DeFi. Pendle is the only protocol that enables direct yield curve trading on-chain

### 4. LP on Pendle AMM (Moderate difficulty)

- **Mechanism**: Provide liquidity to PT/underlying trading pools on Pendle's AMM. Earn swap fees + PENDLE token incentives
- **APY**: 5-20% from fees + incentives (varies by pool and market conditions)
- **Capital requirement**: $5,000+
- **Difficulty**: Intermediate -- requires understanding Pendle's AMM mechanics and impermanent loss profile (which differs from [[uniswap]] due to time-decay dynamics)
- **Risk**: IL from PT price convergence; smart contract risk; PENDLE incentive value compression

### 5. Points/Airdrop Farming via Pendle (Time-sensitive, speculative)

- **Mechanism**: Many protocols distribute points to Pendle YT holders (because YT represents the yield/points stream). Holding YT of a points-eligible asset concentrates the points exposure at a fraction of the capital cost
- **Example**: A restaking protocol distributes points to stETH depositors. Buying YT of the restaked stETH asset gives you the full points stream for ~3-5% of the capital cost of holding the underlying
- **Risk**: Points may convert to worthless tokens; protocol may change points distribution rules; YT time decay erodes capital if the airdrop is delayed

## Risk Framework

### Time Decay (Theta Risk)

YT tokens decay to zero at maturity. This is the dominant risk for YT holders and is analogous to options theta decay. Unlike equity options where the underlying can appreciate to offset theta, YT value is purely a function of future yield -- if yields drop, there is no offsetting mechanism.

### Smart Contract Risk

Pendle wraps yield-bearing assets from external protocols, creating a dependency chain: underlying protocol (e.g., [[lido]], [[aave]]) → Pendle tokenization contracts → Pendle AMM. An exploit in any layer affects Pendle users. Pendle's contracts have been audited but are complex (time-decay AMM math, multi-asset wrapping).

### Liquidity Risk

Pendle markets for some asset/maturity combinations may have thin liquidity, leading to high slippage on large trades. Check pool TVL and recent trading volume before entering positions. Major pools (stETH, USDC-based) generally have adequate liquidity; niche pools may not.

### Underlying Asset Risk

PT and YT tokens inherit the risk of the underlying yield-bearing asset. If [[lido|stETH]] depegs, PT-stETH and YT-stETH are both affected. If an [[aave]] lending pool is exploited, aToken-based Pendle positions lose value.

### Maturity Mismatch

PT tokens must be held to maturity to realize the full fixed yield. Selling PT before maturity exposes the holder to mark-to-market risk (PT price may have decreased if implied yields rose since purchase).

- **TVL multiple** — Pendle's market cap should be read against its [[total-value-locked|TVL]] (historically a wide $500M-$5B band, highly cycle-dependent — verify on [[defillama|DefiLlama]]). - **Category monopoly** — Pendle has no meaningful competitor at scale in on-chain yield tokenization, supporting a premium to generic [[automated-market-maker|AMM]] DEX tokens.
- **Reflexivity risk** — much of Pendle's 2023-24 TVL was driven by restaking/points farming. Valuation is therefore sensitive to whether new structural yield sources (RWAs, BTCfi) replace the fading LRT-points narrative.

This is a qualitative framing, not a price target.

## How & Where It Trades

PENDLE has deep liquidity on major centralized venues -- **Binance**, **Kraken**, **Upbit**, **Bitget**, **KuCoin** and **Crypto.com** all list it against USDT/USD/KRW -- and spot DEX liquidity on **Uniswap V3** and **SushiSwap** on [[ethereum]]. The token's own utility lives on the Pendle AMM (vePENDLE governance/boosting and LP rewards), so a meaningful share of supply is locked rather than freely floating.

For leveraged exposure, **PENDLE-PERP** trades on [[hyperliquid]] and other perpetual-futures venues. As a mid-cap DeFi token, funding and open interest are sensitive to TVL momentum and points-farming cycles: when restaking/airdrop narratives are hot, longs crowd in and funding turns persistently positive; in risk-off regimes like the current extreme-fear backdrop, OI thins out and funding can flip negative. Spot drives price discovery here -- 24h volume (~$26M) is modest relative to market cap, so the token can gap on TVL headlines.

## Trading Profile

### Venues & liquidity

PENDLE is one of the deeper mid-cap DeFi markets and trades across **two liquid derivatives venues**. On [[hyperliquid]] it is available as **PENDLE-PERP** (USDC-margined, leverage up to ~40-50x), and on **Binance** it lists as both **spot** (PENDLE/USDT) and a **USD-margined perpetual**. This dual-venue depth matters for execution: size can be split across CEX and on-chain order books to reduce slippage, and the two funding regimes can diverge, opening cross-venue funding and basis trades. Spot liquidity (Binance, Kraken, Upbit, Bitget, KuCoin, Crypto.com plus Uniswap V3 / SushiSwap) leads price discovery, so the perp basis tends to track spot closely rather than gapping in isolation. A meaningful share of supply is locked as vePENDLE, tightening free float and amplifying moves on TVL/points headlines — size positions accordingly and avoid over-leveraging into thin-book weekend sessions.

### Applicable strategies

- [[funding-rate-harvest]] — the two-venue perp market lets you collect funding when restaking/points narratives crowd PENDLE longs and funding turns persistently positive.
- [[hl-vs-cex-funding-divergence]] — Hyperliquid PENDLE-PERP and Binance's USD-margined perp price funding independently, so divergences between the two can be arbitraged.
- [[cash-and-carry]] — deep spot (Binance/Uniswap) plus a liquid perp allows a delta-neutral long-spot / short-perp carry to capture positive basis during hot yield-narrative phases.
- [[crowded-long-funding-fade]] — PENDLE's reflexive TVL/airdrop cycles frequently over-crowd longs; fading extended positive-funding extremes is a recurring setup.
- [[liquidation-cascade-fade]] — as a high-beta DeFi alt with up to ~40-50x leverage available, PENDLE is prone to liquidation cascades that overshoot and mean-revert.
- [[oi-confirmed-trend]] — pairing open-interest expansion with price on TVL-headline breakouts helps separate durable trends from thin-liquidity fakeouts.

### Volatility & regime character

PENDLE is a **high-beta DeFi / yield-infrastructure token**. Its price is tightly coupled to the DeFi TVL and restaking/points-farming cycle rather than moving purely as generic alt beta: it can diverge from BTC/ETH when a yield narrative is hot (as in the +8.36% 7d outperformance during a broad extreme-fear tape). Directionally it still carries elevated positive beta to ETH (its dominant collateral ecosystem) and to BTC in risk-on/risk-off swings, but with a fundamentals overlay — TVL momentum, fee revenue, and the health of the LRT/RWA/BTCfi yield pipeline all drive regime shifts. Expect large drawdowns in bear regimes (retraced ~82% from ATH) and sharp reflexive rallies when new structural yield sources emerge.

### Risk flags

- **Narrative dependence** — much of PENDLE's demand is reflexive on restaking/points/airdrop cycles; a cooling of that meta compresses both TVL and token price.
- **Emissions / dilution** — uncapped supply with ~40% of total still to be emitted as vePENDLE/LP incentives; free float is reduced by vote-escrow locks, which can amplify volatility.
- **Perp funding dislocations** — crowded longs during hot yield narratives push funding persistently positive, raising carry cost and cascade risk on unwinds.
- **Venue / liquidity concentration** — while two-venue, derivatives depth still concentrates on Binance and Hyperliquid; thin books in off-peak sessions widen slippage.
- **Underlying-protocol / smart-contract risk** — as a DeFi token, spot demand is exposed to exploit or depeg events in the underlying yield-bearing assets Pendle wraps.
