# Kamino

**Kamino** (KMNO) is the governance token of **Kamino Finance**, a [[solana|Solana]] [[defi|DeFi]] protocol that unifies **Lending, Liquidity, and Leverage** into a single product suite. It began as one-click, auto-compounding concentrated-liquidity vaults — which became the most popular LP products on Solana — and expanded into a full money market (Kamino Lend) and one-click leverage (Multiply). It is frequently described as **Solana's largest lending protocol** by [[total-value-locked|TVL]].

## Overview

Kamino Finance was originally created to offer users the easiest possible way of providing liquidity and earning yield on-chain.

The protocol's one-click, auto-compounding concentrated liquidity strategies quickly became the most popular LP products on Solana, and laid the foundation for what Kamino is now.

Today, Kamino is a first-of-its-kind DeFi protocol that unifies Lending, Liquidity, and Leverage into a single, secure DeFi product suite.

## Automated Liquidity Vaults

Kamino's flagship product is its **automated liquidity vaults** -- set-and-forget concentrated liquidity management for [[solana]] DEXes.

### The Problem Kamino Solves

Providing concentrated liquidity on [[raydium]] or [[orca]] CLMM pools requires active management: choosing price ranges, rebalancing when price drifts out of range, and compounding earned fees. Most retail LPs either set wide ranges (earning less) or set tight ranges and forget about them (earning nothing when price moves).

### How Kamino Vaults Work

1. **Deposit:** User deposits a single asset or pair into a Kamino vault
2. **Strategy execution:** Kamino's algorithms automatically set and adjust the concentrated liquidity range on the underlying DEX (Raydium, Orca, or Meteora)
3. **Auto-rebalance:** When price drifts outside the range, Kamino rebalances the position to stay in range
4. **Auto-compound:** Earned trading fees are automatically reinvested into the position
5. **Withdraw:** User can withdraw at any time, receiving the underlying assets

### Vault Performance Characteristics

| Aspect | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Target DEXes** | [[raydium]], [[orca]], Meteora |
| **Yield sources** | Trading fees from concentrated LP positions |
| **Management fee** | Varies by vault; typically 1-10% of earned fees |
| **Rebalancing** | Algorithmic, based on volatility and range width |
| **Impermanent loss** | Still present -- Kamino reduces but does not eliminate [[impermanent-loss]] |

### Vault Strategies for Traders

- **SOL/USDC vault:** Moderate yield, moderate IL risk -- good for passive Solana exposure with fee income
- **Stablecoin vaults (USDC/USDT):** Low risk, low yield -- parking capital productively between trades
- **LST vaults (JitoSOL/SOL, mSOL/SOL):** Very low IL (correlated assets), earn swap fees on top of staking yield
- **Volatile pair vaults:** Highest fees but extreme IL risk -- suitable only for pairs you expect to range-trade

## Lending Protocol (Kamino Lend)

Kamino expanded from LP management into **lending and borrowing**, becoming a full DeFi money market on [[solana]].

### How Kamino Lend Works

- **Supply:** Deposit assets (SOL, USDC, USDT, JitoSOL, mSOL, etc.) to earn variable interest from borrowers
- **Borrow:** Use deposited collateral to borrow other assets
- **Liquidation:** If collateral value drops below the required ratio, positions are liquidated

### Lending Strategies

| Strategy | How It Works | Risk |
|---|---|---|
| **Leveraged staking** | Deposit JitoSOL as collateral, borrow SOL, stake the SOL for more JitoSOL, repeat | Liquidation risk if JitoSOL depegs from SOL |
| **Carry trade** | Borrow low-interest assets, deposit into high-yield Kamino vaults | Interest rate and IL risk |
| **Stablecoin lending** | Deposit USDC/USDT for yield | Low risk; smart contract risk only |
| **Short exposure** | Borrow a token, sell it, repay later at lower price | Unlimited upside risk on the borrowed asset |

### Multiply (Leverage)

Kamino's **Multiply** product automates leveraged strategies in a single transaction. Users can create leveraged positions on liquid staking tokens (e.g., 3x leveraged JitoSOL staking) with one click, rather than manually looping borrow/deposit cycles.

## KMNO Token Analysis

### What Drives KMNO Price

1. **Solana DeFi TVL** -- Kamino's revenue depends on total value locked in its vaults and lending protocol
2. **Protocol revenue** -- fees from vault management, lending spreads, and liquidation penalties
3. **Token utility** -- KMNO is used for governance and may accrue protocol fees through staking
4. **Dilution schedule** -- only ~4.88B of 10B max supply circulating (~49%), significant future unlock pressure
5. **Ecosystem positioning** -- Kamino is a core DeFi primitive on Solana; integration with [[jupiter-exchange-solana|Jupiter]], [[raydium]], and [[orca]] creates network effects

### Trading Characteristics (Extended)

- **High beta to Solana DeFi:** KMNO moves with [[solana]] ecosystem sentiment, amplified
- **Low market cap:** At ~$91.5M, KMNO is susceptible to sharp moves on relatively small volume
- **Drawdown:** ~ -92.4% from ATH -- severe bear-market damage typical of DeFi governance tokens; trading near its all-time low
- **Volume:** $8.58M daily (~9.4% of cap) -- adequate for position trading but thin for large size

### Risk Factors

- **Smart contract risk:** Kamino manages billions in TVL through complex automated strategies; a smart contract exploit could be catastrophic
- **Oracle risk:** Incorrect price feeds could trigger improper rebalancing or liquidations
- **Competition:** Tulip, Francium, marginfi, Save (formerly Solend), and other Solana yield/lending protocols compete for capital
- **Token dilution:** ~51% of max supply yet to unlock; check vesting schedule before sizing positions
- **Solana dependency:** Single-chain protocol; no diversification across other L1s — inherits [[solana]] outage/halt and congestion risk

## Valuation Framing (qualitative)

KMNO is a **fee-generating DeFi governance token** with a wide gap between protocol fundamentals and token price:

- **TVL vs market cap** — Kamino's [[total-value-locked|TVL]] sits in a broad ~$2.2B-$4.1B range (early-2026 writeups cited >$3.2B), yet KMNO's market cap is only ~$91.5M. A low market-cap-to-TVL ratio is the bull case; the bear case is that governance tokens may never capture protocol value, and the persistent gap reflects that skepticism plus heavy emissions.
- **Revenue capture** — durable value depends on whether KMNO accrues a real share of lending spreads, vault management fees, and liquidation penalties (vs. pure governance with no cash flow).
- **Emissions drag** — multi-season airdrop/incentive emissions and ~51% locked supply mean fundamental growth must outrun dilution for price to re-rate.
- **Solana-DeFi beta** — KMNO is a high-beta proxy on Solana DeFi activity; it tends to lead on SOL ecosystem risk-on and lag on risk-off.

This is a qualitative framing, not a price target.

## Trading Profile

### Venues & liquidity

KMNO is tradable on [[binance|Binance]] — both **spot** (KMNO/USDT) and a **USD-margined perpetual**, which exposes [[funding-rate|funding]], [[open-interest|open interest]], and [[liquidations]] data. It is **NOT listed on [[hyperliquid|Hyperliquid]]**. Binance is the primary leveraged venue, so directional leverage, funding-based carry, and liquidation-driven setups all route through a single perp order book. This venue concentration means perp depth and funding signals are Binance-centric; with a sub-$100M cap and thin daily turnover, leveraged size must be scaled down to avoid slippage and self-inflicted liquidation cascades. Size positions conservatively and treat the Binance perp as the reference for OI/funding reads.

### Applicable strategies

- [[funding-rate-harvest]] — collect KMNO perp funding when the Binance rate is persistently positive/negative, sized small given thin depth.
- [[crowded-long-funding-fade]] — fade over-leveraged longs when funding spikes positive during Solana-DeFi risk-on bursts.
- [[cash-and-carry]] — long spot KMNO / short the Binance perp to capture basis when funding pays, hedging directional risk on a high-dilution token.
- [[liquidation-cascade-fade]] — fade forced-liquidation flushes on the single Binance perp, where cascades in a thin book overshoot fair value.
- [[oi-confirmed-trend]] — require rising open interest to confirm KMNO breakouts, filtering low-conviction moves in a low-cap name.
- [[breakout-and-retest]] — trade breakouts from KMNO's compressed near-ATL range, entering on the retest to manage the high slippage risk.

### Volatility & regime character

KMNO is a **small-cap Solana [[defi|DeFi]] governance/infra token** with **high beta to Solana ecosystem sentiment** — it leads on SOL risk-on and lags on risk-off, amplified by its low market cap. It is not a memecoin but exhibits reflexive drawdowns typical of DeFi governance tokens (~-92% from ATH, trading near all-time lows). Correlation to BTC/ETH is real but secondary to its SOL-DeFi beta; expect sharp moves on modest volume and elevated realized volatility around emission/reward-season events.

### Risk flags

- **Venue/liquidity concentration:** Binance is effectively the only leveraged venue; a sub-$100M cap with thin daily turnover means large orders slip and perp liquidations overshoot.
- **Dilution/emissions:** ~51% of max supply is still locked, with multi-season KMNO reward emissions adding persistent sell-side pressure — a structural headwind for leveraged longs.
- **Narrative dependence:** Price is tied to Solana-DeFi TVL and sentiment; the usage-vs-token-valuation gap can persist regardless of protocol growth.
- **Smart-contract/oracle risk:** Protocol-level exploit or oracle failure would hit the token directly, independent of market structure.
