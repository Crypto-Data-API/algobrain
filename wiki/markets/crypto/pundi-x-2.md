# Pundi X

**Pundi X** (PUNDIX) is a crypto [[payments]] project best known for building **XPOS**, a [[point-of-sale]] device that lets merchants accept cryptocurrency the way they accept card payments, alongside the **XPASS** card and **XWallet** app. The project's stated mission is to make buying crypto "as easy as buying bottled water," pushing crypto into everyday retail. PUNDIX is the project's current token, governing and powering its ecosystem; it also underpins **[[function-x|Function X]]**, Pundi X's purpose-built blockchain. ## Overview

Pundi X was founded around 2017 with a focus on real-world crypto acceptance. Its flagship hardware, the **XPOS**, is a [[point-of-sale]] terminal that allows brick-and-mortar merchants to accept and process cryptocurrency payments, settle in stablecoins or local currency, and manage loyalty programs. The **XPASS** card and **XWallet** mobile app extend the same payment rails to consumers.

The project later developed **[[function-x|Function X]]** (FX), its own Layer-1 blockchain designed for decentralized applications and, ambitiously, decentralized communications hardware. PUNDIX serves as the ecosystem/utility and [[governance-token]] sitting on top of this stack.

### Architecture — How the payments stack works

Pundi X's thesis is to own the **physical point of acceptance**, not just a wallet app. The stack has three consumer/merchant surfaces plus an underlying chain:

**1. XPOS (the hardware).** XPOS is a dedicated [[point-of-sale]] terminal — Android-based hardware, similar in form to a card terminal — that a merchant places on the counter. It lets the merchant accept crypto (and stablecoins) the way they accept cards: the customer taps/scans, the terminal quotes a price, and the merchant can choose to **settle in a stablecoin or local fiat** to avoid holding volatile assets. It also handles receipts, loyalty/rewards, and basic merchant accounting. Owning the terminal is the differentiator versus QR-only or wallet-only competitors, because it slots into the existing checkout workflow.

**2. XPASS (the card) and XWallet (the app).** **XPASS** is an NFC payment card backed by the user's crypto balance — a consumer-side companion to XPOS. **XWallet** is the mobile app that manages balances, on/off-ramps, and dApp access. Together they extend the same rails to the consumer side of the counter.

**3. [[function-x|Function X]] (the chain).** Function X (FX) is Pundi X's own Layer-1, built (using a Cosmos/Tendermint-style stack) to host decentralized applications and the project's broader "decentralized internet hardware" ambitions. It moved Pundi X from "payments app" toward "L1 infrastructure," which broadened the story but also widened the set of much larger competitors.

**4. Settlement / volatility handling.** The practical adoption hurdle for any crypto-payments rail is volatility; Pundi X addresses this by routing acceptance toward stablecoins and offering fiat settlement so merchants are not forced to carry price risk.

### The NPXS → PUNDIX redenomination

Pundi X originally launched the **NPXS** token in a 2018 ICO. In 2021 the project carried out a **token swap and redenomination**: holders converted NPXS to the new **PUNDIX** token at a ratio that compressed the very large NPXS supply (in the tens of billions) into a far smaller PUNDIX supply (hundreds of millions). The swap was a "redenomination" — it changed the unit and per-token price optics, not the underlying economic stake — and PUNDIX became the canonical, actively traded token thereafter. Older references to NPXS describe the same project under its pre-swap ticker.

## Comparison vs Competitors

Pundi X competes across two fronts at once — crypto **payments acceptance** and, via Function X, general **L1 infrastructure**.

| Project | Approach to crypto payments | Hardware? | Token | Differentiator |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Pundi X** (PUNDIX) | Merchant POS + card + wallet, fiat/stablecoin settlement | Yes (XPOS terminal, XPASS card) | PUNDIX | Owns the physical checkout layer; own L1 (Function X) |
| **[[bitcoin\|Bitcoin]] Lightning** (Strike/CashApp rails) | Instant low-fee BTC payments | No (app/QR) | — | Network effect of BTC + Lightning liquidity |
| **Stablecoin checkout** (USDC/USDT + processors) | Stablecoin acceptance via processors/wallets | No | — | Price-stable, fintech-grade integrations |
| **Binance Pay / exchange checkout** | Wallet-to-merchant QR payments | No | — | Huge exchange user base, zero hardware cost |

Pundi X's differentiator is **owning the physical [[point-of-sale]] layer (XPOS)** rather than relying purely on QR codes or wallets. The constraints: it faces stablecoin-native rails, exchange/fintech checkout integrations, and the general headwind that most merchants and consumers still prefer fiat. The Function X pivot reframed Pundi X partly as L1 infrastructure, pitting it against the far larger field of general-purpose blockchains.

## Risks

- **Adoption risk** — Real-world merchant crypto acceptance remains niche; XPOS deployment numbers do not translate cleanly into sustained transaction volume. This is the core fundamental risk.
- **Strategic dilution** — Spanning payments hardware, wallets, and a Layer-1 chain (Function X) spreads a small team across very different battles.
- **Long downtrend** — Down ~98% from its 2021 ATH with no sustained recovery.
- **Redenomination confusion** — The NPXS→PUNDIX swap can mislead newcomers comparing historical prices/supply across the two tickers.
- **Unlimited max supply** — PUNDIX has no hard cap; while it currently shows MC/FDV ~1.00 (fully circulating), there is no protocol-level scarcity guarantee.
- **Microcap liquidity** — At ~$25M market cap, PUNDIX is a small-cap altcoin prone to sharp volatility and [[slippage]], amplified in the current risk-off tape.

This is not investment advice; figures above are point-in-time market data, not a valuation.
