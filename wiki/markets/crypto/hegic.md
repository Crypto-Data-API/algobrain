# Hegic

**Hegic** (HEGIC) is an on-chain [[options]] protocol on [[ethereum|Ethereum]] and [[arbitrum|Arbitrum]] that lets users buy American-style call and put options directly from a smart-contract [[liquidity-pool]] rather than from an order-book counterparty. It pioneered the **peer-to-pool** model for [[decentralized]] options, in which liquidity providers collectively underwrite every option and earn the premiums. As of 2026-06-22 HEGIC trades at **$0.01289627**, ranked **#997** by market capitalization with a market cap of roughly **$13.88M** (24h **-0.29%**, 7d **+1.35%**) — a microcap that has fallen more than 97% from its 2021 all-time high amid the broad risk-off mood (Fear & Greed Index 21 / Extreme Fear; long-horizon regime = Established Bear Market; BTC ~$64,211).

## What Hegic Does

Hegic is one of the earliest DeFi [[derivatives]] protocols, launched in 2020 by the pseudonymous developer "Molly Wintermute." Its core innovation is the **peer-to-pool (liquidity-pool) options model**: instead of matching individual buyers and sellers in an order book, all option buyers transact against a shared pool of capital supplied by [[liquidity-pool|liquidity providers]] (LPs). When a trader buys a call or put, the pool acts as the writer and collects the premium; when an option expires in-the-money, the payout is settled from pooled collateral.

This design removes the need for active market makers and lets retail users gain options exposure permissionlessly. The trade-off is that LPs bear the aggregate short-options ([[volatility|short-vol]]) risk of the entire pool, which can produce sustained losses in trending or high-volatility markets — a structural weakness of pooled-underwriting designs and the reason Hegic later layered in capped, pre-built option "strategies."

---

## Architecture — How It Works

- **Peer-to-pool underwriting.** A pool (or per-asset pools, e.g. ETH, BTC) collateralizes all written options. Premiums flow to the pool; payouts are drawn from it. LPs do not pick individual trades — they are passively short the basket of options the pool sells.
- **Pricing.** Early versions priced premiums from an implied-volatility input (initially sourced off-chain) plus a settlement-fee model. Later "Hegic v8888 / Hardcore" iterations moved toward profit-and-loss-capped strategies and pre-built option "strategies" that bundle strikes, expiries, and a maximum payout, so the pool's tail risk is bounded.
- **American-style exercise.** Holders could historically exercise any time before expiry, settled on-chain — distinct from European-style settlement on most centralized options venues.
- **Cross-chain deployment.** Contracts live on [[ethereum|Ethereum]], [[arbitrum|Arbitrum]] One, Fantom, and Energi (see contract addresses below). Arbitrum is used to cut gas costs for active traders, since on-chain options involve frequent state changes.
- **Settlement-fee model.** Protocol settlement fees generated by options activity historically accrued to staked HEGIC ("staking lots"), tying token value to options throughput rather than directional price.

## The HEGIC Token & Value Accrual

HEGIC is the protocol's native governance and incentive token (max / total / circulating supply all ≈ 1.08B, giving a market-cap-to-FDV ratio of 1.00 — i.e. fully diluted, no emission overhang). Its roles:

- **Stake for fee share.** Staked HEGIC ("staking lots") entitled holders to a share of protocol settlement fees from options activity — the primary value-accrual channel.
- **Govern.** Signal and vote on protocol parameters and treasury decisions.
- **Bootstrap liquidity.** Early liquidity-mining incentives distributed HEGIC to LPs and traders.

With circulating supply equal to total supply, there is no future dilution, but the microcap valuation and thin liquidity (24h volume historically in the low thousands of dollars) mean price is highly sensitive to small flows.

## Competitive Position

Hegic competes in the on-chain options niche against order-book and AMM-style venues, as well as off-chain crypto-options giants. Its peer-to-pool simplicity was influential but has lost ground: order-book and concentrated-liquidity designs generally offer tighter pricing and better LP risk management, and centralized venues dominate options volume. Hegic's microcap rank (#997) reflects its diminished share of a market where liquidity and capital efficiency are decisive.

| Protocol | Model | Chain(s) | Edge vs Hegic | Trade-off |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Hegic** | Peer-to-pool, pre-built strategies | Ethereum, Arbitrum | Simplicity, permissionless, fully diluted token | LP short-vol risk; wide pricing; thin liquidity |
| **Lyra / Derive** | AMM with delta/vega hedging | Optimism, Arbitrum | Hedged LP risk, market-driven IV | More complex; smart-contract surface |
| **Premia** | Hybrid pool + RFQ order book | Ethereum, Arbitrum | Customizable strikes/expiries, RFQ pricing | Liquidity fragmentation |
| **Aevo (ex-Ribbon)** | Off-chain order book, on-chain settlement | Aevo L2 | CEX-like UX, deep order book | More centralized matching |
| **Deribit** (CEX) | Central limit order book | Off-chain | Dominant options liquidity/OI | Custodial, KYC, not permissionless |

See [[options]] and [[derivatives]] for the broader category, and [[volatility]] for the IV mechanics that drive options pricing.

## How & Where It Trades

HEGIC the token is a thin-liquidity microcap traded primarily on [[ethereum|Ethereum]] DEXs (Uniswap V2/V3, Sushiswap). There is no meaningful centralized-exchange depth at this rank, so spreads are wide and price impact from even modest orders is large. The protocol's *product* (options) is used on Ethereum and [[arbitrum|Arbitrum]]; the token's value is tied to options settlement fees, not to spot trading of HEGIC itself.

## Risks

- **Microcap / liquidity risk** — rank #997, ~$13.9M cap, and historically very thin volume; large slippage and gap risk.
- **LP short-vol risk** — the pooled-underwriting model can impose sustained losses on liquidity providers in trending or high-IV regimes (see [[volatility]]).
- **Smart-contract risk** — options settlement, collateral, and pricing logic are complex attack surfaces.
- **Pricing/oracle risk** — reliance on volatility and price inputs introduces oracle and mispricing exposure.
- **Pseudonymous team / governance concentration** — founder pseudonymity and concentrated token holdings raise key-person and governance risks.
- **Competitive decline** — losing share to more capital-efficient venues.

*Nothing here is investment advice. Microcap [[derivatives]] tokens are exceptionally volatile and can go to zero.*
