# Akash Network

**Akash Network** (AKT) is the native token of a **decentralized cloud-computing marketplace** ("Supercloud") in the [[depin|DePIN]] / [[artificial-intelligence|AI]] compute sector, built on the [[cosmos|Cosmos]] SDK. It operates a reverse-auction marketplace where providers with idle CPU/GPU hardware compete to host tenant workloads, typically at 60-80% below centralized clouds (AWS/GCP/Azure). Its GPU marketplace (NVIDIA H100/A100) for AI training and inference is the primary growth driver.

## Overview

What is Akash Network?

Akash Network is spearheading a paradigm shift in cloud computing, disrupting conventional cloud services, and pioneering a revolution in access to essential cloud resources. Leveraging the power of blockchain technology, Akash Network has developed an open-source, decentralized, marketplace for cloud computing, offering an unprecedented level of speed, efficiency, and affordability. This innovation is set to transform the way users perceive and utilize cloud services.
What are the key features of Akash Network?

Decentralized Cloud Computing: Akash Network, built on a blockchain-based framework, eliminates dependence on centralized cloud providers, offering superior security, transparency for users' data and transactions, and enhanced scalability.

Permissionless Marketplace: By offering an open marketplace, Akash Network allows anyone with computational resources to become a cloud provider. Users can lease out their unused computing capacities, fostering competition and driving down prices.

Flexible and Secure: With Akash, developers can effortlessly deploy applications and workloads. Moreover, the platform offers high security by using the native AKT token to ensure the integrity and authenticity of transactions on the network.

Staking and Incentive Mechanism: Holders of the AKT token can participate in the network by staking their tokens. This not only helps secure the network but also earns them rewards.

Interoperable Ecosystem: Akash Network is designed to be blockchain agnostic and is built on the Cosmos SDK, allowing for easy integration with other blockchain networks and fostering cross-chain collaborations.

Eco-friendly: Compared to traditional cloud services, Akash Network is more energy-efficient. The network's consensus mechanism is based on Proof-of-Stake, which is considered to be more environmentally friendly than Proof-of-Work used by many other blockchain networks.

## Decentralized Cloud Compute Model

Akash operates a **reverse auction marketplace** for cloud computing resources. Unlike traditional cloud providers (AWS, Google Cloud, Azure) that set fixed prices, Akash lets compute providers compete on price:

1. **Tenants** (users needing compute) submit a deployment specification describing their needs (CPU, GPU, RAM, storage)
2. **Providers** (operators with idle hardware) bid on the deployment in a reverse auction
3. **Lowest qualifying bid wins** the deployment
4. **Payment flows in AKT** or stablecoins, settled on-chain

This model typically offers **60-80% cost savings** compared to centralized cloud providers, though with trade-offs in reliability and enterprise support.

### GPU Marketplace for AI/ML

Akash's GPU marketplace is the primary growth driver. As [[artificial-intelligence|AI]] model training and inference demand explodes, GPU supply remains constrained (NVIDIA H100s have multi-month wait times). Akash aggregates idle GPU capacity from data centers, crypto miners, and enterprises, making it available at market-clearing prices.

## AKT Token: Compute Payment and Staking

### Token Utility

AKT serves multiple functions in the Akash ecosystem:

- **Payment for compute:** Tenants pay for deployments in AKT (or USDC, with AKT used for settlement)
- **Staking:** AKT holders stake to secure the network (Cosmos-based proof-of-stake), earning staking rewards (~15-20% APY)
- **Governance:** Staked AKT votes on protocol parameters, fee structures, and upgrade proposals
- **Provider incentives:** Providers earn AKT for offering compute resources

### What Drives AKT Price

1. **GPU demand from AI boom** -- the core thesis: as AI training/inference demand grows, demand for decentralized GPU compute grows, driving AKT usage
2. **Network utilization metrics** -- active deployments, total GPU hours, and revenue are the fundamental KPIs
3. **AI narrative correlation** -- AKT trades as part of the "AI x crypto" basket alongside [[render-token|RNDR]], [[aethir|ATH]], and [[aioz-network|AIOZ]]
4. **Staking yield** -- ~15-20% staking APY provides a yield floor that supports price
5. **Cosmos ecosystem** -- built on Cosmos SDK with IBC interoperability; benefits from Cosmos ecosystem momentum

## AKT Trading Characteristics (Extended)

- **AI narrative beta:** AKT moves sharply on AI-related news (NVIDIA earnings, OpenAI announcements, AI regulation)
- **Cosmos ecosystem correlation:** Also moves with ATOM, OSMO, and other Cosmos tokens during IBC/Cosmos-specific catalysts
- **Staking lockup:** ~21-day unbonding period for staked AKT; this reduces circulating supply but creates selling delays during downturns
- **High FDV efficiency:** ~99% of total supply already circulating (MC/FDV ~0.75 vs the 388.5M max) -- low dilution risk relative to DePIN peers
- **Drawdown:** ~ -90.6% from ATH of $8.07 -- but ATH was during the 2021 bull market, before the AI narrative even existed

### Risk Factors

- **Centralized cloud competition:** AWS, Google, and Azure could match Akash's pricing, especially as GPU supply normalizes
- **Reliability concerns:** Decentralized compute is inherently less reliable than enterprise cloud; uptime SLAs are weaker
- **Regulatory risk:** Decentralized compute could face regulation around KYC/AML (who is running what on the network?)
- **AI hype cycle:** If the AI bubble deflates, the premium on GPU compute tokens deflates with it
- **Cosmos ecosystem risk:** Cosmos has struggled with governance issues and chain coordination
- **Migration execution risk:** the publicly discussed move off the Cosmos chain (Solana cited) could fragment the community and introduces technical execution risk

## Valuation Framing (qualitative)

AKT is a **revenue-linked infrastructure token**, so it should be read against network usage, not just AI beta:

- **Usage vs price gap** — the Q1 2026 tension (lease count +27% but lease revenue **-45%**, GPU utilization ~33.7%) is the central bear case: more activity at lower prices signals oversupply, not demand-driven pricing power. Watch revenue and utilization, not lease count.
- **BME deflation thesis** — post-BME, every dollar of on-chain compute spend triggers an AKT buy-and-burn. If compute spend scales, AKT becomes structurally deflationary; if it stalls, emissions still dilute. BME makes AKT a **leveraged bet on real compute revenue growth**.
- **Sector relative value** — compare revenue / market-cap across the AI-compute basket ([[render-token|RNDR]], [[aethir|ATH]], [[aioz-network|AIOZ]]) to gauge whether AKT is cheap or rich relative to actual usage.
- **Staking floor** — ~15-20% staking APY plus a ~21-day unbonding period locks supply and provides a partial yield floor.

This is a qualitative framing, not a price target.

## Major News & Events

- **2023-08:** Akash tested its first **AI Supercloud**, marking its pivot toward GPU compute for AI workloads. (Verified via WebSearch, 2026-06-11)
- **2026-03-23:** **Burn-Mint Equilibrium (BME) went live.** Under BME, all on-chain compute spending triggers a market buy-and-burn of AKT and mints a stable settlement credit (ACT) used for paying providers. - **2026 Q2:** **Virtual Machines (VM) launch** — full VM support added for greater developer flexibility and enterprise adoption (previously container/Kubernetes-focused). (Verified via WebSearch, 2026-06-11)
- **Mid-2026:** "Akash at Home" initiative announced, enabling private AI workloads on home computing resources.
- **2026 (ongoing):** The team is publicly **evaluating a migration off its Cosmos-based chain**, with Solana cited as a possible destination. This could improve scalability but introduces execution and community-fragmentation risk — a notable change from the page's earlier "own Cosmos chain" framing. (Verified via WebSearch, 2026-06-11)
