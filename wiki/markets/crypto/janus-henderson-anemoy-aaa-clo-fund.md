# Janus Henderson Anemoy AAA CLO Fund

**Janus Henderson Anemoy AAA CLO Fund** (ticker **JAAA**) is a tokenized [[real-world-assets|real-world-asset]] fund giving on-chain investors exposure to **AAA-rated [[clo|collateralized loan obligations]] (CLOs)**, managed by **Janus Henderson** — the same portfolio team behind its ~$21B AAA CLO ETF (NYSE: JAAA) — and issued on-chain by **Anemoy** via [[centrifuge-2|Centrifuge]]. Launched in mid-2025 with a **$1 billion seed from the [[sky|Sky Ecosystem]] (via Grove)**, it was the first AAA-rated CLO strategy brought fully on-chain and the fastest tokenized fund to reach $1B AUM. For traders it is not a directional asset — it is a NAV-accruing yield token (~$1.04) that functions as institutional-grade collateral in DeFi and as a barometer of the [[real-world-assets|RWA]] tokenization trend.

## Overview

Anemoy is a Web3-native asset manager powered by Centrifuge, offering investors access to tokenized real-world-asset funds. Its product suite includes:

1. [[janus-henderson-anemoy-treasury-fund|Janus Henderson Anemoy Treasury Fund]] (JTRSY) — tokenized T-bills, Janus Henderson's first tokenized fund (partnership announced 2024)
2. **Janus Henderson Anemoy AAA CLO Fund (JAAA)** — this page
3. Anemoy Tokenized Apollo Diversified Credit Fund
4. Janus Henderson Anemoy S&P 500® Fund

JAAA brings the AAA tranche of the CLO market — historically zero defaults for AAA CLO tranches, floating-rate yield above T-bills — onto public blockchains with programmable settlement. It is managed by the same portfolio managers as Janus Henderson's ~$21B exchange-traded AAA CLO fund and seeks capital preservation with attractive floating-rate yield. (Sources: Centrifuge blog; Janus Henderson press release.)

## Market Structure

- **Not a tradeable token.** $0 secondary volume, near-zero price volatility; you **subscribe and redeem at NAV** through permissioned channels, you do not trade JAAA on exchanges.
- **Who can hold it**: KYC'd, eligible (largely institutional) investors via Anemoy/Centrifuge subscription; the token then circulates within whitelisted DeFi venues (e.g. Aave Horizon) as collateral.
- **Liquidity caveat**: "liquidity" is the redemption mechanism plus DeFi collateral integrations, not an order book. In a credit shock, NAV marks can lag and redemptions can gate.

## Trading Relevance

- **Not a directional trade.** JAAA has essentially no price volatility; you do not trade it on exchanges. Its relevance is as **yield-bearing collateral** (Aave Horizon loops, stablecoin backing) and as an allocation alternative to T-bill tokens (JTRSY, BUIDL, OUSG) when the floating-rate credit spread over T-bills is attractive.
- **RWA narrative signal.** JAAA's $1B seed from Sky and its Aave/Ethena integrations are the strongest 2025-2026 evidence that the [[real-world-assets]] thesis has moved from pilots to scale. Growth in JAAA supply is a leading indicator for the RWA basket (CFG, ONDO, etc.) — the tradeable expression is usually [[centrifuge-2|Centrifuge]]'s CFG token (which surged 116% on an Upbit listing in February 2026), not JAAA itself.
- **Credit-cycle tail risk**: AAA CLO tranches have never defaulted, but in a sharp credit event their NAV marks lag and DeFi loops built on JAAA (leveraged collateral on Aave Horizon) could force unwinds — a new, untested transmission channel from corporate credit into DeFi.
- **Stablecoin plumbing**: watch Ethena/USDe and Sky allocations to JAAA as a proxy for how much stablecoin float is reaching into credit risk for yield ([[stablecoin-yields]]).

---

## Risks

- **Credit-cycle tail risk** — AAA CLO tranches have never defaulted, but in a sharp corporate-credit event their NAV marks lag, and **DeFi loops built on JAAA** (leveraged collateral on Aave Horizon) could force unwinds — a new, untested transmission channel from corporate credit into DeFi.
- **Rate sensitivity** — floating-rate yield falls as short rates fall; the spread-over-T-bills attractiveness vs [[tokenized-treasuries]] varies with the cycle.
- **Redemption / liquidity gating** — there is no secondary order book; in stress, exits depend on the redemption mechanism, which can gate.
- **Permissioning / regulatory** — eligibility, transfer whitelists, and the regulatory status of a tokenized fund interest constrain who can hold and move it.
- **Concentration of demand** — much of the float is institutional allocators (Sky, Ethena evaluation); a large allocator pulling back would shrink visible supply quickly.
