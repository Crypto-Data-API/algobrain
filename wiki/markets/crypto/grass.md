# Grass

**Grass** (ticker **GRASS**) is a decentralized bandwidth marketplace on [[solana|Solana]] that rewards users for sharing their idle residential internet connection, which is then resold to AI companies as a web-scraping and training-data source. It sits at the data layer of [[decentralized-ai|decentralized AI]] and within the [[depin|DePIN]] (Decentralized Physical Infrastructure) category — an unusual position because the scarce resource being tokenized is neither GPU compute nor storage but **residential IP-address reputation**, which large centralized scrapers cannot easily replicate.

## Technology & Protocol

Large AI companies rely on web-scale scraped data to train models, and the supply of clean, un-rate-limited residential IPs is a real and growing bottleneck. Grass (by Wynd Network) monetizes this via a [[depin|DePIN]] architecture:

- Users run a **browser extension or mobile app** that contributes their idle bandwidth to fulfill scraping jobs.
- Requests route through the user's residential IP, which centralized scrapers cannot easily fake at scale, and the user earns **GRASS** for uptime and successful requests.
- Grass packages the scraped, structured data and resells it to AI labs as training/inference data, optionally with provenance/attestation (a "Grass dataset" layer).

This is a [[tokenized-compute|tokenized-compute]] model applied to **bandwidth and IP reputation** rather than GPUs, overlapping with the general DePIN thesis that also covers Helium, Hivemapper, and similar projects. The scarce, tokenized resource is **residential IP-address reputation** — Grass's core differentiator.

## Market Structure & Derivatives

**Centralized spot venues:** Kraken (GRASS/EUR), Bitget, KuCoin, and Crypto.com Exchange; GRASS first reached wide distribution through Binance Alpha / Spotlight programs.

**On-chain spot:** Orca on [[solana|Solana]] (GRASS/SOL) is a primary [[dex|DEX]] market.

**Derivatives:** GRASS has a perpetual market on [[hyperliquid|Hyperliquid]] (GRASS-PERP) plus CEX perps. As a momentum-driven AI-narrative token, **funding tends to flip strongly positive during rallies** (longs paying to chase), and open interest can build quickly on AI-sector catalysts — a setup prone to sharp long-squeeze reversals. This makes GRASS a higher-beta, perp-active name than most peers in this loop cohort.

## Valuation Framing

GRASS price tracks the [[ai-narrative-arc|AI narrative cycle]] more than realized protocol revenue today, so macro AI-hype beta dominates short-horizon returns. The fundamental bull case requires AI labs to keep paying for residential-scraped/structured data at scale and for Grass to convert that into recurring token demand faster than emissions dilute holders. With ~61% supply out and continuing rewards, it screens as a **high-beta picks-and-shovels bet on AI data scarcity** — qualitative only, not a price target.

## Use Case / Narrative / Category

Grass belongs to the **DePIN / decentralized-AI data** category. The product (Grass nodes) turns consumer bandwidth into a sellable resource for AI web-scraping; the GRASS token rewards providers and is the speculative vehicle for the thesis. Its differentiator is the focus on residential-IP reputation as the scarce input, positioning it as data-layer infrastructure rather than compute. It is frequently grouped with the AI-agent and tokenized-compute narratives even though its core business is bandwidth/data.

## Risks

- **Narrative-beta dependence** — price is driven by AI hype cycles more than by realized revenue.
- **Legal / regulatory** — residential scraping and proxy resale sit in a contested legal area; a crackdown would hit the core model.
- **Competitive substitution** — synthetic data, proprietary datasets, or cheaper centralized proxy providers could undercut demand.
- **Unlock overhang** — ~0.61 MC/FDV with continuing emissions to node operators (FDV ~1.6x cap).
- **Bear-market fragility** — momentum names give back gains fast in extreme-fear regimes (Fear & Greed 23); current strength may not persist.
- **Perp-driven volatility** — positive funding and fast OI builds make GRASS prone to sharp long-squeeze reversals.
