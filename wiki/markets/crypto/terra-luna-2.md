# Terra (LUNA 2.0)

**Terra** (ticker **LUNA**) is the post-collapse successor chain to the original Terra blockchain — a [[cosmos|Cosmos]]-SDK / Tendermint smart-contract platform. It launched on **May 28, 2022**, via a community governance vote proposed by Do Kwon, just two weeks after the [[terra-luna-collapse]] destroyed ~$40 billion in value. The new chain was forked **without the algorithmic stablecoin** — the mechanism that caused the collapse was deliberately excluded.

This page covers the **new LUNA (Terra 2.0)** — distinct from the original chain, which was rebranded "Terra Classic" ([[terra-luna|LUNC]]), and the original UST, renamed [[terrausd|USTC]].

## Technology / Protocol (post-collapse Terra relaunch)

Terra 2.0 is a general-purpose [[cosmos|Cosmos]]-SDK smart-contract chain secured by Delegated Proof-of-Stake (Tendermint BFT consensus). Its defining design choice is what it **omits**:

| Property | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Consensus** | Delegated Proof-of-Stake (Tendermint) |
| **Stablecoin** | **None** — the algorithmic UST mechanism was deliberately removed |
| **Inflation** | ~7% fixed annual (uncapped supply) |
| **Origin** | Hard fork of original Terra at the May-2022 collapse |
| **Positioning** | DeFi-focused general smart-contract platform ("Phoenix" rebrand) |

The original Terra ran a two-token mint/burn mechanism: UST ([[terrausd|TerraUSD]]) was an [[stablecoins|algorithmic stablecoin]] kept near $1 by minting/burning LUNA. When UST lost its peg in May 2022, the mechanism hyperinflated LUNA supply into the trillions, collapsing both. Terra 2.0 keeps the chain and developer tooling but **excludes the stablecoin entirely** — there is no native algorithmic stable, so the reflexive death-spiral mechanism cannot recur.

New LUNA was created entirely by **airdrop** to holders of the original LUNA and UST at various pre- and post-collapse snapshots. Only ~60% of total supply is circulating (MC/FDV ~0.60), with the remainder under community-pool and contributor vesting — a residual dilution overhang. More structurally, the chain runs **~7% annual inflation with no max supply**, so long-run dilution (not a single cliff) is the persistent supply vector.

## Use Case / Narrative / Category

Terra 2.0's category is **post-collapse relaunch / DeFi smart-contract platform**, but its narrative is dominated by **stigma**. It has struggled to gain meaningful adoption:

- **TVL** — minimal compared to the original Terra ecosystem.
- **Developer activity** — low.
- **Market perception** — tainted by association with the collapse and Do Kwon's legal troubles.
- **Terraform Labs** — in bankruptcy proceedings; Do Kwon faced criminal charges in the US.
- **Community** — small but active via the "Phoenix" rebrand initiative.

It is effectively a micro-cap speculative asset with a limited fundamental thesis.

## Valuation Framing

LUNA cannot be valued on adoption metrics that barely exist; with negligible TVL and developer activity, the chain's intrinsic productive value is minimal. The honest framing is that LUNA trades as a **distressed, headline-driven micro-cap** — a residual call option on an ecosystem revival that has not materialized, repriced mostly by legal news and governance proposals rather than usage. The ~7% inflation and uncapped supply argue against a durable floor absent a genuine adoption catalyst.

## Risks

- **Reputational stigma (severe):** permanently associated with one of crypto's largest collapses and ongoing Do Kwon litigation.
- **No demonstrated adoption:** minimal TVL and developer activity; weak fundamental thesis.
- **Uncapped inflation:** ~7% annual emissions with no max supply mean ongoing dilution.
- **Structural downtrend:** ~99.7% below ATH, trading near all-time lows with no reversal evidence.
- **Liquidity:** thin ~$4M daily volume; high slippage and news-driven volatility.
- **Legal/entity overhang:** Terraform Labs bankruptcy and regulatory actions can resurface as price catalysts in either direction.
- **Bear-market beta:** high-beta micro-cap in an extreme-fear regime (Fear & Greed = 23).
