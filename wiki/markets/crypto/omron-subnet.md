# Omron (SN21)

**Omron** (SN21) is a Bittensor subnet focused on **verifiable machine-learning inference** -- producing zero-knowledge proofs (or comparable verifiable attestations) that a specific model was run on specific inputs to produce specific outputs. The subnet is part of the [[zkml]] layer of the Bittensor stack: connecting decentralized AI outputs to on-chain consumers who need cryptographic guarantees, not just trust.

## How It Works

Miners serve model inference with accompanying proofs. Validators verify proofs and score throughput, latency, and proof size. The core trick is keeping zkML proof generation cheap enough to be economically viable at real model sizes.

## Trading Relevance

Omron is the key "verifiable inference" subnet on Bittensor. Thesis: if on-chain DeFi applications need to consume AI inference (risk scores, forecasts, signals) they need proof that inference was actually executed against the claimed model. SN21's emission share grows with on-chain AI adoption. Revenue model is thin in April 2026 but the category is structurally important for the longer-term integration narrative.
