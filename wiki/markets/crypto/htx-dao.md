# HTX DAO

**HTX DAO** (ticker **HTX**, native to [[tron|TRON]]) is the governance token of the DAO tied to the HTX exchange (formerly Huobi), closely associated with [[tron|TRON]] founder Justin Sun, who serves as advisor to HTX. For traders it is an [[exchange-tokens|exchange-token]] burn play: 50% of HTX exchange quarterly revenue is committed to burning the token, against an enormous quadrillion-scale supply. It succeeded the legacy [[huobi-token|Huobi Token (HT)]] after the exchange's 2023 rebrand.

## Overview

HTX is the governance token of HTX DAO, a decentralized autonomous organization relying on the collective autonomy of HTX token holders. Holders vote on proposals and can delegate voting rights. The token launched in late 2023/early 2024 alongside the Huobi-to-HTX rebrand, with a deliberately huge unit supply (price quoted in millionths of a dollar).

The DAO's defining economic mechanism is the burn commitment: **50% of HTX exchange quarterly revenue goes to $HTX burns**, formally integrated into the whitepaper with regular disclosure of revenue and burn data.

## 2025–2026 Developments

- **Record burns** — Q1 2025 alone burned **11.3 trillion HTX**; subsequent quarters set records, with Decrypt reporting the largest-ever quarterly burn as governance and ecosystem value grew. The quarterly cadence continued into 2026 (Q1 2026 burn executed mid-April 2026).
- **Staking launch (March 2026)** — HTX staking entered public beta 16 March 2026 and launched officially 23 March 2026: users stake HTX on TRON and receive **sHTX** (1 sHTX = 1 governance vote) with an estimated base yield of ~5% APY.
- **Sole fee-offset token (1 April 2026)** — HTX became the only fee-offset token on the HTX exchange, with a 25% fee discount for paying in HTX (retiring legacy HT utility).
- **Holder growth** — token-holder addresses expanded from ~40,000 early on to **over 800,000** by 2026.
- **2026 initiatives** — opening on-chain product interfaces to external developers, support programs for emerging narratives (RWA), and expanded ambassador/governance programs; HTX DAO and HTX Ventures presented at Consensus Hong Kong (February 2026).

## Market Structure & Derivatives

- **Where it trades**: primarily on the **HTX exchange itself**; off-platform liquidity is thinner (KuCoin HTX/USDT, Uniswap v2 on Ethereum, TRON DEXs). Like [[okb|OKB]] and the rest of the [[exchange-tokens]] cohort, price discovery is dominated by the home exchange — exit-liquidity risk if HTX-the-exchange has problems. That said, at ~$11.9M/day the token's third-party turnover is far deeper than smaller peers like [[coinex-token|CET]] (~$51K).
- **Derivatives**: no widely-tracked, persistent perpetual/[[derivatives]] funding or open-interest series for HTX appears in the snapshot; it is treated as a predominantly spot-driven instrument with liquidity centered on its home venue.
- **Narrative basket**: the **CEX-token basket** ([[bnb|BNB]], [[okb|OKB]], BGB) and the broader **Justin Sun complex** ([[tron|TRX]], [[usdd|USDD]], [[tronbank]]-era history, WIN, JST) — these assets are correlated through Sun-driven news flow and shared market-making.
- **Catalysts**: quarterly burn announcements (mid-April, mid-July, mid-October, mid-January), staking adoption, HTX exchange volume/revenue trends.

## Valuation Framing

As an [[exchange-tokens|exchange token]], HTX is a **claim on HTX-the-exchange's revenue, intermediated by the burn**:

- **Revenue-burn linkage** — HTX is unusually explicit: 50% of quarterly exchange revenue is burned, so the token is, in effect, a deflationary instrument whose pace of supply destruction is mechanically tied to exchange fee income. Higher HTX volume → larger burns → tighter float. This makes burn disclosures the single most important fundamental data point.
- **Verification caveat** — the burn math depends on self-reported revenue; an analyst cannot independently audit HTX's fee take, so the deflation thesis carries a trust assumption absent from on-chain-native protocols.
- **Key-man premium/discount** — HTX's valuation is inseparable from Justin Sun's reputation and regulatory standing; the Sun complex tends to re-rate together on his news flow.

Qualitative framing only — see valuation.

## Risk Flags

- **Key-man & regulatory exposure** via Justin Sun (past SEC action against Sun entities); the entire Sun complex re-rates on his news.
- **Quadrillion-unit supply optics** can deter institutional participation despite the burn.
- **Self-reported revenue** underpins the burn math — the deflation thesis cannot be independently audited.
- **Exchange-counterparty risk** — value is inseparable from HTX-the-exchange's solvency, security, and reputation (see [[centralized-exchange]]).
- **Regime risk** — extreme-fear (F&G 23) bear-market tape pressures all CEX tokens, though HTX has been relatively resilient.
