# STEPN (GMT)

**STEPN** is a Web3 "move-to-earn" lifestyle app in which users buy NFT sneakers and earn tokens by walking, jogging, or running outdoors with their phone's GPS tracking the activity. **GMT** (Green Metaverse Token) is STEPN's [[governance-token|governance token]], capped at 6 billion supply, and the higher-tier of its **dual-token model** — the in-game soft currency **GST** (Green Satoshi Token) is earned from movement, while GMT is used for premium actions (minting high-quality sneakers, upgrading gems, governance) and is partially burned. STEPN was the flagship app of the 2022 [[move-to-earn]] boom and its subsequent bust. As of 2026-06-21 GMT trades at **$0.00830358**, ranking **#706** by market capitalization (~**$25.8M**).

## Overview

STEPN is built by **Find Satoshi Lab**, an Australian studio that won the Gaming track of the 2021 Solana Ignition Hackathon. The app combines fitness, [[gamefi|GameFi]], and SocialFi: users equip NFT sneakers and earn tokens by moving outdoors, with earnings constrained by an energy system tied to how many (and how good) sneakers they own.

**Dual-token model:**

- **GST (Green Satoshi Token)** — the uncapped, in-game soft currency earned from movement and spent on minting and repairs. It is the "inflationary" sink-and-faucet token.
- **GMT (Green Metaverse Token)** — the capped (6B) [[governance-token]] used for higher-level actions (minting/upgrading high-quality sneakers and gems, voting). GMT is partially **burned** by these actions, giving it a deflationary mechanic when usage is high.

Users can lease or trade NFT sneakers on the in-app marketplace, and the in-app wallet includes a built-in swap. STEPN deployed across multiple chains (Solana, BNB Chain, Polygon, Ethereum), with Solana as the original home.

## How the move-to-earn economy works (sinks and faucets)

STEPN's economy is the textbook dual-token [[move-to-earn]] machine, and understanding its sinks (token-burning demand) and faucets (token-emitting supply) is the key to understanding why it boomed and busted.

**Faucets (where tokens enter circulation / are earned):**

- **Earn by moving** — equipping an NFT sneaker and walking/jogging/running outdoors emits **GST** at a rate gated by an **Energy** system. Energy regenerates over time and scales with the number and quality of sneakers owned, capping how much a single user can earn per day. This is the primary faucet and the main inflation source.
- **GMT earning** — at higher sneaker levels and via certain modes, users can earn the capped **GMT** instead of/in addition to GST.

**Sinks (where tokens leave circulation / create demand):**

- **Minting** — combining two sneakers to produce a new sneaker NFT ("Mint") costs both **GST and GMT**. This is the dominant historical sink: it was the activity that absorbed the most emitted GST during the boom.
- **Repairs and gem upgrades** — sneakers lose durability with use and must be repaired with GST; sockets/gems are upgraded and re-rolled using GST/GMT.
- **Level-ups and enhancement** — leveling a sneaker consumes GST (and GMT at higher levels), with a time-lock that can be skipped by spending GMT.
- **GMT burns** — premium actions burn a portion of GMT directly, giving the governance token a deflationary mechanic *when activity is high*. The reflexive flaw: burns scale with usage, and usage scales with new-buyer inflows.

The fragility is structural: faucets (movement rewards) run continuously regardless of demand, while sinks (minting, repairs, upgrades) only fire when users believe future sneaker prices justify the spend. When new-buyer growth stalls, sinks collapse faster than faucets, GST inflates, and sneaker NFT floor prices fall — the classic [[emissions]]-versus-sinks death spiral that defines [[play-to-earn]] reflexivity.

## The 2022 move-to-earn boom and bust

STEPN is the defining case study of the [[move-to-earn]]/[[play-to-earn]] cycle, and its history should be read honestly:

- **Boom (early–mid 2022):** STEPN went viral. Demand for NFT sneakers exploded, GST and GMT prices soared, and GMT reached an all-time high around **$4.11 in April 2022**. Early adopters earned large dollar-denominated returns, which attracted a flood of new users buying sneakers expecting similar payouts.
- **The flywheel was reflexive:** Earnings were paid in GST/GMT whose prices depended on *new* buyers entering to mint sneakers. This is structurally similar to other "X-to-earn" tokenomics (e.g., Axie Infinity's SLP) where rewards are funded by new entrants rather than external revenue — a dynamic critics compared to a Ponzi-like or "ponzinomics" flywheel.
- **Bust (mid–late 2022):** As new-user growth slowed and the broader crypto market collapsed, GST inflation overwhelmed demand, sneaker NFT prices crashed, and the earning value per user fell sharply. A **May 2022 announcement that STEPN would block users in mainland China** accelerated the decline. GMT has since fallen roughly **99%+ from its 2022 ATH**.
- **Aftermath:** Find Satoshi Lab has continued to iterate — adjusting tokenomics, adding sinks, launching the **MOOAR** marketplace and the **DOOAR** DEX, and integrating with mainstream fitness platforms (e.g., Apple Health) to pursue more sustainable, less purely-speculative engagement. The app survives as a much smaller, post-hype product.

The honest takeaway: STEPN demonstrated genuine viral consumer appeal but also exposed the core fragility of move-to-earn economics — rewards funded by token emissions and new-buyer inflows are very hard to sustain once growth stalls.

## Competitive Position

STEPN remains the best-known [[move-to-earn]] app and one of the more recognizable [[gamefi|GameFi]] brands, with backing from prominent names (it was historically associated with Sequoia Capital and Binance Labs / YZi Labs investments, and was an early Solana ecosystem success). Its challenge is category-wide: move-to-earn and play-to-earn as a whole lost mainstream credibility after 2022, and competitors (Sweatcoin/SWEAT, Genopets, StepApp and others) face the same sustainability problem. STEPN's relative advantage is a real, shipping app with iteration on tokenomics and mainstream-fitness integrations; its disadvantage is that the original "earn real money by walking" promise is no longer believable at scale.

## Narrative, category & catalysts

GMT trades as a **high-beta proxy for [[gamefi|GameFi]] and "consumer crypto" sentiment** rather than on fundamentals. In the current **Established Bear Market** regime (Fear & Greed 21 / Extreme Fear, BTC ~16% below its 200-day MA as of 2026-06-22), the entire GameFi basket — including the gaming/metaverse cohort (AXS, SAND, GALA, IMX, RON) — has been weak and sentiment-driven, and GMT moves with that tape.

Potential catalysts (positive): renewed move-to-earn or "consumer dApp" narrative; a credible new STEPN product cycle or mainstream-fitness partnership (Apple Health-style integrations, employer/insurer wellness tie-ins); a broad altcoin risk-on rotation lifting [[solana|Solana]]-ecosystem and GameFi names together. Catalysts (negative): further deterioration in the GameFi narrative, additional geographic/regulatory restrictions, or renewed GST inflation if sinks weaken.

## Trading playbook (bear-tape context)

> Not investment advice — sentiment/structure notes only.

- **Regime first.** GMT is a sentiment-driven [[gamefi|GameFi]] micro-cap; in an Extreme-Fear, established-bear regime it tends to bleed and chop, with sharp, low-quality bounces. It is a beta-on-beta instrument (altcoin beta × GameFi-theme beta).
- **It needs a narrative, not just a market.** Micro-cap GameFi tokens typically need a *category* catalyst (a GameFi/consumer-crypto rotation) plus a *coin-specific* catalyst (product news) to sustain a move. Absent both, mean-reversion bounces fade.
- **Liquidity / slippage.** At ~$25M cap and a few million in daily volume, depth is thin; size positions for [[slippage]] and avoid market orders into illiquid books. Perp markets ([[hyperliquid|Hyperliquid]] GMT-PERP, CEX perps) add leverage-driven volatility and funding cost.
- **Asymmetry awareness.** Down ~99%+ from ATH, the token can still halve again; "it's already down 99%" is not a floor. Treat as a high-variance, small-allocation speculative position, not a value play.

## Trading Profile

**Venues & liquidity.** GMT is unusually well-supported for a sub-$30M micro-cap: it trades on **Binance** (GMT/USDT spot plus a USD-margined perpetual) and on **[[hyperliquid|Hyperliquid]]** (GMT-PERP, up to ~40-50x leverage), giving it a genuinely two-venue derivatives market rather than a single thin book. That dual availability tightens spreads, deepens aggregate order-book depth, and lets traders route and split size across CEX and on-chain venues, run cross-venue [[funding-rate]] plays, and manage inventory more precisely than most GameFi micro-caps allow. The high available leverage means most directional flow and price discovery happens in perps; spot depth is comparatively shallow, so sizing should still respect [[slippage]] on the spot leg and avoid market orders into the thin on-chain books.

**Applicable strategies.**
- [[funding-rate-harvest]] — a liquid two-venue GMT perp market with recurring funding lets a delta-neutral book collect funding while hedging spot exposure.
- [[hl-vs-cex-funding-divergence]] — Binance and Hyperliquid GMT-PERP funding can dislocate, and both venues being deep enough to trade makes the spread capturable.
- [[cash-and-carry]] — Binance spot plus the USD-margined perp supports a long-spot/short-perp carry when the perp trades at a persistent premium.
- [[crowded-long-funding-fade]] — as a reflexive GameFi micro-cap, GMT is prone to sentiment-driven long crowding that shows up as stretched positive funding, offering a fade signal.
- [[liquidation-cascade-fade]] — high perp leverage and thin spot depth make GMT prone to sharp liquidation flushes that overshoot and mean-revert.
- [[narrative-trading]] — GMT trades on move-to-earn / consumer-crypto narrative flow (product cycles, GameFi rotations) far more than on fundamentals.

**Volatility & regime character.** GMT is a **high-beta GameFi / consumer-crypto micro-cap** with pronounced [[play-to-earn]] reflexivity in its underlying tokenomics. It carries beta-on-beta behavior — broad altcoin beta multiplied by GameFi-theme beta — and is highly correlated to BTC/ETH risk direction while amplifying it: it bleeds and chops in risk-off/Extreme-Fear tapes and produces sharp, often low-quality bounces on any risk-on or narrative impulse. It tracks the wider GameFi/metaverse cohort (AXS, SAND, GALA, IMX, RON) and the [[solana|Solana]] ecosystem more than idiosyncratic fundamentals.

**Risk flags.**
- **Liquidity / venue concentration** — despite two venues, aggregate depth is thin at a ~$25M cap; large orders move price and perp liquidity can evaporate in stress.
- **Emissions / supply** — uncapped GST inflation and ongoing GMT unlocks toward the 6B max supply (circulating ~3.11B of ~5.07B total) are a structural overhang; MC/FDV ~0.61 signals meaningful dilution ahead.
- **Narrative dependence** — needs both a category catalyst (GameFi/consumer-crypto rotation) and coin-specific product news to sustain moves; absent both, bounces fade.
- **Perp funding dislocations** — leverage-driven crowding can push funding to extremes and trigger liquidation cascades; funding cost erodes leveraged holds.
- **Regulatory / geographic** — the 2022 mainland-China block showed how policy shifts can rapidly shrink the user base and demand.
