# GoMining Token

**GoMining Token** (GOMINING) is the utility token of **GoMining**, a platform that tokenizes real-world [[bitcoin|Bitcoin]] [[bitcoin-mining|mining]] infrastructure. Holders buy "Digital Miners" — NFT-like assets backed by hashrate hosted in GoMining's data centres that pay out daily BTC rewards — and use the GOMINING token across the ecosystem (fees, hashrate/efficiency upgrades, the Miner Wars [[gamefi|GameFi]] arena, the GoMining wallet, and BTC finance products). The token is **multi-chain**, deployed natively on [[ethereum|Ethereum]], BNB Chain ([[binance|BNB]]), Solana, and TON. As a claim on hosted, real-world hashrate, GOMINING sits at the intersection of the **[[real-world-assets|real-world-asset (RWA)]]** and Bitcoin-mining narratives.

> **Disambiguation:** This "GMT" is **GoMining Token (GOMINING)** and is entirely distinct from [[stepn|STEPN's]] **GMT (Green Metaverse Token)** — they are unrelated projects that happen to share the "GMT" ticker abbreviation. This page covers GoMining only.

## How GoMining Works

GoMining's central product is **tokenized Bitcoin mining hashrate**. Rather than buying, shipping, and operating physical ASICs, a user buys a **Digital Miner** — an NFT that represents a defined amount of **terahash (TH/s)** of [[bitcoin-mining|hashing power]] at a stated **energy-efficiency rating (W/TH)** running in GoMining's hosted data centres.

| Component | What it is | Role of GOMINING |
|---|---|---|
| **Digital Miner NFTs** | NFT representing X TH/s of hosted hashrate at a given efficiency | Bought/upgraded; hashrate & efficiency can be increased with GOMINING |
| **Daily BTC rewards** | Mining proceeds, net of electricity & maintenance, paid out **daily in BTC** | Maintenance/electricity fees can be paid in GOMINING |
| **Miner Wars (GameFi)** | Clan-based [[gamefi|GameFi]] competition; teams compete for prize pools | Rewards paid in **BTC and GOMINING**; participation gated by token/miners |
| **GoMining Wallet** | Custodial/non-custodial wallet for BTC + GOMINING + ecosystem assets | Holds, stakes, and spends GOMINING; entry point to products |
| **BTC finance suite** | Card/cashback, "Mine-Now-Pay-Later" financing, BTC yield products | Cashback and incentives denominated partly in GOMINING |

The pitch is **"Bitcoin mining without owning hardware":** hosting, electricity, cooling, and maintenance are handled by GoMining, and the holder receives net daily BTC. Economically, a Digital Miner behaves like a **leveraged claim on Bitcoin mining profitability** — its yield rises when BTC price is high and network difficulty is low, and compresses when difficulty climbs or BTC falls (see [[bitcoin-mining]] and Risks below).

## Market Structure & Derivatives

Because it is *not* an exchange-issued token, GOMINING **avoids the single-venue concentration risk** that defines exchange tokens such as [[bitkub-coin|KUB]] or [[coinex-token|CET]]: liquidity is spread across several independent CEXes plus DEX markets, which is structurally healthier.

## Use Case / Narrative / Category

GOMINING blends three overlapping narratives:

1. **Tokenized Bitcoin mining / [[real-world-assets|RWA]]-adjacent** — Digital Miners are fractional, NFT-backed claims on real-world hashrate. This is the core thesis and what differentiates GoMining from pure-software tokens.
2. **[[gamefi|GameFi]]** — Miner Wars adds a play-to-earn competitive layer that recycles BTC/GOMINING rewards and drives engagement.
3. **Crypto wallet / consumer finance** — wallet, card/cashback, and BTC-yield products aim to make GoMining a retail on-ramp to "passive BTC."

The unifying pitch is **"Bitcoin mining without owning hardware."** The token's fortunes are tied to **Bitcoin's price and network difficulty** (which jointly set miner profitability), to **GoMining's data-centre economics** (real electricity, hosting, and uptime costs), and to **continued user growth** in its mining/GameFi products.

## Valuation Framing (Qualitative)

GOMINING is best understood as a **bet on the economics of hosted Bitcoin mining**, intermediated by a token. There is no single clean multiple; instead, value is driven by:

- **Bitcoin price.** Higher [[bitcoin|BTC]] lifts the USD value of daily mining payouts, increasing demand to own/upgrade Digital Miners and, indirectly, the GOMINING needed to do so.
- **Network difficulty & hashprice.** Rising global [[bitcoin-mining|mining difficulty]] (more competition) **dilutes per-TH rewards**; falling hashprice compresses miner yields regardless of token mechanics. GOMINING's value proposition is strongest when BTC is high *and* difficulty growth is contained.
- **Hosting economics & spreads.** GoMining captures a margin between the BTC it mines and the electricity/hosting cost it charges. Efficient fleets (low W/TH) and cheap power widen that spread.
- **Ecosystem velocity.** Utility demand (upgrades, fees, Miner Wars, cashback) determines how much GOMINING is actually consumed vs. held speculatively.

**Sanity checks from the snapshot:** the **MC/FDV ≈ 0.93** caps near-term dilution at ~7%; **~8% daily turnover** indicates real liquidity; price **~51% below ATH** reflects the broader bear-market de-rating of mining/GameFi tokens. A rational thesis hinges on **BTC strength + contained difficulty + sustained ecosystem usage**; the bear case is **rising difficulty, falling BTC, or operator/counterparty failure** eroding payouts faster than token utility can offset.

## Risks

- **Bitcoin-price exposure:** GOMINING and the Digital Miners are effectively a **leveraged claim on [[bitcoin|BTC]] economics**. A falling BTC price directly reduces the USD value of daily payouts and the appeal of buying/upgrading hashrate — token demand and price tend to move with, and amplify, BTC drawdowns.
- **Mining-difficulty exposure:** rewards per terahash are a function of **global network [[bitcoin-mining|difficulty]]**. As more hashrate comes online, per-TH BTC payouts shrink — rising difficulty compresses miner yields even if BTC price is flat, undermining the value proposition.
- **Counterparty / custody / hosting risk:** Digital Miners depend on GoMining **actually operating** the underlying hashrate, data centres, and custody. This is a trust-the-operator **[[real-world-assets|RWA]]** model — operational failure, data-centre outages, insolvency, or mismanagement directly impair payouts and token value. The wallet/custody layer adds further custodial risk.
- **Token-vs-hardware claim risk:** an NFT "miner" is a **contractual/representational claim**, not physical possession of an ASIC. Holders rely on GoMining honoring the link between the token and the real-world hashrate; if that link weakens (re-pricing of TH/s, fee changes, opaque accounting), the asset's backing is diluted.
- **Regulatory risk:** tokenized mining "yield" products and the GoMining card/finance suite may attract **securities, commodities, or money-transmission scrutiny** in various jurisdictions; classification as an investment contract would be materially negative.
- **GameFi / sustainability risk:** Miner Wars and incentive emissions must remain **economically sustainable** — over-emission or declining engagement can create sell pressure and erode the GameFi flywheel.
- **Multi-chain / bridge risk:** deployment across [[ethereum|ETH]], [[binance|BNB]], Solana, and TON fragments liquidity and introduces **bridge and contract-management risk**.
- **Macro / regime risk:** **"extreme fear" (F&G ~23)** within an **established bear market** weighs on speculative mining/GameFi tokens, even ones with healthier liquidity than venue tokens.
