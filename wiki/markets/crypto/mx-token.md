# MX

**MX** (MX Token, ticker **MX**) is the native **exchange utility / centralized-exchange (CEX) token** of the **[[mexc|MEXC]]** trading platform (formerly MXC), an ERC-20 on [[ethereum|Ethereum]] (also deployed on Morph L2). It functions as a "proof of rights and interests" on MEXC: holders access fee discounts, platform-revenue rebates, launchpad/airdrop ("Kickstarter") participation, voting and other benefits -- the same [[exchange-tokens|exchange-token]] model used by [[binance-coin|BNB]] (Binance), [[whitebit|WBT]] (WhiteBIT) and others.

## Overview

MX is the proof of rights and interests of MXC trading platform itself. Its total circulation is 3 billion constantly and never be added. MX holders can enjoy a series of rights and interests including asset appreciation, rebates on platform revenue, supervision, voting, etc. MX can be obtained in two ways: mining and buying.

## Use Case, Narrative & Category

MX is a **centralized-exchange (CEX) token** -- its value is fundamentally a bet on the success and trading volume of the **MEXC** exchange. Core utilities include:

- **Trading-fee discounts** for holders.
- **Platform-revenue rebates / "dividend"-style benefits** distributed to MX holders.
- **Launchpad / Kickstarter access** -- staking or holding MX to qualify for new-token sales and airdrops on MEXC.
- **Governance / voting** on platform decisions.
- **Buyback-and-burn** programs in which MEXC uses revenue to repurchase and burn MX, reducing supply over time.

The narrative is therefore tightly coupled to MEXC's market share among retail-focused exchanges (MEXC is known for listing a very large number of small-cap tokens early). This is the same exchange-token playbook as [[binance-coin|BNB]] (Binance) and others.

> **Data note / contradiction:** The legacy lead text describes MX's circulation as "3 billion constantly and never added," but the snapshot-derived **Tokenomics** table below shows ~91.84M circulating against a ~409.02M total supply and *unlimited* max supply. These figures are inconsistent and likely reflect different historical supply schedules and buyback/burn adjustments. Treat the snapshot figures (circulating ~91.84M; FDV ~$732M; MC/FDV ~0.22) as the data-block values, but verify current supply directly on MEXC/CoinGecko before relying on either.

## How & Where It Trades

As an exchange-native token, the overwhelming majority of MX's liquidity and trading is on **MEXC itself**. In the broader snapshot only **Bitget** (MX/USDT) is listed among external centralized venues, with limited spot DEX presence via **Uniswap V2** on [[ethereum]]. There is **no major Hyperliquid/perpetual-futures market** for MX in this snapshot -- consistent with its profile as a low-float, exchange-centric utility token rather than a widely traded speculative asset. The practical implication is **venue concentration risk**: price discovery, liquidity and most utility all depend on a single platform, and 24h volume (~$5.3M) is thin, so large orders can move the price.

## Risks

- **Platform/counterparty risk** -- MX's value is almost entirely dependent on MEXC's continued operation, solvency, and reputation; exchange-specific risk (hacks, regulatory action, withdrawal freezes) maps directly onto the token.
- **Regulatory risk** -- centralized-exchange tokens face evolving regulatory scrutiny across jurisdictions; MEXC's "Made in China" origin and global retail footprint add jurisdictional uncertainty.
- **Liquidity / venue concentration** -- thin external liquidity and no major derivatives market mean exits can be difficult in stress.
- **Supply opacity** -- conflicting supply figures (see data note) make fundamental valuation harder; unlimited max supply leaves room for dilution absent disciplined buyback/burn.
- **Macro / regime risk** -- while lower-beta than L1/L2 tokens, MX is still exposed to a falling broad market and to declining exchange volumes during the current **extreme-fear, established-bear** environment.
