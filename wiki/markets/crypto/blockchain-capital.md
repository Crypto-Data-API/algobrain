# Blockchain Capital (BCAP)

**Blockchain Capital** is one of the oldest crypto venture firms (founded 2013 by Bart Stephens, Brad Stephens and Brock Pierce), and **BCAP** is the token of its pioneering tokenized venture fund — issued in **April 2017 at $1.00** as one of the first security-token offerings (~$10M raise), giving holders exposure to the firm's BC III Digital Liquid Venture Fund. The token now lives on **ZKsync** after a December 2024 migration and prices off fund NAV via a RedStone/Securitize on-chain feed. For traders the key point is that BCAP is a **NAV-marked, essentially illiquid security token** — quoted "price moves" (like the +260% print in April 2026) reflect NAV repricing on zero volume, not tradable order flow.

## Overview

As a sector-specific and stage-agnostic fund managed by BC Manager, BC III DLVF is a leading investor in the cryptocurrency and blockchain technology industry. BC III DLVF's core business strategy is to leverage the proprietary resources and experience of BC Manager with the objective of generating growth and income from its investments. Holdings primarily consist of new and growing blockchain technology and cryptocurrency businesses.

Blockchain Capital focuses on the nexus where blockchain-based innovations augment and replace legacy systems. As one of the earliest and most active investors in the blockchain industry, the firm has access to proprietary dealflow and an extensive network. Its portfolios span companies facilitating the Bitcoin ecosystem, companies providing blockchain technology to financial institutions, and companies enabling non-financial institutions to leverage blockchain. The firm takes a diversified, early-stage approach — often beginning at seed — and follows on into successful portfolio companies. Notable portfolio names across its funds include Coinbase, Kraken, Circle, Securitize, Matter Labs (ZKsync), [[worldcoin-wld|Worldcoin]] and [[filecoin|Filecoin]] (per CoinGecko portfolio tags).

## Trading Relevance

- **Not tradable for most market participants:** Reg D security token, allowlisted transfer, zero secondary volume. Do not treat CoinGecko rank (#68 as of 2026-06-21; #79 in April 2026) as investable liquidity — it is a NAV-derived mark, not order flow.
- **Use as a signal, not a position:** the on-chain NAV feed makes BCAP a rare **live mark on a crypto VC portfolio** — rising BCAP NAV implies private crypto valuations (Securitize, Matter Labs, custody/infra names) are being marked up, a useful leading indicator for the venture/RWA cycle.
- **Template for tokenized funds:** the ZKsync migration + USDC dividend + RedStone NAV feed is the cleanest blueprint yet for tokenized private funds — relevant to the broader RWA basket ([[superstate-short-duration-us-government-securities-fund-ustb|USTB]], [[ondo-finance|Ondo]]).
