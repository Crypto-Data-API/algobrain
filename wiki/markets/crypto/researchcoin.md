# ResearchCoin

**ResearchCoin** (RSC) is the governance and rewards token of **ResearchHub**, a [[decentralized-science|decentralized science (DeSci)]] platform whose stated mission is to accelerate the pace of science by building "GitHub for research" — an open, web/mobile application for collaborating on, sharing, and peer-reviewing scientific work. ResearchHub was co-founded by Coinbase CEO Brian Armstrong, who has publicly backed the project as a way to align scientific contribution with on-chain incentives. RSC is an ERC-20 token native to [[base|Base]] (with an Ethereum deployment) and ranks **#877** by market capitalization.

## Overview

ResearchHub aims to do for scientific research what GitHub did for software engineering: provide an open, collaborative, version-controlled environment where papers, data, peer review, and replication efforts can be posted, discussed, and rewarded. The platform's reward layer is **ResearchCoin (RSC)**, a community governance and incentive token. RSC is distributed to scientific contributors for behaviors such as uploading literature and earning upvotes on content, and it is used by participants to tip content and to open **bounties** that incentivize specific scientific tasks — for example paying for peer review, requesting a meta-analysis, or commissioning graphical abstracts.

The project sits squarely in the [[decentralized-science|DeSci]] movement, which uses crypto incentives, tokens, and on-chain coordination to attack chronic problems in academia: slow and opaque peer review, paywalled journals, weak incentives for replication and negative results, and misaligned credit. Brian Armstrong's involvement gives ResearchHub unusual mainstream visibility for a DeSci project; he has framed it as a long-term bet on improving how science is funded and validated.

A short (~2 minute) overview of ResearchHub/ResearchCoin's value proposition is available at: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mbIdAODhcXo

### Architecture & how it works

ResearchHub is a consumer web/mobile application; the blockchain layer is the **incentive and coordination rail** rather than the place papers physically live. The system has four interacting parts:

1. **The platform (ResearchHub).** Users post papers, preprints, datasets, and discussion threads, and the community upvotes/downvotes content — a Reddit-/GitHub-like reputation surface aimed at surfacing high-quality science and replication efforts.
2. **Reward issuance.** **RSC** is distributed to contributors for uploading literature and for content that earns community upvotes, so token issuance is tied to peer-judged scientific value rather than raw volume.
3. **Bounties — a market for scientific labor.** Anyone can lock RSC into a **bounty** to commission specific work: a peer review, a meta-analysis, a replication, a literature summary, or a graphical abstract. This is RSC's most distinctive mechanic — it tries to put a price on tasks that the traditional journal system relies on volunteers to perform for free, attacking the slow, unpaid peer-review bottleneck.
4. **Tipping & curation.** RSC can be tipped to creators as a micro-incentive, and reputation accrues to consistent contributors, building a track record that is portable and on-chain.

The blockchain choice — [[base|Base]] (an OP-Stack [[layer-2|L2]]) with an Ethereum deployment — keeps fees low enough that micro-rewards, tips, and bounties are economically viable, which an [[ethereum|Ethereum]]-mainnet-only design would not be.

### Value accrual & governance

- **Demand sinks:** bounties (RSC locked to commission work) and tipping create circulating-supply sinks tied to real platform activity; the more research labor flows through ResearchHub, the more RSC is escrowed and transacted.
- **Governance:** RSC holders participate in community governance of the platform and the **ResearchHub Foundation**, steering treasury and protocol direction.
- **The core question:** like all incentive tokens, RSC accrues value only if platform usage compounds. The honest framing is that RSC is a **coordination token for a real, working product** — stronger than a pure-treasury research DAO — but its price still rides DeSci-narrative cycles more than fundamentals today.

## Token Role & Mechanism

RSC functions as both a **reward** and a **coordination** asset on ResearchHub:

- **Rewards:** contributors earn RSC for uploading research and for content that the community upvotes, tying token issuance to peer-judged scientific value.
- **Bounties:** users lock RSC into bounties to commission specific work (peer reviews, meta-analyses, replications), creating a market for scientific labor.
- **Tipping:** RSC can be tipped to creators, a micro-incentive for useful contributions.
- **Governance:** RSC holders participate in community governance of the platform and the ResearchHub Foundation.

## Competitive Position

RSC is one of the more prominent tokens in the [[decentralized-science|DeSci]] sector, alongside projects like VitaDAO, Molecule, and other research-DAO and bio/longevity-focused efforts. Its differentiators are (1) a working consumer-facing platform (ResearchHub) rather than only a DAO treasury, and (2) the credibility and reach that come from Brian Armstrong's backing. The competitive challenge is the same one facing all DeSci: converting token incentives into sustained, high-quality scientific output and real adoption by working researchers, rather than speculative trading.

## Risks
