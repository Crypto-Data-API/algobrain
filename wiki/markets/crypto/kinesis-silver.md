# Kinesis Silver

**Kinesis Silver** (ticker **KAG**) is a tokenized-silver [[real-world-assets|real-world asset]]: each KAG is backed 1:1 by one ounce of investment-grade silver bullion held in Kinesis's insured, audited vaults, redeemable on demand. For traders it is effectively on-chain spot silver with a monthly holder yield — and it became one of the standout RWA performers of 2025–2026 as the silver rally drove KAG from single digits (2024 lows) to a ~$130 all-time high in early February 2026, before a deep multi-month retrace. Its [[gold]] sibling is [[kinesis-gold|Kinesis Gold (KAU)]].

## Overview

Kinesis silver (KAG) is a silver-backed cryptocurrency, launched by Kinesis — a global trading and digital asset utility platform. Each KAG token is backed by 1 ounce of investment-grade silver bullion, securely stored in Kinesis's fully insured, audited vaults. The mission behind KAG is to reintroduce physical silver backing to money and provide the global community with a stable store of value, with the everyday utility of a fiat currency and the borderless efficiency of a cryptocurrency.

**What makes KAG unique:** all KAG holders receive a passive yield — paid monthly in KAG — simply for holding their metal on the Kinesis platform. The yield is calculated from a 15% share of Kinesis's global transaction-fee revenue. KAG can also be spent via the Kinesis Virtual Card anywhere Mastercard is accepted.

**Redemption:** every ounce of silver underpinning KAG is available for physical redemption, with lower minimums than most competing silver tokens. Its gold sibling is [[kinesis-gold|Kinesis Gold (KAU)]].

## Mechanism & Backing

KAG is a **physically-backed tokenized commodity** — the cleanest RWA model among the tokens in this cluster: there is no peg to defend, only a 1:1 claim on metal.

- **Backing** — each KAG = 1 ounce of investment-grade (LBMA-grade) silver bullion held in Kinesis's fully insured, **audited vaults** (vaulting via the ABX vault network). Supply is minted when metal is deposited and burned on redemption, so circulating supply tracks vaulted ounces and MC/FDV stays at 1.0.
- **Redemption & custody** — every underlying ounce is available for **physical redemption**, with lower minimums than most competing silver tokens; Kinesis is the custodian/counterparty for vaulting and redemption.
- **Yield** — all KAG holders receive a **monthly "holder's yield" paid in KAG**, funded by a 15% share of Kinesis's global transaction-fee revenue — a genuine differentiator vs non-yielding metal tokens like SLV-style wrappers or [[tether-gold|XAUT]].
- **Spendability** — KAG can be spent via the Kinesis Virtual Card anywhere Mastercard is accepted, making it "spendable hard-asset money."
- **KYC / access** — holding and redeeming via the Kinesis platform requires account onboarding (KYC); the ERC-20 representation can move permissionlessly on Ethereum.

## Trading Relevance

- **What it actually is**: a silver spot proxy — trade it like XAG/USD with a small platform premium/discount, not like a crypto alt. Correlation to crypto beta is low; correlation to silver futures is ~1.
- **Where it trades**: primarily on Kinesis's own exchange (KAG/USD, KAG/USDT); listed pricing on CoinGecko/CoinMarketCap/Kraken price pages; thin third-party CEX liquidity (~$0.19M daily volume on 2026-06-21, down from ~$1.5M at the April snapshot) — size accordingly, slippage is the main cost.
- **Use cases for a trader**: (1) 24/7 silver exposure outside futures market hours; (2) crypto-native hedge/flight-to-hard-assets leg; (3) yield-bearing silver — the monthly fee-share yield is a genuine differentiator vs SLV or [[tether-gold|XAUT]]-style tokens (which pay nothing); (4) basis monitoring — KAG premium/discount to silver spot is a sentiment gauge for retail metal demand.
- **Key risks**: platform/custodian risk (Kinesis is the counterparty for vaulting and redemption), thin order books vs futures, and the silver price itself — the Feb 2026 -44% retrace shows the volatility of a squeezed metal.
- **Narrative basket**: RWA / tokenized commodities, precious-metals bid, de-dollarization trades.

## Risks

- **Underlying-metal risk** — KAG *is* silver; the -56% drawdown from the Feb 2026 peak (and the -13% week into 2026-06-21) is pure silver-price risk, not a token-specific failure. Silver is more volatile than gold.
- **Platform / custodian risk** — Kinesis is the counterparty for vaulting, audits, redemption, and the fee-share yield; platform insolvency or vault-audit failure is the key idiosyncratic risk vs holding metal directly.
- **Liquidity risk** — thin order books (~$0.19M/day) vs deep silver futures; large size moves the price.
- **Yield-sustainability risk** — the monthly yield depends on Kinesis platform transaction-fee revenue; a fall in platform activity reduces the holder's yield.
- **Redemption-friction risk** — physical redemption is available but has minimums and logistics; the ERC-20 wrapper adds smart-contract risk.
