# Re Protocol reUSDe

**reUSDe** is a **value-accruing, yield-bearing synthetic-dollar token** from Re Protocol, a DeFi project that bridges traditional (re)insurance markets and on-chain capital. reUSDe represents a position whose value grows as the underlying strategy earns yield, so it is best understood as a [[yield-bearing-stablecoin|yield-bearing]] dollar wrapper rather than a fixed $1.00 [[stablecoin|stablecoin]] — its name echoes Ethena's [[ethena-usde|USDe]] family of [[synthetic-dollar|synthetic dollars]].

reUSDe is the **junior, risk-bearing tier** of Re Protocol's two-token structure: it sits *below* the senior, principal-protected [[re-protocol-reusd|reUSD]] and **absorbs first-loss underwriting risk** in exchange for a higher, variable yield (advertised up to ~23% APR). In credit-structure terms, reUSD is the senior tranche and reUSDe is the junior/equity tranche over the same reinsurance cash-flow pool. Holders of reUSDe are effectively *providing the reinsurance capital* and being paid the underwriting premium for taking catastrophe risk — a real, off-chain, crypto-uncorrelated return source, but one that can be impaired or wiped out by a large insured-loss event.

The price **well above $1.00 is by design, not a [[depeg]]**: reUSDe is a value-accruing token, so accumulated yield is reflected directly in its unit value over time. The positive 24h/7d drift is consistent with ongoing accrual (and any mark-to-market of the underlying), not a broken peg.

## Overview

Re Protocol bridges the gap between traditional insurance / reinsurance markets and decentralized finance (DeFi). Built on [[ethereum|Ethereum]], it aims to bring transparency, efficiency and accessibility to the global reinsurance market — a multi-trillion-dollar industry historically characterised by opacity and reliance on intermediaries.

At its core, Re Protocol facilitates direct, efficient capital allocation to reinsurance contracts through a network of interconnected smart contracts. Participants gain exposure to reinsurance-sector returns without traditional barriers to entry, while benefiting from on-chain transparency and automation. **reUSDe** is the protocol's dollar-denominated, value-accruing token that lets holders capture this yield in its highest-risk, highest-reward form.

## Architecture — How reUSDe Works

### Collateral / backing model & tranching
reUSDe is the **junior tranche** of Re Protocol's senior/junior pair. Capital entering as reUSDe is deployed as **first-loss reinsurance underwriting capital**: it earns the underwriting premium but is the first to absorb claims when insured losses occur. The senior [[re-protocol-reusd|reUSD]] tier sits above it and is shielded by this buffer — which is precisely why reUSD can offer a *fixed* coupon while reUSDe's yield is *variable* and uncapped on the downside. Backing is the pool of tokenized reinsurance contracts plus associated collateral, not fiat reserves or a crypto-perp hedge.

### Value-accrual mechanism (why reUSDe trades above $1)
reUSDe is a **value-accruing synthetic dollar**: the yield generated by the underlying strategy (reinsurance-linked returns and associated collateral) is reflected **directly in the token's unit value** rather than paid out separately. As yield compounds, each reUSDe is worth progressively more than $1.00, which is why it quotes at **$1.35** — *accrual by design, not a de-peg*. It is conceptually similar to staked-synthetic-dollar wrappers such as Ethena's sUSDe (vs [[ethena-usde|USDe]]); see [[yield-bearing-stablecoin]] and [[synthetic-dollar]]. Because it is the junior tranche, its NAV can also *fall* if underwriting losses exceed premiums — the ATL of $1.04 and the $1.04→$1.64→$1.35 path reflect both compounding and mark-to-market of the underlying risk.

### Mint / redeem & gating
Redemption is at NAV through the protocol. Because the underlying reinsurance contracts are illiquid and loss-sensitive, redemption of the junior tier is the most exposed to gating/queueing during a loss event — exactly when holders would most want to exit, the buffer they provide is most needed.

## Narrative / Category & Catalysts

reUSDe rides the **insurance-linked / RWA-yield** narrative as its highest-octane expression: a token that pays the reinsurance equity return on-chain. Catalysts mirror reUSD's but with amplified sensitivity:

- **Hard reinsurance market = rich premiums:** when reinsurance pricing is firm, the junior tranche's variable yield is most attractive — a tailwind for reUSDe demand.
- **Uncorrelated-yield demand:** in the current bottoming/accumulation crypto regime, a ~20%-area yield uncorrelated to crypto funding is a strong draw for risk-tolerant capital.
- **Catastrophe-loss year (headwind):** a major insured-loss event hits reUSDe first and hardest, potentially impairing NAV — the defining downside catalyst.
- **Senior-tier growth:** more reUSD demand requires more reUSDe first-loss buffer, structurally linking the two tiers' growth.

## Risks

- **First-loss / yield-source risk** — as the junior tranche, reUSDe absorbs underwriting losses first; returns depend on reinsurance-contract performance, and large insured-loss events can reduce or wipe out returns and impair principal. This is a **materially higher** risk than the senior reUSD tier.
- **Collateral / backing risk** — value depends on the protocol's collateral and strategy holding up; an impairment can push reUSDe below its accrued value (a downside [[depeg]] relative to NAV).
- **Off-chain dependency** — reinsurance exposure ties on-chain value to off-chain contracts, claims processes and counterparties.
- **Liquidity / redemption-gating risk** — exiting at fair (accrued) value depends on redemption terms and secondary-market depth, which can be thin at this ~$19M market-cap size; the junior tier is the most exposed to gating during a loss event.
- **Smart-contract & oracle risk** — complex contract architecture and reliance on accurate valuation of illiquid insurance positions.
- **Regulatory risk** — tokenized (re)insurance plus yield distribution sits at the intersection of insurance and securities regulation.
- **Macro backdrop** — at the 2026-06-23 snapshot the market is in **Extreme Fear (F&G 21)**, market-health 29/100, regime **bottoming / accumulation**; the insurance-linked yield is uncorrelated to crypto, but thin junior-tranche liquidity widens spreads in risk-off conditions.

## Trading / Usage Playbook

- **Treat it as reinsurance equity, not a stablecoin.** The up-to-~23% headline is compensation for taking *first-loss catastrophe risk*; size it as a risk asset, never as a dollar-par parking spot.
- **Mark to accrued NAV, accept it can fall.** Unlike the senior reUSD coupon, reUSDe NAV declines if losses exceed premiums — the $1.04→$1.64→$1.35 path shows real two-way movement.
- **Pair-trade the stack:** within Re Protocol, holding reUSD (senior) vs reUSDe (junior) is an explicit risk-dial — move down the stack for yield, up for protection.
- **Liquidity discipline:** with ~$196K daily volume, exits are slippage-prone; do not assume you can leave at NAV on demand, especially around a loss event when gating is most likely.

> *Not financial advice. reUSDe is a loss-bearing junior tranche; a large insured-loss event can permanently impair it.*
