# Bityuan

## Overview

Bityuan (BitYuan) is a public, permissionless [[layer-1]] blockchain that originated in China. The platform pairs a base-layer chain with a consumer wallet and Blockchain-as-a-Service (BaaS) tooling, aiming to let developers and enterprises issue assets and build decentralized applications without standing up their own chain. The architecture has historically been associated with the Chain33 framework lineage, emphasizing modular, parallel-chain design and support for [[smart-contracts]].

### Architecture: how the chain works

Bityuan's design follows the **Chain33** lineage — a modular, **parallel-chain** architecture. Instead of forcing all activity onto a single monolithic ledger, the framework separates the base/main chain from application-specific **parallel chains** that anchor to it. The practical implications:

- **Modular base + parallel chains.** A main chain handles consensus and security; developers and enterprises can spin up parallel chains for their own applications/assets that settle back to the base layer, rather than standing up an entirely independent network. This is the technical underpinning of the **Blockchain-as-a-Service (BaaS)** pitch.
- **Wallet + asset tooling.** A consumer-facing **Bityuan wallet** plus asset-management tooling let users hold BTY and issued assets, while the BaaS layer targets enterprises wanting to issue tokens or build dApps quickly.
- **Smart-contract support.** The platform supports [[smart-contracts]], enabling programmable applications atop the parallel-chain structure.
- **BTY as the economic anchor.** All of this is metered and secured by the native BTY token (fees, security, value transfer), so the chain's economic health and BTY demand are linked to actual on-chain activity — which, for a regionally-rooted L1, is hard to verify externally.

### Token role

**BTY** is the native token of the Bityuan chain. It is used to:

- Pay transaction (gas) fees on the network
- Secure and operate the base layer
- Serve as the unit of account and value transfer within the Bityuan wallet/ecosystem

The token has a circulating supply roughly equal to total supply (Market Cap / FDV ≈ 1.0), so there is limited future-emission dilution overhang relative to many peers.

**Value accrual.** As a base-layer gas/security token, BTY accrues value through **network usage** — fees paid for transactions and contract execution, and its role securing the chain — rather than through fee-discount or buyback schemes. The clean MC/FDV ≈ 1.0 supply profile (essentially all tokens circulating) is a structural positive: unlike most pages in this cohort, BTY carries **little future-emission dilution overhang**. The flip side is that with supply fully out, the value case rests entirely on *demand* — real, sustained on-chain activity — which is difficult to verify for a chain whose ecosystem sits largely in Chinese-language communities.

### Competitive position

As a smaller, regionally-rooted L1, Bityuan competes for developer and enterprise mindshare against far larger general-purpose smart-contract platforms (e.g., [[ethereum]], BNB Chain) and other BaaS providers. Its visibility outside Chinese-language communities is limited, and regulatory conditions for crypto in China add structural uncertainty (see Risks).

#### Comparison vs. other L1 / BaaS platforms

| Chain | Type | Token supply profile | Edge | Key weakness vs. Bityuan |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Bityuan** | Chain33-lineage L1 + BaaS | ~Fully circulating (MC/FDV ≈ 1.0) | Parallel-chain BaaS; low dilution overhang | Tiny, China-regulatory exposure, low external visibility |
| **[[ethereum\|Ethereum]]** | General-purpose L1 | Large, no hard cap | Deepest developer ecosystem, security, liquidity | Higher fees; far larger/harder to displace |
| **BNB Chain** | EVM L1 | Burn-reduced supply | Huge user base, exchange-backed distribution | Centralization criticism; vastly bigger |
| **Polkadot** | Parachain L1 | Inflationary | Shared-security parachain model (conceptually similar to parallel chains) | Much larger ecosystem; different consensus |

The comparison frames Bityuan as a **niche, regionally-scoped take on the "issue your own anchored chain" idea** that Polkadot/parachains popularized globally — but at microcap scale and with China-specific regulatory headwinds rather than a broad international developer base.

## Narrative & catalysts

BTY sits in the **regional L1 / BaaS** narrative — a China-origin chain offering parallel-chain infrastructure for issuing assets and building dApps. Its catalysts are largely *local and activity-driven*: visible growth in on-chain usage or parallel-chain deployments, new enterprise/BaaS adoption, or a broader/credible exchange listing that improves liquidity and external legitimacy. The dominant negative catalyst is regulatory — China's strict stance on crypto trading and mining caps the addressable on-shore market and keeps structural uncertainty elevated.
