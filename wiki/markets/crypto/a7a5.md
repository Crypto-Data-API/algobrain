# A7A5

**A7A5** is a Russian-ruble-pegged stablecoin issued in Kyrgyzstan by **Old Vector LLC**, backed by ruble deposits at the sanctioned Russian bank **Promsvyazbank (PSB)** and distributing 50% of deposit interest to holders. It matters to traders almost entirely as a **sanctions story**: by mid-2025 it had processed over **$51 billion** in volume as the settlement rail of the Garantex-successor exchange **Grinex**, and in 2025 it was sanctioned by the US, UK, and EU. For Western traders it is untradeable; for analysts it is the cleanest on-chain window into Russia's shadow crypto economy.

## Overview

A7A5 launched in early 2025 as a "digital ruble" with weekly reserve reports and quarterly audits, marketed for carry trade against USD stablecoins and DeFi liquidity provision (Curve, Uniswap, Convex). In practice, blockchain analytics firms (TRM Labs, Chainalysis) identified it as the core settlement asset of **Grinex**, the successor exchange to **Garantex** (the sanctioned Russian exchange taken down in a March 2025 international operation). The token is linked to **A7 LLC**, a cross-border payments company associated with sanctioned Moldovan oligarch Ilan Shor and Russian state interests.

---

## Major News & Events

- **Mar 2025 —** Garantex disrupted by international law enforcement; liquidity and users migrate to successor **Grinex**, with A7A5 as the bridge asset.
- **Aug 14, 2025 — OFAC + UK OFSI sanctions.** The US Treasury sanctioned Grinex, Old Vector, the A7A5 token's network, and Garantex's founder/co-owners; the UK acted simultaneously. Through end-July 2025, A7A5 had processed **over $51.17 billion in volume** (Chainalysis/TRM figures).
- **Aug 2025 —** Token price/ATH-ATL volatility on CoinGecko (ATH $0.2296 on 2025-08-14, ATL $0.0000105 a week later) reflects broken/manipulated thin DEX pricing around the sanctions event, not peg behavior.
- **Oct 23, 2025 — EU 19th sanctions package** imposed a transaction ban on A7A5 and the affiliated Kyrgyz firms Old Vector and Grinex — the first time the EU directly banned a specific stablecoin.
- **Late 2025/2026 —** Grinex reported suspending operations after sanctions (Chainalysis); the EU's 20th package (2026) and **UK May 2026 sanctions** continued targeting the A7-linked evasion network. A7A5 promoters pitch it as a "trade settlement tool" for sanctioned commerce.

---

## Trading Relevance

- **Do not touch:** A7A5 is a sanctioned asset in the US, UK, and EU. Interacting with it (even via DEX LPing) creates direct sanctions exposure. Exchanges and DeFi front-ends block associated addresses.
- **As a signal:** A7A5 mint/burn and Tron-side flows are a proxy for **Russian cross-border settlement demand** — useful macro color on sanctions-evasion volumes, RUB liquidity stress, and enforcement effectiveness. Chainalysis and TRM publish periodic flow studies.
- **Market-structure note:** its ~$483M "market cap" sits in the top-100 tables next to freely traded assets, but accessible float and venue liquidity are negligible (~$6K/day on Uniswap V2) — a standing example of why market-cap screens need liquidity filters.
