# Brett

**Brett** (ticker **BRETT**, often styled "Based Brett") is a community-driven [[meme-coin]] native to [[base]], Coinbase's [[ethereum]] Layer-2 network. Themed on the "Brett" character from Matt Furie's *Boy's Club* comic (the same universe as Pepe), it became the flagship frog-themed meme of the Base ecosystem and is widely branded "the Pepe of Base." It is an ERC-20 token with no inherent utility beyond community and speculation.

## Origin & Narrative

BRETT is drawn from Matt Furie's *Boy's Club* comic — the same artist behind Pepe the Frog — making "Brett" a culturally pre-loaded character in crypto's frog-meme canon. It launched on [[base]] in early 2024 just as the chain attracted a wave of retail attention, and it rapidly became Base's highest-cap meme, earning the "Pepe of Base" tag. Its narrative fuses three durable threads: the Boy's Club/Pepe lineage, the rise of Base as a low-fee L2, and frog-meme tribalism. As with all memes, there is **no product, revenue, or staking** — value is a reflexive bet on continued attention and Base ecosystem flows.

## Valuation Framing

BRETT has **no fundamental valuation** — no cash flows, revenue, or discountable stream. Memes are **reflexive and sentiment-driven**: price is a self-referential function of attention, Base ecosystem flows, and liquidity. The only meaningful anchors are *relative* (market cap vs peers, MC/FDV, turnover/liquidity), which describe positioning, not intrinsic worth. Any price target is a sentiment bet.

## Risks

Brett carries the full risk profile of a utility-free [[meme-coin]]. **These risks are severe and can result in near-total loss:**

- **Extreme volatility & drawdown potential** — double-digit daily swings are routine (note the -4.98% move on the snapshot date); already ~97.5% below ATH, and meme cycles can collapse permanently toward zero.
- **Narrative dependence** — with no cash flows, price depends entirely on attention; if Base flows or frog-meme interest fade, demand can vanish with no support.
- **Reflexivity** — attention drives price drives attention; the loop runs just as hard on the way down.
- **Insider / whale concentration** — early buyers and large wallets can dominate float; meme tokens are prone to coordinated dumps.
- **Liquidity / leverage / rug risk** — perp funding and OI can spike, and thin order books amplify liquidations during de-risking; on-chain meme pools carry drain/rug exposure.
- **Macro / regime risk** — at the snapshot the market was in "extreme fear" (index ~23) and an established bear market; high-beta memes typically underperform sharply in such regimes.

This is a speculative asset; position sizing and [[risk-management]] discipline are essential, and capital should be treated as fully at risk.
