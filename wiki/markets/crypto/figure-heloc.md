# Figure Heloc

**Figure Heloc** (ticker **FIGR_HELOC**) is not a conventional cryptocurrency: it is the aggregate of **tokenized home-equity-line-of-credit (HELOC) loans** originated by **Figure Technology Solutions** on the **[[provenance-blockchain|Provenance]]** blockchain (a Cosmos-SDK chain built for regulated finance), which CoinGecko lists as a single "asset". Its top-10 "market cap" rank (~$19.7B in the June 2026 snapshot) reflects **tokenized loan principal, not a freely traded token** — a critical distinction for traders screening by market cap. The investable exposure to this business is **FIGR equity on Nasdaq**, not the on-chain instrument.

## Mechanism & Backing

| Dimension | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Underlying asset** | Home-equity lines of credit (HELOCs) — secured consumer loans against US residential property — originated by Figure and tokenized 1:1 on Provenance. |
| **Yield source** | Borrower interest on the HELOC pool. The on-chain unit accretes value (~$1 principal + accrued) rather than paying a separate coupon; downstream products like [[hastra-prime|PRIME]] route this interest spread to token holders. |
| **NAV behavior** | Unit price hugs ~$1 plus accrued value; it is not a directional/volatile asset (qualitative — no fixed APY is asserted here). |
| **Custody / settlement** | Figure Connect (primary market) standardizes and tokenizes the loans; Figure Markets (secondary venue) enables 24/7 settlement via decentralized custody with crypto and fiat on/off ramps. |

## Overview

This project is building the foundational protocol layer for institutional debt markets on blockchain. Powered by Provenance Blockchain, Figure Connect and Figure Markets are transforming the way loan assets are originated, traded, and settled. Figure Connect functions as a primary market for credit—standardizing and tokenizing debt assets such as HELOCs and private credit into composable on-chain formats. Figure Markets acts as a secondary trading venue, enabling 24/7 settlement of these digital loan assets through decentralized custody, with support for both crypto and fiat on/off ramps. Together, they offer an integrated protocol for real-world asset finance, with deep roots in the lending ecosystem.

**The company behind it.** Figure was founded in 2018 by Mike Cagney (co-founder of SoFi) and built its lending stack on the [[provenance-blockchain|Provenance]] blockchain. From late 2018 through 2025-06-30 it originated over **$16 billion** in HELOCs on-chain, and was profitable with **$36M net income on $376M revenue** for the twelve months ended June 2025. The FIGR_HELOC listing tracks the outstanding pool of these tokenized loans, which reached **~$19.7B in principal by June 2026**.

## Trading Relevance

- **You cannot really trade FIGR_HELOC.** There are no major exchange listings; reported volume (~$60M/day in the June 2026 snapshot vs a ~$19.7B "cap") reflects primary subscription/redemption, not a liquid market. Screening pipelines that rank by market cap should exclude or special-case it (the same applies to other tokenized-loan listings).
- **Tradable proxies:** **FIGR equity (Nasdaq)** for the origination/marketplace business; the [[hastra-prime|PRIME]] token for on-chain exposure to Figure HELOC interest; the broader [[real-world-assets|RWA]] / [[tokenization]] narrative basket (ONDO, etc.) for sector beta.
- **Why it matters anyway:** FIGR_HELOC's size is the best single datapoint on real tokenized-credit adoption — growth in the pool (now ~$19.7B) is a leading indicator for the RWA narrative that periodically drives alt rotations.
- **Catalysts:** FIGR earnings, rate cuts (HELOC origination volume is rate-sensitive), US tokenization/market-structure legislation.

## Risks

- **Mislabeled "market cap"** — the #9 rank is a screening trap; the figure is loan principal, not tradeable value. Do not size or compare against real tokens.
- **Credit / housing cycle** — the pool is consumer HELOC debt; a US housing or consumer-credit downturn raises default risk in the underlying loans.
- **Rate sensitivity** — HELOC origination volume (pool growth) is rate-driven; tighter policy slows the headline adoption metric.
- **Permissioning / regulatory** — value and access depend on Figure's regulated lending stack and on US tokenization rules; the on-chain unit is not an open, redeemable retail instrument.
- **Single-issuer concentration** — the entire asset is one originator (Figure); downstream products like PRIME inherit this concentration.
