As of June 2026 it sits at **rank #74** (~$0.88B).

## Overview

Polygon began as Matic Network (2017), an Ethereum sidechain, and grew into one of the most-used EVM networks for payments, stablecoins and low-cost transactions. The MATIC→POL migration (from September 2024, ~99% complete by 2025) rebranded the token for "Polygon 2.0": POL stakes across the ecosystem and the **AggLayer**, Polygon's aggregation layer designed to unify liquidity and interoperability across chains so users do not have to think about which chain they are on.

### Polygon 2.0 and the strategic pivot

Under co-founder **Sandeep Nailwal** (CEO of the Polygon Foundation from June 2025), Polygon deprecated the zkEVM chain and refocused everything on **Polygon PoS + AggLayer**, with payments, stablecoins, and [[real-world-assets|RWA]] settlement as the core narrative rather than generic L2 scaling. The **Gigagas roadmap** targets 100,000 TPS; 2026 upgrades pushed sustained throughput above ~3,800 TPS, aimed squarely at stablecoin payments. POL is meant to be the staking/security asset across this expanding surface (PoS, AggLayer services, CDK chains).

### POL migration mechanics

The MATIC→POL swap is **1:1** and was ~99% complete by 2025. Beyond the rename, POL's design intent is to be **restaked across multiple Polygon-ecosystem services** (a "hyperproductive" token), so a single staked POL can secure several chains/services and earn multiple reward streams — the value-capture thesis if AggLayer adoption materializes.

### Venues & liquidity

POL is tradable on **both** [[binance|Binance]] (spot POL/USDT plus a USD-margined perpetual) and [[hyperliquid|Hyperliquid]] (POL-PERP, leverage up to ~40-50x). This is a deep, liquid two-venue market: Binance provides the CEX spot/perp anchor with heavy order-book depth and tight spreads, while Hyperliquid contributes a transparent on-chain perp with visible funding and OI. The two-venue setup means cross-venue basis and funding are directly observable and executable, so sizing can lean on aggregated depth rather than a single book — but Hyperliquid depth is thinner than Binance, so large clips should be worked across both and staged to avoid slippage on the smaller book.

### Volatility & regime character

POL is a **high-beta Layer-2 / infrastructure altcoin** trading as a deep-value turnaround story near all-time lows. It carries elevated realized volatility typical of a sub-$1B-cap alt, with strong positive beta to ETH (as an Ethereum-scaling asset) and to BTC broad-market risk appetite. In risk-on rotations it can outrun the majors (recently the top mover of its peer set); in extreme-fear / bear regimes it bleeds harder than large caps but is prone to violent short-covering mean-reversion bounces off ATL levels.
