# Crypto Markets

The hub for cryptocurrency markets — major assets, layer-1/layer-2 networks, stablecoins, DeFi, exchanges, and on-chain analytics. Crypto is the most accessible, volatile, and innovation-rich market in the world: it trades 24/7/365, offers permissionless leverage, and provides on-chain transparency that traditional markets cannot match. This page is the **market map** — a structured index that links to the canonical page for every major asset, sector, and venue in the wiki.

[[bitcoin]] dominates as the largest and most liquid asset and the market's risk bellwether; [[ethereum]] is the settlement layer underpinning [[defi|DeFi]] and smart contracts; [[stablecoins]] are the on-chain dollar that settles most volume; [[layer-2]] rollups scale Ethereum throughput; and on-chain derivatives venues like [[hyperliquid]] represent the next generation of leveraged trading. The [[crypto-markets]] page maps the full landscape across CEXs, DEXs, stablecoins, and the still-forming regulatory environment.

## The Crypto Market Map

Crypto is not one market but a stack of overlapping sectors, each with its own drivers, risk profile, and trading dynamics. The table below is the high-level taxonomy this index organizes:

### How the sectors interact

- **Bitcoin sets the risk tone.** As the highest-liquidity, lowest-beta crypto asset, [[bitcoin|BTC]] leads cycles; capital then rotates outward into [[ethereum|ETH]], large-cap [[altcoins]], and finally memecoins. The [[btc-dominance]] index measures where in that rotation the market sits.
- **Ethereum and the L2 stack** host the bulk of [[defi|DeFi]] value. [[layer-2]] rollups (Arbitrum, Base, Optimism) inherit Ethereum security while cutting [[gas-fees]], pushing most retail DeFi activity off the mainnet.
- **Stablecoins are the plumbing.** [[stablecoins|Dollar-pegged tokens]] are the dominant quote asset, the settlement rail for [[defi|DeFi]], and a real-time risk gauge — a flight into stablecoins is the on-chain equivalent of moving to cash.
- **DeFi yield** is generated by [[liquidity-providers|supplying liquidity]] to [[automated-market-maker|AMMs]], lending markets, and via [[liquidity-mining]] incentives — the engine behind [[yield-farming]].

## Crypto vs. Traditional Markets

| Dimension | Crypto | Traditional (equities) |
|---|---|---|
| Hours | 24/7/365, no close | Exchange hours, weekends/holidays off |
| Settlement | Minutes, on-chain, self-custody possible | T+1, custodial intermediaries |
| Leverage access | Permissionless, up to 100x+ on perps | Gated, regulated margin |
| Transparency | On-chain flows public ([[on-chain-analysis]]) | Quarterly filings; opaque order flow |
| Volatility | Very high; 70-90% [[crypto-winter|drawdowns]] are normal | High but rarely >50% for indices |
| Regulation | Fragmented, still forming | Mature, enforced |

## Reading the Market Regime

Crypto cycles swing between euphoria and despair faster and harder than any other market. Two qualitative gauges frame the current backdrop:

- **Sentiment** — The crypto Fear & Greed Index sits at **23 ("Fear")**, the kind of depressed reading that accompanies the back half of a drawdown rather than a euphoric top.
- **Cycle regime** — The market is in an **Established Bear Market** phase: lower highs, suppressed volumes, and risk-averse positioning. Historically these phases align with the post-top half of the four-year [[halving]] cycle and resemble the [[crypto-winter]] template, with [[btc-dominance|BTC dominance]] elevated (~59%) as capital concentrates defensively in [[bitcoin]] rather than rotating into [[altcoins]].
