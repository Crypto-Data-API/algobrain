# Usual

**Usual** is a decentralized [[stablecoin]] protocol that issues **USD0**, a fiat-style stablecoin backed by tokenized [[real-world-assets|real-world assets]] (RWAs) — principally short-term US Treasury bill instruments held via tokenized money-market products. The protocol's design redistributes the yield generated by those reserves to the community rather than retaining it as issuer profit, and it is governed by the **USUAL** [[governance-token|governance token]]. It ranks **#870** by market capitalization.

## Overview

Usual is a French-origin [[defi|DeFi]] protocol built around an RWA-collateralized [[stablecoin]]. It rose to prominence in late 2024 by offering aggressive token incentives, briefly pushing its total value locked and USD0/USD0++ supply into the billions. The core pitch is "ownership of the issuer": where centralized issuers (e.g., Tether, Circle) keep the interest earned on reserve assets, Usual routes that yield to participants and to USUAL governance-token holders, framing the protocol as a community-owned alternative.

### The three-token model

1. **USD0 — the stablecoin.** A [[stablecoin]] minted 1:1 against tokenized [[real-world-assets|RWA]] collateral (primarily tokenized US T-bill / money-market exposure). It is intended to be fully collateralized and to hold a $1.00 peg, redeemable against the underlying reserves.
2. **USD0++ — the "liquid bond."** Users lock USD0 into USD0++ for up to a **4-year term** in exchange for USUAL token rewards (and a share of yield). USD0++ is a *staked/bonded* form of USD0 — crucially, it is **not** itself a $1.00 stablecoin, even though early marketing and secondary-market trading often treated it as roughly par.
3. **USUAL — the governance token.** Captures protocol value, governs parameters, and is distributed as the incentive that drove demand for USD0++. This is the volatile token whose market figures are quoted above.

## Comparison vs Other Yield-Bearing / RWA Stablecoins

Usual sits in the yield-bearing and [[real-world-assets|RWA]]-backed [[stablecoin]] niche, distinct from pure fiat-collateralized issuers (USDT, USDC) and crypto-overcollateralized designs ([[dai|DAI]], crvUSD). The defining tension is between **passing yield to holders** and **maintaining instant, par redeemability**.

| Protocol | Stablecoin | Backing model | Yield mechanism | Key risk profile |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Usual** | USD0 / USD0++ | Tokenized short-dated US T-bills (RWA) | Reserve yield routed to community + USUAL emissions; USD0++ is a 4-yr locked bond | Term/liquidity mismatch in USD0++; governance redemption-rule changes |
| **Ethena** | USDe / sUSDe | Crypto collateral + perp short (delta-neutral basis) | Funding-rate + staking yield to sUSDe stakers | Negative-funding regimes, custodial/exchange counterparty risk |
| **Maker / Sky** | [[dai\|DAI]] / USDS, sUSDS | Crypto overcollateral + RWA T-bill allocations | DSR (savings rate) from RWA + stability fees | Governance/RWA legal exposure; relatively conservative |
| **Mountain Protocol** | USDM | Tokenized US T-bills (RWA) | Rebasing yield direct to holders | Centralized issuer, regulatory/custody risk |
| **Ondo** | USDY | Tokenized T-bills + bank deposits | Yield accrues to token value (non-rebasing) | KYC-gated, issuer/custody risk |
| **Tether / Circle** | USDT / USDC | T-bills + cash (off-chain reserves) | Issuer keeps the yield (no pass-through) | Centralization, opacity (USDT), regulatory |

Usual's pitch — "ownership of the issuer," routing reserve yield to participants instead of an issuer — is most directly comparable to Ethena's stake-to-earn model and to Ondo/Mountain's T-bill pass-through, but it layered on a *locked bond* (USD0++) and *reflexive token emissions* (USUAL) that the others largely avoid. That structure is exactly what amplified both its 2024 rise and its January 2025 unwind.

## The January 2025 USD0++ Depeg / Redemption Controversy

This event is central to understanding Usual and must be presented honestly. In **early January 2025**, Usual governance changed the redemption terms for **USD0++**. Many holders had treated USD0++ as effectively pegged to $1.00 and redeemable near par on demand. The protocol clarified/introduced an early-unstaking mechanism that allowed redemption at a **floor well below $1.00** (around **$0.87**) unless holders waited out the full 4-year lock to receive $1.00, with an alternative "1:1 exit" requiring forfeiture of accrued USUAL rewards.

Markets reacted immediately: **USD0++ [[depeg|depegged]]**, trading down to roughly **$0.89–$0.90** and briefly lower on secondary markets, and the move triggered cascading unwinds in leveraged DeFi positions (notably on lending markets where USD0++ had been used as collateral and looped via the USUAL incentive trade). The USUAL governance token, already off its December 2024 high, sold off sharply, and TVL drained rapidly as the incentive flywheel reversed.

**Honest framing:** the underlying **USD0** stablecoin remained substantially collateralized by its reserves; the breakdown was primarily in **USD0++**, a *time-locked bond product that the market had mistakenly priced as a par stablecoin*. The episode is widely cited as a case study in (a) [[depeg|depeg]] risk arising from term/liquidity mismatch, (b) the danger of treating a yield-bearing locked instrument as a cash-equivalent, and (c) governance changing redemption rules under stress. It illustrates that "stablecoin-adjacent" yield tokens can carry duration and exit risk that is not obvious from their price hovering near $1.00.

### Value accrual and governance

- **USUAL emissions as the demand engine.** USUAL is minted and distributed to USD0++ lockers. The model is explicitly *reflexive*: a higher USUAL price increases the value of emissions, which attracts more USD0 → USD0++ locking, which grows the stablecoin supply and reserve yield. The reverse also holds — a falling USUAL price drains the incentive to lock, which is what reversed the flywheel in January 2025.
- **USUALx / staking.** Holders can stake USUAL (USUALx) to earn a share of the protocol's revenue (the yield on reserves not otherwise distributed) and to participate in [[governance-token|governance]]. This is the intended long-term value-accrual path: tying token holders to real reserve income rather than emissions alone.
- **Earnings-distribution mechanism.** Because reserves sit in tokenized T-bill instruments earning a real risk-free rate, the protocol generates genuine off-chain yield. The governance question is how much of that yield flows to USD0++ lockers vs USUAL/USUALx stakers vs the treasury — a parameter that [[governance-token|governance]] controls and has adjusted under pressure.
- **Mcap/FDV near 1.0** indicates most supply is already circulating, so dilution overhang is comparatively low versus newer tokens — the price collapse is driven by demand destruction, not unlock pressure.

## Peg Mechanism and How It Is Meant to Hold

- **USD0 (the stablecoin)** targets $1.00 via **full collateralization** by tokenized [[real-world-assets|RWA]] reserves (short-dated Treasury exposure). In principle, USD0 can be minted by depositing eligible collateral and redeemed against reserves, with arbitrage keeping the secondary price near $1.00 as long as redemption and [[collateralization|collateral]] integrity are intact.
- **USD0++ (the bond)** is **not** a $1.00 instrument: it is locked USD0 with a 4-year maturity. Its fair value depends on time-to-maturity, the discount rate, and redemption terms — which is precisely why the January 2025 rule change repriced it.
- The peg's resilience therefore rests on (1) the quality/liquidity of the RWA reserves, (2) clear and stable redemption rights, and (3) the market not conflating the locked bond with the liquid stablecoin.

## Risks

- **Depeg risk** — demonstrated in practice. [[depeg|Depeg]] risk is acute for the USD0++ bond (term/liquidity mismatch) and is not zero for USD0 if reserve quality, custody, or redemption access deteriorate.
- **Governance / redemption-rule risk** — the protocol changed USD0++ redemption terms under stress, harming holders who assumed par redeemability. Future governance can alter incentives, fees, or exit terms again.
- **RWA / collateral risk** — backing depends on tokenized off-chain Treasury instruments, introducing custody, issuer, legal, and tokenization-layer risk beyond pure on-chain [[collateralization|collateralization]].
- **Incentive-flywheel / reflexivity risk** — much of the 2024 growth was driven by USUAL token emissions; when emissions value fell, demand for USD0++ unwound rapidly, a reflexive collapse that hit TVL and the USUAL price (~99% below its ATH).
- **Liquidity / small-cap risk** — the USUAL governance token (~$18M market cap, rank ~#868) is volatile and sensitive to broad [[bitcoin|BTC]]-led sentiment.
- **General DeFi / regulatory risk** — smart-contract vulnerabilities and evolving stablecoin/RWA regulation.

*Nothing here is investment advice; figures are point-in-time snapshots that can change rapidly. USUAL (governance token) is distinct from USD0 (stablecoin).*

## Trading Profile

### Venues & liquidity

USUAL trades as a **deep, liquid two-venue market**. On **Binance** it is available as **spot (USUAL/USDT)** and as a **USD-margined perpetual**, giving it centralized order-book depth, tight spreads, and a large retail/quant taker base. On **[[hyperliquid|Hyperliquid]]** it trades as **USUAL-PERP** with leverage up to roughly **40–50x** on a fully on-chain order book. The presence of both a CEX spot leg and two independent perp venues is what makes USUAL cleanly tradable for basis and funding structures: a spot inventory can be sourced on Binance while the short leg is placed on either perp. For a small-cap (~#935) token, sizing should still respect that Binance carries the majority of true depth — large clips on Hyperliquid can move USUAL-PERP mark meaningfully, so execution is best worked passively or split across venues rather than swept at market.

### Applicable strategies

- [[hl-vs-cex-funding-divergence]] — USUAL runs a USD-margined Binance perp and a Hyperliquid USUAL-PERP simultaneously, so their funding rates routinely diverge; long the cheaper-funded venue, short the richer one.
- [[funding-rate-harvest]] — a reflexive incentive-token that periodically over-heats to the long side lets a delta-neutral short-perp/long-spot book collect elevated positive funding on USUAL.
- [[cash-and-carry]] — buy USUAL spot on Binance and short the perp when the basis is positive, capturing carry on a token whose emissions-driven demand spikes can push perps to a premium.
- [[crowded-long-funding-fade]] — narrative-driven USUAL rallies (yield/RWA hype) tend to crowd longs; fade persistently rich positive funding once open interest and price diverge.
- [[liquidation-cascade-fade]] — thin off-Binance depth and up-to-50x leverage make USUAL prone to sharp liquidation flushes; fade the overshoot after a cascade exhausts.
- [[event-driven-trading]] — USUAL price is highly sensitive to protocol governance and redemption-rule events (as in the January 2025 USD0++ episode); position around scheduled/known catalysts rather than passively holding.

### Volatility & regime character

USUAL is a **high-beta DeFi / RWA-stablecoin governance token**, not a large-cap. It carries strong **reflexivity** — its own emissions flywheel amplifies moves in both directions — and has demonstrated extreme drawdowns (~99% below its December 2024 ATH). Realized volatility is high and event-driven: quiet drift punctuated by governance/redemption shocks. Directionally it behaves as a **risk-on alt**, with elevated downside beta to [[bitcoin|BTC]]/[[ethereum|ETH]]-led sentiment (it sells off hard in "Extreme Fear" regimes) but only weak upside correlation, since its rallies depend on protocol-specific narrative rather than broad market beta. Note the governance token (USUAL) is volatile and distinct from **USD0**, the protocol's peg-targeting stablecoin.

### Risk flags

- **Liquidity / venue concentration** — real depth is concentrated on Binance; Hyperliquid USUAL-PERP is thinner and easier to push, raising slippage and mark-manipulation risk on size.
- **Narrative & reflexivity dependence** — price is driven by the USUAL emissions flywheel and RWA-yield narrative; when the incentive value falls, demand can unwind reflexively and fast.
- **Governance / redemption-rule risk** — the protocol has changed redemption terms under stress (USD0++, January 2025), a discrete event risk that can gap the token.
- **Perp funding dislocations** — small-cap perps on up-to-50x leverage can see funding spike and whipsaw; crowded positioning resolves violently via liquidation cascades.
- **Depeg / regulatory adjacency** — while USUAL itself is not a stablecoin, its value is tied to the health and peg integrity of USD0/USD0++ and to evolving stablecoin/RWA regulation.
