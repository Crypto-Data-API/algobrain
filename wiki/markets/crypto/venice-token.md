# Venice Token (VVV)

**Venice Token** (VVV) is the utility token of Venice.ai, the privacy-focused, uncensored generative-[[artificial-intelligence|AI]] platform founded by **Erik Voorhees** (ShapeShift founder) in May 2024. Launched on [[base|Base]] on **January 27, 2025**, VVV uses a distinctive staking model: staking X% of VVV grants X% of Venice's API inference capacity in perpetuity at zero marginal cost — making it one of the few AI tokens with a direct, usage-linked utility claim. For traders it is a core name in the **decentralized AI / AI-inference narrative basket**.

## Overview

Venice.ai launched in May 2024 as a private, uncensored generative-AI platform: prompts are processed via decentralized GPU providers, conversations are stored locally (not on Venice servers), and the platform offers leading open-source models (e.g. DeepSeek R1, Llama family) for text, code and image generation, plus an unrestricted AI Characters feature in Venice Pro.

The **VVV token** launched January 27, 2025 on Base with an airdrop to Venice users and crypto-AI communities. Its design targets AI-agent and developer demand: rather than paying per-call API fees, holders **stake VVV for a pro-rata share of Venice's compute capacity ("VCUs") forever** — converting inference cost into a capital asset. Stakers also earn yield (~19% APY reported in early 2026).

### 2025-2026 developments

- **Jan 27, 2025** — TGE on Base; initial hype run to ATH ~$22.58 (2025-01-28) followed by a year-long decay to ~$0.92 ATL (2025-12-01) — a classic AI-token round trip.
- **Feb 10, 2026** — Venice **permanently cut annual emissions 25%** (8M → 6M VVV), increasing scarcity; by then 33M+ VVV (~40%+ of supply issued to date) had been burned via unclaimed-airdrop burns and ongoing buybacks.
- **Mar 2, 2026** — **OpenClaw**, a decentralized AI-agent framework, named Venice its primary recommended model provider — the catalyst that accelerated a ~150% 30-day rally, pushing market cap back above $322M by late March 2026.
- Through Q2 2026 VVV remains one of the better-performing AI-infrastructure tokens; by 2026-06-20 it had rallied to $14.87 (~$696M cap, #86), only -34% from its ATH. (Source: The Block, CCN, Phemex; verified via web search 2026-06-10.)

## Protocol & Technology

Venice.ai is a **privacy-first, uncensored generative-AI platform**, and VVV is the economic layer that turns its inference capacity into a tradable capital asset.

### Decentralized inference
- Prompts are routed to a network of **decentralized GPU providers** rather than centralized Venice servers; conversations are stored **locally in the user's browser**, not on Venice infrastructure — the core privacy claim.
- Venice serves **open-source models** (e.g. DeepSeek R1, Llama family, Qwen, image models) for text, code, and image generation, plus an unrestricted "AI Characters" feature in Venice Pro. It deliberately avoids the content filtering of centralized providers like [[openai|OpenAI]] and [[anthropic|Anthropic]].
- This positions Venice in the **decentralized-AI / inference** segment alongside [[bittensor|Bittensor]], [[render-token|Render]], and [[akash-network|Akash]] — but uniquely as a consumer-facing app with a token-gated API.

### VVV stake-for-compute model
The token's distinguishing mechanic: **staking X% of circulating VVV entitles the holder to X% of Venice's total daily API inference capacity ("VCUs" — Venice Compute Units), in perpetuity, at zero marginal cost.**
- Instead of paying per-API-call, AI agents and developers can **stake VVV once** and draw inference forever, converting a recurring operating cost into a capital asset.
- Stakers also earn **yield** (~19% APY reported early 2026) from ongoing emissions.
- Demand for VVV is therefore (in theory) a direct function of **real API usage** by AI agents — if agent frameworks standardize on Venice, staking demand becomes structural rather than speculative.

### Why it matters
This makes VVV one of the purest expressions of "**AI compute as staking yield**." The fundamental signal is the relationship between **staked share of supply** and **API utilization**: rising utilization against fixed daily VCUs increases the economic value of each staked token's capacity claim.

## Valuation Framework

VVV is one of the few AI tokens with a **usage-linked utility claim**, so valuation centers on inference economics:

- **API utilization vs total VCUs** — the core fundamental: rising daily inference against fixed capacity increases the value of each staked token's capacity share.
- **Staked share of supply** — higher staking ratio = tighter float + signal of real demand for capacity (vs pure speculation).
- **Cost of "free" inference vs centralized API pricing** — VVV's value claim is the present value of perpetual inference; if [[openai|OpenAI]]/[[anthropic|Anthropic]] cut API prices, the value of token-staked "free" inference compresses.
- **Burn/buyback net flow vs emissions** — net deflation (burns > 6M/yr emission) is bullish for scarcity.
- **Venice Pro user / revenue growth** — consumer demand that ultimately funds buybacks.
