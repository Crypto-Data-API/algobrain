# Kinesis Gold

**Kinesis Gold** (ticker **KAU**) is a gold-backed [[real-world-assets|real-world-asset]] token in which each unit represents **1 gram of investment-grade bullion** held in insured, audited Kinesis vaults. It is issued by **Kinesis Money** (operated by Bullion Capital) on the **Kinesis blockchain**, a fork of the [[stellar|Stellar]] network, with an ERC-20 representation on [[ethereum|Ethereum]]. KAU is one of the three main tokenized-[[gold|gold]] instruments alongside [[paxg|PAXG]] and [[tether-gold|XAUT]] — but, unlike those, it pays holders a monthly yield from platform fee revenue, and it trades far thinner, producing meaningful premium/discount dislocations versus spot gold.

## Mechanism & Backing

| Dimension | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Underlying asset** | Physical, investment-grade (LBMA-standard) gold bullion. 1 KAU = 1 gram of allocated gold. |
| **Custody** | Bullion held in fully insured, audited third-party vaults; each gram is allocated (specific bars assigned), not pooled paper claims. |
| **Minting / NAV** | New KAU is minted only against newly vaulted gold (1:1), keeping MC/FDV ≈ 1.00. The token's "NAV" is simply the spot price of 1 gram of gold. |
| **Yield source** | A *holder's yield* paid monthly in KAU, funded by **15% of Kinesis' global transaction-fee revenue** — a share of platform activity, not interest or lending income. Unique among tokenized-gold tokens (PAXG/XAUT pay no yield). |
| **Redemption** | Every gram is redeemable for physical metal or fiat, with **low minimums** versus the high bars (e.g. ~430 oz) imposed by some competitors. |
| **KYC / permissioning** | Holding, spending, and redemption run through KYC'd Kinesis accounts; the ERC-20 wrapper can move on-chain but core utility (yield, redemption, the Kinesis card) is account-gated. |
| **Regulatory wrapper** | A gold-backed e-money/commodity utility token, not a registered security; not a yield product marketed as an investment contract. |

> The yield model is qualitative and platform-dependent: payout size scales with Kinesis transaction volume, so it is not a fixed APY. Do not infer a guaranteed rate.

## Overview

Kinesis gold (KAU) is a gold-backed cryptocurrency, launched by Kinesis — a global trading and digital asset utility platform. Each KAU token is backed by 1 gram of investment-grade gold bullion, securely stored in Kinesis' fully insured, audited vaults. The mission behind KAU is to reintroduce physical gold backing to money and provide a stable store of value with the everyday utility of a fiat currency and the borderless efficiency of a cryptocurrency.

What makes KAU distinct among gold tokens:

- **Holder's yield** — all KAU holders receive a passive monthly yield, paid in KAU, from a 15% share of Kinesis' global transaction-fee revenue. Payouts crossed $3M by early 2025 and **$11M+ by November 2025**.
- **Spendability** — the Kinesis Virtual Card converts KAU to local currency at point of sale anywhere Mastercard is accepted.
- **Low-minimum redemption** — every gram is redeemable, without the high minimum bars (e.g., 430 oz for XAUT) other gold tokens impose.

## Market Structure

- **Where it trades**: primarily the native **Kinesis Exchange**; price feeds on Kraken/Coinbase price pages exist but liquidity is concentrated in-platform. Daily volume is tiny (~$22k against a ~$333M cap in the June 2026 snapshot) — **do not treat KAU as a liquid gold proxy**; use [[paxg]] or [[tether-gold]] (or gold futures) for tradable gold exposure.
- **Who can hold it**: KYC'd Kinesis account holders for the full feature set (yield, redemption, the Kinesis Virtual Card that spends KAU at point of sale wherever Mastercard is accepted). The ERC-20 wrapper can be held in any Ethereum wallet but is detached from platform yield/redemption.
- **Liquidity caveats**: wide effective spreads, episodic premiums/discounts to spot gold, and meaningful tracking error. Large orders cannot be filled on public books.

### What KAU is actually useful for
1. **Yield-bearing physical-gold holding** — unique among gold tokens; combines bullion exposure with a monthly KAU payout and card spendability.
2. **Premium/discount monitor** — tokenized-gold dislocations are a clean retail-froth indicator; the March 2026 premium collapse was a textbook example.
3. **RWA / tokenized-commodities narrative basket** exposure.

## Risks

- **Liquidity** — near-zero public-market depth; wide spreads and large tracking error vs spot; exits effectively require the Kinesis platform.
- **Platform dependence** — both yield and redemption run through Kinesis/Bullion Capital; counterparty, custody, and operational risk concentrate there.
- **Yield variability** — the holder's yield is a share of platform transaction volume, not a contractual rate; it falls if Kinesis activity falls.
- **Premium/discount risk** — thin liquidity periodically pushes KAU well above or below spot gold (March 2026 ATH was a premium that fully unwound).
- **Gold price risk** — ultimately a leveraged-to-sentiment gold proxy; a gold drawdown flows straight through to NAV.
