# Enjin Coin

**Enjin Coin** (ticker **ENJ**; ERC-20 on [[ethereum]], now native to the **Enjin Blockchain**) is the token behind the Enjin gaming- and NFT-focused ecosystem, which pioneered the **ERC-1155** multi-token standard in 2017–2019. Originally a community gaming platform founded in 2009 by Maxim Blagov and Witek Radomski, Enjin pivoted to blockchain in 2017 and became one of the earliest infrastructure providers for backing in-game items as on-chain [[nft]]s. ENJ historically served as the reserve asset "minted into" Enjin NFTs to give them intrinsic, redeemable value; the ecosystem now runs its own Substrate-based **Enjin Blockchain** (a PoS chain purpose-built for digital assets).

## Use Case, Narrative & Category

ENJ is a foundational **gaming / NFT infrastructure** token ([[nft]], [[gamefi]]):

- **ERC-1155 legacy:** Enjin's Witek Radomski co-authored ERC-1155, the multi-token standard now ubiquitous for game items (batching fungible and non-fungible assets in one contract). This gives Enjin durable mindshare in NFT infrastructure.
- **Developer tooling:** the Enjin Platform, SDKs, Enjin Wallet, NFT.io marketplace and "Beam" (QR-based NFT distribution) target studios that want to add blockchain assets quickly.
- **Enjin Blockchain:** an app-specific Substrate/PoS chain optimized for high-throughput, low-fee NFT operations — Enjin's bid to own the full gaming-NFT stack rather than rent Ethereum block space.

ENJ sits in the broader [[gaming-tokens]] basket and rotates with the GameFi / NFT narrative; it tends to lead on the way up (high beta) and bleed hardest in risk-off regimes.

## Valuation Framing

ENJ's value thesis is **demand for Enjin's gaming/NFT infrastructure translating into token demand** — historically via the reserve-minting mechanic (ENJ locked into NFTs) and now via gas/utility on the Enjin Blockchain. The structural weakness is that its flagship contribution, ERC-1155, is an open standard that benefits the whole ecosystem rather than exclusively ENJ holders, so mindshare does not automatically convert to token accrual. With no cash flows to holders, ENJ is valued as a near-fully-circulating (MC/FDV ~0.99) GameFi infrastructure bet whose price is dominated by sector sentiment. The +14% weekly move against a flat-to-down cohort shows it still trades as a high-beta sentiment proxy; the ~99% ATH drawdown reflects both the post-2021 collapse of play-to-earn user retention and the deep bear regime.

## Notable History

- **2009:** Enjin founded as a gaming community platform.
- **2017:** ICO and pivot to blockchain; launch of ENJ on Ethereum.
- **2019:** ERC-1155 standard finalized; Enjin a key author/promoter.
- **2021 bull market:** ENJ reached its all-time high of **$4.82 (2021-11-25)** on the NFT/metaverse mania — the current price is ~99% below that peak.
- **2023–2024:** Launch and rollout of the standalone Enjin Blockchain.

## Risks

- **Bear-market beta:** ENJ is a high-beta GameFi token; in an extreme-fear regime it is prone to sharp drawdowns, as the fresh March 2026 all-time low shows.
- **GameFi adoption risk:** the value thesis depends on real games and studios issuing NFTs that drive ENJ demand; the broader play-to-earn/NFT-gaming sector has struggled to retain users post-2021.
- **Standard vs. token:** Enjin's biggest contribution (ERC-1155) is an open standard that benefits the whole ecosystem, not exclusively ENJ holders — capturing that mindshare as token value is not automatic.
- **Competition:** crowded NFT-infrastructure and gaming-chain landscape (Immutable, Polygon gaming, app-chains, etc.).
- **Liquidity concentration:** despite broad listings, a deep bear market thins order books and widens spreads.

## Overview

Enjin
Enjin is a leading blockchain ecosystem dedicated to revolutionizing the gaming industry by integrating blockchain technology into games, applications, and businesses.
Founded in 2009 by Maxim Blagov and Witek Radomski, Enjin began as a community gaming platform of 20 million gamers. Pivoting to blockchain in 2017 and kickstarting the NFT movement, Enjin pioneered the advanced ERC-1155 token standard on Ethereum. Building upon this foundation, Enjin launched the Enjin Platform, the world's first NFT integration platform, allowing developers to seamlessly incorporate blockchain assets into their applications in less than 24 hours.
Enjin has since evolved into a comprehensive suite of blockchain products, with the Enjin Blockchain platform now powering billions of digital assets.
The Enjin Ecosystem
Enjin is a feature-rich ecosystem that covers a wide range of blockchain software products. Enjin’s tools enable developers, businesses, and individuals to create, manage, trade, distribute, and integrate NFTs with ease. Enjin's technology is used to power digital assets by millions of users world-wide.
Enjin Blockchain: A Substrate-based Proof-of-Stake (PoS) blockchain custom-built for Non-Fungible-Tokens (NFTs) and other digital assets. Optimized for gaming, it offers high transaction throughput, low fees, and eco-friendly transactions.
Enjin Wallet: A secure, multi-functional mobile wallet supporting various cryptocurrencies and NFTs, enabling users to manage their digital assets with ease.
NFT.io Marketplace: An intuitive platform for creating, trading, and distributing NFTs, fully integrated with the Enjin Blockchain to provide a seamless user experience.
Enjin Platform API and SDKs: Developer toolkit that facilitate the integration of blockchain assets into games and applications, supporting multiple programming languages and platforms.
