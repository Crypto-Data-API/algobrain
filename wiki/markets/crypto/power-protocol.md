# Power Protocol

**Power Protocol** (POWER) is a Web3 gaming protocol and game-studio token associated with **Fableborne**, an action-RPG / base-raiding game developed by **Pixion Games**. Despite the generic "protocol" name, POWER is a [[gamefi|GameFi]] / game-studio asset — its on-chain identity (Discord, litepaper, and category tags) ties it to the Fableborne game economy rather than to any social-engagement or loyalty protocol. The token is deployed across [[ethereum|Ethereum]], BNB Chain, and the gaming-focused [[ronin|Ronin]] network, and was featured in Binance Alpha Spotlight.

## What Power Protocol does

Power Protocol provides the token and economic layer for Web3 gaming, anchored to the **Fableborne** title from Pixion Games. The model is typical of the [[gamefi|GameFi]] / play-and-own category: in-game progression, items, and competitive activity intersect with an on-chain token economy, and assets such as characters or land are represented as [[nft|NFTs]]. The use of [[ronin|Ronin]] — the gaming-optimized chain originally built for Axie Infinity — alongside [[ethereum|Ethereum]] and BNB Chain reflects an emphasis on low-fee, high-throughput rails suited to game transactions.

As a game-studio token, POWER's fundamentals are driven less by abstract protocol usage and more by the success, retention, and live-service economy of the underlying game(s).

### Deep dive — GameFi token + multi-chain rails

The structure that defines POWER as a [[gamefi|GameFi]] / play-and-own asset:

- **Game-economy token** — POWER is the on-chain economic unit of the **Fableborne** economy (Pixion Games): an action-RPG / base-raiding title where progression, items and competitive activity intersect with the token, and characters/land are represented as [[nft|NFTs]].
- **Play-and-own loop** — value is intended to flow from real player demand (cosmetics, progression, asset markets) into the token, rather than from pure "play-to-earn" emissions that historically collapsed when rewards outpaced demand (the Axie/[[ronin|Ronin]] cautionary tale).
- **Multi-chain deployment** — POWER lives on [[ethereum|Ethereum]] and BNB Chain (same contract `0x9dc44ae5be187eca9e2a67e33f27a4c91cea1223`) plus the gaming-optimized [[ronin|Ronin]] network (different contract). Ronin's low fees and high throughput suit frequent in-game transactions; ETH/BSC provide liquidity and listings.
- **NFT layer** — in-game assets as NFTs create secondary markets whose health is tied to the game's player base.

The honest framing: this is a *single-game-studio* bet wearing a generic "protocol" name. Fundamentals track Fableborne's live-service success, not abstract protocol usage.

## Token role & value accrual

POWER is intended to serve the standard set of GameFi token functions:

- **In-game economy / utility** — used within the Fableborne ecosystem for game-economy activity.
- **Access / participation** — gating or enabling participation in game features, events, and asset markets.
- **Speculative / ecosystem token** — traded on exchanges as the liquid representation of the studio's ecosystem.

**Value accrual** for a game-studio token depends almost entirely on whether the game generates *real, sustained player spending* that creates token demand (sinks) exceeding emission (sources). The classic GameFi failure is a sources-heavy economy: rewards/unlocks flood supply while genuine demand lags, and the token deflates. POWER's structural weakness here is acute — only ~21% of the 1B max supply was circulating at the earlier snapshot (MC/FDV ≈ 0.21), so future unlocks/emissions are a heavy dilution headwind, and FDV (~$95M historically) dwarfs market cap. Until Fableborne demonstrates a self-sustaining live-service economy, POWER's accrual is speculative and emission-pressured.
