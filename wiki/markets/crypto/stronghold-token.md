# Stronghold

**Stronghold** (SHX) is the utility token of Stronghold, a US-based financial-services / payments company building products such as Stronghold USD (a USD-backed stablecoin) and platform APIs for payments, trading, and reserves. SHX is a **Stellar-native** asset (with a bridged Ethereum ERC-20 representation) used for fees, rewards, and platform incentives within the Stronghold ecosystem.

## Architecture / What It Does

Stronghold operates as a **regulated financial-services and payments company** rather than a pure decentralised protocol — closer in shape to a fintech with a token than to a base-layer blockchain. Its product surface:

- **Stronghold USD** — a US-dollar-backed [[stablecoins|stablecoin]] / reserve product aimed at payments and settlement use cases.
- **Payments & trading APIs** — infrastructure for businesses to embed payments, FX, and crypto rails (deposit, settlement, on/off-ramp).
- **SHX utility** — the token is used for platform fees, rewards, and ecosystem incentives within the Stronghold rails.

**Why Stellar.** SHX is issued **natively on the [[stellar|Stellar]] network**, whose design — fast (~3–5s) finality, sub-cent fees, a built-in decentralised exchange (SDEX), and a native asset-issuance and anchor model — is purpose-built for payments and tokenised-fiat use cases. That makes Stellar a natural home for a USD-settlement product. A **bridged ERC-20 representation on [[ethereum|Ethereum]]** extends SHX into the larger DeFi/exchange liquidity pool at the cost of added bridge/custody attack surface.

The economic model is essentially **fintech revenue + token incentives**: real-world payment and stablecoin activity drives platform usage, and SHX is the incentive/fee unit wrapped around it. The token's value therefore depends heavily on the company's commercial traction, not just on-chain mechanics.

### Value Accrual & Governance

- **Fees / incentives** — SHX is consumed or rewarded within Stronghold's payment and trading products; demand is tied to platform throughput.
- **No clear hard-cap governance model surfaced** beyond utility/rewards; SHX reads more as a utility/incentive token than a governance asset (do not overstate governance rights absent a verified source).
- **Demand driver** — adoption of Stronghold USD and the payments APIs by businesses. The "Made in USA," regulated-entity angle is the differentiator versus offshore stablecoin issuers, but also exposes the project to US money-transmission and stablecoin regulation.

**Business & regulatory**

- **Commercial-traction dependence** — as a company-token, SHX value tracks Stronghold's real payments adoption, which is hard to verify from market data.
