# Beam

**Beam** (BEAM, base chain **Avalanche L1**) is the native gas and governance token of the **Beam Network**, a [[gaming-tokens|gaming]]-focused blockchain ecosystem incubated by the gaming DAO **Merit Circle**. Beam launched in 2023 as an [[avalanche|Avalanche]] subnet (now an Avalanche L1) and has since become a multi-chain ERC-20 asset bridged to [[ethereum|Ethereum]], [[bnb|BNB Chain]], and [[base|Base]]. BEAM powers transaction fees, validator staking, and developer incentives across the network's catalogue of Web3 games, NFT marketplaces, and gaming-related dApps. It is a classic [[gamefi|GameFi]] / [[non-fungible-token|NFT]] infrastructure token — a gaming **L1** play rather than a single game.

The MC/FDV ratio of ~0.88 means the large majority of the maximum supply is already circulating, so **future emission-driven dilution is relatively limited** compared with newer gaming tokens that still have most of their supply locked. BEAM was migrated from Merit Circle's earlier **MC** token at a 1:100 ratio, which is why the nominal token count is in the tens of billions. The remaining ~7B uncirculated tokens are earmarked for ecosystem incentives, the Beam Foundation, and team/treasury allocations that vest over time.

## How & Where It Trades

**Spot venues:** BEAM is broadly listed on major centralized exchanges including [[binance|Binance]] (ticker shown as BEAMX), [[kraken|Kraken]], Upbit (KRW market), Bitget, and Crypto.com Exchange. On-chain liquidity exists on [[uniswap|Uniswap]] V2/V3 on Ethereum (BEAM/WETH pools) plus DEX pools on Base and BNB Chain.

**Derivatives:** Perpetual futures are available on several large CEX derivatives desks (Binance, Bitget). The token is not among the headline listings on [[hyperliquid|Hyperliquid]]'s on-chain perp board as of this snapshot; traders seeking leverage primarily use the CEX perp markets. Open interest and funding for the perps are modest given the small market cap — funding tends to track spot sentiment and has been broadly neutral-to-negative during the current bear phase.

At ~$4.7M daily volume against a $72M cap, BEAM has **acceptable but not deep liquidity**; large market orders will move the price, and slippage on DEX pools is meaningful beyond five-figure sizes.

## Use Case / Narrative / Category

Beam positions itself as a **dedicated gaming network** rather than a single game. Its pitch: give studios a low-fee, sovereign Avalanche L1 with native tooling (the Beam Companion wallet, the Sphere NFT marketplace, and developer SDKs) so players can transact in BEAM across many titles. Categories CoinGecko assigns include Smart Contract Platform, Gaming (GameFi), NFT, Avalanche L1, BNB Chain Ecosystem, and DWF Labs Portfolio.

The investment narrative is leveraged to two cyclical themes: (1) the broad **[[gamefi|GameFi]]** / play-to-earn cycle, and (2) the **Avalanche subnet/L1 ecosystem**. Both are high-beta to overall crypto risk appetite.

## Valuation Framing (qualitative)

- **An infrastructure call option on Web3 gaming.** Unlike a single-game token, BEAM is a claim on aggregate activity across whatever titles ship on the Beam L1; its bull case is a basket bet on the network attracting studios and players, its bear case is that gas demand never materialises.
- **vs ATH:** -96.8% frames the depth of the GameFi de-rating; the 2024 ATH was a cycle-top print and a weak fair-value anchor.
- **Low dilution overhang:** MC/FDV ~0.88 is a *positive* relative to newer gaming tokens — only ~12% of supply is uncirculated, so emission-driven sell pressure is comparatively limited.
- **Fundamental gauge:** the meaningful signal is on-chain — daily active addresses, transactions, and live games on the Beam L1 — not the token tape.

## Risks

- **Gaming-token cyclicality** — GameFi is one of the most boom/bust crypto sub-sectors. BEAM is down ~97% from its peak and any recovery is highly dependent on a renewed risk-on cycle and on actual game traction, not just token speculation.
- **Adoption dependency** — Beam's value rests on third-party studios shipping games people play. Web3 gaming has repeatedly over-promised on user numbers; if active titles stall, fee demand for BEAM evaporates.
- **Token unlocks** — Although MC/FDV is high (~0.88), remaining team/treasury/ecosystem allocations can still add sell pressure into thin demand.
- **Thin liquidity** — Daily volume (~$4.7M) is small relative to the float; exits in size are costly and the token is exposed to sharp gap moves.
- **Macro / sentiment** — With the [[crypto-fear-and-greed-index|Fear & Greed Index]] at ~23 (Extreme Fear) and an Established Bear Market backdrop, high-beta small-cap gaming tokens like BEAM are among the first to be sold and the last to recover.
