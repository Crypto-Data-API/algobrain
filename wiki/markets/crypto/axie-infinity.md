# Axie Infinity

**Axie Infinity** (AXS) is the **governance and staking token of Axie Infinity**, a pioneering [[nft|NFT]]-based "play-to-earn" ([[gaming-tokens|GameFi]]) game in which players collect, breed, and battle creatures called Axies. Built by Sky Mavis and running primarily on the purpose-built **Ronin** sidechain (with [[ethereum]]-based contracts), AXS lets holders stake to earn rewards and vote on the direction of the game universe. It is one of the defining tokens of the 2021 GameFi/[[metaverse]] cycle.

## Overview

AXS is the governance token for the Axie Infinity game. Token holders will be able to shape and vote for the direction of the game universe. This is unlike traditional games where all decisions are made by the game developers. AXS holders will be able to stake their tokens to earn more AXS and even&nbsp;vote for governance proposals.

AXS is the archetypal **GameFi / play-to-earn** token, distinct from the metaverse-LAND peers ([[the-sandbox|SAND]], [[decentraland|MANA]]) in that its value is tied to an actual game economy and the **Ronin** gaming sidechain rather than virtual real estate. ## Valuation Framing

- **GameFi-economy thesis** — AXS value-capture depends on a sustainable game economy: breeding fees, staking demand, and treasury accrual all scale with genuine, fun-first player activity rather than pure earn-speculation. The bull case is Sky Mavis successfully running Ronin as a broader gaming hub; the bear case is that P2E economies are reflexive and decay once new-player inflows stall (as in 2022).
- **Narrative-beta / option value** — like its metaverse peers, AXS trades as a high-beta call option on the GameFi narrative returning. The +20% 2026-06-20 flare on >market-cap volume is a textbook example of momentum/narrative-driven repricing in a thin float.
- **Capped supply** — the 270M hard cap gives AXS a cleaner long-term dilution story than uncapped GameFi tokens, but near-term price is dominated by sentiment, not tokenomics.

## Use Case, Narrative & Category

Axie Infinity sits in the **GameFi / play-to-earn (P2E) / NFT gaming** category and was the breakout title of that 2021 narrative. The economy historically ran on a two-token model:

- **AXS (Axie Infinity Shards)** -- the governance and value-capture token. Holders **stake AXS** for rewards and govern the ecosystem (treasury, game direction). AXS is also used in breeding fees and as a premium reward.
- **SLP (Smooth Love Potion)** -- the high-emission in-game utility token earned through play and spent on breeding Axies (an uncapped "soft currency").

The core gameplay loop -- buy Axie NFTs, play to earn SLP/AXS, breed and sell new Axies -- made Axie the archetype for "earn while you play," especially in emerging markets. The long-term thesis depends on Sky Mavis sustaining genuine, fun-first gameplay and a balanced token economy rather than purely speculative earning, plus the growth of the broader **Ronin** gaming ecosystem.

## How & Where It Trades

AXS is liquid on all major centralized exchanges -- **Binance** (an early Launchpad listing), **Kraken**, **Upbit**, **Bitget**, **KuCoin** and **Crypto.com** -- with strong volumes in USDT, USD and KRW (Korean retail interest is notable). On-chain, AXS trades on **Uniswap V3** on [[ethereum]] and on Ronin-native DEXs (e.g. Katana).

For leverage, **AXS-PERP** trades on [[hyperliquid]] and other perp venues. As a high-beta GameFi token with relatively low float, AXS is prone to sharp, momentum-driven moves (as on 2026-06-20): perp open interest and funding can spike rapidly during rallies, and crowded longs make it vulnerable to long-squeeze reversals. Spot turnover (~$142.5M/24h, exceeding its own market cap) led the move here, but derivative positioning often amplifies the swings.

## Notable History

- **2018** -- Sky Mavis launches Axie Infinity on [[ethereum]].
- **2021** -- Explosive P2E adoption (especially in the Philippines, Venezuela and other emerging markets); AXS reaches its **all-time high of $164.90 on 2021-11-06** at the peak of the GameFi/metaverse mania.
- **2022-03** -- The **Ronin Bridge hack** (~$625M stolen, one of the largest crypto exploits ever) and the collapse of the P2E earning model triggered a severe decline; SLP hyperinflation undermined the token economy.
- **2022-2026** -- AXS falls more than 99% from ATH; Sky Mavis re-tools toward "play-and-earn," builds out the Ronin gaming ecosystem, and works to rebalance tokenomics. Price near **$1.13** on 2026-06-20 after a sharp short-term rally (+20% 24h on ~$142.5M volume).

## Risks

- **GameFi sustainability** -- play-to-earn economies are reflexive: token value depends on new-player inflows, making them fragile when speculation fades (as in 2022).
- **Security / infrastructure risk** -- the 2022 Ronin Bridge hack is a stark reminder of bridge and sidechain security risk.
- **Regulatory risk** -- AXS appears in "Alleged SEC Securities" lists; gaming-token regulation is uncertain.

- **Macro / regime risk** -- speculative GameFi assets are among the hardest hit in **extreme-fear, established-bear** conditions like the current backdrop.

## Trading Profile

**Venues & liquidity** — AXS is a genuinely two-venue derivatives market: it trades as **spot + a USD-margined perpetual on Binance** and as **AXS-PERP on [[hyperliquid]]** (up to ~40-50x). Binance provides the deepest USDT book and the reference funding rate, while Hyperliquid adds an on-chain venue with transparent order-book depth and its own funding. This dual availability means AXS is liquid enough to size meaningful positions and to run cross-venue relative-value or funding trades, but as a ~#182 mid-cap the depth is still thinner than large-caps — large market orders will walk the book, so scale entries/exits and lean on limit orders during the sharp momentum flares this token is prone to.

**Applicable strategies**
- [[hl-vs-cex-funding-divergence]] — AXS funding is quoted independently on Binance and Hyperliquid, and thin GameFi float lets the two venues drift apart during rallies, creating a harvestable spread.
- [[crowded-long-funding-fade]] — momentum-driven +20% spikes pack the perp with crowded longs and richly positive funding, setting up a funding-fade against over-extended positioning.
- [[cash-and-carry]] — long spot AXS on Binance against a short AXS-PERP captures the basis/funding when perps trade at a persistent premium during narrative flares.
- [[liquidation-cascade-fade]] — low float and high leverage make AXS prone to violent long-squeeze liquidation cascades that overshoot, offering mean-reversion entries into the flush.
- [[oi-confirmed-trend]] — pairing the sharp GameFi momentum moves with rising open interest (via the HL OI feed) helps separate real trend continuation from thin, unconfirmed pops.
- [[narrative-trading]] — AXS repricings are catalyst/GameFi-narrative driven (the isolated +20% flare against broad extreme-fear is textbook), rewarding trades keyed to narrative rotation rather than macro beta.

**Volatility & regime character** — AXS is a **high-beta GameFi / play-to-earn altcoin** with a relatively low circulating float, producing violent, momentum-driven swings in both directions. It is broadly correlated to BTC/ETH beta in risk-on/risk-off regimes, but its idiosyncratic, catalyst-driven flares (like the 2026-06-20 rally that ran counter to a broad extreme-fear backdrop) mean it frequently decouples from majors on the upside during GameFi-narrative rotations, then reverts hard.

**Risk flags**
- **Venue/liquidity concentration** — mid-cap depth means large orders move the book; positioning is concentrated on Binance and Hyperliquid.
- **Perp funding dislocations** — funding and OI can spike rapidly during rallies, and crowded longs make the perp vulnerable to long-squeeze reversals.
- **Narrative dependence** — price is dominated by GameFi sentiment and catalysts rather than tokenomics; flares reverse quickly when narrative inflows stall.
- **Emissions / supply** — ongoing staking-reward emissions add modest float (hard-capped at 270M); the *separate* uncapped SLP token is a reminder of GameFi economy fragility.
- **Security / regulatory** — legacy Ronin bridge-hack and "Alleged SEC Securities" listing are structural overhangs relevant to holding risk.
