# IOTA

**IOTA** (ticker **IOTA**; native to its own **MoveVM Layer 1** since 2025) is a 2015-vintage distributed ledger originally built as a feeless DAG ("the Tangle") for Internet-of-Things payments, which in **May 2025 abandoned that architecture entirely** in the "Rebased" upgrade — relaunching as a Move-based, delegated-proof-of-stake Layer 1. For traders it is a case study in legacy-coin reinvention: a top-10 asset of the 2017 cycle now ranked ~#178, trading **at fresh all-time lows** despite a full technical reboot, with liquid perps on [[hyperliquid]] and Binance spot.

## Overview

### Original design (2015–2025)

IOTA launched as a feeless distributed ledger for the Internet of Things. Its "Tangle" was a directed acyclic graph (DAG) rather than a blockchain: transactions confirmed each other, there were no miners and no fees, and the design was marketed as quantum-resistant and infinitely scalable. The pitch targeted blockchain's perceived problems — fee escalation, chain bloat (BTC/ETH chains were already >150GB), and mining centralization (top-2 Bitcoin pools held ~56% of hashrate at the time). In practice the network long depended on a centralized **Coordinator** node, a single point of failure that became the project's defining criticism.

### IOTA Rebased (May 2025) — full architectural reboot

On **May 5, 2025**, after a Genesis Ceremony run by the IOTA Foundation and 12 initial validator entities, IOTA migrated from the legacy Stardust/UTXO network to an entirely new chain:

- **MoveVM object ledger** — making IOTA one of the few Move-language L1s (alongside Sui and Aptos), with native L1 smart contracts for the first time.
- **Mysticeti consensus + delegated PoS** — ~50 permissionless validators at launch, scaling toward 150; the centralized Coordinator was removed.
- **Performance claims** — 50,000+ TPS throughput, sub-500ms confirmation.
- **Native staking** — initial inflation of 6–7%/yr paid to validators/delegators, yielding roughly 10–15% APY at launch. This ended IOTA's original fixed-supply, feeless model: the chain now has (small) fees and issuance.
- **IOTA EVM** sidechain continues alongside the Move L1 for Solidity compatibility.

### Positioning and adoption

The Foundation's current narrative centers on **real-world trade, asset tokenization, and supply-chain coordination** (e.g. trade-document digitization initiatives), shifting away from pure IoT messaging. Despite the reboot, the token's 2025–2026 price trend was persistently weak: roughly -60% in the year to April 2026 and -74% in the year to June 2026, setting successive all-time lows in Q1–Q2 2026 (~$0.047–0.052 region).

The defining tokenomics change came with **Rebased (May 2025)**: IOTA abandoned its original fixed-supply, feeless model for **~6–7%/yr inflation** paid to validators and delegators, targeting roughly 10–15% staking APY at launch. This is a **persistent dilution headwind** — the ~9% gap between circulating (~4.50B) and total (~4.92B) supply reflects not-yet-issued staking rewards, and the supply schedule has no hard cap. For a token already making fresh all-time lows, ongoing issuance into a falling price is structurally bearish unless staking demand absorbs the new units. Compare this with the clean, fully-circulating supply of fixed-cap peers; IOTA traded its scarcity narrative for a more conventional PoS security budget.

## Narrative & Category

IOTA spans three loosely-related baskets that traders rotate through (see [[narrative-trading]]):

- **Move ecosystem** — since Rebased it is one of the few MoveVM L1s alongside [[sui|Sui]] and Aptos, so it can catch SUI/APT sympathy beta it never had as a DAG.
- **RWA / trade-finance** — the Foundation's current pitch (trade-document digitization, supply-chain coordination) lines it up with the real-world-asset narrative.
- **"2017 dinosaur revival"** — a legacy top-10 coin from the last-cycle-but-one, occasionally bid in nostalgia/rotation trades.

**Catalysts to watch**: validator-decentralization milestones (50→150), Move DeFi TVL on the new L1, trade-logistics partnerships, Upbit/Korea volume spikes. Old "feeless IoT / quantum-proof" claims in pre-2025 material are **obsolete** — do not trade on stale narratives.

## Valuation Framing

IOTA is best framed as an **L1 security/utility token that the market has not re-rated despite a clean-sheet reboot**. The bull case is optionality: a credible MoveVM chain trading at a ~$200M cap (vs. multi-billion caps for Sui/Aptos) is cheap *if* the new chain attracts DeFi TVL, staking participation, and RWA flows. The bear case is the price action itself — fresh all-time lows in June 2026, ~99% off the 2017 peak, against a backdrop of ~6%/yr dilution and far better-funded Move competitors. With negligible on-chain TVL relative to peers and no token cash-flow accrual beyond staking yield (itself funded by inflation), there is no fundamental floor; the asset trades on narrative and Move-ecosystem beta. The reboot reset the technology but not, so far, the market's verdict.

## Risks

- **Persistent dilution**: ~6–7%/yr inflation into a falling price; no hard supply cap.
- **No re-rate**: fresh all-time lows in June 2026 *after* the reboot signal the market has not rewarded the technical reset.
- **Competition**: out-funded by Sui and Aptos in the Move-L1 niche; thin DeFi TVL.
- **Liquidity**: ~$5.5M/24h spot — slippage and gap risk on size.
- **Regime**: high-beta legacy alt in an Established Bear Market (Fear & Greed 23).
- **Narrative obsolescence**: pre-2025 "feeless IoT / quantum-resistant" pitch no longer applies.
