# SynFutures

**SynFutures** (token ticker **F**) is a decentralized [[perpetual-futures|perpetual-futures]] [[decentralized-exchange|DEX]] that lets users trade derivatives on virtually any asset and permissionlessly list new perpetual markets. Its signature innovation is the **Oyster AMM**, a model that fuses an on-chain [[automated-market-maker|AMM]] with an on-chain order book to deliver order-book-style pricing with AMM liquidity. F trades at **$0.00382603**, ranking **#910** by market capitalization (~**$16.41M** market cap), essentially flat over 24h (**-0.10%**) and down **-3.19%** over the past 7 days. It is built across [[ethereum|Ethereum]]-ecosystem chains including [[base|Base]] and [[bnb-chain|BNB Chain]].

## Overview

SynFutures is built for [[perpetual-futures|perpetual futures]] (perps) — derivatives that track an underlying spot price with no expiry, kept in line with spot via a periodic [[funding-rate|funding-rate]] mechanism. The protocol's core differentiators are:

- **Oyster AMM (V3)** — a "unified AMM + on-chain order book" design. Concentrated-liquidity AMM positions and discrete limit orders coexist in the same on-chain liquidity space, so traders get order-book-like execution while liquidity providers supply capital AMM-style. SynFutures describes this as the first integration of a single unified AMM with an on-chain matching order book.
- **Permissionless market creation** — anyone can list a new perpetual market for any asset within seconds by supplying a price reference and liquidity, rather than waiting for a centralized listing decision. This "trade any asset" model targets the long tail of markets that centralized venues will not list.
- **Single-token liquidity** — LPs can provide liquidity to a perp market with a single asset rather than a paired deposit, lowering the barrier to market-making.

SynFutures has raised over $38 million across funding rounds from investors including **Pantera Capital, Polychain Capital, Dragonfly Capital, Standard Crypto, and SIG**. It is deployed across [[ethereum|Ethereum]]-ecosystem chains including [[base|Base]] and [[bnb-chain|BNB Chain]].

## Architecture — Oyster AMM Deep Dive

The Oyster AMM is what distinguishes SynFutures from oracle-AMM perps (like GMX-style designs) and pure order-book perps (like dYdX/[[hyperliquid|Hyperliquid]]):

- **Unified liquidity space.** Rather than running an AMM and an order book as separate liquidity venues, Oyster places concentrated-liquidity AMM ranges and discrete limit orders into one on-chain structure. A market order can fill against AMM curve liquidity *and* resting limit orders in the same transaction, improving price and depth.
- **Concentrated liquidity for perps.** LPs choose price ranges (à la Uniswap V3) but for a perpetual, so capital sits where trading actually happens, raising capital efficiency versus a flat `x*y=k` curve.
- **On-chain matching engine.** Limit orders are matched fully on-chain, preserving self-custody and transparency while approximating CEX-style execution.
- **Funding mechanism.** Like all perps, SynFutures uses a [[funding-rate|funding rate]] that periodically transfers value between longs and shorts to tether the perp price to the underlying index; this is the lever that prevents perpetual-vs-spot divergence.
- **Stabilized mark price / oracle reference.** A reference price anchors each market; for permissionless long-tail listings the quality of that reference is the central risk (see Risks).

This hybrid is a bet that traders want order-book precision *and* passive AMM liquidity in the same pool, instead of choosing one or the other.

## Token Role: F & Value Accrual

The **F** token is the protocol's governance and incentive asset:

- **Governance** — F holders steer protocol parameters, market-listings policy, fee settings, and treasury use.
- **Incentives / liquidity mining** — F is emitted to reward liquidity providers and active traders, bootstrapping depth in perpetual markets.
- **Fee and value alignment** — trading fees generated by the venue can be directed toward the token/stakers, aligning F with protocol usage and volume.

Because a perp DEX's value is driven by trading volume and open interest, F's fundamental backing is the **fee throughput of the exchange**. With circulating supply (3.89B) at only ~39% of max supply (10B), ongoing emissions are a structural headwind for the token even when volume grows.

## Competitive Position

SynFutures competes in the crowded on-chain perpetuals arena. Its bet is that the Oyster AMM (order-book precision with AMM liquidity) plus permissionless listing of long-tail markets carves out a niche that order-book-only and oracle-AMM competitors do not serve. The risk is that perps liquidity is winner-take-most: traders and market-makers concentrate where depth and volume already are, making it hard for smaller venues to sustain share. F's deep drawdown from its 2024 high reflects that competitive pressure plus the sector-wide repricing.

| Venue | Execution model | Custody | Edge | Trade-off |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **SynFutures** | Oyster AMM (unified AMM + on-chain order book) | Self-custody | Permissionless long-tail listings, single-token LP | Liquidity must be bootstrapped vs incumbents |
| **[[hyperliquid|Hyperliquid]]** | Fully on-chain central limit order book (own L1) | Self-custody | Deep liquidity, fast UX, large share | Newer chain; ecosystem lock-in |
| **dYdX** | Order book (app-chain) | Self-custody | Established brand, deep majors | Long-tail listing slower |
| **GMX** | Oracle-priced pool (GLP) | Self-custody | Zero-slippage on majors, LP yield | Oracle dependency; limited assets |

See [[perpetual-futures]], [[funding-rate]], and [[decentralized-exchange]] for the underlying mechanics.

## Risks

- **Competitive/liquidity-fragmentation risk** — dominant perp DEXs capture most volume; sustaining liquidity is the central challenge.
- **Smart-contract & oracle risk** — the Oyster AMM, matching engine, funding mechanism, and price oracles are complex attack surfaces; a perps protocol failure can be catastrophic (bad-debt cascades).
- **Permissionless-listing risk** — easy market creation invites thin, manipulable markets and oracle-manipulation attacks on illiquid pairs.
- **Tokenomics/dilution** — circulating supply is a fraction of max supply; ongoing emissions are a structural headwind for the token price.
- **Market backdrop** — derivatives venues are volatility-sensitive; the 2026-06-21 environment is risk-off ([[bitcoin|BTC]] ~$64,180; Fear & Greed 22, "Extreme Fear").

## Trading Profile

### Venues & liquidity

F is tradable on [[binance|Binance]] as both spot and a USD-margined [[perpetual-futures|perpetual]], exposing full derivatives telemetry: [[funding-rate|funding]], open interest, and liquidations. It is NOT listed on [[hyperliquid|Hyperliquid]], so Binance is the primary leveraged venue for F. This concentration means execution, depth, and borrow/leverage availability hinge on a single CEX order book; the FUSDT perp is where funding, OI, and liquidation data are actionable. Given F's small-cap size (~#951) and thin book, size positions conservatively — leverage amplifies slippage on a shallow perp, and venue concentration adds tail risk if Binance de-lists or throttles the pair. Spot-plus-perp availability on one venue does enable clean same-venue basis and funding capture without cross-exchange transfer friction.

### Applicable strategies

- [[funding-rate-harvest]] — Binance FUSDT perp exposes funding; a small-cap DeFi token often carries persistent funding skew that can be harvested delta-neutral against spot.
- [[cash-and-carry]] — F trades spot + perp on the same venue (Binance), enabling a long-spot / short-perp carry that collects funding with minimal transfer risk.
- [[crowded-long-funding-fade]] — narrative-driven perp-DEX rotations can crowd longs into F; richly positive funding flags a fade back toward spot.
- [[liquidation-cascade-fade]] — a thin, high-beta perp is prone to liquidation wicks; fading forced-sell cascades on FUSDT targets the mean-reversion snapback.
- [[oi-confirmed-trend]] — pairing Binance OI with price confirms whether an F move is real leverage-backed conviction or a squeeze to fade.
- [[rsi-mean-reversion]] — F is range-bound and volatility-sensitive; oscillator extremes on the FUSDT chart mark reversion entries in quiet tape.

### Volatility & regime character

F is a small-cap, high-beta DeFi/derivatives-infrastructure token with strong directional correlation to [[bitcoin|BTC]]/[[ethereum|ETH]] risk appetite but far larger amplitude. It underperforms majors in sustained risk-off tape and can spike sharply on perp-DEX or long-tail-listing narrative bursts. A heavy FDV overhang (MC/FDV ~0.39) and ongoing emissions cap sustained token upside, so rallies tend to be volatility-driven and mean-reverting rather than durable trends. Expect low-volume drift punctuated by sentiment-led spikes.

### Risk flags

- **Venue concentration** — leveraged trading depends on Binance alone; no Hyperliquid fallback, so a listing/liquidity change is a concentrated tail risk.
- **Thin liquidity** — low market cap and modest volume mean wide slippage and liquidation sensitivity, especially at leverage.
- **Emissions / dilution** — circulating supply is ~39% of max; ongoing unlocks and emissions are a structural token headwind.
- **Narrative dependence** — price is tied to perp-DEX / long-tail-listing narratives; when that flow fades, volume and funding activity dry up.
