# Pi Network

**Pi Network** (PI) is a mobile-first "tap to mine" cryptocurrency with one of the largest retail user bases in crypto (tens of millions of "pioneers"), which finally opened its mainnet in February 2025 after six years of closed beta. For traders it is primarily a **supply-overhang and retail-sentiment story**: PI hit $2.99 at launch euphoria, then fell ~96% to fresh all-time lows by mid-2026 under relentless monthly token unlocks, while remaining unlisted on Binance and Coinbase.

## Protocol / Product

Pi Network is a social cryptocurrency and developer platform designed to make digital-currency mining accessible to everyday people through **mobile devices**. The project aims to build a widely distributed currency for real-world goods and services rather than purely speculative trading. Its core value proposition is a mobile-first mining system that lets users earn rewards on their phones without specialized hardware — the "tap to mine" daily check-in.

### How it works

- **Consensus** — runs on the [[stellar|Stellar Consensus Protocol]] and Federated Byzantine Agreement (FBA); security comes from a global *trust graph* built from user "security circles" where members vouch for one another.
- **Roles** — users participate as **pioneers** (daily miners), **contributors** (build security circles), **ambassadors** (referrals), and **nodes** (run the network).
- **Ecosystem** — native Pi wallet, Pi Browser, and a dApp platform where PI is used for P2P exchange, fees, an internal marketplace, launchpad staking, and **voluntary lockups that boost mining rates** (a key supply-control lever).
- **Governance/management** — managed by the **Pi Core Team / Social Chain Inc.**; the project lists no external institutional investors in its documentation.

### Recent protocol milestones

- **Open Network launch (20 Feb 2025)** — mainnet opened to external connectivity after six years of closed mainnet.
- **Protocol V23 (May 2026)** — full **smart-contract functionality** launched, enabling dApps and a native DEX on Pi mainnet.
- **Node migrations (mid-2026)** — v24 finalized by 2 June 2026; node operators given until 18 June 2026 to complete the v25 migration.

**Dilution flag (severe)**: MC/FDV of 0.65 against *total* supply understates the true overhang, because total (16.6B) is itself only ~17% of the 100B max. Mining rewards, referral bonuses, and **monthly vesting unlocks** (tens to hundreds of millions of PI per month; **~163M scheduled for June 2026**) create continuous structural sell pressure. Voluntary lockups (which boost mining rates) are the main offsetting sink, but they have not absorbed the unlock flow — hence the 96% decline from ATH. Core Team token holdings are opaque, compounding the supply uncertainty.

## Ecosystem & Use Cases

- **Mobile mining** — the daily-engagement loop and the source of the huge user base.
- **Pi wallet / Pi Browser** — self-custody and dApp access.
- **dApp platform + native DEX** — enabled by V23 smart contracts (May 2026).
- **Internal marketplace / P2P** — PI as a medium of exchange within the ecosystem; launchpad staking; voluntary lockups.

The thesis bull case is "convert tens of millions of pioneers into real economic users"; the bear case is "engagement is mining-incentive-driven, not demand-driven," which the post-listing price action has so far supported.

## Valuation Framework

Fundamentals-based valuation of PI is **essentially impossible**, and that is itself the key finding:

- **No cash flows, opaque float** — there is no protocol revenue, and Core Team holdings / the true unlock schedule are not transparent, so MC/FDV understates dilution.
- **Supply-overhang model** — the only tractable framing is flow-based: known monthly unlocks vs. a shrinking, mining-driven retail bid. This biases the asset toward "fade rallies into unlock dates."
- **Listing-option value** — a Tier-1 listing (Binance/Coinbase) would re-rate liquidity and legitimacy; PI carries embedded *option value* on that event, which is the main asymmetric upside.
- **Sentiment proxy** — given the massive retail base, PI price is a rough gauge of unsophisticated retail engagement in crypto.

---

## Trading Playbook

- **Unlock-overhang short / fade-rallies** — the textbook structure: known monthly supply (e.g., ~163M in June 2026) hitting a thin retail bid; rallies into unlock dates have repeatedly faded.
- **Squeeze risk (the other side)** — squeezes are event-driven against heavy short positioning: a major exchange listing or an ecosystem catalyst (V23 dApps gaining traction) can force sharp short-covering. Size shorts with that tail in mind.
- **Listing lottery** — long-PI bulls are largely buying the Binance/Coinbase-listing option; the absence of that listing is both the bear thesis and the call option.
- **Sentiment gauge** — use PI's behaviour as a read on retail risk appetite in the broader bear regime (F&G 23).

- **2019** — founded by Stanford PhDs Nicolas Kokkalis and Chengdiao Fan; six-year closed beta begins (mobile "tap to mine").

- **2025–2026** — persistent monthly unlock pressure against thin demand.

- **2 Jun 2026** — v24 finalized; v25 node migration deadline 18 Jun 2026.
- **6 Jun 2026** — **fresh all-time low $0.1189**.
- **20 Jun 2026** — **$0.134**, ~96% below ATH, ~13% above ATL.

### Controversy / criticism (stated factually)

- **No Tier-1 listing** — repeated failure to secure Binance/Coinbase listings has fueled scepticism about legitimacy and listing prospects.
- **Distribution/transparency concerns** — opaque Core Team holdings and the true unlock/vesting schedule draw persistent criticism; with a 100B max vs ~10.8B circulating, the long-term dilution path is unclear.
- **Centralized development** — an April 2026 GitHub snapshot of the public platform-docs repo showed **1 contributor and 1 merged PR**, raising open-source/decentralization questions (note: this is the public *docs* repo, not necessarily core protocol development, which is not fully open).
- **KYC / mining-incentive critiques** — the mobile-mining model has drawn comparisons to engagement-farming; critics argue the user base is incentive-driven rather than demand-driven.

These are reported as documented criticisms, not verdicts; PI nonetheless maintains one of the largest claimed user bases in crypto.

## Competitive Positioning

PI is hard to peer-map cleanly — it is a mobile-mining/social L1 rather than a DeFi or exchange token. Rough comparisons:

| Project | Category | Distinguishing feature | Tier-1 listed? |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Pi Network** | Mobile-mining L1 | Phone "tap to mine", huge claimed retail base, FBA consensus | **No (not on Binance/Coinbase)** |
| [[stellar\|Stellar (XLM)]] | Payments L1 | Shares the SCP/FBA consensus lineage | Yes |
| Worldcoin | Identity/airdrop crypto | Biometric distribution, large user base | Yes |
| Other "mining-app" tokens | Retail engagement | Daily-claim mechanics | Mostly no |

**PI's edge**: scale of claimed user base and brand recognition. **PI's weakness**: no Tier-1 listing, opaque supply, severe unlock overhang, and centralized development — the combination that has driven the 96% drawdown.

## Risks

- **Supply/unlock overhang (primary)** — continuous monthly unlocks against a 100B max and thin demand; the dominant price driver and the core bear thesis.
- **Listing risk** — no Binance/Coinbase listing caps liquidity, legitimacy, and institutional access; failure to secure one keeps the overhang unabsorbed.
- **Transparency/distribution risk** — opaque Core Team holdings and vesting schedule make true float and dilution unknowable; honest valuation is impossible.
- **Centralization risk** — Pi Core Team controls the network and roadmap; public-repo development activity is minimal.
- **No fundamentals** — no protocol revenue or cash flows; value rests entirely on adoption hope and the listing option.
- **Bear-market regime** — at Fear & Greed 23 and fresh ATLs, downside momentum and capitulation risk are elevated; squeezes (the upside tail) require an external catalyst.
