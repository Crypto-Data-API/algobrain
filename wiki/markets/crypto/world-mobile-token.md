# World Mobile Token

**World Mobile Token** (ticker **WMTX**) is the utility token powering World Mobile, a **[[depin|DePIN]] telecom network** that builds decentralized mobile/wireless connectivity using AirNode operators and a sharing-economy incentive model. Originally a Cardano-based project, the token migrated to a multi-chain footprint and is now [[ethereum|Ethereum]]-native with deployments on Base, BNB Chain, Arbitrum, Solana and Cardano. World Mobile targets the trillion-dollar telecom industry by letting anyone operate network infrastructure (AirNodes) and earn rewards for providing or securing connectivity.

## Use Case, Narrative & Category

World Mobile is a **DePIN telecom** play: instead of a centralized carrier owning all towers and spectrum, World Mobile incentivizes a distributed network of **AirNode operators** who deploy and run wireless infrastructure (Wi-Fi/connectivity nodes) and earn WMTX rewards. Token holders can also stake to AirNodes to share in rewards and help secure the network.

The DePIN-telecom economics are the crux of the thesis: real-world connectivity revenue (subscribers paying for data/voice) must eventually exceed the value of token emissions paid to operators for the model to be sustainable rather than purely subsidy-driven. World Mobile has emphasized underserved/emerging markets (notably parts of Africa) where it can deploy capacity competitively. CoinGecko tags it under [[depin|DePIN]] and lists DWF Labs among associated parties.

### Network & Coverage Model

World Mobile's network is built bottom-up from independently owned hardware rather than carrier-owned towers:

- **AirNodes** — operator-deployed wireless access points (Wi-Fi / connectivity nodes) that provide last-mile coverage. Operators buy/run the hardware and earn WMTX for the connectivity they supply.
- **EarthNodes** — validator-style nodes that secure and govern the network; holders can stake WMTX to EarthNodes to share in rewards without running physical access hardware.
- **Sharing-economy incentive loop** — the token aligns three parties: subscribers (pay for data/voice), AirNode operators (provide coverage, earn tokens), and stakers (secure the network, earn a cut). The model only becomes self-funding when subscriber revenue outpaces the WMTX emitted to operators.
- **Geographic focus** — deliberately targets underserved/emerging markets (notably parts of Africa) where incumbent carrier economics are weak, letting a distributed, low-capex model compete.

This is the canonical **[[depin|DePIN]]** structure (token subsidies bootstrap real-world infrastructure that should later be revenue-sustaining), placing WMTX alongside other DePIN networks rather than with the memes or RWAs in this cohort.

## Risks

- **Unlock / emission overhang** — with only ~43% of max supply circulating, ongoing AirNode-reward emissions roughly double the float over time; this dilution is a persistent price headwind, acute in a bear market.
- **Subsidy-vs-revenue gap** — DePIN telecom only works if real connectivity revenue outgrows token rewards; until proven, the network is incentive-subsidized.
- **Very thin liquidity** — ~$1.3M daily volume is among the lowest in this cohort; entries and exits are slippage-heavy and gap-prone.
- **Capital-intensive, real-world execution** — physical telecom rollout (hardware, regulation, local partnerships) is slow, costly, and jurisdiction-dependent.
- **At/near ATL in extreme fear** — sitting ~3.5% above a fresh 2026 ATL ($0.0391, set 2026-06-05) signals weak demand; the Established Bear Market and [[crypto-fear-and-greed-index|Fear & Greed]] of 23 amplify downside risk.
