# Kaia

**Kaia** (KAIA) is the EVM-equivalent Layer 1 formed by the August 2024 merger of Klaytn (Kakao) and Finschia (LINE) — giving it native distribution into KakaoTalk and LINE, the dominant messengers of Korea and Japan with 200M+ combined monthly users. By 2025–2026 it repositioned as **"the foundation for stablecoin settlement and on-chain finance across Asia"**, making KAIA the listed proxy for the Korean/Asian stablecoin regulatory theme. KAIA is a [[layer-1]] gas/staking token; the chain is EVM-equivalent.

## Overview

Kaia is an Ethereum-equivalent Layer 1 public blockchain that brings Web3 to the fingertips of hundreds of millions across Asia via their preferred messenger superapps. Kaia utilizes Practical Byzantine Fault Tolerance (pBFT), a highly-optimized version of BFT, to achieve 4,000 real-world TPS with 1-second block times and immediate absolute finality.

Its structural moat is distribution: **Mini Dapps inside LINE Messenger** (via LINE NEXT's Dapp Portal) and Kakao-ecosystem integration in Korea — channels no other L1 has.

## 2025–2026 Developments

- **Mini Dapps launch (Jan 2025)** — Web3 Mini Dapps went live inside LINE Messenger and attracted **130M+ new registered users** in the following months (LINE Messenger: ~194–250M MAU depending on source/date).
- **Native USDT (May 2025)** — Tether deployed native USDT on Kaia, the foundation of its stablecoin-settlement pivot.
- **Stablecoin Summer Hackathon (Aug 2025)** — Kaia, [[tether-limited|Tether]], and LINE NEXT co-hosted the "Kaia Wave Stablecoin Summer Hackathon" for USDT-powered DeFi Mini Dapps; it ran in parallel with the First Korea Stablecoin Hackathon co-hosted by Tether, KakaoPay, and LINE NEXT (from 4 Aug 2025).
- **Project Unify (announced 2025–26)** — Kaia DLT Foundation + LINE NEXT plan a stablecoin-powered super-app supporting stablecoins pegged to USD, JPY, KRW, THB, IDR, PHP, MYR, and SGD — a play to consolidate Asia's fragmented payments market (The Block).
- **KRW stablecoin pilot with KB Kookmin** — completed a pilot with South Korea's largest bank covering KRW-stablecoin issuance, offline merchant payments, and cross-border remittance settlement on Kaia — positioning Kaia as the settlement layer if Korea's won-stablecoin legislation passes.
- **Price**: ATH $0.4067 (3 Dec 2024, post-merger rally); ATL $0.0348 (10 Jun 2026). April 2026 snapshot: $0.047 / $275M; by 21 June 2026: $0.0370 / ~$217M / rank #168, up +59% on the week off the June low but still ~91% below ATH — the token has so far badly lagged the ecosystem's user numbers.

Near-full float means there is little unlock overhang — unusual for a recent-vintage L1 — so KAIA's price reflects live demand rather than scheduled dilution. The flip side is that ongoing governance-controlled issuance is uncapped, and the token must capture value from network usage (gas, staking, stablecoin settlement) to offset inflation; user growth that does not translate into on-chain fee demand leaves KAIA structurally soft (see the persistent user-growth-vs-price divergence below).
