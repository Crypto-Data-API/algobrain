# Tezos

**Tezos** (XTZ) is a [[liquid-proof-of-stake|Liquid Proof-of-Stake]] [[layer-1|Layer 1]] launched in 2018, distinguished by formal-verification-friendly smart contracts and a pioneering self-amending [[on-chain-governance|on-chain governance]] system that has shipped 18+ forkless protocol upgrades (most recently Quebec in January 2025 and Rio in May 2025). Its current strategic bet is **Tezos X** — a rollup-centric architecture anchored by **Etherlink**, its [[ethereum|EVM]]-compatible [[layer-2|Layer 2]] — with a decisive Etherlink governance vote scheduled for **June 2026** to bring Tezos X to mainnet. For traders, XTZ is a deeply out-of-favor legacy L1 (price ~-97% from its 2021 high, near all-time-low territory) whose tradeable story is upgrade-event catalysts against persistent downtrend.

## Overview

Tezos is a high-performing blockchain and open-source platform for assets and applications, with a strong focus on code security, on-chain governance, and decentralization. Built with formal verification in mind, the protocol enables builders to avoid bugs when developing smart contracts, suiting applications requiring a high degree of security — a property that won early institutional tokenization experiments (Societe Generale, others).

Tezos' **LPoS (Liquid Proof-of-Stake)** consensus lets any stakeholder participate directly or by delegation ("baking"). Its on-chain governance system enables stakeholders to propose and vote on protocol upgrades, allowing the chain to **self-amend without forks** — the mechanism behind its 18+ smooth upgrades since 2018.

### Founders

Tezos was conceived by Arthur and Kathleen Breitman in a 2014 white paper published under the pseudonym L.M. Goodman. The Breitmans argued Bitcoin's shortcomings were the lack of community governance, proof-of-work cost/centralization, and limited programmability. After a record $232M ICO in 2017 and a governance dispute with the Tezos Foundation's first president, mainnet launched in September 2018 and has run uninterrupted since.

## 2024–2026 Developments

- **2024 — Paris upgrade**: activated the **Data Availability Layer (DAL)**, designed to raise on-chain data throughput by up to ~4,000x and cut costs for rollups like Etherlink — the foundation of the Tezos X roadmap. (Source: Nomadic Labs / Tezos Spotlight.)
- **2025-01 — Quebec (17th upgrade)**: reduced block times to **8 seconds**; developed by Nomadic Labs, Trilitech and Functori, adopted via on-chain baker vote. (Source: tezos.com media center.)
- **2025-05-01 — Rio (18th upgrade)** activated at block #8,767,488: cycles shortened from 3 days to **1 day**, and **10% of participation rewards** allocated to DAL participation to harden L2 data availability. (Sources: Nomadic Labs Rio announcement; Tezos Spotlight "Rio is Live".)
- **Etherlink growth**: the EVM-compatible L2 grew TVL 6,200% QoQ in Q1 2025 to $10.8M (Messari via @tezos) and became the center of Tezos DeFi activity.
- **2026-06 — Tezos X decision point**: after testnet validation, an Etherlink upgrade proposal goes to bakers in a June 2026 governance vote; if approved, **Tezos X goes live on mainnet** with Michelson and EVM interfaces fully composable — effectively re-platforming Tezos around its rollup. (Source: Tezos Spotlight, "Tezos X: From Roadmap to Reality".)
- **Price**: XTZ printed $0.3366 on 2026-03-29 — an all-time low on some feeds and -96% from the October 2021 ATH — despite the technical roadmap progress, illustrating the gap between protocol delivery and market demand for legacy L1s.

## Tokenomics & Supply

| Metric | Value |
|---|---|
| **Circulating Supply** | ~1.088B XTZ |
| **Total Supply** | ~1.108B XTZ |
| **Max Supply** | Unlimited (staking issuance; adaptive) |
| **Market Cap / FDV Ratio** | ~0.98 |

Tezos uses **adaptive issuance**: there is no fixed max supply, and inflation funds staking ("baking") and delegation rewards. The mechanism targets a staking ratio and adjusts the issuance rate dynamically — when a large share of supply is staked, issuance falls; when staking participation drops, issuance rises to incentivise it. With ~98% of total supply already circulating, there is **no meaningful unlock cliff** — dilution comes only from modest ongoing issuance, most of which is recycled to stakers. The structural sell pressure is reward liquidation by bakers/delegators, not vesting unlocks. See [[proof-of-stake]] and [[token-emissions]].

## Trading Relevance

- **Upgrade-event calendar**: Tezos' governance pipeline is fully public (proposal → exploration → cooldown → promotion → activation), giving dated catalysts. The **June 2026 Etherlink/Tezos X vote** is the biggest binary event in the chain's recent history — approval re-rates the "Tezos as rollup-centric chain" thesis; rejection or delay reinforces the legacy-L1 decay path.
- **Legacy-L1 basket short/rotation leg**: XTZ (with similar 2017-2018-era L1s) has been a persistent relative-value short against newer L1s in [[l1-l2-rotation]] frameworks; -39% over the year to April 2026 while majors rallied.
- **Staking-yield floor**: LPoS delegation yield (~4-6% historically, adaptive issuance) gives holders carry but adds structural sell pressure from reward liquidation.
- **Institutional-tokenization optionality**: Tezos retains real institutional pilots (e.g., European bank tokenization) — low-probability, high-impact headline risk to any short.

## Risks

- **Demand decay** — despite delivering 18+ upgrades, XTZ keeps making lower lows; the core risk is that a technically excellent legacy L1 simply loses the demand/developer-mindshare battle to newer chains and [[ethereum|Ethereum]] L2s.
- **Binary governance event** — the June 2026 Tezos X vote is a single point of failure for the re-rating thesis; rejection or repeated delay caps upside.
- **Adaptive-issuance sell pressure** — staking rewards create structural supply that bakers/delegators liquidate, a persistent headwind in a no-demand tape.
- **Etherlink traction risk** — the rollup pivot only works if Etherlink attracts real TVL and users; current TVL is small relative to market cap.
- **Macro/regime** — extreme-fear sentiment ([[fear-and-greed-index|F&G]] 23) and an established bear market amplify drawdowns in out-of-favor L1s.

## Trading Profile

### Venues & liquidity

XTZ is tradable on [[binance|Binance]] — spot (XTZ/USDT) plus a **USD-margined perpetual** with full [[funding-rate|funding]], [[open-interest|open interest]] and [[liquidations|liquidation]] telemetry. It is **NOT listed on Hyperliquid**, so Binance is the primary leveraged venue for XTZ. With a ~$250M cap and thin ($10-11M) 24h spot turnover, perp depth is the practical constraint: order books are shallow relative to majors, so leverage should be sized conservatively, funding/OI can swing sharply on small flows, and larger positions must scale in/out to avoid slippage and self-inflicted liquidation wicks. Venue concentration on a single dominant perp venue also means funding and basis signals are driven almost entirely by Binance positioning rather than a diversified cross-venue book.

### Applicable strategies

- [[funding-rate-harvest]] — thin, single-venue XTZ perp funding can spike positive on event-driven long crowding, letting a delta-neutral spot-long/perp-short harvest the carry.
- [[crowded-long-funding-fade]] — bounces on upgrade headlines (e.g. the June 2026 Tezos X vote) draw crowded longs into a decayed L1; fade over-extended positive funding.
- [[cash-and-carry]] — with ~98% of supply circulating and no unlock overhang, spot XTZ vs the USD-M perp is a clean basis/carry leg when the perp trades at premium.
- [[liquidation-cascade-fade]] — shallow perp depth makes XTZ prone to liquidation wicks near its all-time low; fade forced-seller flushes into support.
- [[event-driven-trading]] — Tezos' dated on-chain governance pipeline (proposal → promotion → activation) provides scheduled binary catalysts to trade around.
- [[range-mean-reversion]] — pinned just above ATL with flat weekly action, XTZ oscillates in tight ranges that suit reversion between defined bounds.

### Volatility & regime character

Small-cap legacy L1 (rank ~143, ~$250M cap) trading -97% from its 2021 ATH and within a few percent of its all-time low. High-beta to BTC/ETH on the downside with weak upside participation — a chronic underperformer in [[l1-l2-rotation]] frames. Not a memecoin; volatility is infra/DeFi-token in character, dominated by upgrade-event catalysts (Etherlink, Tezos X) against a persistent structural downtrend rather than reflexive hype cycles. Extreme-fear sentiment amplifies drawdowns.

### Risk flags

- **Venue concentration** — leveraged exposure is effectively Binance-only (no Hyperliquid); a single perp book drives funding, basis and liquidation dynamics.
- **Liquidity** — thin spot turnover and shallow perp depth raise slippage and liquidation-wick risk; size and leverage must stay small.
- **Narrative dependence** — the thesis hinges on a single binary catalyst (June 2026 Tezos X / Etherlink governance vote); rejection or delay reinforces the legacy-decay path.
- **Emissions** — adaptive-issuance staking rewards create ongoing baker/delegator sell pressure (no unlock cliff, but a structural bid-side headwind).
