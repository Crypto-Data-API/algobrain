# FunFair (FUN)

**FunFair** (token: **FUN**) is one of the earliest Ethereum-based blockchain casino / iGaming technology platforms, launched via a 2017 ICO. Its core technical contribution was **Fate Channels** — a gaming-specific variant of state channels designed to let casino games settle off-chain with provable fairness and near-instant, low-cost play, while only opening and closing positions on the Ethereum mainnet. The FUN token is the in-platform currency for casino operators built on FunFair technology. The project later rebranded its token-side efforts as **FUNToken**, and it is categorized as a Gambling / **GambleFi** asset, an Ethereum-ecosystem and Energi-ecosystem token, and a former Pantera Capital portfolio holding. It ranks **#667** by market capitalization.

## Overview

FunFair Technologies was founded in 2017 by **Jez San** (a veteran games-industry figure, founder of Argonaut Games) and raised a large ICO at the peak of the 2017 token boom. The thesis was that online casinos suffer from trust, transparency, and high-fee problems that a blockchain could solve, while pure on-chain gaming was too slow and expensive for fast-paced casino play.

### Mechanism / architecture

- **Fate Channels** — FunFair's signature design. Rather than sending every spin or hand to the Ethereum mainnet, a player and operator open a state channel, play many rounds off-chain with cryptographically signed, provably-fair outcomes, and settle the net result on-chain. This delivers instant play, low cost, and verifiable fairness (random number generation that neither party can manipulate). See [[ethereum]] state channels.
- **B2B casino toolkit** — FunFair positioned itself as picks-and-shovels infrastructure: white-label casino software, game logic, and wallet integration that third-party operators license to launch branded blockchain casinos, rather than running a single casino itself.
- **FUN token utility** — FUN is the unit of account inside FunFair-powered casinos: players wager in FUN, operators settle in FUN, and the token historically carried staking/locking mechanics tied to operator licensing and revenue capture. As **FUNToken**, the project has emphasized burns, staking, and broader [[gamefi]] / GambleFi positioning to renew demand.

### Token sinks and faucets (GambleFi economy)

FUN's economy differs from [[play-to-earn]] reward tokens: there is no large "earn-by-playing" emission faucet. Instead, demand is driven by **wagering throughput and deliberate burn/stake mechanics**.

**Demand / sinks:**

- **Wagering medium** — players bet in FUN inside FunFair-powered casinos; higher casino activity raises baseline FUN demand.
- **Token burns** — as FUNToken, the project has run **burn** programs that permanently remove FUN from supply, intended to offset the very large fixed supply over time.
- **Staking / locking** — staking and operator-licensing locks remove FUN from liquid circulation and tie token demand to platform usage.

**Supply pressure:**

- A very large supply (~10.6B circulating of ~11B) with effectively unlimited max supply means burns and staking must continuously offset any new issuance and unlocks to support price. Unlike [[gamefi|GameFi]] reward tokens, the risk is less runaway gameplay emission and more a structurally large float that needs persistent burn demand to tighten.

### Competitive position

FunFair is a long-running incumbent in the on-chain gambling niche but competes today against a wave of newer [[gamefi]] / GambleFi platforms and crypto-casino brands, many running on faster, cheaper chains than Ethereum L1. Its advantages are longevity, brand recognition, and the Fate Channel IP; its disadvantages are a 2017-era stack, the regulatory baggage of online gambling, and an ~80% one-year price decline that reflects waning attention versus newer entrants.

## Risks

- **Regulatory exposure** — online gambling / GambleFi is one of the most heavily regulated and jurisdictionally fragmented sectors in crypto; licensing, KYC, and outright bans can directly impair the platform's addressable market.
- **GambleFi narrative dependence** — demand for FUN is tied to attention on on-chain gambling, a cyclical and reputationally sensitive theme.
- **Aging stack on Ethereum L1** — Fate Channels were innovative in 2017, but high mainnet fees and newer L2 / alt-L1 casino platforms erode the original cost advantage. See [[ethereum]] scaling trade-offs.
- **Supply / inflation** — FUN carries a very large supply (10.6B circulating of ~11B) with an effectively unlimited max supply per its listing, so token-burn and staking mechanics must continually offset dilution to support price.
- **Low liquidity and concentration** — despite a Binance listing, daily volume is modest for a token of this age; large positions are difficult to move without [[slippage]].
