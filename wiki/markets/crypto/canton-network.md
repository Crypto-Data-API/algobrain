# Canton Network

**Canton Network** is the institutional "privacy-enabled network of networks" built by Digital Asset (the Daml smart-contract company spun out of DRW in 2014), designed for regulated finance: tokenized [[treasuries|Treasuries]], repo, bonds, and fund shares with transaction-level privacy. Its native token **Canton Coin (CC)** began public trading in late 2025 and immediately entered the top 20 by market cap, making it the purest listed exposure to the Wall-Street-RWA-settlement narrative.

## Overview

Canton Coin (CC), the network's native utility token, was designed to reward real network usage over speculation. It aligns the success of the network with the participants who make it work, fairly rewarding app builders and app users, as well as those operating decentralized infrastructure. The result is a fundamentally different model where rewards flow to those creating value through real activity, not only miners or early investors.

Canton's differentiator vs public chains like [[ethereum]] is **sub-transaction privacy with selective disclosure** — banks can transact without broadcasting positions, while regulators can be given visibility. Applications run as sovereign chains (built in Daml) that interoperate atomically through the Global Synchronizer, which is operated by a decentralized set of super-validators.

### Institutional adoption (the core thesis)

- Participants/integrators include **Goldman Sachs, BNP Paribas, HSBC, Microsoft, Broadridge, Circle, DTCC, Nasdaq, Tradeweb**.
- Reported scale: over **$6 trillion in tokenized real-world assets** touching the network and up to **$280B in daily repo transactions** (Broadridge's distributed repo runs on Daml/Canton rails).
- **DTCC** has targeted 2026 for tokenizing a subset of DTC-custodied US [[treasuries]] on Canton.

## Trading Relevance

- **Narrative basket**: the flagship "institutional RWA / TradFi settlement" token, traded alongside [[chainlink]], ONDO, and tokenized-Treasury plays. When RWA headlines run (DTCC, BlackRock, bank pilots), CC is the high-beta vehicle.
- **Where it trades**: spot on Kraken, KuCoin, Crypto.com; **CC-PERP on [[hyperliquid]]**. Liquidity is thin relative to market cap (2026-06-20: ~$7.14M daily volume vs ~$5.95B cap, a ~0.12% volume/cap ratio) — wide spreads, large slippage; size accordingly.
- **Float/valuation caution**: circulating figures (~38.84B CC) come from utility-minting since 2024, heavily concentrated with super-validators and DRW/Cumberland-linked entities. The Tharimmune vehicle and any future DATs are both demand events and eventual supply overhangs. Volume-to-cap ratio is among the worst in the top 25 — the printed ~$5.95B market cap is soft and should not be read as deployable liquidity.
- **Catalysts**: DTCC Treasury tokenization go-live (2026), new super-validator/bank announcements, additional treasury vehicles, fee-burn growth (burn–mint equilibrium means usage directly reduces net issuance).
- **Valuation framing (qualitative)**: at ~$5.95B CC commands a top-20 valuation on minimal listed liquidity, so the price reflects an **option on institutional adoption** far more than current on-chain cash flows. Bulls point to the unmatched roster of TradFi participants (Goldman, BNP, HSBC, DTCC, Nasdaq, Tradeweb), reported multi-trillion-dollar RWA touchpoints, and a fee-burn model that directly ties usage to scarcity. Bears note the headline cap is a thin-float mirage: the freely tradeable supply is small, holdings are concentrated in DRW/Cumberland-linked super-validators and the Tharimmune treasury vehicle, and most "adoption" metrics are pilots and integrations rather than recurring tokenized volume. CC is best understood as the **highest-conviction, lowest-liquidity expression** of the RWA-settlement thesis — high narrative beta, severe execution risk on size.

CC has **no fixed max supply** but operates a **burn–mint equilibrium**: there was no pre-mine or pre-sale, all CC is minted as utility rewards (validators, super-validators, app builders), and network fees are USD-denominated, paid in CC, and burned. Because reported circulating equals total supply, **market cap = FDV (~1.00)** — there is no classic unlock-cliff overhang. The real supply caveat is **concentration, not dilution**: the float was created by utility-mining heavily weighted to super-validators and DRW/Cumberland-linked entities, so the freely tradeable portion is much smaller than the headline 38.84B, which (combined with ~$7M daily volume) is why the printed cap is soft.

## Trading Profile

### Venues & liquidity

CC is a **perp-first asset**: it trades as **CC-PERP on [[hyperliquid]]** (leverage up to ~40-50x), but is **not listed on Binance**, and its spot access is limited/offshore (Kraken, KuCoin, Crypto.com). With no deep, tier-1 spot venue, price discovery and directional flow concentrate almost entirely on the Hyperliquid perp. Book depth is thin relative to the printed top-20 market cap (the ~0.12% volume/cap ratio noted above), so spreads widen quickly and market orders slip; size positions to HL book depth, not to headline market cap, and prefer limit/passive entries. High available leverage against a thin book makes CC prone to fast liquidation-driven moves — a feature to exploit or defend against, not to lean into blindly.

### Applicable strategies

- [[funding-rate-harvest]] — as a PERP-FIRST, narrative-driven new listing, CC-PERP funding can run persistently rich or negative; harvesting the funding leg (delta-hedged where possible) is a core carry play.
- [[crowded-long-funding-fade]] — RWA-headline chases into a thin book crowd the long side and push funding to extremes; fade the crowd when funding spikes with no fresh catalyst.
- [[liquidation-cascade-fade]] — up to ~40-50x leverage on a thin CC book produces sharp liquidation flushes that overshoot; fade the wick once the cascade exhausts.
- [[oi-confirmed-trend]] — because nearly all flow is on the HL perp, open interest is a clean tape; rising OI confirming a directional move is a high-quality trend signal for CC.
- [[narrative-trading]] — CC is the flagship institutional-RWA/TradFi-settlement token, so trades are best framed around DTCC/bank-pilot and tokenization headlines.
- [[event-driven-trading]] — discrete catalysts (DTCC go-live, treasury-vehicle/DAT announcements, super-validator news) drive step changes; position around scheduled and headline events.

### Volatility & regime character

CC is a **thin-float infra / institutional-RWA token**, not a large-cap beta name. Its price is driven far more by RWA/TradFi-settlement headlines and thin-float reflexivity than by general crypto beta, so BTC/ETH correlation is loose and episodic — it can decouple hard on narrative catalysts. Realized volatility is high (a 3.3x range in its first trading quarter) and regime-sensitive to institutional-adoption newsflow rather than to broad market risk-on/risk-off.

### Risk flags

- **Venue concentration**: directional liquidity is effectively single-venue (Hyperliquid perp); no Binance listing and thin offshore spot mean limited hedging/exit routes.
- **Thin float / soft cap**: freely tradeable supply is concentrated among super-validators and DRW/Cumberland-linked entities, so the top-20 headline cap is not deployable liquidity — slippage risk on size is severe.
- **Narrative dependence**: valuation is an option on institutional adoption; RWA-headline reversals or delayed go-lives can gap the price.
- **Perp funding dislocations**: with flow crowded onto one perp, funding can dislocate sharply and liquidation cascades can overshoot in both directions.
- **Regulatory**: as a regulated-finance / RWA-settlement play, policy and tokenization-rule changes are a direct catalyst and risk.
