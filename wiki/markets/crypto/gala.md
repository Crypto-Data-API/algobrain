# GALA

**GALA** (ticker GALA) is the utility and governance token powering the **Gala Games** ecosystem and its purpose-built **GalaChain** Layer-1, an entertainment-focused blockchain network spanning gaming, music, and film. Originally an ERC-20 token on [[ethereum|Ethereum]], GALA also trades as a wrapped asset on [[solana|Solana]] and natively on GalaChain. It is one of the longest-running [[gamefi|GameFi]] / [[play-to-earn|play-to-earn]] brands in crypto and functions as a liquid proxy for sentiment toward blockchain [[gaming-tokens|gaming]].

## Technology & Protocol

Gala is a **vertically integrated entertainment platform** built on its own chain:

- **GalaChain (Layer-1)** — a purpose-built blockchain for entertainment assets. It uses a network of community-run **Founder's Nodes** that validate and earn GALA emissions, rather than a generic PoS validator set.
- **Game asset infrastructure** — in-game items, characters, and land are minted as [[nft|NFTs]] on GalaChain, with GALA as the settlement currency for in-ecosystem purchases.
- **Multi-vertical** — beyond games (its flagship titles), Gala operates **Gala Music** (artist/track NFTs and node rewards) and **Gala Film**, extending the same node-and-token model across entertainment media.
- **Multi-chain presence** — GALA exists as an ERC-20 on [[ethereum|Ethereum]], a wrapped asset on [[solana|Solana]], and natively on GalaChain, with bridges connecting the venues.

## Market Structure & Derivatives

**Centralized spot venues:** Binance (GALA/USDT), Kraken (GALA/USD), Bitget, KuCoin, and Crypto.com Exchange list GALA, with Binance historically the deepest book.

**On-chain spot:** Uniswap V3 on Ethereum (GALA/WETH) is the primary DEX market; wrapped GALA also trades on [[solana|Solana]] DEXs.

**Derivatives:** GALA has a perpetual market on [[hyperliquid|Hyperliquid]] (GALA-PERP) alongside perps on the major CEXs. As a high-beta gaming name in a bear regime, [[funding-rate|funding]] tends to skew negative or choppy during selloffs as traders lean short; open interest is thin relative to large-cap tokens, so OI can spike sharply on game-launch or partnership news (the day's +6.2% move is consistent with that catalyst-driven behavior).

## Use Case / Narrative / Category

GALA sits in the **GameFi / play-to-earn** category. The thesis is that Gala operates a vertically integrated entertainment platform: a roster of games, GalaChain infrastructure for in-game assets and [[nft|NFTs]], plus expansions into music (Gala Music) and film. Token holders can run nodes, govern, and use GALA as the settlement currency for in-ecosystem purchases. As a narrative, GALA is a bet that on-chain ownership of game assets eventually attracts mainstream players — a thesis that remains largely unproven at scale across the [[gaming-tokens|gaming-token]] sector.

## Valuation Framing (qualitative)

At a ~$131M cap with MC/FDV ~1.00, GALA is valued essentially on full dilution — there is little hidden unlock overhang, so the valuation question is purely **demand-side**: does ecosystem usage (game spend, node demand, NFT sales) absorb ongoing emissions? The ~99.7% drawdown from the 2021 ATH reflects the collapse of first-cycle play-to-earn economics, where token emissions vastly outpaced real player demand. GALA's survival as a top-250 brand across multiple cycles gives it franchise value, but a durable re-rating requires shipping games with genuine retention rather than speculative node yield. As a liquid GameFi proxy, it often trades on sector sentiment more than fundamentals.

## Notable History

- GALA launched in 2019 as the in-ecosystem currency for Gala Games.
- In 2022 a minting exploit and node-reward concerns prompted a token redesign, leading to the **2023 v2 migration**.
- GALA reached an all-time high near **$0.82 in November 2021** during the play-to-earn mania, and has since drawn down ~99.7% from that peak — a typical trajectory for first-cycle GameFi tokens whose token economics outpaced actual player demand.

## Trading Profile

### Venues & liquidity

GALA is a genuinely **two-venue market**, tradable on both **Binance** (GALA/USDT spot plus a USD-margined GALA perpetual) and **Hyperliquid** (GALA-PERP, up to ~40-50x leverage). Binance historically carries the deepest spot and perp books, while Hyperliquid provides an on-chain, transparent-orderbook perp with visible L2 depth and funding. The combination gives reasonable aggregate depth for a small-cap (~#265 by rank), but books thin out quickly beyond top-of-book, so **large orders should be sliced and executed with limit/TWAP-style entries** rather than crossing the spread. Dual-venue availability enables cross-venue relative-value work (spot-vs-perp, CEX-vs-HL funding) and lets sizing be split across venues to reduce impact, but it also means liquidity can fragment during stress — always check aggregate depth before sizing up.

### Volatility & regime character

GALA is a **high-beta GameFi / play-to-earn alt** with strong directional beta to BTC/ETH risk appetite — it typically amplifies majors on the way up and down, and underperforms in risk-off regimes given its discretionary-spend demand base. It exhibits **reflexive, catalyst-driven volatility**: relatively quiet drift punctuated by sharp OI-fueled spikes around game launches, node offerings, or partnership news. In bear regimes it tends to bleed with the broader alt complex; in risk-on rotations it can outrun majors as speculative capital chases the gaming narrative.

### Risk flags

- **Liquidity / venue concentration** — depth is concentrated in Binance and Hyperliquid; a disruption at either venue can widen spreads and gap prices sharply for a small-cap this size.
- **Emission / supply overhang** — ongoing GalaChain node-reward emissions add steady, demand-dependent sell pressure; supply mechanics are tied to network participation rather than a fixed schedule.
- **Narrative dependence** — valuation is almost entirely demand/sentiment-driven, so price is fragile to fading GameFi narrative and can round-trip catalyst spikes quickly.
- **Perp funding dislocations** — thin OI makes funding prone to violent swings during crowded positioning, raising the cost/risk of leveraged directional carry.
- **Security history** — a past minting exploit (pre-v2 migration) weighs on confidence and can amplify sentiment shocks.
