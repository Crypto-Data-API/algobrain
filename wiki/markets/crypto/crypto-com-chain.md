# Cronos

**Cronos** (CRO) is the exchange token of Crypto.com (launched 2018 as Crypto.com Chain) and the native asset of the EVM-compatible, [[cosmos|Cosmos-SDK]] Cronos blockchain. For traders its defining 2025–2026 story is political: **Trump Media & Technology Group and Crypto.com formed a multi-billion-dollar CRO treasury vehicle**, making CRO — alongside [[world-liberty-financial|WLFI]] and [[official-trump|TRUMP]] — a core member of the "Trump-affiliated crypto" basket.

## Overview

Cronos is an institutional-grade, high-performance, EVM-compatible Layer-1 blockchain built on the Cosmos SDK and integrated with the Inter-Blockchain Communication (IBC) protocol, designed as the backbone for open, compliant, and AI-accessible tokenized markets. It supports up to 60,000 transactions per second (TPS), 500ms block times, instant finality, and sub-$0.01 fees via Proof of Authority consensus.

CRO originated as the loyalty/utility token of the Crypto.com exchange and Visa card program; its value remains tightly coupled to Crypto.com's business. Note on supply: CRO's total supply (~98.7B vs the 30B that circulated after the famous 2021 burn of 70B CRO) reflects the controversial March 2025 governance vote that **re-issued the 70B previously burned tokens** to fund a strategic reserve — a decision widely criticized as undermining burn credibility, and the supply backdrop against which the Trump Media treasury deal was struck.

## Technology & Consensus

| Property | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Architecture** | [[cosmos\|Cosmos-SDK]] Layer 1, EVM-compatible (Cronos EVM chain) with [[cosmos\|IBC]] (Inter-Blockchain Communication) connectivity |
| **Consensus** | Proof of Authority / permissioned validator set (institutional-grade, compliance-oriented) rather than open permissionless PoW/PoS |
| **Performance** | Up to ~60,000 TPS, ~500ms block times, instant finality, sub-$0.01 fees |
| **EVM compatibility** | Solidity / [[ethereum]]-tooling compatible; ports Ethereum dApps with minimal changes |
| **ERC-20 representation** | CRO also exists as an ERC-20 token on [[ethereum]] (`0xa0b73e1ff0b80914ab6fe0444e65848c4c34450b`), bridging the Cronos and Ethereum ecosystems |
| **Smart contracts** | Full EVM smart-contract support; positioned for "open, compliant, AI-accessible tokenized markets" |

Cronos sits in a hybrid position: it is a [[cosmos]]-built, IBC-connected chain (so it can route assets to/from the broader Cosmos ecosystem) while remaining EVM-compatible (so it can host [[ethereum]]-style DeFi). Its Proof-of-Authority validator model trades decentralization for throughput and predictable finality — a deliberate "institutional rails" design choice that differentiates it from open L1s but also concentrates trust in a small validator set. The dual on-chain footprint (native Cronos + ERC-20 on [[ethereum]]) is similar in spirit to how exchange tokens like [[bnb]] straddle their own chain and Ethereum representations.

## 2025–2026 Developments

- **Trump Media partnership (August 2025)** — Crypto.com and Trump Media & Technology Group (DJT) announced a strategic partnership including a CRO treasury company and CRO integration plans for Truth Social / Truth+ rewards.
- **$105M CRO purchase closed (5 September 2025)** — Trump Media acquired **684.4M CRO (~$0.153/token)** in a 50% cash / 50% stock exchange with Crypto.com.
- **Trump Media Group CRO Strategy, Inc.** — Yorkville Acquisition Corp (Nasdaq: **MCGA**) filed an S-4 to create a publicly traded CRO treasury targeting **>$6B of CRO**, expected to open with **6.3B CRO, $200M cash, $220M in warrants and a $5B equity line of credit**; Steve Gutterman named CEO. Business combination expected to close Q1 2026, with the vehicle running a Cronos validator targeting ~**6% staking APY** and custody at Crypto.com Custody.
- **Conflict-of-interest scrutiny** — the deal drew sustained media and political scrutiny (PBS and others) as a direct financial link between the sitting US president's family business and a crypto exchange token.

CRO's supply history is the single most important — and most contentious — fact about the token:

- **2021 burn:** Crypto.com burned **70B CRO** (then ~70% of supply), reducing circulating/total supply to ~30B and marketing CRO as a credibly scarce asset.
- **March 2025 re-issuance:** a governance vote **re-created the 70B previously burned tokens** to fund a strategic reserve, pushing total supply back toward ~98–99B. This was widely criticized as undermining the credibility of the original burn and of CRO's supply policy generally.
- **Overhang today:** with ~46B circulating against ~98.7B total and a 100B max, a large reserve/non-circulating tranche sits above the market — a structural MC/FDV discount (~0.46) and a reminder that CRO supply is governance-mutable rather than fixed.

This re-issued-supply backdrop is the context against which the [[official-trump|Trump]] Media CRO treasury deal was struck.

CRO's fundamental value driver remains demand from the **Crypto.com business** — exchange volume, card adoption, and the staking/loyalty flywheel — more than independent on-chain activity. That tight coupling is both its moat (a large CEX behind it) and its key dependency (see [[#Risks]]).

## Trading Playbook

- **Primary lens — political news flow.** CRO is a core member of the **Trump-affiliated basket** with [[official-trump|TRUMP]] and [[world-liberty-financial|WLFI]]. Since the Trump Media partnership, CRO trades on US political headlines more than on crypto-native fundamentals; a Trump-news downdraft tends to hit CRO, WLFI and TRUMP together (high intra-basket correlation).
- **CEX-token basket.** Also trades in the exchange-token cohort vs [[bnb|BNB]] and OKB. Relative-value here is governed by listing breadth, supply credibility, and parent-exchange health.
- **Catalysts to watch:**
  - MCGA/CRO-treasury business-combination **closing** (Q1 2026) and subsequent CRO purchases toward the **>$6B** target
  - **Truth Social / Truth+ CRO rewards** integration
  - Crypto.com product/licensing news (card, US licensing, listings)
  - Staking-yield and validator announcements
- **Cross-asset / NAV trades.** DJT and MCGA equity give a way to express a CRO view through stock; watch for treasury premium/discount-to-NAV dislocations (the [[microstrategy]] template).
- **Liquidity discipline.** Thin book (~$7.49M/day vs ~$2.69B cap) — size positions for slippage and expect violent two-way moves around catalysts.
- **Regime context (2026-06-20):** Fear & Greed = 22 (extreme fear), Established Bear Market — politically-driven names like CRO can decouple from the broad tape on idiosyncratic headlines but remain beta-down in risk-off.

## Risks

- **Supply-policy reversal / trust discount.** The March 2025 re-issuance of the 70B previously burned CRO proved that supply is governance-mutable. Holders cannot treat any "burn" or cap as final, which imposes a structural trust discount on valuation (MC/FDV ~0.46).
- **Concentrated, politically-exposed demand.** A large share of CRO's bid is tied to the [[official-trump|Trump]]-affiliated narrative. Political/legal/news shocks can hit CRO, [[world-liberty-financial|WLFI]] and [[official-trump|TRUMP]] simultaneously, with little diversification within the basket.
- **Conflict-of-interest / regulatory scrutiny.** The Trump Media–Crypto.com link drew sustained scrutiny (PBS and others) as a direct financial tie between the sitting president's family business and a crypto exchange token — a regulatory/headline overhang.
- **Thin liquidity.** ~$7.49M daily volume vs ~$2.69B cap (2026-06-20) means high slippage and gap risk; absence from [[binance]]/[[coinbase]] caps natural distribution.
- **Business dependency.** CRO's fundamental value tracks the health of the Crypto.com exchange/card business; a downturn or licensing setback there flows straight through to the token.
- **Centralization.** Proof-of-Authority/permissioned validator set concentrates trust relative to open L1s.
