# B3 (Base)

**B3** ("Become," tagline **"Based Gaming"**) is a high-performance **gaming-focused rollup** built on [[base|Base]] — itself an [[ethereum|Ethereum]] [[layer-2]] — making B3 effectively a **layer-3 / gaming settlement layer** in the Ethereum/Base stack. It is designed to power [[gamefi|onchain gaming]] through its **Open Gaming** ecosystem, lowering transaction costs, simplifying player onboarding, and letting games share liquidity, incentives, and audiences.

## Overview

B3 is a gaming-dedicated rollup that settles to [[base|Base]], placing it a layer above Base in the modular Ethereum stack (a "layer-3" relative to Ethereum L1). It is built to address the core pain points of blockchain gaming — high and unpredictable fees, clunky wallet onboarding, and fragmentation across chains — by giving games a low-cost, high-throughput execution environment plus shared infrastructure. Its **Open Gaming** ecosystem is the organizing idea: a network of games that pool liquidity, players, and incentives rather than competing in isolation.

### Architecture and positioning

By inheriting security from Base (and ultimately Ethereum) while running its own optimized execution layer, B3 aims to deliver the cheap, fast transactions that real-time games need without sacrificing the composability and settlement guarantees of the [[layer-2|L2]]/L1 below it. This "appchain for gaming on top of Base" design is part of a broader 2024-2026 wave of gaming-specific rollups.

### The L3 stack — how settlement and DA work

B3's place in the modular stack is the key technical fact, and it is a layered chain of dependencies:

- **Ethereum L1** — the ultimate settlement and security root.
- **[[base|Base]] (L2)** — an [[optimistic-rollup|optimistic rollup]] built on the OP Stack that settles to Ethereum. Base is where B3 anchors.
- **B3 (L3)** — a rollup that settles to *Base* rather than directly to Ethereum, which is why it is described as a **layer-3** in the OP Stack / "superchain" sense. Built on the same OP-Stack tooling, B3 batches game transactions, posts them to Base, and lets Base (then Ethereum) provide the dispute/settlement backstop.

The practical trade-off of an L3: transactions are **even cheaper** than on Base (because B3 amortizes its own batches and only pays Base's already-low fees), and B3 can tune throughput for gaming. The cost is **more layers of dependency** — B3's liveness and finality depend on Base's sequencer and Base depends on Ethereum. Like virtually all current OP-Stack rollups, B3 in practice relies on a **centralized sequencer** for ordering, and optimistic rollups carry a **fraud-proof challenge window** that introduces withdrawal latency back down the stack. None of this is unique to B3 — it is the standard OP-Stack L3 posture — but it means "inherits Ethereum security" comes with sequencer-trust and challenge-window caveats.

### The $B3 token

The **$B3** token is the backbone of the ecosystem and is intended to enable seamless interaction across multiple games and chains, with roles including:

- **Governance** — participation in ecosystem decisions.
- **Staking** — securing or participating in the network and ecosystem programs.
- **In-game transactions** — a shared unit of value across Open Gaming titles.

Supply is large (100B total) and effectively fully circulating per the tokenomics table, so the very low unit price reflects the large supply base rather than any single fundamental.

### Value accrual & governance

B3's MC/FDV ≈ 1.00 is a genuinely distinguishing feature in this cohort: with supply effectively fully circulating, B3 carries **little to no future-unlock overhang** — unusual among the low-float small-caps elsewhere in this section (Mango, B², SOON all sit at MC/FDV well below 0.35). The flip side is that there is no "locked supply released to fund growth" lever, and a 100B fully-diluted base means new demand must spread across the entire supply to move price.

Value accrual depends on **gameplay-driven transaction demand** (gas/fees on Open Gaming titles), **staking/ecosystem participation**, and **governance** over the gaming network. The hard part for any gaming-chain token is the link between *players* and *token demand*: players want fun games, not to hold a gas token, so B3 must engineer demand through in-game economies, fee capture, and incentives without that demand being purely mercenary. Network usage does not automatically become $B3 demand — value capture is the open question.

## Comparison vs competitor chains

B3 competes in the **onchain-gaming L2/L3** category — chains purpose-built for games rather than general DeFi.

| Chain | Position in stack | Settlement root | Differentiator vs B3 |
|---|---|---|---|
| **B3 (Base)** | L3 on [[base\|Base]] | Base → [[ethereum\|Ethereum]] | "Open Gaming" shared-liquidity ecosystem; Base-native; ~fully circulating supply |
| **[[immutable\|Immutable (IMX)]]** | Ethereum L2 (zkEVM) | Ethereum | Largest established web3-gaming platform; zk validity proofs; deep studio relationships |
| **Ronin (RON)** | Gaming sidechain | Own validators (Axie origin) | Proven hit-game economy (Axie/Pixels); but sidechain trust + 2022 bridge hack history |
| **[[base\|Base]]** | Ethereum L2 | Ethereum | The chain B3 sits on; general-purpose, far larger, not gaming-specific |

The strategic question for B3 is whether a *dedicated gaming L3* beats a *gaming-focused L2* (Immutable, Ronin) or simply building directly on a cheap general L2 (Base itself). B3's bet is that pooled liquidity/players across an "Open Gaming" network is worth the extra layer; the risk is that gaming chains live or die on hit titles, which no architecture guarantees.

## Narrative, category & catalysts

B3 is an **onchain-gaming / Base-ecosystem** narrative play. It rides two themes: the **Base/Coinbase "superchain"** momentum (Base is one of the most active Ethereum L2s, and Base-native tokens get attention when Coinbase pushes its ecosystem) and the recurring **GameFi** narrative. The bull thesis is that cheap, dedicated infrastructure plus pooled liquidity finally produces sticky onchain games; the bear thesis is that GameFi has consistently failed to retain players once token incentives fade.

Plausible **catalysts** (positive): a breakout hit game on B3, Coinbase/Base ecosystem promotion, new studio partnerships, demonstrable daily active users. **Negative catalysts:** GameFi narrative fatigue, failure of flagship titles to retain players, Base-stack technical issues cascading down to L3.

## Risks

- **GameFi adoption risk:** B3's thesis depends on onchain games attracting and retaining real players. The [[gamefi|GameFi]] sector has a poor track record of sustained engagement; without hit titles, ecosystem activity (and token demand) can stall.
- **Severe drawdown / high volatility:** B3 trades far below its all-time high and is a micro-cap (~$27M) with sharp price swings; it is highly sensitive to broad crypto sentiment.
- **Dependence on Base/Ethereum stack:** As a layer-3 on [[base|Base]], B3 inherits the technical and economic dependencies of the chains beneath it.
- **Competition:** Multiple gaming rollups and appchains compete for the same developers and players; differentiation is not guaranteed.
- **Token value-capture uncertainty:** Network usage does not automatically translate into $B3 demand; utility and incentive design must hold up over time.
