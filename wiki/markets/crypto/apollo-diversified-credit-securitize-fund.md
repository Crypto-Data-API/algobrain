# Apollo Diversified Credit Securitize Fund

**Apollo Diversified Credit Securitize Fund** (ticker **ACRED**) is a tokenized [[real-world-assets|real-world-asset]] **feeder fund** issued by **[[securitize|Securitize]]** that provides on-chain exposure to **Apollo's Diversified Credit Fund** — a multi-asset private and public credit strategy run by **Apollo Global Management**, one of the largest alternative-credit managers in the world. Each token is a share in the feeder fund, which in turn invests in the underlying Apollo credit fund; token value **accrues with the feeder's NAV** (price-accretion, non-rebasing) rather than holding a fixed $1.00 par. It is issued natively on [[ethereum|Ethereum]] and bridged across several chains (Solana, Avalanche, Polygon, Aptos, Ink). ACRED is a landmark in bringing institutional private credit on-chain.

## How It Works (Architecture Deep Dive)

ACRED is a **tokenized feeder fund**: the on-chain token is a share in a Securitize-administered feeder that allocates capital into Apollo's Diversified Credit Fund (the "underlying fund").

- **Fund structure & manager:** The feeder is administered/tokenized by **Securitize**; the underlying portfolio is managed by **Apollo Global Management**. This two-layer structure (on-chain feeder → off-chain master fund) is how regulated private-credit exposure is delivered to token holders.
- **Underlying assets:** A diversified credit portfolio spanning **corporate direct lending, asset-backed lending, performing credit, dislocated credit, and structured credit (including CLOs and mortgage-backed securities)**, managed by Apollo. Income is the primary return driver, with low stated volatility and low correlation to broad markets.
- **Custody / administration:** Securitize provides the tokenization, transfer-agent, and primary-market layer; the underlying assets sit within Apollo's regulated fund structure with its own custodians and administrators.
- **Eligible-investor gating:** A **permissioned, qualified/accredited-investor product** offered under securities-law exemptions (Reg D for U.S. accredited/qualified purchasers, Reg S for non-U.S.). Tokens are transferable only among Securitize-whitelisted, KYC/AML-screened holders. Not a freely circulating retail token.
- **Mint / redeem flow & settlement:** Subscriptions and redemptions occur through Securitize's primary market against the feeder-fund NAV, restricted to eligible investors and governed by the offering documents' liquidity terms (private-credit redemptions are typically periodic, not continuous).
- **Yield source & distribution:** Yield is generated by the underlying credit portfolio (loan interest, spread income) and is reflected through **NAV price-accretion** — the token price climbs as income accrues, rather than minting new tokens.
- **Oracle / NAV feed:** Price tracks fund NAV, set administratively from the underlying credit portfolio's daily valuation, not from market order flow. ACRED has been notable for daily on-chain NAV transparency.
- **Transfer restrictions / whitelist:** Transfers are confined to the permissioned holder set; the multi-chain footprint (Ethereum native, bridged to Solana, Avalanche, Polygon, Aptos, Ink) still respects the whitelist.

## How & Where It Trades / Is Used

- **Primary issuance:** Subscriptions/redemptions occur through Securitize's primary market against the feeder-fund NAV, restricted to eligible investors.
- **Eligibility:** KYC/AML-cleared accredited/qualified investors only (Reg D / Reg S); not open retail.
- **Secondary trading:** Negligible on public venues — CoinGecko reported $0 24h volume. Transfers are confined to the permissioned holder set; this is a **hold-to-NAV** instrument, not an actively traded token.
- **DeFi composability:** ACRED has been used as **collateral in permissioned DeFi** (e.g., borrowing against tokenized credit positions), a notable proof-of-concept for institutional RWA composability — but availability is gated to whitelisted venues and holders.
- **Tracking:** Price tracks fund NAV, set administratively from the underlying credit portfolio's valuation, not market order flow.
- **Hours:** On-chain transfer among permitted holders is 24/7; pricing and redemptions follow the fund's schedule.

## Risks

- **Credit risk (dominant):** This is exposure to private and structured credit. Defaults, downgrades, or stress in the underlying loans/CLOs reduce NAV — the headline risk, unlike Treasury tokens.
- **Issuer / administration risk:** Holders rely on both Apollo (asset management) and Securitize (tokenization, transfer agency, redemption processing).
- **Redemption-gating / liquidity risk:** With effectively no secondary market and gated, periodic redemptions, exit can be slow — especially in credit-market stress when redemptions are most desired.
- **NAV-gap / valuation risk:** NAV depends on the marks of often-illiquid private credit; valuations can lag real conditions, so the on-chain price may overstate realizable value during stress.
- **Regulatory / securities-law risk:** Tokenized private-credit funds operate under evolving securities rules; eligibility and structure can change.
- **Smart-contract / bridge risk:** Multi-chain deployment adds contract and bridge attack surface.

## Trading / Usage Playbook

- **Who can hold it:** KYC/AML-cleared accredited/qualified investors whitelisted through Securitize (Reg D / Reg S). Not for open retail.
- **Primary use case:** On-chain access to **diversified private-credit yield** for institutions and qualified investors seeking income with low crypto correlation.
- **Collateral use:** Can be used as collateral in permissioned DeFi venues that whitelist it; treat liquidity as limited and redemption-gated.
- **Exit discipline:** Plan for periodic, NAV-based redemption windows; do not expect to exit quickly via secondary venues, particularly in credit stress.

## Overview

This feeder fund invests in the Apollo Diversified Credit Fund ("Underlying Fund") which seeks to generate a return comprised of both current income and capital appreciation, emphasizing current income with low volatility and low correlation to the broader markets.

✓ Seasoned Asset Manager: Apollo draws on 30+ years of experience, aiming to achieve attractive returns across the risk spectrum through proprietary origination, credit strategies, and a flexible approach to borrower needs.(1)

✓ Historical Track Record of Outperformance: A diversified, global credit strategy with potential for enhanced income and attractive risk-adjusted returns across various market cycles.

✓ 0% Performance Fee

✓ $0 Redemptions

✓ Pricing Transparency: Daily pricing transparency and daily liquidity.(2)

The Underlying Fund takes a multi-asset private and public credit approach centered around five key pillars:

✓ Corporate Direct Lending: Targets large scale corporate originations and sponsor-backed issuers of first lien, senior secured and unitranche loans, utilizing Apollo’s proprietary sourcing channel.

✓ Asset-Backed Lending: Focuses on agile deployment of capital into origination and proprietary sourcing channels across a broad mandate of asset-backed investments, with a focus on investments collateralized by tangible investments.

✓ Performing Credit: Primarily pursues liquid, performing senior secured corporate credits to generate total return.

✓ Dislocated Credit: Seeks to use contingent capital to tactically pursue “dislocated” credit opportunities such as stressed, performing assets that sell-off due to technical and/or non-fundamental reasons.

✓ Structured Credit: Focuses on structured credit opportunities across diverse asset types, vintages, maturities, jurisdictions, and capital structure priorities (for example, CLOs, residential, and commercial mortgage backed securities among others).9
