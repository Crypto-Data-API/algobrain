# Global Dollar

**Global Dollar** (USDG) is a regulated, fiat-backed USD [[stablecoin]] issued by [[paxos|Paxos]] (Singapore MAS-supervised; EU via a Finnish EMI, MiCA-compliant), native on [[solana|Solana]] and also on [[ethereum|Ethereum]], launched November 2024 as the settlement asset of the **Global Dollar Network (GDN)** — a consortium founded by Paxos, Robinhood, Kraken, Galaxy Digital, Anchorage Digital, Bullish and Nuvei that **shares reserve yield with distribution partners**. That yield-sharing model is its differentiator vs [[usdt|USDT]]/[[usdc|USDC]], and it has scaled fast: from ~$1B supply (late 2025) to **~$2.81B and 130+ partners by mid-2026** (rank #33).

## Overview

Global Dollar (USDG) is a single-currency stablecoin pegged to the US dollar, fully redeemable from Paxos one-to-one. It is issued by Paxos Digital Singapore (a Major Payments Institution supervised by the Monetary Authority of Singapore) and, in the EU, by Paxos Issuance Europe Oy (an Electronic Money Institution regulated by the Finnish FIN-FSA), making USDG fully compliant with the EU's Markets in Crypto Assets Regulation (MiCA).

Issuance adheres to strict standards: value stability, capital adequacy, par redemption, monthly independent attestations of reserves, annual audits, and bankruptcy-remoteness via segregated trust accounts held for USDG holders.

**The economic model is the point**: GDN partners that drive USDG adoption and liquidity earn a share of the returns generated by USDG reserves (T-bill yield). Since issuers themselves cannot pass yield to end-holders under the GENIUS Act, routing reserve economics to *distributors* (exchanges, brokers, wallets) is the competitive wedge against Tether/Circle, who historically keep the float income.

### 2025–2026 developments

- **2024-11** — Paxos and partners launch USDG and the Global Dollar Network; Robinhood and Kraken among founders.
- **2025** — EU launch under MiCA via Paxos Issuance Europe; network expands past 100 partners (incl. Mastercard, DBS, OKX, Worldpay).
- **2025-07-18** — **GENIUS Act** signed into US law: federal framework requiring 100% liquid reserve backing and monthly public reserve disclosures. Regulated incumbents like Paxos are seen as primary beneficiaries; OCC/Treasury rulemaking continued into 2026.
- **Late 2025** — USDG crosses **$1B market cap**, GDN >100 partners (PR, Dec 2025).
- **2026-05** — ~**$2.75B supply, 130+ partners** — one of the fastest-growing top-tier stablecoins, though still ~2% the size of USDT.
- **2026-06-20** — **$2.81B supply, rank #33** by market cap (CoinGecko snapshot).

## Mechanism & Backing

| Dimension | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Reserves** | 100% high-quality liquid assets — cash and short-dated [[treasury-bills|US Treasury bills]]/repo — held 1:1 against USDG outstanding |
| **Attestation** | Monthly independent reserve attestations + annual audits, per [[paxos|Paxos]] standards and GENIUS Act disclosure requirements |
| **Peg mechanism** | Par mint/redeem with Paxos; arbitrage against 1:1 redemption keeps the peg, the same mechanism as [[usdc|USDC]]/USDT |
| **Custody** | Reserves in bankruptcy-remote segregated trust accounts held *for USDG holders*, separated from Paxos operating funds |
| **Yield routing** | Reserve yield (T-bill income) is **shared with GDN distribution partners** (exchanges, brokers, wallets), not the issuer's float income and — under the [[genius-act|GENIUS Act]] — **not** paid to end-holders. This distributor yield-share is the competitive wedge vs Tether/Circle, who keep the float income |
| **Regulatory wrapper** | Dual-issued: Paxos Digital Singapore (MAS Major Payments Institution) and Paxos Issuance Europe Oy (FIN-FSA EMI, [[mica|MiCA]]-compliant); GENIUS Act-aligned in the US |

> **De-peg risk profile:** USDG is a regulated, fully-reserved, attested fiat stablecoin — its de-peg risk is *low* and reserve-asset-driven (e.g. a T-bill custodian or banking failure) rather than the credibility-shock pattern that hit [[first-digital-usd|FDUSD]] in April 2025. The historical $1.65/$0.91 wicks are launch-illiquidity artifacts, not redemption-cleared depegs.

## Trading Relevance

- **Not a directional trade** — a $1 peg asset. Relevance is infrastructure and flow:
- **Stablecoin wars tracker**: USDG supply growth is the cleanest read on whether the *yield-sharing distribution* model takes share from USDT/USDC post-GENIUS Act. Relevant to Circle (CRCL) and Coinbase equities, and to exchange tokens of GDN members.
- **Venue liquidity**: spot pairs on Kraken (USDG/USD) and KuCoin; integrated in Robinhood's crypto stack; DEX liquidity on Orca ([[solana]]) and Uniswap v3 (Ethereum). Watch for USDG quote-pairs proliferating on partner venues — each adds captive float.
- **Peg/arb**: standard stablecoin basis trades (USDG/USDT, USDG/USDC) are tight; historical CoinGecko "ATH $1.65 / ATL $0.91" prints are thin-liquidity artifacts from launch, not real depegs.
- **Catalysts**: new GDN members (banks, payment networks), GENIUS Act rulemaking milestones, MAS/MiCA expansions, and partner yield-share disclosures.

## Risks

- **Issuer risk**: USDG depends on [[paxos|Paxos]]'s solvency and operational integrity across two regulated entities (Singapore + Finland). Paxos's regulated, attested track record makes this low but non-zero.
- **Custodial / reserve-asset risk**: reserves sit in bank deposits and short-dated [[treasury-bills|T-bills]] with third-party custodians; a banking or custodian failure is the primary de-peg vector (cf. [[usdc|USDC]]/SVB March 2023).
- **Regulatory risk**: subject to MAS, FIN-FSA/[[mica|MiCA]], and [[genius-act|GENIUS Act]] regimes simultaneously; rulemaking shifts (especially around the legality of distributor yield-sharing) could force model changes.
- **De-peg risk**: low and reserve-driven, not credibility-driven — distinct from the [[first-digital-usd|FDUSD]] April-2025 allegation-driven episode. Historical $1.65/$0.91 prints are launch-illiquidity wicks.
- **Concentration / counterparty risk**: USDG float is concentrated in GDN partner venues; loss of a major partner ([[kraken]], [[robinhood]], OKX) would shrink captive float quickly, as the [[stablecoin-supply|FDUSD/Binance]] precedent shows.
- **Competitive risk**: still ~2% of USDT's size; the yield-sharing edge only holds if GENIUS Act keeps issuers from paying holders directly.
