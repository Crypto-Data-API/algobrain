# Delysium

**Delysium** (token: **AGI**) is an AI-agent gaming and "AI society" project that aims to build a blockchain-based virtual world populated by autonomous **AI Virtual Beings** alongside human users. Originally launched as a AAA multiplayer game, it pivoted toward AI-agent infrastructure — frameworks like its "Lucy" AI-agent operating system and the **YKILY** (You Know I Love You) network for creating, owning, and transacting with AI agents on-chain. It is a multi-chain ([[ethereum]], BNB Chain, [[solana]]) AI-agent [[altcoins|altcoin]] and ranks **#987** by market capitalization.

## Overview

Delysium describes its mission as building an "AI-powered virtual society" — a long-horizon vision of a blockchain world where humans coexist with large numbers of autonomous **AI Virtual Beings**. The project began life as a Web3 AAA game and has since repositioned around **AI-agent infrastructure**, riding the 2024-2025 "AI agents" narrative.

### Mechanism / architecture

- **Lucy — AI-agent OS** — Delysium's framework for spawning and operating AI agents that can plan, use tools, and act on a user's behalf across applications.
- **YKILY network** — an on-chain layer for creating, owning (as [[nft|NFTs]]), and transacting between AI agents, intended as the economic substrate of the "AI society."
- **Multi-chain deployment** — the AGI token exists across [[ethereum]], BNB Chain, and [[solana]], broadening reach but also fragmenting liquidity.

### Token role

The **AGI** token is the native utility and value-capture asset of the ecosystem: it is used to pay for AI-agent services and compute, to mint/operate agents, and for governance and ecosystem incentives. As with other [[gamefi]] and AI-agent tokens, AGI has no independent cash-flow claim — its value tracks adoption of the agent platform and the strength of the AI-agent narrative.

### How the economy works: sinks vs faucets

- **Sinks (demand)** — paying for AI-agent compute and services, minting and operating agents (some represented as [[nft|NFTs]] in the YKILY network), governance participation, and any staking that locks AGI. The thesis is that an "AI society" of many agents generates recurring transactional demand for the token.
- **Faucets (supply)** — ecosystem incentives and the unlock schedule toward the 3B max supply; with ~2.46B of 3B circulating (MC/FDV ≈ 0.82), there is still ~18% unlock overhang to absorb over time.

The economic risk is the standard AI/GameFi one: **agent-compute demand must materially exceed incentive emissions and unlocks** for the token to hold value. In a bear tape with the AI-agent narrative cooled, that balance is hard to achieve, which is reflected in AGI's ~98% drawdown from its 2024 ATH.

### Competitive position & notes

Delysium competes in the fast-moving on-chain AI-agent field against projects such as Virtuals Protocol, Fetch.ai/ASI, and a long tail of agent-launchpad tokens. Its assets are an established brand from its gaming origins and a multi-chain footprint; its challenges are heavy narrative dependence, a crowded category, and association in some classifications with the defunct **Alameda Research** portfolio — a legacy backer tag rather than an endorsement. The token sits ~98% below its 2024 ATH, reflecting how sharply AI/gaming sentiment has cycled.

## Risks

- **AI-agent narrative dependence** — AGI's price is closely tied to the volatile on-chain "AI agents" hype cycle; the ~98% drawdown from its 2024 ATH shows the downside when sentiment fades.
- **Crowded, fast-moving category** — many well-funded competitors are chasing the same agent-economy thesis, raising execution and differentiation risk.
- **Pivot / delivery & player-churn risk** — the move from AAA game to AI-agent OS is a significant repositioning; roadmap delivery is largely unverified, and the original game's player base does not automatically transfer to an agent platform.
- **Token inflation / dilution** — ~2.46B of 3B AGI circulates (MC/FDV ≈ 0.82), leaving ~18% unlock overhang plus incentive emissions to absorb; if agent-compute demand lags, faucets outrun sinks.
- **Fragmented liquidity / low float** — multi-chain deployment splits depth; at ~$14M cap positions are hard to trade at size. See [[liquidity]] and [[slippage]].
- **Legacy backer tag** — historic association with the Alameda Research portfolio is reputational baggage rather than active support.
