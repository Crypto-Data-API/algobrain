# XRP

**XRP** is the native token of the **[[xrp-ledger|XRP Ledger]] (XRPL)**, a Layer 1 blockchain designed for fast, low-cost cross-border payments and currency exchange. Created in 2012, XRP is closely associated with **[[ripple|Ripple Labs]]**, the company that developed it and promotes its adoption among financial institutions. XRP's multi-year [[sec|SEC]] lawsuit and its resolution made it one of the most legally consequential tokens in crypto history, and its spot-ETF approval in November 2025 marked a major milestone for altcoin institutional adoption.

## The SEC Lawsuit

The defining legal battle for XRP and a landmark case for crypto regulation:

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| December 2020 | SEC sues Ripple Labs, CEO Brad Garlinghouse, and co-founder Chris Larsen, alleging XRP is an unregistered security |
| 2021-2022 | Discovery phase; Ripple argues SEC gave no fair notice; internal SEC documents debated |
| July 2023 | **Judge Torres rules**: XRP sold on exchanges to retail buyers is **not a security**; institutional direct sales were securities |
| October 2023 | SEC drops charges against Garlinghouse and Larsen individually |
| 2024 | Case moves toward settlement; partial victory widely celebrated as favorable precedent |
| 2024-2025 | Regulatory clarity emboldens institutional interest, paving the way for ETF approval |

**Trading impact**: The lawsuit created persistent uncertainty that suppressed XRP relative to other majors for 3+ years. Each favorable ruling triggered outsized rallies (20-50% in days), while negative developments caused sharp selloffs. XRP became the primary proxy for crypto regulatory sentiment.

## Ripple Labs and the Ecosystem

Ripple Labs (San Francisco) is the primary corporate entity behind XRP adoption. Key products and relationships:

- **RippleNet / On-Demand Liquidity (ODL)**: Uses XRP as a bridge asset for cross-border payments. Financial institutions convert source currency → XRP → destination currency, settling in seconds instead of days via correspondent banking
- **Ripple Escrow**: Ripple holds ~55 billion XRP in cryptographic escrow, releasing up to 1 billion XRP per month. Unused portions are returned to escrow. These monthly releases are predictable supply events that traders monitor
- **[[ripple-usd|RLUSD]]**: Ripple's USD-pegged stablecoin launched via subsidiary Standard Custody & Trust Company. Built on both XRPL and Ethereum
- **Partnerships**: Ripple has partnered with 300+ financial institutions globally including Santander, SBI Holdings, and various remittance corridors in Asia and the Middle East

| **Deflationary mechanism** | Small amount of XRP burned per transaction (anti-spam) |
| **Inflation** | None — supply can only decrease |
| **Ripple escrow** | ~37B XRP locked; up to 1B released monthly |

**Dilution profile:** XRP was 100% pre-mined at genesis, so there is no protocol issuance and the only "new" supply entering markets is the monthly escrow release. The ~38B gap between circulating (~62B) and max (100B) supply is the persistent overhang concern — but it is a *known, scheduled* float expansion, not open-ended inflation. Ripple has historically returned the majority of each 1B monthly release back to escrow, so net float growth has been far below the headline release rate, and the schedule is transparent and predictable. This makes XRP's MC/FDV ratio (~0.62) far healthier than many newer tokens (e.g. [[optimism|OP]] at ~0.50) where multi-year vesting cliffs loom.

## Valuation Framing

XRP resists conventional crypto valuation models because it is neither a productive smart-contract platform (no staking yield or fee-burn accruing to holders the way [[ethereum|ETH]] does post-EIP-1559) nor a pure store-of-value like [[bitcoin]]. Three qualitative lenses traders use:

- **Payments-utility thesis** — value tied to XRP's use as a bridge asset in RippleNet/ODL corridors. The bull case is that institutional payment volume creates real, recurring demand; the bear case is that most ODL flows are transient (XRP is bought and sold within seconds), so they generate turnover but little persistent holding demand.
- **Regulatory-clarity premium** — XRP is the only major altcoin with both a favorable court ruling *and* a US spot ETF, giving it a "compliance moat" over peers still in legal limbo. Much of the 2023-2025 re-rating priced in this clarity.
- **Network/escrow supply** — with ~38B XRP still in escrow, bears argue the effective float (and thus dilution risk) is understated; bulls counter that the predictable, mostly-returned release schedule is already discounted.

Net: XRP trades as a hybrid of a regulatory-sentiment proxy and a payments-infrastructure bet, with its large-cap status and ETF access providing a structural floor that smaller peers lack.

## Overview

XRP is a decentralized digital asset and the native token of the XRP Ledger, a Layer 1 blockchain designed for high-performance global payments. Its primary value proposition is offering a fast, cost-effective alternative to traditional banking systems by enabling near-instant cross-border settlements that finalize in three to five seconds. By serving as a neutral bridge asset between different fiat currencies, it helps financial institutions lower liquidity costs and eliminates the need for pre-funded accounts.

The network is unique because it utilizes a federated consensus protocol instead of energy-intensive mining or staking. This system achieves deterministic finality through a Unique Node List of trusted validators who must reach an eighty percent agreement threshold to permanently record transactions. This infrastructure supports a sustained throughput of 1,500 transactions per second and includes a built-in decentralized exchange that allows for the automated trading of any tokenized asset without intermediaries.

The project traces its origins back to 2004 with Ryan Fugger before being co-founded in its modern form by Jed McCaleb and Chris Larsen. The company, now known as Ripple, works with major financial institutions as partners and investors to build global payment solutions. To protect the network from spam, a small amount of XRP is burned during every transaction, and the token is further utilized by institutions as collateral to access traditional markets and manage digital asset infrastructure.

Institutional adoption of the asset reached a major milestone in November 2025 when the SEC approved the first wave of spot XRP ETFs. These products, offered by major issuers including Bitwise, Grayscale, 21Shares, Canary Capital, and Franklin Templeton, are now listed on prominent exchanges like the NYSE Arca, Nasdaq, and Cboe BZX.
