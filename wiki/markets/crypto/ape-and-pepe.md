# Ape and Pepe

**Ape and Pepe** (APEPE) is a frog-themed memecoin (an **ERC-20-class fungible token**, not an NFT) on [[polygon]] that markets itself as a community coin for small investors, combining the APE and PEPE memes. Despite a headline market cap in the ~$250–280M range it has **no major centralized-exchange listings**, and independent analysis flags its reported volume as likely inflated — for traders it is primarily a case study in **wash-trading red flags**, not a tradeable asset.

> **Disclaimer**: This page is a screening/case-study reference, not a recommendation. APEPE is a low-liquidity, unlisted memecoin with manipulation indicators; treat its headline market-cap rank as unverified. Nothing here is financial advice. As of **2026-06-24** the broad crypto tape is in an **Established Bear Market** (Fear & Greed = 22, "Extreme Fear"; BTC ≈ $62.6k, ETH ≈ $1.66k) — a regime in which thin, manipulation-prone microcaps are especially dangerous. **Market-data blocks below preserve their original "as of" dates and are not updated.**

## Overview

APEPE launched on Polygon in 2024 (all-time high $0.00000689 on 2024-11-22). The project's self-description: "a new meme that combines APE and PEPE to symbolize small investors"; the community "aims to bring together small investors and become a force to be reckoned with" and claims that "unlike many fake memecoins, it is driven by the power of the community and is fully prepared for success." This is promotional copy with no technical substance — there is no protocol, product, or revenue.

By June 2026 the token had retreated roughly 84% from its all-time high but still printed a ~$282M market cap on aggregators — anomalously high for a token with zero meaningful CEX listings.

## Red Flags / Volume Quality

- **Wash-trading indicator**: CoinPaprika's liquidity analysis flags a "bad liquidity to volume ratio," concluding there is "a high probability that volume is inflated through wash-trading" or that markets are too illiquid to support the reported figures. Reported 24h volume (~$34–40M) equal to ~13–15% of market cap daily, with no CEX order books, is inconsistent with organic memecoin flow.
- **Listing gap**: a genuine ~top-150 asset would normally carry Binance/Bybit/Upbit listings. APEPE has none — a structural signal that the headline market cap is not validated by exchange due-diligence.
- **Sentiment anomaly**: 100% positive CoinGecko sentiment (April 2026 snapshot) is itself a manipulation marker; organic communities are never unanimous.
- **Concentrated venue risk**: liquidity exists only in Polygon DEX pools; the effective exit liquidity is a small fraction of the quoted market cap.

## Narrative & Catalysts

APEPE belongs to the **frog-themed / "Boy's Club" memecoin** narrative basket (alongside [[pepe|PEPE]] and other frog tokens; see [[narrative-trading]]). In principle, memecoins in a basket beta-track the leader (PEPE) during narrative rotations. In practice, **APEPE does not reliably track basket flow** — its price action appears dominated by its own thin Polygon pool dynamics rather than genuine narrative demand. There are no fundamental catalysts (no product, protocol, or revenue); the only "catalysts" are social-promotion pushes and pool-level activity. In the mid-2026 Extreme-Fear regime, microcap meme demand is broadly absent.

## Trading Relevance & Risks

- **Tradeability**: spot only, on Polygon DEXes (QuickSwap/Uniswap-class pools). No perps, no [[hyperliquid]] listing, no major CEX pairs — effectively untradeable at size, and unshortable.
- **Liquidity / exit risk**: effective exit liquidity is a small fraction of the quoted ~$250–280M cap; attempting to sell size would collapse the price against the thin DEX pools.
- **Wash-trading / manipulation risk**: independent liquidity analysis flags the reported volume as likely inflated (see Red Flags). Treat reported volume and the cap rank as **unvalidated**.
- **Sentiment-anomaly risk**: 100% positive aggregator sentiment is itself a manipulation marker; organic communities are never unanimous.
- **Rug/contract risk**: a single Polygon contract with no CEX due diligence, no lockups disclosed, and promotional-only documentation carries elevated rug/abandonment risk.
- **No short / no hedge**: with no perps or CEX pairs, the token cannot be shorted or hedged — there is no way to express a bearish view except avoidance.

## Screening Playbook (what this page is for)

The main value of this entry is as a **screening example**. The pattern to recognize and exclude:

> *Top-150 cap rank **without** any tier-1 CEX listing + reported volume/liquidity mismatch (wash-trading flag) + unanimous (100%) sentiment + single-chain DEX-only liquidity = exclude from baskets, screens, and any size allocation.*

- **Validate caps via listings**: a genuine top-150 asset carries multiple tier-1 CEX listings; their absence is a red flag, not a buying opportunity.
- **Check liquidity-to-volume ratio**: a "bad" ratio (high reported volume on thin pools) signals inflated volume.
- **Distrust unanimous sentiment**: 100% positive is a manipulation tell.
- **Do not basket it**: because it doesn't beta-track PEPE, it adds idiosyncratic manipulation risk to a frog-meme basket without the intended exposure.
