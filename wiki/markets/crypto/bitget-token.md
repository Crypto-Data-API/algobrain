# Bitget Token

**Bitget Token** (BGB) is the exchange token of the [[bitget|Bitget]] exchange and Bitget Wallet, which since a September 2025 strategic partnership has also become the **gas and governance token of the Morph layer-2 chain**, with the non-profit Morph Foundation responsible for its future development. For traders, BGB is one of the major [[centralized-exchange|CEX]]-token plays (alongside [[binance-coin|BNB]], [[okb|OKB]], [[gatechain-token|GT]], [[kucoin-shares|KCS]]) — a bet on Bitget's exchange volumes plus an aggressive **buyback-and-burn programme targeting a final supply of 100 million tokens**.

## Protocol / Product

Launched in 2021, Bitget Token (BGB) was initially positioned as the platform token for the **[[bitget|Bitget]] exchange** and Bitget Wallet — granting fee discounts, Launchpool new-token mining access, and ecosystem perks. Bitget's leadership includes CEO Gracy Chen, COO Vugar Usi Zade, and CLO Hon Ng, with backgrounds spanning Goldman Sachs, Facebook, and Binance.

**In September 2025, Bitget agreed a strategic partnership with the Morph chain.** BGB became the primary token of **Morph's blockchain** — serving as the gas, governance, and payment token across the layer — and the Morph Foundation, a non-profit decentralized organization, became solely responsible for the future development of BGB. BGB initially operates on Ethereum as an ERC-20; a growing share is being migrated to the Morph chain.

### What BGB does

- **Trading-fee discounts** on Bitget — the core demand sink tied to exchange volume.
- **Launchpool / Launchpad access** — BGB holdings gate new-token allocations, a recurring demand pulse.
- **Morph L2 gas + governance** — post-partnership, BGB is the gas, payment, and governance token of the Morph chain; network activity becomes a BGB sink.
- **Copy-trading / ecosystem perks** — Bitget is known for social/copy-trading, where BGB integrates as a utility asset.

## Tokenomics & Supply

BGB runs an aggressive deflationary glide path — the most aggressive in the CEX-token basket on a percentage-of-genesis basis.

### Burn programme

- **30 December 2024**: Bitget burned **800 million BGB** (40% of the original 2B supply) in a single landmark burn, then moved to a quarterly buyback-and-burn model funded from exchange profits.
- Post-Morph-partnership, the burn mechanism is being linked to **Morph network activity**, continuing until total supply is reduced to **100 million BGB** — a 95% reduction from genesis supply.
- A recent burn destroyed **30 million BGB (~$130M)**, with one report estimating ~42.5% of total BGB supply destroyed over a six-month window.

## Valuation Framework

- **Exchange-volume proxy** — BGB demand scales with Bitget trading-fee revenue (fee discounts + launchpad gating). Bitget's market-share gains/losses drive the idiosyncratic component.
- **Buyback/burn proxy** — value accrual rests on the credibility of the burn glide path to 100M; faster burns funded by higher profits compress supply and support price.
- **Morph usage proxy** — incremental demand from L2 gas as Morph adoption grows (TVL, transactions).
- **Relative** — compare BGB market cap / Bitget volume against [[okb|OKB]]/OKX and [[binance-coin|BNB]]/Binance to judge richness within the basket. BGB's higher dilution (0.77 vs OKB's 1.00) is a structural discount factor.

## Risks

- **Exchange counterparty risk** — BGB's value and liquidity are inseparable from Bitget; any solvency, security, or operational failure impairs the token directly. See [[exchange-collapses]].
- **Regulatory risk** — concentrated issuer risk: regulatory action against Bitget, license loss, or a forced market exit would hit BGB demand and the fee-discount utility (cf. the [[kucoin-shares|KCS]] DOJ template).
- **Dilution overhang** — MC/FDV 0.77 means residual non-circulating supply; the thesis depends on burns retiring it faster than any unlocks reach the market.
- **Burn-policy discretion** — the burn programme is governance/profit-dependent and can be slowed; weaker Bitget profits or Morph adoption shrink the burn.
- **Venue-concentration risk** — ~1% turnover and primary liquidity on Bitget make off-venue exit and hedging difficult.
- **Bear-market regime** — extreme fear (F&G 23) compresses CEX-token premia; the -79% ATH drawdown demonstrates the downside.
