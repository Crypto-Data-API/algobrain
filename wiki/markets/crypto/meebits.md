**Meebits** are a collection of 20,000 unique 3D voxel character [[nft|NFTs]] created by [[larva-labs|Larva Labs]] (the creators of [[cryptopunks|CryptoPunks]] and [[autoglyphs|Autoglyphs]]) in May 2021. Designed as 3D avatars for use in metaverse environments and games, Meebits were Larva Labs' attempt to extend their NFT legacy into the emerging 3D virtual world space. [[cryptopunks|CryptoPunks]] and [[autoglyphs|Autoglyphs]] holders received free claims, while the remaining supply was sold at 2.5 ETH per mint. The collection was acquired by [[yuga-labs|Yuga Labs]] alongside CryptoPunks in March 2022.

---

## History

By early 2021, Larva Labs was the most iconic studio in NFTs, having created [[cryptopunks|CryptoPunks]] in 2017 and [[autoglyphs|Autoglyphs]] in 2019. With the metaverse narrative gaining momentum (fueled by buzz around virtual worlds, VR, and Facebook's eventual rebrand to Meta), Larva Labs launched Meebits on **May 3, 2021** as 3D voxel characters intended for use as avatars in virtual environments.

The launch gave free claims to existing CryptoPunks and Autoglyphs holders -- a model that rewarded early Larva Labs supporters and created instant demand. The remaining supply was sold via a Dutch auction starting at 2.5 ETH.

However, the launch was not without controversy. Shortly after launch, a bug in the minting contract was exploited, allowing one user to selectively mint rare Meebits by reverting transactions until they received desirable traits. This undermined trust in the fairness of the distribution.

In **March 2022**, Yuga Labs acquired the CryptoPunks and Meebits IP from Larva Labs. As part of the deal, Yuga Labs granted commercial IP rights to Meebits holders -- something Larva Labs had not done, and a policy Yuga Labs had pioneered with [[bored-ape-yacht-club|BAYC]].

## Collection Mechanics

| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Supply** | 20,000 unique 3D voxel characters (fixed) |
| **Blockchain** | [[ethereum|Ethereum]] mainnet |
| **Token standard** | [[erc-721|ERC-721]] (non-fungible) |
| **Mint price** | Free for [[cryptopunks|CryptoPunks]]/[[autoglyphs|Autoglyphs]] holders; 2.5 ETH for public Dutch auction |
| **Art style** | 3D voxel characters (Minecraft-style blocky 3D models) |
| **Intended use** | Metaverse avatars, gaming, virtual worlds |
| **IP rights** | Commercial rights granted to holders after [[yuga-labs|Yuga Labs]] acquisition ([[nft-ip-rights]]) |
| **Types** | Human, elephant, pig, skeleton, robot, bull, shark, visitor, dissected, and more |

Each Meebit is a procedurally generated 3D voxel model with various character types, attributes, and accessories. The 3D files are provided in **OBJ format**, allowing holders to import their characters into 3D environments, games, and animation tools — a deliverable beyond a static 2D image. The voxel art style is distinctive: chunky, pixelated 3D figures with a retro-gaming aesthetic.

[[nft-rarity-scoring|Rarity]] is dominated by **character type**: human Meebits are by far the most common, while non-human types (pig, robot, skeleton, elephant, and especially the rare **visitor** and **dissected** types) are scarce and command large premiums over the [[nft-floor-price|floor]]. Within types, accessories and attributes provide secondary rarity.

## Cultural Significance

Meebits occupy an awkward position in NFT history -- created by the most respected studio in the space, but never achieving the cultural resonance of their predecessors:

- **Larva Labs pedigree**: Being the third collection from the CryptoPunks/Autoglyphs creators gave Meebits instant credibility, but the "third album" effect meant expectations were sky-high
- **Metaverse thesis test**: Meebits were an early bet on the idea that 3D avatar NFTs would become essential as virtual worlds grew. That thesis has not yet been validated -- the metaverse buildout has been far slower than anticipated
- **IP rights catalyst**: The Yuga Labs acquisition and subsequent granting of commercial IP rights to Meebits holders was part of a broader shift in NFT culture toward holder-owned IP, a model pioneered by [[bored-ape-yacht-club|BAYC]]
- **Cautionary narrative**: Meebits is often cited as evidence that a strong creator brand alone is not enough -- without a compelling community, cultural narrative, or proven use case, even blue-chip pedigree cannot sustain price levels
- **Minting exploit**: The vulnerability that allowed selective minting of rare traits raised important questions about smart contract fairness and the value of provably random distribution

## Collector & Trading Risks

- **High supply, moderate demand:** 20,000 supply (double [[cryptopunks|CryptoPunks]]/[[bored-ape-yacht-club|BAYC]]) dilutes scarcity; demand has not matched, yielding lower prices and thinner liquidity.
- **Illiquidity & floor volatility:** Wide spreads and slow exits, especially for rare non-human types that trade in a separate thin sub-market; the [[nft-floor-price|floor]] can gap on single sweeps.
- **Unrealized use-case risk:** The 3D avatar / metaverse thesis remains speculative — renewed interest depends on mainstream NFT-avatar virtual worlds emerging, which is not guaranteed.
- **Ecosystem dependency:** Value is partially tied to [[yuga-labs|Yuga Labs]] execution and the broader portfolio ([[bored-ape-yacht-club|BAYC]], [[apecoin|ApeCoin]], [[otherside|Otherside]]) — concentration risk.
- **Royalty erosion:** Optional-royalty marketplaces ([[nft-royalties]]) reduced creator revenue across the portfolio.
- **Wash trading:** Reported volume can be inflated by [[wash-trading]] and incentive farming; verify with [[nft-aggregators|aggregators]].
- **"Deep value" trap:** Some traders view Meebits as undervalued versus their pedigree, but this thesis has been tested and unproven for several years — pedigree alone has not produced a catalyst.
- **ETH-denominated dual exposure:** Floors are quoted in [[ethereum|ETH]]; holders carry combined NFT + ETH price exposure.

Even Yuga-ecosystem collections trade well below 2021-2022 peaks on thin volume, and the metaverse-avatar narrative that underpinned Meebits has cooled.
