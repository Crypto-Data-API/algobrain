# Big Time

**Big Time** (BIGTIME) is a free-to-play, multiplayer action-RPG developed by **Big Time Studios** — a team founded by industry veterans (including former leadership from Decentraland, EA, Epic, and Blizzard) — that combines fast, co-op dungeon-crawler combat with an NFT-based cosmetics and creator economy. Unlike pay-to-win [[gamefi]] models, Big Time positions the game as the product first and the BIGTIME token as an emissions/economy layer earned through in-game activity rather than required to play. It ranks **#904** by market capitalization.

## Overview

Big Time is a free-to-play, co-op action-RPG ("dungeon crawler") in which players battle through time-and-space themed dungeons ("Adventures") and collect loot, cosmetics, and tokens. The defining design choice is that the game is **free to play and the core loop does not require buying [[nft]] assets** — NFTs are primarily **cosmetics** and productivity items rather than mandatory pay-to-win gear.

### Architecture and the creator economy

The economy revolves around two main NFT/asset types:

- **Cosmetics** — collectible items used to customize avatars; tradable on the marketplace.
- **SPACE** — a special NFT that gives players a personal "metaverse" space and access to the in-game **creator economy**, where players use Time Crystals and tokens to manufacture (forge) new Cosmetics. SPACE owners effectively run small production businesses inside the game.

Assets are issued on **Ethereum**, and the project has explored its own scaling infrastructure (the "Open Loot" ecosystem and an associated [[layer-2]] approach) for the marketplace and in-game items. Time Crystals are the in-game utility resource; BIGTIME is the tradable token layered on top.

### Token sinks and faucets

Big Time deliberately separates *gameplay* from *token requirement* — you do not need to spend BIGTIME to play — which changes the sink/faucet dynamics relative to pure [[play-to-earn]] designs.

**Faucets (token/asset emission):**

- **Time Crystals** — the in-game crafting resource, accumulated through play (capped per account to throttle farming). Time Crystals are the throttle on cosmetic production.
- **BIGTIME emissions** — distributed as activity/marketplace rewards and ecosystem incentives; this is the dilution channel that weighs on price.
- **Cosmetic / loot drops** — NFTs minted through the SPACE forging loop using Time Crystals + BIGTIME.

**Sinks (demand / token removal):**

- **Forging** — SPACE owners burn Time Crystals **and BIGTIME** to manufacture new Cosmetic NFTs, the dominant designed sink.
- **Marketplace fees** — trading Cosmetics and SPACE on the Open Loot marketplace routes value (and fees) through the economy.
- **Armory / upgrades** — refining and upgrading items consumes resources.

The intended virtuous loop: gamers play for fun → SPACE owners forge cosmetics (burning BIGTIME) → cosmetics sell to players → demand for BIGTIME and SPACE rises. The fragility, shared with all of [[gamefi|GameFi]], is that cosmetic demand is discretionary; if the active, paying player base shrinks, forging sinks dry up while BIGTIME emissions continue, and the token dilutes — visible in the multi-year drawdown.

### Backing and origin

Big Time Studios raised venture funding from prominent crypto and gaming investors. Historical investor categories associated with the token have included FTX/Alameda-era portfolios and other venture funds — relevant context given the 2022 FTX collapse, which created overhang concerns for tokens connected to those balance sheets.

## Competitive Position

Big Time sits in the "AAA-quality Web3 game" segment alongside titles attempting to win mainstream gamers rather than yield farmers. Its pitch — strong production values, free-to-play access, NFTs as optional cosmetics — contrasts with the breed-to-earn model of [[axie-infinity|Axie Infinity]] / [[smooth-love-potion|SLP]] that defined the 2021 [[play-to-earn]] boom. Competitors include other ecosystem-game tokens and broader [[gamefi]] plays such as [[bora|BORA]] and [[deapcoin|DEAPcoin]]. Its key challenge is the perennial Web3-gaming problem: converting crypto-speculator interest into durable, non-financial player engagement.

## Trading playbook (bear-tape context)

> Not investment advice — sentiment/structure notes only.

- **Beta-on-beta.** BIGTIME is altcoin beta layered on GameFi-theme beta; in Extreme Fear it tends to bleed with the cohort and bounce sharply but unreliably.
- **Watch the player loop, not just the chart.** Because the design is game-first, durable token demand tracks active/paying players and cosmetic-marketplace volume more than yield-farming inflows. Sustained re-rating likely needs real engagement, not just speculation.
- **Leverage matters.** BIGTIME-PERP on [[hyperliquid|Hyperliquid]] and CEX perp venues means funding and liquidation cascades amplify both directions; manage for that volatility.

## Risks

- **Gaming-narrative dependence** — valuation is highly cyclical and tied to the [[gamefi]] / [[play-to-earn]] sentiment cycle, which has been depressed since 2021–22.
- **Emissions / dilution** — low MC/FDV (~0.38) means substantial future supply from unlocks and in-game [[emissions]] (see [[emissions]]).
- **FTX/Alameda overhang** — historical ties to FTX-era investors created supply and reputational concerns post-2022.
- **Retention risk** — Web3 games frequently see player counts spike then collapse; sustained value requires real ongoing engagement, not just token speculation.
- **Leverage/volatility** — active perp markets amplify drawdowns; the token is a sub-$20M cap exposed to risk-off regimes (Fear & Greed 22 / Extreme Fear).
