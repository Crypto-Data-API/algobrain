# Trust Wallet Token

**Trust Wallet Token** (ticker **TWT**) is the utility and governance token of **Trust Wallet**, a leading multi-chain self-custody mobile [[wallets|wallet]] (acquired by Binance in 2018). TWT is a [[bnb|BNB Chain]] BEP-20 token used in-app for fee discounts, governance over new chain/token support, and promotional rewards. It is one of the largest "wallet ecosystem" tokens by market cap.

## Technology & Protocol

TWT's utility is wallet-centric rather than protocol-native — it does not secure a chain or oracle. Its functions inside **Trust Wallet** (a non-custodial, multi-chain mobile [[wallets|wallet]] supporting hundreds of networks):

- **Governance** — TWT holders vote on which new blockchains, tokens, and features Trust Wallet adds.
- **Fee discounts** — reduced costs on in-app [[dex|DEX]] swaps and crypto purchases.
- **Rewards & promotions** — incentives, bounties, and campaign rewards distributed to users; convertible to BNB/ETH for gas.

A persistent critique is that TWT's **must-hold utility is limited** — most wallet functionality (send, receive, swap, [[self-custody|self-custody]]) works without owning the token — so value capture rests largely on governance and discretionary rewards.

## Valuation Framing

TWT value is tied to **Trust Wallet adoption** and the broader [[self-custody]] / [[account-abstraction]] narrative rather than direct cash flows. The ~0.42 MC/FDV signals meaningful uncirculated supply (dilution risk), and weak must-hold demand caps the fundamental floor. It trades as a **proxy on the Binance/self-custody franchise** with optionality on new utility (staking, account abstraction) — qualitative only, not a price target.

## Use Case, Narrative & Category

TWT's category is a **wallet-ecosystem / self-custody token** (utility + governance), with the model centered on Trust Wallet itself. Acquired by Binance in 2018, Trust Wallet benefits from being the recommended self-custody [[wallets|wallet]] within the Binance ecosystem and serves tens of millions of users. The investment thesis ties TWT to Trust Wallet adoption and the broader [[self-custody]] / [[account-abstraction]] narrative — a bet on retail demand for non-custodial storage (which spiked after the FTX collapse). The category's structural weakness, as noted above, is that the token is rarely required to use the wallet.

## Risks

- **Supply overhang** — low MC/FDV (~0.42) means substantial uncirculated supply that could dilute holders over time.
- **Weak must-hold utility** — Trust Wallet largely works without TWT; demand depends on discretionary rewards and governance value.
- **Centralization** — Trust Wallet is Binance-owned, so TWT is exposed to Binance's strategic and regulatory situation.
- **No hard cap** — uncapped max supply reported; supply-policy uncertainty.
- **Macro / regime** — Established Bear Market with Fear & Greed at 23; mid-cap utility tokens are sensitive to risk-off rotations.
