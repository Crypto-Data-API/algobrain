# DUSK

**Dusk** (ticker **DUSK**) is a public, permissionless **Layer 1 blockchain purpose-built for regulated financial markets**. It is designed for the native issuance, trading, and settlement of **real-world assets (RWAs)** — particularly digital securities — in compliance with EU frameworks such as MiFID II, MiCA, and the DLT Pilot Regime. Dusk combines privacy-preserving smart contracts with [[zero-knowledge-proofs|zero-knowledge]] compliance infrastructure so that regulated instruments can be tokenized and traded on-chain while meeting disclosure and KYC obligations. DUSK is the native [[proof-of-stake]] token, ranked **#449** by market capitalization as of this snapshot.

## Technology & Consensus

Dusk runs its own **Layer 1** using a **Proof-of-Stake** consensus (the Succinct Attestation protocol, where staked "provisioners" reach committee-based finality). Its core technical differentiators are **privacy-preserving, confidential smart contracts** and **zero-knowledge compliance** — letting issuers enforce transfer restrictions, identity checks, and regulatory rules on tokenized securities without exposing all transaction data publicly. The stack includes institutional tooling such as **Dusk Vault** (custody) aimed at regulated market participants. (See [[layer-1]], [[proof-of-stake]], [[real-world-assets]].)

## Use Case, Narrative & Category

Dusk's category is the **regulated RWA / compliant-securities Layer 1** for Europe ([[real-world-assets]]). The narrative centers on tokenizing real-world financial instruments — equities, bonds, funds — under EU law, with [[privacy-coins|privacy]] and compliance built into the protocol. Dusk has pursued partnerships with regulated infrastructure such as **NPEX** (a Dutch MTF-regulated exchange) and **Quantoz** (a MiCA-compliant EMI issuing the EURQ stablecoin) to seed secondary markets for digital securities. CoinGecko category tags include Layer 1 (L1), Real World Assets (RWA), Privacy Blockchain, and Zero Knowledge ([[zero-knowledge-proofs|ZK]]). The thesis rides the broader [[real-world-assets]] tokenization trend, betting that European regulatory frameworks (MiCA, DLT Pilot Regime) create demand for a compliant settlement layer.

## Valuation Framing (qualitative)

- **MC ≈ FDV (~1.00 on total supply):** circulating equals total supply, so there is no locked-token unlock cliff on *issued* tokens. The 1B hard cap means future supply (~411M DUSK still to be emitted via staking rewards) enters gradually and predictably rather than via investor unlocks — a slow, scheduled inflation rather than an overhang.
- **Regulatory optionality:** DUSK is effectively a leveraged bet on EU digital-securities regulation (MiCA, DLT Pilot Regime) producing real issuance demand. This is a binary-ish, slow-burn catalyst: if European regulated venues route meaningful tokenized-securities volume through Dusk, the re-rating could be large; if not, it remains a well-built chain with thin usage.
- **Best-positioned-but-unproven:** unlike pure-narrative peers, Dusk has *named regulated counterparties* (NPEX, Quantoz/EURQ), which is a tangible differentiator. The open question is settlement volume, not technology.
- **Drawdown context:** ~92% below the 2021 ATH; a return to the high implies ~13x. The realistic bull case is a partial re-rating on demonstrated regulated-RWA throughput.
