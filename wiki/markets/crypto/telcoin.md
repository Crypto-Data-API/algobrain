# Telcoin

**Telcoin** (ticker **TEL**) is the native token of Telcoin, a compliance-focused fintech building blockchain-based remittances and mobile banking for phone users worldwide. It pairs telecom/mobile-money partnerships with DeFi rails, offering the Telcoin Wallet (a multi-signature self-custody wallet supporting 100+ assets) and low-cost cross-border transfers to eWallets and bank accounts. TEL is a multi-chain token issued across [[ethereum|Ethereum]], [[polygon|Polygon]], Arbitrum, and Base.

## Technology & Protocol

Telcoin's product stack blends regulated payments with on-chain rails:

- **Telcoin Wallet** — a multi-signature self-custody mobile [[wallets|wallet]] supporting 100+ digital assets plus fiat remittance corridors to eWallets and bank accounts.
- **Remittance network** — designed to plug into telecom, mobile-money, and e-wallet partners to deliver cheap, fast cross-border transfers ([[remittances]]), settling in fiat and/or blockchain.
- **Regulated stablecoins** — Telcoin has pursued issuing bank-grade, compliant stablecoins (e.g. Digital Cash / eUSD efforts) under US state banking frameworks, central to its remittance thesis. See [[stablecoins]].
- **TELx / DeFi modules** — in-app liquidity pools and staking where TEL is the incentive/utility asset.

## Valuation Framing

TEL value is tied to **remittance volume** and **regulated stablecoin adoption** rather than speculative DeFi yield. With supply ~95% circulating there is little dilution overhang, but realized usage must scale to justify a ~$226M cap on ~$808K daily volume. The thesis is execution- and regulation-dependent; it screens as a binary fintech bet (license wins + stablecoin launch vs. stalled rollout). Qualitative only — not a price target.

## Use Case, Narrative & Category

Telcoin's category is **blockchain remittances / mobile fintech / neobank**, blending [[defi|DeFi]] with regulated payments. Key elements:

- **Telcoin Wallet** — a multi-signature self-custody mobile wallet supporting 100+ digital assets plus fiat remittance corridors to eWallets and bank accounts.
- **Compliance-first posture** — Telcoin holds licenses across multiple jurisdictions, regulated as a Virtual Asset Service Provider in the EU (Lithuania) and Argentina, a Major Payment Institution in Singapore, and a Money Services Business in the US, Canada, and Australia.
- **Telecom rails** — designed to plug into telecom, mobile-money, and e-wallet partners to deliver cheap, fast cross-border transfers, with both fiat and blockchain settlement.
- **Stablecoin ambitions** — Telcoin has pursued issuing regulated, bank-grade stablecoins (e.g. Digital Cash / eUSD efforts) under US state banking frameworks, central to its remittance thesis.

The narrative ties TEL value to remittance volume and regulated stablecoin adoption rather than speculative DeFi yields, targeting the multi-hundred-billion-dollar global remittance market.

## Risks

- **Regulatory dependence** — the entire model hinges on obtaining and keeping money-transmission/banking licenses and a compliant stablecoin charter; setbacks would directly hit the thesis.
- **Execution risk** — long-promised stablecoin/remittance products must reach scale to justify TEL demand.
- **Liquidity** — thin volume (~$808K/24h) and few external CEX listings; hard to exit large positions without slippage.
- **Competition** — remittances and stablecoin payments are crowded (incumbents plus large stablecoin issuers like [[stellar]] and [[ripple]]).
- **Macro / regime** — Established Bear Market with Fear & Greed at 23; speculative fintech mid-caps trade weakly in risk-off conditions.
