# Sushi

**Sushi** (ticker: **SUSHI**, [[ethereum|Ethereum]]-native, multi-chain) is a decentralized, multi-chain [[dex|DEX]] and [[defi|DeFi]] suite that lets users swap assets across 40+ blockchain networks, governed by the Sushi DAO. It launched in 2020 as a fork of [[uniswap|Uniswap]] that added a governance/fee token and famously executed a "vampire attack" to drain Uniswap liquidity. SUSHI is the protocol's governance token and historically captured a share of trading fees via the xSUSHI staking model. It sits in the [[decentralized-exchange|DEX]] / [[automated-market-maker|AMM]] DeFi category, one of the original "DeFi 1.0" blue chips.

## Overview

SushiSwap is a decentralized, multi-chain DEX that allows users to swap a wide range of assets across 40+ blockchain networks. It is powered by the industry's most advanced swap and aggregation stack, providing access to superior liquidity and competitive pricing. SushiSwap also offers smart liquidity solutions for liquidity providers including Smart Pools and no-IL AMM.

Sushi Labs, the development arm of Sushi DAO, continues to innovate by building a multi-DEX ecosystem with projects like Susa, Saru, and Wara with various blockchain networks.

For more information, visit https://sushi.com/

**Dilution flag:** SUSHI has **no fixed max supply** — issuance is governed by the Sushi DAO and historically expanded via liquidity-mining (yield-farming) rewards. The original tokenomics minted SUSHI as emissions to LPs, which fueled the 2020-21 growth but also created persistent sell pressure. With ~94% of total supply already circulating, near-term unlock overhang is modest, but holders depend on DAO discipline to avoid renewed inflation. The core token thesis is **fee accrual vs. dilution**: SUSHI's value rests on the protocol redirecting a slice of swap fees to stakers (the xSUSHI model) faster than emissions and competition erode it.

## DEX Model & Fee Capture

SushiSwap is a constant-product [[automated-market-maker|AMM]] at its core — liquidity providers deposit token pairs into pools and earn a share of the swap fee (historically 0.30%, with 0.25% to LPs and 0.05% routed to the protocol/xSUSHI buyback). The product surface has since broadened well beyond the original x*y=k pools:

- **Cross-chain swap aggregator/router** — routes a single order across 40+ chains and many external liquidity sources, competing with aggregators like 1inch and CowSwap rather than just other AMMs.
- **Concentrated-liquidity pools (V3-style)** — capital-efficient pools comparable to [[uniswap|Uniswap V3]].
- **Smart Pools and no-impermanent-loss AMM** — managed LP strategies that rebalance ranges automatically.
- **Multi-DEX ecosystem** — Sushi Labs is building successor venues (Susa, Saru, Wara) under the Sushi DAO.

**Fee capture mechanics (the SUSHI thesis):** value accrues to the token only insofar as protocol fees are redirected to holders. The xSUSHI model lets users stake SUSHI to receive xSUSHI, which historically accrued a portion of all swap fees converted to SUSHI — a "real yield" claim on protocol revenue. The recurring debate is whether thin per-chain TVL and intense fee competition leave enough margin for that buyback to matter versus emissions.

### TVL & Volume Context (qualitative)

Sushi is a multi-chain venue but its TVL and daily swap volume sit well below category leader [[uniswap|Uniswap]] and stablecoin-AMM leader [[curve-dao-token|Curve]]. Most spot DEX volume has consolidated into Uniswap and aggregator front-ends; Sushi competes on breadth (40+ chains) and routing rather than dominant single-chain depth. With ~$7M reported 24h token volume against a ~$48M cap, SUSHI itself is a small-cap, liquid-enough-to-trade but easily moved name.

## Valuation Framing (qualitative)

SUSHI is a **fee-multiple / governance-token** asset, best framed by how much protocol revenue actually reaches holders relative to its ~$48M cap. Bull case: a recovery in multi-chain DEX volume plus disciplined emissions lets xSUSHI buybacks compound, and the brand/router still has distribution. Bear case: spot DEX volume keeps consolidating into Uniswap and aggregators, emissions or governance dysfunction dilute holders, and the token stays a beta-to-DeFi proxy near its all-time low. As an uncapped sub-$100M name in an Established Bear Market, it carries high regime sensitivity. *This is framing, not a price target or recommendation.*

## Use Case, Narrative & Category

Sushi belongs to the **DEX / DeFi blue-chip** narrative — community-owned decentralized exchanges that compete with [[uniswap|Uniswap]] for spot swap volume. Its product surface has expanded well beyond the original constant-product [[automated-market-maker|AMM]] to include a cross-chain swap aggregator/router (routing orders across 40+ chains and many liquidity sources), concentrated-liquidity pools, and an evolving multi-DEX ecosystem (projects such as Susa, Saru, and Wara) built by Sushi Labs under the Sushi DAO.

The SUSHI token's value thesis rests on governance rights and protocol fee-sharing (the xSUSHI staking model historically distributed a portion of swap fees to stakers). As with most "DeFi 1.0" governance tokens, the central debate is **fee accrual to token holders vs. dilution and competition** from Uniswap, Curve, and aggregators.

## How & Where It Trades

- **Centralized spot venues:** SUSHI trades on Binance (SUSHI/USDT), Kraken (SUSHI/USD), Bitget, KuCoin, and Crypto.com.
- **Decentralized spot venues:** SUSHI is deeply liquid on its own SushiSwap pools and on Uniswap V2/V3 (Ethereum), and is deployed across many chains (Arbitrum, Polygon, Base, Avalanche, BNB Chain, Solana, and more).
- **Derivatives:** A **SUSHI-PERP** perpetual market trades on [[hyperliquid|Hyperliquid]]. With ~$7.09M 24h spot volume, perp positioning can move price; watch [[funding-rate|funding]] and [[open-interest|open interest]] for crowded longs/shorts, especially in the current bear regime.

## Risks

- **Smart-contract risk:** AMM/router contracts and cross-chain integrations are exposed to exploits and oracle/routing failures (see [[decentralized-exchange]]).
- **Governance / treasury risk:** Sushi has a turbulent history of leadership changes, contributor disputes, and treasury/legal uncertainty, which has periodically weighed on the token.
- **Fee competition:** Intense competition from [[uniswap|Uniswap]] (incl. v4 hooks), Curve, and DEX aggregators pressures both volume and the value captured by SUSHI holders.
- **Macro / liquidity risk:** As a sub-$100M-cap altcoin, SUSHI is highly sensitive to the broad risk-off regime (Fear & Greed 23 / Established Bear Market as of 2026-06-20).
