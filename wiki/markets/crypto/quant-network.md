# Quant

**Quant** (QNT) is the access token for London-based Quant Network's **Overledger**, an enterprise blockchain-interoperability operating system aimed at banks, central banks and large institutions. QNT has a hard-capped supply of ~14.6M tokens (one of the smallest supplies among top-100 assets) and is the canonical "bank-chain interoperability / CBDC" narrative token. As of 2026-06-20 it sits at **#65** with a ~$1.03B market cap, trading near $70.67 on very thin liquidity (~$9.8M/day, Vol/MC ~1%) against an **extreme-fear** market backdrop (Fear & Greed = 23, an "Established Bear Market" regime).

## Overview

London-based Quant Network develops Overledger, billed as the first interoperable blockchain operating system that facilitates internet-scale development of decentralised, multi-chain applications. Overledger is an agnostic API gateway layer that connects existing networks and enterprise systems to multiple blockchains without locking users into a single vendor or technology — it can unlock and distribute value and applications across current and future chains. The company was founded by Gilbert Verdian, creator of the Blockchain ISO Standard TC 307, and positions itself around regulated-finance standards such as **ISO 20022**.

Unlike most interoperability plays ([[cosmos]] IBC, LayerZero), Quant sells top-down to institutions; QNT is required for licenses and gateway access, which is the token's value-accrual mechanism.

## Protocol / Technology

Quant's product is **Overledger**, positioned not as a blockchain but as an *operating system for blockchains* — an interoperability layer that sits above existing networks and connects them, plus enterprise back-office systems, to multiple distributed ledgers simultaneously. Its design goals distinguish it from crypto-native bridges:

- **Agnostic API gateway.** Overledger abstracts away the underlying ledger. Rather than deploying smart contracts onto a single canonical interoperability chain, enterprises call standardised APIs that route transactions and read/write state across heterogeneous networks ([[ethereum]], permissioned ledgers, payment rails). There is no "Overledger token gas" model — value accrues through **licensing and gateway access**, paid for and secured with QNT.
- **Multi-Ledger Tokens (MLTs) & treaty contracts.** Overledger supports tokens and "treaty" contracts that can span chains, letting an institution issue or settle an instrument without committing to one ledger's long-term survival — the core enterprise pitch ("don't bet your infrastructure on a single chain").
- **Standards alignment.** Founder **Gilbert Verdian** chairs/created the blockchain ISO standard work under **ISO/TC 307**, and Quant explicitly aligns Overledger with **ISO 20022**, the messaging standard underpinning modern interbank payments (SWIFT migration, real-time gross settlement). This standards-first posture is the spine of its regulated-finance and CBDC credibility.
- **Overledger Fusion ("Layer 2.5").** Announced 2025 and live on mainnet **2026-06-02**, Fusion is a multi-chain rollup/settlement network that unifies **74 blockchain networks** into a single execution environment built for banks and large institutions — Quant's move from "interoperability middleware" toward an institutional settlement venue (Disruption Banking, 2026-06-03).

**QNT's role in the stack:** QNT is a fixed-supply ERC-20 on [[ethereum]] used to license Overledger access (developer and enterprise licenses are denominated in fiat but require QNT to be locked/held to obtain a "MApp" gateway). The token therefore behaves as a demand sink tied to enterprise adoption rather than a fee/gas token — which makes its value accrual real but **hard to observe on-chain**.

## Tokenomics & Supply

Quant has one of the **smallest token supplies in the top-100** — a hard cap of **14,612,493 QNT**, with **14,544,176 circulating (99.5% of max)**. There is effectively **no future emission or unlock overhang**: MC/FDV ≈ 1.00, so dilution risk is negligible and the printed market cap is "honest." The flip side is microstructure fragility:

- **High unit price, low float.** At ~$70/token and only ~14.5M units, even modest dollar flows move price sharply; order books are thin and the asset gaps in both directions.
- **No staking/burn mechanics.** QNT is not inflationary and is not burned at the protocol level; supply is static. Demand is the only lever, and it is gated by enterprise license uptake.
- **Demand sink, not gas token.** QNT must be held/locked to obtain Overledger gateway licenses, so adoption translates to lock-up of float rather than recurring on-chain fees.

## Ecosystem & Use Cases

Quant's addressable market is **regulated institutional finance**, not retail DeFi — which makes its ecosystem look very different from a typical L1:

- **CBDC infrastructure.** Quant is an [[ecb|ECB]] digital-euro **pioneer partner**, contributing to the conditional-payments workstream of the digital-euro innovation platform. This is the single most important reference for the "central-bank money on-chain" thesis and the spine of the QNT narrative.
- **Tokenized deposits.** Reported participation in the UK **Great British Tokenized Deposit (GBTD)** programme involving major UK banks (HSBC, Barclays), targeted for completion around mid-2026 *[MEDIUM confidence — trade-press sourced]*. Tokenized bank deposits are an institutional on-ramp distinct from stablecoins.
- **[[real-world-assets|RWA]] / settlement.** Overledger's multi-ledger token and treaty-contract primitives target tokenized securities, payments and settlement that must remain ledger-agnostic — overlapping the [[real-world-assets]] basket alongside XRP, [[xdce-crowd-sale|XDC]] and Hedera.
- **Enterprise integration.** The pitch to banks is connecting legacy core-banking and payment systems (ISO 20022 messaging) to multiple ledgers via a single API, avoiding single-chain lock-in. **Fusion** (live 2026-06-02) extends this from middleware toward an institutional settlement venue spanning 74 networks.

- **Spot venues.** Deep-ish CEX spot on Binance (QNT/USDT), Kraken (QNT/USD), Bitget, KuCoin and Crypto.com; on-chain liquidity on Uniswap V2/V3 (QNT/WETH) on [[ethereum]]. Korean-won and EUR pairs add periodic regional flow.
- **Derivatives.** [[perpetual-futures|Perps]] exist on major CEXs (Binance, Bitget, etc.), but QNT is **not** a high-OI, high-funding speculative name — open interest and funding are modest relative to majors and meme coins. It is traded more as a narrative/positioning vehicle than a leverage casino.
- **Thin liquidity is the defining feature.** At ~$9.8M of 24h volume against a ~$1.03B cap (**Vol/MC ~1%**, 2026-06-20), QNT is among the least-liquid top-65 assets. Combined with a tiny 14.6M float and a high unit price, this produces sharp, illiquid moves on catalyst headlines and equally sharp air-pockets when flow dries up. Slippage on size is material; position sizing must assume a market doing ~$10M/day, not a $1B asset.

## Valuation Framework

QNT is unusually hard to value because its cash-flow analogue is **opaque enterprise licensing**, not on-chain fees:

- **No observable fee revenue.** Unlike a gas-token L1, Overledger generates fiat-denominated license/gateway revenue that does not flow on-chain. There is no public, auditable "protocol revenue" line to anchor a multiple, so the token trades on *narrative and reference customers* more than measurable fundamentals.
- **Demand-sink, fixed-supply model.** With MC/FDV ≈ 1.00 and a hard 14.6M cap, value accrues purely through demand for licenses locking up float. If enterprise adoption compounds, the fixed float is a leverage point; if it stalls, there is no yield or buyback to defend price.
- **Narrative premium.** A meaningful part of QNT's valuation is a *proxy premium* for ISO 20022 / CBDC exposure — investors paying for optionality on central-bank and bank-chain adoption (the [[ecb]] partnership is the headline reference). This premium expands and contracts with the news cycle.
- **Anchors.** -83% from the 2021 ATH ($427); ~$1.03B cap with FDV ≈ MC. The bull case is enterprise/CBDC adoption converting to license demand; the bear case is that revenue stays small and the narrative re-rates down.

## Trading Playbook

- **Narrative basket.** Bank-chain / CBDC / [[real-world-assets|RWA]] infrastructure (QNT, XRP, [[xdce-crowd-sale|XDC]], HBAR…). QNT is the purest "ISO 20022 / central bank" narrative proxy and rallies on CBDC and tokenized-deposit headlines.
- **Catalyst-momentum, fade the spike.** Because the float is tiny and liquidity is thin, QNT spikes hard on Fusion-adoption, ECB digital-euro, and GBTD/tokenized-deposit news — then mean-reverts as flow exits. Treat sharp narrative pops as [[event-driven]] trades with pre-defined exits rather than buy-and-hold conviction.
- **Microstructure discipline.** Size for ~$10M/day liquidity; use limit orders; expect slippage and gappy fills. The high unit price flatters per-token volatility — risk should be expressed in dollars, not tokens.
- **Catalysts to watch.** Fusion mainnet adoption metrics post-2026-06-02, ECB digital-euro decisions, GBTD completion (mid-2026), and new institutional/standards announcements.

## Competitive Positioning

QNT competes in **blockchain interoperability and institutional settlement**, but with a top-down, enterprise/standards-first model that contrasts with crypto-native bridges:

| Project | Model | Approach | Token role | Contrast vs Quant |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Quant (QNT)** | Enterprise OS / Overledger | Top-down, sells to banks & central banks; ISO 20022 / CBDC | License/gateway access; fixed 14.6M supply | Reference case (this page) |
| [[cosmos]] (ATOM) | App-chain interoperability (IBC) | Bottom-up, crypto-native sovereign chains | Staking/security for the hub | Permissionless & crypto-first vs Quant's permissioned enterprise focus |
| Chainlink (LINK / CCIP) | Oracle + cross-chain messaging | Crypto-native + some enterprise | Node staking & fees | Broader DeFi data focus; CCIP is the closest interop rival |
| LayerZero (ZRO) | Omnichain messaging protocol | Bottom-up developer bridge | Protocol fees | Pure crypto bridging, not enterprise integration |
| [[xdce-crowd-sale|XDC]] | Enterprise hybrid L1 | Trade-finance / RWA chain | Gas + staking | Owns a chain vs Quant sitting above many chains |
| Hedera (HBAR) | Enterprise DAG L1 | Council-governed enterprise ledger | Gas/fees | Single network vs Quant's multi-ledger abstraction |
| XRP | Payments settlement asset | Cross-border payments / ISO 20022 | Bridge asset / liquidity | Shares the ISO-20022/bank narrative but is a settlement asset, not interop middleware |

Quant's differentiator is that it does **not** ask institutions to migrate to a new chain — it abstracts over the ones they already use — which is also its weakness: value accrual is harder to observe and the moat is commercial/standards-based rather than network-effect-based.

## Risks

- **Opaque, unauditable value accrual.** Overledger's revenue is fiat-denominated enterprise licensing with no on-chain footprint, so token holders cannot verify fundamentals; the market trades narrative, not measurable cash flow.
- **Thin liquidity / low float.** ~$9.8M daily volume (Vol/MC ~1%) on a tiny 14.6M float means sharp, illiquid swings and meaningful slippage on size — both up and down.
- **Narrative dependence.** Retail and momentum interest is episodic, clustering around CBDC and tokenized-deposit headlines; between catalysts the tape drifts and liquidity thins further.
- **Deep, persistent drawdown.** -83% from the 2021 ATH; the asset has not recovered prior-cycle highs and sits in an **Established Bear Market** regime with extreme fear (Fear & Greed = 23, 2026-06-20).
- **CBDC political/regulatory uncertainty.** The core thesis rests on central-bank and bank adoption (e.g. the digital euro), which is subject to political will, multi-year timelines, and the risk that institutions build in-house or pick a competing standard/vendor.
