# MANTRA

**MANTRA** (ticker **OM**) is a [[proof-of-stake]] [[layer-1]] blockchain positioned as a regulatory-compliant settlement layer for tokenized [[real-world-assets|real-world assets (RWA)]]. Built with the Cosmos SDK and offering MultiVM support including Ethereum Virtual Machine (EVM) compatibility, MANTRA lets developers and institutions embed compliance at the chain or smart-contract level — branded "a permissionless blockchain for permissioned applications." OM is the chain's staking, gas and governance token.

## Technology / Consensus

MANTRA Chain is a Cosmos SDK [[layer-1]] using Tendermint/CometBFT [[proof-of-stake]] consensus, with MultiVM support and EVM compatibility. Its differentiator is built-in compliance tooling: identity, permissioning and regulatory controls can be applied at the chain or contract level, targeting institutional issuers of tokenized assets. MANTRA has emphasized regulatory licensing, having pursued/secured virtual-asset licensing in Dubai (VARA) to position itself for Middle East institutional RWA flows.

## Use Case / Narrative / Category

MANTRA's narrative is **regulated RWA tokenization** — bringing real estate, funds and other off-chain assets on-chain within a compliant framework. Category tags include Smart Contract Platform, [[layer-1]], and [[real-world-assets|Real World Assets (RWA)]]. The RWA theme has been one of the more durable institutional narratives, though MANTRA's own credibility was severely damaged by the 2025 crash.

## Valuation Framing

OM's **MC/FDV is ~0.73** ($39.0M circulating vs $53.4M fully diluted), with circulating supply at ~53% of the 10B max — so beyond the documented unlock history there remains substantial future emission. The headline valuation challenge is not dilution mechanics but **reflexivity and trust**: after a ~90%+ single-day collapse, OM trades on a credibility discount that no fundamentals can quickly repair. The [[real-world-assets|RWA]] tokenization narrative MANTRA targets is one of the more durable institutional themes, and the chain's Dubai (VARA) licensing is a genuine differentiator — but value capture to the OM token depends on actual tokenized-asset volume settling on-chain and paying fees, which remains unproven at scale. OM is best framed as a deep-distressed, high-risk option on the team rebuilding credibility *and* the regulated-RWA thesis paying off — two independent conditions that both must hold.

## Notable History

- **April 13, 2025 — the OM crash.** OM collapsed from roughly $6 to under $0.50 within hours, wiping out an estimated $5B+ in market capitalization in one of the largest single-day token collapses on record. The team attributed the move to forced/cascading liquidations of large positions on centralized exchanges during low-liquidity hours; critics pointed to concentrated token holdings and tokenomics concerns. The crash devastated holder confidence and remains the defining event in OM's history.
- **2026** — Trading at a small fraction of pre-crash levels; CoinGecko's tracked "ATH" of $0.0263 (March 2026) reflects post-crash pricing.

> The April 2025 crash is essential context for any OM thesis: the token's structural risks (supply concentration, liquidation cascades, thin off-hours liquidity) are not hypothetical — they materialized.

## Trading Profile

### Venues & liquidity

OM is tradable on [[binance]] as both spot (OM/USDT) and a USD-margined [[perpetual-futures|perpetual]], which exposes [[funding-rate|funding]], [[open-interest|open interest]] and [[liquidations|liquidation]] data. It is **NOT** listed on Hyperliquid, so Binance is the primary leveraged venue and the reference tape for perp-based signals. Because leveraged flow, funding and liquidation depth are concentrated on a single exchange, execution and hedging depend on Binance-specific liquidity — there is no meaningful cross-venue perp to spread against or fall back on if Binance depth thins. Combined with a small ~$36M market cap and modest ~$5M daily volume, the order book is thin and gap-prone: size positions small, expect slippage and wide funding swings, and treat perp OI as capable of unwinding violently (as the April 2025 crash demonstrated).

### Applicable strategies

- [[liquidation-cascade-fade]] — OM's defining event was a liquidation cascade; the token's thin off-hours book makes forced-liquidation flushes on Binance perps recurring fade opportunities into washed-out levels.
- [[post-liquidation-rebound]] — sharp liquidation-driven wicks on a low-liquidity name frequently overshoot, offering mean-reverting rebound entries once forced selling exhausts.
- [[funding-rate-harvest]] — volatile funding on a small-cap RWA perp lets a spot-long / perp-short book collect funding when leveraged positioning skews one way.
- [[crowded-long-funding-fade]] — narrative-driven RWA rallies can crowd longs and push funding rich; fading persistently positive funding captures the reversion on a reflexive token.
- [[oi-price-exhaustion]] — rising open interest into a stalling price on OM's thin tape flags exhausted leverage prone to a cascade, a high-signal setup here.
- [[news-trading]] — OM is heavily narrative/event-driven (VARA licensing, RWA milestones, crash-anniversary headlines), making scheduled and breaking catalysts tradable.

### Volatility & regime character

Small-cap (rank ~560), high-beta [[layer-1]] / [[real-world-assets|RWA]] infrastructure token with pronounced reflexivity — its history includes a ~90% single-day collapse, so realized volatility and tail risk run well above large-caps. Directionally correlated to BTC/ETH risk-on/risk-off regimes but with a much larger amplitude, and its price is unusually sensitive to idiosyncratic RWA-narrative and credibility news. Behaves like a distressed, sentiment-driven small-cap rather than a stable infra token.

### Risk flags

- **Venue concentration** — leveraged liquidity is centralized on Binance; no Hyperliquid or deep alternative perp venue to diversify execution.
- **Liquidity/gap risk** — thin book and low absolute depth make it prone to slippage and violent liquidation cascades, especially in low-liquidity hours.
- **Supply overhang** — only ~53% of a 10B max supply circulates; future emissions/unlocks are a persistent dilution and supply-event risk.
- **Narrative dependence** — valuation hinges on the unproven regulated-RWA thesis and on rebuilding post-crash credibility; sentiment can reverse abruptly.
- **Reputational/regulatory** — lingering distrust from the 2025 crash plus dependence on continued licensing (Dubai VARA) add event risk.
