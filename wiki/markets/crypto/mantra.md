# MANTRA

**MANTRA** (ticker **OM**) is a [[proof-of-stake]] [[layer-1]] blockchain positioned as a regulatory-compliant settlement layer for tokenized [[real-world-assets|real-world assets (RWA)]]. Built with the Cosmos SDK and offering MultiVM support including Ethereum Virtual Machine (EVM) compatibility, MANTRA lets developers and institutions embed compliance at the chain or smart-contract level — branded "a permissionless blockchain for permissioned applications." OM is the chain's staking, gas and governance token.

## Technology / Consensus

MANTRA Chain is a Cosmos SDK [[layer-1]] using Tendermint/CometBFT [[proof-of-stake]] consensus, with MultiVM support and EVM compatibility. Its differentiator is built-in compliance tooling: identity, permissioning and regulatory controls can be applied at the chain or contract level, targeting institutional issuers of tokenized assets. MANTRA has emphasized regulatory licensing, having pursued/secured virtual-asset licensing in Dubai (VARA) to position itself for Middle East institutional RWA flows.

---

## Use Case / Narrative / Category

MANTRA's narrative is **regulated RWA tokenization** — bringing real estate, funds and other off-chain assets on-chain within a compliant framework. Category tags include Smart Contract Platform, [[layer-1]], and [[real-world-assets|Real World Assets (RWA)]]. The RWA theme has been one of the more durable institutional narratives, though MANTRA's own credibility was severely damaged by the 2025 crash.

## Valuation Framing

OM's **MC/FDV is ~0.73** ($39.0M circulating vs $53.4M fully diluted), with circulating supply at ~53% of the 10B max — so beyond the documented unlock history there remains substantial future emission. The headline valuation challenge is not dilution mechanics but **reflexivity and trust**: after a ~90%+ single-day collapse, OM trades on a credibility discount that no fundamentals can quickly repair. The [[real-world-assets|RWA]] tokenization narrative MANTRA targets is one of the more durable institutional themes, and the chain's Dubai (VARA) licensing is a genuine differentiator — but value capture to the OM token depends on actual tokenized-asset volume settling on-chain and paying fees, which remains unproven at scale. OM is best framed as a deep-distressed, high-risk option on the team rebuilding credibility *and* the regulated-RWA thesis paying off — two independent conditions that both must hold.

## Notable History

- **April 13, 2025 — the OM crash.** OM collapsed from roughly $6 to under $0.50 within hours, wiping out an estimated $5B+ in market capitalization in one of the largest single-day token collapses on record. The team attributed the move to forced/cascading liquidations of large positions on centralized exchanges during low-liquidity hours; critics pointed to concentrated token holdings and tokenomics concerns. The crash devastated holder confidence and remains the defining event in OM's history.
- **2026** — Trading at a small fraction of pre-crash levels; CoinGecko's tracked "ATH" of $0.0263 (March 2026) reflects post-crash pricing.

> The April 2025 crash is essential context for any OM thesis: the token's structural risks (supply concentration, liquidation cascades, thin off-hours liquidity) are not hypothetical — they materialized.

## Risks

- **History of catastrophic crash** — OM experienced a ~90%+ single-day collapse in April 2025; the conditions that enabled it (concentrated supply, liquidation cascades, thin liquidity) are structural.
- **Supply overhang** — only ~53% of a 10B max supply circulates; future unlocks/emissions dilute holders.
- **Small-cap liquidity risk** — ~$39M market cap with low absolute depth; vulnerable to gaps and slippage.
- **Bear-market regime** — extreme fear (F&G = 23) and an "Established Bear Market" weigh on high-beta alts.
- **Regulatory/execution risk** — the compliant-RWA thesis depends on actual institutional adoption and continued licensing.
- **Reputational risk** — lingering distrust following the 2025 crash.
