# ChainOpera AI

**ChainOpera AI** (ticker **COAI**) is an **AI-agent platform and AI-native blockchain** project on the [[bnb|BNB Chain]] that aims to enable collaborative intelligence — a network of AI agents co-created and co-operated by the community. CoinGecko tags it across *Artificial Intelligence (AI)*, *AI Applications*, *AI Framework*, *BNB Chain Ecosystem* and *Binance Alpha Spotlight*, placing it in the infrastructure tier of the [[ai-agents|AI-agent meta]].

## Architecture / what it does

ChainOpera describes a **full-stack AI platform** built on three pillars:

1. **"Super AI" app + creator economy** — a consumer-facing application plus a marketplace for designing, distributing, and using AI agents. This is the demand/distribution side: users and creators interact with agents and creators are rewarded for the agents they build.
2. **Agent-centric model training and inference on distributed GPUs** — the compute side: a decentralised-GPU layer that trains and serves the models behind the agents, aligning infrastructure (GPU) providers through shared participation. This places COAI in the [[decentralized-ai]] / DePIN-adjacent compute corner as well as the agent corner.
3. **AI-native blockchain** — an L1 settlement layer for **verifiable ownership, attribution, and transparent participation**, so contributions (data, models, agents, compute) are tracked and rewarded on-chain.

The unifying thesis is to align **users, developers, and infrastructure providers** through shared participation mechanisms — an "open, collaborative AI" stack rather than a single agent or model. Docs/whitepaper are at `paper.chainopera.ai`.

**Verification caveat:** the scope is ambitious (decentralized compute + agent marketplace + L1 simultaneously); independent verification of live, at-scale infrastructure is **not present** in the wiki's ingested data. Weigh the roadmap against delivery — this is a real, tradeable project, but its three-pillar stack is largely a stated design rather than confirmed production infrastructure. Execution risk is correspondingly high.

## Tokenomics & supply

- **Float:** 188M circulating / 1.0B total / 1.0B max — ~19% in circulation.
- **MC/FDV = 0.19:** ~812M tokens still locked/uncirculated; significant future supply pressure.
- **Capped at 1B:** fixed max supply (no open-ended inflation), but emissions to GPU providers / agent creators (if any) and investor/team unlocks are the dilution variables to watch.

The ~19% float is among the tightest in its batch, which cuts both ways: it can amplify upside on thin liquidity, but it also means the **bulk of supply is still to enter circulation**, a structural headwind that can cap rallies as vesting proceeds. Always value COAI on FDV (~$347M), not the ~$65M circulating cap.

## Value accrual / governance

In ChainOpera's framing, COAI is the coordination unit across all three pillars: it is meant to reward GPU providers and agent creators, settle activity on the AI-native L1, and underpin the attribution/ownership accounting. A verified, audited fee-capture or burn mechanism is **not confirmed** in the wiki's ingested data, and no formal on-chain governance framework is confirmed here. Treat value accrual as design intent: *if* the compute + marketplace + L1 stack achieves real usage, COAI captures throughput; absent that, it trades as a high-beta AI-infrastructure narrative token. ## Narrative / category & catalysts

COAI sits in the **AI-native infrastructure** corner of the [[ai-agents|AI-agent meta]] (decentralized compute + agent marketplace + L1), thematically near [[fetch-ai]] and the agent-framework names like [[unifai-network|UAI]]. This is one of the most narrative-sensitive baskets in crypto: tokens re-rate hard on AI-meta enthusiasm and Binance-ecosystem catalysts, then collapse when attention rotates — the ~-99% fall from the October 2025 ATH and the -8.83% 7d are illustrative.

Plausible catalysts: demonstrable usage of the app/marketplace or GPU layer, a mainnet/L1 milestone, additional tier-1 CEX listings (it currently leans on a single primary CEX), or a broad AI-meta risk-on rotation. With macro in **Extreme Fear** (F&G 21 on 2026-06-22) but a long-horizon **bottoming / accumulation** regime (BTC ≈ $64.6K), speculative AI-infrastructure tokens face weak near-term appetite and elevated unlock risk — but are also high-beta candidates to lead a bounce if AI-meta sentiment turns.

## Risks

- **~19% float / ~5.3× FDV:** the largest dilution overhang in its batch; value on FDV, not circulating cap.
- **ATH artifact:** -99% from ATH likely reflects a thin listing print — do not treat the ATH as a recovery target.
- **Ambitious, unproven scope (high execution risk):** L1 + decentralized GPU + agent marketplace is hard to deliver simultaneously; vaporware risk is elevated.
- **Venue concentration:** primarily one CEX (Bitget) plus DEX liquidity — access and slippage risk.
- **No confirmed value-accrual loop:** fee/attribution mechanics are design intent, not demonstrated cash flow.
- **Macro / reflexivity:** bear regime (F&G 21) and AI-meta rotation risk; high-beta both ways.

## Trading playbook

High-beta, **ultra-low-float** AI-infrastructure token in a bear / Extreme-Fear but bottoming tape:

- **Value on FDV, trade the float:** the ~$65M cap understates true size (~$347M FDV); the tight ~19% float means liquidity is thin and price is easily moved — keep size small and expect [[slippage]].
- **Don't anchor to the ATH:** the $43.81 print is a listing artifact; frame upside relative to current/FDV levels, not a 100x "recovery."
- **Relative volume is a positive:** COAI had the most active two-way trading in its batch — useful for execution, but not a fundamentals signal on its own.
- **Catalyst-driven:** a verifiable mainnet/usage milestone or a second tier-1 CEX listing is the realistic re-rating trigger; until then manage as a narrative/momentum trade with stops.
- **Macro overlay:** scale exposure to the AI-meta sentiment turn — in continued Extreme Fear, low-float AI infra is among the first sold and the unlock overhang compounds downside.

Not financial advice; ultra-low-float AI tokens carry severe drawdown, dilution, and execution risk.
