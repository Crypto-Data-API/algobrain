CryptoPunks is a collection of 10,000 unique 24x24 pixel art characters on the [[ethereum]] blockchain, created by Larva Labs (Matt Hall and John Watkinson) in June 2017. Originally free to claim for anyone with an Ethereum wallet, CryptoPunks became the most iconic and culturally significant NFT collection in existence — the collection that defined the PFP (profile picture) NFT movement and inspired the ERC-721 token standard. CryptoPunks are to NFTs what [[bitcoin]] is to cryptocurrency: the original, the benchmark, and the cultural touchstone. The most expensive Punk ever sold — Punk #5822, an alien — fetched $23.7 million in February 2022.

> **Note — not a fungible token.** CryptoPunks is a **non-fungible collection** of 10,000 individually-priced assets, not a fungible cryptocurrency. It therefore has no coins/markets entry, no single price, market cap, or 24h volume in the way a token does. Value is expressed through a **[[nft-floor-price|floor price]]** (cheapest available Punk) and a rarity-tiered price curve. Figures below are historical, drawn from cited sales and prior market reporting; **current floor and volume should be verified live on a marketplace (e.g., OpenSea, Blur) or NFT data aggregator before relying on them** — NFT prices move quickly and are not captured in the fungible-token snapshot used for the wiki's coin pages.

## History

Matt Hall and John Watkinson, two Canadian software developers working under the name Larva Labs, created CryptoPunks as an experiment in digital scarcity and generative art. They wrote an algorithm that generated 10,000 unique 24x24 pixel characters — each with a distinct combination of attributes like hairstyles, accessories, and skin types. The characters were inspired by the London punk scene, cyberpunk fiction, and the electronic music group Daft Punk.

**Key dates:**

- **June 9, 2017**: CryptoPunks smart contract deployed to Ethereum. All 10,000 Punks were claimable for free — holders only paid gas fees (fractions of a dollar at the time). Larva Labs kept 1,000 Punks for themselves. The remaining 9,000 were claimed over the following days and weeks.
- **Late 2017**: An early secondary market developed. Punks traded for 1-10 ETH on Larva Labs' custom marketplace. Total trading volume was minimal.
- **2018-2019**: The crypto winter reduced activity to a trickle. Punks could be purchased for 1-3 ETH (~$100-$600) during this period. A small community of dedicated holders maintained interest.
- **January 2021**: The NFT boom begins. CryptoPunks floor price climbs past 10 ETH for the first time.
- **March 2021**: Beeple's $69.3 million Christie's sale drives mainstream attention to NFTs. CryptoPunk prices spike as collectors seek "blue-chip" NFTs.
- **June 2021**: Punk #7523 (alien, wearing a medical mask) sells at Sotheby's for **$11.75 million** — a landmark moment for NFTs entering the traditional art auction world.
- **August 2021**: Visa purchases Punk #7610 for approximately $150,000, signaling institutional interest. Floor prices exceed 80 ETH.
- **January 2022**: Floor price peaks at approximately 125 ETH (~$400,000+). CryptoPunks represent the most valuable NFT collection by total market capitalization.
- **February 12, 2022**: Punk #5822 (alien) sells for **$23.7 million** (8,000 ETH) — the most expensive CryptoPunk ever sold.
- **March 11, 2022**: **Yuga Labs** (creators of [[bored-ape-yacht-club|Bored Ape Yacht Club]]) acquires CryptoPunks and Meebits intellectual property from Larva Labs for an undisclosed sum. Yuga Labs grants commercial IP rights to individual Punk holders, aligning with the BAYC model.
- **2022-2023**: Floor prices decline alongside the broader NFT market but CryptoPunks retain their status as the benchmark blue-chip collection.
- **2024-2026**: CryptoPunks continue to trade as the "store of value" in the NFT market. While far below 2022 peak floors, they remain the most recognized and culturally significant NFT collection globally. Through the prolonged NFT bear market, Punks have consistently held a higher floor and lower relative drawdown than most PFP peers, reinforcing their "blue-chip" status even as overall NFT trading volumes collapsed from their 2021–2022 highs. (Current floor varies — verify on marketplace.)

## Collection Details

- **Blockchain**: [[ethereum]]
- **Token standard**: Custom contract (predates ERC-721; the CryptoPunks contract inspired the creation of the ERC-721 standard)
- **Total supply**: 10,000 unique characters
- **Image size**: 24x24 pixels
- **Mint price**: Free (gas only) — June 2017
- **Creator**: Larva Labs (Matt Hall & John Watkinson)
- **Current IP owner**: Yuga Labs (since March 2022)

### Attributes

Each Punk has 0-7 attributes drawn from a set of 87 possible traits including:
- Accessories: pipe, cigarette, earring, clown nose
- Hair/hats: mohawk, beanie, top hat, bandana, wild hair
- Facial features: big beard, mole, spots, rosy cheeks
- Eyewear: 3D glasses, VR, classic shades, nerd glasses

"Attribute-less" Punks (those with zero attributes) are paradoxically rare and valuable, as are Punks with 7 attributes (the maximum). The rarest attribute combinations, combined with rare types (alien, ape, zombie), determine the most valuable individual Punks.

## Cultural Significance

**The OG NFT**: CryptoPunks is the collection that defined what an NFT collection is. The 10,000-piece generative PFP model that CryptoPunks established became the template for thousands of subsequent projects — [[bored-ape-yacht-club|BAYC]], Azuki, Doodles, and virtually every PFP collection that followed.

**Inspired ERC-721**: The CryptoPunks contract, while predating the ERC-721 standard, directly inspired its creation. Dieter Shirley (who developed the ERC-721 standard while working on [[cryptokitties|CryptoKitties]]) has cited CryptoPunks as a key influence. Every ERC-721 NFT that exists is, in a sense, a descendant of CryptoPunks.

**Fine art crossover**: CryptoPunks were among the first NFTs to cross into the traditional art world. The Sotheby's and Christie's auctions in 2021 legitimized NFTs as a category within fine art collecting. The Centre Pompidou in Paris acquired CryptoPunks for its permanent collection in 2023.

**Social identity**: Using a CryptoPunk as a Twitter profile picture became one of the strongest status signals in crypto culture. Owners included Jay-Z, Snoop Dogg, Serena Williams, and numerous crypto-native celebrities and venture capitalists. The Punk you chose (and could afford) signaled your place in the crypto social hierarchy.

**Provenance moat**: CryptoPunks' June 2017 creation date gives the collection an unassailable historical advantage. No Ethereum NFT project (aside from [[curio-cards|Curio Cards]], which has a May 2017 claim) can match its on-chain age. This provenance premium has proven durable even as newer collections have offered "better" art, more utility, or larger communities.

## Market Structure & Trading

**Liquidity**: CryptoPunks have among the highest liquidity of any individual NFT collection. They trade on a native marketplace, [[opensea|OpenSea]], Blur, and through OTC deals. Even during bear markets, multiple Punks trade in most weeks. However, liquidity is still **orders of magnitude lower than fungible tokens** — each Punk is unique, order books are thin, and large sales can move the [[nft-floor-price|floor]]. This is the defining structural difference between an NFT collection and a coin: there is no continuous deep order book, only a sparse set of unique listings.

**Marketplaces & aggregators**: CryptoPunks' custom contract predates ERC-721 and historically required marketplace-specific support; the native (Larva Labs, now under Yuga Labs) marketplace remains active, and OpenSea and Blur support Punk trading through wrapper contracts or direct integration. Price discovery is increasingly mediated by **[[nft-aggregators|NFT aggregators]]** (e.g., Blur, Gem, Reservoir-powered front-ends) that sweep listings across venues and surface the live floor, recent sales, and rarity rankings. Anyone valuing Punks should read the floor and depth from these tools rather than a single marketplace, since listings fragment across venues.

**Floor vs. rarity pricing**: The CryptoPunks market has a steep price curve based on rarity. **Floor Punks** (common male/female with common attributes) trade near the [[nft-floor-price|floor price]], while rare **types** (alien, ape, zombie) trade at massive premiums — aliens have sold for 100x+ the floor. Attribute count and rare attribute combinations create additional price tiers, so a single "floor price" understates the dispersion within the collection: the floor is the *entry ticket*, not the *average Punk*.

**Holder demographics**: A mix of long-term "OG" holders (some who claimed Punks for free in 2017), institutional buyers (Visa, hedge funds), and high-net-worth crypto natives. The community is generally sophisticated and long-term oriented, with lower turnover than newer collections.

**IP rights**: Since Yuga Labs' acquisition in March 2022, individual Punk holders have commercial IP rights to their specific Punk's image. This enables merchandise, derivatives, and commercial use — though in practice, fewer Punk holders have commercialized their Punks compared to BAYC holders.

**Tax and regulatory**: High-value Punk transactions are subject to capital gains taxes in most jurisdictions. The IRS and other tax authorities have been increasingly focused on NFT transactions, and CryptoPunks' high values and public sales make them visible targets for tax enforcement.

## Risks

CryptoPunks carry a distinct risk profile from fungible tokens — most of it flows from non-fungibility and the immaturity of the NFT market:

- **Illiquidity**: Each Punk is unique; there is no continuous order book. Selling at or near the quoted [[nft-floor-price|floor]] can take time, and forced/fast sales typically execute below floor. Bid-ask spreads on individual Punks are wide, and the listed floor is an *ask*, not a guaranteed exit.
- **Floor-price volatility**: NFT floors are far more volatile and gap-prone than large-cap token prices. A single large listing or distressed seller can reset the floor sharply; the floor is also denominated in ETH, so holders carry **ETH price exposure** on top of collection-specific risk.
- **Marketplace & smart-contract risk**: Trading relies on marketplaces and wrapper contracts; bugs, exploits, or marketplace insolvency can impair access or custody. The custom (pre-ERC-721) contract also means tooling support is non-standard.
- **Royalty / business-model risk**: The NFT market's shift toward optional or zero royalties (driven by aggregators like Blur) has pressured creator economics across the sector; changes in royalty enforcement affect the broader collection economy and Yuga Labs' incentives.
- **Concentration & wash-trading**: Reported NFT volumes have historically included significant wash trading; on-chain "volume" should be treated cautiously. Holder concentration among a few large collectors can also amplify floor moves.
- **Narrative / demand risk**: Punk value is heavily provenance- and status-driven. A durable loss of cultural relevance, a broader NFT-demand collapse, or migration of status signaling to other assets would weigh on price with no cash-flow backstop.
- **Regulatory & tax risk**: High-value, publicly-visible sales make Punks salient for tax authorities; evolving NFT classification (security vs. collectible) is an open regulatory question in several jurisdictions.
