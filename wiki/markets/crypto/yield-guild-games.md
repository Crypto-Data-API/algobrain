# Yield Guild Games

**Yield Guild Games** (YGG) is a Web3 gaming guild and [[dao|DAO]] that pioneered the [[play-to-earn]] "scholarship" model: the guild acquires in-game [[nft|NFT]] assets and lends them to players who otherwise could not afford the entry cost, sharing the resulting yield. The YGG token is a [[governance-token|governance]] and staking token. Today YGG positions itself as a community-based user-acquisition and questing platform for Web3 games.

## Overview

Yield Guild Games (YGG) is a [[dao|decentralized autonomous organization]] that invests in and manages [[nft|NFT]] assets used in blockchain games and virtual worlds. Founded in 2020 with roots in the Philippines, YGG rose to prominence during the 2021 [[play-to-earn]] boom — most famously through *Axie Infinity*, where the guild bought breeding/battle "Axie" NFTs and lent them to players (called **scholars**). Scholars earned in-game tokens and split the proceeds with the guild and asset managers. This **scholarship model** lowered the capital barrier to P2E and, at its peak, provided meaningful income to players in emerging markets.

### Structure

YGG operates a main DAO plus **SubDAOs** — regional or game-specific guilds (e.g., country-focused guilds and titles like Axie) with their own assets and tokens — and **vaults** that let YGG holders [[staking|stake]] toward specific reward streams. More recently the guild has pivoted toward questing and reputation: programs such as the **Guild Advancement Program (GAP)**, Superquests, and on-chain achievement/reputation systems aim to position YGG as a community-driven **user-acquisition** layer for Web3 games rather than purely a yield farm.

### Token role

The **YGG** token (1 billion max supply) is the guild's [[governance-token|governance token]]:

- **Governance** — voting on treasury allocation, partnerships, and guild direction.
- **Staking** — staking into YGG vaults to earn rewards tied to specific guild activities or revenue streams.
- **Access / coordination** — used across questing, rewards, and SubDAO coordination.

## The Play-to-Earn Cycle: Boom and Bust (handled honestly)

YGG is inseparable from the 2021–2022 [[play-to-earn]] cycle, and an honest account is essential to understanding the token:

- **The boom (2021):** P2E games — led by *Axie Infinity* — generated genuine income for players, and YGG's scholarship model scaled rapidly. YGG's token reached an all-time high around **$11** in November 2021.
- **The bust (2022 onward):** The dominant P2E designs proved economically unsustainable. Most relied on **inflationary token emissions** and a steady influx of *new* buyers to pay *existing* earners — a structure widely criticized as Ponzi-like or "play-to-earn that becomes play-to-quit." When new-user growth stalled, in-game token prices collapsed, scholar earnings evaporated, and demand for guild assets fell. *Axie*'s economy in particular contracted sharply (compounded by the March 2022 Ronin bridge hack affecting its ecosystem). YGG's token fell roughly 99% from its peak.
- **The pivot (post-2022):** The industry narrative shifted from "play-to-earn" toward "play-and-own" / sustainable game economies. YGG re-oriented toward questing, reputation, and helping games acquire engaged users — an attempt to find durable utility beyond pure yield extraction.

The lesson generally drawn: returns that depend on perpetual new-capital inflows and high token emissions decay once growth slows. See [[play-to-earn]] and [[gamefi|GameFi]].

## How the Guild Model Works (Architecture)

YGG is best understood as a **community-owned investment-and-coordination layer for Web3 games**, structured as a [[dao|DAO]] holding productive game assets and an evolving questing/reputation network on top.

- **Main DAO + treasury.** The core YGG DAO holds the guild's treasury — a portfolio of [[nft|NFT]] game assets, partner tokens, and capital. Token holders govern how that treasury is deployed across games and SubDAOs.
- **SubDAOs.** Semi-autonomous guilds organised by **region** (e.g., country-specific guilds, historically strong in Southeast Asia) or by **game** (e.g., an Axie-focused SubDAO). Each can hold its own assets and, in some designs, issue its own token, while remaining linked to the main DAO. This lets local communities manage their own players and economics while sharing in the broader network.
- **Vaults.** YGG **vaults** let token holders [[staking|stake]] YGG toward specific reward streams tied to particular guild activities or revenue sources — a way to express which parts of the guild's activity a holder wants exposure to, rather than one undifferentiated yield.
- **The scholarship mechanism (legacy core).** The guild buys game NFTs (e.g., Axies) and lends them to **scholars** who cannot afford the upfront cost. Scholars play, earn in-game tokens, and split proceeds with the guild and a community **manager** who trains and supports them — a three-way revenue split. This lowered the P2E entry barrier and, at peak, delivered real income in emerging markets (and exposed those same players to the bust).
- **The questing/reputation pivot (current core).** Programs like the **Guild Advancement Program (GAP)**, **Superquests**, and on-chain achievement/reputation systems re-cast YGG as a **user-acquisition and engagement layer**: games pay/partner with YGG to bring engaged, verified players and to run quests, with on-chain reputation tracking contribution. The bet is that durable value comes from *coordinating players and proving engagement*, not from extracting yield from inflationary game tokens.

## Governance & Value Accrual

- **Governance.** YGG (1B max supply) is the guild's [[governance-token|governance token]]: holders vote on treasury allocation, partnerships, SubDAO support, and strategic direction. Governance over a real NFT/token treasury makes the token a claim on guild decision-making, not just a meme.
- **Value-accrual channels.** Value can flow to YGG via (a) **vault staking** rewards tied to specific guild revenue streams, (b) appreciation/yield of the **treasury's NFT and token holdings**, and (c) any fees/partnership revenue from the questing/user-acquisition business. Each of these is contingent on GameFi demand recovering — accrual is real but cyclical and unproven at scale post-pivot.
- **Supply overhang.** With ~731M of a 1B max in circulation, remaining unlocks/emissions are a structural sell-pressure factor (relevant to the -6.64% single-day move). The treasury itself is also exposed to the **mark-to-market value of game NFTs**, which can fall sharply with the underlying games.

## Competitive Position & Risks

YGG remains one of the best-known gaming-guild brands, with a global network of regional guilds and partnerships across many blockchain games and infrastructure projects. Its future depends on whether Web3 games can produce sustainable economies and whether guild/questing infrastructure adds lasting value.

Key risks:

- **Gaming-narrative dependence** — YGG is a high-beta proxy for [[gamefi|GameFi]]/[[play-to-earn]] sentiment, which remains depressed in the current bear regime.
- **Sustainability of the model** — the original scholarship economics broke once emissions outpaced real demand; the questing/UA pivot is still proving itself.
- **Emissions / token unlocks** — with circulating supply well below the 1B max, future unlocks add potential sell pressure.
- **Treasury / NFT asset risk** — the guild's balance sheet includes game NFTs whose value can fall sharply with the underlying games.
- **Low liquidity** — at a ~$21M market cap, YGG is small-cap and prone to outsized volatility on modest flows.
