# Popcat

**Popcat** (ticker **POPCAT**) is a [[solana|Solana]]-based [[meme-coins|meme coin]] built around the viral "Pop Cat" internet meme — a cat named Oatmeal photoshopped to open its mouth in a wide "O" shape. It has no utility, product, or cash flows; it is a pure attention/community asset whose value is driven entirely by social momentum, speculation, and Solana meme-sector rotation. POPCAT is one of the better-known cat-themed memes in the Solana ecosystem alongside dog-themed names like [[bonk|BONK]] and [[dogwifhat|dogwifhat]].

## Technology / Protocol

There is no protocol. POPCAT is an [[spl-token|SPL token]] on [[solana|Solana]] — a single fixed-supply token contract with no smart-contract logic, treasury, or roadmap. Its "technology" is entirely the [[solana|Solana]] base layer: cheap (sub-cent) fees and fast (~400ms) block times that make rapid speculative trading economical for retail, which is the precondition for Solana's meme-coin culture. The contract is `7GCihgDB8fe6KNjn2MYtkzZcRjQy3t9GHdC8uHYmW2hr` on Solana.

## Market Structure & Derivatives

- **Spot (DEX):** Solana DEXs are the native venues — Orca and Raydium carry the deepest SOL-quoted liquidity.
- **Spot (CEX):** Listed on Tier-1/2 centralized exchanges including [[kraken|Kraken]] (POPCAT/USD), KuCoin (POPCAT/USDT) and Crypto.com.
- **Derivatives:** POPCAT trades as a perpetual on [[hyperliquid|Hyperliquid]] (POPCAT-PERP) and other meme-friendly perp venues. Meme perps are notorious for violent funding swings — funding can flip sharply positive when crowds chase longs (then flush) and sharply negative on capitulation. Open interest is thin and unstable relative to majors, so liquidation cascades are common; check live funding/OI before sizing.

## Use Case / Narrative / Category

POPCAT is a **Solana meme coin** — its "category" is attention. There is no roadmap, revenue, or protocol; the thesis is purely **reflexive**: virality drives buying, buying drives price, price drives more attention. Popcat benefits from the broader Solana meme-coin flywheel (cheap, fast transactions and a deep retail trading culture) and from being an established, recognizable "cat" brand in a sector that rotates between dog and cat narratives. It is frequently grouped with community-favorite "blue-chip memes" and has appeared in curated meme baskets and on exchange "alpha"/spotlight lists.

## Valuation Framing

Meme coins cannot be valued on cash flows or fundamentals — there are none. The only honest framing is **reflexive and relative**: price is a function of attention, and "valuation" reduces to (a) where POPCAT trades versus its own history (down ~98% from ATH; up ~1,066% from its ATL), and (b) its market cap versus other Solana memes as a gauge of relative community mindshare. With supply fully circulating, market cap equals FDV, so there is no dilution discount to apply. Treat any "fair value" as the price the next buyer will pay — i.e., pure speculation, not investment with intrinsic value.

## Risks

- **No intrinsic value / total-loss risk:** memes can and do go to near-zero; there is no cash flow or product to support a floor.
- **Reflexivity & sentiment:** value depends entirely on attention; when narrative fades, liquidity and price collapse together.
- **High beta to risk-off regimes:** in **extreme fear** / **bear-market** conditions (Fear & Greed = 23 as of 2026-06-21), speculative memes are typically the first and hardest sold.
- **Liquidity & manipulation:** thin order books and concentrated holders make POPCAT susceptible to wash trading, pump-and-dump dynamics, and perp liquidation cascades.
- **Solana-ecosystem dependency:** outages, congestion, or a loss of Solana retail interest directly impair meme-sector liquidity. See [[solana]].

> Cryptocurrency is highly volatile and speculative; meme coins are among the riskiest assets in the market. Nothing here is financial advice. Always verify live data before trading.
