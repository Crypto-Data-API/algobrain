## Architecture / What It Does

Audiera is a dual-platform Web3 rhythm game built on [[bnb|BNB Chain]], billed as the Web3 evolution of the classic "Audition" dance game. Its design layers a game loop on top of a token economy:

- **Mobile dance-rhythm game** — the flagship experience, an "Audition"-style music/dance game where players time inputs to music.
- **Telegram mini-game** — a lightweight, viral on-ramp leveraging Telegram's distribution (a common 2024-25 GameFi user-acquisition tactic, popularised by tap-to-earn titles).
- **Web3 economy** — NFTs (characters/items), AI-driven interaction, and the **BEAT** token for in-game rewards, purchases, and a [[play-to-earn]] loop.

**Token mechanics & the GameFi flywheel.** BEAT is a BNB Chain **BEP-20** (contract `0xcf32...3e36`). The intended flywheel is: players earn BEAT and NFTs by playing, spend to upgrade/compete, and new users are pulled in via the Telegram mini-app and [[non-fungible-token|NFT]] sales. The structural weakness — endemic to [[play-to-earn]] economies — is that durability depends on **real, retained players versus mercenary reward-farmers**. When token rewards (sell pressure) outpace genuine demand to play, the economy enters a death spiral: token price falls, rewards lose value, players leave, and price falls further. BEAT's **-75.91% week** is a textbook expression of that dynamic, amplified by a large emissions overhang (~71% of supply uncirculated) feeding the play-to-earn reward pool.

### Value Accrual & Governance

BEAT accrues value almost entirely through **in-game utility**: rewards earned by playing, spending to purchase/upgrade NFTs and items, and access to competitive/social features. Unlike an infrastructure token, there is no fee-burn-from-external-usage backstop — demand is endogenous to the game. This makes the token's value reflexive: it rises with player growth and falls hard when growth stalls, because emissions keep printing sell pressure into a shrinking buyer base. Any governance role is secondary to this play-to-earn utility, and should not be overstated absent a verified source. The combination of **~71% uncirculated supply + reward emissions + reflexive demand** is the core fundamental risk and the most likely driver of the recent collapse.
