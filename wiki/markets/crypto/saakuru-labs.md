## What Saakuru does

Saakuru is a **gasless, consumer-focused [[layer-2]]** built on the **OP Stack** (the same modular rollup framework behind Optimism and Base). Its defining feature is **zero transaction fees for end users**: instead of users paying gas per action, the network runs a credit/sponsorship model where applications underwrite usage — conceptually similar in spirit to [[account-abstraction]] / gas-sponsorship, removing the single biggest UX hurdle for mainstream (Web2) audiences. The aim is to let any product add on-chain features without forcing users to acquire a gas token or manage wallets directly.

Around the chain, **Saakuru Labs** ships a **Developer Suite** of interoperable modules so teams can embed Web3 quickly:

- **Mobile Wallet SDK** — embedded, abstracted wallets inside consumer apps.
- **NFT management platform** — minting and asset management.
- **Blockchain data API** — indexed on-chain data access.
- **Gamification API** — rewards/loyalty primitives for consumer engagement.

The project's stated traction (e.g. "more than 1.4 million transactions/week," a "top-20 chain by weekly transactions") is **project-stated marketing** and should be treated as unverified rather than as confirmed on-chain fact.

## Token role

**SKR** is a multi-purpose **utility and governance** token. Per the project, its economics rest on:

- **Burn mechanism** — supply is reduced over time via transaction fees, developer profits, and governance actions (a deflationary sink).
- **Staking for the credit system** — developers stake SKR to obtain credits that sustain the gasless environment and lower their operational costs, tying token demand to ecosystem activity.
- **Governance** — holders participate in protocol decisions.

Supply is partially circulating (~702M of ~1B max; circulating below total), so unlocks remain a dilution factor. The token has experienced a severe long-term drawdown (down ~99.9% from its 2024 ATH and ~96% over the trailing year per the prior ingest), and at sub-$0.0001 it is a deep microcap.

### Value accrual & governance (SKR)

SKR's design ties token demand to the **gasless-credit economy** of the chain:

- **Developer staking → credits** — apps stake SKR to mint credits that underwrite users' zero-fee transactions. This is the primary demand driver: more sponsored activity should require more staked SKR.
- **Burn sink** — supply is reduced via transaction fees, developer profits and governance actions (project-stated deflation).
- **Governance** — holders vote on protocol decisions, giving SKR a control role beyond pure utility.
- The fragility: the credit model is **reflexive** — a collapsing token price (down ~99.9% from ATH) makes staking-funded credits cheaper to deplete and harder to replenish, weakening the very mechanism that funds free gas. Value accrual depends on sponsored-transaction growth outpacing this decay.

## Competitive position

Saakuru competes in the **consumer/gaming app-chain and gasless-UX** niche against other OP Stack chains and "Web2-to-Web3 onboarding" platforms — for example Immutable and other gaming L2s, plus account-abstraction/paymaster stacks that deliver gasless UX on existing chains. Its angle is a turnkey developer suite plus a fully gasless end-user experience to court mainstream consumer apps. The challenge is severe: the L2 market is saturated, gasless UX is increasingly available as a feature elsewhere via [[account-abstraction]], and Saakuru's token has lost the vast majority of its value, which complicates the staking-driven credit economics it depends on.

## Risks

- **Severe price decay** — down ~99.9% from ATH; a collapsing token undermines the staking/credit model that funds the gasless system.
- **Sustainability of "free" gas** — someone must pay for execution; if app sponsorship or token-funded credits dry up, the zero-fee model is hard to maintain.
- **Crowded category** — many OP Stack chains and AA-based gasless solutions compete for the same consumer/gaming developers.
- **Unverified traction claims** — transaction/throughput figures are project-stated marketing.
- **Deep microcap / liquidity** — sub-$0.0001 price, ~$26M cap, and an unavailable 24h volume/print at the snapshot indicate very thin trading.
