# Animecoin

**Animecoin** (ANIME) is the community and culture token of the **Azuki** ecosystem — one of the most prominent Web3 anime/[[nft|NFT]] brands. Issued largely via airdrop to Azuki NFT holders and the wider anime community, ANIME is positioned as the economic and governance layer for what Azuki calls "Anime 2.0": turning anime fans from passive consumers into participants who help fund, shape, and own anime culture. It launched in early 2025 and runs primarily on [[arbitrum|Arbitrum One]], with an [[ethereum|Ethereum]] deployment.

## Overview

Backed by [[azuki|Azuki]], a leading Web3 anime brand, ANIME aims to turn anime fans into participants in a community-driven creative economy rather than passive consumers — a positioning the project brands as "Anime 2.0," in which creators and fans collaborate to fund and shape new anime IP. Animecoin sits at the intersection of [[nft|NFT]] culture, fandom, and meme/community-token dynamics; aggregators tag it as both an NFT-project token and a meme/anime-themed token.

---

## Architecture / how it works

ANIME is an ERC-20 fungible token with its canonical deployment on [[arbitrum|Arbitrum One]] (Arbitrum being Azuki's chosen low-fee L2 for ecosystem activity) and a bridged [[ethereum|Ethereum]] mainnet deployment for liquidity and CEX custody. It is not a protocol with its own consensus or smart-contract product surface — there is no Animecoin chain, AMM, or lending market. Instead, ANIME is the **culture/utility token layer** that the Azuki team and the **Anime Foundation** (the steward entity behind the "Anime 2.0" thesis) wrap around the existing Azuki IP, which comprises several flagship NFT collections:

- **Azuki** — the original 10,000-piece PFP collection on Ethereum (minted 2022).
- **Azuki Elementals** — a large 2023 expansion collection.
- **BEANZ** — the companion/sidekick collection that often functions as the "free float" of Azuki engagement.

The token's function is to give that NFT-anchored community a single fungible unit of account for participation: airdrop distributions to holders, allocations to anime-creator funding, event/gating access, and (per the project's framing) governance signaling over which anime IP gets funded. Because the value sits in branding and community coordination rather than enforceable cash-flow rights, ANIME is best modeled as a **reflexive fandom/meme token** whose price tracks Azuki brand momentum and the broader [[nft|NFT]]/anime cycle rather than protocol revenue.

## Token role

ANIME functions as the ecosystem and culture token of the Azuki universe:

- **Community / fandom currency** — the medium for participating in Azuki's anime-culture initiatives, drops, and creative funding.
- **Governance / ownership signal** — intended to give holders a voice in shaping the ecosystem's direction ("fans own the future of anime culture").
- **Access** — used to access ecosystem activities, events, and NFT-linked experiences.

Tokenomics show meaningful future supply expansion: at the earlier snapshot ~5.54B of a 10B max supply was circulating (MC/FDV ≈ 0.55), so roughly 45% of supply remained to be released — a dilution consideration for holders.

### Value accrual / utility

ANIME has **no contractual claim** on Azuki royalties, the Anime Foundation treasury, or future IP revenue. Value accrues only indirectly, through three channels:

1. **Attention/brand premium** — when Azuki ships a successful drop, anime collaboration, or media moment, speculative demand rotates into the ecosystem token.
2. **Holder gating and airdrop loops** — holding ANIME (or Azuki/BEANZ NFTs) can confer access to drops and allocations, creating a reflexive "hold-to-farm" incentive.
3. **Governance signaling** — soft voice over creative-funding direction, which is brand-engagement value rather than enforceable control.

This is the standard weakness of fandom tokens: the upside is real during hype, but there is no floor from cash flows, so drawdowns (here ~97% from ATH) are violent.

## How & where it trades

ANIME has unusually broad market access for a ~$16M-cap token, a legacy of the strength of the Azuki brand and the Binance HODLer-airdrop listing:

- **Centralized:** Binance (ANIME/USDT), Kraken (ANIME/USD), Upbit (ANIME/KRW — Korean retail access), Bitget, KuCoin, Crypto.com Exchange. The Upbit KRW pair in particular exposes ANIME to Korean retail sentiment, which can drive outsized intraday swings.
- **Decentralized:** Uniswap V3 on Ethereum (ANIME/WETH) and on-chain Arbitrum pools for the native deployment.
- **Derivatives:** a [[hyperliquid|Hyperliquid]] ANIME-PERP, which is rare at this cap and lets traders lever or hedge directionally — but also means funding-rate and liquidation-cascade dynamics amplify spot moves.

Reported 24h spot volume (~$8.27M at the earlier snapshot) is high relative to the ~$16M cap, but this turnover is concentrated on a handful of CEX pairs; effective depth thins out quickly for larger size. Treat displayed market cap as an upper bound on realizable value and budget for [[slippage]].

## Competitive Position

ANIME's competitive moat is the strength of the Azuki brand and its NFT community, which is among the more recognizable in Web3 culture. It competes for attention with other culture/IP and anime-themed tokens and with the broader meme-token market. Unlike a pure protocol, its value is heavily tied to community sentiment, the continued relevance of the Azuki brand, and the project's ability to convert fandom into recurring on-chain activity. The token is liquid on major venues (Binance, Kraken, Upbit, Bitget, KuCoin, Crypto.com) and has a Hyperliquid perpetual, giving it more market access than most ~$16M-cap tokens.

## Risks

- **Narrative / hype dependence** — as a culture and meme-adjacent token, ANIME's price is driven by sentiment and the Azuki brand's momentum rather than cash flows. It has fallen ~97% from its January 2025 ATH, illustrating how quickly fandom-driven valuations deflate.
- **Emissions / dilution** — substantial uncirculated supply (~45% at the earlier snapshot) implies ongoing unlocks and sell pressure.
- **Brand / IP single-point risk** — value is concentrated in one brand (Azuki); any reputational damage or loss of cultural relevance directly impairs the token.
- **Risk-off sensitivity** — in Extreme Fear (F&G 21), speculative culture tokens are among the first sold; broad-market weakness amplifies ANIME drawdowns.
- **Utility ambiguity** — beyond community participation and governance signaling, concrete must-have utility is limited, which is common for fandom tokens.

## Trading Profile

### Venues & liquidity

ANIME is tradable across a genuinely deep two-venue derivatives-and-spot footprint that is unusual for a ~#949 market-cap name:

- **Binance** — spot (ANIME/USDT) plus a **USD-margined perpetual**, giving both a cash leg and a levered/hedgeable perp on the deepest CEX orderbook. This is the primary price-discovery venue and the anchor for any cross-venue basis or funding work.
- **[[hyperliquid|Hyperliquid]]** — **ANIME-PERP**, offering up to **~40-50x** leverage with fully on-chain funding, mark, and open-interest data.

The result is a **deep, liquid two-venue market** with tight top-of-book on both perps and a real spot leg on Binance. Practically, dual venue availability means execution can be split across Binance and Hyperliquid to reduce impact, and it opens genuine cross-venue plays (funding divergence, spot-vs-perp basis) that single-venue small caps cannot support. Still, effective depth thins out for larger clips — the displayed cap is small — so size to L2 depth, work orders with limits, and avoid market orders that walk the book during volatility.

### Applicable strategies

- [[hl-vs-cex-funding-divergence]] — ANIME runs a Binance USD-margined perp AND a Hyperliquid perp simultaneously, so funding can diverge between the two venues; harvest the spread by going long the cheaper-funding leg and short the richer.
- [[cash-and-carry]] — with a Binance spot leg plus perps on both venues, positive perp funding lets you hold spot ANIME and short the perp to capture carry with delta neutralized.
- [[funding-rate-harvest]] — a high-beta memecoin perp like ANIME frequently prints extreme funding during narrative spikes; collect funding by taking the counter-positioned perp leg.
- [[liquidation-cascade-fade]] — thin effective depth plus 40-50x Hyperliquid leverage makes ANIME prone to violent stop-runs; fade the wick after a liquidation flush once open interest resets.
- [[crowded-long-funding-fade]] — Azuki brand catalysts pull in leveraged retail longs on ANIME; when funding turns sharply positive with rising OI, fade the crowded long into exhaustion.
- [[meme-coin-cycle]] — ANIME is a reflexive anime/NFT culture token; position around the sentiment-driven pump-and-fade rhythm typical of the memecoin cohort rather than fundamentals.

### Volatility & regime character

ANIME is a **high-beta, memecoin-style culture/NFT token** with pronounced reflexivity: price is driven by Azuki brand momentum, anime/NFT narrative cycles, and retail sentiment (including Korean retail via the Upbit KRW pair) rather than cash flows. It carries **high positive beta to BTC/ETH** — it sells off first and recovers last in risk-off tapes — but adds idiosyncratic, catalyst-driven spikes on top of that broad-market correlation. Expect large realized-volatility swings, sharp drawdowns (already ~97% from ATH), and liquidation-amplified moves on the perps.

### Risk flags

- **Liquidity/venue concentration** — despite two-venue coverage, effective depth is thin for size; a large share of turnover sits on a handful of CEX pairs, so impact and [[slippage]] rise quickly.
- **Token emissions/dilution** — roughly ~45% of max supply was still to unlock at the earlier snapshot, implying periodic sell pressure that can cap rallies.
- **Narrative dependence** — value hinges on the Azuki brand and the anime/NFT cycle; loss of cultural relevance or a reputational hit to the single underlying brand directly impairs the token.
- **Perp funding dislocations** — 40-50x Hyperliquid leverage plus dual-venue perps make funding prone to spikes and cross-venue divergence, and crowded leverage sets up cascade risk in both directions.
