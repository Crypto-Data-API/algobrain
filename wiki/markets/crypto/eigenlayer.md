# EigenCloud (prev. EigenLayer)

**EigenCloud** (ticker **EIGEN**, formerly **EigenLayer**) is the protocol that pioneered **[[restaking]]** on **[[ethereum]]** — a mechanism that lets staked ETH (and liquid staking tokens) be "re-used" to secure additional services beyond Ethereum consensus. Those services, called Actively Validated Services (AVSs), can be data-availability layers, oracles, bridges, co-processors, or verifiable-AI/compute networks. The project has rebranded toward "EigenCloud," a developer platform for building provably verifiable apps and AI services, powered by the EIGEN token.

EIGEN launched via a multi-season airdrop ("stakedrop") to restakers and AVS participants rather than a public sale. With circulating supply at roughly 40% of total supply, a substantial portion remains locked under team, investor, and ecosystem vesting schedules — a structural overhang because unlocks add sell-side pressure even when demand is flat. The token is a governance and "intersubjective" security asset: a novel forking mechanism allows the token itself to be used to slash and arbitrate faults that are observable to the community but not provable on-chain.

## Tokenomics, Valuation & Dilution

EIGEN's **MC/FDV is ~0.40** ($186.5M circulating vs $461.1M fully diluted), one of the more dilutive ratios in this peer set. Because there is no hard max supply, FDV understates true long-run issuance: the ~60% of total supply still locked (team, investor, ecosystem) represents a structural sell-side overhang that grinds against price even in flat-demand periods. A useful framing is **fee capture vs. valuation**: restaking's bull case rests on AVSs paying real fees back to restakers and, ultimately, to the EIGEN security model. To date, AVS fee revenue has been modest relative to the tens of billions in restaked TVL, so the token trades far more on narrative and unlock-flow dynamics than on a fee multiple. Until AVS economics mature, EIGEN is best understood as a high-beta call option on the restaking + verifiable-AI thesis rather than a cash-flow asset.

## Use Case & Narrative

EigenLayer's core innovation is **[[restaking]]**: ETH stakers opt into additional slashing conditions to provide "pooled security" to new services, earning extra yield in exchange for extra risk. This lets new protocols bootstrap economic security from Ethereum's ~$100B+ staked base instead of launching their own token-secured validator set from scratch. The category sits at the intersection of **[[proof-of-stake]]** infrastructure, **[[depin]]**-style verifiable services, and **[[decentralized-ai]]** (verifiable inference / co-processing) — all themes EigenCloud explicitly targets. CoinGecko tags it across Restaking, Infrastructure, Data Availability, AI, and the Ethereum/Base ecosystems.

## Risks

- **Unlock overhang** — ~60% of supply not yet circulating; scheduled vesting is a persistent headwind.
- **Slashing / smart-contract risk** — restaking concentrates correlated risk; a bug or correlated slashing event in an AVS can cascade to restakers.
- **Narrative dependency** — valuation leans heavily on the restaking and verifiable-AI narratives actually generating fee revenue; AVS fee capture to date has been modest relative to TVL.
- **Drawdown depth** — still ~95% below ATH; the June rally is a counter-trend move within an "Established Bear Market."
- **Centralization concerns** — restaking can recentralize Ethereum security around a few operators if pooled security dominates.
