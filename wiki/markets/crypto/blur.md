# Blur

**Blur** (ticker **BLUR**, [[ethereum|Ethereum]] [[erc-20|ERC-20]]) is the governance token of **Blur**, a pro-trader [[nft|NFT]] marketplace and aggregator that rose to prominence in early 2023 by combining zero marketplace fees, sweep/snipe tooling, and aggressive token incentives to capture market share from OpenSea. It is paired with **Blend**, a peer-to-peer [[nft-lending|NFT lending]] protocol built with [[paradigm]]. The BLUR token governs the protocol and has historically been distributed via airdrops and ongoing trading/lending incentives.

> **Disambiguation:** This page covers the **Blur NFT marketplace** token (`blur` on CoinGecko, ticker BLUR). It is unrelated to *Blend Labs* (the mortgage-tech company) or to any other "Blur"-named project.

## Technology & Protocol

Blur is an [[ethereum|Ethereum]]-based NFT **marketplace + aggregator** purpose-built for high-volume traders rather than casual collectors. Its design choices are what made it disruptive:

- **Aggregation** — Blur surfaces listings across marketplaces (OpenSea, LooksRare, X2Y2, etc.) so a trader can sweep the cheapest available NFTs in a collection in one transaction.
- **Pro trading tools** — floor sweeping, real-time depth/orderbook-style views, portfolio analytics, sniping, and batch listing/cancellation, optimized for gas efficiency.
- **Blur Bid pools** — pooled, collection-wide bids that give NFTs a continuous, fungible-like bid side. This is the liquidity primitive that turned illiquid JPEGs into something closer to an orderbook market.
- **Blend (Blur Lending)** — a peer-to-peer [[nft-lending|NFT-backed lending]] protocol co-developed with [[paradigm]]. Blend uses perpetual, oracle-free loans with off-chain order matching and on-chain settlement; either side can trigger a Dutch auction refinancing, and borrowers can buy NFTs on installment ("Buy Now, Pay Later"). Blend deepened NFTFi leverage and was a second front in Blur's incentive war.

The protocol is governed by the **Blur DAO**, with BLUR as the voting token.

## Narrative & Category

BLUR sits squarely in the **NFT marketplace + NFTFi** category on [[ethereum]]. Its categories include: NFT, Ethereum Ecosystem, NFT Marketplace, NFTFi, FTX Holdings, eGirl Capital Portfolio, [[paradigm|Paradigm Portfolio]], GMCI DeFi Index, GMCI Index, Made in USA, and Coinbase 50 Index.

The bull thesis is that Blur is the **dominant venue for serious NFT traders** and that Blend gives it a durable NFTFi flywheel; if NFT trading ever recovers, Blur is the highest-beta way to express it. The bear thesis is that Blur's prominence was **manufactured by token incentives**, that much measured volume was **wash-traded or farm-driven**, and that the optional-royalty model damaged creator relationships — a recurring NFT-space debate. The 2023 episode in which Blur **flipped OpenSea on Ethereum NFT volume** and forced OpenSea to cut fees remains a landmark case study in incentive-driven market-share capture.

## Valuation Framing (qualitative)

BLUR has no protocol cash flows accruing to the token (no fee switch live), so it cannot be valued on yield. It is best understood as a **leveraged call option on NFT trading activity** — among the weakest crypto verticals since 2022. Reasonable framing questions: (1) what is *organic* (non-incentivized) marketplace + Blend volume, and is it growing? (2) what governance value does the token carry if/when a fee switch is enabled? (3) how does it screen against NFTFi peers on volume-per-FDV? With the token at a fresh ATL and the sector in extreme fear, the market is pricing close to "incentive-farm with optionality, not a cash-flowing business."

## Risks

- **Incentive dependence** — much of Blur's historical volume was driven by token rewards; as emissions decline, organic (non-wash) demand becomes the key question.
- **NFT-sector beta** — BLUR is a leveraged bet on NFT trading activity, one of the weakest crypto verticals since 2022; it printed a **fresh ATL on 2026-06-19**.
- **Token unlock / supply** — though ~94% circulates, residual investor/team vesting plus incentive-pool emissions create ongoing sell-pressure risk (max supply 3.00B; ~2.83B circulating).
- **Royalty / reputation overhang** — the optional-royalty model and wash-trade criticism are reputational risks if creators and platforms migrate elsewhere.
- **Perp positioning whipsaw** — low float plus active perps on [[hyperliquid]] make BLUR prone to liquidation cascades in both directions.
- **No token cash flow** — without a live fee switch, BLUR holders capture governance rights but not protocol revenue.
- **Extreme-fear regime** — the **Fear & Greed 23 / "Established Bear Market"** backdrop (2026-06-20) is hostile to high-beta NFT tokens.

- **2022 (Oct)** — Blur marketplace launches, founded by "Pacman" (Tieshun Roquerre), backed by [[paradigm]] at a valuation exceeding $1 billion.
- **2023-02-14** — BLUR token launches via a large airdrop to active NFT traders; token reaches its all-time high near **$5.02** in the launch window.

## Trading Profile

**Venues & liquidity.** BLUR is tradable on **both** [[binance]] (BLUR/USDT spot **and** a USD-margined perpetual) and [[hyperliquid]] (**BLUR-PERP**, leverage up to ~40-50x). This is a genuinely two-venue derivatives market rather than a single-venue microcap: the Binance perp anchors CEX flow and price discovery, while the Hyperliquid perp offers on-chain, transparent [[funding-rate|funding]] and [[open-interest|OI]]. Depth is decent for the ~$45M cohort but the **float is thin**, so book depth thins quickly beyond the top of book. Practically, the two-venue structure supports size splitting, cross-venue [[funding-rate|funding]] comparison, and cash-and-carry construction, but it also means execution should be staged and size scaled to on-screen liquidity rather than headline turnover.

**Applicable strategies.**
- [[crowded-short-funding-fade]] — BLUR [[funding-rate|funding]] flips firmly negative during NFT-sector selloffs as shorts crowd in, setting up squeeze-prone fades against extreme negative funding.
- [[funding-rate-harvest]] — incentive-season hype can push funding sharply positive on a low-float name, letting a delta-neutral book collect elevated carry.
- [[hl-vs-cex-funding-divergence]] — funding on Hyperliquid BLUR-PERP and the Binance perp can diverge, since the two order books clear independent flow around catalysts.
- [[cash-and-carry]] — spot on Binance/Uniswap against a short perp captures basis/funding when BLUR trades in positive-funding, hype-driven regimes.
- [[liquidation-cascade-fade]] — thin float plus active perps make BLUR prone to two-sided liquidation cascades that overshoot and mean-revert, offering fade entries after wicks.
- [[oi-confirmed-trend]] — rising [[open-interest]] alongside directional moves helps separate real incentive-driven trends from noise on this reflexive small-cap.

**Volatility & regime character.** BLUR is a **high-beta NFT/NFTFi altcoin** on [[ethereum]] — effectively a leveraged call option on NFT trading activity, one of the weakest crypto verticals since 2022. It carries high [[ethereum|ETH]]/BTC beta on the downside, sells off hard in risk-off tape, and shows sharp, reflexive rallies around incentive seasons and NFT-narrative catalysts. Realized volatility is elevated versus large caps, and price action is strongly regime-dependent (see [[market-regime]]).

**Risk flags.**
- **Liquidity / thin float** — small cap and thin book depth mean sizing must respect on-screen liquidity; slippage rises fast in stress.
- **Narrative dependence** — value tracks the NFT/NFTFi narrative and Blur incentive programs rather than protocol cash flow (no live fee switch).
- **Emissions / supply overhang** — ongoing incentive-pool emissions plus residual vesting create sell-pressure even with ~94% of max supply circulating.
- **Perp funding dislocations** — low float plus swingy [[funding-rate|funding]] make BLUR prone to funding spikes and two-sided liquidation cascades on [[hyperliquid]] and Binance.
