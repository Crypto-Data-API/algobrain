# Raydium

**Raydium** (ticker **RAY**) is a leading automated market maker (AMM) and [[dex|decentralized exchange]] (DEX) built on the [[solana|Solana]] blockchain. It launched in 2021 with a first-mover advantage as an AMM that integrated with the Serum on-chain central limit order book. By 2025-2026 Raydium had evolved well beyond that original hybrid model into a Concentrated Liquidity (CLMM), launchpad (LaunchLab), and perpetuals platform, and remained one of the two dominant Solana DEXs alongside [[orca]].

## Overview

Raydium launched in 2021 as an automated market maker (AMM) and liquidity provider on the Solana blockchain. Its original design provided on-chain AMM liquidity that was also posted to the Serum central limit order book, so Raydium LPs got access to both AMM swaps and the orderbook's orderflow — a hybrid model unusual among AMMs at the time.

**Evolution to OpenBook then beyond it:** Raydium originally integrated with Serum's orderbook. After FTX collapsed in November 2022 and Serum was abandoned, the ecosystem migrated to **OpenBook**, a community fork of Serum, and Raydium routed orderbook liquidity through OpenBook. By 2025-2026, however, the orderbook leg had shrunk to a small residual of Raydium's activity (~2.76% of Q4 2025 volume), while Concentrated Liquidity (CLMM) pools came to dominate (~94.8%). Raydium today is best understood as a CLMM-first DEX with a launchpad and perpetuals, rather than a Serum/OpenBook hybrid.

Raydium's stated long-term goal remains capturing and maintaining a leadership position among AMMs and liquidity providers on [[solana]], leveraging Solana's throughput to drive the evolution of [[defi-lending|decentralized finance]] (DeFi).

## OpenBook Integration (now a minor leg)

Raydium's original architectural differentiator was its integration with **OpenBook** (and before it, Serum), the community-run central limit order book (CLOB) on [[solana]]. In that hybrid model:

1. **LP orders sit on the orderbook** -- Raydium AMM positions were represented as resting limit orders on OpenBook, so LPs earned from both AMM swaps and orderbook fills
2. **Tighter spreads** -- the orderbook component attracted market makers who posted competitive quotes, reducing execution costs
3. **Composability** -- any protocol that routes through OpenBook (including [[jupiter-exchange-solana|Jupiter]]) could access Raydium's liquidity

**As of 2026 this is no longer Raydium's primary engine.** OpenBook-routed volume fell to roughly 2.76% of Raydium's Q4 2025 trading volume, with CLMM pools accounting for ~94.8%. The hybrid orderbook design remains available but is now a small residual rather than the core of the venue.

## Concentrated Liquidity (CLMM)

Raydium launched **Concentrated Liquidity Market Maker (CLMM)** pools, similar to [[uniswap|Uniswap V3]]. LPs can concentrate their capital within specific price ranges rather than across the full price curve.

### How It Works

- LPs select a price range (e.g., SOL/USDC from $20 to $30)
- Capital within that range earns proportionally more fees than a full-range position
- If price moves outside the range, the position becomes 100% one asset and earns zero fees until price returns

### LP Strategies on Raydium CLMM

| Strategy | Description | Risk Level |
|---|---|---|
| **Tight range on stables** | Provide SOL/USDC liquidity within +/-2% of current price | Low (but requires active management) |
| **Wide range passive** | Set a broad range and collect moderate fees without rebalancing | Medium |
| **New token launch LP** | Provide liquidity for newly launched tokens via AcceleRaytor -- high fees but extreme [[impermanent-loss]] risk | Very high |
| **Automated vaults** | Use [[kamino]] or similar vault protocols to auto-manage Raydium CLMM positions | Medium (delegated management) |

### Impermanent Loss Considerations

Concentrated liquidity amplifies both fee earnings and [[impermanent-loss]]. A tight range on a volatile pair can suffer severe IL if the price trends away. For Solana meme coins that can move 50-90% in a day, concentrated LP positions require either very wide ranges or active monitoring.

## Launchpads: AcceleRaytor and LaunchLab

**AcceleRaytor** was Raydium's original launchpad for new token launches on [[solana]]. Projects used it to bootstrap initial liquidity and distribute tokens via an Initial DEX Offering (IDO), with participants depositing USDC or RAY to receive the new token at a set price. Tokens launched this way got Raydium liquidity pools immediately, creating day-one trading opportunities (and volatility).

**LaunchLab** (rolled out in 2025) is Raydium's newer, more permissionless token-launch product and a key driver of its 2025-2026 growth — over **12,000 tokens** have been launched through it. LaunchLab positions Raydium directly in the Solana memecoin/token-launch stack, competing with (and at times integrating alongside) **Pump.fun**, the dominant Solana memecoin launchpad. This launch-stack exposure makes RAY a leveraged play on Solana memecoin activity.

- **RAY token utility:** Staking RAY can provide priority allocation in launchpad offerings, giving the token utility beyond governance and fee capture.

## RAY Token Trading Analysis

### What Drives RAY Price

1. **Solana DEX volume** -- as one of the two dominant Solana AMMs (alongside [[orca]]) and holder of 50%+ (some estimates ~65%) of Solana DEX volume in early 2026, RAY benefits when total Solana swap volume rises
2. **Meme coin / launch season** -- many Solana meme coins launch with initial liquidity on Raydium (via LaunchLab and Pump.fun routing), making RAY a leveraged play on meme coin activity
3. **Fee revenue** -- protocol fees from swaps, CLMM positions, and AcceleRaytor launches
4. **Competition with Jupiter** -- [[jupiter-exchange-solana|Jupiter]] aggregates across Raydium, so Jupiter's growth indirectly benefits Raydium's volume
5. **Token supply** -- 268.7M of 555M max supply circulating (48.4%), meaning dilution pressure is moderate

### Trading Characteristics (Extended)

- **High correlation with SOL:** RAY moves directionally with [[solana|SOL]], typically with 1.5-2x beta
- **Volume spikes:** RAY trading volume surges during Solana meme coin manias (e.g., Bonk, WIF, POPCAT seasons)
- **Drawdown:** -96.31% from ATH -- deep bear market damage, but 362% above ATL
- **Liquidity:** Available on [[binance]], Kraken, and major CEXes; perpetual on [[hyperliquid]] absent from current listings

### Risk Factors

- **Solana single-chain risk:** Raydium operates exclusively on Solana -- a Solana failure would be existential
- **AMM competition:** [[orca]]'s concentrated liquidity, Meteora, and Lifinity all compete for Solana LP capital
- **Aggregator dependency:** Jupiter routes most Solana volume; if Jupiter changes routing preferences, Raydium volume could decline
- **OpenBook risk:** The OpenBook integration depends on a community-run project that could face governance or technical issues
- **Dilution:** ~51% of max supply is still to be emitted; sustained emissions weigh on price absent matching demand growth

## Valuation Framing (Qualitative)

Unlike a stablecoin or pure meme, RAY is a **cash-flow-bearing DEX token** and can be framed against real protocol economics:

1. **Fee revenue & take rate** — Raydium earns swap fees across CLMM/legacy pools plus LaunchLab launch fees. As a CLMM-first venue carrying 50%+ of Solana DEX volume, RAY is effectively a levered claim on Solana on-chain trading activity. Compare protocol fees/revenue (e.g., via Token Terminal) against market cap for a P/S-style read.
2. **Volume durability** — the bull case rests on Raydium retaining DEX market share against [[orca]], Meteora and Lifinity, and on Jupiter continuing to route through it. Volume is the numerator of the whole thesis.
3. **Launchpad optionality** — LaunchLab (12,000+ tokens) makes RAY a leveraged play on Solana memecoin/launch cycles; this is high-torque but cyclical and competes with Pump.fun.
4. **Dilution drag** — any fair-value estimate must use FDV (~$343M), not just circulating cap (~$167M), because ~half the supply is still to emit.

Net: RAY is best valued as Solana-DEX-volume beta with a launchpad call option, discounted for ongoing dilution and single-chain concentration.
