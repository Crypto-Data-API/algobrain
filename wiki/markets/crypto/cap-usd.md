# Cap USD

**Cap USD** (ticker **CUSD**; native chain [[ethereum|Ethereum]]) is a fully-reserved, USD-pegged [[stablecoins|stablecoin]] issued by the **Cap** protocol. It is the dollar token of a two-product system — the base **cUSD** and a separate yield-bearing savings token, **stcUSD**. cUSD targets a **1:1 peg to the US dollar**, is backed by a basket of **regulated blue-chip stablecoins and tokenised money-market funds**, and is **1:1 redeemable** for its underlying reserve assets. The yield model is a **shared-security / operator** design: yield is not native to cUSD but is generated for stcUSD stakers by an autonomous layer of restaked "operators" running against a protocol hurdle rate, with code-enforced downside protection. As of the snapshot below it trades on peg at ~$0.9997.

## Architecture — How Cap Works

### Reserve / collateral model

Cap USD is a **reserve-backed "stablecoin-of-stablecoins"** backed by a basket of blue-chip stablecoins and tokenised money-market funds — [[usdc|USDC]], [[usdt|USDT]], PayPal's pyUSD, BlackRock's **BUIDL**, and Franklin Templeton's **BENJI** — i.e. tokens issued by regulated institutions with transparent attestations. Because every reserve constituent is itself a high-quality, redeemable dollar instrument, cUSD's backing is conservative relative to crypto-collateralised peers like [[dai]] or BTC-CDP designs like [[usda-2|USDa]]. The tradeoff is that cUSD inherits the **aggregate** counterparty risk of all of those issuers at once.

### Peg & stability mechanism, mint/redeem & gating

cUSD is **1:1 redeemable** for any *available* reserve asset, so the peg is anchored by **redemption arbitrage**: if cUSD trades below $1, arbitrageurs buy it and redeem for $1 of reserves; if above, they mint new cUSD by depositing reserves and sell. The phrase "any available reserve asset" is the gating subtlety — redemption is satisfied from whatever the reserve currently holds, so in stress a redeemer may receive a less-preferred constituent (e.g. a tokenised fund rather than USDC) rather than their first choice. There is no fiat redemption desk; par enforcement is purely on-chain arbitrage against the reserve.

### Yield source & distribution — the operator / shared-security model

Yield is **not** native to cUSD. Holders who want yield stake cUSD into **stcUSD**, a savings token whose return is generated by an autonomous layer of **"operators"** that self-select to deploy capital when returns clear the protocol's **hurdle rate**. Operators post (re)staked security as a backstop, and Cap states the yield-generation risk is **covered with code-enforced downside protection** for stakers — i.e. losses are intended to be absorbed by operator-posted collateral before they reach stcUSD principal. This is structurally different from a single in-house strategy (as in [[ethena-usde]]) or a passive T-bill pass-through; it is closer to a restaking/shared-security marketplace for stablecoin yield. The protocol's design was published via Stanford Blockchain Review.

## Tokenomics & Supply

- **cUSD supply:** ~79.90M, fully circulating; mint-on-deposit / burn-on-redeem so supply tracks reserve inflows.
- **stcUSD** is the optional yield wrapper; staking cUSD into stcUSD is what exposes a holder to operator/strategy risk and the corresponding return. Holding bare cUSD is the lower-risk, zero-yield choice.
- No large unvested token overhang is surfaced in the snapshot; this is a reserve token, not a governance-emission play.

## How & Where It Trades / Is Used

Liquidity is **very thin** at this snapshot: reported 24h volume is only **~$401** against an $80M market cap, with a 24h range pinned to ~$0.9999. In practice the credible way to get $1 of value is **on-chain redemption** for the underlying reserve stablecoins, not selling into a secondary market — there is no deep named exchange order book in the snapshot. Composability comes mainly from the cUSD/stcUSD pair itself: hold cUSD as a redeemable dollar, stake into stcUSD for yield. Note the historical range is wide — an ATH of $1.17 (2025-10-10) and an ATL of $0.9071 (2026-04-01) — so cUSD has both spiked above and dropped ~9% below peg in the past, likely during low-liquidity dislocations.

## Narrative / Category & Catalysts

cUSD sits in the **"institutional-grade reserve" + restaked-yield** narrative — appealing to users who want a dollar backed by regulated issuers (USDC, BUIDL, BENJI) rather than crypto CDPs, with optional yield sourced from a transparent operator marketplace. Catalysts: growth of tokenised money-market funds (BUIDL/BENJI) as on-chain collateral, demand for "safer-than-DAI" decentralised dollars, the maturation of restaking as a yield-security backstop, and any move by the operator set to compress or widen stcUSD yields. Headwind: in the current **bottoming/accumulation** regime (Fear & Greed 21, Extreme Fear, as of 2026-06-22), reserve-basket coins inherit the market's broader stablecoin-confidence sentiment, and the thin secondary liquidity limits adoption.

## Risks

- **De-peg risk:** Despite trading on peg now, history shows a drop to $0.907 — thin liquidity makes transient [[depeg|de-pegs]] possible.
- **Collateral / contagion risk:** Backing is itself other stablecoins/MMF tokens — a de-peg, freeze, or redemption halt of [[usdc|USDC]], [[usdt|USDT]], pyUSD, BUIDL, or BENJI would propagate **directly and simultaneously** into cUSD. The basket diversifies issuer risk but concentrates *systemic* stablecoin risk.
- **Yield-source / operator counterparty risk:** stcUSD yield depends on the operator marketplace performing; "code-enforced downside protection" relies on operator-posted (re)staked collateral being sufficient and slashable in time. A correlated operator loss could exceed the backstop.
- **Redemption-gating risk:** Redemption is for "any *available* reserve asset" — in stress a redeemer may receive a less-liquid constituent rather than their preferred dollar, an effective soft gate.
- **Smart-contract risk:** The two-token system plus operator/hurdle-rate logic and restaking integrations add a meaningful exploit surface.
- **Liquidity risk:** Near-zero secondary volume; exit at par is redemption-dependent and not guaranteed in a crunch.
- **Regulatory risk:** Aggregating regulated-issuer tokens and tokenised money-market funds invites layered securities/stablecoin regulatory exposure.

## Trading / Usage Playbook

- **Hold cUSD for a regulated-reserve dollar; stake into stcUSD only if you accept operator/strategy risk.** They are different risk products.
- **Monitor the reserve composition.** Your real exposure is to the *worst* constituent — track USDC/USDT/pyUSD/BUIDL/BENJI health as if you held each.
- **Plan to exit via redemption, not the order book** (~$401/day volume); accept you may be redeemed in a non-preferred reserve asset during stress.
- **Treat the $0.9071 ATL as the realistic stress floor** for transient dislocations and size liquidity needs accordingly.

## Overview

Cap is a stablecoin protocol that provides credible financial guarantees via two products: the dollar-denominated cUSD and the yield-bearing stcUSD. 

cUSD is a digital dollar issued on the Ethereum blockchain that can be used on any network. cUSD's reserve is backed by blue chip stablecoins such as USDC, USDT, pyUSD, BUIDL, and BENJI, i.e. issued by regulated institutions with transparent attestations. It is 1:1 redeemable for any of the available reserve assets. 

stcUSD is a savings product issued by staking cUSD. Any cUSD holder has open access to stcUSD.  Yield is generated via an autonomous layer of operators, who self-select in and out based on the current hurdle rate of the protocol. The risk of yield generation is covered, meaning users have full downside protection that is verifiable by code.
