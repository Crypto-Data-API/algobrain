# Artificial Superintelligence Alliance

**Artificial Superintelligence Alliance** (FET) is the merged token of crypto's flagship decentralized-AI coalition: formed in April–July 2024 by uniting Fetch.ai ($FET), SingularityNET ($AGIX) and Ocean Protocol ($OCEAN) into a single $FET token (with CUDOS joining later). For traders it is one of the most liquid pure plays on the **AI-crypto narrative** — and since October 2025 also a governance-risk case study, after Ocean Protocol abruptly exited the alliance and was sued by Fetch.ai over ~263M FET of undisclosed sales.

## Overview

The Artificial Superintelligence (ASI) Alliance is the world’s largest open-source initiative dedicated to decentralized Artificial General Intelligence (AGI). Formed in April 2024, the ASI Alliance unites SingularityNET, Fetch.ai, and Ocean Protocol, with CUDOS joining as a network member shortly after. The ASI Alliance was established through a community-approved tokenomic merger—combining $AGIX, $FET, and $OCEAN into a single token: $FET. This unified token underpins a collaborative framework designed to scale open-source AI and stand as the largest open-sourced, independent entity in AI research and development. The Alliance is committed to accelerating the advancement of decentralized Artificial General Intelligence (AGI) and, ultimately, Artificial Superintelligence (ASI). It provides a robust, open-source innovation stack, empowering developers, enterprises, and researchers globally to build ethical, scalable, and groundbreaking AI solutions. This ensures that advanced intelligence remains a shared, accessible resource, fostering innovation beyond centralized systems and promoting a future where AI benefits all.

## 2025–2026 Developments: The Ocean Exit and Lawsuit

- **2025-06-27** — Ocean created "Ocean Expeditions" and transferred OceanDAO assets to it; from ~2025-07-01 it began converting OCEAN to FET.
- **2025-10-08/09** — the **Ocean Protocol Foundation announced immediate withdrawal from the ASI Alliance**, citing a desire for independent tokenomics and OCEAN buyback-and-burn funding (The Block, Bankless).
- **The dispute** — Fetch.ai sued Ocean, alleging the foundation converted **~661M OCEAN into ~286M FET** and quietly sold **~263M FET** (>10% of circulating supply at the time) into the market, pressuring the price (CryptoSlate; Yahoo Finance put the sales at ~$84–100M).
- **Settlement (early 2026)** — per reporting, the suit was settled with Ocean returning ~286M FET (~$120M) to the alliance.
- **Price impact** — FET fell from its March 2024 ATH of $3.45 to the $0.20–0.30 range by spring 2026 (-93% from ATH), with the Ocean overhang a named contributor; April 2026 snapshot showed a +67% 30-day bounce, illustrating its high beta within the AI basket.
- Fetch.ai also shipped its own LLM efforts (the ASI-1 model line) during 2025; alliance product cadence continued despite the governance fracture.

## Protocol & Project — The ASI Alliance Stack

FET is not a single product but the unifying token across a federation of decentralized-AI projects. Understanding the stack is essential to valuing the token:

- **Fetch.ai** — the agentic backbone. Fetch.ai builds *autonomous economic agents* (AEAs): software agents that negotiate, transact and coordinate on behalf of users (booking travel, optimizing energy grids, DeFi automation). Its flagship consumer product is the **ASI-1 / "ASI:One"** LLM line and the **Agentverse** marketplace where developers register and monetize agents. Fetch.ai runs its own [[cosmos|Cosmos]]-SDK chain (`fetchd`) while FET-the-token lives primarily on [[ethereum|Ethereum]] as an ERC-20.
- **SingularityNET** — the original decentralized AI marketplace founded by Ben Goertzel, focused on a platform where AI services are bought/sold and on the long-arc mission of open-source **Artificial General Intelligence (AGI)**. (See [[artificial-intelligence]].)
- **Ocean Protocol** — the data layer: tokenized data assets, compute-to-data, and data marketplaces. *Exited the alliance in October 2025* (see below) but its technology underpinned the "data" leg of the thesis.
- **CUDOS** — distributed compute / GPU network member, supplying the hardware leg for AI workloads.

The combined pitch: agents (Fetch) + AI services/AGI research (SingularityNET) + data (Ocean) + compute (CUDOS), all transacting in one token. This makes FET the most diversified single-token bet on the **decentralized-AI thesis** — and also exposes it to coordination/governance risk across independent foundations, which is exactly what materialized in the Ocean dispute.

**Dilution profile.** FET has a *low* unlock overhang relative to the memecoins in this basket: ~83% of the max supply is already circulating, so only ~455M FET (~17%) remains to be emitted. The merger fixed conversion ratios — AGIX → FET at ~0.433335 and OCEAN → FET at ~0.433226 — establishing the unified 2.71B max supply. Watch the returned 286M FET tranche from the settlement as a potential future overhang.

## Ecosystem & Use Cases

- **Autonomous agents** — Fetch.ai's AEAs and the Agentverse: a marketplace where agents are deployed, discovered and paid in FET. Use cases span DeFi automation, supply-chain coordination, mobility (parking/charging), and "agentic commerce."
- **AI services marketplace** — SingularityNET's decentralized exchange of AI models/APIs, paid in the unified token.
- **Data & compute** — historically Ocean (data) and CUDOS (GPU compute) supplied the inputs that agents and models consume; post-Ocean-exit the data leg is being reconstituted internally.
- **ASI-1 LLM** — a self-hosted, agent-oriented large language model the alliance ships as a sovereign alternative to centralized frontier models (see [[large-language-model]] if present, else [[artificial-intelligence]]).
- **Staking/governance** — FET is staked on the Fetch.ai chain for network security and used in alliance governance votes (the merger itself was approved by community vote).

## Market Structure & Derivatives

- **Spot venues** — Binance (FET/USDT), Kraken (FET/USD), Bitget, KuCoin, Crypto.com, plus Uniswap V3 on Ethereum. Liquidity is genuine top-tier for an AI alt; bid/ask depth is sufficient for institutional-size entries on Binance.

Open interest and funding are far calmer than the memecoins in this comparison set: FET funding mean-reverts around neutral and rarely shows the extreme persistent-positive/negative funding seen on TRUMP, BONK or PENGU perps. This makes it a cleaner vehicle for basis and carry, and for expressing directional AI-narrative views without paying punitive funding.
- **Basis/funding trade** — when AI-narrative FOMO spikes, FET funding turns positive enough to support cash-and-carry (long spot / short perp); in the current Extreme-Fear regime funding is muted.

## Valuation Framework (qualitative)

FET sits between a pure narrative/attention asset and a quasi-fundamental one. Inputs to a qualitative valuation:

1. **AI-narrative beta** — the dominant driver. FET reprices on macro-AI news (frontier-model launches, GPU/AI-capex headlines, NVIDIA earnings) more than on its own product metrics.
2. **Adoption proxies** — Agentverse agent counts, ASI-1 usage, developer activity (GitHub PRs, contributors), and on-chain transaction volume on `fetchd`. These are the closest thing to "fundamentals."
3. **MC/FDV discount** — at 0.83, dilution risk is modest versus memecoins; the multiple should command less of a dilution discount than TRUMP (0.24) or PUMP (0.40).
4. **Governance discount** — the Ocean episode justifies a *coordination-risk* haircut: the multi-foundation structure can dump supply or fracture, as it did. This is an idiosyncratic discount peers like TAO (single-protocol) do not carry.

Bottom line: value FET as "AI-narrative beta with a real (if early) agent/AI product stack, discounted for alliance-governance fragility."

## Trading Playbook

- **Regime read** — in Extreme Fear / Established Bear Market (current), default to range/mean-reversion and small size; AI alts bleed in risk-off tape.
- **Basket pair** — long/short FET against another AI name (TAO, RENDER) to isolate idiosyncratic alpha and strip out sector beta.
- **Catalyst trade** — fade vertical pops on AI-headline FOMO unless confirmed by sustained volume; FET's -94% ATH drawdown shows how violently narrative premium can evaporate.
- **Supply-watch** — monitor known alliance-treasury wallets; a large outbound transfer (à la Ocean 2025) is a short-side tell.
- **Funding/basis** — harvest positive funding on FET-PERP during narrative spikes via delta-neutral carry.

## Risks

- **Sector beta / narrative decay** — FET lives and dies on the AI-crypto narrative; -94.5% from ATH shows how much premium can drain when the story cools. In Extreme Fear, downside continuation risk dominates.
- **Alliance-governance fragility** — the Ocean exit and ~263M FET dump (>10% of float) is a demonstrated, repeatable hazard: any member foundation can fracture the alliance or sell treasury into the bid.
- **Treasury supply overhang** — the returned 286M FET from the Ocean settlement and other foundation holdings are potential future sell pressure.
- **Execution risk on the product thesis** — agents/AGI is a long-arc bet; if Agentverse/ASI-1 adoption stalls, FET reverts to pure narrative beta with no fundamental floor.
- **Liquidity contraction** — 24h volume more than halved April→June 2026; thinner books amplify drawdowns.
