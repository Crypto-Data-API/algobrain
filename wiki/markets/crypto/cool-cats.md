**Cool Cats** is a collection of 9,999 randomly generated cartoon cat PFP (profile picture) [[nft|NFTs]] on [[ethereum]], launched in July 2021 at a mint price of 0.06 ETH. Featuring a distinctive blue cat design with varying traits, clothing, and accessories, Cool Cats built one of the strongest communities in the 2021 NFT era and secured partnerships with TIME Magazine and Animoca Brands. The collection's floor price peaked above $30,000 in late 2021 before declining over 95% in the subsequent bear market, making it representative of the "mid-tier PFP" category that suffered the most severe losses.

---

## History

Cool Cats was created by a team of four -- **clon**, **Lynqoid**, **ELU**, and **Tom** -- and launched on **July 1, 2021**, during the heart of the PFP NFT mania. The collection's blue cat aesthetic was immediately distinctive and accessible, and the team cultivated a family-friendly, community-first brand that differentiated Cool Cats from the more "degen" culture of many NFT projects.

The project gained rapid traction through community engagement, social media marketing, and a series of high-profile partnerships. A collaboration with **TIME Magazine** in September 2021 was a watershed moment, lending mainstream legitimacy to the project. Cool Cats also partnered with **Animoca Brands**, one of the largest blockchain gaming companies, to explore gaming integrations.

In early 2022, Cool Cats launched **Cool Pets**, a companion NFT collection where holders could evolve their pets through an on-chain quest system, along with the **$MILK** token ecosystem. These expansions were intended to add utility and engagement beyond static profile pictures.

However, the 2022 bear market hit mid-tier PFP collections especially hard. Cool Cats lacked the extreme cultural cachet of [[bored-ape-yacht-club|BAYC]] or the historical significance of [[cryptopunks|CryptoPunks]], and the floor price collapsed as speculative demand evaporated.

Cool Cats feature a consistent blue cat base character with randomized clothing, accessories, headwear, and facial expressions. The art style is clean, approachable, and family-friendly -- in contrast to the more "edgy" or abstract aesthetics of many competitors. Trait categories include body type, hat, face, and outfit. [[nft-rarity-scoring|Rarity]] is concentrated in the **"cool," "wild,"** and **"exotic"** class tiers: most cats are standard "cool" cats, while wild and exotic cats (unique non-blue designs) are the scarcest and command the largest premiums over the [[nft-floor-price|floor]].

## Cultural Significance

Cool Cats occupies a specific niche in NFT history as a representative example of the mid-tier PFP phenomenon:

- **Community-first model**: Cool Cats was frequently cited as one of the best NFT communities in 2021, with active Discord engagement, events, and a welcoming culture that contrasted with the more exclusive vibe of [[bored-ape-yacht-club|BAYC]]
- **Family-friendly brand**: The approachable, non-threatening cat aesthetic positioned Cool Cats as one of the most accessible PFP projects, appealing to a broader demographic than many competitors
- **Brand partnerships**: The TIME Magazine and Animoca Brands partnerships demonstrated that NFT projects could secure mainstream corporate partnerships, though these deals did not ultimately sustain the floor price
- **Ecosystem expansion**: Cool Pets, $MILK token, and gaming integrations represented the "build utility to maintain value" strategy that many mid-tier PFP projects pursued -- with mixed results
- **Bear market case study**: Cool Cats is frequently cited as an example of how mid-tier PFP collections -- strong community, solid art, real partnerships -- could still lose 95%+ of their value when speculative demand dried up. Community and brand alone were not enough without the extreme cultural dominance of the top-tier collections

## Collector & Trading Risks

- **Mid-tier risk profile:** Cool Cats sits in the difficult middle ground between blue chips ([[bored-ape-yacht-club|BAYC]], [[cryptopunks|CryptoPunks]]) and micro-cap PFPs — not iconic enough to be a "must-own," not cheap enough for pure speculation. This category suffered the most severe and durable losses.
- **Illiquidity & floor volatility:** Volume has collapsed from 2021 peaks; expect wide spreads, slow exits, and a [[nft-floor-price|floor]] that gaps on single sweeps.
- **Ecosystem-token risk:** The $MILK token and Cool Pets layer adds complexity — token price, quest mechanics, and companion-collection dynamics all feed back into Cool Cats valuation, and can detract as well as add.
- **Royalty erosion:** Optional-royalty marketplaces ([[nft-royalties]]) cut the team's secondary revenue, undermining the utility build-out the recovery thesis depends on.
- **Wash trading:** Reported volume can be inflated by [[wash-trading]] and incentive farming; treat figures skeptically.
- **Relevance decay:** Bears note that crypto attention moves forward, not backward; a community-strong project can still bleed value if it loses cultural mindshare.
- **ETH-denominated dual exposure:** Floors are quoted in [[ethereum|ETH]]; holders carry combined NFT + ETH price exposure.
