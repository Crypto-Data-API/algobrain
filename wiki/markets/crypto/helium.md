# Helium

**Helium** (ticker: **HNT**) is a [[depin|DePIN]] (Decentralized Physical Infrastructure Network) project that incentivizes individuals to deploy and operate wireless hotspots — for IoT (LoRaWAN) coverage and, via Helium Mobile, 5G cellular — earning HNT tokens for the coverage and data transfer they provide. Originally its own Layer-1, Helium migrated its network and token to [[solana|Solana]] in 2023 to inherit Solana's throughput and tooling. HNT is the network's reward and value-accrual token, with sub-tokens (IOT and MOBILE) for the respective coverage networks.

## Overview

Helium is a decentralized machine network powered by a physical blockchain. The network is created by gateways who also double as miners on our network. These gateway operators can earn tokens based on coverage they provide as well as transaction fees gained from machines connecting to their gateway

Helium is a new cryptocurrency. Hotspots earn for providing and validating wireless coverage and when devices on the network connect to the internet through Hospots

Helium is mined and distributed to Hotspot Owners, Helium Inc, and investors.

Helium's token model is a **burn-and-mint equilibrium (BME)**: network usage is paid in **Data Credits** (fixed at $0.00001 each), which are created by burning HNT and are non-transferable. Net HNT supply is the balance between scheduled emissions (which **halve roughly every two years**, à la Bitcoin) and HNT burned to mint Data Credits. With circulating ≈ total supply (~182M) against a 223M max, the **~0.82 MC/FDV** ratio means only modest remaining emissions — there is relatively little hidden dilution overhang compared with low-float tokens. The sub-tokens **IOT** and **MOBILE** reward the two coverage networks and can be converted into HNT, so HNT is the network's terminal value-accrual asset.

## Use Case, Narrative & Category

Helium is a flagship of the **[[depin|DePIN]] (Decentralized Physical Infrastructure)** narrative — using token incentives to crowdsource the buildout of real-world wireless infrastructure rather than relying on a centralized carrier's capex. There are two main coverage networks: the original **IoT/LoRaWAN** network (long-range, low-power sensor connectivity) and **Helium Mobile**, a consumer 5G offering that has run low-cost (and at times free/discounted) U.S. phone plans, offloading traffic onto a community-operated hotspot network with carrier roaming as backup. Coverage providers earn token rewards, and the system uses a "Proof-of-Coverage" mechanism to verify that hotspots genuinely provide the wireless coverage they claim.

After migrating to [[solana|Solana]] in 2023, Helium adopted a Solana-native token (HNT) plus the IOT and MOBILE sub-tokens, with a "burn-and-mint equilibrium" design (Data Credits are burned to pay for network usage). The investment thesis is exposure to real, usage-driven demand for decentralized wireless — distinct from the more speculative mid-caps in this report.

## Valuation Framing

HNT is best valued on **real network usage** rather than pure speculation. The qualitative anchors:

- **Usage → burn.** Under burn-and-mint equilibrium, sustained growth in Data Credit consumption (IoT data transfer + Helium Mobile data offload) burns HNT and tightens supply. The bull case is that decentralized wireless usage compounds; the bear case is that emissions outpace genuine paying demand.
- **Subsidy economics.** Helium Mobile's aggressive consumer pricing is partly subsidized; valuation hinges on whether unit economics turn self-sustaining.
- **Cycle context.** At ~$51M cap and ~99.5% off its 2021 ATH, HNT prices in deep skepticism about the original DePIN thesis — leaving asymmetric upside if usage inflects, but no floor if it does not. The high ~0.82 MC/FDV means limited dilution cushion either way.

## Risks

- **Demand/usage risk:** DePIN value ultimately depends on paying users of the network (IoT data, Helium Mobile subscribers); reward emissions can outpace real usage, pressuring token value.
- **Subsidy dependence:** Aggressive consumer pricing for Helium Mobile is partly subsidized; sustainability of unit economics is a key question.
- **Solana dependency:** Post-migration, network performance and security are tied to [[solana|Solana]].
- **Macro / liquidity risk:** As a sub-$100M-cap altcoin with thin volume, HNT is highly sensitive to the broad risk-off regime ([[fear-and-greed-index|Fear & Greed]] 23 / Established Bear Market as of 2026-06-20).
