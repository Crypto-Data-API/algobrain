# Fluid

**Fluid** (ticker **FLUID**) is the [[governance-token]] of the Fluid protocol, the rebrand of **Instadapp** — a [[defi]] infrastructure project originally built as a [[account-abstraction|smart-account]] management layer for [[ethereum]]-based DeFi. Fluid combines a lending/borrowing money market with a [[decentralized-exchange|DEX]] in a unified **"Liquidity Layer,"** letting the same deposited collateral simultaneously back loans and provide trading liquidity for maximum capital efficiency. The CoinGecko coin id is `instadapp` (legacy), reflecting the project's origin.

## How & Where It Trades

**Centralized venues:** FLUID is listed on **Upbit** (FLUID/KRW), giving it notable Korean retail exposure despite a relatively low overall float.

**On-chain / protocol mechanics:** Fluid is itself a [[decentralized-exchange|DEX]] plus money market. Its core innovation is the **Liquidity Layer** with "smart collateral" and "smart debt": liquidity deposited as collateral can also serve as an [[automated-market-maker|AMM]] trading pair, so a position earns lending yield *and* trading fees at once. FLUID itself trades on-chain primarily via **[[uniswap]] V3** (FLUID/WETH) on [[ethereum]].

**Derivatives:** FLUID is not a major perpetuals listing on [[hyperliquid]] or large derivatives venues; price discovery is dominated by spot. Modest 24h volume (~$3.4M against a ~$79M cap, ~4.3% turnover) implies thin liquidity and elevated slippage on size — a [[liquidity]] risk for traders.

## Valuation Framing (qualitative)

Unlike the narrative-priced AI tokens in this batch, FLUID has a **revenue-linked DeFi-token profile**: it is a hard-capped governance asset over a live protocol that earns fees from its Liquidity Layer (lending interest + trading fees). At a ~$79M cap with ~79% of supply circulating, the dilution overhang is modest, so the valuation hinges more on **protocol economics** — TVL, DEX volume, fee capture, and how much of that accrues to FLUID stakers/governance — than on unlock mechanics. The bull case is that the smart-collateral/smart-debt design keeps winning share in DEX volume and lending TVL on [[ethereum]]; the bear case is that entrenched giants ([[aave]], [[uniswap]]) defend their turf and FLUID stays a thin-liquidity small-cap (~96% below its 2021 INST-era ATH). In an extreme-fear DeFi tape, high-beta governance tokens like this remain exposed to further drawdown. Qualitative framing only — not a price target.

## Use Case, Narrative & Category

Fluid sits in the **DeFi capital-efficiency** narrative. The pitch: rather than siloing capital in separate lending markets and AMM pools, Fluid lets one deposit do both jobs, narrowing spreads and lifting yields. It competes with [[aave]] (lending) and [[uniswap]] (AMM) by collapsing the two into a single primitive, and is frequently cited as a fast-growing challenger in DEX volume and lending TVL on Ethereum. Backers historically include Coinbase Ventures and Pantera Capital (per CoinGecko category tags).

## Risks

- **Thin liquidity** — ~$3.4M daily volume makes large entries/exits costly; spreads widen in stress.
- **Smart-contract & design risk** — the smart-collateral/smart-debt model is novel; a bug in the Liquidity Layer could affect both lending and AMM legs simultaneously.
- **Competition** — directly competes with entrenched giants ([[aave]], [[uniswap]]); edge depends on sustained capital-efficiency advantage.
- **Bear-market beta** — DeFi governance tokens are high-beta; with the market in extreme fear ([[crypto-fear-and-greed-index|F&G]] 23), FLUID is exposed to further drawdown.
- **Concentration** — Upbit/KRW being a primary CEX venue concentrates flow in one jurisdiction.
