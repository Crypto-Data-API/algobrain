# The Sandbox

**The Sandbox** (ticker **SAND**) is a community-driven voxel [[metaverse]] and [[gaming-tokens|GameFi]] platform where creators monetize user-built ASSETS, LAND parcels, and gaming experiences as [[nft|NFTs]]. SAND is the ERC-20 utility and governance token of the ecosystem, issued on [[ethereum]] (and bridged to [[polygon]] and Base), used for transactions, staking, and game fees. It is a flagship name in the [[animoca-brands|Animoca Brands]] portfolio and is widely grouped with [[decentraland]] and [[apecoin]] as a "metaverse/play-to-earn" bellwether.

## Overview

The Sandbox is a community-driven platform where creators can monetize voxel ASSETS and gaming experiences on the blockchain.

SAND is the utility token used throughout The Sandbox ecosystem as the basis of transactions and interactions. It is an ERC-20 utility token built on the Ethereum blockchain. There is a finite supply of 3,000,000,000 SAND.

## Tokenomics & Supply

SAND has a **fixed maximum supply of 3.0B tokens**, with roughly 2.67B circulating (MC/FDV ~0.89, so most dilution is already in the float). The token was distributed across the company/founders, an ecosystem fund, [[binance|Binance]] Launchpad sale (2020), and play-to-earn/staking rewards. SAND functions as the medium of exchange for buying LAND and ASSETS, paying transaction fees, staking to earn rewards and gems, and voting in the Sandbox DAO. A meaningful portion of platform fees is recycled to the Foundation and to creators, giving SAND a partial fee-capture role.

## Use Case, Narrative & Category

The Sandbox belongs squarely to the **[[metaverse]] / NFT / [[gamefi|GameFi]] / play-to-earn** category. Its pitch is a Roblox/Minecraft-style creator economy where:

- **LAND** (a fixed-supply grid of NFT parcels) is owned by users and brands;
- **ASSETS** are user-created voxel NFTs usable in games;
- **Game Maker / VoxEdit** let non-coders build experiences;
- celebrity, brand, and IP partnerships (e.g. Snoop Dogg, Adidas, Gucci, Warner Music) drove the 2021-2022 hype.

The investment narrative is a **high-beta bet on a metaverse/NFT revival**. During the 2021 bull run SAND and [[decentraland]] (MANA) were the two reference metaverse tokens; both have since deflated heavily as on-chain activity and LAND prices collapsed. SAND is backed by [[animoca-brands|Animoca Brands]], a major Web3 gaming conglomerate, which ties its fortunes to the broader Web3-gaming thesis.

## How & Where It Trades

- **Spot CEX**: deep liquidity on [[binance|Binance]] (SAND/USDT), Kraken, Upbit (SAND/KRW — strong Korean retail interest historically), Bitget, KuCoin, and Crypto.com.
- **Spot DEX**: Uniswap V2/V3 on [[ethereum]]; also liquid on [[polygon]] and Base where SAND is bridged.
- **Derivatives**: SAND has a perp on [[hyperliquid]] (SAND-PERP) and on most major CEX futures venues. As a small-cap metaverse name in a bear regime, its perps tend to carry **persistently slightly-negative-to-flat [[funding-rate|funding]]** (shorts paying little, longs occasionally paying during squeezes), with modest [[open-interest]] that spikes around metaverse-narrative or Animoca news. See [[funding-rate-arbitrage]] and [[perpetual-futures]] for how this is traded.

## Valuation Framing (Qualitative)

SAND has limited direct cash-flow accrual to the token — platform fees partly recycle to the Foundation and creators, but it is best understood as a **high-beta option on a metaverse/GameFi revival** rather than a yield asset. Qualitative anchors:

- **Optionality, not fundamentals.** With daily active users and LAND turnover far below 2021 peaks, current on-chain demand does not support the price on usage alone; the cap embeds an option on the next metaverse hype cycle.
- **Brand/IP moat.** SAND's edge over generic GameFi tokens is its catalog of marquee brand and celebrity LAND partnerships and [[animoca-brands|Animoca]]'s distribution — a real but slow-to-monetize asset.
- **Clean supply.** ~89% circulating and a fixed 3B cap mean limited future dilution, unlike many newer gaming tokens still on multi-year vesting cliffs.
- **Korea-weighted liquidity.** Strong Upbit (KRW) flow makes SAND sensitive to Korean retail risk appetite; depth thins quickly outside a few venues.

## Risks

- **Narrative dependence** — SAND is highly correlated to the metaverse/NFT hype cycle; without a sustained revival it has weak organic demand.
- **Bear-market beta** — small-cap, high-beta token; in an extreme-fear, established-bear regime it can fall faster than majors like [[bitcoin]]/[[ethereum]].
- **Activity/usage gap** — daily active users and LAND turnover are far below 2021 peaks; the creator economy has not reached self-sustaining scale.
- **Regulatory** — SAND appears on CoinGecko's "Alleged SEC Securities" tag, reflecting US regulatory overhang for tokens sold in launchpad-style distributions.
- **Liquidity/depeg of attention** — outside Korea and a few CEXs, organic spot depth is thin; large exits can move the price.

## Trading Profile

**Venues & liquidity.** SAND is a deep, liquid two-venue derivatives name. It trades on **[[binance|Binance]]** (SAND/USDT spot plus a USD-margined perpetual) and on **[[hyperliquid]]** (SAND-PERP, with leverage up to roughly 40-50x). Binance carries the bulk of centralized spot and perp depth and sets the reference price, while Hyperliquid provides transparent on-chain [[funding-rate|funding]], [[open-interest]], and L2 book data. Having a mid-cap alt live on both a top CEX and the leading on-chain perp venue means tight spreads for normal size and a clean cross-venue basis to trade, but book depth still thins beyond modest clip sizes — position sizing should respect that liquidity concentrates on Binance and Upbit (KRW), and that on-chain depth is shallower on the tail.

**Applicable strategies.**
- [[funding-rate-harvest]] — SAND perps tend to carry persistently slightly-negative-to-flat funding in a bear regime, letting a delta-neutral book collect the spread between spot and the SAND-PERP.
- [[hl-vs-cex-funding-divergence]] — with SAND live on both Binance and Hyperliquid, funding can diverge between the CEX perp and SAND-PERP, creating a two-venue relative-funding trade.
- [[cash-and-carry]] — clean ~89%-circulating supply and deep spot on Binance make it straightforward to hold spot SAND against a short perp to capture basis/funding carry.
- [[oi-confirmed-trend]] — SAND [[open-interest]] spikes around metaverse-narrative or [[animoca-brands|Animoca]] catalysts; OI expansion confirming a directional move filters real trends from thin relief bounces.
- [[liquidation-cascade-fade]] — as a high-beta small-cap, SAND overshoots on forced deleveraging, so fading liquidation-driven wicks back toward VWAP is a recurring edge.
- [[narrative-trading]] — SAND is a pure metaverse/GameFi narrative proxy; positioning around revival hype, brand/IP announcements, and MANA-correlated flows is central to trading it.

**Volatility & regime character.** SAND is a **high-beta metaverse/GameFi altcoin** with pronounced narrative reflexivity — it rallies hard on metaverse/NFT hype and deflates faster than majors in risk-off tape (currently ~99% below its 2021 ATH). It carries strong positive beta to [[bitcoin]]/[[ethereum]] and trades as a correlated narrative proxy alongside [[decentraland|MANA]] and other Web3-gaming tokens, amplifying broad-market moves in both directions.

**Risk flags.**
- **Liquidity/venue concentration** — depth clusters on Binance and Upbit (KRW); Hyperliquid and DEX books thin quickly, so large exits move price and slippage rises fast in stress.
- **Narrative dependence** — without a sustained metaverse/GameFi revival, organic demand is weak and price is largely an option on the next hype cycle.
- **Emissions/supply** — most dilution is behind it (~89% circulating, fixed 3B cap), but ecosystem-fund and play-to-earn/staking issuance still adds gradual sell pressure.
- **Perp funding dislocations** — thin OI means funding can swing sharply during squeezes, whipsawing crowded [[funding-rate|funding]] and carry positions.
- **Regulatory** — SAND appears on CoinGecko's "Alleged SEC Securities" tag, a standing US regulatory overhang for launchpad-distributed tokens.
