# Naoris Protocol

**Naoris Protocol** (NAORIS) is a post-quantum, decentralized cybersecurity mesh — a [[depin|DePIN]] (Decentralized Physical Infrastructure Network) that turns ordinary devices into validators of one another's security posture. Rather than relying on a centralized perimeter, every participating device continuously attests to its cyber-status, creating a peer-validated "trust mesh" intended to detect compromise in real time. The project markets a post-quantum cryptographic stack (branded "Sub-Zero" / dPoSec) designed to resist attacks from future quantum computers.

## Overview

Naoris Protocol reframes cybersecurity as a decentralized, community-validated function rather than a centralized, siloed one. By delivering verified cyber-status that extends beyond traditional network perimeters, it aims to let enterprises and governments shift from perimeter-based defense to a [[depin|decentralized physical infrastructure]] model where security is continuously and collectively attested.

The protocol positions itself at the intersection of three narratives: [[depin|DePIN]], post-quantum cryptography, and decentralized cybersecurity. Its core premise is that conventional security architectures present single points of failure, while a mesh of mutually-validating devices removes the central trust anchor an attacker would otherwise target.

---

## Mechanism & Architecture

- **Devices as validators.** Each enrolled device runs a lightweight agent that participates in a consensus layer Naoris calls **dPoSec** (decentralized Proof of Security). Devices continuously evaluate and attest to the security posture of their peers, so that a compromised node is flagged by the surrounding mesh rather than trusted by default.
- **Sub-Zero / post-quantum layer.** Naoris emphasizes [[post-quantum-cryptography|post-quantum]]-resistant primitives intended to remain secure against future quantum-capable adversaries — a forward-looking differentiator versus conventional security vendors.
- **Trust mesh.** The aggregate of attestations forms a real-time trust map. The thesis is that distributed validation produces faster breach detection and removes the centralized control plane that attackers exploit.
- **Cross-chain footprint.** The NAORIS token is issued on Ethereum with a mirrored deployment on BNB Smart Chain (see contract addresses below).

## Token Role

NAORIS is the protocol's native utility and incentive token. Per the project's public materials, it is used to:

- **Reward validation** — devices that contribute to the security mesh earn NAORIS for honest attestation work.
- **Stake / secure** — [[staking|staking]] aligns validator incentives and underpins the dPoSec consensus.
- **Access / payment** — enterprises and applications consuming the protocol's verified cyber-status pay in NAORIS.
- **Governance** — token holders are positioned to influence protocol parameters.

As with most DePIN tokens, value accrual depends on real demand for the underlying service (verified security) outpacing token emissions paid to the supply side (validators).

## Competitive Position

Naoris competes in an unusual niche: decentralized cybersecurity is far less crowded than DeFi or general-purpose [[layer-1|L1]] DePIN, giving it a relatively distinct narrative. Its differentiators are the post-quantum angle and the "devices-as-validators" mesh. Against this, the addressable market (enterprise/government security) is conservative and slow to adopt unproven, token-incentivized infrastructure, and the project must demonstrate that a decentralized mesh outperforms mature centralized security tooling.

| Project | Niche | Differentiator | Token role | Contrast vs Naoris |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Naoris (NAORIS)** | Decentralized cybersecurity mesh ([[depin\|DePIN]]) | Post-quantum (Sub-Zero) + devices-as-validators (dPoSec) | Validation rewards, staking, access, governance | — |
| **[[quant-network\|Quant]] / Hacken-type security tokens** | Audit / security services | Centralized security tooling, audits | Service payment | Centralized incumbents Naoris must outperform |
| **General [[depin\|DePIN]] infra (e.g. Helium, [[render\|Render]])** | Wireless / compute resource networks | Physical-resource mesh | Resource payment, rewards | Same DePIN model, different (non-security) resource |
| **Post-quantum L1s / privacy chains** | Quantum-resistant settlement | PQ cryptography at the chain layer | Gas, staking | Share the PQ narrative; Naoris targets security attestation, not settlement |

Naoris's narrowest differentiator is the **combination** of a post-quantum cryptographic stack with a mutually-validating device mesh aimed specifically at *security posture* — a distinct narrative, but one whose conservative enterprise/government buyers adopt slowly.

## How & Where It Trades

NAORIS liquidity is thin and concentrated. The snapshot data shows a single material centralized listing — **Bitget** (NAORIS/USDT) — plus on-chain liquidity via **Uniswap V3** on [[ethereum|Ethereum]], with a mirrored deployment on [[bnb|BNB Smart Chain]] (same contract address on both chains). The narrow venue base means wide spreads and high slippage on size, and no meaningful derivatives market. The dominant structural feature is severe **emission overhang**: only ~599M of a **4.0B** total/max supply circulates (MC/FDV ≈ 0.15), the lowest ratio in this cohort — large future unlocks and validator rewards are a persistent supply pressure. The large 41k-member Telegram suggests an active retail community relative to the small float.

## Risks

- **Narrative dependence.** Pricing leans heavily on the DePIN + post-quantum + AI-security narratives; sentiment rotations hit it hard (note the **-15.10% 7d** drawdown).
- **Emissions / supply overhang.** A large total supply (4.0B) against a much smaller circulating float means future unlocks and validator rewards can pressure price; the low market-cap-to-FDV ratio signals significant dilution still to come.
- **Adoption risk.** Enterprise/government security procurement is slow and skeptical of crypto-incentivized infrastructure; real, paying usage is the key unknown.
- **Low liquidity.** A sub-$20M cap and thin volume make the token volatile and vulnerable to slippage.
- **Technical / verification risk.** Post-quantum and decentralized-consensus security claims are difficult for outsiders to validate without independent audits.
