# MANTRA [Old] (OM)

**MANTRA [Old]** (OM) is the legacy token of MANTRA Chain, a real-world-asset (RWA) focused Layer 1 blockchain. OM is best known to traders for one of the most violent single-day collapses in crypto history — the **April 13, 2025 crash**, when OM fell roughly 90% in hours from above $6 to under $0.60, erasing several billion dollars of market cap — and for the subsequent **November 2025 redenomination** of OM into a new MANTRA token at a 4:1 ratio. The "[Old]" label denotes the pre-redenomination listing that continues to trade residually.

## Overview

MANTRA began in 2020 as MANTRA DAO, a Polkadot-ecosystem staking/DeFi project, and pivoted into MANTRA Chain — a Cosmos-SDK Layer 1 marketed as "The World's First RWA Layer 1 Blockchain" with MultiVM support and full Ethereum Virtual Machine (EVM) compatibility. By enabling developers and institutions to embed compliance at either the chain level or the smart contract level, MANTRA positioned itself as a "Permissionless Blockchain for Permissioned Applications."

MANTRA Chain is the home of MANTRA Finance, which holds a DeFi license from Dubai's Virtual Assets Regulatory Authority (VARA) and provides regulatory pathways through Broker-Dealer and VA Management and Investment Services licenses, targeting regulated tokenization of real estate, commodities, private credit and tokenized funds. In January 2025 MANTRA signed a headline $1 billion tokenization agreement with Dubai conglomerate DAMAC Group, which powered OM's run to its all-time high near $9 in February 2025.

### The April 2025 crash

On **April 13, 2025**, OM collapsed from roughly $6.30 to under $0.60 within hours — a ~90% drawdown that wiped out approximately $5+ billion in market value. The team attributed the crash to "reckless forced liquidations" by centralized exchanges during low-liquidity weekend hours; critics pointed to extreme supply concentration (a large share of OM held by team-linked wallets and OTC deals) and a low-float/high-FDV structure. CEO John Patrick Mullin subsequently announced a token burn program of ~300 million OM (including ~150M from his own team allocation) to rebuild trust. The episode is now a standard case study in **low-float / high-FDV risk**, weekend liquidity gaps, and CEX liquidation cascades.

### Redenomination (November 2025)

In November 2025 the community approved **Proposal 26**, redenominating OM into a new **MANTRA** token at **4:1**. Holders on MANTRA Chain and supported exchanges were migrated automatically; this legacy "[Old]" listing tracks the residual pre-migration asset. The project continues under MANTRA branding with an RWA-infrastructure narrative, though the 2025 crash remains a significant credibility overhang. (Verified via Perplexity, 2026-06-10.)

## Trading Relevance

- **Where it trades**: legacy OM perps remain listed on [[hyperliquid|Hyperliquid]] (OM-PERP); spot on Uniswap V2 and residual CEX pairs. The active asset is the redenominated MANTRA token on major CEXs (Binance, Coinbase, etc.).
- **Narrative basket**: [[real-world-assets|RWA]] / tokenization basket, alongside Ondo, Plume, and similar — but OM trades with a permanent "rug discount" post-April-2025.
- **Why it matters to traders**: the April 2025 crash is the canonical recent example of how supply concentration + thin weekend books + cascading CEX liquidations can destroy a top-25 token in hours. Useful as a risk template when screening low-float/high-FDV listings.
- **Catalysts**: RWA partnership news (DAMAC-style deals), burn-program execution, migration completion, and any regulatory wins via VARA licensing.

## Valuation Framing (qualitative)

- **Permanent "rug discount":** post-April-2025, OM/MANTRA trades at a structural discount to RWA peers (Ondo, Plume) regardless of partnership news, because the crash exposed supply concentration and weekend-liquidity fragility. Credibility, not technology, is the binding constraint.
- **Legacy vs active token:** the **OM ([Old])** listing here is a residual, low-liquidity artifact of the 4:1 redenomination; valuation should be done on the active [[mantra|MANTRA]] token, not this stale OM line.
- **Unlimited legacy max supply:** legacy OM had no hard cap, an additional dilution concern the redenomination aimed to address — contrast with fixed-cap RWA peers.
- **Optionality on RWA narrative + VARA edge:** if MANTRA executes burns, completes migration cleanly, and lands further regulated-tokenization deals, the regulatory moat (VARA license) is a genuine differentiator — but the market will demand sustained delivery before closing the trust gap.
- **Risk-template value:** for traders, OM's chief ongoing utility is as the **canonical case study** in [[risk-management|low-float/high-FDV liquidation-cascade risk]] (see Trading Relevance), not as an investment.
