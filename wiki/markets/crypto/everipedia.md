# IQ (IQ.wiki / Everipedia)

**IQ** is the native token of **IQ.wiki** (formerly **Everipedia**), a blockchain-based knowledge base — effectively a crypto/Web3-focused, on-chain alternative to Wikipedia where contributions are recorded immutably and editors are rewarded in tokens. The project is now stewarded by **BrainDAO** and is closely associated with the [[frax-share|Frax]] ecosystem. More recently, the IQ token has been extended to power an **Agent Tokenization Platform (ATP)** for launching tokenized AI agents.

## Overview

Everipedia launched in 2017 as a fork-and-improve on Wikipedia's model, later rebranding to **IQ.wiki** with a focus on cryptocurrency, blockchain, and Web3 knowledge. The IQ token economically incentivizes contributors to write, edit, and curate articles, with edits and stakes recorded on-chain to create a more transparent, tamper-resistant knowledge base than a centrally edited encyclopedia. Governance and treasury are coordinated through **BrainDAO**, and the project maintains a notable association with the [[frax-share|Frax]] stablecoin ecosystem.

Building on this knowledge-base foundation, the team has expanded IQ's utility into AI. The **Agent Tokenization Platform (ATP)** is positioned as a way for developers to launch tokenized [[artificial-intelligence|AI]] agents that are intended to be verifiably autonomous and "sovereign" — capable of owning and managing digital (and, in the longer-term vision, physical) assets. This places IQ across two narratives: decentralized knowledge/media and AI agents.

### Architecture & how it works

IQ's design has two layers that map to its two narratives:

**1. The knowledge base (IQ.wiki).** Wiki content is authored and edited by the community, but rather than living only in a centralized database, edits and the metadata around them are anchored on-chain. This gives the knowledge base properties a centrally-run encyclopedia lacks:

- **Immutable edit history** — every revision is recorded, so the provenance of a claim is auditable and edits cannot be silently rewritten.
- **Stake-weighted curation ("brainpower").** Participants lock IQ — historically via a **HiIQ** vote-escrow mechanism similar to [[curve|Curve's]] veCRV — to earn governance weight called *brainpower*. Brainpower determines influence over which edits stand and how rewards and treasury are directed. This turns curation into a skin-in-the-game market rather than an unpaid volunteer effort.
- **Contribution rewards** — editors and curators earn IQ for accepted contributions and upvoted content, tying issuance to peer-judged quality.

**2. The Agent Tokenization Platform (ATP).** ATP lets developers launch **tokenized [[artificial-intelligence|AI]] agents**. Each agent can have its own token and treasury; the vision is "sovereign agents" that hold assets and act autonomously on-chain. IQ functions as the platform's access and incentive asset, so growth in agent launches is meant to create structural IQ demand. ATP is what re-positions IQ from a slow-monetizing media token into the fast-moving **AI-agent** narrative.

**3. Multi-chain deployment.** IQ is an ERC-20 on [[ethereum|Ethereum]] with bridged versions on [[polygon|Polygon]] and [[bnb|BNB Smart Chain]], widening exchange and DeFi reach.

## Mechanism & Token Role

- **Contribution rewards.** IQ rewards editors and curators of the IQ.wiki knowledge base, aligning incentives toward accurate, well-sourced content.
- **Staking on content (HiIQ / brainpower).** The ecosystem uses staking mechanics so participants can lock IQ to gain governance weight ("brainpower") and influence over edits and treasury — a curation-by-stake model.
- **Governance via BrainDAO.** Token holders govern protocol direction, treasury allocation, and product expansion (including ATP).
- **ATP / AI-agent platform.** IQ serves as the access and incentive token for launching and operating tokenized AI agents.
- **Multi-chain.** IQ is deployed on Ethereum with bridged versions on Polygon and BNB Smart Chain.

## Value Accrual & Governance

IQ's value-accrual story is split across its two products:

- **Knowledge base:** accrual is weak and indirect — a decentralized encyclopedia is structurally hard to monetize, so IQ demand from the wiki comes mainly from staking-for-brainpower (locking float) rather than fee revenue.
- **ATP / AI agents:** the intended stronger lever — if agent launches require or consume IQ (access, fees, treasuries), agent-platform growth creates direct buy-pressure. This is the thesis that drives IQ's re-rates, but it is contingent on ATP winning real usage in a crowded field.

**Governance** runs through **BrainDAO**, which stewards the treasury and product roadmap; stake-weighted brainpower gives long-term lockers outsized say over edits and capital allocation. The honest read: IQ is a **dual-narrative token where the AI-agent leg, not the wiki, sets the price**, while the wiki provides legitimacy and a real operating history.

## Competitive Position

IQ.wiki is one of the most established blockchain knowledge bases and benefits from a long operating history, brand recognition (via the Everipedia legacy), and ties to the [[frax-share|Frax]] ecosystem. Its newer AI-agent direction places it among a crowded field of AI-agent launchpads and frameworks. The dual identity is both a strength (two narratives, real product) and a risk (focus dilution): the knowledge-base business has limited direct monetization, while the AI-agent space is intensely competitive and narrative-driven.

## Risks

- **Narrative dependence.** Recent price action is heavily tied to the AI-agent narrative; IQ tends to rally and fade with AI-agent sentiment rather than knowledge-base fundamentals.
- **Monetization risk.** A decentralized encyclopedia is hard to monetize directly, which historically pressured the token's value accrual.
- **Pivot / focus risk.** The shift toward ATP and AI agents is a meaningful strategic bet; execution and differentiation against many competitors are uncertain.
- **Low absolute price / liquidity.** A sub-cent token with a ~$21M cap is volatile and slippage-prone.
- **Long drawdown.** IQ remains far below its 2018 ATH, reflecting years of muted monetization.

## Narrative, Category & Catalysts

IQ trades on a **two-narrative split**, and recognizing which is driving price matters:

- **DeSci-adjacent / decentralized knowledge & media** — the original, slow-burning identity; provides legitimacy more than price momentum.
- **[[artificial-intelligence|AI]] agents / agent launchpads** — the dominant price driver in recent cycles; IQ rallies and fades with AI-agent mania (ATP).
- **[[frax-share|Frax]] ecosystem** — distribution and credibility tailwind via the Frax association.

**Catalysts:** ATP traction (notable agent launches, real fee/treasury flows); broad AI-agent narrative revival; Frax-ecosystem integrations; exchange or DeFi-liquidity expansion. **Anti-catalysts:** AI-agent narrative fatigue, focus-dilution between wiki and ATP, and the chronic difficulty of monetizing the knowledge base.
