# Optimism

**Optimism** (OP) is an **[[ethereum|Ethereum]] [[layer-2|Layer 2]] (L2) [[optimistic-rollup|optimistic rollup]]** and, more broadly, a growing network of chains called the **Superchain**, all built on the open-source **OP Stack**. While OP Mainnet was the first, many prominent chains now run on the OP Stack -- including Base (by Coinbase), Unichain (by Uniswap), Ink (by Kraken), and World Chain (by Worldcoin) -- making OP Stack a leading L2 framework alongside its main rival [[arbitrum]]. The OP token is a governance asset used to direct the Optimism Collective and its retroactive public-goods funding.

## Overview

Optimism is more than just a single blockchain – it's a growing network of chains called the Superchain, all built on the open-source OP Stack. While OP Mainnet was the first, many prominent chains now run on the Optimism’s OP Stack, including Base (by Coinbase), Unichain (by Uniswap), Ink (by Kraken), and World Chain (by Worldcoin). The OP Stack has become the leading framework for Ethereum Layer 2 chains, powering the majority of L2 activity today.
Every OP Chain in the Superchain contributes back to the Optimism Collectiv and all chains follow a standardized revenue-sharing model: the greater of 2.5% of total chain revenue or 15% of onchain profit (defined as fees minus L1 gas costs) is paid back to the Optimism.
By aligning economic incentives across many chains, Optimism transforms Ethereum scaling into a sustainable flywheel by delivering fast, low-cost transactions while reinvesting in the future of the ecosystem. OP holders participate in governance to shape protocol upgrades, funding decisions, and more.

OP and [[arbitrum|Arbitrum (ARB)]] are the two dominant **optimistic rollups** and the closest direct rivals; both share the low-MC/FDV "large future emission" profile that has weighed on L2 governance tokens broadly. Against ZK rollups like [[starknet|Starknet]], optimistic rollups trade a 7-day fraud-proof withdrawal window for cheaper, simpler proving. OP's differentiator is the **horizontal Superchain** strategy (many OP Stack chains sharing standards and revenue), whereas Arbitrum emphasizes its single dominant chain plus Orbit appchains.

## Valuation Framing

The core OP debate is **value accrual**:

- **Superchain-adoption thesis (bull)** — OP Stack powers a large and growing share of L2 activity (Base alone is a top-tier chain). Each OP Chain pays the Collective the greater of 2.5% of total chain revenue or 15% of onchain profit; if Superchain usage compounds, that revenue-share is meant to flow to the Collective and, via governance, the token.
- **Value-accrual skepticism (bear)** — most Superchain *usage* accrues to the individual OP Chains (e.g. Base's sequencer revenue largely benefits Coinbase), and the link between that activity and OP-token value is indirect, governance-mediated, and debated. OP captures little of the economics that flow through chains it merely provides the framework for.
- **Supply overhang** — even a constructive adoption story must absorb the ~50% of supply still to be emitted (see Tokenomics). Until emissions slow materially, fundamental upside is diluted.

Net: OP is a leveraged, governance-mediated bet on aggregate Superchain (especially Base) adoption, discounted heavily for an unresolved value-accrual question and a large unlock schedule.

## Use Case, Narrative & Category

Optimism is an **Ethereum Layer 2** in the **optimistic rollup** category. Optimistic rollups assume transactions are valid by default and rely on a fraud-proof challenge window (vs. the validity proofs used by ZK rollups like [[starknet]]). Optimism's distinguishing bet is the **Superchain** -- a horizontally scaled network of interoperable OP Stack chains that share security, a communication layer, and a governance/revenue framework:

- **OP Stack** -- the open-source, modular codebase that lets anyone launch an L2; adopted by Base, Unichain, Ink, World Chain and others.
- **Superchain revenue share** -- each OP Chain pays the Optimism Collective the greater of 2.5% of total chain revenue or 15% of onchain profit, intended to create a sustainable flywheel.
- **OP token utility** -- governance over protocol upgrades, treasury, and the Collective's **Retroactive Public Goods Funding (RetroPGF)** programs.

The investment thesis is leverage on aggregate Superchain adoption: as more chains (especially Base) scale on the OP Stack, value is meant to accrue to the Collective and, via governance, the OP token.

## How & Where It Trades

OP is liquid on all major centralized exchanges -- **Binance**, **Kraken**, **Upbit**, **Bitget**, **KuCoin** and **Crypto.com** -- with USDT/USD/KRW pairs. Native on-chain liquidity is deep on the OP Mainnet via DEXs such as Velodrome and Uniswap.

For leveraged exposure, **OP-PERP** is widely traded on [[hyperliquid]] and other perp venues, and OP carries comparatively healthy derivatives liquidity for a mid-cap L2 (24h spot volume ~$44M, the largest in this group of L2 peers). Funding tends to track the broad L2 risk cycle and can swing negative around token-unlock dates given the large non-circulating supply. Spot leads price discovery, but perp OI build-ups around governance/Superchain catalysts can amplify moves.

## Trading Profile

**Venues & liquidity** — OP trades on **both** major venue types, making it a deep, liquid two-venue market. On **Binance** it is available as **spot (OP/USDT)** and a **USD-margined perpetual**, giving it the largest 24h spot volume of the L2 peers in this loop. On **[[hyperliquid|Hyperliquid]]** it trades as **OP-PERP** with leverage up to ~40-50x. The dual-venue footprint (CEX spot + CEX perp + on-chain perp) supports tight spreads, reliable borrow for spot-perp structures, and enough depth to size mid-cap positions without excessive slippage — though as a rank ~151 mid-cap, order-book depth thins well below that of majors, so large clips should be worked and staged rather than dumped. The CEX/DEX split also opens cross-venue funding and basis edges (Binance perp vs. Hyperliquid OP-PERP).

**Applicable strategies**

- [[funding-rate-harvest]] — deep OP-PERP liquidity on two venues makes systematic collection of perp funding (vs. hedged spot/opposite perp) practical for a mid-cap.
- [[hl-vs-cex-funding-divergence]] — OP funding can diverge between Binance USD-M perp and Hyperliquid OP-PERP; the two-venue market lets you long the cheap-funding leg and short the rich one.
- [[cash-and-carry]] — long Binance spot OP against a short USD-margined perp captures positive basis/funding with a fully hedged delta.
- [[oi-confirmed-trend]] — as a high-beta L2 governance token, OP trends cleanly on Superchain/L2 catalysts when rising open interest confirms directional flow.
- [[token-unlock-supply-event]] — OP's large multi-year emission/unlock schedule (~50% of max supply still to vest) creates recurring, calendar-driven supply events to position around.
- [[liquidation-cascade-fade]] — thinner mid-cap depth means OP over-extends on liquidation cascades, offering mean-reversion fades once forced flow exhausts.

**Volatility & regime character** — OP is a **high-beta Ethereum L2 / infra-governance altcoin**, closely correlated to ETH beta and the broad L2 risk cycle, and amplified further by its large emission overhang. It typically moves with ETH but with a higher amplitude in both directions, and it underperforms structurally in risk-off regimes because unlock-driven sell pressure compounds macro weakness. Expect elevated realized volatility around governance decisions, Superchain/Base adoption headlines, and unlock dates.

**Risk flags**

- **Supply / unlock overhang** — only ~50% of the 4.29B max supply circulates; scheduled emissions are persistent sell pressure and drive negative-funding episodes around unlock dates.
- **Narrative dependence** — value accrual is governance-mediated and debated (much Superchain usage benefits individual OP Chains like Base, not the OP token), so price is sensitive to shifting L2/Superchain narrative rather than direct cash flows.
- **Mid-cap liquidity / venue concentration** — depth is solid for the tier but far below majors; wide moves and funding dislocations can occur when one venue leads, and slippage rises for large size.
- **High-beta macro risk** — as a high-beta L2, OP amplifies broad-market drawdowns; perp funding can swing sharply negative in risk-off, established-bear conditions.
