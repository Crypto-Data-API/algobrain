# SafePal

**SafePal** (ticker **SFP**, on [[bnb|BNB Chain]] and [[ethereum|Ethereum]]) is the utility token of **SafePal**, a self-custody crypto [[wallets|wallet]] provider offering both hardware and software wallets managed through the SafePal App. SafePal was the first hardware-wallet company backed by [[binance|Binance]] Labs (now YZi Labs) and launched via Binance Launchpad in 2021. The SFP token is used for fee discounts, governance, and ecosystem incentives across the SafePal App, where users store, swap, trade, and grow crypto. It is primarily a **BNB Chain / Ethereum ecosystem wallet token** (CoinGecko categories include Wallets, BNB Chain Ecosystem, and the Animoca Brands and YZi Labs/Binance Labs portfolios).

## Technology / Product

SafePal's product is a **self-custody [[wallets|wallet]] suite** spanning three tiers — air-gapped hardware wallets (the S1/X1 line, which sign transactions offline via QR codes with no USB/Bluetooth/Wi-Fi attack surface), a mobile software wallet, and a browser extension — all unified under the SafePal App. The app aggregates wallet management, multi-chain support across 100+ blockchains and thousands of tokens, in-app token swaps (DEX aggregation), fiat on-ramps, NFT support, dApp browsing, and staking. The hardware/software pairing is the core differentiator: users can hold keys fully offline on the hardware device while still transacting through the convenient app layer.

The SFP token threads through this stack as a loyalty/utility layer — fee discounts on in-app swaps, governance participation, and periodic reward/staking programs. Crucially, SafePal is a **non-custodial** product: the company does not hold user funds, which materially changes its risk profile versus the centralized exchanges behind [[coinex-token|CET]], [[bitmart-token|BMX]] and [[htx-dao|HTX]].

## Market Structure & Derivatives

SFP trades on major **centralized exchanges** — most importantly **[[binance|Binance]]** (its launch venue and deepest market), plus Bitget and KuCoin. Because SFP is a *wallet* token rather than an exchange-issued token, it is not subject to the issuing-exchange concentration risk of [[bitkub-coin|KUB]] or [[coinex-token|CET]]; however, much of its liquidity is concentrated on Binance, so Binance's listing status and regional access materially affect SFP's tradability. With ~$1.5M of daily turnover against a ~$117M cap, SFP is mid-cap but relatively thin. The snapshot does not list a significant regulated [[derivatives]]/funding/open-interest market, so **spot dominates**. Contracts exist on BNB Chain (`0xd41fdb03ba84762dd66a0af1a6c8540ff1ba5dfb`) and Ethereum (`0x12e2b8033420270db2f3b328e32370cb5b2ca134`).

## Use Case / Narrative / Category

SafePal is a **self-custody wallet narrative** — hardware + software wallets aimed at mainstream, security-conscious users. The token thesis rests on wallet adoption, in-app swap volume, and SafePal's strategic backing ([[binance|Binance]] Labs/YZi Labs, Animoca Brands). It competes in the same broad space as other [[wallets|wallet]] tokens and self-custody providers; its differentiator is air-gapped hardware wallets paired with a unified app. The category sits adjacent to — but distinct from — [[exchange-tokens]]: a wallet token captures self-custody and on-chain swap activity rather than a CEX's order-book volume.

## Valuation Framing

SFP is best framed as a **bet on SafePal wallet adoption and in-app swap volume**, not on exchange revenue:

- **Adoption proxy** — value scales with active wallets, hardware-device sales, and the fee take on in-app DEX swaps. Unlike an exchange token, there is no large captive trading venue funneling volume into a burn.
- **No burn floor** — with supply fully circulating and no revenue-funded buyback, SFP lacks the deflationary support that anchors [[bnb|BNB]]-style tokens; the offset is the absence of dilution overhang.
- **Strategic-backing premium** — Binance Labs/YZi Labs and Animoca backing plus a Binance Launchpad pedigree give SFP distribution and listing advantages, but proximity to Binance is double-edged (see Risks).

## Risks

- **Exchange / liquidity concentration:** while SafePal itself is a wallet (self-custody), SFP token liquidity is concentrated on a few CEXs, especially Binance — listing or regional-access changes there would impair tradability.
- **Regulatory risk:** wallet tokens with fee-discount and governance features can attract securities scrutiny; SafePal's regulatory posture varies by jurisdiction.
- **Adoption risk:** the token's value depends on continued growth of SafePal wallet users and in-app activity in a crowded wallet market.
- **Near all-time-low momentum:** trading just ~8% above its ATL signals weak demand and downside momentum in the current regime.
- **Macro / regime risk:** "extreme fear" (F&G 23) and an established bear market pressure mid-cap utility tokens broadly.
