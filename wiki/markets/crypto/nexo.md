# NEXO

**NEXO** is the loyalty/utility token of Nexo, a centralized crypto lending and wealth platform operating since 2018 that offers yield accounts, crypto-backed credit lines, trading, and a crypto card across 200+ jurisdictions. The token (fixed 1B supply, [[ethereum|Ethereum]] ERC-20) functions as a CeFi platform token: holding it unlocks higher yields, lower borrow rates and cashback tiers, making it effectively a levered bet on Nexo's business. The headline 2025 event for traders was Nexo's **return to the US market in April 2025** after its 2022-23 regulatory exit, re-entering with roughly **$11B in assets under management**.

## Overview

Nexo (nexo.com) is a digital-assets wealth platform for growing, managing, and preserving crypto holdings. Since 2018, Nexo has served clients across more than 200 jurisdictions. The platform combines high yields on flexible and fixed-term savings, crypto-backed loans, advanced trading tools, and liquidity solutions including one of the first crypto debit/credit cards.

**The NEXO Token** is Nexo's native cryptocurrency. Holders can earn up to 12% annual interest on tokens held within Savings and Credit Line Wallets. Owning NEXO Tokens grants entry into the Nexo Loyalty Program, providing higher yields on digital assets, up to 0.5% cashback on Nexo Exchange purchases or swaps, borrowing rates starting from 2.9%, and up to 2% cashback on the Nexo card.

**Where it trades:** NEXO trades on centralized exchanges — most actively [[binance|Binance]] (NEXO/USDT), plus Bitget, Bybit, Bitstamp, Bitfinex, and Gate.io — and on Uniswap V2/V3.

## Protocol / Product

Nexo is a **CeFi (centralized finance) lending and wealth platform**, not a smart-contract protocol — the "protocol" is the company's product stack, and NEXO is the loyalty token that prices in access to it.

### Core products

- **Earn / yield accounts.** Clients deposit crypto and stablecoins to earn interest (flexible and fixed-term). Headline rates reach up to ~12% on certain assets, with the top tiers gated behind NEXO-token holdings and payout-in-NEXO. This competes directly with on-chain [[stablecoin-yields]] and other CeFi lenders.
- **Crypto-backed credit lines.** Borrowers post crypto as collateral and draw fiat/stablecoin loans without selling, with borrow rates **from 2.9%** for top loyalty tiers. The loan book is overcollateralized; rates and LTVs improve with NEXO holdings.
- **Nexo Exchange & trading.** In-app spot trading and swaps, with up to 0.5% cashback in NEXO on activity.
- **Nexo Card.** A crypto-linked debit/credit card offering up to **2% cashback** (paid in NEXO or BTC), one of the earliest such products.
- **Institutional liquidity & custody.** OTC, lending and custody services for institutional clients.

### The NEXO loyalty engine

The token's utility is a **tiered loyalty system**: the higher a user's NEXO balance as a percentage of their portfolio, the better their yields, borrow rates and cashback. This creates structural, sticky demand for the token from the platform's own user base and locks a meaningful share of supply into long-hold balances — a key reason NEXO exhibits low volatility relative to the broad alt market.

NEXO has a **fixed 1.00B supply that is fully circulating**, so the MC/FDV ratio is **1.00** — there is **no unlock cliff or future dilution** to discount, a structural contrast with most newer tokens (e.g. [[jito-governance-token|JTO]] at ~0.49). Supply contraction, when it happens, comes from **discretionary buybacks**: Nexo has historically repurchased NEXO in the open market and has at times distributed dividends to holders, both supporting the token. Because the loyalty program rewards holding NEXO as a share of portfolio value, a portion of supply is effectively yield-locked, dampening free float.

## Ecosystem & Use Cases

- **Loyalty/yield access:** the dominant use — holding NEXO upgrades earn rates, borrow rates and cashback tiers.
- **Cashback settlement:** card and exchange cashback is paid in NEXO, creating recurring buy/distribution flow.
- **Collateral within Nexo:** NEXO can itself be used as collateral on the platform.
- **Speculative platform proxy:** a liquid public-market expression of Nexo's private business performance.

## Valuation Framework (qualitative)

NEXO is best valued as **equity-like exposure to a private CeFi business** wrapped in a token:

1. **Platform-traction proxy.** AUM (~$11B at the 2025 US relaunch), loan-book size, and active users are the fundamental drivers; NEXO should track the health and growth of Nexo's lending business.
2. **Buyback/dividend support.** Discretionary buybacks and historical dividends put a soft floor under the token and reward holders, akin to a capital-return policy.
3. **Loyalty-locked demand.** Tiered benefits create structural, recurring demand and reduce free float — a source of the token's low beta.
4. **Regulatory-discount overlay.** The token carries an "Alleged SEC Securities" tag and a settlement history; valuation must discount renewed regulatory risk despite the favorable post-2025 US re-entry.

## Trading Playbook

- **Relative-strength holding in drawdowns.** Fixed supply, buyback support and a yield-locked holder base make NEXO a defensive, low-beta CeFi-token candidate when the broad alt market sells off (its near-flat 2026-06-20 action vs an extreme-fear tape is the template). The trade-off is that it lags badly in alt-season breadth rallies.
- **Catalyst trade.** Position around platform-specific catalysts: US business traction post-relaunch, buyback or dividend announcements, loyalty-program changes, and any renewed regulatory action.
- **Liquidity discipline.** With ~$4-7M daily volume, scale entries/exits and use limit orders; the token can gap on thin books.
- **CeFi-basket pair.** Trade NEXO against BNB / OKB / CRO to express a view on CeFi/exchange-token strength versus the broad market.

## History

- **2018** — Nexo founded; launches crypto-backed lending and yield products, scaling to 200+ jurisdictions.
- **2021-05-12** — NEXO token all-time high of **$4.07** during the bull market.
- **Jan 2023 — US exit.** Nexo paid a **$45M settlement** with the SEC and state regulators over its Earn Interest Product (flagged as unregistered securities) and wound down US operations. The token carries an "Alleged SEC Securities" tag on CoinGecko stemming from this.
- **2025 — "Growth 2025" strategy.** Plans to integrate AI-driven tools for portfolio management, trading and predictive analytics.
- **2025-04-28 — US relaunch.** Nexo announced its return to the US market after a two-year absence, citing renewed regulatory optimism under the Trump administration. The re-entry launched with **~$11B in AUM**, with infrastructure support from US-based **Bakkt**; offerings include high-yield savings, asset-backed credit lines, advanced trading and institutional liquidity. (CoinDesk, 2025-04-28)
- **2026-06-20** — $0.793, market cap $793M, rank #79; near-flat on the day and week amid extreme fear, ~80% below its 2021 ATH.

## Risks

- **Counterparty / custodial risk:** Nexo holds client assets; it is a CeFi entity, and the 2022 cycle showed how quickly CeFi lenders (Celsius, BlockFi) can fail.
- **Regulatory:** "Alleged SEC Securities" tag plus a $45M 2023 settlement; renewed US/EU action remains a tail risk despite the 2025 re-entry.
- **Liquidity:** thin (~$4-7M/day) for its cap; slippage and gap risk on platform news.
- **Single-business concentration:** the token is a proxy for one private company; idiosyncratic operational or solvency problems hit it directly.
- **Lagging beta:** underperforms in alt-season breadth trades.
