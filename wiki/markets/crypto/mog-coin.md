# Mog Coin

**Mog Coin** (ticker **MOG**) is a community-driven **[[meme-coins|meme coin]]** with a cat / "mogging" internet-culture theme, launched in mid-2023 on [[ethereum|Ethereum]] and later bridged to **[[base|Base]]** and **Avalanche**. It has no intrinsic utility — it is a pure attention/narrative asset, notable for its enormous supply (hundreds of trillions of tokens, so each unit prices in the nano-dollar range) and for appearing in meme-focused buckets such as "Murad Picks" and Binance Alpha Spotlight. As a meme coin, its value is driven entirely by social momentum and speculation.

## Overview

Mog Coin is a **meme coin** built around cat / internet-"mogging" culture, with no roadmap of intrinsic utility — by design, its thesis is purely cultural and speculative. It launched on [[ethereum]] in 2023 and later expanded to **[[base]]** and **Avalanche** via bridged contracts, positioning itself as a "cats > dogs" entrant against the dog-themed meme incumbents like Dogecoin and [[shiba-inu]].

The project's marketing leans heavily on community/meme hype rather than fundamentals; the original project copy is promotional rather than informational and is not reproduced here. What is verifiable is the supply structure and the categories CoinGecko assigns it: **Meme, Cat-Themed, 4chan-Themed, Murad Picks, Binance Alpha Spotlight**, spanning the Ethereum, Base and Avalanche ecosystems.

## Use Case, Narrative & Category

- **Category:** [[meme-coin]] — value derives from attention, virality and trader speculation, not cash flows or protocol revenue.
- **Theme:** cat / "mogging" meme culture; explicitly part of the "cat coin" cohort that emerged as a counter-narrative to dog memes.
- **Multichain:** native on [[ethereum]], bridged to [[base]] and Avalanche, broadening its trading footprint and DEX liquidity.
- **Endorsement narratives:** inclusion in the "Murad Picks" basket (associated with meme-supercycle thesis advocate Murad Mahmudov) and "Binance Alpha Spotlight" tagging are part of its attention loop.
- **Huge unit supply:** with ~390T tokens circulating, each MOG prices in the nano-dollar range, which is itself a meme/retail-psychology feature (low per-unit price encourages "lottery ticket" buying).

## How & Where It Trades

MOG trades both **on-chain (DEX)** and on **centralized exchanges**. Because it is multichain, the bulk of organic price discovery happens on **Uniswap** (V2/V3) on [[ethereum]] and on DEXs across [[base]] and Avalanche, with CEX listings (Kraken, Bitget, KuCoin, Crypto.com) layered on top. Meme coins like MOG can also appear on perpetual-futures venues including [[hyperliquid]] when narrative is hot; perp funding on such low-float memes is highly volatile — spiking sharply positive during euphoric pumps (longs crowded) and flipping negative on dumps — making funding/OI useful but noisy signals. Liquidity is shallow relative to large caps (~$7.5M daily volume), so slippage and gap risk are significant.

## Risks

- **Pure meme / no utility** — there is no product, cash flow or protocol revenue; value depends entirely on sustained social attention, which can evaporate quickly.
- **Extreme volatility & drawdown** — meme coins routinely fall 80-95% from local highs; MOG's own ~97% drawdown from ATH is illustrative.
- **Liquidity & concentration** — relatively thin liquidity and likely concentrated holder distribution raise the risk of sharp dumps and price manipulation.
- **Multichain / bridge surface** — bridged contracts across [[base]] and Avalanche add smart-contract and bridge risk (see [[cross-chain-bridge]]).
- **Regime sensitivity** — as one of the highest-beta asset classes, memes are acutely exposed to the prevailing **[[fear-and-greed-index|Fear & Greed]] 23 / "Established Bear Market"** backdrop (2026-06-20). See [[market-regime]].
