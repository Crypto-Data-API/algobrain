# DEAPcoin

**DEAPcoin** (DEP) is the ERC-20 utility token of **PlayMining**, a [[play-to-earn]] gaming and [[nft]] platform operated by **Digital Entertainment Asset (DEA)**, a company headquartered in Singapore with strong roots in Japan. PlayMining lets users earn DEP by playing free-to-play games and consuming content (including manga/comics), and use DEP to buy and trade in-game items and digital art NFTs on the PlayMining NFT marketplace, with a share of transaction value returned to creators. It ranks **#662** by market capitalization.

## Overview

**PlayMining** is a [[play-to-earn]] entertainment platform where users acquire DEP by enjoying free-to-play games and content (including cartoons/manga). In-game roles and goods are represented as [[nft]]s and traded for DEP, including via a digital-art marketplace, with a portion of each transaction returned to creators. The model aims to attract a broad global user base by distributing token rewards for engagement, then capturing value through NFT and item commerce.

### Operator and architecture

DEAPcoin is issued by **Digital Entertainment Asset Pte. Ltd. (DEA)**, founded by Naohito Yoshida and Kozo Yamada, headquartered in **Singapore** and operationally rooted in **Japan**. DEP is an **ERC-20** token originally on **Ethereum**, and has expanded to a multi-chain presence (BNB Chain, Solana, Avalanche) to lower transaction costs for gameplay and marketplace activity. PlayMining hosts a portfolio of titles (e.g., JobTribes and other partner games) and operates the PlayMining NFT marketplace as the commerce hub of the ecosystem.

### Token role

- **Reward currency** — earned by playing games and engaging with content ([[play-to-earn]]).
- **Marketplace medium** — used to buy, sell, and auction NFTs and in-game items, with creator revenue-share.
- **Platform utility** — the common settlement token across PlayMining's games and services.

### Sinks and faucets

Unlike pure dual-token battlers, PlayMining runs a **single-token** model where DEP is both the reward and the settlement currency across a portfolio of titles — so the sink/faucet balance is managed at the platform level rather than within one game.

**Faucets (DEP emission / earning):**

- **Play-to-earn rewards** — DEP distributed for playing free-to-play titles (e.g., JobTribes) and engaging with content including manga/comics.
- **Campaigns and incentives** — promotional rewards to attract and retain users across the PlayMining ecosystem.

**Sinks (DEP demand / removal):**

- **NFT marketplace purchases** — buying, selling, and auctioning in-game items and digital-art NFTs, with a creator revenue-share routing value back to IP holders.
- **In-game item commerce** — acquiring roles, goods, and upgrades across PlayMining titles.
- **Hard supply cap** — DEP's **30B max supply** caps long-run dilution, distinguishing it from uncapped emitters like [[smooth-love-potion|SLP]]; with MC/FDV ~0.92 most supply is already circulating, so remaining unlock dilution is modest.

The platform-level challenge is the same as all of [[gamefi|GameFi]]: gameplay rewards continuously add DEP to circulation (faucet), and genuine NFT/item demand (sink) must keep pace or the token erodes. The hard cap limits the worst-case inflation but does not solve demand decay — engagement and marketplace volume are the real fundamentals (see [[emissions]]).

## Competitive Position

DEAPcoin/PlayMining competes in the **Asia-focused [[play-to-earn]] and NFT-platform** segment, drawing on DEA's Japanese content and IP relationships. It is most comparable to [[bora|BORA]] (Korea) as a multi-title regional gaming-content network, and sits in the broader cohort with [[smooth-love-potion|SLP]] / [[axie-infinity|Axie Infinity]] and [[big-time|Big Time]]. Its differentiators are a creator-revenue-share model, manga/content integration, and an established NFT marketplace; its challenge, like all P2E platforms, is sustaining genuine player demand beyond reward-farming.
