# OriginTrail

**OriginTrail** (ticker **TRAC**) is the utility token of the OriginTrail Decentralized Knowledge Graph (DKG), an infrastructure project building "verifiable data" for AI and supply chains. It combines the DKG with the **NeuroWeb** blockchain (a [[polkadot|Polkadot]] parachain) to let enterprises and AI agents publish, discover, and verify "Knowledge Assets" — including [[real-world-assets|real-world-asset]] data. TRAC is an [[ethereum|Ethereum]]/Base ERC-20 token used to pay for publishing data to the DKG.

TRAC has a hard cap of 500 million tokens, with ~89% already in circulation, leaving only modest remaining unlock overhang (~52.7M TRAC, ~12% of float). This is one of the **lowest dilution profiles** in the AI/RWA mid-cap cohort — most peers (e.g. [[allora|ALLO]] at ~0.30 MC/FDV) carry far heavier future supply pressure. Unusually, TRAC was distributed without a large team/VC pre-mine emphasis and is **non-inflationary** at the TRAC layer — TRAC is spent (paid into the network) to publish Knowledge Assets, creating recurring usage-driven demand rather than emissions-driven sell pressure.

The protocol uses a **two-token design**: the separate **NEURO** token secures the [[polkadot|NeuroWeb]] parachain (staking/validation/block rewards), so the architecture splits "data publishing" (TRAC, demand-side fuel) from "network security" (NEURO, inflationary staking rewards). For a TRAC holder this matters: dilution and inflation risk are pushed onto NEURO, while TRAC's value accrual is tied to throughput of Knowledge-Asset publishing.

## Use Case, Narrative & Category

OriginTrail's category spans **Decentralized Knowledge Graph (DKG) / verifiable-data infrastructure**, intersecting [[ai-trading|AI]], [[real-world-assets|RWA]], [[depin|DePIN]], and decentralized-science (DeSci) narratives. The system:

- **DKG** — a decentralized graph where "Knowledge Assets" are published with cryptographic provenance, making data discoverable and verifiable (provenance, ownership, integrity).
- **NeuroWeb** — a [[polkadot|Polkadot]] parachain providing the blockchain layer and incentives for the DKG.
- **AI angle** — the DKG is pitched as a trusted knowledge source to ground AI models and agents, reducing hallucination/misinformation by anchoring answers to verifiable data (e.g. the experimental ChatDKG demo).

OriginTrail emphasizes enterprise traction, citing collaborations and integrations referenced with organizations such as the British Standards Institution, Walmart, Oracle, Polkadot/Parity, and the EU Commission's Next Generation Internet program. Its long-running narrative is supply-chain traceability that has since broadened into the AI-data / RWA-data thesis, positioning the DKG as a "verifiable memory" layer for autonomous [[ai-agents|AI agents]].

## Valuation Framing (qualitative)

TRAC is best understood as a **usage-fee token**: its long-term value is a function of how much real data gets published to the DKG (each publish consumes TRAC). Bullish case — enterprise + AI-agent demand for verifiable, provenance-anchored data turns the DKG into critical infrastructure, and the fixed supply means rising usage maps to rising token demand. Bearish case — "verifiable data for AI" remains a narrative without paying throughput, in which case TRAC is a ~$150M mid-cap priced on a promise. Key on-chain signal to watch: **Knowledge-Asset publishing volume / TRAC burned-into-network per epoch** versus market cap. As always, this is framing, not a price target or investment advice.

## Trading Profile

### Venues & liquidity

TRAC is a **spot-primary** asset: it is tradable on **Binance SPOT only** among the major venues here, with **no liquid perpetual venue** — so leverage and short access are limited and perp funding/basis/liquidation strategies do **not** apply. Practically, positions are built and exited with real inventory (no synthetic shorts), so directional exposure must be expressed long-only or via rotation into/out of stablecoins. With moderate 24h volume (~$1.9M), execution should favor limit orders, staged/scaled entries and exits, and modest position sizing; larger market orders carry meaningful slippage. The absence of a perp bid also means no funding-rate carry and no easy hedge, reinforcing spot-DCA and swing-style approaches over high-frequency leveraged tactics.

### Applicable strategies

- [[dca-strategy]] — spot-only, low-dilution infrastructure token ~90% below ATH; dollar-cost averaging suits accumulating a long base without timing the AI/RWA narrative turn.
- [[buy-and-hold]] — fixed 500M cap and usage-fee (TRAC-burn) demand model make TRAC a candidate long-horizon spot hold on the "verifiable data for AI" thesis.
- [[breakout-and-retest]] — thin, range-bound liquidity means clean breaks above multi-month bases can be entered on the confirming retest rather than chasing on spot.
- [[narrative-trading]] — TRAC re-rates with AI, RWA, DePIN and DeSci narrative cycles; positioning ahead of/into theme rotations captures its beta.
- [[range-trading]] — with no perp leverage and a bear-market chop regime, fading the edges of established price ranges fits a spot-only mid-cap.
- [[atr-trailing-stop]] — volatility-scaled trailing stops manage the wide swings of a low-liquidity mid-cap while keeping the position long-only.

### Volatility & regime character

TRAC is a **small-to-mid-cap infrastructure token** (rank ~#207–234, ~$150M cap) that behaves as a high-beta AI/RWA/DePIN theme play rather than a memecoin — no reflexive supply-driven meme dynamics, but strong sensitivity to broad crypto risk-on/risk-off rotations. It is **highly correlated to BTC/ETH** direction and amplifies moves in the crypto-AI cohort (e.g. [[bittensor|TAO]]); in risk-off regimes like the current Established Bear Market it tends to underperform. Its low dilution (MC/FDV ~0.89) removes emissions overhang but does not shield it from narrative-driven drawdowns.

### Risk flags

- **Narrative dependence** — valuation rests on AI/RWA/DePIN adoption translating into real DKG publishing (TRAC-burn) demand; a stalled narrative leaves it a mid-cap priced on a promise.
- **Two-token complexity** — the TRAC/NEURO split and Polkadot-parachain dependency add execution and ecosystem risk.
- **Supply overhang** — modest remaining unlock (~52.7M TRAC, ~12% of float) is small but still a periodic supply consideration.
