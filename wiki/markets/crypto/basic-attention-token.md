# Basic Attention Token

**Basic Attention Token** (ticker **BAT**) is an [[ethereum|Ethereum]] ERC-20 [[utility-token|utility token]] powering the advertising and rewards economy of the **Brave** privacy browser. Advertisers pay in BAT to reach users' attention, and users who opt into privacy-respecting Brave Ads earn BAT rewards. Created by Brendan Eich (creator of JavaScript, co-founder of Mozilla) and Brian Bondy, BAT held its ICO on May 31, 2017, raising ~$35M in under 30 seconds.

BAT has a fixed 1.5 billion supply that is essentially fully circulating (MC/FDV ≈ 1.00), so there is **negligible future dilution risk** — a structural positive versus the inflationary L1s and reserve-funded projects in this cohort. The original distribution allocated 1B BAT to the public ICO, with the remainder split between a user-growth pool and the development team. BAT's utility is closed-loop: it is the unit of account for the Brave advertising marketplace, flowing from advertisers to publishers and to opted-in users as rewards.

## Use Case, Narrative & Category

BAT's category is **Brave-ads / attention-economy utility token**. The model:

- **Brave browser** blocks third-party trackers and ads by default; ad targeting is performed on-device, so user data never leaves the device.
- **Brave Ads** — users opt in to privacy-preserving ads and earn BAT; advertisers pay in BAT to access attention; publishers/creators receive BAT tips and rewards.
- The thesis is to "fix digital advertising" — replacing surveillance-based ad-tech with a privacy-respecting, attention-priced marketplace.

The narrative depends on Brave browser user growth and the depth of the ads marketplace. A key structural critique is that Brave has progressively allowed users to earn rewards/cash without holding BAT, and ad payouts can be made in [[stablecoin|stablecoins]], which dilutes BAT's must-hold utility and weakens the token-to-product flywheel.

---

## Valuation Framing

As a [[utility-token]], BAT's value should in theory track demand for its in-product role: advertisers needing BAT to buy attention, and the volume of the Brave ads marketplace. In practice the link has weakened — Brave's drift toward non-BAT and stablecoin payouts means the token captures less of the product's success than the original whitepaper implied. There are no cash flows, dividends, or buybacks accruing to holders, so BAT is not valuable on a discounted-cash-flow basis; it is a fixed-supply network/utility token whose price reflects speculative demand plus whatever residual must-hold utility remains in the rewards loop. Versus peers, BAT screens **cheap on dilution risk** (MC ≈ FDV) and **deep on liquidity** (~$25M/24h, the strongest in this cohort), but **structurally challenged on token-demand capture**. The ~95% drawdown from the 2021 ATH reflects both the bear regime and the market's discount for the weakening flywheel.

## Risks

- **Utility erosion** — Brave's move toward non-BAT and stablecoin reward options reduces the token's must-hold demand.
- **Ad-market dependence** — BAT value is tied to Brave ad spend and browser adoption, which face stiff competition from Chrome/Safari and shifting privacy regulation.
- **Mature/declining narrative** — BAT is a 2017-era project; attention has rotated to newer themes, and it trades ~95% below ATH.
- **Macro / regime** — Established Bear Market with [[fear-and-greed-index|Fear & Greed]] at 23 pressures mid-cap legacy alts.
- **Concentration** — sizable team/foundation holdings historically influenced supply dynamics.
