# KUB Coin

**KUB Coin** (KUB) is the native gas and utility token of **Bitkub Chain**, an EVM-compatible Layer 1 blockchain built by **Bitkub**, Thailand's largest domestic cryptocurrency exchange. KUB pays for on-chain gas, can be converted into trading-fee credits on the Bitkub Exchange, and is staked by validators and delegators to secure and govern the network. It is effectively an **[[centralized-exchange|exchange-linked]] ecosystem token** whose value is tied to the activity and regulatory standing of the Bitkub group in Thailand.

KUB has a hard-capped maximum supply of 110M tokens, with roughly 63% currently circulating — meaning a meaningful share remains to be released, a dilution overhang to watch. Unlike pure burn-model exchange tokens, KUB's utility centres on three pillars:

- **Gas:** all transactions on Bitkub Chain are paid in KUB.
- **Fee credits:** KUB can be converted into trading-fee discounts/credits on the Bitkub Exchange, linking token demand to exchange volume.
- **Staking:** validators and delegators stake KUB to secure and govern the chain and earn rewards.

This fee-utility design ties demand to the health of the Bitkub exchange and chain rather than to an explicit buyback-and-burn (contrast [[coinex-token|CET]], which historically used buyback/burn mechanics).

## How & Where It Trades

KUB is most actively traded on the **Bitkub Exchange** itself (its home venue) and has secondary listings on international centralized exchanges such as Bitget. This concentration is the defining structural risk of the token: **most genuine liquidity sits on a single, jurisdiction-bound venue (Bitkub, Thailand)**. If the Bitkub exchange faced an outage, regulatory action, or delisting on third-party venues, off-platform price discovery would be extremely thin — the global ~$139K/day volume already illustrates how shallow non-Bitkub liquidity is. No significant regulated derivatives, funding, or open-interest market for KUB is listed in the snapshot data, so the token trades essentially **spot-only** outside its home exchange. There is no [[hyperliquid|Hyperliquid]] or major-CEX KUB perp, meaning holders cannot readily hedge or short, and the token's price is governed almost entirely by spot flow on Bitkub.

## Use Case / Narrative / Category

KUB sits at the intersection of two narratives: a **national/regional exchange token** (the "Thai crypto" play) and a **Layer 1 gas token**. Bitkub dominates regulated crypto trading in Thailand, and KUB is the on-ramp to that ecosystem — gas, staking, and discounted fees. Its investment thesis is therefore closely correlated with Thai retail crypto adoption, the Thai SEC's regulatory posture, and Bitkub's corporate trajectory.

### Peer comparison — exchange tokens

KUB belongs to the **exchange-linked token** family, but its single-jurisdiction footprint sets it apart from global majors. Approximate orientation:

| Token | Exchange | Reach | Mechanic | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **KUB** | Bitkub | Thailand (domestic) | Gas + fee credits + staking | L1 gas token; concentrated venue/jurisdiction risk |
| [[bnb\|BNB]] | Binance | Global | Burn + gas (BNB Chain) + fee discount | Largest exchange token; deep global liquidity |
| OKB | OKX | Global | Buyback/burn + ecosystem | Large-cap, global |
| [[coinex-token\|CET]] | CoinEx | Global | Historically buyback/burn | Smaller global exchange token |
| KCS | KuCoin | Global | Fee-share/dividend model | Mid-cap, revenue-share style |

KUB's distinguishing feature is that it is simultaneously a **gas token for its own L1** (utility-driven demand) and a **regional exchange token** (volume-driven demand), but it lacks the global liquidity and burn-driven scarcity of BNB/OKB. Its fortunes are tied to one country's retail market rather than a global user base.

### Valuation framing (qualitative)

KUB is best understood as a **leveraged play on Thai retail crypto activity and Bitkub's corporate standing**, not a globally diversified L1. With no disclosed protocol revenue multiple to anchor it, valuation hinges on (1) Bitkub exchange volumes and market share in Thailand, (2) Thai SEC regulatory posture (a binary, concentrated risk), and (3) on-chain usage of Bitkub Chain driving organic KUB gas demand. At ~$52M cap / ~$83M FDV (MC/FDV ~0.63), the market prices KUB as a small, regionally-bound ecosystem token deep in a multi-year drawdown. The thin ~$139K/day turnover means the quoted price is fragile and not representative of executable size off the home venue — a key caveat for any valuation work.

## Risks

- **Exchange counterparty / venue concentration:** KUB's value and liquidity are tightly coupled to the Bitkub exchange. Counterparty failure, hack, or solvency issues at Bitkub would directly impair the token (see [[centralized-exchange]] counterparty risk).
- **Regulatory risk:** Bitkub operates under the Thai SEC; adverse Thai regulation, licensing changes, or enforcement actions are an outsized, concentrated risk for a single-jurisdiction exchange token.
- **Liquidity risk:** ~$139K daily volume is very thin; entering or exiting size off the home exchange incurs heavy [[slippage]], and there is no perp market to hedge with.
- **Dilution overhang:** ~37% of max supply is not yet circulating (MC/FDV ~0.63).
- **Macro / regime risk:** with the market in "extreme fear" (F&G ≈ 23) and an Established Bear Market, low-cap, low-liquidity exchange tokens are especially vulnerable to drawdowns.

## Overview

The KUB Coin (KUB) is the native utility coin powering the KUB Chain, serving as a cornerstone of its growing ecosystem. It facilitates on-chain gas fee payments, can be converted into fee credits on the Bitkub Exchange, and enables staking for Validators and Delegators to actively secure and govern the network. KUB also supports seamless exchanges with various digital assets and unlocks exclusive services and benefits from KUB Chain's partners, driving innovation and collaboration within the blockchain ecosystem. Additionally, KUB is listed on Thailand’s leading Bitkub Exchange and other international cryptocurrency exchanges, ensuring accessibility and global reach.
