# Humanity

**Humanity Protocol** (ticker **H**, deployed on [[ethereum]] and BNB Smart Chain) is a blockchain whose flagship feature is a native **Proof of Humanity (PoH)** mechanism that verifies a user is a unique human via decentralized identifiers (DID), verifiable credentials (VC), and zero-knowledge proofs. The goal is privacy-preserving sybil resistance, self-sovereign identity, and user-owned data — positioning H as a [[proof-of-personhood]] / [[proof-of-humanity]] rail for an internet increasingly populated by [[ai-agents|AI agents]] and bots. It is a direct competitor to [[worldcoin-wld|Worldcoin]] in the [[proof-of-humanity]] / [[decentralized-ai|decentralized-AI]] category.

## Valuation Framing (qualitative)

H is a **narrative + network-effect bet priced on a high FDV**. At ~$370M market cap but ~$2.03B FDV, the market is paying a fully-diluted price that assumes the proof-of-humanity network becomes a default identity rail — yet ~82% of supply is still locked, so realized dilution will pressure price as it unlocks. The fundamental driver is verified-human count and integrations (airdrop sybil resistance, fair governance, human-vs-[[ai-agents|agent]] gating), none of which is independently audited here. With a -76% drawdown from a three-week-old ATH and -21% on the week, the token is repricing from peak euphoria toward something closer to demonstrated adoption. The bull case is the [[decentralized-ai]] tailwind (bots proliferating raises the value of personhood proofs); the bear case is unlock supply + unproven integration depth + direct [[worldcoin-wld|Worldcoin]] competition. Qualitative framing only — not a price target.

## Use Case, Narrative & Category

Humanity Protocol targets the **proof-of-personhood** problem: distinguishing real humans from bots and sybils without surrendering privacy. Its differentiator versus [[worldcoin-wld|Worldcoin]] is a palm-scan / credential approach that it frames as less invasive than iris biometrics, combined with ZK proofs and DID/VC standards. The use cases — airdrop sybil resistance, fair governance, KYC-light onboarding, and human-vs-agent gating — are increasingly relevant as AI agents proliferate, tying it to the [[decentralized-ai]] and [[artificial-intelligence]] narratives.
