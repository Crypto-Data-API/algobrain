# WEMIX

**WEMIX** (ticker **WEMIX**) is the native token of the **WEMIX gaming blockchain** built by **Wemade**, a publicly listed South Korean game developer. WEMIX powers a Korean blockchain-gaming ([[gamefi]]) ecosystem — the WEMIX PLAY game portal, the **WEMIX3.0 Layer-1 mainnet**, an inter-game token/NFT economy, and the (former) WEMIX DOLLAR stablecoin — aimed at bringing Wemade's commercial game titles on-chain with play-to-earn ([[play-to-earn]]) mechanics.

## Technology & Protocol

WEMIX is a **Korean gaming Layer-1** stack, distinct from a single-game token:

- **WEMIX3.0** — an EVM-compatible Layer-1 mainnet (proof-of-stake, validator-based) on which the broader ecosystem settles. WEMIX is its gas, staking and governance asset.
- **WEMIX PLAY** — a blockchain-game publishing portal aggregating titles (built around Wemade's MIR franchise and third-party games) with on-chain assets and tokens.
- **Inter-game economy** — a multi-token / [[nft|NFT]] framework intended to let in-game items and currencies move across titles, with WEMIX as the reserve/settlement layer.
- **Studio backing** — the key differentiator versus pure-crypto [[gamefi]] projects is that WEMIX is backed by an **established, revenue-generating traditional game studio (Wemade)** rather than a crypto-native startup.

## Valuation Framing

WEMIX is partly a **utility/infrastructure token** (gas + staking on WEMIX3.0) and partly a **speculative bet on Wemade's ability to drive on-chain game demand**:

- **Fundamental anchor:** unlike a [[meme-coins|meme]], there is a real studio with shipping games and recurring revenue. In principle, WEMIX demand should track active players, in-game spend, and validator staking.
- **Reality check:** [[play-to-earn]] token economies have a poor record of retaining users once emissions slow; sustained *game-driven* (not speculative) demand is unproven at scale. The 2022 supply scandal and a 2025 security incident impose a persistent **credibility discount**.
- **Net:** valuation rests on Wemade execution + Korean exchange access; the studio backing makes it less reflexive than a meme but more dependent on **single-sponsor and single-jurisdiction** outcomes than a typical Layer-1.

## Notable History

- **2020–2021:** Launched by Wemade; rode the 2021 GameFi/play-to-earn boom. WEMIX reached its all-time high of **$24.71 (2021-11-21)** — current price is roughly **99% below** that peak.
- **2022 delisting:** South Korea's **DAXA** exchange consortium (Upbit, Bithumb, Coinone, Korbit) **delisted WEMIX** over discrepancies between disclosed and actual circulating supply; the token crashed sharply.
- **2023:** WEMIX was relisted on some Korean venues after remediation.
- **2025:** WEMIX suffered a security incident (a bridge/exchange-related hack reported in 2025) that again pressured the token and tested community trust.
- **All-time low:** **$0.1284 (2021-06-22)**.

## Risks

- **Regulatory/exchange risk in home market:** WEMIX's value is unusually exposed to Korean exchange listing decisions (DAXA), which have already removed and restored the token once. A renewed delisting would severely impair liquidity.
- **Trust and disclosure:** the supply-transparency controversy and a subsequent security incident are reputational scars; the market applies a credibility discount.
- **Bear-market beta:** a small-cap GameFi token in an extreme-fear regime (Fear & Greed = 23), high-beta to the downside with very thin liquidity.
- **GameFi sustainability:** play-to-earn economies struggle to retain users once emissions slow; durable game-driven demand is unproven at scale.
- **Dilution:** ~22% of max supply not yet circulating.
- **Centralization:** heavy dependence on a single corporate sponsor (Wemade).
