# Holo

**Holo** (ticker **HOT**) is the token of the Holo/[[Holochain]] project — an agent-centric, peer-to-peer "post-blockchain" application framework. Rather than a single global ledger, Holochain gives every agent its own source chain validated against a shared distributed hash table (DHT), aiming for an energy-efficient distributed web with user data ownership built in. **HOT** ("HoloToken") is an [[ethereum]]-based ERC-20 (with deployments across [[base]], [[arbitrum]], Harmony, Energi, and Sora) originally issued to fund development and intended to be redeemable for HoloFuel hosting credits on the Holo network.

## Tokenomics & Supply

- **Supply:** ~177.62B HOT, with circulating equal to total — the market-cap-to-FDV ratio is ~1.00, meaning effectively all tokens are already in circulation and there is **no large future-unlock overhang** (a structural contrast to the newer tokens in this category).
- **No fixed max supply** is recorded; HOT was distributed in 2018 via Holo's ICO/crowdfund.
- **Design intent:** HOT was created as a placeholder/IOU for **HoloFuel**, the mutual-credit currency meant to settle hosting payments between app publishers and hosts on the live Holo network. The long-running gap between HOT and a fully live HoloFuel economy is central to the project's history and risk profile.
- **Utility today:** HOT trades primarily as a speculative ERC-20; its end-state utility depends on the Holo hosting marketplace reaching scale.

Holochain is the architectural outlier: it rejects global consensus entirely in favour of per-agent chains validated against a shared DHT. That is technically distinctive but means it does not compete head-to-head on smart-contract throughput; its value case rests on the live Holo hosting economy, which has lagged.

## Valuation Framing (qualitative)

HOT is best read as a **long-dated option on the Holo hosting marketplace reaching scale**. With ~1.0 MC/FDV there is no unlock dilution, so the cap table is not the risk — *delivery* is. The persistent gap between HOT-as-IOU and a fully live HoloFuel economy means the ~$56M cap prices the narrative and brand tenure rather than measurable hosting revenue. Compared with storage-DePIN peers (Filecoin, Arweave) that have observable paid usage, HOT trades more on legacy recognition and beta to the [[depin]] theme. The extreme-fear regime compresses speculative names like this; a durable re-rate would need evidence of actual app publishers paying hosts in HoloFuel at scale. Qualitative framing only — not a price target.

## Use Case, Narrative & Category

Holochain pitches an **agent-centric**, scalable alternative to global-consensus blockchains for distributed apps, often grouped with the [[depin]] / decentralized-compute-and-storage narrative (it appears in DePIN index baskets). The vision is a peer-hosted web where users run their own chains and host each other's apps for HoloFuel, removing reliance on centralized cloud and giving users control of their data. Aggregator categories include Smart Contract Platform, Storage, DePIN, and multiple chain-ecosystem tags (Ethereum, Base, Arbitrum, Harmony, Energi, Sora).

## Risks

- **Roadmap / delivery risk:** the headline risk for years has been the slow path from HOT-as-IOU to a live, revenue-generating HoloFuel hosting economy; sustained value depends on real hosting adoption.
- **Deep drawdown / dead-money risk:** ~99% below ATH; without a fresh catalyst the token can languish.
- **Architectural trade-offs:** the agent-centric/DHT model is technically distinctive but unproven at large scale relative to mainstream smart-contract platforms.
- **Macro/beta:** high sensitivity to risk sentiment in a bear market currently at extreme fear ([[crypto-fear-and-greed-index|Fear & Greed]] = 23).
- **Note (mitigant):** unlike the newer tokens here, HOT has ~1.0 MC/FDV, so dilution from unlocks is not a meaningful incremental risk.
