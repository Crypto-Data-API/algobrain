# Superstate Short Duration U.S. Government Securities Fund (USTB)

**USTB** is a tokenized short-duration US Treasury fund — a [[tokenized-treasuries|tokenized-treasury]] [[real-world-assets|real-world asset (RWA)]] — issued by **Superstate** (founded by Compound creator Robert Leshner), targeting the federal funds rate with a 0.15% management fee. With roughly **$967M+ AUM as of early 2026** it is one of the five largest tokenized US Treasury funds globally, and in **March 2026 Invesco became its investment manager** — the first traditional asset manager to run money on Superstate's digital transfer-agent rails (CoinGecko now lists the asset as the **"Invesco Short Duration US Government Securities Fund"** to reflect this). For traders it is not a speculative asset but a benchmark of the tokenized-RWA sector and an on-chain cash-management/collateral instrument.

## Overview

USTB's structure and partnership with established service providers broke new ground in the private funds space. The fund invests in short-duration U.S. Treasury and U.S. Agency securities, targeting the federal funds rate, with a 0.15% management fee.

Accepted U.S. Qualified Purchasers are able to subscribe to and redeem from the Fund using USDC at a daily NAV calculated by NAV Consulting. USTB's Ethereum-based Allowlist enables on-chain ownership, transfer, and compliance. The Fund's flexibility carries through to custody: investors can self-custody USTB in on-chain addresses of their choice (EOA, multi-sig, or MPC wallet), or use Anchorage Digital Bank (a federally chartered bank) or BitGo (a New York chartered trust) as qualified custodians.

Superstate, an Exempt Reporting Adviser, originally served as the Fund's investment manager, with Federated Hermes as sub-advisor; USTB is legally separate from Superstate. USTB marked the start of Superstate's mission to tokenize traditional assets, since extended via the USCC crypto-carry fund (USTB + USCC exceed $1B combined AUM on Superstate's FundOS) and the **Opening Bell** tokenized-equities platform.

- **Not a trading asset:** zero secondary volume; QP-gated, allowlisted. Its relevance is as **on-chain T-bill yield infrastructure** — the "risk-free leg" for crypto-native cash management and increasingly accepted as collateral.

## Risks

USTB is a low-risk cash-equivalent by crypto standards, but it is not risk-free:

- **Smart-contract / custody risk:** on-chain ownership depends on the Ethereum allowlist contract and the integrity of chosen custody (self-custody, Anchorage, or BitGo). Contract bugs or custody failures are a tail risk distinct from the underlying T-bills.
- **Permissioning / liquidity risk:** access is gated to US Qualified Purchasers and subscribe/redeem happens at daily NAV — there is **no instant secondary exit**, so redemption timing depends on the transfer agent and fund mechanics.
- **Interest-rate risk:** the NAV grind tracks the federal funds rate; falling Fed rates compress the yield and reduce USTB's appeal versus DeFi-native yields ([[ethena-usde|sUSDe]], [[stablecoin-yields]]).
- **Regulatory risk:** tokenized securities are an evolving area; changes to QP rules, transfer-agent regulation, or on-chain securities treatment could affect eligibility and transferability.
- **De-peg-from-NAV risk in stress:** while USTB is a NAV token (not a stablecoin), any secondary venue that lists it could trade at a discount to NAV during liquidity crunches, even though primary redemption is at NAV.
