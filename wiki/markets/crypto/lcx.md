# LCX

**LCX** (Liechtenstein Cryptoassets Exchange) is a **regulated** cryptocurrency exchange and tokenization platform headquartered in Vaduz, Liechtenstein, focused on compliant digital assets, security tokens, and real-world-asset (RWA) tokenization. It operates under Liechtenstein's Blockchain Act (TVTG) and holds multiple registrations/licenses there, positioning itself at the intersection of regulated capital markets and crypto. **LCX** is the platform's utility token, used across its product suite (fees, token sales, staking, and ecosystem access).

## What LCX Does

Unlike most entries in this cluster, LCX is primarily a **regulated centralized exchange and tokenization platform** rather than a purely on-chain [[defi]] protocol. Its core offerings include:

- **Regulated exchange (LCX Exchange)** — buying, selling, transferring, and custody of digital assets under a compliance-first framework, with KYC/AML and licensing in Liechtenstein.
- **Tokenization / security tokens** — issuance and management of compliant security tokens and tokenized real-world assets, leveraging Liechtenstein's Token and Trusted Technology Service Providers Act (the "Blockchain Act").
- **Token Launchpad / sales** — a venue for compliant token sales and offerings.
- **Supporting infrastructure** — terminal, vault/custody, DeFi-adjacent tooling, and APIs aimed at both retail and institutional users.

This regulatory positioning is LCX's defining differentiator: it markets itself as a bridge between traditional finance compliance and blockchain-based assets.

### How it works (architecture)

LCX is best understood as a **regulated venue + tokenization engine + utility token**, with most of its differentiation living in the legal/compliance wrapper rather than in novel on-chain mechanics:

- **Regulated CEX rails** — order matching, custody, and fiat on/off-ramps operated under Liechtenstein supervision, with KYC/AML applied at the account level. This is a custodial, off-chain-matched venue, not an AMM.
- **Tokenization stack** — the core RWA/[[security-token|security-token]] offering: structuring, issuing, and administering compliant tokenized assets under Liechtenstein's **Token and Trusted Technology Service Providers Act (TVTG / "Blockchain Act")**, which gives a legal definition to on-chain "tokens" and to the service-provider roles (token issuer, custodian, etc.) required to handle them. This statutory clarity is LCX's moat in the [[real-world-assets|RWA]] narrative.
- **Launchpad** — a compliance-gated venue for token sales/offerings, distinct from permissionless launchpads because issuers and participants pass KYC and the offering is structured to fit the regulatory framework.
- **Supporting tooling** — terminal/trading interface, vault/custody, DeFi-adjacent connectors, and APIs targeting both retail and institutional users.

The LCX token itself is a standard **ERC-20 on [[ethereum|Ethereum]]** — the regulation lives at the *operator/venue* level, not in the token contract.

## Token Role: LCX

The LCX token is the utility token of the LCX ecosystem. Typical roles include:

- **Fee utility / discounts** — used within the exchange and platform, often with benefits for holders.
- **Token sales and access** — participation in launchpad offerings and ecosystem features.
- **Staking / rewards** — staking programs to align holders with platform usage.

The token's market cap is close to its fully-diluted valuation (most of the ~950M max supply circulating), so dilution overhang is limited relative to many newer tokens.

**Value accrual / governance:** LCX is a *utility* token, not an equity claim on the LCX company — holders do not receive dividends or a legal share of exchange revenue. Value accrual is indirect: fee discounts, launchpad access, and staking/reward programs are designed to create holding demand that scales with platform usage. The practical implication for traders is that the token tracks *platform adoption and the RWA narrative* sentimentally rather than via a hard revenue-share or burn mechanism. The near-fully-circulating float (MC/FDV ~1.0) means moves are demand-driven with little unlock overhang.

LCX competes with other compliance-focused exchanges and tokenization platforms rather than with pure DEXs. Its niche — Liechtenstein licensing plus RWA/security-token tooling — differentiates it in the growing real-world-asset narrative, but it competes against far larger regulated exchanges and a fast-growing field of institutional tokenization platforms. ## Regulatory Status

LCX has built its brand on regulation: it is registered in Liechtenstein under the Blockchain Act and emphasizes compliance with EU-aligned frameworks. This is a double-edged attribute — it can attract institutional and compliance-sensitive users, but it also exposes the project to regulatory classification risk. Notably, the LCX token has been categorized by some data providers under "Alleged SEC Securities," reflecting that LCX appeared in U.S. SEC enforcement-related contexts that named various tokens as alleged securities. (The "alleged" label denotes a regulatory characterization, not a final adjudication; the classification's status should be verified against primary regulatory sources.)

LCX also experienced a significant security incident: in January 2022, the platform suffered a hot-wallet hack with funds stolen. LCX subsequently pursued legal recovery efforts. Prospective users should weigh both the regulatory posture and the historical security event.
