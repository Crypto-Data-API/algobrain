## Overview

Civic delivers on‑chain identity infrastructure that verifies humans and AI agents, thwarts fraud, and safeguards user privacy. Civic  Auth offers universal single‑sign‑on—supporting users with or without crypto wallets—together with granular permissioning, verifiable credentials, and bot resistance across leading blockchain networks. Civic sets the global standard for scalable, privacy‑preserving digital identity.

### How it works

Civic's model separates **identity verification** from **identity reuse**. A user completes verification (e.g., proof-of-personhood or KYC) once with an identity validator; the result is recorded as a reusable, on-chain credential — a **Civic Pass** — that can be presented to many applications without re-uploading documents each time. Applications integrate Civic Pass as a gating requirement: a dApp can demand that a wallet hold a valid pass before allowing it to mint, trade, or access a feature, which helps with bot resistance, Sybil resistance, and regulatory compliance. The design emphasizes selective disclosure, so users share attestations ("is a verified human," "passed KYC") rather than the underlying personal data.

This positions Civic within [[decentralized-identity|decentralized identity (DID)]] infrastructure — a category aiming to give users portable, self-sovereign credentials instead of siloed logins controlled by each platform.

### Token role

The **CVC** token is the protocol's native utility/payment token. In Civic's identity marketplace it is used to compensate the parties in the verification flow — for example, requesters paying validators for performing and attesting to identity checks. It is an ERC-20 token (with bridged deployments on Polygon and others) and historically functioned as the settlement medium within the Civic ecosystem rather than as a fee or [[staking]] token of a Layer 1.

### Notable history

- **2017 ICO** — Civic raised ~$33M in its June/July 2017 token sale (CVC, an ERC-20), one of the higher-profile identity ICOs of that cycle; CVC reached its all-time high (~$1.35) in January 2018 before the broad 2018 bear market.
- **Identity.com** — Civic open-sourced an identity-verification ecosystem (Identity.com) to broaden the validator/requester network beyond Civic itself.
- **Civic Pass (2021–)** — launched on-chain, reusable verifiable credentials adopted by [[solana|Solana]] and [[ethereum|Ethereum]]-ecosystem dApps for gating mints, trading, and access (Sybil/bot resistance and compliance).
- **Civic Auth (2024–2025)** — a universal single-sign-on product supporting users with or without crypto wallets, extending Civic from pure crypto-DID toward mainstream auth and AI-agent verification (proof-of-humanity for agentic use).

### Competitive position

Civic is one of the earliest crypto identity projects (2017) and competes in a crowded [[decentralized-identity|DID]] / verifiable-credentials and proof-of-personhood field alongside newer, often better-funded entrants. Its long history is both an asset (brand, integrations) and a liability (slow category adoption).

| Project | Approach | Verification method | Token | Distinguishing angle |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Civic** | Reusable verifiable credentials (Civic Pass) + Civic Auth SSO | KYC / proof-of-personhood via validators | CVC (payment/utility) | Long-standing brand; wallet-optional auth; AI-agent verification |
| **[[worldcoin\|Worldcoin]] / World ID** | Biometric proof-of-personhood | Iris scan ("Orb") | WLD | Hardware-biometric uniqueness at scale; controversy over biometrics |
| **Gitcoin Passport** | Aggregated "stamps" reputation score | Web2/web3 credential stacking (no single ID) | (none core) | Sybil-resistance scoring for grants/airdrops |
| **Polygon ID / Privado ID** | Self-sovereign ID with ZK proofs | Issuer-signed credentials + zero-knowledge | (none core) | ZK selective disclosure, enterprise SSI |
| **Fractal ID** | KYC/identity provider | Centralized KYC + on-chain attestations | (none core) | B2B compliance/KYC rails |

Civic's differentiation is a *reusable, wallet-optional credential* plus growing focus on verifying **AI agents** as well as humans — but it must defend share against biometric proof-of-personhood (Worldcoin) and ZK-native SSI stacks (Polygon ID), several of which do not require a token at all, sharpening Civic's token-utility question.

Key risks:

- **Adoption-narrative dependence** — decentralized identity has been a perennial "next big thing" that has yet to see mass on-chain adoption; CVC's value is tied to demand for paid on-chain verification that may stay niche.
- **Token-utility decoupling** — much of Civic's product (Civic Auth, enterprise verification) can function without users ever touching CVC, so business success does not automatically accrue to token holders.
- **Competition** — proof-of-personhood and DID solutions from well-funded rivals could marginalize CVC.
- **Low liquidity** — at a ~$17.6M market cap, CVC is a small-cap token prone to sharp moves on thin volume, and it sits in the lower half of the top-1000 in a bear regime.
