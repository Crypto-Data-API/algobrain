# Maple Finance

**Maple Finance** (SYRUP) is "DeFi's institutional lender" — an on-chain asset manager whose lending engine offers institutional-quality yield, overcollateralized by digital assets, through products like syrupUSDC and syrupUSDT. With $4.6B+ AUM and over $20B in cumulative loan originations by 2026, it is one of the largest real-revenue protocols in DeFi, and SYRUP (which replaced the old MPL token in late 2024) is the canonical "institutional DeFi credit" trade.

## Overview

Maple is "DeFi's Institutional Lender". The Maple Finance lending engine powers institutional yield for both Maple and Syrup, providing lenders with institutional-quality yield overcollateralized by digital assets. The protocol combines the best of institutional lending and DeFi. Syrup is the permissionless-access product line built on Maple's lending engine, integrated with the broader DeFi ecosystem including Balancer, [[pendle|Pendle]], and (since 2026) [[aave|Aave]].

Business model in one line: borrowers (trading firms, institutions) post collateral and pay interest; lenders deposit USDC/USDT into syrup pools; the protocol takes a spread — making SYRUP one of the few DeFi tokens backed by measurable, recurring revenue.

- **Builder Codes (2026)** — transition from MapleKit to permissionless "Maple Builder Codes" integrations with Aave, Fluid, Sky, Kamino, and Jupiter.

## Trading Profile

**Venues & liquidity** — SYRUP trades on **both** major venue types: [[binance|Binance]] (SYRUP/USDT spot plus a USD-margined perpetual) and **[[hyperliquid|Hyperliquid]]** (SYRUP-PERP, leverage up to roughly ~40-50x). This two-venue perp footprint plus deep CEX spot (Kraken, Upbit, Bitget, KuCoin, Crypto.com) gives SYRUP genuinely two-sided, liquid markets for a rank-~155 alt — order books are deep enough that moderate size can be worked without heavy slippage, but the book still thins in stress, so scale entries and respect that a Binance/Hyperliquid split means funding, mark price, and depth can diverge between venues. The dual-venue setup is exactly what makes cross-venue and basis expressions executable here rather than theoretical.

**Applicable strategies**
- [[cash-and-carry]] — long SYRUP spot (Binance/Kraken/Uniswap) versus short the USD-margined perp captures positive funding/basis while netting out directional risk on a token with a fundamental (AUM/revenue) anchor.
- [[hl-vs-cex-funding-divergence]] — SYRUP-PERP on Hyperliquid versus the Binance perp lets you harvest funding-rate gaps between the two venues, which is the cleanest edge a genuine two-venue listing creates.
- [[funding-rate-harvest]] — a rank-~155 DeFi alt with retail perp interest tends to run persistently skewed funding; systematically fading the crowded side collects that carry.
- [[oi-confirmed-trend]] — pairing open-interest builds with SYRUP's catalyst-driven moves (buyback activation, Aave-integration flows, ARR headlines) separates real trend from thin-book noise.
- [[liquidation-cascade-fade]] — high leverage (~40-50x) on a mid-cap perp makes SYRUP prone to liquidation flushes that overshoot; fading capitulation wicks back toward the revenue-anchored value zone is a recurring setup.
- [[news-trading]] — SYRUP is unusually catalyst-sensitive (buyback proposal, Aave partnership, Revolut listing, $100M ARR target), so discrete headline events drive tradable repricings.

**Volatility & regime character** — SYRUP is a **high-beta DeFi / institutional-credit (on-chain lending) token**, not a memecoin or large-cap. It carries elevated beta to BTC/ETH risk-on/risk-off (it retraced sharply with the broader bear tape from ~$0.24 to ~$0.137), but its recurring protocol revenue and near-fully-diluted supply (~0.96 MC/FDV) give it more fundamental floor than emissions-heavy peers. Behaviorally it trends hard on DeFi-credit narrative and catalysts, then mean-reverts inside ranges when the sector is quiet.

**Risk flags**
- **Credit-cycle risk** — the core bear case is a borrower-default event (cf. the 2022 Orthogonal episode under legacy MPL), which would gap the token independent of technicals.
- **Liquidity/venue concentration** — despite two perp venues, depth is still modest for a mid-cap; a Binance or Hyperliquid outage/depth-shock can dislocate the other leg of any cross-venue trade.
- **Narrative & catalyst dependence** — price leans on institutional-DeFi flow (Aave integration, ARR trajectory); stalled catalysts or yield compression (Fed cuts squeezing lending spreads) remove the bid.
- **Perp funding dislocations** — high leverage and a two-venue split make funding prone to spikes and inter-venue divergence; crowded positioning can trigger liquidation cascades.
- **Governance/tokenomics shifts** — the proposed end of staking and 25%-of-revenue buyback fund changes holder incentives; execution risk on that transition is real.
