# Shentu (CertiK Chain)

**Shentu** (CTK), formerly known as CertiK Chain, is a blockchain protocol focused on security verification, on-chain security scoring, and decentralized reimbursement mechanisms for [[defi|DeFi]] applications. The protocol shares its origins with **CertiK**, one of the most prominent (and controversial) smart contract auditing firms in the crypto industry. While Shentu the chain and CertiK the auditing company have separated operationally, they remain deeply associated in the market's perception. Understanding both is essential for traders who use audit reports as due diligence signals.

## Overview

Shentu is a Cosmos SDK-based blockchain designed to bring provable security to cross-chain DeFi. Its core features include:

- **Security Oracle**: A decentralized oracle that provides on-chain security scores for smart contracts and DeFi protocols
- **ShentuShield**: A decentralized reimbursement pool that provides coverage against smart contract exploits (similar to decentralized insurance)
- **CertiK Virtual Machine (CVM)**: A security-oriented execution environment designed to verify smart contract safety properties

The protocol was originally launched as "CertiK Chain" before rebranding to Shentu in 2022 to distinguish itself from the CertiK auditing company.

## CertiK: The Auditing Company

### What CertiK Does

CertiK (the company, distinct from the Shentu chain) is one of the largest blockchain security auditing firms. Founded in 2018 by professors Ronghui Gu (Columbia) and Zhong Shao (Yale), CertiK provides:

- **Smart contract audits**: Code review and formal verification of DeFi protocols, NFT projects, and blockchain applications
- **CertiK Security Scores (Skynet)**: A 0-100 scoring system that rates crypto projects across multiple security dimensions
- **Penetration testing**: Offensive security testing for blockchain projects
- **KYC verification**: Team identity verification for crypto projects

CertiK has audited over **4,000+ projects** and claims to have reviewed code securing over $300 billion in digital assets.

### CertiK Security Scores Methodology

CertiK's **Skynet** platform assigns security scores based on:

| Dimension | Weight | What It Measures |
|---|---|---|
| **Code Security** | High | Smart contract audit results, formal verification status |
| **Fundamental Health** | Medium | Tokenomics, liquidity, governance structure |
| **Operational Resilience** | Medium | Team track record, incident response capability |
| **Community Trust** | Low | Social sentiment, community engagement metrics |
| **Market Stability** | Low | Price volatility, liquidity depth |

Scores are displayed on CertiK's public leaderboard and are frequently cited in project marketing materials ("CertiK audited, 95/100 security score").

### How Traders Use CertiK Scores to Assess Risk

Traders reference CertiK scores as one data point in DeFi due diligence:

**What scores tell you:**
- **High scores (85-100)**: The project's smart contract code was reviewed and no critical vulnerabilities were found at the time of audit
- **Medium scores (60-85)**: Some issues were identified; may or may not have been resolved
- **Low scores (<60)**: Significant security concerns; higher risk of exploit

**What scores do NOT tell you:**
- **Post-audit changes**: Projects can modify code after the audit; the score reflects a point-in-time review, not ongoing security
- **Economic exploits**: CertiK audits focus on code-level vulnerabilities, not economic design flaws (oracle manipulation, flash loan attacks, governance attacks)
- **Team integrity**: A clean audit does not mean the team will not rug-pull
- **Future vulnerabilities**: New attack vectors emerge constantly; an audit from 2022 may not cover 2024 attack methods

### Audit Track Record and Controversies

CertiK's track record is mixed, and this matters for how much weight traders should give CertiK scores:

#### Notable CertiK-Audited Projects That Were Hacked

| Project | Date Hacked | Amount Lost | CertiK Audit? | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Merlin DEX** | Apr 2023 | $1.8M | Yes | Rug pull by insider; CertiK had flagged centralization risks but project launched anyway |
| **Saddle Finance** | Apr 2022 | $10M+ | Yes | Exploited via price manipulation |
| **Grim Finance** | Dec 2021 | $30M | Yes | Reentrancy exploit |
| **Uranium Finance** | Apr 2021 | $50M | Yes | Math error in swap function |
| **Various rug pulls** | Ongoing | Hundreds of millions cumulative | Yes | Multiple CertiK-audited projects executed rug pulls |

These incidents demonstrate that **a CertiK audit is not a guarantee of safety** — it is one input in a broader risk assessment.

#### CertiK vs. Kraken Controversy (2024)

In June 2024, CertiK and [[kraken|Kraken]] exchange had a public dispute that damaged CertiK's reputation:

- CertiK researchers found a critical vulnerability in Kraken's deposit system
- Instead of following standard responsible disclosure (report, wait for fix, then disclose), CertiK researchers allegedly exploited the bug to withdraw ~$3 million from Kraken
- CertiK claimed it was a "white hat" test; Kraken called it extortion
- The funds were eventually returned, but the incident raised serious questions about CertiK's operational practices
- The crypto security community largely sided with Kraken's interpretation

#### Other Controversies

- **Audit quality concerns**: Security researchers have publicly criticized CertiK audits as superficial compared to firms like Trail of Bits, OpenZeppelin, or Consensys Diligence
- **Volume over depth**: CertiK's business model of auditing thousands of projects has led to criticism that audits are rushed — a "CertiK audit" may be a quick automated scan plus brief manual review, not a thorough security assessment
- **Pay-for-score allegations**: Some in the community allege that CertiK's scoring system is influenced by commercial relationships (projects that pay for audits receive higher scores), though CertiK denies this

### Practical Guidance for Traders

Given CertiK's mixed track record, here is how to calibrate its scores in your [[risk-management|risk management]]:

1. **Treat CertiK audits as necessary but not sufficient**: A project with no audit at all is riskier than one with a CertiK audit, but a CertiK audit alone does not make a project safe
2. **Check the audit date**: If the audit is more than 6 months old and the project has had major code changes, the audit is stale
3. **Read the actual audit report, not just the score**: CertiK publishes detailed reports; look for the "Findings" section — critical and major findings that were "Acknowledged" but not "Resolved" are red flags
4. **Compare with other auditors**: Projects audited by multiple firms (CertiK + Trail of Bits, or CertiK + OpenZeppelin) have higher security confidence than single-audit projects
5. **Watch for the "audited rug" pattern**: Scam projects deliberately get CertiK audits to create a false sense of security; the audit badge is marketing, not a safety guarantee
6. **Monitor CertiK's real-time alerts**: CertiK's Skynet platform does provide real-time alerts when audited projects show suspicious on-chain activity — these alerts have genuine signal

## Trading Profile

### Venues & liquidity

CTK is tradable on [[binance]] — spot (CTK/USDT) plus a USD-margined [[perpetual-futures|perpetual]] contract that surfaces [[funding-rate|funding]], open interest, and liquidation data. It is NOT listed on Hyperliquid, so Binance is effectively the primary — and near-sole — leveraged venue for the token. This concentration means the Binance perp order book and funding regime dictate almost all price discovery for leveraged flow. With a small-cap market position (~rank 896) and thin 24h volume, book depth is shallow: perp leverage amplifies moves, spreads widen quickly, and even modest size can slip. Traders should size conservatively, favor limit/maker execution, scale into positions, and treat the single-venue setup as a concentration risk where an exchange outage or a delisting removes the only viable leveraged market.

### Applicable strategies

- [[funding-rate-harvest]] — Small-cap CTK perp funding can persist at stretched levels on Binance; a delta-neutral spot-long/perp-short harvest collects it while netting out price risk.
- [[crowded-long-funding-fade]] — Thin liquidity lets narrative-driven CTK longs pile into the perp and push funding sharply positive, setting up fades of over-crowded positioning.
- [[liquidation-cascade-fade]] — Low depth on the single Binance venue makes CTK prone to sharp liquidation wicks; fading the flush after a cascade exhausts targets these mechanical overshoots.
- [[rsi-mean-reversion]] — A range-bound, low-cap token like CTK repeatedly overshoots to oversold/overbought extremes intraday, favoring reversion entries.
- [[range-trading]] — CTK spends long stretches chopping between support and resistance without a dominant trend, rewarding defined-range fade tactics.
- [[cash-and-carry]] — When the CTK perp trades at a premium to spot, a long-spot/short-perp carry captures the basis on Binance with hedged directional exposure.

### Volatility & regime character

CTK is a small-cap infrastructure/[[defi|DeFi]]-adjacent security token with high beta to broader crypto risk and a strong directional correlation to BTC/ETH, especially during risk-off flushes where low-cap alts are sold indiscriminately. It lacks memecoin-style reflexivity but is highly reflexive to CertiK-brand and audit-industry narrative flow. Volatility is elevated relative to majors: thin liquidity produces frequent gap moves and liquidation-driven wicks, while trending regimes are short-lived and interspersed with extended low-volume ranges.

### Risk flags

- **Venue/liquidity concentration** — Binance is the sole meaningful spot and leveraged venue; a delisting, outage, or listing-status change would sever price discovery and liquidity.
- **Thin depth** — Low 24h volume means high slippage and outsized impact from single large orders or liquidation cascades.
- **Emissions/supply** — Max supply is uncoded/unlimited under the chain's inflation model; ongoing staking emissions add structural sell pressure.
- **Narrative dependence** — Price is tightly coupled to CertiK-brand sentiment and audit-industry headlines (e.g., audited-project hacks, the Kraken dispute), which can drive sudden repricing detached from chain fundamentals.
- **Regulatory** — As a security/audit-associated token, adverse regulatory or reputational developments affecting the CertiK company can spill over to CTK.
