# Conflux

**Conflux** (CFX) is a permissionless [[layer-1|Layer 1]] blockchain with hybrid [[proof-of-work|PoW]]/[[proof-of-stake|PoS]] consensus, positioned as the only regulatory-compliant public blockchain in China. For traders, CFX is the canonical "China narrative" token: it spikes violently on China policy and partnership headlines — most recently a >50% surge in July 2025 on the announcement of Conflux 3.0 and the AxCNH offshore-yuan [[stablecoins|stablecoin]].

## Overview

Conflux connects decentralized economies across borders and protocols, offering high throughput with low fees via its Tree-Graph hybrid PoW/PoS consensus. As China's only compliant public chain, it has collaborated with the city of Shanghai, McDonald's China, and Oreo on blockchain/metaverse initiatives, giving it unique access for projects expanding into Asia.

**CFX token**: native gas, staking, storage-fee and governance asset. Each CFX = 10^18 Drip; transaction mechanics resemble Ethereum's (gas limit × gas price denominated in CFX). CFX can be staked via community staking pools for network interest.

### 2025–2026 developments

- **2025-07** — Conflux announced **Conflux 3.0** and an **offshore yuan (CNH) stablecoin** initiative at an event in Shanghai. CFX spiked from ~$0.15 to above $0.22 (+50%+) on the news — one of the sharpest China-narrative moves of 2025.
- **AxCNH stablecoin** — issued in partnership with fintech **AnchorX** and IT-security firm **Eastcompeace**; AnchorX had received in-principle approval for AxCNH from Kazakhstan's Astana Financial Services Authority (AFSA) in Feb 2025. Targets offshore Chinese entities and Belt-and-Road corridor payments (Singapore, Indonesia, Malaysia, Central Asia). Conflux confirmed AxCNH is the stablecoin it is working on, not a separate project.
- **2025-08** — **Conflux 3.0** launched: ~15,000 TPS processing capability, aimed at large-scale cross-border payment settlement, real-world-asset tokenization, and AI-agent integration.
- **2026** — CFX retraced with the broader alt market to ~$0.048 (June 2026) despite the upgrade cycle; the offshore-yuan story remains the key optionality.

## Trading Relevance

- **Venues**: [[binance|Binance]] (CFX/USDT, primary), [[kraken|Kraken]], Bitget, KuCoin, Gate, OKX spot; CFX-PERP on [[hyperliquid|Hyperliquid]]. Volume ~$7M/day in calm tape (2026-06-20; turnover ~2.8% of cap) but expands 10x+ on China headlines.
- **Narrative basket**: the purest listed "China coin" — historically rallies on PBoC/digital-yuan news, China tech-policy easing, Hong Kong crypto liberalization, and stablecoin regulatory developments. Pairs with broader China risk proxies (HSI tech, USDCNH).
- **Event playbook**: CFX exhibits sharp, fast-decaying headline spikes (e.g., +57% July 2025 on AxCNH/3.0 news; similar patterns in 2023 on Hong Kong rules). Momentum entries must respect the rapid mean-reversion after the initial pop.
- **Catalysts**: AxCNH rollout volumes in Belt-and-Road corridors, further Chinese/HK stablecoin policy, Conflux 3.0 ecosystem adoption, RWA tokenization pilots.
- **Risks**: Chinese policy reversal, low organic DeFi activity relative to valuation, unlimited max supply.

## Valuation Framing (qualitative)

- **MC/FDV ~1.0** — fully diluted; the ~$251M cap is the FDV. Cleaner than newer small-caps with large locked supply — no vesting overhang to price in.
- **Headline-beta, not usage** — CFX is valued primarily as a *China-policy call option*, not on organic DeFi fees (on-chain DeFi activity is modest vs cap). The AxCNH offshore-yuan stablecoin and Belt-and-Road payment corridors are the live optionality.
- **Mean-reversion structure** — headline pops (+50%+ in July 2025) decay fast; valuation snaps back to a low baseline absent sustained policy tailwinds.
- **Relative-value** — near-identical profile to [[tezos|Tezos]] (both ~$250M cap, ~-97% from ATH, effectively fully diluted L1s trading on catalysts rather than throughput).

## Risks

- **China policy reversal** — the entire bull case rests on favourable Chinese / Hong Kong crypto and stablecoin policy; a regulatory crackdown or AxCNH stall removes the core catalyst.
- **Narrative dependence** — CFX trades as a headline-beta option; absent China news it drifts on a low baseline with fast mean-reversion after pops.
- **Low organic usage vs valuation** — DeFi activity is modest relative to the ~$251M cap; the chain is valued on optionality, not throughput.
- **Unlimited max supply** — ongoing issuance is a slow structural headwind even though there is no near-term unlock cliff.
- **Geopolitical/sanctions risk** — China-linked, Belt-and-Road-adjacent positioning carries cross-border regulatory and sanctions sensitivity for Western venues.
- **Macro/regime** — [[fear-and-greed-index|F&G]] 23 (extreme fear) and an established bear market amplify drawdowns in headline-driven small-caps.
