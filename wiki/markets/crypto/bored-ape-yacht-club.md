**Bored Ape Yacht Club (BAYC)** is a collection of 10,000 unique cartoon ape PFP (profile picture) [[nft|NFTs]] on [[ethereum]], created by [[yuga-labs|Yuga Labs]]. Launched on April 29, 2021 at a mint price of 0.08 ETH (~$190), BAYC became the defining cultural phenomenon of the NFT era -- a status symbol adopted by celebrities, a speculative asset that peaked at a floor price of ~$430,000 (152 ETH) in April 2022, and the foundation of a crypto entertainment empire that raised $450 million at a $4 billion valuation. It is simultaneously a case study in community-driven value creation and a cautionary tale about hype-cycle losses.

---

## History

BAYC was created by four pseudonymous founders -- **Gordon Goner**, **Gargamel**, **Emperor Tomato Ketchup**, and **No Sass** -- operating under the company name [[yuga-labs|Yuga Labs]]. The concept was a "swamp club for apes" -- an irreverent, meme-inflected take on exclusive membership. The collection of 10,000 procedurally generated ape illustrations launched on **April 29, 2021**, and all 10,000 sold out within **12 hours** at a mint price of **0.08 ETH** (~$190 at the time).

What set BAYC apart from other PFP projects was the combination of **commercial IP rights** (holders could use their ape's image for any commercial purpose), **community events** (yacht parties, concerts, meetups), and **celebrity adoption** that created a virtuous cycle of cultural cachet and speculative demand.

## Collection Mechanics

| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Supply** | 10,000 unique Bored Apes (fixed) |
| **Blockchain** | [[ethereum|Ethereum]] mainnet |
| **Token standard** | [[erc-721|ERC-721]] (non-fungible) |
| **Mint price** | 0.08 ETH (~$190 at launch) |
| **Traits** | Background, clothes, earring, eyes, fur, hat, mouth -- 170+ trait variations |
| **IP rights** | Full commercial use rights granted to holders (see [[nft-ip-rights]]) |
| **Companion collections** | Mutant Ape Yacht Club (MAYC, 20,000 supply), Bored Ape Kennel Club (BAKC, dog companions) |
| **Token** | [[apecoin|ApeCoin ($APE)]] -- governance and utility token |
| **Metaverse** | [[otherside|Otherside]] -- virtual world built by Yuga Labs |

Each Bored Ape is an [[erc-721|ERC-721]] token whose image and metadata were generated procedurally from a fixed set of seven trait categories. Like [[cryptopunks|CryptoPunks]] and most early PFP projects, BAYC art was originally hosted off-chain (via [[ipfs|IPFS]]) rather than fully on-chain -- a distinction relevant to long-term provenance and a contrast with on-chain projects like [[art-blocks|Art Blocks]] and [[autoglyphs|Autoglyphs]].

### Trait Rarity and Distribution

[[nft-rarity-scoring|Trait rarity]] drives enormous price variation within the collection. Apes with rare fur types (solid gold ~0.5%, trippy, zombie, robot), laser eyes, king's crown, or specific rare combinations command large multiples of the [[nft-floor-price|floor price]]. The single rarest fur is **solid gold** (only 46 apes), and apes combining multiple rare traits have sold for millions. Rarity is commonly scored using [[nft-rarity-scoring|trait-rarity tools]] (Rarity Sniper, Rarity Tools) and surfaced by [[nft-aggregators|NFT aggregators]], creating a continuum of sub-markets within the single 10,000-piece collection rather than a homogeneous asset.

## Market Structure

| Venue | Role for BAYC |
|---|---|
| [[opensea|OpenSea]] | Original primary secondary marketplace; deep listing book, broad retail reach |
| [[blur|Blur]] | Pro-trader marketplace that captured much BAYC blue-chip volume from 2023 via zero fees, [[nft-perpetuals|trait bidding]] and token incentives |
| [[magic-eden|Magic Eden]] | Multi-chain aggregator/marketplace; later expanded to Ethereum collections |
| [[nft-aggregators|Aggregators]] (Gem, Blur) | Sweep multiple listings/marketplaces in one transaction |

- **Floor price and volume:** BAYC is among the most liquid NFT collections, but liquidity remains thin versus fungible [[ethereum|ETH]]. The [[nft-floor-price|floor price]] is the headline metric, but it represents only the cheapest 1-2 listings and can be moved by a single large sale or a sweep. *Verify live floor and 24h volume on a marketplace -- do not rely on cached figures.*
- **Royalty regime:** BAYC historically earned ~2.5% creator royalties on secondary sales. The 2022-2023 "[[nft-royalties|royalty wars]]" -- led by [[blur|Blur]] and marketplaces offering optional or zero royalties -- eroded enforced royalties across the market, including blue chips.
- **Liquidity layers:** Beyond spot sales, BAYC has spawned [[nft-finance|NFTfi]] products -- collateralized lending (NFTfi, BendDAO), fractionalization, and perpetual/floor-derivative markets -- that add leverage and forced-liquidation dynamics on top of the spot floor.

## Valuation Drivers

- **Rarity tier:** A "floor ape" and a rare-fur/laser-eyes ape are fundamentally different assets. [[nft-rarity-scoring|Trait rarity]] is the largest within-collection price determinant.
- **Commercial IP rights:** BAYC's grant of full commercial-use rights ([[nft-ip-rights]]) to holders is a core differentiator -- it enabled ape-branded restaurants (Bored & Hungry), music projects, and merch, theoretically letting holders monetize their asset's image.
- **Membership / utility:** Access to token-gated events, [[airdrop|airdrops]] ([[apecoin|ApeCoin]], Otherside land/Kodas, Mutant Serums), and the broader [[yuga-labs|Yuga Labs]] ecosystem is bundled into the asset.
- **Cultural cachet / Veblen dynamics:** Celebrity adoption made BAYC a status good whose demand rose with price -- a dynamic that cuts both ways on the way down.
- **Ecosystem execution:** Value is tied to [[apecoin|ApeCoin]] price action, [[otherside|Otherside]] metaverse progress, and Yuga Labs' overall execution.

## Cultural Significance

BAYC was the most culturally impactful NFT collection of the 2021-2022 era:

- **Celebrity status symbol**: When Jimmy Fallon and Paris Hilton discussed their apes on The Tonight Show, it marked the moment NFTs fully entered mainstream pop culture. Eminem and Snoop Dogg created a music video featuring their apes. Steph Curry changed his Twitter profile picture to his BAYC
- **Commercial IP rights**: Unlike [[cryptopunks|CryptoPunks]] (at the time), BAYC granted holders full commercial use rights. This led to ape-branded restaurants, clothing lines, and media projects -- demonstrating that NFTs could function as decentralized IP
- **Community as product**: "Membership" in the Bored Ape Yacht Club -- with access to private events, Discord channels, and airdrops -- became as valuable as the art itself. The community-first model influenced every subsequent PFP project
- **Crypto entertainment empire**: Yuga Labs used BAYC as the foundation for a broader ecosystem (ApeCoin, Otherside metaverse, acquisition of Punks and Meebits), attempting to build the "Disney of Web3"
- **Cautionary tale**: Most buyers who purchased near the peak in early-to-mid 2022 lost 80-90%+ of their investment. The BAYC price trajectory became a textbook example of speculative bubble dynamics -- hype, celebrity endorsement, institutional validation, and then a sustained, brutal decline

## Collector & Trading Risks

- **Illiquidity:** Even the most liquid PFP collection is far less liquid than fungible [[ethereum|ETH]]. Exiting a non-floor or rare ape can take days to weeks, and large sales can meaningfully move the [[nft-floor-price|floor price]].
- **Floor volatility & drawdown:** BAYC is the "blue chip" PFP alongside [[cryptopunks|CryptoPunks]], yet has experienced 90%+ drawdowns from its 2022 peak. "Blue chip" status did not prevent catastrophic loss for peak buyers.
- **Wash trading:** NFT marketplaces have been plagued by [[wash-trading]] -- self-dealing to inflate apparent volume and floor, often to farm marketplace token incentives ([[blur|Blur]] season rewards). Reported volume should be treated skeptically.
- **Royalty erosion:** The shift to optional/zero royalties ([[nft-royalties]]) cut creator revenue and changed marketplace economics; collections that depend on royalty-funded operations face structural headwinds.
- **IP / legal risk:** Commercial IP rights ([[nft-ip-rights]]) create complexity -- and Yuga Labs has faced legal disputes (e.g., the RR/BAYC "Ryder Ripps" infringement case, SEC scrutiny of [[apecoin|ApeCoin]]). Holder IP value depends on the validity and defensibility of those rights.
- **ETH-denominated dual exposure:** Floors are quoted in [[ethereum|ETH]]; holders carry combined exposure to NFT-specific demand and ETH price.
- **NFTfi leverage cascades:** Collateralized lending against BAYC ([[nft-finance|NFTfi]], BendDAO) introduces forced-liquidation risk -- a falling floor can trigger liquidations that push the floor lower.
- **Ecosystem dependency:** Value is intertwined with [[apecoin|ApeCoin]], [[otherside|Otherside]], and Yuga Labs execution -- concentration risk in a single company's roadmap.

### Macro Backdrop (mid-2026, qualitative)

The NFT market is in a deeply depressed phase of the cycle. With the broader crypto [[fear-and-greed-index|Fear & Greed Index]] around 23 ("Established Bear Market"), NFT floors and volumes sit far below 2021-2022 peaks; speculative PFP demand has largely evaporated and blue chips trade on a fraction of cycle-peak liquidity. This is qualitative context only -- there is no fungible price feed for an NFT collection; confirm any live figure on a marketplace.
