# Starknet

**Starknet** (ticker **STRK**) is a permissionless, decentralized **[[ethereum|Ethereum]] [[layer-2|Layer 2]] (L2) validity ([[zk-rollup|ZK]]) rollup** built by StarkWare that scales Ethereum using cryptographic STARK proofs while inheriting Ethereum's security. It competes in the L2 landscape alongside [[arbitrum]] and [[optimism]], differing in that it is a [[zk-rollup|zero-knowledge rollup]] using its own Cairo programming language rather than an optimistic rollup. The STRK token is used for transaction-fee payment, governance, and staking in Starknet's consensus mechanism.

## Overview

StarkNet is a permissionless decentralized Layer 2 (L2) validity rollup, built to allow Ethereum to scale via cryptographic protocols called STARKs, without compromising Ethereum’s core principles of decentralization, transparency, inclusivity and security. The StarkNet Token is needed to operate the ecosystem, maintain and secure it, decide on its values and strategic goals, and direct its evolution. This token will be required for (i) governance, (ii) payment of transaction fees on StarkNet, and (iii) participation in StarkNet’s consensus mechanism.

## Use Case, Narrative & Category

Starknet is an **Ethereum Layer 2 scaling solution** in the **zero-knowledge (ZK) rollup** category. Rather than re-executing every transaction on Ethereum, it batches transactions off-chain and posts a single STARK validity proof to [[ethereum]] mainnet, dramatically lowering per-transaction cost while preserving L1-grade security. Its key technical differentiators are:

- **STARK proofs** -- post-quantum-secure, no trusted setup (unlike SNARKs).
- **Cairo** -- a purpose-built, provable smart-contract language (a moat but also an adoption hurdle, since it is not EVM-equivalent like [[arbitrum]] or [[optimism]]).
- **STRK token utility** -- fee payment, governance, and staking/validator participation as the network decentralizes its sequencer and prover.

The investment narrative is a bet on ZK rollups winning long-term L2 market share on cost and security, and on Starknet capturing value as it expands beyond Ethereum (it has explored Bitcoin scaling / "Bitcoin sidechain" positioning).

As a large-float L2 governance token still working through its emission/unlock schedule, STRK perps tend to carry a downward [[funding-rate|funding]] bias during distribution periods (shorts paid to hold), and [[open-interest]] is modest relative to majors -- 24h spot volume is ~$12.7M, so liquidations and unlock events can move price sharply. ## Valuation Framing (Qualitative)

STRK is a **governance/utility token, not an equity claim** on StarkWare or on L2 fees — its value rests on (a) demand for it as a fee/gas and staking asset and (b) speculative bets on Starknet capturing L2 market share. Qualitative anchors:

- **Dilution-dominated.** With only ~65% of max supply circulating (MC/FDV ~0.65), STRK carries one of the heavier remaining-unlock profiles in the L2 cohort. Until emissions normalize, holders face structural supply headwinds — the single most-cited bear input.
- **Fee leakage.** Most economic value on an L2 accrues to the sequencer/prover operator and to [[ethereum]] (via data/proof posting), not automatically to the governance token. STRK's staking design aims to route more value to the token as the sequencer decentralizes, but that is unproven.
- **Tech moat vs. adoption tax.** STARK proofs (post-quantum, no trusted setup) and Cairo are genuine technical edges, but Cairo's non-EVM design is an adoption tax versus EVM-equivalent rollups, capping the developer-network effect that drives L2 value.
- **Activity-linked optionality.** STRK is a high-beta call option on ZK rollups winning L2 share on cost/security; the cap embeds that optionality more than current usage.

## Risks

- **Supply / unlock pressure** -- ~6.52B of 10B max supply circulating; continued emissions and team/investor unlocks are a persistent overhang (MC/FDV ~0.65).
- **L2 competition** -- intense competition from [[arbitrum]], [[optimism]] and other ZK rollups (zkSync, Linea, Scroll); Cairo's non-EVM design can slow developer adoption.
- **Execution / decentralization risk** -- sequencer and prover decentralization are still in progress; until then, key infrastructure is partially centralized.
- **Macro / regime risk** -- as a high-beta L2 token, STRK is highly sensitive to the broad crypto risk cycle; the current **extreme-fear, established-bear** backdrop is unfavorable.
