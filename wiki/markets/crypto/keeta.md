# Keeta

**Keeta** (ticker **KTA**) is a high-performance **payments-focused Layer-1 blockchain** designed to unify transactions across different blockchains and fiat payment systems, cutting out costly intermediaries, reducing fees, and enabling near-instant settlement. The project claims sub-second (~400ms) finality and very high throughput, and targets regulated financial-institution use cases with native on-chain compliance (KYC/AML), tokenization, and digital identity. The tracked KTA token lives on [[base|Base]]; the project was **founded in 2022 and is backed by Eric Schmidt**, the former CEO of Google.

## Overview

Keeta is engineered for institutional payments: it advertises ~400-millisecond finality and very high throughput, advanced on-chain compliance (KYC/AML), and native support for asset tokenization and digital identity — making it a candidate rail for [[stablecoins|stablecoins]] and [[real-world-assets|real-world-asset]] transfers. By bridging cross-chain transactions and existing fiat payment systems, the project aims to connect crypto and traditional finance in a compliant way.

---

## Valuation Framing

KTA is a **narrative-premium payments L1 priced on its backer and its pitch, not on realized usage**. The qualitative driver is unusual for a sub-$100M token: backing reportedly associated with Eric Schmidt (former Google CEO) gives KTA an attention premium that pure-retail payment chains lack. But the valuation case is fragile on three counts: (1) the marketing claims (e.g. "10M TPS," 400ms finality) are unverified project figures, not independently demonstrated sustained performance; (2) the ~0.56 MC/FDV means ~442M KTA of latent dilution (team/investor vesting) sits ahead of price — typical of a venture-backed launch; and (3) liquidity is thin and venue-concentrated (~$1.7M/day, primarily one major CEX pair), so exit risk is high. At ~$80M cap (vs ~$143M FDV) the market is pricing Keeta as a speculative institutional-payments option whose re-rating depends on converting compliance-first design into actual regulated-payment volume — adoption that is unproven and slow to materialize. Versus payment/settlement peers like [[xrp|XRP]] and [[stellar|Stellar]] (established networks, deeper liquidity) Keeta is far earlier and far thinner.

## Risks

- **High-profile backing ≠ adoption.** Eric Schmidt's involvement drives attention and narrative premium, but institutional-payments adoption is unproven and slow to materialize.
- **Big-number claims unverified.** Marketing throughput figures (e.g. "10M TPS") and 400ms finality are project claims; real-world sustained performance and decentralization tradeoffs warrant scrutiny.
- **Thin, concentrated liquidity.** Limited CEX coverage and ~$1.7M daily volume make for wide spreads and difficult exits.
- **Severe drawdown.** ~91% off ATH underscores the speculative, high-volatility profile.
- **Unlock overhang.** ~442M KTA still to circulate; venture/team vesting can pressure price. See [[token-unlocks]].
- **Regulatory exposure.** A compliance-first payments chain is directly exposed to evolving regulation of stablecoins, RWAs, and money transmission.
- **Market beta.** In an established [[bear-market|bear market]] (F&G 23), low-liquidity payment-L1 tokens underperform — KTA's -14% week versus a mid-single-digit cohort illustrates the point.
