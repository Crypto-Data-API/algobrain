## Overview

Capx is an [[ethereum|Ethereum]] Layer 2 positioned as the "Nasdaq of AI Apps," where users can trade AI App tokens. The project combines scalable blockchain infrastructure with application and decentralized-cloud layers, purpose-built to support a tokenized [[artificial-intelligence|AI]] application layer. Capx enables developers to build consumer-facing AI agent apps, tokenize them, and trade their tokens in a composable environment.

The Capx Chain is built using **Arbitrum Orbit** and offers fast finality and low-cost transactions.

The Capx Superapp (app.capx.ai) is the primary interface for both developers and users. It functions as a platform for discovering, using, and trading AI apps. Available on web and mobile, the Capx App is structured to provide app store-like discoverability of AI apps, while integrating token-based trading mechanics inspired by modern consumer trading platforms.

Capx Cloud is the [[decentralized-compute|decentralized hosting and deployment layer]] for AI apps and agents. It is built on top of **Symbiotic**, a [[restaking]] protocol that lets cloud operators provide compute and availability guarantees for hosted agents.

Capx is focused on supporting the consumer-facing AI-app layer by enabling the end-to-end lifecycle of AI agent apps — from development and deployment to discovery and monetization.

## Architecture — How It Works

Capx is built as a vertically integrated stack for a **tokenized AI-app economy**, with three layers:

1. **Capx Chain (settlement layer).** An [[ethereum|Ethereum]]-aligned Layer 2 built with **Arbitrum Orbit**, the framework for launching custom Arbitrum-tech rollups. It provides fast finality and low fees so that high-frequency AI-app token trading and agent transactions are economical — the same reason consumer trading apps need cheap rails. The Orbit choice plugs Capx into the broader [[ethereum|Ethereum]] / Arbitrum security and tooling ecosystem rather than a standalone L1.
2. **Capx Superapp (application / distribution layer).** The user-facing product at app.capx.ai, available on web and mobile. It is structured like an **app store** for AI agents — discovery, ratings, and usage — fused with **token-trading mechanics** modeled on modern consumer trading platforms (the "Nasdaq of AI Apps" framing). This is the demand funnel: discovery drives usage drives token trading.
3. **Capx Cloud (compute / hosting layer).** A [[decentralized-compute|decentralized hosting and deployment layer]] for AI apps and agents, built on top of **Symbiotic**, a [[restaking]] protocol. Cloud operators provide compute and availability guarantees for hosted agents, and Symbiotic restaking provides the crypto-economic security/slashing backing those guarantees — so hosting commitments are bonded rather than trust-based.

The end-to-end loop is: a developer builds a consumer-facing AI agent app, tokenizes it, deploys it on Capx Cloud, lists it for discovery in the Superapp, and the app's token trades on Capx Chain. CAPX is the settlement and coordination asset threading through all three layers.

> **Note on launchpad-model risk.** Tokenized-app / agent-launchpad platforms are structurally reflexive: the same mechanics that drive explosive upside during a hot narrative (cheap launches, speculative trading) also drive sharp drawdowns and proliferation of low-quality or abandoned tokens when the narrative cools. This is a category-level dynamic, not specific to Capx.

## Token Role & Mechanism

CAPX is the platform's native gas, settlement, and coordination token. It pays for transactions on the Capx Chain, underpins the launchpad mechanics for new AI-app tokens, and is intended to capture value from activity across the app store, trading layer, and decentralized cloud. The economic thesis is reflexive and **narrative-driven**: value accrues to CAPX if a large ecosystem of tokenized AI agents launches and trades on the platform. This makes the token highly sensitive to the AI-agent / launchpad meta — when that narrative is hot, app launches and speculative trading drive demand; when it cools, both activity and token value contract. The flat 24h/7d action (essentially 0%) into 2026-06-22 reflects a quiet, low-conviction tape amid market-wide **Extreme Fear** (Fear & Greed 21).

### Value Accrual & Governance

CAPX is positioned to accrue value from *platform activity* across all three layers:

- **Gas / settlement.** Transactions on Capx Chain consume CAPX, so chain usage (agent activity, token trades) is a direct demand source.
- **Launchpad mechanics.** New AI-app token launches route through CAPX-denominated mechanics, tying primary-market activity to the token.
- **Trading-fee capture.** Activity in the Superapp's trading layer is intended to feed value back to CAPX.
- **Cloud / restaking.** Hosting on Capx Cloud (Symbiotic-secured) integrates CAPX into the compute layer's economics.

The hard caveat is the **~0.10 MC/FDV ratio**: roughly 90% of max supply is not yet circulating, so even healthy platform usage must contend with a large structural unlock overhang. Value accrual is only as strong as the gap between real, recurring platform fees and the emission schedule.

## How & Where It Trades

- **Spot venues.** CoinGecko data in the snapshot showed **no major centralized-exchange listings** for CAPX; the token is deployed across BNB Chain (primary), [[ethereum|Ethereum]], and Arbitrum One (see contract addresses), so liquidity is fragmented across chains and skews toward DEX/early-listing venues.
- **Derivatives.** No liquid CAPX perpetual-futures market exists at this size; the token is spot/DEX-driven and cannot be readily hedged.
- **Float / unlock overhang.** Only ~10% of max supply circulates (102.5M of 1.0B; MC/FDV ≈ 0.10) — one of the heaviest dilution profiles in this batch. The ~$166M FDV versus ~$16M cap signals very large future supply. This low-float/high-FDV structure is a recurring source of post-listing drawdown in AI-launchpad tokens.

## Narrative, Category & Catalysts

CAPX is a leveraged expression of the **AI-agent / app-launchpad meta** — one of the most reflexive corners of crypto. Demand is tightly coupled to that narrative being "hot": when AI-agent speculation runs, launchpad platforms see app launches, trading volume, and chain activity; when it cools, all three contract simultaneously. Plausible catalysts: a wave of successful AI-app launches on the Superapp, a major CEX listing improving liquidity, demonstrable platform fee revenue, or a broad AI-agent narrative rotation. The dominant de-rating risks are narrative fatigue and the unlock schedule.

## Risks

- **Narrative dependence** — tightly coupled to the AI-agent / launchpad meta; demand evaporates when the narrative cools.
- **Low MC/FDV ratio (~0.10)** — large future token unlocks create persistent dilution and sell-pressure risk.
- **Platform adoption risk** — value requires a thriving ecosystem of tokenized AI apps; without sustained launches and trading, the chain is underutilized.
- **Low liquidity** — a sub-$16M cap is easily moved and unsuitable for size.
- **Smart-contract / L2 / restaking risk** — exposure to Arbitrum Orbit chain, multi-chain deployments, and Symbiotic restaking security.
