Tether (USDT) is the largest [[stablecoins|stablecoin]] by market capitalisation at ~$110-120B+ (2024-2025), and the most traded [[crypto|cryptocurrency]] by daily volume. Issued by [[tether-limited|Tether Limited]], USDT is the backbone of global crypto liquidity -- particularly dominant in Asian and emerging markets -- despite persistent controversies around reserve transparency and corporate governance.

## Overview

USDT was originally launched in 2014 as "Realcoin" before rebranding to Tether. It was founded by Brock Pierce, Reeve Collins, and Craig Sellars. Tether Limited is a subsidiary of iFinex Inc, which also owns the Bitfinex cryptocurrency exchange -- a relationship that has been a source of ongoing controversy.

As of 2025, USDT is issued on over a dozen blockchains, with the majority of supply and transaction volume on Tron (due to low fees) and [[ethereum|Ethereum]] (due to DeFi integration). BTC/USDT is the single most traded pair in all of crypto.

## Market Dominance

USDT's market position is overwhelming:

- **~$110-120B+ market cap** (2024-2025), representing approximately 65-70% of the total stablecoin market
- **Daily trading volume** frequently exceeds $50B, more than [[bitcoin|Bitcoin]]
- **Available on every major exchange** -- it is the default quote currency for crypto trading globally
- **First mover advantage**: Launched in 2014, years before competitors. Network effects compound -- traders, exchanges, and protocols all default to USDT because everyone else does

The scale of USDT's dominance means that understanding Tether risk is equivalent to understanding systemic crypto risk. If USDT were to fail, the resulting cascade would likely be the most severe crash in crypto history.

## Reserve Composition

As of Q4 2024, Tether's reported reserve composition:

| Category | Approximate % |
|----------|--------------|
| US Treasury securities | ~85% |
| Cash and bank deposits | ~5% |
| Bitcoin | ~5% |
| Gold and precious metals | ~3% |
| Secured loans | ~1% |
| Other investments | ~1% |

Tether has significantly improved its reserve composition since 2021, when reserves included substantial commercial paper (since eliminated) and other less liquid assets. The shift toward US Treasuries reflects both market pressure and increased profitability from rising interest rates.

Tether publishes **quarterly attestation reports** by BDO Italia. These are attestations, not full audits -- a critical distinction. An attestation confirms reserves exist at a point in time; an audit examines controls, processes, and financial statements comprehensively. No Big Four accounting firm has conducted a full audit of Tether.

## Controversies

### NYAG Settlement (2021)

The New York Attorney General (NYAG) investigated Tether and Bitfinex from 2019. Key findings:

- Tether's reserves were **not always 100% backed by cash** as claimed. At various points, reserves included commercial paper, loans to affiliated entities, and other non-cash assets
- Tether and Bitfinex **commingled funds** and misrepresented the state of reserves to customers
- Settlement: **$18.5 million fine** and agreement to provide quarterly transparency reports. No admission of fraud.

### Bitfinex Loan (2018-2019)

In late 2018, Tether lent **$850 million to Bitfinex** to cover funds seized from payment processor Crypto Capital by various governments. This blurred the line between Tether reserves (which should back USDT) and Bitfinex operations. The NYAG investigation revealed this arrangement, which Tether had not publicly disclosed.

### Lack of Full Audit

Despite managing over $110B in assets (larger than most banks), Tether has never undergone a **comprehensive audit by a Big Four accounting firm** (Deloitte, PwC, EY, KPMG). Critics argue this is insufficient transparency for an entity of Tether's systemic importance. Tether has repeatedly stated a full audit is forthcoming but has not delivered one.

Contrast with [[usdc|USDC]]: Circle receives monthly attestations from Deloitte and has filed for a public IPO (which requires full SEC-audited financial statements).

### US DOJ Investigation

In 2021, Bloomberg reported that the US Department of Justice was investigating Tether for potential bank fraud and anti-money laundering violations. Tether denied wrongdoing and stated it cooperates with law enforcement. As of 2025, no charges have been filed.

### CFTC Fine (2021)

The Commodity Futures Trading Commission fined Tether **$41 million** for making untrue or misleading statements about USDT being fully backed at all times. The CFTC found that USDT was only fully backed for 27.6% of the days in a 26-month sample period (2016-2018).

## Revenue and Profitability

Tether generated approximately **$6.2 billion in net profit** in 2023, making it one of the most profitable financial companies per employee in history (Tether has approximately 100 employees). This revenue comes primarily from the yield on reserve assets invested in US Treasuries (4-5% yield on ~$110B = ~$5-6B annually).

Like [[usdc|USDC]], USDT holders receive **none** of this yield. The stablecoin model is essentially a money market fund where depositors earn nothing. See [[stablecoin-yields]] for alternatives.

Tether has used its profits to invest in:
- **Bitcoin**: ~$5B+ in BTC holdings
- **Sustainable energy**: Mining and renewable energy projects
- **AI and technology**: Various venture investments
- **Neurotechnology**: Brain-computer interface research

## Why USDT Dominates Despite Controversy

Despite every controversy listed above, USDT has grown from ~$1B in 2017 to $110B+ in 2024-2025. Several factors explain this:

1. **First mover advantage**: Available since 2014, deeply embedded in crypto infrastructure
2. **Deepest liquidity**: Lowest slippage on most trading pairs across most exchanges
3. **Exchange integration**: Default trading pair on [[binance|Binance]], OKX, Bybit, and most major exchanges
4. **Emerging market adoption**: Dominant in Asia, Africa, Middle East, and Latin America for dollarization and remittances. Tron's low fees make USDT accessible to users in low-income economies. See [[stablecoin-use-cases]]
5. **Track record**: USDT has never fully broken its peg for an extended period, surviving every major crypto crash (2018, 2020, May 2022, FTX collapse)
6. **Network effects**: Traders use USDT because other traders use USDT. Liquidity begets liquidity

The market has effectively concluded that USDT's **redeemability** is more important than its **governance transparency**. This may be rational -- or it may be a catastrophe waiting to happen.

## De-Peg History

USDT has experienced brief de-pegs:
- **October 2018**: Traded at ~$0.92-0.95 amid fears about Tether's banking relationships. Recovered within days.
- **May 2022**: Traded at ~$0.95 during the [[terra-luna-collapse|Terra/Luna collapse]] as panic spread to all stablecoins. Recovered within hours.

In both cases, Tether processed billions in redemptions without issue, which paradoxically strengthened market confidence. See [[stablecoin-depegs]] for a complete history.

## For Traders

- BTC/USDT is the most liquid crypto trading pair. Most [[funding-rates|funding rate]] calculations on perpetual futures reference USDT-margined contracts
- When evaluating exchange risk, consider whether the exchange holds reserves in USDT -- this adds a layer of Tether counterparty risk on top of exchange risk
- During market stress, monitor USDT's peg on DEXs (Curve 3pool ratio is a key indicator). A sustained de-peg of USDT would be a market-wide systemic event
- USDT-margined and USD-margined perpetual futures behave differently during USDT stress events -- understand which you are trading
