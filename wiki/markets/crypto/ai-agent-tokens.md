# AI Agent Tokens

These tokens range from serious decentralized AI infrastructure to speculative meme-tier agent launches. Understanding the landscape helps traders distinguish genuine technology bets from narrative-driven speculation.

## Category Breakdown

### Infrastructure Tokens
Projects building the compute, data, and network layers that AI agents need:
- **Compute**: Render (RNDR), Akash (AKT), Aethir (ATH), io.net
- **Data**: The Graph (GRT), Ocean Protocol (merged into FET)
- **Networks**: Bittensor (TAO), Allora, Ritual

These are the "picks and shovels" of the AI narrative — they benefit regardless of which specific agents succeed.

### Agent Platform Tokens

## The Honest Assessment

Most AI agent tokens are **narrative plays**, not technology investments. The key questions for evaluating any AI token:

1. **Does this project need a token?** Many AI projects function fine without one — the token is often just a fundraising mechanism
2. **Is there genuine AI technology?** Or is "AI" just marketing for a simple bot?
3. **What's the revenue model?** Infrastructure tokens (compute, data) have clearer monetization than agent tokens
4. **Token distribution**: Are early investors/team dumping on retail?
5. **Survival beyond hype**: Did the project exist before the [[ai-narrative-arc|AI mania]], or was it launched to ride the wave?

## Trading Characteristics

- **High correlation**: AI tokens move together — when the narrative is hot, they all pump; when it fades, they all dump
- **Beta to BTC**: AI tokens typically have 2-5x beta to [[bitcoin|BTC]], amplifying both rallies and drawdowns
- **Narrative-driven**: Price action correlates more with AI news (new model releases, OpenAI announcements) than with project fundamentals
- **Liquidity varies wildly**: Top tokens (TAO, RNDR, FET) have deep liquidity; long-tail tokens have thin books and high slippage
