# Smooth Love Potion

**Smooth Love Potion** (SLP) is the in-game **reward and utility token of [[axie-infinity|Axie Infinity]]**, the Ronin-based [[play-to-earn]] game built by Vietnamese studio **Sky Mavis**. Players earn SLP by playing (battle/adventure modes) and spend it — together with the governance token AXS — to **breed new Axie [[nft]] creatures**. SLP is the textbook case study of a [[play-to-earn]] reward currency that suffered catastrophic **hyperinflation** when token issuance vastly outpaced sinks during the 2021 boom. It ranks **#853** by market capitalization.

## Overview

Smooth Love Potion (SLP) tokens can be earned as rewards by Axie Infinity players through battle or adventure mode. SLP are tokens that can be used to breed new digital pets, known as Axies. It was formally known as Small Love Potion before the token upgrade to the Ronin blockchain.

The cost of breeding begins at 100 SLP but increases gradually — rising to 200 SLP for the second breed, 300 for the third, 500 for the fourth, 800 for the fifth, and 1,300 for the sixth. Axies can be bred a maximum of seven times, and the seventh breed costs 2,100 SLP. This limit exists in order to prevent hyperinflation in the marketplace.

SLP paves the way to the concept of Play-to-earn which popularizes the Axie Infinity games among players who are looking to earn some money or participate in DeFi. It is especially popular in countries like Philippines.&nbsp;

### Faucets, sinks, and the dual-token model

[[axie-infinity|Axie Infinity]] runs a **dual-token** economy: **AXS** (Axie Infinity Shards) is the capped governance/staking token, while **SLP** is the uncapped in-game reward currency. This division matters because the two tokens have opposite supply profiles, and SLP's uncapped design is the root of its inflation problem.

**Faucets (SLP emission — uncapped):**

- **Gameplay rewards** — historically, players earned SLP by winning battles (Arena/PvP) and clearing Adventure/PvE modes, plus daily quests. Emission was effectively unlimited and grew directly with the number of active grinders.
- **"Scholarship" scaling** — guild managers lent Axie teams to scholars (especially in the Philippines, Venezuela, Indonesia), multiplying the number of accounts farming SLP and so multiplying emission.

**Sinks (SLP burns — demand):**

- **Breeding** — the single major sink. Creating a new Axie [[nft]] consumes SLP (plus AXS). The cost escalates with each breed: **100 → 200 → 300 → 500 → 800 → 1,300 → 2,100 SLP**, capped at seven breeds per Axie, an intentional brake on supply.

The fatal asymmetry: breeding demand depends on *new players* wanting Axie teams. When new-player growth slowed, breeding (the only big sink) collapsed while gameplay emission continued — uncapped SLP supply exploded against shrinking burns, producing hyperinflation. This is the archetypal [[emissions]]-versus-sinks failure that every later [[gamefi|GameFi]] economy ([[stepn|STEPN]], [[big-time|Big Time]], [[deapcoin|DEAPcoin]]) explicitly designs to avoid.

### The 2021–22 play-to-earn boom and bust (handled honestly)

SLP is the defining case study of [[play-to-earn]] economics. During 2021, [[axie-infinity|Axie Infinity]] became a global phenomenon, especially in the **Philippines, Venezuela, and Indonesia**, where players (and "scholarship" managers who lent out Axie teams) could earn meaningful real-world income farming SLP. At its peak Axie processed billions of dollars in NFT volume and SLP briefly traded near **$0.40 (July 2021)**.

The economy was structurally unsustainable: **SLP was emitted almost without limit through gameplay, while the only major sink — breeding new Axies — could not absorb the supply** once new-player growth slowed. This is a classic emissions-versus-sinks imbalance (see [[emissions]]). As demand for new players (and thus new Axies to breed) cooled in late 2021 and 2022, SLP issuance massively exceeded burns, producing **hyperinflation**: the token's purchasing power collapsed and the price fell ~99.8% from its high.

Sky Mavis responded with repeated economic interventions — removing SLP rewards from the unranked/adventure modes, adjusting breeding costs, and introducing "Origins" and seasonal designs to better balance issuance and sinks. The **March 2022 Ronin Bridge hack (~$625M)** further damaged confidence in the ecosystem. SLP today is a deeply deflated reward token whose value tracks the health of the Axie player economy.

## Competitive Position

SLP is inseparable from [[axie-infinity|Axie Infinity]] — its value is a derivative of that game's player economy. As the pioneer and cautionary tale of [[play-to-earn]] tokenomics, it is the benchmark against which newer gaming economies (e.g., [[big-time|Big Time]], [[bora|BORA]], [[deapcoin|DEAPcoin]]) explicitly design to avoid the same emissions trap. Its competitive moat is brand and an established (if much-reduced) player base; its weakness is that the original "earn a living by grinding" thesis has been broadly discredited and the design has had to pivot toward fun-first gameplay.

SLP trades as a **[[play-to-earn]] legacy proxy** — high name recognition, but value entirely derivative of Axie's much-reduced player economy. In the 2026-06-22 **Established Bear Market** (Fear & Greed 21 / Extreme Fear; the gaming/metaverse cohort AXS, SAND, GALA, IMX, RON broadly weak), SLP moves with GameFi sentiment.

## Trading Profile

### Venues & liquidity

SLP is tradable on **Binance** — both **spot** (SLP/USDT) and a **USD-margined perpetual** with the usual leveraged-derivative telemetry (funding, open interest, liquidations). It is **not** listed on Hyperliquid, so **Binance is the primary leveraged venue** for the token. This concentration matters for execution: perp depth, funding, and liquidation flow are effectively a single-venue signal, and spot liquidity is spread thin across Binance, Bitget, KuCoin, and Crypto.com plus bridged Ronin/Ethereum DEX pools. With a sub-$20M cap and tens of billions of tokens outstanding, the perp order book is shallow relative to majors — size positions for [[slippage]], keep leverage modest, and expect that large market orders (or clustered liquidations) can move price sharply. Absence of a second major perp venue also removes an easy cross-venue funding/basis arbitrage leg, so most derivative strategies here are single-venue by necessity.

### Applicable strategies

- [[funding-rate-harvest]] — collect perp funding on Binance USD-M when SLP funding runs persistently positive or negative during GameFi sentiment swings.
- [[crowded-long-funding-fade]] — fade over-leveraged longs when a low-quality GameFi bounce drives funding and OI to crowded extremes on the sole leveraged venue.
- [[liquidation-cascade-fade]] — the shallow single-venue perp book makes SLP prone to liquidation cascades; fade the wick after forced selling exhausts.
- [[oi-price-exhaustion]] — rising open interest into a stalling price on a thin micro-cap flags exhaustion of a speculative move.
- [[rsi-mean-reversion]] — a high-supply, deeply deflated token trading near its all-time low mean-reverts off oversold/overbought extremes on spot.
- [[range-trading]] — outside catalysts, SLP grinds in ranges near its lows; trade defined support/resistance rather than trend.

### Volatility & regime character

Small-cap (rank ~829, sub-$20M) GameFi/[[play-to-earn]] reward token with **beta-on-beta** behaviour: it moves with broad altcoin risk appetite multiplied by GameFi-theme sentiment (correlated with AXS, SAND, GALA, IMX, RON). Directionally it tracks BTC/ETH regime for risk-on/risk-off, but amplifies both moves. Its uncapped-supply overhang and single-game dependence give it reflexive, low-quality bounces in bear tape and no fundamental floor — high realized volatility with sharp, mean-reverting spikes rather than durable trends.

### Risk flags

- **Liquidity / venue concentration** — Binance is the only major leveraged venue; no Hyperliquid listing means thin, single-venue perp depth and cascade-prone liquidations.
- **Uncapped supply / emissions** — no max supply; renewed gameplay issuance can swamp price if sinks stay weak (see [[emissions]]).
- **Narrative dependence** — value is entirely derivative of Axie Infinity's player economy and the broadly discredited [[play-to-earn]] thesis.
- **Ecosystem security history** — the 2022 Ronin Bridge hack (~$625M) is a reminder of side-chain/bridge risk in the underlying stack.
- **Micro-cap fragility** — sub-$20M cap with tens of billions of tokens outstanding; size for [[slippage]] and treat as high-variance speculation.
