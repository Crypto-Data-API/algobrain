# Powerledger

**Powerledger** (ticker **POWR**) is an Australian-founded technology company building software for distributed, decentralized energy markets. Its blockchain-based platform enables tracking and trading of **energy, grid-flexibility services, and environmental commodities** — most notably peer-to-peer (P2P) energy trading and renewable-energy certificates (RECs / Renewable Energy Certificates). The **POWR token** is an [[ethereum|Ethereum]] ERC-20 (with energy transactions processed on a native [[solana|Solana]]-based chain). It is part of the energy-sector [[depin|DePIN]] segment and ranks **#735** by market capitalization.

## Overview

Powerledger (POWR) is a technology company that builds software for distributed and decentralised energy markets for a sustainable future. It has developed a blockchain-based platform that enables the tracking and trading of energy, flexibility services and environmental commodities. Its goal is to provide the platform for a fully modernised, market-driven grid that offers consumers a choice in their energy while driving the democratisation of power.
Headquartered in Zug, Switzerland, Powerledger operates in more than 10 countries. Powerledger operates on two blockchain layers; the POWR token is issued on Ethereum as an ERC-20 token, and the Powerledger blockchain - a native Solana based blockchain to process energy transactions on a more scalable network. 

The POWR token is an ERC-20 token that acts as a licence required for businesses such as; utilities, renewable energy operators, microgrids, companies committed to 100% renewable energy and property developers to access Powerledger’s platform, and will be able to be used to pay for transactions on the Powerledger blockchain in future. In 2015, 30 countries had reached grid parity, meaning that the price of solar-generated energy was equal to or less than the cost of local retail electricity. Powerledger combines renewable energy and blockchain technology to offer energy solutions that are cheaper and more sustainable than traditional energy alternatives.

Powerledger was founded in 2016 to solve a pressing problem: Energy that comes from solar and wind is intermittent and lacks the steady quality that fossil fuels provide, and this causes problems for the grid.
Powerledger’s approach was to create a highly agile market that could help regulate this intermittency and start to create a transition to a more responsive, distributed network with better storage. Powerledger’s software is used by utilities and large corporations to track, trace, and trade every kilowatt of energy to improve grid stability.

## Architecture & Token Role — The Energy-On-Chain Pipeline

Power Ledger runs across two layers: the **POWR token** is an ERC-20 on [[ethereum|Ethereum]], while energy transactions are processed on a native **[[solana|Solana]]-based Powerledger blockchain** chosen for higher throughput and lower fees. The product suite turns physical grid activity into tradable on-chain records:

- **P2P energy trading** — rooftop-solar and other generators sell surplus kilowatt-hours directly to nearby consumers; the platform meters, matches, and settles these micro-transactions.
- **Virtual power plant (VPP) coordination** — aggregating distributed batteries/solar so they can be dispatched and traded as a single flexible resource for grid stability.
- **EV charging settlement** — metering and settling electric-vehicle charging sessions.
- **Environmental commodities (RECs / carbon-style certificates)** — issuing and trading tradable renewable-energy certificates, creating a transparent on-chain registry for green attributes.

The **POWR token** functions primarily as an **access licence**: utilities, renewable-energy operators, microgrids, corporations pursuing 100%-renewable goals, and property developers acquire and hold POWR to access the Power Ledger platform, and POWR is intended to settle transactions on the Powerledger chain. This "licence to participate" model means the demand-vs-emissions dynamic depends on the number and scale of platform deployments (the company states it operates in 10+ countries) rather than on inflationary block rewards — supply is capped at 1 billion POWR, of which roughly half circulates.

POWR is **capped at 1 billion tokens** with roughly half circulating (MC/FDV ≈ 0.53), meaning ~47% of supply is still to enter circulation — a meaningful **dilution overhang** for holders. **Value accrual** is via the licence model: businesses acquire and hold POWR to access the platform, so demand scales with the breadth and depth of real-world deployments rather than with on-chain emissions or fee burns. The weakness of this model is that licence purchases can be infrequent and lumpy, and value can decouple from platform usage if settlement migrates off-token.

## Competitive Position & Comparison

Power Ledger's most direct crypto-native comparison is [[energy-web-token|Energy Web (EWT)]], though they target different layers: Power Ledger builds the trading/marketplace application, while Energy Web positions as the neutral identity/coordination infrastructure. Both compete more fundamentally against incumbent grid-software vendors and conventional REC registries.

| Project / token | Layer | Distinguishing trait |
|---|---|---|
| **Power Ledger (POWR)** | Energy trading/marketplace application | P2P trading, VPP, EV, RECs; deployed in 10+ countries; Solana-based settlement chain |
| **[[energy-web-token|Energy Web (EWT)]]** | Neutral grid identity/coordination infrastructure | Enterprise-grade decentralized OS for grid operators; complements rather than directly overlaps |
| **WePower (WPR)** | Renewable-energy financing/tokenized PPAs | Focus on tokenizing future energy production / financing, not live P2P trading |
| **Incumbent grid software / REC registries** | Traditional vendors | The real-world competition; entrenched, regulated, slow to adopt blockchain |

Power Ledger's edge is its **head start and live deployments** in real energy markets; its challenge is that the addressable adopters (regulated utilities and grids) move slowly and are politically gated.

## Risks

- **Adoption velocity** — like all energy DePIN, value depends on regulated utilities and grids adopting on-chain trading, which is slow and politically gated.
- **Token-utility linkage** — the "licence" model means token value can decouple from platform usage if licences are acquired infrequently or settlement migrates off-token.
- **Regulatory overhang** — the "Alleged SEC Securities" tag reflects unresolved questions around 2017 ICO-era tokens.
- **Liquidity / size** — a ~$24M cap with modest volume implies high volatility and slippage risk; the large FDV-vs-MCap gap (roughly half the supply still to unlock) adds dilution risk.
- **Long downtrend** — ~97% below its 2018 ATH with no sustained multi-cycle recovery.
