# Arweave

**Arweave** (AR) is a [[depin|DePIN]] decentralized-storage Layer-1 that offers **permanent, pay-once-store-forever data storage** via a novel "proof-of-access" consensus and a structure called the *blockweave*. **AR** is its native token: users pay a one-time fee in AR to store data permanently, and that fee seeds an endowment that pays miners to keep serving the data far into the future. Arweave is a leading peer of [[filecoin]] and [[siacoin]] in decentralized storage, and is increasingly framed as **AI/data-availability infrastructure** (its "AO" hyper-parallel compute layer) — it is backed by [[a16z|a16z]], Coinbase Ventures, and Multicoin Capital.

Arweave is the only major decentralized-storage project selling **true permanence** — a one-time fee buys forever-storage funded by an endowment — versus [[filecoin|Filecoin]]'s renewable, time-bound deal model and [[siacoin|Sia]]'s redundancy/collateral model. On tokenomics, AR is the cleanest of the group: hard-capped at 66M and ~99.5% emitted, so essentially **no future dilution** (MC/FDV ~1.00), unlike Filecoin's heavy emission schedule or Sia's uncapped supply. The trade-off is that permanence requires the endowment's long-run solvency assumption to hold.

## Valuation Framing

AR behaves like an **infrastructure/commodity token** rather than a governance or cash-flow asset:

- **Bytes-stored demand thesis** — the cleanest on-chain fundamental: AR demand scales with data permanently committed to the network (each store is a one-time AR buy). Rising bytes-stored is a measurable, non-speculative demand signal; the bear case is that paid storage demand, while growing, remains small relative to a ~$132M market cap.
- **AI/AO optionality** — much of the recent narrative re-rating leans on the **AO** hyper-parallel compute layer and the "permanent storage for AI datasets / model provenance" angle (Arweave carries an AI category tag). This is upside optionality but also a dependency: AO adoption disappointment is a downside catalyst.
- **Endowment-model risk premium** — permanence economics assume storage costs keep falling (Kryder's-law style) and the endowment stays solvent over very long horizons. If costs stop falling or AR price stays depressed, long-run miner incentives could be strained — a tail risk unique to the pay-once model that the market discounts.
- **Near-zero dilution** — with supply essentially fully emitted, AR has none of the unlock overhang weighing on tokens like [[optimism|OP]]; price is driven by demand and narrative, not emission.

## Tokenomics & Supply

AR has a **max supply of 66M** (~65.65M circulating, essentially fully emitted; MC/FDV ~1.00), so there is minimal future dilution. The economic core is the **storage endowment model**: when a user pays a one-time fee to store data, a large share of that fee is placed into an endowment that pays out to miners over time, with the model assuming the long-run real cost of storage keeps falling (per Kryder's-law-style declines). Miners earn AR for storing and, critically, **proving access** to old data (proof-of-access) — recall of rare historical blocks is what earns rewards, incentivizing full replication of the dataset. AR therefore behaves more like an **infrastructure/commodity token** (demand scales with data permanently stored) than a governance token.

## Use Case, Narrative & Category

Arweave is a **[[depin|DePIN]] / decentralized-storage / data-availability** project, and one of the few in crypto with a clearly differentiated product: **permanent** storage (vs. [[filecoin|Filecoin]]'s renewable contract model and [[siacoin|Sia]]'s redundancy model). Key narratives:

- **Permaweb** — websites and apps hosted permanently on Arweave, censorship-resistant and immune to link rot.
- **NFT / data permanence** — many [[nft|NFT]] projects and L2s store metadata or transaction data on Arweave for durability.
- **AI & data availability** — its **AO ("Actor Oriented") hyper-parallel compute** layer and the "permanent storage for AI datasets / model provenance" angle align it with the AI-infrastructure thesis (Arweave carries an "Artificial Intelligence (AI)" category tag).
- **Rollup data availability** — Arweave-based DA (e.g. via integrations) competes for the same demand as other DA layers.

The bull case is real, growing usage (bytes stored is a measurable on-chain demand metric); the bear case is that storage demand is still small relative to valuation, and DA/storage competition is intense.

## Risks

- **Endowment-model assumption risk** — permanence economics rely on storage costs continuing to fall and the endowment remaining solvent over very long horizons; if costs stop falling or AR's price stays low, long-run miner incentives could be strained.
- **Demand vs. valuation** — paid storage demand, while measurable and growing, is still modest relative to market cap.
- **Competition** — faces [[filecoin]], [[siacoin]], Storj, and emerging rollup data-availability layers (e.g. Celestia, EigenDA) for overlapping demand.
- **Narrative dependence on AI/AO** — much of the recent re-rating leans on the AI/AO story; execution or adoption disappointment is a downside catalyst.
- **Bear-market beta & liquidity** — mid-cap with moderate volume; can underperform [[bitcoin]]/[[ethereum]] and gap on thin order books in risk-off regimes.
