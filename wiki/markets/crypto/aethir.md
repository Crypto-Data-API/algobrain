# Aethir

**Aethir** (ATH) is a decentralized, **enterprise-grade GPU-as-a-service** provider in the [[depin|DePIN]] / [[artificial-intelligence|AI]] compute sector. Its decentralized cloud connects GPU providers (who run "containers") with enterprise clients needing NVIDIA H100-class chips for professional AI/ML training and inference, plus cloud-gaming and telecom workloads. The token is deployed on [[ethereum|Ethereum]], [[solana|Solana]] and [[arbitrum|Arbitrum]].

## Overview

Aethir is a decentralized, enterprise-grade GPU-as-a-service provider. Its decentralized cloud-computing infrastructure lets GPU providers (containers) serve enterprise clients who need powerful NVIDIA H100 chips for professional AI/ML tasks. 

Aethir also support cloud gaming clients with their virtual computing phones and GPU's with contracts with the world’s largest telecommunication company. Everything within Aethir ecosystem will be decentralized and community-owned.

## Decentralized GPU Cloud: Enterprise Focus

Aethir differentiates from [[akash-network|Akash]] and [[render-token|Render]] by targeting **enterprise-grade GPU computing** with a particular focus on two verticals:

### AI/ML Training and Inference

- **Hardware tier:** Aethir focuses on high-end NVIDIA H100 and A100 GPUs -- the same chips used by OpenAI, Google, and Meta for large language model training
- **Enterprise contracts:** Rather than a spot marketplace (like Akash's reverse auction), Aethir emphasizes longer-term enterprise agreements with SLA guarantees
- **Container architecture:** GPU resources are packaged into "containers" that providers offer to the network; each container has defined specs (GPU type, VRAM, bandwidth)

### Cloud Gaming

Aethir powers **cloud gaming infrastructure** by providing GPU rendering for game streaming services. This is a distinct use case from AI compute:

- **Telecom partnerships:** Aethir has contracts with major telecommunications companies to provide virtual computing phones and cloud gaming GPUs
- **Edge computing:** Gaming requires low latency, so Aethir positions GPU containers at network edges (closer to end users)
- **Revenue diversification:** Gaming revenue provides a non-AI revenue stream, reducing single-narrative dependency

## ATH Token Analysis

### Token Utility

- **Payment:** Enterprises pay for GPU services in ATH or stablecoins (with ATH settlement)
- **Staking:** Container providers and checkers stake ATH as collateral, earning rewards
- **Governance:** ATH holders vote on network parameters and treasury allocation

### What Drives ATH Price

1. **GPU demand narrative** -- ATH moves with the broader AI/GPU compute thesis; NVIDIA earnings and AI model releases are key catalysts
2. **Enterprise adoption announcements** -- new telecom or gaming partnerships drive price spikes
3. **Network metrics** -- active containers, GPU hours sold, and revenue growth are fundamental drivers
4. **AI x crypto basket correlation** -- ATH trades alongside [[akash-network|AKT]], [[render-token|RNDR]], and [[aioz-network|AIOZ]] as a sector
5. **Token unlock schedule** -- only ~20.1B of 42B max supply circulating (~48%); significant dilution risk from future unlocks (FDV ~2x market cap)

### Trading Characteristics (Extended)

- **AI narrative high-beta:** ATH moves aggressively on AI-related news -- both up and down
- **Multi-chain presence:** Available on [[ethereum|Ethereum]], [[solana|Solana]], and [[arbitrum|Arbitrum]], providing diverse on-chain liquidity
- **Large supply:** 42B max supply makes the per-token price very low (~$0.005), which can attract retail "cheap coin" buying but also means large absolute moves require massive capital
- **Drawdown:** ~ -96.0% from ATH -- typical for AI/DePIN tokens that launched during hype and deflated; trades near its February 2026 all-time low
- **Volume-to-cap ratio:** $11.76M daily volume vs $94.55M cap = ~12.4% daily turnover, the highest active trading interest in this loop's basket

### Risk Factors

- **Enterprise execution risk:** Enterprise sales cycles are long; announced partnerships may take years to generate meaningful revenue
- **GPU price normalization:** As NVIDIA ramps H100/B100 production, the GPU shortage eases, reducing the premium for decentralized alternatives
- **Massive dilution:** Only 43.7% of supply is circulating; check the vesting schedule before sizing positions
- **Cloud gaming uncertainty:** Cloud gaming has been "the next big thing" for a decade without achieving mass adoption
- **Competition:** Both centralized (CoreWeave, Lambda) and decentralized ([[akash-network|Akash]], [[render-token|Render]]) competitors are well-funded

## Valuation Framing (qualitative)

Aethir is unusual among small-cap DePIN tokens in **publishing concrete fundamentals**, which makes the usage-vs-valuation gap explicit:

- **Revenue vs market cap** — Aethir has reported ~$156M-$166M annualized recurring revenue (ARR), 440k+ containers across 94 countries, and ~1B compute hours delivered (Q3 2025 figures; see Major News). Against a ~$94.6M market cap, that is a low revenue multiple **if** the ARR is durable enterprise revenue rather than incentive-subsidized usage — the central bull/bear debate.
- **Dilution offset** — the bull case (real revenue) must outrun a heavy emissions schedule (~52% of max supply still to release, FDV ~2x cap). Token price has stayed near all-time lows precisely because emissions have, so far, overwhelmed revenue growth in the market's view.
- **Up-the-stack move** — Aethir Mesh (inference-as-a-service over its GPU network) shifts Aethir from selling raw compute toward higher-margin model serving; execution here is a re-rating catalyst.
- **Sector beta** — ATH is a high-beta AI/GPU proxy; NVIDIA earnings, AI model releases, and GPU-supply normalization (which compresses the decentralized premium) are the dominant swing factors.

This is a qualitative framing, not a price target.
