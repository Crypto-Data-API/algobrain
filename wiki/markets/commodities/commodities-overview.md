# Commodities Markets

Physical and financial commodity markets -- precious metals, energy, agriculture.

Commodities are the raw materials that power the global economy. Unlike stocks or crypto, commodity prices are ultimately anchored by physical supply and demand -- harvests, drilling output, mining production, and geopolitical disruptions. This makes them a distinct asset class with unique trading dynamics: contango and backwardation in futures curves, seasonal patterns, and sensitivity to weather, wars, and policy decisions.

[[gold]] is the traditional safe-haven asset and inflation hedge. [[natural-gas]] is among the most volatile tradeable commodities. [[silver]] straddles the line between precious metal and industrial input. [[copper]] is "Dr. Copper" — the metal with a PhD in economics.

### Industrial / Base Metals
- [[copper]] -- "Dr. Copper"; barometer of global economic health; EV transition demand driver
- [[aluminum]] -- Most produced non-ferrous metal; "solidified electricity"
- [[nickel]] -- Dual identity: stainless steel input and EV battery material
- [[zinc]] -- Fourth most consumed metal; galvanizing steel
- [[iron-ore]] -- Australia's largest export; China consumes 70% of seaborne supply

### Energy
- [[crude-oil]] -- Global energy benchmark; WTI and Brent; OPEC+ supply management
- [[natural-gas]] -- Extreme volatility driven by weather, storage, and seasonality
- [[heating-oil]] -- Refined petroleum product; NYMEX HO; diesel proxy; crack spread component
- [[gasoline]] -- RBOB gasoline futures; driving season seasonality; summer/winter blend dynamics
- [[coal]] -- Thermal and metallurgical coal; steelmaking and power generation
- [[uranium]] -- Nuclear power fuel; supply deficit from underinvestment
- [[nuclear-power]] -- Energy source; growing demand driver for uranium

- [[wheat]] -- Global staple grain; geopolitically sensitive; CBOT ZW
- [[soybeans]] -- Most important oilseed; crush spread anchor; CBOT ZS
