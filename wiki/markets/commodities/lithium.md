# Lithium

Lithium (Li) is the critical battery metal at the heart of the electric-vehicle (EV) and grid-storage build-out. Unlike exchange-traded base metals, lithium has historically been an opaque, contract-priced market dominated by a handful of producers and assessed-price reporting agencies, but it has rapidly financialised since 2023 with the launch of cash-settled lithium futures. Lithium prices are among the most violently cyclical of any commodity: the battery-grade lithium carbonate price ran from roughly US$6,000/tonne in 2020 to over US$80,000/tonne in late 2022, then collapsed back toward US$10,000/tonne by late 2025 as a supply glut met slowing EV growth.

## What It Is and What It Tracks

Lithium is traded in several distinct chemical and mineral forms, and traders must be precise about which one a quote refers to:

| Form | Description | Primary use |
|------|-------------|-------------|
| **Lithium carbonate (Li2CO3)** | Battery-grade, ~99.5% purity | LFP cathodes, the dominant China benchmark |
| **Lithium hydroxide (LiOH)** | Required for high-nickel NMC/NCA cathodes | High energy-density EV batteries |
| **Spodumene concentrate** | Hard-rock ore (~6% Li2O, "SC6") | Feedstock converted to carbonate/hydroxide |

The benchmark price most traders watch is **battery-grade lithium carbonate, China spot**, assessed by reporting agencies such as Fastmarkets, Benchmark Mineral Intelligence, and Asian Metal. As of late 2025 the China spot carbonate price was around US$10,000–10,300/tonne, with 2026 assessments showing a tentative recovery on stronger demand and tighter sentiment rather than a clear return to deficit (Source: Perplexity sonar, June 2026; USGS Mineral Commodity Summaries 2026).

## How Traders Access It

Lithium is not yet traded on a deep, transparent physical exchange like [[copper]] on the LME, but financial contracts now exist:

- **CME lithium hydroxide and lithium carbonate futures** — cash-settled against Fastmarkets assessments; the primary Western financial hedge.
- **GFEX (Guangzhou Futures Exchange) lithium carbonate futures** — launched July 2023; now the most liquid lithium futures contract globally, denominated in RMB and physically deliverable. GFEX price action increasingly leads the global market.
- **SGX (Singapore Exchange)** — lithium and battery-metal contracts targeting the international market.
- **Equity proxies** — pure-play miners (Albemarle, SQM, Pilbara Minerals, Mineral Resources, Ganfeng, Tianqi) and the [[australian-dollar|ASX]] lithium complex, which is highly geared to spodumene prices.
- **ETFs** — LIT (Global X Lithium & Battery Tech) holds miners and battery makers; it tracks the equity complex, not the spot metal.

## Supply and Demand Drivers

**Supply** is concentrated in the "Lithium Triangle" brines (Chile, Argentina, Bolivia), Australian hard-rock spodumene (the world's largest production source), and rapidly growing Chinese lepidolite and African (Zimbabwe) output. Australia and Chile together account for the majority of mined supply, while China dominates refining/conversion capacity. Brine extraction is low-cost but slow to ramp; hard-rock is faster but more expensive. The 2023–2025 price crash triggered mine curtailments and project delays, which set the stage for the tentative 2026 rebalancing.

**Demand** is overwhelmingly batteries (~80%+), split between EVs and grid-scale storage, with the remainder in ceramics, glass, and greases. Key demand swing factors:

- **EV sales growth**, especially in China, Europe, and the US, and the cathode-chemistry mix.
- **LFP vs NMC chemistry** — LFP (lithium iron phosphate) uses carbonate and no [[nickel]]/cobalt; its rising market share shifts demand toward carbonate over hydroxide.
- **Grid storage** — the fastest-growing new demand segment, partly insulated from EV cyclicality.
- **Chinese subsidy and inventory policy**, which can whipsaw spot prices.

## Typical Catalysts

- Chinese EV subsidy and stimulus announcements.
- Major producer guidance cuts or mine curtailments (e.g., CATL/Yichun lepidolite suspensions).
- Quarterly results and shipment data from Albemarle, SQM, and Pilbara Minerals.
- GFEX futures speculative positioning and inventory data.
- New supply coming online from Argentina brines and African projects.

## Correlations

Lithium is loosely correlated with the broader EV/battery-metal complex ([[nickel]], cobalt, graphite) and with risk-on EV sentiment, but its extreme supply-driven cyclicality means it frequently decouples from base metals. ASX lithium equities are a key driver of the materials sector and a sentiment input to the [[australian-dollar]].
