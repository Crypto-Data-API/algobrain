# Platinum

Platinum (Pt) is a precious metal with significant industrial applications, distinguishing it from [[gold]] and [[silver]] which are more heavily weighted toward investment and jewelry demand. Approximately 40% of platinum demand comes from automotive catalytic converters, specifically for [[diesel-vehicles|diesel engines]], where platinum is used to reduce harmful emissions.

## How Platinum Is Traded

- **Futures**: NYMEX platinum futures (PL) on [[cme-group|CME Group]], with each contract representing 50 troy ounces.
- **Spot Market**: Priced in USD per troy ounce, with the [[lbma|LBMA]] Platinum Price set twice daily via electronic auction.
- **ETFs**: PPLT (Aberdeen Standard Physical Platinum Shares) is the primary physical platinum ETF.
- **London Market**: OTC trading through LBMA-approved dealers, settled in loco London.

## Supply

South Africa produces approximately 70% of the world's platinum supply, creating significant concentration risk. The Bushveld Igneous Complex in South Africa contains the world's largest platinum reserves. Other producers include Russia (~12%), Zimbabwe (~8%), and North America (~5%). Mining is deep-level, labor-intensive, and subject to frequent disruptions from power outages (Eskom), labor strikes, and safety stoppages. Platinum is part of the [[palladium|Platinum Group Metals (PGM)]] complex, meaning it is often co-produced with palladium, rhodium, and other PGMs.

## Price Dynamics

Platinum historically traded at a premium to [[gold]] — often $200-400/oz above gold — reflecting its rarity and industrial value. However, since 2015 platinum has traded at a persistent discount to gold, driven by:

- **Diesel emission scandals** (Volkswagen "Dieselgate" 2015) — reduced diesel vehicle market share in Europe, lowering catalytic converter demand
- **Substitution**: Automakers increasingly substituting cheaper [[palladium]] for platinum in gasoline catalytic converters (though some reverse substitution is now occurring as palladium became more expensive)
- **Gold's safe-haven rally**: Gold benefited from central bank buying, geopolitical risk, and falling real rates, while platinum's industrial exposure weighed on it

The platinum-to-gold ratio is watched as a relative value indicator. Extended periods of discount may signal potential mean-reversion opportunities.

## Hydrogen Economy Optionality

Platinum is the primary catalyst in proton exchange membrane (PEM) fuel cells used in hydrogen-powered vehicles and stationary power generation. If the hydrogen economy scales as proponents expect, platinum demand could see a structural step-change. This optionality is not yet fully priced in, though estimates vary widely.
