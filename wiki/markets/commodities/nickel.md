# Nickel

Nickel (Ni) is an industrial metal with a dual identity: the traditional stainless steel input (~70% of demand) and the emerging battery material for electric vehicles. Traded primarily on the [[london-metal-exchange|LME]], nickel gained notoriety after the [[2022-lme-nickel-squeeze|2022 LME nickel squeeze]] — one of the most dramatic market dislocations in commodity history.

As of mid-2026 the nickel market remained broadly **oversupplied**, driven by continued growth in Indonesian (largely Chinese-financed) supply, which has kept prices well below the 2022 squeeze spike and pressured high-cost producers in Australia and New Caledonia (Source: Perplexity sonar, June 2026).

## How Nickel Is Traded

- **LME**: The [[london-metal-exchange|London Metal Exchange]] is the primary global venue. Contracts are quoted in USD per tonne, with standard lots of 6 tonnes. Settlement includes physical delivery of Class 1 nickel (minimum 99.8% purity).
- **SHFE**: Shanghai Futures Exchange trades nickel domestically in China.
- **CME**: [[cme-group|CME Group]] has been building nickel futures volumes, partly in response to LME credibility issues post-2022 squeeze.

## Class 1 vs Class 2 Nickel

A critical distinction in the nickel market that confuses many observers:

| Type | Purity | Primary Use | Key Forms | LME Deliverable? |
|------|--------|-------------|-----------|-------------------|
| Class 1 | ≥99.8% | Batteries, specialty alloys | Briquettes, cathode, powder | Yes |
| Class 2 | <99.8% | Stainless steel | Ferronickel, Nickel Pig Iron (NPI) | No |

Class 2 nickel (ferronickel and NPI) accounts for the majority of global production but is NOT deliverable against LME contracts. This disconnect between what the LME prices (Class 1) and what most of the market produces (Class 2) contributed to the [[2022-lme-nickel-squeeze|2022 squeeze]] dynamics.

## Supply

Indonesia has rapidly become the dominant global nickel producer, driven by Chinese-backed investment in nickel processing (HPAL plants for battery-grade nickel and NPI for stainless). Other major producers include the Philippines, Russia, New Caledonia, and Australia.

| Producer | Share | Notes |
|----------|-------|-------|
| Indonesia | ~50% | Rapid expansion; export ban on raw ore since 2020 |
| Philippines | ~10% | Laterite ore, lower grade |
| Russia (Norilsk Nickel) | ~8% | Class 1 producer; sanctions risk |
| New Caledonia | ~5% | High-cost laterite operations |
| Australia | ~5% | Class 1 and laterite |

Indonesia's nickel export ban (raw ore) has been a key structural shift — forcing domestic processing and dramatically increasing Indonesia's share of refined nickel production. Chinese investment (notably Tsingshan Holding) built out massive processing capacity in Indonesia's Morowali and Weda Bay industrial parks.

## EV Battery Demand

Nickel is a key cathode material in lithium-ion batteries, specifically in NMC (Nickel-Manganese-Cobalt) and NCA (Nickel-Cobalt-Aluminum) chemistries. Higher nickel content = higher energy density = longer EV range. The trend toward "high-nickel" cathodes (NMC 811, with 80% nickel) is supportive of nickel demand. However, the competing LFP (Lithium Iron Phosphate) chemistry — which contains no nickel — has been gaining market share, particularly in China and for standard-range EVs, creating uncertainty about the pace of nickel demand growth from batteries.

## The 2022 LME Nickel Squeeze

On March 8, 2022, nickel prices on the LME more than doubled in a single session, rising from ~$30,000/tonne to over $100,000/tonne. See [[2022-lme-nickel-squeeze]] for the full account of this extraordinary market dislocation, including the controversial LME decision to cancel trades and suspend the market.

## Price Drivers

- **Stainless steel production** (especially Chinese 300-series output)
- **EV battery demand** and cathode chemistry trends (NMC vs LFP)
- **Indonesian production policy** (export bans, processing mandates, environmental regulations)
- **LME warehouse stocks** and warrant cancellations
- **Russian sanctions risk** (Norilsk Nickel is a key Class 1 producer)
- **Energy costs** at processing facilities (HPAL plants are energy-intensive)

## Key Indicators

- LME nickel warehouse stock levels
- Indonesian nickel ore export policy
- Chinese stainless steel production data
- EV battery cathode chemistry mix (NMC vs LFP market share)
- Norilsk Nickel production reports
