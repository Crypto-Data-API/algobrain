[[fred-mcnaught|Fred McNaught's]] sector-by-sector analysis of the Australian stock market, applying the [[gics|GICS]] classification framework. The article provides clear invest/avoid recommendations for each sector with reasoning rooted in competitive advantage, government policy, and Australia's commodity strengths. Fred favours individual stock selection over ETFs but pragmatically uses ETFs in sectors where he lacks specialised knowledge.

## Key Claims

### GICS Framework

1. [HIGH] [[gics|GICS]]: 11 sectors, 25 industry groups, 74 industries, 163 sub-industries
2. [HIGH] Some sectors favour Australia, some don't. Don't need to own them all.
3. [HIGH] "As value investors we focus more on the stock's individual fundamental analysis over sector coverage. We don't buy it just to get sector coverage."
4. [HIGH] Individual stocks favoured over ETFs, but use ETFs in high-growth sectors lacking specialised knowledge

### Sectors to Invest

5. [HIGH] **Energy** (careful of fossil fuel policy): [[bhp-group|BHP Group]] (coal, iron ore, copper) + [[origin-energy|Origin Energy]] (gas)
6. [HIGH] **Materials** (Australia has commodity advantage): target [[uranium]] (PDN + BOE), copper, and gold ([[northern-star|EVN]] + [[northern-star|NST]])
7. [HIGH] **Information Technology** (megatrends: cloud, AI, robotics): Fred uses ETFs — NDQ (NASDAQ-100, 0.48% fee), RBTZ (BetaShares Robotics & AI, 0.57% fee)
8. [HIGH] **Healthcare** (target biotech/pharmaceutical, avoid high-debt models): "Australia has an ageing population, more needs to be spent here"
9. [HIGH] **Utilities** (small allocation via ETFs for diversification): Franklin Templeton, Macquarie Fund, Magellan Utility Fund — all with DRP
10. [HIGH] **Real Estate** (small allocation, favour government contract REITs): "Buy investment properties when real estate market crashes. Add REITs with government clients for larger portfolios."

### Sectors to Avoid

11. [HIGH] **Industrials** — labour-dependent, except waste management companies ([[close-the-loop|CLG]], CWY) which benefit from government incentives
12. [HIGH] **Financials** — "Big banks growth days are over" — keep if already owned, otherwise avoid
13. [HIGH] **Consumer Staples/Discretionary** — too much competition
14. [HIGH] **Communications** — telecoms perform badly. Exception: [[macquarie-technology|MAQ]]

### ETF Performance

15. [MEDIUM] RBTZ performance poor (11%/year vs NDQ 18%/year) — filtering to >$10B market cap companies might improve returns dramatically

### Fred's Holdings (as disclosed)

16. [HIGH] Fred owns: QUBE (transport, 70% return in 10 years), Endeavour (hotels/bottleshops), Coles (consumer staples), HCW REIT + Centuria Capital (real estate)
17. [HIGH] Fred uses 3 utility ETFs as currency-hedged portfolio diversification: Franklin Templeton, Macquarie Fund, Magellan Utility Fund — all with DRP

### Policy and Government

18. [HIGH] Government policies and grants can sway sector opinions
