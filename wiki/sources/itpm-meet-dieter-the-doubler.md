Source summary of ITPM webinar featuring "Dieter," a Belgium-based ITPM mentoring program graduate who achieved a ~100% return in 6 months trading a long-short-equity [[options]] portfolio. Serves as an ITPM **marketing case study** demonstrating the mentoring program methodology. **Confidence note:** this is a promotional webinar showcasing a single successful student; the performance statistics were shown via on-screen brokerage data but are self-reported, unaudited, single-source, and selection-biased (ITPM showcases winners, not the full distribution of outcomes). All performance figures and vendor return *targets* are marked [MEDIUM]. Descriptions of the ITPM *method* and the four-stages-of-learning model are faithful summaries of what is taught and are marked [HIGH] as accurate records of the method, not as proof it works.

## Key Claims

### Dieter's Background and Setup

1. [MEDIUM] Dieter is based in Belgium with a finance and economics university degree, background in value investing and financial analysis. Started value investing with his father after the 2008 crisis. (Source: Dieter's direct statement — self-reported)
2. [MEDIUM] Completed an ITPM Thailand mentoring program starting October 2022, mentored by Kreil from first week of November to third week of February (~12 weeks). (Source: Kreil's direct statement — self-reported)
3. [MEDIUM] Starting capital: $122,868. Cleaned up all previous stock positions and options to start clean on November 2, 2022. (Source: Dieter showing brokerage data — self-reported, unaudited)
4. [MEDIUM] First two trades initiated during the mentoring itself (Lululemon and Splunk), approved by Kreil. (Source: Dieter's direct statement — self-reported)

### Performance Statistics

5. [MEDIUM] Total realized P&L: ~$133,732 minus commissions, yielding ~100% net return in 6 months. (Source: brokerage data dump shown on screen — self-reported, unaudited, selection-biased)
6. [MEDIUM] Win-loss rate: approximately 65% winners / 35% losers. (Source: spreadsheet data shown — self-reported)
7. [MEDIUM] Total winners booked: ~$360,000 profit. Total losers booked: ~$224,000 loss. (Source: spreadsheet data shown — self-reported)
8. [MEDIUM] R-score (dollar wins / dollar losses): 1.6. (Source: spreadsheet data shown — self-reported)
9. [MEDIUM] Average days per trade: 23 days. (Source: spreadsheet data shown — self-reported)

### ITPM Mentoring Program Target Statistics

10. [MEDIUM] Target annual return on retail accounts ($25K-$1M): 50-100%. With [[options]], returns can exceed 100%. (Source: Kreil's direct statement — vendor performance target, aspirational)
11. [MEDIUM] Target win-loss rate: minimum 60/40, ideally 65/35. (Source: Kreil's direct statement — vendor performance target)
12. [MEDIUM] Target R-score (dollars won / dollars lost): minimum 1.5. (Source: Kreil's direct statement — vendor performance target)
13. [MEDIUM] Target Sharpe ratio: minimum 3, possibly 4+ with options. (Source: Kreil's direct statement — vendor performance target)
14. [MEDIUM] Risk taken: 20-25% annualized, leveraged 3-4x through options. Average holding period: 20-25 days. (Source: Kreil's direct statement — stated method/target)

### Methodology and Process

15. [HIGH] All trades are [[options]] (puts and calls) on individual stocks — never stock positions. Every trade is either a long put, long call, short put, or short call. (Source: Dieter showing trade log)
16. [HIGH] Portfolio construction: long-short-equity options portfolio. Trades tracked in a detailed spreadsheet recording type (put/call), ticker, long/short, strike, expiry, entry/exit price, dates, contracts, and commissions. (Source: Dieter showing spreadsheet)
17. [HIGH] Equity curve management: track realized P&L against an "ideal line" (target trajectory). Job as a trader is to "make the number at the bottom of the screen go higher — I didn't say lower." (Source: Kreil's direct statement)
18. [HIGH] "Managing the business" — professionals manage statistics (win rate, R-score, Sharpe) not individual trades. "This is the difference between professional traders and retail traders — we have a professional process." (Source: Kreil's direct statement)

### Four Steps of Learning Applied to Trading

19. [HIGH] Pyramid model: (1) Unconscious Incompetence (99% of retail — "delusion, denial, lies"), (2) Conscious Incompetence (awareness of gaps after proper education), (3) Conscious Competence (making money using the information over ~6 months), (4) Unconscious Competence (~150+ trades over 12 months = statistical significance, "not luck"). (Source: Kreil's direct statement)
20. [HIGH] ITPM mentoring is 12 weeks. After that, trader must execute independently. "There's no point in ever relying on other people for the rest of your life for trade ideas." (Source: Kreil's direct statement)
