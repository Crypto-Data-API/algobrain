Comprehensive share investing guide authored by [[fred-mcnaught|Fred McNaught]], a retired expert stock market investor based in Brisbane, Australia. This source summary merges three progressive versions of the document, with the latest being a collaborative version between Fred and [[sam-deering|Sam Deering]]. The guide covers the full spectrum of fundamental investing: valuation methods, financial metrics, portfolio construction, wealth creation principles, and practical buying/selling discipline.

## Key Claims

### Investing Philosophy

1. [HIGH] Two parts to investing: (a) identifying the best stocks, (b) buying them for less than they are worth
2. [HIGH] Only buy when share price is less than intrinsic worth
3. [HIGH] Invest for the long term — "Before you buy a share know WHY you are buying it. Make a list of reasons. Only sell if those reasons change."
4. [HIGH] "When you find a truly wonderful business, stick with it. Patience pays, and one wonderful business can offset the many mediocre decisions that are inevitable."
5. [HIGH] Go with value not momentum, know the difference between speculating and investing, management quality matters
6. [HIGH] Always keep cash on hand as an asset — to deploy when markets misprice quality businesses

### Valuation Methods

7. [HIGH] Three methods to calculate intrinsic value: (a) [[dcf-analysis|DCF analysis]], (b) EPS x P/E x (1+r) where r is expected earnings growth, (c) Asset-based valuation (company assets / shares on issue)
8. [HIGH] DCF formula: DCF = (CF / 1+r)^1 + (CF / 1+r)^2 + ... — "a dollar in the future is worth less than a dollar today"
9. [HIGH] These valuation tools are "just tools" — "I use them as a rough guide only"

### Fundamental Metrics — Benchmarks

10. [HIGH] [[roe|ROE]] benchmark: at least 4% above current cash rate. A rate of 15%+ is considered good. Look at last 5 years.
11. [HIGH] [[roa|ROA]] benchmark: 5% or above generally acceptable. Under 5% requires explanation.
12. [HIGH] "[[eps|EPS]] growth and dividend per share growth are two of the main keys to investing"
13. [HIGH] Dividend yield benchmark: compare with yield on bank deposits (risk-free return). Government guarantees deposits up to $250,000.
14. [HIGH] [[franking-credits|Franking credit]] formula: (Dividend Amount / (1 - Company Tax Rate)) - Dividend Amount. Company tax rate 30%.
15. [HIGH] DRP (Dividend Reinvestment Plan): "a way to build your shareholding over time" — shares issued at/below market price, free of brokerage
16. [HIGH] [[pe-ratio|P/E ratio]]: compare to sector average, understand why higher/lower. Historical ASX average P/E is 14.5. When interest rates rise, P/E ratios compress.
17. [HIGH] "Investor enthusiasm for a stock can lead to P/E expansion even though EPS remains stable"
18. [HIGH] [[gross-profit|Gross Profit]] benchmark: "GP is the oxygen of any business." If GP remains constant or rises as revenue increases, very good sign.
19. [HIGH] [[debt-to-equity|D/E]] benchmark: above 50% requires explanation. Companies with secure cash flows (toll roads) can tolerate higher D/E. Price-takers (resources) must operate with lower D/E.
20. [HIGH] [[current-ratio|Current Ratio]] benchmark: >2:1. [[quick-ratio|Quick Ratio]] benchmark: >1:1.
21. [HIGH] "Companies survive on cash" — ensure cash flow is positive

### Moats and Timing

22. [HIGH] Look for companies with significant [[economic-moat|barriers to entry]] (moat). "A moat that can last 20 years is a wide moat."
23. [HIGH] Timing matters: "Buy on dips when all stock prices fall. Stocks always overshoot on both upside and downside."
24. [HIGH] "In every boom a dominant theme emerges that provides scope for valuations to disengage with reality"

### Portfolio and Wealth Creation

25. [HIGH] Wealth creation formula: Y = C + I (Income = Consumption + Investment). "The lower C is the higher I becomes."
26. [HIGH] Two portfolio types: (a) long-term growth + dividends, (b) trading for capital gain ("gambling"). Ratio depends on age.
27. [HIGH] Borrow to invest: negative gearing, interest is tax deductible. "Debt, properly managed, is a great way to build wealth."
28. [HIGH] Hold infrastructure assets for defensive portfolio quality
29. [HIGH] Diversify across sectors and internationally
30. [HIGH] Rule of 72: divide 72 by annual return = years to double your money

### Case Study

- PFP case study: P/E of 40 too high for a funeral services company. Bought only 500 shares at $5.17 to start, planned to accumulate on P/E compression. "Patience and discipline are the key."
