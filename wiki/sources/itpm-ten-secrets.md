The original flagship ITPM video, filmed as an "ask me anything" format with Tom following [[anton-kreil]] on a business trip from Singapore to New York to London (circa 2015). Kreil discusses foundational financial principles including understanding money, respecting it while being indifferent to it, distinguishing assets from liabilities, building your own infrastructure, and the importance of risk management as priority #1. The video includes scenes of Kreil managing live portfolios while traveling, revealing operational aspects of how professional traders work. As promotional content for a paid trading-education business, the claims are first-person opinion and life-philosophy rather than independently verifiable fact; confidence is MEDIUM overall.

> **Note on confidence:** This is marketing material for a paid course. Claims reflect Kreil's stated views and recollections, not audited or third-party data. Treat the financial-philosophy claims as [MEDIUM] opinion.

## Key Claims

### Understanding the Function of Money

1. [MEDIUM] The two major problems most people have with money: (a) not understanding its function — why it exists, and (b) having emotional barriers toward money. (Source: [[anton-kreil]])

2. [MEDIUM] Money exists to solve the "double coincidence of wants" — it eliminates the barter economy. Without money, society literally would not function. Understanding this function leads to respecting money. (Source: [[anton-kreil]])

3. [MEDIUM] Common messaging about money ("money is the root of all evil," "money doesn't make you happy," "the more money you have the more problems you have") is misleading. Greed that harms others is the root of evil, not money itself. There is a "positive greed" — wanting to do better for yourself and your family. (Source: [[anton-kreil]])

4. [MEDIUM] Money is a commodity with a price. In debt markets, the price is interest. In equity markets, the price is equity stake. Capital markets are absolutely necessary — society would not function without them because capital must be transacted. (Source: [[anton-kreil]])

5. [MEDIUM] Enterprise has only two inputs: **capital** and **labor**. Both are commodities with prices (interest for capital, wages for labor). When labor takes too much of the share and capital gets squeezed, capital leaves — which is "not good for labor long term." (Source: [[anton-kreil]])

### Being Indifferent to Money

6. [MEDIUM] The "$20 bill experiment": Kreil puts increasing amounts of cash on the table ($20, $100, $1,000, $5,000) and observes increasing emotional response. Then asks: "What does the $20 think of you? Nothing — it's a piece of paper." All problems with money exist with the person, not the money. (Source: [[anton-kreil]])

7. [MEDIUM] The key is to be simultaneously respectful of money (having awareness of its function) AND indifferent to it (no emotional barriers regardless of the amount). This combination is "the key to starting on the road to becoming successful." (Source: [[anton-kreil]])

### Assets vs Liabilities

8. [MEDIUM] Most people confuse assets and liabilities. A mortgage is a major liability, not an asset — the bank owns the property until the final payment. The mortgage-holder ends up on a "scheduled payment hamster wheel" that is a trap. (Source: [[anton-kreil]])

9. [MEDIUM] Personal debt is fundamentally different from corporate debt. Corporate debt has limited liability (the company bears the risk). Personal debt (mortgages, credit cards, overdrafts, car loans) puts you personally at risk of insolvency. (Source: [[anton-kreil]])

10. [MEDIUM] "No wealthy people have liabilities. Wealthy people stick to the principle that they don't want to take on liabilities — they want to own assets." (Source: [[anton-kreil]])

11. [MEDIUM] Renting is an asset because it preserves freedom — you can leave the next month. Freedom is an asset. Buy everything for cash when you can. "Rent for 10-15 years, and even if the property price goes up, it doesn't matter — you had freedom over that period." (Source: [[anton-kreil]])

12. [MEDIUM] The infrastructure of the Western world where people borrow money to eventually own assets "isn't necessarily designed to benefit you — it's primarily designed to benefit the owners of the infrastructure." You must own your own infrastructure with no liabilities. (Source: [[anton-kreil]])

### Risk Management as Priority #1

13. [MEDIUM] Before traveling on business, Kreil's first calls are about risk management: reducing exposure by 40% across the board, putting a 30% hedge on the book, and selling S&P and DAX futures ($5M each). When going "off the grid," the right thing is to reduce overall and net exposure and hedge out portfolios. (Source: [[anton-kreil]], phone call to "Chris")

14. [MEDIUM] ITPM manages 40+ trading accounts. Off-the-grid means "checking once a day instead of 20 times a day." A well-defined process leads to live positions, after which "it's all about risk management." (Source: [[anton-kreil]])

15. [MEDIUM] When calling to review Institute traders' portfolios, Kreil asks for: full breakdown top-to-bottom, ensure exposures are in line, flag anyone over 4-5x leverage, and identify where unique risk lies. (Source: [[anton-kreil]], phone call to "Alan")

16. [MEDIUM] A professional trader's win ratio is "closer to 50/50 than you'd think" — but it's not like a basketball game, "it matters how much you win by." (Source: [[anton-kreil]])

### Building Your Own Infrastructure

17. [MEDIUM] The path from zero to owning assets requires: saving, having no liabilities, building your asset base, living well within your means. During this period, avoid signing up for liabilities based on a salary. (Source: [[anton-kreil]])

18. [MEDIUM] For explosive wealth, build your own businesses, reinvest profits into enduring investments that generate 10%+ annual returns, and compound. "It starts shallow, gets steeper, takes time, then goes exponential." (Source: [[anton-kreil]])

19. [MEDIUM] Pensions infrastructure has failed in the Western world. Average pension value at retirement equals approximately one year of national average salary. People cannot retire properly. Outsourcing pensions to third parties has produced "pathetic" returns. (Source: [[anton-kreil]])

20. [MEDIUM] Average national salaries (cited circa 2015): US $68,000, UK 28,000, Europe 26,000. "These are numbers that allow people to live on a month-to-month basis." (Source: [[anton-kreil]])

21. [MEDIUM] Insurance is a fear pitch — actuarial models ensure companies pay out on only 2-4% of policies per year. The real solution is to become wealthy enough to self-insure. (Source: [[anton-kreil]])

22. [MEDIUM] Credit reports reward debt compliance with bigger liabilities — "complete insanity." The road to success requires doing "the opposite of what everybody tells you to do." (Source: [[anton-kreil]])

### Singapore and Tax Efficiency

23. [MEDIUM] Kreil operates from Singapore for three reasons: (a) gateway to Asia and Southeast Asian demographics, (b) easiest place to do business in the world, (c) highest standard of living in the world. (Source: [[anton-kreil]])

24. [MEDIUM] Investment and portfolio management is run from a quiet, disturbance-free environment. High concentration and logical decision-making require peace and quiet — "lots of disturbances in the office creates an environment where you don't actually get anything done." (Source: [[anton-kreil]])

### Professional Trader Mindset

25. [MEDIUM] "If you become good at trading, it overflows and seeps into other areas of your life — you become really good at everything else." (Source: [[anton-kreil]])

26. [MEDIUM] "Common sense, wanting to do it, applying common sense, and being consistent. You don't have to reinvent the wheel to be successful in the financial markets." (Source: [[anton-kreil]])

27. [MEDIUM] "It's hard for someone to admit they were wrong by getting rid of a stock that was a loser. If you lose money on a trade, it's fine — it's just part of the overall game." (Source: [[anton-kreil]])

### ITPM Student Types

28. [MEDIUM] Two types of ITPM students: (a) older traders who have lost money learning from people with conflicts of interest and "always end up coming to us," and (b) young people wanting to enter the profession (investment bank or hedge fund) who need to learn properly first. Both share a common thread — wrong financial decisions and lack of proper examples. (Source: [[anton-kreil]])
