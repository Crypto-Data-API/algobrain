Combined source summary for the six-part university Q&A interview with [[anton-kreil]], covering his career trajectory from age 16 through goldman-sachs, lehman-brothers, and jp-morgan, his role in BBC's "Million Dollar Traders," the founding of itpm, and his trading philosophy. Early-era ITPM content (2013) with more biographical detail than later presentations. **Confidence note:** the career autobiography is entirely self-reported by Kreil and cannot be independently verified — these claims are marked [MEDIUM] (single-source, plausible, but uncorroborated). Kreil's general market-structure and trading-psychology observations are opinion ([MEDIUM]). Verifiable public facts (the existence and air dates of BBC's "Million Dollar Traders", the broad statistic that most retail traders lose money) are marked [HIGH].

## Key Claims

### Career Autobiography

1. [MEDIUM] Kreil began trading at age 16 in Liverpool (1994-95), flipping IPOs through a physical stockbroker with T+3 settlement. (Source: Kreil direct statement — self-reported)
2. [MEDIUM] At university (1997-2000), typical day: up at 6am for research, markets open at 8, positions on, attend lectures, flip at lunchtime. Caught the tech boom. (Source: Kreil direct statement — self-reported)
3. [MEDIUM] Got hired at Goldman Sachs by telling a trader at a campus presentation about his personal track record. Phone interview next day, in-person interviews next week, offered the job same day. (Source: Kreil direct statement — self-reported)
4. [MEDIUM] Goldman Sachs training: official program in New York lecture theatre + seat on Wall Street trading floor. "You don't really learn how to trade" there — real learning happens back on the desk. (Source: Kreil direct statement — self-reported)
5. [MEDIUM] Started at Goldman with $10M proprietary capital. Team of 4 grew to run ~$500M, approximately a quarter of the desk's total exposure. Traded TMT (technology, media, telecoms). (Source: Kreil direct statement — self-reported)
6. [MEDIUM] At Lehman Brothers (from 2004): colleagues went from $10-20M risk to $200-300M risk in two years — "to say it got out of hand is an understatement." This was one reason Kreil left. (Source: Kreil direct statement — self-reported)
7. [MEDIUM] At JP Morgan: $50-100M personal risk limit, with biggest runs at $200-300M. (Source: Kreil direct statement — self-reported)
8. [MEDIUM] Work-life balance: wake at 5am, taxi at 5:30, office by 5:50, markets close 4:35pm but trading US until 9-10pm, client nights 2-3 times/week, Asia calls at 2am. "Forget about work-life balance — it just didn't exist." (Source: Kreil direct statement — self-reported)
9. [MEDIUM] 8% of Goldman's original analyst program cohort remained at the company after 2 years. (Source: Kreil direct statement — self-reported figure)
10. [MEDIUM] Retired May 2007, sold London property, traveled for 14 months. Correctly anticipated market downturn ("being short when everyone's long, you don't get paid"). (Source: Kreil direct statement — self-reported)

### Goldman Sachs Culture

11. [MEDIUM] Goldman Sachs was "head and shoulders above anybody else" in culture — strong work ethic, intelligence, curiosity, and entrepreneurial innovation. (Source: Kreil direct statement — opinion, self-described bias)
12. [MEDIUM] The broader City culture shifted during Kreil's career from "working for the company" to "a political culture of greed" with untalented people in important positions. (Source: Kreil direct statement — opinion)
13. [MEDIUM] Post-crisis, talented professionals migrated to hedge funds, independent trading, or emerging/frontier markets — "what's left is the dead weight." (Source: Kreil direct statement — opinion)

### Information Advantage and Market Structure

14. [MEDIUM] Traditional argument: investment banks have order-flow advantage. Reality from the inside: "they think everybody else has the advantage" because hedge funds were "hot money" that knew things before the banks. (Source: Kreil direct statement — opinion/anecdote)
15. [MEDIUM] Order-flow advantage is now largely gone because "everything goes in a machine" — electronic execution and dark pools have eliminated the old information edge. (Source: Kreil direct statement — opinion, broadly consistent with market-structure trends)
16. [MEDIUM] "What's great about the internet is all the information is actually there. You can get it all for very cheap or free. But what's more important is knowing how to interpret that information." (Source: Kreil direct statement — opinion)

### Trading Philosophy

17. [MEDIUM] Humans are "very bad traders" because human psychology is the opposite of how you should trade. Success requires suppressing natural emotional responses over many years of practice. (Source: Kreil direct statement — opinion, consistent with [[behavioral-finance]])
18. [MEDIUM] Computers have destroyed short-term opportunities but "can't make rational decisions" in extreme events. "We're still yet to see whether a computer can trade through a stock market crash and make money." (Source: Kreil direct statement — opinion, dated 2013)
19. [MEDIUM] Approach: 1-3 month time horizon, not day trading. "I don't need to worry about the machines on a minute-to-minute basis." (Source: Kreil direct statement — stated method)
20. [MEDIUM] Technical analysis is "not unimportant" — it serves as a "head check" overlay on fundamental-analysis. When fundamentals say buy but technicals say wait, the idea goes on the watch list. "Then all the planets are aligned." (Source: Kreil direct statement — stated method)
21. [MEDIUM] Personal portfolio allocation: 65% equities (most volatile), 20% commodities, 10% forex, remainder in cash/margin reserve. (Source: Kreil direct statement — self-reported)
22. [MEDIUM] Equities indices are 1.5-2x more volatile than major currency pairs historically (50+ years of data). (Source: Kreil direct statement — figure cited without source; directionally plausible)

### Retail Trading Reality

23. [MEDIUM] 80-90% of retail traders lose money (broadly corroborated by broker disclosures and regulator data — that portion is [HIGH]); the ITPM community figure of 60-65% of accounts profitable at time of recording is self-reported and unverified ([MEDIUM]). (Source: Kreil direct statement with community data)
24. [MEDIUM] Minimum 12-18 months of track record before anyone can credibly say "you're actually pretty good at trading" and offer outside capital. (Source: Kreil direct statement — opinion)
25. [MEDIUM] Win ratio target: 50-55% over 100+ trades in 12 months, with good behavior metrics in losing months. (Source: Kreil direct statement — stated target)
26. [MEDIUM] What retail traders get taught by brokers and educators is "the reverse of what professional traders do" — likely by design from "the big boss" of the brokerage. (Source: Kreil direct statement — opinion/speculation)

### BBC Million Dollar Traders

27. [HIGH] Concept of BBC's [[million-dollar-traders]]: train ordinary people for two weeks, give them ~$1M to trade during the 2008 market environment. Objective: prove anyone can trade with the right instruction, but not everyone will succeed. (The show's existence and premise are verifiable public record; Kreil was a trainer on it.) (Source: Kreil direct statement + public record)
28. [HIGH] Filmed over ~10 weeks in 2008, aired on BBC Two in January 2009. Kreil interviewed and trained participants. (Show air date is verifiable public record.) (Source: Kreil direct statement + public record)
