[[fred-mcnaught|Fred McNaught's]] original outline for what became the [[alfred-report-methodology|Alfred 1-Page Stock Report]] format. This document defines the core metrics that Fred considers essential for evaluating any stock on a single page — the template that [[alfred|Alfred's]] automated reports are built upon.

## Key Claims

1. [HIGH] Essential 1-page report contents:
   - **Price** — Current share price
   - **EPS** — going up = good
   - **ROE** — compare vs interest rates + risk margin
   - **Dividends** — paying? up? franked? DRP available?
   - **D/E Ratio** — <50%, stable/down, balance sheet cash, current liability ratio
   - **P/E** — vs competitors and sector average
   - **Revenue growth** — trending direction
   - **Red flags** — anything concerning
   - **News** — recent material developments
   - **Commentary/recommendation** — overall assessment

## Significance

This template is the direct ancestor of [[alfred-report-methodology|Alfred's 1-Pager format]]. Every metric Fred listed here appears in Alfred's automated reports, with the addition of quantitative scoring, verdict categories (UNDERVALUED/BUY, FAIR VALUE/HOLD, OVERPRICED/WATCHLIST, OVERVALUED), and expanded benchmarking. The evolution from Fred's outline to Alfred's implementation represents the codification of decades of investing experience into a repeatable analytical framework.
