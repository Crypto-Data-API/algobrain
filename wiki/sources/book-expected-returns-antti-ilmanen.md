*Expected Returns: An Investor's Guide to Harvesting Market Rewards* by Antti Ilmanen (Wiley, 2011) is a comprehensive reference on return sources across asset classes. Covers historical returns, risk premia (equity, bond, credit, volatility, illiquidity), factor investing, and forward-looking return estimation. Written from the perspective of a practitioner at Brevan Howard (and formerly at Salomon Brothers/LTCM), the book bridges academic asset pricing theory and institutional portfolio construction. A foundational text for [[asset-allocation]] and [[risk-management-overview|risk management]].

## Key Claims

1. [HIGH] Long-run equity risk premium has historically been approximately 3-5% over government bonds, but forward-looking estimates should be lower than the historical average due to repricing effects and survivorship bias in US data.
2. [HIGH] **Risk premia are the primary source of long-run returns.** Ilmanen decomposes returns into: equity premium, term premium, credit premium, illiquidity premium, volatility risk premium, and insurance premium.
3. [HIGH] **Factor investing** — value, momentum, carry, and defensive/low-volatility — provides diversified sources of return that are partially independent of traditional market beta.
4. [HIGH] **Momentum** is the most robust empirical anomaly across asset classes and time periods, present in equities, bonds, currencies, and commodities.
5. [HIGH] **Carry** strategies (buying high-yielding assets, selling low-yielding ones) earn positive returns on average but are exposed to crash risk — they effectively sell insurance.
6. [MEDIUM] **Mean reversion** in valuations (e.g., Shiller CAPE for equities) is a useful predictor of long-horizon returns (10+ years) but has poor short-horizon timing ability.
7. [HIGH] **Diversification** is the primary free lunch in investing — combining weakly correlated risk premia improves risk-adjusted returns more reliably than concentrating in the best single premium.
8. [HIGH] The **volatility risk premium** (implied volatility exceeding realised volatility) is one of the most reliable return sources, reflecting investors' structural demand for insurance/hedging.
9. [MEDIUM] Forward-looking return estimation should weight current valuations, structural economic factors, and the return environment more heavily than historical averages.
10. [HIGH] **Illiquidity premium** is real but hard to harvest — investors overestimate their ability to tolerate illiquidity during stress periods, and illiquidity premia tend to spike precisely when liquidity is needed most.
