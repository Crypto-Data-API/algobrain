Documentary-style ITPM (Institute of Trading and Portfolio Management) marketing video following [[anton-kreil]] across conferences in Poland and London, featuring interviews with ITPM mentors [[chris-cathey]] (ex-goldman-sachs) and [[ben-berggreen|Ben Berggreen]]. It centers on the role of mentorship, emotional detachment in trading, and the shifting information advantage from institutional to retail traders. As promotional content for a paid trading-education business, its claims are largely first-person opinion and experiential anecdote — useful as a record of the ITPM teaching philosophy rather than as independently verifiable fact. Confidence is therefore MEDIUM overall, with individual claims marked below.

> **Note on confidence:** This is marketing material for a paid course. Claims reflect the presenters' stated views and recollections, not audited or third-party-verified data. Treat trading-philosophy claims as [MEDIUM] opinion and biographical claims as [MEDIUM] single-source.

## Key Claims

### Conference Teaching — Core Principles

1. [MEDIUM] Professional traders and hedge fund managers "have systematic processes, realistic expectations, realistic targets, and they aim to get rich slowly." (Source: Kreil seminar clip)
2. [MEDIUM] Day 1 of the ITPM conference framework: learn "how not to screw things up." Day 2: learn "how to actually go and make money." Two-step process. (Source: Kreil seminar clip)
3. [MEDIUM] Implementation with real money takes "a good 6 to 12 months" before results become consistent. (Source: Kreil seminar clip)
4. [MEDIUM] Forex approach: focus on fundamental drivers, not short-term technical/price action. "Retail are totally missing fundamental analysis because they're so focused on short-term trading and technical and price action trading." (Source: Kreil seminar clip)
5. [MEDIUM] "All the noise coming to you every day in the market through your email, through your phone — it's all bullshit and noise." The fundamental framework "tells you everything you need to know whether there's risk and opportunity in an asset class." (Source: Kreil seminar clip)

### The Importance of Mentorship

6. [MEDIUM] Mentoring in trading is "no different to any other industry" — you need experienced people who have "been there, done it, bought the t-shirt" to provide guidance. (Source: Kreil discussion)
7. [MEDIUM] Two dimensions of mentorship in trading: (1) understanding how the industry operates, and (2) guidance in the details of becoming a successful trader. (Source: Kreil discussion)
8. [MEDIUM] The most critical lesson from mentors: how to "eliminate emotion" from trading and achieve "emotional detachment" from P&L. (Source: Kreil discussion)

### Emotional Detachment — Key Philosophy

9. [MEDIUM] "Money is unemotional and you have to be unemotional about your trading decisions and your P&L." If you achieve emotional detachment, you achieve consistency through both wins and losses. (Source: Kreil discussion)
10. [MEDIUM] "The moment you press the button when you trade, you are losing" — because you buy at the offer and are marked on the bid (and vice versa for shorts). "Losses are just a part of the game and you just have to accept that." (Source: Kreil discussion)
11. [MEDIUM] Chris Cathey was Kreil's mentor at Goldman Sachs starting in 2000 — "he really nailed it into me how to eliminate your emotions from trading." (Source: Kreil direct statement)

### Chris Cathey Interview — Risk Management

12. [MEDIUM] "Controlling emotions is absolutely essential to being a successful trader. You need to increase and decrease risk appropriately and make the right decision at the right time. That only happens if you're objective." (Source: Cathey direct statement)
13. [MEDIUM] "The natural human nature is to take profits too quickly and to cut losers too late. We teach the reverse — cut losers quickly and let profits run — which is counterintuitive to human nature." (Source: Cathey direct statement)
14. [MEDIUM] You cannot learn emotional control through paper trading — "you need to have some skin in the game." Only real money creates the emotional rollercoaster you must learn to manage. (Source: Cathey direct statement)
15. [MEDIUM] Trading is 50% idea generation (academic component) and 50% [[risk-management-overview|risk management]] (practical, emotional component). (Source: Cathey direct statement)
16. [MEDIUM] When stopped out: "you are admitting to yourself that you are wrong... that is very very difficult to learn how to do." Once mastered, that is where emotion leaves trading. (Source: Cathey direct statement)

### Information Advantage Shifting

17. [MEDIUM] The information advantage has shifted from professional to retail traders. Professionals are at a disadvantage due to "big processes" and needing to "move a lot more size." Small retail traders "can really do well" — but need proper education. (Source: Cathey direct statement)
18. [MEDIUM] In the 1990s, retail investors had virtually no information access. Now "the advantage is actually leaning towards the retail trader" — but with added potential for loss without proper training. (Source: Cathey direct statement)
