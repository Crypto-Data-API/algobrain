## Historical Boom/Bust Cycles

- [MEDIUM] 1970s uranium boom driven by cost-of-living crisis and nuclear buildout — US went from fewer than 20 to 75 reactors. Current conditions drawing comparisons. (Source: [[alfred-report-uranium-commodity]])
- [HIGH] 2007 bubble to $140 USD/oz caused by Cigar Lake mine flood. Followed by 2008 GFC crash and 2011 Fukushima disaster, which crushed uranium prices for a decade. (Source: [[alfred-report-uranium-commodity]])
- [HIGH] Price performance comparable to [[bitcoin]] — extreme booms and busts. (Source: [[alfred-report-uranium-commodity]])

## Energy Alternatives Comparison

- [MEDIUM] Thorium: more abundant but harder to process into fuel. (Source: [[alfred-report-uranium-commodity]])
- [MEDIUM] Wind: approximately 200 turbines needed to match output of 1 nuclear reactor. (Source: [[alfred-report-uranium-commodity]])
- [MEDIUM] Solar: cheaper per unit but only generates approximately 30% of the day. (Source: [[alfred-report-uranium-commodity]])
- [MEDIUM] Hydro: reliable but limited to suitable geographic locations. (Source: [[alfred-report-uranium-commodity]])
- [MEDIUM] Fossil fuels: millions of premature deaths attributed to air pollution annually. (Source: [[alfred-report-uranium-commodity]])
- [MEDIUM] Nuclear has the highest energy output per land area required and the lowest CO2 emissions among baseload sources. 89 days of solar production approximately equals 341 days of nuclear production. (Source: [[alfred-report-uranium-commodity]])
