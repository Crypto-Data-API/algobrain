A focused article by [[fred-mcnaught|Fred McNaught]] explaining [[gross-profit|gross profit]], [[operating-income|operating income]], and [[net-income|net income]] — including how terminology differs between Australia and the United States, the profit hierarchy from revenue to net income, and which margin metric matters most depending on business type.

## Key Claims

### Terminology

1. [HIGH] In Australia it's called Gross Profit, in America it's called Gross Margin. USA uses "Gross Profit" for the dollar amount and "Gross Margin" for the percentage.

### Formulas and Hierarchy

2. [HIGH] Formula: Gross Profit = Total Revenue - COGS
3. [HIGH] Profit hierarchy: Revenue --> Gross Profit (minus direct costs) --> Operating Income (minus indirect costs/overhead) --> Net Income (minus interest and taxes)

### Benchmarks and Analysis

4. [HIGH] "Gross profit is the oxygen of any business" — look for companies that maximise GP
5. [HIGH] Benchmarks: higher GP is better, compare to similar companies in same sector, if GP remains constant or rises as revenue increases = very good sign
6. [HIGH] Which margin matters depends on business type: high operating expenses --> focus on Operating Profit Margin; high tax/interest costs --> focus on Net Profit Margin
7. [HIGH] If Operating Margin is close to Gross Profit Margin = business has low direct and indirect production costs

### Catalysts

8. [HIGH] Catalysts affecting gross margin: cost of materials/labour/manufacturing, production efficiency, pricing changes, new products, competition, market demand, supply chain issues, regulation changes, returns/warranty claims
