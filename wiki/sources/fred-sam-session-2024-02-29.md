Fred and Sam discuss the bull case for [[gold]] and [[silver]], driven by US debt fears and central bank buying (particularly China). Fred outlines his approach to gold as a trade rather than a long-term hold (except [[evolution-mining|EVN]] which pays dividends), the potential effects of US dollar devaluation on global markets, Bitcoin vs. gold as a store of value, and Ray Dalio's 15 uncorrelated positions framework. Fred shares a Barclays historical study showing gold merely keeps pace with inflation over centuries.

## Key Claims

### Gold Price Prediction and Buying Thesis

1. [MEDIUM] A US-based research firm ($75 subscription) predicts gold could reach $15,000/oz, based on massive global debt and US dollar fears. Fred does not endorse this specific target but is increasing his gold position.

2. [HIGH] Fred's gold thesis: demand is rising due to (a) central bank purchases, especially China (both publicly and through third parties), (b) industrial use in electronics and devices is increasing, (c) Chinese and Indian individuals buying heavily, (d) gold now sold at Costco and Walmart in the US. Meanwhile, world gold production is reportedly declining and new supply takes 5-8 years to bring online.

3. [HIGH] All-in production cost for gold mines is approximately $1,000 USD per ounce (up from $600 historically, then $800). If a mine produces under $1,000/oz all-in cost, it has a pretty good mine.

4. [HIGH] Gold price is not set by production cost — it is set by supply and demand. Cost rising does not automatically push price up; demand must increase.

5. [MEDIUM] Fred paid $75 to a US research firm (Stansberry) that recommended SAND (Sandstorm Gold, NYSE) for gold/silver exposure.

### Gold as a Trade, Not a Hold

6. [HIGH] At a sports bar in Singapore, a Barclays Asia Pacific manager shared their historical investment study (going back to the late 1700s): gold bought and held merely kept pace with inflation over centuries. It maintained purchasing power but generated no real return. Fred: "Fuck me, that's interesting. I will never buy gold and hold it. It's a trade."

7. [HIGH] The exception is [[evolution-mining|EVN]], which Fred holds long-term because it pays a yearly dividend — making it function like a real stock rather than a pure gold play. Fred got inside information from a friend who was the CEO, and the board committed to a strategy of paying annual dividends unlike other gold miners.

8. [HIGH] Warren Buffett's gold argument: fill a room with gold bars — you pay rent, storage, security, and your return is nil. You could buy productive businesses with the same capital instead.

### Silver

9. [MEDIUM] The historical gold-to-silver ratio has averaged around 14:1 over the long term, but was at approximately 60-65:1 at the time of discussion (down from a peak of 90:1). Fred's research firm predicts silver will be dragged up toward the long-term average ratio.

10. [HIGH] Silver historically determined the wealth of nations — all Chinese trade was in silver going back hundreds of years. The Chinese would not export unless paid in silver of their standard quality.

11. [MEDIUM] Fred's conclusion from earlier research: the majority of his precious metals allocation should be in silver because there is more upside potential relative to gold.

### Physical Gold/Silver vs. Digital Assets

12. [HIGH] Physical gold has storage, security, and resale problems. When Sam tried to sell brand-new Perth Mint gold (in original packaging with certificates), the broker offered only 75% of spot price, calling it "secondhand."

13. [MEDIUM] Bitcoin is a "pristine asset" compared to physical gold — you can transfer it without it becoming secondhand, and you can get very close to spot price when selling.

### US Debt Crisis

14. [HIGH] In the year 2000, total US debt accumulated over 200+ years was $8 trillion. By December 31, 2023, it exceeded $100 trillion (including all obligations). Biden ran up $1 trillion of debt in 160 days.

15. [HIGH] The US can keep running deficits as long as people are prepared to buy US Treasuries. If buyers demand higher yields, rates must rise to attract them — potentially reaching the 18% levels seen in the 1970s-80s.

16. [HIGH] There was a "virtuous circle": China sent goods to America, America paid in dollars, China used those dollars to buy US Treasuries, sending money back. Trump disrupted this by attacking the trade deficit, and the cycle is now breaking down.

17. [MEDIUM] Fred considers it possible (not certain) that the US dollar could collapse — not gradually over decades but "quickly and aggressively" when it happens. "Things will be going as per normal, no one's worried, until down it goes."

### Effects of US Dollar Devaluation

18. [HIGH] If the USD devalues: US exporters win (Microsoft, Amazon, Caterpillar) because their goods become cheaper for other countries. Losers: American consumers face higher import costs, real estate gets bought up by foreigners at cheap prices, and inflation spirals. "On balance, you are always better off having a strong currency."

19. [HIGH] If gold price rises due to USD devaluation: those who bought gold beforehand maintain their relative purchasing power. The 80% of Americans with no hedge see their standard of living drop as everything imported becomes more expensive.

20. [HIGH] Fred holds US tech stocks despite USD risk because: "Where are you going to find a Microsoft in Australia? The US government may be fucked, but not the US businesses." These companies will continue through thick and thin because of excellent management.

### Bitcoin and Ethereum

21. [MEDIUM] Fred holds approximately 0.75 Bitcoin in his self-managed super fund. He has been surprised by Bitcoin's price resilience around $97-100K while other assets declined.

22. [MEDIUM] Fred on Bitcoin as "digital gold": "When you look at the figures, it doesn't substantiate it." There should be correlation between Bitcoin and the real world, but there is none — Bitcoin becomes a "fear of missing out" momentum play.

23. [MEDIUM] Fred on Ethereum: "The price should not be over just under $4,000, yet it went to $6,000." The yield on staking is tiny, and beyond that it is "momentum and hope."

24. [HIGH] Bitcoin ETFs have been a price support mechanism. BlackRock and others accumulating Bitcoin through ETFs (4% of all Bitcoin already in ETFs), with all inflows and no significant outflows, creates sustained buying pressure.

### Ray Dalio's 15 Uncorrelated Positions

25. [HIGH] Ray Dalio stated on Bloomberg that the optimal risk-minimized portfolio holds 15 uncorrelated positions. Fred interprets this as diversification across asset classes: tech stocks, gold, real estate (residential and commercial), Bitcoin, infrastructure, US Treasury bonds, data centers, etc.

26. [MEDIUM] Fred's own diversification includes: shares, real estate, infrastructure (Macquarie, Rio, Transurban/Magellan), and the Aberdeen Asian Development Fund (running approximately 8% per year, doubling every 9 years).

### Interest Rates and Stagflation

27. [HIGH] Stagflation (inflation + no growth + high unemployment) is the worst-case scenario. It happened in the 1970s (US interest rates reached 15%, Australian rates reached 18%, unemployment 10%). Fred: "That's a bad time for everyone."

28. [HIGH] The best way out of stagflation is to "move heaven and earth to increase productivity."

29. [MEDIUM] Fred does not believe the Fed will voluntarily cut rates before the 2024 US election (last announcement is September, 5 days before). "Politics now is playing a much, much, much larger role in the economy than any time in my lifetime."

30. [HIGH] Fred's portfolio strategy against macro uncertainty: "Buy shares that are not going to be affected least by the macroeconomic conditions." Hold cash for downturns. Gold is an inflation hedge. Infrastructure assets (toll roads, water, airports) are more protected than other assets.
