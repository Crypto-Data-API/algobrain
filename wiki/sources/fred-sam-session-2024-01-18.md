Fred McNaught expanding on investing concepts in 2 solo clips. Covers wealth creation through compounding, the two-portfolio strategy (core + trading), borrowing to invest, and specific holdings philosophy. Deepens content from [[fred-sam-session-2024-01-02]].

## Key Claims

### Wealth Creation

1. [HIGH] Time-proven method: combine regular savings/investment with compounding returns as a long-term investor (Source: Clip 1)
2. [HIGH] "The lower your consumption and the higher your investment, the more wealthy you will become" (Source: Clip 1)

### Two Portfolio Strategy

3. [HIGH] Two types of portfolios: (1) long-term growth and dividends, (2) short-term capital gain (sometimes called "gambling") (Source: Clip 1)
4. [HIGH] Allocation between portfolios depends on age — while young, trading portfolio can be somewhat high; as you get older, it must be reduced and investments revert to portfolio one (Source: Clip 1)

### Borrowing to Invest

5. [HIGH] If starting out with no parental support and lacking funds, borrowing is one way to overcome that deficiency (Source: Clip 1)
6. [HIGH] Can borrow from bank, stockbroker, or credit providers — must make informed choice on provider and be careful about which stocks to buy with borrowed funds (Source: Clip 1)
7. [HIGH] [[negative-gearing]] applies: interest on borrowed funds can be less than dividends received, and dividends carry [[franking-credits|franking credits]] (Source: Clip 1)
8. [HIGH] "Debt properly managed and understood is a great way to build your wealth. Debt, along with compound interest, may be the eighth and ninth wonder of the world." (Source: Clip 1)

### Portfolio Construction Principles

9. [HIGH] Hold infrastructure assets — they add defensive quality and in any market fall should fall much less than equities due to better cash flow (Source: Clip 2)
10. [HIGH] Allocate some capital to gold stocks — can make money over time providing you trade them up and down (Source: Clip 2)
11. [HIGH] Go with value, not momentum when purchasing (Source: Clip 2)
12. [HIGH] Demergers always worth a closer look — Coles from Wesfarmers example: "coal stocks have performed much, much better than anticipated" due to greater management focus and available capital (Source: Clip 2)
13. [HIGH] Predictable earnings deliver more consistent returns over time — compounding of those returns ensures a more wealthy future (Source: Clip 2)
14. [HIGH] Diversify within portfolio and choose sectors offering best growth opportunities (Source: Clip 2)
15. [HIGH] Important to diversify outside Australia — carries currency risk but potential for greater returns. Fred invests in US via SelfWealth (Source: Clip 2)
16. [HIGH] Fred does not hedge currency — "unpredictable and expensive" — if not a seller, can choose time to sell when currency suits (Source: Clip 2)
