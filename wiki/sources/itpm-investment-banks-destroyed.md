Combined source summary for the five-part "Investment Banks Destroyed" university lecture series by [[anton-kreil]], delivered at UCL. Kreil — former [[goldman-sachs]], [[lehman-brothers]], and [[jp-morgan]] trader — deconstructs the investment banking career path, compares it to hedge fund careers and independent trading, and quantifies the [[opportunity-cost]] of each pathway. **Confidence note:** the compensation figures, time-allocation percentages, and structural ratios are Kreil's own assertions (some described as cross-checked with managing directors) — directionally plausible but single-source and unverified, marked [MEDIUM]. The 10-year wealth projections are explicitly *spreadsheet models* with chosen assumptions (e.g. 20% net returns, 5x leverage for the independent-trading path) and are marked [MEDIUM] as illustrative, not forecasts. Generic, independently-verifiable facts (the standard hedge-fund "2-and-20" fee structure) are [HIGH].

## Key Claims

### Investment Bank Trader Function

1. [MEDIUM] 90% of a professional trader's time at an [[investment-banking|investment bank]] is spent [[market-making]], not proprietary trading. (Source: [[anton-kreil]] direct statement — assertion)
2. [MEDIUM] Of that 90%, approximately 80% is agency business (monitoring [[algorithmic-trading|algorithms]] executing client orders) and 20% is risk business (committing capital to clients). (Source: Kreil direct statement — assertion)
3. [MEDIUM] 72% of a trader's day at an investment bank is spent monitoring algorithms — "there is no human skill in market making anymore." (Source: Kreil direct statement — assertion)
4. [MEDIUM] The remaining 18% of market-making time is "putting out fires" — unwinding unwanted positions from the "negative selection portfolio." (Source: Kreil direct statement — assertion)
5. [MEDIUM] Only ~10% of an investment bank trader's time is spent on [[proprietary-trading]], running [[long-short-equity]] portfolios with 1-3 month time horizons. (Source: Kreil direct statement — assertion)

### Commission Decline and Structural Change

6. [MEDIUM] Typical secondary-market equity commission has dropped from ~25 basis points to ~5 basis points over the prior decade — an 80% decline. (Source: Kreil direct statement — assertion; long-run commission compression is broadly real but the exact figures are unverified)
7. [MEDIUM] This 80% commission decline has cascaded across the entire investment bank: prop capital allocations down ~80%, bonuses down ~80%, headcount down ~80%. (Source: Kreil direct statement — assertion/extrapolation)
8. [MEDIUM] Junior traders in 2000 had $250M proprietary capital; by the time of recording, senior traders were "lucky to get $50M." (Source: Kreil direct statement — self-reported)

### Investment Bank Structure

9. [MEDIUM] For every 1 profit-center employee on the secondary market side, there are 4 cost-center support employees (technology, compliance, operations). (Source: Kreil direct statement — assertion)
10. [MEDIUM] The research analyst function is effectively dead as a profit center — clients no longer pay for research directly or through execution. (Source: Kreil direct statement — opinion; consistent with post-MiFID II research unbundling trends)
11. [MEDIUM] Hedge fund sales is the first real profit center at the investment bank; sales traders are viewed as "order clerks" and distribution extensions of the trader. (Source: Kreil direct statement — opinion)

### Compensation Realities

12. [MEDIUM] Financial Analyst (FA) starting salary: £40K basic + £5-10K bonus. These are 2-year contractor positions (not permanent). 10,000 applicants per trading position. (Source: Kreil, stated as verified with 3 managing directors at top banks — single-source, anecdotal cross-check)
13. [MEDIUM] Associate (promoted after 2 years, ~20% make it): £80K basic + £50-100K bonus. Stock portion begins at ~15-20%, locked up for 5 years. Resignation forfeits unvested stock. (Source: Kreil direct statement — assertion)
14. [MEDIUM] VP/Executive Director (after 6-8 years): £200K basic + £125K bonus. Stock lockup increases, resignation penalty continues. (Source: Kreil direct statement — assertion)
15. [MEDIUM] After tax at VP level: ~£8,300/month cash flow + ~£50K annual bonus deposit. "You can live on it, but you'd live a basic life" in central London. (Source: Kreil direct statement — illustrative)
16. [MEDIUM] Senior Managing Director: £500K basic + £1M bonus. Probability of reaching this level is "close to zero." (Source: Kreil direct statement — assertion)
17. [MEDIUM] Employees cannot have personal trading accounts — track records are owned by the bank and cannot be used for external employment. (Source: Kreil direct statement — broadly consistent with bank personal-account-dealing rules)

### Hedge Fund Alternative

18. [MEDIUM] Hedge fund portfolio managers spend 100% of their time on positive selection portfolios — no market making. (Source: Kreil direct statement — assertion)
19. [MEDIUM] Typical $1B hedge fund: 25 long + 25 short positions, 2% position limit, targeting 20% return with 15% volatility. (Source: Kreil direct statement — stylized example)
20. [MEDIUM] The "1-2% management fee + ~20% performance fee" structure is the standard hedge-fund model (that fee convention is independently verifiable — [HIGH]); the claim that managers reinvest at least 50% of performance fees / "trade their own money" is Kreil's assertion ([MEDIUM]). (Source: Kreil direct statement + general industry knowledge)
21. [MEDIUM] Hedge fund applications ratio: 100-200 per position (vs. 10,000:1 at investment banks) because "no one has heard of them." (Source: Kreil direct statement — assertion)
22. [MEDIUM] $1B hedge fund career: Junior analyst (£100K) -> Junior PM (£200K + £250K bonus) -> Senior PM (£500K + £2M+ bonus). (Source: Kreil direct statement — illustrative)

### Opportunity Cost Quantified

23. [MEDIUM] 10-year inflation-adjusted wealth from investment banking career (bullish case): £340K — "cannot buy a one-bedroom apartment in central London." (Source: Kreil spreadsheet model — assumption-dependent illustration)
24. [MEDIUM] 10-year wealth from $1B hedge fund career (standard case): £5.7M. (Source: Kreil spreadsheet model — assumption-dependent illustration)
25. [MEDIUM] 10-year wealth from independent trading (20% net annual return, 5x leverage, starting £25K): potential £25M. (Source: Kreil spreadsheet model — relies on a sustained 20% net return with 5x leverage, a heroic assumption; illustrative only)
26. [MEDIUM] "Tipping point 1" for independent traders: after 2-3 years, personal trading income matches FA salary, and "they don't interview you — you interview them." (Source: Kreil direct statement — opinion)
27. [MEDIUM] "Tipping point 2": first £1M from trading at approximately 63 months. (Source: Kreil spreadsheet model — assumption-dependent illustration)

### Career Guidance

28. [MEDIUM] Professional leverage norms: equities 4-5x, commodities 4-5x, currencies 6-8x. Retail 100x forex leverage is "for stupid retail traders." (Source: Kreil direct statement — assertion/opinion; retail forex leverage caps are now regulated well below 100x in many jurisdictions)
29. [MEDIUM] The single best way to bypass HR filtering at banks: have a personal trading track record. HR "don't understand what it means" and must send CVs to the trading desk. (Source: Kreil direct statement — opinion/advice)
30. [MEDIUM] "If you go to an investment bank, stay for two years max to learn infrastructure, then leave. After two years, you're done — you're not getting out for a very long time." (Source: Kreil direct statement — opinion/advice)
31. [MEDIUM] Best career path: trade for yourself -> go to hedge fund, or trade for yourself forever while earning an income elsewhere. (Source: Kreil direct statement — opinion/advice)
32. [MEDIUM] ITPM pathway: Professional Trading Masterclass (PTM) education -> implementation via mentoring/remote trading desk -> managed account structure or hedge fund placement. (Source: promotional segment)
