A London seminar presented by [[anton-kreil]] (May 2018) that distills the underlying principles behind the "10 Secrets to Achieve Financial Success" video. Kreil identifies five foundational principles — Acceptance, Self-Determination, Freedom, Risk IQ, and Hierarchy — that underpin the ten secrets. This is primarily a financial philosophy and life-framework presentation rather than a trading methodology video, but it contains critical thinking about risk assessment, opportunity cost, and building financial infrastructure that directly applies to trading decisions. **Confidence note:** the bulk of the content is Kreil's personal opinion, ideology, and argument (the merits of capitalism, why mortgages and pensions are bad deals, tax-jurisdiction recommendations). These are marked [MEDIUM] as single-source opinion regardless of how forcefully asserted; ideological/speculative assertions are marked [LOW]. Only independently verifiable items (a sourced quote, a published statistic, a statutory fact) are marked [HIGH]. The page documents what Kreil argues, not endorsed financial advice.

## Key Claims

### Principle 1: Acceptance

1. [LOW] Capitalism combined with democracy and freedom, while not perfect, is "the most effective system in the history of mankind for raising living standards" — proven by 200+ years of history. (Source: [[anton-kreil]] — ideological opinion)

2. [HIGH] Milton Friedman quote cited: "The record of history is absolutely crystal clear that there is no alternative way so far discovered of improving the lot of ordinary people that can hold a candle to the productive activities that are unleashed by a free enterprise system." (Source: [[anton-kreil]], citing [[milton-friedman]])

3. [HIGH] Global GDP was $79.2 trillion in 2017, growing at ~3.5% annually for the prior 6 years. Whether GDP expands or contracts doesn't matter if you know how to make money in both directions. The real question is: "How much do you want?" (Source: [[anton-kreil]])

4. [MEDIUM] Acceptance means accepting that the capitalist system works, embracing it, and operating successfully within it by understanding its advantages and disadvantages. Fighting the system guarantees you "never become wealthy." (Source: [[anton-kreil]] — opinion)

5. [LOW] Traveling extensively is essential for gaining perspective on which economic systems succeed and which fail. Without travel, you have "no perspective whatsoever" on the system you live in. (Source: [[anton-kreil]] — opinion)

6. [MEDIUM] Buying your "dream life" for cash (property in your ideal location) removes all personal risk and creates enormous negotiating leverage. "Nine times out of ten you get what you want" because the counterparty detects no weakness. (Source: [[anton-kreil]] — opinion)

7. [MEDIUM] Western education systems (public and largely private) fail to provide financial or business education because "the people in the system have never done it." Teachers overwhelmingly lack entrepreneurial success and often come from failed personal financial situations. (Source: [[anton-kreil]] — opinion)

8. [LOW] Kreil's own path: grew up in Liverpool (90-95% left-wing, anti-capitalist, high welfare dependency, crime). Resolution: "Get on a train and leave. Physically remove yourself." (Source: [[anton-kreil]] — self-reported, unverifiable demographic characterization)

### Principle 2: Self-Determination

9. [MEDIUM] Most people confuse assets and liabilities. A mortgage is a **liability**, not an asset. You own nothing until the final payment of 360 months — the bank owns the property. (Source: [[anton-kreil]] — opinion/framing)

10. [MEDIUM] Mortgage math example (US, repayment): $500K property, $75K deposit, $425K mortgage at 3.75% over 30 years = $708K total paid. If property is worth $1M at end, net gain is $291K — but you surrendered your freedom for 30 years. (Source: [[anton-kreil]] — illustrative calculation)

11. [MEDIUM] **Opportunity cost of capital**: The $75K deposit compounded at 10% per year grows to $1.3M over 30 years. The property would have to be worth $1.8M at the end of 30 years to equal the opportunity cost of not investing the deposit elsewhere. (Source: [[anton-kreil]] — illustrative calculation assuming a 10% return)

12. [MEDIUM] Renting + investing the deposit at 10%: Even after paying $600K in rent over 30 years, you end up $708K richer than the mortgage scenario, with 100% freedom maintained. (Source: [[anton-kreil]] — illustrative calculation)

13. [MEDIUM] "Passive income" from buy-to-let properties is a lie — landlords work constantly on properties. For genuine passive real estate income, buy REITs (Real Estate Investment Trusts) instead. (REITs are legally required to distribute the large majority of taxable income to shareholders — e.g. ~90% under the US REIT regime — a verifiable statutory fact [HIGH]; the "buy-to-let is a lie" framing and the cited 6-9% residential REIT yields are opinion/period-specific.) (Source: [[anton-kreil]])

14. [MEDIUM] Demographic headwinds: Western birth rates are plummeting. If a country's birth rate is 1 child per family, the population halves over a generation. "The buildings will still be there but there's half the amount of people." This undermines long-term property speculation. (Source: [[anton-kreil]] — opinion; declining Western fertility is broadly verifiable but the property conclusion is argument)

15. [MEDIUM] True self-determination means no bank, bank manager, central bank, or government decides your financial future. Avoid all personal debt: mortgages, credit cards, overdrafts, car loans. (Source: [[anton-kreil]] — opinion/philosophy)

16. [LOW] Credit scores are absurd: "You get scored on your ability to borrow, and the higher your score the more you can borrow." The reward for debt compliance is bigger liabilities. (Source: [[anton-kreil]] — rhetorical opinion)

### Principle 2 (continued): Building Infrastructure

17. [MEDIUM] Pensions infrastructure has failed in the Western world. The average person's pension value at retirement equals approximately one year of the national average salary — meaning they can maintain current living standards for only one year. This is why retirees downgrade their homes. (Source: [[anton-kreil]] — assertion, specific figure unverified)

18. [MEDIUM] Long-only pension illustration: Index starts at 100, compounds 15%/year for 5 years to ~200. In year 6, a 50% crash takes it back to 100. Repeat cycle. An insourced pension (one that can profit from down markets) could be at 300 at that same point. (Source: [[anton-kreil]] — illustrative stylized example)

19. [LOW] Insurance is a "fear pitch" — actuarial mathematicians ensure companies pay out on only ~2-4% of policies per year. The real insurance is becoming wealthy enough to self-insure. (Source: [[anton-kreil]] — rhetorical opinion, payout figure unverified)

20. [MEDIUM] Build your own infrastructure: own your pension, self-insure, invest profits into assets generating 10%+ annual returns, and compound. "It starts off shallow, gets a little steeper, takes time, and then goes exponential." (Source: [[anton-kreil]] — opinion/philosophy)

### Tax Efficiency and Global Arbitrage

21. [MEDIUM] UK vs Singapore tax comparison for a 50% gross margin service company: Out of $100 invoiced, the UK entrepreneur keeps ~$12 (after VAT, corp tax, income tax, national insurance). The same company in Singapore keeps low 40s. "40 years of work in the UK is equivalent to 10 years in Singapore." (Source: [[anton-kreil]] — illustrative calculation, directionally plausible)

22. [MEDIUM] Singapore government balance sheet: $1 trillion in assets with a population of 5.5 million, despite producing no agriculture or commodities. (Source: [[anton-kreil]] — figure approximate/unverified here)

23. [MEDIUM] Employer's National Insurance in the UK is effectively "a tax on jobs" — an additional cost of hiring that doesn't exist in Singapore. (Source: [[anton-kreil]] — opinion/framing of a real cost)

24. [LOW] Zero-tax jurisdictions (Dubai DIFC, Caribbean) offer no public services in return (poor roads, schools, hospitals). Singapore offers the best "deal" — low taxes with world-class infrastructure and lifestyle. (Source: [[anton-kreil]] — subjective opinion)

### Freedom

25. [LOW] "Freedom is the number one asset you will ever own." The capitalist system provides freedom if you want it — but only if you participate. (Source: [[anton-kreil]] — philosophy)

26. [MEDIUM] Without total freedom, you cannot assess risk objectively. Financial obligations (mortgages, debt) create bias and cloud decision-making. (Source: [[anton-kreil]] — opinion, consistent with behavioral-finance literature)

27. [MEDIUM] Freedom of thought is required for proper [[risk-management-overview|risk assessment]]. If you're trapped in liabilities, you take unnecessary excessive risks (lottery mentality) or make fear-based decisions. (Source: [[anton-kreil]] — opinion)

### Risk IQ

28. [MEDIUM] Most people cannot properly assess risk because they lack freedom and perspective. Proper risk assessment requires: (a) no liabilities/debt, (b) understanding opportunity cost, (c) considering intergenerational timeframes (25-50-100 years), and (d) understanding demographic trends. (Source: [[anton-kreil]] — opinion/framework)

### Hierarchy

29. [MEDIUM] The hierarchy principle relates to understanding where you sit in economic and social structures, and positioning yourself to move up through self-determination rather than expecting the system to elevate you. (Source: [[anton-kreil]])

### Financial Principles Summary

30. [MEDIUM] The path: Accept the system works, physically remove yourself from bad environments, seek alternative education, have no liabilities, rent (don't mortgage), build your own infrastructure (pension, insurance, business), invest in compounding assets at 10%+ annually, become tax efficient, buy your dream life for cash. (Source: [[anton-kreil]] — summary of opinion/framework)
