Fred McNaught explaining key fundamental metrics across 6 short solo clips recorded January 10-11, 2024. Each clip focuses on a specific metric — EPS, dividends/franking, DRP, PE ratio, and intrinsic value. Supplements [[alfred-fundamental-analysis]] with Fred's verbal reasoning.

## Key Claims

### Earnings Per Share

1. [HIGH] EPS growth and dividend per share growth are "two of the keys to investing" (Source: Clip 1)
2. [HIGH] If EPS is growing and dividends are growing, then the share price will soon follow (Source: Clip 1)
3. [HIGH] Dividends are paid from EPS — always understand this relationship (Source: Clip 1)
4. [HIGH] Dividends are important because the Australian tax system provides specific benefits to dividends (Source: Clip 1)

### Franking Credits

5. [HIGH] [[franking-credits|Franking credits]] are almost unique to Australia (Source: Clip 2)
6. [HIGH] Companies pay tax at 30% on net profit before tax. When shareholders receive dividends, the tax has already been paid — shareholders receive a franking credit (Source: Clip 2)

### Dividend Reinvestment Plans (DRP)

7. [HIGH] DRP allows shareholders to take new shares instead of cash dividend, usually issued at or slightly below market price, free of brokerage (Source: Clip 3)
8. [HIGH] DRP is a way to build shareholding over time — Fred is "greatly in favour" of it (Source: Clip 3)
9. [HIGH] DRP also prevents spending dividend income on consumption rather than investment (Source: Clip 3)

### PE Ratio

10. [HIGH] A PE of 10 means investors pay $10 for every $1 of current earnings — if nothing changes, they get their money back in 10 years (Source: Clip 4)
11. [HIGH] Before buying, always know the PE ratio and compare it to the sector average for guidance (Source: Clip 4)
12. [HIGH] A PE higher than sector average implies greater risk but also higher potential reward — it becomes a risk-reward analysis (Source: Clip 4)

### Intrinsic Value

13. [HIGH] There are three methods to calculate intrinsic value: (1) [[dcf-analysis|discounted cash flow]], (2) EPS × PE method, (3) asset-based evaluation (Source: Clip 5)
14. [MEDIUM] All three methods require assumptions and "do not provide a lot of clarity" — Fred uses them as a "rough guide only" (Source: Clip 5)

### Patience

15. [HIGH] "When you find a truly wonderful business, stick with it. Patience pays and one wonderful business can offset the many mediocre decisions that are inevitable." (Source: Clip 6)
