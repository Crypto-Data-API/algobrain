## Key Claims Extracted

- **[MEDIUM]** Tick-level Bitcoin studies (2017–2021) show volume, volatility, and liquidity peak during London–New York (LNY) overlap; BTC/USD and BTC/EUR show inverted-U intraday activity; BTC/JPY shows dual peaks (Asian + LNY).
- **[MEDIUM]** Practitioner mapping: Asia session ~00:00–07:00 UTC (lower liquidity, tighter ranges, false breakouts), London ~08:00–16:00 UTC (volume pickup, directional moves), New York ~13:00–21:00 UTC (trend extension or violent reversal).
- **[HIGH]** US spot BTC ETFs launched 2024; Kaiko data shows ~half of BTC volume now occurs during US sessions post-ETF launch vs. more even distribution in earlier cycles.
- **[HIGH]** Weekday BTC volumes now roughly 2× weekend volumes in the ETF era; liquidity hollows out on weekends as institutional desks go offline.
- **[MEDIUM]** BTC fell from ~78k to <76k over a weekend amid Strait of Hormuz tensions; >$100M in liquidations. Cited as canonical example of thin-weekend-book + leverage cascade.
- **[HIGH]** Perpetual futures dominate crypto trading volume; well-designed perps are 5–8× more liquid than quarterly futures with 49–83% tighter spreads.
- **[HIGH]** Funding is typically every 8h on CEX perps, sometimes hourly on DeFi perps. Creates a funding-by-hour rhythm.
- **[MEDIUM]** At 5× leverage, ~20% adverse move triggers liquidation; at 40× leverage, ~2.5% does. Thin-session liquidation cascades feed back into further liquidations.
- **[MEDIUM]** Hyperliquid: 24h volumes periodically >$8–9B, open interest >$15B across ~200 pairs.
- **[MEDIUM]** Derivative DEX volume grew from ~$33.3B to ~$342B in 2024 (~872% increase).
- **[HIGH]** USDT supply ~$190B, ~60% of global stablecoin float, primarily backed by US Treasuries.
- **[HIGH]** Coinbase Prime custodies a double-digit share of global crypto market cap; flows concentrate during US trading hours.
- **[HIGH]** CME BTC/ETH futures trade Sunday 17:00 – Friday 16:00 CT with a daily 60-minute break; basis vs. 24/7 spot is a live cross-venue stress measure.
- **[MEDIUM]** 30-year US Treasury yields touched 5% in 2024–2026 regime; risk-off episodes around macro data releases cluster in US hours.
