# Open-Source AI Movement

The **open-source AI movement** refers to the release of high-quality [[foundation-models|foundation model]] weights that anyone can download, run, modify, and deploy. Led by [[meta-ai|Meta]] (LLaMA), [[mistral-ai|Mistral]], and distributed through [[hugging-face|Hugging Face]], this movement democratized access to powerful AI and directly enabled the [[ai-narrative-arc|crypto AI agent ecosystem]].

## Key Timeline

| Date | Event | Impact |
|------|-------|--------|
| Feb 2023 | [[meta-ai|LLaMA 1]] leaked, then officially released | First high-quality open model |
| Jul 2023 | LLaMA 2 released with commercial license | Legal to use in products |
| Sep 2023 | [[mistral-ai|Mistral 7B]] released | Proved small models can compete |
| Dec 2023 | Mixtral 8x7B released | MoE architecture goes open-source |
| Apr/Jul 2024 | LLaMA 3 / 3.1 released (up to 405B) | First open model competitive with GPT-4 class |
| Dec 2024 | DeepSeek-V3 released (open weights, MoE) | Frontier-level reasoning at a fraction of training cost |
| Jan 2025 | DeepSeek-R1 (open reasoning model) | Open-weight reasoning rivals closed o1-class models; triggered a sharp AI-equity selloff on cost-disruption fears (2025-01-27) |
| 2025-2026 | Qwen, DeepSeek, Llama, and Mistral successors | Open weights stay roughly 6-12 months behind the closed frontier; gap narrows on reasoning, widens on agentic/long-horizon work |

## Why It Matters for Trading

1. **Privacy**: Run models locally — proprietary strategy details never leave your machine
2. **Cost**: No per-token API fees for research and analysis
3. **Customization**: [[fine-tuning-llms|Fine-tune]] on proprietary financial data
4. **Decentralized AI**: Open models power crypto AI networks ([[bittensor-subnets|Bittensor]], [[ritual-network|Ritual]])
5. **Accessibility**: Quantized models run on consumer hardware ($1,000 GPU vs $100K API spend)

## The Tension

Open-source AI creates a tension: it levels the playing field (retail traders get institutional-grade tools) but also erodes edge (when everyone has the same model, alpha comes from data and execution, not the model itself). This dynamic is central to the [[ai-narrative-arc|2025 AI narrative]].

## The DeepSeek Shock (Jan 2025) — A Trading Case Study

On 2025-01-27, the release of DeepSeek-R1 — an open-weight reasoning model reportedly trained at a small fraction of US-frontier-lab budgets — triggered one of the largest single-day losses in market history for [[nvidia-ai|NVIDIA]] (roughly -17%, ~$600B of market cap erased in a day) and dragged the broader AI-infrastructure and power complex with it. The thesis under attack: if frontier capability can be reached cheaply with far less compute, the multi-trillion-dollar capex assumptions baked into AI-infrastructure valuations may be too high.

The trading lesson cuts both ways: the move was an over-reaction in the short run (NVDA recovered much of the drop within weeks as inference demand and the "Jevons paradox" — cheaper compute drives *more* total usage — reasserted), but it correctly flagged that **open-weight cost disruption is a real, recurring tail risk** for richly-valued AI-capex names. Open-source progress is now a macro variable that AI-exposed equity positions must price.
