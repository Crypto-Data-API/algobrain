# History of Artificial Intelligence

The history of AI spans eight decades — from theoretical foundations in the 1940s to the [[foundation-models|foundation model]] revolution reshaping trading today. Understanding this history reveals a recurring pattern of breakthroughs followed by overpromise, disappointment, and eventual real-world adoption — a pattern that directly parallels the [[ai-narrative-arc|2024-2026 AI agent hype cycle]] in crypto markets.

## Timeline

### The Foundations (1940s-1950s)

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1943 | McCulloch & Pitts publish first mathematical model of a [[neural-networks|neural network]] |
| 1950 | Alan Turing publishes "Computing Machinery and Intelligence" — proposes the [[turing-test|Turing Test]] |
| 1956 | **Dartmouth Conference** — the term "Artificial Intelligence" is coined by John McCarthy, Marvin Minsky, and others. This is considered the birth of AI as a field |
| 1957 | Frank Rosenblatt builds the Perceptron — the first trainable neural network |
| 1958 | John McCarthy creates Lisp, the first AI programming language |

The Dartmouth Conference participants predicted that "every aspect of learning or any other feature of intelligence can in principle be so precisely described that a machine can be made to simulate it." They estimated this would take "a summer." It took 70 years.

### Early Enthusiasm (1960s-1970s)

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1966 | ELIZA — MIT's Joseph Weizenbaum creates the first chatbot (the namesake of the [[eliza-framework]]) |
| 1969 | Minsky & Papert publish "Perceptrons," showing limitations of single-layer networks |
| 1970s | Expert systems emerge — rule-based AI for narrow domains |

### First AI Winter (1974-1980)

Funding collapsed after AI failed to deliver on grand promises. The UK's Lighthill Report (1973) concluded that AI had achieved nothing of significance. Government and corporate funding dried up. This was the first [[ai-winters|AI winter]].

**Trading parallel**: The post-dot-com bust in 2000 — technology was real but overhyped.

### Expert Systems Boom & Second Winter (1980s-1990s)

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1980 | Expert systems become commercially viable (XCON saves DEC $40M/year) |
| 1986 | Backpropagation algorithm enables training multi-layer neural networks |
| 1987 | Second [[ai-winters|AI winter]] — expert systems prove brittle, expensive to maintain |
| 1997 | IBM's Deep Blue defeats world chess champion Garry Kasparov |

### Machine Learning Era (2000s-2010s)

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 2006 | Geoffrey Hinton demonstrates deep learning with restricted Boltzmann machines |
| 2011 | IBM Watson wins Jeopardy! |
| 2012 | **AlexNet** wins ImageNet — deep learning revolution begins |
| 2014 | Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs) introduced |
| 2016 | [[google-deepmind|DeepMind's]] AlphaGo defeats world Go champion Lee Sedol |
| 2017 | **"Attention Is All You Need"** — the [[transformer-architecture|transformer]] paper that enabled everything that followed |
| 2018 | BERT released — pre-trained language models become practical. [[finbert|FinBERT]] follows for finance |

### The Foundation Model Revolution (2020s)

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 2020 | GPT-3 demonstrates emergent capabilities at scale (175B parameters) |
| 2022 | [[chatgpt-moment|ChatGPT launches]] (Nov 30) — reaches 100M users in 2 months |
| 2023 | [[openai|GPT-4]] released (Mar) — enables tool use and function calling |
| 2023 | [[meta-ai|LLaMA]] released — catalyzes [[open-source-ai-movement|open-source AI]] |
| 2023 | [[anthropic|Claude]] family launches — Constitutional AI approach |
| 2024 | [[ai-narrative-arc|AI agent token mania]] in crypto |
| 2024-25 | AI becomes standard infrastructure for trading (code generation, research, analysis) |
| 2024-25 | Reasoning models (OpenAI o-series, Claude extended thinking) and the [[model-context-protocol|Model Context Protocol]] standardise tool-using agents |
| 2025-26 | Agentic workflows mature; frontier models ([[anthropic|Claude Opus 4.8/Sonnet 4.6]], GPT-4o-class, Gemini) power [[ai-trading-agents|research and execution copilots]] |

## Key Insight for Traders

Every major AI advancement has followed the same pattern: **breakthrough → hype → disillusionment → genuine adoption**. The traders who profit are those who can distinguish the breakthrough (real) from the hype (early) and position for the adoption phase (durable). The [[ai-winters|AI winter]] pattern is not a bug — it's how transformative technology gets priced by markets.
