# AI Winters

An **AI winter** is a period of reduced funding, interest, and progress in artificial intelligence following a cycle of overhype and unmet expectations. There have been two major AI winters (1974-1980, 1987-1993), and understanding them is directly relevant to evaluating whether the current AI boom will sustain or follow the same pattern.

## The Two Major AI Winters

### First AI Winter (1974-1980)

**Trigger**: AI researchers in the 1960s promised human-level intelligence within a decade. They couldn't deliver.

| Factor | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Overpromise** | "Machines will be capable of doing any work a man can do within 20 years" (Herbert Simon, 1965) |
| **Catalyst** | UK Lighthill Report (1973) — concluded AI was failing on its promises |
| **Consequence** | US and UK governments slashed AI funding. DARPA cut AI research budgets |
| **Duration** | ~6 years |
| **What survived** | Foundational theory, lisp programming, basic expert systems |

### Second AI Winter (1987-1993)

**Trigger**: Expert systems (rule-based AI) were oversold to corporations, proved brittle and expensive.

| Factor | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Overpromise** | Expert systems market projected to reach $5B by 1990 |
| **Reality** | Systems were expensive to build, impossible to maintain, couldn't handle edge cases |
| **Catalyst** | Lisp machine market collapsed; Japan's Fifth Generation Computer project failed |
| **Consequence** | Corporate AI budgets eliminated. "AI" became a dirty word in business |
| **Duration** | ~6 years |
| **What survived** | Neural network research (quietly continued), statistical ML approaches |

## The Pattern

Every AI winter follows the same cycle:

1. **Breakthrough** → genuine capability advance
2. **Hype** → media, investors, and corporations project unrealistic timelines
3. **Disappointment** → reality falls short of inflated expectations
4. **Funding collapse** → investment dries up, talent leaves
5. **Quiet progress** → researchers continue working without hype
6. **Next breakthrough** → cycle restarts

## Are We in a Pre-Winter Now?

The [[chatgpt-moment|post-ChatGPT]] AI boom (2023-present) shows familiar warning signs:

| Warning Sign | Current Evidence |
|-------------|-----------------|
| **Inflated expectations** | "AGI within years" predictions from [[openai|OpenAI]] leadership |
| **Indiscriminate investment** | $100B+ in AI startups, many without revenue |
| **Marketing abuse** | Every product claims "AI-powered" regardless of actual AI content |
| **Token speculation** | [[ai-agent-tokens|127 AI tokens]] in top 1000, most with minimal technology |

**However**, this cycle has a critical difference: **the technology actually works**. [[foundation-models|GPT-4 and Claude]] are genuinely useful products generating billions in revenue. Previous AI winters followed overpromise with no delivery. This time, the products exist — the question is whether the market has overpriced the trajectory.

The [[ai-narrative-arc|2024-2026 cycle]] in crypto (mania → bust → utility) may be a **mini-winter** within a longer adoption curve, rather than a full winter.

## Trading Lessons

- **Sell the hype, buy the utility**: The pattern rewards traders who exit during mania and re-enter during the disillusionment phase
- **Infrastructure outlasts applications**: In both previous winters, foundational research survived while flashy products died. In crypto AI, [[nvidia-ai|compute infrastructure]] and [[bittensor-subnets|decentralized networks]] are more likely to survive than individual [[ai-agent-tokens|agent tokens]]
- **"AI" as a label is cyclical**: When "AI" becomes a marketing buzzword (as it is now), the label itself becomes less predictive of actual capability
