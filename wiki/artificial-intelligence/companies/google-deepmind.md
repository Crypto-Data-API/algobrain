# Google DeepMind

**Google DeepMind** is Google's primary AI research lab, formed from the April 2023 merger of DeepMind (founded 2010 in London by Demis Hassabis, Shane Legg, and Mustafa Suleyman; acquired by Google in 2014 for ~$500M) and Google Brain. It is responsible for the **Gemini** family of foundation models — Google's answer to [[openai|GPT]] and [[anthropic|Claude]] — and for traders it matters both as the engine of Alphabet's (GOOGL) AI narrative and as a major API provider for [[llm-market-analysis|financial analysis]] workloads.

## Model Timeline (2023-2026)

- **Dec 2023** — Gemini 1.0 (Ultra/Pro/Nano) launched, unifying DeepMind and Brain efforts
- **Feb 2024** — Gemini 1.5 Pro with 1M-token context window
- **Dec 2024 - early 2025** — Gemini 2.0 family, agentic focus ("Project Mariner", Deep Research)
- **June 17, 2025** — Gemini 2.5 Pro and 2.5 Flash reach general availability; I/O 2025 added Live API native audio and hardened defenses against indirect prompt injection
- **Late 2025 - 2026** — Gemini 3 generation rolls out; **Gemini 3.1 Flash-Lite** (May 7, 2026) and **Gemini 3.5 Flash** (May 19, 2026) listed in Google's model changelog

## Trading Relevance

- **Gemini models** compete with GPT and Claude for [[llm-market-analysis|financial analysis]] workloads; Gemini's long context windows (1M+ tokens) suit filing-scale document analysis
- **AlphaGo's legacy**: Demonstrated that [[reinforcement-learning-trading|reinforcement learning]] can master complex strategic games — the same approach applied to trading
- **GOOGL narrative driver**: Gemini release cadence and benchmark wins move Alphabet's stock; the Gemini 3 launch (late 2025) was widely credited with a re-rating of GOOGL versus other AI plays
- **Google Cloud AI / TPU**: Custom TPU silicon (v5p, Trillium, and 2025-26 "Ironwood" generation) is the principal non-[[nvidia-ai|NVIDIA]] training stack at scale — TPU adoption news is a trading signal for both GOOGL and NVDA
- **Vertex AI**: Google's managed ML platform used by institutional quant teams
- DeepMind's research on multi-agent systems directly informs [[ai-trading-agents|trading agent]] architectures

## Parent Company

Google DeepMind is a division of [[alphabet|Alphabet Inc.]] (NASDAQ: GOOGL). Alphabet's AI capex is among the largest in the industry — guidance for 2026 capex (datacenters and TPUs) was raised repeatedly through 2025 into the hundreds of billions of dollars range industry-wide, with Alphabet alone committing $90B+ annually. DeepMind's research feeds directly into Google Search (AI Overviews/AI Mode), YouTube, Google Cloud, and Workspace.
