# AI Conferences & Publication Venues

AI breakthroughs are published at specific conferences and on arXiv (open preprint server). These publication events cluster capability announcements — new models, benchmark results, and architecture innovations often drop around conference deadlines. Traders tracking AI company catalysts should be aware of these schedules.

## Top-Tier AI Conferences

| Conference | Full Name | When | Focus | Why It Matters |
|-----------|----------|------|-------|---------------|
| **NeurIPS** | Neural Information Processing Systems | December | General ML/AI (largest, most prestigious) | Major model releases timed around this |
| **ICML** | International Conference on Machine Learning | July | ML theory and methods | Architecture innovations |
| **ICLR** | International Conference on Learning Representations | May | Deep learning, representation learning | LLM and vision model papers |
| **ACL** | Association for Computational Linguistics | July | NLP and language technology | NLP advances, [[foundation-models|LLM]] research |
| **EMNLP** | Empirical Methods in NLP | December | Applied NLP | Financial NLP, [[nlp-sentiment-analysis|sentiment]] papers |
| **CVPR** | Computer Vision and Pattern Recognition | June | [[computer-vision-overview|Computer vision]] | Vision models, multimodal AI |
| **AAAI** | Association for Advancement of AI | February | Broad AI | Applied AI, planning, knowledge |
| **IJCAI** | International Joint Conference on AI | August | Broad AI | International, cross-disciplinary |

## arXiv: The Real-Time Publication Channel

Most AI research appears on **arXiv** (arxiv.org) weeks to months before conference publication:
- Papers are posted as preprints with no peer review
- The community discusses and validates on Twitter/X
- Conference acceptance comes later but the ideas spread immediately

Key arXiv categories for trading:
- **cs.LG** — Machine Learning
- **cs.AI** — Artificial Intelligence
- **cs.CL** — Computation and Language (NLP)
- **cs.CV** — Computer Vision
- **q-fin** — Quantitative Finance
- **stat.ML** — Statistical Machine Learning

## 2025-2026 Publication Trends

Two shifts matter for traders tracking the research pipeline:

1. **The academic/industry split widened.** The most commercially significant work (frontier LLMs, agentic systems) increasingly ships via lab blog posts, system cards, and model releases rather than peer-reviewed papers — labs guard methods as trade secrets. Academic conferences (NeurIPS, ICML, ICLR) still drive the open methods that propagate into open-weight ecosystems.
2. **Enterprise/applied AI events** (Ai4, HumanX, AI Infra Summit, AI & Big Data Expo) have grown sharply, reflecting the shift from "can we build it" to "can we deploy and monetise it." These are better catalyst-watch venues for adoption and capex narratives (data-centre buildout, inference demand) than for capability breakthroughs.
3. **Benchmark releases are now research output in themselves** — the publication of a new hard benchmark (e.g. Humanity's Last Exam) is a field event that resets the SOTA conversation. See [[ai-benchmarks]].
