# AI in Cybersecurity

AI is both the **weapon** and the **shield** in modern cybersecurity. Attackers use AI to generate phishing, find vulnerabilities, and automate attacks. Defenders use AI for real-time threat detection, automated response, and vulnerability analysis. The cybersecurity sector ($200B+ market) is one of the fastest-growing AI application areas, with direct trading exposure through pure-play security companies.

## Defensive AI

| Application | AI Method | Impact |
|-------------|----------|--------|
| **Threat detection** | [[unsupervised-learning|Anomaly detection]] on network traffic | Identifies novel attacks without prior signatures |
| **Endpoint protection** | ML classifies file behaviour in real-time | Catches malware that evades signature-based detection |
| **Email security** | [[foundation-models|LLM]] detects phishing, social engineering | Understands context, not just keywords |
| **SIEM/SOAR** | AI triages alerts, automates response | Reduces alert fatigue (analysts get 10,000+ alerts/day) |
| **Vulnerability management** | ML prioritises vulnerabilities by exploitability | Focus patching on highest-risk CVEs |
| **Identity/access** | Behavioural analytics detect compromised credentials | Flags unusual login patterns, impossible travel |

## Offensive AI (Threats)

| Threat | AI Method | Risk |
|--------|----------|------|
| **AI-generated phishing** | LLMs write convincing, personalised phishing emails | Bypass human detection, scale attacks |
| **Deepfake fraud** | Voice/video cloning for CEO fraud | $25M lost in a single deepfake CEO fraud case (2024) |
| **Automated vulnerability discovery** | AI scans code for exploitable weaknesses | Accelerates zero-day discovery |
| **Adversarial ML** | Craft inputs that fool AI systems | Evade AI-based security, manipulate AI trading models |

## The Arms Race Dynamic

Cybersecurity AI creates a self-reinforcing investment cycle:
1. AI makes attacks more sophisticated
2. Companies must invest in AI defences
3. Security vendors capture the spending → revenue growth
4. Better defences force attackers to use better AI → cycle continues

This means cybersecurity AI spending is **structurally growing** regardless of economic cycle — one of the most durable AI investment themes.

## Trading Relevance

- Cybersecurity stocks are high-beta AI plays with recurring revenue (SaaS model)
- Major breach events at large companies create short-term volatility and sector rotation
- Government cybersecurity mandates (CISA, NIS2) are regulatory catalysts for the sector
- AI-powered attacks on financial infrastructure are a systemic risk — affects all market participants
