# AI in Healthcare

AI is transforming healthcare across the entire value chain — from drug discovery to diagnostics to clinical decision-making to operational efficiency. For traders, healthcare AI represents one of the largest addressable markets ($4+ trillion global healthcare spend) with clear paths to value creation and identifiable public companies.

## Application Areas

### Drug Discovery & Development

| Application | AI Role | Impact | Key Players |
|-------------|--------|--------|-------------|
| **Target identification** | ML identifies novel drug targets from genomic/proteomic data | Reduces discovery timeline from years to months | Recursion (RXRX), Exscientia, Insilico Medicine |
| **Molecule design** | Generative AI designs candidate molecules with desired properties | 10-100x more candidates screened computationally | Schrödinger (SDGR), Relay Therapeutics (RLAY) |
| **Clinical trial optimisation** | AI selects patients, predicts outcomes, identifies sites | Reduces trial cost and duration by 20-40% | Tempus (TEM), Veracyte (VCYT) |
| **Protein structure prediction** | [[ai-science|AlphaFold]] predicts 3D protein structures from amino acid sequences | Accelerates understanding of disease mechanisms | [[google-deepmind|DeepMind]] (Alphabet) |

The drug discovery pipeline is $50B+ annually. AI-driven efficiency gains represent massive cost savings and faster time-to-market.

### Medical Imaging & Diagnostics

[[computer-vision-overview|Computer vision]] applied to medical images:

| Modality | AI Application | FDA Status |
|----------|---------------|-----------|
| **Radiology** (X-ray, CT, MRI) | [[image-segmentation|Tumour detection]], anomaly screening | Multiple FDA-cleared products |
| **Pathology** | Cancer grading from tissue slides | FDA-cleared (Paige AI) |
| **Ophthalmology** | Diabetic retinopathy screening from retinal images | FDA-cleared (IDx-DR) |
| **Dermatology** | Skin lesion classification | Consumer apps + clinical tools |
| **Cardiology** | ECG interpretation, echo analysis | FDA-cleared (multiple) |

Medical imaging AI is the most mature healthcare AI application — multiple products are FDA-cleared and deployed in clinical practice.

### Clinical Decision Support

[[foundation-models|LLMs]] for clinical workflows:
- Summarising patient records and clinical notes
- Generating differential diagnoses from symptoms
- Drafting clinical documentation (reducing physician burnout)
- Answering medical questions from literature ([[retrieval-augmented-generation|RAG]] over medical databases)

### Operational Efficiency

- **Revenue cycle**: AI automates coding, billing, and claims processing
- **Scheduling**: Optimise operating room utilisation, staff allocation
- **Supply chain**: Predict drug/supply demand, reduce waste

## Regulatory Landscape

- **FDA**: AI/ML-based Software as Medical Device (SaMD) framework; 500+ AI-enabled devices cleared
- **EU MDR**: Medical Device Regulation applies to AI diagnostics
- Key concern: **algorithmic bias** — models trained on non-diverse patient populations may underperform for minority groups
- Requirement for **continuous monitoring** — model performance must be tracked post-deployment

## Trading Angles

- Biotech stocks with AI-first discovery platforms trade at premiums to traditional pharma
- FDA clearance events for AI diagnostic products are binary catalysts
- Hospital IT spending on AI is a leading indicator for healthcare IT stocks (VEEV, HIMS)
- [[google-deepmind|DeepMind's]] medical AI breakthroughs (AlphaFold, Med-PaLM) are Alphabet catalysts but hard to isolate financially
