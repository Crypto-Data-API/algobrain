# AI in Creative Industries

Generative AI is disrupting the $2.4 trillion creative economy — visual art, writing, music, video, advertising, and design. For traders, this represents both an investment theme (companies building or adopting generative tools) and a disruption risk (companies whose value depends on human creative labour).

## Application Areas

| Domain | AI Tools | Disruption Level | Affected Companies |
|--------|---------|-----------------|-------------------|
| **Visual art / design** | [[image-generation-ai|Stable Diffusion, DALL-E, Midjourney]] | High | ADBE (Adobe), Shutterstock, Getty |
| **Writing / copywriting** | [[foundation-models|ChatGPT, Claude, Jasper]] | High | Media companies, content farms |
| **Video generation** | Sora ([[openai|OpenAI]]), Runway, Kling | Medium (emerging) | DIS, NFLX, studios |

| **3D/Game assets** | AI mesh generation, texture synthesis | Medium | U (Unity), RBLX |

## Winners & Losers

### Winners
- **Adobe (ADBE)**: Integrated Firefly (generative AI) into Photoshop, Illustrator — AI enhances existing workflow
- **Meta (META)**: AI-generated ad creatives improve advertiser ROI → more ad spend
- **Google (GOOGL)**: AI in search, ads, YouTube content tools
- **Canva**: AI design tools democratise graphic design

### At Risk
- **Stock photo companies**: Why buy stock photos when you can generate them? Shutterstock pivoted to licensing AI training data
- **Freelance creative labour**: Writing, illustration, basic design face AI substitution
- **Traditional media**: AI-generated content increases supply, compresses content value
- **Music labels** (UMG, WMG): AI-generated music threatens licensing revenue but also creates new tools for artists

## The Copyright Question

Unresolved legal battles that will shape the industry:
- **Training data**: Can AI models be trained on copyrighted work? (NYT vs OpenAI, Getty vs Stability AI)
- **AI output ownership**: Who owns an AI-generated image — the prompter, the model creator, or nobody?
- **Deepfakes**: AI-generated likenesses of real people (regulatory/legal risk)

Outcomes of these cases will affect valuations of AI companies, media companies, and content platforms. As of 2026 the *NYT v. OpenAI/Microsoft* and *Getty v. Stability AI* suits remain the bellwether cases; partial fair-use rulings in related matters (e.g. the 2025 Anthropic and Meta training-data decisions) have nudged the market toward licensed-data models (Adobe Firefly, Getty's own generator) rather than open web-scraped training.

## Trading-Relevance Summary

The investable framing of generative AI in creative industries follows the **additive vs substitutive** test (see [[ai-education]] for the same framework):
- **Additive** (creative tools embedded in an existing moat): ADBE, MSFT, GOOGL, META — AI raises ARPU and ad ROI, valuations re-rate upward.
- **Substitutive** (revenue model replaceable by a general model): stock-media, content farms, low-end freelance marketplaces — terminal-value compression.
- **Second-order market risk:** AI-generated disinformation (fake imagery of disasters, factory fires, geopolitical events) can move commodity and equity prices on no real news. See [[image-generation-ai#3. Deepfake & Manipulation Detection]] for the detection angle.
