# AI in Education

AI is transforming the $6 trillion global education market through personalised tutoring, automated assessment, content generation, and adaptive learning. For traders, the sector is early-stage but investable through edtech companies adopting AI and the broader [[foundation-models|LLM]] platforms powering educational tools.

## Application Areas

| Application | AI Method | Impact |
|-------------|----------|--------|
| **AI tutoring** | [[foundation-models|LLM]] + [[retrieval-augmented-generation|RAG]] | 1-on-1 tutoring at scale (previously only available to the wealthy) |
| **Adaptive learning** | ML adjusts difficulty and content to student performance | Personalised curriculum pacing |
| **Automated grading** | NLP for essay scoring, code evaluation | Instant feedback, reduced teacher workload |
| **Content generation** | LLMs create exercises, explanations, study materials | 10x faster course development |
| **Language learning** | LLM conversation partners, speech AI | Immersive practice without human tutors |
| **Accessibility** | Speech-to-text, translation, text simplification | Education accessible across languages and abilities |

### The Chegg Cautionary Tale

Chegg (homework help platform) lost over half its market cap when ChatGPT launched — students switched to free AI for homework answers. This is the most dramatic example of AI disruption in education and a warning for any company whose product can be replicated by a general-purpose [[foundation-models|LLM]].

## Trading Relevance

- **AI-native edtech** (DUOL) trades at a premium — AI enhances the product
- **AI-disrupted edtech** (CHGG) trades at a discount — AI replaces the product
- The distinction depends on whether AI is **additive** (makes the product better) or **substitutive** (makes the product unnecessary)
- This framework applies beyond education to any sector evaluating AI impact
