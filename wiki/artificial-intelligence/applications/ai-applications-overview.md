# AI Applications

AI is being deployed across every major industry, creating investment opportunities and disruption risk. This section maps the application landscape — which sectors are most transformed by AI, which companies lead, and how traders can position around AI adoption curves.

Understanding cross-industry AI adoption also contextualises trading-specific AI: the same [[foundation-models|foundation models]], [[computer-vision-overview|computer vision]], and [[reinforcement-learning|reinforcement learning]] used in healthcare and gaming are adapted for [[ai-trading-agents|trading agents]] and [[ai-market-making|market making]].

## Cross-Cutting Themes

### 1. Foundation Model Platform Shift
Every industry is rebuilding workflows around [[foundation-models|LLMs]]. The companies that control the models ([[anthropic]], [[openai]], [[google-deepmind]]) and the compute ([[nvidia-ai|NVIDIA]]) capture value across all applications.

### 2. Data Moats
AI capabilities are commoditising (everyone can use GPT-4). The durable advantage is **proprietary training data**. Companies with unique datasets in their domain (medical records, financial transactions, game telemetry) have natural AI moats.

### 3. Vertical AI vs Horizontal AI
- **Horizontal**: General-purpose AI (ChatGPT, Claude) applicable across industries
- **Vertical**: Industry-specific AI trained on domain data (medical imaging, legal document review, financial risk)

Vertical AI companies often have higher margins and stickier customers than horizontal platforms.

### 4. The Automation Spectrum
AI adoption follows a consistent pattern within each industry:

```
Augmentation → Automation of routine tasks → Automation of complex tasks → Full autonomy
```

Most industries are between stages 1-2. [[autonomous-vehicles|Self-driving]] and [[ai-trading-agents|trading agents]] are attempting stage 3-4. No industry has achieved full AI autonomy.
