# AI Ethics, Safety & Governance

AI ethics, safety, and governance address the societal implications of deploying artificial intelligence — bias and fairness, transparency and explainability, regulatory frameworks, environmental costs, labour displacement, and intellectual property. For traders, these aren't abstract concerns: they directly affect company valuations, regulatory risk, litigation exposure, and the reliability of AI trading tools.

## Why Traders Must Understand AI Ethics

| Ethical Issue | Trading Impact |
|-------------|---------------|
| **[[algorithmic-bias|Bias]]** | Companies with biased AI face lawsuits, fines, reputational damage → stock risk |
| **[[explainability-interpretability|Explainability]]** | Regulators require explainable AI in finance → compliance cost for trading firms |
| **[[ai-governance-frameworks|Governance]]** | EU AI Act, US executive orders create compliance requirements → sector-wide cost |
| **[[ai-regulation-global|Regulation]]** | Regulatory actions are binary catalysts (positive: clarity; negative: restriction) |
| **[[ai-environmental-impact|Environmental]]** | AI compute energy costs affect data centre stocks, ESG mandates |
| **[[ai-labour-displacement|Labour displacement]]** | Automation threats reshape sector economics (see [[ai-education|Chegg example]]) |
| **[[ai-intellectual-property|IP/Copyright]]** | Training data lawsuits threaten AI company valuations |
| **[[hallucinations-ai|Hallucination]]** | AI trading tools that fabricate data create direct financial risk |

## The Ethics-to-Regulation Pipeline

Ethical concerns follow a predictable path to market impact:

```
Academic/activist concern → Media attention → Regulatory inquiry → 
Proposed regulation → Enacted law → Compliance cost → Stock impact
```

Traders who track early-stage ethical concerns can anticipate regulatory risk before it's priced.
