# AI & Labour Displacement

AI automation is reshaping the labour market across every sector. For traders, understanding which jobs are most exposed to AI helps evaluate company economics (margin expansion from automation), sector disruption (which industries lose workers), and macro signals (employment data interpretation in an AI era).

## The Exposure Framework

| Exposure Level | Job Characteristics | Examples |
|---------------|-------------------|---------|
| **High** | Routine cognitive tasks, rule-based decisions, data processing | Data entry, basic accounting, customer service, paralegal |
| **Medium-High** | Pattern recognition, content creation, analysis | Radiologists, copywriters, financial analysts, translators |
| **Medium** | Complex decision-making with data input | Portfolio managers, software developers, consultants |
| **Medium-Low** | Physical + cognitive combination | Surgeons, electricians, skilled trades |
| **Low** | Complex physical tasks, unpredictable environments, deep relationships | Plumbers, emergency responders, therapists, care workers |

## Sector-by-Sector Impact

| Sector | AI Impact | Stock Implication |
|--------|----------|-------------------|
| **Customer service** | [[chatbot-architectures|Chatbots]] handle 50-80% of queries | Headcount reduction → margin expansion (banks, telcos) |
| **Content creation** | [[ai-creative|Generative AI]] produces text, images, code | Reduced creative headcount, lower content costs |
| **Financial services** | [[ai-finance|AI]] for analysis, compliance, operations | Junior analyst roles reduced; senior roles augmented |
| **Legal** | AI reviews documents, generates contracts | Paralegal roles at risk; lawyers augmented |
| **Manufacturing** | Robotics + AI for production | Ongoing since 1960s; accelerating with [[humanoid-robots]] |
| **Healthcare** | [[ai-healthcare|AI diagnostics]], admin automation | Admin staff reduced; doctors augmented, not replaced |
| **Education** | [[ai-education|AI tutoring]] changes teacher role | Teacher role shifts from instruction to facilitation |
| **Software engineering** | Code generation (Claude Code, Copilot) | Junior developer roles compressed; senior roles augmented |

## The Augmentation vs Replacement Distinction

Most AI displaces **tasks**, not entire jobs. A financial analyst who spent 60% of time on data gathering and 40% on judgment now spends 10% on data (AI handles it) and 90% on judgment. The role transforms — fewer analysts needed, but remaining analysts are more productive.

The companies that benefit most from this are those with high labour-cost ratios that adopt AI early — labour savings flow directly to operating margins.

## Trading Angles

- **Margin expansion**: Companies announcing AI-driven efficiency gains (fewer employees, same/more output) → operating margin improvement → stock re-rating
- **Sector disruption**: Chegg (-50%) when ChatGPT launched is the template for AI disruption events
- **Labour market data**: Traditional employment metrics (NFP, unemployment) become harder to interpret as AI changes the relationship between output and employment
- **New job creation**: AI creates new roles (prompt engineers, AI trainers, AI safety researchers) — net employment impact is debated
- **Wage pressure**: AI competition puts downward pressure on wages for exposed occupations, upward pressure on AI-skilled roles
