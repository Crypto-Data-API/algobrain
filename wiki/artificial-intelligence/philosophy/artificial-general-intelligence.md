# Artificial General Intelligence (AGI)

**AGI** — artificial intelligence that matches or exceeds human cognitive ability across all domains — is the stated goal of [[openai|OpenAI]], a core concern of [[anthropic|Anthropic's]] safety research, and the implicit assumption behind trillions of dollars in AI-related market capitalisation. Whether AGI arrives in 5 years or 50 (or never) is the most consequential uncertainty in technology investing.

## What AGI Would Mean

An AGI system could:
- Master any intellectual task a human can do (not just one narrow domain)
- Transfer knowledge across domains without retraining
- Reason about novel situations it has never encountered
- Learn autonomously from minimal examples
- Understand context, nuance, and abstract concepts

**For trading**: A true AGI could replace every analyst, portfolio manager, and quant researcher. It would be the last trading system anyone needs to build. This is why AGI timelines are the most important variable in AI company valuations.

## The Timeline Debate

| Position | Proponents | Estimated Timeline | Market Implication |
|----------|-----------|-------------------|-------------------|
| **Imminent** (< 5 years) | Sam Altman ([[openai|OpenAI]]), Elon Musk, Ray Kurzweil | 2025-2030 | Justifies current AI valuations, massive compute investment |
| **Near-term** (5-20 years) | Demis Hassabis ([[google-deepmind|DeepMind]]), many AI researchers | 2030-2045 | Current investments pay off eventually, but timing matters |
| **Long-term** (20-50+ years) | Many academic researchers, Yann LeCun (Meta) | 2045-2075+ | Current AI company valuations are significantly ahead of reality |
| **Never / Wrong question** | Some cognitive scientists, philosophers | N/A | AI develops differently than human intelligence — "general" is the wrong frame |

### The Scaling Hypothesis

The most market-relevant argument: do you just need **more compute and data** to reach AGI, or do you need **new fundamental breakthroughs**?

| View | Argument | Implication |
|------|---------|-------------|
| **Scaling is enough** | GPT-3 → GPT-4 → GPT-5 shows steady improvement with more compute | Massive GPU investment is rational; [[nvidia-ai|NVIDIA]] thesis strengthens |
| **Scaling hits diminishing returns** | Gains per dollar of compute are shrinking; new architectures needed | Current compute buildout may be overinvestment; bubble risk |
| **Hybrid** | Scaling gets you 80% there; new ideas needed for the last 20% | Moderate investment thesis; R&D matters as much as compute |

## Where We Are Now

Current [[foundation-models|frontier models]] (Claude Opus 4.x, GPT-5-class, Gemini 2.x) are:
- **Superhuman** at: knowledge retrieval, text generation, code, translation, summarisation
- **Human-level** at: reasoning about familiar domains, following complex instructions
- **Below human** at: novel problem-solving, common sense, physical reasoning, long-horizon planning, genuine understanding (debated)
- **Missing entirely**: Autonomous goal-setting, real-world interaction, persistent learning, consciousness (see [[consciousness-machine-intelligence]])

This is sometimes called "narrow superintelligence" — far better than humans at specific tasks, far worse at general capability.

## Investment Framework

| AGI Timeline Belief | Portfolio Implication |
|-------------------|---------------------|
| **< 5 years** | Max AI exposure: NVDA, MSFT, GOOGL. AI disrupts every industry. Cash is a losing position |
| **5-15 years** | Strong AI allocation but diversified. Current leaders may not be final winners. Watch for architectural disruption |
| **15-30 years** | Moderate AI allocation. Current AI stocks may be overvalued on hype. Focus on near-term revenue, not AGI promises |
| **30+ years / never** | Treat AI as incremental technology improvement, not paradigm shift. Current valuations are a bubble |

## The Honest Position

Nobody knows when (or if) AGI will arrive. The market's job is to price uncertainty, and AGI uncertainty is among the largest the market has ever faced. The wisest trading approach is:

1. **Don't bet on a specific timeline** — position for a range of outcomes
2. **Focus on near-term capabilities** — what can AI do *today* that generates revenue?
3. **Track capability benchmarks** — [[ai-benchmarks]] provide objective measurement of progress
4. **Watch for discontinuities** — a sudden capability jump would be the signal to increase exposure
