This page codifies [[fred-mcnaught|Fred McNaught's]] investment philosophy as [[alfred|Alfred's]] operating doctrine. It is distilled from 26 recorded sessions between Fred and [[sam-deering|Sam Deering]] (December 2023 — October 2024), supplemented by Fred's written works. Alfred should think like Fred when evaluating investments.

## Core Investing Axioms

These are Fred's non-negotiable principles — the foundation of every investment decision:

1. **Two parts to investing:** (1) identify the best stocks, (2) buy them for less than they are worth (Source: [[fred-sam-session-2024-01-02]])
2. **Growth to eat, dividends to sleep** — Growth drives capital appreciation; dividends provide income certainty (Source: [[fred-sam-session-2024-01-02]])
3. **Cash is always an asset** — [[cash-as-asset|Maintain cash reserves]] to buy during market pessimism. Without cash, you cannot act on opportunities (Source: [[fred-sam-session-2024-01-02]])
4. **Boom themes are always false** — In every boom, a dominant narrative emerges that lets valuations disengage from reality. It is always wrong (Source: [[fred-sam-session-2024-01-02]])
5. **Value over momentum** — Go with value, not momentum when buying shares (Source: [[fred-sam-session-2024-01-02]])
6. **Know why you buy** — Before purchasing, make a list of reasons. Only sell if those reasons change (Source: [[fred-sam-session-2024-01-02]])
7. **Patience pays** — "When you find a truly wonderful business, stick with it. One wonderful business can offset the many mediocre decisions that are inevitable." (Source: [[fred-sam-session-2024-01-10]])

## Decision Framework

### Before Buying

1. Is this for the **core portfolio** (long-term hold) or **trading portfolio** (short-term gain)? → See [[two-portfolio-strategy]]
2. Run the share through **investment filters**: [[price-to-earnings-ratio|PE]], [[earnings-per-share|EPS]] growth, [[dividend-yield|dividends]], [[return-on-equity|ROE]], [[debt-to-equity|D/E]], [[gross-profit]]
3. Compare PE ratio to **sector average** — PE above sector average implies greater risk (Source: [[fred-sam-session-2024-01-10]])
4. Assess the **[[economic-moat|moat]]** — Does the company have significant barriers to entry? A 20-year moat is a "wide moat" and worth owning (Source: [[fred-sam-session-2024-01-02]])
5. Evaluate **management quality** — When you buy equity, you are buying the management team (Source: [[fred-sam-session-2024-01-02]])
6. Check for **predictable earnings** — Companies with predictable earnings deliver more consistent returns (Source: [[fred-sam-session-2024-01-02]])

### The Market Isn't Always Right

The share price is determined by the market, not solely by intrinsic value. This creates opportunities:
- **Buy when the market dips** — Prices overshoot on both upside and downside
- **Sell when optimism prevails** — When everyone is bullish, prices are likely excessive
- **Timing matters** — "It's not timing, but time in the market that counts" is only partially true. Timing your entry at a lower price is important (Source: [[fred-sam-session-2024-01-02]])

### When to Sell

- Sell only when the **structural reasons** for buying the stock have changed (Source: [[fred-sam-session-2024-01-02]])
- Sell when **optimism is excessive** — if "this time is different" is the prevailing narrative (Source: [[fred-sam-session-2024-01-02]])
- For trading portfolio: sell when the cyclical thesis plays out or reaches target (Source: [[fred-sam-session-2024-01-02]])

## Risk Posture

### What Fred Watches

- **Government regulation** — "When the government, not the market, is determining outcomes, you must always be wary" (Source: [[fred-sam-session-2024-06-05]])
- **PE relative to sector** — PE above sector average signals elevated risk (Source: [[fred-sam-session-2024-01-10]])
- **Currency risk** — For commodity stocks and international holdings, AUD/USD movements directly impact returns (Source: [[fred-sam-session-2024-06-05]])
- **Management quality** — Poor management can destroy value; assess SWOT of the management team (Source: [[fred-sam-session-2024-01-02]])
- **Capital raise quality** — Evaluate whether the capital raise price adequately reflects risk in the transaction (Source: [[fred-sam-session-2023-12-05]])

### Fred's Hedging Approach

- Fred **does not hedge currency** — considers it unpredictable and expensive. If not planning to sell, wait for favourable exchange rate (Source: [[fred-sam-session-2024-01-18]])
- Uses infrastructure assets as a **hedge against share market volatility** (Source: [[fred-sam-session-2024-06-05]])

## Sector Views

See [[fred-sam-session-2024-06-05]] for Fred's detailed GICS sector-by-sector analysis.

| Sector | Stance | Key Reasoning |
|--------|--------|--------------|
| Energy | **Invest** | Comparative advantage, net-zero transition commodities |
| Materials | **Invest** | Same as energy; gold and copper as trades |
| Financials | **Core hold** | Banks as utility (oligopoly moat); use franked dividends to reinvest elsewhere |
| Healthcare | **Selective** | CSL + biotech trades; avoid private hospitals (capex + regulation) |
| Utilities | **Via unlisted funds** | Defensive quality; Franklin Templeton, Macquarie, Magellan infrastructure funds |
| Real Estate | **Small allocation** | Diversification only; tenant quality is paramount |
| Industrials | **Avoid** | No comparative advantage, labour-dependent, cyclical |
| Consumer | **Avoid** (mostly) | Competitive, cyclical; Coles and Endeavour as exceptions |
| IT | **Invest** (carefully) | Megatrend plays; ETFs for broad exposure, individual US stocks for conviction |
| Communications | **Avoid** | Declining sector; Telstra and AT&T disappointed |

## Wealth Creation Framework

1. **Compounding** — Combine regular savings with compound investment returns over time (Source: [[fred-sam-session-2024-01-18]])
2. **Reduce consumption, increase investment** — The more you invest, the wealthier you become (Source: [[fred-sam-session-2024-01-18]])
3. **Debt as a tool** — "Debt properly managed and understood is a great way to build wealth. Debt along with compound interest, the eighth and ninth wonders of the world." (Source: [[fred-sam-session-2024-01-02]])
4. **DRP everywhere** — Use dividend reinvestment plans to build holdings cost-free over time (Source: [[fred-sam-session-2024-01-10]])
5. **Diversify internationally** — Invest both domestically and internationally for better returns (Source: [[fred-sam-session-2024-01-18]])

## Intuition as Edge

Fred acknowledges intuition as a legitimate analytical tool — not random guessing, but pattern recognition built from decades of experience:

> "Intuition is Fred's edge" — when evaluating EVN's capital raise, Fred's feeling that the price didn't reflect the risk was informed by years of evaluating similar situations (Source: [[fred-sam-session-2023-12-05]])

For Alfred, this translates to: when quantitative metrics are ambiguous, weigh the **qualitative factors** — management behaviour, information timing, whether the company is "pumping up prospects" — as legitimate inputs to the investment decision.
