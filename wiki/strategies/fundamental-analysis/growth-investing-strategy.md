# Growth Investing Strategy

## Overview

Growth Investing targets companies with **above-average revenue and earnings growth**, willing to pay premium valuations for the expectation that rapid expansion will justify today's price. Unlike [[value-investing-strategy|value investing]], which seeks undervaluation, growth investing seeks **acceleration** -- companies growing earnings at 20-50%+ annually, disrupting industries, and expanding their total addressable market. The philosophy -- articulated by Philip Fisher -- is that a great company at a fair price is better than a mediocre company at a cheap price (Source: [[book-common-stocks-and-uncommon-profits]]). [[peter-lynch]] demonstrated this approach at scale, achieving 29% annual returns at Fidelity Magellan by finding growth stocks in everyday life (Source: [[book-one-up-on-wall-street]]).

The most structured growth investing framework is **CANSLIM**, developed by **William O'Neil**, founder of Investor's Business Daily (Source: [[book-how-to-make-money-in-stocks]]). CANSLIM is an acronym: **C**urrent quarterly earnings (up 25%+), **A**nnual earnings growth (25%+), **N**ew products/management/price highs, **S**upply and demand (look for tight share supply), **L**eader (RS rating 80+), **I**nstitutional sponsorship (mutual fund accumulation), **M**arket direction (only buy in uptrends). The PEG ratio (P/E divided by earnings growth rate) helps identify growth stocks that are not excessively expensive -- a PEG below 1.0-1.5 suggests reasonable pricing relative to growth.

## Rules

### Entry
1. **Earnings growth screen:** Identify companies with current quarterly EPS growth of 25%+ and annual EPS growth of 25%+ over the last 3-5 years. Revenue growth should confirm the earnings growth (not just cost-cutting or buybacks).
2. **PEG ratio filter:** The P/E ratio divided by the projected earnings growth rate should be below 1.5. A PEG of 1.0 means you are paying "fair value" for growth; below 1.0 is undervalued relative to growth.
3. **Technical confirmation:** The stock should be near a 52-week high or breaking out of a [[cup-and-handle]], [[flat-base]], or other constructive base pattern. [[relative-strength]] rating should be 80+ (outperforming 80% of all stocks).
4. **Institutional sponsorship:** Check that mutual funds and institutional investors are accumulating (rising number of institutional holders over recent quarters). This confirms smart money sees the growth potential.
5. **Market direction:** Only initiate new positions when the broad market ([[spy|S&P 500]]) is in a confirmed uptrend. CANSLIM's M factor: 3 out of 4 stocks follow the general market direction.

### Exit
1. **Sell at 20-25% profit** if the gain occurs within 1-3 weeks (fast mover -- take profits). Hold for 8+ weeks if the stock rises steadily (let winners run).
2. **Cut losses at 7-8% below purchase price.** This is CANSLIM's strict risk management rule -- no exceptions. Small losses preserve capital for the next winner.
3. **Climax top:** Sell if the stock surges 25-50% in 1-2 weeks on massive volume after already being extended -- this is exhaustion behavior.
4. **Earnings deceleration:** Exit if quarterly earnings growth drops below 15% or decelerates for two consecutive quarters.

### Position Sizing
Risk 1% of portfolio per trade (7-8% stop-loss means position size is ~12-14% of portfolio). Concentrate in 5-8 high-conviction names. Never fully invest -- keep 10-25% in cash for new breakouts.

## Indicators Used
- Quarterly and annual EPS growth rate
- Revenue growth rate (should confirm EPS growth)
- PEG ratio (P/E / earnings growth rate)
- [[relative-strength]] rating (IBD-style, percentile rank)
- [[volume]] -- breakouts should occur on 50%+ above-average volume
- Institutional ownership trend (accumulation vs. distribution)
- [[cup-and-handle]], [[flat-base]], and other O'Neil chart patterns

## Example Trade
**Asset:** Shopify (SHOP) breaking out from a base
1. SHOP has grown quarterly EPS by 45% and annual EPS by 55% over 3 years. Revenue growth is 35%+. PEG ratio is 1.2. RS rating is 94.
2. The stock forms a 7-week [[cup-and-handle]] pattern with a buy point at $68. Volume has dried up during the handle (constructive).
3. SHOP breaks out above $68 on volume 120% above average. Enter at $69. Set stop-loss at $63.50 (8% below entry).
4. Over the next 12 weeks, SHOP rallies to $96, a 39% gain. Earnings remain strong. Hold because the gain took more than 3 weeks and growth is accelerating.
5. After 6 months, SHOP reaches $115. Quarterly earnings decelerate to 18% growth. Sell at $110.
6. **Result:** +$41 per share (59% gain) over 6 months. The 8% stop was never threatened.

## Performance Characteristics
- **Win Rate:** 35-45%. Growth investing accepts a lower win rate because winners dramatically outpace losers (cutting losses at 7-8% while letting winners run 50-200%+).
- **Profit Factor:** 2.0-4.0. The asymmetric risk-reward (small losses, large gains) drives profitability.
- **Best Market Conditions:** Bull markets, especially those driven by innovation cycles (internet 1990s, mobile 2010s, AI 2023-2025). Low interest rate environments favor growth stocks by increasing the present value of future earnings.
- **Worst Market Conditions:** Rising interest rate environments, value rotations, and bear markets. Growth stocks can fall 50-80% in severe downturns because premium valuations compress.
- **Annual Returns:** Top growth investors (O'Neil, Minervini) report 20-40% CAGR over decades, though with significant drawdown periods.

## Advantages
- Captures the biggest stock market winners -- most 10x+ stocks are growth stories
- CANSLIM provides a structured, repeatable framework with clear buy and sell rules
- Strict 7-8% stop-loss limits downside and preserves capital for future opportunities
- Aligns with secular innovation trends (AI, biotech, fintech) that create enormous wealth
- Combines [[fundamental-analysis]] (earnings growth) with [[technical-analysis]] (base patterns, relative strength)

## Disadvantages
- **Low win rate** can be psychologically difficult -- expect to be stopped out on 50-65% of trades
- Premium valuations mean growth stocks are vulnerable to sharp de-ratings when growth slows
- Requires active monitoring: earnings reports, market direction, and price action demand regular attention
- Market timing component (the M in CANSLIM) is difficult and subjective
- Highly concentrated in technology and innovation sectors, creating sector risk
