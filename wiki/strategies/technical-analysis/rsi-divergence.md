# RSI Divergence Strategy

## Edge source

**Behavioral (primary)**: RSI divergence identifies momentum exhaustion — the crowd is still positioned for the prior trend's continuation, but the rate of new highs (or lows) is decelerating. Buyers are paying up for each new high but with less force than before; the divergence in RSI exposes this weakness before price confirms the reversal. At a known support/resistance level, stop orders cluster and reversal orders cluster simultaneously, creating a high-probability confluence zone.

**Analytical (secondary)**: Divergence requires multi-swing analysis across the RSI and price simultaneously, creating a signal that cannot be generated by a single indicator alone. The multi-timeframe confirmation (identify on daily, time entry on 4H) narrows entry to the precise swing within the broader exhaustion zone, improving the stop placement to risk/reward ratio substantially.

**Crypto-specific**: In crypto, RSI divergence at key levels resolves faster and more violently than in equity markets because a reversal at a major resistance level with leveraged longs is amplified by perp liquidations — the moment price turns, long liquidations cascade and accelerate the reversal. The divergence is the warning; the liquidation cascade is the execution. Similarly, bullish divergence at support in negative-funding regimes (where shorts are at peak positioning) can trigger a sharp short squeeze. The funding rate direction at the divergence point is a valuable confirmation: bearish divergence + positive funding (many longs) = highest-probability short.

## Null hypothesis

Under the null, RSI divergence is pattern-fitting on past price data — any higher high on price with a lower high on RSI is retrospectively identifiable on any price series, including random walks, because RSI is a smoothed function of price. The counter-argument is that the signal only has edge when: (1) it occurs at a predefined support/resistance level (not anywhere on the chart), (2) it is confirmed by a price-action trigger (bearish engulfing, failure candle), and (3) the RSI reading is at an extreme (above 60 for bearish, below 40 for bullish). When all three filters are applied, the null becomes much harder to maintain, as the confluence of technical selling, stop-order clustering, and exhausted momentum creates a genuine structural edge. A program trading raw RSI divergence without these filters cannot reject the null.

## Overview

RSI divergence occurs when price action and the [[relative-strength-index]] (RSI) move in opposite directions, signaling a potential reversal. This is one of the most powerful tools in a technical trader's arsenal because it reveals weakening [[momentum]] beneath the surface of price. **Regular divergence** signals reversals: bearish divergence (price makes a higher high, RSI makes a lower high) and bullish divergence (price makes a lower low, RSI makes a higher low). **Hidden divergence** confirms trend continuation. Combining divergence with key [[support-resistance]] levels dramatically improves accuracy.

## Rules

### Entry Rules
1. **Bearish Divergence (Short):** Price prints a **higher high** while RSI prints a **lower high**. RSI should ideally be in or near the overbought zone (above 60-70). Enter short when price confirms the reversal with a bearish candle pattern (e.g., [[bearish-engulfing]], [[evening-star]]) at a [[resistance]] level.
2. **Bullish Divergence (Long):** Price prints a **lower low** while RSI prints a **higher low**. RSI should be in or near oversold territory (below 30-40). Enter long on a bullish confirmation candle at a [[support]] level.
3. **Hidden Divergence (Trend Continuation):** In an uptrend, price makes a higher low but RSI makes a lower low -- this confirms the trend will continue. Enter on the pullback.
4. **Multi-Timeframe Confirmation:** Identify divergence on the daily chart, then drop to the 4H or 1H chart for precise entry timing.

### Exit Rules
1. **Target:** Aim for the previous swing high (for longs) or swing low (for shorts) as the first target.
2. **RSI Extreme Exit:** Close when RSI reaches the opposite extreme (e.g., overbought after a bullish divergence entry).
3. **Stop Loss:** Place stops beyond the extreme of the divergence pattern -- below the lowest low for bullish divergence, above the highest high for bearish.
4. **Trailing Stop:** Use a [[chandelier-exit]] or ATR-based trailing stop once the trade moves 1R in your favor.

## Example Trade

**Setup:** ETH/USD daily chart. Price rallies to $3,800, printing a higher high compared to the prior swing at $3,600. However, RSI reads 62, lower than the prior swing's RSI of 71. Bearish divergence identified at a known [[resistance]] zone.

**Entry:** A [[bearish-engulfing]] candle forms at $3,780. Enter short at $3,760 on the next candle open.

**Management:** Stop loss placed at $3,850 (above the high). Risk = $90 per unit. First target at prior support $3,500 (reward = $260, R/R = 2.9:1).

**Exit:** Price drops to $3,500 target. Partial profit taken. Remaining position trailed with 2x [[atr]] stop, eventually closing at $3,350.

## Performance Characteristics

- **Win Rate:** 55-65% when combined with support/resistance and confirmation candles
- **Best Conditions:** Extended trends approaching exhaustion at key levels
- **Worst Conditions:** Strong trending markets where divergence can persist for extended periods before resolving
- **Average Holding Period:** 3-15 days depending on timeframe
- **Key Risk:** Divergence can "stack" -- multiple divergences may form before price actually reverses

## Advantages

- Identifies reversals **before** they happen, offering excellent risk/reward entries
- Works on all timeframes and all liquid markets
- Hidden divergence provides a powerful trend continuation tool often overlooked by traders
- Combines naturally with [[support-resistance]], [[fibonacci-retracements]], and [[candlestick-patterns]]
- RSI is available on every charting platform

## Disadvantages

- Divergence is **not** a timing tool -- it shows potential, not certainty, and can persist for many bars
- Requires patience and discretion; not easily automated without additional filters
- In strong trends, bearish divergence can form repeatedly while price keeps rising (the "divergence trap")
- Subjective element in identifying swing points for comparison
- Should always be confirmed with price action; never trade divergence alone without [[confluence]]

## Capacity limits

RSI divergence is a pattern-recognition swing strategy with no inherent capacity constraint at the instrument level — BTC/ETH perps can absorb very large positions. The constraint is **signal frequency**: high-quality divergences with all three filters (level + extreme RSI + price trigger) are rare — perhaps 3–8 per year on the BTC daily chart. Scaling requires running the strategy across multiple assets and timeframes, but note that most crypto alts are highly correlated to BTC, so "10 divergence signals" in a crypto portfolio may represent 1–2 independent bets.

## What kills this strategy

1. **Divergence trap**: a strong trending market produces repeated bearish divergence as each new high is "less enthusiastic" in RSI terms — but price keeps going higher. The divergence can "stack" through multiple swings before price reverses; traders who enter on the first divergence are stopped out multiple times before the actual top.
2. **Cascade amplification on the wrong side**: in crypto, a bullish divergence at support that is invalidated by a liquidation cascade can produce a violent move through the support level before any recovery; the stop must be wide enough to survive one cascade leg, making the risk/reward worse than in equity markets.
3. **Level subjectivity**: support/resistance levels in crypto shift with each macro regime; a level that held for 6 months may become irrelevant after an exchange failure or regulatory event changes market structure.
4. **RSI period sensitivity**: the default 14-period RSI on a daily chart was calibrated for equity markets with regular sessions; crypto's 24/7 nature compresses and extends cycles differently, and the "overbought/oversold" zones (70/30 or 60/40) may need to be shifted for crypto's characteristically stronger trends.

## Kill criteria (numeric)

*(From frontmatter — duplicated here for reference)*
- Divergence signal persists more than 10 candles (on entry timeframe) without price-action confirmation → signal is stale; exit or reduce size rather than holding indefinitely.
- Three consecutive losing divergence trades in the same direction (e.g., three failed bearish divergences) → stop trading that direction for 30 days; the market may be in a strong trend where divergence systematically fails.
- Rolling 90-day P&L negative with > 10 completed trades → pause; reassess whether support/resistance confluence is being applied rigorously (trades should only be taken at predefined levels, not at arbitrary chart points).
