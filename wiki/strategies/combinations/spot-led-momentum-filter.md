# Spot-Led Momentum Filter

Spot-led momentum filter is a [[trend-following-cta|momentum/trend]] strategy on crypto perpetuals that restricts new entries to moves where the **signal is generated by spot market capital inflow** rather than leveraged perp speculation. A move is classified as "spot-led" when three cross-venue conditions hold simultaneously: (1) a positive Coinbase premium (BTC trading at a premium on Coinbase vs Binance spot — the canonical signal for US institutional / ETF-driven buying), (2) funding rate on perps is flat or below a modest threshold (the leveraged crowd is not yet stretched), and (3) spot trading volume is growing faster than perp open interest over the entry window (new capital is entering spot, not just leverage cycling in perps). Moves meeting all three conditions reflect genuine capital inflow; moves failing one or more reflect leveraged speculation that is more prone to mean-reversion.

This is explicitly differentiated from [[funding-filtered-momentum]] (B1 page) — that strategy gates momentum entries on the **level** of the funding rate (blocking longs when funding is stretched positive, seeking the non-consensus trend). This page gates on the **origin or flow structure** of the move: it asks whether the move is being driven by spot buyers (real capital) or perp buyers (leveraged speculation), which requires cross-venue comparison (Coinbase premium, spot volume vs OI growth) and is not captured by the funding level alone. A move can have moderate funding (passing funding-filtered-momentum's gate) but still be perp-led with spot volume lagging — this page would block it. Conversely, a move can have flat funding AND a strong Coinbase premium AND spot-led volume — this page would pass it even though funding alone would also have passed. The two filters are complementary rather than redundant, but they are sufficiently distinct to warrant separate pages.

This is differentiated from [[oi-confirmed-trend]] — that page uses OI *growth* as a trend *confirmation* (momentum is more likely to persist when OI is expanding, confirming new money entering). This page uses the *relationship between* spot volume growth and OI growth to discriminate spot-led from perp-led moves — a fundamentally different signal that can produce opposite conclusions (high OI growth relative to spot volume is a *negative* signal in this page's framework, while it is a *positive* signal in [[oi-confirmed-trend]]).

This is differentiated from [[etf-flow-directional]] — that page uses the aggregate net ETF flow (daily/weekly institutional ETF demand) as a directional signal for BTC price. This page uses the *Coinbase premium* as a real-time, intraday proxy for US institutional/ETF flow rather than actual ETF flow data (which has a publication lag of hours to days). The Coinbase premium is a higher-frequency, lower-lag indicator of the same underlying demand.

## Edge source

Per [[edge-taxonomy]], **behavioral + structural + informational**:

- **Behavioral (primary)** — leveraged retail participants are prone to momentum-chasing in the perp market: buying perps when price rises, creating the funded-positive-funding cycle. These participants are fragile: when the price reverses even modestly, forced liquidations and stop-losses amplify the move, producing the "perp-led reversal" pattern. In contrast, real-money spot buyers (institutional via Coinbase, ETF arbitrageurs, long-term accumulators) are less leveraged, have longer holding periods, and do not contribute to the same forced-liquidation dynamics. Their buying is fundamentally more persistent.
- **Structural** — the Coinbase premium is structurally explained by US institutional and ETF-driven demand. Coinbase is the primary institutional custody and trading venue for US ETF arbitrageurs, large family offices, and regulated institutions. When they are net buyers, BTC trades above its Binance price — a cross-venue structural signal of institutional demand that is more durable than retail perp speculation.
- **Informational** — the spot volume vs OI growth divergence is informational: it tells the observer *who* is driving the price move. A move where spot volume is 2× normal but OI is barely moving indicates genuine new buyers taking delivery; a move where OI is up 15% but spot volume is flat indicates the same capital re-levering in perps, not new capital entering. This is real-time structural flow analysis.

## Why this edge exists

**Three structural reasons spot-led momentum persists longer than perp-led momentum:**

1. **Spot buyers have no liquidation mechanism.** A real-money spot buyer at $70K BTC cannot be forced out by an adverse price move below a liquidation threshold. They can only decide to sell. This means their demand is a permanent price floor, not a conditional order that disappears at a lower price. Perp longs, by contrast, are liquidated if BTC falls far enough — their demand disappears at the worst time and generates forced selling.

2. **ETF arbitrage creates a structural bid-ask around Coinbase premium.** When the Coinbase premium is positive, ETF arbitrageurs who manage the BTC ETF NAV premium/discount must buy spot BTC via Coinbase (or authorised participants must). This is a mechanical, recurring buying program that is more predictable and less sentiment-dependent than retail perp speculation. A positive Coinbase premium therefore signals an *ongoing mechanical bid* that is likely to continue as long as ETF inflows persist.

3. **Perp-led momentum is self-liquidating.** When a move is driven primarily by perp OI expansion, the crowded long base grows. The funding rate rises, increasing the carry cost on longs and attracting short sellers. The eventual reversion is partly mechanical: as longs pay increasing funding and the perp basis compresses, the economic incentive to hold diminishes. This is the same mechanism [[funding-filtered-momentum]] exploits — but this page detects it at the *source* (spot vs perp flow origin) rather than at the *outcome* (funding level).

**Who is on the other side:** the momentum-chasing retail participant who enters perps on a price surge driven by the same speculation they are joining, and finds themselves holding a crowded leveraged position that mean-reverts when the institutional real-money flow does not follow.

## Null hypothesis

Under the null, the spot-vs-perp flow origin carries **no incremental predictive power** for momentum persistence over and above the standard price momentum signal:
- Forward 10-day returns on spot-led momentum entries should not differ from perp-led entries at equivalent momentum signal strength.
- The Coinbase premium at entry should not predict forward BTC returns on a statistically significant basis after controlling for the momentum signal and the funding level.
- The spot-volume / OI-growth ratio at entry should not predict subsequent liquidation-cascade risk or mean-reversion probability.

Currently not rejected (`backtest_status: untested`). Testable prediction: classify all momentum signal fires (N-day channel high breakout) by the three cross-venue conditions (Coinbase premium positive, funding ≤ threshold, spot volume growth > OI growth). Predict that the spot-led subset shows materially higher average forward 10d return and lower maximum adverse excursion than the perp-led subset.

## Rules

### Entry conditions (long)

**1. Momentum signal active (any one of):**
- 20-day Donchian channel breakout: close above the 20-day high.
- 12/26 EMA crossover on 4h bars: fast EMA crosses above slow EMA.
- 14-day Rate-of-Change (RoC) > +8%.

**2. Flow origin: spot-led (all three sub-conditions):**

**Sub-condition A: Coinbase premium positive**
- Coinbase BTC spot price > Binance BTC spot price (after accounting for BTC/USDC vs BTC/USDT FX adjustment).
- Required: positive Coinbase premium for ≥ **2 of the last 3 hours** (sustained, not momentary).
- Threshold: premium ≥ **0.05%** (material premium, not noise).
- Source: [[coinbase-premium]] concept; `/api/v1/market-intelligence/coinbase-premium` endpoint on CryptoDataAPI.

**Sub-condition B: Funding rate flat or moderate**
- 8h funding rate ≤ **0.03%** on the entry day.
- *Rationale:* flat funding confirms the perp crowd is NOT yet crowded long. If funding is above 0.03%, the perp market is already pricing a crowded-long premium — the move is at risk of being perp-led.
- Note: this is a softer version of the funding gate in [[funding-filtered-momentum]] (which uses 0.04%). The softer threshold here is by design: this page primarily relies on the Coinbase premium and volume structure for discrimination; the funding gate is a secondary check.

**Sub-condition C: Spot volume leading OI growth**
- 24h spot trading volume growth (current 24h vs 7-day average) > 24h perp OI growth (current OI vs OI 3 days ago), i.e.:
  - `spot_vol_24h / spot_vol_7d_avg > 1.20` (spot volume is at least 20% above its 7-day average)
  - AND `perp_oi_change_3d / perp_oi_3d_ago < 0.15` (OI has not grown more than 15% in 3 days)
  - *Or equivalently:* spot vol growth rate > perp OI growth rate by at least 10 percentage points.
- *Rationale:* new capital entering spot creates a higher-liquidity, lower-leverage bid; OI growing faster than spot volume signals the same capital is re-levering in perps rather than new real money entering.

**3. No regime kill:** the current regime from `/api/v1/regimes/current` is not `Established Bear` or `Structural_Shock`.

**4. OI context (awareness only; does not block):** note whether OI is at a multi-week high (high OI while Coinbase premium is positive = institutional real-money buying despite elevated leverage = higher conviction). Note if OI is falling while Coinbase premium is positive (accumulation without perp speculation = highest conviction signal).

### Entry conditions (short)

Mirror conditions:
- Momentum signal fires to the downside (below 20-day Donchian low, bearish EMA cross, negative RoC).
- Coinbase premium is **negative** (BTC trading at a discount on Coinbase vs Binance = US institutions selling or absent).
- Funding ≥ **−0.01%** (funding not deeply negative — if funding is very negative, shorts are crowded; see [[funding-flush-reversal]] for that regime).
- Spot volume leading on the downside: spot vol above average, OI not collapsing faster than spot (indicating real selling, not a deleveraging flush that will revert).

*Note: short entries are less common than long entries in crypto bull-market periods. Apply the same logic symmetrically but expect fewer qualifying short setups.*

### Position sizing

- Base size: 2% of strategy sleeve per signal.
- Conviction multiplier: +0.5× if all three sub-conditions are confirmed with margins to spare (Coinbase premium > 0.15%, funding ≤ 0.01%, spot vol ≥ 1.5× 7d avg and OI growing < 5% in 3d).
- Maximum position: 3% of strategy sleeve.

### Exit conditions

1. **Momentum reversal:** price breaks below the 10-day Donchian low (long) or above (short). Close.
2. **Perp takeover signal:** if funding rises above **0.05%/8h** while the position is open (perp speculators have crowded in), trim 50% — the spot-led move has been captured; the remainder now has the same squeeze risk as a generic perp-led position.
3. **Coinbase premium turns negative** (while long): US institutional demand has reversed; reduce exposure by 50%.
4. **Time stop:** 20-day maximum hold.
5. **Hard stop:** −8% from entry.

## Implementation pseudocode

```python
# spot_led_momentum_filter.py

from dataclasses import dataclass
from typing import Optional

# ---- thresholds ----
MOMENTUM_ROC_THRESHOLD         = 0.08    # 14-day RoC threshold for momentum signal
COINBASE_PREMIUM_MIN_PCT       = 0.0005  # 0.05% minimum premium (positive = bullish)
COINBASE_PREMIUM_HOURS_MIN     = 2       # out of 3 hours premium must be positive
FUNDING_8H_MAX                 = 0.0003  # 0.03%/8h max for non-stretched entry
SPOT_VOL_VS_7D_MIN             = 1.20    # spot vol ≥ 120% of 7d avg
OI_GROWTH_3D_MAX               = 0.15    # OI growth ≤ 15% in 3 days
CONVICTION_PREMIUM_HIGH        = 0.0015  # 0.15% premium for high-conviction multiplier
CONVICTION_FUNDING_MAX         = 0.0001  # 0.01%/8h for high-conviction
CONVICTION_SPOT_VOL_HIGH       = 1.50    # 1.5× 7d avg for high-conviction

# exit thresholds
FUNDING_TRIM_THRESHOLD         = 0.0005  # 0.05%/8h — perp takeover signal (trim 50%)
HARD_STOP_PCT                  = 0.08
TIME_STOP_DAYS                 = 20
BASE_SIZE_PCT                  = 0.02
CONVICTION_EXTRA               = 0.005  # +0.5% size for conviction entries
MAX_SIZE_PCT                   = 0.03

@dataclass
class CrossVenueState:
    # momentum
    momentum_long:          bool
    momentum_short:         bool
    # sub-conditions
    coinbase_premium_pct:   float   # positive = Coinbase > Binance
    coinbase_premium_hours: int     # of last 3 hours the premium was positive (bullish direction)
    funding_8h:             float
    spot_vol_24h:           float   # current 24h spot vol
    spot_vol_7d_avg:        float   # 7-day average 24h spot vol
    oi_3d_ago:              float
    oi_current:             float
    regime:                 str

def spot_led_conditions_met(s: CrossVenueState, direction: str) -> tuple[bool, str]:
    """Check all three sub-conditions for spot-led flow."""
    if direction == "LONG":
        # sub-A: Coinbase premium positive
        if s.coinbase_premium_pct < COINBASE_PREMIUM_MIN_PCT:
            return False, f"Coinbase premium {s.coinbase_premium_pct*100:.3f}% < {COINBASE_PREMIUM_MIN_PCT*100:.3f}%"
        if s.coinbase_premium_hours < COINBASE_PREMIUM_HOURS_MIN:
            return False, f"Coinbase premium sustained only {s.coinbase_premium_hours}/3h"
        # sub-B: funding flat
        if s.funding_8h > FUNDING_8H_MAX:
            return False, f"funding {s.funding_8h*100:.4f}%/8h > {FUNDING_8H_MAX*100:.4f}% (perp crowd building)"
    elif direction == "SHORT":
        if s.coinbase_premium_pct > -COINBASE_PREMIUM_MIN_PCT:
            return False, "Coinbase premium not negative — no US institutional selling signal"
        if s.funding_8h < -0.0001:
            return False, "funding too negative — shorts crowded; use funding-flush-reversal framework"
    # sub-C: spot volume leading OI growth
    spot_vol_ratio = s.spot_vol_24h / s.spot_vol_7d_avg if s.spot_vol_7d_avg > 0 else 1.0
    oi_growth = (s.oi_current - s.oi_3d_ago) / s.oi_3d_ago if s.oi_3d_ago > 0 else 0.0
    if spot_vol_ratio < SPOT_VOL_VS_7D_MIN:
        return False, f"spot vol ratio {spot_vol_ratio:.2f} < {SPOT_VOL_VS_7D_MIN} (spot not leading)"
    if oi_growth > OI_GROWTH_3D_MAX:
        return False, f"OI growth {oi_growth*100:.1f}% in 3d > {OI_GROWTH_3D_MAX*100:.1f}% (perp-led)"
    return True, ""

def is_high_conviction(s: CrossVenueState, direction: str) -> bool:
    if direction != "LONG":
        return False
    spot_vol_ratio = s.spot_vol_24h / s.spot_vol_7d_avg if s.spot_vol_7d_avg > 0 else 1.0
    oi_growth = (s.oi_current - s.oi_3d_ago) / s.oi_3d_ago if s.oi_3d_ago > 0 else 0.0
    return (s.coinbase_premium_pct >= CONVICTION_PREMIUM_HIGH and
            s.funding_8h <= CONVICTION_FUNDING_MAX and
            spot_vol_ratio >= CONVICTION_SPOT_VOL_HIGH and
            oi_growth < 0.05)

def entry_decision(s: CrossVenueState, book: dict) -> dict:
    if book.get("drawdown", 0) > 0.22:
        return {"action": "FLAT", "reason": "drawdown kill"}
    if s.regime in ("Established_Bear", "Structural_Shock"):
        return {"action": "WAIT", "reason": f"regime kill: {s.regime}"}

    direction = None
    if s.momentum_long:
        direction = "LONG"
    elif s.momentum_short:
        direction = "SHORT"
    else:
        return {"action": "WAIT", "reason": "no momentum signal"}

    spot_led_ok, reason = spot_led_conditions_met(s, direction)
    if not spot_led_ok:
        return {"action": "WAIT", "direction": direction, "reason": reason,
                "note": "momentum signal active but flow origin is perp-led — skip entry"}

    high_conv = is_high_conviction(s, direction)
    size = book["sleeve_capital"] * (BASE_SIZE_PCT + (CONVICTION_EXTRA if high_conv else 0))
    size = min(size, book["sleeve_capital"] * MAX_SIZE_PCT)

    return {
        "action":           f"ENTER_{direction}",
        "direction":        direction,
        "notional":         size,
        "coinbase_premium": s.coinbase_premium_pct,
        "funding_8h":       s.funding_8h,
        "spot_vol_ratio":   s.spot_vol_24h / s.spot_vol_7d_avg,
        "high_conviction":  high_conv,
        "note": (f"spot-led: premium={s.coinbase_premium_pct*100:.3f}%, "
                 f"funding={s.funding_8h*100:.4f}%, "
                 f"spot_vol_ratio={s.spot_vol_24h/s.spot_vol_7d_avg:.2f}x, "
                 f"high_conv={high_conv}"),
    }

def mid_hold_check(pos: dict, s: CrossVenueState) -> Optional[dict]:
    if pos["direction"] == "LONG":
        if s.funding_8h > FUNDING_TRIM_THRESHOLD:
            return {"action": "TRIM_50PCT",
                    "reason": f"funding rose to {s.funding_8h*100:.4f}%/8h — perp crowd has taken over"}
        if s.coinbase_premium_pct < 0:
            return {"action": "TRIM_50PCT",
                    "reason": "Coinbase premium turned negative — US institutional demand reversed"}
    return None
```

The production system adds: Coinbase vs Binance real-time price feeds for the premium calculation; a daily OI/spot-volume ratio trend tracker; and a session filter (premium calculation uses the 3h window defined in UTC, not 24h, to ensure recency).

## Indicators / data used

- **Coinbase premium** — `/api/v1/market-intelligence/coinbase-premium`: the real-time Coinbase vs Binance BTC spot price differential. Primary sub-condition A gate.
- **Funding rate (8h)** — `/api/v1/derivatives/funding-rates?coin=BTC`: sub-condition B. Flat or low funding confirms the perp market is not yet crowded.
- **Open interest** — `/api/v1/derivatives/open-interest?coin=BTC`: current OI vs OI 3 days ago for the OI growth calculation in sub-condition C.
- **Spot trading volume** — `/api/v1/market-data/klines?symbol=BTCUSDT&interval=1d&limit=10`: daily OHLCV includes volume; compute current 24h spot volume vs 7-day average from the klines volume series.
- **OHLCV (daily and 4h)** — `/api/v1/market-data/klines?symbol=BTCUSDT&interval=4h&limit=120` and `interval=1d&limit=30`: for momentum signal (Donchian channel, EMA crossover, RoC).
- **Regime classification** — `/api/v1/regimes/current`: block entries in `Established_Bear` and `Structural_Shock`.

## Example trade

**Setup (illustrative — BTC spot-led breakout):**

- Date: a hypothetical bull-market momentum breakout.
- BTC close: $78,400 (20-day Donchian high breakout confirmed). Momentum signal LONG.
- **Flow origin check:**
  - Sub-A (Coinbase premium): Coinbase BTC = $78,520; Binance BTC = $78,390. Premium = +0.17%. Sustained 3 of 3 hours. Passes (≥ 0.05%, ≥ 2 of 3 hours).
  - Sub-B (Funding): current 8h funding = 0.018%/8h. Passes (≤ 0.03%).
  - Sub-C (Volume vs OI): 24h spot volume = $42B (vs 7d avg = $28B → ratio = 1.50×). OI 3 days ago = $24B; OI current = $25.6B (+6.7% in 3d). Passes (spot vol ≥ 1.20×; OI growth 6.7% ≤ 15%).
- **High-conviction check:** premium = 0.17% ≥ 0.15%; funding = 0.018% ≤ 0.01%? No — 0.018% > 0.01%. Not high conviction. Standard size.
- **Sleeve:** $100,000. Base size = 2% = $2,000.
- **Entry:** long BTC-PERP at $78,450. $2,000 notional = 0.0255 BTC.
- **Stop:** hard stop at −8% = $72,174. 10-day Donchian low at entry = $73,800 (momentum reversal stop).

**Hold (13 days):** BTC trends to $87,600 (+11.7% from entry). Funding during the move rises to 0.038%/8h by Day 10 — below the 0.05% trim threshold; no mid-hold trim. At Day 13, BTC pulls back to $85,400. Donchian 10-day low = $83,200 — not yet breached; hold.

**Day 15:** BTC drops to $83,100 — breaches the 10-day Donchian low of $83,200 (momentum reversal exit).
- Exit at $83,100. Entry $78,450 → exit $83,100 = +5.9% × $2,000 = **+$118 gross**.
- Funding cost paid: 15 days × 3 funding periods/day × 0.028%/period × $2,000 = ~$25 funding drag.
- Transaction costs: 2 fills × 5 bps = $2.
- **Net P&L: +$91** (+4.6% on deployed notional, +0.09% of sleeve).

**Perp-led rejection scenario:** same BTC breakout, but Coinbase premium = −0.10% (negative, US institutions not buying) and spot vol ratio = 0.95× (below 7d average). Sub-condition A fails; Sub-condition C fails. The strategy does not enter. BTC subsequently rallies +6% and then reverses −12% (perp-led pump-and-dump). The spot-led filter correctly identified the perp-led move and avoided the squeeze.

## Performance characteristics

| Metric | Value | Note |
|---|---|---|
| Expected net Sharpe | ~1.0 | Filtering to spot-led moves reduces signal frequency but improves per-trade quality; higher conviction per entry vs unfiltered momentum |
| Expected max drawdown | ~22% | Momentum strategies carry drawdown risk; spot-led filter reduces but does not eliminate false momentum entries |
| Win rate (per signal) | ~55–65% | Spot-led momentum entries expected to persist more often than perp-led; funding-filtered-momentum (similar concept) estimates 55-60% |
| Average win / average loss | ~1.5–2.5× | Trending moves can run 10–20%; hard stop at 8% caps the loss — asymmetric if the trend persists |
| Breakeven cost budget | 30 bps | 4 fills (entry + exit × 2 legs counting funding calc) at 5 bps = 10 bps transaction costs; funding drag 5–15 bps/week; 30 bps budget is adequate for swing entries |
| Signal frequency | Low-medium | All three sub-conditions simultaneously: estimated 2–5 qualifying entries per month on BTC/ETH |

**Comparison with funding-filtered-momentum:** funding-filtered-momentum is expected to generate similar or slightly more signals (funding level is a single gate) while this strategy requires three cross-venue conditions simultaneously. This strategy is expected to produce fewer but higher-quality signals, at the cost of missing perp-led moves that happen to trend (which do occur in bull markets).

## Capacity limits

- **Per entry:** spot BTC and BTC-PERP have $50B+ daily volume; a $10M momentum entry is achievable without market impact.
- **Binding constraint:** not execution but *signal quality as AUM grows*. At large book sizes, the strategy's own spot entries may contribute to the Coinbase premium signal, creating a reflexive self-confirmation loop. The `capacity_usd: 100000000` estimate reflects the threshold at which this self-referential risk becomes material.

## What kills this strategy

1. **Coinbase premium decays as an informational signal (#4: Crowding).** As the Coinbase premium becomes more widely monitored by systematic traders, arbitrageurs may eliminate the premium faster (within minutes rather than hours), reducing its predictive window. The 2h sustained threshold becomes impossible to meet because arbitrageurs eliminate the premium before it sustains.
2. **US institutional trading migrates from Coinbase to other venues.** If ETF custodians begin using Binance, LMAX, or OTC desks primarily rather than Coinbase spot, the Coinbase premium stops being a reliable institutional-demand proxy. The signal becomes noise.
3. **Funding and OI structure changes (#5: Regime change).** If a futures market reform (e.g. mandatory margin increases or OI caps) forces OI to stay low regardless of spot activity, Sub-condition C becomes permanently uninformative.
4. **Perp-led moves trend longer in bull markets (#2: Cost structure).** In extreme bull markets (2021 Q1-Q4), perp-led moves with stretched funding can trend for weeks. The strategy misses these entirely by blocking on Sub-condition B (funding > 0.03%). This is a feature (avoiding crowded entries) but also a cost (missing the bulk of the bull move when momentum is funded-and-stretched).
5. **Data quality for spot volume.** Spot volume on Binance and Coinbase has historically been subject to wash-trading noise. The 7-day average provides some smoothing, but if a venue manipulates volume reporting, Sub-condition C becomes unreliable.

## Kill criteria

Pause on any of:

1. **Strategy drawdown > 22%** in any rolling 90-day window.
2. **Rolling 6-month Sharpe < 0** on a minimum of 20 completed momentum signals.
3. **Coinbase premium positive during 5+ consecutive failed entries** — the premium is predicting a move that fails to materialise; either the premium-to-persistence relationship has broken or the premium is being generated by noise rather than institutional demand.
4. **Spot-led vs perp-led mean forward returns not statistically different** on a 30-signal sample — the cross-venue discrimination is no longer adding information; the three sub-conditions are failing to select a better-than-average momentum subset.
5. **Signal frequency < 2 qualifying entries per month for 3 months** — the cross-venue conditions are too restrictive for the current market structure; recalibrate thresholds.

See [[when-to-retire-a-strategy]] for the broader framework.

## Advantages

- **Selects the highest-persistence subset of momentum:** spot-led moves backed by institutional demand (Coinbase premium) and genuine capital inflow (spot volume leading OI) are structurally more likely to persist than perp-led speculative moves. This directly targets the most common failure mode of naive momentum: entering a perp-led pump that reverses when the leveraged crowd unwinds.
- **Complementary to funding-filtered-momentum:** the two strategies share the momentum primitive but different overlay logic. Running both as a dual-confirmation filter (momentum signal + funding gate passes + cross-venue filter passes) selects an even smaller, higher-conviction subset.
- **Real-time flow intelligence:** the Coinbase premium is a high-frequency, publicly observable signal of institutional demand that updates intraday. This is more timely than ETF flow data (daily/weekly lag) and more direct than on-chain exchange outflow data (reflects prior-day activity).
- **Interpretable exit signals:** the mid-hold funding threshold and Coinbase premium reversal exits provide continuous flow-quality monitoring — not just an entry filter but a *position quality monitor* during the hold.
- **Applicable beyond BTC:** the Coinbase premium is most reliable for BTC (the primary asset for institutional flows); the spot volume vs OI framework can be applied to ETH and major altcoins using their own spot/perp volume profiles.

## Disadvantages

- **Misses perp-led trends in extreme bull markets:** in late-cycle bull markets, perp-led moves with stretched funding can produce the largest absolute price gains. The funding gate (Sub-condition B) and spot-volume gate (Sub-condition C) will often block entry during these periods, leaving the strategy uninvested during the most euphoric move.
- **Coinbase premium can be gamed or noisy:** large OTC block trades, custody migrations (not speculation), or temporary exchange technical issues can create Coinbase premium spikes that are not indicative of sustained institutional demand.
- **Three simultaneous sub-conditions reduce frequency substantially:** a typical BTC breakout may have a Coinbase premium but flat spot volume; or rising spot volume but slightly elevated funding. The triple condition creates significant opportunity cost (missed moves that would have worked).
- **Cross-venue data dependency:** the strategy requires reliable, low-latency Coinbase vs Binance price comparison and real-time OI + spot volume data. Data latency or discrepancies between venue feeds can produce incorrect sub-condition classifications.
- **Requires ongoing recalibration of premium threshold:** the 0.05% Coinbase premium threshold may need adjustment as the BTC market cap grows (larger market cap = smaller premium at the same institutional dollar flow).
