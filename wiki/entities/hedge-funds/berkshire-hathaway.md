# Berkshire Hathaway

**Berkshire Hathaway** is a multinational conglomerate holding company built by [[warren-buffett]] and the late [[charlie-munger]], and — since January 1, 2026 — led by CEO **Greg Abel**, with Buffett remaining chairman of the board. Originally a textile manufacturer, it was transformed into a diversified investment vehicle with major holdings in insurance (GEICO), railroads (BNSF), energy, and public equities.

Berkshire's Class A shares (BRK.A) are among the most expensive per-share stocks in history, with the company commanding a market capitalization of roughly **$1.1 trillion** as of early 2026. Its annual shareholder letters and Omaha meetings are essential reading for [[value-investing]] practitioners.

## Major Holdings and Operations

Berkshire's public equity portfolio includes massive positions in **Apple** (still the largest holding even after Berkshire sold roughly two-thirds of the stake during 2024), **American Express**, **Bank of America** (also substantially trimmed in 2024-2025), **Coca-Cola**, and **Chevron**. On the wholly-owned subsidiary side, the insurance operations form the backbone of the conglomerate: **GEICO** (auto insurance), **General Re** (reinsurance), and Berkshire Hathaway Reinsurance Group generate the "float" -- premiums collected before claims are paid -- that [[warren-buffett]] invests for long-term returns.

Beyond insurance, Berkshire owns **BNSF Railway** (one of North America's largest freight railroads), **Berkshire Hathaway Energy** (utilities), and dozens of manufacturing, service, and retail businesses including Dairy Queen, See's Candies, and Precision Castparts.

## Cash Pile and Investment Philosophy

Berkshire is known for maintaining an enormous cash reserve, which grew from over $150 billion in the early 2020s to a record **$381.6 billion by September 2025**, stood at about **$373 billion at year-end 2025**, and exceeded **$397 billion after Q1 2026** — the largest corporate cash hoard in history, mostly in Treasury bills. Buffett (and now Abel) view this dry powder as optionality -- the ability to make large acquisitions or investments when markets dislocate, as demonstrated during the [[2008-global-financial-crisis]] when Berkshire invested billions in Goldman Sachs and Bank of America on favorable terms. In his first shareholder letter (February 2026), Abel told investors the cash pile "is not a retreat from deal-making."

## Succession: The Abel Era (2025-2026)

- **May 3, 2025**: At the annual meeting, the 94-year-old Warren Buffett announced he would ask the board to make **Greg Abel** (vice chairman, non-insurance operations, and long-designated successor) chief executive at year-end — ending a 60-year run as CEO.
- **January 1, 2026**: Abel formally became CEO; Buffett remains chairman of the board. Berkshire shares dipped modestly as the transition took effect (CNBC, January 2, 2026).
- **February 2026**: Berkshire's full-year 2025 results showed the ~$373 billion cash position; Abel's first letter emphasized continuity of capital-allocation discipline.
- **Q1 2026**: First quarter under Abel produced a record cash pile above $397 billion.

For traders, the succession is a live test of whether the "Buffett premium" in BRK's valuation persists under new management, and Berkshire's cash deployment (or buyback resumption) is one of the most-watched signals of perceived value in U.S. equities.

The annual **Berkshire Hathaway Shareholder Meeting** in Omaha, Nebraska -- dubbed "Woodstock for Capitalists" -- draws tens of thousands of attendees each year. Buffett's annual shareholder letters, written since 1965, are considered the best free education in business and investing available anywhere. [[charlie-munger]] served as Vice Chairman and Buffett's indispensable intellectual partner until his death in November 2023.

## Trading Relevance

- **BRK.B as a defensive equity proxy**: widely used as a lower-beta, insurance-float-levered compounding vehicle; AQR's "Buffett's Alpha" paper attributes the track record largely to quality/low-beta factor exposure plus ~1.6x float leverage (see [[aqr-capital]]).
- **13F signal**: Berkshire's quarterly 13F filings move the stocks it buys or exits; the 2024 Apple sales were among the most market-discussed position changes of the decade.
- **Cash-pile signal**: the record Treasury holdings are routinely cited as a market-valuation caution indicator.
- **Annual meeting and letters**: primary-source education on [[value-investing]], [[margin-of-safety]], and capital allocation.
