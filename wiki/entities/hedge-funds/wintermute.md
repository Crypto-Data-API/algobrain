# Wintermute

Wintermute is a crypto-native algorithmic market maker and one of the largest liquidity providers in the digital asset ecosystem. Operating across 50+ centralized and decentralized exchanges, Wintermute plays a critical role in maintaining orderly crypto markets.

## What Wintermute Does

Wintermute provides **liquidity** across the cryptocurrency market:

- **Centralized exchanges (CEX)**: Maintains order books on Binance, Coinbase, Kraken, OKX, and dozens of others by continuously placing buy and sell orders
- **Decentralized exchanges (DEX)**: Provides liquidity on Uniswap, Curve, dYdX, and other [[defi]] protocols through on-chain transactions
- **OTC trading**: Facilitates large block trades for institutional clients who need to move size without impacting the market
- **Token market making**: Partners with crypto projects to provide liquidity for newly listed tokens

## Scale and Position

Wintermute is one of the **three largest crypto market makers** alongside:

- **Cumberland** (DRW subsidiary) — established TradFi-backed operation
- **Alameda Research** (defunct) — collapsed alongside FTX in November 2022

With Alameda's demise, Wintermute and a handful of others absorbed the liquidity gap, increasing their market share significantly.

## The $160M Hack (2022)

In September 2022, Wintermute suffered a **$160 million exploit** targeting its DeFi operations. The attack exploited a vulnerability in vanity address generation (Profanity tool), allowing the attacker to derive private keys for Wintermute's DeFi wallet.

Key details:
- Only DeFi operations were affected; centralized exchange operations were unimpaired
- Wintermute remained solvent and continued operating
- The firm treated it as a "white hat" situation, offering a bounty for return of funds
- The hack highlighted the operational risks of running large-scale [[defi]] operations

## DeFi Governance

Beyond market making, Wintermute participates actively in DeFi governance:

- Holds governance tokens in major protocols
- Votes on protocol parameter changes, fee structures, and upgrades
- Acts as a delegate in protocols like Optimism, Arbitrum, and others
- This dual role (market maker + governance participant) has drawn some criticism regarding conflicts of interest

## Business Model

Wintermute profits from:

1. **Bid-ask spreads** on the thousands of trading pairs it quotes
2. **Token market making fees** charged to projects for liquidity provision
3. **OTC trading margins** on block trades
4. **DeFi yield** from liquidity provision and governance rewards
