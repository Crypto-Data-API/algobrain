# Two Sigma

Two Sigma Investments is a quantitative hedge fund that applies machine learning, distributed computing, and massive datasets to systematic trading strategies. With over $60 billion in assets under management, it is one of the largest and most technology-driven investment firms in the world.

## Founding and Philosophy

David Siegel (computer scientist) and John Overdeck (mathematician, former [[de-shaw]] employee) founded Two Sigma on the premise that rigorous application of technology and data science to financial markets could generate consistent returns. The name "Two Sigma" refers to two standard deviations — a statistical measure of outperformance.

The firm's culture sits at the intersection of a technology company and a hedge fund. Engineers and data scientists outnumber traditional finance professionals.

## Technology Approach

Two Sigma processes **petabytes of data** daily, including:

- Traditional market data (prices, volumes, order books)
- Alternative data (satellite imagery, credit card transactions, web scraping)
- News and social media sentiment
- Government filings and economic indicators

Their infrastructure relies on distributed computing frameworks, custom-built data pipelines, and [[ml-models]] that span classical statistics through deep learning.

## Venn: Open-Source Contribution

Two Sigma developed **Venn**, a free factor analysis tool that lets investors decompose their portfolio returns into common risk factors. It represents the firm's unusual willingness to share tools publicly — most quant funds are entirely secretive.

## Recruiting

Two Sigma recruits heavily from:

- Physics and mathematics PhD programs
- Computer science (systems, ML, distributed computing)
- Statistics and quantitative social sciences
- Competition math and programming olympiad backgrounds

The interview process emphasizes quantitative reasoning, coding ability, and statistical intuition over finance knowledge. They prefer to teach finance to scientists rather than teach science to financiers.

## Strategies

The firm runs multiple systematic strategies across:

- Global equities (long/short)
- Macro (currencies, rates, commodities)
- Quantitative alternatives
- Venture capital (Two Sigma Ventures)
- Insurance (Two Sigma ILS)
