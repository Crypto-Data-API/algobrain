AQR Capital Management is a Greenwich, Connecticut-based quantitative asset manager founded in 1998 by Cliff Asness, David Kabiller, Robert Krail, and John Liew, and one of the firms most responsible for popularizing systematic [[factor-investing]] (value, [[momentum]], carry, defensive/quality) in institutional portfolios. For traders, AQR matters both as a major systematic flow in markets and as the publisher of some of the most-cited practitioner research on factor premia, [[trend-following]], and portfolio construction.

## Overview

- **Founded**: 1998 by Cliff Asness, David Kabiller, Robert Krail, and John Liew (Asness and Liew came out of Goldman Sachs Asset Management's quantitative research group; Asness's University of Chicago PhD work under Eugene Fama helped establish the momentum factor literature).
- **Headquarters**: Greenwich, Connecticut, with offices globally.
- **AUM**: Assets recovered to roughly **$175 billion by October 2025** (Morningstar) and were reported at about **$189 billion firm-wide at end-2025**, with hedge-fund assets of **$109.1 billion — up 89% from $57.7 billion** over the growth period tracked by Alternative Fund Insight, which ranked AQR top of its 2025 hedge-fund "power list."
- **Style**: Systematic, research-driven strategies across equities, macro, fixed income, and alternatives — long/short factor portfolios, [[managed-futures]]/trend, risk parity, defensive equity, and multi-strategy.

## The Quant Winter and the Rebound

AQR's history is the canonical case study in factor-strategy drawdown and recovery:

- **1998-2017**: Growth into one of the world's largest quant managers (peak AUM above $200 billion in 2017-2018), built on academic factor premia delivered in liquid, fee-efficient vehicles.
- **2018-2020 ("quant winter")**: The [[value-factor]] suffered its worst drawdown on record; AQR's AUM roughly halved as value-tilted strategies underperformed and clients redeemed. Asness's essays from this period ("Never Has a Venial Sin Been Punished This Quickly and Brutally") argued the value spread had reached irrational extremes.
- **2021-2025**: The value spread normalized and AQR's multi-strategy and alternative funds produced strong returns, driving the AUM recovery described above.

## Trading Relevance

- **Research as a public good**: AQR's papers ("Value and Momentum Everywhere," "Time Series Momentum," "Betting Against Beta," "Buffett's Alpha") define how practitioners think about factor premia and are foundational reading for systematic strategy design (see [[edge-taxonomy]]).
- **Crowding signal**: AQR's AUM cycle (peak → halving → recovery) is the clearest observable proxy for capital in liquid factor strategies — relevant to [[crowding-risk|crowding]] assessment for value/momentum trades.
- **Buffett's Alpha**: AQR's decomposition of berkshire-hathaway returns into quality/low-beta factor exposure plus leverage is a key reference for evaluating discretionary track records.
- **Trend-following capacity**: AQR's managed-futures funds are among the largest systematic trend programs, making the firm a meaningful participant in futures-market flows during regime shifts.

## Key People

- **Cliff Asness** — co-founder, managing principal, CIO; prolific essayist on factor investing, market efficiency, and (more recently) the "less-efficient-markets" hypothesis attributing rising mispricing to social media and indexing.
- **David Kabiller** — co-founder, head of business development.
- **John Liew** — co-founder, portfolio management and research.
