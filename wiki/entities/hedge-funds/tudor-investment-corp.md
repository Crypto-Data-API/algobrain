# Tudor Investment Corp

**Tudor Investment Corp** is a hedge fund founded by [[paul-tudor-jones]] in 1980. The firm is known for [[global-macro]] trading and discretionary strategies.

Paul Tudor Jones famously predicted and profited from the [[1987-crash]], cementing Tudor's reputation. The firm blends macro analysis with technical trading and [[trend-following]] approaches.

## The 1987 Crash and "Trader" Documentary

Tudor Jones' fame was sealed by the 1987 PBS documentary *Trader*, which followed him in the months leading up to Black Monday. The film captured his analysis comparing 1987's market structure to the [[1929-crash]], and his subsequent massive short position that reportedly tripled his fund's money during the crash. Jones later attempted to buy back and suppress all copies of the documentary, adding to its mystique. It remains one of the most legendary trading stories in financial history.

## Firm Operations and Strategy

Tudor Investment Corp manages assets reported in the **$10–13 billion** range (2024–2025 filings and press reports vary; ~$12 billion is commonly cited) through its flagship BVI Global Fund and related vehicles. The firm's approach is primarily **macro discretionary** -- Jones and his team analyze global economic trends, central bank policy, geopolitical events, and market technicals to make large directional bets across currencies, bonds, equities, and commodities.

Jones is known for combining top-down macro conviction with disciplined [[risk-management]] and tight stop-losses. He has said his most important rule is "don't be a hero" -- cutting losses quickly and living to trade another day. The firm also incorporates [[quantitative-trading]] models to complement discretionary decisions.

## Bitcoin and Later Career

In 2020, [[paul-tudor-jones]] made headlines by publicly allocating to Bitcoin as an [[inflation]] hedge, comparing it to gold in the 1970s. His endorsement was a watershed moment for institutional crypto adoption. Jones described Bitcoin as "the fastest horse" in a race against currency debasement from central bank money printing.

### 2024–2026 Positioning

- **October 2024**: In a widely cited CNBC interview, Jones declared **"all roads lead to inflation"**, saying he was **long gold, long bitcoin, long commodities, and long Nasdaq, and owned zero fixed income** — arguing the US fiscal position makes inflating away the debt the path of least resistance.
- **2024–2025**: Jones repeatedly warned that US deficits (~6-7% of GDP) and the federal debt trajectory are unsustainable, framing gold and bitcoin as the core hedges against fiscal dominance. Tudor's 13F filings showed substantial positions in spot bitcoin ETF (IBIT) during 2024-2025.
- **2025**: Jones flagged parallels between the AI-driven equity rally and 1999, arguing the melt-up could run further but warning of correction risk once retail euphoria peaked — consistent with his trademark approach of riding trends with tight stops.

Tudor Investment Corp remains one of the premier [[global-macro]] firms, and Jones is widely regarded as one of the greatest traders of his generation.

## Trading Relevance

- Tudor's style — **macro thesis + technical timing + asymmetric risk-reward (Jones targets ~5:1)** — is the template most discretionary macro traders study. His 200-day moving average rule ("nothing good happens below the 200-day") is a widely used regime filter.
- Jones's public statements move markets at the margin: his 2020 bitcoin endorsement and October 2024 "all roads lead to inflation" interview were both followed by notable flows into BTC and gold.
- The 1987 *Trader* documentary remains required viewing for understanding crash positioning and the use of historical analogs in [[global-macro]].
