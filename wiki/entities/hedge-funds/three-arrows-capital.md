# Three Arrows Capital

**Three Arrows Capital (3AC)** was a crypto-focused hedge fund founded by **Kyle Davies** and **Su Zhu** in 2012, originally based in Singapore. At its peak, 3AC managed approximately $10 billion in assets and was one of the most prominent and aggressive players in the digital asset space.

## Rise and Fall

3AC built its reputation through early investments in major crypto projects and leveraged directional bets on Bitcoin and Ethereum. The fund operated with extreme [[leverage]], borrowing heavily from crypto lending platforms including [[genesis]], BlockFi, and Voyager Digital to amplify its positions.

The collapse of [[terra-luna]] in May 2022 dealt the first devastating blow -- 3AC had a massive position in Luna and its algorithmic stablecoin UST. As Luna went to zero, 3AC faced margin calls it could not meet. The fund also held large positions in Grayscale Bitcoin Trust (GBTC) at a significant discount to NAV, compounding losses.

## Default and Bankruptcy

In June 2022, 3AC defaulted on approximately **$3.5 billion** in loans from over 20 creditors. The fund filed for bankruptcy in the British Virgin Islands and subsequently in New York under Chapter 15. Founders Davies and Zhu fled Singapore and were uncooperative with liquidators for months. Zhu was eventually arrested in Singapore in September 2023 while attempting to leave the country.

3AC's collapse triggered a cascading wave of failures across the crypto lending ecosystem -- a textbook example of [[counterparty-risk]] and [[contagion]]. Voyager Digital, Celsius Network, and eventually [[genesis]] all filed for bankruptcy in the aftermath, deepening the [[crypto-winter]] of 2022.

## Liquidation and Aftermath (2022–2026)

A British Virgin Islands court appointed **Russell Crumpler and Christopher Farmer of Teneo** as liquidators in June 2022. Key developments since:

- **September 2023**: Su Zhu was arrested at Changi Airport in Singapore while attempting to leave the country, after a Singapore court issued a committal order for failing to cooperate with the liquidators. He was sentenced to four months and released in December 2023. Kyle Davies, who renounced US citizenship (holding Italian citizenship), avoided arrest; a US court had earlier held him in contempt for ignoring subpoenas.
- **2023–2024 — OPNX**: While creditors awaited recovery, Zhu and Davies launched **OPNX**, an exchange for trading bankruptcy claims (including 3AC's own creditors' claims), in April 2023. It was widely criticized, fined by Dubai's regulator VARA, and shut down in February 2024.
- **August 2024**: Teneo filed a **$1.3 billion claim against Terraform Labs** in its Chapter 11 bankruptcy, alleging Terraform's manipulation of the LUNA/UST market induced 3AC's losses (Decrypt, Cointelegraph).
- **October 7, 2025**: A US bankruptcy court approved a **settlement of the $1.3 billion Terraform claim**, classifying 3AC's losses as a "Crypto Loss Claim" in the Terraform estate — one of the largest single recovery items for 3AC creditors (TheStreet).
- The liquidators were also permitted by a Delaware court in 2025 to pursue an expanded **$1.53 billion claim against the FTX estate** (up from an original $120 million), and continue pursuing claims against Zhu and Davies personally. The overall recovery remains an ongoing multibillion-dollar process as of mid-2026.

## Trading Relevance

3AC is the canonical case study in crypto [[leverage]] and [[counterparty-risk]]:

- **Uncollateralized lending kills lenders**: 3AC borrowed billions on thin or no collateral from yield-hungry lenders (Genesis alone had ~$2.3B of exposure). When the borrower is the largest client, lenders stop underwriting.
- **Correlated collateral**: GBTC-at-a-discount, stETH-at-a-discount, and locked LUNA all sold off together in 2022 — "diversified" positions that were one trade: long crypto beta with leverage.
- **Contagion mapping**: After Terra collapsed in May 2022, traders who mapped 3AC's creditor web correctly anticipated the Voyager, Celsius, BlockFi, and Genesis failures — and the further leg down in BTC/ETH. Monitoring large funds' on-chain wallets and lender concentration remains a key crypto risk signal.
