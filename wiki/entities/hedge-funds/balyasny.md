Balyasny Asset Management (BAM) is a Chicago-headquartered multi-strategy hedge fund founded in 2001 by Dmitry Balyasny, together with co-founders Scott Schroeder and Taylor O'Malley. It is one of the "big four" multi-strategy [[pod-shop|pod shops]] alongside [[citadel]], [[millennium-management]], and [[point72]], running roughly $25-28 billion across approximately 170 portfolio-manager teams as of 2025 — making it a significant source of the market-neutral, tightly risk-managed flow that dominates modern equity and macro markets.

## Overview

- **Founded**: 2001, Chicago. Dmitry Balyasny began as a discretionary trader at Schonfeld Securities in the 1990s before launching BAM.
- **Structure**: Classic multi-manager platform — capital is allocated across many independent portfolio teams (long/short equities, macro, commodities, fixed income, systematic, growth equity) under centralized risk limits, with tight drawdown rules and pass-through fee economics.
- **Scale (2025)**: Approximately **$25 billion AUM** per 2025 reporting (a later 2025 interview described the firm at ~$28 billion), about **2,300 employees** and **~170 portfolio teams** (Dmitry Balyasny, Masters in Business interview, September 2025).
- **Flagship fund**: Atlas Enhanced. Bloomberg reported the firm's main fund returned about **15.2% in 2023**; Bloomberg's 2025 analysis of hedge-fund fees used BAM among its examples of how pass-through expenses at multistrats consume a large share of gross returns.
- **Recognition**: Institutional Investor honored Dmitry Balyasny with a 2025 hedge-fund Lifetime Achievement award.

## History and Evolution

BAM's defining episode came in **2018**, when the flagship lost ~7% and assets fell from ~$12 billion toward ~$6 billion. Dmitry Balyasny circulated an internal memo ("we are getting our butts kicked") that famously leaked, and [[citadel]]'s Ken Griffin displayed it to his own staff as a cautionary example. The firm responded by rebuilding its equities business around tighter factor-neutral risk discipline, expanding macro and commodities, and adding centralized risk and data infrastructure — a turnaround that took AUM from ~$6 billion in 2019 to ~$25+ billion by the mid-2020s.

## Trading Relevance

- **Pod-shop flow**: BAM and its multistrat peers run high gross leverage against market-neutral books. Their simultaneous de-grossing episodes (e.g., January 2021 during the [[gamestop-short-squeeze|GameStop squeeze]], and factor unwinds in 2024-2025) produce sharp, correlated moves in crowded longs/shorts — a regime traders monitor via prime-broker crowding data.
- **Talent market signal**: BAM competes in the multistrat talent war (guaranteed packages, pass-through fees), a structural force pushing hedge-fund economics toward platform models; see [[multi-strategy-funds]].
- **Risk model**: The platform's tight stop-out discipline (PMs typically cut at low single-digit drawdowns) is a practical template for the [[risk-management-overview|risk management]] frameworks individual traders adapt.
