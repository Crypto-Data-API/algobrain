Millennium Management is one of the world's largest multi-strategy hedge funds, founded in 1989 by **Israel "Izzy" Englander** with $35 million. With roughly **$79-84 billion in AUM (late 2025 / January 2026)** allocated across **330+ independent trading teams ("pods")**, Millennium — alongside [[citadel|Citadel]] and [[point72|Point72]] — defines the [[pod-shop|pod-shop]] model that dominates modern discretionary trading and sets the market for trading talent.

## Overview

Millennium pioneered the multi-manager architecture: capital is allocated to hundreds of autonomous teams pursuing fundamental long/short equity, [[statistical-arbitrage]], fixed income/macro, commodities, and quantitative strategies, each operating under tight centralized [[risk-management]] (drawdown limits typically force a 50% capital cut at ~5% loss and termination near ~7.5-10%). The result is a highly diversified, [[market-neutral]]-leaning return stream levered roughly 5-6x at the platform level.

Key facts and recent history:

- **Track record**: approximately 14% annualized since 1989 with only one losing year (2008, about -3%). 2024 return: **+15%**; 2025 return: approximately **+10.5%** (per industry performance roundups, vs. Citadel ~10.2% and Point72 ~17.5%).
- **Pod growth**: from ~265 pods in 2020 to **330+ pods by late 2025**, with several thousand investment professionals globally.
- **March 2025**: lost ~**$900 million (-1.3%)** in a single month — its first monthly loss exceeding 1% since 2018 — driven partly by missed [[index-rebalancing]] bets, a reminder that even diversified pod platforms carry crowded-trade exposure.
- **Fee model**: Millennium popularized "pass-through" expenses (clients pay compensation and costs directly, often equating to 5-7% per year plus performance fees) — now the industry standard among top multi-strats.
- **Succession / stake sale**: Englander (b. 1948) has no named successor. In 2024 talks with BlackRock about a minority stake were reported; in **November 2025 Bloomberg reported Millennium's minority stake sale** to outside investors as preparation "for life after Izzy Englander," with reported valuations around $14 billion. The firm has also moved to longer-term capital lockups (5-year share classes) to stabilize its asset base.

## Trading Relevance

- **The marginal price-setter in many micro-strategies.** With 330+ pods running tight risk, Millennium (and peers) are frequently the other side of short-horizon equity, ETF-arb, and event trades. Crowding among pod shops is now a recognized [[market-regime]] risk: simultaneous de-grossing by multi-strats amplifies factor unwinds (e.g., the March 2025 index-rebalance episode and prior de-grossing waves).
- **Talent and capacity benchmark.** Millennium's guaranteed-payout packages set the clearing price for portfolio managers; its pod economics (cut at -5%, fired at -7.5%) explain why pod-shop trading is tightly mean-reverting and low-vol — useful context when reverse-engineering institutional flow behavior.
- **Index-rebalancing case study.** The March 2025 loss is a documented example of [[index-rebalancing]] arbitrage capacity limits — a strategy this wiki tracks.
- **Business-model signal.** Pass-through fees, multi-year lockups, and external stake sales mark the institutionalization (and possible peak) of the multi-strategy boom — relevant to allocators assessing diminishing returns at $80B+ scale.

## Key People

- **Israel "Izzy" Englander** — Founder, Chairman, CEO. Started on the AMEX floor; co-founded Jamie Securities before launching Millennium in 1989.
