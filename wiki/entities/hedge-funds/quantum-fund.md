# Quantum Fund

The Quantum Fund (originally the Soros Fund) was the flagship hedge fund managed by [[george-soros]] through Soros Fund Management, and one of the most successful investment vehicles in history. From its founding in 1973 to 2000, it generated average annual returns of approximately 30%, turning $1,000 into over $4 million.

## Overview

Co-founded by Soros and Jim Rogers (who left in 1980), the Quantum Fund pioneered the global macro strategy — taking large, leveraged positions in currencies, [[commodities]], bonds, and equities based on macroeconomic analysis. The fund's name was inspired by Heisenberg's uncertainty principle, reflecting Soros's belief that market participants' observations change the markets themselves ([[reflexivity]]).

## Notable Trades

- **1992 — [[black-wednesday]]**: The fund's most famous trade. Soros shorted approximately $10 billion worth of British pounds, earning roughly $1 billion when the UK was forced to exit the European Exchange Rate Mechanism.
- **1992 — Italian lira**: Simultaneously profited from the devaluation of the Italian lira during the ERM crisis.
- **1997 — Asian currencies**: Traded against Thai baht and other Asian currencies during the Asian Financial Crisis, generating profits but also intense political backlash.
- **1998 — Russian crisis**: Lost approximately $2 billion during the Russian ruble crisis and [[ltcm]] collapse.
- **2000 — [[dot-com-bubble]]**: Suffered roughly $3 billion in losses after the tech bubble burst.

## From Quantum Fund to Family Office

After the 2000 dot-com losses, Soros restructured the fund as the lower-risk **Quantum Endowment Fund**. In July 2011, Soros Fund Management returned all outside investor capital (roughly $1 billion of ~$25.5 billion then managed) and converted to a **family office**, partly to avoid registration requirements under the Dodd-Frank Act. Key milestones since:

- **2017** — Soros transferred the bulk of his fortune (~$18 billion) to the Open Society Foundations; Soros Fund Management remains the principal asset manager for OSF.
- **2017–present** — **Dawn Fitzpatrick** has served as Chief Investment Officer (later CEO & CIO), running the portfolio across public equities, credit, macro, and private investments.
- **June 2023** — George Soros (then 92) handed control of the Soros empire to his son **Alexander Soros**, who chairs both the Open Society Foundations and Soros Fund Management.
- **AUM**: approximately **$25 billion** as of 2023; the firm describes itself as "a global asset manager and family office."

## Trading Relevance

The Quantum Fund's track record demonstrates both the extraordinary potential and the inherent risks of leveraged [[macro-trading]]. Its ~30% annualized returns over nearly three decades remain the benchmark against which discretionary [[global-macro]] managers are measured, and its history of massive wins followed by multi-billion-dollar drawdowns (1998 Russia, 2000 tech) illustrates that even the greatest traders experience painful losses. For traders, the fund's legacy contributions are:

- **The [[black-wednesday]] template** — identifying unsustainable currency pegs and sizing aggressively when the asymmetry is extreme (limited loss if the peg holds, huge gain if it breaks)
- **[[reflexivity]]** — Soros's framework that market prices feed back into the fundamentals they supposedly reflect, producing boom-bust cycles tradeable in both directions
- **Position sizing doctrine** — Soros's dictum that "it's not whether you're right or wrong, but how much you make when you're right and how much you lose when you're wrong"

The fund converted to a family office in 2011, ending outside investor access, but its legacy defines the global macro strategy category.
