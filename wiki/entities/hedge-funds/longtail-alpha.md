LongTail Alpha is a Newport Beach-based investment manager founded in 2015 by **Vineer Bhansali**, the former PIMCO portfolio manager and author of the practitioner textbook *Tail Risk Hedging* (McGraw-Hill, 2014). The firm runs explicit long-volatility and tail-risk strategies for institutional clients and is frequently described in industry coverage as the "second tail fund" alongside [[universa-investments|Universa]] -- a comparable convex-hedge mandate run with a different intellectual lineage and a more quantitative, factor-aware lens.

## Overview

Bhansali spent 16+ years at PIMCO, where he co-managed the firm's tail-risk hedging program and built much of the institutional infrastructure for systematic long-vol overlays. He left in 2015 to found LongTail Alpha as an independent boutique focused exclusively on convex hedging, asymmetric risk premia, and crisis-alpha strategies. The firm's pitch to allocators is essentially the same as Universa's -- a small allocation to convex protection raises the geometric return of the combined portfolio -- but with greater willingness to combine pure tail hedges with related convex factor exposures (rates volatility, currency convexity, dispersion).

LongTail Alpha is materially smaller than Universa in AUM (low single-digit billions versus Universa's $16B+ in assets under advisement), but it has been profiled in the *Wall Street Journal*, *Bloomberg*, and *Barron's* repeatedly as one of the small handful of dedicated tail funds that successfully monetized the [[covid-crash|March 2020 COVID crash]] for institutional clients.

**Status as of June 2026**: the firm remains active, with Bhansali as Founder and CIO. Beyond the core tail-hedge mandate, regulatory filings show the firm also runs systematic vehicles such as the *LongTail Alpha Enhanced Systematic Trend Following Fund LP*, consistent with its stated focus areas of portfolio construction, tail-risk management, and momentum. Bhansali continues to publish frequently (Forbes columns, firm research notes at longtailalpha.com).

## Strategy / Mechanics

LongTail Alpha runs a multi-strategy long-vol book whose core is similar to Universa's but with broader factor coverage:

- **Equity tail core**: Continuous ladders of OTM SPX puts and put spreads, sized so that a 25-35% equity drawdown produces a multiple-of-premium payoff.
- **VIX optionality**: [[vix-calls|VIX call]] structures and VIX call spreads chosen to capture the [[volatility-of-volatility|vol-of-vol]] convexity that pure SPX puts do not always pick up cleanly.
- **Rates and FX convexity**: Bhansali has written extensively about [[interest-rate-volatility|rates vol]] and currency convexity as cheap sources of crisis-alpha. The firm has used swaptions, payer/receiver structures, and FX options as complements to the equity tail.
- **Risk-premia overlay (separate strategy)**: LongTail Alpha also runs alpha strategies that are not pure tail hedges -- factor-tilt and dispersion strategies that are intended to reduce the carry cost of the tail hedge.
- **Dynamic monetization**: The firm sells convexity into shocks rather than holding to expiration, locking in P&L during VIX spikes and re-establishing the hedge after vol mean-reverts.

The book is **net long premium, net long vega, net long gamma** -- by design. See [[long-volatility-strategies]] and [[long-vol-vs-short-vol]] for the doctrinal context.

## Track Record

Public reporting on LongTail Alpha's returns is partial (the funds are private), but several waypoints are documented:

- **2018 Volmageddon**: The firm's tail-hedging strategy was reported to have produced strongly positive returns during the [[volmageddon|February 2018 vol shock]] that destroyed [[ljm-preservation-and-growth|LJM]] and [[xiv-velocity-shares|XIV]]. Bhansali was widely quoted in financial media in the days after the event.
- **March 2020 COVID Crash**: LongTail Alpha's tail strategy reportedly delivered multi-hundred-percent returns during the [[covid-crash|February-March 2020 crash]]. Bhansali was profiled in the *WSJ* and *Bloomberg* as one of the managers who had warned for years about complacent VIX positioning before the event.
- **Calm regimes (2017, 2019, 2021, 2023)**: Like all tail funds, the strategy bleeds premium in calm regimes. Public commentary from Bhansali has consistently framed this as a feature, not a bug -- the cost of insurance.
- **August 2024 VIX spike**: The yen-carry-unwind shock ([[vix-august-2024-spike]]) was a meaningful but smaller monetization event for long-vol books; LongTail Alpha was among the funds reported to have delivered positive returns.

## Crisis Performance

LongTail Alpha and Universa are usually cited together as the two dedicated tail funds that performed as designed during the major shocks of the post-2015 era. A few features distinguish LongTail Alpha's profile:

- **Broader convexity sources** -- not all crisis-alpha came from SPX puts. In some shocks (e.g., regional bank stress), rates and FX convexity contributed alongside equity puts.
- **Active monetization** -- Bhansali has emphasized that the harder problem is not buying tail hedges but selling them at the right moment during a shock. Premature monetization gives back too much; late monetization watches profits decay.
- **Quantitative signal overlay** -- the firm uses systematic signals (e.g., [[skew]] regime, term-structure inversion, cross-asset stress measures) to size hedges through the cycle.

This mix means the carry cost in calm regimes can be lower than a pure SPX-put book, at the cost of slightly less explosive payoff in a pure equity crash. Allocators who want the maximum convex equity beta typically pair LongTail Alpha with a more concentrated SPX-put hedger; those who want broader protection use it as a stand-alone convex sleeve.

## Why It Matters / Lessons

1. **Long vol is not a one-firm strategy.** The widespread perception of [[universa-investments|Universa]] as the only credible long-vol manager understates the institutional ecosystem. LongTail Alpha demonstrates that disciplined, well-executed tail hedging can work outside a single house style.
2. **Bhansali's *Tail Risk Hedging* (2014) is the practitioner companion to Spitznagel's *Safe Haven*.** Where Spitznagel argues philosophically and historically, Bhansali writes the operations manual: how to structure ladders, how to roll, how to monetize, how to budget the bleed. The two together cover the long-vol playbook.
3. **Convexity comes in multiple flavors.** Equity puts are the most familiar convex hedge but are not always the cheapest convex exposure on the board. Rates and FX vol can carry better in certain regimes and contribute to crisis-alpha in different shocks.
4. **The same core insight as Universa.** A small allocation (3-5% of NAV) to a convex hedge can offset a large drawdown in an equity-heavy book. The math of [[geometric-mean]] compounding rewards survival.
