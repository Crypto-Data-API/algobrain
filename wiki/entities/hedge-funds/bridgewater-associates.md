# Bridgewater Associates

**Bridgewater Associates** is one of the world's largest hedge funds, founded by [[ray-dalio]] in 1975 and managing roughly **$92 billion** as of 2025. The firm is known for its [[global-macro]] strategies, [[risk-parity]] investing, and radical transparency culture; its flagship Pure Alpha fund and [[all-weather-portfolio]] strategy have been widely influential in institutional portfolio management.

## Fund Strategies and Scale

With over $150 billion in assets under management at its peak (and approximately $92 billion as of 2025), Bridgewater primarily serves institutional investors including pension funds, endowments, sovereign wealth funds, and central banks (Source: [[book-more-money-than-god]]). The firm runs two main strategies:

- **Pure Alpha** -- a [[global-macro]] systematic fund that takes positions across currencies, bonds, equities, and commodities based on fundamental economic analysis encoded into systematic models (Source: [[book-more-money-than-god]]). Pure Alpha aims to generate alpha uncorrelated to market direction. In **2025 Pure Alpha returned roughly 33% net** -- reported as the best performance in the fund's 50-year history.
- **All Weather** -- a [[risk-parity]] strategy that balances risk across asset classes (stocks, bonds, commodities, inflation-linked bonds) rather than balancing dollar amounts. The goal is a portfolio that performs reasonably well in any economic environment -- growth, recession, inflation, or deflation.

## Culture of Radical Transparency

Bridgewater is equally famous for its unusual corporate culture, detailed in [[ray-dalio]]'s book *Principles*. The firm practices "radical transparency" -- meetings are recorded, employees rate each other openly, and disagreement is encouraged through a system of "believability-weighted decision-making." This culture has been polarizing: some view it as a meritocratic ideal, while critics describe it as cult-like and psychologically intense.

## Leadership Transition (2022-2025)

Dalio stepped back from management in 2022, transitioning the firm to new leadership. **Nir Bar Dea** became sole CEO in March 2023. The transition completed on **July 31, 2025**, when Dalio **sold his last remaining equity stake and stepped off the board**, ending 50 years of involvement; Bridgewater bought back his shares, and Brunei's sovereign wealth fund (Brunei Investment Agency) separately took a stake of nearly 20% in the firm. Bridgewater's Westport, Connecticut headquarters and its macro systematic approach have made it one of the most studied and debated institutions in the hedge fund world.

## Trading Relevance

- **Risk parity flows**: All Weather popularized [[risk-parity]]; when volatility spikes, risk-parity deleveraging is a recognized cross-asset flow that traders monitor (e.g., February 2018, March 2020).
- **Macro research signal**: Bridgewater's *Daily Observations* and public research (debt cycles, "beautiful deleveraging") shape institutional macro consensus; Dalio's public commentary (e.g., 2025 warnings on debt and an AI-driven equity bubble) moves narrative even after his exit.
- **Positioning**: As one of the largest holders of ETFs and macro instruments, Bridgewater's 13F changes are widely tracked, though its true book is mostly derivatives-based and not visible in filings.
