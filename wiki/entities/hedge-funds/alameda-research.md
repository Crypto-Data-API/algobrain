# Alameda Research

**Alameda Research** was a quantitative cryptocurrency trading firm founded in 2017 by [[sam-bankman-fried|Sam Bankman-Fried (SBF)]], initially focused on crypto [[arbitrage]] before expanding into market making, venture investing, and leveraged directional trading. Alameda was at the center of the [[ftx|FTX]] fraud -- secretly receiving **$8+ billion** in FTX customer deposits to cover its trading losses, a scheme that led to the collapse of both entities in November 2022.

## Origins

Alameda's early business was **cross-border [[arbitrage]]** -- exploiting the "kimchi premium" (higher BTC prices on Korean exchanges) and similar dislocations. This legitimate and profitable strategy built SBF's initial reputation and capital base. Alameda then became the primary market maker on FTX, creating a deeply intertwined and conflicted relationship.

---

## The Fraud

The core of the fraud was a secret, undisclosed pipeline between FTX and Alameda:
- FTX customer deposits were funneled to Alameda through a backdoor in FTX's systems
- Alameda used the funds for leveraged trades, venture investments (often in illiquid tokens), political donations, and real estate
- **FTT** (FTX's exchange token) was used as collateral for loans between the entities -- creating circular, fragile dependencies
- When [[terra-luna|Terra/LUNA]] collapsed in May 2022, Alameda suffered massive losses but concealed them using FTX customer funds

Caroline Ellison pleaded guilty and cooperated with prosecutors. She received a 2-year sentence.

---

## Legal Aftermath and FTX Repayments (2024-2026)

- **March 28, 2024**: [[sam-bankman-fried|Sam Bankman-Fried]] was sentenced to **25 years in prison** and ordered to forfeit ~$11 billion; his conviction appeal remains pending as of mid-2026.
- **September 24, 2024**: Caroline Ellison (Alameda CEO) sentenced to **2 years**; co-conspirators Gary Wang and Nishad Singh received no prison time for cooperation; FTX Digital Markets co-CEO Ryan Salame received 7.5 years.
- **2025-2026 creditor distributions**: The FTX Recovery Trust, having recovered an estimated **$14.7-16.5 billion** in assets, repaid creditors in successive rounds — **$454 million (February 18, 2025)**, **$5 billion (May 30, 2025)**, **$1.6 billion (September 30, 2025)**, and **$2.2 billion (March 31, 2026)** — roughly **$10 billion total** by mid-2026. Claims are paid in dollar terms at November 2022 petition-date crypto prices, so creditors received "100%+ of claims" in nominal terms while forgoing the subsequent crypto rally — a much-debated outcome among traders who bought FTX claims at 10-20 cents in 2023.

## Trading Relevance

- Alameda's collapse demonstrated how a market maker's failure can destabilize an entire exchange and ecosystem
- The FTX/Alameda entanglement is a textbook case of **conflicts of interest** between exchanges and affiliated trading firms
- Post-FTX, traders demand greater transparency about exchange market makers and proprietary trading relationships
- Alameda's early arbitrage strategies were legitimate and well-documented -- a reminder that the line between legitimate quantitative trading and fraud is about ethics, not sophistication
