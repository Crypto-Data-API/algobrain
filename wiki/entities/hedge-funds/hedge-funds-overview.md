# Hedge Funds Overview

Notable hedge funds, quantitative firms, and investment-management companies. **[[hedge-funds|Hedge funds]]** are private, lightly regulated pools of accredited-investor capital that use [[leverage]], [[short-selling]], and [[derivatives]] to pursue absolute (market-uncorrelated) returns, typically under a "2-and-20" fee model. They are the apex participants of financial markets -- deploying the most capital, attracting top talent, and pioneering strategies that later trickle down to smaller firms and retail traders.

Understanding how [[renaissance-technologies]] uses [[statistical-arbitrage]], how [[citadel]] dominates market-making and runs a multi-strategy book, and how [[bridgewater-associates]] applies systematic [[global-macro]] principles gives insight into what the most sophisticated participants are actually doing -- and where crowding and forced deleveraging create opportunities and risks.
