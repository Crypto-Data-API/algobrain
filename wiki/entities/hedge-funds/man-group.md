Man Group plc is the world's largest publicly listed hedge fund manager, with **$228.7 billion in assets under management as of 31 March 2026**. Headquartered in London and listed on the London Stock Exchange (ticker EMG), the firm is best known to traders for **Man AHL**, one of the founding houses of systematic [[trend-following]] — AHL's performance is still treated as a benchmark for the entire [[managed-futures]] / [[cta|CTA]] industry.

## Overview

Man Group traces its history to 1783, when James Man founded a sugar cooperage and brokerage in London; the firm held the British Royal Navy's rum-supply contract for over 150 years. It evolved through commodities broking into alternative asset management, listing on the London Stock Exchange in 1994 and joining (and later leaving) the FTSE 100; today it is a FTSE 250 constituent.

Key milestones:

- **1987** — AHL (Adam, Harding & Lueck) founded by Michael Adam, [[david-harding|David Harding]] and Martin Lueck as a pioneering systematic trend-following shop. Man bought a majority stake in 1989 and full ownership in 1994. Harding left and later founded [[winton-group|Winton]]; Adam and Lueck co-founded Aspect Capital.
- **2010** — acquired GLG Partners (discretionary strategies) for $1.6 billion.
- **2012** — acquired FRM (fund-of-funds / managed accounts).
- **2014** — acquired Numeric Investors (Boston quant equity), now Man Numeric.
- **2023** — acquired a controlling stake in US private credit manager Varagon Capital Partners (~$2.4B deal value), extending into private markets.
- **September 2023** — **Robyn Grew** became CEO, the first female chief executive of a major listed hedge fund firm, succeeding Luke Ellis.
- **FY 2025** — record net inflows of ~$29 billion; year-end AUM $227.6 billion, rising to $228.7 billion by 31 March 2026.

The investment engines are **Man AHL** (systematic macro/trend, the quant engine), **Man Numeric** (systematic equities), **Man GLG** (discretionary long/short and credit), **Man FRM** (solutions/managed accounts) and **Man Group's private markets / credit units** (including Varagon). The firm also runs the Oxford-Man Institute partnership with Oxford University, a notable academic-industry pipeline for [[machine-learning]] in finance.

## Trading Relevance

- **AHL is the reference CTA.** AHL Diversified and AHL Dimension/Alpha/Evolution programs are among the longest live track records in [[trend-following]]; allocators and researchers use AHL (alongside Winton, Aspect, and the SG CTA Index members) to judge whether [[momentum]] in futures is alive or decaying. The 1987-founded AHL lineage is the direct ancestor of much of today's CTA industry.
- **Capacity and crowding signal.** As the largest listed manager running systematic strategies at $200B+ scale, Man's flow disclosures (quarterly AUM statements, required as a public company) are a rare transparent window into institutional appetite for quant strategies — useful for assessing [[crowding-risk|crowding]] in trend and quant-equity factors.
- **Public-company economics of hedge funds.** EMG's share price reacts to performance-fee generation, making it a tradeable proxy on hedge-fund-industry health; FY2025 results showed record inflows but margin pressure, a recurring theme in fee compression across the industry.
- **Research output.** Man Institute publications (e.g., on trend-following in inflationary regimes, "The Best Strategies for Inflationary Times") are widely cited practitioner research relevant to [[regime-detection]] and strategy design.
