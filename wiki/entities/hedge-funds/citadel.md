# Citadel

Citadel is a global financial institution founded by [[ken-griffin]] in 1990, operating two distinct but related businesses: Citadel LLC (one of the world's largest and best-performing [[hedge-funds]]) and Citadel Securities (one of the dominant [[market-maker|market makers]] in U.S. equities and options). The firm's dual role as both hedge fund and market maker has made it one of the most powerful and controversial entities in modern finance.

## Overview

**Citadel LLC** (the hedge fund) manages approximately **$66 billion in investment capital as of February 1, 2026** (after returning roughly $5 billion of 2025 profits to investors) and employs multi-strategy approaches across equities, fixed income, [[commodities]], credit, and quantitative strategies (Source: [[book-the-quants]]). It has been one of the top-performing hedge funds over the past decade, and its 2022 gain of ~$16 billion was reported as the largest single-year dollar profit in hedge fund history. The firm relocated its headquarters from Chicago to Miami in 2022.

**Citadel Securities** (the market-making arm, a legally separate firm also controlled by Griffin) executes roughly 25-40% of all U.S. retail equity order flow and is a major participant in [[options]] markets. It generates revenue through the [[bid-ask-spread]] and [[payment-for-order-flow]] (PFOF) arrangements with brokers like [[robinhood]].

## Recent Performance (2023-2025)

- **2024**: Flagship multi-strategy **Wellington fund +15.1%**; all five underlying strategies (commodities, equities, fixed income, credit, quantitative) were positive. Tactical trading fund +22.3%, equities fund ~+18%, global fixed income +9.7%.
- **2025**: Wellington **+10.2%** — a solid but more subdued year; Citadel planned to return ~$5 billion of profits to investors in early 2026, trimming investment capital to roughly $66-67 billion.
- Ken Griffin remains founder and CEO of Citadel and majority owner of both businesses.

## GameStop Controversy

During the [[gamestop-short-squeeze]] (January 2021), Citadel (the hedge fund) and Point72 injected $2.75 billion into [[melvin-capital]] to shore up its collapsing short position. Simultaneously, Citadel Securities was the largest PFOF customer of [[robinhood]], which controversially restricted GME buying. While Griffin testified that Citadel did not influence Robinhood's decision, the perceived conflict of interest intensified scrutiny of [[payment-for-order-flow]] and [[market-structure]].

## Trading Relevance

Citadel's dominance in market making means it is a counterparty to a significant portion of retail trades. Understanding how PFOF, internalization, and [[market-structure]] work is essential for traders evaluating execution quality. The firm's influence on [[liquidity]], pricing, and order routing makes it a central entity in debates about market fairness. As the archetypal multi-strategy "pod shop," Citadel (with [[millennium-management]] and [[point72]]) also drives the crowding and deleveraging dynamics that can amplify factor unwinds across equities.
