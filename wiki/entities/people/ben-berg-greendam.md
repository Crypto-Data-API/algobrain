Ben Berggreen is a retired institutional trader and Senior Trading Mentor at the Institute of Trading and Portfolio Management (ITPM), featured in Anton Kreil's "Trading Legends" interview series. Over a roughly 20-year buy-side career he traded for Franklin Templeton, Nuveen Investments, and Nuveen's boutique affiliate Tradewinds Global, where he rose to Senior Vice President of Institutional Trading. For traders he is a source of practitioner-level insight into institutional execution, futures and options trading, and quantitative systems development.

## Overview

Per his ITPM mentor biography and Trading Legends interview (itpm.com):

- **Unusual entry**: Berggreen spent five years as a commercial airline pilot before switching to finance.
- **Franklin Templeton**: joined the trading team in the United States, where he traded for Templeton's European Equity Value portfolios.
- **UK and Asia coverage**: after building what ITPM describes as an industry-leading track record at Pacific in the UK, he was hired by Chicago-based **Nuveen Investments** to cover the Asian region.
- **Tradewinds Global (2008-2015)**: relocated to Southern California in 2008 to head Asian coverage for Nuveen's international boutique affiliate **Tradewinds Global Investors**. During his tenure the firm grew from roughly **$10B to $40B AUM**, and he became **Senior Vice President of Institutional Trading**.
- **Retirement (2015)**: retired after about twenty years in professional trading and portfolio management; lives in Los Angeles. He has since been contracted on a part-time consultancy basis by hedge funds and continues to manage his own portfolio as a quantitative systems developer and **futures and options** trader.
- **ITPM**: joined ITPM as a **Senior Trading Mentor**, mentoring mostly US and Canadian Institute traders and managing the Institute's global portfolio that allocates capital to profitable students.

## Trading relevance

- His Trading Legends interview is part of the [[itpm-professional-traders-amazing-advice]] material in this wiki — institutional perspective on execution, position sizing, and career path.
- Representative of the **institutional execution trader** archetype: value-fund order flow, Asia coverage, and the transition from discretionary buy-side trading to personal quantitative futures/options systems after retirement.
- ITPM content featuring Berggreen covers topics such as quants and AI in trading, big funds vs small hedge funds, and "intellectual snobbery" in trading.
