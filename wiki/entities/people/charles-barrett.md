Charles Barrett is an Australian investor known to Fred McNaught, whose crash-buying buy list Fred copied during the [[crash-of-1987|1987 stock market crash]]. When the market collapsed in October 1987, Barrett had a prepared list of quality companies to buy at distressed prices — a disciplined approach that Fred adopted and which shaped his long-term investment philosophy.

Fred references Barrett's crash-buying strategy as a formative influence on his own approach to [[market-crashes|market crashes]]. The lesson was that crashes are buying opportunities for prepared investors — those who have pre-identified quality businesses and the conviction to act when others are panicking. Barrett's buy list methodology aligns with Fred's broader teaching that investment success comes from preparation and discipline, not from predicting market timing.
