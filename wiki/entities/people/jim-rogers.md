Jim Rogers (born October 19, 1942) is an American investor and author, co-founder of the Quantum Fund with [[george-soros|George Soros]] and one of the best-known commodity bulls of the modern era. For traders he matters in three ways: as the creator of the Rogers International Commodity Index (RICI), as the original modern champion of the long-commodities/long-Asia secular thesis, and as a perennially quotable contrarian whose crash warnings serve as a sentiment data point.

## Overview

Rogers was born in Baltimore and raised in Demopolis, Alabama. He studied history at Yale (BA, 1964) and PPE at Balliol College, Oxford, before entering Wall Street. In 1973 he co-founded the **Quantum Fund** with George Soros; over the 1970s the portfolio reportedly gained roughly 4,200% while the S&P 500 rose about 47%. Rogers left the fund in 1980, at age 37, declaring himself "retired," and subsequently taught finance at Columbia Business School.

He then became famous for two record-setting investing road trips: a 1990-1992 round-the-world motorcycle journey (chronicled in *Investment Biker*, 1994) and a 1999-2002 drive through 116 countries (*Adventure Capitalist*, 2003). In 1998 he launched the **Rogers International Commodity Index (RICI)**, a broad consumption-weighted commodity index spanning energy, metals, and agriculture that still underlies tradeable products. His 2004 book *Hot Commodities* laid out the secular commodity-supercycle argument — chronic underinvestment in supply meeting emerging-market demand — that anticipated the 2000s commodity boom.

In 2007 Rogers sold his New York home and moved to **Singapore**, on the thesis that "the 19th century belonged to Britain, the 20th century to the United States, and the 21st century belongs to Asia." Other books include *A Bull in China* (2007), *A Gift to My Children* (2009), and *Street Smarts* (2013). He is chairman of Beeland Interests, Inc.

## Status as of 2026

Rogers, 83, remains an active commentator from Singapore. In interviews in early 2026 he said he had **sold essentially all of his stock holdings** a few months earlier and was positioned mostly in **US dollar cash, gold and silver**, with small positions in China and Uzbekistan; he reported recently adding to **silver**, which he views as cheap relative to [[gold]]. He has spent much of 2025-2026 warning that record global debt levels will produce "the worst bear market of my lifetime." (Verified via Perplexity, 2026-06-10.)

## Relevance to Trading

- **Commodity exposure vehicles**: the RICI family of indices remains a benchmark for broad commodity allocation, distinct from GSCI/BCOM in its wider agricultural weighting.
- **Secular framework**: Rogers' supply-underinvestment argument ([[capex-cycle]]) is the template for every subsequent commodity-supercycle thesis, including the 2020s energy/metals underinvestment narrative.
- **Sentiment calibration**: Rogers has predicted imminent crashes almost continuously for over a decade; his warnings are best treated as a contrarian/perma-bear sentiment input rather than a timing signal. His concrete *positioning* changes (e.g., the 2026 move to cash and silver) are more informative than his rhetoric.
- **Asia/frontier angle**: his early, public moves into China, Singapore, and frontier markets (Uzbekistan) flag where long-horizon macro investors hunt for demographic and development premia.
