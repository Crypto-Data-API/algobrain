Pierre Andurand (born 1977) is a French oil trader and hedge fund manager who founded Andurand Capital Management in 2010. He is one of the most prominent modern commodity traders, known for making large, concentrated directional bets on energy and soft commodities based on deep fundamental analysis.

## Career

### Early Career

Andurand studied mathematics and computer science before entering the energy trading world. He traded oil at **Goldman Sachs** and subsequently at **Vitol**, the world's largest independent oil trading house. At Vitol, he gained extensive experience in the physical oil markets — understanding cargo flows, refinery economics, and storage logistics at a level of detail that most financial traders never achieve.

### Andurand Capital Management (2010-present)

Andurand founded his eponymous hedge fund in 2010 in London. The flagship **Andurand Commodities Fund** trades primarily [[crude-oil]] and related energy products, though it has expanded into other commodities.

#### Key Trades and Performance

- **2014-2015**: Profited significantly from oil's collapse from $100+ to below $30, having positioned short based on US shale supply growth and [[opec]]'s November 2014 decision to maintain production
- **2020**: The fund reportedly returned **+155%** during the COVID oil crash and recovery — one of the best single-year performances in hedge fund history. Andurand navigated both the collapse (oil went negative in April 2020) and the subsequent recovery
- **2022**: Profited from the Russia-Ukraine energy crisis, with oil spiking above $120
- **2023**: Record loss — the leveraged Andurand Commodities Discretionary Enhanced Fund fell roughly **-55%**, caught long oil as Brent declined about 10% on the year (Bloomberg, March 2024)
- **2024**: Strong rebound, reported around **+50%** for the year, driven by an early and successful long [[cocoa]] position — cocoa roughly tripled on West African supply disruptions (CSSVD disease, adverse weather in Ivory Coast and Ghana), peaking near $12,900/ton in December 2024. Andurand also publicly pivoted research focus from oil toward **[[copper]]** and cocoa, projecting copper could reach ~$40,000/tonne within about a decade (Financial Times interview, May 2024)
- **2025**: Severe drawdown — the enhanced fund was reported down roughly **-57%** by mid-2025 after the cocoa position was whipsawed. The fund re-ramped its cocoa bet in April 2025 ahead of grindings data, then took an ~18% monthly loss in the volatility following the April 2025 US "Liberation Day" tariff announcement; cocoa collapsed from its late-2024 highs. By August 2025 Andurand publicly retreated from cocoa, citing "extreme" volatility (Bloomberg, Aug 2025)

### Trading Approach

Andurand's methodology represents the modern fundamental commodity trader:

1. **Physical market intelligence**: Builds a bottom-up view of [[supply-demand-balance|supply and demand]] using production data, shipping/tanker tracking, refinery throughput, inventory data, and conversations with physical market participants. This "boots on the ground" approach provides informational edge over traders relying solely on published data.

2. **[[opec]] dynamics analysis**: Deep understanding of OPEC internal politics — Saudi Arabia's strategy, compliance/cheating behavior of member states, spare capacity constraints. OPEC decisions are among the most impactful single events for oil prices, and Andurand's track record suggests a strong ability to anticipate them.

3. **Macro and geopolitical overlay**: Integrates [[geopolitical-risk-premium|geopolitical analysis]] (sanctions, conflicts, elections) and macroeconomic factors (global growth, dollar, rates) with the physical supply/demand picture.

4. **Aggressive position sizing**: Willing to take large, concentrated directional bets when conviction is high. The fund's volatility is substantially higher than typical hedge funds — reflecting the concentrated, directional nature of the strategy.

5. **Public market views**: Andurand is unusually vocal about his oil price views, frequently sharing analysis on social media and in financial press. He has publicly called for $100+ oil on multiple occasions, typically based on underinvestment in supply (the [[capex-cycle]] argument).

## Significance in Commodity Markets

Andurand represents the evolution of commodity trading from the old-school "oilman" model ([[t-boone-pickens]]) to a modern hybrid approach that combines:

- Physical market expertise (from his Vitol background)
- Quantitative and analytical rigor (from his mathematical training)
- Macro awareness (integrating central bank policy, geopolitics, and currency effects)
- Hedge fund risk management (portfolio construction, position sizing, drawdown limits)

His success demonstrates that fundamental edge in commodity markets still exists, even as systematic/CTA approaches ([[trend-following-cta]]) and algorithmic strategies grow in prominence. The key differentiator is the depth of physical market intelligence — understanding ship positions, storage economics, and pipeline constraints at a level of specificity that generic quantitative models cannot replicate.

The 2023-2025 period is equally instructive as a **risk-management case study**: the same concentration and leverage that produced +155% (2020) produced -55% (2023) and roughly -57% (mid-2025). Concentrated discretionary commodity funds exhibit return distributions closer to a single levered trade than to a diversified portfolio — investors and traders copying Andurand's views should size accordingly, and note that his public bullish calls (oil at $140, copper at $40,000/t) are theses with multi-year horizons and violent interim drawdowns, not timing signals.
