Marc Faber (born February 28, 1946) is a Swiss investor, economist, and publisher of the monthly *Gloom, Boom & Doom Report*, known by the nickname "Dr. Doom" for his persistently contrarian, bearish outlook. For traders he is a long-running source of contrarian macro views — gold, Asia, and anti-central-bank positioning — and a case study in how to calibrate perma-bear commentary as a sentiment input.

## Overview

Faber was born in Zurich and earned a PhD in economics *magna cum laude* from the University of Zurich at age 24. He worked at White Weld & Co. in the 1970s, then ran the Hong Kong office of **Drexel Burnham Lambert** as managing director from 1978 until the firm's 1990 collapse, after which he founded his own Hong Kong-based advisory and fund-management firm, **Marc Faber Limited**. He has lived for many years in **Chiang Mai, Thailand**.

His reputation rests on several prescient macro calls: advising clients to exit US equities ahead of the **October 1987 crash**, predicting the bursting of the **Japanese bubble** around 1990, and warning of overheating in Asian markets before the **1997 Asian financial crisis**. His 2002 book *Tomorrow's Gold: Asia's Age of Discovery* argued — early and correctly for the 2000s — for a secular shift of wealth toward Asia and commodities. His framework draws heavily on Austrian economics: skepticism of credit expansion, central-bank money printing, and asset bubbles, with a standing preference for [[gold]] and real assets and broad diversification across geographies and asset classes.

## 2017 Controversy

In October 2017, Faber's newsletter contained racist remarks (asserting that America prospered because it was settled by white Europeans). The backlash was immediate: he was removed from or resigned the boards of **Sprott Inc.**, **NovaGold Resources**, and **Ivanhoe Mines**, and CNBC, Fox Business, and Bloomberg TV said they would no longer book him as a guest. The episode ended his mainstream financial-media presence, though he continued publishing.

## Status as of 2026

Faber, 80, continues to publish the *Gloom, Boom & Doom Report* from Thailand (still in publication as of 2025, verified via Perplexity 2026-06-10) and gives interviews on alternative finance media. His themes are unchanged: long precious metals, skepticism of US equity valuations and Western fiscal/monetary policy, and favoring emerging-Asia assets.

## Relevance to Trading

- **Contrarian sentiment input**: like [[jim-rogers]], Faber has been structurally bearish on Western assets for decades. His specific *allocations* (gold, Asian equities, real estate) have more signal than his crash rhetoric, which has a high false-positive rate.
- **Calibration lesson**: "Dr. Doom" commentary illustrates the asymmetry of perma-bear punditry — a handful of celebrated correct calls (1987, Japan, Asia 1997) against many missed bull markets. Traders should weight such sources by base rates, not headlines.
- **Asia and gold framework**: *Tomorrow's Gold* remains a reference text for the secular emerging-Asia and hard-assets thesis that has shaped [[commodities]] and [[emerging-markets]] allocation debates since the early 2000s.
