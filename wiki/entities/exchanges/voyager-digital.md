# Voyager Digital

**Voyager Digital** was a publicly traded crypto brokerage and lending platform that filed for **Chapter 11 bankruptcy in July 2022** after suffering massive losses from its exposure to Three Arrows Capital (3AC), which defaulted following the [[terra-luna]] collapse. Voyager's failure was a key domino in the 2022 contagion cascade that also brought down [[blockfi]], Celsius, and eventually [[ftx]].

## What Happened

Voyager offered customers interest-bearing accounts (up to 12% APY on stablecoins) and commission-free trading. To generate those yields, Voyager lent customer assets to institutional borrowers -- most critically, **Three Arrows Capital (3AC)**.

When [[terra-luna|Terra/LUNA collapsed]] in May 2022, 3AC suffered catastrophic losses on its LUNA position and other leveraged bets. 3AC defaulted on its $650M loan from Voyager, leaving Voyager insolvent. Voyager froze withdrawals on July 1, 2022, and filed for bankruptcy four days later.

FTX initially bid to acquire Voyager's assets, but FTX's own collapse in November 2022 torpedoed that deal. Binance.US then won the auction with a ~$1.3 billion bid (December 2022) and obtained bankruptcy-court approval, but abandoned the acquisition in April 2023 citing a "hostile and uncertain regulatory climate" amid SEC and CFIUS objections — forcing Voyager to self-liquidate and distribute assets directly to customers.

---

## Bankruptcy Outcome and Enforcement (2023–2025)

- **Initial distribution (mid-2023)** — after the Binance.US deal collapsed, customers received an initial in-kind distribution of roughly **35%** of their claims via the Voyager app.
- **Estate recoveries** — the Voyager estate (Voyager Digital Plan Administrator) later recovered approximately **$484 million** through settlements with the FTX estate (~$450M, court-approved) and Three Arrows Capital liquidators, funding supplemental distributions to creditors and lifting total recoveries above the initial 35%.
- **CFTC and FTC actions (October 2023)** — the [[cftc|CFTC]] charged former CEO Stephen Ehrlich with fraud and registration failures; the FTC simultaneously sued, alleging Voyager falsely told customers their deposits were FDIC-insured ("as safe with us as at a bank").
- **FTC settlement (June 2025)** — Ehrlich agreed to a permanent ban on handling consumer assets and a **$2.8 million** payment to resolve FTC charges.
- **CFTC consent order (2025)** — a Manhattan federal court order required Ehrlich to pay **$750,000** in disgorgement, returned to Voyager customers through the bankruptcy liquidation (CFTC Press Release 9122-25).

## Genesis Trading Connection

**Genesis Trading**, a major crypto prime brokerage and lending desk (subsidiary of Digital Currency Group), was similarly caught in the 2022 contagion. Genesis had significant exposure to 3AC and later to FTX/Alameda. Genesis halted withdrawals in November 2022 and filed for bankruptcy in January 2024. The Voyager and Genesis failures together represented the collapse of crypto's institutional lending infrastructure.

## Trading Relevance

- Voyager's failure demonstrates how **concentration risk** (one large borrower defaulting) can destroy a lending platform
- The contagion sequence ([[terra-luna]] > 3AC > Voyager > Genesis > [[ftx]] > [[blockfi]]) is a textbook case of systemic risk in interconnected crypto lending
- Unsustainable yield promises (10-12% APY) were a red flag that counterparties were taking excessive risk
- Post-2022, credit risk assessment and platform due diligence became essential for any crypto investor using CeFi services
- The case strengthened the argument for [[self-custody]] and transparent [[defi]] lending
