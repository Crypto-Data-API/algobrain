Morgans is Australia's largest full-service national stockbroking and wealth management firm. With over 40 offices across the country, Morgans operates a relationship-driven model where clients work with personal advisers for portfolio management, financial planning, and market access — including IPO and placement allocations that are typically unavailable to retail investors on discount platforms.

## Overview

Unlike discount brokers such as [[selfwealth]] or [[stake]] where investors make their own decisions, Morgans is a full-service firm where the adviser relationship is central. Clients pay higher fees but receive personalised investment advice, proprietary research, access to IPOs and capital raisings, [[superannuation|SMSF]] administration, retirement planning, and portfolio management.

Morgans is particularly strong in regional Australia, with offices in cities and towns across Queensland, New South Wales, Victoria, South Australia, and Western Australia — a geographic footprint unmatched by any other Australian stockbroking firm. The firm is [[chess-sponsorship|CHESS-sponsored]], and all client shareholdings are held directly under individual HINs.

## History

- **1982**: Founded in Brisbane, Queensland
- **1990s–2000s**: Expanded through organic growth and acquisition of regional broking firms across Australia
- **2007**: Merged with ABN AMRO Morgans, gaining significant scale
- **2013**: Rebranded to "Morgans" after separating from ABN AMRO following the GFC restructuring
- **2020s**: Continued to build national network to 40+ offices, focusing on wealth management and SMSF services alongside traditional stockbroking
- **Present**: Remains Australia's largest independent full-service national stockbroker by office count and adviser numbers

## Key Features

- **Personal Adviser Relationship**: Every client is assigned a dedicated adviser who provides tailored investment recommendations and portfolio oversight
- **Proprietary Research**: In-house research team covering ASX-listed companies — widely regarded as one of the most thorough and respected research houses in Australia
- **IPO and Placement Access**: Morgans frequently receives allocations in IPOs, secondary raisings, and block trades that retail investors on discount brokers cannot access — a major differentiator for active investors
- **CHESS Sponsored**: All ASX shareholdings registered directly in client names
- **Morgans Private**: Dedicated service tier for high-net-worth individuals with portfolios of $500,000+, offering enhanced advice, bespoke portfolio construction, and priority service
- **Financial Planning**: Comprehensive financial planning including retirement, estate planning, insurance, and tax strategies
- **SMSF Services**: Full [[smsf|SMSF]] administration, setup, and investment management — including [[superannuation|superannuation]] strategy advice, [[franking-credits|franking credit]] optimisation, and pension phase portfolio construction
- **40+ National Offices**: Face-to-face adviser access in capital cities and regional centres across Australia

## Fee Structure

Morgans operates on an **adviser-negotiated fee model**, which differs fundamentally from discount brokers:

### Typical Fee Arrangements

| Model | Typical Cost | Description |
|-------|-------------|-------------|
| **Commission-based** | 1–2% of trade value | Traditional per-trade brokerage, negotiated with adviser |
| **Fixed annual fee** | 0.5–1.5% of portfolio p.a. | Annual management fee for ongoing portfolio oversight |
| **Fee-for-service** | Hourly or project-based | Financial planning and specific advice engagements |

### Online Trading

Morgans also offers lower-cost online trading for clients who want to execute their own trades:
- Available at reduced brokerage rates (competitive with mid-range brokers)
- Accessible via web and mobile platforms

### Important Note

Fees are **individually negotiated** — larger portfolios typically receive lower percentage rates. There is no published flat-fee schedule like [[selfwealth]] or [[commsec]].

## Pros and Cons

### Pros
- **Personal advice**: Dedicated adviser who knows your financial situation, goals, and risk tolerance
- **IPO/placement access**: Allocations in capital raisings unavailable on discount platforms — can be highly valuable
- **Research quality**: Proprietary in-house research team widely respected across the industry
- **CHESS sponsored**: Direct share ownership
- **National office network**: 40+ offices — face-to-face access in regional areas where other brokers have no presence
- **Full financial planning**: One-stop shop for investing, retirement, insurance, tax planning
- **SMSF expertise**: Deep experience with self-managed super fund investment and administration
- **Morgans Private**: Premium service for HNW investors

### Cons
- **Higher fees**: Significantly more expensive than discount brokers — 1-2% per trade vs $9.50 at [[selfwealth]]
- **Not ideal for small portfolios**: The advice model is less cost-effective for portfolios under $100,000
- **Less suited for active self-directed traders**: The platform and pricing model are designed for advised clients, not frequent self-directed trading
- **Adviser quality varies**: Experience depends heavily on the individual adviser assigned to your account
- **Slower execution for advised trades**: Trades placed through an adviser may be slower than self-directed online execution

## Who It's Best For

- **High-net-worth individuals** ($500K+ portfolios) seeking professional portfolio management
- **Retirees and pre-retirees** wanting comprehensive retirement planning and income-focused portfolios
- **SMSF trustees** who need professional advice on super fund investment strategy
- **Investors seeking IPO access** — Morgans' institutional relationships provide allocations unavailable elsewhere
- **Regional investors** who value face-to-face adviser access
- **Investors who prefer delegated management** — those who want someone else to monitor and manage their portfolio
