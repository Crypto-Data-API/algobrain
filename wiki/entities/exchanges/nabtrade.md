nabtrade is the online stockbroking platform owned by National Australia Bank (NAB), one of Australia's Big Four banks. As the second-largest bank-owned broker after [[commsec]], nabtrade offers ASX equities, international shares, options, and managed funds with deep integration into NAB's banking ecosystem — instant transfers, linked accounts, and a unified digital experience for NAB customers.

## Overview

Launched in 2012 as NAB's answer to [[commsec]]'s dominance, nabtrade carved out a solid position as a mid-range broker — more feature-rich than discount platforms like [[selfwealth]] but less expensive than full-service firms like [[morgans]]. The platform is [[chess-sponsorship|CHESS-sponsored]], supports ASX options trading, and provides access to 10+ international markets — making it a comprehensive choice for NAB banking customers.

nabtrade benefits from NAB's institutional research partnerships and offers free access to Morningstar reports, market news, and trading ideas. For more advanced traders, the platform offers access to the iress ViewPoint trading terminal (at additional cost).

## History

- **2012**: Launched as NAB's online trading platform, replacing earlier NAB broking services
- **2013–2019**: Steady growth, positioned as a more modern alternative to CommSec for NAB customers
- **2020–2021**: Significant account growth during COVID retail trading boom
- **2022**: Enhanced mobile app and platform features
- **2023–2024**: Continued improvements to user interface and product range, facing increasing competition from fintech brokers

## Key Features

- **NAB Banking Integration**: Instant transfers between NAB bank accounts and trading account, unified digital banking experience
- **CHESS Sponsored**: All ASX shares held directly in your name with a personal HIN
- **International Shares**: Access to 10+ international markets including US (NYSE, NASDAQ), UK (LSE), Hong Kong (HKEX), Japan, Germany, and more
- **Options Trading**: ASX Exchange Traded Options — puts, calls, and complex strategies
- **mFunds**: Managed fund settlement via ASX mFund platform — buy/sell managed funds like shares
- **Morningstar Research**: Free access to Morningstar analyst reports, star ratings, and fair value estimates
- **iress ViewPoint**: Advanced trading terminal with Level 2 market depth, advanced charting, and professional-grade tools (additional subscription cost)
- **SMSF Accounts**: Dedicated [[smsf|SMSF]] trading account type with [[chess-sponsorship|CHESS]]-sponsored HIN — supports [[superannuation|superannuation]] investing, [[franking-credits|franking credit]] tracking, and pension phase portfolios
- **Term Deposits**: Invest in NAB and third-party term deposits directly through the platform

## Fee Structure

### ASX Trading

| Trade Value | Brokerage Fee |
|-------------|---------------|
| Up to $5,000 | $14.95 flat |
| $5,001 – $20,000 | $19.95 flat |
| $20,001+ | 0.11% of trade value |

### International Trading

| Market | Fee |
|--------|-----|
| US (NYSE, NASDAQ) | $14.95 USD for trades up to $5,000 USD; $19.95 USD for $5,001–$20,000 USD; 0.11% for $20,001+ USD |
| UK (LSE) | From £14.95 |
| Other markets | Varies by market |
| FX conversion | Spread applied on currency conversion |

### Other Fees

- **Account keeping**: Free
- **Inactivity fee**: None
- **Real-time ASX data**: Free for active traders
- **iress ViewPoint**: Subscription fee applies (varies by data package)

## Trading Products

- **ASX Equities**: Full access to all ASX-listed shares
- **ETFs**: All ASX-listed and select international ETFs
- **Options**: ASX Exchange Traded Options
- **International Shares**: 10+ markets — US, UK, Hong Kong, Japan, Germany, France, and more
- **mFunds**: Managed fund settlement via ASX mFund platform
- **Managed Funds**: Additional managed fund options beyond mFunds
- **Term Deposits**: NAB and third-party term deposits
- **Margin Lending**: Available through NAB's margin lending facility

**Not available**: CFDs, forex, cryptocurrency

## Pros and Cons

### Pros
- **NAB banking integration**: Seamless fund transfers and unified digital experience for NAB customers
- **CHESS sponsored**: Direct share ownership with personal HIN
- **Decent research**: Free Morningstar reports, market news, and trading ideas
- **Options trading**: ASX Exchange Traded Options available — not offered by many competitors
- **International shares**: 10+ markets with direct share ownership
- **mFunds access**: Buy and sell managed funds through the ASX settlement system
- **iress ViewPoint available**: Professional-grade platform for active traders (at extra cost)

### Cons
- **Fees higher than discount brokers**: $14.95 minimum vs $9.50 at [[selfwealth]], $3 at [[stake]], $2 at [[superhero]]
- **Platform feels dated**: User interface less modern than fintech competitors like [[stake]] or [[superhero]]
- **NAB lock-in**: Best experience requires NAB banking relationship
- **iress ViewPoint costs extra**: Advanced platform features require additional subscription
- **Less brand recognition than CommSec**: Smaller market share and less top-of-mind for new investors
- **International fees add up**: $14.95 USD minimum for US trades is uncompetitive vs [[stake]] ($3 USD)

## Who It's Best For

- **NAB banking customers** who want seamless integration between banking and investing
- **Intermediate investors** who want options trading and international access without going full-service
- **Managed fund investors** who value mFund access for easy managed fund trading
- **SMSF trustees** who bank with NAB and want a single-institution solution
- **Research-oriented investors** who appreciate free Morningstar reports
- **Investors wanting a bank-backed broker** with CHESS sponsorship and broad product range
