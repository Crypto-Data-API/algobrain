Intercontinental Exchange (ICE) is a global exchange operator, clearing house, and data services provider headquartered in Atlanta, Georgia. Founded in 2000 by Jeffrey Sprecher, ICE began as an electronic platform for trading over-the-counter energy [[commodities]] and has grown through aggressive acquisitions into one of the world's largest exchange groups. ICE operates regulated exchanges, clearing houses, and listings venues across North America, Europe, and Asia. Full-year 2025 revenue was approximately $11.8 billion gross (net revenues, excluding transaction-based expenses, of roughly $10 billion), and the company trades on the NYSE under ticker ICE.

## History

Jeffrey Sprecher founded ICE in 2000 with backing from major energy companies including Goldman Sachs, Morgan Stanley, and BP. The original mission was to bring transparency and electronic trading to the opaque over-the-counter energy markets. ICE's first major acquisition was the International Petroleum Exchange (IPE) in London in 2001, which gave it control of the [[brent-crude|Brent crude]] oil futures contract — one of the world's most important commodity benchmarks.

The defining moment in ICE's history was its $11 billion acquisition of [[nyse|NYSE Euronext]] in 2013, which gave ICE ownership of the New York Stock Exchange, the world's most iconic equities venue. This was a bold move for what had been primarily a derivatives exchange, and it transformed ICE into a diversified financial infrastructure company. Sprecher subsequently streamlined NYSE operations, cutting costs and modernizing technology. ICE also acquired the Liffe derivatives exchange (part of NYSE Euronext), strengthening its European [[futures]] franchise.

## Key Products and Divisions

**Exchange and Trading:**
- **[[nyse|NYSE]]**: The world's largest stock exchange by market capitalization of listed companies, hosting blue-chip listings including many of the largest global corporations
- **ICE Futures U.S.**: Trades agricultural commodities (coffee, sugar, cocoa, cotton) and financial futures (Russell 2000 index, MSCI index futures)
- **ICE Futures Europe**: Home to the Brent crude oil futures contract, the global oil pricing benchmark, as well as natural gas, emissions, and soft commodity futures
- **ICE Futures Canada**: Canola futures and options

**Clearing:**
- **ICE Clear Credit**: Clears credit default swaps (CDS), established after the 2008 financial crisis when regulators pushed for central clearing of OTC derivatives
- **ICE Clear Europe** and **ICE Clear U.S.**: Clear exchange-traded futures and options

**Data and Connectivity:**
- ICE Data Services provides pricing, reference data, analytics, and connectivity to financial institutions globally. This is a high-margin, recurring revenue business that has become an increasingly important part of ICE's overall strategy.

**Mortgage Technology:**
- ICE acquired Ellie Mae in 2020 for $11 billion and Black Knight in 2023 for $11.7 billion, creating a dominant position in U.S. mortgage technology. The ICE Mortgage Technology platform handles a significant share of U.S. mortgage originations, providing loan origination systems, closing technology, and data analytics.

## 2025–2026: Prediction Markets Push

On 7 October 2025, ICE announced a strategic investment of up to $2 billion in [[polymarket|Polymarket]], the largest crypto-settled [[prediction-markets|prediction market]], at a valuation of roughly $8 billion pre-investment (~$9 billion post). The deal made ICE a global distributor of Polymarket's event-driven sentiment data and included a partnership on future tokenization initiatives. ICE completed the commitment with a final $600 million tranche in March 2026. The investment positions ICE directly against [[cme-group|CME Group]] (which has partnered with FanDuel on event contracts) and [[kalshi|Kalshi]] in the rapidly institutionalizing event-contract space, and signals that major exchange groups now treat prediction-market data as a monetizable sentiment feed.

## Competitive Position

ICE competes primarily with [[cme-group|CME Group]] in the global derivatives space. While CME dominates U.S. interest rate and equity index futures, ICE holds key positions in energy (Brent crude), soft [[commodities]], and credit derivatives clearing. The two companies have largely complementary product suites, though they compete intensely in areas of overlap. ICE's diversification into mortgage technology and data services distinguishes it from pure-play exchange operators.

## Why It Matters to Traders

- **Brent crude benchmark**: ICE Futures Europe hosts the Brent contract that prices roughly two-thirds of the world's internationally traded crude — the WTI–Brent spread is a core energy trade.
- **Soft commodities**: ICE Futures U.S. is the global price-setting venue for coffee (KC), sugar (SB), cocoa (CC) and cotton (CT) — the contracts behind the historic cocoa squeeze of 2024.
- **TTF natural gas and EUA carbon**: ICE Endex's Dutch TTF contract is Europe's gas benchmark and was the epicentre of the 2022 energy crisis; ICE also dominates EU carbon-allowance futures.
- **Data and sentiment**: ICE Data Services feeds pricing and reference data to most institutional desks, and the Polymarket stake adds event-probability data as a new signal class.
- **Equities plumbing**: NYSE listings, auctions (open/close) and circuit-breaker mechanics shape US equity execution.
