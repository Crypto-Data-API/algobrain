# MEXC

**MEXC** (formerly MEXC Global / MXC) is a global centralized cryptocurrency exchange ([[centralized-exchange|CEX]]) founded in 2018, notable for one of the **widest low- and micro-cap altcoin listing policies** in the industry. MEXC routinely lists very early-stage, small, and speculative tokens — often well before any top-tier venue — which makes it a primary destination for traders hunting newly launched assets. Its native [[mx-token|MX]] token provides fee discounts and unlocks participation in MEXC's launchpad / "Kickstarter" listing-vote events.

## Overview & History

MEXC launched in 2018 and built its identity around **breadth of listings**: it offers an exceptionally large number of trading pairs, with a strong tilt toward newly issued and micro-cap tokens. This aggressive, fast-listing approach positioned MEXC as the venue where many tokens first reach a centralized order book. The exchange expanded into derivatives, Earn products, and launch mechanisms (including community listing votes), but its defining characteristic remains the sheer size and freshness of its spot catalog.

## Products & Markets

| Product | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Spot** | One of the largest spot catalogs in crypto, heavily weighted to low- and micro-cap altcoins |
| **Derivatives** | USDT-margined and coin-margined [[perpetual-futures|perpetual]] contracts and futures |
| **Leverage** | Margin and leveraged futures trading (verify current per-asset maximums on the official venue) |
| **Earn** | Savings, staking, and yield products |
| **Launchpad / Kickstarter** | Token-launch and community listing-vote events; participation often tied to [[mx-token|MX]] holding |
| **Low/zero-fee promos** | MEXC has run extended zero- or low-fee promotions on many spot pairs as a user-acquisition tactic |

MEXC's signature trait is **micro-cap listing breadth**: it is frequently the first centralized venue to list a brand-new token, making it central to the earliest stage of CEX price discovery for speculative assets.

## Exchange Token — MX

MEXC's native [[exchange-tokens|exchange token]] is [[mx-token|MX]]:

- **Fee discounts** for paying trading fees in MX
- **Launchpad / Kickstarter utility**: holding MX grants access to and allocation weighting in MEXC's launch events and community listing votes
- **Ecosystem utility** across MEXC products and promotions

MX's value is closely tied to MEXC's launch-event activity and trading volume. See [[mx-token|MX]] for current tokenomics.

## Trading Relevance

- **Listing strategy / earliest liquidity**: MEXC is among the very first CEXs to list new and micro-cap tokens, making it the default venue for early speculative trading before a token reaches larger exchanges.
- **Liquidity tier**: On major pairs MEXC sits in the second tier behind [[binance]]/[[okx]]/[[bybit]]; on the long tail it is often the only CEX market, but those books can be extremely thin.
- **Cross-venue arbitrage**: First-mover listings produce large, frequent price gaps versus DEXs and later-listing venues — an arbitrage source paired with high thin-book and execution risk.
- **Positioning vs. peers**: MEXC competes almost entirely on **listing breadth, speed, and low fees** rather than liquidity depth or derivatives volume. It occupies the most aggressive end of the listing spectrum, beyond even [[kucoin]] and [[gate-io|Gate]].

## Security & Regulatory

- MEXC publishes **[[proof-of-reserves]]** attestations, as is now standard for major CEXs following the 2022 [[ftx|FTX]] collapse.
- As a Seychelles-registered offshore exchange, MEXC operates across many jurisdictions with varying licensing and access; like other offshore CEXs it has restricted or withdrawn access in some markets. Verify current jurisdictional availability before relying on it.

## Risks

- **Counterparty / custody risk**: Exchange-custodied funds carry insolvency, mismanagement, and hack risk; proof-of-reserves attestations mitigate but do not remove it.
- **Regulatory risk**: Offshore registration means uncertain and shifting regulatory standing and possible jurisdictional access restrictions.
- **Listing-quality risk**: An aggressive micro-cap listing policy means heavy exposure to scam tokens, rug pulls, low-float assets, and short-lived projects — the highest listing-quality risk among the venues in this group.
- **Wash-trading / manipulation risk**: Thinly traded micro-cap markets are particularly vulnerable to [[wash-trading]] and price manipulation; reported volumes on long-tail pairs should be treated with skepticism.
