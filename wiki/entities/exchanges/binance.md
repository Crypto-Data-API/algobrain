# Binance

**Binance** is the world's largest [[crypto-markets|cryptocurrency]] exchange by trading volume. Founded in 2017 by **Changpeng Zhao (CZ)**, Binance rapidly rose to dominance through aggressive listing policies, low fees, a powerful product suite, and the [[bnb|BNB]] token ecosystem. Despite significant regulatory challenges -- including a **$4.3 billion settlement** with the U.S. Department of Justice in November 2023 that saw CZ step down as CEO -- Binance remains the single most influential venue in crypto [[price-discovery]] and [[liquidity]].

The **BTC/USDT** pair on Binance is the most traded cryptocurrency pair in the world by volume, and Binance's order books often set the reference price for assets across the entire crypto market.

## History & Rise to Dominance

### Founding & Early Growth (2017-2019)

CZ, a former developer at Blockchain.info and CTO of OKCoin, launched Binance in July 2017 with a $15 million ICO for the [[bnb|BNB]] token. Binance's rise was meteoric:

- **Rapid listing strategy**: Binance listed new tokens faster than competitors, attracting users who wanted early access
- **Low fees**: A 0.1% trading fee (with further discounts for BNB holders) undercut most rivals
- **Multilingual platform**: Early localization into dozens of languages captured global market share
- **"CZ effect"**: CZ's active presence on social media (especially Twitter/X) built a cult-like following and made Binance synonymous with crypto trading

Within six months of launch, Binance was the world's largest exchange by volume.

### BNB & Binance Smart Chain (2019-2021)

Binance expanded beyond a simple exchange into a full ecosystem:

- **[[bnb|BNB]]**: Originally an ERC-20 utility token for fee discounts, BNB evolved into the native token of Binance's own blockchain
- **Binance Smart Chain (BSC)**: Launched in September 2020 as an EVM-compatible chain with low fees, BSC (now BNB Chain) became a major [[defi]] hub, particularly for retail users priced out of [[ethereum|Ethereum]] gas fees
- **Launchpad & Launchpool**: Token launch platforms that gave BNB holders exclusive early access to new projects, creating persistent demand for BNB

### Regulatory Reckoning (2022-2024)

Binance's aggressive growth and regulatory evasion caught up with it:

| Date | Event |
|---|---|
| 2021-2022 | Multiple countries (UK, Japan, Germany, Netherlands) restrict or ban Binance operations |
| Jun 2023 | [[sec|SEC]] files lawsuit alleging Binance operated an unregistered exchange and sold unregistered securities |
| Nov 2023 | **DOJ settlement**: Binance pleads guilty to money laundering and sanctions violations; agrees to $4.3 billion in penalties |
| Nov 2023 | **CZ steps down as CEO**, pleads guilty to federal charges; Richard Teng appointed as successor |
| Apr 2024 | CZ sentenced to 4 months in prison (served) |

Despite these setbacks, Binance maintained its market-leading position, suggesting the platform's [[liquidity]] moat and global user base are durable.

## Market Dominance & Price Discovery

Binance's role in crypto [[price-discovery]] cannot be overstated:

- **Volume dominance**: Binance consistently accounts for 40-60% of global centralized exchange spot volume
- **Reference pricing**: Many smaller exchanges, DeFi protocols, and oracle networks use Binance prices as reference feeds
- **Listing impact**: A new token being listed on Binance (the "Binance listing effect") frequently causes a 20-100%+ price surge due to the sudden liquidity and exposure
- **Delisting impact**: Conversely, a Binance delisting announcement can devastate a token's price
- **Futures dominance**: Binance Futures [[open-interest]] and [[funding-rate|funding rates]] are the most-watched derivatives metrics for gauging market positioning

For traders on any platform -- including [[hyperliquid|Hyperliquid]], [[coinbase|Coinbase]], or [[dydx|dYdX]] -- Binance's order flow often sets the tone for short-term price action.

## Controversies & Risks

### Regulatory Issues

Binance has faced the broadest and most severe regulatory actions of any major crypto exchange:

- The **$4.3 billion DOJ settlement** (2023) is the largest penalty in crypto history
- The [[sec|SEC]] lawsuit alleges fundamental securities law violations, including operating an unregistered exchange and commingling customer funds
- Binance has been banned or restricted in multiple major markets (UK, US full operations, Japan, Germany, Australia, and others)
- Despite hiring compliance staff and implementing KYC requirements, critics argue Binance's regulatory culture was fundamentally evasive for years

### Proof of Reserves Concerns

Following the [[ftx|FTX]] collapse, Binance published "proof of reserves" audits. However:

- Early reports were criticized for not including liabilities alongside assets
- The auditing firm (Mazars) subsequently paused all crypto audit work
- Questions remain about the true segregation of customer funds and corporate assets

### Competition & the FTX Rivalry

The Binance-[[ftx|FTX]] rivalry was one of the defining narratives of 2022 crypto:

- CZ's tweet announcing Binance would sell its FTT holdings is widely credited as the catalyst for FTX's bank run and collapse
- This event simultaneously eliminated Binance's biggest competitor and triggered an industry-wide reckoning about centralized exchange risks
- The episode demonstrated both Binance's enormous market influence and the risks of concentrated power in crypto markets

### Decentralization Headwinds

The rise of [[decentralized-exchanges|decentralized exchanges]] like [[hyperliquid|Hyperliquid]], [[dydx|dYdX]], and [[gmx|GMX]] represents a structural competitive threat to Binance. Traders who value self-custody and permissionless access may increasingly prefer on-chain alternatives, particularly for [[perpetual-futures|derivatives trading]].

### Fees and Tiers

Binance uses a maker-taker fee schedule that decreases with 30-day rolling volume and [[bnb|BNB]] balance; holding and paying fees in BNB grants an additional discount. VIP tiers reduce both spot and futures fees materially for high-volume accounts. (Exact rates change frequently — verify current values on the official fee schedule before sizing cost-sensitive strategies.)

## Execution Mechanics for Arbitrage

Critical operational details for running arbitrage strategies on Binance. See [[exchange-api-reference]] for API endpoints and [[leg-risk]] for multi-leg execution risks.

### Liquidation Engine

Binance uses a **mark price** based on a weighted average of major exchange spot prices (not the Binance last-traded price), which reduces manipulation-driven liquidations.

**Liquidation price formula (isolated margin, long):**
```
liquidation_price = entry_price × (1 - initial_margin_rate + maintenance_margin_rate)
```

**Liquidation price formula (isolated margin, short):**
```
liquidation_price = entry_price × (1 + initial_margin_rate - maintenance_margin_rate)
```

Maintenance margin rates are **tiered by position size**:

| Position Notional (USDT) | Maintenance Margin Rate | Max Leverage |
|---|---|---|
| 0 - 50,000 | 0.40% | 125x |
| 50,000 - 250,000 | 0.50% | 100x |
| 250,000 - 1,000,000 | 1.00% | 50x |
| 1,000,000 - 10,000,000 | 2.50% | 20x |
| 10,000,000 - 50,000,000 | 5.00% | 10x |
| > 50,000,000 | 10.00% | 5x |

**Implication for arb:** At $10M+ notional (common for institutional funding rate arb), maintenance margin is 2.5-5%, meaning a 3-5% adverse move triggers liquidation even at "low" leverage. Always use 1x collateralization for arb positions.

### Auto-Deleveraging (ADL)

When the insurance fund cannot cover a bankrupt position's losses, Binance uses ADL to **force-close profitable positions** on the opposing side. ADL priority is ranked by profit percentage and leverage ratio — the most profitable, most leveraged positions are closed first.

**ADL risk for arb:** If you are short BTC perps (as in [[funding-rate-arbitrage]]) and the market drops sharply causing long liquidations, and the insurance fund is depleted, your *profitable short* may be force-closed via ADL. This breaks your hedge, leaving you with an unmatched spot long. Monitor the ADL indicator light (1-5 dots on the position panel).

### Insurance Fund

Binance maintains the **SAFU (Secure Asset Fund for Users)**, historically valued at ~$1B. Funded by a percentage of trading fees. Used to cover: exchange-side losses from liquidation gaps, ADL buffer, and potential hack recovery.

**Adequacy:** At $1B+, the insurance fund covers typical daily liquidation volume ($100-500M). In extreme events (March 2020 crash: $1.6B liquidated in 24 hours), the fund could be tested.

### Margin Modes

| Mode | Behavior | Arb Implication |
|---|---|---|
| **Isolated** | Each position has its own margin. Liquidation limited to that position's margin | Preferred for arb — limits maximum loss per position |
| **Cross** | All open positions share margin pool. Unrealized profits offset unrealized losses | More capital-efficient but riskier — one blowup position can liquidate everything |

**Recommendation:** Use **isolated margin** for arb perp positions. Cross margin is dangerous because a loss on one arb's perp leg can cascade into liquidating other positions.

### Position Limits

| Asset | Max Position (Binance Futures) |
|---|---|
| BTC | Up to $200M notional at lowest leverage tier |
| ETH | Up to $100M notional |
| Major alts (SOL, DOGE, etc.) | $10-50M depending on liquidity tier |

Position limits are effectively set by the tiered margin system — larger positions require higher maintenance margin, making extreme leverage impossible at size.

### Clawback / Socialized Loss

Binance's terms of service include provisions for **socialized loss** — in extreme circumstances, losses exceeding the insurance fund can be distributed across profitable traders. This has never been invoked, but the ToS allows it. Read Section 7.3 of the Binance Futures Terms.
