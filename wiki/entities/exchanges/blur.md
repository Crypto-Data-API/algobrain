Blur is an [[nft|NFT]] marketplace designed for professional traders, launched in October 2022 by pseudonymous founder "Pacman" (later revealed as Tieshun Roquerre). By offering zero marketplace fees, a faster trading interface, and token-based incentives, Blur overtook [[opensea|OpenSea]] in trading volume by February 2023, fundamentally shifting the NFT market from a collector-driven culture to a trader-driven one.

## History

Blur launched in October 2022, entering a market dominated by [[opensea|OpenSea]]. Unlike OpenSea's collector-friendly approach, Blur targeted professional NFT traders with a Bloomberg Terminal-like interface featuring real-time floor prices, portfolio analytics, and advanced bidding tools.

The platform's breakout moment came in February 2023 with the $BLUR token airdrop, which rewarded users for trading activity and loyalty to the platform. The airdrop and ongoing trading incentives drove massive volume to Blur, allowing it to surpass OpenSea in total trading volume within months of launch.

Blur introduced several innovations that changed NFT trading dynamics, including bid pools (concentrated liquidity for NFT collections) and "trait bidding" (placing bids on specific trait combinations within a collection). These features made NFT markets more efficient but also more speculative.

The platform's decision to make creator royalties optional proved controversial, angering NFT artists and creators who relied on secondary sale royalties as a revenue stream. This move pressured [[opensea|OpenSea]] and other marketplaces to reconsider their own royalty enforcement policies.

Blur was backed by Paradigm at a valuation exceeding $1 billion. In 2024, Blur expanded beyond NFT trading by launching Blast, a Layer 2 blockchain, signaling broader ambitions in the crypto infrastructure space.

## Significance

Blur fundamentally transformed the NFT marketplace landscape. Its zero-fee model and trader-focused features shifted the NFT market from a collector culture (where users bought NFTs to hold and display) to a trader culture (where users actively flipped NFTs for profit). This shift had profound implications for the NFT ecosystem.

By making creator royalties optional, Blur catalyzed an industry-wide debate about the sustainability of NFT creator economics. The resulting "royalty wars" forced every major marketplace to reconsider its approach to creator compensation.

Blur's success demonstrated that token incentives could rapidly redistribute market share in crypto markets — a lesson that extended well beyond NFTs. The platform's competitive pressure forced [[opensea|OpenSea]] to reduce fees and launch aggregator products, permanently changing the economics of NFT marketplaces.

The launch of Blast L2 showed Blur's ambition to become more than just an NFT marketplace, positioning itself as a broader crypto infrastructure company.
