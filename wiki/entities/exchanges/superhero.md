Superhero is an Australian fintech broker that launched in 2020 with a focus on ultra-low-cost share and ETF trading. Its headline feature is **$0 brokerage on all ETF trades** — making it the only Australian broker to offer completely free ETF investing — alongside $2 flat brokerage on ASX shares, making it the cheapest share trading platform in Australia.

## Overview

Superhero entered the Australian market during the COVID-era retail trading boom and grew rapidly by targeting beginners and ETF-focused investors with rock-bottom fees. The platform's original use of a custodial/nominee model (rather than [[chess-sponsorship|CHESS sponsorship]]) was controversial and drew criticism from industry commentators and competitors. In response, Superhero transitioned to CHESS sponsorship for ASX shares in 2023, addressing the primary concern while maintaining its ultra-low pricing.

The platform offers ASX shares, ETFs, and US shares, though its product range is deliberately limited compared to full-featured brokers like [[commsec]] or [[cmc-markets]] — a trade-off for simplicity and cost.

## History

- **2020**: Founded and launched in Sydney — entered market with $0 ETF brokerage and $5 share brokerage using a custodial/nominee model
- **2020–2021**: Rapid growth during COVID retail boom — attracted a younger demographic of first-time investors
- **2021**: Controversy over custodial model — industry debate about whether non-CHESS models adequately protect investors
- **2022**: Reduced ASX brokerage to $2 per trade
- **2023**: **Transitioned to CHESS sponsorship for ASX shares** — a major move that addressed the primary competitive criticism while keeping fees low
- **2024**: Continued growth, added US share trading (via custodial model through DriveWealth/Stake infrastructure)

## The CHESS Transition Story

Superhero's custody model history is worth understanding:

**2020–2022 (Nominee/Custodial Model)**:
- Shares were held by a custodian (initially HSBC) on behalf of investors
- Investors did not have individual HINs — shares were pooled under the custodian's account
- **Concerns raised**: In the event of custodian or Superhero insolvency, the process of recovering individual shareholdings could be complex. Investors also could not easily transfer shares to another broker
- **Industry criticism**: Competitors and financial commentators argued this model was inferior to CHESS and potentially risky for investors

**2023–Present (CHESS Sponsored)**:
- Superhero transitioned all ASX holdings to CHESS sponsorship
- Every investor now has an individual HIN
- Shares are directly registered in the investor's name on the ASX subregister
- This addressed the primary criticism while maintaining the $2/$0 fee structure
- Demonstrated that ultra-low fees and CHESS sponsorship are not mutually exclusive

US shares remain on a custodial model (via DriveWealth), which is standard for Australian brokers offering US market access.

## Pros and Cons

### Pros
- **Free ETF trading**: $0 brokerage on ETFs is unmatched — ideal for passive investors building diversified portfolios
- **Cheapest ASX brokerage**: $2 per share trade is the lowest flat fee in Australia
- **Now CHESS sponsored**: Direct share ownership with personal HIN (since 2023)
- **No minimums or inactivity fees**: Fully accessible for small and infrequent investors
- **Simple interface**: Clean design that doesn't overwhelm beginners
- **Great for dollar-cost averaging**: Free ETF trades make regular small investments cost-effective

### Cons
- **Limited product range**: No options, no international beyond US, no CFDs, no margin lending — very narrow offering
- **Basic platform**: Minimal charting, no advanced order types, limited market depth — not suitable for active traders
- **Newer company**: Founded 2020 — less established track record compared to [[commsec]] (1995) or [[ig-markets]] (1974)
- **US shares on custodial model**: Not CHESS for US holdings (though this is standard across Australian brokers)
- **Limited research**: No analyst reports or in-depth research tools — investors must source research independently
- **Customer support**: As a lean fintech, support resources are more limited than bank-owned brokers

- **Beginners**: Simple interface, low cost, no minimums — an ideal starting platform

- **Cost-minimising investors**: $2 share trades for anyone who wants the absolute lowest brokerage
