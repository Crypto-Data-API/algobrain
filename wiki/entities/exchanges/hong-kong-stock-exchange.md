The Hong Kong Stock Exchange (HKEX), formally Hong Kong Exchanges and Clearing Limited, is one of the world's largest exchange groups and the primary gateway for international capital into mainland China via the Stock Connect programmes (Shanghai–Hong Kong, launched 2014; Shenzhen–Hong Kong, 2016). Equity market capitalisation reached HK$47.1 trillion (~US$6 trillion) at end-May 2026, and in 2025 Hong Kong ranked first globally for IPO fundraising. Ticker prefix: HKG / SEHK.

## Overview

- **Origins**: Formal stockbroking in Hong Kong dates to 1891 (Association of Stockbrokers in Hong Kong). The modern group, Hong Kong Exchanges and Clearing Ltd (HKEX), was formed in 2000 by the merger of the Stock Exchange of Hong Kong, the Hong Kong Futures Exchange, and the Hong Kong Securities Clearing Company. HKEX is itself listed (ticker 388.HK).
- **London Metal Exchange**: HKEX acquired the [[london-metal-exchange|LME]] in 2012 for £1.4 billion, giving it the world's dominant base-metals venue.
- **Leadership**: Bonnie Chan Yiting became CEO in March 2024 — the first woman to lead HKEX.
- **Stock Connect**: Mutual market access lets international investors trade Shanghai/Shenzhen A-shares ("Northbound") and mainland investors trade Hong Kong stocks ("Southbound"). Southbound flows have become a major driver of Hong Kong liquidity and a closely watched sentiment signal for China risk appetite.

## 2025–2026: Record Year and IPO Boom

- **2025 results (reported Feb 2026)**: Record revenue and other income of HK$29.16 billion (~US$3.73 billion), up 30% year-on-year; profit attributable to shareholders HK$17.75 billion (~US$2.27 billion), up 36% — the second consecutive record year. Cash market turnover surged roughly 95%.
- **IPO leadership**: Hong Kong ranked #1 globally for IPO funds raised in 2025 with 119 new listings raising HK$286.9 billion, up 226% from 2024, including two of the world's five largest IPOs (notably large A-to-H dual listings by mainland companies such as CATL).
- **Momentum into 2026**: 62 new listings raised HK$166.8 billion in the first five months of 2026; the listing pipeline exceeded 200 active applications during 2025.
- Drivers: the AI-led re-rating of Chinese tech, A-share companies seeking offshore listings, and listing-regime reforms (e.g., Chapter 18A/18C for biotech and specialist tech companies).

## Why It Matters to Traders

- **China proxy access**: HKEX hosts H-shares and the offshore listings of China's largest companies (Tencent, Alibaba, Meituan, BYD, Xiaomi), tradable without QFII access or mainland capital controls — and without US ADR delisting risk.
- **Index complex**: The Hang Seng Index (HSI), Hang Seng China Enterprises Index (HCEI) and Hang Seng TECH Index underpin liquid futures, options and ETFs traded globally.
- **Flow signals**: Daily Southbound Stock Connect net buying is a widely used gauge of mainland sentiment toward Hong Kong equities.
- **Dual-listing arbitrage**: A/H share premium-discount spreads between Shanghai/Shenzhen and Hong Kong listings of the same company are a classic relative-value trade.
- **No stamp-duty-free shorting nuances**: Hong Kong levies stamp duty on stock trades (cut from 0.13% to 0.1% in November 2023), a material cost input for high-turnover strategies.
