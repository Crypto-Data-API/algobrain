# Kraken

**Kraken** is one of the oldest and largest cryptocurrency exchanges, founded in 2011 by Jesse Powell (with co-founder Thanh Luu) and launched to the public in September 2013. Operated by parent company Payward, Inc., Kraken is a top-tier global venue for spot and derivatives crypto trading, known for never having suffered a major exchange hack, for deep EUR liquidity, and — since 2025 — for an aggressive push into traditional assets (equities, futures, tokenized stocks) ahead of a planned IPO at a **$20 billion valuation**.

## Overview

- **Founded**: 2011 in San Francisco, partly in response to the Mt. Gox hack; public launch September 2013.
- **Leadership**: co-CEOs **Arjun Sethi** and **David Ripley** (Powell stepped back from the CEO role in 2023 and chairs the board).
- **Scale**: roughly $1.5 billion in revenue reported for 2024 with continued growth in 2025; over 10 million clients across ~190 countries; consistently a top-5 global exchange by trusted spot volume.
- **Products**: spot trading (350+ assets), margin, regulated US crypto derivatives, staking, OTC desk, Kraken Pro, custodial services, and US equities trading (launched 2025).

## Key Events 2023–2026

- **Feb 2023**: settled with the [[sec|SEC]] for $30 million and shut its US staking-as-a-service program.
- **Nov 2023**: SEC sued Kraken for operating as an unregistered securities exchange; the case was **dismissed with prejudice in March 2025** after the SEC's post-2024-election leadership dropped it — part of the broad US regulatory thaw toward crypto.
- **Mar 2025**: agreed to acquire **NinjaTrader**, the US retail futures platform, for **$1.5 billion** — the largest-ever combination of a crypto exchange and a traditional derivatives broker, giving Kraken CFTC-registered FCM rails.
- **May–Jun 2025**: launched **xStocks** — tokenized US equities (Apple, Tesla, Nvidia, etc.) issued via Backed Finance on Solana — for non-US clients, plus regulated US equity trading in-app.
- **Sep–Nov 2025**: raised $500 million at a $15 billion valuation (September), then **$800 million across two tranches at a $20 billion valuation** (November); **confidentially filed an S-1 with the SEC on 19 November 2025**.
- **2026**: IPO timeline shifted with market conditions — initially targeted for Q1 2026, paused, then guided toward **H2 2026**; in May 2026 parent Payward was reported raising again at the same $20 billion mark, with co-CEO Sethi describing the company as "~80% ready" to list.

## Why It Matters to Traders

- **Reference liquidity and data**: Kraken's BTC/USD and BTC/EUR books are among the most trusted price references; its EUR pairs are the deepest in crypto, making Kraken data important for EUR-quoted basis and triangular-arbitrage monitoring.
- **Regulated US derivatives**: via Kraken Futures (Crypto Facilities, FCA-regulated, acquired 2019) and NinjaTrader (CFTC), Kraken offers one of the few regulated paths for US retail to trade both crypto perps-style products and CME futures from one stack.
- **Everything-exchange convergence trade**: Kraken's equities + tokenized-stock rollout, alongside [[robinhood|Robinhood]]'s crypto push, defines the 2025–2026 broker/exchange convergence theme; its IPO (when it prices) will be a key comp for [[coinbase|Coinbase]] valuation.
- **Proof-of-reserves and counterparty risk**: Kraken pioneered proof-of-reserves audits (first in 2014), relevant to post-[[ftx|FTX]] exchange counterparty-risk assessment.
