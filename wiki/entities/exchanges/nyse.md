The New York Stock Exchange (NYSE) is the world's largest stock exchange by total market capitalisation (~$44.7 trillion as of December 2025). Founded in 1792 under the Buttonwood Agreement, it operates a hybrid trading model combining a physical trading floor on Wall Street with electronic execution. NYSE is the primary listing venue for blue-chip companies, financials, industrials, and many international ADRs — making it the reference venue for most large-cap US equity, options, and index trading.

## Overview

NYSE is owned by Intercontinental Exchange (ICE), which acquired it in 2013 for ~$11 billion. It uses Designated Market Makers (DMMs) — human specialists obligated to maintain orderly markets for their assigned stocks. This hybrid model provides stability during volatile conditions that fully electronic exchanges may lack. The NYSE Group also operates NYSE Arca (ETF-dominant electronic exchange), NYSE American (small/mid-cap), NYSE National, NYSE Chicago, and — since 2025 — NYSE Texas.

## 2025–2026 Developments

- **NYSE Texas (2025)** — In February 2025, ICE announced the reincorporation of NYSE Chicago in Texas as **NYSE Texas**, a fully electronic equities exchange headquartered in Dallas, positioning NYSE to capture the wave of corporate relocations to Texas and to compete with the upstart Texas Stock Exchange (TXSE). Early listings included Trump Media & Technology Group as a dual listing.
- **Extended-hours / near-24h trading** — In February 2025, NYSE Arca became the first established US equity exchange to receive SEC approval to extend its trading day to **22 hours** (1:30am–11:30pm ET Mon–Thu). Subsequent filings push toward a ~23-hour day (9:00pm–8:00pm ET next day, 5 days/week, with a 1-hour maintenance break). Launch is targeted for **late 2026** (December 2026 per NYSE's published target), pending SIP and DTCC infrastructure readiness. This is part of an industry-wide move (Nasdaq and Cboe have parallel plans) toward 24x5 US equities.
- The NYSE's listed-company count was ~2,223 (July 2024), with total domestic market cap of ~$44.7 trillion by December 2025 — roughly two-thirds of the ~$69 trillion total US equity market value (October 2025).

## Reporting Obligations

Companies listed on NYSE report to the [[sec|SEC]] through the [[edgar|EDGAR]] electronic filing system:

| Filing | Description | Deadline |
|--------|-------------|----------|
| **10-K** | Annual report | 60–90 days after fiscal year end depending on filer size (typically end of Feb–Mar for Dec FY companies) |
| **10-Q** | Quarterly report | 40–45 days after quarter end |
| **8-K** | Major events report | Promptly upon occurrence (generally within 4 business days) |

Standard financial year end is December 31. Filings also include annual meeting statements, insider transactions, beneficial ownership transactions, and earnings statements (which can be reported through 8-K forms).

## Key Characteristics

- **Total market cap**: ~$44.7 trillion (Dec 2025); earlier ~$28 trillion figure dates to ~2024 (vs ~$1.5 trillion for [[asx-limited|ASX]])
- **Listings**: ~2,200–2,400 companies (2,223 as of July 2024)
- **Sector focus**: Blue chips, financials, industrials, healthcare, consumer staples
- **Famous listings**: Berkshire Hathaway, JPMorgan, Walmart, Coca-Cola, Goldman Sachs
- **Initial listing fee**: $150,000–$295,000
- **Annual listing fee**: $46,000–$500,000
- **Trading mechanism**: Hybrid — electronic + physical floor with DMMs
- **Owner**: Intercontinental Exchange (NYSE: ICE)

## Trading Relevance

- NYSE listing status and index membership ([[s-and-p-500]], [[dow-jones]]) drive passive flows; index add/delete events are tradeable.
- The opening and closing auctions on NYSE are the largest liquidity events of the US trading day — closing auction volume regularly exceeds 5–10% of daily volume in large caps, central to MOC/LOC order strategies.
- DMM-stabilised opens matter during high-volatility sessions (e.g. limit-up/limit-down events); NYSE stocks often reopen more orderly than purely electronic venues.
- The 2026 move to ~23-hour trading will materially change overnight risk management, gap behaviour, and the economics of extended-hours [[liquidity]] provision.
