IG Markets (commonly known as IG) is one of the world's largest and longest-established CFD and [[forex]] brokers. Founded in London in 1974, IG entered the Australian market in 2002 and has become a major player, offering CFDs on 17,000+ global markets alongside traditional ASX share trading with [[chess-sponsorship|CHESS sponsorship]]. Listed on the London Stock Exchange (LON: IGG), IG is one of the most well-capitalised and heavily regulated retail brokers globally.

## Overview

IG's Australian operation, headquartered in Melbourne and regulated by ASIC, serves as a hub for both Australian and Asia-Pacific clients. The broker's key strength is the sheer breadth of its product range — from ASX share trading at a competitive $8 flat fee to CFDs on global shares, forex, indices, commodities, and cryptocurrencies. IG also offers multiple platform options, including its proprietary platform, MetaTrader 4, and ProRealTime for advanced charting.

With over 50 years of operating history globally, IG brings a level of institutional credibility and financial stability that newer fintech brokers cannot match.

## History

- **1974**: Founded in London by Stuart Wheeler as "Investors Gold Index" — the world's first financial spread betting firm
- **1998**: Launched online trading platform
- **2000**: Listed on the London Stock Exchange (LON: IGG)
- **2002**: Entered the Australian market
- **2014**: Rebranded from "IG Markets" to simply "IG" globally (though "IG Markets" remains commonly used in Australia)
- **2019**: Expanded ASX share trading offering alongside core CFD business
- **2021**: Adapted to ASIC's new CFD regulations (leverage limits, negative balance protection)
- **2023–2024**: Continued platform development, expanded ASX share trading, enhanced educational content

## Key Features

- **17,000+ Markets**: One of the largest product ranges of any retail broker — CFDs on global shares, indices, forex, commodities, crypto, and more
- **Competitive ASX Share Trading**: $8 flat fee per trade — among the cheapest of the established brokers (cheaper than [[commsec]], [[nabtrade]], and [[cmc-markets]] for trades under $8,000)
- **CHESS Sponsored**: ASX share trading is fully CHESS-sponsored with personal HIN
- **Multiple Platforms**: Proprietary IG platform (web + mobile), MetaTrader 4 (MT4), and ProRealTime (advanced charting — free with 4+ trades/month)
- **ProRealTime**: Institutional-grade charting platform with automated trading capabilities, pattern recognition, and backtesting
- **Demo Account**: $20,000 virtual funds demo account for practice — available indefinitely
- **Extensive Education**: IG Academy with structured courses from beginner to advanced, webinars, and market analysis
- **Weekend Trading**: Select markets (crypto, weekend indices) available for trading on weekends

## Pros and Cons

### Pros
- **Massive product range**: 17,000+ instruments — one of the widest selections available to Australian traders
- **Competitive share trading fees**: $8 flat for ASX trades — cheaper than most bank-owned brokers
- **Advanced platforms**: Proprietary platform, MT4, and ProRealTime — options for every skill level
- **Well-regulated**: ASIC + FCA + 15+ other jurisdictions — among the most heavily regulated brokers globally
- **50+ years of operation**: Unmatched track record and financial stability
- **CHESS sponsored**: Direct share ownership for ASX trades
- **Excellent education**: IG Academy is among the best free trading education resources available
- **Demo account**: Unlimited practice with virtual funds

### Cons
- **CFD risk**: Like all CFD brokers, a high percentage of retail accounts lose money trading CFDs — IG discloses approximately 70% loss rate
- **Share trading product is more limited**: ASX shares only — no international share trading (only via CFDs). [[commsec]] and [[nabtrade]] offer direct international share ownership
- **Inactivity fee**: $18/month after 2 years of no trading — penalises dormant accounts
- **ProRealTime costs money**: Free only with 4+ monthly trades, otherwise $40/month
- **CFD and share trading feel separate**: The share trading interface is less integrated with the CFD platform than [[cmc-markets]]'s combined offering
- **Complexity for beginners**: The breadth of products and platforms can be overwhelming

## Who It's Best For

- **Active CFD and forex traders** who want a well-regulated broker with tight spreads and advanced platforms
- **Cost-conscious ASX investors**: $8 flat fee is among the cheapest from an established broker
- **Traders wanting platform choice**: MT4, ProRealTime, and proprietary platform cover every style
- **Experienced traders** comfortable with [[leverage|leveraged products]] who understand [[risk-management|risk management]]
- **Beginner traders** (for education): IG Academy and demo accounts provide excellent learning resources before committing real capital
- **Weekend traders**: Select markets available on Saturdays and Sundays

> **Risk Warning**: CFDs are complex instruments and come with a high risk of losing money rapidly due to [[leverage]]. Approximately 70% of retail investor accounts lose money when trading CFDs with IG. You should consider whether you understand how CFDs work and whether you can afford the high risk of losing your money.
