Nifty Gateway was a premium, curated NFT drops platform founded in 2018 and acquired by the Gemini exchange in 2019 (announced November 19, 2019). It differed from open secondary marketplaces like [[opensea]] or [[blur]] in two ways: drops were curated, one-off or limited releases by prominent digital artists, and users could buy directly with fiat via credit card, lowering the on-ramp barrier for non-crypto-native collectors. Gemini announced the platform's closure in January 2026; Nifty Gateway entered withdrawal-only mode, with a final asset-withdrawal deadline of April 23, 2026.

## History and founding

Nifty Gateway was founded in 2018 by twin brothers Duncan Cock Foster and Griffin Cock Foster. The original thesis was that mainstream art collectors would not self-custody or learn wallet mechanics to participate in NFT drops, so fiat rails and a curated release schedule were needed.

In 2019 (publicly announced November 19, 2019), Nifty Gateway was acquired by Gemini, the regulated U.S. crypto exchange founded by Cameron and Tyler Winklevoss. It was the first acquisition by a member of the Gemini group. Under Gemini's ownership, Nifty Gateway retained its brand and curatorial focus while gaining access to Gemini's fiat payment infrastructure and compliance stack.

The platform's most prominent commercial moment came in early 2021 during the Beeple ("Everydays") era, when high-profile drops by Beeple, Pak, XCOPY, Grimes, and other digital artists drove substantial primary sale revenue and significant secondary trading on the platform itself.

## What it does

- **Curated primary drops**: Nifty Gateway schedules "drops" where artists release editions or 1/1 works on a fixed date/time. Drops include open editions (unlimited during a time window), limited editions, and auctions.
- **Fiat on-ramp**: Users can fund purchases with credit card or USD wire via the Gemini-integrated rails, avoiding the need to hold ETH.
- **Custodial wallet by default**: Nifty Gateway runs a custodial model out of the box, though users can withdraw NFTs to an external wallet and trade them on open marketplaces.
- **Internal secondary market**: A secondary marketplace exists on-platform for listings and bids on works originally dropped via Nifty Gateway.
- **Artist roster**: Historically includes Beeple, Pak, XCOPY, Grimes, Refik Anadol, FEWOCiOUS, and numerous others from the crypto-art scene.

## Trading relevance

Nifty Gateway occupies a distinct niche from volume-oriented secondary venues:

- **Primary vs. secondary**: Unlike [[opensea]], [[blur]], [[looksrare]], [[rarible]], or [[x2y2]], Nifty Gateway's core value is primary issuance. Secondary trading exists but is not the platform's main role.
- **Drop arbitrage and flipping**: Traders participating in high-demand open editions or limited editions often list on Nifty Gateway's secondary market or withdraw to [[opensea]] to flip. Spreads between Nifty Gateway secondary and open markets can persist because custodial/withdrawal friction segments the order book.
- **Fiat rails reduce wallet risk for counterparties**: For traders dealing with non-crypto-native buyers (traditional art collectors, institutions), Nifty Gateway's fiat acceptance is a practical routing advantage.
- **Curation as a filter**: Because drops are gated by editorial selection, the platform's catalog skews toward established digital artists rather than the long tail of PFP and generative projects that dominate [[opensea]].

## Current status

Nifty Gateway is in the process of shutting down. In late January 2026, Gemini publicly announced the closure of Nifty Gateway, stating the decision would let Gemini "sharpen its focus" on building a one-stop super app. Key facts as of April 2026:

- **Withdrawal-only mode**: Upon the announcement, the platform entered withdrawal-only mode — users can no longer buy, list, or trade, only withdraw NFTs and balances.
- **Final withdrawal deadline**: The initial shutdown target was February 23, 2026, later extended to give users a full 90 days to withdraw assets; the final deadline is **April 23, 2026**.
- **Asset preservation plan**: Gemini committed to permanent custody of token metadata and media files, with most NFT data migrated to **Arweave**. NFT metadata minted before 2021 is to be hosted indefinitely on Nifty Gateway's own servers.
- **Bulk withdrawal tool**: A bulk withdrawal tool was being built to help users extract multiple assets efficiently before the deadline.

Traders holding Nifty Gateway assets after April 23, 2026 face the risk of losing platform-level access to those assets, with only the Arweave/server-preserved metadata guaranteed post-shutdown.
