# BlockFi

**BlockFi** was a centralized crypto lending and interest-bearing platform that offered users yield on their cryptocurrency deposits and provided crypto-backed loans. Founded in 2017, BlockFi collapsed in **November 2022** following its deep financial entanglement with [[ftx|FTX]], filing for Chapter 11 bankruptcy just weeks after FTX's implosion.

## Rise and Fall

BlockFi attracted customers by offering **high-yield interest accounts** (up to 8-10% APY) on Bitcoin, Ethereum, and stablecoin deposits -- far exceeding traditional bank savings rates. The yields were generated by lending customer assets to institutional borrowers, including [[alameda-research|Alameda Research]].

### Path to Collapse

1. **Feb 2022** -- SEC fined BlockFi $100M for unregistered securities (interest accounts)
2. **May-Jun 2022** -- [[terra-luna]] collapse and Three Arrows Capital default created losses across crypto lending
3. **Jul 2022** -- FTX provided a $400M credit facility to BlockFi, effectively a rescue deal with an option to acquire
4. **Nov 2022** -- FTX collapsed; BlockFi's assets were trapped on FTX, and the credit facility evaporated
5. **Nov 28, 2022** -- BlockFi filed for bankruptcy, listing $1-10B in liabilities

---

## Bankruptcy Resolution (2023-2025)

BlockFi's wind-down turned out far better for creditors than feared at filing:

- **Sep-Oct 2023** -- Bankruptcy court approved BlockFi's Chapter 11 plan to repay roughly 100,000+ claimants; the plan went effective in October 2023 and BlockFi shifted to wind-down mode under a court-appointed plan administrator.
- **Mar 2024** -- BlockFi reached an **$874.5 million settlement** with the FTX and [[alameda-research|Alameda Research]] estates -- comprising a $250M secured claim and $439M general unsecured claim against Alameda, plus a $185M FTX customer claim.
- **Jul 2024** -- BlockFi sold its FTX/Alameda claims to a third party at a "substantial premium to face value" (sale ran June 24 - July 10, 2024), de-risking the estate and pulling forward distributions.
- **2024-2025** -- Final distributions were paid. Eligible customer and general unsecured creditors ultimately recovered **approximately 100% of US-dollar claim values** (valued at petition-date crypto prices -- in-kind, customers still lost the 2023-2024 crypto appreciation). Counsel Haynes Boone publicized the result as a full fiat-terms recovery, a rare outcome among the 2022 crypto bankruptcies.

The recovery was driven less by BlockFi's own balance sheet than by the dramatic rise in value of FTX-estate assets (Anthropic stake, Solana) and crypto prices during the FTX bankruptcy.

## Trading Relevance

- BlockFi's collapse illustrates the systemic risk in **centralized crypto lending (CeFi)** -- unsustainable yields signal hidden risk-taking
- The contagion chain (Three Arrows > Voyager > FTX > BlockFi) shows how interconnected counterparty risk cascades in crypto
- "If the yield looks too good to be true, find out where it's coming from" became a defining lesson of the 2022 [[crypto-winter]]
- BlockFi's failure reinforced the case for [[self-custody]] and transparent, [[defi|decentralized]] lending alternatives like [[aave]]
- The ~100% fiat-terms recovery is a key data point for **bankruptcy-claims trading**: distressed buyers who bought BlockFi/FTX customer claims at 20-40 cents in 2023 realized outsized returns -- crypto bankruptcy claims became a recognized distressed-asset class
- Petition-date dollarization of claims is the recurring trap: "100% recovery" still meant forfeiting the BTC/ETH upside between Nov 2022 and distribution, a lesson in [[counterparty-risk]] denominated in the wrong unit
