Stake is an Australian fintech broker that pioneered low-cost US share access for Australian investors. Initially launching with zero brokerage on US trades — the first Australian broker to offer this — Stake became the go-to platform for Australians wanting to buy shares in US-listed companies like Apple, Tesla, and NVIDIA without paying the $19.95+ fees charged by incumbents like [[commsec]].

## Overview

Founded in Sydney in 2017, Stake identified a clear gap in the Australian market: Australian investors wanted exposure to US tech giants and growth stocks, but existing brokers charged high brokerage and FX fees that made small-to-medium US trades uneconomical. Stake's solution was a mobile-first platform with minimal fees, leveraging a partnership with US execution partner DriveWealth.

Stake subsequently expanded into ASX trading, offering $3 flat brokerage with [[chess-sponsorship|CHESS sponsorship]]. The platform grew rapidly during the 2020–2021 retail trading boom, particularly among younger investors attracted to US markets.

## History

- **2017**: Founded in Sydney; launched with zero brokerage on US shares
- **2018**: Rapid early adoption — filled a gap no Australian broker was addressing
- **2019**: Reached significant scale; established as the default platform for Australians trading US shares
- **2020–2021**: Explosive growth during COVID retail boom — GameStop, Tesla, and meme stock frenzy drove record signups
- **2021**: Launched ASX trading with $3 flat fee and CHESS sponsorship
- **2022**: Introduced Stake Black premium subscription
- **2023**: Adjusted pricing — US trades now $3 USD on free plan (previously $0), $0 on Stake Black subscription
- **2024**: Continued expansion of product range and platform features

## Key Features

- **Low-Cost US Trading**: $3 USD per trade (free plan) or $0 (Stake Black) — dramatically cheaper than traditional brokers
- **ASX Trading**: $3 AUD flat fee per trade, CHESS sponsored
- **Stake Black Subscription**: $9 AUD/month — includes $0 US brokerage, priority execution, more detailed market data, and extended trading hours access
- **US Market Access**: Full access to NYSE and NASDAQ-listed equities and ETFs
- **Mobile-First Design**: Clean, modern mobile app designed for younger investors
- **Instant Funding**: Quick AUD to USD conversion for US trading
- **Market Orders and Limit Orders**: Standard order types available

| **Stake Black** ($9/month) | $0 per trade | FX spread on AUD/USD conversion (~0.70%) |

### ASX Share Trading

| Trade Type | Fee |
|------------|-----|
| Any ASX trade | $3 AUD flat |

### FX Conversion
- AUD/USD conversion spread of approximately 0.70% — this is the primary hidden cost for US trading
- No separate FX fee, but the spread is applied on every conversion

### Other Fees
- **Account keeping**: Free
- **Inactivity fee**: None
- **Withdrawal (USD to AUD)**: FX spread applies

## Trading Products

- **US Equities**: NYSE and NASDAQ-listed shares
- **US ETFs**: Full range of US-listed ETFs
- **ASX Equities**: All ASX-listed shares (CHESS sponsored)
- **ASX ETFs**: All ASX-listed ETFs

**Not available**: Options, fractional shares, international markets beyond US, margin lending, managed funds, crypto (discontinued)

## Ownership and Custody Models

This is an important distinction for Stake users:

| Market | Custody Model | What This Means |
|--------|--------------|-----------------|
| **ASX** | CHESS sponsored | Shares registered in your name with a personal HIN — you directly own them |
| **US** | Custodial (via DriveWealth) | Shares held by DriveWealth on your behalf — you have beneficial ownership but shares are not in your name |

The custodial model for US shares means that if DriveWealth experienced financial difficulty, the process of recovering shares could be more complex than with CHESS-sponsored holdings. However, DriveWealth is regulated by FINRA and shares are protected by SIPC insurance up to $500,000.

## Pros and Cons

### Pros
- **Very low US trading fees**: $3 or $0 (Black) per trade — makes small US investments viable
- **Clean mobile app**: Modern, intuitive interface designed for a new generation of investors
- **Easy US market access**: Simplified onboarding for Australians accessing US shares
- **Cheap ASX trading**: $3 flat fee is among the cheapest in Australia
- **CHESS sponsored for ASX**: Direct share ownership for Australian trades
- **No minimums**: Start investing with any amount

### Cons
- **Custodial model for US shares**: Not CHESS — shares held by DriveWealth, not directly in your name
- **FX spread is a hidden cost**: ~0.70% on every AUD/USD conversion adds up, especially for larger amounts
- **Limited research**: No analyst reports, minimal market analysis tools
- **No options trading**: Cannot trade options on either ASX or US markets
- **No fractional shares**: Must buy whole shares (unlike [[robinhood]] in the US)
- **Limited product range**: US and ASX equities/ETFs only — no bonds, managed funds, or other markets

## Who It's Best For

- **Young Australian investors** wanting easy, affordable access to US tech stocks and growth companies
- **Small-to-medium US investors**: The low brokerage makes investing $500–$5,000 in US shares economical
- **ASX investors seeking rock-bottom fees**: $3 flat is hard to beat
- **Investors comfortable with custodial model**: Those who understand and accept the US custody arrangement
- **Mobile-first investors**: Those who prefer trading via app rather than desktop platforms
