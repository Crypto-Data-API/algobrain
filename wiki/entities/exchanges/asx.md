The Australian Securities Exchange (ASX) is Australia's primary securities exchange, operated by [[asx-limited|ASX Limited]]. Founded in 1987 through the merger of six state-based stock exchanges, the ASX provides listing, trading, clearing, and settlement services for equities, [[derivatives]], ETFs, fixed income, and [[futures]]. It is a top-15 global exchange by market capitalisation of listed companies (approximately AUD $2.5 trillion), with roughly 1,800 listed companies and over 440 ETFs as of 2025.

The exchange uses the [[chess-sponsorship|CHESS]] (Clearing House Electronic Subregister System) for electronic settlement and share registration. Trading hours are 10:00 AM to 4:00 PM Sydney time. The ASX also operates ASX 24, a derivatives exchange for interest rate, commodity, and energy futures. The primary benchmark index is the [[asx-200|S&P/ASX 200]], which tracks the 200 largest companies by market capitalisation and serves as the main gauge of Australian equity market performance (record high of 9,202.9 set on 26 February 2026).

Recent regulatory history (covered in detail at [[asx-limited]]): ASIC sued ASX in August 2024 over allegedly misleading statements about the abandoned blockchain-based CHESS replacement, which is now being rebuilt on TCS BaNCS; a CHESS batch settlement failure on 20 December 2024 prompted the RBA to downgrade ASX's operational risk compliance to "not observed" and ASIC to order an independent technical review of CHESS (March 2025).

For listing rules, [[gics-classification|GICS classification]] of sectors, capital raise regulations, financial data, company history, and investment analysis, see the full article at [[asx-limited]].
