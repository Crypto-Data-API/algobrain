SelfWealth is an Australian discount stockbroker that disrupted the domestic broking market with a simple, flat-fee pricing model. Charging a flat $9.50 per trade regardless of trade size, SelfWealth attracted cost-conscious investors away from incumbents like [[commsec]] and became one of the fastest-growing brokers in Australia during the 2020–2021 retail trading boom.

## Overview

Founded in Melbourne in 2012, SelfWealth was built on a straightforward value proposition: flat, low brokerage fees with [[chess-sponsorship|CHESS sponsorship]]. At a time when [[commsec]] charged up to $29.95 per trade, SelfWealth's $9.50 flat fee — whether trading $500 or $500,000 — was a genuine market disruptor. The company IPO'd on the ASX in 2017 (ASX: SWF), making it a publicly traded broker accountable to shareholders.

SelfWealth also pioneered a social investing feature in Australia, allowing users to anonymously compare their portfolio performance against the SelfWealth community, adding a competitive and educational dimension to the platform.

## History

- **2012**: Founded in Melbourne as an online-first discount broker
- **2017**: IPO'd on the ASX (ASX: SWF)
- **2019**: Reached 50,000 active accounts
- **2020–2021**: Rapid growth during COVID retail trading boom; surpassed 100,000+ accounts
- **2021**: Launched US share trading via DriveWealth partnership
- **2022**: Added cryptocurrency trading via partner integration
- **2023–2024**: Continued to compete on price as newer entrants ([[superhero]], [[stake]]) pushed fees even lower

## Key Features

- **Flat $9.50 Brokerage**: The same fee whether you trade $100 or $100,000 — a significant advantage for larger trade sizes compared to percentage-based pricing
- **CHESS Sponsored**: All ASX shares held directly in your name with a personal HIN
- **US Share Trading**: Access to NYSE and NASDAQ-listed stocks (via DriveWealth), $9.50 AUD per trade plus 0.60% FX conversion fee
- **Social Portfolio Comparison**: View anonymised community portfolio performance, see how your returns rank — unique among Australian brokers
- **No Inactivity Fees**: No charges for inactive accounts
- **No Minimum Account Balance**: Start with any amount
- **Cryptocurrency**: Access to crypto trading via platform integration

| Component | Fee |
|-----------|-----|
| US trade brokerage | $9.50 AUD flat |

### Other Fees

- **Account keeping**: Free
- **Inactivity fee**: None
- **Real-time ASX data**: Free
- **Transfer in (CHESS)**: Free — move your existing HIN to SelfWealth

## Trading Products

- **ASX Equities**: Full access to all ASX-listed shares
- **ASX ETFs**: All ASX-listed exchange-traded funds
- **US Equities**: NYSE and NASDAQ-listed shares (via DriveWealth)
- **Cryptocurrency**: Select crypto assets via platform partnership

**Not available**: ASX options, international markets beyond US, margin lending, managed funds (mFunds), warrants

## Pros and Cons

### Pros
- **Flat low fee**: $9.50 regardless of trade size — unbeatable for larger trades (a $50,000 trade costs $9.50 vs $60 at [[commsec]])
- **CHESS sponsored**: Direct share ownership with personal HIN
- **Social comparison feature**: Unique portfolio benchmarking against community — motivating and educational
- **No minimums or inactivity fees**: Accessible for all investor levels
- **ASX-listed (SWF)**: Publicly traded with regulatory oversight and financial transparency
- **Simple, clean interface**: Easy to navigate for investors who don't need advanced tools

### Cons
- **Limited research tools**: No analyst reports, basic market news only — investors need to source research elsewhere
- **No options trading**: Cannot trade ASX Exchange Traded Options
- **Basic charting**: Limited technical analysis tools compared to [[cmc-markets]] or [[interactive-brokers]]
- **Smaller product range**: No international markets beyond the US, no margin lending, no mFunds
- **US trading costs add up**: $9.50 + 0.60% FX means a $5,000 USD trade costs ~$39.50 AUD all-in — less competitive than [[stake]] for US shares
- **Smaller company**: Less established than bank-owned brokers, though ASX listing provides some assurance

## Who It's Best For

- **Cost-conscious investors** making medium to large trades on the ASX
- **Buy-and-hold investors** who trade infrequently but want low fixed costs
- **Investors switching from CommSec** who want CHESS sponsorship at a lower price
- **Self-directed investors** comfortable doing their own research
- **Competitive investors** who enjoy benchmarking performance against the community
