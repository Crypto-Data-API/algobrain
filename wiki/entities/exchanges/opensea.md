OpenSea is the world's first and most recognized [[nft|NFT]] marketplace, founded by Devin Finzer and Alex Atallah in December 2017. At its peak in January 2022, OpenSea processed over $5 billion in monthly trading volume and achieved a $13.3 billion valuation, making it the dominant platform for buying, selling, and discovering non-fungible tokens across multiple blockchains.

## History

OpenSea launched in December 2017, positioning itself as a general-purpose marketplace for [[nft|NFTs]] on the [[ethereum|Ethereum]] blockchain. The platform grew steadily during the early NFT era and exploded in popularity during the 2021-2022 NFT boom.

In January 2022, OpenSea raised a $300 million Series C round at a $13.3 billion valuation, reflecting its dominance of the NFT market at the time. That same month, the platform hit its all-time high of over $5 billion in monthly trading volume.

However, the platform faced several challenges. In September 2021, an insider trading scandal emerged when an OpenSea employee was caught front-running NFT listings — buying pieces before they were featured on the homepage, then selling them for a profit after the promotion drove up prices. This was one of the first high-profile cases of NFT insider trading.

As the broader NFT market crashed through 2022, OpenSea laid off approximately 50% of its staff. The company then faced an existential competitive threat when [[blur|Blur]] launched in October 2022 with a zero-fee model and token incentives, overtaking OpenSea in trading volume by February 2023.

In response, OpenSea launched OpenSea Pro (an NFT aggregator) and reduced its marketplace fees to compete, but it struggled to recapture its former market share against Blur's trader-focused approach.

- **Peak valuation**: $13.3 billion (January 2022 Series C)
- **Peak monthly volume**: $5 billion+ (January 2022)
- **Revenue model**: 2.5% fee on all sales, plus optional creator royalties

- **Layoffs**: ~50% of staff cut in 2022 as NFT market contracted
- **Competitor response**: Launched OpenSea Pro aggregator to compete with [[blur|Blur]]

## Significance

OpenSea played a foundational role in the NFT ecosystem. It was the first major general-purpose NFT marketplace and served as the primary on-ramp for millions of users entering the NFT space. The platform's dominance during 2021-2022 made it synonymous with NFT trading itself.

OpenSea's 2.5% fee model and creator royalty system established the economic norms for the NFT marketplace industry — norms that were later challenged by [[blur|Blur]]'s zero-fee approach. The insider trading scandal at OpenSea also highlighted the regulatory gray areas in NFT markets and contributed to calls for greater oversight.

Despite losing market share, OpenSea remains the most recognized NFT marketplace brand globally and continues to serve as an important infrastructure layer for the broader NFT ecosystem.
