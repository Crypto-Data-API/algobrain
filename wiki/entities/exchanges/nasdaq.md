# NASDAQ

The NASDAQ (National Association of Securities Dealers Automated Quotations) is the world's first electronic stock exchange and the second-largest stock exchange globally by market capitalization. It is heavily weighted toward technology and growth companies and serves as the listing venue for many of the world's largest firms including Apple, Microsoft, Amazon, [[nvidia|NVIDIA]], and [[tesla|Tesla]].

## Overview

Founded in 1971, NASDAQ pioneered electronic trading, replacing the traditional floor-based open outcry system. This innovation laid the groundwork for modern [[algorithmic-trading]] and electronic [[market-structure]]. Today, NASDAQ operates as both an exchange and an index provider.

## Key Indices

- **NASDAQ Composite**: Includes all 3,000+ stocks listed on the NASDAQ exchange. Heavily tech-weighted, making it more volatile than the [[s-and-p-500]] or [[dow-jones]].
- **NASDAQ-100 (NDX/QQQ)**: The 100 largest non-financial companies on NASDAQ. The QQQ ETF tracking this index is one of the most actively traded securities in the world.

## Historical Significance

The NASDAQ Composite became the symbol of the [[dot-com-bubble]], rising from roughly 1,000 in 1995 to a peak of 5,048 on March 10, 2000, before crashing 78% to 1,114 by October 2002. It did not recover its 2000 high until 2015. This history makes the NASDAQ a key reference point for understanding speculative excess and technology-driven market cycles.

The AI-driven bull market of 2023–2026 pushed the Composite to successive records: it returned roughly +20.4% in 2025 and stood near **25,930 on June 8, 2026** — more than five times the dot-com peak — powered by mega-cap AI names led by [[nvidia|NVIDIA]].

## 24-Hour Trading (announced December 2025)

In December 2025, Nasdaq filed with the [[sec|SEC]] to extend US equities trading to **24 hours a day, five days a week**, targeting launch in the **second half of 2026** pending regulatory approval and readiness of industry infrastructure (SIPs, clearing). The proposed structure expands the current 16-hour day to 23 hours: a "day session" from 4:00 a.m. to 8:00 p.m. ET, a one-hour maintenance/clearing pause, then a "night session" from 9:00 p.m. to 4:00 a.m. ET. The move responds to surging overnight demand from Asian retail investors and competition from 24-hour venues; critics warn of thin overnight liquidity and wider spreads. For traders, round-the-clock sessions would change gap-risk dynamics (fewer opening gaps), overnight-session liquidity profiles, and stop placement around the maintenance window.

Nasdaq also faces new listings competition from the Texas Stock Exchange (TXSE), the Dallas-based venue backed by BlackRock and Citadel Securities that is preparing to compete for corporate listings.

## Trading Relevance

The NASDAQ and its derivatives (QQQ options, NQ futures) are among the most liquid and actively traded instruments globally. Because of its tech concentration, the NASDAQ is highly sensitive to [[interest-rates]] (growth stock valuations are more rate-sensitive), AI/technology developments, and risk appetite. It is the primary benchmark for technology-focused traders and a critical component of any macro trader's dashboard.
