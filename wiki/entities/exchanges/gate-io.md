# Gate.io

**Gate.io** (rebranded simply as **Gate**) is one of the oldest continuously operating centralized cryptocurrency exchanges ([[centralized-exchange|CEX]]), founded in 2013. It is known for an extremely **deep altcoin catalog** — among the broadest of any major venue — and for being an early **[[proof-of-reserves]] pioneer**, having published Merkle-tree-based reserve attestations well before the practice became industry standard after the 2022 [[ftx|FTX]] collapse. Its native [[gatechain-token|GT (GateToken)]] powers fee discounts and the broader Gate / GateChain ecosystem.

## Overview & History

Gate.io traces its origins to 2013, making it one of the longest-running exchanges in crypto — a notable distinction in an industry where many venues have collapsed or shut down. Longevity gave Gate a reputation among long-tail traders as a reliable home for obscure and early-stage tokens. The exchange built out its own infrastructure over time, including the **GateChain** blockchain and the [[gatechain-token|GT]] token, and later adopted the shorter "Gate" brand. Gate was among the first exchanges to commission and publish independent **proof-of-reserves** audits using Merkle-tree methodology, predating the post-FTX wave of reserve transparency.

Gate's signature trait is **listing breadth and depth on the long tail**: many tokens trade on Gate that are unavailable on top-tier venues, making it a key venue for early price discovery on small-cap assets.

## Exchange Token — GT

Gate's native [[exchange-tokens|exchange token]] is [[gatechain-token|GT]] (GateToken):

- **Fee discounts** for trading-fee payment in GT
- **Ecosystem utility** across Gate products and the **GateChain** blockchain (GT is its native gas/utility asset)
- **Startup / launchpad access** and allocation weighting for holders
- Associated with **buyback-and-burn** style supply reduction typical of [[exchange-tokens|exchange tokens]] (see [[gatechain-token|GT]] for current tokenomics)

## Trading Relevance

- **Listing strategy / early liquidity**: Like [[kucoin]], Gate is a go-to venue for trading altcoins early; it frequently lists tokens long before they reach [[binance]] or [[okx]].
- **Liquidity tier**: Gate sits in the second tier on major pairs but is often the deepest — or only — CEX market for many long-tail tokens.
- **Cross-venue arbitrage**: Early and exclusive listings produce frequent price gaps between Gate and other venues or DEXs, a source of arbitrage opportunities alongside elevated thin-book risk.
- **Positioning vs. peers**: Gate competes primarily on **longevity and listing depth** rather than raw liquidity or derivatives volume. Its early proof-of-reserves adoption is a differentiator on the transparency axis relative to peers that only began attestations after FTX.

| Venue | Gate's edge | Their edge |

| [[kucoin]] | Comparable altcoin breadth, longer track record | Similar profile; KCS daily fee-share dividend |

## Security & Regulatory

- **Proof-of-reserves pioneer**: Gate was an early adopter of cryptographic [[proof-of-reserves]] (Merkle-tree) attestations, publishing reserve reports before the practice became standard.
- As an offshore exchange operating across many jurisdictions, Gate's licensing and access status vary by country and over time; like other offshore CEXs it has restricted access in some markets. Verify current jurisdictional availability before relying on it.

## Risks

- **Counterparty / custody risk**: Funds custodied on Gate carry the usual exchange insolvency, mismanagement, and hack risks; proof-of-reserves attestations reduce but do not eliminate them.
- **Regulatory risk**: Offshore status means uncertain and shifting regulatory standing and possible access restrictions by jurisdiction.
- **Listing-quality / liquidity risk**: An exceptionally broad long-tail catalog includes many illiquid, low-quality, and high-volatility tokens; thin order books raise slippage, manipulation, and wash-trading exposure.
