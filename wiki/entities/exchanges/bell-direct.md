Bell Direct is an Australian online stockbroker operated by Bell Financial Group — one of Australia's largest and most established independent financial services firms. Positioned in the mid-range between discount brokers ([[selfwealth]], [[superhero]]) and full-service firms ([[morgans]]), Bell Direct combines competitive pricing with access to Bell Potter's highly regarded institutional-quality research — a combination that appeals to serious self-directed investors who value research depth.

## Overview

Bell Financial Group (ASX: BFG) has a long history in Australian financial markets, with Bell Potter Securities being one of the country's leading institutional and retail broking firms. Bell Direct is the group's online trading arm, designed for self-directed investors who want to manage their own portfolios but benefit from the research firepower of an institutional broking house.

The platform is [[chess-sponsorship|CHESS-sponsored]], supports ASX equities, ETFs, options, warrants, and managed funds, and offers a distinctive AutoInvest feature for automated regular investment plans — making it well-suited for disciplined [[dollar-cost-averaging|dollar-cost averaging]] strategies.

## History

- **2006**: Launched as Bell Financial Group's online broking platform
- **2010s**: Established a loyal user base attracted by Bell Potter research access and competitive pricing
- **2018**: Enhanced mobile app and platform features
- **2020–2021**: Grew alongside the broader Australian retail trading boom
- **2022–2024**: Continued development of AutoInvest feature and platform upgrades, competing against increasing pressure from ultra-low-cost fintechs

## Key Features

- **Bell Potter Research Access**: Subscribers get access to Bell Potter Securities' proprietary research — one of the most respected institutional research teams in Australia, covering 200+ ASX-listed companies. This is a significant differentiator, as Bell Potter research is typically available only to institutional clients or Bell Potter's full-service broking clients
- **CHESS Sponsored**: All ASX shares held directly in your name with a personal HIN
- **AutoInvest**: Automated regular investment plans — set up recurring purchases of shares or ETFs on a schedule (weekly, fortnightly, monthly). Ideal for [[dollar-cost-averaging|dollar-cost averaging]]. Brokerage still applies per transaction
- **BellDirect Pro**: Advanced trading platform with enhanced charting, Level 2 market depth, advanced order types, and real-time streaming data
- **Options Trading**: ASX Exchange Traded Options — one of the few mid-range brokers to offer options
- **Margin Lending**: Available through margin lending partners
- **SMSF Accounts**: Dedicated [[smsf|SMSF]] account type with [[chess-sponsorship|CHESS]]-sponsored HIN — supports [[superannuation|superannuation]] investing, [[franking-credits|franking credit]] tracking, and AutoInvest for SMSF [[dollar-cost-averaging]] strategies

## Fee Structure

### ASX Trading

| Trade Value | Brokerage Fee |
|-------------|---------------|
| Up to $10,000 | $15.00 |
| $10,001 – $25,000 | $25.00 |
| $25,001+ | 0.10% of trade value |

### Options Trading

| Trade Type | Fee |
|------------|-----|
| Online options | $28.00 or 0.15% (whichever is greater) |
| Exercise/assignment | $28.00 |

### Research Subscription

| Tier | Monthly Cost | Includes |
|------|-------------|----------|
| Basic (free) | $0 | Market news, basic data |
| Bell Potter Research | Included with active trading or paid subscription | Full analyst reports, stock recommendations, price targets |

### Other Fees
- **Account keeping**: Free
- **Inactivity fee**: None
- **Real-time ASX data**: Free for active traders
- **mFunds**: Standard brokerage rates apply

## Trading Products

- **ASX Equities**: Full access to all ASX-listed shares
- **ETFs**: All ASX-listed ETFs
- **Options**: ASX Exchange Traded Options (puts, calls, multi-leg strategies)
- **Warrants**: ASX-listed warrants
- **mFunds**: Managed fund settlement via ASX mFund platform
- **Margin Lending**: Via partner facility
- **AutoInvest Plans**: Automated regular investment plans

**Not available**: International shares, CFDs, forex, cryptocurrency

## Pros and Cons

### Pros
- **Bell Potter research**: Access to institutional-quality research that is unavailable on discount platforms — stock-specific reports, price targets, and buy/sell recommendations from a respected research team
- **CHESS sponsored**: Direct share ownership with personal HIN
- **Options trading**: Full ASX options access — not common among mid-range brokers
- **AutoInvest feature**: Automated regular investing for disciplined portfolio building
- **Part of Bell Financial Group**: Backed by a large, ASX-listed financial group with decades of market experience
- **Competitive for larger trades**: 0.10% for trades above $25,000 is competitive with [[commsec]] (0.12%)

### Cons
- **Fees not as competitive as discount brokers**: $15 per trade vs $9.50 at [[selfwealth]], $3 at [[stake]], $2 at [[superhero]]
- **Smaller brand recognition**: Less well-known than [[commsec]] or [[nabtrade]] among mainstream investors
- **No international shares**: Cannot trade US or other international markets — must use a separate broker for global access
- **No CFDs or forex**: Limited to ASX products only
- **Platform less modern than fintechs**: Interface is functional but not as polished as [[stake]] or [[superhero]]

## Who It's Best For

- **Research-focused self-directed investors** who want institutional-quality analysis to inform their trading decisions
- **Options traders** seeking a mid-range broker with ASX Exchange Traded Options access
- **Regular investors** who want to automate purchases via AutoInvest for dollar-cost averaging
- **Intermediate to advanced investors** who need more tools than [[selfwealth]] but don't want to pay for full-service advice from [[morgans]]
- **SMSF trustees** looking for a solid platform with research, options, and CHESS sponsorship
- **Larger portfolio holders**: The 0.10% rate above $25K is competitive for bigger trades
