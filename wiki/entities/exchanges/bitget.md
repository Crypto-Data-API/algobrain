# Bitget

**Bitget** is a global centralized cryptocurrency exchange ([[centralized-exchange|CEX]]) registered in the Seychelles, best known for its derivatives products and for popularizing **copy trading** in crypto. Founded in 2018, Bitget grew rapidly during the 2021-2024 cycle to become one of the larger second-tier venues by volume — positioned behind [[binance]], [[okx]], and [[bybit]] but ahead of most regional exchanges. Its native [[bitget-token|BGB]] token underpins fee discounts and the broader Bitget ecosystem, which also includes the self-custodial **Bitget Wallet**.

## Overview & History

Bitget launched in 2018 and initially focused on derivatives trading. Its breakout differentiator was an emphasis on **social / copy trading**: the platform let retail users automatically mirror the positions of designated "elite traders," which became a major retail-acquisition funnel. Over subsequent years Bitget broadened into spot markets, an Earn suite, a launchpad, and a non-custodial wallet, evolving from a pure derivatives shop into a full-stack exchange ecosystem. The company has invested heavily in marketing and sponsorships to build brand recognition outside its original Asian base.

## Products & Markets

| Product | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Spot** | Broad catalog of spot trading pairs across majors and altcoins |
| **Derivatives** | USDT-margined and coin-margined [[perpetual-futures|perpetual]] contracts; derivatives are Bitget's historical core |
| **Leverage** | High leverage available on major perps (verify current per-asset maximums on the official venue) |
| **Copy trading** | Flagship feature — mirror "elite trader" positions automatically; also available for spot and futures |
| **Earn** | Savings, staking, and structured yield products |
| **Launchpad / Launchpool** | New-token distribution events, often tied to [[bitget-token|BGB]] holding |
| **Bitget Wallet** | Self-custodial multi-chain wallet (formerly BitKeep) with swap and DApp browser |

Derivatives volume is the center of gravity for Bitget, and its copy-trading flow produces a distinctive retail order pattern: many "follower" accounts open correlated positions when a lead trader trades, which can create predictable, clustered flow.

## Exchange Token — BGB

Bitget's native [[exchange-tokens|exchange token]] is [[bitget-token|BGB]]. Like peers, it functions as a fee-discount and utility token:

- **Fee discounts** for paying trading fees in BGB
- **Launchpad / Launchpool access** and allocation weighting for holders
- **Ecosystem utility** across Bitget products and, increasingly, the broader Bitget/Morph ecosystem
- The token has been associated with periodic **buyback-and-burn** style supply mechanics typical of [[exchange-tokens|exchange tokens]] (see the [[bitget-token|BGB]] page for current tokenomics)

BGB is one of the larger exchange tokens by market capitalization and serves as a rough proxy for Bitget's business momentum.

## Trading Relevance

- **Liquidity tier**: Bitget sits in the second tier of CEX liquidity — deep enough for retail and mid-size trading, but thinner than [[binance]] or [[okx]] on most pairs, so large orders incur more [[slippage]].
- **Listing strategy**: Bitget lists a wide range of altcoins and frequently runs launchpad events, making it a venue where newer tokens trade before reaching the top-tier exchanges.
- **Copy-trading flow**: The follower-driven order pattern can create exploitable microstructure (clustered entries/exits) and means some flow is non-discretionary.
- **Positioning vs. peers**: Against [[bybit]] (the closest comparable, also derivatives-and-copy-trading-focused), Bitget competes on copy-trading depth and aggressive marketing; against [[binance]]/[[okx]] it competes on listings breadth and promotions rather than raw liquidity.

| Venue | Bitget's edge | Their edge |
|---|---|---|
| [[binance]] | More aggressive promos/listings, copy-trading focus | Far deeper liquidity, reference pricing |
| [[okx]] | Copy-trading depth, marketing | Deeper books, unified-account capital efficiency |
| [[bybit]] | Comparable derivatives + copy trading | Larger derivatives volume, broader institutional use |

## Security & Regulatory

- Bitget publishes **[[proof-of-reserves]]** attestations (Merkle-tree-based reserve reporting became standard across major CEXs after the 2022 [[ftx|FTX]] collapse).
- As a Seychelles-registered offshore exchange, Bitget operates across many jurisdictions with varying licensing; like other offshore CEXs it has faced regional access restrictions and regulatory scrutiny over time. Specific licensing status varies by country and changes frequently — verify before relying on it.

## Risks

- **Counterparty / custody risk**: Funds held on Bitget are custodied by the exchange; proof-of-reserves attestations reduce but do not eliminate the risk of insolvency, mismanagement, or hacks. Not your keys, not your coins.
- **Regulatory risk**: Offshore registration means uncertain and shifting regulatory standing; access can be restricted in specific jurisdictions.
- **Listing-quality risk**: A broad altcoin and launchpad catalog includes thinly traded, high-volatility tokens; listing breadth raises exposure to low-quality or short-lived assets.
- **Liquidity risk**: Second-tier liquidity means wider spreads and more slippage at size than top venues.
