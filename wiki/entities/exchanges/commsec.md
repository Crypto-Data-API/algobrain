CommSec (Commonwealth Securities) is Australia's largest retail stockbroker, wholly owned by the Commonwealth Bank of Australia (CBA) — the country's biggest bank by market capitalisation. With over 3 million accounts in a country of 26 million people, CommSec has the highest market penetration of any Australian broker and is often the default first platform for Australian investors.

## Overview

Launched in 1995 as CBA's online trading arm, CommSec has dominated Australian retail broking for decades. Its deep integration with CBA banking services — instant fund transfers, linked transaction accounts, and unified login — gives it an enormous structural advantage. CommSec is [[chess-sponsorship|CHESS-sponsored]], meaning investors directly own their shares on the [[australian-investing|ASX]] register under their own Holder Identification Number (HIN).

CommSec serves a broad market from beginners (via CommSec Pocket) to active traders (via CommSec Pro), though its fee structure is significantly higher than discount competitors like [[selfwealth]] and [[superhero]].

## History

- **1995**: Launched as Commonwealth Securities, one of Australia's first online brokers
- **2000s**: Grew rapidly alongside internet adoption and the Australian mining boom
- **2012**: Launched mobile trading app
- **2019**: Launched CommSec Pocket — micro-investing app targeting beginners
- **2020–2021**: Experienced record account openings during COVID retail trading boom
- **2024**: Remains Australia's largest broker by accounts despite increasing competition from low-cost alternatives

## Key Features

- **CBA Integration**: Instant transfers between CBA bank accounts and trading account, unified app experience for banking and investing
- **CommSec Pocket**: Simplified micro-investing app — invest from $50 in curated themed ETFs (e.g., "Aussie Top 200", "Tech Savvy", "Sustainability Leaders"). Brokerage: $2 for trades up to $1,000, 0.20% above $1,000
- **CommSec Pro**: Advanced trading platform with Level 2 market depth, customizable watchlists, advanced order types, and real-time streaming data (free for clients making 10+ trades per month)
- **Research**: Free access to analyst reports from Morningstar, Citi, and Bell Potter — one of the most comprehensive free research libraries among Australian brokers
- **CHESS Sponsored**: All ASX trades are CHESS-sponsored with a personal HIN
- **Margin Lending**: Available through CBA's margin lending facility
- **SMSF Accounts**: Dedicated [[smsf|SMSF]] trading account type with [[chess-sponsorship|CHESS]]-sponsored HIN separate from personal accounts — supports [[superannuation|superannuation]] investing, [[franking-credits|franking credit]] tracking, and pension phase portfolios
- **International Trading**: Access to 20+ international markets including NYSE, NASDAQ, LSE, HKEX

## Fee Structure

### ASX Trading

| Trade Value | Brokerage Fee |
|-------------|---------------|
| Up to $1,000 | $10.00 |
| $1,001 – $10,000 | $19.95 |
| $10,001 – $25,000 | $29.95 |
| $25,001+ | 0.12% of trade value |

### International Trading

| Market | Fee |
|--------|-----|
| US (NYSE, NASDAQ) | $19.95 USD or 0.31% (whichever is greater) |
| Other markets | Varies by market, generally $29.95 or percentage |
| FX conversion | Spread applied on currency conversion |

### Other Fees

- **Account keeping**: Free
- **Inactivity fee**: None
- **Live pricing**: Free (delayed 20 min for non-CBA customers without active trading)

## Trading Products

- **ETFs**: All ASX-listed ETFs
- **Options**: ASX-listed equity and index options (Exchange Traded Options)
- **mFunds**: Managed funds settlement via ASX mFund platform
- **International Shares**: 20+ markets including US, UK, Hong Kong, Japan, Germany
- **Margin Lending**: Via CBA margin lending facility
- **CommSec Pocket**: Themed ETF micro-investing
- **Warrants**: ASX-listed warrants

## Pros and Cons

### Pros
- **Trusted brand**: Backed by CBA, Australia's largest bank — high trust factor
- **CBA banking integration**: Seamless money movement, unified digital experience
- **Comprehensive research**: Free Morningstar, Citi, and Bell Potter reports
- **CHESS sponsored**: Direct share ownership under personal HIN
- **Product breadth**: Equities, options, international, mFunds, margin lending — nearly everything under one roof
- **CommSec Pocket**: Excellent onramp for beginner investors
- **Market leader**: 3M+ accounts, proven reliability, rarely experiences outages

### Cons
- **Higher fees than discount brokers**: $19.95 for a $5,000 trade vs $9.50 at [[selfwealth]] or $3 at [[stake]]
- **Basic charting tools**: Standard platform charting is limited compared to [[interactive-brokers]] or [[cmc-markets]]
- **No fractional ASX shares**: Cannot buy fractional shares on the ASX (CommSec Pocket uses whole ETF units)
- **International fees are expensive**: $19.95 USD minimum for US trades is uncompetitive vs [[stake]] ($3 USD) or [[interactive-brokers]]
- **CBA lock-in**: Best experience requires CBA banking relationship

## Who It's Best For

- **CBA banking customers** who want seamless integration between banking and investing
- **Beginner to intermediate investors** who value research access and a trusted brand
- **Investors who prioritise safety and trust** over cost minimisation
- **Portfolio sizes above $25,000** where the 0.12% rate becomes competitive
- **Options traders** seeking an Australian broker with ASX options access
- **Investors wanting a single platform** for ASX, international, and managed funds
