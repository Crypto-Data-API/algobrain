# KuCoin

**KuCoin** is a global centralized cryptocurrency exchange ([[centralized-exchange|CEX]]) founded in 2017 and widely branded as **"The People's Exchange."** It is best known for an exceptionally **wide altcoin listing policy** — KuCoin frequently lists smaller and earlier-stage tokens before they appear on top-tier venues — and for its native [[kucoin-shares|KCS]] token, which shares a portion of the exchange's trading-fee revenue with holders. In 2024, KuCoin and two of its founders reached a settlement with U.S. authorities over anti-money-laundering and money-transmission violations.

## Overview & History

KuCoin launched in 2017 and positioned itself as a venue for the broad universe of altcoins rather than just blue-chip assets, cultivating a reputation as the place to find tokens early — hence the "People's Exchange" branding. This wide-listing strategy made KuCoin a key early-stage liquidity venue for many projects that later graduated to [[binance]] or [[okx]]. KuCoin expanded over time into derivatives, an Earn/lending suite, a launchpad, and other ecosystem products, while keeping spot altcoin breadth as its signature feature.

KuCoin's defining product trait is **listing breadth**: it is often among the first centralized venues to list a new altcoin, which makes it important for price discovery on small- and micro-cap tokens.

## Exchange Token — KCS

KuCoin's native [[exchange-tokens|exchange token]] is [[kucoin-shares|KCS]] (KuCoin Shares / KuCoin Token):

- **Daily fee-share dividend**: KCS holders historically receive a daily distribution ("KCS Bonus") funded by a share of the exchange's trading-fee revenue — a relatively distinctive, dividend-like mechanic among exchange tokens.
- **Fee discounts** for paying trading fees in KCS.
- **Ecosystem utility** across KuCoin products and the associated KCC (KuCoin Community Chain) ecosystem.

The fee-revenue-share design ties KCS's value directly to exchange volume, making the token a proxy for KuCoin's trading activity. See [[kucoin-shares|KCS]] for current tokenomics.

## Trading Relevance

- **Listing strategy / early liquidity**: KuCoin is a primary venue for discovering and trading altcoins early; tokens often have meaningful KuCoin liquidity well before a [[binance]] listing.
- **Liquidity tier**: On major pairs KuCoin sits in the second tier behind [[binance]]/[[okx]]/[[bybit]]; on long-tail altcoins it is sometimes the deepest available CEX market.
- **Cross-venue arbitrage**: Because KuCoin lists tokens early, price gaps between KuCoin and other venues (or DEXs) are common during a token's early life and can create arbitrage opportunities — alongside elevated risk on thin books.
- **Positioning vs. peers**: KuCoin competes less on raw derivatives volume than [[bybit]] and more on spot listing breadth; against [[binance]] it offers earlier access to long-tail tokens at the cost of liquidity depth.

## Security & Regulatory

- **2020 hack**: KuCoin suffered a major security breach in September 2020 in which a large amount of crypto was stolen; the exchange recovered or reimbursed a substantial portion of the funds through asset freezes, recovery efforts, and insurance, and continued operating.
- **2024 U.S. settlement**: KuCoin and two co-founders reached a settlement with U.S. authorities (Department of Justice / FinCEN) resolving charges tied to operating an unlicensed money-transmitting business and AML failures. As part of the resolution, the founders stepped back from operational roles and KuCoin agreed to exit the U.S. market for a period and pay penalties. (Treat exact figures as subject to verification.)
- KuCoin publishes **[[proof-of-reserves]]** attestations, as is now standard for major CEXs post-[[ftx|FTX]].

## Risks

- **Counterparty / custody risk**: Exchange-custodied funds carry insolvency, mismanagement, and hack risk — underscored by KuCoin's own 2020 breach.
- **Regulatory risk**: The 2024 U.S. settlement illustrates concrete regulatory exposure; offshore registration means uncertain and shifting standing across jurisdictions, with U.S. access restricted.
- **Listing-quality / liquidity risk**: A very wide altcoin catalog includes low-quality, illiquid, and high-volatility tokens; thin order books raise slippage and manipulation risk, and some long-tail markets are vulnerable to wash trading.
