# Robinhood

Robinhood Markets, Inc. (NASDAQ: HOOD) is a commission-free brokerage platform that popularized zero-fee stock and [[options]] trading, fundamentally changing the retail brokerage industry. It became the center of intense controversy during the [[gamestop-short-squeeze]] when it restricted buying of heavily shorted stocks, then re-invented itself in 2024–2025 as a diversified "fintech super-app" spanning equities, options, crypto (via Bitstamp), retirement accounts, and [[prediction-markets|prediction-market]] event contracts — joining the S&P 500 in September 2025.

## Overview

Founded in 2013 by Vlad Tenev and Baiju Bhatt, Robinhood launched its mobile-first platform in 2015 with the mission of "democratizing finance." By eliminating commissions — historically $5-10 per trade at traditional brokers — Robinhood attracted millions of new, predominantly young retail traders. The app's gamified interface, confetti animations, and fractional share trading lowered barriers to entry. The company went public via IPO in July 2021 (NASDAQ: HOOD).

## Revenue Model: Payment for Order Flow

Robinhood's primary revenue source is [[payment-for-order-flow]] (PFOF), where [[market-maker|market makers]] like [[citadel]] Securities pay Robinhood to route customer orders to them. Critics argue this creates a conflict of interest — the broker's incentive is to maximize PFOF revenue rather than execution quality for customers. Defenders counter that retail traders still receive better prices than displayed on exchanges.

## The GameStop Controversy

On January 28, 2021, during the [[gamestop-short-squeeze]], Robinhood restricted buying of GME and other [[meme-stocks]], allowing users only to sell. Robinhood cited DTCC collateral requirements, but the decision triggered outrage, congressional hearings, and lasting reputational damage. The perceived conflict — restricting buying while its largest PFOF partner [[citadel]] was bailing out short seller [[melvin-capital]] — fueled widespread distrust.

## 2025–2026: Diversification and S&P 500 Inclusion

Robinhood's 2025 was transformational — HOOD rallied roughly **220% in 2025**, making it one of the year's best-performing large-cap financials:

- **Bitstamp acquisition (closed June 2025)** — Robinhood completed its ~$200M acquisition of Bitstamp, a crypto exchange holding 50+ active licenses and registrations globally, giving it an institutional crypto business and regulatory footprint across Europe and Canada. Bitstamp notional crypto volumes were ~$7B in the period following the close.
- **Prediction markets / event contracts** — launched with the 2024 US election (event contracts routed through CFTC-regulated venues, initially Kalshi). By Q3 2025, prediction markets were generating $100M+ in annualized revenue and tracking toward a ~$300M annualized run rate, with ~9 billion contracts traded in the first year. In November 2025, Connecticut issued cease-and-desist orders against Robinhood, [[kalshi]], and Crypto.com alleging the sports event contracts amount to unlicensed sports betting; Robinhood maintains the contracts are federally regulated by the [[cftc|CFTC]].
- **Tokenized stocks (EU, June 2025)** — launched tokenized US stock and ETF trading for EU customers on-chain, plus crypto perpetual futures for European users.
- **S&P 500 inclusion (22 September 2025)** — HOOD replaced Caesars Entertainment in the S&P 500, pulling in billions in passive index flows; the announcement drove a ~14.5% surge.

## Trading Relevance

Robinhood forced the entire brokerage industry to adopt zero commissions and demonstrated the power of accessible trading platforms. However, traders should understand the implications of PFOF on execution quality, the risks of platform outages during [[volatility]], and the limitations of "free" trading. The platform's role in the [[gamestop-short-squeeze]] made it central to ongoing debates about [[market-structure]] reform.

As a stock, HOOD trades as a high-beta proxy for retail trading activity, crypto volumes, and speculative risk appetite — revenue is driven by options PFOF, crypto transaction rebates, net interest income, and now event contracts. Its retail order flow data (and that of its PFOF partners) is itself a sentiment signal. As a venue, its launch of event contracts brought [[prediction-markets]] to tens of millions of mainstream brokerage users, a structural catalyst for that asset class's 2025 volume explosion.
