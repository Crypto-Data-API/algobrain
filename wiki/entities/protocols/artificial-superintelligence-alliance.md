# Artificial Superintelligence Alliance

The **Artificial Superintelligence Alliance (ASI)** is a coalition of decentralized-AI projects — originally **Fetch.ai**, **SingularityNET**, and **Ocean Protocol** — that merged their tokens (FET, AGIX, OCEAN) into a single token under the FET ticker. Announced in March 2024 at the peak of the crypto-AI narrative, it created the largest "AI token" by market cap and became the flagship trade for the decentralized-AI theme — and later a cautionary tale, after Ocean Protocol's acrimonious October 2025 exit and a >90% drawdown in FET.

---

## Overview

- **Formation**: announced March 2024 as a tokenomic merger of Fetch.ai (FET), SingularityNET (AGIX), and Ocean Protocol (OCEAN). The combined entity was branded "Artificial Superintelligence Alliance," trading under the FET ticker pending a full rebrand to ASI.
- **Token merger (Phase 1, mid-2024)**: AGIX merged into FET at a rate of 1 AGIX = 0.433350 FET/ASI; OCEAN converted at a similar fixed rate; 1 FET = 1 ASI. Phase 1 (exchange and treasury consolidation) completed in July 2024 with over 50% of circulating alliance supply merged.
- **CUDOS** joined the alliance in October 2024 as a compute partner, with CUDOS tokens also converting into FET.
- **Phase 2** (full ASI token deployment and final rebrand) was still pending as of mid-2026, listed as a 2026-2027 roadmap item.

## Ocean Protocol Exit and Lawsuit (2025)

- **2025-10-10**: Ocean Protocol formally withdrew from the alliance, citing a desire for independent funding and control over its own tokenomics. OCEAN surged ~30% on the news; FET fell ~6%.
- **2025-11-04**: Fetch.ai entities filed suit (*Fetch Compute, Inc., et al. v. Bruce Pon, et al.*, case no. 1:25-cv-9210) against Ocean Protocol Foundation, OceanDAO, and Ocean co-founders Bruce Pon, Trent McConaghy, and Christina Pon. The complaint alleges Ocean converted ~661M OCEAN "community" tokens into ~286M FET (~$191M at the time) and sold large amounts via exchanges, depressing FET's price.
- The dispute left Fetch.ai, SingularityNET, and CUDOS as the active members under the FET banner.

## Products and 2026 Developments

- **ASI-1 Mini** (February 2025): Fetch.ai launched what it billed as the first Web3-native LLM, integrated with the alliance's agent stack.
- **ASI:Create** (February 2026): closed-alpha platform for building AI agents at scale — the current focal point of the alliance roadmap.
- As of late May 2026, the full ASI rebrand (Phase 2) remained pending, with traders front-running official updates.

## Token Performance

- FET peaked near **$3.22 in March 2024** during the AI-token mania; by late 2025 / 2026 it traded around **$0.26**, a drawdown of more than 93%, wiping out billions in market value.
- FET remains one of the most liquid AI-sector tokens and a benchmark for the [[ai-agent-tokens]] / decentralized-AI basket.

## Trading Relevance

- **Sector beta**: FET is a primary liquid proxy for the crypto-AI narrative; it trades with high beta to AI news cycles (see [[ai-narrative-arc]]).
- **Event-driven setups**: the token merger (2024), CUDOS addition, Ocean exit (Oct 2025), the Fetch-Ocean lawsuit (Nov 2025), and the still-pending ASI rebrand have each been tradeable catalysts; speculation reliably front-runs Phase 2 rebrand rumors.
- **Governance/treasury risk**: the Ocean dispute demonstrated that multi-project token mergers concentrate treasury-dumping risk — large "community" allocations from merged projects can become sell pressure with little warning.
- **Relative-value**: the AGIX/OCEAN→FET fixed conversion rates created convergence-arbitrage trades during the 2024 merger window — a template for future token-merger arbitrage.
