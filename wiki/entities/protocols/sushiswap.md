# SushiSwap

**SushiSwap** is a multichain decentralized exchange (AMM and swap aggregator) launched in August 2020 as a fork of [[uniswap|Uniswap]]. It is historically significant for executing DeFi's most famous **"vampire attack"** — using SUSHI token incentives to drain roughly $1B of Uniswap's liquidity at launch — and survives in 2026 as a mid-tier but actively rebuilding DEX operating on 40+ chains under the **Sushi Labs** development entity.

---

## Overview

- **Origins (2020)**: Launched by the pseudonymous "Chef Nomi" as a Uniswap fork that added the SUSHI token, redirecting protocol fees (0.05% of the 0.30% swap fee) to SUSHI stakers (xSUSHI). The migration-based vampire attack pulled on the order of **$800M-$1B in liquidity** from Uniswap in September 2020. Days later Chef Nomi sold ~$14M of dev-fund SUSHI, crashing the token, then returned the funds and handed control to FTX's Sam Bankman-Fried for the liquidity migration — an early DeFi governance drama.
- **Peak and decline**: TVL peaked in the multi-billions (roughly $7B) in late 2021 across products including BentoBox and Kashi lending; years of leadership churn, treasury runway scares (a 2022 disclosure that the treasury had ~1.5 years of runway), and loss of share to Uniswap v3 and aggregators followed.
- **Sushi Labs (June 2024)**: the "Burū no Shinka" governance proposal restructured the project, creating **Sushi Labs** as the development company operating alongside the Sushi DAO, responsible for products, chain deployments, and strategy.

## 2025-2026 Status

- **Scale**: TVL fluctuated in the **$38-48M** range over the 12 months to early 2026 (top-30 DEX by multichain breadth, far below leaders); **cumulative trading volume exceeds $251B** since launch.
- **Aggregation stack**: SushiXSwap (bridgeless cross-chain swaps) and **Route Processor 6** passed **$20B+ cumulative volume in 2025 alone** — Sushi increasingly competes as an aggregator/routing layer rather than purely on its own AMM liquidity.
- **Product pipeline ("Super Swap" roadmap)**: **Blade** (an AMM designed to eliminate impermanent loss), **Wara** (Solana trading platform), **Susa** (on-chain order-book perpetuals), and **Kubo** (delta-neutral perps).
- **Coverage**: 40+ blockchains, Uniswap-v3-style concentrated liquidity, cross-chain swaps without manual bridging.
- Analysts' bull case is a return of TVL above $100M in H2 2026 contingent on roadmap execution — treat as speculative.

## Trading Relevance

- **Historical case study**: the vampire attack is the canonical example of incentive-driven liquidity migration — required background for evaluating any new DEX's token-emission "liquidity rental" and its durability.
- **SUSHI token**: a fee-sharing DEX token whose value tracks protocol volume; it has lost ~99% from its 2021 ATH, illustrating how DEX governance tokens decay when volume share erodes (compare [[uniswap|UNI]], [[curve-finance|CRV]]).
- **Venue utility**: still useful for long-tail token access and cross-chain routing on chains where larger DEXs are absent; for [[decentralized-exchanges|DEX]] traders, Sushi's router can source liquidity competitive with major aggregators on some pairs.
- **Governance/treasury risk**: Sushi's repeated treasury and leadership crises are a template for the "DAO operational risk" a trader should price into any DEX token.
