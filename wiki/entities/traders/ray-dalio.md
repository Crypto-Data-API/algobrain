# Ray Dalio

Ray Dalio is the founder of [[bridgewater-associates|Bridgewater Associates]], for decades the world's largest [[hedge-funds|hedge fund]] by assets under management, and a pioneer of systematic [[macro-trading]] and [[risk-parity]] investing. His "Principles" framework for decision-making has influenced both finance and corporate management, and his public macro commentary — especially on debt cycles and US fiscal sustainability — remains widely followed by traders.

## Background

Born August 8, 1949 in Queens, New York, Dalio began investing at age 12 and founded Bridgewater Associates out of his apartment in 1975 (Source: [[book-more-money-than-god]]). The firm grew into a powerhouse known for its radical transparency culture and systematic approach to understanding global economies, peaking at roughly $150–160B AUM in the late 2010s; AUM was reported at approximately $92B in 2025 following years of client redemptions and the firm's deliberate capacity management.

## Succession and Exit from Bridgewater

- **2017–2022** — Dalio progressively handed over management, stepping back from the CEO role and then relinquishing the co-CIO role in **October 2022**, transferring control to the board (Nir Bar Dea became sole CEO in 2023).
- **July 2024** — Bridgewater announced Dalio had **fully exited ownership** of the firm and no longer sits on its board, ending a near-50-year association.

## Investment Philosophy

Dalio's approach centers on understanding the "economic machine" — how credit cycles, [[interest-rates]], productivity growth, and [[central-bank]] policy drive asset prices. His key contributions include:

- **[[all-weather-portfolio|All-Weather Portfolio]]**: A [[risk-parity]] strategy that balances *risk* across asset classes (stocks, bonds, [[commodities]], [[gold]]) to perform in any economic environment — rising/falling growth crossed with rising/falling inflation. In March 2025 the strategy was opened to retail investors via the **SPDR Bridgewater All Weather ETF (ALLW)**, launched in partnership with State Street.
- **Debt Cycle Framework**: Dalio's study of long-term debt cycles across history informed his prescient positioning ahead of the [[2008-global-financial-crisis]] (Source: [[book-more-money-than-god]]).

### How risk parity / All Weather actually works

The mechanical insight behind [[all-weather-portfolio|All Weather]] is the **four-quadrant** environment grid. Asset returns are dominated by surprises in two variables — economic growth and inflation — each of which can come in above or below market expectations, producing four "boxes":

| | Growth rising | Growth falling |
|---|---|---|
| **Inflation rising** | commodities, gold, EM debt | inflation-linked bonds, commodities |
| **Inflation falling** | equities, EM credit | nominal Treasuries / long bonds |

Dalio's claim is that the market does not know in advance which box it is in, so an investor should hold assets that win in *each* box, **balanced by risk contribution rather than by capital**. Because bonds are far less volatile than equities, a conventional 60/40 portfolio is actually ~90% equity *risk*; risk parity fixes this by sizing each sleeve to contribute equal volatility, then applying modest [[leverage]] to the low-volatility (bond) sleeve so the total portfolio reaches an equity-like expected return at lower drawdown. Mechanically: weight each asset inversely to its volatility (and account for [[correlation]]), then lever to a target portfolio volatility. The trade-off — exposed in 2022 — is that risk parity is hurt badly when stocks *and* bonds fall together (a positive stock–bond correlation regime driven by an inflation shock), the one environment where diversification across the four boxes breaks down.

**Pure Alpha**, by contrast, is Bridgewater's discretionary-systematic [[macro-trading|macro]] fund: a large book of ~20+ uncorrelated return streams ("the Holy Grail of investing" in Dalio's phrasing — combining many bets with low correlation cuts portfolio risk far faster than it cuts return), expressed across global rates, currencies, equity indices, and commodities.

## Books and Recent Activity (2024–2026)

- *Principles: Life and Work* (2017), *Principles for Navigating Big Debt Crises* (2018), *Principles for Dealing with the Changing World Order* (2021).
- ***How Countries Go Broke: The Big Cycle*** (published **June 3, 2025**) — argues the US is approaching the late stage of a long-term debt cycle and proposes the "**3% solution**": cutting the federal deficit from ~6–7% of GDP to 3% via spending, tax, and rate adjustments.
- Through 2025–2026 Dalio repeatedly warned of a US "debt death spiral" risk — supply of Treasuries exceeding demand, forcing higher rates or [[federal-reserve]] monetization — a thesis traders track for its implications for [[treasuries]], [[gold]], and the dollar.

## Trading Relevance

Bridgewater's Pure Alpha fund returned approximately 12% annualized net of fees over four decades. Dalio's "How the Economic Machine Works" video is considered essential viewing for understanding [[macro-trading]]. His framework for analyzing debt cycles, central bank responses, and currency dynamics offers practical tools for traders navigating complex macro environments — and his 2025–2026 fiscal warnings map directly onto positioning in long-duration bonds, gold, and inflation hedges.

His public disagreements with traditional economics and his emphasis on studying historical analogs (including the rise and fall of reserve currencies) make him one of the most intellectually influential voices in modern finance, alongside [[george-soros]] and [[nassim-taleb]].
