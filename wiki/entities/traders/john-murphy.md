John Murphy is one of the most influential figures in modern [[technical-analysis-overview|technical analysis]], best known as the author of *[[technical-analysis-of-the-financial-markets|Technical Analysis of the Financial Markets]]* (1999) -- widely considered the definitive textbook on TA. He is also a pioneer of [[intermarket-analysis]], studying how relationships between asset classes (stocks, bonds, commodities, currencies) generate trading signals.

## Overview

Murphy spent over three decades as a professional technical analyst and market commentator. He served as the director of Merrill Lynch's Technical Analysis Futures division and later became the chief technical analyst for CNBC, where he brought chart analysis to mainstream financial television. His accessible teaching style and systematic approach to TA made complex concepts understandable to retail traders worldwide.

## Key Contributions

### Technical Analysis of the Financial Markets

Originally published as *Technical Analysis of the Futures Markets* (1986), the expanded 1999 edition became the standard reference for TA education. The book covers:

- [[chart-patterns|Chart patterns]] (head and shoulders, triangles, flags, wedges)
- [[trend-following|Trend analysis]] and [[moving-averages|moving averages]]
- [[oscillators|Oscillators]] and momentum indicators ([[rsi|RSI]], [[macd|MACD]], [[stochastic-oscillator|Stochastics]])
- [[volume|Volume analysis]] and [[open-interest|open interest]]
- [[candlestick-patterns|Candlestick charting]]
- [[elliott-wave|Elliott Wave]] and cycle analysis
- [[dow-theory|Dow Theory]] (the philosophical foundation of TA)
- [[multiple-timeframe-analysis|Multiple timeframe analysis]]
- [[adx|ADX]] and [[atr|ATR]] (Wilder's trend strength and volatility tools)

The book is required reading for the [[cmt-association|CMT (Chartered Market Technician)]] designation and remains the most widely recommended TA textbook decades after publication.

### Intermarket Analysis

Murphy's second major contribution is his work on [[intermarket-analysis]], formalized in *Intermarket Technical Analysis* (1991) and *Trading with Intermarket Analysis* (2013). His framework examines how:

- **Bond prices** and **interest rates** lead stock market turns
- **[[commodities|Commodity]] prices** signal inflationary pressures that affect bonds and stocks ([[commodity-inflation-link]])
- **Currency movements** (particularly the [[dxy-commodity-correlation|US dollar]]) reflect relative economic strength and affect commodity prices
- **[[sector-rotation|Sector rotation]]** follows the business cycle through predictable intermarket relationships

This cross-asset approach was groundbreaking at a time when most technical analysts focused on single-market charts.

### Visual Investor

Murphy also authored *The Visual Investor* (1996), aimed at individual investors who want to use charts without deep mathematical knowledge. The book emphasizes practical pattern recognition and sector analysis.

## Commodity Market Analysis

Murphy's original 1986 book was specifically about futures markets, and [[commodities]] remain central to his framework. His intermarket analysis chain places commodities as the crucial link between currencies and bonds — rising commodity prices signal [[inflation]], which drives bond yields higher and eventually pressures equities. Murphy's TA tools are extensively applied to commodity markets:

- [[moving-averages|Moving averages]] (50/200-day) for trend identification in [[crude-oil]], [[gold]], [[copper]]
- [[rsi|RSI]] and [[macd|MACD]] for momentum and divergence signals in energy and metals
- [[bollinger-bands|Bollinger Bands]] for volatility assessment and squeeze setups in [[natural-gas]]
- [[adx|ADX]] for trend strength measurement in commodity futures (where strong trends are common)
- [[commodity-seasonality-patterns|Seasonal patterns]] combined with technical signals for agricultural commodities

The [[trend-following-cta|CTA/managed futures]] industry — which trades a diversified portfolio of commodity and financial futures — is built on the systematic application of Murphy's technical framework.

## Legacy

Murphy's impact on trading education is difficult to overstate. His textbook has been translated into dozens of languages and sold millions of copies. Nearly every modern TA course, certification program, and trading educator builds on the framework he systematized. His intermarket analysis work also laid the groundwork for macro-technical trading approaches used by institutional traders today.
