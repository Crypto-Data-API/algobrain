# John Watkinson

John Watkinson is a Canadian software developer and entrepreneur who co-founded [[larva-labs|Larva Labs]] alongside Matt Hall. Together, they created [[cryptopunks|CryptoPunks]] in June 2017 -- a collection of 10,000 unique 24x24 pixel art characters generated algorithmically on the [[ethereum]] blockchain. CryptoPunks are widely considered the first major [[nft|NFT]] project on Ethereum and a foundational influence on the entire NFT ecosystem, including the development of the ERC-721 token standard.

## Larva Labs

Watkinson and Matt Hall founded [[larva-labs|Larva Labs]] as a two-person software studio based in New York. Before CryptoPunks, the studio had worked on various software projects including mobile apps. The creation of CryptoPunks emerged from their experimentation with pixel art character generation and their interest in [[ethereum|Ethereum's]] then-new smart contract capabilities.

## CryptoPunks

### Creation and Launch (June 2017)

CryptoPunks launched in June 2017 as a free claim for anyone with an [[ethereum]] wallet. Key characteristics:

- **10,000 unique characters**: Each CryptoPunk is a 24x24 pixel art image algorithmically generated with randomized attributes (hats, glasses, hairstyles, skin tones, etc.)
- **Rarity tiers**: The collection includes 6,039 male Punks, 3,840 female Punks, 88 Zombies, 24 Apes, and 9 Aliens -- with the rarer types commanding dramatically higher prices
- **Free to claim**: All 10,000 Punks were initially available for free (gas fees only), with Watkinson and Hall retaining 1,000 for themselves
- **Pre-ERC-721**: CryptoPunks launched before the ERC-721 standard existed. They were built on a custom smart contract that predated the formal NFT standard, which is why they are technically not ERC-721 tokens (later wrapped versions added ERC-721 compatibility)

### Influence on the ERC-721 Standard

CryptoPunks directly inspired the creation of the ERC-721 non-fungible token standard. The concept of unique, non-interchangeable tokens on Ethereum -- demonstrated practically by CryptoPunks -- led [[dieter-shirley|Dieter Shirley]] and the [[dapper-labs|Dapper Labs]] team to formalize the ERC-721 standard in late 2017. Without CryptoPunks as a proof of concept, the NFT standard might have taken a different form or emerged later.

### Market Performance

CryptoPunks became one of the most valuable NFT collections in history:

- **2021 peak**: During the NFT boom, individual CryptoPunks sold for millions of dollars. CryptoPunk #5822 (an Alien) sold for $23.7 million in February 2022
- **Cultural status**: CryptoPunks became status symbols in crypto culture, used as profile pictures by prominent investors and celebrities
- **Blue-chip benchmark**: CryptoPunks floor price became a key barometer for the broader NFT market's health

## Other Larva Labs Projects

Following CryptoPunks, Watkinson and Hall created additional projects through [[larva-labs|Larva Labs]]:

- **[[autoglyphs]]** (April 2019): An on-chain generative art project -- 512 unique text-based artworks generated and stored entirely on the Ethereum blockchain. Considered a precursor to [[art-blocks]]
- **[[meebits]]** (May 2021): A collection of 20,000 3D voxel characters, positioned as the "3D CryptoPunks." Meebits were distributed partly through a Dutch auction and partly through free claims for CryptoPunks and Autoglyphs holders

## Sale to Yuga Labs

In March 2022, Watkinson and Hall sold the intellectual property rights to CryptoPunks and Meebits to [[yuga-labs|Yuga Labs]] (creators of [[bored-ape-yacht-club|Bored Ape Yacht Club]]) for an undisclosed sum. The deal was significant:

- **IP consolidation**: Yuga Labs became the dominant force in blue-chip NFTs, controlling both BAYC and CryptoPunks
- **Commercial rights**: Yuga Labs granted CryptoPunks holders full commercial rights to their individual Punks, matching the rights model that had made BAYC popular
- **Creator exit**: The sale marked Watkinson and Hall's effective departure from the CryptoPunks project, transitioning stewardship to a larger organization

## 2025–2026: NODE Stewardship and Return to Art Blocks

- **May 2025**: Yuga Labs transferred the CryptoPunks IP to the **Infinite Node Foundation (NODE)**, a non-profit dedicated to preserving digital art. Watkinson and Matt Hall returned to the project in advisory roles, joining NODE's advisory board — reuniting the creators with CryptoPunks stewardship for the first time since 2022. NODE's announcement cited cumulative CryptoPunks sales of over $3 billion (figures of $3.07B–$3.2B+ appear across sources).
- **October 2025**: Larva Labs announced **Quine**, its first major new project since Meebits (2021), released as an Art Blocks Curated collection — marking Watkinson and Hall's return to on-chain generative art, the lineage they helped start with [[autoglyphs]].
- Watkinson also serves in arts-institution roles, including an affiliation with the Toledo Museum of Art's leadership/advisory structure.

## Legacy

Watkinson's creation of CryptoPunks established several paradigms that defined the NFT market:

- **PFP collections**: The concept of algorithmically generated profile picture collections became the dominant NFT format, spawning thousands of derivatives
- **Rarity-based valuation**: The attribute rarity system pioneered by CryptoPunks became the standard model for pricing NFT collectibles
- **Free mint model**: The initial free distribution demonstrated that value could be created through community adoption rather than initial sales revenue
- **On-chain provenance**: CryptoPunks proved that blockchain-based provenance could create value for digital art

## Trading Relevance

CryptoPunks function as the blue-chip benchmark of the [[nft]] market: their floor price is the most-watched gauge of NFT-market risk appetite, and large Punk sales historically marked sentiment extremes in both the 2021 mania and subsequent drawdowns. For [[nft-trading]], Watkinson's projects define the structural patterns traders price — rarity-tier premiums (Aliens/Apes/Zombies), free-mint-to-blue-chip value migration, and IP-transfer events (the 2022 Yuga acquisition and 2025 NODE handover both repriced the collection). The 2025 NODE transfer is also a case study in how custodial/IP news, not just market beta, drives NFT collection valuations.
