# Mark Minervini

Mark Minervini (born c. 1965) is an American stock trader, author, and two-time winner of the US Investing Championship who developed the SEPA (Specific Entry Point Analysis) methodology -- a systematic approach to [[momentum]]-based stock trading that combines the CAN SLIM principles of William O'Neil with proprietary [[technical-analysis|chart pattern]] recognition and strict [[risk-management]] rules. Minervini averaged approximately **220% annual returns** for five consecutive years early in his career, reportedly turning $100,000 into over $30 million, and won the US Investing Championship in 1997 with a **155% return** and again in 2021 with a remarkable **334.8% return**. Featured in Jack Schwager's *Stock Market Wizards* (2001), Minervini is regarded as one of the most successful practitioners of [[momentum-trading|growth momentum]] trading in modern markets (Source: [[book-market-wizards]]).

## Early Life and Background

Mark Minervini grew up in a working-class family in the northeastern United States. He had no formal financial education and did not attend a prestigious university -- a background that he frequently contrasts with the Ivy League pedigrees typical of Wall Street. Minervini was largely self-taught as a trader, spending years studying price charts, reading books on [[technical-analysis]], and analyzing the characteristics of winning stocks.

His early years in trading were marked by significant losses and mistakes -- a period he later described as "paying tuition to the market." Through obsessive study of historical stock market leaders (companies like Cisco, Dell, Microsoft, and AOL during the 1990s tech boom), Minervini identified recurring patterns in how stocks behaved before, during, and after major price advances. This empirical research formed the foundation of his SEPA methodology.

Minervini has cited William O'Neil's *How to Make Money in Stocks* and the CAN SLIM system as a major influence on his approach, particularly O'Neil's emphasis on buying stocks showing strong earnings growth and relative price strength (Source: book-how-to-make-money-in-stocks). However, Minervini refined and extended O'Neil's framework with more precise entry criteria, stricter loss-cutting rules, and a sophisticated understanding of [[volatility]]-based patterns.

## Trading and Investment Philosophy

Minervini's SEPA (Specific Entry Point Analysis) methodology is a comprehensive system for identifying, entering, and managing [[momentum]] trades in US equities. Its core elements:

### 1. Stage Analysis: Only Buy Stage 2 Uptrends

Minervini divides a stock's lifecycle into four stages, adapting the framework originally developed by Stan Weinstein:

- **Stage 1 (Basing/Accumulation)**: Stock trades sideways after a decline; institutions quietly accumulate
- **Stage 2 (Advancing/Markup)**: Stock breaks out of the base and enters a sustained uptrend -- **this is the ONLY stage where Minervini buys**
- **Stage 3 (Topping/Distribution)**: Uptrend stalls; institutions begin distributing shares to retail
- **Stage 4 (Declining/Markdown)**: Stock enters a sustained downtrend -- never buy here

The Stage 2 filter is defined by specific [[moving-averages]] criteria:

- Stock price must be **above both the 150-day and 200-day [[moving-averages|moving averages]]**
- Both the 150-day and 200-day moving averages must be **rising** (positive slope)
- The 50-day moving average must be above the 150-day and 200-day
- The stock price must be **at least 25% above its 52-week low** and preferably within **25% of its 52-week high**

These criteria ensure that Minervini is only buying stocks with strong institutional sponsorship and established [[trend|uptrends]].

The full **SEPA Trend Template** can be summarized as a checklist (a stock must satisfy all of it to qualify):

| # | Trend Template criterion |
|---|---|
| 1 | Price is above both the 150-day and 200-day [[moving-averages|MAs]] |
| 2 | The 150-day MA is above the 200-day MA |
| 3 | The 200-day MA is trending up (rising slope, ideally for 1+ month) |
| 4 | The 50-day MA is above both the 150-day and 200-day MAs |
| 5 | Price is above the 50-day MA |
| 6 | Price is at least ~25-30% above its 52-week low |
| 7 | Price is within ~25% of its 52-week high (the closer the better) |
| 8 | [[relative-strength]] rank is high (ideally 70+, preferably 80-90+) |
| 9 | Price action is constructive (tight, orderly bases -- not wide and loose) |

### 2. The Volatility Contraction Pattern (VCP)

The VCP is Minervini's signature entry pattern -- the specific "buy signal" within a Stage 2 uptrend. The VCP occurs when a stock consolidates in a series of **contracting price ranges**:

- After an advance, the stock pulls back (e.g., 25%)
- It then rallies and pulls back again, but the pullback is smaller (e.g., 15%)
- It rallies again and pulls back even less (e.g., 8%)
- Each successive contraction shows diminishing [[volume]], indicating that sellers are being absorbed and supply is drying up
- The buy point is the [[breakout-strategies|breakout]] from the final, tightest contraction, ideally on a surge in [[volume]]

The VCP is fundamentally a supply/demand pattern: each contraction shakes out weak holders and transfers shares to stronger hands (institutions), until the remaining supply is so thin that even modest buying pressure triggers a [[breakout-strategies|breakout]].

### 3. Fundamental Fuel: Earnings Acceleration

While Minervini's entry timing is technical, his stock selection requires strong fundamentals:

- **Earnings growth**: Look for companies with accelerating quarterly earnings per share (EPS) growth -- e.g., 20%, then 30%, then 50% year-over-year
- **Revenue growth**: Confirmed by top-line revenue increases, not just earnings management
- **[[relative-strength]]**: The stock should be outperforming 90%+ of all other stocks on a 6-12 month [[relative-strength]] basis
- This combines elements of O'Neil's CAN SLIM approach with Minervini's own empirical observations about what drives major stock advances

### 4. Risk Management: Cut Losses Without Hesitation

Minervini's [[risk-management]] rules are absolute and non-negotiable:

- **Maximum loss per position: 5-8%** from the purchase price -- if the stock drops this much, sell immediately, no exceptions
- **Average loss should be smaller**: By buying at precise entry points (VCP breakouts), Minervini aims for average losses of 3-5% on failed trades
- **[[position-sizing]]**: Size positions so that a maximum loss on any single trade represents no more than 1-2% of total portfolio equity
- **Offense wins, but defense keeps you in the game**: "[[position-sizing]] plus loss cutting equals survival, and survival equals compounding"

This [[risk-management]] framework ensures that even with a win rate of 40-50% (typical for [[momentum]] strategies), the average winner is significantly larger than the average loser, producing positive expectancy over time.

### 5. Compounding Through Asymmetry

Minervini's expected trade profile:

- Lose small, lose fast (5-8% maximum, cut immediately)
- Win bigger (20-100%+ on successful trades held for weeks to months)
- Let winning positions run as long as the [[trend]] remains intact
- Add to winners ([[position-sizing|pyramiding]]) when the stock makes constructive consolidations after advancing

This asymmetric risk/reward profile -- risking 5-8% to potentially gain 20-100%+ -- is the mathematical engine behind Minervini's compounding record. It is structurally similar to the approach used by william-o-neil, [[jesse-livermore]], and other great [[momentum]] traders throughout history.

## Lessons and Takeaways

1. **Trade only Stage 2.** Minervini's central discipline is refusing to buy stocks that are not in a confirmed [[trend|uptrend]] (above rising 150/200-day [[moving-averages]]). Most retail losses come from "bottom-fishing" Stage 4 declines; SEPA simply forbids it.
2. **Precision entries shrink risk.** Buying the [[breakout-strategies|breakout]] from the tightest VCP contraction means the logical stop (below the pivot) is close, so a failed trade costs 3-5% rather than 15-20%. The entry *is* the risk-management.
3. **Asymmetry is the engine, not win rate.** A 40-50% win rate compounds powerfully when losers are capped at 5-8% and winners run 20-100%+. Expectancy, not accuracy, drives the [[compounding|compounding]] record.
4. **Cut losses mechanically and without ego.** The 5-8% hard stop is non-negotiable; SEPA's edge collapses if a trader lets one big loss erase many small wins. This is the rule most traders cannot follow emotionally (see [[trading-psychology]]).
5. **Fundamentals fuel, technicals time.** Stocks must show accelerating earnings/revenue and elite [[relative-strength]] to qualify; the chart only decides *when* to act on an already-strong fundamental candidate.
6. **The big money is early-cycle.** Minervini stresses that the best leaders emerge from sound bases in the first 1-2 years of a new bull [[market-regime]]; position-management adapts to where the broad market is in its cycle.
