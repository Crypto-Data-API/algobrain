# Benjamin Graham

**Benjamin Graham** (1894-1976) is considered the father of [[value-investing]] and security analysis. His books *The Intelligent Investor* and *Security Analysis* remain foundational texts in finance.

Graham introduced core concepts including [[margin-of-safety]], intrinsic value, and the "Mr. Market" allegory. He was the mentor of [[warren-buffett]], who studied under him at Columbia Business School.

## Overview

- Born **May 9, 1894** in London; his family emigrated to New York the following year. Died **September 21, 1976** in Aix-en-Provence, France.
- Graduated Columbia University in 1914 (second in his class, offered teaching posts in three departments) and went to Wall Street at Newburger, Henderson & Loeb.
- Formed the Graham-Newman partnership with Jerome Newman in 1926; the **Graham-Newman Corporation** (1936-1956) is reported to have compounded at roughly **20% annually versus about 12% for the broad market** over its life — one of the longest documented outperformance records of its era.
- His personal account was nearly wiped out in the [[1929-crash]] and its aftermath (the partnership lost roughly 70% from 1929-1932), an experience that hardened his insistence on [[margin-of-safety]].
- In 1948 Graham-Newman bought **50% of GEICO for $712,000** — a single position that eventually grew to be worth more than all the partnership's other investments combined, and which Graham himself noted ironically violated his diversification rules.
- Taught security analysis at Columbia Business School from 1928 until 1956.

## Key Works

Graham's two landmark books defined the discipline of security analysis:

- ***Security Analysis*** (1934, co-authored with David Dodd) (Source: [[book-security-analysis]]) -- The foundational academic text on analyzing stocks and bonds based on financial statements, earnings power, and asset values. It was written in response to the speculative excesses that led to the [[1929-crash]] and established rigorous methods for separating investment from speculation.
- ***[[the-intelligent-investor]]*** (1949) (Source: [[book-the-intelligent-investor]]) -- A more accessible guide aimed at individual investors. [[warren-buffett]] has called it "the best book about investing ever written." It introduces the **Mr. Market** allegory (the market as an emotional business partner who offers daily prices you can accept or ignore) and the concept of [[margin-of-safety]] -- only buying when price is significantly below intrinsic value.

## Teaching and Legacy

Graham taught at Columbia Business School from the 1920s through the 1950s, where his students included Buffett, Walter Schloss, Irving Kahn, and other legendary investors. His "net-net" strategy -- buying stocks trading below their net current asset value (current assets minus all liabilities) -- was a systematic deep-value approach that generated extraordinary returns in the mid-20th century.

While Graham's quantitative deep-value methods are less directly applicable in modern efficient markets, his philosophical framework -- that stocks are fractional ownership of businesses, that the market exists to serve you rather than instruct you, and that a [[margin-of-safety]] is the central principle of investment -- remains the intellectual foundation of [[value-investing]] worldwide. His influence extends through Buffett, [[charlie-munger]], and generations of practitioners who consider themselves "Graham-and-Dodd" investors.

## Trading Relevance

Graham matters to traders, not only investors, for three reasons: (1) his **net-net screen** (price below net current asset value) is the ancestor of every quantitative value factor used in systematic equity strategies today; (2) the **Mr. Market** allegory is the founding text of [[behavioral-finance]]-aware trading — treating price as an offer to exploit rather than information to obey; (3) his speculation-vs-investment distinction (an operation that "promises safety of principal and an adequate return" on thorough analysis) remains the standard test for separating an edge from a gamble. Modern factor research (Fama-French value factor, quality-minus-junk) is essentially Graham formalized.
