# Ken Griffin

**Ken Griffin** (b. 15 October 1968) is the founder and CEO of Citadel LLC, one of the world's largest [[hedge-funds]], and Citadel Securities, a dominant [[market-maker]] handling a significant share of U.S. equity order flow. He is the richest person in finance and arguably the most powerful single figure in U.S. equity market structure.

Griffin started trading from his Harvard dorm room in **1987**, installing a satellite dish on the roof to get real-time market data (Source: [[book-the-quants]]). He launched his first fund at age 22 — founding Citadel in Chicago in **November 1990** with $4.6 million, backed by Glenwood Capital's Frank Meyer — and Citadel LLC has since grown into one of the world's most powerful financial institutions.

## Citadel LLC (Hedge Fund)

[[citadel]] LLC managed approximately **$62 billion** in the early 2020s (Source: [[book-the-quants]]); as of early 2026 AUM stands at roughly **$65–67 billion**, after Citadel returned about **$5 billion of 2025 profits** to investors (a recurring practice to manage capacity). The firm employs a multi-strategy approach spanning equities, fixed income, macro, quantitative strategies, and commodities. Citadel is known for its technology infrastructure, aggressive talent recruitment, and consistently strong returns — LCH Investments has ranked it the most profitable hedge fund in history by cumulative net gains (over $65 billion since inception, and still top of the LCH league table in the mid-2020s).

Recent flagship (Wellington) performance:
- **2022**: +38% — a record ~$16 billion profit year
- **2024**: +15.1% (reported)
- **2025**: **+10.2%** — solid but trailing some multi-strategy peers in a year Griffin characterized as disciplined rather than spectacular

## Citadel Securities (Market Maker)

Separately from the hedge fund, Griffin founded **Citadel Securities** (2002), which has become the dominant [[market-maker]] in U.S. equity markets, handling roughly **20–25% of all U.S. equity trading volume** and a substantially larger share of retail order flow. The firm executes trades for retail brokers under [[payment-for-order-flow]] (PFOF) arrangements, where brokers like Robinhood route customer orders to Citadel Securities in exchange for rebates.

- **January 2022**: First outside investment — $1.15 billion from Sequoia Capital and Paradigm at a **$22 billion valuation**
- **2025**: Record trading revenue of approximately **$12.2 billion**; the firm has kept expanding into fixed income, FX, and crypto-adjacent markets. It remains privately held — periodic IPO speculation has not produced a confirmed listing plan as of mid-2026

This dual role — running both a hedge fund and the largest equity market maker — drew intense scrutiny during the [[gamestop-short-squeeze]] of January 2021, when questions arose about potential conflicts of interest. Griffin testified before Congress and denied any coordination between the hedge fund and market-making operations.

## Wealth and Influence

Griffin's net worth was estimated at roughly **$51 billion in January 2026** (Bloomberg Billionaires Index; other estimates cluster at $48–51 billion), up from ~$35 billion in the early 2020s. He is known for extraordinary purchases including a first-edition copy of the U.S. Constitution bought at auction for **$43.2 million** (November 2021) and a $238 million New York penthouse. He relocated Citadel's headquarters from Chicago to Miami in **2022**, is building a record-setting Florida estate, and is one of the largest Republican political donors, as well as a major donor to Harvard ($300M gift, 2023) and museums.

## Why Griffin Matters to Traders

- **Market structure**: Citadel Securities sets the de facto terms of U.S. retail execution; PFOF debates, tick-size reform, and SEC equity-market-structure proposals all orbit the firm
- **The pod-shop template**: Citadel's centralized-risk, multi-team model is the industry's dominant hedge fund architecture — its hiring, leverage, and de-grossing behavior moves markets (pod de-risking episodes are a recognized flow signal)
- **Capacity discipline**: Citadel's habit of returning profits to investors annually is a live example of managing strategy capacity (relevant to any strategy's `capacity_usd` thinking)
