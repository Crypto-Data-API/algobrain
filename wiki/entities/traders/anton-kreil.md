# Anton Kreil

Anton Kreil is a former goldman-sachs proprietary trader and the Managing Partner of the Institute of Trading and Portfolio Management (ITPM). He is known for his systematic approach to long-short-equity portfolio management and his outspoken criticism of the retail trading education industry.

## Overview

Kreil began trading at age 16 and became one of Goldman Sachs' youngest Pan European Equities traders at age 20. After careers at goldman-sachs, Lehman Brothers, and JP Morgan, he retired from banking in May 2007 — months before the Global Financial Crisis. He founded ITPM in 2011 to teach institutional-grade trading methodology to retail traders.

## Trading Philosophy

Kreil's methodology is built on several core principles derived from his institutional experience:

### 80/20 Fundamental-Technical Split
Professional traders use 80% fundamental analysis and 20% technical analysis. Technical analysis is used solely for entry/exit timing — never for generating trade ideas. The fundamentals are interpreted differently from how retail traders use them. (Source: [[itpm-god-like-trader-status]])

### Long/Short Portfolio Construction
Rather than being directionally biased (always long or always short), professional traders maintain balanced portfolios with both long and short positions. This reduces market correlation and allows profit in any market environment. (Source: [[itpm-master-compounding]])

### Macro-First Approach
Trade ideas start with macroeconomic analysis (global economic cycle, central bank policy, leading indicators), flow down to sector selection, then individual stock selection. (Source: [[itpm-five-principles]])

### Capital Preservation
Kreil emphasizes that the primary goal is not making money but preserving capital. Losing trades should be managed aggressively through [[trade-repair-and-rolling|rolling and repair]] rather than holding and hoping. (Source: [[itpm-ten-secrets]])

### Patience and Discipline
Only ~20% of the time do markets offer enough [[volatility]] for active trading. The remaining 80% should be spent on portfolio management and research, not trading. The best traders spend most of their time waiting. (Source: [[itpm-god-like-trader-status]])

## Trading Relevance

Kreil represents the institutional trading perspective in a retail-dominated education market. His methodology is most relevant for:

- Medium-term equity and options traders (20-60 day horizons)
- Traders interested in long-short-equity portfolio construction
- Those seeking to understand how investment bank proprietary desks operate
- Options traders wanting to integrate directional strategies within portfolio management
