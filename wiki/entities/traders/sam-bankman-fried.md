# Sam Bankman-Fried (SBF)

**Sam Bankman-Fried (SBF)** is the founder of [[ftx|FTX]] and [[alameda-research|Alameda Research]] who was **convicted of fraud and conspiracy** in November 2023 and sentenced to **25 years in federal prison** in March 2024. Once celebrated as a crypto wunderkind with a net worth estimated at $26 billion, SBF orchestrated one of the largest financial frauds in history, misappropriating $8+ billion in FTX customer funds.

## Status as of June 2026

- **Appeal pending**: the Second Circuit heard oral argument on November 4, 2025; a ruling is expected during 2026. His separate pro se new-trial motion was rejected/withdrawn (April 2026).
- **Projected release**: the Bureau of Prisons lists a projected release date of **December 14, 2044** — roughly four years earlier than the nominal 25-year term, reflecting good-conduct and program credits plus pre-sentencing detention.
- **FTX creditor repayments** (FTX Recovery Trust, paying claims at *November 2022 petition-date dollar values*):
  - Feb 18, 2025 — first distribution (~$454M; "convenience class" creditors under $50K received ~119% of claims including 9% annual interest)
  - May 30, 2025 — second distribution, ~$5B
  - Sep 30, 2025 — third distribution, ~$1.6B
  - By early 2026, cumulative distributions reached roughly $7–10B out of $15B+ in recovered assets (including the estate's Anthropic stake, sold for ~$884M against a $500M cost basis)
  - Major creditor grievance: claims were fixed in USD at petition-date crypto prices — BTC was ~$16-17K in Nov 2022 versus $100K+ in 2025 — so "119% recovery" represents a large opportunity loss in crypto terms

## The Fraud

SBF directed FTX to secretly transfer customer deposits to Alameda Research. The funds were used for:
- **Leveraged trading** -- Alameda took massive directional bets that went wrong (particularly around [[terra-luna]])
- **Venture investments** -- billions deployed into illiquid crypto startups
- **Political donations** -- $100M+ in political contributions using customer money
- **Personal expenses** -- luxury real estate in the Bahamas

SBF publicly promoted "effective altruism" and positioned himself as a responsible industry leader while the fraud was ongoing.

## Trading Relevance

- SBF's story is the most prominent cautionary tale about **counterparty risk** in crypto
- His background at Jane Street illustrates how legitimate quantitative trading skills can be misapplied
- The SBF case accelerated global crypto regulation and exchange oversight requirements
- "Effective altruism" branding was used to deflect scrutiny -- a lesson in evaluating public figures' stated motivations vs actual behavior
- The trial revealed how thin the oversight was at a top-3 global exchange, reinforcing the case for [[self-custody]]
