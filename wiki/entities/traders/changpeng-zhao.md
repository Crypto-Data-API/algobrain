# Changpeng Zhao

Changpeng Zhao (born September 10, 1977), universally known as **CZ**, is a Chinese-Canadian entrepreneur who founded [[binance|Binance]] in 2017 and built it into the world's largest [[crypto-markets|cryptocurrency]] exchange by trading volume within six months of launch. Under CZ's leadership, [[binance|Binance]] expanded from a simple spot trading platform into a sprawling ecosystem encompassing [[futures]], [[margin]], lending, staking, NFTs, and [[defi]] through BNB Chain (formerly Binance Smart Chain). CZ's rise parallels the explosive growth of the [[crypto-markets|crypto industry]] itself -- and his fall, culminating in a guilty plea to US federal money laundering charges in November 2023 and a four-month prison sentence, mirrors the industry's regulatory reckoning. At his peak, CZ's net worth was estimated at approximately $65 billion (2022), making him one of the wealthiest people in the world.

## Early Life and Background

Changpeng Zhao was born on September 10, 1977, in Jiangsu Province, China. His family emigrated to Vancouver, Canada, when he was 12 years old. Growing up in an immigrant family with limited resources, CZ worked a series of part-time jobs as a teenager, including shifts at McDonald's and overnight shifts at a gas station -- experiences he has frequently referenced to contrast with his later wealth.

CZ studied computer science at McGill University in Montreal. His technical education gave him a strong foundation in software engineering -- a skill set that would prove decisive when he needed to build a trading platform rapidly and iterate on it faster than any competitor.

### Pre-Crypto Career

After graduating from McGill, CZ built a career in traditional financial technology:

- **Bloomberg LP (2001-2005)**: CZ worked as a developer at Bloomberg, building trading systems for the company's Tradebook platform. This experience gave him firsthand understanding of how institutional trading systems work -- [[order-book]] management, trade matching, [[market-microstructure|market microstructure]], and the importance of low [[latency]]
- **Fusion Systems (2005-2013)**: CZ founded Fusion Systems, a company that built high-frequency trading systems for brokers. The company operated in Tokyo and Shanghai, giving CZ exposure to Asian financial markets and the technology infrastructure that powers them
- **Blockchain.info (2013)**: CZ joined Blockchain.info (now Blockchain.com) as the third member of the team, getting his first deep exposure to [[bitcoin|Bitcoin]] and [[blockchain]] technology
- **OKCoin (2014-2015)**: CZ served as CTO of OKCoin, one of China's largest [[crypto-markets|cryptocurrency]] exchanges at the time. This role gave him practical experience in operating a crypto exchange -- understanding the technical challenges, regulatory landscape, and user acquisition dynamics

By 2015, CZ had accumulated a rare combination of skills: institutional trading system development (Bloomberg), high-frequency trading infrastructure (Fusion Systems), [[blockchain]] technology (Blockchain.info), and crypto exchange operations (OKCoin). This precise skill stack made him uniquely positioned to build the next-generation crypto exchange.

## Career and Key Trades

### Founding Binance (July 2017)

CZ launched [[binance|Binance]] in July 2017, funding the launch through an Initial Coin Offering (ICO) for the BNB (Binance Coin) token that raised approximately **$15 million**. The timing was critical: the 2017 [[crypto-markets|crypto]] bull market was accelerating, demand for trading platforms was exploding, and the existing exchanges (Coinbase, Bitfinex, Poloniex, Bittrex) were struggling with capacity and user experience issues.

CZ's competitive advantages were execution speed and product breadth:

- **Rapid listing strategy**: [[binance]] listed new tokens faster than any competitor, attracting users who wanted early access to emerging projects. While competitors spent weeks or months evaluating token listings, [[binance|Binance]] moved in days
- **Low fees**: A 0.1% base trading fee (with further discounts for BNB holders) undercut most rivals
- **Multilingual platform**: Early localization into dozens of languages captured global market share, particularly in Asia, the Middle East, Africa, and Latin America
- **Performance**: CZ's background in building trading systems meant [[binance|Binance]]'s matching engine could handle significantly higher throughput than competitors -- critical during the frenzied trading volumes of late 2017

Within **six months** of launch, [[binance|Binance]] was the world's largest crypto exchange by trading volume -- an unprecedented rise in financial services.

### BNB Token: From Utility to Ecosystem

The BNB token, initially sold in the ICO at approximately $0.15, evolved far beyond its original purpose as a fee-discount utility token:

- BNB became the native token of [[binance|Binance]]'s own [[blockchain]], BNB Chain
- [[binance]] implemented quarterly BNB "burns" (destroying tokens to reduce supply), creating deflationary tokenomics
- BNB reached a peak market capitalization exceeding **$100 billion**, making it one of the top-5 [[crypto-markets|cryptocurrencies]] by market cap
- The BNB token became effectively a proxy equity stake in the [[binance|Binance]] ecosystem -- holders benefited from fee discounts, launchpad access, staking yields, and token appreciation driven by [[binance|Binance]]'s growth

### Binance Smart Chain / BNB Chain (2020)

In September 2020, CZ launched Binance Smart Chain (BSC), later rebranded to BNB Chain -- an EVM-compatible [[blockchain]] designed to compete with [[ethereum|Ethereum]] by offering dramatically lower transaction fees:

- BSC attracted massive [[defi]] activity by providing an [[ethereum|Ethereum]]-compatible environment where gas costs were fractions of a cent instead of $10-100+ on [[ethereum|Ethereum]]
- The ecosystem quickly grew to become the **#2 [[defi]] platform** by total value locked (TVL), behind only [[ethereum|Ethereum]]
- Major BSC protocols like PancakeSwap, Venus, and BakerySwap attracted billions in TVL
- Critics argued that BSC sacrificed decentralization for speed and cost -- the chain operated with only 21 validators (compared to [[ethereum|Ethereum]]'s hundreds of thousands), and [[binance|Binance]] effectively controlled the validator set
- The "decentralization vs. usability" tradeoff embodied by BSC became one of the defining debates in [[defi]] and [[blockchain]] philosophy

### The FTX Crisis (November 2022)

CZ played a pivotal -- and controversial -- role in the collapse of FTX, the exchange run by [[sam-bankman-fried]]:

- [[binance]] was an early investor in FTX and held a significant position in FTT (FTX's native token)
- On November 6, 2022, CZ tweeted that [[binance|Binance]] would sell its entire FTT holdings (~$580 million) "due to recent revelations" -- widely interpreted as a reference to concerns about Alameda Research's balance sheet
- This announcement triggered a bank run on FTX as users rushed to withdraw funds
- On November 8, CZ briefly offered to acquire FTX, signing a non-binding letter of intent
- Within 24 hours, CZ withdrew the acquisition offer, citing concerns about mishandled customer funds and ongoing regulatory investigations
- FTX filed for bankruptcy on November 11, 2022, in one of the largest financial frauds in history ([[ftx-collapse]])

CZ's role in the FTX collapse remains debated: some view his FTT sell announcement as a calculated move to destroy a competitor; others argue he was simply protecting [[binance|Binance]]'s interests and that FTX's insolvency was a pre-existing condition that CZ merely exposed. Regardless of intent, the episode demonstrated [[binance|Binance]]'s enormous market influence -- a single tweet from CZ could trigger a [[bank-run]] that destroyed a $32 billion company.

### Regulatory Reckoning and Criminal Charges (2023)

CZ's most significant moment came on November 21, 2023, when he pleaded guilty to federal charges and stepped down as CEO of [[binance|Binance]]:

- [[binance]] agreed to pay **$4.3 billion** in fines and penalties -- the largest penalty in [[crypto-markets|crypto]] history
- CZ personally pleaded guilty to one count of failing to maintain an effective anti-money laundering program, a violation of the Bank Secrecy Act
- The DOJ alleged that [[binance|Binance]] had knowingly processed transactions for users in sanctioned countries (Iran, Syria, Cuba, North Korea) and failed to implement adequate Know Your Customer (KYC) controls
- CZ was sentenced to **four months in federal prison** in April 2024 and served his sentence
- Richard Teng was appointed as [[binance|Binance]]'s new CEO
- Despite the criminal charges, CZ retained a significant ownership stake in [[binance|Binance]] and his personal net worth remained in the billions

## Trading and Investment Philosophy

CZ's approach is fundamentally different from traditional investors and traders -- he is primarily a builder and operator, not a portfolio manager. Nevertheless, his decisions contain clear philosophical principles:

### 1. Build, Ship, Iterate

CZ's primary competitive advantage was speed of execution. While competitors spent months on product development, compliance review, and risk analysis, CZ shipped products quickly, iterated based on user feedback, and fixed problems on the fly. This "move fast" philosophy produced:

- The fastest exchange listing process in [[crypto-markets|crypto]]
- Rapid expansion into new product categories ([[futures]], [[margin]], lending, staking, NFTs, [[defi]])
- Quick localization and market entry into dozens of countries
- The trade-off: regulatory corners were cut, compliance was deprioritized, and legal risks accumulated

### 2. Ecosystem Thinking

CZ built [[binance|Binance]] not as a single product but as an interconnected ecosystem:

- The BNB token created economic incentives for users to stay within the [[binance|Binance]] ecosystem (fee discounts, launchpad access, staking)
- BNB Chain extended the ecosystem from centralized exchange to [[defi]], creating a parallel financial infrastructure under [[binance|Binance]]'s influence
- [[binance]] Academy provided free education, onboarding new users into [[crypto-markets|crypto]] through [[binance|Binance]]'s own platform
- This ecosystem approach created powerful [[network-effects]] and switching costs -- a user with BNB staked, launchpad allocations, earn products, and BNB Chain [[defi]] positions had strong economic reasons to remain

### 3. Global-First, Regulators-Second

CZ deliberately operated [[binance|Binance]] without a fixed headquarters, moving between jurisdictions (China, Japan, Malta, Singapore, Dubai) to minimize regulatory exposure. This "regulatory arbitrage" strategy:

- Allowed [[binance|Binance]] to serve users globally without the restrictions that compliant exchanges like [[coinbase|Coinbase]] accepted
- Generated enormous growth and market share
- Ultimately proved unsustainable -- the $4.3 billion DOJ settlement and CZ's criminal conviction demonstrated that regulatory arbitrage has limits, particularly when it involves money laundering and sanctions violations

### 4. Crypto-Native Conviction

CZ is a true believer in [[crypto-markets|cryptocurrency]] and [[blockchain]] technology, not merely an opportunist:

- He reportedly converted most of his personal net worth into [[bitcoin|Bitcoin]] and BNB during the 2014-2015 period, before founding [[binance|Binance]]
- He has consistently advocated for [[bitcoin|Bitcoin]] as a store of value and for [[blockchain]] as a foundational technology
- His conviction drove bold decisions (launching BSC to compete with [[ethereum|Ethereum]], building [[binance|Binance]] Futures to challenge BitMEX) that paid off enormously

CZ's significance is less as a trader than as the operator of the single most important venue in crypto market structure. A few dimensions:

| Listing power | A [[binance]] listing materially moves a token's liquidity and price ("the Binance effect") |

CZ's career sits at the intersection of [[market-microstructure]] (he built matching engines at [[bloomberg]] and Fusion Systems) and crypto-native venue operation. His decisions are studied less for entry/exit timing than for what they reveal about [[liquidity]] concentration, venue [[exchange-risk|counterparty risk]], and the [[regulation|regulatory]] limits of offshore operation. ## Context

CZ's story cannot be understood in isolation from the broader [[crypto-markets|crypto industry]]. He is simultaneously:

- The most successful crypto entrepreneur in history (building [[binance|Binance]] from $15 million ICO to the dominant global exchange)
- A convicted federal criminal (money laundering charges, 4-month prison sentence)
- A symbol of crypto's promise (financial inclusion, innovation, permissionless access) and its excesses (regulatory evasion, compliance failures, concentrated power)

His trajectory from immigrant McDonald's worker to the world's wealthiest crypto figure to federal prisoner and back to billionaire is one of the most dramatic personal stories in modern finance.
