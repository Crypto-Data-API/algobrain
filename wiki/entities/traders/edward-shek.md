# Edward Shek

Edward Shek is a Senior Trading Mentor at the [[itpm|Institute of Trading and Portfolio Management]] with 25 years of professional trading experience (1994-2019) across [[goldman-sachs]], Cazenove, [[jp-morgan|JP Morgan]], and BCS Global.

## Overview

Shek is notable for profitably navigating the 2008-2009 financial crisis while trading European bank and insurance equities at Cazenove — an experience very few traders worldwide achieved. His career spans developed and emerging market equities, with expertise in managing $100M+ [[long-short-equity|long/short portfolios]].

## Career History

### Goldman Sachs (1994-2003)
- Started in London trading convertible bonds, warrants, and equities
- Worked across Tokyo, New York, and London offices
- Specialized in European equities managing a $100M Long/Short portfolio
- Covered Oil & Gas, Pharmaceuticals, and Technology sectors

### Cazenove (2006-2009)
- Traded European Banks and Insurance equities
- Profited through the 2008-2009 financial crisis — a rare achievement among institutional traders
- This crisis-trading experience informs his approach to [[risk-management]] and position sizing

### JP Morgan (2009-2019)
- Managed global emerging markets equities following the Cazenove acquisition
- Ran the South African trading desk for 6 years
- Oversaw CEEMEA (Central & Eastern Europe, Middle East, Africa) trading
- Operated multiple $100M Long/Short portfolios

### BCS Global
- Brief tenure as Head of Equities Trading before retirement

### ITPM (2020-present)
- Hired by ITPM as Senior Trading Mentor in May 2020 (announced via PR Newswire)
- Conducts 3-month structured mentoring programs; capacity limited to twelve ITPM mentee students per year
- Emphasizes high-risk/reward and ROI strategies
- Manages Institute's global portfolio and allocates capital to profitable traders
- Still listed on ITPM's mentor roster as of June 2026

## Trading Approach

Shek's approach, informed by his crisis-trading experience, emphasizes:

- **Starting with half positions** when market conditions are unclear, then scaling up on confirmation
- **Cutting losing trades early** (around 50% loss) rather than hoping for recovery
- **Repairing positions later** through new entries at better prices rather than averaging down (Source: [[itpm-education-methodology-overview]])

## Trading Relevance

Shek's track record through the 2008 crisis gives him unique credibility on [[risk-management]] during extreme market events. His emerging markets experience adds breadth beyond the typical U.S.-centric retail trading approach.
