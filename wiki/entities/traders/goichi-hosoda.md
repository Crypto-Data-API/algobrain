# Goichi Hosoda

Goichi Hosoda (1898–1982) was a Japanese financial journalist who developed [[ichimoku|Ichimoku Kinko Hyo]] ("one-glance equilibrium chart") in secret starting in the late 1930s, publishing his work in a 7-volume series beginning 1969. He used the pen name "Ichimoku Sanjin" (Source: [[2026-04-20-comprehensive-guide-technical-trading-indicators]]). His system is one of only a handful of *complete* charting frameworks — [[trend-following|trend]], momentum, [[support-and-resistance|support/resistance]], and time projection in a single overlay — and it remains a default tool on every major platform.

## Overview

Hosoda's system combines five components — Tenkan-sen, Kijun-sen, Chikou Span, Senkou Span A and B (forming the cloud/Kumo) — with Time Theory, Wave Theory, and Price (Target) Theory into a single visual framework. Long dominant in Japanese equities and FX, the system spread globally only in the 1990s due to translation lag (Source: [[2026-04-20-comprehensive-guide-technical-trading-indicators]]).

### The five lines (mechanics)

All five lines derive from highs, lows, and the close — no volume — and project both backward and forward in time, which is what makes [[ichimoku-cloud|Ichimoku]] a complete picture rather than a single oscillator:

1. **Tenkan-sen (Conversion Line)** = (9-period high + 9-period low) / 2. A fast midpoint; momentum/short-term trend.
2. **Kijun-sen (Base Line)** = (26-period high + 26-period low) / 2. A slower midpoint acting as a trend anchor and trailing support/resistance; a Tenkan crossing above/below Kijun is a classic entry signal (the "TK cross").
3. **Senkou Span A (Leading Span A)** = (Tenkan-sen + Kijun-sen) / 2, plotted **26 periods ahead**.
4. **Senkou Span B (Leading Span B)** = (52-period high + 52-period low) / 2, plotted **26 periods ahead**. The band between Span A and Span B is the **Kumo (cloud)** — projected future [[support-and-resistance|support/resistance]]; price above the cloud is bullish, below is bearish, inside is neutral/transitional, and the cloud's thickness signals the strength of that zone.
5. **Chikou Span (Lagging Span)** = the current close plotted **26 periods *back***, used to confirm a signal by comparing present price action against the past.

Note the midpoints use the *median of the range*, not a moving average of closes — a deliberate "equilibrium" measure, reflected in the name *kinko* (balance/equilibrium). The 9/26/52 defaults trace to the historical six-day Japanese trading week (roughly 1.5, 4–5, and 9–10 weeks).

Biographical detail:

- Born in 1898; joined the Tokyo newspaper **Miyako Shimbun** in 1924, eventually heading its financial/commodities markets department. He initially wrote market commentary under the pseudonym **Tarō Sagami** before adopting "Ichimoku Sanjin" ("the man of the mountain who sees at one glance").
- Development of the system began in the 1930s and reportedly involved years of manual back-calculations performed by teams of students — decades before computers made such parameter testing trivial.
- He kept the method private until revealing it to a small circle in the 1950s, then published the first book, *Ichimoku Kinko Hyo*, in **1969**; seven volumes had appeared by 1981. The books became standard reading among Japanese institutional traders.
- Hosoda died in 1982.

## Why Hosoda Matters to Traders

- **Ichimoku is a complete system**, not an indicator: Kumo breaks define trend regime, Tenkan/Kijun crosses give entries, the cloud projects support/resistance 26 periods forward, and Chikou confirms — all from price alone with default settings (9/26/52) Hosoda derived from the six-day Japanese trading week.
- It is heavily used in **JPY FX pairs** to this day — cloud levels on USD/JPY are watched closely enough to act as self-fulfilling support/resistance — and has a large following in crypto trend trading.
- Hosoda's **Time Theory** (kihon suchi, the basic numbers 9, 17, 26...) is an early systematic attempt at time-based rather than price-based forecasting, a lineage that runs parallel to [[william-d-gann|W.D. Gann]]'s work in the West.

## Influence and Legacy

For decades Ichimoku was a closely held edge among Japanese institutional traders; because Hosoda published only in Japanese, it reached Western markets only in the 1990s and 2000s, well after [[candlestick-charting|candlestick charting]] (also Japanese) crossed over via Steve Nison. Today it is a built-in default on TradingView, MetaTrader, and effectively every charting platform, and it is one of the most-watched systems in [[forex|JPY FX]] — USD/JPY cloud levels are followed closely enough to act as partly self-fulfilling [[support-and-resistance|support/resistance]] — and in crypto [[trend-following|trend trading]]. Hosoda's broader legacy is the demonstration that a single, self-contained overlay can answer trend, momentum, support/resistance, and *timing* at one glance, an ambition matched by few other technical frameworks.
