# Zagabond

Zagabond is the pseudonymous founder of the [[azuki|Azuki]] NFT collection, one of the most prominent profile-picture (PFP) NFT projects on [[ethereum]]. Azuki launched in January 2022 as a 10,000-piece anime-inspired collection and quickly rose to blue-chip status. However, Zagabond became one of the most controversial figures in the NFT space after revealing in May 2022 that he had previously created and abandoned three NFT projects before Azuki -- a revelation that caused a dramatic price crash and raised fundamental questions about founder accountability and due diligence in NFT markets.

## Azuki

### Launch and Rise

Azuki launched in January 2022 with a mint price of 1 ETH per NFT. The collection featured 10,000 anime-styled profile pictures and positioned itself as a premium brand with a strong community focus, streetwear-inspired aesthetic, and ambitious roadmap. Key milestones:

- **Rapid sellout**: The collection sold out quickly, generating significant primary market revenue
- **Blue-chip status**: Azuki's floor price rose rapidly, establishing it alongside [[cryptopunks]], [[bored-ape-yacht-club|Bored Ape Yacht Club]], and a handful of other elite PFP collections
- **Community building**: The project cultivated a dedicated community (the "Garden") with exclusive events, merchandise, and branding
- **Innovation**: Azuki introduced ERC-721A, an optimized smart contract standard that reduced gas costs for batch minting on [[ethereum]]

### The Blog Post Controversy (May 2022)

On May 9, 2022, Zagabond published a blog post titled "A Builder's Journey" in which he revealed that before Azuki, he had created and subsequently abandoned three previous NFT projects:

1. **CryptoPhunks**: A derivative of [[cryptopunks|CryptoPunks]] (mirrored images), abandoned after launch
2. **Tendies**: A chicken-themed NFT collection, abandoned
3. **CryptoZunks**: Another derivative project, abandoned

Zagabond framed the post as transparency about his learning journey, but the NFT community reacted with outrage. The core concern: if Zagabond had walked away from three projects and their communities before, what guarantee did Azuki holders have that he would not do the same again?

### Market Impact

The fallout was immediate and severe:

- **Floor price crash**: Azuki's floor price dropped approximately 50% within hours of the blog post, falling from roughly 20 ETH to under 10 ETH
- **Liquidity exodus**: Many holders rushed to sell, creating a cascading sell-off
- **Trust erosion**: The incident became a widely cited example of why founder anonymity and pseudonymity in [[nft|NFT]] projects carries risk for investors
- **Community split**: The Azuki community divided between those who remained loyal ("diamond hands") and those who felt betrayed

## Recovery and Elementals

Despite the controversy, Azuki did not collapse entirely:

- **Price recovery**: Over subsequent months, Azuki's floor price partially recovered as the remaining community consolidated and the project continued to deliver on roadmap items
- **Elementals collection**: In June 2023, Azuki launched Elementals, a companion 20,000-piece collection. However, this launch was itself controversial -- many buyers complained that the Elementals art was too visually similar to the original Azuki collection, sparking further backlash
- **Brand resilience**: Azuki demonstrated that strong branding and community engagement can partially survive founder controversy, though at the cost of reduced market capitalization and trust

## Pivot to Anime and the ANIME Token (2024-2026)

Under Zagabond's leadership, Azuki repositioned itself from a PFP collection into an anime media brand:

- **Anime.com**: Azuki acquired and built out anime.com as a community/streaming hub for its anime ambitions
- **ANIME token launch (January 23, 2025)**: Together with the Animecoin Foundation, Azuki launched the ANIME token simultaneously on [[ethereum]] and Arbitrum. Total supply is 10 billion tokens, with 50.5% allocated to the community overall and 37.5% claimable by holders of the three Azuki NFT collections (Azuki, Elementals, Beanz). The team, contractors, and advisors received 15.62%, with roughly two-thirds of team tokens vesting over three years. About 77% of supply was unlocked at launch
- **NFT-to-token playbook**: The announcement triggered significant rallies in Azuki, Elementals, and Beanz floor prices in late 2024 as traders positioned for the airdrop — a now-standard pattern where NFT collections convert community value into fungible token liquidity
- **Status 2025-2026**: ANIME gave Azuki holders a liquid, tradeable claim on the brand's anime strategy, though like most 2025 token launches it traded well below its initial hype in subsequent months. Azuki remains one of the few 2021-2022 era PFP brands still actively shipping products

Zagabond's real-world identity remains publicly unconfirmed; he has continued to operate pseudonymously as CEO of Chiru Labs, Azuki's parent company.

## Lessons for NFT Markets

The Zagabond saga illustrates several critical dynamics in NFT investing:

- **Founder due diligence**: In pseudonymous markets, investors have limited ability to assess a founder's track record. Zagabond's history of abandoned projects was not widely known before his own disclosure
- **[[rug-pulls|Rug pull]] spectrum**: Zagabond's actions exist on a spectrum between full rug pulls (where founders disappear with funds) and legitimate pivots. The abandoned projects were not outright scams, but the pattern of abandonment created legitimate concern
- **Pseudonymity risk**: While pseudonymity is a core feature of crypto culture, it complicates accountability. Zagabond's case showed how past failures can be hidden behind new pseudonymous identities
- **PFP project fragility**: Even blue-chip PFP collections are vulnerable to sudden trust crises, underscoring the importance of diversification in NFT portfolios
- **Transparency paradox**: Zagabond's disclosure was voluntary, yet it damaged the project more than continued silence likely would have -- creating a perverse incentive against transparency
