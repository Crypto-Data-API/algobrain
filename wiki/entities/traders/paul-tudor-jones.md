# Paul Tudor Jones

Paul Tudor Jones II is a macro trader and founder of Tudor Investment Corp, best known for calling and profiting massively from the [[1987-crash|Black Monday crash of 1987]]. He is widely regarded as one of the greatest discretionary macro traders in history.

## Background

Born in 1954 in Memphis, Tennessee, Jones began trading cotton [[futures]] after college and quickly made a name in commodity markets. He founded Tudor Investment Corp in 1980, which has generated strong risk-adjusted returns over four decades. His 1987 documentary "Trader" (later suppressed at his request) offered a rare glimpse into his trading process.

## The 1987 Call

Jones used [[elliott-wave|Elliott Wave]] analysis and classic chart patterns to study the parallels between the 1987 market and the 1929 crash, noting similar technical patterns and excessive speculation. He positioned short ahead of Black Monday (October 19, 1987), when the [[dow-jones]] fell 22.6% in a single day — the largest one-day percentage decline in history. His fund reportedly earned ~200% that year, cementing his reputation (Source: [[2026-04-20-comprehensive-guide-technical-trading-indicators]]).

## Major Trades After 1987

Jones turned the 1987 success into a four-decade career; Tudor Investment Corp grew to over $13B AUM at peak. Major trade themes:

| Year | Trade | Outcome |
|------|-------|---------|
| **1990** | Short Japanese equities (Nikkei) ahead of and during the bubble burst | Estimated 8-figure profits as Nikkei -39% in 1990 |
| **1992** | Participated in [[1992-black-wednesday-erm-crisis\|Black Wednesday]] short on GBP and ITL alongside Quantum / Caxton | Mid-9-figure profit on the cohort |
| **1994** | Short Mexican peso during [[1994-tequila-crisis\|Tequila Crisis]] (Dec 1994) | $200-500M estimated |
| **1998** | Short Russian ruble + GKO during [[1998-08-russia-ruble-default\|Russian default]] | $200-500M estimated; Tudor was a major winner while LTCM was the loser |
| **1999** | Short Brazilian real during [[1999-01-brazil-real-devaluation\|Plano Real anchor break]] (Jan 1999) | $300-500M estimated |
| **2007-2008** | Long crude oil + short subprime housing exposure | Tudor BVI Global Fund +20%+ in 2008 |
| **2017-2021** | Public Bitcoin advocacy from May 2020; portfolio allocation 1-3% Bitcoin | Significant 2020-2021 bull-cycle gains |
| **2022-2024** | Long gold; bearish on USD long-term; vocal about US fiscal sustainability | Gold rally validated thesis |
| **2024-2026** | "Debt trap" thesis: long gold + bitcoin + commodities + Nasdaq, zero/short fixed income | Gold and bitcoin both rallied strongly into 2025; see below |

His sustained relevance across 40+ years places him alongside [[george-soros|Soros]], [[stanley-druckenmiller|Druckenmiller]], and [[bruce-kovner|Kovner]] in the top tier of macro discretionary traders.

## 2024–2026: The "All Roads Lead to Inflation" Thesis

Jones's public positioning since late 2024 has centered on US fiscal unsustainability:

- **October 22, 2024 (CNBC Squawk Box)**: With federal debt near 100% of GDP (vs ~40% twenty-five years earlier), Jones declared **"all roads lead to inflation"** — "I'm long gold, I'm long bitcoin." He recommended a basket of **gold, bitcoin, commodities and Nasdaq, with zero fixed income**, and said he would short longer-duration Treasuries, arguing the only viable playbook is to inflate and outgrow the debt.
- **June 2025**: Said **bitcoin should be in every portfolio** as US debt mounts, and predicted President Trump would appoint an "uber-dovish" successor to Fed chair Jerome Powell — keeping real rates suppressed below inflation (the financial-repression scenario).
- **October 6, 2025 (CNBC Squawk Box)**: Returned with the same framework, recommending a vol-adjusted combination of **bitcoin, gold, and stocks** as "probably your best portfolio to fight inflation," while flagging late-cycle speculative conditions.
- **April 28, 2026**: Called bitcoin "unequivocally the best inflation hedge that there is — more than gold," citing fixed supply, while warning US equities were overvalued — arguing S&P 500 valuations implied **negative 10-year forward returns** and it would be "really hard to make money from here."

This sequence is a live, canonical example of the **fiscal-dominance / debt-debasement macro trade** that defined 2024–2026 cross-asset positioning (gold, bitcoin, steepeners, real assets over duration).

## Trading Philosophy

Jones blends technical analysis, macro fundamentals, and an acute awareness of market psychology. Key principles include:

- **Capital preservation first**: "The most important rule of trading is to play great defense, not great offense." (Source: [[book-market-wizards]])
- **Asymmetric risk/reward**: Only take trades where the potential reward far exceeds the risk (Source: [[book-market-wizards]]).
- **The 200-day moving average**: Jones has cited this as his preferred long-term trend indicator.
- **Emotional discipline**: He emphasizes controlling ego and being willing to reverse positions quickly.

## Trading Relevance

Jones's career demonstrates that discretionary [[macro-trading]] — combining top-down economic analysis with [[technical-analysis-overview|technical analysis]] and disciplined [[risk-management]] — remains a viable edge. His approach influenced generations of macro traders and contrasts with the purely systematic methods of [[jim-simons]].

Jones is the canonical example of how the same discretionary macro-trader rotates through successive crises — see [[crisis-currency-triangular-arbitrage]] for the pattern across Tequila, Russia, Brazil, Argentina, and beyond. The "rotate through EM crises" playbook he, [[bruce-kovner|Kovner]], [[stanley-druckenmiller|Druckenmiller]], and Soros all ran in the 1990s remains a template.

## Robin Hood Foundation

Jones founded the **Robin Hood Foundation** in 1988 — one of the largest poverty-fighting charities in NYC. Distributes $200M+/year. Makes Jones notable not just as a trader but as a model of profit-maximize-then-give-away philanthropic ethos.
