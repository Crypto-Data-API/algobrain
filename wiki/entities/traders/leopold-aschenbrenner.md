**Leopold Aschenbrenner** is a German-American AI researcher-turned-investor and the founder and portfolio manager of [[situational-awareness-lp|Situational Awareness LP]], a hedge fund focused on the AI scaling thesis. Aschenbrenner became publicly prominent in June 2024 with the publication of *Situational Awareness: The Decade Ahead*, a 165-page essay outlining a high-conviction view that AGI is achievable by 2027 and that the geopolitical, economic, and infrastructure consequences are larger than markets are pricing. He worked on the OpenAI Superalignment team until April 2024, was a Columbia University graduate, and previously worked at the FTX Future Fund prior to its collapse. After launching Situational Awareness LP in mid-2024 with just under $225M, the fund's disclosed 13F book grew to approximately $13.7B by the 31 March 2026 filing (reported May 2026), with a bifurcated long-physical-AI-infrastructure / short-semiconductor-puts thesis as the dominant trade structure.

## The *Situational Awareness* essay (June 2024)

The essay made several specific high-conviction claims that became the intellectual foundation of his fund:

1. **AGI by 2027** based on extrapolating compute and algorithmic progress curves through 2024
2. **OOM (orders of magnitude)** framing — each ~10x scale-up in effective compute produces predictable capability jumps
3. **National-security stakes** — frontier AI is treated as USG/CCP-strategic by default; foreign-intelligence threats to AI labs are underrated
4. **Drastic infrastructure requirements** — multi-trillion-dollar buildout of compute and power required to sustain the projected scaling trajectory
5. **The cluster-of-the-future** thesis — single trillion-dollar AI compute clusters by ~2030 driven by national security imperatives
6. **Power as the binding constraint** — US power grid capacity is the practical ceiling on AI buildout pace; this is the connecting thread to the fund's investment thesis

The essay is not a wiki page itself but is the **primary public source** for understanding the fund's investment philosophy. The fund's trades are direct expressions of the essay's specific claims.

## Investment thesis (as expressed in the fund)

See [[aschenbrenner-bifurcated-ai-thesis]] for the durable analytical version. Headline:

- **Long the physical buildout** (power, data centres, storage, fuel cells, HPC miners pivoting to AI infrastructure) — bets on the *scarcity* of physical AI infrastructure
- **Short the consensus-priced chip beneficiaries via puts** (semis ETFs, NVDA, ORCL, AVGO, AMD, MU, TSM, ASML, INTC) — bets that the *crowded* trade has already absorbed too much of the upside while the physical layer is structurally underpriced
- **Bifurcated, not paired** — the puts and longs are independent thesis legs, not a market-neutral pair trade. He is *long* the AI scaling cycle in aggregate, with specific views on which layer captures the value

## Why this is wiki-relevant

Three reasons the Aschenbrenner positioning is worth structured wiki coverage independent of any view on the trades themselves:

1. **Public 13F-disclosed sizing** — unlike most hedge fund theses which are inferred, his put-and-long positions are mandatorily disclosed at quarterly cadence with notional sizing
2. **High concentration** — his top 10 positions account for >80% of fund AUM, making them high-signal individual data points rather than diversified noise
3. **Clear thesis-to-trade mapping** — most fund disclosures are hard to interpret because the thesis is hidden; with Aschenbrenner the essay tells you exactly what each position is expressing

This makes the fund unusually useful as a public **disagreement target** — if you have a view different from his on AI capex, his sized bets give you a concrete benchmark for what you're effectively short or long against.
