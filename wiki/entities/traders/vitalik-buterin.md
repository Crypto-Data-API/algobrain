Vitalik Buterin (born January 31, 1994) is the Russian-Canadian programmer who co-founded [[ethereum]], the second-largest cryptocurrency by market capitalization and the settlement layer for most of [[defi]]. He authored the Ethereum whitepaper in late 2013 at age 19, and remains the protocol's most influential voice — his blog posts, roadmap statements, and even on-chain wallet movements are treated as market-moving signals by crypto traders.

## Background

Buterin was born in Kolomna, Russia, and moved to Canada at age six. He co-founded *Bitcoin Magazine* in 2011 with Mihai Alisie, writing extensively about [[bitcoin]] before concluding that blockchains needed a general-purpose programmable layer. He published the Ethereum whitepaper in November 2013, proposing a blockchain with a Turing-complete scripting language for [[smart-contracts]]. In 2014 he received a $100,000 Thiel Fellowship and dropped out of the University of Waterloo to work on Ethereum full-time. Ethereum's crowdsale in mid-2014 raised roughly $18 million in BTC, and the network went live on July 30, 2015. Co-founders included [[gavin-wood]], Joseph Lubin (later founder of ConsenSys), Charles Hoskinson (later Cardano), and Anthony Di Iorio.

## Role in Ethereum's Evolution

Buterin has shaped every major phase of the protocol:

- **EIP-1559 (August 2021)** — the fee-market reform that introduced ETH burning, a key input to ETH supply models
- **The Merge (September 15, 2022)** — Ethereum's transition from proof-of-work to [[proof-of-stake]], cutting energy use ~99.95% and reducing ETH issuance
- **Rollup-centric roadmap** — Buterin's 2020 pivot to scaling via [[layer-2]] rollups rather than on-chain sharding defined the L2 ecosystem (Arbitrum, Optimism, Base)
- **Pectra upgrade (May 7, 2025)** — eleven EIPs including EIP-7702 (account abstraction for ordinary wallets) and EIP-7251 (validator stake limit raised from 32 to 2,048 ETH)
- **Fusaka upgrade (December 3, 2025)** — introduced PeerDAS, expanding blob capacity from 6 to a target of 48 per block and raising the gas limit toward 150 million, sharply lowering L2 data costs

### Ethereum Foundation restructuring (2025)

In March 2025 the [[ethereum-foundation]] restructured its leadership: Aya Miyaguchi moved from executive director to president, and Hsiao-Wei Wang and Tomasz Stańczak were appointed co-executive directors effective March 17, 2025. Buterin stepped back from operational management, continuing as founder and board member providing technical and research direction. The 2025 priorities he championed were Layer 1 scaling, blob scaling, and user experience.

## Writings and Ideas

Buterin is a prolific essayist (vitalik.eth.limo). Influential concepts include the "blockchain trilemma" (decentralization / security / scalability trade-off), quadratic funding for public goods, soulbound tokens, and "d/acc" (defensive accelerationism, 2023) — his framework for differentially accelerating defensive technology, which broadened his influence into AI-safety debates.

## Trading Relevance

- **Statement risk**: Buterin's blog posts and conference remarks routinely move ETH and related tokens — comments on roadmap priorities, L2s, or specific projects are parsed as forward guidance for the ecosystem
- **On-chain signal**: His known wallets are monitored by analytics services; transfers to exchanges or token sales (e.g., his 2021 burn/donation of ~$6 billion notional in SHIB sent to him unsolicited) have triggered sharp moves in the affected tokens
- **Protocol catalysts**: Upgrades he architects (Merge, Pectra, Fusaka) are tradeable events — they alter ETH issuance, staking economics, and L2 fee structures, feeding directly into ETH supply/demand models
- **Concentration/key-man risk**: Despite decentralization, markets still price Buterin as key-man risk for [[ethereum]]; the 2025 EF restructuring was partly designed to reduce this dependency
