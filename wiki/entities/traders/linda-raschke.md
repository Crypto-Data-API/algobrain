# Linda Raschke

Linda Bradford Raschke (born 1959) is an American professional trader, author, and money manager who has been actively trading since 1981 -- one of the longest continuously active careers in professional trading. She is one of the few women featured in Jack Schwager's *New Market Wizards* (1992), and she won the US Investing Championship in 2002. Raschke specializes in short-term [[swing-trading]] across [[futures]] markets, holding positions from hours to days, and her trading style combines [[momentum]], [[mean-reversion]], and classical pattern recognition with an emphasis on preparation, routine, and psychological discipline. She co-authored *Street Smarts: High Probability Short-Term Trading Strategies* (1996) with Larry Connors, a book that became a classic reference for short-term traders (Source: [[book-market-wizards]]).

## Early Life and Background

Linda Raschke grew up in the United States and developed an early interest in financial markets. She began her trading career in 1981 as a market maker in equity [[options]] on the Pacific Coast Options Exchange floor in San Francisco. Floor trading in the early 1980s was an intensely physical and competitive environment -- dominated almost entirely by men -- and Raschke's survival and eventual success in that environment forged the mental toughness that would characterize her entire career.

After her initial years as an options market maker, Raschke transitioned to trading her own account and eventually moved from floor trading to screen-based trading as markets evolved. Unlike many floor traders who struggled with the transition to electronic markets, Raschke adapted successfully -- a testament to the robustness of her pattern-based methodology, which was not dependent on order-flow advantages unique to the trading floor.

Raschke's early experiences taught her several foundational lessons: the importance of cutting losses immediately (on the floor, slow decision-making meant instant financial pain), the value of reading price action in real time (connecting to [[tape-reading]] and [[price-action-trading]]), and the reality that trading is a performance discipline requiring daily preparation and physical conditioning.

## Career and Key Trades

### New Market Wizards Feature (1992)

Raschke was featured in Jack Schwager's *New Market Wizards* (1992), the sequel to his legendary *Market Wizards* (1989). At the time of the interview, Raschke had been trading for over a decade and had developed a reputation for consistent profitability in [[swing-trading|short-term trading]] across multiple [[futures]] markets (Source: [[book-market-wizards]]).

Key points from the Schwager interview:

- Raschke described herself as primarily a pattern trader who reacts to what the market is doing rather than forecasting what it will do
- She emphasized the importance of having a set of reliable, tested patterns that she could execute repeatedly
- Her holding period was typically hours to days -- true [[swing-trading]]
- She stressed that trading is more like athletic performance than intellectual analysis: preparation, routine, focus, and execution matter more than being "smart"

### Street Smarts (1996)

Raschke co-authored *Street Smarts: High Probability Short-Term Trading Strategies* with Larry Connors in 1996. The book cataloged a collection of short-term trading strategies with specific rules for entry, exit, and [[risk-management]]. It became one of the most widely read books on [[swing-trading]] and introduced several strategies that are still referenced by traders decades later:

- **Holy Grail**: Buy the first pullback to the 20-period [[moving-averages|EMA]] in a strong uptrend (as confirmed by a rising 14-period [[adx|ADX]] above 30). This pattern exploits the tendency of strong [[trend|trends]] to resume after shallow pullbacks
- **80-20 Strategy**: An opening range reversal pattern. If a market closes in the top 20% of its daily range one day and opens the next day above the prior close but then reverses, fade the reversal. This is a [[mean-reversion]] setup exploiting exhaustion after strong closes
- **Turtle Soup / Turtle Soup Plus One**: A false [[breakout-strategies|breakout]] fading strategy. When price breaks a 20-day high or low (the same entry signal used by [[richard-dennis|the Turtles]]) but immediately reverses, enter in the direction of the reversal. This strategy explicitly fades the [[turtle-trading]] breakout system, exploiting the fact that breakouts fail more often than they succeed in range-bound markets

These strategies share common characteristics: they are rules-based, have defined [[risk-management|risk parameters]], work across multiple [[futures]] markets, and exploit either [[momentum]] continuation or [[mean-reversion]] exhaustion.

### LBR Group and Money Management

Raschke founded LBR Group, through which she managed money and traded proprietary capital. Unlike many celebrity traders who pivot entirely to asset management or education, Raschke continued actively trading her own capital throughout her career, maintaining direct market exposure alongside her managed accounts.

### US Investing Championship (2002)

Raschke won the US Investing Championship in 2002 -- a notable achievement given that 2002 was a brutal bear market year (S&P 500 declined ~22%). Winning a trading competition in a down year demonstrated that her [[swing-trading]] methodology could generate returns independent of market direction -- a hallmark of skilled short-term traders who profit from [[volatility]] rather than from beta exposure.

## Trading and Investment Philosophy

Raschke's philosophy is distinctive in several ways:

### 1. Trading as Athletic Performance

Raschke consistently describes trading as more analogous to sports than to intellectual analysis. She emphasizes:

- **Daily preparation**: Reviewing markets, updating charts, and preparing trade plans before the market opens -- every single day
- **Physical conditioning**: Raschke has been a competitive equestrian and emphasizes that physical fitness and mental clarity are directly linked
- **Routine and ritual**: Consistent pre-market preparation, consistent execution, consistent post-market review -- the routine IS the edge
- **Performance under pressure**: Like an athlete, a trader must execute reliably under stress. This requires practice, not just knowledge

This perspective connects to [[trading-psychology]]: Raschke's edge is not primarily informational or analytical -- it is the ability to execute a proven methodology consistently, day after day, year after year.

### 2. Pattern Trading: React, Don't Predict

"I'm trading patterns, not opinions about where the market should go." This is perhaps Raschke's most quoted statement, and it captures her entire approach:

- She does not attempt to forecast macroeconomic events, earnings, or geopolitical developments
- Instead, she identifies recurring [[price-action-trading|price action patterns]] that offer favorable risk/reward ratios
- The patterns work because they reflect recurring dynamics in market structure -- [[momentum]] continuation, [[mean-reversion]] exhaustion, false [[breakout-strategies|breakouts]], [[trend]] resumption
- She trades many different [[futures]] markets because the patterns are market-agnostic -- human behavior and market microstructure produce similar patterns across asset classes

### 3. Combine Momentum and Mean Reversion

Unlike traders who are exclusively [[momentum]] or exclusively [[mean-reversion]], Raschke uses both:

- **[[momentum]] setups**: The Holy Grail (buying pullbacks in strong [[trend|trends]]) is a [[momentum]] continuation strategy
- **[[mean-reversion]] setups**: The 80-20 and Turtle Soup strategies are [[mean-reversion]] plays that fade exhaustion and false [[breakout-strategies|breakouts]]
- The key is context: in trending markets, trade with the [[trend]] ([[momentum]]); in range-bound or exhaustion markets, fade the extremes ([[mean-reversion]])

This dual capability -- knowing when to ride [[momentum]] and when to fade it -- is one of the hallmarks of experienced short-term traders and connects to [[regime-detection]] concepts used in [[algorithmic-trading]].

### 4. Strict Risk Management

Raschke's [[risk-management]] principles:

- **Every trade has a predetermined stop-loss**: Before entering any trade, the exit point for a loss is already defined. See: [[stop-loss]]
- **Small position sizes**: No single trade should represent a significant risk to the portfolio
- **Cut losses immediately**: If the pattern fails, exit. Do not wait, do not hope, do not rationalize
- **Live to trade another day**: Capital preservation is the first priority; profit generation is the second
- **Accept many small losses**: A high frequency of small losses is acceptable if the wins are significantly larger than the losses

### 5. Market-Agnostic Application

Raschke trades across multiple [[futures]] markets -- stock index futures ([[sp500|S&P 500]], [[nasdaq|NASDAQ]]), [[bond-market|bonds]], [[forex|currencies]], [[commodities]] -- because her pattern-based methodology is not tied to any single asset class. The same [[momentum]], [[mean-reversion]], and [[breakout-strategies|breakout]] dynamics occur across all liquid markets.
