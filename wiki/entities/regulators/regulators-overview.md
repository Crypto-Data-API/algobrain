# Regulators

The hub for the financial regulatory bodies that define what you can trade, how you can trade it, and what must be disclosed. Their actions — enforcement cases, rulemakings, and interest-rate decisions — are among the most powerful market-moving forces. The [[sec]] oversees US securities, the [[federal-reserve]] sets the monetary policy that reprices every asset class, and the [[cftc]] regulates derivatives and increasingly weighs in on crypto.

Traders who ignore regulation do so at their peril: understanding the landscape helps you anticipate market reactions to policy and avoid compliance pitfalls.

## United States

- [[sec]] — Securities and Exchange Commission; securities-market conduct and disclosure
- [[cftc]] — Commodity Futures Trading Commission; derivatives and crypto-commodity oversight
- [[finra]] — Self-regulatory body for US broker-dealers
- [[federal-reserve]] — Monetary policy, interest rates, and QE/QT
- [[occ]] — Options Clearing Corporation; central counterparty for listed options

## Europe & UK

- [[ecb]] — European Central Bank; euro-area monetary policy
- [[esma]] — EU securities-markets regulator (MiCA, MiFID II)
- [[esrb]] — European Systemic Risk Board; macroprudential oversight
- [[bank-of-england]] — UK monetary policy and financial stability

## Asia-Pacific

- [[bank-of-japan]] — Japanese monetary policy, YCC, and the global carry trade
- [[reserve-bank-of-australia|RBA]] — Australian monetary policy

## Australia (Twin Peaks)

Australia uses a "Twin Peaks" model separating conduct regulation from prudential regulation, plus dedicated AML/CTF oversight. See [[australian-regulatory-framework]] for how the bodies interact.

- [[asic]] — Conduct regulator: market integrity, AFSL licensing, consumer protection (≈ SEC + FINRA)
- [[apra]] — Prudential regulator for banks, insurers, and super funds; oversees ~AUD $8T in assets
- [[austrac]] — AML/CTF and financial-intelligence agency; regulates crypto exchanges (DCE registration)

## Commodity & Sector Agencies

- [[eia]] — US Energy Information Administration; oil/gas inventory and supply data
- [[usda]] — US Department of Agriculture; crop and livestock reports
