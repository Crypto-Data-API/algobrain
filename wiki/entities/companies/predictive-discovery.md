**Predictive Discovery Limited** (ASX: PDI) is a gold explorer and developer, an [[asx-200]] constituent whose flagship asset is the **Bankan Gold Project** in north-eastern Guinea, West Africa — the largest new gold discovery in West Africa in a decade (~5.5 Moz resource). For traders it is a pre-production [[gold]] development play: pre-revenue and loss-making by design, priced on optionality (resource growth, feasibility economics, financing, jurisdiction risk) and, through 2025-2026, dominated by the merger with Canadian producer Robex Resources. The company is headquartered in Subiaco, Western Australia and was incorporated in 2007.

> **Status note (June 2026):** The Robex merger of equals **completed in mid-April 2026** (Quebec Superior Court approval mid-January 2026; transaction recorded as completed ~16 April 2026). The combined group continues to be ASX-listed. Sections below referring to the deal as "pending" or "expected in the March quarter" reflect earlier reporting and are retained for history; the deal is now closed (Verified via Perplexity, 2026-06-10).

## Business Model & Segments

Predictive Discovery's sole development-stage asset is the Bankan Gold Project, located 550 kilometres by road from Guinea's capital Conakry. The project covers 356 square kilometres across four exploration permits: Kaninko, Saman, Bokoro and Argo. Four deposits have been delineated to date with a total mineral resource of 5.53 million ounces of gold, including the NEB deposit at 4.89 million ounces - the largest new gold discovery in West Africa in a decade.

The Definitive Feasibility Study (DFS), completed in June 2025, outlines a 250,000 ounce per annum operation over a mine life of more than 12 years, with first gold production targeted in 2029. The DFS estimated a post-tax NPV5 of approximately US$1.6 billion, an IRR of 46%, payback of under 2.0 years, capital costs of approximately US$463 million and AISC of approximately US$1,057/oz at a US$2,400/oz gold price assumption. Expected Bankan production at steady state is approximately 272,000 ounces per annum.

The Government of Guinea has approved the project's Environmental and Social Impact Assessment (ESIA) and issued a Certificate of Environmental Compliance, with the Exploitation Permit application in the final stages.

## Economic Moat & Competitive Position

A pre-production, single-asset [[gold]] developer has **essentially no durable [[economic-moat|economic moat]]** in the conventional sense: it owns no franchise, no pricing power, and no recurring revenue. Like any [[gold]] miner it is ultimately a **price-taker** on the bullion price. What it has instead is *optionality*, and any "edge" is asset-specific rather than structural:

- **Asset quality and scale** — Bankan is a large, relatively high-grade deposit (NEB ~4.89 Moz anchoring a ~5.53 Moz resource), which sits at the favourable end of the [[gold]] development cost curve on the DFS assumptions (AISC ~US$1,057/oz). Low-cost ounces are the only defensible advantage a producer can hold against the gold price.
- **Resource endowment** — the largest new West African gold discovery in a decade is hard to replicate; exploration success of this magnitude is rare and confers scarcity value.
- **The Robex combination** — merging with a cash-generating producer is explicitly intended to **de-risk Bankan financing**, reducing reliance on dilutive equity raises. This is a financing advantage, not a competitive moat.

Honest framing: until Bankan is in production and generating cash, the equity is a bet on **execution and the gold price**, priced on optionality. There is no switching-cost, network, or brand moat here — only the quality of the orebody and management's ability to bring it online on time and budget.

## History

Predictive Discovery was incorporated in 2007 as a West Africa-focused gold explorer and listed on the ASX. Following a strategic review, the company re-focused its effort on Guinea, with systematic exploration at Bankan beginning late in the 2010s. A maiden resource of 3.65 million ounces was announced, which was expanded to 4.2 million ounces within approximately two years. Subsequent drilling programs delivered a 29% resource increase to 5.38 million ounces (100.5 million tonnes at 1.66 g/t gold), with approximately 77% of ounces in the Indicated category. Further drilling lifted the total to the current 5.53 million ounce resource.

Institutional support for the project was highlighted when BlackRock became a significant shareholder in 2022. The company progressed through Scoping Study, Pre-Feasibility and DFS stages, and obtained critical environmental approvals required for a development decision.

In October 2025, Predictive Discovery announced a "merger of equals" with Canadian gold producer Robex Resources Inc. Revised terms were agreed in December 2025 at an exchange ratio of 7.862 PDI shares per Robex share (53.5% PDI shareholders / 46.5% Robex shareholders). Robex shareholders approved the transaction in late December 2025 and Quebec Superior Court approval was received in mid-January 2026, with completion expected in the March quarter of 2026. The merger is intended to reduce financing risk for the Bankan development by combining PDI's asset with Robex's producing operations and cash flow.

## Recent Developments

Predictive Discovery released its interim financial report for the half-year ended 31 December 2025 in line with ASX disclosure requirements.

- **Resource / reserve upgrades** — further drilling at NEB and the satellite deposits can extend mine life and lift NPV.
- **The [[gold]] price** — the DFS assumed **US$2,400/oz**, so spot well above that is a strong tailwind to NPV (high leverage), while a reversal toward the assumption compresses the economics.
- **Recurring catalysts** — quarterly activity reports, drilling results, permitting headlines, financing announcements, and Guinea policy/fiscal news.

## Bull Case vs Bear Case

### Bull case
- A **large, relatively high-grade orebody** (~5.53 Moz; NEB ~4.89 Moz) — the biggest new West African [[gold]] discovery in a decade.
- **Strong DFS economics** — post-tax NPV5 ~US$1.6bn, IRR 46%, payback <2.0 yrs, AISC ~US$1,057/oz — low-cost ounces at the favourable end of the cost curve.
- **Gold-price tailwind** — with the DFS struck at US$2,400/oz, spot above that materially lifts NPV.
- **Robex merger de-risks financing** — producing cash flow reduces the equity-dilution path to building Bankan.
- Institutional backing (BlackRock on the register) and a board with West African build credentials (ex-Perseus).

### Bear case
- **Guinea sovereign / political risk** — a military-led government, evolving mining code, and fiscal-stability uncertainty common to West African gold; permitting and tenure are not guaranteed.
- **Single-asset concentration** — until Bankan produces, the equity lives or dies on one project in one jurisdiction.
- **Financing dilution** — even with Robex, building a ~US$463m mine can require equity that erodes per-share upside.
- **Execution and timeline risk** — first gold not until 2029; cost/schedule overruns and integration risk on the Robex combination.
- **Gold-price reversal** — a move back toward (or below) the US$2,400/oz DFS assumption compresses NPV and sentiment hard.

## Key Risks

- **Jurisdiction / political** — Guinea's military-led government, mining-code revisions, and fiscal-stability/tenure concerns are the dominant risk; West African gold has a history of sudden state interventions.
- **Permitting** — the Exploitation Permit grant is not yet final; timing slippage delays the entire development path.
- **Single-asset concentration** — no production and no revenue diversification until Bankan is online; one adverse event is existential.
- **Financing / dilution** — capital-intensive build; the mix of debt and equity (and any equity raise) directly drives shareholder dilution.
- **Gold-price sensitivity** — pre-revenue NPV is highly geared to the [[gold]] price relative to the US$2,400/oz DFS assumption.
- **Construction & integration** — cost/schedule overruns on a first-time build, plus execution risk integrating the Robex operations into the combined group.

## Valuation & How the Stock Trades

Predictive Discovery is **priced on optionality, not earnings** — it is pre-revenue and loss-making by design, so the screen P/E (negative) and most static multiples are **meaningless**. The market values it on a **NAV / resource-multiple and DFS-NPV** basis: dollars-per-resource-ounce and a (heavily) risk-discounted share of the ~US$1.6bn post-tax NPV5, with the discount reflecting Guinea jurisdiction risk, permitting, financing dilution and time-to-production (2029). Mechanically:

- **High beta to [[gold]]** — NPV is geared to the bullion price versus the US$2,400/oz DFS assumption; the equity amplifies gold moves.
- **Milestone-driven** — the stock steps on discrete catalysts (Exploitation Permit, financing terms, Robex completion, construction sanction) far more than on financials.
- **Institutional register dampens junior-explorer volatility** — BlackRock and other holders make day-to-day moves less erratic than a typical junior, but it remains a development-stage, single-jurisdiction name.
- **No meaningful listed-options market** — exposure is expressed through the shares; position-sizing and an explicit thesis on Guinea risk matter more than for an index name.
- It is one of the few pre-production [[gold]] developers in the [[asx-200]] ([[materials]] sector) — index inclusion adds passive demand but does not change the speculative, optionality-led nature of the equity.

## Leadership

The company is led by Chief Executive Officer and Managing Director Matthew Wilcox, who brings deep in-country experience in Guinea's gold sector. Andrew Pardey serves as Non-Executive Chairman and brings leadership experience from Perseus Mining, one of Africa's most successful mid-tier gold developers. The Board and management team have complementary capabilities in exploration, project development, financing and African operations.
