**4DMedical Limited** (ASX: 4DX) is a medical devices company listed on the [[asx-limited|Australian Securities Exchange]] and a constituent of the [[asx-200]] index. 4DMedical is a Melbourne-based medical technology company developing four-dimensional respiratory imaging software and hardware, marketed under the XV Technology platform. Its products provide quantitative lung function and cardiothoracic analysis from dynamic X-ray and CT sequences, delivered primarily as a cloud-based service to clinicians in the United States and Australia.

## Business Model & Segments

4DMedical's flagship XV Technology combines fluoroscopy and advanced motion-analysis algorithms, originally adapted from wind-tunnel imaging used in engineering, to visualise and quantify lung airflow and regional ventilation. The company's software portfolio includes XV LVAS (lung airflow imaging), CT LVAS (regional lung function from CT), CT:VQ (non-contrast post-processing for ventilation/perfusion), CAC (cardiothoracic risk assessment), IQ-UIP (idiopathic pulmonary fibrosis), LDAf, LDAi, LTA and PHA Analysis. Products are delivered through a SaaS model: existing imaging equipment scans the patient and images are analysed by 4DMedical remotely. The company also develops the XV Scanner, a dedicated lung-function scanner, via its Australian Lung Health Initiative (ALHI) subsidiary.

> **Pre-profit / speculative medtech caveat.** 4DMedical is an early-commercial, loss-making company that funds operations from equity raises rather than positive cash flow. Revenue is tiny relative to market value, adoption is at an early stage, and the investment case rests on clinical evidence, reimbursement and regulatory clearances that are inherently uncertain. Treat all forward-looking statements as unproven and size any position accordingly (see the Fundamentals block — Fred profile "Speculative / unprofitable").

### Business overview & segments

4DMedical is effectively a single-platform company (XV Technology) sold as a portfolio of cleared software modules plus a hardware program; it does not report multiple operating segments in a conventional sense.

| Line / module | What it does | Stage (qualitative) |
|---|---|---|
| **XV LVAS / CT LVAS** | Quantitative regional lung-airflow imaging from dynamic X-ray (LVAS) and from CT | FDA-cleared; foundational platform |
| **CT:VQ** | Non-contrast post-processing for ventilation/perfusion mapping | Lead commercial product; the main 2025–26 adoption and re-rating driver; CE Mark obtained (EU) |
| **CAC / IQ-UIP / other analyses** | Cardiothoracic risk (CAC), idiopathic pulmonary fibrosis (IQ-UIP) and related lung-analysis modules | Earlier-stage pipeline modules broadening the clinical use cases |
| **XV Scanner (via ALHI)** | Dedicated lung-function scanner hardware, part-funded by the Australian Medical Research Future Fund | Development/build-out; longer-dated optionality |
| **Delivery model** | Cloud/SaaS: existing hospital imaging equipment scans the patient; 4DMedical analyses remotely and returns quantitative reports | Recurring, capital-light revenue model once adoption scales |

The strategic logic is software-defined lung imaging: rather than selling expensive hardware, 4DMedical layers high-margin analysis onto imaging equipment hospitals already own, which (if adoption scales) is a capital-light, recurring SaaS model. The constraint is the long, uncertain path to broad clinical adoption and reimbursement.

## History

4DMedical was founded by Professor Andreas Fouras, who conceptualised four-dimensional X-ray imaging in 2005 and filed the first XV Technology patent in 2009, with roots in Monash University research. The company was incorporated in 2012 and has since accumulated more than 70 patents and patent applications and over 100 peer-reviewed publications. Its wholly-owned subsidiary ALHI received A$28.9 million from the Australian Federal Government's Medical Research Future Fund to develop the XV Scanner. 4DMedical listed on the ASX in August 2020 under ticker 4DX and has since expanded into the U.S. market, obtaining FDA clearance for a series of its imaging software modules.

## Economic Moat & Competitive Position

4DMedical's moat is **narrow and unproven but potentially deep** — characteristic of an early-stage [[healthcare]] medtech rather than an established compounder:

- **Intellectual property** — 70+ patents/applications and 100+ peer-reviewed publications around four-dimensional lung-airflow imaging create [[barriers-to-entry]] in a specialised niche.
- **Clinical evidence base** — a growing body of validation studies is the real currency in medtech; it underpins regulatory clearance, reimbursement and clinician trust.
- **Regulatory clearances** — accumulated FDA clearances plus CE Mark are slow and costly to replicate and gate market access.
- **First-mover & data flywheel** — early U.S. academic-centre deployments build reference accounts and a proprietary imaging dataset that can compound into [[switching-costs]] as the platform embeds in radiology workflows.

The critical caveat: none of these advantages is yet monetised at scale. The moat is a *hypothesis* validated by clearances and marquee deployments, not by durable, profitable revenue. A larger imaging player (e.g. GE HealthCare, Siemens Healthineers, Philips) bundling competing functional-imaging analytics is the structural threat.

## Growth Drivers & Catalysts

- **U.S. hospital adoption of CT:VQ** — the single most important driver; converting marquee academic deployments into volume contracts.
- **Reimbursement progress** — CMS/private-payer coverage in the U.S. is the gate to scaled, recurring revenue.
- **EU expansion** — CE Mark opens European commercial deployment for CT:VQ.
- **DoD / VA programs** — U.S. Department of Defense and Veterans Affairs contracts provide non-commercial demand and validation.
- **Pipeline module clearances** — further FDA/CE clearances (CAC, IQ-UIP, others) broaden the addressable use cases.
- **Recurring catalysts** — quarterly 4C cash-flow reports (burn rate), hospital-contract announcements, reimbursement updates, regulatory clearances, and capital raises (a recurring feature given the pre-profit model).

## Bull Case vs Bear Case

### Bull case
- Large, under-served clinical need: quantitative regional lung function is something current spirometry cannot deliver.
- Capital-light SaaS model layered on imaging hospitals already own — high gross margin if adoption scales.
- Strong IP, clinical-evidence and regulatory moat in a specialised niche.
- 2026 raises materially de-risk the balance sheet and fund the U.S. commercial push; marquee academic deployments validate the technology.

### Bear case
- Pre-profit with heavy cash burn; entirely dependent on capital markets, with ongoing dilution (shares ~209M in FY22 to ~480M+ after 2026 raises).
- Reimbursement is unproven at scale — without broad payer coverage, adoption stalls.
- Revenue is tiny relative to market value; the valuation discounts years of execution that may not materialise.
- After a parabolic re-rating (sub-A$1 in 2024 to A$5.90 placements in 2026), momentum reversal risk is high.
- Incumbent imaging giants could compete or render the niche commoditised.

## Key Risks

- **Going-concern / financing risk** — losses are funded by equity; any closed capital-markets window forces dilutive raises or a slowdown (Fred profile: "Speculative / unprofitable").
- **Reimbursement** — failure to secure broad CMS/private coverage caps the revenue model.
- **Adoption pace** — hospital sales cycles are long; converting pilots to volume is slow and uncertain.
- **Clinical / regulatory** — adverse study data or delayed clearances would undercut the entire thesis (clinical-evidence risk is the core uncertainty for a pre-profit medtech).
- **Dilution** — continued share issuance erodes per-share value even when the business advances.
- **Valuation / momentum** — extreme P/S multiple and a parabolic chart make the stock acutely vulnerable to de-rating in risk-off regimes.

## Valuation & How the Stock Trades

4DMedical cannot be valued on earnings (it is loss-making — the P/E is negative and meaningless); it trades as a **speculative, high-beta option on commercial success**. Mechanically:

- **No dividend** — all capital is reinvested into commercialisation and R&D; there is no income case.
- **High beta / momentum-driven** — the stock re-rated dramatically through late 2025/2026 on CT:VQ traction (placements at A$5.90 vs sub-A$1 in 2024), and is correspondingly exposed to sharp drawdowns on any disappointment or risk-off rotation.
- **Capital-markets cadence** — equity raises are a recurring, price-relevant event; placements price at a discount and dilute, so financing news moves the stock as much as clinical news.
- **Catalysts that move it** — quarterly 4C cash-burn reports, U.S. hospital contracts, reimbursement milestones, regulatory clearances. See the Fundamentals block for the figures as pulled; treat per-share metrics as fast-moving given the dilution.

## Recent Developments

In February 2026 4DMedical reported half-year results to 31 December 2025 showing sales of A$2.85 million and a net loss of A$154.14 million, reflecting continued investment in U.S. commercialisation. During 2025 the company raised more than A$100 million in new equity funding to accelerate U.S. deployment of its lung imaging technology. Regulatory and commercial milestones during the year included expanded adoption of CT:VQ in U.S. health systems and ongoing Department of Defense and Veterans Affairs programs in the United States.

### 2026 update (as of June 2026)

- **January 2026** — A$150 million (US$100M+) institutional placement to accelerate US adoption of CT:VQ and fund R&D; the raise made 4DX one of the strongest ASX 200 performers that month.
- **March 2026** — A$83 million follow-on equity offering (14.07 million new shares at A$5.90 each); CT:VQ also received **CE Mark approval**, opening EU commercial deployment.
- **US deployments** — within four months of FDA clearance, CT:VQ was deployed at Stanford, Cleveland Clinic, University of Miami and UC San Diego Health.
- The heavy 2026 raises materially de-risk the balance sheet but add dilution: shares on issue have grown from ~209M (FY22) to roughly 480M+.
