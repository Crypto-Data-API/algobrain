PPG Industries (NYSE: PPG) is one of the world's largest paints and coatings producers (founded 1883 as Pittsburgh Plate Glass), competing globally with Sherwin-Williams and AkzoNobel across automotive, aerospace, industrial and architectural coatings. For traders it is a [[s-and-p-500]] [[materials|Materials]] name whose margins are a real-time spread trade between selling prices and petrochemical raw-material costs — a classic mid/late-cycle industrial proxy.

## Business Overview & Segments

PPG is a [[materials|Materials]] / specialty-chemicals company whose products are coatings — paints, finishes, sealants — applied across automotive, aerospace, industrial, and architectural end-markets. Revenue is roughly globally balanced (Americas / EMEA / Asia), so European industrial activity and China auto builds matter as much as US housing.

| Segment | What it is | Role (qualitative) |
|---|---|---|
| **Performance Coatings** | Automotive refinish (collision repair — recurring, high-margin), **aerospace coatings & sealants**, protective & marine, traffic solutions, and architectural (Comex Mexico/Latin America) | The larger, higher-margin segment; aerospace and refinish are the standout franchises |
| **Industrial Coatings** | Automotive OEM coatings, general industrial, packaging coatings (can linings), specialty materials | Cyclical, volume-levered to auto build rates and global industrial production |
| **Architectural (Comex / Latin America)** | Comex's crown-jewel concessionaire/dealer network in Mexico and Latin America | A durable, branded distribution moat within an otherwise commoditised category |

In 2024 PPG **divested its US/Canada architectural business (to American Industrial Partners) and its silicas business**, sharpening the portfolio toward higher-margin technology coatings (aerospace, refinish, industrial) and away from lower-margin DIY paint.

## 2025-2026 Developments

- **FY2025**: organic sales growth resumed through the year — by Q1 2026 PPG had recorded its **fifth consecutive quarter of organic growth**, driven by pricing with flat volumes
- **Q1 2026** (reported April 2026): net sales **$3.9B (+7% YoY)**; GAAP EPS $1.70, **adjusted EPS $1.83 (+6% YoY)** — above the company's own guidance; aerospace and Latin America architectural led; segment EBITDA margins beat plan. Stock dipped on the print despite the beat (high expectations)
- **Guidance**: Q2 2026 organic sales and adjusted EPS flat to up low-single-digits YoY; **FY2026 adjusted EPS reaffirmed at $7.70–$8.10**
- Ongoing buybacks and a 50+ year record of annual dividend increases (dividend aristocrat)

## Economic Moat & Competitive Position

PPG holds a **narrow-to-moderate [[economic-moat|economic moat]]** — meaningfully wider than a pure commodity producer but narrower than its larger rival [[sherwin-williams]]:

- **Specification & switching costs** — in aerospace and automotive OEM, coatings are *qualified* into a customer's manufacturing process (regulatory certification, tested durability). Once specified, re-qualifying a rival imposes real [[switching-costs]], giving PPG sticky, recurring volume.
- **Automotive refinish franchise** — collision-repair coatings are sold through bodyshop relationships, colour-matching technology, and training; high-margin, recurring, and relationship-locked.
- **Brand & distribution (Comex)** — the Comex concessionaire network in Mexico is a genuine distribution moat in Latin American architectural paint.
- **Scale & formulation IP** — global manufacturing scale, raw-material purchasing leverage, and decades of formulation know-how across thousands of products.

The honest limit: in **architectural/DIY and general industrial coatings, PPG is closer to a price-taker** whose margins are a **real-time spread between selling prices and petrochemical raw-material costs** (oil/propylene, titanium dioxide). When input costs spike, PPG lags on price recovery; when they fall, margin expands. PPG also persistently **trades at a multiple discount to [[sherwin-williams]]**, which has a tighter US architectural moat via its company-owned store network. PPG's moat is real in aerospace/refinish, thinner elsewhere.

PPG competes on technology and specification depth; it rarely wins on price in commoditised lines, which is why portfolio-sharpening toward aerospace and refinish matters to the margin story.

## Growth Drivers & Catalysts

- **Aerospace cycle** — commercial-aero build rates and aftermarket demand ([[boeing]]/Airbus rates) drive PPG's highest-margin franchise.
- **Price/cost spread recovery** — pricing ahead of (or input costs falling below) petrochemical raw-material costs expands margin; the central swing variable.
- **Auto OEM build rates** — global vehicle production, especially China, drives Industrial Coatings volume.
- **Portfolio sharpening** — the 2024 divestitures shift mix toward higher-margin technology coatings.
- **Latin America architectural** — Comex network growth.
- **Capital return** — buybacks plus a 50+ year dividend-increase record.
- **Recurring catalysts** — quarterly earnings (price/cost spread commentary), oil/propylene/TiO2 input costs, Europe/China industrial PMIs, FX (large non-USD revenue).

## Bull Case vs Bear Case

### Bull case
- Five-plus consecutive quarters of organic growth (pricing-led) demonstrate restored pricing power.
- Portfolio sharpening toward aerospace/refinish lifts through-cycle margin quality.
- Aerospace cycle and Latin American architectural provide structural growth above GDP.
- Dividend-aristocrat record and buybacks support the stock; trades at a discount to [[sherwin-williams]] that could narrow.
- Falling petrochemical input costs would re-expand the price/cost spread.

### Bear case
- A genuine commodity-cycle exposure: a global industrial/auto slowdown hits Industrial Coatings volume hard.
- Margins are a spread trade — input-cost spikes (oil/propylene/TiO2) compress earnings faster than PPG can pass through price.
- Structurally lower-margin and lower-multiple than [[sherwin-williams]]; the discount may be deserved, not an opportunity.
- Large non-USD revenue means FX is a recurring headwind/tailwind outside management control.
- High expectations: the stock has dipped even on earnings beats when guidance disappoints.

## Key Risks

- **Raw-material cost spikes** — oil, propylene, and titanium dioxide (TiO2) compress the price/cost spread.
- **Cyclical demand** — auto OEM build rates and global industrial PMIs drive volume; a slowdown hits the industrial segment.
- **FX translation** — large non-USD revenue exposes earnings to currency swings.
- **Aerospace cycle** — a downturn in commercial-aero build rates would hit the standout franchise.
- **Competitive multiple gap** — persistent discount to [[sherwin-williams]] reflects a structurally tighter rival.

## Valuation & How the Stock Trades

PPG trades as a **mid/late-cycle industrial coatings name** whose margins are a real-time spread between selling prices and petrochemical input costs — the data block below shows the cyclicality (note the FY2020 margin distortion from divested-business accounting). Mechanically:

- **Commodity-spread sensitivity** — the stock reprices on price/cost commentary; falling input costs or successful price pass-through expand margin and the multiple.
- **Cyclical beta** — moves with global industrial and auto-production sentiment; a [[materials|Materials]]-sector and dividend-aristocrat-fund holding (top weight in the Materials ETF, XLB).
- **Multiple discount to peer** — PPG persistently trades below [[sherwin-williams]]; the spread is itself a watched relative-value trade.
- **Dividend** — 50+ year increase record gives an income-oriented base and downside support.
- **Options** — liquid monthlies, modest IV; earnings moves typically 3-7%, occasionally a dip on a beat when guidance underwhelms.
