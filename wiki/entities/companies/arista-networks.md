Arista Networks (NYSE: ANET) is the leading vendor of high-speed data-center Ethernet switches and the EOS network operating system, and one of the purest large-cap plays on [[artificial-intelligence|AI]] data-center networking spend. A constituent of the [[s-and-p-500]], it competes with Cisco, [[nvidia]] (InfiniBand/Spectrum-X) and white-box vendors, with [[microsoft]] and Meta historically its two largest customers — making it a high-beta proxy for hyperscaler capex.

## Business Overview & Segments

Arista sells 100G–800G Ethernet switching and routing platforms for cloud data centers, AI clusters, campus and enterprise networks, all running a single binary image of its EOS software. Gross margins in the low-60s and operating margins above 40% (see Fundamentals below) reflect a software-differentiated hardware model with outsourced manufacturing — Arista designs the silicon-merchant-based switches and the software, and contracts out the building.

| Line / sector | What it is | Role in the story |
|---|---|---|
| **Cloud & AI data center** | High-speed (100G–800G) Ethernet switches/routers for hyperscaler back-end and front-end fabrics | The core — revenue concentrated among cloud titans; the AI back-end fabric is the new growth engine |
| **EOS software** | A single binary network operating system image running across the whole portfolio | The differentiator and source of [[switching-costs]] — one OS, programmable, with rich telemetry |
| **Enterprise & campus** | Switching for enterprise data centers and campus networks | The diversification leg — reduces hyperscaler dependence, slower but stickier |
| **Routing & services** | Routing platforms, CloudVision management, subscription software/support | High-margin attach; grows the recurring-software mix |

Revenue is concentrated among cloud titans — **[[microsoft]] and Meta** have each exceeded 10% of revenue in past years — with growing enterprise and "AI back-end network" contributions. That concentration is the franchise's defining feature: it makes Arista a high-beta proxy for hyperscaler capex but also exposes it to customer-specific demand swings.

## 2025–2026 Developments

- **FY2025 results (reported Feb 2026):** revenue $9.006B; Q4 2025 revenue $2.488B with non-GAAP EPS $0.82 and Arista's first-ever quarter of over $1B net income. The stock nonetheless dropped ~12% post-print on profit-taking after a strong run.
- **Q1 2026 (reported May 5, 2026):** revenue $2.709B, non-GAAP EPS $0.87; Q2 2026 guided to ~$2.8B revenue, 46–47% non-GAAP operating margin.
- **FY2026 guidance:** ~$11.25B revenue (~25% growth) including an AI networking revenue target of $3.25B as Ethernet wins share from InfiniBand in AI back-end fabrics.
- 4-for-1 stock split took effect in December 2024, boosting options accessibility.

## Economic Moat & Competitive Position

Arista's [[economic-moat|moat]] is narrower than a [[synopsys]] or [[nvidia]] but real, and it rests on software plus design-win lock-in rather than raw scale:

- **EOS software** — a single, programmable network operating system with a consistent binary image and deep telemetry. Once hyperscalers and enterprises build automation/tooling around EOS, replacing it imposes meaningful [[switching-costs]] and operational risk.
- **Merchant-silicon agility** — Arista builds on [[broadcom]] merchant switch silicon (Tomahawk/Jericho) and integrates fast, repeatedly beating incumbents to market at each new speed node (now 800G). This "design + software, outsource fab" model keeps it capital-light and quick.
- **Hyperscaler co-design relationships** — being designed into Microsoft's and Meta's fabrics for years creates reference architectures and trust that are hard for challengers to dislodge mid-buildout.
- **Ethernet-vs-InfiniBand structural shift** — the industry's push to standardize AI back-end fabrics on open Ethernet (Ultra Ethernet) plays to Arista's strengths against [[nvidia]]'s proprietary InfiniBand.

The moat is **contested at the edges**: [[nvidia]] bundles Spectrum-X Ethernet with its accelerators; hyperscalers periodically in-house networking or favor white-box switches; and [[cisco]] is pushing hard back into AI data-center networking. Customer concentration is the single biggest structural vulnerability.

## Competitors / Peer Set

| Company | Primary battleground | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| [[cisco]] | Enterprise & data-center networking incumbent | The legacy giant Arista was built to disrupt; far larger and more diversified, fighting to win AI networking share back |
| [[nvidia]] | InfiniBand + Spectrum-X Ethernet | Both a partner-of-the-ecosystem and a rival — Spectrum-X bundled with GPUs is the most direct competitive threat in AI fabrics |
| [[broadcom]] | Merchant switch silicon | Supplier (Tomahawk/Jericho) not competitor — but also enables white-box rivals; a single-point dependency |
| White-box / ODM vendors | Bare-metal switches for hyperscalers | Celestica and ODMs offer cheaper hardware; the threat is hyperscalers buying boxes and running their own software |
| [[astera-labs]] | Connectivity/interconnect silicon | Adjacent AI-infrastructure connectivity name; correlated, not directly competing |

Arista commands premium margins for a hardware vendor because of EOS; the watch items are Spectrum-X bundling and white-box substitution, not price wars with Cisco.

## Growth Drivers & Catalysts

- **AI back-end networking** — the central growth driver: AI clusters need vast high-speed fabrics, and Ethernet is taking share from InfiniBand. Arista's $3.25B FY2026 AI-networking target is the headline number traders watch (see [[ai-data-center-power-demand]] for the broader buildout context).
- **Hyperscaler capex super-cycle** — Arista revenue is effectively a derivative of [[microsoft]]/Meta/cloud capex guidance.
- **800G transition** — each speed-node upgrade resets average selling prices and pulls forward refresh demand.
- **Enterprise/campus diversification** — broadening beyond the two big cloud customers to reduce concentration.
- **Software/subscription mix** — CloudVision and EOS subscriptions grow the recurring, high-margin base.
- **Recurring catalysts** — quarterly earnings (large post-print moves in both directions), hyperscaler capex commentary ([[nvidia]]/[[microsoft]]/Meta), Ethernet-vs-InfiniBand design-win news, and customer-concentration disclosures.

## Bull Case vs Bear Case

### Bull case
- AI back-end Ethernet is a multi-year share-shift away from InfiniBand, and Arista is the purest large-cap beneficiary.
- EOS software differentiation sustains best-in-class margins for a networking hardware vendor.
- Capital-light merchant-silicon model lets Arista hit each new speed node first.
- Enterprise/campus expansion gradually diversifies away from hyperscaler concentration.

### Bear case
- Customer concentration — Microsoft and Meta drive a large share of revenue; a single customer's capex pause hits hard.
- [[nvidia]] Spectrum-X bundling and hyperscaler in-housing/white-box adoption structurally pressure share.
- ~68x P/E leaves no room for a capex digestion phase; the post-print ~12% drop on the FY2025 print showed how unforgiving positioning is.
- Dependence on [[broadcom]] silicon is a single-point supply and roadmap risk.

## Key Risks

- **Customer concentration** — Microsoft and Meta historically each >10% of revenue; demand is lumpy and customer-specific.
- **Competitive / bundling** — [[nvidia]] Spectrum-X sold with GPUs; [[cisco]] pushing back into AI networking; white-box ODM substitution.
- **Supply dependence** — reliance on [[broadcom]] merchant switch silicon for both supply and roadmap timing.
- **Valuation / positioning** — high multiple and high beta to AI-capex headlines; sharp drawdowns on any capex-digestion signal.
- **Cyclicality** — a hyperscaler capex pause (an AI "digestion" phase) would hit revenue and the multiple simultaneously.

## Valuation & How the Stock Trades

Arista trades as a high-beta, high-multiple **AI-capex proxy** — the market pays up for its software-differentiated margins and AI-networking exposure, leaving little cushion for disappointment. Mechanically:

- **High beta to AI-capex headlines** — [[nvidia]], [[microsoft]] and Meta earnings/capex guides move ANET sympathetically; it is a sympathetic-move stock as much as a standalone one.
- **Large post-print moves** — earnings routinely gap several percent (the ~12% drop after a strong FY2025 print is illustrative); guidance on AI-networking revenue and operating margin drives the reaction more than the headline.
- **Options** — liquid weekly chain (deepened by the Dec-2024 4-for-1 split); elevated IV around earnings and hyperscaler capex events.
- **Capital return** — **no dividend**; capital return is via buybacks. Total-return-via-growth, not yield.
- **Correlated complex** — moves with [[broadcom]], [[astera-labs]], [[cisco]] and the broader data-center-networking and [[ai-data-center-power-demand|AI data-center]] basket.
