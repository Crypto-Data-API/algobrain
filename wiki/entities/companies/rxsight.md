RxSight, Inc. (NASDAQ: RXST) is a small-cap ophthalmic medical-device company that sells the only FDA-approved intraocular lens that can be adjusted *after* cataract surgery — the Light Adjustable Lens (LAL/LAL+), customized post-operatively with its Light Delivery Device (LDD). For traders it is a classic single-product med-tech story: high gross margins and a razor/razor-blade model (LDD consoles seed recurring LAL procedure volume), but currently in a growth air-pocket that has made the stock highly event-driven around quarterly results and guidance.

## Business Overview

RxSight's system has two parts:

- **Light Delivery Device (LDD)** — the capital-equipment console sold to ophthalmology practices (the installed base).
- **Light Adjustable Lens (LAL / LAL+)** — the premium intraocular lens implanted during cataract surgery, then fine-tuned with UV light treatments after the eye heals. Each procedure generates recurring lens revenue across the installed base.

The value proposition is better refractive outcomes in premium cataract surgery (a cash-pay segment on top of standard reimbursement), competing against fixed premium IOLs from Alcon, Johnson & Johnson Vision and Bausch + Lomb.

## 2025-2026 Developments

- **FY2025 results**: revenue $134.5M, down ~4% YoY — LAL lens unit sales rose 12% to 109,615, but LDD console sales fell ~48% as US capital-equipment purchasing stalled. Q4 2025 revenue was $32.6M, down 19% YoY.
- **Margins/losses**: FY2025 gross margin improved to 76.6% (from 70.7% in 2024), but opex of $151.2M widened the net loss to ~$38.9M.
- **2026 guidance**: revenue of $120M–$135M (flat to down), issued with Q4 results and reiterated with Q1 2026 results in May 2026 — the guide implies the LDD placement slowdown persists.
- **International expansion**: regulatory approval received in Australia, adding to existing OUS markets and expanding the addressable market.

## Business Model & Products

RxSight runs a razor/razor-blade (installed-base) model: place capital equipment to seed a practice, then earn recurring per-procedure lens revenue across that installed base. The leading indicator of future revenue is therefore *console placements*; the realized revenue lever is *utilization* (LAL implants per console).

| Component | What it is | Revenue type | Role in the model |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Light Delivery Device (LDD)** | Capital console sold to ophthalmology practices | One-time capital sale | "Razor" — seeds the installed base; leading indicator |
| **Light Adjustable Lens (LAL / LAL+)** | Premium IOL implanted in cataract surgery, tuned post-op with UV light | Recurring per-procedure | "Blade" — the recurring high-margin engine |
| **Post-op light treatments** | UV adjustments after the eye heals | Procedure-tied | Differentiator vs fixed IOLs |

The value proposition is superior refractive outcomes in *premium* cataract surgery — a cash-pay upsell on top of standard reimbursement. The structural tension visible in FY2025 (per file): LAL units +12% (utilization healthy) while LDD sales fell ~48% (placements stalled), so the bull case now rests on utilization growth across the existing installed base rather than new console sales.

## Competitive Positioning & Peers

RxSight is the only player with a *post-operatively adjustable* IOL; competitors sell fixed premium IOLs. The moat is the adjustability technology and FDA approval; the vulnerability is being a single-product small-cap against diversified medtech giants.

| Company | Ticker | Premium IOL offering | Contrast with RxSight |
|---|---|---|---|
| **RxSight** | RXST | Light Adjustable Lens (adjustable post-op) | Only adjustable IOL; single-product small-cap |
| [[alcon]] | ALC | Fixed premium IOLs (PanOptix, Vivity) | Diversified eye-care giant; scale & distribution |
| [[bausch-and-lomb]] | BLCO | Fixed premium IOLs + broad eye-care | Diversified incumbent |
| [[staar-surgical]] | STAA | Implantable Collamer Lens (refractive, not cataract) | Adjacent refractive niche, not premium cataract IOL |
| Johnson & Johnson Vision | JNJ | Fixed premium IOLs (Tecnis) | Mega-cap diversified |

Versus [[alcon]] and [[bausch-and-lomb]], RxSight trades scale and distribution reach for a unique, differentiated outcome (adjustability); versus [[staar-surgical]], the overlap is "premium vision correction" but the procedures differ (cataract IOL vs refractive ICL).

## Bull vs Bear Case

**Bull case**
- Only FDA-approved adjustable IOL — a genuine technical and regulatory moat.
- High and improving gross margin (per file: 76.6% FY2025) on the recurring lens "blade."
- Large installed base creates a recurring revenue annuity as utilization grows.
- International expansion (Australia approval per file) extends the TAM.

**Bear case**
- Single-product concentration — one technology, one franchise.
- Console placements stalled (per file: LDD −48% in FY2025), capping the installed-base growth that drives future lens volume.
- 2026 guidance embeds a revenue decline (per file: $120M–$135M) — the model is in a growth air-pocket.
- Capital-equipment cyclicality: practices defer LDD purchases when financing is tight.

## Valuation Framework (commercial medtech)

A single-product commercial medtech, valued on the installed-base annuity rather than current profit (qualitative lenses):

- **Installed base × utilization** — the core driver: cumulative LDD consoles placed × LAL procedures per console determines the recurring revenue trajectory.
- **Gross margin** — the recurring lens line carries a high margin (per file: 76.6%); margin expansion as the mix shifts to lenses is a key lever.
- **TAM** — penetration of the premium cataract-surgery market plus international expansion; see [[total-addressable-market]].
- **Path to profitability** — still loss-making (per file: ~$38.9M net loss FY2025), so [[cash-runway]] and the inflection from console-led to utilization-led revenue matter for the multiple.

## Trading Relevance

- **Not an index heavyweight**: small-cap, NASDAQ-listed; options exist but liquidity is thin — wide spreads, position sizing matters.
- **Catalysts**: quarterly LDD placement and LAL utilization numbers (the leading indicators of the model), guidance revisions, international approvals/launches, competitive premium-IOL launches by Alcon/J&J.
- **Risks**: single-product concentration; capital-equipment cycle sensitivity (practices defer LDD purchases when rates/financing are tight); the 2026 guidance already embeds a revenue decline, so the bull case rests on utilization growth across the installed base.
- **AI/tech angle**: grouped in AI eye-care baskets for its software-driven, light-programmable lens customization — but revenue is med-tech, not AI software.

## Capital Structure & Dilution Risk

RxSight is loss-making (per file: ~$38.9M net loss FY2025) and not yet self-funding, so it depends on its balance sheet and potential capital-markets access to fund opex while the installed base scales. As a small-cap, an equity raise would be relatively more dilutive than for a large peer, and stock-based compensation adds to share-count growth. The key items to monitor are [[cash-runway]] against the ~$151M opex base (per file) and any signal of needing to raise. No dividend.

## Risks

- **Single-product concentration** — the entire thesis rests on one lens/console franchise.
- **Capital-equipment cycle** — LDD placements (and thus future lens volume) are sensitive to practice financing and rates.
- **Guidance-embedded decline** — 2026 guidance (per file) already implies flat-to-down revenue; the bull case needs utilization to offset.
- **Competition** — diversified premium-IOL incumbents ([[alcon]], [[bausch-and-lomb]], J&J Vision) with scale and distribution.
- **Liquidity** — thin stock and options liquidity amplify volatility and slippage.
- **Financing/dilution** — sustained losses imply potential capital-markets dependence.
