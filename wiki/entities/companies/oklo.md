Oklo Inc. (NYSE: OKLO) is a pre-revenue advanced-nuclear (fast-reactor SMR) developer building the **Aurora powerhouse**, a 75 MWe liquid-metal-cooled, metal-fueled fast reactor, with its first deployment under construction at Idaho National Laboratory (INL). It is one of the most heavily traded retail/AI-power-theme speculation vehicles, with a very active options market and extreme volatility.

## Business Overview

Founded in 2013 by Jacob DeWitte (CEO) and Caroline Cochran, Oklo went public in **May 2024 via merger with AltC Acquisition Corp**, Sam Altman's SPAC (Altman chaired the board until stepping down in **April 2025**, partly to clear the way for potential OpenAI-related power deals). The business model is unusual for nuclear: Oklo intends to **own and operate** its plants and sell power under long-term contracts rather than sell reactors. It cites a multi-gigawatt customer pipeline dominated by data-center operators (including a landmark non-binding agreement with Switch for up to 12 GW announced December 2024) plus industrial and defense users. Subsidiary **Atomic Alchemy** (acquired 2024-25) targets radioisotope production as an earlier revenue stream. The company is pre-revenue and funds itself via equity raises; it was well capitalized after large 2025 raises (cash position >$1B as of late 2025, approximate).

## Business Segments & Assets

Oklo's value is almost entirely forward-looking, so the trade hinges on understanding the asset and contracting model rather than current financials. Three pillars define the company:

| Pillar | What it is | Why it matters |
|---|---|---|
| **Aurora powerhouse** | 75 MWe liquid-metal-cooled, metal-fueled fast reactor; first unit (Aurora-INL) under construction at Idaho National Laboratory | The core product; a fast reactor that can run on fresh HALEU or recycled fuel and is designed to scale to larger outputs |
| **Fuel recycling** | Ambition to use recycled nuclear fuel / used-fuel feedstock in its fast-reactor design | A potential structural cost and ESG edge; fast reactors can extract energy from material that conventional light-water reactors treat as waste |
| **Power-purchase model (own-and-operate)** | Oklo builds, owns, and operates plants, selling electricity under long-term PPAs | Converts the business from one-off reactor sales into recurring contracted-power revenue — the basis of the multi-gigawatt pipeline |

- **Aurora-INL** is the lighthouse deployment. Because Oklo's prior combined license application was famously denied by the NRC in 2022, every subsequent licensing milestone is a discrete de-risking event for the equity.
- **The PPA pipeline** is the demand engine: a stated multi-gigawatt backlog skewed toward [[ai-data-center-power-demand|data-center operators]] (e.g., the non-binding Switch agreement for up to 12 GW), plus industrial and defense customers. The quality of the trade rests on how much of this non-binding pipeline converts into firm, financeable contracts.
- **Atomic Alchemy** targets radioisotope production as a potential earlier revenue stream ahead of first power.
- **Fuel (HALEU)** supply is both an enabler and a risk — fast reactors need high-assay low-enriched uranium, whose Western supply chain is still maturing.

## Competitive Positioning & Peers

Advanced nuclear / SMR is a pre-commercial field, so "competition" is really a race to first deployment, licensing, and bankable customers. Oklo's differentiators are its fast-reactor (vs light-water) design, its own-and-operate / sell-power model (vs selling reactors), and its data-center-skewed PPA pipeline.

| Company | Ticker | Technology | Model | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Oklo** | NYSE: OKLO | Liquid-metal fast reactor (75 MWe Aurora) | Own & operate, sell power via PPA | Pre-revenue; AI-power-theme momentum name; recycled-fuel ambition |
| [[nuscale-power\|NuScale Power]] | NYSE: SMR | Light-water SMR (NRC-certified design) | Sell reactor modules | Most NRC-advanced SMR design; different (LWR) technology |
| [[nano-nuclear-energy\|Nano Nuclear Energy]] | NASDAQ: NNE | Microreactor (earlier-stage) | Sell/deploy microreactors | Much smaller, earlier-stage micro-SMR developer |
| Centrus Energy | NYSE-A: LEU | HALEU fuel enrichment | Fuel supplier | Supply-chain enabler for the whole fast-reactor cohort |
| X-energy, TerraPower (private) | — | HTGR / sodium fast reactor | Various | Well-funded private peers (Amazon/Gates-backed respectively) |

Versus [[nuscale-power|NuScale]], Oklo is less licensing-advanced on paper (NuScale's LWR design is certified) but pursues a more capital-light commercial model (selling power rather than reactors) and a fast-reactor technology with recycled-fuel optionality. Versus [[nano-nuclear-energy|Nano Nuclear]], Oklo is larger, better-capitalized, and further along. The whole group trades together as the nuclear/SMR + AI-power complex and shares dependence on HALEU supply and NRC/DOE timelines. See [[nuclear-renaissance-small-cap-long]] for the basket thesis.

## 2025-2026 Developments

- **September 2025:** broke ground on **Aurora-INL**, intended to be among the first liquid-metal fast reactors deployed under the DOE reactor pilot program; the May 2025 executive order targets test-reactor criticality via DOE authorization by ~July 2026.
- **DOE approvals (late 2025):** Preliminary Documented Safety Analysis approved for the **Aurora Fuel Fabrication Facility** at INL as assembly began.
- **NRC progress (2025-2026):** completed the NRC pre-application readiness assessment for Phase 1 of its combined license application (COLA); the **Principal Design Criteria topical report for Aurora-INL was approved in 2026** on an accelerated schedule (less than half the traditional review time). Oklo's original COLA was famously denied in 2022, so each NRC milestone is a key de-risking event.
- Stock behavior: OKLO was a top "AI power trade" momentum name through 2025 with multi-hundred-percent swings; market cap has ranged in the tens of billions despite zero revenue (approximate, as of June 2026).

## Bull vs Bear

**Bull case**
- Pure-play, well-capitalized vehicle for the convergence of two of the biggest themes — the **nuclear renaissance** and **AI data-center power demand**.
- Own-and-operate / sell-power model creates recurring, contracted revenue rather than lumpy reactor sales — a more durable business if it works.
- Multi-gigawatt PPA pipeline skewed to hyperscalers/data centers signals deep latent demand; each firm contract is a major catalyst.
- Accelerating regulatory momentum: NRC Principal Design Criteria approval in 2026 (well under half the traditional review time) and DOE pilot-program support reverse the 2022 license denial narrative.
- Fast-reactor + recycled-fuel design offers a potential long-run cost and waste advantage over light-water SMRs.

**Bear case**
- **Pre-revenue with a multi-billion-dollar valuation** — the gap between a >$10B-scale market cap and zero revenue prices in near-flawless, multi-year execution.
- Nuclear licensing and construction have a long history of slippage and cost overruns; first criticality is years out (~late 2027 target for Aurora-INL) and could move.
- Funded by dilutive equity raises; the path to first power requires continued capital and likely more issuance.
- HALEU fuel supply chain is still immature, a genuine bottleneck for fast reactors.
- Sentiment-driven story stock: extreme volatility, momentum unwinds, and pipeline announcements that are largely non-binding.

## Valuation Framework

Oklo cannot be valued on earnings; it is a **pre-revenue option on commercialized advanced nuclear**. The relevant levers:

- **Cash runway.** With cash >$1B (late 2025, approximate) and no revenue, the burn rate and time-to-first-power determine how many more raises are needed — the central dilution clock.
- **PPA backlog conversion.** Headline pipeline (e.g., the up-to-12 GW non-binding Switch agreement) is the demand signal, but value accrues only as non-binding LOIs convert into firm, financeable, priced PPAs. Track signed-and-binding GW, not announced GW.
- **Licensing de-risking.** Each NRC/DOE milestone (PDC approval, COLA phases, fuel loading, criticality) collapses a piece of the discount; the 2022 denial means milestones are unusually re-rating.
- **Dilution trajectory.** Future share-count growth is part of the cost of getting to first power; model fully-diluted, not just current shares.
- **Optionality premium.** Much of the market cap is the embedded option on multi-gigawatt deployment; size to the probability of execution, not a base-case DCF.

## Capital Structure & Dilution

Oklo is a pre-revenue developer that **funds itself almost entirely through equity raises**, so dilution is structural, not incidental. Large 2025 raises left it well capitalized (cash >$1B as of late 2025, approximate), but reaching first power and scaling the Aurora fleet will require ongoing capital — meaning further issuance is the base case. Traders should model the fully-diluted share count, watch the cadence and pricing of capital raises (each is both a dilution event and a vote of market confidence), and treat the cash-runway-vs-time-to-revenue gap as the dominant balance-sheet risk.

## Risks

- **Licensing slippage** — NRC/DOE timelines can extend; first criticality could move beyond the ~late-2027 target.
- **Fuel (HALEU) supply** — immature Western supply chain for fast-reactor fuel.
- **Financing / dilution** — recurring equity raises to fund a pre-revenue build-out.
- **Valuation gap** — a multi-billion-dollar cap against zero revenue prices in flawless execution.
- **Construction/cost overruns** — endemic to nuclear; first-of-a-kind risk on Aurora-INL.
- **Sentiment reversal** — momentum-driven story stock vulnerable to sharp, news-driven drawdowns.
