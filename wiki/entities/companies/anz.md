Australia and New Zealand Banking Group (ASX: ANZ) is the fourth of Australia's Big Four banks by market capitalisation (~AUD $85 billion+), historically the most internationally focused of the group with significant operations across the Asia-Pacific region. ANZ completed the acquisition of [[suncorp|Suncorp Bank]] in 2024 for ~$4.9 billion — the largest bank acquisition in Australia in decades.

## Overview

ANZ differentiates itself from [[commonwealth-bank|CBA]], [[westpac|Westpac]], and [[nab|NAB]] through its Asia-Pacific strategy and institutional banking strength. While the other Big Four banks are primarily domestic, ANZ has operations in over 30 countries and has historically positioned itself as Australia's "super-regional" bank — the gateway between Australia/New Zealand and Asian capital markets.

ANZ's brokerage offering, ANZ Share Investing, is powered by a partnership with [[cmc-markets|CMC Markets]], providing access to ASX and international share trading. ANZ typically offers the highest [[dividend]] yield of the Big Four, reflecting its lower valuation multiple.

## History

- **1835**: Bank of Australasia founded in London to serve the Australian colonies
- **1951**: ANZ Bank formed through merger of Bank of Australasia and Union Bank of Australia
- **1970**: Merged with English, Scottish and Australian Bank to become Australia & New Zealand Banking Group
- **2000s**: Aggressive Asia expansion under CEO Mike Smith — "super-regional" strategy, acquired stakes in banks across Asia (China, Indonesia, Vietnam, Cambodia)
- **2007**: Acquired E*Trade Australia (online broker) — not related to the US E*Trade
- **2015-2017**: Began retreating from Asia strategy — sold minority stakes in Asian banks, refocused on Australia/NZ
- **2019**: E*Trade Australia rebranded to ANZ Share Investing, moved to CMC Markets platform
- **2022**: Announced acquisition of [[suncorp|Suncorp Bank]] for ~$4.9 billion
- **2024**: Suncorp Bank acquisition completed after ACCC and Treasurer approval — added ~$50 billion in home loans and significant retail presence in Queensland and NSW

## Investment Services

### ANZ Share Investing

ANZ's online brokerage platform, powered by a partnership with [[cmc-markets|CMC Markets]]:

- **ASX shares**: Full access to all ASX-listed equities, [[chess-sponsorship|CHESS-sponsored]]
- **ETFs**: All ASX-listed ETFs
- **International shares**: Access to international markets via CMC Markets partnership
- **Brokerage fees**: $19.95 per trade (or $14.95 for frequent traders making 10+ trades per month)
- **No options trading**: Unlike [[commsec]] and [[nabtrade]], ANZ Share Investing does not offer ASX Exchange Traded Options
- Integrated with ANZ banking — instant fund transfers for ANZ customers

ANZ Share Investing is functional but has lower brand recognition and fewer features than [[commsec]] or [[nabtrade]]. The CMC Markets partnership means the platform is essentially a white-labelled version of CMC's technology.

### ANZ Private

Wealth management for high-net-worth clients with investable assets typically exceeding $1 million:

- **Portfolio management**: Customised investment portfolios managed by ANZ's investment team
- **Estate planning**: Intergenerational wealth transfer structuring
- **Lending**: Specialised lending for investment purposes — margin lending, investment property loans, structured facilities
- **Dedicated relationship manager**: Personalised banking and investment advisory

### ANZ Super Advantage

ANZ's [[superannuation]] product — a MySuper-compliant default super fund. Offers multiple investment options from conservative to high-growth. Available for both employer-sponsored and personal contributions.

### Institutional Banking

ANZ's institutional and corporate banking division is its strongest competitive asset. It provides:

- **Foreign exchange (FX)**: ANZ is a major dealer in AUD, NZD, and Asian currency pairs
- **Fixed income**: Government and corporate bond trading
- **Trade finance**: Facilitating international trade across Asia-Pacific
- **Capital markets**: Debt and equity capital markets advisory

This institutional strength means ANZ benefits when Australia-Asia trade flows grow, though it does not directly translate into retail investor services.

### Dividend History

ANZ typically pays the highest [[dividend]] yield of the Big Four banks — reflecting its lower market valuation rather than higher absolute dividend payments. Dividends are fully [[franking-credits|franked]]. Like all Big Four banks, ANZ reduced dividends during COVID-19 (2020) but has since recovered. Semi-annual payments (interim and final).

## Suncorp Bank Acquisition (2024)

The ~$4.9 billion acquisition of [[suncorp|Suncorp Bank]] was the largest bank acquisition in Australia in decades:

- **Added ~$50 billion in home loans** — significantly strengthening ANZ's retail lending book
- **Expanded Queensland and NSW presence** — Suncorp Bank was the dominant regional bank in Queensland
- **~1.2 million additional customers** — boosted ANZ's retail customer base
- **ACCC approval**: Required undertakings including divestiture of some Suncorp branches
- **Treasurer approval**: Required under the Banking Act for major bank acquisitions
- Post-acquisition, [[suncorp|Suncorp Group]] became a pure insurance company

## For Investors and Traders

ANZ is the highest-yielding Big Four bank and the most internationally exposed.

**Bull case**: Highest [[dividend]] yield of the Big Four (5-5.5%), fully [[franking-credits|franked]] — maximum income for retirees and [[smsf|SMSFs]]. Suncorp acquisition significantly strengthened domestic retail franchise. Asia-Pacific exposure provides diversification. Strong institutional banking franchise. CET1 ratio of ~13% is the highest of the Big Four, providing capital buffer.

**Bear case**: Historically the weakest performer of the Big Four by total shareholder return. Asia strategy was partially unwound — raises questions about strategic clarity. CMC-powered brokerage is less integrated than [[commsec]]. Lower ROE than [[commonwealth-bank|CBA]] and [[nab|NAB]]. Suncorp integration execution risk.

**Income investor perspective**: ANZ's ~5-5.5% fully franked yield is the standout feature. For an investor in the 30% tax bracket, the effective grossed-up yield exceeds 7.8%. For [[smsf|SMSFs]] in pension phase (0% tax), the franking credit refund pushes the effective yield even higher. ANZ is often the largest Big Four holding in income-focused portfolios for this reason.

## Economic Moat & Competitive Position

ANZ shares the wide collective [[economic-moat|economic moat]] of the Australian banking oligopoly, but its distinctive edge is institutional and trans-regional:

- **Institutional & trade-finance franchise** — ANZ's strongest competitive asset is its corporate/institutional bank: FX (a major dealer in AUD/NZD and Asian pairs), trade finance, transaction banking and capital markets across 30+ countries. This "super-regional" platform is hard for domestic-only peers to replicate.
- **NZ leadership** — ANZ Bank New Zealand is the largest bank in NZ, a durable scale franchise.
- **Deposit franchise** — like its peers, ANZ funds its book largely with low-cost deposits, the foundational banking moat, now augmented by the [[suncorp|Suncorp Bank]] deposit base.
- **Regulatory oligopoly** — [[apra|APRA]] capital rules and the "four pillars" policy protect the Big Four collectively; ANZ is one of the four protected incumbents and carries the highest CET1 buffer of the group.

The moat is **wide but ANZ has the weakest domestic retail moat of the majors**: it has historically been the smallest and lowest-ROE Big Four retail player, with a less integrated brokerage ([[cmc-markets|CMC]]-powered) and a part-unwound Asia strategy that raised questions about strategic clarity. The [[suncorp|Suncorp Bank]] acquisition (2024) and the ANZ Plus digital build are the central efforts to strengthen the domestic franchise.

## Growth Drivers & Catalysts

- **Suncorp Bank integration** — extracting cost and revenue synergies and migrating ~1.2M customers (targeted to complete by mid-2027) is the single biggest domestic catalyst.
- **ANZ Plus rollout** — migrating all ~8M retail customers to the modern digital platform (targeted September 2027) to lift efficiency and engagement.
- **Institutional & trade flows** — ANZ benefits when Australia-Asia trade and capital flows grow; markets income adds cyclical upside.
- **Net interest margin (NIM)** — repricing as the [[reserve-bank-of-australia|RBA]] moves the cash rate; the dominant earnings swing factor.
- **Capital management** — the highest CET1 buffer of the majors supports buybacks (though ANZ trimmed a buyback in 2025 to fund Suncorp/technology).
- **Cost reduction** — role reductions (3,500 by Sep-2026) under the ANZ 2030 plan.
- **Recurring events** — full-year results (Nov, fiscal year ends 30 Sep), half-year (May), quarterly updates, dividend declarations and [[reserve-bank-of-australia|RBA]] decisions.

## Trading Relevance

- **Index weight** — a top [[asx-200]] constituent; trades within the "bank basket" alongside [[commonwealth-bank|CBA]], [[westpac]] and [[nab]].
- **Highest-yield major** — tends to lead in income-seeking and value rotations; the standout name for grossed-up franked yield.
- **Macro & FX sensitivity** — more exposed than peers to [[australian-dollar|AUD]]/Asian-currency moves and Asia-Pacific trade conditions via its institutional book.
- **Rate sensitivity** — trades off [[reserve-bank-of-australia|RBA]] expectations and bond yields.
- **Beta** — a moderate, income-style large-cap; institutional/markets income adds some earnings cyclicality.

## Key Risks

- **Australian housing concentration** — a large (and now larger, post-Suncorp) residential-mortgage book exposes ANZ to a house-price downturn or unemployment spike like its peers.
- **Suncorp integration execution** — a ~A$4.9B acquisition with multi-year customer migration carries cost-overrun and disruption risk.
- **Lower domestic ROE / TSR record** — historically the weakest Big Four performer; the franchise must prove the ANZ 2030 turnaround.
- **Net interest margin compression** — broker-driven mortgage competition and deposit competition squeeze NIM.
- **Regulatory / [[apra|APRA]] capital & conduct** — capital-ratio increases, macroprudential rules and ongoing conduct/markets-trading scrutiny constrain returns; ANZ has faced regulatory attention over its markets business.
- **International / FX exposure** — the institutional and Asia-Pacific footprint adds geopolitical, credit and currency risk that domestic-only peers avoid.

## Valuation & How the Stock Trades

ANZ typically trades at the lowest valuation and the highest dividend yield of the Big Four (see the data blocks above for the figures as pulled), reflecting its historically weaker domestic returns and total-shareholder-return record. The bull case is a successful Suncorp integration plus ANZ Plus efficiency gains narrowing the ROE gap to peers and supporting a re-rating. Mechanically:

- **Dividend & franking profile** — semi-annual dividends, usually the highest cash yield of the majors; note the FY25 final dividend was **70% franked** (not fully franked), because a meaningful share of earnings comes from offshore operations — a real distinction for franking-dependent investors. See [[franking-credits]] and [[dividend]].
- **Beta** — a moderate-beta income large-cap, with extra sensitivity to [[australian-dollar|AUD]] and Asia-Pacific conditions.
- **Index weight** — a heavyweight [[asx-200]] constituent and a core income holding.
- **Capital return** — the strongest CET1 position of the majors underpins buybacks, though capital is currently prioritised for Suncorp integration and technology.

## Business Model & Segments

ANZ reports three customer-facing divisions within its banking group:
- Personal - retail banking, home loans, deposits, cards, personal lending and private banking for Australian consumers, supported by digital platforms (ANZ Plus) and a branch network. The division now also includes the former Suncorp Bank business.
- Business & Agri - banking for small and medium enterprises and the agribusiness sector, including lending, deposits and payments.
- Institutional - banking for large corporates, financial institutions and government, including loan syndication, project and export finance, debt structuring, acquisition finance, trade and supply-chain finance, cash management, and markets (FX, rates, credit, commodities, DCM).
ANZ also operates ANZ Bank New Zealand (the largest bank in NZ) and a significant Pacific banking business.

## Recent Developments

For the 2025 full year (to 30 September 2025, reported November 2025) ANZ delivered cash return on tangible equity of 10.5%, declared a final dividend of 83 cents (70% franked) taking the full-year dividend to 166 cents per share, and ended the period with a CET1 ratio of 12.03%. CEO Nuno Matos, who commenced on 12 May 2025 after a career at HSBC, presented his first FY result and articulated the "ANZ 2030" strategy aimed at materially improving Australia Retail and Business & Private Banking while extending leadership in Institutional and New Zealand. In October 2025 ANZ cancelled the remaining A$800 million of a A$2.0 billion share buyback to preserve capital for Suncorp integration and technology investment, and announced plans to reduce 3,500 roles by September 2026 plus 1,000 managed-services contractors. Suncorp Bank customer migration is targeted for completion by June 2027, alongside bringing ANZ Plus to all eight million retail customers by September 2027.
