# Alpha Architect

**Alpha Architect** is a quantitative asset management and financial education firm founded in 2010 by Dr. Wesley Gray, a former U.S. Marine and Drexel University finance professor. The firm bridges the gap between academic finance research and practical investing through transparent, research-driven ETFs and an extensive educational platform. Alpha Architect is headquartered in Broomall, Pennsylvania, and manages several hundred million dollars across its ETF lineup.

## Research Philosophy

Alpha Architect's core philosophy rests on three pillars:

1. **Transparency** — All investment methodologies are published openly in academic papers and books, a sharp contrast to the "black box" approach of most quantitative managers. The firm believes that sustainable alpha comes from behavioral edge (the discipline to stick with a strategy through underperformance), not informational secrecy.

2. **Concentration** — Rather than holding 200-500 stocks like most factor ETFs, Alpha Architect portfolios hold approximately 50 stocks. Academic research suggests that factor premiums are strongest at the extremes of the distribution, so diluting with hundreds of moderate-factor stocks reduces expected returns. This makes Alpha Architect funds higher-tracking-error but potentially higher-alpha.

3. **Evidence-based** — Every strategy decision is backed by peer-reviewed research or the firm's own published papers. Dr. Gray and his co-authors have published extensively on [[value-factors]], [[momentum-screening|momentum]], and the intersection of the two.

## Key Publications

- **"Quantitative Value"** (2012) by Wesley Gray and Tobias Carlisle — Presents a systematic deep-value strategy that screens for cheapness (enterprise value multiples) and quality (forensic accounting screens to avoid value traps). Forms the basis of the QVAL ETF.
- **"Quantitative Momentum"** (2016) by Wesley Gray and Jack Vogel — Introduces the concept of "quality of momentum" — not just which stocks have gone up the most, but which have the smoothest, most consistent momentum paths. Smooth momentum (fewer large gap days) predicts better forward returns than choppy momentum.
- **"DIY Financial Advisor"** (2015) — Argues that simple, systematic rules outperform human judgment in most financial decisions.

## ETF Products

| Ticker | Name | Strategy | Holdings |
|--------|------|----------|----------|
| **QVAL** | Alpha Architect Quantitative Value | Concentrated US deep value + quality screen | ~50 stocks |
| **QMOM** | Alpha Architect Quantitative Momentum | US momentum with quality-of-momentum filter | ~50 stocks |
| **VMOT** | Alpha Architect Value Momentum Trend | Tactical blend of value, momentum, and [[trend-following]] | Multi-asset |
| **IVAL** | Alpha Architect International Quantitative Value | Non-US developed market deep value | ~50 stocks |
| **IMOM** | Alpha Architect International Quantitative Momentum | Non-US developed market momentum | ~50 stocks |
| **BOXX** | Alpha Architect 1-3 Month Box ETF | Equity-options box spreads replicating T-bill returns with tax-deferred character | Options |

**QVAL** screens the US large/mid-cap universe for cheap stocks using enterprise multiples, then applies forensic accounting filters (accruals, earnings manipulation flags) to remove [[value-factors|value traps]]. The result is a 50-stock portfolio rebalanced quarterly.

**QMOM** ranks stocks by 12-month price momentum (excluding the most recent month to avoid short-term reversal), then filters for "quality of momentum" — stocks whose gains came from steady daily returns rather than a few large jumps. This quality filter is Alpha Architect's key innovation in the [[momentum-screening|momentum space]].

**VMOT** is a tactical allocation fund that combines value and momentum signals with a [[trend-following]] overlay that moves to bonds or cash when equity trends break down, aiming to reduce drawdowns during bear markets.

**BOXX** (launched December 2022) became the firm's breakout commercial success: it uses S&P 500 [[options|box spreads]] to deliver T-bill-like returns while structuring gains to be tax-deferred (and potentially long-term) rather than taxed annually as interest. Assets grew from ~$9.4B in January 2026 to approximately **$10.8B as of 31 March 2026** (SEC N-CSRS filing), making it one of the largest options-based cash-management ETFs and dwarfing the firm's factor funds. The IRS/tax treatment of the structure remains the key risk flagged by commentators.

## 2025–2026 Developments

- **BOXX asset surge:** ~$10.8B AUM at 31 Mar 2026, riding demand for tax-aware cash management in a higher-rate environment.
- **ETF Architect white-label growth:** Alpha Architect's sister platform (EA Series Trust / "ETF Architect") operates as a major white-label ETF issuer, hosting dozens of third-party funds — a quietly significant revenue stream beyond the in-house factor lineup.
- The factor ETFs (QVAL, QMOM, IVAL, IMOM) remain niche by comparison — roughly hundreds of millions to ~$1B each depending on flows — with the concentrated-factor thesis intact.

## Educational Platform

Alpha Architect's blog and research library is one of the most comprehensive free resources for [[quantitative-equity|quantitative investing]] education. The site publishes summaries of academic papers, original research, and practical implementation guides. Topics span [[value-factors]], momentum, multi-factor portfolio construction, transaction costs, and behavioral finance. This educational mission distinguishes Alpha Architect from pure asset managers and has built a dedicated following among sophisticated retail investors and financial advisors.
