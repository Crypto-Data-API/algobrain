AvalonBay Communities (NYSE: AVB) is one of the two largest US apartment [[reits|REITs]], owning ~90,000+ apartment homes concentrated in coastal "knowledge economy" metros with a growing Sunbelt expansion footprint. ## Business Overview & Segments

AvalonBay owns, develops and manages upscale apartment communities under the Avalon, AVA, Eaves and Kanso brands. Key features of the model:

- **Established coastal core** — New England, NY/NJ metro, Mid-Atlantic, Seattle and California supply the bulk of NOI; these supply-constrained markets held up better than the oversupplied Sunbelt during the 2024-2025 apartment glut.
- **Expansion markets** — Texas, Florida, Denver, Charlotte and Raleigh, targeted to grow toward ~25% of the portfolio over time.
- **Development engine** — unlike most peers, AVB is a serial developer, self-funding new communities; development yield spread over cap rates is a core value-creation lever (~$2.7 billion of starts across 2024-2025).

## Economic Moat & Competitive Position

AvalonBay's [[economic-moat|moat]] is a **location + development-capability** [[reits|REIT]] moat:

- **Supply-constrained coastal locations** — its core metros (coastal California, the Northeast, Seattle) have high barriers to new construction (zoning, land scarcity, entitlement timelines), so existing assets enjoy durable pricing power and the Sunbelt oversupply largely bypassed them.
- **Development platform** — unlike most apartment REITs that mostly *buy* stabilized assets, AVB *builds* them, capturing the spread between development yield and the cap rate at which finished communities are valued. This in-house capability is hard to replicate and is the clearest source of excess returns.
- **Scale & cost of capital** — investment-grade balance sheet and low leverage (D/E ~0.68x) give cheaper funding than smaller landlords, widening the development spread.
- **Brand laddering** — the Avalon/AVA/Eaves/Kanso brand stack lets AVB serve multiple renter price points within one metro, smoothing occupancy across cycles.

The moat is real but **modest versus a tower or interconnection REIT**: apartments are a competitive, locally fragmented asset class with low [[switching-costs]] (renters move easily), so AVB's edge is location quality and development skill rather than tenant lock-in.

## Growth Drivers & Catalysts

- **Development deliveries** — the self-funded pipeline (11 communities in 2026 lease-up) adds NOI at attractive yields; ~$47M of development NOI expected in 2026, ~$75M more in 2027.
- **Coastal rent growth** — supply-constrained core markets with limited new deliveries support same-store rent and occupancy.
- **Sunbelt normalization** — as the 2024–25 apartment glut is absorbed, expansion-market headwinds fade.
- **Rate relief** — lower discount rates compress cap rates, lifting NAV and the FFO multiple.
- **Capital recycling** — selling lower-growth assets to fund development is accretive when the development spread is wide.
- **Recurring catalysts** — quarterly Core FFO guidance revisions, the 10-year Treasury yield, national rent-growth data (Zillow/Apartment List), supply-delivery data, and coastal-vs-Sunbelt migration/jobs prints.

## 2025-2026 Developments

- **FY2025 results (reported early February 2026)**: Core FFO rose to **$11.24 per share**; record-low resident turnover; the Q4 print modestly missed consensus and the stock dropped on the release.
- **Dividend raised 1.7%** alongside the FY2025 results.
- **Initial 2026 outlook**: slower same-store NOI growth; development earnings contributing ~$0.10 of Core FFO growth ($47 million development NOI expected in 2026, ~$75 million more projected for 2027); transaction activity a ~$0.10 drag. Full-year 2026 EPS guided to **$5.92-$6.42** (revised for disposition mix); February 2026 FFO outlook affirmed at Q1.
- **Lease-up pipeline**: 11 communities in 2026 lease-up, concentrated in New York, New Jersey and South Florida.

## Bull Case vs Bear Case

### Bull case
- Coastal, supply-constrained portfolio with durable pricing power and record-low turnover.
- In-house development engine earns a yield spread over cap rates that pure buyers cannot match.
- Low leverage (D/E ~0.68x, interest coverage ~8.7x) and investment-grade access give cheap development funding.
- Rate relief would compress cap rates and re-rate the FFO multiple directly.
- Sunbelt supply glut largely bypassed the coastal core, so 2024–25 was a relative-strength period.

### Bear case
- Rate-sensitive long-duration income vehicle — higher-for-longer pressures both NAV and the multiple.
- Same-store NOI growth is decelerating; the 2026 outlook is muted with a transaction drag.
- Coastal markets carry regulatory risk (rent control, eviction rules) that can cap pricing power.
- Development is capital-intensive and exposed to construction-cost and lease-up timing risk.
- Apartments are a low-switching-cost, competitive asset class — little tenant lock-in versus other REIT subsectors.

## Key Risks

- **Rate cycle** — as a long-duration income REIT, valuation moves inversely with the 10-year Treasury; higher-for-longer is the central risk.
- **Supply** — new apartment deliveries in expansion (and some core) markets pressure rent growth and occupancy.
- **Regulatory** — coastal-market rent control, eviction moratoria and zoning changes can cap pricing power.
- **Development execution** — construction-cost inflation, entitlement delays and lease-up timing can erode the development spread.
- **Economic sensitivity** — job losses in coastal "knowledge economy" metros directly hit demand for premium apartments.

## Valuation & How the Stock Trades

As an apartment [[reits|REIT]], AVB is valued on **Core FFO/AFFO per share** and an implied **cap rate / NAV** basis, not GAAP EPS — the API margin percentages in the fundamentals below are revenue-scaling artifacts and should be ignored. Mechanically:

- **Price/FFO multiple** — the market pays a multiple of Core FFO (~$11.24/share in FY2025) keyed to expected NOI and development growth.
- **Cap rate & NAV** — the portfolio's value is the capitalized NOI; the development pipeline creates value by delivering assets at a yield above the prevailing cap rate, lifting net asset value.
- **Rate sensitivity / duration** — a long-duration income vehicle that trades inversely to the 10-year Treasury; rates are the dominant valuation driver, more than idiosyncratic news.
- **Dividend** — a steadily growing dividend (raised 1.7% with FY2025 results) covered on an FFO basis; the dividend plus development-driven FFO growth is the total-return story.
- **Beta** — relatively low equity beta but high rate sensitivity; behaves like a yield instrument with a development-growth kicker.
