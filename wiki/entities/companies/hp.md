HP Inc. (NYSE: HPQ) is one of the world's largest makers of personal computers and printers, formed in 2015 when the old Hewlett-Packard split into HP Inc. (PCs/printing) and Hewlett Packard Enterprise. For traders it is a low-multiple, high-yield [[technology|Information Technology]] hardware name in the [[s-and-p-500]] whose stock trades on the PC replacement cycle, printing-supplies attach rates, and aggressive capital returns. **Note:** HP Inc. (HPQ — PCs and printing) is a separate public company from [[hewlett-packard-enterprise|Hewlett Packard Enterprise]] (HPE — servers, networking, hybrid-cloud); the two were split in 2015 and trade independently.

## Business Overview & Segments

HP operates two reporting segments. The economic heart of the company is not the PC but the **printing-supplies annuity** — the high-margin ink/toner stream that funds the dividend and buybacks (a classic "razor-and-blades" model where hardware is the razor and supplies are the recurring blades).

| Segment | What it is | Revenue mix (qualitative) | Economics |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Personal Systems** | Consumer and commercial notebooks, desktops, workstations | ~68% of revenue | Lower-margin, volume/cycle-driven; the AI-PC refresh and Windows 10 end-of-support are the 2025-26 catalysts |
| **Printing — hardware** | Printers (consumer, office, graphics/industrial) | Part of ~32% | Low-margin placement of the installed base |
| **Printing — supplies (ink/toner)** | High-margin consumables tied to the installed base | The profit core within the ~32% | A cash-generative annuity in secular decline; supplies margins fund capital return |

Years of buybacks have driven book equity **negative** — which is why the ROE and debt-to-equity figures in the Fundamentals section below look extreme. Read leverage via interest coverage (~7.4x) instead.

## Economic Moat & Competitive Position

HP's [[economic-moat|economic moat]] is **narrow and concentrated in printing supplies**, with Personal Systems essentially moat-light:

- **Printing-supplies annuity** — the installed base of HP printers generates recurring, high-margin ink/toner demand. Original-supplies attach (and the friction of using third-party/refill cartridges) is the durable, cash-generative edge — though it is under secular and competitive pressure.
- **Brand & enterprise distribution** — a globally recognized brand and deep commercial/enterprise channel relationships create modest [[switching-costs|switching costs]] in commercial PCs and managed print services.
- **Scale procurement** — volume scale in components (a swing factor when memory/commodity costs spike).
- **Personal Systems is commoditized** — PCs are a low-margin, share-and-cycle business with thin differentiation versus [[dell-technologies]], Lenovo and [[apple]]; little durable moat there.

The net is a narrow moat: defensible cash flows from printing supplies, but a structurally declining annuity and a commoditized PC business — which is precisely why the stock trades at a low single-to-high-single-digit multiple and is valued as a yield/return-of-capital story.

## Growth Drivers & Catalysts

- **AI-PC refresh cycle** — AI-capable PCs as the key volume/mix driver for Personal Systems through 2026.
- **Windows 10 end-of-support** — the upgrade wave drives a commercial PC replacement cycle.
- **Restructuring / cost savings** — the Nov 2025 plan targets ~$1B gross run-rate savings by FY2028 (cutting ~4,000-6,000 roles), supporting margins and EPS.
- **Supply-chain shift** — moving North America-bound production out of China to mitigate tariff exposure.
- **Capital return** — the ~4%+ dividend plus buybacks (the source of negative book equity) is the core return story; sustaining free cash flow funds both.
- **Recurring catalysts** — quarterly earnings (Feb/May/Aug/Nov, FY ends October), PC shipment data (IDC/Gartner), memory-price moves, tariff headlines, restructuring progress.

## Bull Case vs Bear Case

### Bull case
- Cheap, high-yield value/income name: ~9x trailing earnings and a ~4%+ dividend backed by real free cash flow.
- The printing-supplies annuity remains a durable, cash-generative profit core funding capital return.
- AI-PC refresh and the Windows 10 upgrade wave provide a multi-year Personal Systems volume tailwind.
- The Nov 2025 restructuring (~$1B savings target) adds margin/EPS leverage.
- Aggressive buybacks shrink the share count and compound per-share value at a low multiple.

### Bear case
- **Secular decline in printing** — the supplies annuity that funds the dividend is in structural decline; third-party supplies and the paperless trend erode the profit core.
- Personal Systems is commoditized and low-margin, with limited pricing power versus [[dell-technologies]], Lenovo and [[apple]].
- **Cost/tariff exposure** — memory and commodity inflation and US tariff policy compress thin hardware margins (FY2025 showed revenue growth without margin leverage).
- A negative-book-equity, buyback-levered balance sheet leaves little cushion.
- The low multiple reflects a value trap risk if free cash flow erodes faster than buybacks can offset.

## Key Risks

- **Secular printing decline** — erosion of the high-margin supplies annuity that funds the dividend and buybacks.
- **Margin pressure** — memory/commodity cost spikes and tariffs squeeze thin hardware margins.
- **PC commoditization** — limited pricing power and share competition in Personal Systems.
- **Balance sheet** — negative book equity from buybacks; read leverage via interest coverage (~7.4x), not D/E.
- **Macro/cycle** — PC demand is cyclical; a refresh air pocket after the Windows 10 wave would pressure volumes.

## Valuation & How the Stock Trades

HPQ is a **low-multiple, high-yield value/income hardware name** (~9x trailing earnings, ~4%+ dividend per the fundamentals below); the central debate is whether free cash flow and the supplies annuity hold up long enough to keep funding the dividend and buybacks. Mechanically:

- **Yield + buyback story** — the ~4%+ dividend and aggressive repurchases (the source of negative book equity) are the core return drivers; this is an income/return-of-capital name, not a growth stock.
- **Income/covered-call flow** — the high yield attracts active covered-call and income flow in the options chain.
- **Low beta, value-tilted positioning** — trades as a value/income hardware name; less volatile than high-beta tech, with sentiment driven by PC data, memory prices and tariffs.
- **13F sentiment factor** — Berkshire Hathaway's 2022-2024 stake build and exit made 13F filings a sentiment input; positioning remains value/income-driven.
- **Catalysts** — quarterly earnings (FY ends October), PC shipment data, memory-price moves, tariff headlines, restructuring progress.

## 2025-2026 Developments

- **FY2025 results (year ended 31 Oct 2025):** net revenue US$55.3B, up 3.2% YoY; GAAP diluted EPS fell to $2.65 (from $2.81) and non-GAAP EPS to $3.12 (from $3.43) — revenue growth without margin leverage, pressured by tariffs and commodity (memory) costs.
- **November 2025 AI restructuring:** board approved a plan to cut approximately 4,000-6,000 roles by end of FY2028, citing AI adoption, targeting ~$1B gross run-rate savings by FY2028 with ~$650M total restructuring charges (~$250M in FY2026).
- **AI PC cycle:** HP has leaned into AI-capable PCs as the key volume/mix driver for Personal Systems through 2026.
- **Tariff exposure:** as a US-centric hardware vendor with Asian supply chains, HPQ remained sensitive to 2025 US tariff policy headlines; the company has been shifting North America-bound production out of China.
