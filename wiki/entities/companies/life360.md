**Life360, Inc.** (ASX: 360 / NASDAQ: LIF) is a U.S.-headquartered family location-sharing and safety [[technology]] company whose mobile platform helps households locate and coordinate with one another, plus track pets and belongings via Tile and Jiobit hardware. It is a rare ASX-listed U.S. consumer-tech *growth* name — a constituent of the [[asx-200]] index — whose value compounds on [[network-effects]] within households: the more family members join a "Circle," the stickier the product. The company is dual-listed, trading on the [[asx-limited|Australian Securities Exchange]] (ticker 360) since 2019 and on the Nasdaq Global Select Market (ticker LIF) since 2024, and it crossed into GAAP profitability for the first time in 2025.

## Business Model & Segments

Life360's core product is the Life360 mobile app, which operates on a freemium model. Free users get basic location sharing, while paid Gold and Platinum membership tiers add features such as crash detection, roadside assistance, driver behaviour reports, identity-theft protection, stolen-funds reimbursement, credit monitoring and SOS with emergency dispatch. The company also sells Tile Bluetooth tracking devices through retailers and online channels, plus Jiobit wearable GPS devices aimed at children, pets and seniors. Revenue is diversified across three streams: subscriptions (app memberships and Tile Premium/Premium Protect), hardware sales, and "other" revenue including partnership, data and advertising revenue. The platform is global, with North America its largest market and growing penetration in Europe, the Middle East, Africa and Asia-Pacific.

## Economic Moat & Competitive Position

Life360's [[economic-moat]] is narrower than a megacap's but real, and it rests on several reinforcing layers:

- **[[network-effects]] within the household** — the app is only useful if family members are *on it together*. Each added member to a "Circle" raises the product's value to every other member, so adoption tends to spread household-by-household and lock in. This is the core flywheel.
- **High [[switching-costs]]** — migrating off Life360 requires the *whole family* to agree and re-onboard onto another tool simultaneously. Individual inertia compounds into family-level inertia, which is far stickier than a single-user SaaS churn decision.
- **Brand as category default** — "Life360" has become shorthand for family location-sharing, particularly among parents of teens in North America. Mindshare drives organic, low-CAC acquisition.
- **Installed-base scale** — ~95.8 million global monthly active users (Q4 2025) is a large, hard-to-replicate distribution asset and the substrate for the emerging data/advertising business.
- **Ecosystem breadth** — Tile Bluetooth trackers, Jiobit wearable GPS and the newer Pet GPS device extend the platform from "where are my people" to "where are my things and pets," widening the surface area and the reasons to stay.
- **Data/advertising flywheel (emerging)** — a large, engaged, location-aware user base underpins the Place Ads advertising platform (Fantix + Nativo acquisitions), a potential second high-margin revenue engine layered on the existing base.

The moat's central vulnerability is that **free, OS-bundled location sharing from [[apple]] (Find My) and [[alphabet]] (Google Family Link / Find My Device) is "good enough" for many users** — the platform owners can give away the base feature at zero marginal cost. Life360's defense is depth (crash detection, driver reports, SOS dispatch, identity protection) and the family-network lock-in, but the commoditisation of basic location sharing is the structural bear vector.

## Growth Drivers & Catalysts

- **MAU growth** — ~20% monthly-active-user growth guided for 2026; the top of the funnel that feeds every other revenue stream.
- **Free-to-paid conversion** — upsell of free users into Gold and Platinum membership tiers (crash detection, roadside assistance, identity-theft protection, SOS dispatch) is the core subscription-economics lever.
- **Advertising platform build-out** — Place Ads, built on the Fantix and Nativo acquisitions, is the optionality the growth multiple partly prices; turning the large MAU base into a high-margin ad engine.
- **International expansion** — North America is the largest market; EMEA and Asia-Pacific are the penetration runway.
- **Hardware attach** — Tile, Jiobit and the new Pet GPS tracker broaden the ecosystem and add a hardware/subscription cross-sell.
- **Recurring catalysts** — quarterly results on a U.S.-style calendar cadence, MAU/net-adds disclosures, advertising-platform milestones, 2026 guidance updates (US$640–680M revenue / US$128–138M adjusted EBITDA), and index rebalances in *both* the ASX and Nasdaq markets.

## History

Life360 was founded in 2007-2008 by Chris Hulls and Alex Haro in San Francisco, partly inspired by gaps in family communication exposed by Hurricane Katrina. The company won Google's 2008 Android Developer Challenge with a $275,000 prize and launched its Android app in 2009. It raised successive rounds of venture capital (Series A, B and C) and integrated with BMW ConnectedDrive in 2013. In May 2019 Life360 listed on the ASX under ticker 360, raising roughly A$145.4 million. In November 2021 it agreed to acquire Bluetooth-tracker maker Tile for about US$205 million, completing the deal in early 2022 and adding the Jiobit business it had bought earlier that year. Life360 completed a U.S. IPO on Nasdaq in June 2024 at US$10 per share.

## Recent Developments

For the full year 2025 Life360 reported revenue of US$489.5 million, up 32% year-on-year, and its first annual GAAP net income of US$150.8 million (US$32.5 million excluding a US$118.4 million one-time non-cash tax benefit). Adjusted EBITDA more than doubled to US$93.2 million. Global monthly active users reached 95.8 million in Q4 2025, with 16.2 million net adds for the year. During 2025 the company launched a Pet GPS tracker and acquired Fantix, a location-based advertising business, to support its Place Ads product. In January 2026 Life360 completed the acquisition of Nativo to build a full-stack advertising platform. 2026 guidance calls for roughly 20% MAU growth, revenue of US$640-680 million and adjusted EBITDA of US$128-138 million.

## Bull Case vs Bear Case

### Bull case
- The household [[network-effects]] flywheel and family-level [[switching-costs]] make the ~95.8M-MAU base durable and growing (~20% MAU growth guided for 2026).
- 2025 marked the first GAAP net income and a doubling of adjusted EBITDA (to US$93.2M) — the model has inflected from cash-burning growth to self-funding profitable growth.
- The advertising platform (Place Ads via Fantix + Nativo) is a credible high-margin second revenue engine layered on an existing, engaged base — optionality not fully proven in the numbers.
- Diversified revenue (subscriptions + hardware + advertising) and international runway reduce single-stream dependence.
- Category-default brand drives low-cost organic acquisition.

### Bear case
- **Free OS-bundled competition** from [[apple]] (Find My) and [[alphabet]] (Google) can satisfy the base location-sharing use case at zero marginal cost — the structural threat to the core funnel.
- Trades at rich growth multiples (P/S ~9.5x, P/E ~38x per the data above), so any guidance miss or MAU-growth deceleration can de-rate it hard.
- The advertising platform is early and unproven at scale; if it underdelivers, the bull thesis loses a leg.
- Location data invites privacy and regulatory scrutiny that could constrain the data/ad business.
- Subscription conversion and churn dynamics are sensitive to consumer discretionary spend and to feature parity with free alternatives.

## Key Risks

- **Free OS-bundled competition** — [[apple]] Find My and [[alphabet]]'s Google location/parental-control tools are free and pre-installed; commoditisation of basic location sharing is the central long-term risk.
- **Valuation / multiple de-rating** — a high-growth multiple means the stock is highly sensitive to guidance revisions and risk-off rotations.
- **Subscription conversion & churn** — the economics depend on converting free MAUs to paid Gold/Platinum tiers and retaining them.
- **Location-data privacy & regulation** — handling household location data is a privacy and regulatory exposure, especially as the advertising business scales.
- **Advertising-platform execution** — Place Ads (Fantix/Nativo) is early; integration and monetisation are unproven at scale.
- **Key-person** — founder/Chair Chris Hulls is central to the company's identity and strategy.
- **Dual-listing mechanics** — ASX CDIs vs Nasdaq common stock introduce FX (AUD reporting noise) and cross-market price-discovery dynamics; US hours now carry most price discovery, with the ASX line gapping to match.
