**Woodside Energy Group Ltd** (ASX: WDS, NYSE: WDS, LSE: WDS) is an oil and gas exploration and production company listed on the [[asx-limited|Australian Securities Exchange]] and a constituent of the [[asx-200]] index. It is Australia's largest independent dedicated oil and gas company and one of the world's major listed LNG producers, making it for traders a large-cap, high-yield proxy on LNG and [[oil|oil]] prices with major growth projects (Scarborough, Louisiana LNG) in execution. Headquartered at Mia Yellagonga in central Perth, Western Australia, Woodside engages in exploration, development, production, marketing and sale of LNG, pipeline gas, crude oil, condensate and natural gas liquids across assets in Australia, Senegal, Trinidad and Tobago, the Gulf of Mexico, and the United States onshore (Louisiana LNG).

## Business Overview & Segments/Assets

Woodside is an upstream-to-marketing [[energy]] producer rather than a refiner/marketer: it finds, develops and sells molecules — chiefly [[lng]], pipeline gas, crude [[oil]] and condensate — into global energy markets. The portfolio is built around a handful of large, long-life, operated [[lng]] hubs in Western Australia, a spread of producing oil assets (domestic FPSOs plus the US Gulf of Mexico and Senegal), and a multi-year growth pipeline (Scarborough/Pluto Train 2, Louisiana LNG, Trion, Browse). It is fundamentally a price-taker: revenue tracks Brent and Asian [[lng]] (JKM) prices, while value is created by asset quality, scale and execution rather than by setting price.

## Economic Moat & Competitive Position

Woodside has **no pricing power** — like every commodity producer it is a price-taker that sells [[lng]] and [[oil]] into global markets at prices set by supply, demand and the marginal barrel/cargo. Its [[economic-moat]] is therefore not a brand or switching-cost story; it is **asset quality and position on the cost curve**:

- **Low-cost, long-life [[lng]] assets** — large, operated WA [[lng]] hubs (NWS, Pluto) with decades of reserve life are expensive and slow to replicate; a favourable cost-curve position lets Woodside stay cash-generative deeper into price downturns than higher-cost producers.
- **Scale & integration** — one of the world's larger listed [[lng]] producers post the BHP Petroleum merger, with the scale to fund multi-billion-dollar projects (Scarborough, Louisiana LNG) and absorb cyclical capex that smaller peers cannot.
- **Reserve life & resource depth** — a development pipeline (Scarborough, Browse, Trion, Louisiana LNG) provides the reserve replacement that ultimately governs through-cycle survival for an extractive business.
- **Marketing & offtake** — a global [[lng]] marketing book with long-term contracts (often oil-linked) plus spot optionality smooths some price volatility and provides demand visibility versus a pure spot seller.
- **Infrastructure control** — ownership/operatorship of [[lng]] trains, pipelines and FPSOs creates a tollkeeper-like position over third-party and equity gas.

The honest framing: the moat is a **cost and scale advantage, not a margin shield**. When Brent and JKM fall, Woodside's margins fall with them (see the FY2022→FY2025 revenue and margin decline in the data below); the edge is that quality assets earn through the cycle while marginal producers do not.

## Business Model & Segments

Woodside's portfolio spans operated and non-operated producing assets plus development projects:

- **Australia (Operated)**:
  - **North West Shelf (NWS)** — Australia's foundational LNG project, with offshore gas fields supplying the Karratha Gas Plant (KGP).
  - **Pluto LNG** — Woodside-operated single-train LNG plant (plus Pluto Train 2 under construction for Scarborough).
  - **Ngujima-Yin, Okha, Pyrenees** FPSOs — offshore oil and condensate production.
  - **Macedon** — domestic gas project.
- **Australia (Non-operated)**:
  - **Wheatstone / Julimar-Brunello** (operated by Chevron), **Bass Strait** (Exxon-operated; acquired via BHP Petroleum).
- **International**:
  - **Sangomar** (Senegal) — offshore oil project, first oil 2024.
  - **Shenzi, Mad Dog, Atlantis** (US Gulf of Mexico) — producing oil assets.
  - **Greater Angostura** (Trinidad and Tobago) — gas production.
  - **Trion** (Mexico) — oil development project with Pemex.
  - **Calypso** (Trinidad), **Liard / Ruby** (Canada), **Sunrise / Troubadour** (Timor Sea).
- **Development projects**:
  - **Scarborough / Pluto Train 2** — major LNG growth project off WA, targeting first LNG in 2H 2026.
  - **Browse** — WA gas project under regulatory review.
  - **Louisiana LNG (ex-Driftwood)** — US Gulf Coast export project acquired via Tellurian.
- **Low-carbon**: Solar, hydrogen, ammonia and CCS initiatives under development.

## History

- **26 July 1954**: Incorporated as Woodside (Lakes Entrance) Oil Co NL, named for the small town of Woodside in Victoria. Floated by accountant Rees Withers.
- **1962**: Recruited geologist Nicholas Boutakoff, who directed the company to Western Australia. Paid £100 for a permit to explore 370,000 km² off the WA coast.
- **1960s–70s**: Formed the original North West Shelf Venture with Burmah Oil, Shell Development Australia and others.
- **1989**: First LNG cargo exported from the North West Shelf to Tokyo — Australia's entry into global LNG markets.
- **2012**: First LNG from Pluto, Woodside's first operated LNG train.
- **2014**: First gas from the BHP-operated Macedon project.
- **2016**: Acquired ConocoPhillips' stake in Senegal's Sangomar project.
- **June 2022**: Completed merger with BHP Petroleum (announced August 2021), adding Bass Strait, US Gulf of Mexico, Trinidad and other assets. Renamed Woodside Energy Group Ltd; added NYSE and LSE listings.
- **2024**: Acquired Tellurian for approximately US$1.02 billion, gaining the Driftwood / Louisiana LNG project on the US Gulf Coast. First oil from Sangomar.
- **2025**: Final investment decision on Louisiana LNG targeting start-up in 2029.

## Recent Developments

- **Scarborough**: Over 91% complete at end-2025, targeting first LNG cargo in 2H 2026.
- **Louisiana LNG**: Final investment decision in 2025 on the approximately US$17.5 billion project; start-up targeted 2029.
- **Sangomar (Senegal)**: Ramp-up continued through 2025 following first oil in 2024.
- **Financial performance**: Strong free cash flow generation supported by production growth (Sangomar, Scarborough approaching) and disciplined capital management.
- **Leadership transition (December 2025)**: Meg O'Neill — CEO since 2021 — announced her appointment as CEO of BP, effective 1 April 2026. Liz Westcott (previously EVP and COO Australia) appointed Acting CEO from 18 December 2025 while the board conducted a permanent succession process.
- **Strategic focus**: Delivering Scarborough to start-up, executing Louisiana LNG construction, managing portfolio through a potentially softer LNG pricing cycle.

## Bull Case vs Bear Case

### Bull case
- Scarborough first LNG (2H 2026) lifts production and free cash flow off a low FY2025 base (note negative FCF in FY2025 during peak capex).
- Long-life, low-cost operated [[lng]] hubs earn through the cycle; a favourable cost-curve position protects cash flow in downturns.
- Structural global [[lng]] demand growth plus a deep, funded growth pipeline (Scarborough, Louisiana LNG, Browse) underpins reserve replacement and long-dated volumes.
- High franked dividend yield in up-cycles makes it a core income holding for Australian investors; [[asx-200]] heavyweight with global ([[oil]]/[[lng]]) exposure.
- Scale and balance-sheet capacity (modest D/E) to fund multi-billion-dollar projects competitors cannot.

### Bear case
- Pure price-taker: earnings and the dividend swing with Brent and JKM — the FY2022→FY2025 revenue, margin and net-income decline shows how fast the cycle cuts.
- Heavy concurrent capex (Scarborough then Louisiana LNG) pressures free cash flow (FY2025 FCF was negative) and can squeeze the dividend if prices stay soft.
- Australian regulatory/approval risk (Browse, heritage and environmental processes) can stall growth and reserve backfill.
- Leadership transition risk after Meg O'Neill's departure to BP; execution on Scarborough/Louisiana LNG is management-intensive.
- Long-term energy-transition demand risk and rising decarbonisation/ESG capex and financing pressure on [[oil]] and gas producers.

## Key Risks

- **Commodity-price cycle** — the dominant risk; Brent and Asian [[lng]] (JKM) prices set revenue, and a soft pricing cycle compresses margins, FCF and the dividend simultaneously.
- **Capex / FCF strain** — large concurrent projects (Scarborough, Louisiana LNG ~US$17.5bn) pressure free cash flow; note FY2025 FCF was negative.
- **Execution & project risk** — cost/schedule overruns on Scarborough first LNG and Louisiana LNG construction.
- **Regulatory & approval risk** — Australian environmental/heritage approvals (Browse) and broader permitting can delay or block growth and reserve replacement.
- **Reserve replacement** — as an extractive business, failure to backfill long-life [[lng]] (e.g. NWS) erodes through-cycle value.
- **Leadership transition** — permanent CEO succession after Meg O'Neill's move to BP.
- **Energy-transition / ESG** — long-run demand uncertainty for [[oil]] and gas, plus rising decarbonisation capex and financing/insurance constraints.
- **FX translation** — AUD-reporting producer with USD-priced commodities; AUD/USD moves affect reported earnings and dividends.

## Valuation & How the Stock Trades

Woodside trades as a **commodity beta** name, not a multiple-expansion compounder: the share price and earnings are a geared function of Brent and Asian [[lng]] (JKM) prices, so the stock behaves cyclically and the trailing P/E and margins are best read through-cycle rather than at a point in time. Mechanically:

- **Commodity beta** — the stock is a high-yield proxy on [[lng]] and [[oil]]; it rises and falls with Brent/JKM and tends to lead on energy-price moves.
- **Dividend** — a high franked dividend yield in up-cycles is central to the total-return story for Australian investors (the Fundamentals data flags a high payout ratio); the dividend is a function of cash flow and therefore commodity-price-sensitive and not guaranteed through a downturn.
- **Capex cycle** — the stock's free cash flow (and dividend cover) swings with the project capex cycle; FY2025 FCF was negative during peak Scarborough/Louisiana LNG spend, with the expectation that Scarborough first LNG reverses that.
- **Through-cycle read** — Fred's framework classifies Woodside as *Cyclical / commodity*; size and read it around the cycle (low-cost asset quality, reserve life, cost-curve position) rather than by static metrics. See the data blocks below for the figures as pulled.
- **Index & liquidity** — a top-weighted [[asx-200]] constituent (with NYSE/LSE listings), large-cap and relatively liquid, and one of the most heavily traded ASX energy options names.
