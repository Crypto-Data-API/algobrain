Gilead Sciences (NASDAQ: GILD) is a large-cap biopharmaceutical company built on a dominant HIV franchise, with growing oncology and liver-disease businesses. It is a [[s-and-p-500]] constituent and a core defensive healthcare holding — relatively low beta, a meaningful dividend, and catalysts centred on drug launches, label expansions and patent dynamics.

> Note: GILD trades on the NASDAQ; the frontmatter "exchange" field reads NYSE but the listing is NASDAQ: GILD.

> Clinical/pharma disclaimer: Drug-development outcomes are inherently uncertain. Trial readouts, FDA/EMA decisions, label expansions, and patent-litigation results can move the stock sharply and are not predictable from the wiki. Treat all pipeline and launch-trajectory commentary below as qualitative context, not investment guidance.

## Business Overview & Segments

HIV is Gilead's engine: Biktarvy (the leading HIV treatment) plus the Descovy/Truvada PrEP franchise and the broader portfolio generated roughly $20.8 billion of HIV sales in 2025. The company also runs a hepatitis (HCV/HBV) franchise, a liver-disease push (Livdelzi), and an expanding **oncology** business via Trodelvy (sacituzumab govitecan, breast/urothelial cancer) and the cell-therapy unit Kite (Yescarta, Tecartus). Veklury (remdesivir) revenue has normalised lower post-pandemic. Gilead's edge is HIV franchise durability layered over a deep virology R&D base; the long-term debate is whether oncology and next-generation HIV modalities (notably the long-acting lenacapavir platform) can offset eventual Biktarvy patent erosion.

## Economic Moat & Competitive Position

Gilead's [[economic-moat|economic moat]] is rooted in **intangible assets** — patents, regulatory approvals, and two decades of accumulated virology know-how — rather than scale or network effects. The reinforcing layers:

- **Patent-protected HIV franchise** — Biktarvy is the single most prescribed HIV regimen, giving Gilead pricing power and a durable cash engine through the life of the patents.
- **Lenacapavir platform** — the long-acting capsid inhibitor (Yeztugo for twice-yearly PrEP) is a genuinely differentiated modality that could extend the franchise's exclusivity runway and reset the competitive frontier in prevention.
- **Regulatory and manufacturing barriers** — biologics and cell therapies (Kite) carry high approval, manufacturing, and delivery complexity that deters easy entry.
- **Switching costs in treatment** — HIV is chronic; physicians and patients are reluctant to switch a working, well-tolerated regimen, which entrenches the installed base.

The moat is **real but time-bounded**: the dominant risk is the eventual Biktarvy loss-of-exclusivity (patent cliff). The bull case for the moat is that lenacapavir-based long-acting regimens, plus a diversifying oncology base, refill the cliff before it bites.

## Growth Drivers & Catalysts

- **Yeztugo (lenacapavir) ramp** — FDA-approved June 2025 as the world's first twice-yearly HIV prevention (PrEP) therapy; EU authorisation August 2025. It contributed ~$150M in 2025 ($96M in Q4) and reached ~90% U.S. payer coverage by year-end, with management guiding to ~$800M of sales in 2026 — the single most important new growth driver.
- **HIV franchise durability** — Biktarvy grew 7% to $14.3B in 2025; total HIV sales of $20.8B (+6%); management guided to ~6% HIV growth in 2026.
- **Oncology diversification** — Trodelvy label expansions and Kite cell-therapy demand; potential new cancer therapy launches flagged for 2026.
- **Livdelzi (liver disease) ramp** — a steady newer contributor.
- **Recurring catalysts** — quarterly earnings and HIV-franchise sales prints, Yeztugo launch trajectory, FDA/EMA decisions, oncology trial readouts (Trodelvy, Kite), and Biktarvy patent-cliff commentary.

## Bull Case vs Bear Case

### Bull case
- HIV is a durable, high-margin cash engine with leadership economics (Biktarvy) and a long runway.
- Lenacapavir/Yeztugo is a differentiated long-acting platform that can extend exclusivity and open the large PrEP-prevention market.
- Oncology (Trodelvy, Kite) and Livdelzi diversify the revenue base away from a single franchise.
- Strong free cash flow funds a meaningful, growing dividend plus tuck-in M&A and buybacks — a defensive [[healthcare]] holding with relatively low beta.
- Normalised earnings power is materially higher than the 2024 one-off-charge year suggests (see valuation note).

### Bear case
- HIV concentration: a large share of profit rides on one franchise facing an eventual Biktarvy patent cliff.
- ViiV/GSK long-acting injectables contest the HIV franchise, and lenacapavir uptake must outrun that competition.
- Oncology execution is unproven at scale; Kite cell therapy is operationally complex and reimbursement-sensitive.
- Drug-pricing policy (IRA Medicare negotiation) and broader pricing pressure cap the franchise's pricing power.
- Pipeline binary risk: trial failures or regulatory setbacks can re-rate the stock quickly.

## Key Risks

- **HIV concentration / patent cliff** — eventual Biktarvy loss-of-exclusivity is the structural risk; the diversification thesis depends on filling that gap in time.
- **Competition** — ViiV/GSK long-acting injectables in HIV; broad oncology competition for Trodelvy and Kite.
- **Pricing / policy** — IRA drug-price negotiation, 340B dynamics, and general pricing pressure on a high-price franchise.
- **Pipeline / clinical** — trial readouts and FDA/EMA decisions are binary and can move the stock sharply (see disclaimer above).
- **Balance sheet** — leverage from acquisitions (D/E above the [[healthcare|Health Care]] Fred bar) leaves less headroom than a net-cash peer.

## Valuation & How the Stock Trades

Gilead trades as a **defensive, income-tilted biopharma** rather than a high-growth biotech: relatively low beta, a meaningful and growing dividend, and a valuation anchored to franchise durability plus pipeline optionality. Mechanically:

- **Headline trailing P/E is distorted** — the multiple shown in the Fundamentals block is inflated by a one-off charge year (2024 net margin compressed by acquired-IPR&D and litigation items). Normalised earnings power is materially higher; read the multi-year trend rows rather than the single 2024 print.
- **Dividend / yield** — a core part of the total-return case; the API "dividend yield" figure uses a non-standard definition (see source page) and is not the market yield.
- **Beta** — below the market; GILD is a defensive [[healthcare]] holding that tends to hold up better in risk-off rotations.
- **Options** — one of the more liquid biotech/pharma options markets, used for earnings plays and yield/covered-call income; implied moves cluster around earnings and major data/regulatory catalysts.
- **Capital return** — dividend plus buybacks and tuck-in M&A, funded by HIV-franchise free cash flow.
