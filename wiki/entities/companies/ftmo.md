FTMO is one of the largest and most established proprietary trading (funded trader) firms in the world, primarily focused on [[forex]] but also supporting indices, [[commodities]], [[crypto]], and stocks. Founded in 2015 in Prague, Czech Republic, FTMO evaluates traders through a two-phase challenge process and provides successful participants with funded accounts of up to $200,000 in simulated capital, with profit splits of up to 90%.

## Evaluation Structure

FTMO uses a two-step evaluation process:

**Phase 1: FTMO Challenge**
- Duration: 30 calendar days (extensions available)
- Profit target: 10% of account size
- Maximum daily loss: 5% of account size
- Maximum overall loss: 10% of account size
- Minimum trading days: 4
- Fee: Varies by account size (refunded upon passing the full evaluation)

**Phase 2: Verification**
- Duration: 60 calendar days
- Profit target: 5% of account size (reduced from Phase 1)
- Same daily and overall loss limits as Phase 1
- Minimum trading days: 4
- No additional fee

Traders who pass both phases receive an FTMO funded account. The two-phase structure is designed to verify that Phase 1 performance was not a fluke -- the extended Verification period and lower profit target test whether traders can maintain discipline and consistency.

## Funded Account Terms

- **Profit split**: 80% to the trader by default, scaling to 90% for consistent performers
- **Payout cycle**: Bi-weekly or monthly, with the first payout eligible 14 days after receiving the funded account
- **Scaling plan**: Traders who demonstrate consistent profitability over 4+ months with a minimum 10% return can have their account size increased by 25%, up to a maximum of $400,000
- **Loss limits**: The 5% daily and 10% overall loss rules remain active. Breaching either limit results in account termination, though FTMO offers a "free retry" for traders who were profitable before breaching

## Supported Instruments

Unlike [[futures]]-only firms such as [[topstep]], FTMO offers a broad range of instruments through CFD (Contract for Difference) execution:

- **Forex pairs**: All major, minor, and select exotic pairs (EUR/USD, GBP/JPY, USD/ZAR, etc.)
- **Indices**: S&P 500, NASDAQ 100, DAX, FTSE 100, Nikkei 225
- **Commodities**: [[gold]], silver, crude oil, natural gas
- **Crypto**: [[bitcoin|BTC]]/USD, [[ethereum|ETH]]/USD, and select altcoin pairs
- **Stocks**: Select US and EU equities (limited availability)

The CFD model means traders do not interact directly with exchange order books, which makes [[order-flow-analysis]] less applicable compared to exchange-traded [[futures]] at firms like [[topstep]]. However, [[price-action]] and [[smart-money-concepts]] strategies are widely used by FTMO participants.

## Trading Rules and Restrictions

FTMO enforces several rules beyond the loss limits:

- **No holding through major news events**: Some account types restrict trading during high-impact economic releases (NFP, FOMC, CPI)
- **No overnight/weekend holding** on certain account types (Swing accounts are exempt)
- **No copy trading or account management**: Each trader must trade their own account independently
- **Consistency expectation**: While not a formal rule, extremely inconsistent performance (e.g., one massive winning day carrying the entire target) may trigger review

## Industry Position

FTMO is widely regarded as one of the most legitimate and well-run prop trading firms. It has paid out over $200 million in profits to traders (as of company-reported figures through 2024), operates under Czech financial regulations, and maintains a transparent public profile. The company has won multiple industry awards and maintains a strong reputation relative to many competitors in the prop firm space.

The broader prop trading evaluation industry has grown rapidly since 2020, with dozens of firms launching similar challenge models. FTMO's first-mover advantage (alongside [[topstep]] in [[futures]]) and track record of consistent payouts have kept it at the top of the market. However, the industry faces ongoing regulatory scrutiny in several jurisdictions regarding whether these models constitute regulated financial services.

## 2025–2026 Developments

- **OANDA acquisition completed 2025-12-01:** FTMO closed its acquisition of OANDA Global Corporation — the multi-asset retail broker and FX-data provider founded in 1996 — from CVC Asia Fund IV. The deal, signed in early 2025, required approvals from five regulators (~8-month process); terms were not disclosed. FTMO is running OANDA as a fully standalone business, marking its expansion from prop-evaluation into regulated brokerage — a "global trading powerhouse" strategy per founders Otakar Suffner and Marek Vasicek.
- **OANDA Prop Trader wind-down (March 2026):** OANDA's own prop-trading arm was discontinued, with clients transitioned to FTMO as the group consolidated prop trading under the FTMO brand.
- The acquisition gives FTMO regulated entities in New York, Toronto, London, Warsaw, Singapore, Tokyo and Sydney — a significant credibility and infrastructure step as regulators tighten scrutiny of the prop-firm industry.

## How Traders Get Exposure

FTMO is privately held (Prague, Czech Republic) — there is no listed stock. Exposure is operational, not financial: traders participate via its challenge/funded-account model, or trade through the OANDA brokerage it now owns. The OANDA deal is also a bellwether for consolidation between prop-evaluation firms and regulated brokers.
