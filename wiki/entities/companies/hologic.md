Hologic (formerly NASDAQ: HOLX) is a women's-health medical technology company — mammography/breast health, molecular diagnostics (Panther, ThinPrep), GYN surgical, and skeletal health. **On 2026-04-07 Blackstone and TPG completed their take-private of Hologic; the stock is delisted** and the page is retained for the deal record and historical fundamentals. It was an S&P 500 constituent until the buyout closed.

## Take-private by Blackstone and TPG (2025-2026)

- **2025-10-21**: Hologic agreed to be acquired by funds managed by [[blackstone]] and TPG in one of the largest healthcare LBOs since the financial crisis — total value up to ~$18.3B.
- **Consideration**: $76.00/share in cash **plus a non-tradable contingent value right (CVR)** worth up to $3.00/share (two payments of up to $1.50 each), contingent on Breast Health global revenue goals for fiscal 2026 and 2027 — total up to $79.00/share.
- Significant minority investments came from a subsidiary of the Abu Dhabi Investment Authority (ADIA) and an affiliate of GIC.
- **2026-02-05**: stockholders approved the merger. **2026-04-07**: transaction completed; José (Joe) E. Almeida (ex-Baxter CEO) appointed Hologic CEO at close.
- For traders this was a clean, well-telegraphed [[merger-arbitrage]] situation; the CVR structure (revenue-milestone-linked) is a useful template for valuing non-tradable CVRs in healthcare deals.

### Reading the CVR — Valuing the Contingent Stub

The $76.00 cash floor was the easy part; the up-to-$3.00 contingent value right is the instructive part. The CVR pays in two tranches of up to $1.50 each, gated on **Breast Health global revenue thresholds for fiscal 2026 and 2027** — and it is **non-tradable**, so a holder could not monetize it before delisting and had to value it as an embedded option:

- **Expected value = payout × probability of hitting the milestone, discounted for time.** A risk-neutral holder marks the CVR well below $3.00 because the milestones are not certain and payment is deferred 1-2 years.
- **The arb decision** reduced to whether the deal-price floor ($76.00 cash) alone cleared the spread hurdle, treating the CVR as free optionality rather than required return — the standard way to underwrite milestone CVRs in healthcare LBOs.
- **Why a CVR here:** it bridged a valuation gap on Breast Health's growth trajectory, letting the sponsors anchor the cash price while sharing upside if the franchise re-accelerates — a recurring structure (e.g., biopharma development/sales milestones).

This makes Hologic a clean template for [[contingent-value-right|CVR]] valuation in the wiki: revenue-milestone CVRs are options, value them as options, and never assume face value.

### Why an LBO Fit Hologic

Hologic was an attractive sponsor target because it pairs a **stable, high-margin razor-and-blade installed base** (Panther diagnostics instruments pull recurring assay consumables; Dimensions mammography systems pull service and disposables) with **post-COVID-normalized, depressed reported earnings** that public markets were valuing cautiously. That combination — durable cash flows plus a perceived undervaluation versus normalized earnings — is exactly what [[private-equity]] sponsors like [[blackstone]] and TPG underwrite. ADIA and GIC co-investing supplied the equity scale for an ~$18B deal, one of the largest healthcare LBOs since the financial crisis.

## Business Overview (at time of delisting)

Hologic ran a women's-health-centric medtech portfolio built on installed-base / recurring-consumable economics:

| Segment | Key products | Economics |
|---|---|---|
| **Breast Health** | Genius 3D mammography, Dimensions systems, biopsy | Capital systems + service/disposables; the CVR-linked franchise |
| **Diagnostics** | Panther molecular platform, ThinPrep cytology, Aptima assays | Razor/blade — instrument placements pull recurring assay revenue; COVID windfall normalized |
| **GYN Surgical** | MyoSure tissue removal, NovaSure ablation | Procedure-driven disposables |
| **Skeletal Health** | Bone densitometry (Horizon DXA) | Smallest segment; capital equipment |

The Diagnostics COVID-era windfall is visible in the 2021-2022 margin spike in the Fundamentals trend below and normalized thereafter — the post-COVID earnings reset is central to both the depressed-multiple narrative and the LBO timing.

## Risks (as a public company / deal risks)

- **Deal-completion risk (now resolved):** the take-private cleared shareholder vote (2026-02-05) and closed (2026-04-07); residual risk was regulatory/financing, both low for this structure.
- **CVR realization risk:** the $3.00 upside depends on FY2026-27 Breast Health revenue hitting thresholds — uncertain and non-tradable.
- **Post-COVID normalization:** Diagnostics demand and margins reset lower after the pandemic testing windfall, pressuring reported returns (low single-digit ROA/ROE in the Fundamentals block).
- **Capital-equipment cyclicality:** Breast Health and Skeletal system placements depend on hospital capex budgets.
- **Reimbursement & regulatory:** screening reimbursement, FDA pathways, and procedure-volume shifts affect the consumable pull-through.
