**HUB24 Limited** (ASX: HUB) is Australia's leading independent specialist investment-platform provider, supplying platform, technology and data solutions to financial advisers, stockbrokers and accountants. A constituent of the [[asx-200]], it is one of the fastest-growing names in Australian wealth management — for traders it is a high-multiple structural-growth stock whose price is geared to platform net inflows, funds under administration (FUA) and equity-market levels.

## Business Overview & Segments

HUB24 is an adviser-facing wealth-management technology business. Its economics are dominated by the **Platform** segment, where revenue scales with **funds under administration (FUA)** — the larger the asset pool sitting on the platform, the larger the recurring administration-fee base. A smaller **Tech Solutions** segment sells software (SMSF administration, compliance, data) into the same advice-and-accounting ecosystem, deepening the relationship with adviser firms and feeding clients toward the core platform.

| Segment / line | What it is | Revenue mix (qualitative) |
|---|---|---|
| **HUB24 Platform** (Platform) | Investment, superannuation and pension administration platform used by [[financial-advisor\|financial advisers]] to invest, administer and report on client assets | Dominant — FUA-linked administration fees plus **pooled-cash margin** on platform cash balances; the engine of the growth story |
| **PARS** — Portfolio Administration & Reporting Services (Platform) | Non-custody portfolio service: administration, corporate-action management and tax reporting for assets held off-platform | Smaller, but extends the platform's reach to assets it doesn't custody |
| **myprosperity** (Platform) | Client portal connecting advisers, accountants and clients with consolidated financial information | Engagement layer — drives stickiness and feeds platform flow rather than large standalone fees |
| **Class** (Tech Solutions) | Cloud-based SMSF administration, trust accounting, portfolio management and corporate-compliance software | Recurring SaaS subscription fees; high gross margin, scale-driven |
| **NowInfinity** (Tech Solutions) | Cloud entity management, legal documentation and corporate compliance (owned via Class) | Subscription/transaction software fees |
| **HUBconnect** (Tech Solutions) | Data management, software and infrastructure products for wealth and advice firms | Software/data fees; ties advice firms more tightly into the HUB24 ecosystem |

Three revenue engines underpin the model: **FUA administration fees** (the largest and most market-sensitive), **software subscription fees** from Tech Solutions, and **pooled-cash margin** — the net interest HUB24 earns on aggregated platform cash balances, which rises with interest rates and acts as a partial hedge against the multiple-compression that rate cuts inflict on other long-duration growth names.

## History

HUB24 was incorporated in 2007. It was originally listed as Investorfirst Ltd and rebranded as HUB24 Limited in August 2013. Under the leadership of CEO Andrew Alcock (appointed July 2013), the company pivoted to become a technology-led investment platform specialist serving the independent financial advice market.

Through the 2010s HUB24 built its platform organically, positioning itself as a challenger to the major institutional platforms controlled by the big four banks and AMP. A series of strategic acquisitions broadened the group's reach:

- **Xplore Wealth** - acquired in 2021, expanding platform capabilities in the high-net-worth and managed accounts segments.
- **Class Limited** - acquired in 2022, adding SMSF administration software and its subsidiary NowInfinity.
- **myprosperity** - announced in May 2023 for approximately A$51.2 million (with up to A$11.5 million in contingent HUB24 scrip subject to performance hurdles), adding client engagement tools.

These acquisitions shifted HUB24 from a pure platform business to a broader wealth technology group connecting advisers, accountants and clients.

## Economic Moat & Competitive Position

HUB24's [[economic-moat|economic moat]] is built primarily on **[[switching-costs]]**, reinforced by best-of-breed functionality and scale economics on a largely fixed technology base:

- **Adviser workflow lock-in** — a financial adviser builds their entire practice workflow (client onboarding, model portfolios, reporting, fee billing, compliance) around the platform. Once that workflow and the supporting client relationships are embedded, moving books to a rival platform is disruptive, time-consuming and risks client attrition. The adviser, not the end-client, is the decision-maker, and advisers are deeply reluctant to re-platform.
- **Asset stickiness** — client assets, tax histories, in-specie holdings and pension structures sitting on the platform are costly and operationally painful to migrate; inertia keeps FUA on-platform once it arrives.
- **Best-of-breed functionality** — as a specialist independent platform, HUB24 iterates faster than the legacy bank/insurer-owned incumbents, repeatedly topping adviser-satisfaction surveys and winning the "platform of choice" mantle that drives flow.
- **Scale economics on a fixed tech base** — platform technology is a largely fixed cost; each additional dollar of FUA carries high incremental margin, so growth compounds operating leverage.
- **Adviser-migration tailwind** — the structural shift of advisers away from the vertically integrated incumbents (post Royal Commission, post-bank-exit from wealth) toward independent specialists is a multi-year flow tailwind that HUB24 and its closest peer [[netwealth-group]] have captured disproportionately.

The moat is **wide but not impregnable**: switching costs protect the installed base, but new flow is still won deal-by-deal on price, functionality and service, and a fee war or a feature leapfrog by [[netwealth-group]] or a resurgent incumbent could compress economics at the margin.

## Recent Developments

HUB24 reported strong FY2025 results (year ended 30 June 2025) with reported revenue up approximately 24.6%, underlying EBITDA growth of more than 40%, and adjusted NPAT up about 40%. The fully franked dividend rose by roughly 30% to 24 cents per share.

For 1H FY2026 (half year ended 31 December 2025), the group reported record platform net inflows, higher earnings and an increased interim dividend. Management described the result as an "outstanding" half, citing continued investment in technology. Andrew Alcock reaffirmed and updated FY2027 guidance, with the custody FUA range lifted to A$160-170 billion at 30 June 2027.

Strategic initiatives disclosed around the 1H FY2026 result include:

- **myhub** - an ecosystem platform integrating advice technology solutions, with a first release scheduled to be piloted in 1H FY2027 and featuring AI and human-advice components.
- A lifetime retirement solution being developed in partnership with TAL, scheduled to launch on the platform in the months following the result.

## Growth Drivers & Catalysts

- **Adviser migration** — the multi-year structural shift of advisers and their books away from the vertically integrated incumbents toward independent specialists; HUB24's primary FUA-flow engine.
- **FUA guidance** — management's custody FUA target (lifted to **A$160–170 billion at 30 June 2027**) anchors the growth narrative; flow running ahead of trajectory re-rates the stock, a miss de-rates it.
- **Net inflows + market beta** — FUA grows from both organic net inflows and market returns on existing balances; a rising equity market mechanically lifts FUA-linked revenue, and vice versa.
- **myhub** — an integrated advice-technology ecosystem (with AI and human-advice components), first release piloted in 1H FY2027; a potential expansion of the addressable wallet per adviser firm.
- **TAL retirement solution** — a lifetime retirement product launching on the platform, extending HUB24 into the large decumulation/retirement-income opportunity.
- **Tech Solutions cross-sell** — Class/NowInfinity/HUBconnect deepen adviser-firm relationships and feed platform flow.
- **Pooled-cash margin / rates** — net interest on aggregated platform cash; higher rates lift this high-margin revenue line and partly hedge rate-driven multiple compression.
- **Recurring catalysts** — quarterly FUA updates (Jan/Apr/Jul/Oct), half-year (Feb) and full-year (Aug) results, FUA guidance revisions, and large adviser-group/distribution wins.

## Bull Case vs Bear Case

### Bull case

- Structural adviser-migration tailwind still has years to run; HUB24 and [[netwealth-group]] keep taking share from incumbents.
- High [[switching-costs]] make the installed FUA base sticky and the revenue stream recurring and predictable.
- Operating leverage on a fixed technology base means margins expand as FUA compounds — earnings can grow faster than revenue.
- Optionality from myhub, the TAL retirement solution and Tech Solutions cross-sell not fully in numbers.
- Pooled-cash margin gives a partial rate hedge that most long-duration growth peers lack.

### Bear case

- The stock carries a **steep growth multiple** (PE well above the market); any flow disappointment or slowdown in adviser migration can de-rate it hard.
- FUA-linked revenue is **directly geared to equity-market levels** — a market drawdown mechanically shrinks FUA and the fee base at the same time the multiple compresses, a double hit.
- Pooled-cash margin reverses as a tailwind if rates are cut.
- Competition from [[netwealth-group]] and modernising incumbents could trigger fee pressure on new flow.
- Maturing migration tailwind would slow the flow growth the valuation depends on.

## Key Risks

- **Valuation / positioning** — a high-multiple structural-growth name; expectations are elevated and the stock is unforgiving of flow misses.
- **Equity-market sensitivity** — FUA, and therefore the bulk of revenue, falls with markets; a sustained drawdown hits earnings and the multiple together.
- **Flow concentration & migration maturity** — growth depends on continued adviser migration; if that normalises toward system growth, the re-rating reverses.
- **Competitive / fee pressure** — [[netwealth-group]] and reinvigorated incumbents competing on price and functionality for new flow.
- **Rate reversal** — pooled-cash margin shrinks if interest rates fall.
- **Execution / integration** — delivering myhub, the TAL retirement solution and acquired software (Class/NowInfinity) without disrupting the core platform.
- **Regulatory** — changes to platform fee structures, superannuation rules, or adviser-licensing regimes could reshape platform economics.

## Valuation & How the Stock Trades

HUB24 is a high-multiple structural-growth name: it trades at a steep premium to the [[asx-200]] (a PE that sits well above the market — see the figure in the fundamentals block below), because the market capitalises a long runway of FUA growth and the recurring, sticky nature of platform fees. The valuation is a bet on **funds-under-administration flow**, not on current earnings. Mechanically:

- **FUA-flow is the master variable** — revenue is dominated by administration fees on FUA, so the stock re-rates on net inflows and FUA growth. The **quarterly FUA/flow updates (Jan/Apr/Jul/Oct)** are the single highest-frequency catalyst and routinely move the stock more than the headline half-year/full-year profit prints.
- **Equity-market beta into revenue** — because FUA rises and falls with markets, a market drawdown shrinks the fee base at the same moment a risk-off tape compresses the growth multiple — a compounding downside. Conversely, a rising market is a tailwind to both FUA and the rating.
- **Pooled-cash rate sensitivity** — net interest margin on platform cash balances is high-margin and grows with interest rates, giving HUB24 a partial hedge against the rate-cut-driven de-rating that hits other long-duration growth stocks.
- **Pair trade** — moves in close relationship with [[netwealth-group]], the other specialist platform; relative FUA-flow momentum drives which of the two leads.
- **Capital return** — fully-franked dividend, but a small yield and modest payout; HUB24 is a growth/reinvestment story, not an income stock. Total return is dominated by FUA-driven earnings growth and multiple, not the dividend.
- **Liquidity** — [[asx-200]] mid/large-cap; held widely in Australian growth and quality funds.
