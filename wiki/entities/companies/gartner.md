Gartner (NYSE: IT) is the dominant IT research and advisory firm, selling subscription research, conferences (including the Magic Quadrant franchise) and consulting to CIOs and enterprise leaders. It is a constituent of the [[s-and-p-500]] index in the [[technology|Information Technology]] sector. For traders it is the cleanest single-stock expression of the "does generative AI disrupt human expert research?" debate — the stock collapsed in 2025 on exactly that narrative plus US federal spending cuts, leaving a former 30x+ compounder at a single-digit headline PE.

## Business Overview & Segments

Gartner runs three segments, but the engine is **Research**: recurring subscription contracts to CIOs, IT leaders, and increasingly functional leaders (HR, finance, supply chain, marketing). The metric traders watch above all else is **contract value (CV)** — annualized committed subscription revenue — and its growth rate, retention, and federal-government mix. The model is high-gross-margin (~68%) and capital-light, historically paired with aggressive buybacks (which drive the extreme book-equity ROE figures below).

| Segment / line | What it is | Revenue mix (qualitative) |
|---|---|---|
| **Research** | Recurring subscription access to analyst research, advisory inquiries, Magic Quadrant ratings and benchmarking; tracked by contract value (CV) | ~75-80% — the dominant, recurring, high-margin core |
| **Conferences** | In-person/virtual events (e.g. Gartner Symposium) for IT and functional leaders | Mid-single-digit-percent; high incremental margin, more cyclical |
| **Consulting** | Project-based advisory and benchmarking, often a feeder into Research subscriptions | The remainder; lumpier and lower-margin than Research |

The franchise asset is the **Magic Quadrant / analyst brand**: a near-standard reference in enterprise-technology buying that both vendors and buyers feel they must subscribe to — a soft network effect layered on recurring CV.

## 2025-2026 Developments

- **Q1 2025** — DOGE-driven federal austerity hit hard: Gartner retained slightly less than half of US federal contract value eligible for renewal; federal research CV fell from **$275M (Dec 2024) to $225M (Mar 2025)**.
- **Q2 2025** — CV growth slowed to **5%** (from 7% in Q1 and historical double digits); guidance reduced.
- **August 2025** — Slashed revenue/profit outlook; the stock fell **27.6% in a single day**. A parallel narrative took hold that LLMs and cheaper AI research tools would commoditize Gartner's analyst product.
- **FY2025** — Shares ended the year down ~47%; Q4 2025 EPS beat but the stock dropped again as **2026 revenue guidance missed expectations**, with shares hitting 52-week lows in early 2026.
- Management has leaned on buybacks and cost control; bulls argue CV stabilization plus repurchases at a depressed multiple set up a classic "hated compounder" mean reversion.

## Economic Moat & Competitive Position

Gartner's [[economic-moat|moat]] has historically been **wide for a services business**, resting on:

- **Subscription stickiness and [[switching-costs]]** — research is embedded in enterprise buying processes; CIOs and procurement teams rely on Gartner inquiries and Magic Quadrants, and dropping the subscription means losing a reference point used company-wide. Retention has historically run high.
- **Brand and "standard of record"** — the **Magic Quadrant** is a quasi-standard in technology evaluation; vendors subscribe partly to engage with the rating, buyers subscribe to cite it. This is a soft, self-reinforcing network effect.
- **Scale of analyst base and data** — the largest stable of technology analysts and the broadest benchmarking dataset, hard for a smaller rival to replicate.
- **Capital-light economics** — ~68% gross margins and low capital intensity fund continuous buybacks.

The central 2025-26 question is whether **generative AI erodes the moat**: if LLMs and AI-native research tools can synthesize comparable insight at a fraction of the seat cost, switching costs and the brand premium weaken. Bulls argue Gartner's proprietary analyst access, peer benchmarking, and Magic Quadrant remain defensible and that AI is a productivity tool for analysts; bears argue the value of curated human research is structurally impaired. The stock's de-rating reflects that unresolved debate.

## Growth Drivers & Catalysts

- **Contract value (CV) re-acceleration** — the single most important driver; a return toward double-digit CV growth would re-rate the stock.
- **Federal-government stabilization** — recovery of US federal research CV after the 2025 austerity hit.
- **Functional-leader expansion** — growing beyond IT into HR, finance, supply chain, marketing seats widens the addressable base.
- **Conferences recovery** — high-margin event revenue as enterprise travel/budgets normalize.
- **AI-as-tailwind** — the bull framing that enterprises need *more* guidance to navigate AI adoption, lifting demand for Gartner research.
- **Buybacks** — capital-light model funds aggressive repurchases at a depressed multiple.
- **Recurring catalysts**: quarterly CV growth, federal renewal rates, annual guidance revisions, buyback pace, any evidence AI tools are (or are not) displacing research seats.

## Bull Case vs Bear Case

### Bull case
- Recurring, high-retention subscription model with a quasi-standard brand (Magic Quadrant) and real [[switching-costs]].
- A former 30x+ compounder now at a single-digit headline PE — a "hated compounder" mean-reversion setup if CV stabilizes.
- Capital-light economics fund heavy buybacks that compound EPS at a depressed multiple.
- AI may *increase* demand for enterprise guidance, making Gartner a beneficiary rather than a victim.

### Bear case
- **AI disruption** — LLMs and AI-native tools may commoditize curated human research, structurally impairing seat economics.
- **CV deceleration** — growth slowed to ~5% (from historical double digits); federal cuts hit a meaningful contract base.
- **Federal exposure** — DOGE-era austerity showed how fast a government-spending shift can hit CV.
- **Cyclicality** — research and conference budgets are discretionary and shrink in IT-spending downturns.
- The low PE may not be "cheap" if it correctly prices a structural CV deceleration.

## Key Risks

- **Generative-AI substitution** — the central existential debate; cheaper AI research could erode seat demand and the brand premium.
- **Contract-value deceleration** — CV growth is the stock's master variable; sub-mid-single-digit growth de-rates it.
- **Federal/government spending** — austerity (e.g. the 2025 DOGE-driven cuts) directly reduces a large contract base.
- **Macro/IT-budget cyclicality** — discretionary research and conference spend falls in downturns.
- **Concentration of value in Research** — Conferences and Consulting cannot offset a structural Research decline.
- **Valuation re-rating risk** — a low multiple offers limited downside cushion if the disruption thesis proves correct.

## Trading Relevance

- **Catalysts**: quarterly **contract value growth** (the single number that moves the stock), federal renewal rates, 2026 guidance revisions, buyback pace, any evidence AI tools are (or are not) displacing research seats.
- **Profile**: liquid large/mid cap (~$25-30B market cap, approximate); now trades as a battleground/value name with elevated earnings-day moves (±10-25% prints in 2025).
- **Correlated names**: information-services peers Forrester (direct comp, weaker), [[moodys]], [[s-and-p-global]], RELX; sentiment overlaps with the broader "AI disruption victims" basket ([[accenture]], legacy IT services).
- **Note on the 9.8x PE**: partly optical — depressed price against pre-slowdown earnings; the market is pricing structural CV deceleration.

## Valuation & How the Stock Trades

Gartner has flipped from a **premium compounder to a battleground value/disruption name**, and the stock now trades on the AI-disruption narrative as much as on numbers:

- **Subscription/recurring-revenue lens** — historically valued on CV growth and retention like a software/SaaS compounder (25-30x), Gartner now trades at a single-digit headline PE as the market prices structural CV deceleration and AI risk.
- **Optical PE** — the ~9.8x snapshot multiple is partly a depressed price against pre-slowdown earnings; it is not a clean "cheap" signal.
- **Earnings-day volatility** — CV growth and guidance drive large single-day moves (±10-25% prints in 2025); the stock behaves like a battleground, not a low-vol compounder.
- **Capital return** — no dividend historically; the capital-return story is **buybacks**, which a depressed multiple makes more accretive per dollar.
- **Sentiment correlation** — moves with the "AI disruption victims" basket alongside other research/information-services names.
