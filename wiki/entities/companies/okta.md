Okta, Inc. (NASDAQ: OKTA) is the leading independent identity and access management (IAM) vendor — workforce single sign-on/MFA plus customer identity (Auth0) — founded in 2009 by Todd McKinnon (CEO) and Frederic Kerrest. For traders it is a mid-cap SaaS security name whose story shifted from growth-at-any-cost to free-cash-flow discipline through 2024-2026.

## AI Involvement

[[artificial-intelligence|AI]]-driven identity and access management platform; in 2025-26 Okta pushed "identity security for AI agents" (securing non-human/agentic identities) as its next growth vector.

## Business Overview

Okta sells subscription identity software in two platforms: **Workforce Identity** (SSO, MFA, lifecycle management — competes most directly with [[microsoft]] Entra ID) and **Customer Identity** (Auth0, acquired 2021 for $6.5B — competes with Ping/ForgeRock and build-it-yourself). Revenue is >97% subscription. The 2022 Lapsus$ and 2023 support-system breaches were reputational setbacks the company has largely worked through; security credibility remains the key competitive variable versus Microsoft bundling.

## Business Segments / Products

Okta reports as a single subscription segment but the franchise splits into two go-to-market motions plus a growing security-product layer:

| Platform / Product line | What it does | Buyer | Competes with |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Workforce Identity Cloud (WIC)** | Employee/contractor SSO, adaptive MFA, lifecycle management (provisioning/deprovisioning), directory integration | IT / CISO | [[microsoft]] Entra ID, Ping Identity |
| **Customer Identity Cloud (CIC / Auth0)** | Developer-first authentication and authorization for apps the customer builds (login, social/passwordless, fraud detection) | Developers / product teams | Build-in-house, [[microsoft]] Entra External ID, AWS Cognito |
| **Okta Identity Governance (OIG)** | Access reviews, certifications, separation-of-duties — the IGA upsell | CISO / compliance | SailPoint, [[microsoft]] |
| **Privileged Access (PAM)** | Just-in-time privileged credential control | Security | [[cyberark-software]], BeyondTrust |
| **Identity Threat Protection (ITP)** | Continuous risk evaluation, session revocation, integration with [[crowdstrike]]/EDR signals | SecOps | [[microsoft]], identity-security startups |
| **Identity Security for AI agents** | Securing non-human / agentic identities (machine credentials, MCP/agent auth) — the 2025-26 growth narrative | Platform / security | Emerging category |

The strategic shift is from selling *access* (SSO/MFA) to selling *identity security* (governance, privileged access, threat detection) — higher-margin, stickier modules that raise net revenue retention and reduce the single-product dependence on SSO seats. Revenue is recognized almost entirely as ratable subscription; remaining performance obligations (RPO) and the current portion (cRPO) are the leading indicators traders watch (Source: [[stockmarketapi-ai-stocks-2026-04-13]]).

## Competitive Positioning / Peers

Okta is the largest *independent* identity vendor — its core differentiation is neutrality (it integrates 7,000+ apps regardless of cloud) versus [[microsoft]], which bundles Entra ID essentially for free inside E3/E5 licensing. That bundling is the structural overhang on Okta's valuation.

| Company | Ticker | Identity niche | Relationship to Okta |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Okta** | OKTA | Independent workforce + customer identity (IAM/CIAM) | Subject |
| [[microsoft]] | MSFT | Entra ID bundled in M365 | Primary threat — owns the desktop; "good enough + free" |
| [[crowdstrike]] | CRWD | Endpoint → identity-threat detection (Falcon Identity) | Partner *and* encroaching competitor in ITP |
| [[zscaler]] | ZS | Zero-trust network access | Adjacent zero-trust peer; trades in same basket |
| [[cyberark-software]] | CYBR | Privileged access management (PAM) | Direct competitor as Okta pushes into PAM |
| Ping Identity / ForgeRock | private (Thoma Bravo) | Enterprise/CIAM | Legacy displacement target |
| SailPoint | SAIL | Identity governance (IGA) | Competitor as Okta scales OIG |

Okta's moat is the breadth of its integration network (the Okta Integration Network) and switching costs once it is the identity backbone of an enterprise. The bear's counter is that for the average mid-market company, [[microsoft]] Entra is already paid for and "secure enough," so Okta must keep winning on best-of-breed depth and on selling the security upsell layer.

## 2025-2026 Developments

- **FY2026 (ended 31 Jan 2026, reported 4 Mar 2026):** total revenue **$2.919B, +12% YoY** (subscription $2.855B); free cash flow **$863M (30% margin)** — a model franchise transition from losses to 30% FCF margins.
- **FY2027 guidance:** revenue **$3.17-3.19B (+9%)**, non-GAAP operating margin **25-26%**; Q1 FY27 guide $749-753M (+9%) with cRPO +10%. The single-digit growth guide keeps the stock in the "value SaaS" bucket.
- 2025 product cycle: identity threat protection, governance (OIG), privileged access, and agentic-AI identity announcements at Oktane.

## Bull vs Bear Case

**Bull case**
- Profitability inflection achieved — moved from cash-burn growth to 30% FCF margins while still growing, the classic "Rule of 40" maturation that re-rates SaaS multiples.
- Security upsell (OIG governance, PAM, ITP) expands wallet share and lifts net revenue retention, reducing dependence on raw SSO seat growth.
- Independent-identity neutrality is structurally defensible for security-conscious enterprises that distrust single-vendor ([[microsoft]]) lock-in.
- "Identity for AI agents" opens a genuinely new TAM as machine/non-human identities proliferate faster than human ones.

**Bear case**
- [[microsoft]] Entra bundling is a relentless price-to-zero pressure on the core SSO/MFA business.
- Single-digit revenue growth (FY27 guide ~9%) keeps the stock in the "value SaaS" bucket — limited multiple expansion without a re-acceleration.
- Seat-based model is exposed to tech-sector headcount cuts and macro softness.
- Breach history (Lapsus$ 2022, support-system 2023) means any new security incident carries asymmetric downside for a *security* vendor — the credibility tax.

## Valuation Framework

Okta is valued as a maturing SaaS franchise, so the qualitative levers are:

- **Net revenue retention (NRR / NDR)** — the dollar-based expansion metric; once a swing factor above 120%, now a watch item as it normalizes. The security-upsell thesis lives or dies here.
- **Rule of 40** — revenue growth + FCF margin. With ~9-12% growth and ~30% FCF margin, Okta now clears 40 on profitability rather than growth — the market rewards the durability but pays a lower multiple for slow top-line.
- **cRPO growth** — current remaining performance obligations is the cleanest forward-bookings tell and the metric the stock gaps on each quarter.
- **EV/Sales and FCF yield** — as growth slowed, the stock de-rated from a high-growth to a value-SaaS multiple; FCF yield became a floor under the equity.
- **Breach overhang discount** — a persistent valuation haircut versus clean-record peers like [[crowdstrike]]; an incident-free multi-year stretch is required to close it.

Do not treat any specific multiple here as a price target — these are the qualitative inputs an analyst would model.

## Capital Return / Dilution

Okta pays no dividend and reinvests free cash flow into the business. Like most SaaS firms, **stock-based compensation (SBC)** is material and is the principal source of share-count dilution — the GAAP-vs-non-GAAP gap is largely SBC. As FCF generation matured, management introduced share-repurchase capacity to offset dilution; the framing for traders is whether buybacks neutralize SBC creep. Capital allocation has also funded M&A (Auth0, $6.5B, 2021) rather than returns.
