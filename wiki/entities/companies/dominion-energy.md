Dominion Energy (NYSE: D) is a regulated electric and gas [[utilities|utility]] serving Virginia and the Carolinas, a [[utilities|Utilities]] constituent of the [[s-and-p-500]]. For traders it is the purest listed exposure to the Northern Virginia "Data Center Alley" load boom — the single largest data-center electricity market in the world — plus completion risk on the largest US offshore wind project. Founded 1983; headquartered in Richmond, Virginia.

## Business Overview & Segments

Dominion is a **regulated, rate-regulated utility**: like its peers it earns an *allowed return* on its **rate base** rather than taking commodity-price risk, so building approved infrastructure drives earnings. What makes Dominion distinctive is *where* its rate base sits — in **Northern Virginia, the world's largest concentration of data centers** — giving it the strongest structural load-growth tailwind in the US utility sector. A multi-year simplification has refocused the company on this regulated core.

| Segment / line | What it is | Role / mix (qualitative) |
|---|---|---|
| **Virginia Electric & Power (Dominion Energy Virginia)** | The flagship regulated electric utility serving Virginia, including Data Center Alley | The dominant earnings driver and the [[ai-data-center-power-demand|AI power-demand]] growth engine |
| **Dominion Energy South Carolina** | Regulated electric and gas utility in South Carolina | Steady rate-base contributor |
| **Contracted Energy** | Coastal Virginia Offshore Wind (CVOW) and other contracted generation | The 2.6 GW CVOW farm — the largest US offshore wind project; growth plus completion risk |

After divesting its gas-distribution utilities to Enbridge (completed 2024) in a strategic simplification, Dominion is now centered on the Virginia and South Carolina regulated utilities plus contracted generation. The dividend was cut in 2020 and held roughly flat since (~US$2.67/yr) while the balance sheet is repaired; payout remains high relative to earnings.

**Note on the Fundamentals section below:** the headline PE of 441.7x and near-zero margins for FY2024 are distorted by one-off charges and discontinued-operations accounting from the gas-utility sales; Dominion's operating-earnings guidance for 2026 is in the ~US$3.5/share area, implying a normalized PE in the mid-to-high teens.

## Economic Moat & Competitive Position

Dominion has the standard **regulated-monopoly** [[economic-moat|moat]], with an unusually strong demand-growth overlay:

- **Legal monopoly franchise** — sole electricity provider across its Virginia and South Carolina territories; customers cannot switch, so revenue is stable and visible.
- **Regulated rate-base returns** — Virginia and South Carolina regulators grant the opportunity to earn an allowed return on prudently invested capital, turning capex into earnings growth.
- **Best-in-class load growth** — Northern Virginia's "Data Center Alley" gives Dominion the largest, most durable demand-growth driver in the sector; rate base expands to serve a customer base that is *adding* load rather than stagnating.
- **Barriers to entry** — replacing a transmission/distribution network is economically impossible; the franchise is effectively unassailable.

The honest framing, sharper here than at most peers: returns are regulator-*capped*, the balance sheet is **heavily leveraged**, recent reported earnings are distorted by restructuring, and the marquee CVOW project carries real **execution/cost-overrun risk**. The moat is the regulated franchise plus the data-center load — not freedom from regulatory or project risk.

## Growth Drivers & Catalysts

- **Data-center load** — contracted data-center capacity reached ~51 GW as of March 2026 (+2.5 GW vs. Dec 2025); management calls demand "accelerating and durable" — the central [[ai-data-center-power-demand|AI power-demand]] growth story.
- **CVOW completion** — the 2.6 GW Coastal Virginia Offshore Wind farm (>75% complete on a revised ~US$11.4B budget; first power March 2026) adds regulated rate base as it enters service.
- **Rate-base expansion** — generation and grid upgrades to serve Virginia load growth feed the regulated earnings algorithm.
- **Balance-sheet repair** — post-divestiture deleveraging supports the dividend and credit profile.
- **Recurring catalysts** — CVOW construction/cost milestones, Virginia SCC rate rulings (including who pays for data-center grid upgrades), data-center load disclosures, hurricane season, and Fed/rate moves.

## Bull Case vs Bear Case

### Bull case
- Purest regulated exposure to the world's largest data-center market — structurally the best load-growth profile in US utilities.
- CVOW completion adds large regulated rate base and removes a major overhang as it enters service.
- Post-divestiture, a simpler, focused regulated business with a repairing balance sheet.
- High dividend yield (~4.5-5%) provides income and a valuation floor; normalized P/E is mid-to-high-teens, not the distorted headline.

### Bear case
- CVOW execution/cost-overrun and turbine-installation risk on the largest US offshore wind project.
- High leverage (D/E ~1.56x) and weak reported coverage; reported earnings distorted by restructuring.
- Rate-sensitive bond proxy — rising long-end yields de-rate the multiple and raise financing cost.
- Virginia regulatory risk over who pays for data-center grid upgrades (rate-class proceedings).

## Key Risks

- **CVOW project risk** — cost overruns and turbine-installation delays on the 2.6 GW offshore wind farm are the key company-specific watch item.
- **Regulatory risk** — Virginia SCC rate rulings, allowed-ROE outcomes, and data-center rate-class proceedings (who pays for grid upgrades).
- **Leverage / balance sheet** — D/E ~1.56x with weak reported interest coverage; ongoing balance-sheet repair.
- **Interest-rate sensitivity** — bond-proxy behavior; rising long-end yields pressure the multiple and financing cost.
- **Storm / operational risk** — Atlantic hurricane season exposure in Virginia/the Carolinas.

## Valuation & How the Stock Trades

Dominion trades as a **rate-sensitive regulated-utility bond proxy with a data-center growth kicker** — and its headline metrics are unusually misleading. Mechanically:

- **Normalize the multiple** — the 441.7x reported P/E in the Fundamentals block reflects one-off charges and discontinued-operations accounting from the gas-utility sales; on ~US$3.5/share of 2026 operating-earnings guidance the *normalized* P/E is mid-to-high-teens. Investors value D on operating EPS and rate-base growth, not GAAP headline.
- **Bond-proxy behavior** — trades inversely to long-end [[treasuries|Treasury]] yields; rate moves drive much of the day-to-day action.
- **Dividend yield as anchor** — the ~4.5-5% yield (held flat since the 2020 cut) is a valuation floor and the core of the income thesis; the API dividend-yield figure in Fundamentals is the API's own definition, not market yield.
- **Theme torque** — re-rates on data-center load disclosures and CVOW milestones, though as a *regulated* utility its upside capture on the AI-power theme is smaller than merchant peers. See the Fundamentals block below for the figures as pulled.
