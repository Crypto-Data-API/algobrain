**TPG Telecom Limited** (ASX: TPG) is an telecom services company listed on the [[asx-limited|Australian Securities Exchange]] and a constituent of the [[asx-200]] index. TPG Telecom Limited (ASX: TPG) is Australia's second-largest ASX-listed telecommunications company and the country's third-largest mobile network operator. Headquartered at Tower Two, International Towers Sydney in Barangaroo, TPG provides mobile and fixed telecommunications services to consumer, business, enterprise, government and wholesale customers across Australia.

## Business Model & Segments

TPG operates two reportable segments:

- **Consumer** — telecommunications services to residential and small office / home office (SOHO) customers across mobile, fixed broadband, voice and entertainment. Consumer brands include:
  - **Vodafone** — flagship mobile brand.
  - **TPG** — postpaid mobile and NBN broadband.
  - **iiNet** — NBN broadband and fixed voice.
  - **Internode** — premium NBN for technical consumers and SOHOs.
  - **Lebara** — prepaid mobile.
  - **felix** — subscription-based mobile.
- **Enterprise, Government and Wholesale (EGW)** — corporate, enterprise, government, SME and wholesale connectivity.

Historically, the group owned more than 27,000 km of metro and inter-capital fibre, but in 2024 agreed to sell most of these fibre and fixed network infrastructure assets to Vocus Group for approximately A$5.25 billion (further detail below). TPG retains its mobile network (5,600+ sites) and consumer-facing fixed business.

Post-Vocus, TPG is a leaner mobile-and-consumer-fixed operator: it kept the Vodafone-branded mobile network (5,600+ sites) and consumer fixed base, while divesting the inter-capital fibre to fund deleveraging and capital management.

## History

- **1986**: Total Peripherals Group (TPG) founded by Malaysian-born Australian entrepreneur David Teoh (with wife Vicky Teoh), initially selling computer hardware before pivoting into telecommunications.
- **2005**: Acquired internet service provider TPG Internet (after earlier purchases of SP Telemedia assets and Adelaide-based Chariot in 2007).
- **April 2008**: David Teoh orchestrated an A$225 million reverse takeover of SP Telemedia.
- **25 November 2009**: SP Telemedia Ltd renamed TPG Telecom Ltd.
- **2009**: Vodafone Australia and Hutchison's "3" mobile business merged into Vodafone Hutchison Australia (VHA), a 50:50 joint venture.
- **2015**: TPG acquired iiNet — then Australia's second-largest DSL ISP — for A$1.56 billion.
- **August 2018**: TPG and VHA announced an A$15 billion merger of equals.
- **2019**: Australian Competition and Consumer Commission opposed the merger; the Federal Court ruled in favour of the parties.
- **13 July 2020**: Merger completed; combined entity became TPG Telecom Limited (with Vodafone Hutchison Australia renamed to TPG Telecom Limited).
- **2023–2024**: Strategic review of fixed network assets; Enterprise & Government business experienced pressure from NBN migration and competitive dynamics.
- **15 October 2024**: Announced sale of fibre and fixed network infrastructure assets to Vocus Group for A$5.25 billion.
- **2025**: Vocus transaction approved by the ACCC and completed, generating net cash proceeds of approximately A$4.65–4.75 billion for TPG.

## Economic Moat & Competitive Position

TPG's [[economic-moat|moat]] is the **narrow but durable moat of a scale mobile-network operator** in a structurally concentrated market:

- **Three-player mobile market** — Australian mobile is effectively a three-MNO oligopoly ([[telstra]], Optus, TPG). Owning one of only three national networks is an enormous barrier to entry; spectrum and tower infrastructure are scarce and capital-intensive.
- **Spectrum & network assets** — licensed spectrum, 5,600+ mobile sites and 5G infrastructure are hard-to-replicate, regulator-gated assets.
- **Multi-brand reach** — Vodafone, TPG, iiNet, Internode, Lebara and felix span premium to value/prepaid, capturing different segments with shared network economics.
- **Switching friction** — bundling, handset financing and number/plan inertia create modest [[switching-costs|switching costs]] in consumer telco.

The moat's limit: TPG is the **#3 challenger**, structurally smaller than [[telstra]] (the dominant incumbent) and Optus, with weaker network coverage perception and less pricing power. On fixed broadband, the NBN wholesale model commoditises retail and compresses margins. The franchise is real but sub-scale relative to the leader.

## Growth Drivers & Catalysts

- **Mobile ARPU & subscriber growth** — the central value driver post-Vocus; rational three-player pricing supports ARPU.
- **5G monetisation** — continued rollout and modernisation of the Vodafone-branded network.
- **Deleveraging & capital management** — Vocus proceeds applied to debt reduction; potential further capital returns.
- **Cost/asset simplification** — a leaner perimeter improves free cash flow (operating FCF ~doubled to ~A$1.29bn in FY25).
- **Catalysts** — FY results and FY26 EBITDA guidance (A$1.665–1.735bn), mobile subscriber/ARPU trends, capital-management decisions, NBN pricing/regulatory changes, and competitive pricing moves by [[telstra]]/Optus.

## Bull vs Bear

**Bull case**
- Simplified, deleveraged mobile-and-consumer-fixed business after the A$5.25bn Vocus sale — an income-and-recovery story.
- One of only three national mobile networks; structural oligopoly with rational pricing.
- Strong free cash flow funding a high, franked dividend.
- Self-help upside from cost discipline and 5G monetisation.

**Bear case**
- Structural #3 with weaker scale, coverage perception and pricing power than [[telstra]]/Optus.
- Three-player price competition can erode mobile ARPU.
- Post-Vocus earnings-base reset and continued 5G capex intensity.
- Concentrated shareholder register (Vodafone Group, CK Hutchison interests) can overhang the float.

## Key Risks

- **Mobile price competition** — irrational pricing in the three-player market would compress ARPU and margins.
- **Capex intensity** — ongoing 5G rollout and network modernisation absorb cash.
- **Earnings reset** — smaller post-Vocus revenue/EBITDA base; growth must come from mobile, not the divested fibre.
- **Regulatory** — NBN pricing changes and telecom regulation affect fixed economics.
- **Register concentration** — large strategic holders (Vodafone Group, Hutchison/CK Hutchison) influence liquidity and control.

## Valuation & How the Stock Trades

Post-Vocus, TPG is a **simplified, deleveraged mobile-and-consumer-fixed business with a high dividend yield and franking credits — an income-and-recovery story rather than a growth name**. Mechanically:

- **Valuation lens** — a mid-teens trailing PE (~15x) and P/B near ~1x; the market prices it on mobile ARPU/subscriber trajectory, FY26 EBITDA guidance (A$1.665–1.735bn) and capital-return potential rather than top-line growth.
- **Dividend** — a high, partially franked yield supported by strong post-deal free cash flow; the income case is central (the large "API dividend yield" figure in Fundamentals is a data-definition artefact, not the true market yield).
- **Beta / behaviour** — a relatively defensive, lower-beta telco; the stock moves on results, guidance, ARPU prints and competitive-pricing headlines from [[telstra]]/Optus.
- **Liquidity/options** — concentrated register limits free float; positioning is via the equity.
- **Correlated names** — Australian telecom/infrastructure peers ([[telstra]], [[chorus]]) and the broader [[communication-services]] sector.

## Recent Developments

Under CEO Iñaki Berroeta, TPG has been simplifying its asset base and strengthening its balance sheet:

- **Vocus transaction (2025)**: Completed the divestment of fibre and fixed EGW assets to Vocus for A$5.25 billion. Net cash proceeds applied primarily to debt reduction and capital management.
- **FY25 results (year to 31 December 2025)**: Described by management as a "milestone year". Operating free cash flow approximately doubled to A$1.29 billion, reflecting Vocus proceeds, handset receivables financing and lower capex and borrowing costs.
- **Asset simplification**: Smaller but more focused asset perimeter post-Vocus, enabling sharper focus on mobile and consumer fixed services.
- **FY26 guidance**: EBITDA guidance of A$1.665–1.735 billion; capex guidance of A$750 million (additions basis).
- **Network investment**: Continued 5G mobile network rollout and modernisation on the Vodafone-branded network.
- **Competitive dynamics**: Continued pressure from Telstra and Optus, alongside disruptive mobile pricing.
