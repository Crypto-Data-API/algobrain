Zscaler, Inc. (NASDAQ: ZS) is a cloud-delivered cybersecurity company and a leader in **zero-trust** network security, inspecting internet and SaaS traffic for enterprise users and devices through its globally distributed cloud (the Zscaler Zero Trust Exchange). For traders it is a high-growth, durable-recurring-revenue software name (FY2025 ARR ~$3.0B growing ~22%, accelerating to ~26% in early FY2026) whose stock trades on billings/ARR momentum and is sensitive to any deceleration.

## AI Involvement

[[artificial-intelligence|AI]]-powered [[ai-cybersecurity|zero-trust]] cloud security platform. Zscaler positions AI both as a product (AI-driven threat detection, data-loss prevention, AI/SaaS-app security and risk management) and as a demand driver, as enterprises seek to secure AI-era traffic and workloads. Because every byte of inspected traffic flows through Zscaler's cloud, the company sits on one of the largest security-telemetry datasets in the industry — the raw material it uses to train detection models and to pitch "secure your AI usage" modules (e.g. controlling employee access to public LLMs and preventing data leakage into them).

## Business Overview

Zscaler's core is the **Zero Trust Exchange**, a cloud proxy that brokers secure connections between users, devices, workloads and applications without exposing the corporate network — displacing legacy VPN/firewall hardware. Flagship products are **ZIA** (Zscaler Internet Access) and **ZPA** (Zscaler Private Access), extended into data protection, digital experience monitoring (ZDX), workload and IoT/OT security, and AI-security modules. The business model is subscription/ARR-based with high gross margins and strong net retention; growth is driven by new logos plus upsell of additional modules onto a "platform consolidation" thesis. It competes with [[palo-alto-networks]], [[crowdstrike]], Netskope, Cisco, and Cloudflare.

## Business Segments / Products

Zscaler reports a single subscription business, but the platform is sold as a set of distinct module families that drive the land-and-expand motion:

| Module family | What it does | Role in the model |
|---|---|---|
| **ZIA — Internet Access** | Secure web gateway / SWG, inline inspection of internet & SaaS traffic | The original land product; replaces proxy/firewall appliances |
| **ZPA — Private Access** | Zero-trust network access (ZTNA) to internal apps | Replaces VPN; the second pillar of the SASE story |
| **ZDX — Digital Experience** | Monitors end-user performance across the network path | Upsell that increases stickiness and "platform" lock-in |
| **Data Protection (DLP/CASB)** | Inline data-loss prevention and SaaS posture | Higher-ASP upsell; ties into AI-data-leakage controls |
| **Workload / Cloud & IoT/OT security** | Zero trust for cloud workloads and connected devices | Expands the addressable footprint beyond user traffic |
| **AI / Risk modules** | AI-driven threat detection, risk quantification, securing employee AI usage | The current growth narrative |

The "consolidation" thesis is that customers land with ZIA or ZPA and expand to a full **SASE / SSE** (secure access service edge / security service edge) suite, raising net retention and average contract value over time.

## Competitive Positioning / Peers

Zscaler is a pure-play cloud-security platform competing across the SASE/SSE and broader cybersecurity landscape. Its closest public comps are the security platform consolidators:

| Company | Ticker | Primary overlap | Note |
|---|---|---|---|
| [[palo-alto-networks]] | PANW | SASE/SSE (Prisma Access), firewalls, broad platform | Largest, most diversified pure-play; "platformization" rival |
| [[crowdstrike]] | CRWD | Endpoint-led platform expanding into identity/cloud/SIEM | Different entry point (endpoint) but converging platform thesis |
| [[sentinelone]] | S | Endpoint/cloud security | Smaller, endpoint-native rival in the security basket |
| Netskope | private/IPO | SSE/CASB, direct SASE competitor | Closest functional competitor to ZIA/ZPA |
| Cisco | CSCO | Networking + security (incl. legacy VPN/firewall) | The incumbent hardware Zscaler displaces |
| Cloudflare | NET | Cloudflare One / Zero Trust | Edge-network challenger in SSE |

Zscaler's edge is being **cloud-native from inception** (no appliance legacy to defend) and operating one of the largest inline-inspection clouds; the bear view is that platform rivals ([[palo-alto-networks]], [[crowdstrike]]) bundle "good enough" SASE into broader suites.

## Valuation Framework (qualitative)

Zscaler is valued as a premium-multiple recurring-revenue security platform; the metrics that matter are SaaS operating metrics, not GAAP earnings:

- **ARR & calculated billings growth** — the master variables. Billings (a forward-looking proxy for bookings) tend to move the stock more than reported revenue; re-acceleration is rewarded and deceleration punished hard.
- **Net retention rate (NRR)** — measures upsell of additional modules into the existing base; the engine of the consolidation thesis.
- **Rule of 40** — Zscaler comfortably clears it, combining ~20%+ growth with strong free-cash-flow margins, which underpins the premium multiple.
- **FCF margin** — unusually strong for the growth rate; the cash-flow story partly offsets GAAP losses.
- **GAAP vs SBC** — GAAP net losses are driven mainly by heavy **stock-based compensation**; bulls discount this in favor of FCF and non-GAAP profitability, while bears flag the resulting share-count dilution.
- **Multiple regime** — as a high-multiple software name, ZS is sensitive to rates and the software-multiple cycle and trades within the high-growth cybersecurity basket.

## Capital Return / Dilution

Zscaler pays **no dividend** and has not historically run a material buyback — capital is reinvested in sales capacity, R&D, and cloud expansion. The key shareholder consideration is **stock-based-compensation dilution**: SBC is large enough to push GAAP results into losses despite strong cash generation, and it expands the diluted share count over time. The bull case treats SBC as a known cost offset by free cash flow; the bear case treats persistent dilution as a real drag on per-share value. Any future repurchase would most likely be positioned as mopping up SBC dilution rather than as a return-of-capital program.
