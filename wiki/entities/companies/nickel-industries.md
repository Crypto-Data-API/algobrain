**Nickel Industries Limited** (ASX: NIC) is an ASX-listed nickel miner and processor with a portfolio of mining and low-cost downstream nickel processing assets in Indonesia, and a constituent of the [[asx-200]] index. The group produces nickel pig iron (NPI) for the stainless steel industry and nickel matte and mixed hydroxide precipitate (MHP) products for the electric vehicle (EV) battery supply chain. For traders it is the most direct ASX exposure to the Indonesian nickel complex — a leveraged play on NPI/nickel prices, Indonesian mining policy (RKAB quotas), and the Tsingshan partnership.

## Business Model & Segments

Nickel Industries' integrated Indonesian platform comprises:

- **Hengjaya Mine (Indonesia):** Large open-pit nickel laterite mine in Sulawesi supplying ore to downstream rotary kiln electric furnace (RKEF) operations. Among the 10 largest nickel deposits globally.
- **RKEF Projects (80% interests unless stated):**
  - **Hengjaya Nickel** (IMIP) - NPI and nickel matte.
  - **Ranger Nickel** (IMIP) - NPI.
  - **Oracle Nickel** (IMIP) - NPI.
  - **Angel Nickel** (IWIP) - NPI; production commenced January 2022.
- **HPAL Projects (High-Pressure Acid Leach, EV-grade nickel):**
  - **Huayue Nickel Cobalt (HNC):** 10% indirect interest (completed August 2023).
  - **Excelsior Nickel Cobalt (ENC):** 44% interest, under construction at IMIP.

The group sells nickel products (NPI, nickel matte, MHP) to global customers via Tsingshan's commercial channels.

## Economic Moat & Cost Position

Nickel Industries is a pure [[economic-moat|price-taker]] — it sells globally fungible [[nickel]] units (NPI, matte, MHP) and has no pricing power. Its only competitive advantage is **cost-curve position**, where, unusually, it sits on the *winning* side of the curve:

- **First-quartile Indonesian cost base** — NIC's RKEF operations inside the Tsingshan IMIP/IWIP parks enjoy captive laterite ore (Hengjaya), integrated power, cheap labour and scale. This is the **low-cost supply that has been pushing higher-cost producers off the curve** — most visibly [[bhp|BHP]]'s Nickel West and [[igo|IGO]]'s WA sulphide mines (Forrestania, Nova), which went to care-and-maintenance/closure precisely because they could not compete with Indonesian laterite economics.
- **The double edge** — being low-cost is a moat against peers, but the same Indonesian supply wave that NIC rode is the **structural bear case for the nickel price itself**. NIC is simultaneously a beneficiary (low cost) and a contributor (adds supply) to a glutted market — low cost protects margins per tonne, but cannot protect the tonne price.
- **Tsingshan dependency** — the partnership provides access, technology, and commercial channels (a genuine advantage), but also concentrates counterparty and governance risk.
- **HPAL optionality** — HNC and ENC give exposure to higher-value EV-grade (class-1) nickel, a partial hedge against NPI/stainless commoditisation if the battery thesis plays out.

Honest verdict: a low-cost producer in a structurally oversupplied commodity — the cost moat is real but it is a moat in a flooding market.

## Growth Drivers & Catalysts

- **NPI margin recovery** — NPI margins are the single biggest earnings lever; management cited margins rising ~150% to ~US$2,800/t in early 2026 versus ~US$1,114/t in Dec 2025.
- **ENC HPAL commissioning** — the 44% Excelsior project ramping adds EV-grade nickel volume and diversifies away from stainless-grade NPI.
- **Hengjaya Mine sales** — captive ore sales (record quarterly EBITDA in the Mar-2026 quarter) lift integration margins.
- **Indonesian policy (RKAB quotas)** — quota approvals and export policy directly gate mining volumes (delays cost an estimated ~A$50-60m of EBITDA in 2025).
- **Recurring catalysts** — quarterly production/EBITDA reports, LME nickel and NPI price prints, dividend declarations.

## Bull Case vs Bear Case

### Bull case
- First-quartile Indonesian cost position — profitable when higher-cost Western producers are losing money.
- Integrated mine-to-metal platform (Hengjaya ore + RKEF) plus HPAL growth (ENC) into EV-grade nickel.
- Leveraged to an NPI/nickel-price recovery — early-2026 margin expansion shows the torque.
- Pays a dividend, unusual for a high-beta commodity name.

### Bear case
- Structural nickel oversupply (Indonesian laterite flood) caps the price NIC can realise — low cost cannot rescue a falling tonne price.
- Heavy reliance on Tsingshan for technology, ore-park integration and commercial channels — concentration and governance risk.
- Indonesian sovereign/regulatory risk: RKAB quota delays, royalty/tax and export-policy changes.
- Loss-making at FY2024-FY2025 prices (negative trailing P/E); balance-sheet sensitivity if margins stay depressed.

## Key Risks

- **Commodity price** — nickel/NPI price weakness is the dominant risk; the Indonesian supply glut is the structural bear case.
- **Demand** — stainless-steel demand (NPI) and EV-battery demand (HPAL/MHP) drive the two halves of the book; a slowdown in either bites.
- **Sovereign / regulatory** — Indonesian mining policy: RKAB quota timing, royalties, ore-export rules, and resource-nationalism risk.
- **Counterparty concentration** — Tsingshan dependence across ore parks, technology and offtake channels.
- **Permitting / ESG** — laterite mining, HPAL tailings disposal and emissions face rising scrutiny from EV-chain customers.
- **Capex execution** — ENC HPAL build and ramp risk.

## Valuation & How the Stock Trades

NIC's trailing P/E is **negative** (FY24-FY25 losses), so it is a **price-cycle trade, not an earnings-multiple story**:

- **High commodity beta** — the equity trades primarily on LME nickel/NPI price moves and Indonesian policy headlines rather than company-specific execution.
- **Margin-spread sensitivity** — because cost is low and fixed-ish, small moves in the NPI price translate into large EBITDA swings (the ~150% margin jump into early 2026 illustrates the operating leverage).
- **Dividend** — pays a modest dividend (5y avg yield positive), distinguishing it from non-paying ramp-up miners, though payouts flex with the cycle.
- **Valuation lens** — valued on EV/EBITDA at a normalised NPI price and on the optionality of ENC HPAL, rather than trailing earnings; broker targets in early 2026 (~A$1.30 vs ~A$0.90 price) framed it as a margin-recovery year.

## History

Nickel Industries was incorporated in 2007 as Nickel Mines Limited and listed on the ASX in 2018 via an approximately A$200 million IPO. The company has been operating in Indonesia for over 15 years.

Key milestones:

- **2017:** Entered the Collaboration and Subscription Agreement with Tsingshan governing the Hengjaya Nickel Project.
- **2018:** Listed on the ASX in an approximately A$200 million IPO.
- **Acquisitions:**
  - Hengjaya Nickel: Acquired across several tranches for total consideration of approximately US$180 million.
  - Ranger Nickel: Acquired across several tranches for total consideration of approximately US$230.1 million.
  - Oracle Nickel: Acquired across several tranches for total consideration of approximately US$600 million.
- **January 2022:** Commenced NPI production at Angel Nickel (IWIP).
- **June 2022:** Changed name from Nickel Mines Limited to Nickel Industries Limited.
- **July 2023:** Shareholders approved acquisition of a 10% indirect stake in the Huayue Nickel Cobalt HPAL project; completion August 2023.
- **2024:** Committed to a 44% interest in the Excelsior Nickel Cobalt HPAL project.
