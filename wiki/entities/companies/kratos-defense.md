Kratos Defense & Security Solutions (NASDAQ: KTOS) is a mid-cap defense-technology company best known for the XQ-58A Valkyrie low-cost jet-powered combat drone, plus hypersonic test vehicles, small jet engines, microwave electronics, and satellite communications. It became one of the market's flagship "drone war / attritable airpower" momentum trades in 2025-2026, with active options flow around government contract announcements.

## Business Overview

Kratos (founded 1994 as Wireless Facilities, renamed in 2007; HQ San Diego) deliberately builds affordable, producible systems with internal funding ahead of contracts. Key franchises:

- **Unmanned Systems (KUS):** XQ-58A Valkyrie collaborative combat aircraft (CCA) and high-performance jet target drones (BQM-167, BQM-177); segment revenue US$292.0M in 2025 (vs US$270.5M in 2024).
- **Kratos Government Solutions (KGS):** space/satcom (OpenSpace virtualized ground stations), microwave electronics, C5ISR, turbine technologies (small jet engines for cruise missiles and drones), and hypersonic flight-test services — Kratos leads the US$1.45B+ MACH-TB 2.0 hypersonic test-bed program.

## 2025-2026 Developments

- **FY2025 results:** consolidated revenue US$1.347B, +16.6% organic over 2024's US$1.136B ([Q4/FY2025 release](https://finance.yahoo.com/news/kratos-reports-fourth-quarter-full-210000996.html)).
- **Valkyrie program of record:** the U.S. Marine Corps designated the XQ-58A Valkyrie an official program of record — the first collaborative combat aircraft to reach production status for the Marines.
- **Airbus partnership:** Airbus and Kratos are developing a European Valkyrie variant for Germany's air force, with operational deployment anticipated by ~2029.
- **2026 guidance:** base-case revenue of US$1.595-1.675B (+15-20% organic) and adjusted EBITDA of US$157-167M.
- **Sector tailwind:** Pentagon drone-procurement surge initiatives put KTOS alongside AeroVironment (AVAV) and others in the 2025-2026 defense-drone rally.

## Business Segments & Products

Kratos runs two reporting segments, each spanning multiple high-growth defense franchises. The unifying strategy is **affordable, producible, internally-funded systems** built ahead of contracts — a deliberate bet that the Pentagon's shift toward attritable, low-cost mass favors Kratos's model.

| Segment | Key franchises | Strategic role |
|---|---|---|
| **Unmanned Systems (KUS)** | XQ-58A Valkyrie collaborative combat aircraft (CCA); BQM-167 / BQM-177 high-performance jet target drones | The flagship "attritable airpower" growth story |
| **Kratos Government Solutions (KGS)** | Space/satcom (OpenSpace virtualized ground stations); microwave electronics; C5ISR; turbine technologies (small jet engines for cruise missiles/drones); hypersonic flight-test (MACH-TB 2.0) | Diversified defense-tech base; hypersonics and jet engines are the higher-growth slices |

The **turbine/jet-engine** business is a quietly important franchise: Kratos supplies low-cost small jet engines for cruise missiles and drones, an area with surging demand as munitions stockpiles are rebuilt. The **hypersonics** franchise (leading the US$1.45B+ MACH-TB 2.0 test-bed program) positions Kratos as critical test infrastructure for the entire US hypersonic enterprise.

## Bull vs Bear Case

**Bull case**
- Pentagon's structural shift toward **attritable, low-cost mass** (CCA, drones, cheap munitions) plays directly to Kratos's affordable-systems model.
- **Valkyrie program of record** (USMC) converts the flagship drone from concept to production status — a major de-risking milestone.
- **Airbus partnership** opens a European Valkyrie market (Germany, deployment ~2029).
- Hypersonics leadership (MACH-TB 2.0) and jet-engine demand provide diversified, sticky growth.
- Strong revenue growth (+16.6% organic FY2025) and double-digit FY2026 guidance; book-to-bill momentum.

**Bear case**
- **Rich valuation** on traditional metrics — the stock re-rated sharply on drone enthusiasm and is priced for execution; multiple compression is the key risk.
- **Low margins** (single-digit/~10-11% adjusted EBITDA) for a defense name, reflecting heavy growth investment.
- **Program timing risk** — contract awards and CCA increments can slip; fixed-price development exposes Kratos to cost overruns.
- **Dilution** — Kratos has historically raised equity to fund capacity expansion.
- Defense-budget and procurement-priority shifts could slow the drone-procurement surge.

## Valuation Framework

Unlike the EV/clean-energy names, Kratos is profitable on an adjusted-EBITDA basis, so the lens blends growth-stock and defense-contractor metrics:

- **Program backlog & book-to-bill** — the most important visibility metric for a contract-driven defense business; rising backlog underwrites forward revenue.
- **Organic revenue growth** — +16.6% organic in FY2025; the durability of double-digit growth justifies (or fails to justify) the multiple.
- **Adjusted EBITDA margin trajectory** — single-digit/~10-11% today; the bull case needs margins to expand as franchises mature past their investment phase.
- **Valuation multiple** — KTOS trades at a growth-stock multiple (EV/EBITDA, EV/sales) far above the primes; multiple compression is the dominant valuation risk.
- **Catalyst optionality** — Valkyrie international sales, CCA down-selects, and hypersonic task orders carry binary upside not fully in the base case.

## Capital Structure & Dilution

- Kratos has **historically raised equity** to fund capacity expansion and internal product development ahead of contracts — dilution is a recurring, deliberate feature of the build-ahead model.
- The strategy of self-funding production lines before awards (e.g., jet engines, Valkyrie capacity) consumes cash and can require periodic raises.
- As a profitable mid-cap (vs the cash-burning EV/clean-energy names), Kratos's dilution is growth-investment-driven rather than survival-driven — a healthier profile, but still a watch item for per-share value.
- **Fixed-price development** contracts shift cost-overrun risk onto Kratos, which can pressure the balance sheet on troubled programs.
