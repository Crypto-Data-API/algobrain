Entergy (NYSE: ETR) is a regulated electric [[utilities|utility]] serving more than 3 million customers in Arkansas, Louisiana, Mississippi and Texas, and an [[s-and-p-500]] constituent. It has become one of the purest "AI power demand" plays among regulated utilities — a primary public-equity expression of the [[ai-data-center-power-demand]] theme — building the generation and transmission for Meta's giant Hyperion AI data center in Richland Parish, Louisiana, one of the largest single load additions in US utility history.

## Business Overview & Segments

Entergy's earnings come almost entirely from its regulated **Utility** segment — vertically integrated operating companies with a Gulf-Coast service territory rich in industrial load (LNG, petrochemicals, hydrogen — and now hyperscale data centers). It also operates a regulated nuclear fleet (e.g., River Bend, Waterford 3, Grand Gulf) after exiting merchant nuclear. Growth is **rate-base driven**: capital deployed into generation, transmission and storm hardening earns a regulated return on equity, so the investment case rests on industrial load growth and constructive regulation in its four states.

Because earnings are regulated, the model is read on **rate base growth, allowed ROE, EPS guidance, and the equity-financing needed** to fund the capex plan — not on the GAAP P/E (44.8x below), which is inflated by storm/one-off items.

## Economic Moat & Competitive Position

Entergy's [[economic-moat|moat]] is the classic **regulated-utility advantage** — a legal monopoly over an essential service:

- **Regulated monopoly franchise** — within its service territories Entergy is the sole provider of electric distribution (and largely transmission/generation), with no direct competitor; this is among the most durable moat structures in equities.
- **Rate-base / allowed-return model** — regulators set rates to let the utility recover prudent capital investment plus an allowed ROE, converting capex into a predictable, growing earnings stream insulated from competition.
- **High barriers to entry** — building duplicate grid and generation is uneconomic and legally precluded; the franchise is effectively permanent.
- **Large-load attractiveness** — abundant Gulf-Coast land, water, and an industrial-grid backbone make Entergy a magnet for hyperscale data-center load, expanding the rate base.

The moat is wide but **regulator-dependent and capital-intensive**: returns are capped by the allowed ROE, the company is heavily leveraged (D/E ~1.9x) and rate-sensitive, and value hinges on **constructive regulation** — an adverse rate case or disallowance is the key idiosyncratic risk. It is the textbook "cyclical/leveraged" balance sheet attached to a wide regulatory moat.

## 2025-2026 Developments

- **FY2025 results** — Adjusted EPS of **$3.91** (vs $3.65 adjusted in 2024); Utility segment earned $2.28 billion ($5.06/share). Initiated **2026 adjusted EPS guidance of $4.25-$4.45** (Source: Entergy press release, verified via Perplexity (sonar), 2026-06-10).
- **Q1 2026** — Adjusted EPS $0.86 (reported $0.83); guidance reaffirmed. Entergy held its **2026 Investor Day on 2026-06-09** to lay out the long-term growth plan.
- **Meta Hyperion buildout** — The Louisiana PSC approved (4-1, August 2025) three combined-cycle gas plants totaling **2,260 MW** to serve Meta's $10B+ Richland Parish AI data center (operational target late 2028), plus a ~$1.2B, 100-mile 500kV transmission project. In March 2026 Meta agreed to fund a major expansion: **seven additional gas plants (~5.2 GW)**, taking dedicated generation for the site above 7 GW, with Entergy seeking expedited LPSC approval before end-2026.
- **Capex supercycle** — The data-center pipeline has pushed Entergy's multi-year capital plan and load-growth forecast to the top of the regulated-utility peer group, underpinning above-average EPS growth guidance.

## Growth Drivers & Catalysts

- **Data-center load supercycle** — the Meta Hyperion buildout (multi-GW dedicated gas generation + 500kV transmission) is one of the largest single load additions in US utility history; it underpins above-peer rate-base and EPS growth — the core [[ai-data-center-power-demand]] driver.
- **Rate-base growth** — generation, transmission, and storm-hardening capex converting into regulated earnings at the allowed ROE.
- **Industrial Gulf-Coast load** — LNG, petrochemical, and hydrogen demand on top of data centers.
- **Constructive regulation** — LPSC and other commissions approving capex and rate recovery (e.g. the seven-plant expansion).
- **Recurring catalysts** — LPSC rulings on the expansion, new hyperscaler load announcements, quarterly guidance updates, Investor Day targets, and hurricane season (storm-cost recovery/securitization).

## Bull Case vs Bear Case

### Bull case
- A wide regulatory moat plus the largest data-center-driven rate-base pipeline in the regulated peer group supports top-tier, visible EPS growth (2026 guidance $4.25–4.45).
- Captures the AI-power theme with utility-style downside protection — rate-base returns rather than volatile merchant prices.
- Constructive Louisiana regulation has approved the multi-GW Meta buildout; more hyperscaler load could follow.
- Defensive, dividend-paying utility that re-rates with the "power trade."

### Bear case
- Heavy leverage (D/E ~1.9x) and a large capex plan require continual debt/equity financing — dilutive and rate-sensitive.
- Returns are capped by allowed ROE; an adverse rate case or cost disallowance is a direct earnings hit.
- Concentration on a single mega-customer (Meta) and on Louisiana regulatory outcomes raises idiosyncratic risk.
- Gulf-Coast hurricane exposure: large storm-restoration costs and recovery timing are a recurring overhang.
- Rate sensitivity: rising long-end yields de-rate the bond-like utility multiple.

## Key Risks

- **Regulatory risk** — adverse rate-case outcomes, cost disallowance, or slower capex recovery cap the rate-base earnings engine.
- **Rate / duration risk** — as a leveraged regulated utility, ETR trades partly as a bond proxy; sensitive to long-term [[treasuries]] yields (see [[interest-rate-risk]]).
- **Customer / project concentration** — dependence on the Meta Hyperion load and on Louisiana regulatory approvals.
- **Storm / catastrophe exposure** — Gulf-Coast hurricanes drive large restoration costs; recovery relies on securitization and regulator support.
- **Financing / leverage** — large capex plan needs ongoing capital-markets access; equity issuance dilutes EPS.

## Valuation & How the Stock Trades

Entergy is valued as a **rate-base growth utility**, not on the headline GAAP P/E (44.8x is inflated by storm/one-off items):

- **EPS growth vs. rate-base growth** — the stock trades on adjusted-EPS guidance ($4.25–4.45 for 2026) and the durability of the data-center-driven rate-base CAGR; a higher rate-base growth rate justifies a premium regulated-utility multiple.
- **Dividend & yield** — a core income holding; the growing dividend (~2.5% historical yield) plus EPS growth is the total-return story.
- **Rate sensitivity / bond-proxy behavior** — leveraged regulated utilities are sensitive to long-end yields; ETR is a beneficiary of falling rates and a casualty of rate spikes, often more than of operating news.
- **Beta / behavior** — low-beta, defensive within equities but high-beta to interest rates; it re-rated alongside [[vistra]]/[[constellation-energy]] in the 2024-2026 "power trade" but with utility-style risk.
- **Correlated names** — [[american-electric-power]], [[southern-company]], [[dominion-energy]], [[nextera-energy]], and the XLU utilities complex.
