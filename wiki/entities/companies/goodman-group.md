**Goodman Group** (ASX: GMG) is a diversified [[real-estate|real estate]] group — structured as a stapled [[reits|REIT]] — listed on the [[asx-limited|Australian Securities Exchange]] and a constituent of the [[asx-200]] index. Goodman is one of the world's largest listed specialist industrial-property and data-centre groups, and the **largest property group on the ASX by market capitalisation** (~A$58B). The group owns, develops and manages a global portfolio of logistics and data-centre assets concentrated in major metropolitan ("infill") markets across Australia, New Zealand, Asia (including Greater China and Japan), Continental Europe, the UK and the Americas. Critically, Goodman has re-rated from a yield REIT into an **AI/data-centre infrastructure growth story** — see [[artificial-intelligence]] and the AI-capex theme.

## Business Model & Segments

Goodman's business model combines four reinforcing activities:

- **Investment Management:** Direct and co-invested ownership of income-producing logistics and data centre assets, earning base rental income.
- **Third-Party Funds Management:** Management of a global suite of wholesale real estate funds and partnerships, earning base and performance management fees from institutional capital partners such as CPP Investments, APG, and others.
- **Development:** Construction of logistics and data centre facilities on strategic land, delivering stabilised assets into the investment/funds management platforms and earning development profit.
- **Services:** Property, asset and investment management services across the portfolio.

The group's property portfolio comprises logistics and distribution centres, data centres, warehouses, light industrial, multi-storey industrial and business parks. Increasingly, Goodman's development pipeline is weighted to data centres in major metropolitan AI/cloud hubs.

### Why Goodman is not a traditional REIT

A conventional [[reits|REIT]] is valued chiefly on rental yield and net tangible assets. Goodman is a **hybrid developer/fund-manager/owner** whose value increasingly comes from development profit and funds-management fees, not just rent. The pivot of the development pipeline toward **data centres** (with a ~6.0 GW global power bank, 3.6 GW secured) ties Goodman's earnings to the AI/cloud-capex cycle — which is why it trades on a growth multiple (~36x P/E) and with higher volatility than a yield-REIT peer like [[charter-hall-retail-reit|a pure retail REIT]] or [[bwp-trust]].

## Economic Moat & Competitive Position

Goodman has one of the wider [[economic-moat|moats]] in global real estate, built on **irreplaceable infill land and secured power**:

- **Infill land bank** — a long-assembled portfolio of land in supply-constrained, high-barrier metropolitan locations (close to population/consumption and grid power) that cannot be replicated; this is the durable scarcity asset.
- **Secured power for data centres** — in an era where grid-connection capacity is the binding constraint on data-centre development, Goodman's ~3.6 GW of *secured* power (of a 6.0 GW bank) is a genuine, hard-to-replicate advantage and the crux of the AI thesis.
- **Capital partnerships & funds-management flywheel** — relationships with the world's largest institutional investors (CPP, APG) provide low-cost third-party capital, letting Goodman develop at scale while earning fees and keeping its own balance sheet lightly geared (D/E ~0.20x).
- **Development capability & scale** — an integrated global development platform that delivers complex logistics and data-centre projects most peers cannot match.

The competitive position is strong, but the moat is being tested by the capital and competition flooding into data centres globally (hyperscalers, dedicated DC developers, and infrastructure funds), which could compete down development returns over time.

## Growth Drivers & Catalysts

- **Data-centre / AI-capex super-cycle** — the central growth driver: development WIP reached ~A$14.4bn in H1 FY26 (data centres ~73%), heading toward >A$17.5bn by mid-2026.
- **Secured power conversion** — converting the 6.0 GW power bank (3.6 GW secured) into pre-leased, income-producing data centres.
- **Funds-management AUM growth** — rising assets under management lifting capital-light base and performance fees.
- **Logistics demand & automation** — customers investing in AI/robotics automation, reinforcing demand for prime large-format metropolitan logistics.
- **Recurring catalysts** — half-year (Feb) and full-year (Aug) operating-EPS guidance, development-WIP and power-bank updates, data-centre pre-leasing/power milestones, capital raises (it raised ~A$4bn equity in early 2025 to fund the DC build-out), bond-yield/cap-rate moves, and global AI-capex sentiment.

## Bull Case vs Bear Case

### Bull case
- Best-positioned listed beneficiary of the AI/data-centre build-out, with scarce **secured power** and **infill land** rivals cannot easily replicate.
- Capital-light funds-management model generates high returns on equity with a lightly geared balance sheet.
- Long development runway: WIP rising toward >A$17.5bn with data centres the majority.
- Global, diversified, institutional-grade platform; the heaviest weight in the ASX property sector.

### Bear case
- The premium ~36x multiple prices in flawless execution; any data-centre demand "digestion," delayed power, or cap-rate de-rating hits the stock hard.
- Development is cyclical and capital-intensive; the 2025 equity raise diluted holders and signals the capital intensity of the DC pivot.
- Rising bond yields lift cap rates and pressure asset values — Goodman is still a rate-sensitive property name.
- Competition for data-centre development (hyperscalers, infrastructure funds) could erode development margins.

## Key Risks

- **AI-capex cyclicality** — a slowdown or "digestion" in data-centre demand would de-rate a stock priced for growth.
- **Execution risk** — delivering a ~A$17.5bn+ development pipeline (power, construction, leasing) on time and on return.
- **Interest-rate / cap-rate risk** — higher [[interest-rates]] lift cap rates, pressuring NTA and asset valuations.
- **Capital intensity / dilution** — the DC pivot is capital-hungry; further equity raises could dilute.
- **Concentration & power** — reliance on securing grid power; concentration in a handful of large data-centre projects.

## Valuation & How the Stock Trades

Goodman trades like a **growth stock wearing a REIT label** — it is valued on development pipeline, funds-management AUM growth and the AI/data-centre option far more than on dividend yield or a simple discount to NTA. Mechanically:

- **Valuation lens** — a premium P/E (~36x) and a premium to NTA, unusual for a REIT, reflecting the data-centre growth option; operating EPS growth and development WIP are the numbers the market underwrites, not the (modest, ~1% gross) dividend yield.
- **FFO / operating EPS** — like all REITs, cash earnings are better measured by operating EPS / funds-from-operations than statutory net profit (which swings with asset revaluations — note the FY24 statutory loss versus positive operating profit).
- **Rate sensitivity** — still rate-sensitive (higher yields lift cap rates and pressure asset values), but the AI-capex growth narrative now dominates the rate story, so it can de-couple from pure-yield REITs.
- **Dividend** — a low payout/yield for a REIT because cash is reinvested into the development pipeline; this is a growth-reinvestment story, not an income story.
- **Beta & flows** — the single largest ASX stock by market cap and the heaviest [[asx-200]] property weight; it moves the index and passive AU equity/REIT funds, and trades alongside the global AI-capex complex ([[digital-realty]], [[equinix]]) as much as AU REITs.

## History

Goodman Group originated as Goodman Hardie Industrial Property Trust in 1989 in Sydney and listed on the ASX in 1995. Under the leadership of co-founder and CEO Gregory Goodman, the group progressively expanded into Asia and Europe, forming major capital partnerships and building a worldwide industrial property platform. In recent years, the group has pivoted its development pipeline materially toward data centres on the back of strong cloud and AI demand, leveraging its land bank in key metropolitan cities.

## Recent Developments

For the full year ended 30 June 2025 (FY25), Goodman delivered operating profit of A$2,311.2 million, up 13%, and operating earnings per security (EPS) of 118.0 cents, up 9.8%. For the half year ended 31 December 2025 (H1 FY26), the group reported operating profit of approximately A$1.2 billion, supported by a strong development pipeline and rising assets under management.

Key data centre updates:

- Global power bank of 6.0 gigawatts across 16 major metropolitan markets, with 3.6 GW of secured power.
- Development work-in-progress reached A$14.4 billion in H1 FY26, with data centres accounting for 73% of WIP.
- Total projected WIP across data centre and logistics projects is expected to increase to more than A$17.5 billion by 30 June 2026.
- Kraig Knight appointed to lead global data centre operations (2025).

Logistics customers are increasingly investing in AI and robotic technology to drive automation, reinforcing demand for prime, large-format metropolitan logistics facilities.
