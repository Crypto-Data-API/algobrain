## Share-Class Notes for Traders

- Class B shares carry one vote each; Class A (FOXA) shares are non-voting. The Murdoch Family Trust controls Fox through a ~40% block of Class B votes, so the public Class B float's voting power is largely symbolic — Class B typically trades at a small **discount** to Class A (fundamentals below: FOX P/E 12.4x vs FOXA 13.9x), reflecting FOXA's better liquidity.
- The FOXA/FOX spread is a standard dual-class pair trade: the gap widens and narrows with index-flow imbalances and any speculation about share-class collapses or control transactions (e.g., Murdoch trust developments settled in 2025 leaving Lachlan Murdoch in control).
- FOXA carries the more liquid options chain; FOX options are thinner. Most single-name traders use FOXA; FOX suits voting-rights or discount-capture theses.

## Business Segments

Fox Corporation is the "live-and-leaning-into-it" media company that the Murdochs retained after selling most entertainment assets to [[disney]] in 2019. Both share classes (FOX voting, FOXA non-voting) represent the same underlying business:

| Segment | What it is | Economics |
|---|---|---|
| **Cable Network Programming** | Fox News, Fox Business, FS1/FS2, Big Ten Network | The profit engine; affiliate (carriage) fees + advertising. Fox News is the dominant cable-news ratings leader |
| **Television** | The Fox broadcast network, owned-and-operated local stations | Retransmission consent fees + advertising; cyclical political/sports ad swings |
| **Tubi (& other)** | Free ad-supported streaming TV (FAST) service | Fast-growing ad revenue; reached profitability and is the company's digital-growth vehicle |
| **Fox One** | Direct-to-consumer streaming bundle of Fox's live news and sports | The DTC pivot — monetizing cord-cutters directly |

The strategic thesis is **"unbundling-resistant" live content** — news and sports are the genres viewers will not time-shift or skip, which protects affiliate fees and advertising even as scripted entertainment migrates to streaming. See [[fox-corporation-(class-a)]] for the full business narrative and 2025-2026 developments.

## Competitive Positioning / Peers

Fox competes across cable news, broadcast, sports rights, and FAST streaming. It is deliberately smaller and more focused than the diversified media conglomerates.

| Company | Overlap with Fox | Contrast |
|---|---|---|
| [[comcast]] (CMCSA) | Broadcast (NBC), cable networks, sports rights, streaming (Peacock) | Far larger, owns broadband + Universal; diversified beyond media |
| [[disney]] (DIS) | Broadcast (ABC), ESPN sports rights, streaming | Massive entertainment/parks empire; bought most of old Fox in 2019 |
| [[charter-communications]] (CHTR) | Distribution counterparty in carriage negotiations | A pipe (cable/broadband operator), not a content owner — sits on the other side of affiliate-fee deals |
| Paramount / Warner Bros. Discovery | Cable networks, broadcast, sports rights | Carry heavier debt and broad scripted libraries; more secular-decline exposure |
| [[news-corp]] (NWSA) | Sister Murdoch-controlled company (publishing, Dow Jones, REA) | Print/digital publishing, not TV; common family control |

Within the [[s-and-p-500]] [[communication-services]] sector ([[xlc]]), Fox is a relatively low-multiple, cash-generative, live-content pure play versus the larger, more leveraged, more diversified media conglomerates.

## Bull vs Bear Case

**Bull case**
- Live news + sports are the most cord-cutting-resistant content genres, protecting affiliate fees and ad revenue.
- Fox News is the cable-news ratings leader, a durable franchise with pricing power on carriage fees.
- Tubi gives genuine, profitable digital ad growth; Fox One monetizes DTC cord-cutters.
- Lean cost structure, strong free cash flow, consistent buybacks, and a clean(er) balance sheet than peers (D/E 0.55x — see Fundamentals below).
- Murdoch control resolved (Lachlan in control) removes a succession overhang.

**Bear case**
- Structural cord-cutting shrinks the cable affiliate-fee base that drives most profit.
- Sports-rights inflation: securing/renewing rights (NFL and others) is expensive and competitive, pressuring margins.
- Advertising is cyclical and lumpy (political-cycle and sports-schedule dependent — note the FY2025 vs FY2024 margin swings in the trend table below).
- Concentrated voting control via the Class B / Murdoch Family Trust limits public-shareholder influence (acutely relevant to *this* FOX line).
- Reputational/regulatory/litigation exposure tied to Fox News content.

## Valuation Framework (qualitative)

Fox is valued as a **cash-generative, live-content media company**, and the multiple is driven by the durability of three streams plus capital discipline:

1. **Affiliate / carriage fees** — recurring, contracted per-subscriber payments from distributors ([[charter-communications]], etc.). The market rewards pricing power and penalizes the pace of subscriber (cord-cut) declines. This is the most valued, most annuity-like stream.
2. **Advertising** — higher-multiple when secular-growing (Tubi/digital) but discounted where cyclical/linear (broadcast, cable). Political and sports schedules create predictable multi-year swings.
3. **Sports-rights economics** — a cost line that can be value-accretive (drives carriage and ad pricing) or value-destructive (if rights inflation outruns monetization). The market scrutinizes each major renewal.

Fox typically trades at a **discount to the broad market and to growthier media peers** (P/E ~12-14x — see Fundamentals) because of cable-decline overhang, offset by free-cash-flow strength and buybacks. The **FOX vs FOXA spread** adds a share-class layer: the voting Class B usually trades at a small discount to Class A given FOXA's superior liquidity, and that spread is itself a tradeable, mean-reverting relationship.

## Trading & Options Playbook

**Index / ETF membership**
- Both FOX and FOXA are [[s-and-p-500]] constituents and sit in the [[communication-services]] sector ETF [[xlc]]; index-rebalance and sector-rotation flows move both lines.
- The dual S&P listing means index funds hold both classes, which mechanically anchors the FOX/FOXA spread within a range.

**Volatility behavior**
- A relatively low-volatility, large-cap media name versus the meme/eVTOL names elsewhere in this cohort; [[implied-volatility]] is modest and rises mainly around earnings, major sports-rights renewals, and political-ad-cycle inflection points.
- FOX (Class B) options are **thinner** than FOXA; size and tight spreads favor FOXA for most options expression.

**Catalysts calendar**
- Quarterly earnings (affiliate-fee growth, ad trends, Tubi/Fox One metrics).
- Carriage-renewal negotiations with distributors (blackout risk/headlines).
- Sports-rights auctions and renewals (NFL and others).
- Political and major-sports calendars (predictable ad-revenue swings).
- Murdoch-trust / control-structure developments (settled 2025, but any change is a Class B-specific catalyst).

**Share-class pair trade**
- The classic expression on this line is **long/short FOX vs FOXA** to trade the voting-discount spread — widening on index-flow imbalances or control-event speculation, narrowing on liquidity normalization.

## Capital Return Policy

- **Dividend:** Fox pays a modest semi-annual cash dividend (low payout ratio — see Fundamentals, payout ~12), leaving ample cash for buybacks.
- **Buybacks:** the primary capital-return tool; Fox has run sustained, large share-repurchase programs across both classes, a meaningful driver of per-share metrics and the elevated ROE shown in the Fundamentals block.
- **Balance sheet:** conservative leverage (D/E 0.55x) preserves buyback capacity and rights-acquisition firepower through the cycle.

## Risks

- **Secular cord-cutting** eroding the affiliate-fee base.
- **Sports-rights inflation** compressing margins on renewal.
- **Advertising cyclicality** (political/sports schedule dependence, macro ad pullbacks).
- **Concentrated Murdoch voting control** limiting public Class B shareholder influence and creating event risk around the control structure.
- **Litigation / reputational** exposure tied to news content.
- **Liquidity discount** specific to the FOX (Class B) line versus FOXA.
