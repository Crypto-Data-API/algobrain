Artrya Limited (ASX: AYA) is an Australian medical-AI company whose Salix platform uses artificial intelligence to detect and quantify coronary artery disease (CAD) from coronary CT angiography (CCTA) scans in near real time. It is a speculative micro-cap on the [[asx-limited|ASX]] whose share price is driven by US FDA regulatory milestones and the ramp of per-scan reimbursement revenue.

## Business Overview

Artrya's Salix Coronary Anatomy platform and the newer Salix Coronary Plaque module analyze CCTA scans at the point of care to assess stenosis and plaque, aiming to speed CAD diagnosis and reduce downstream costs. The commercial model is per-scan/SaaS fees, with the US — its largest addressable market — the strategic priority. The company has signed pilot/partnership deals with mid-sized US hospital systems (e.g., Tanner Health).

## Product & Technology

The Salix family is a coronary-CT-angiography (CCTA) analysis suite built around deep-learning image processing. The two disclosed modules address adjacent steps in the cardiac-imaging workflow:

| Module | What it does | Clinical purpose |
|---|---|---|
| **Salix Coronary Anatomy** | Segments the coronary tree from a CCTA scan and quantifies luminal stenosis | Triages obstructive vs non-obstructive coronary artery disease |
| **Salix Coronary Plaque** | Characterises and quantifies atherosclerotic plaque (calcified vs non-calcified, plaque burden) | Risk stratification beyond simple stenosis; supports preventive treatment decisions |

The clinical rationale is that traditional CCTA reads are time-consuming and operator-dependent; automating segmentation and plaque quantification promises faster turnaround, more reproducible reads, and earlier identification of high-risk-but-non-obstructive disease that visual reads can miss. Salix is positioned as a point-of-care tool that integrates into existing CCTA workflows rather than requiring new hardware, which lowers the adoption barrier for hospital systems.

The business model is **per-scan / SaaS reimbursement** rather than a capital-equipment sale. In the US, plaque-quantification AI sits under emerging CPT/reimbursement pathways; management has guided to reimbursement in the order of US$950 per assessment (figure as disclosed by the company — see [[2025–2026 Developments]]). Revenue therefore scales with installed-base scan volume, not licence count, which makes the **scan-volume ramp** the single most important commercial metric for the equity story. This is a classic "regulatory-clearance-then-reimbursement-then-volume" medtech adoption curve — each stage is a discrete catalyst and a discrete point of failure.

## Competitive Positioning & Peers

Coronary-CT AI is a crowded and well-funded niche; Artrya is one of the smallest listed players and competes against larger, often better-capitalised private firms.

| Company | Listing | Focus | Relative scale |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Artrya (AYA)** | ASX (micro-cap) | CCTA stenosis + plaque (Salix) | Smallest; early-revenue |
| **HeartFlow** | US-listed | FFR-CT (functional flow modelling from CCTA); large reimbursement footprint | Category leader; well-capitalised |
| **Cleerly** | Private (US) | AI plaque quantification / "Cleerly score" | Large private funding rounds |
| **Elucid** | Private (US) | Plaque characterisation from CCTA | Venture-backed |

Implications for the thesis:
- **Reimbursement is the moat, not the algorithm.** The incumbents' advantage is established CPT codes, payer relationships, and clinical-evidence libraries — areas where a micro-cap is structurally disadvantaged and where catch-up is slow and capital-intensive.
- Artrya's differentiation argument rests on workflow integration, speed, and a combined anatomy-plus-plaque offering at a competitive per-scan price. This is plausible but unproven at scale.
- Sentiment is correlated with the broader [[ai-healthcare|AI healthcare]] and medical-imaging theme; a private-peer funding round or an incumbent reimbursement win can move AYA without any AYA-specific news.

## Bull vs Bear Case

**Bull case**
- FDA 510(k) clearance of the Plaque module (Aug 2025) removes the binary regulatory gate for the headline product and opens the US per-scan revenue path.
- Large and growing CCTA market; plaque quantification is shifting from research to standard-of-care, expanding the addressable scan pool.
- Combined anatomy + plaque offering and point-of-care integration could win mid-tier hospital systems that the larger incumbents under-serve.
- Tiny float means a genuine commercial inflection (a few large hospital contracts converting to recurring scan revenue) could re-rate the stock sharply.

**Bear case**
- Early-revenue with ongoing cash burn; almost certainly needs further capital raises, and micro-cap placements are typically dilutive and discounted.
- Reimbursement adoption in US hospitals is slow, bureaucratic, and outside the company's control; clearance does not equal payment.
- Competes against far larger, better-funded incumbents (HeartFlow, Cleerly) with entrenched payer relationships.
- Thin liquidity and binary catalysts mean drawdowns can be severe and hard to exit; a single delayed milestone or capital raise can halve sentiment.
- Going-concern / runway risk is ever-present until the company demonstrates self-sustaining scan-revenue cashflow.

## Capital Structure & Dilution Risk

- AYA is **pre-profit and cash-consuming**; the funding model for ASX micro-cap medtech is recurring equity raises (placements, entitlement offers, share purchase plans) rather than debt.
- Expect **dilution as the base case**, not the exception. Placements are usually priced at a discount to the prevailing price and often accompany — or pre-empt — positive newsflow.
- **Register concentration:** small-cap registers are typically dominated by founders, a handful of institutions/family offices, and retail. This means low free float (amplifying volatility) and the risk that a large holder selling into thin volume drives an outsized move.
- **Escrow / placement cliffs:** watch for escrowed founder/early-investor stock coming free and for placement shortfall shares, both of which add latent supply.
- A raise is itself a catalyst (often negative on announcement due to the discount, sometimes positive if it removes runway overhang). Always re-check the **current** share count after any raise before sizing a position.

## Trading Playbook (ASX micro-cap)

- **Liquidity first.** AYA is thinly traded; average daily turnover is small relative to position sizes that matter. Use **limit orders**, expect **wide spreads**, and assume you may not be able to exit at the screen price in a fast move. Position size for the *exit*, not the entry.
- **No listed options market.** There is no meaningful exchange-traded options chain for AYA — hedging or leverage via listed options is not available. Do not assume one exists.
- **Catalyst-driven, binary moves.** Expect large single-day percentage moves around FDA clearances/label expansions, US hospital contract wins, reimbursement-coding news, scan-revenue disclosures, and capital raises. These are step-changes, not trends.
- **Register concentration & free float.** Low free float means modest order flow drives price; news can gap the stock through stop levels.
- **Trading halts.** ASX small-caps frequently enter trading halts ahead of announcements and capital raises — a halt itself signals a pending material event, and you cannot trade through it.
- **Not in the [[asx-200]].** AYA sits in the broader small/micro-cap universe (All Ordinaries tail), so it lacks index-fund support and screens out of many institutional mandates — a structural liquidity headwind.

## Risks

- **Going-concern / runway:** ongoing cash burn ahead of self-sustaining revenue; risk of a near-term, dilutive raise or, in a stress scenario, financing difficulty.
- **Reimbursement adoption:** US hospital uptake of paid per-scan assessment is slow and outside the company's control; FDA clearance does not guarantee payment or volume.
- **Competition:** larger, better-funded incumbents (HeartFlow, Cleerly, Elucid) with entrenched payer relationships.
- **Dilution:** recurring discounted equity raises are the expected funding path. See [[Capital Structure & Dilution Risk]].
- **Liquidity / volatility:** micro-cap thin trading, wide spreads, gap risk, frequent trading halts; difficult to exit in size.
- **Regulatory & clinical:** future clearances/label expansions are not assured; adverse clinical-evidence findings could undermine the value proposition.
- **Listing compliance:** as with any ASX micro-cap, ongoing compliance with listing rules and continuous-disclosure obligations is a background risk if cash or the business deteriorates.
