Myomo, Inc. (NYSE American: MYO) is a micro-cap medical device company whose **MyoPro** powered arm brace (myoelectric orthosis) restores function to paralyzed or weakened arms after stroke, brachial plexus injury, or other neurological conditions. The trading story is a reimbursement story: revenue stepped up sharply after Medicare established lump-sum fee-schedule payment for MyoPro in 2024, and the stock trades on CMS policy, payer authorization rates, and the path to cash-flow breakeven.

> **Speculative micro-cap warning.** MYO is a single-product, not-yet-profitable micro-cap with a history of dilutive offerings; its revenue depends heavily on Medicare/CMS reimbursement policy that can change. Nothing here is investment advice. Size for volatility and dilution risk.

## AI Involvement

[[artificial-intelligence|AI]]-powered myoelectric orthoses for neurological conditions — the device reads EMG signals from the user's own muscles and uses pattern-recognition software to drive motorized arm/hand movement.

## Business Overview

Myomo is spun out of MIT research (founded 2004). It sells the MyoPro line (Motion W and Motion G models) direct-to-patient and through orthotics & prosthetics providers, with a candidate pipeline driven by direct-to-consumer advertising. Reimbursement anchors the model: CMS pays lump-sum fees under HCPCS codes **L8701 and L8702** — 2026 fee-schedule amounts approximately **$34,970 (Motion W)** and **$68,800 (Motion G)**. Medicare Part B patients accounted for 54% of 2025 revenue; Medicare Advantage plans about 20% of product revenue. The company also operates in Germany (statutory health insurance coverage) and is expanding internationally.

## Product & Reimbursement Engine (detail)

MyoPro is a powered upper-limb orthosis: surface EMG electrodes read residual muscle signals, a pattern-recognition controller interprets intent, and motors actuate the elbow/hand so the user moves their own arm. It is prescribed for chronic neuromuscular impairment (post-stroke, brachial plexus injury, ALS, MS, SCI) where no alternative restores function.

The investable story is **less about the device and more about the reimbursement pipeline**:

| Funnel stage | What happens | Why it matters to the stock |
|---|---|---|
| Candidate generation | Direct-to-consumer advertising drives patient enquiries | Top of funnel; marketing spend is a swing cost |
| Clinical evaluation | Therapist confirms candidacy and fits the device | Conversion-rate lever |
| Insurance authorization | Prior authorization with Medicare Part B / Medicare Advantage / commercial payers | The bottleneck; authorization rates are the key operating KPI |
| Revenue recognition | Recognized on delivery/acceptance; lump-sum CMS fee under L8701/L8702 | Drives quarterly lumpiness |

Because Medicare Part B was ~54% of 2025 revenue and Medicare Advantage ~20%, the company is **structurally exposed to CMS policy** — both the annual fee-schedule amounts and Medicare Advantage authorization behaviour. Lifting MA authorization rates (the goal of the University of Utah RCT) is the single biggest organic growth lever.

## Competitive Positioning

MyoPro has limited direct competition: it is the only powered myoelectric arm orthosis with established Medicare reimbursement codes, which is itself the moat. The realistic substitutes are passive/static braces, conventional rehabilitation therapy, and "do nothing." That gives a quasi-category-of-one position, but it also means the addressable market and payer policy — not competitive share — determine the trajectory. ## Bull vs Bear Case

**Bull case**

- Established, high-value Medicare reimbursement (L8701/L8702 at ~$34,970 / ~$68,800) — the 2024 lump-sum policy transformed unit economics and drove the FY2025 revenue step-up to **$40.9M (+26%)**.
- Quasi-monopoly product with a defensible reimbursement moat; large unaddressed stroke-survivor population.
- Visible path to breakeven: 2026 guide of **$43-46M** with opex growth at ~half of revenue growth, ~20% material-cost reduction program, and cash burn cut roughly in half vs 2025.
- RCT data could lift Medicare Advantage authorization rates and unlock a larger payer pool.
- International optionality (Germany statutory coverage, further expansion).

**Bear case**

- **Single-product, single-payer-system concentration:** ~74% of revenue tied to Medicare Part B + Advantage; a CMS fee cut or authorization tightening hits the whole P&L.
- Still not profitable; reliant on continued execution to reach breakeven before cash runs short.
- History of dilutive equity offerings — per-share value leaks even as revenue grows.
- Demonstrated quarterly lumpiness: Q4 2025 revenue $11.4M was **down 6% YoY**, the first soft quarter after the Medicare ramp, and it hit the stock.
- Reimbursement-policy risk is exogenous and binary in a way management cannot control.

- **FY2025:** revenue **$40.9 million, up 26%** vs 2024. Q4 2025 revenue was $11.4 million, down 6% YoY (208 MyoPro revenue units, ASP ~$54,600) — the first soft quarter after the big Medicare ramp, which knocked the stock.
- **2026 guidance (reiterated Q1 2026):** revenue of **$43-46 million** (~10% growth), opex growth at roughly half the rate of revenue growth, and cash burn cut roughly in half vs 2025.
- **Q1 2026:** revenue $10.1 million, up 3% YoY on higher ASP; Q2 2026 guided to $10.3-10.8 million.
- **Cost program:** initiatives underway to cut material costs ~20%, full benefit expected 2H 2026.
- **Clinical:** randomized controlled trial at the University of Utah (18 of 50 subjects enrolled as of early 2026) aimed at lifting Medicare Advantage authorization rates.

## Trading Relevance

MYO is a thinly traded micro cap (market cap roughly $50-100 million as of June 2026, approximate) with limited options liquidity and high headline sensitivity. Catalysts: quarterly results vs the $43-46M guide, annual CMS fee-schedule updates (typically published in Q4 for the following year), Medicare Advantage coverage decisions, RCT data, and any capital raise (the company has a history of dilutive offerings). Key risks: reimbursement changes, slowing pipeline conversion, single-product concentration, and cash burn before reaching breakeven.

## Valuation Framework (qualitative)

Unlike most names in this cohort, MYO has *real, growing* revenue, so it can be framed on growth-toward-breakeven rather than pure survivability — but it is still pre-profit:

- **Revenue trajectory vs guide** — FY2025 $40.9M (+26%); 2026 guide $43-46M (~10% growth). The deceleration from +26% to ~10% is the central debate; quarterly prints vs the guide drive re-rating.
- **Path to breakeven** — the bull thesis is opex growth < half of revenue growth + ~20% material-cost cuts (full benefit 2H 2026) + cash burn roughly halved vs 2025. Hitting breakeven before needing capital is the value-inflection.
- **Cash runway & burn** — micro-cap with ongoing (shrinking) burn; the timing of breakeven vs cash position determines whether another raise is needed. Dilution history means the market prices in raise risk.
- **Reimbursement as the valuation anchor** — the entire multiple is hostage to CMS L8701/L8702 fee levels and Medicare Advantage authorization rates; these are the upside (RCT-driven) and the tail risk (policy cut).
- **NYSE American listing-compliance** — as a micro-cap, sustained price weakness can raise continued-listing concerns (price/equity standards); not currently the headline issue but a structural micro-cap consideration.

## Capital Structure & Dilution Risk

- **Track record of dilutive equity offerings** — the primary historical funding mechanism for an unprofitable micro-cap; every raise expands the share count.
- The investment debate is fundamentally *will revenue growth + cost cuts reach breakeven before the next raise is forced?* If yes, dilution risk fades; if no, expect another offering. See [[dilution]].
- Headline sensitivity means raises are often timed to strength.

## Trading & Options Playbook

- **Microstructure:** thinly traded micro-cap, high headline sensitivity, large moves around quarterly prints and CMS news. Limited options liquidity — thin chain, wide spreads, elevated IV into catalysts.
- **Squeeze / volatility behaviour:** low float + retail attention can produce sharp momentum moves and LULD halts; see [[short-squeeze]]. Less of a pure squeeze name than the pre-revenue cohort because it has a real fundamental anchor, but still gappy.
- **Calendar catalysts:** quarterly results vs the $43-46M guide; **annual CMS fee-schedule updates (published Q4 for the following year)** — the single most important recurring event; Medicare Advantage coverage decisions; University of Utah RCT data; capital raises (negative).
- **Asymmetry:** binary CMS policy outcomes argue for defined-risk option structures over linear exposure when carrying through fee-schedule releases.

## Risks

- **Reimbursement/CMS policy** — fee-schedule cuts or authorization tightening; the dominant exogenous risk given ~74% Medicare exposure.
- **Single-product concentration** — MyoPro is essentially the whole company.
- **Pipeline conversion** — authorization bottleneck; soft conversion shows up immediately (cf. Q4 2025 -6% YoY).
- **Cash burn before breakeven** — execution risk on the cost-reduction and growth plan.
- **Dilution** — history of equity offerings.
- **Liquidity** — thin float and options; gap risk.
