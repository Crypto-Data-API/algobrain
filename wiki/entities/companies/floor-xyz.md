Floor (floor.xyz) was a mobile-first NFT portfolio tracking and discovery app that launched in 2021 at the peak of the NFT cycle, later pivoting to NFT yield and liquidity strategies before announcing a full shutdown effective January 31, 2026. It is widely cited as a cautionary example of how NFT-native tooling businesses struggled to monetize during the post-2022 contraction.

## History and founding

Floor launched in 2021 as an iOS-first app focused on letting NFT collectors view their portfolios, track floor prices across collections on [[opensea]] and other venues, and receive price alerts. The product gained traction during the 2021-2022 bull market, when mobile-accessible portfolio tracking for Ethereum NFTs was underserved.

The company raised a reported Series A of roughly $18M, backed by crypto-native and consumer-app investors. Funding supported an expansion from pure tracking into social and discovery features, and later into NFT-backed yield and liquidity products.

## What it did

- **Portfolio tracker**: Mobile app that indexed an address and displayed NFT holdings, estimated floor value, profit/loss, and trait-level data.
- **Price alerts and watchlists**: Push notifications for floor price movements, listings, and collection events.
- **Discovery feed**: Ranked trending collections and wallets.
- **Yield / deep liquidity pivot**: In the later phase of the business, Floor launched products aimed at earning yield on NFTs and providing deeper liquidity for collections beyond the top names, positioning closer to products like [[nftfi]] and other NFT financialization infrastructure.

## Trading relevance

- **Mobile-first portfolio management**: Floor was one of the few credible mobile tools for active NFT traders to monitor positions, complementing desktop-focused tools like [[blur]] and [[magic-eden]]. Traders accustomed to checking floor prices and PnL on mobile now need to migrate to general crypto portfolio trackers.
- **Shutdown as a market signal**: Floor's closure in January 2026, despite substantial funding and a recognizable brand, is a data point on the economics of NFT-dependent SaaS/consumer apps in a contracted market. Traders evaluating NFT tooling vendors should weigh the ongoing-viability risk demonstrated by this case.
- **Case study on vertical-specific tooling**: Floor's trajectory illustrates that monetization paths for NFT-only tooling (subscriptions, take rate on trades, token models) have not been durable through a full cycle.

## Current status

Floor announced the shutdown of its consumer interface effective January 31, 2026. Per the floor.xyz homepage notice ("THE FLOOR INTERFACE IS BEING SHUT DOWN ON JANUARY 31ST, 2026"), users holding the FLOOR token were instructed to unstake and migrate their tokens before the deadline. As of April 2026:

- The **Floor interface is no longer operational** for portfolio tracking or alerts.
- **FLOOR token holders** were directed to unstake and migrate ahead of the shutdown deadline.
- **User data** should be considered subject to the terms under which shutdown notices were issued; traders who used Floor for record-keeping should retain any exported transaction history independently.
- **No known successor product** has been launched by the same team at the time of this page's creation.

Traders looking for equivalent mobile NFT portfolio tracking in 2026 typically fall back on general multi-chain portfolio trackers that also index NFTs, rather than dedicated NFT-only mobile apps.
