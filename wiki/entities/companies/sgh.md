**SGH Limited** (ASX: SGH) is an industrial distribution company listed on the [[asx-limited|Australian Securities Exchange]] and a constituent of the [[asx-200]] index. SGH Limited (ASX: SGH, formerly SVW) is an Australian diversified operating and investment conglomerate headquartered in Sydney, New South Wales. Formed in its modern structure in 2010 via the merger of Australian Capital Equity with Seven Network Limited, SGH has a market capitalisation of approximately A$16.91 billion and is a member of the S&P/ASX 50.

## Business Overview & Segments

SGH is a diversified operating-and-investment conglomerate structured across three reported segments — **Industrial Services**, **Energy** and **Media** — with Industrial Services (WesTrac + Boral + Coates) the overwhelming earnings driver and the strategic core of the group. The Energy and Media legs are largely investment stakes (Beach Energy, Seven West Media) rather than wholly-owned operating businesses. The portfolio is anchored by businesses that hold #1 or franchise positions in their Australian markets, which is the source of SGH's [[economic-moat|economic moat]] (see below).

| Segment / business | What it is | Role in group |
|---|---|---|
| **WesTrac** (Industrial Services) | Sole authorised [[caterpillar\|Caterpillar]] dealer across WA, NSW and the ACT — sells and services mining, construction and heavy equipment, plus parts, technology and field service | The crown jewel: largest single profit pool, with a recurring parts-and-service annuity off a vast installed equipment base |
| **Boral** (Industrial Services) | Australia's largest integrated construction-materials supplier — quarry products, cement, concrete and asphalt. SGH took 70% in July 2021 and moved to 100% in July 2024 | Scale construction-materials play; the recent margin-turnaround story and a large source of group EBIT growth |

| **Allight** (Industrial Services) | Lighting and power-equipment manufacturer | Smaller specialist industrial product line |

| **Seven West Media** (Media) | Interests in SWM — the Seven Network free-to-air TV network, *The West Australian* and associated digital assets | Legacy media exposure; strategically de-emphasised relative to industrials |

## Economic Moat & Competitive Position

SGH's appeal rests not on the conglomerate wrapper but on the franchise quality of its underlying [[industrials]] businesses, each of which holds a leading or protected position in its Australian market:

- **WesTrac — exclusive [[caterpillar|Caterpillar]] dealership territories.** WesTrac is the *sole* authorised Caterpillar dealer in WA, NSW and the ACT. This is a genuine franchise [[economic-moat|moat]]: Caterpillar's dealer-territory model grants exclusivity within a region, so within-territory there is no rival Cat dealer. The durable advantage is the **parts-and-service annuity** — a huge installed base of Cat machines in the WA/NSW mining and construction sector generates decades of recurring, high-margin aftermarket revenue, and operators face high [[switching-costs]] given fleet standardisation, parts availability and resale value.
- **Boral — #1 scale in Australian construction materials.** Construction materials is a logistics business: cement, aggregates and concrete are heavy and low-value-to-weight, so the economics are dominated by proximity of quarries and plants to demand. Boral's national footprint of quarry reserves, cement capacity and concrete batching plants is difficult and slow to replicate (permitting, planning, scarce reserves), giving local pricing power.
- **Coates — #1 equipment-hire scale and network density.** A dense national branch-and-fleet network lets Coates service large infrastructure projects nationally with utilisation and logistics advantages that sub-scale hirers cannot match.

The group-level question is whether this collection of good businesses is worth more inside the conglomerate (capital-allocation discipline, cross-cycle funding) or trades at a **conglomerate discount** to its sum of the parts.

## Growth Drivers & Catalysts

- **Australian mining-capex cycle** — WesTrac is the most direct beneficiary: new-machine sales and, more durably, the parts-and-service annuity scale with WA/NSW mining activity and fleet utilisation.
- **Infrastructure & construction pipeline** — Boral (materials) and Coates (hire) are geared to Australia's road, rail and civil-infrastructure spend.
- **Boral integration synergies & margin turnaround** — having moved to 100% ownership in July 2024, SGH is extracting pricing traction and margin gains; in FY25 Boral delivered a standout ~26% EBIT increase on pricing and margin improvement.
- **Beach Energy oil & gas prices** — the ~30% stake provides leveraged exposure to energy prices and Beach's production growth.
- **Media consolidation** — in November 2025, regulatory approval was granted for Seven West Media's merger with Southern Cross Austereo, reshaping SGH's media exposure.
- **FY25 result (context):** revenue A$10.74 billion (up 1.3% on FY24), underlying EBIT A$1.54 billion (up 8.3%), net profit A$924 million; WesTrac posted ~4% revenue growth and ~A$639m EBIT, Boral a ~26% EBIT lift; group operating cash flow surged ~49% to ~A$1.95b with all industrial-services divisions converting cash above 90%. SGH declared an interim dividend of 32 cents per share (up ~7%).
- **Recurring catalysts** — half-year/full-year results (Feb/Aug), Australian mining-capex and commodity-price prints, infrastructure-pipeline and construction-materials demand reads, Boral synergy delivery, Beach Energy oil/gas prices, and Stokes-family/board succession news.

## Bull Case vs Bear Case

### Bull case
- WesTrac is a **durable franchise** — exclusive Cat territories plus a recurring, high-margin parts-and-service annuity off a vast installed base.
- Boral's **margin turnaround** is delivering, with full ownership letting SGH capture 100% of the upside and synergies.
- **Infrastructure tailwind** — a long Australian civil-infrastructure pipeline supports Boral and Coates volumes.
- **Proven Stokes capital allocation** — a long record of well-timed, value-accretive acquisitions (Coates, Beach, Boral) and disciplined balance-sheet management.
- **Strong cash generation** — group operating cash flow surged ~49% in FY25 with >90% cash conversion across industrial-services divisions.

### Bear case
- **Cyclical exposure** — earnings ride the mining-capex and construction cycles; a synchronized downturn hits WesTrac, Boral and Coates together.
- **Family-control overhang** — ACE/Stokes control limits free float and minority-holder leverage; minorities are along for the ride on capital-allocation decisions.
- **Conglomerate discount** — diversified industrials + energy + media can trade below the sum of the parts.
- **Leverage from the Boral acquisition** — moving to 100% of Boral added balance-sheet gearing that must be serviced through the cycle.
- **Beach Energy and media drag** — the energy stake adds commodity-price volatility and the media leg faces structural free-to-air decline.

## Key Risks

- **Commodity / mining-capex cycle** — the single biggest swing factor; a WA mining-capex slowdown compresses WesTrac new-machine sales and, eventually, the parts annuity.
- **Construction downturn** — higher rates or a housing/infrastructure slowdown hits Boral materials volumes and Coates hire utilisation.
- **Family-control overhang** — Australian Capital Equity (the Stokes vehicle) controls a large stake, reducing free float, liquidity and minority influence.
- **Balance-sheet leverage** — gearing taken on for the Boral mop-up must be serviced through any cyclical trough.
- **Media structural decline** — the Seven West Media exposure faces secular free-to-air advertising erosion.
- **Succession** — Chairman Terry James Davis is set to retire in 2026; founder/patriarch Kerry Stokes' eventual succession is a long-dated governance watch item.

## Recent Developments

For FY25 (year to 30 June 2025), SGH delivered revenue of A$10.74 billion (up 1.3% on FY24), underlying EBIT of A$1.54 billion (up 8.3%) and net profit of A$924 million, reflecting synergies from the Boral integration and resilient demand in mining and infrastructure.

In November 2025, regulatory approval was granted for Seven West Media's merger with Southern Cross Austereo, consolidating SGH's media exposure. SGH continues to benefit from strong infrastructure and mining investment cycles in Australia and has indicated continued focus on industrial services as its primary growth engine.

Within FY25, WesTrac posted ~4% revenue growth and ~A$639m EBIT on strong capital-equipment sales, while Boral delivered a standout ~26% EBIT increase on pricing traction and margin improvement. Group operating cash flow surged ~49% to ~A$1.95b with all industrial-services divisions converting cash above 90%. SGH declared an interim dividend of 32 cents per share (up ~7%).

Leadership transitions include Chairman Terry James Davis (appointed November 2021) who is set to retire in 2026, with an orderly succession process underway.

## Valuation & How the Stock Trades

SGH is valued as a diversified [[industrials]] conglomerate, which shapes both the analytical lens and the trading behaviour:

- **Sum-of-the-parts / conglomerate-discount lens** — the cleanest way to value SGH is to value WesTrac, Boral and Coates as operating businesses, add the market value of the Beach Energy and Seven West Media stakes, and net off corporate debt. The security frequently trades at a **conglomerate discount** to that sum; a narrowing of the discount (or a portfolio simplification) is a recurring re-rating thesis.
- **Cyclical earnings gearing** — the stock is a leveraged play on the Australian **mining-capex** and **infrastructure/construction** cycles via WesTrac, Boral and Coates; it tends to track commodity-price and construction-activity sentiment more than the broad index.
- **Controlled free float / liquidity** — Australian Capital Equity (the Stokes family vehicle) holds a large controlling stake, which **reduces free float and on-market liquidity** and limits minority-holder influence. This makes SGH a lower-turnover name than its ~A$17B market cap implies and is a structural feature, not a temporary one.
- **Dividend + franking** — SGH pays semi-annual dividends (interim 32c in FY25, up ~7%) carrying franking credits, materially improving the after-tax yield for Australian-resident holders.
- **Beta** — geared to the commodity/construction cycle; behaves cyclically rather than defensively.
- **Index membership**: [[asx-200]] and S&P/ASX 50 constituent; one of the larger ASX [[industrials]] by market cap (~A$17B).
- **Catalysts**: half-year/full-year results (Feb/Aug), Australian mining-capex and commodity-price trends, infrastructure pipeline and construction-materials demand, Boral integration synergies, Beach Energy oil/gas prices, and the Stokes-family control overhang.
- **Correlated names**: ASX industrials/mining-services peers [[aurizon]], [[als]], [[monadelphous]], [[emeco]], [[austal]]; construction-materials peers [[james-hardie]], [[adbri]]; [[caterpillar|Caterpillar]]-cycle exposure; Beach Energy (BPT) for the energy leg.

## Leadership

SGH is led by Managing Director and Chief Executive Officer Ryan Stokes AO, son of Kerry Stokes and in the role since 2015. Kerry Stokes AC remains Executive Chairman via ACE's controlling interest. Terry James Davis is Independent Chairman, having been appointed in November 2021; he is set to retire in 2026. The wider Board and executive team comprise leaders with backgrounds in industrials, construction, energy, media, capital markets and private equity.
