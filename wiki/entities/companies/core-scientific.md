Core Scientific Inc. (NASDAQ: CORZ) is an American digital infrastructure company founded in 2017 in Seattle and now headquartered in Austin, Texas. Originally focused on [[bitcoin]] mining, Core Scientific has pivoted to become a major provider of high-performance computing (HPC) hosting for AI workloads, leveraging its large-scale, energized data center footprint. The company emerged from Chapter 11 bankruptcy in January 2024 and relisted on NASDAQ; in October 2025 its shareholders **rejected a ~$9B all-stock takeover by [[coreweave]]**, leaving CORZ independent with roughly $10B of contracted HPC hosting revenue — and one of the most actively traded retail options names at the bitcoin/AI crossover.

## Business Overview

Core Scientific owns and operates large grid-connected data center campuses (Texas, Oklahoma, Georgia, North Dakota and elsewhere) originally built for bitcoin mining. The strategic pivot is converting that energized capacity into **AI/HPC colocation** — long-dated, take-or-pay hosting contracts where the customer (chiefly CoreWeave) supplies the GPUs and Core Scientific supplies powered shells, cooling and operations. Legacy self-mining and hosted bitcoin mining are being wound down or monetized to fund the conversion. The scarce asset is contracted, energized megawatts: in an interconnection-queue-constrained market, existing power access is the moat.

## Business Model

Core Scientific is the cohort's clearest example of a **mining-to-AI-landlord conversion**, and it is the furthest along on contracted backlog:

- **AI/HPC colocation (primary engine).** Long-dated, **take-or-pay** hosting contracts where the tenant (chiefly [[coreweave]]) supplies GPUs and Core Scientific supplies powered shells, cooling, and operations. Take-or-pay means revenue is contracted whether or not the tenant fully utilizes the capacity — the most bond-like, infrastructure-grade cash flow in the group, which is why the market values it on an infrastructure/REIT-style multiple rather than a mining multiple. See [[data-center]] and [[ai-data-center-power-demand]].
- **Bitcoin mining (legacy, being wound down/monetized).** Self-mining and hosted mining convert power into hashrate into BTC; unit economics turn on **fleet efficiency (J/TH)**, **all-in power cost ($/kWh)**, and **hash price** (BTC price × network difficulty). CORZ is deliberately shrinking this to free up energized megawatts for higher-value HPC and to fund the conversion (e.g., the March 2026 BTC sale).
- **The scarce asset: contracted, energized, interconnected megawatts.** In an interconnection-queue-constrained market, *existing* power access is the moat — the reason a company that exited Chapter 11 in early 2024 could attract a ~$9B buyout bid and ~$10B of contracted hosting revenue.

The throughline matches the cohort ([[mara]], [[hut-8]], [[terawulf]], [[applied-digital]], [[iren]], [[hive-digital-technologies]]): migrate from *price-taker on BTC* to *landlord of compute*. CORZ's distinctive position is that the conversion is largely *done on paper* — the backlog is contracted; the remaining work is energization and customer diversification.

## 2025–2026 Developments

- **July 2025:** CoreWeave agreed to acquire Core Scientific in an all-stock deal valued near **$9B** (~$20.40/share at announcement).
- **30 October 2025:** shareholders **voted the deal down** — proxy advisers and large holders (with Jefferies among analysts flagging the likely rejection) argued the fixed exchange ratio undervalued CORZ after CRWV's stock slid; the merger agreement was terminated the same day.
- **Post-termination:** the commercial relationship continued — CoreWeave exercised its **final ~120 MW contract option**, taking contracted CoreWeave revenue to roughly $10B. Core Scientific refocused as an independent AI data-center developer.
- **Q1 2026:** reported large-scale AI-focused expansions in **Texas and Oklahoma**, supported by multi-billion-dollar hosting contracts and ~$3.3B of fresh financing; sold ~$175M of bitcoin in March 2026 to fund the buildout.

## Bull vs Bear Case

**Bull case**
- Largest, highest-quality contracted backlog in the cohort (~$10B, take-or-pay) — bond-like, infrastructure-grade revenue.
- The rejected ~$9B CoreWeave bid validated standalone strategic value; renewed M&A interest is live optionality.
- ~$3.3B of financing in place to execute the buildout; energized, interconnected megawatts are a scarce, financeable moat.
- Successfully reorganized post-Chapter 11 and re-rated as a credible AI data-center developer.

**Bear case**
- Customer concentration: CoreWeave dominates the book; any CRWV stumble or renegotiation is a single point of failure.
- Capital intensity and ~$3.3B of new debt/converts add leverage and interest expense during the buildout.
- BTC drawdowns still pressure the residual mining business and overall cohort sentiment.
- Execution/energization risk on multi-MW conversions; GAAP profitability remains lumpy (warrant/convert marks).
- High beta and basket membership mean sharp drawdowns in risk-off / crypto-down regimes.

## Capital Structure & Dilution Risk

- **Debt and convertible financing.** CORZ backed the AI buildout with ~$3.3B of capital (debt/convertible raises through 2025-2026). This adds leverage and interest expense and creates convertible overhang (potential future dilution plus hedging flows).
- **Bitcoin monetization as a funding lever.** Sold 1,992 BTC (~$175M) in March 2026 to fund the HPC transition — using the residual reserve to reduce reliance on equity at the margin.
- **Post-bankruptcy share base.** Having reorganized through Chapter 11, the equity/warrant structure carries dilution mechanics (warrant marks drive lumpy GAAP results); always normalize per-share metrics for warrants and converts.
- **Implication for traders:** track the financing/convert calendar and any new raises alongside customer-diversification wins; raises into weakness are recurring drawdown catalysts, while contract wins and self-funding are de-risking signals.

## Risks

- **Customer concentration** — CoreWeave dominates the backlog; a CRWV stumble or renegotiation is the central single-point risk.
- **Capital intensity & leverage** — ~$3.3B financing adds interest expense and convertible overhang.
- **BTC drawdowns** — pressure the residual mining business and cohort sentiment.
- **Execution / energization risk** — converting contracted MW into operational, revenue-producing capacity on schedule.
- **GAAP volatility** — warrant/convert marks and conversion capex make reported earnings lumpy and a poor guide to the business.
