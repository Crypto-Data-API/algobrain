Qorvo, Inc. (NASDAQ: QRVO) is a US semiconductor company specializing in radio-frequency (RF) systems — power amplifiers, filters and front-end modules used in smartphones, defense/aerospace, Wi-Fi, and connectivity. For traders it is a mid-cap RF chip name defined in 2025-2026 by two things: a heavy revenue concentration in **Apple**, and a **pending ~$22 billion merger with rival Skyworks Solutions** that, if completed, would create the dominant Western RF front-end supplier.

## Business Overview

Qorvo was formed in 2015 from the merger of RF Micro Devices and TriQuint Semiconductor. It reports across three segments: **Advanced Cellular Group (ACG)** — RF front-end content for smartphones, the largest and most Apple-exposed business; **Connectivity and Sensors Group (CSG)** — Wi-Fi, ultra-wideband, automotive and sensing; and **High Performance Analog (HPA)** — defense/aerospace, infrastructure and power. Apple accounts for roughly half of revenue, making handset content gains/losses the dominant driver. Approximate scale as of June 2026: annual revenue in the ~$3.3-3.7 billion range with non-GAAP gross margin in the low-50s% and rising on restructuring (approximate, from reported quarters).

## Business Segments

Qorvo's three reportable segments differ sharply in margin profile, cyclicality and customer concentration — a structure that frames the entire bull/bear debate. The strategic direction (reinforced by [[starboard-value|Starboard]] activism and the restructuring program) is to deliberately shrink the lowest-margin parts of ACG and grow the higher-margin HPA and CSG mix.

| Segment | What it sells | Key end-markets | Strategic role |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Advanced Cellular Group (ACG)** | RF front-end modules, power amplifiers, antenna tuners, discrete filters (BAW/SAW) | Smartphones (heavily [[apple]], plus Samsung/Android) | Largest revenue base; cyclical and concentration-heavy; the segment being "pruned" of low-tier Android |
| **High Performance Analog (HPA)** | Defense/aerospace RF, base-station & infrastructure components, power management, GaN | Defense, 5G infrastructure, automotive power | Highest-quality, least cyclical earnings; the diversification engine |
| **Connectivity and Sensors Group (CSG)** | Wi-Fi 6/7 front-ends, ultra-wideband (UWB), matter/IoT connectivity, sensing | Smart home, automotive, IoT, PCs | Growth/diversification away from handsets; longer design-in cycles |

The unifying technology is RF front-end engineering: **BAW (bulk-acoustic-wave) and SAW (surface-acoustic-wave) filters**, **power amplifiers**, and **integrated front-end modules (FEMs)** that bundle filters, amplifiers and switches. BAW filters are the prized high-performance assets (premium handsets and 5G mid/high bands), an area where Qorvo and [[broadcom]] are the two strongest Western players. UWB and Wi-Fi 7 are the bets on rising RF content per device beyond the phone.

## Competitive Positioning and Peers

The RF front-end market is a tight oligopoly: a handful of Western incumbents plus integrated platform vendors. Qorvo's defining competitive event is the pending combination with its nearest peer, [[skyworks-solutions|Skyworks]], which would reshape this table entirely.

| Peer | Ticker | Overlap with Qorvo | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| [[skyworks-solutions]] | SWKS | Direct head-to-head in RF front-ends; both heavily Apple-exposed | **Pending ~$22B merger partner** — closest comp, would become the same company if deal closes |
| [[broadcom]] | AVGO | FBAR/BAW filters, RF front-ends, plus a vast diversified semis + software empire | Larger, far more diversified; the high-performance-filter rival |
| [[qualcomm]] | QCOM | Sells integrated RF front-ends ("RF360") bundled with its modems | Platform threat: can bundle RF with baseband to win sockets Qorvo cannot |
| [[murata]] | (Tokyo: 6981) | SAW filters and RF modules; dominant in passive components | Japanese incumbent; strong in filters and multilayer components |
| [[cirrus-logic]] | CRUS | Not RF, but a fellow concentrated Apple supplier (audio/power) | Useful read-through comp for Apple-supply-chain sentiment |

**Positioning takeaway:** Qorvo is a focused RF specialist competing against (1) a larger diversified filter rival ([[broadcom]]), (2) a platform vendor that can bundle ([[qualcomm]]), and (3) its merger partner ([[skyworks-solutions]]). The Skyworks combination is explicitly a defensive consolidation to gain scale and pricing power against these forces.

## 2025-2026 Developments

- **Skyworks merger (announced 2025-2026):** Qorvo and [[skyworks-solutions|Skyworks Solutions]] agreed to combine in a cash-and-stock deal valued at **~$22 billion** — a transformational consolidation of the RF front-end market that would unite the two largest Western suppliers (subject to regulatory and antitrust review).
- **Starboard activism:** activist Starboard Value built a stake and Qorvo expanded its board to ten members, nominating Starboard's **Peter Feld**; Starboard withdrew its nomination notice after the agreement. The campaign pushed margin expansion and portfolio focus, with analysts modeling significantly higher EPS.
- **Restructuring:** Qorvo exited low-margin business (forecasting a ~$200M/year reduction from low-tier Android over FY2026-2027), closed its North Carolina fab and consolidated manufacturing in Texas (~$48.65M restructuring charges). FY2026 non-GAAP gross margin expanded ~370 bps; Q4 FY2026 (March quarter) revenue was **$808.3 million** with non-GAAP EPS of **$1.69**.

## Bull vs Bear Case

**Bull case**
- The [[skyworks-solutions|Skyworks]] merger, if it clears antitrust, creates the dominant Western RF front-end supplier with scale, pricing power and large synergy potential — a structural re-rating event.
- Restructuring is working: exiting low-tier Android (a forecast ~$200M/year revenue reduction) is margin-accretive, and FY2026 non-GAAP gross margin expanded ~370 bps. [[starboard-value|Starboard]]'s campaign reinforces a credible path to higher EPS.
- RF content per device keeps rising (5G mid/high bands, Wi-Fi 7, UWB) — a secular content tailwind even in a flat handset-unit market.
- The HPA segment (defense/aerospace, infrastructure) provides higher-quality, less cyclical earnings to diversify away from handset concentration.

**Bear case**
- ~50% revenue concentration in [[apple]]: any socket loss, in-housing, or design-share shift to [[broadcom]]/[[skyworks-solutions]] is a severe, single-customer risk.
- Deal risk: the Skyworks merger could be blocked or delayed on antitrust grounds (it combines the #1 and #2 Western RF suppliers), and a break would remove the central thesis and the elevated multiple.
- Smartphone RF is mature and cyclical, with sharp inventory corrections; unit growth is structurally low.
- [[qualcomm]] can bundle RF front-ends with its modem/baseband, a platform threat Qorvo cannot match on its own.

## Valuation Framework (qualitative)

Qorvo trades as a special-situation / margin-turnaround story rather than a clean earnings-multiple name. Key qualitative drivers:

- **Merger-arb overlay:** with the [[skyworks-solutions|Skyworks]] deal pending, a meaningful component of the share price reflects deal probability and terms (cash-and-stock), not standalone fundamentals. The spread to deal value embeds the market's antitrust-completion odds.
- **Apple/Android concentration:** the durability of Apple RF content is the single biggest input to standalone value; low-tier Android runoff is being deliberately sacrificed for margin.
- **RF-content / 5G cycle:** RF dollar content per phone, 5G mid/high-band adoption, and Wi-Fi 7/UWB ramps drive the revenue trajectory more than handset unit growth.
- **Margin trajectory:** the restructuring-driven gross-margin expansion (FY2026 +~370 bps) and the EPS uplift modeled by activists are the core of the standalone re-rating case.
- **Comps:** valued against [[skyworks-solutions]] (the direct comp / merger partner) and the broader RF/analog group ([[broadcom]], [[qualcomm]], [[murata]]).

## Capital Return

Qorvo has historically returned capital primarily through **share repurchases** rather than a dividend, buying back stock opportunistically. The restructuring program (North Carolina fab closure, manufacturing consolidation in Texas) is oriented toward margin and free-cash-flow expansion that funds buybacks. A pending stock-and-cash merger with [[skyworks-solutions]] is itself the dominant capital-allocation event; capital-return cadence around it is contingent on the deal's structure and timing. *(Specific buyback amounts and any dividend policy are not detailed in this page's sources — treat capital-return mechanics as structural, not numerical.)*

## Risks

- **Deal break / antitrust risk** on the [[skyworks-solutions|Skyworks]] merger — combining the two largest Western RF suppliers invites scrutiny; a block removes the central thesis.
- **Heavy [[apple]] concentration** (~half of revenue) — socket loss or in-housing is an existential single-customer risk.
- **Smartphone-RF cyclicality** and inventory corrections — sharp, lumpy demand swings.
- **Low-tier Android revenue runoff** — a deliberate but real top-line headwind during the transition.
- **Competitive pressure** from [[broadcom]] (filters) and [[qualcomm]] (RF bundling).
