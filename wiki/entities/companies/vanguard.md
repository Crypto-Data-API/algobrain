The Vanguard Group is one of the world's two largest asset managers (alongside [[blackrock]]), overseeing roughly $10 trillion in assets under management as of 2026. Founded in 1975 by [[jack-bogle|John C. "Jack" Bogle]], Vanguard pioneered low-cost [[index-investing]] for retail investors and is the structural force behind the multi-decade shift from active to [[passive-investing|passive management]]. For traders, Vanguard's ETFs — especially VOO, VTI and BND — are among the most liquid, lowest-cost building blocks for index exposure, hedging and cash management.

## Business Overview

Vanguard's defining feature is its **mutual ownership structure**: the funds own the management company, and fund shareholders own the funds. This means Vanguard operates effectively at cost rather than for outside-shareholder profit, enabling industry-leading low expense ratios. Its flagship products include:

- **VOO** — Vanguard S&P 500 ETF (tracks the [[s-and-p-500]])
- **VTI** — Total US Stock Market ETF
- **VXUS** — Total International Stock ETF
- **BND** — Total Bond Market ETF
- **VT** — Total World Stock ETF

The competitive pressure Vanguard created has driven expense ratios down across the entire fund industry — the so-called **"Vanguard effect"** — and the firm's index funds collectively make it one of the largest shareholders in nearly every S&P 500 company, giving it significant (and increasingly scrutinised) corporate-governance and proxy-voting influence.

## 2025-2026 Developments

- **Fee cuts:** In early 2025 Vanguard announced one of the largest single fee reductions in its history across dozens of ETFs and mutual funds, intensifying the ongoing price war with [[blackrock]]'s iShares and other passive providers.
- **AUM milestone:** Total assets surpassed roughly $10 trillion, cementing its position as the second-largest manager globally behind BlackRock.
- **Active and bond expansion:** Continued growth in low-cost active strategies, money-market funds and fixed-income ETFs as investors used Vanguard cash products amid an elevated-rate environment.

## Trading Relevance

- **Not publicly traded:** Vanguard is privately/mutually owned — there is no Vanguard stock. Traders gain exposure to its products (the ETFs), not the firm.
- **ETF liquidity:** VOO, VTI and BND have very high average daily volume and extremely tight bid-ask spreads, making them efficient vehicles for index exposure, portfolio core holdings, and short-term tactical allocation. VOO is a primary alternative to SPY for S&P 500 exposure.
- **Market significance:** As a top-three holder of most large-cap U.S. equities, Vanguard's index-fund flows are a structural source of buying pressure on index constituents; its proxy voting affects governance outcomes across the market.
- **Correlated themes:** the index-fund/ETF complex, [[blackrock]] (iShares), [[passive-investing]] flows, and the broader [[index-investing]] ecosystem.
