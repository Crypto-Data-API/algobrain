Rigetti Computing, Inc. (NASDAQ: RGTI) is a full-stack quantum-computing company that designs and builds superconducting quantum processors and offers cloud access to them via its Quantum Cloud Services platform. Founded in 2013 by Chad Rigetti and headquartered in Berkeley, California (with its own Fab-1 chip foundry), it went public via SPAC in 2022. RGTI is one of the most heavily traded pure-play quantum names and behaves as a highly speculative, sentiment- and milestone-driven momentum stock with extreme volatility.

## Business Overview

Rigetti operates a vertically integrated model: it designs superconducting qubit chips, fabricates them in-house, builds the control systems and software stack, and sells access through cloud and on-premises systems to government, research and enterprise customers. Revenue is small and lumpy — derived from cloud access, government/defense research contracts (e.g. DARPA, US and UK programs) and system sales — and the company is deeply unprofitable, funding operations through equity raises that have left it with a sizable cash balance but ongoing dilution. The core technical bet is scaling qubit count while improving two-qubit gate fidelity through a modular, multi-chip architecture.

## Business Segments & Products

Rigetti's distinguishing asset is **Fab-1**, its in-house superconducting-chip foundry — the only dedicated commercial quantum-chip fab among the pure plays — which lets it iterate hardware faster than fabless rivals. The strategic bet is the **gate-model** (universal) approach, in contrast to [[d-wave-quantum]]'s annealing focus, and a **modular, multi-chip** architecture to scale qubit count without a single monolithic chip.

| Line | What it is | Revenue model |
|---|---|---|
| **Quantum Cloud Services (QCS)** | Cloud access to Rigetti QPUs (Ankaa-class systems) | Usage/subscription — the recurring revenue line |
| **On-premises / system sales** | Sale or hosting of dedicated quantum systems for governments and research labs | Lumpy, large-ticket |
| **Government / defense research** | Funded R&D contracts (DARPA, US and UK / Innovate UK programs) | Contract/grant revenue; validates technology and partly funds R&D |
| **Fab-1 foundry** | In-house superconducting-chip fabrication | Not a standalone revenue line, but the core competitive asset enabling rapid hardware iteration |

The key technical metric for investors is **two-qubit gate fidelity** alongside qubit count — fidelity, not raw qubit count, gates the path to useful, error-corrected computation. The 2026 roadmap centres on a 108-qubit system at ~99.5% median two-qubit gate fidelity, advancing from the Ankaa-3 platform.

## Competitive Positioning & Peers

Rigetti is a gate-model pure play competing within the quantum basket and against far larger, better-funded incumbents.

| Company | Ticker | Architecture | Contrast with Rigetti |
|---|---|---|---|
| [[d-wave-quantum]] | QBTS | Annealing (special-purpose) | Higher qubit counts and real deployed customers today, but not universal computing; Rigetti is general-purpose |

Rigetti shares superconducting technology with the best-funded players (IBM, Google), so its differentiation rests on Fab-1 speed, modular scaling and partnerships rather than balance-sheet depth.

## 2025-2026 Developments

- **Q1 2026:** total revenue of just **$4.4M**, an operating loss of **~$26.0M** and a non-GAAP net loss of **~$14.7M**; full-year 2025 revenue was **~$7.1M** with a non-GAAP net loss of ~$50.5M. Analysts model 2026 revenue more than tripling to roughly **$23.6M** (off a tiny base).

## Valuation Framework

RGTI is an optionality bet on universal quantum computing, not a cash-flow story. The qualitative levers:

- **Cash runway** — the cash balance (built through equity raises) against the operating burn (~$26M operating loss in Q1 2026) determines how long the roadmap is funded before further dilution; this runway is the main fundamental support.
- **Revenue quality** — small and lumpy, split between recurring QCS cloud usage, one-time system sales, and government research contracts; the bull thesis needs recurring/cloud mix to grow off a tiny base.
- **Fidelity & qubit-count milestones** — technical execution (gate fidelity, modular scaling) is the real value driver and the binary catalyst, far more than reported revenue.
- **SBC dilution & equity raises** — ongoing stock-based comp and recurring capital raises steadily expand the share count, the principal per-share risk.
- **Extreme EV/sales & narrative premium** — with negligible revenue, EV/sales is in the hundreds and meaningless as a fundamental anchor; the market cap reflects narrative and optionality, gapping with quantum-sector sentiment rather than results.

## Capital Structure & Dilution Risk

- **Equity-funded, low-debt:** operations are financed by recurring equity raises rather than leverage; the resulting cash balance funds R&D but at the cost of continual dilution.
- **ATM / follow-on dilution:** capital raises into share-price strength are the defining dilution mechanism — they extend runway while steadily growing the share count and capping per-share appreciation.
- **No dividend / heavy SBC:** all cash is reinvested into hardware development; stock-based compensation adds further dilution.

## Risks

- Negligible revenue scale versus a multi-billion-dollar valuation.
- Execution/roadmap slippage on qubit-count and gate-fidelity milestones.
- Persistent losses and continual equity dilution.
- Better-funded superconducting rivals ([[ibm]], [[google]]) out-investing on the same technology.
- Extreme valuation sensitivity — a quantum-sector de-rating can halve the stock quickly.
