Topstep is a proprietary trading firm that evaluates aspiring [[futures]] traders through a simulated trading program known as the Trading Combine. Traders who demonstrate consistent profitability and disciplined [[risk-management]] during the evaluation receive a funded trading account backed by real capital, allowing them to trade [[cme|CME Group]] futures products without risking their own money.

## Business Model

Topstep operates on a funded-trader evaluation model. Aspiring traders pay a monthly subscription fee (typically $49-$165/month depending on account size) to participate in the Trading Combine -- a simulated trading environment with real market data. If they meet the profitability and risk parameters, they receive a funded account. Topstep earns revenue from subscription fees and takes a percentage of funded traders' profits.

This model differs from traditional proprietary trading firms that hire traders as employees. Topstep's participants are independent contractors who trade remotely, bearing no risk of personal capital loss beyond the subscription fee.

## Evaluation Process

The **Trading Combine** is Topstep's core evaluation. Traders must demonstrate consistent profitability while adhering to strict risk rules:

- **Account sizes**: $50,000, $100,000, and $150,000 simulated buying power
- **Profit target**: Varies by account size (e.g., $3,000 for a $50K account, $6,000 for $150K)
- **Maximum daily loss limit**: A hard daily drawdown that, if breached, results in a failed day. For example, -$1,000 on a $50K account
- **Maximum trailing drawdown**: An overall loss limit that follows the account's peak equity. Breaching this ends the evaluation
- **Minimum trading days**: Typically 5+ trading days required before qualifying
- **Consistency rule**: Traders cannot earn more than a certain percentage of their total profit on a single day, encouraging steady performance over windfall trades

## Funded Accounts and Payouts

Traders who pass the Combine receive a funded account. Key terms include:

- **Profit split**: Topstep takes a portion of profits (historically 10-20%, with the trader keeping 80-90%)
- **Payout eligibility**: Traders can request withdrawals after meeting minimum thresholds (typically 5 trading days and a minimum profit amount)
- **Scaling plan**: Funded traders may increase position size limits as their account grows
- **Loss limits persist**: The trailing drawdown and daily loss limits remain active on funded accounts. Breaching them results in account termination

## Supported Markets

Topstep focuses exclusively on [[futures]] traded on [[cme|CME Group]] exchanges:

- **E-mini S&P 500 (ES)** and **Micro E-mini (MES)** -- the most popular contracts among Topstep traders
- **E-mini Nasdaq 100 (NQ)** and **Micro E-mini (MNQ)**
- **Crude Oil (CL)** and **Micro Crude Oil (MCL)**
- **Gold (GC)** and **Micro Gold (MGC)**
- **Treasury futures (ZB, ZN, ZF)**
- **E-mini Russell 2000 (RTY)**

The futures-only focus differentiates Topstep from [[forex]]-oriented prop firms like [[ftmo]]. Futures markets offer transparent [[order-flow]] data via the [[cme|CME]] exchange, which is why [[order-flow-analysis]]-based strategies tend to perform well in the Topstep environment.

## Performance and Pass Rates

Topstep does not publish official pass rates, but community estimates suggest approximately 5-15% of Combine participants ultimately receive and sustain funded accounts. Traders using structural analysis combined with [[order-flow]] tools -- such as the [[smart-money-concepts]] + [[order-flow-analysis|order flow]] combination -- reportedly achieve the highest success rates, as these approaches align well with the consistency and risk-management rules that Topstep enforces.

## Role in the Prop Trading Industry

Founded in 2012 in Chicago, Topstep was one of the earliest companies to pioneer the remote funded-trader evaluation model. The company helped establish the template that dozens of competing firms later adopted. Its Chicago location and futures focus give it proximity to the [[cme|CME]] trading ecosystem and credibility within the professional futures trading community.

## Trading Relevance and Current Status

Topstep is **privately held** — there is no ticker and no direct equity exposure. Traders engage with it as a route to leveraged [[futures]] capital without risking personal funds beyond evaluation fees. As of 2026 it remains one of the largest and most established futures-focused funded-trader firms, operating alongside a crowded field of competitors (Apex Trader Funding, TopstepX/TradeDay, Take Profit Trader, Earn2Trade, MyFundedFutures and others). The broader funded-futures industry has drawn increasing scrutiny over the sustainability of the model, payout reliability, and the regulatory grey area between "evaluation" products and unregistered managed-money — a sector-wide risk worth tracking for anyone deploying capital through these firms.

Key practical considerations for traders:

- The economics favour the firm: most participants fail the [[risk-management|Combine]] (community estimates ~5-15% sustained-funded rate), and evaluation fees are a primary revenue source.
- Funded-account rules (trailing drawdown, daily loss limits, consistency rules) persist after funding and are strict — discipline matters more than raw edge.
- Because it deals in [[cme|CME]] futures, transparent exchange [[order-flow]] data is available, which is why [[order-flow-analysis]]-based and [[smart-money-concepts|structural]] approaches are popular among successful Topstep traders.
