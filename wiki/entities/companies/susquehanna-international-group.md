Susquehanna International Group (SIG) is a privately held global quantitative trading firm and one of the largest [[options]] [[market-maker|market makers]] in the world. Founded in 1987 by a group of professional poker players and quantitative traders, SIG is generally credited as one of the earliest firms to apply systematic, model-driven decision-making to listed equity options market making.

## Overview

SIG operates as a proprietary trading firm with offices in Bala Cynwyd (Philadelphia suburbs), Dublin, London, Sydney, Mumbai, Hong Kong and Shanghai. It is structurally similar to [[jane-street]] and [[optiver]]: it risks only its own capital, makes markets in tens of thousands of options series, and hedges the resulting risk continuously with the underlying, futures and other options.

The firm is closely associated with **expected-value thinking and game theory**, traditions inherited from its poker-playing founders (notably Jeff Yass). New traders are famously taught poker and Liar's Poker before being put on a trading desk, and the firm runs an in-house multi-month training program in quantitative options theory.

SIG is also notable as an early backer of [[bytedance]] (TikTok's parent), holding a multi-percent equity stake — the venture investing arm sits separately from the trading business but has been a major contributor to the firm's overall returns.

## Use of AI and Quantitative Methods

SIG's options market making was already model-driven by the late 1980s, applying [[black-scholes|Black–Scholes]]-derived theoretical pricing with proprietary [[volatility-skew|skew]] and [[volatility-term-structure|term-structure]] adjustments well before this was standard. Modern SIG systems use:

- Real-time fitted [[volatility-surface]] models calibrated tick-by-tick
- Machine-learning models for [[order-flow]] toxicity and [[adverse-selection]] detection
- Statistical learning for short-horizon price forecasts on the underlying
- Cross-product portfolio hedging spanning options, equities, futures and ETFs
- Large in-house simulation environments for strategy backtesting

SIG was historically one of the loudest opponents of [[payment-for-order-flow]] reform, since it benefits from internalising retail flow at favourable prices.

## Trading Activities

### Listed Options Market Making

SIG is consistently a top-three participant in US listed equity and index options market making, competing with [[citadel-securities|Citadel Securities]], [[optiver]], [[jane-street]] and [[hudson-river-trading]]. The firm is particularly large in single-stock options.

### ETF and Index Arbitrage

SIG markets-makes ETFs and runs index arbitrage between SPX/SPY/ES, NDX/QQQ/NQ and similar baskets.

### Venture Capital

Susquehanna's venture arm has invested in major Asian technology firms, including the very early stage of ByteDance, Toutiao and Meituan. These investments have at times generated more reported P&L than the trading business itself.

## Market Significance and Exposure

SIG is privately held — there is no SIG ticker and no direct way to invest in the firm. Its relevance to outside traders is indirect:

- **Liquidity provider:** as a top-three U.S. options market maker, SIG's quoting directly sets the spreads and depth retail and institutional traders receive, especially in single-stock options.
- **ByteDance / TikTok exposure:** SIG's early ByteDance stake is one of the most valuable private-tech positions held by any trading firm. Through 2024-2025 this stake was caught up in the U.S. forced-divestiture / ban debate over TikTok — a reminder that even a pure trading firm carries concentrated geopolitical risk via its venture book. A TikTok sale or favourable resolution would crystallise a very large gain.
- **Founder wealth/politics:** co-founder Jeff Yass became one of the largest individual political donors in the U.S., raising SIG's public profile.

## Trading Relevance

For active options traders, SIG's behaviour matters in several ways:

- **Single-stock options spreads** — SIG is dominant in many single-stock option books; its quoting determines achievable execution costs in mid-cap names where [[citadel-securities|Citadel]] and [[jane-street]] are less active.
- **Vol-surface tightness** — SIG's continuous recalibration removes most naive smile/skew arbitrages.
- **Retail flow internalisation** — SIG is a major internaliser of retail options flow via PFOF arrangements; this is one reason retail traders see surprisingly tight quotes on small orders.
