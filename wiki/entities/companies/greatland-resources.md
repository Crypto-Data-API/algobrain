**Greatland Resources Limited** (ASX: GGP) is a gold and copper producer listed on the [[asx-limited|Australian Securities Exchange]] and a constituent of the [[asx-200]] index. For traders it is one of the higher-torque ASX gold names: a newly re-rated producer whose value hinges on Telfer mill performance, Havieron development milestones, and the gold price. Greatland Resources Limited is a gold and copper producer and developer operating in Western Australia's Paterson region. The company owns 100% of the Telfer gold-copper mine and the adjacent Havieron gold-copper development project – two geologically linked assets that together form the foundation of a long-life, integrated production platform.

## Business Overview & Assets

Greatland is a [[gold]] and [[copper]] producer-developer in the [[materials]] sector, operating in Western Australia's Paterson Province. Its thesis rests on a tightly integrated "mine plus mill" structure: an existing large-capacity processing plant at Telfer and a high-grade development (Havieron) just 45 km away that will feed it. The company owns 100% of both assets, acquired from Newmont (which divested the ex-Newcrest Paterson assets).

| Asset | Location | Status | Role / notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Telfer** | Paterson, WA | Producing | Open-pit + underground gold-copper mine operating since 1977; ~22 Mtpa processing plant — the production hub and the mill for Havieron |
| **Havieron** | ~45 km E of Telfer | Development (100%) | High-grade brownfield gold-copper underground; decline access, ore processed through Telfer; post-tax NPV(5%) A$2.9B, IRR 22.5%, ~266koz Au + 9,600t Cu/yr steady state, AISC A$1,610/oz |
| **West Dome / satellites** | Paterson, WA | Resource delineation | Multiple satellite deposits being defined to extend Telfer mill life; maiden West Dome Underground resource added in 2026 |

The integrated model is the crux: Havieron's economics are far stronger because it leverages the existing, fully built Telfer mill rather than financing a new plant. Revenue is generated from gold doré (sold to refiners) and copper concentrate.

## Economic Moat

Greatland is a **price-taker** on both gold and copper — it earns prevailing spot prices and has no franchise [[economic-moat]] in the sense a network business like [[brambles]] enjoys. The honest framing is **cost-curve position, asset quality and mine life**. Greatland's genuine, asset-specific advantage is the **Telfer–Havieron integration**: a high-grade development feeding an already-built ~22 Mtpa mill is a real structural cost edge (Havieron's projected AISC of A$1,610/oz sits low on the cost curve), and one a greenfield competitor could not replicate without building both mine and plant. The 14.9 Moz gold / 645kt copper combined resource base and a 150% Telfer resource uplift in 2026 underpin a long mine life. The limitation is that this is asset quality, not pricing power: the value depends on commodity prices, on delivering the Havieron development on time and budget, and on the ageing Telfer mill performing — operational reliability, not a competitive barrier, is the swing factor.

## Bull Case vs Bear Case

### Bull case
- Integrated Telfer mill + high-grade Havieron development is a real structural cost edge (Havieron AISC ~A$1,610/oz, NPV A$2.9B, IRR 22.5%).
- Large, fast-growing resource base (14.9 Moz Au / 645kt Cu; Telfer resource +150% in 2026) underpins a long mine life.
- First full half of Telfer ownership delivered strong earnings (H1 FY26 NPAT A$342.9M, EBITDA A$560.3M).
- High-torque, re-rating story with ASX index inclusion bringing passive/institutional flows; Newmont alignment as a shareholder.

### Bear case
- Pure price-taker on gold and copper; high beta cuts hard on a commodity reversal.
- Ageing Telfer mill (operating since 1977) carries operational-reliability and sustaining-capex risk.
- Havieron development/funding/execution risk — FID, capital cost and ramp are not yet de-risked.
- Thin reported financial history (pre-Telfer exploration shell) and very low data completeness make through-cycle quality hard to read.
- Dual ASX/AIM register and a recently transformed entity add complexity and a less-seasoned cost record.

## Key Risks

- **Gold/copper price reversal** — the largest risk; unhedged, high-beta economics.
- **Telfer operational reliability** — an ageing mill is the production bottleneck for both Telfer and Havieron ore.
- **Havieron development / funding** — FID timing, capital cost and ramp execution.
- **Cost inflation** — labour, power, consumables in remote WA.
- **FX** — a stronger AUD compresses AUD-denominated margins.
- **Data / history caveat** — limited reported financials; the H1 FY26 statutory numbers are the better earnings guide.

## Valuation & How the Stock Trades

- **Gold and AUD beta (plus copper)** — GGP is one of the higher-torque ASX gold names: unhedged producer economics mean it trades with high beta to the AUD gold price, with an added copper driver, and a softer AUD compounds AUD-margin expansion.
- **Dual listing** — primary on the [[asx-200]] following the June 2025 ASX listing, with a retained London AIM quotation (GGP); index inclusion brought passive and institutional flows to a register historically dominated by UK retail.
- **No meaningful dividend** — GGP is a capital-growth/re-rating name reinvesting into Havieron and exploration, not a franked-income play (contrast [[evolution-mining]]).

## History

Greatland Resources Limited was incorporated in 2023 as the acquisition vehicle for Newmont's Paterson assets (Telfer mine and 70% of Havieron), following Newmont's decision to divest non-core assets acquired via its Newcrest takeover. Greatland completed the acquisition of Telfer and the remaining 30% of Havieron it did not already own in December 2024, consolidating 100% ownership of both assets. The legacy Greatland Gold plc (AIM-listed) corporate structure was re-domiciled to Australia with a primary ASX listing.

## Recent Developments

For the half year ended 31 December 2025 (H1 FY26), Greatland reported a net profit after tax of A$342.9 million, underlying EBITDA of A$560.3 million, and net revenue of A$977.3 million – the first full half-year of Telfer ownership. Production for the December 2025 quarter was 86,273 oz of gold and 3,528 tonnes of copper, bringing first-half FY26 production to 167,163 oz of gold and 6,894 tonnes of copper.

The Havieron Feasibility Study, completed on 1 December 2025, confirmed the project as a long-life, lowest-quartile-cost gold-copper development leveraging existing Telfer infrastructure, with a post-tax NPV(5%) of A$2.9 billion, IRR of 22.5%, steady-state annual production of 266,000 oz Au and 9,600 t Cu, and AISC of A$1,610/oz.

In March 2026, Greatland reported a major group mineral resource update. Total Telfer plus Havieron resources increased to 14.9 million ounces of gold and 645,000 tonnes of copper, with a maiden resource added for the high-grade West Dome Underground Project. The overall Telfer resource grew by 150% to 8.0 million ounces of gold, and the Measured and Indicated component grew 163% to 3.8 million ounces. More than 134,000 metres of drilling were completed in 2025, with a further ~100,000 metres planned for 2026 and an updated Telfer ore reserve estimate targeted for Q2 2026.
