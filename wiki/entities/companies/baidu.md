Baidu, Inc. (NASDAQ: BIDU) is a Chinese multinational technology company that dominates internet search in China and has become one of the country's leading [[artificial-intelligence|AI]] companies. Founded in January 2000 by Robin Li and Eric Xu in Beijing, Baidu was an early adopter of AI beginning around 2010 and has built a full AI stack spanning deep learning frameworks (PaddlePaddle), the ERNIE family of [[foundation-models|large language models]], Alibaba Cloud AI services, and the Apollo Go [[autonomous-vehicles|autonomous ride-hailing]] platform. The company is headquartered in Beijing's Haidian District and is often referred to as "China's Google."

## Business Overview

Baidu's revenue mix is shifting from a legacy search-advertising base toward AI. The company reports two principal lines: Baidu Core (search, online marketing, AI Cloud, intelligent driving) and iQIYI (a separately listed streaming subsidiary). Search remains the cash engine but advertising is in structural decline as users move to AI-native interfaces; Baidu is deliberately cannibalising its own search results page with generative AI answers. The growth story is now:

- **ERNIE foundation models** — Baidu's [[foundation-models|LLM]] family, comparable to Western frontier models. ERNIE 5.1 launched in May 2026. ERNIE powers the Ernie Bot consumer chatbot and is sold to enterprises via the cloud.
- **AI Cloud** — the fastest-growing segment, riding Chinese enterprise demand for AI infrastructure and inference. AI Cloud infrastructure revenue rose roughly 79% year-over-year in Q1 2026 to RMB 8.8B.
- **Apollo Go (Luobo Kuaipao)** — one of the world's largest commercial robotaxi fleets, operating fully driverless rides in multiple Chinese cities with expansion testing in Switzerland and London. Apollo Go delivered ~3.2 million driverless rides in Q1 2026, cumulative public rides above 22 million by April 2026.

## Business Segments

Baidu reports two consolidated lines — **Baidu Core** and **iQIYI** — but the investable story is the mix-shift within Core from legacy search advertising toward AI Cloud and autonomous mobility.

| Segment / unit | What it is | Role in the model |
|---|---|---|
| **Search & online marketing (Baidu Core)** | China's dominant search engine + ad system | Legacy cash engine; structurally declining as AI answers cannibalize the results page |
| **AI Cloud (Baidu Core)** | Enterprise AI infrastructure, inference, and platform services | Fastest-growing segment; the near-term AI monetization vehicle |
| **ERNIE foundation models** | Baidu's [[foundation-models|LLM]] family (ERNIE 5.1, May 2026) + Ernie Bot | Powers consumer chatbot and enterprise cloud; sits at the center of a Chinese LLM price war |
| **Apollo Go (intelligent driving)** | Driverless [[autonomous-vehicles|robotaxi]] fleet (Luobo Kuaipao) | Long-dated optionality; scaling unit economics in China, testing abroad |
| **PaddlePaddle** | Deep-learning framework | Developer ecosystem / strategic moat, not a direct revenue line |
| **iQIYI** | Separately listed streaming subsidiary (subscription + ads) | Consolidated but distinct; a China streaming business with its own cycle |

The strategic tension is a race: traditional search-ad revenue is in structural decline as users shift to AI-native answer interfaces, and Baidu is deliberately cannibalizing its own search results with generative AI. The bull case requires AI Cloud growth and AI-native marketing to offset that erosion before the legacy base shrinks too far — which the Q1 2026 disclosures suggest is beginning to happen.

## Competitive Positioning and Peers

Baidu competes across three distinct battlefields — search/advertising, cloud + foundation models, and autonomous mobility — against different rivals in each, while also trading as part of the China-ADR complex within [[communication-services]]/technology.

Versus [[alphabet]], the comparison is almost one-to-one in structure (dominant search + frontier AI models + a robotaxi fleet) but the valuation diverges sharply because of the China-ADR discount and geopolitical overhang. Versus [[alibaba-group-holding]] and [[tencent]], Baidu is the smaller cloud player but arguably the most AI-research-forward of the Chinese majors, with ERNIE and PaddlePaddle giving it a full-stack story. In robotaxi, Apollo Go is among the largest commercial fleets globally, putting Baidu in rare company with Waymo on the mobility-optionality axis.

## Bull vs Bear Case

**Bull case**
- AI Cloud is growing fast enough to begin offsetting the secular decline in search advertising — the mix-shift inflection the whole thesis hinges on.
- ERNIE keeps pace with frontier models; a full AI stack (framework + models + cloud + apps) is hard for narrower rivals to replicate.
- Apollo Go is a genuine, scaled robotaxi business with real ride volumes and international expansion testing — large, under-priced optionality.
- The China-ADR discount means a lot of bad news is already in the price; any de-escalation of US-China tech tension or China stimulus can re-rate the whole complex.
- Strong net cash / balance sheet supports buybacks and reinvestment.

**Bear case**
- Search advertising decline could outrun AI monetization, leaving Core revenue stagnant for years.
- A brutal Chinese LLM price war compresses cloud/model margins even as volumes grow.
- Geopolitics: US chip-export controls limit access to advanced AI accelerators; HFCAA/delisting risk and VIE-structure risk overhang the ADS.
- Apollo Go unit economics and the path to profitability remain unproven at scale.
- CNY/USD translation and China macro/regulatory shocks add volatility unrelated to fundamentals.

## Valuation Framework

Baidu is most naturally valued **sum-of-the-parts (SOTP)**, because the consolidated multiple obscures very different businesses:

- **Search/online marketing** — a declining but cash-generative legacy asset, valued on a low earnings/cash-flow multiple.
- **AI Cloud** — valued on a growth-revenue multiple appropriate to a scaling enterprise-AI business.
- **ERNIE / AI platform** — embedded optionality; hard to value standalone, often folded into Cloud.
- **Apollo Go** — option value on robotaxi scaling; binary-ish, valued on a probability-weighted basis or as a free call option.
- **iQIYI stake** — marked at the listed value of the subsidiary.
- **Net cash** — Baidu typically carries substantial net cash, which a SOTP adds back.

Layered on top is the **China-ADR discount**: a haircut investors apply for VIE-structure risk, HFCAA/delisting risk, opaque regulation, and CNY exposure. The practical bull argument is that the sum of the parts plus net cash often exceeds the market cap, implying the market assigns near-zero or negative value to the AI/robotaxi optionality — the classic "cheap China ADR with free AI optionality" framing.

## Capital Return

Baidu has historically returned capital primarily through **share repurchases** rather than dividends, supported by a large net-cash position. As a growth-and-optionality name reinvesting heavily in AI Cloud, ERNIE, and Apollo Go, buybacks are the main mechanism for returning excess cash and signaling that management views the ADS as undervalued amid the China-ADR discount. (Verify the current buyback authorization and any dividend policy live before trading — China-ADR capital-return policies can change with regulation and macro conditions.)

## Risks

- **Geopolitical / chip access:** US export controls on advanced AI accelerators directly constrain Baidu's AI ambitions.
- **Delisting / structure:** HFCAA delisting risk and VIE-structure risk on the US-listed ADS.
- **Secular search decline:** AI cannibalization could outrun monetization, stalling Core revenue.
- **Price war:** Chinese LLM/cloud price competition compressing margins.
- **FX/macro:** CNY/USD translation and broad China macro and regulatory shocks.
- **Robotaxi execution:** Apollo Go profitability and regulatory clearance remain unproven at scale.
