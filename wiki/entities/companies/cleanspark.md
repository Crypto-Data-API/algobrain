CleanSpark Inc. (NASDAQ: CLSK) is an American [[bitcoin]] mining company headquartered in Henderson, Nevada. While the corporate entity was originally incorporated in 1987, CleanSpark entered the bitcoin mining industry in December 2020 with its acquisition of ATL Data Centers and has since grown into one of the largest publicly traded bitcoin miners in the US, operating multiple mining facilities across Georgia, Mississippi, and other states. The stock is popular among retail options traders for its high correlation to [[bitcoin]] price movements and elevated [[implied-volatility]].

## Business Overview

CleanSpark is one of the largest pure-play public bitcoin miners by hashrate, operating low-cost facilities concentrated in Georgia, Mississippi, Tennessee and Wyoming, with a strategy historically defined by buying distressed sites cheaply during bear markets and holding mined BTC on balance sheet. Unlike peers that pivoted early ([[core-scientific]], [[iren]], [[cipher-mining]]), CleanSpark stayed mining-first through 2025 — making it the higher-beta, BTC-purest member of the listed miner basket — before beginning its own AI/HPC diversification in late 2025-2026.

## Bull vs Bear Case

**Bull case**
- Scaled, low-cost fleet with one of the better efficiency profiles in the listed group; operating leverage to any BTC up-move.
- Large self-funded BTC treasury compounds in a crypto bull market.
- Owned power and land portfolio gives genuine, optionable AI/HPC convertibility — a signed hyperscaler lease would be a step-change re-rating catalyst.
- Disciplined capital allocator with a track record of buying distressed capacity cheaply.

**Bear case**
- Highest BTC beta in the basket cuts both ways — brutal drawdowns when spot [[bitcoin]] falls and hashprice compresses post-halving.
- Late to the AI/HPC pivot versus [[cipher-mining]], [[core-scientific]] and [[iren]]; execution and timing risk on monetizing power into compute leases.
- Heavy reliance on convertible/equity issuance funds growth but dilutes holders.
- Mining is a commoditized, difficulty-adjusted business with no durable moat beyond power cost and scale.

## Valuation Framework

Bitcoin miners are not valued on conventional earnings multiples; the relevant qualitative levers are:

- **BTC beta / hashprice** — the single biggest driver. Revenue ≈ hashrate × hashprice, so the equity behaves like a levered, decaying call on [[bitcoin]] with operating costs as the strike.
- **EV per EH/s and EV per MW** — cross-miner comparison of how much enterprise value the market assigns to each unit of installed hashrate or energized power; the "power" metric increasingly dominates as AI/HPC optionality is priced in.
- **Power cost ($/kWh)** — the durable structural edge; low-cost miners survive hashprice troughs that bankrupt high-cost peers.
- **Fleet efficiency (J/TH)** — newer-generation rigs lower the breakeven BTC price.
- **BTC treasury mark** — sum-of-parts: operating mining business + balance-sheet BTC stack.
- **Dilution adjustment** — convertible and ATM issuance must be netted against per-share value; share count growth is a recurring headwind.
- **AI/HPC option value** — once a lease is signed, a discounted contracted-revenue stream (as at [[cipher-mining]]/[[galaxy-digital]]) can be valued on a data-center multiple rather than a miner multiple.

## Capital Structure & Dilution

CleanSpark funds expansion through a mix of **equity (ATM programs), convertible notes** (including the $1.15 billion zero-coupon convertible noted below) and operating/treasury cash flow. The recurring trade-offs:

- **Dilution risk** — ATM and convertible issuance steadily grow the share count; per-share BTC and earnings can lag headline growth.
- **Convertible overhang** — zero-coupon converts are cheap upfront but create conversion-related share supply and hedging flows that can cap upside.
- **BTC treasury as collateral/liquidity** — the coin stack can be borrowed against or sold to fund capex, linking balance-sheet strength directly to spot [[bitcoin]].
- **Leverage to hashprice** — fixed power and debt-service costs against variable mining revenue amplify both up- and down-moves.

## Risks

- Very high beta to [[bitcoin]] (typically 2-3x spot moves) and hashprice compression, especially post-halving.
- Energy-cost inflation and grid/interconnect/regulatory risk at mining sites.
- Persistent dilution from equity and convertible issuance.
- Execution and timing risk on the AI/HPC pivot; lagging peers on signed leases has driven sharp relative-value swings within the miner basket.
- Network difficulty growth steadily erodes per-unit mining economics absent fleet upgrades.

## 2025–2026 Developments

- **FY2025 (ended September 2025)**: revenue of $766.3 million, up 102% year-over-year; surpassed **50 EH/s** of operational hashrate during the year.
- **Financing**: closed a $1.15 billion zero-coupon convertible note to fund expansion of its power and land portfolio.
- **AI pivot begins**: won the bidding for a 100 MW AI data-center site in Cheyenne, Wyoming (reportedly beating Microsoft); acquired rights to ~271 acres in Austin County, Texas with 285 MW of long-term power agreements for a next-generation data-center campus.
- **Q1 FY2026 tone shift**: management stated that incremental bitcoin-mining investment "doesn't make a lot of sense" at prevailing hashprices versus returns available in AI infrastructure — mining now monetizes existing capacity while AI exposure is built out.
- **Key financials, as of June 2026 (approximate)**: FY2025 revenue ~$0.77 billion; ~50 EH/s hashrate; substantial BTC treasury holdings (thousands of coins); multi-billion-dollar market cap that swings widely with BTC.
