CACI International Inc. (NYSE: CACI) is a major US national-security technology and expertise contractor — software-defined electronic warfare, signals intelligence, cyber, space-ground systems and [[artificial-intelligence|AI]]-enabled mission software for the Department of Defense and intelligence community. For traders it is one of the steadier defense-IT compounders: multi-year funded backlog, recurring federal demand, and a consistent beat-and-raise cadence through fiscal 2025–2026.

## Business Overview

Founded 1962, CACI organizes its work into **Expertise** (mission staffing/services) and **Technology** (software, electronic warfare/EW, SIGINT, optical communications via its space photonics franchise, counter-UAS, network modernization). Differentiators versus body-shop peers: a decade-long pivot toward software-defined, product-like technology offerings (higher margin, harder to recompete away) and deep classified-program positions. Customers are overwhelmingly US federal — DoD, intel agencies, DHS and civilian agencies — with revenue visibility anchored by a $30B+ total backlog. Growth is organic plus disciplined M&A (Azure Summit Technology in FY2025; ARKA Group in 2026).

As an [[industrials]]-sector government-services compounder, CACI's investment character is defined by backlog visibility and the federal budget cycle rather than consumer demand. The single most important strategic shift is the migration of revenue mix from labor-based "Expertise" toward differentiated "Technology" — software-defined EW, SIGINT, and photonics products carry higher margins and are far harder for competitors to recompete away than staff-augmentation contracts.

## Business Segments and Program Portfolio

CACI reports along two dimensions — by **business type** (Expertise vs Technology) and by **customer** (Department of Defense vs Federal Civilian/Other) — but the economically meaningful lens is the Expertise-to-Technology mix.

| Business type | What it is | Economics |
|---|---|---|
| **Expertise** | Mission staffing and professional services — embedded analysts, engineers, operators on classified and unclassified programs | Lower margin, labor-based; sticky via clearances and incumbency but easier to recompete |
| **Technology** | Software, electronic warfare (EW), SIGINT, optical/space photonics communications, counter-UAS, network modernization | Higher margin, product-like, differentiated; the strategic growth and margin driver |

**Program portfolio characteristics.** CACI's work spans signals intelligence, software-defined EW, space-ground systems and optical communications (its photonics franchise, deepened by the **ARKA Group** acquisition closed March 2026, adding EO/IR sensing and space systems), counter-UAS, and broad network/IT modernization for DoD and the intelligence community. Many positions are classified, which raises switching costs and protects incumbency. **Backlog is the headline visibility metric**: total backlog reached **$33.4B at 2026-03-31** (+6.4% YoY) with funded backlog of $5.0B (+19%) — a multiple of annual revenue that gives unusual forward visibility for an equity. The **book-to-bill ratio** (awards divided by revenue) is the leading indicator traders watch: Q2 FY2026 awards were $1.4B (~70% new business) and Q3 FY2026 awards $2.2B (~26% new business).

## 2025–2026 Developments

- **FY2025 (ended 2025-06-30)**: record results — revenue approximately $8.6B, up double digits — and initial FY2026 guidance issued August 2025.
- **FY2026 guidance raised**: full-year revenue outlook lifted to $9.5–9.6B, including ~$150M contribution from the **ARKA Group acquisition (closed March 2026)** — space/defense technology (EO/IR sensing and space systems) that deepens the Technology segment.
- **Award momentum**: Q2 FY2026 awards of $1.4B (~70% new business); Q3 FY2026 awards of $2.2B (~26% new business). Total backlog reached **$33.4B at 2026-03-31** (+6.4% YoY); funded backlog $5.0B (+19%).
- **Budget environment**: elevated US/NATO defense spending, EW/counter-UAS prioritization and intelligence-modernization programs remain tailwinds; government-shutdown brinkmanship and continuing-resolution delays in FY2026 caused only modest award-timing noise.

CACI sits in the defense-IT / government-services tier, distinct from the platform/prime contractors. Its peers are the other large federal IT and mission-services contractors.

Versus [[leidos]] (the scale leader) and [[science-applications-international|SAIC]], CACI's differentiation is its push into software-defined, product-like Technology (EW, SIGINT, photonics) that is harder to recompete and carries better margins than commodity staff augmentation. Versus [[booz-allen-hamilton]], CACI is less consulting/advisory and more systems/EW. The whole group decouples from commercial IT services and trades on the federal budget cycle, geopolitics, and recompete outcomes; CACI is positioned as a "real revenue" government-AI/EW name rather than a narrative AI stock like [[bigbearai]].

## Bull vs Bear Case

**Bull case**
- Exceptional revenue visibility: $33.4B total backlog (a multiple of annual revenue) plus a consistent beat-and-raise cadence through FY2025–FY2026.
- Mix shift toward higher-margin, differentiated Technology (EW/SIGINT/photonics) that is hard to recompete away.
- Elevated US/NATO defense spending with EW, counter-UAS, and intelligence modernization explicitly prioritized.
- Disciplined, accretive M&A (Azure Summit FY2025; ARKA Group 2026) deepening the Technology franchise.
- Defensive "headline hedge" behavior — a defensive bid during geopolitical escalations.

**Bear case**
- Federal budget risk: sequester or DOGE-style efficiency drives that target services spending.
- Recompete losses — single contracts can be $1B+, so a loss materially dents backlog.
- Contract protests and continuing-resolution/shutdown-driven award-timing noise.
- Margin pressure from a cost-plus contract mix and the labor-heavy Expertise base.
- Mid-cap with only moderate options liquidity — harder to express views at size via derivatives.

## Valuation Framework

CACI is valued as a steady government-services compounder, where forward visibility substitutes for growth excitement. The qualitative levers to map:

- **Book-to-bill ratio** — awards / revenue; the leading indicator of future revenue. Sustained >1.0 signals backlog growth.
- **Total and funded backlog** — the visibility anchor ($33.4B total / $5.0B funded at 2026-03-31); a backlog-to-revenue multiple frames forward years.
- **Federal budget cycle** — appropriations, supplementals, and CR/shutdown timing drive award flow.
- **Expertise-to-Technology mix** — the margin lever; a richer Technology mix lifts operating margin and re-rates the multiple.
- **Free-cash-flow conversion** — strong FCF funds M&A and buybacks (no dividend).

Relative anchors are EV/EBITDA and P/E versus [[leidos]], [[science-applications-international|SAIC]], and [[booz-allen-hamilton]]; CACI can command a premium to pure staff-augmentation peers to the extent its Technology mix and backlog growth justify it.

## Capital Return

CACI pays **no dividend**. Capital is deployed primarily into **disciplined, accretive M&A** (Azure Summit Technology in FY2025; ARKA Group in 2026) to build out the higher-margin Technology franchise, with **opportunistic share buybacks** as the secondary use. Strong free-cash-flow conversion funds both. The return case is a compounding-backlog-and-margin story plus buyback-driven share-count reduction, not income.
