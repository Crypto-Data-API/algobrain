HIVE Digital Technologies Ltd. (NASDAQ: HIVE) is a Canadian-listed digital infrastructure company that historically operated as HIVE Blockchain — one of the first publicly listed Bitcoin and Ethereum miners — and rebranded in 2024 to reflect a broader AI / HPC infrastructure strategy. The company operates data centres in Sweden, Iceland, Canada, and (since 2024-2025, now its largest growth vector) Paraguay, with renewable energy access and is part of the **HPC-pivot miner cohort** alongside [[iren|IREN]], [[core-scientific|CORZ]], APLD, and [[riot-platforms|RIOT]] — public miners whose value is increasingly framed around physical data centre and power capacity rather than Bitcoin economics. HIVE was a new long position initiated by [[situational-awareness-lp|Situational Awareness LP]] per the late May 2026 13F filings; sizing was not specifically disclosed but indicated to be a niche initiation alongside SHAZ and T1 Energy.

> **Disambiguation:** This page covers HIVE Digital Technologies (NASDAQ: HIVE) — a publicly listed AI / HPC company. The wiki also has pages on the **Hive blockchain** ([[hive]]) and **Hive Dollar** ([[hive_dollar]]) which are unrelated cryptocurrency projects.

## The HPC-pivot thesis

HIVE's value framing has shifted across three eras:

| Era | Framing | Value driver |
|---|---|---|
| 2017-2020 | Pure-play Ethereum miner | ETH price + mining margins |
| 2021-2023 | Diversified BTC + ETH miner | Crypto cycle |
| 2024-2026 | HPC + AI infrastructure provider | Data centre lease economics |

The pivot value proposition: **the same renewable-powered Nordic data centres that produced PoW hashes can now host AI training and inference clusters**. The lease economics for AI customers are typically 5-10x better per MW than crypto mining, with longer contract durations and stronger counterparty credit.

## Business Model

HIVE runs two engines on a shared base of renewable-powered, owned data-centre capacity:

- **Bitcoin mining (the cash-flowing core, scaling in Paraguay).** Self-mining converts low-cost hydro/renewable power into hashrate into BTC. Unit economics turn on **fleet efficiency (J/TH)**, **all-in power cost ($/kWh)**, and **hash price** (BTC price × network difficulty). HIVE's Paraguay hydro buildout (Yguazú/Valenzuela) is its main hashrate-growth vector, taking installed capacity toward the mid-30s EH/s. Post-[[bitcoin-halving|halving]], driving down J/TH and $/kWh is what protects margin. See [[bitcoin-mining]] and [[hash-rate]].
- **BUZZ HPC (AI cloud / hosting, the re-rating engine).** Rather than only leasing shells, HIVE also operates an **AI cloud** (BUZZ), deploying GPUs (e.g., Nvidia B200) and selling compute on contract — a more operator-like model than the pure-landlord peers (applied-digital, [[core-scientific]]). Per-MW economics for AI are materially better than mining, with longer durations and stronger counterparties, which is the basis for the re-rating from a mining multiple to a data-centre/compute multiple.
- **The scarce asset: owned, renewable, low-cost power.** HIVE's European (Sweden/Iceland) and Paraguayan (hydro) footprint is its differentiator — clean baseload that is increasingly valuable as European sovereign-AI and global AI power demand grows. The trade-off is **smaller US market access** than the US-listed peers.

The throughline matches the cohort ([[iren]], [[core-scientific]], applied-digital, [[riot-platforms]], [[hut-8]], [[terawulf]], [[mara]]): use mining as the cash-flowing bridge while migrating toward selling AI compute/capacity. HIVE's distinctive angle is running its *own* AI cloud (BUZZ) on a *renewable* power base.

## FY2026 results and Paraguay buildout (as of June 2026)

Figures from company releases; HIVE's fiscal year ends March 31. Not covered by the stockmarketapi fundamentals feed — treat as approximate where unaudited.

- **FY2026 (ended 2026-03-31) total revenue $297.8M, +158% YoY**, driven by the Bitcoin mining scale-up and BUZZ HPC growth.
- **Q3 FY2026 record revenue of $93.1M** with $32.1M gross operating margin (up >6x YoY); GAAP net loss of $91.3M in that quarter, driven primarily by $57.4M of accelerated depreciation tied to the Paraguay expansion.
- **Hashrate**: installed capacity reached **25.1 EH/s as of 2026-03-31** (~24.5 EH/s effective after fleet downclocking), with plans for a further ~10 EH/s of growth in Paraguay (hydro-powered Yguazú/Valenzuela sites).
- **BUZZ HPC (AI cloud)**: FY2026 revenue **$19.5M, +94% YoY**. In February 2026 HIVE signed a 2-year, $30M contract for 504 Nvidia B200 GPUs (~$15M ARR, lifting HPC annualized revenue ~75% to ~$35M), and is targeting **$140M ARR by Q4 2026 with ~11,000 GPUs**, subject to deployment and market conditions.

## Bull vs Bear Case

**Bull case**
- Strong FY2026 mining scale-up (revenue +158% YoY) plus a fast-growing AI cloud (BUZZ +94% YoY) — a real, ramping HPC business, not just announced capacity.
- Renewable, low-cost power base (Nordic + Paraguay hydro) is a structurally scarce and ESG-friendly asset as AI power demand and carbon scrutiny rise.
- European footprint positions HIVE for sovereign-AI demand that US-only peers can't address.
- BUZZ targets a meaningful ARR ramp (~$140M by Q4 2026 with ~11,000 GPUs), giving a tangible re-rating path.

**Bear case**
- Smaller scale and thinner liquidity than US-listed peers ([[riot-platforms]], [[core-scientific]]) — more volatility, less institutional sponsorship, options are thin.
- Heavy crypto-cycle residual: mining revenue still tracks BTC/ETH cycles, so it remains a high-beta [[bitcoin]] name.
- Paraguay expansion is capex-heavy (large accelerated depreciation already hitting GAAP results) and execution-dependent.
- HPC-pivot/BUZZ targets are subject to GPU deployment timing and AI-demand conditions; the same AI capex divergence risk applies.
- Geographic dispersion adds regulatory and operational complexity.

## Valuation Framework (qualitative)

HIVE defies a single multiple; the market weighs several axes (no specific multiples beyond the figures already on this page are asserted here):

- **Bitcoin-price beta.** With material residual mining, HIVE remains a high-beta [[bitcoin]] (and historically ETH) name; the BTC path is a primary value driver.
- **EV per EH/s.** Standard mining yardstick — benchmark HIVE's installed hashrate against [[riot-platforms]], [[iren]], [[mara]] on a fleet basis.
- **Power-cost per kWh.** Low-cost hydro/renewable power is the durable margin lever and the gating factor on HPC competitiveness.
- **BUZZ HPC ARR / GPU base.** The AI cloud's annualized recurring revenue and GPU count (and the trajectory toward the stated ARR target) are the core re-rating metric — valued more like a compute/cloud business than mining.
- **Geographic optionality.** European/sovereign-AI access is a qualitative premium; smaller US reach is a discount.
- **Dilution-adjusted per-share.** As a capital-raising small-cap, normalize all metrics for the share-count trajectory.

## Capital Structure & Dilution Risk

- **Equity-funded growth.** HIVE's mining scale-up and Paraguay buildout are capital-intensive; as a small-cap it has historically leaned on equity issuance, diluting per-share metrics — the central structural headwind for the stock.
- **Capex-driven depreciation.** The Paraguay expansion already drove ~$57.4M of accelerated depreciation in a single quarter (per FY2026 reporting), a reminder that the buildout pressures GAAP results even as revenue grows.
- **No large BTC treasury buffer** relative to [[mara]]/[[hut-8]], so funding leans on external capital — making dilution a more acute swing factor.
- **Implication for traders:** track the financing calendar and share count alongside production and BUZZ ARR; raises into weakness are recurring drawdown catalysts for thin small-caps.
