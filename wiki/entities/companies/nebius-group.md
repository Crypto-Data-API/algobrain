Nebius Group N.V. (NASDAQ: **NBIS**) is a Netherlands-headquartered [[technology]]-sector AI cloud infrastructure company — a **neocloud** specialising in GPU-as-a-Service for AI training and inference workloads. Nebius was created in August 2024 from the international assets of Yandex N.V. after the latter sold its Russian operations for approximately $5.2B and rebranded the remaining business. The post-restructuring entity retained Yandex's European AI cloud operations (Mäntsälä, Finland data centre), autonomous driving (Avride), data labelling (Toloka — majority later sold), and edtech (TripleTen), with **Nebius AI Cloud** as the dominant business line driving the equity story. As of Q1 2026, Nebius reported AI cloud quarterly revenue of $389.7M, group adjusted EBITDA turning positive, a cash balance of approximately $9.3B, quarterly capex of $2.5B, and a combined disclosed backlog with Microsoft and Meta totalling roughly $44B. (Source: [[2026-05-31-perplexity-nebius-nbis]])

## Business Overview & Segments

Nebius is a vertically integrated "neocloud" — it owns and operates data-centre capacity packed with [[nvidia]] accelerators and rents that compute to AI labs and hyperscalers, rather than reselling someone else's cloud. The equity story is overwhelmingly the **Nebius AI Cloud** line; the legacy Yandex-derived businesses are non-core and serve mostly as optionality or divestiture candidates. Demand for the company's output is a direct derivative of [[ai-data-center-power-demand|AI data-centre power demand]] and hyperscaler capex.

| Segment / line | What it is | Revenue mix (qualitative) |
|---|---|---|
| **Nebius AI Cloud** (core) | GPU-as-a-Service for AI training/inference on NVIDIA Hopper/Blackwell fleets; full-stack managed cloud (compute, storage, networking, MLOps tooling) | Dominant — effectively the entire equity story; the line that converts the ~$44B MSFT+META backlog to revenue |
| **Avride** | Autonomous-driving / robotics spun out of Yandex's AV programme | Small; strategic optionality, not a near-term earnings driver |
| **TripleTen** | Edtech / re-skilling bootcamps | Small; non-core, candidate for monetisation |
| **Toloka (residual)** | Data-labelling / RLHF data platform (majority stake later sold) | De-emphasised; partly divested |

The durable advantage is **funded, NVIDIA-allocated capacity delivered on schedule** — owning land, power, and GPU allocation that competitors cannot quickly replicate. Nebius increasingly sells multi-year dedicated-capacity contracts (the Microsoft and Meta deals) rather than spot GPU hours, which deepens revenue visibility.

## Economic Moat & Competitive Position

Nebius's [[economic-moat|moat]] is **narrow and contestable** relative to a CUDA-anchored platform like [[nvidia]], but it is real and built on a few reinforcing layers:

- **Privileged NVIDIA allocation + equity tie-up** — a strategic [[nvidia]] equity stake and preferred GPU allocation let Nebius secure scarce Blackwell capacity ahead of less-favoured neoclouds. This is the single most important competitive asset; it is also a single-supplier dependency.
- **Balance-sheet strength as a moat** — the ~$9.3B Yandex-sale war chest lets Nebius self-fund capex with less of the convertible-debt and ATM-dilution pressure that constrains debt-funded rivals like [[coreweave]]. In a capital-intensive business, cheaper capital *is* the edge.
- **Power and cost positioning** — the Mäntsälä (Finland) site offers low-carbon, low-cost, cold-climate grid economics favourable for AI workloads, differentiating from US peers competing in tight Texas/Arizona grid markets. This ties directly to [[ai-data-center-power-demand]] as the binding constraint on the sector.
- **Contracted backlog as switching friction** — multi-year dedicated-capacity deals with Microsoft and Meta create [[switching-costs]] and revenue visibility a spot-GPU reseller lacks.

The moat is **not impregnable**: there are no [[network-effects]] of the CUDA kind, GPU compute is broadly fungible across neoclouds, and the same hyperscaler customers are building their own silicon and clouds. The defensible core is *funded, on-time, NVIDIA-allocated capacity* — the threat is commoditisation of GPU rental and any shift in NVIDIA's allocation favouritism.

## June 2026 update — contract and expansion detail

Specifics behind the ~$44B headline backlog and the latest expansion news:

- **Microsoft (September 2025):** multi-year AI infrastructure agreement with a base contract value of **$17.4B through 2031**, expandable to **$19.4B** at Microsoft's option.
- **Meta (March 2026):** expanded five-year agreement worth **up to $27B** — approximately **$12B of committed dedicated capacity** plus up to **$15B of additional capacity options**, with major deployments beginning in early 2027.
- **UK expansion (announced 8 June 2026):** **£1.7B investment** across four UK sites running NVIDIA Blackwell Ultra infrastructure, reaching **65MW combined capacity** when fully ramped in 2027 — Nebius's largest European commitment outside Finland.
- **Financing history:** ~$1B convertible notes (June 2025) plus ~$4.2B combined equity and convertible raise (September 2025); 2026 capex guidance lifted to **$20–25B**, confirming the funding-gap math in the bear case below.

NBIS sits roughly **between hyperscalers and CRWV** on the AI infrastructure stack: more capital-disciplined than CRWV due to the one-time cash injection from the Yandex sale, but smaller-scale than the hyperscalers and with a more concentrated customer base than either.

## Growth Drivers & Catalysts

- **Backlog conversion** — turning the ~$44B disclosed MSFT+META commitments into recognised revenue over 2026-2031 is the central catalyst path.
- **AI data-centre capex super-cycle** — Nebius revenue is a derivative of hyperscaler and sovereign-AI buildouts; see [[ai-data-center-power-demand]] and the [[ai-capex-vs-cash-flow-divergence]] framework.
- **Blackwell / Blackwell Ultra ramp** — newer NVIDIA architectures reset capacity value per rack and per watt; UK sites are slated for Blackwell Ultra.
- **Geographic expansion** — UK (£1.7B, four sites, 65MW by 2027), Kansas City (US), and Mäntsälä (Finland) expansion milestones.
- **Operating leverage** — group adjusted EBITDA turned positive in Q1 2026; rising utilisation should expand margins as capacity fills.
- **Customer prepayments** — upfront hyperscaler payments convert demand into financing without dilution or debt.
- **Recurring catalysts** — quarterly AI-cloud revenue prints, new contract/expansion announcements, financing events (convertibles/equity), and MSFT/META capex guidance.

## Bull case

1. **Funded growth.** ~$9.3B cash is a structural advantage that no peer has. The Yandex restructuring left Nebius with a war chest that can fund years of capex without the convertible-debt and ATM-issuance pressure that defines CRWV.
2. **Real backlog from real hyperscalers.** ~$44B of disclosed MSFT + META commitments is contracted demand, not narrative. Backlog-to-revenue conversion is the next 6–12 months of catalyst flow.
3. **Positive EBITDA at scale.** Most growth-stage AI infrastructure is still loss-making at the EBITDA line; Nebius hit positive group adjusted EBITDA in Q1 2026, signalling operating leverage as capacity utilisation ramps.
4. **NVIDIA partnership.** Strategic equity stake plus preferred GPU allocation. As long as NVDA's allocation favouritism persists, Nebius can deliver capacity on schedule.
5. **European power-cost advantage.** Mäntsälä's Finnish grid economics are favourable for AI workloads (low-carbon, cold climate for cooling, stable supply). Differentiates from US peers competing in tight Texas/Arizona grid markets.
6. **Customer prepayment model.** Some hyperscaler contracts include upfront payments funding capacity buildout — converts customer demand directly into financing without dilution or debt.

## Bear case

1. **Massive funding gap.** $9.3B cash against $20–25B implied 2026 capex means Nebius needs **$10–15B+ of additional financing** in 2026 alone. Convertible debt issuance, additional equity, or larger customer prepayments are all on the table. Dilution risk is real.
2. **Customer concentration.** MSFT + META are reportedly the bulk of the backlog. Same risk profile as CRWV: if either hyperscaler shifts internal infrastructure investment, NBIS's growth trajectory breaks. Both are *also* competitors building their own AI clouds.
3. **The [[ai-capex-vs-cash-flow-divergence|AI capex divergence]] thesis applies.** NBIS is in the same Scenario 3 risk cohort as CRWV and NVDA. If hyperscaler capex contracts (any reason — capex fatigue, AI revenue disappointment, [[solow-paradox-2026|productivity J-curve fails]]), NBIS de-rates with the cohort.
4. **Backlog ≠ revenue.** $44B headline backlog converts to revenue over multiple years, subject to delivery timing, contract structure, and cancellation/repricing clauses. Traders pricing NBIS on backlog should discount aggressively for conversion risk.
5. **Yandex legacy overhang.** Even after restructuring, governance and headline-risk discounts persist. Russian-origin legacy can trigger sentiment-driven repricing during geopolitical events.
6. **GPU depreciation.** Same problem as CRWV — H100/H200/Blackwell capex base depreciates rapidly economically as next-generation NVDA chips arrive. Multi-billion-dollar asset write-downs are possible if utilisation softens.
7. **NVDA dependency single-supplier risk.** If NVDA allocation priorities shift, Nebius has no substitute supplier at scale.

## Comparison to existing wiki AI infrastructure narratives

NBIS fits cleanly into the [[ai-capex-vs-cash-flow-divergence]] framework but on the *better* side of the spectrum than CRWV:

- **Scenario 1** (AI revenue vindicates capex): NBIS is well-positioned — funded, real customers, positive EBITDA, NVDA partnership
- **Scenario 2** (capex sustained through balance-sheet stretch): NBIS has more runway than CRWV before forced dilution; this is the muddle-through path most likely
- **Scenario 3** (capex contraction): NBIS de-rates with the cohort but its cash position cushions the immediate financing shock that hits CRWV first

In the [[ai-microcap-pump-pattern]] framework, NBIS scores zero pump signals — real revenue, real customers, real cash, real EBITDA, mainstream institutional coverage, no paid IR. **NBIS is a legitimate AI infrastructure business; the question is valuation and execution, not authenticity.**

## SMSF / portfolio context

NBIS is fundamentally a real business — the SHAZ-style "not suitable for SMSF" objection does **not** apply. The question for an Australian SMSF is the standard one for any high-beta AI infrastructure name:

1. **Existing AI exposure.** A portfolio already 47% AI-correlated (per [[ai-capex-vs-cash-flow-divergence]] vulnerability framing) adds more concentration with NBIS. NBIS is highly correlated to NVDA + CRWV + the broader AI cloud cohort.
2. **Funding-gap volatility.** Even as a real business, NBIS will be volatile around the next major financing event. Convertible issuances, equity raises, or new customer prepayment announcements can move the stock ±10–20% in single sessions.
3. **Backlog catalyst path.** The next 12 months of MSFT + META backlog conversion are the bull-case payoff window. If conversion proceeds smoothly, NBIS re-rates. If hyperscaler capex stalls, NBIS falls with the cohort.
4. **Better choice than CRWV?** Arguably yes for a balance-sheet-conscious investor — NBIS's cash cushion materially reduces capital-structure tail risk vs CRWV's debt-funded model. Tradeoff: smaller scale and Yandex-legacy discount.

- **Customer concentration** — MSFT + META dominate the backlog and are *also* competitors building first-party clouds and silicon; a shift in their internal investment breaks the trajectory.

- **GPU depreciation** — Hopper/Blackwell capex depreciates rapidly as next-gen chips arrive; multi-billion write-downs are possible if utilisation softens.
- **AI-capex cyclicality** — the [[ai-capex-vs-cash-flow-divergence]] thesis applies; a synchronized hyperscaler capex pause de-rates the whole cohort, NBIS included.
- **Yandex-legacy / geopolitical overhang** — governance and headline-risk discounts persist and can trigger sentiment-driven repricing.

## Valuation & How the Stock Trades

Nebius is an unprofitable-at-the-net-line, capex-absorbing growth name, so it is valued on **EV/revenue (and EV/contracted-backlog)** and on the **margin trajectory** rather than earnings — it pays no dividend and reinvests every dollar of operating cash into capacity. Mechanically:

- **Valuation lens** — the market prices NBIS on AI-cloud run-rate revenue and the credibility of the ~$44B backlog converting on schedule; positive group adjusted EBITDA (Q1 2026) is the key proof-point that operating leverage is arriving as utilisation ramps. Earnings multiples are not the right frame while the company is in heavy build-out.
- **High beta, AI-cohort proxy** — NBIS is a high-beta expression of the AI-capex theme, highly correlated to [[nvidia]], [[coreweave]], and hyperscaler capex sentiment; it moves with the cohort, not idiosyncratically.
- **Event-driven volatility** — the stock can move ±10-20% in single sessions around financing events (convertibles/equity raises), new contract/expansion announcements, and quarterly AI-cloud revenue prints.
- **No dividend** — capital is fully reinvested into capacity; total return depends entirely on growth execution and backlog conversion.
