OpenAI is an American AI research and deployment company founded in December 2015 by [[sam-altman|Sam Altman]], Elon Musk, Ilya Sutskever, Greg Brockman, and others. Originally chartered as a non-profit, it transitioned to a "capped-profit" structure in 2019 to attract the capital required for frontier model training. It is the builder of the GPT family of large language models, the ChatGPT consumer product, and Sora video generation, and is the central counterparty in [[microsoft|Microsoft]]'s AI distribution strategy through Azure (Source: [[2026-04-22-gap-finder-possible-ai-fueled-global-job-loss-reces]]).

## Overview

OpenAI sits at the centre of the 2025-2026 AI capex narrative that has driven [[2024-nvidia-ai-boom|NVIDIA's]] re-rating, [[microsoft|Microsoft's]] re-rating to a growth-platform multiple, and the broader [[margin-expansion-disparity|tech-vs-non-tech margin gap]]. The company sells:

- **API access** to GPT-class models for developers and enterprises
- **ChatGPT** consumer subscription product (free tier, Plus, Team, Enterprise)
- **Sora** generative video
- **Custom enterprise deployments** including industry-specific fine-tunes
- **Embeddings, fine-tuning, and tool-use APIs** that underpin downstream agent products

Elon Musk departed the OpenAI board in 2018 citing potential conflicts with Tesla's AI work; he later founded competing AI venture xAI.

## The Microsoft partnership

The Microsoft partnership is the single most market-relevant fact about OpenAI. [[microsoft|Microsoft's]] cumulative investment of approximately $49 billion (per Microsoft's own page in this wiki) makes Microsoft the dominant distributor of OpenAI capabilities through:

- **Azure OpenAI Service** — enterprise API access via Microsoft's cloud
- **Microsoft 365 Copilot** — embedded across Office, Teams, Outlook
- **GitHub Copilot** — code completion and agentic coding
- **Bing / Copilot** — search and consumer assistant

Trader implications:

- Mag 7 valuations are partly a bet that Microsoft monetises OpenAI capabilities at premium margin — see [[margin-expansion-disparity]].
- A material change in the OpenAI–Microsoft commercial terms (renegotiation, exclusivity expiry, governance rupture) would reprice MSFT meaningfully.
- Any structural OpenAI funding event (further primary, tender, public listing) becomes a comp for [[anthropic|Anthropic's]] $380B mark from February 2026.

## Competitive position

OpenAI's primary frontier-model competitor is [[anthropic|Anthropic]], with its Claude family. Other competitors include [[alphabet|Google]] (Gemini, Google Cloud AI), Meta's open-weight Llama family, and a long tail of vertical-specific entrants. The intensity of frontier competition is itself a trade thesis: incumbents are forced to defend share at the cost of margin, while frontier-lab wins erode the moat of legacy enterprise software (Source: [[2026-04-22-gap-finder-possible-ai-fueled-global-job-loss-reces]]).

## 2025-2026 structural events

The period from late 2025 reshaped OpenAI's corporate and financing structure — the facts below are the ones most relevant to public-market traders:

- **Restructuring to PBC (28 October 2025)** — OpenAI completed its recapitalization: the for-profit arm became **OpenAI Group PBC** (a Delaware public benefit corporation) controlled by the nonprofit OpenAI Foundation. The restructuring removed the legacy 100x capped-profit return structure and cleared the legal path to an eventual public listing.
- **Microsoft agreement rewritten (October 2025)** — under the new deal, [[microsoft|Microsoft]]'s economic interest was crystallized at approximately 27% on a fully diluted, as-converted basis. Azure exclusivity was replaced with a **right of first refusal** on future OpenAI cloud capacity, freeing OpenAI to buy compute from other providers (Oracle, CoreWeave, AWS, Google).
- **SoftBank round** — SoftBank completed a $41 billion financing program in OpenAI (including a final $22.5 billion tranche closed late December 2025, plus ~$11 billion from co-investors), taking SoftBank's aggregate stake to roughly 11%.
- **March 2026 round** — a ~$122 billion funding round closed in March 2026 at a reported **~$852 billion valuation**, with backers reported to include Amazon, [[nvidia|Nvidia]] and SoftBank.
- **IPO chatter** — press reports through H1 2026 indicate OpenAI is preparing what could be among the largest IPOs in history, with a reported target of raising on the order of $60 billion at a valuation approaching $1 trillion. Timing unconfirmed as of June 2026.
- **Stargate** — the Stargate joint venture (announced January 2025; SoftBank financial lead, OpenAI operational lead, with [[oracle|Oracle]] and MGX as equity partners) targets ~$500 billion of US AI infrastructure over four years. OpenAI's compute contract with Oracle is reported at ~$300 billion across ~4.5 GW of capacity, and is the single largest driver of Oracle's RPO backlog explosion (see [[oracle]] and [[ai-capex-vs-cash-flow-divergence]]).

## Relevance to the AI-recession thesis

OpenAI is central to multiple threads in the AI-recession debate documented in this wiki:

- **[[ai-layoff-trap]]** — GPT-class models are the deployment mechanism for the cognitive-labour substitution the Falk-Tsoukalas model treats as an exogenous shock
- **[[esrb-ai-systemic-risk-channels]]** — OpenAI is the leading example of the foundation-model concentration the ESRB's December 2025 report flagged as a systemic-risk channel (overreliance)
- **[[capital-vs-labor-asymmetry]]** — OpenAI shareholders capture AI productivity gains immediately while displaced cognitive workers absorb the losses on a 2-4 quarter delayed schedule
- **[[ai-capex-vs-cash-flow-divergence]]** — OpenAI is the primary buyer of NVIDIA-class compute that drives the divergence between AI capex headlines and operating cash flow at the customer base
- **AI capex narrative** — central to the [[2024-nvidia-ai-boom]] thesis and to enterprise AI adoption forecasts

## Trader implications

- **Microsoft equity** — direct exposure; track Azure OpenAI revenue disclosures and Copilot attach rates
- **Foundation-model concentration risk** — a single-point-of-failure at OpenAI (outage, governance crisis, regulatory action) could ripple through the financial-services AI stack flagged in [[esrb-ai-systemic-risk-channels]]
- **Comp tables for AI M&A** — OpenAI valuation prints anchor multiples for the 266 AI M&A deals in Q1 2026 (90% YoY growth)
- **Enterprise software disruption** — seat-based SaaS vendors whose work overlaps with GPT-class capability face structural margin compression alongside the [[anthropic|Anthropic]]-driven legal-tech disruption case
- **White-collar displacement thesis** — OpenAI products (ChatGPT, Operator-class agents, custom GPTs) are direct mechanisms for the [[capital-vs-labor-asymmetry]] / [[ai-layoff-trap]] dynamics

## How traders get exposure

OpenAI remains private as of June 2026; exposure routes are indirect:

- **[[microsoft|Microsoft]] (MSFT)** — ~27% as-converted economic interest plus Azure/Copilot distribution
- **[[oracle|Oracle]] (ORCL)** — the ~$300B Stargate compute contract makes ORCL the highest-beta public proxy for OpenAI's capex (and its counterparty risk)
- **[[nvidia|Nvidia]] (NVDA)** and the AI-infrastructure complex (CoreWeave, power/data-centre names) — supply-chain exposure
- **SoftBank (9984.T)** — ~11% direct stake
- **Secondary markets / pre-IPO funds** — employee tender offers have set most valuation marks; retail access is limited
- **The eventual IPO** — would be the first direct exposure and a repricing event for every proxy above
