Chegg Inc. (NYSE: CHGG) is an online education company whose homework-help subscription business was structurally disrupted by generative AI — it is the canonical "ChatGPT casualty" trade. For traders, Chegg is a deep small-cap distressed-tech name whose moves are driven by subscriber attrition data, its antitrust lawsuit against Google, and restructuring/strategic-review headlines rather than normal earnings cadence.

## Business Overview

Chegg's core Subscription Services segment (Chegg Study, Chegg Study Pack) sells step-by-step homework solutions and study tools to college students. It also owns **Busuu**, a language-learning app, which has become the relative bright spot (Busuu grew ~7% YoY in early 2025 and was projected at ~$48 million of 2025 revenue with positive adjusted EBITDA targeted by Q1 2026). The legacy study business has been in freefall since ChatGPT's late-2022 launch substituted free AI answers for paid homework help, compounded by Google's AI Overviews removing search traffic that historically fed Chegg's acquisition funnel.

## Business Segments & Products

Chegg is, in trading terms, a **structural-decline core plus a smaller growth asset** — the value debate is whether the cash from the dying study business plus the residual worth of Busuu exceeds the equity's distressed price.

| Segment / asset | What it is | Trajectory |
|---|---|---|
| **Subscription Services (Chegg Study / Study Pack)** | Step-by-step homework solutions, study tools, expert Q&A for college students | The disrupted core — subscriber collapse since ChatGPT; the "ChatGPT casualty" |
| **Busuu** | Language-learning app (acquired) | Relative bright spot; growing, moving toward positive adjusted EBITDA |
| **Skills / Writing / adjacent tools** | Smaller services (citation tools, skills courses) | Minor; some wound down in restructuring |
| **Google antitrust litigation** | Lawsuit over AI Overviews diverting traffic | Optionality, not a business line — a potential lever/settlement |

The model used to be a flywheel: free organic search traffic (Google) funnelled students to a paid homework-help subscription. **Generative AI broke both ends** — ChatGPT substitutes for the paid answer, and Google's AI Overviews cut off the free-traffic top of the funnel. Busuu and the cash balance are the residual sources of value as the core runs off.

## Competitive Positioning & Peers — the AI-Disruption Map

Chegg is the canonical case study of an incumbent content business disrupted by free generative AI. Its "competitors" are less other ed-tech companies and more the **substitution by general-purpose AI**:

| Competitor / force | Nature of threat | Effect on Chegg |
|---|---|---|
| **ChatGPT / general LLMs** | Free/cheap substitute for paid homework answers | Existential — the primary driver of subscriber collapse |
| [[alphabet]] (Google AI Overviews) | Removes free search traffic that fed the funnel | Cuts customer acquisition at the source; subject of Chegg's antitrust suit |
| **Course Hero / Quizlet** | Direct ed-tech study-tool rivals | Facing the same AI substitution; shared structural pressure |
| [[duolingo]] | Language-learning leader (vs Busuu) | The benchmark Busuu is measured against; far larger and AI-forward |
| **AI-native study tools** | New entrants building on LLMs | Reset the category around free/AI-first models |

Versus **[[duolingo]]**, Busuu is the small challenger in language learning — Duolingo is the scaled, AI-integrated leader, which both validates the category's value and caps Busuu's standalone upside. The over-arching theme: Chegg is the **cleanest public proxy for "AI disrupts incumbent content businesses,"** frequently cited alongside Stack Overflow traffic declines.

## Bull vs Bear Case

**Bull case (deep-value / optionality)**
- Distressed valuation (>95% below peak) may already price in terminal decline; downside partly capped by cash and Busuu's residual value.
- **Google antitrust lawsuit** is a binary upside lever (settlement/ruling).
- Aggressive cost-cutting can preserve cash and buy time for Busuu to scale or be divested.
- Strategic/takeover or break-up optionality after the standalone decision.

**Bear case (value trap)**
- Core study business in terminal, possibly irreversible decline — AI substitution is structural, not cyclical.
- Google AI Overviews keep starving the acquisition funnel; no clear path to re-grow subscribers.
- Cash burn against restructuring; Busuu too small to offset the core's run-off.
- Litigation is slow and uncertain; "AI disrupts content" applies to Chegg's own AI pivots too.

## Valuation Framework

Chegg is a distressed-equity / sum-of-the-parts situation, so the qualitative checklist is about decline rate and residual value, not growth multiples:

- **Subscriber decline rate** — the central metric; the speed and floor of subscriber attrition drive everything.
- **AI existential risk** — the qualitative override: is the core in managed decline or terminal collapse?
- **Cash position vs cash burn** — distressed names trade on balance-sheet runway and downside support.
- **Busuu standalone value** — growth, path to profitability, and divestiture/SOTP optionality.
- **Litigation optionality** — expected value of the Google antitrust case as a non-operating lever.
- **FCF / restructuring savings** — whether cost cuts can keep the business cash-generative through the decline.

## 2025–2026 Developments

- **Subscriber collapse**: Subscription Services subscribers fell from 6.6 million at end-2024 (-14% YoY) to **2.6 million by Q2 2025 (-40% YoY)**. Q1 2025 revenue dropped ~30% YoY.
- **Google lawsuit**: In February 2025 Chegg filed an antitrust complaint against [[alphabet|Google]], alleging Google's AI Overviews unjustly retained traffic that historically came to Chegg, damaging revenue and forcing layoffs. The case remains a key optionality lever for the equity.
- **Strategic review**: Announced February 2025 (options included sale, go-private, or standalone); the Board **concluded the review in October 2025**, electing to remain a standalone public company.
- **Layoffs/restructuring**: 248 employees (~22% of workforce) cut in May 2025, on top of two 2024 restructurings; further cost actions included closing US/Canada offices, cutting marketing and new product development.

## Capital Return & Dilution

Chegg is in cash-preservation mode: capital prioritised toward balance-sheet runway through the decline. Historically the company used convertible debt and conducted buybacks at higher prices; in distress, the watch items are the **cash position, any convertible maturities/refinancing, and dilution risk** if it must raise. Cash-return announcements are treated by the market as a downside-support signal rather than a growth signal.

## Risks

- Terminal-decline risk in the core study business as AI substitution proves structural.

- Cash burn against restructuring costs; potential financing/dilution if runway shortens.
