Hasbro (NASDAQ: HAS) is a toy and games company in the [[consumer-discretionary|Consumer Discretionary]] sector and a constituent of the [[s-and-p-500]] index. The trading story has decisively shifted from toys to games: the high-margin **Wizards of the Coast** segment (Magic: The Gathering, Dungeons & Dragons) plus digital licensing (Monopoly Go! royalties) now drive earnings, while the legacy Consumer Products toy segment shrinks and absorbs tariff volatility.

## Business Overview & Segments

- **Wizards of the Coast and Digital Gaming** — Magic: The Gathering (tabletop + Arena), D&D, and digital-game licensing including Monopoly Go! (Scopely) royalties. Structurally higher margin than toys; now the company's primary growth engine.
- **Consumer Products** — traditional toys and games (Play-Doh, Nerf, Transformers, Monopoly board games), exposed to retailer ordering cycles and China-sourcing tariffs.
- **Entertainment** — film/TV licensing, slimmed down after the eOne film/TV business was sold to Lionsgate (December 2023) following heavy impairments — the source of the distorted 2023 ROE (-137%) and the still-elevated leverage in the [[#Fundamentals]] section.

The strategic pivot is unmistakable: Hasbro has shifted from a **toy company that owns games** to a **games-and-licensing company that owns toys**. Magic: The Gathering is now the single most important franchise — a collectible card game with recurring set releases, a healthy secondary market, and high incremental margins — supplemented by capital-light digital royalties (Monopoly Go!). The legacy toy business is run for cash and brand-licensing optionality while the games engine carries growth.

## Economic Moat & Competitive Position

Hasbro holds a **moat that is wide in games and narrow in toys**, built almost entirely on owned [[intellectual-property|intellectual property]]:

- **Irreplaceable game franchises** — Magic: The Gathering and Dungeons & Dragons are decades-old IP with passionate communities, deep rule ecosystems, and active secondary markets. Players' invested collections and rules knowledge create real [[switching-costs|switching costs]] — a far stronger moat than any toy line.
- **Licensing flywheel** — owning the IP lets Hasbro monetize it across formats (tabletop, digital, film, merchandise) at high margin; Monopoly Go! shows the capital-light royalty model, and Magic's "Universes Beyond" (Marvel, Lord of the Rings, etc.) extends the franchise into partner IP.
- **Brand recognition** — Monopoly, Nerf, Play-Doh, and Transformers are globally recognized, supporting licensing and retail shelf presence.
- **Release-cadence pricing power** — Magic's set calendar and Secret Lair drops let Hasbro tune supply and price to demand.

The moat is **concentrated and franchise-dependent**: the toy business has little defensible advantage (low switching costs, retailer power, tariff exposure, competition from [[mattel]] and Games Workshop), and the durable value sits in Wizards. That concentration is itself the chief vulnerability — a Magic slowdown re-rates the whole equity quickly.

## Growth Drivers & Catalysts

- **Wizards of the Coast / Magic growth** — the single most-watched line; "Universes Beyond" partner sets (Marvel, etc.), Secret Lair drops, Arena, and a healthy release cadence drive the franchise.
- **Digital licensing royalties** — Monopoly Go! (Scopely) and other capital-light royalty streams that flow at very high incremental margin.
- **Cost transformation** — multi-year savings program lifting adjusted operating margin (24.2% in FY2025, +3.9 pts).
- **Margin mix shift** — every point of revenue moving from toys to games is margin-accretive.
- **Deleveraging** — using games cash flow to bring down the elevated D/E (2.85x).
- **Recurring catalysts** — quarterly earnings (Magic growth rate is the key line), Magic set-release and Universes Beyond announcements, tariff-policy headlines, holiday-season sell-through, and the Monopoly Go! royalty trajectory.

## 2025–2026 Developments

- **FY2025 (reported 11 February 2026):** revenue ~US$4.7 billion, **up 14%**, driven by record **45% growth at Wizards of the Coast**; Magic: The Gathering finished its strongest year ever, **up 59%** (Universes Beyond sets — e.g. the Q4 Avatar: The Last Airbender set — plus backlist and Secret Lair strength).
- **Margins:** adjusted operating profit US$1,140M (+36%); adjusted operating margin 24.2%, up 3.9 points on mix and cost-transformation savings.
- **Tariffs:** Consumer Products revenue fell 4% in 2025 amid macro and retailer volatility from the Q2 2025 US tariff announcements; management protected margins via pricing discipline, sourcing shifts, and cost productivity.
- **2026 guidance:** total revenue growth of 3–5%; Wizards expected to grow mid-single digits on a healthy Magic release cadence.

## Trading Relevance

- **Liquidity:** S&P 500 mid-cap with liquid options; heavily traded around quarterly prints.
- **Catalysts:** quarterly earnings (Magic growth rate is the single most-watched line), Magic set-release calendar and Universes Beyond partner announcements (e.g. Marvel sets), tariff policy headlines, holiday-season sell-through data, Monopoly Go! royalty trajectory.

## Bull Case vs Bear Case

### Bull case
- Wizards of the Coast is a structurally high-margin, growing franchise — Magic finished its strongest year ever (+59% in FY2025), powering 14% total revenue growth.
- The mix shift from toys to games is margin-accretive; adjusted operating margin reached 24.2% (+3.9 pts) in FY2025.
- Capital-light digital royalties (Monopoly Go!) add high-incremental-margin cash with no inventory or tariff risk.
- Owned [[intellectual-property|IP]] (Magic, D&D, Monopoly, Transformers) is irreplaceable and monetizable across formats.
- Cost transformation plus games cash flow create a deleveraging path.

### Bear case
- Severe franchise concentration — a Magic slowdown re-rates the stock fast; the bull case rests on one line item.
- Toy segment in secular decline and exposed to China-sourcing tariffs and retailer ordering cycles.
- Elevated leverage (D/E 2.85x) limits flexibility; the dividend (~$2.80/yr) consumed >100% of GAAP EPS in 2024.
- "Universes Beyond" risks over-issuing Magic product and eroding the secondary-market scarcity that underpins collector demand.
- Lumpy, set-cadence-driven earnings make quarters hard to predict.

## Key Risks

- **Magic / franchise concentration** — Wizards is the earnings engine; any deceleration in Magic re-rates the equity quickly.
- **Toy-segment secular decline** — Consumer Products shrinking, with retailer power and changing play patterns pressuring the legacy business.
- **Tariffs / China sourcing** — imported-toy cost exposure to tariff policy (Consumer Products revenue fell 4% in 2025 on macro/retailer volatility).
- **Leverage** — D/E 2.85x constrains flexibility and the dividend's coverage; a residual of the eOne-era impairments.
- **Over-issuance risk** — flooding the market with Magic product (Universes Beyond, Secret Lair) could erode scarcity and collector trust.
- **Earnings lumpiness** — set-release timing makes quarterly results volatile and hard to model.

## Valuation & How the Stock Trades

Hasbro trades increasingly as a **games-and-licensing** story rather than a toy stock: the multiple keys off the Magic growth rate and the margin mix shift, with the toy segment and leverage as discounts (see the Fundamentals block below for the figures as pulled). Mechanically:

- **Magic growth rate is the swing variable** — the single most-watched line; the stock re-rates up or down on the Wizards trajectory more than on the headline print.
- **Mix-shift re-rating** — as high-margin games and royalties grow as a share of revenue, the market can award a higher multiple than the legacy toy comp [[mattel]] commands.
- **Dividend with stretched coverage** — pays a high-yielding dividend (~$2.80/yr) that consumed >100% of GAAP EPS in 2024; an income draw, but with coverage and leverage caveats.
- **Mid-cap liquidity** — an S&P 500 mid-cap with liquid options; heavily traded around quarterly prints and Magic set announcements.
- **Read-throughs** — moves with [[mattel]] on toy sentiment and with [[take-two-interactive]]/[[electronic-arts]] and Games Workshop on games/collectible sentiment; tariff headlines and holiday sell-through are macro overlays.
