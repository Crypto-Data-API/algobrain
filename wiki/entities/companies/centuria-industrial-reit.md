**Centuria Industrial REIT** (ASX: CIP) is the ASX's largest pure-play industrial and logistics [[real-estate|real estate]] [[reits|REIT]], listed on the [[asx-limited|Australian Securities Exchange]] and a constituent of the [[asx-200]] index. CIP owns a nationally diversified portfolio of high-quality industrial assets concentrated in major Australian metropolitan logistics markets, underpinned by a diverse base of corporate tenants across e-commerce, cold storage, manufacturing and distribution. Beyond core logistics, CIP carries embedded **data-centre conversion optionality** — adaptive reuse and new-build schemes on existing industrial sites — giving it a secondary thematic link to [[ai-data-center-power-demand|AI data-centre demand]] alongside peer [[nextdc]].

## Business Overview & Segments

CIP generates returns for unitholders through two principal channels: recurring net rental income from its industrial property portfolio, and capital appreciation from asset management, development and revaluation. The portfolio is weighted toward infill metropolitan industrial locations in Sydney, Melbourne and Brisbane, where vacancy has historically been tight and rental growth strong. The REIT's strategy also includes a data centre development pillar that seeks to unlock additional value from existing industrial sites through adaptive reuse and new-build data centre schemes.

| Segment / activity | What it is | Role |
|---|---|---|
| **Core metropolitan logistics** | Infill warehouses/distribution in Sydney, Melbourne, Brisbane | The earnings engine; tight-vacancy markets driving positive re-leasing spreads |
| **Specialised industrial** | Cold storage, manufacturing, transport/distribution | Diversifies tenant base across e-commerce, food and industrials |
| **Data-centre conversion** | Adaptive reuse / new-build data centres on existing sites (e.g. Clayton ~40MW DA) | Value-add optionality leveraging land in power-rich industrial locations |
| **Capital recycling** | Selling non-core assets, reinvesting in core infill logistics | Improves portfolio quality and funds developments |

The defining feature is **scarcity of well-located industrial land**: tight metro vacancy lets CIP roll under-rented leases to market (a +20% average re-leasing spread in H1 FY26), producing organic income growth that fixed-rent net-lease REITs lack.

## Economic Moat & Competitive Position

CIP's [[economic-moat|moat]] is **location- and supply-driven**, characteristic of infill industrial:

- **Irreplaceable infill land** — well-located metropolitan logistics sites near population centres are supply-constrained by planning and land scarcity, underpinning rental growth and high occupancy (95.7% in H1 FY26).
- **Positive re-leasing spreads** — under-rented in-place leases roll to higher market rents (+20% in H1 FY26), an embedded organic-growth engine.
- **Centuria platform** — management, leasing and development capability from the broader Centuria group, plus access to capital and deal flow.
- **Data-centre optionality** — power-rich industrial land can be converted to higher-value data-centre use, an upside lever competitors with worse-located stock lack.
- **Scale & diversification** — ~85 assets and a broad tenant base reduce single-tenant risk.

The position is **structurally favourable but cyclical**: industrial is the most sought-after property sector, but CIP is a price-taker on cap rates and bond yields, and a wave of new logistics supply or a demand downturn would compress the re-leasing tailwind.

## Growth Drivers & Catalysts

- **Positive re-leasing spreads** — rolling under-rented leases to market (+20% in H1 FY26) drives like-for-like NOI growth (+5.1% in H1 FY26).
- **Tight metro vacancy & rental growth** — supply-constrained infill logistics supports occupancy and rents.
- **Data-centre conversion pipeline** — the Clayton ~40MW development application and further adaptive-reuse schemes are upside optionality tied to [[ai-data-center-power-demand|AI/data-centre power demand]].
- **FFO & distribution guidance** — FY26 FFO guidance upgraded to 18.2–18.5 cpu (up to 6% growth) with 16.8 cpu distribution.
- **Falling rates / cap-rate compression** — supports NTA, narrows the discount-to-NTA and reopens the transaction market.
- **Refinancing on better terms** — A$450M refinanced in H1 FY26 lowers funding cost.

## Bull Case vs Bear Case

### Bull case
- Pure exposure to the most favoured property sector — supply-constrained metro logistics with strong rental growth.
- Embedded organic growth from large positive re-leasing spreads as under-rented leases roll.
- Data-centre conversion optionality on power-rich industrial land — a genuine AI-thematic kicker.
- Trades below NTA (P/B ~0.75), offering an asset-backed discount entry.
- FY26 FFO guidance upgrade signals the operating cycle is improving.

### Bear case
- Pure rate-duration A-REIT — higher-for-longer rates compress NTA and the unit price.
- New logistics supply could erode the vacancy-driven rental tailwind.
- Gearing/cap-rate sensitivity; revaluations swing reported net income (FY23 statutory loss).
- Data-centre conversions are capital-intensive and execution/planning-dependent — optionality, not certainty.
- No meaningful listed options market and minimal franking limit some investor appeal.

## Key Risks

- **Interest-rate / cap-rate sensitivity** — the dominant risk; rate moves drive NTA and the discount/premium to NTA.
- **New supply** — a logistics development wave could compress occupancy and re-leasing spreads.
- **Gearing & refinancing** — covenant headroom and the cost of rolling debt in a higher-rate regime.
- **Revaluation volatility** — fair-value swings can produce statutory losses (as in FY23).
- **Data-centre execution** — conversions require planning approval, power and large capital; delivery risk.
- **Tenant/demand cycle** — an e-commerce or industrial-demand slowdown would soften rents.

## Valuation & How the Stock Trades

CIP is valued as a **rate-sensitive industrial A-REIT** on FFO, distribution yield and NTA, with a data-centre option overlay:

- **FFO & distribution** — the operating yardsticks; FY26 FFO guidance 18.2–18.5 cpu and 16.8 cpu distribution. The market capitalises FFO growth from re-leasing spreads and developments.
- **NTA & discount** — a P/B of ~0.75 means the market prices the portfolio below stated NTA — the classic A-REIT discount debate; the discount narrows as rates fall.
- **Cap rates & bond yields** — portfolio value and the unit price track cap rates, which move with long-bond yields; CIP is rate-duration like all A-REITs.
- **Data-centre option value** — the Clayton ~40MW DA and conversion pipeline add embedded optionality the market only partly prices until projects de-risk.
- **Yield, franking & beta** — distributions are mostly unfranked (REIT income), so the appeal is the cash yield and FFO growth; reasonable mid-cap liquidity but no meaningful listed options market. See the data block above for point-in-time figures as pulled.

## History

Centuria Industrial REIT was originally launched in 2012 and ingested into the Centuria platform following Centuria's acquisition of the former 360 Capital Industrial Fund in 2016. Under Centuria's management, the trust has grown from under A$500 million in assets to a multi-billion-dollar platform through a series of portfolio-level acquisitions, including the A$925 million Telstra Data Centre portfolio in 2020, which established CIP's initial data centre footprint. The trust has progressively recycled capital out of non-core assets and into core metropolitan infill logistics.

## Recent Developments

For the half year ended 31 December 2025 (H1 FY26), CIP reported net property income of A$101.2 million (up from A$96.1 million in the prior corresponding period) and like-for-like net operating income growth of 5.1%. The portfolio comprised 85 assets valued at A$3.9 billion with a 7.1-year weighted average lease expiry. During the half, CIP completed 143,904 sqm of leasing across 18 transactions (approximately 11% of portfolio gross lettable area) at an average re-leasing spread of 20%, with occupancy rising to 95.7%. The trust upgraded FY26 FFO guidance to 18.2–18.5 cents per unit (up to 6% growth over FY25) with distribution guidance of 16.8 cents per unit. CIP also progressed its data centre strategy, lodging a development application for a ~40MW data centre adjacent to its existing Clayton (Victoria) data centre, and refinanced A$450 million of debt on improved terms.

## Trading Relevance

CIP is the ASX's pure-play industrial/logistics A-REIT: the unit price trades on the spread between its distribution yield and Australian bond yields (rate-sensitive, like all A-REITs), with upside optionality from the data-centre conversion pipeline (Clayton ~40MW DA lodged) and persistent positive re-leasing spreads (+20% in H1 FY26) as under-rented leases roll to market. The P/B of ~0.75 (see Valuation) means the market prices the portfolio below stated NTA — the classic A-REIT discount debate. Catalysts: half-year/full-year results (February/August), FFO and distribution guidance changes (FY26 guidance upgraded to 18.2–18.5 cpu), portfolio revaluations, RBA rate decisions, and data-centre planning approvals. Correlated names: Goodman Group (the sector heavyweight), [[charter-hall]], Dexus Industria, [[abacus-storage-king]]. Liquidity is reasonable for a mid-cap A-REIT; no meaningful listed options market, and distributions carry minimal franking (REIT income is mostly unfranked — see Fundamentals).
