Eversource Energy (NYSE: ES) is New England's largest utility, delivering electricity, natural gas, and (pending divestiture) water to ~4.4M customers in Connecticut, Massachusetts, and New Hampshire. A constituent of the [[s-and-p-500]], it spent 2023-2025 as the sector's restructuring story — exiting offshore wind at a heavy loss and selling its Aquarion water business — and now trades as a de-risked, pure-regulated transmission and distribution (T&D) utility with a contentious Connecticut regulatory relationship.

## Business Overview

Eversource (formerly Northeast Utilities; renamed 2015) operates regulated electric T&D (the largest earnings driver, including FERC-regulated transmission earning premium ROEs), natural-gas distribution (NSTAR Gas, Yankee Gas), and the Aquarion water utility being sold. The 2023-2024 exit from its 50% offshore-wind JV stakes (Sunrise Wind, Revolution Wind, South Fork) crystallized well over $2B of cumulative impairments and losses, the original source of the stock's 2023 derating and the elevated leverage visible in the fundamentals below (D/E rising from 1.08x to 1.71x over five years, payout ratio >100% of GAAP EPS). The forward story is a **$26.5B 2026–2030 capital plan** focused on grid modernization and electrification-driven load growth in New England.

### Segments

| Segment | What it is | Earnings / regulatory note |
|---|---|---|
| **Electric Transmission** | High-voltage grid across CT/MA/NH, regulated by FERC | Largest single earnings driver; FERC-regulated formula rates earn premium ROEs versus state distribution; the most stable, capex-driven growth engine |
| **Electric Distribution** | Last-mile delivery in Connecticut, Massachusetts, New Hampshire | State-regulated rate base; Connecticut (PURA) is the contentious jurisdiction, Massachusetts and New Hampshire are more constructive |
| **Natural Gas Distribution** | NSTAR Gas and Yankee Gas local gas delivery | State-regulated; steady rate-base growth tied to system replacement and electrification crosswinds |
| **Aquarion Water** *(pending divestiture)* | Regulated water utility | Sale to South Central Connecticut Regional Water Authority (~$2.4B EV); PURA proposed approval Mar 2026; proceeds earmarked for balance-sheet repair, not in 2026 guidance |

The portfolio is now effectively a **pure regulated transmission-and-distribution (T&D) utility** — no merchant generation, no offshore-wind development, and (on close of Aquarion) no water — leaving a cleaner, lower-risk regulated earnings stream than the pre-2024 company.

## Economic Moat & Competitive Position

Eversource's [[economic-moat|economic moat]] is the classic regulated-[[utilities|utility]] moat: a legally protected service-territory monopoly over electric and gas delivery in southern New England, earning a regulator-approved return on a growing **rate base**. The defensive characteristics are structural:

- **Franchise monopoly** — no competitor can string a parallel grid; demand is inelastic (electricity and gas are non-discretionary), giving highly predictable, recession-resistant cash flows.
- **Rate-base growth → EPS growth** — every dollar of approved capex (the $26.5B 2026–2030 plan) compounds the rate base on which the allowed return is earned, the mechanical engine of the 5–7% long-term EPS-growth target.
- **Premium FERC transmission ROEs** — the transmission segment earns higher, formula-based returns set federally, insulating the best part of the business from state-level rate-case friction.
- **Electrification tailwind** — New England decarbonization (heat pumps, EVs, building electrification) structurally lifts delivered electric load and the need for grid investment.

The crucial qualifier is **regulatory quality, which is jurisdiction-specific**. Eversource's moat is partially eroded by its **Connecticut / PURA relationship** — PURA has been among the more adversarial state regulators (contested rate cases, storm-cost recovery, the initial November 2025 rejection of the Aquarion sale). This is the company's standing **discount factor versus Massachusetts-weighted peers**: a regulated utility is only as valuable as the regulator that sets its returns, and Connecticut exposure caps the multiple the market is willing to pay.

## Growth Drivers & Catalysts

- **$26.5B 2026–2030 capital plan** — the core EPS-growth engine; grid modernization, reliability, and electrification investment that compounds the regulated rate base.
- **Electrification / load growth** — heat-pump and EV adoption plus New England decarbonization policy lift delivered load and grid-investment needs.
- **FERC transmission ROE** — premium federally regulated returns; dockets on transmission ROE are a recurring catalyst and the highest-quality growth lever.
- **Aquarion closing** — a balance-sheet-deleveraging event (~$2.4B EV proceeds) that, once closed, should reduce equity-issuance needs and aid the FFO/debt rebuild.
- **Balance-sheet repair** — rebuilding FFO/debt metrics back toward target after the offshore-wind impairment damage; credit-rating stability is a re-rating catalyst.
- **CT / MA rate cases** — outcomes set allowed ROE and rate base; constructive decisions de-risk the story, adverse ones cap it.
- **Rate cuts / falling 10-year yield** — as a bond proxy, lower long rates expand the multiple and lift the relative attractiveness of the dividend.

## Bull Case vs Bear Case

### Bull case
- **De-risked, pure-T&D utility** — offshore-wind and (on close) water are gone; what remains is a clean, monopoly-franchise regulated wires-and-pipes business with predictable cash flows.
- **Visible rate-base-driven growth** — the $26.5B plan underwrites a credible 5–7% long-term EPS-growth target, with premium FERC transmission ROEs the highest-quality slice.
- **Balance-sheet repair underway** — Aquarion proceeds and disciplined financing rebuild FFO/debt and reduce future equity dilution.
- **Electrification optionality** — New England decarbonization structurally lifts load and the long-run investment runway.
- **Bond-proxy support** — if long rates fall, a high-yielding, de-risked utility re-rates.

### Bear case
- **Connecticut regulatory hostility** — PURA is the persistent overhang; adverse rate-case and cost-recovery outcomes structurally cap returns and the multiple versus MA-weighted peers.
- **Elevated leverage** — D/E rose to 1.71x amid the offshore-wind damage; the balance sheet is still mid-repair and sensitive to rate-case and storm-cost outcomes.
- **Equity-issuance dilution** — funding the large capex plan while repairing the balance sheet implies equity needs that dilute per-share growth.
- **Payout coverage on GAAP** — the dividend exceeds GAAP EPS (payout >100%); coverage rests on the non-GAAP figure and on the capital plan executing as planned.
- **Rate/duration sensitivity** — a high-beta-to-rates bond proxy; rising long yields compress the valuation and raise refinancing costs simultaneously.

## Key Risks

- **Connecticut / PURA regulatory risk** — the single largest idiosyncratic risk; adverse rate-case, ROE, and storm-cost-recovery decisions directly impair earnings power and have produced multi-percent single-day moves.
- **Leverage & balance-sheet repair** — elevated D/E (1.71x) post-offshore-wind; the rebuild of FFO/debt and credit metrics is unfinished and rating-sensitive.
- **Equity dilution** — financing a $26.5B capex plan during deleveraging risks dilutive equity issuance.
- **Rate / duration sensitivity** — as a bond proxy, valuation and refinancing costs move inversely with the [[treasuries|10-year Treasury yield]].
- **Storm-cost recovery** — New England weather events create deferred costs whose recovery depends on (contentious) regulatory approval.
- **Offshore-wind residual exposure** — the divestiture is complete, but residual contractual/true-up exposures and the credibility damage from the impairments linger.

## Valuation & How the Stock Trades

As a regulated utility, Eversource is valued off **rate-base growth converting to EPS growth** at an allowed return, capitalized through a multiple that is largely a function of interest rates and perceived regulatory quality. Mechanically (see the data block and Fundamentals section for the figures as pulled; no current price invented here):

- **Rate-base-to-EPS framework** — the $26.5B capital plan compounds the rate base and underwrites the 5–7% long-term EPS-growth target; the stock trades on confidence that capex is approved and earns its allowed return.
- **Dividend yield** — among the highest in big-cap [[utilities]]; the headline GAAP payout reads **>100%** because impairment-depressed GAAP earnings understate the cash earnings the dividend is actually covered against (the dividend is covered on a **non-GAAP** basis). The yield is the primary reason income investors hold the name.
- **Bond-proxy behavior** — moves largely inverse to the 10-year Treasury yield; a favored **bond-proxy short** when long rates rise and a re-rating candidate when they fall.
- **CT-vs-sector spread** — Eversource's valuation discount versus Massachusetts-weighted and "clean regulated" peers tracks Connecticut/PURA regulatory sentiment; the spread tightens on constructive decisions and widens on adverse ones.
- **Beta** — low absolute beta typical of a defensive utility; the dominant price drivers are rates and CT regulatory headlines rather than the broad market.
