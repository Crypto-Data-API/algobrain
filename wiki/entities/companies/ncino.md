nCino, Inc. (NASDAQ: NCNO) sells the leading cloud "Bank Operating System" — loan origination, onboarding, and account-opening workflow software used by over 1,800 banks and credit unions, originally built on the [[salesforce]] platform. Spun out of Live Oak Bank (founded 2011, IPO July 2020 at the peak of SaaS euphoria), it is now a mid-growth, newly profitable SaaS name whose stock trades on bank IT-spending cycles and its pivot to AI-driven products.

## AI Involvement

[[artificial-intelligence|AI]]-powered cloud banking platform: nCino's "Banking Advisor" generative-AI assistant and continuous-credit-monitoring products automate commercial-lending workflows; AI roadmap execution is now central to the bull case. Because nCino sits on the system of record for lending decisions, its AI thesis is to embed copilots and credit-monitoring directly into the loan lifecycle (see [[ai-finance]]).

## Business Overview

nCino's core is commercial loan origination (its deepest moat — multi-year, sticky bank implementations), extended into retail lending, mortgage (nCino Mortgage, the former SimpleNexus), onboarding/KYC, and portfolio analytics. Acquisitions broadened the platform: SimpleNexus (2021), DocFox and FullCircl (2024), and Sandbox Banking (January 2025). Revenue is ~90% subscription. Leadership changed in **February 2025** when president Sean Desmond replaced co-founder Pierre Naudé as CEO — shortly before a ~30% one-day drawdown in March 2025 on weak initial FY2026 guidance, which reset expectations the company then spent the year beating.

The platform was built **on [[salesforce]]** — a fast time-to-market advantage at launch, but a long-running cost/dependency that the market watches as nCino expands its own data and AI layers.

## Competitive Positioning & Peers

nCino competes against both modern cloud challengers and entrenched core-banking incumbents whose systems it must integrate with.

| Peer | Overlap with nCino | Relative positioning |
|---|---|---|
| Q2 Holdings ([[q2-holdings]], QTWO) | Digital banking / lending software for banks & credit unions | The closest public comp; both small-cap bank-software names with similar end-market beta |
| [[salesforce]] | Underlying platform host; also Financial Services Cloud | Frenemy — nCino runs on Salesforce yet competes with its FSC ambitions; platform dependency is a structural watch-item |
| Temenos | Core/digital banking platform (global) | Larger, core-system incumbent; more international |
| Jack Henry, Fiserv (FIS-adjacent) | Core banking + ancillary lending modules | Entrenched US core processors; nCino integrates with and competes around them |

Bull framing: nCino is the modern, best-of-breed lending cloud displacing paper/legacy workflows across thousands of banks. Bear framing: it is a small-cap reliant on cyclical bank IT budgets, still partly dependent on [[salesforce]], facing both nimble peers ([[q2-holdings]]) and slow-moving incumbents.

## 2025–2026 Developments

- **March 2025:** stock fell sharply on soft FY2026 guidance issued with Q4 FY2025 results; activist pressure and strategic-review speculation followed.
- **FY2026 (ended 2026-01-31):** a recovery year — record gross ACV bookings in both Q4 and the full year; guidance exceeded across all revenue and profitability metrics. **ACV reached $602.4 million, up 17% YoY**, with ACV net revenue retention of 112%. CEO Desmond called it "a landmark year."
- **FY2027 guidance (issued with Q4 FY2026 results):** total revenue **$639-643 million**, subscription revenue $569-573 million, non-GAAP operating income **$165-170 million**, free cash flow $132-137 million — continuing the margin-expansion story.

## Bull vs Bear Case

**Bull case**
- Category-leading commercial-lending cloud with deep, sticky, multi-year bank deployments and ~90% subscription revenue.
- FY2026 recovery proved the demand thesis: record ACV bookings, 112% net retention, and margin expansion into FY2027 guidance.
- Banking Advisor / AI roadmap offers a new monetisation lever atop the lending system of record.
- Recurring revenue and a discrete addressable base make nCino a recurring **M&A / private-equity target**.

**Bear case**
- **Bank IT-budget cyclicality:** customers' spend tracks bank profitability, net-interest margins, deposit health, and M&A activity — demand can stall in a banking downturn.
- **[[salesforce]] platform dependency** is a cost and strategic risk.
- **Mortgage-volume sensitivity** via nCino Mortgage ties part of revenue to rate-driven origination cycles.
- Small-cap multiple and a history of violent guidance-driven gaps; integration risk from a string of acquisitions.

## Valuation Framework (qualitative)

NCNO is valued as a small/mid-cap vertical SaaS where the cyclicality of the end-market is the defining feature:

- **ACV / subscription growth:** annual contract value (ACV) and its net revenue retention (112% in FY2026) are the primary KPIs — bookings momentum drives the multiple.
- **Bank-IT-budget cyclicality:** unlike horizontal SaaS, demand is gated by bank profitability, net-interest margins, and consolidation; the valuation must discount this cyclical beta (sentiment links to bank ETFs like KRE).
- **Non-GAAP operating margin & FCF:** the FY2026→FY2027 margin-expansion and free-cash-flow story is now central to the quality narrative.
- **[[rule-of-40|Rule of 40]]:** mid-teens growth plus expanding margins put NCNO around/over the bar — a key qualitative checkpoint.
- **SBC dilution:** typical for SaaS; net share-count change matters for per-share value.
- **M&A optionality:** recurring takeover speculation can put a floor under the multiple.

## Capital Return & Dilution

nCino pays no dividend; capital allocation has emphasised acquisitions (SimpleNexus, DocFox, FullCircl, Sandbox Banking) and reinvestment over buybacks. Now that it generates non-GAAP operating income and free cash flow (per FY2027 guidance), capital-return flexibility increases. **Stock-based-compensation dilution** is the main per-share headwind; the serial-acquisition strategy also adds integration and share-issuance considerations. Confirm current share count and any buyback against filings.
