Fortive (NYSE: FTV) is a diversified industrial-technology company in the [[industrials]] sector, spun out of Danaher in 2016 and a constituent of the [[s-and-p-500]] index. After spinning off its Precision Technologies segment as Ralliant (NYSE: RAL) in June 2025, Fortive is a leaner software-and-instruments compounder run on the Danaher-style Fortive Business System (FBS) — traders treat it as a quality industrial with ~50% recurring revenue and an active M&A/buyback capital-allocation story.

## Business Overview & Segments

Post-spin Fortive operates two segments, both weighted toward recurring revenue (software subscriptions, consumables, service). The Fortive Business System (FBS) — a lean-operations and capital-deployment playbook inherited from Danaher — is central to the margin-expansion thesis.

| Segment | What it is | Key brands | Recurring-revenue character |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Intelligent Operating Solutions (IOS)** | Workflow software and hardware for facility/asset management, EHS compliance and field service | Fluke (test & measurement), Accruent, Gordian, Intelex | High — SaaS subscriptions + consumables/calibration |
| **Advanced Healthcare Solutions (AHS)** | Sterilization / instrument reprocessing and clinical-workflow products | ASP (Advanced Sterilization Products), Censis, Provation | High — consumables and software tied to installed base |

Roughly half of total revenue is recurring. The spun-off **Ralliant** (NYSE: RAL) took the Precision Technologies businesses (Tektronix, Qualitrol, EMC/sensors), leaving RemainCo Fortive pitched as higher-growth, higher-recurring-revenue and less cyclical.

## 2025–2026 Developments

- **Ralliant spin-off completed 2025-06-28:** Fortive distributed 100% of its Precision Technologies segment (Tektronix, Qualitrol, EMC/sensors businesses) to shareholders — 1 Ralliant share per 3 FTV shares (~113M RAL shares distributed; record date 2025-06-16). Ralliant trades as NYSE: RAL.
- **Leadership change:** Olumide Soroye became President, CEO and Director of Fortive at separation, succeeding long-time CEO James Lico.
- Post-spin, management has emphasized buybacks and bolt-on software M&A; RemainCo is pitched as higher-growth, higher-recurring-revenue, and less cyclical than pre-spin Fortive.

## Economic Moat & Competitive Position

Fortive holds a moderate-to-wide [[economic-moat|economic moat]] in the mold of its Danaher parent, built on recurring revenue, embedded software, and the Fortive Business System:

- **Embedded software & workflow [[switching-costs]]** — once Accruent, Gordian, Intelex, Censis or Provation are wired into a customer's facility-management, EHS, or clinical workflows, the data and process integration make switching costly and disruptive. This drives ~50% recurring revenue and retention.
- **Installed-base razor-and-blade** — AHS sterilization hardware and Fluke instruments pull through high-margin consumables, calibration and service over a long installed-base life.
- **Fortive Business System (FBS)** — a lean-operations and capital-deployment playbook (Kaizen, policy deployment, disciplined M&A) inherited from Danaher; a durable cultural/operational advantage that drives margin expansion and integration of acquisitions.
- **Brand & qualification** — Fluke's brand in test & measurement and ASP's position in sterilization carry trust and (in healthcare) regulatory qualification barriers.
- **Disciplined serial M&A** — a repeatable bolt-on acquisition engine, increasingly weighted to software, compounds the addressable market.

The moat's limit: many product lines compete with capable rivals (Danaher itself, Roper, [[ametek]]), and the post-spin company is smaller, so the M&A flywheel must keep delivering to sustain the compounding thesis.

## Growth Drivers & Catalysts

- **Software/recurring-revenue mix shift** — bolt-on SaaS acquisitions and organic subscription growth raise the recurring share and support multiple expansion.
- **FBS margin expansion** — continued lean-operations gains and pricing on the RemainCo portfolio.
- **Healthcare capex & procedure volumes** — AHS sterilization/consumables track hospital capital spending and surgical procedure volumes.
- **Capital allocation** — post-spin emphasis on buybacks and bolt-on software M&A; deal announcements are recurring catalysts.
- **Post-spin re-rating** — RemainCo is pitched as higher-growth, higher-recurring and less cyclical; the market re-rating it on clean post-spin financials is the central story.
- **Recurring catalysts** — quarterly earnings and core-growth guidance, capital-deployment announcements, healthcare-capex trends, and index/flowback effects following the Ralliant distribution.

## Bull Case vs Bear Case

### Bull case
- Higher-quality RemainCo: ~50% recurring revenue, asset-light software, and less cyclicality than pre-spin Fortive.
- FBS operating system is a proven, durable margin-and-integration engine inherited from Danaher.
- Disciplined serial M&A weighted to software compounds the addressable market and the recurring mix.
- Healthy balance sheet (D/E 0.36) and buyback capacity support per-share compounding.
- Clean post-spin financials (FY2025+) should let the market value the better business properly.

### Bear case
- The FY2024 fundamentals look weak (negative interest coverage, ROE <10%) because they include the divested, pre-spin business — the thesis depends on rebased financials actually proving out.
- Smaller post-spin company; growth leans on continued successful, sensibly priced acquisitions.
- Integration risk on software deals; multiple compression in risk-off industrial regimes.
- Competes with capable peers (Danaher, Roper, [[ametek]]) for both customers and acquisition targets.
- Screeners using pre-spin financials mis-state valuation, creating noise around the print.

## Key Risks

- **Spin-off transition risk** — pre-spin FY2024 financials (incl. negative interest coverage and divested Precision Technologies) overstate risk; the rebased RemainCo profile is not yet fully seasoned.
- **M&A dependence** — growth relies on a steady pipeline of sensibly priced bolt-ons; integration missteps or target scarcity slow the flywheel.
- **Industrial cyclicality** — slower industrial capex pressures IOS hardware/test demand.
- **Healthcare capex** — AHS depends on hospital capital budgets and procedure volumes.
- **Valuation/screening noise** — pre-spin financials mis-state valuation in screens; multiple compression in risk-off regimes.
- **Execution under new leadership** — CEO transition (Olumide Soroye) at separation introduces management-execution risk.
