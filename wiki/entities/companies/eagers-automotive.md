**Eagers Automotive Limited** (ASX: APE) is Australia's largest automotive retail group, listed on the [[asx-limited|Australian Securities Exchange]] and a constituent of the [[asx-200]] index. For traders it is the dominant listed proxy for Australian new-car demand — and increasingly for the Chinese-EV (BYD) rollout in Australia. The group represents dozens of new-car brands (mass-market, luxury, trucks and emerging EV OEMs) through a national network of dealerships in Australia and New Zealand. The company operates across the full retail value chain: new and used vehicles, service, parts, finance and insurance, and owns a large portfolio of dealership properties.

## Business Model & Segments

Eagers Automotive reports two segments: Car Retailing and Property. Car Retailing covers new and used vehicles, servicing and repair, parts, finance and insurance, extended service contracts, vehicle brokerage and protection products; it also includes motor auctions and a forklift rental business. The Property segment owns commercial properties, predominantly used as dealership facility premises, providing a stable earnings base and strategic control of prime automotive retail sites. Eagers has become the retail partner of choice for new Chinese EV brands entering Australia, with multi-year agreements for BYD retail representation and arrangements with other emerging OEMs.

| Segment | What it is | Role |
|---|---|---|
| **Car Retailing** | New and used vehicles, service and parts, finance and insurance, brokerage and protection products, motor auctions, forklift rental | The earnings engine — the overwhelming majority of revenue, geared to new-car demand |
| **Property** | Owned commercial real estate, predominantly dealership facility premises | Stable earnings base, strategic control of prime retail sites, and asset backing under the equity |

The franchise is not just the dealership network: the durable advantage is the combination of **scale, OEM franchise rights, and owned property** layered over a continuous consolidation strategy that buys smaller dealers and extracts synergies — widening the gap versus sub-scale independents.

## Economic Moat & Competitive Position

Eagers holds a meaningful — though cyclical — [[economic-moat]] in Australian [[consumer-discretionary]] retail, built on several reinforcing layers rather than a single advantage:

- **Scale + consolidation** — as Australia's largest automotive retail group, Eagers buys volume on better terms, spreads fixed costs (back-office, finance, marketing, used-car logistics) across a national footprint, and is the natural acquirer of sub-scale independent dealers, extracting synergies on each deal. This consolidator flywheel is the core engine of the model.
- **Privileged OEM franchise agreements** — manufacturers grant a limited number of dealership rights in any given territory. Once awarded, those franchises are a barrier to entry for would-be competitors and impose real [[switching-costs]] on the OEMs themselves (re-establishing distribution through a new partner is slow and costly), which protects Eagers' incumbency.
- **Owned dealership property portfolio** — the Property segment gives Eagers strategic control of prime automotive-retail sites, asset backing under the equity, and the optionality to develop or recycle real estate — an advantage a pure lease-based operator lacks.
- **Chinese-EV partner of choice** — Eagers has positioned itself as the preferred retail partner for new Chinese EV entrants (most notably BYD), capturing distribution economics on the fastest-growing slice of the new-car market.
- **Productivity ("Next 100")** — the "Next 100" strategy has lifted sales-per-person-per-annum to ~A$1.48m (from ~A$909k in 2019), a ~60% productivity improvement that widens the cost gap versus smaller rivals.

The moat is **real but cyclical and not impregnable**: the same OEMs that grant franchises are exploring **agency-model** distribution (the manufacturer sets price and owns the customer, paying the dealer a fixed handling fee), which would compress dealer margins and erode the franchise advantage. Scale and property backing are the durable defences; agency-model adoption and new-car-margin normalisation are the long-term threats.

## Growth Drivers & Catalysts

- **Acquisition-led consolidation** — Eagers is the sector consolidator; bolt-on dealer acquisitions add revenue and synergies (2025 revenue growth was ~30% acquisition-driven, per the page above).
- **Chinese-EV rollout** — the BYD ramp (including ~6,000 BYD Shark ute orders, ~A$400m of incremental 2025 revenue per the page) plus arrangements with other emerging OEMs; a structural new-demand pool.
- **Used-car & finance/insurance attach** — higher-margin used-vehicle, F&I, parts and service revenue layered onto each new-car sale.
- **"Next 100" productivity** — ongoing per-person productivity gains and operating leverage.
- **Property portfolio value** — owned dealership real estate provides asset backing and development/recycling optionality.
- **Recurring catalysts** — monthly VFACTS new-vehicle sales data, half-year/full-year results (February/August), BYD model launches and delivery ramps, RBA rate decisions (which move both floorplan funding costs and financed-purchase demand), and acquisition announcements.

## Bull Case vs Bear Case

### Bull case
- Dominant national scale and the consolidator flywheel give a long runway of bolt-on acquisitions with synergy capture.
- BYD and broader Chinese-EV optionality is a structural new-demand pool less tied to legacy OEM cycles.
- Owned dealership property provides asset backing under the equity and strategic site control.
- "Next 100" productivity gains widen the cost advantage versus sub-scale independents.
- Anchor shareholder (Chair Nick Politis, ~25%+) aligns long-term interests and supports takeover-proofing.

### Bear case
- Net margins are thin (low single digits) — the model is high-revenue, low-margin and operationally geared.
- New-vehicle margins are normalising from COVID-era highs (visible in the falling net margin in the Fundamentals below).
- OEM **agency-model** shifts could structurally compress dealer margins and erode the franchise advantage.
- BYD concentration: representation is no longer exclusive, so the EV tailwind is shareable with rivals.
- Consumer-credit softness and higher rates weigh on financed big-ticket purchases.
- Cyclical exposure to new-car demand makes earnings sensitive to the broader [[consumer-discretionary]] cycle.

## Key Risks

- **New-vehicle margin normalisation** — post-COVID margin compression as supply normalises and discounting returns.
- **OEM agency-model shift** — manufacturers moving to fixed-fee agency distribution would cut dealer margins and weaken the franchise moat.
- **BYD / EV concentration** — reliance on a small number of fast-growing OEM relationships that are no longer exclusive.
- **Consumer-credit & rate softness** — higher rates raise floorplan funding costs and dampen financed-purchase demand.
- **Cyclicality** — big-ticket discretionary spend is sensitive to the economic cycle and consumer confidence.
- **Integration / acquisition execution** — a consolidator's returns depend on disciplined pricing and successful synergy capture on each deal.
- **Limited free float** — the Politis anchor stake (~25%+) reduces free float and liquidity and concentrates control.

## Valuation & How the Stock Trades

Eagers trades on a discretionary-cyclical multiple (~25x on the page's snapshot) that reflects its scale, consolidation runway and EV optionality rather than a deep-value tag; the debate is whether new-car margins hold and the consolidation/BYD growth persists. Mechanically:

- **The listed proxy** for Australian new-car demand and, increasingly, the Chinese-EV (BYD) rollout — sentiment on auto sales and EV adoption flows straight through the name.
- **Franked dividends** — Eagers pays franked dividends, a gross-up benefit for Australian-resident income investors (see Fundamentals).
- **Liquidity / options** — reasonable ASX equity liquidity but thin single-name options, so positioning is mostly outright equity rather than derivatives.
- **Free float / control** — the Politis anchor stake limits free float, supports takeover-proofing, and means the register is closely held.
- **Catalyst sensitivity** — moves on monthly VFACTS data, half-year/full-year results, BYD launches, RBA decisions, and acquisition news rather than on a steady recurring-revenue cadence.
- **Index** — an [[asx-200]] [[consumer-discretionary]] constituent; a benchmark expression of the Australian auto-retail cycle.

## History

Eagers was founded by Edward George Eager in Brisbane in 1913 as E.G. Eager & Son Limited. The company opened a Queensland assembly plant in 1922 and secured General Motors distribution rights for Queensland and northern NSW in 1930. It listed on the ASX as Eagers Holdings Limited in 1957. A 1992 merger with Alan Piper's A.P. Group Limited (A.P. Motors, 1978) created A.P. Eagers Limited. In 2019-2020 A.P. Eagers completed the acquisition of rival Automotive Holdings Group (AHG), nationalising the dealership network, and in July 2020 renamed itself Eagers Automotive Limited.

## Recent Developments

For the 2025 full year Eagers delivered record revenue of A$11.2 billion, with total revenue growth of A$1.9 billion driven 68% organically, 30% from acquisitions and 2% from greenfield sites. Record EBITDA of A$620.9 million was up 12.8%, and underlying profit rose 14.3% to A$424.1 million, though statutory profit fell 25% to A$223 million. CEO Keith Thornton, appointed February 2021, emphasised progress under the "Next 100" strategy, with sales-per-person-per-annum reaching A$1.48 million (up from A$909k in 2019), a 60% productivity improvement. The company signed a new long-term (5+5 year) agreement directly with BYD Australia to provide retail representation for the brand (no longer on an exclusive basis). BYD-related sales, including roughly 6,000 BYD Shark ute orders, contributed about A$400 million of incremental revenue in 2025.

The most recent filing (annual) was lodged on 2023-06-30. See [[#Financial Reports]] below.
