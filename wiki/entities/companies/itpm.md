# Institute of Trading and Portfolio Management (ITPM)

The Institute of Trading and Portfolio Management (ITPM) is a trading education company founded by former [[goldman-sachs]] trader [[anton-kreil]] in 2011. It teaches institutional-grade [[long-short-equity]] portfolio management methodology to retail traders, with a focus on options strategies and macroeconomic analysis.

## Overview

ITPM differentiates itself from typical retail trading education by teaching the systematic processes used at investment banks and hedge funds. The core methodology centers on an 80/20 framework: 80% [[fundamental-analysis]] (macroeconomic and company-level research) combined with 20% [[technical-analysis]] (used solely for entry/exit timing, not trade idea generation).

The Institute operates a mentoring program where graduates build track records managing their own capital under mentor supervision. Successful traders may receive capital allocation from the Institute's global portfolio. As of the mid-2020s, ITPM oversees 1,400+ traders across 30+ countries.

## Core Methodology

### The 80/20 Framework

ITPM teaches that professional traders use fundamentals for 80% of their analysis and technical analysis for only 20%. Critically, the fundamentals are interpreted differently from how retail traders typically use them — professionals look at the same data but draw opposite conclusions based on deeper understanding of market dynamics. (Source: [[itpm-education-methodology-overview]])

### [[long-short-equity|Long/Short Portfolio Management]]

The flagship strategy involves constructing portfolios with both long (bullish) and short (bearish) positions simultaneously. This approach:

- Reduces market correlation — can profit in bull, bear, or sideways markets
- Typically maintains 10-12 concurrent positions
- Targets a 20-60 day time horizon for individual trades
- Aims for 20-30% annual returns in equities (Source: [[itpm-education-methodology-overview]])

### Top-Down / Bottom-Up Systematic Framework

1. **Macro analysis** — Global economic conditions, central bank policy, leading indicators
2. **Sector analysis** — Which industries attract institutional capital flows
3. **Stock selection** — Bottom-up fundamental research within favored sectors
4. **Technical timing** — Price action and indicators used to time entries and exits

### Options Overlay

Through the POTM (Professional Options Trading Masterclass), ITPM teaches directional [[options]] strategies within the long/short portfolio framework:

- [[straddle-strangle|Long straddles and strangles]] for volatility expansion
- Ratio spreads and ladder spreads for asymmetric payoffs
- 20-60 day time horizons aligned with the equity portfolio
- Focus on understanding [[implied-volatility]] and Greeks

### Trade Repair and Management

Rather than simply cutting losing trades, ITPM emphasizes:

- [[trade-repair-and-rolling|Rolling trades]] to different strikes/expirations
- Repairing positions through spread modifications
- Scaling into positions (starting with half-size when uncertain)
- Portfolio-level risk management over individual trade P&L

## Trading Relevance

ITPM represents one of the more rigorous approaches to retail trading education, teaching institutional methodology rather than retail indicator-based approaches. Their emphasis on [[long-short-equity]] portfolios, [[risk-management]], and macro-driven analysis aligns with how professional hedge funds operate. The methodology is particularly relevant for traders interested in medium-term equity and options positions.

## Controversies

- No publicly available verified track record from the mentors' own current trading
- Course pricing is substantial ($2,000-$3,000+ per program)
- Some critics question whether institutional methods translate effectively to retail-sized accounts
- Strong marketing language around returns claims
