Close The Loop Ltd (ASX: CLG) is an Australian recycling and waste management company that recycles printer cartridges, batteries, and plastics, delivering a "circular economy" and "zero waste to landfill" promise. IPO'd on the [[asx-limited|ASX]] in December 2021 with a market capitalisation of approximately $183M. The company has been expanding aggressively through international acquisitions, completing 7 acquisitions since listing, and now operates across Australia, Belgium, South Africa, and the USA. (Source: [[alfred-report-clg-asx]])

## Overview

Close The Loop operates in the **Industrials > Environment & Facility Services** sector. The company's core business model centres on collecting and recycling end-of-life products — primarily printer cartridges, batteries, and various plastics — transforming waste streams into reusable materials. The company promotes itself as a circular economy enabler, with the goal of diverting 100% of collected materials from landfill.

Since its December 2021 IPO, CLG has pursued an acquisition-led growth strategy that has dramatically shifted its geographic revenue profile. What was once a predominantly Australian business now earns the majority of its revenue from the United States. (Source: [[alfred-report-clg-asx]])

## 2024–2026 Developments

The picture has deteriorated materially since the Alfred report data below was compiled:

- **Failed takeover (Nov 2024 – Jan 2025)**: private equity firm **Adamantem Capital** lodged an indicative **A$143.6M bid at A$0.27/share**; the board supported the price and granted exclusivity/due diligence. Days after the bid, CEO Joe Foster and chairman Greg Toll resigned. On **29 January 2025** Adamantem walked away — the parties could not agree commercial terms — and CLG shares **fell ~40% to around A$0.105**, well below the pre-bid level.
- **H1 FY26 (six months to Dec 2025)**: revenue **A$92.3M, -6.9%** on pcp; **statutory loss of A$26.9M** including a **A$23M impairment**; underlying profit only ~A$2.5M. Operating cash flow is negative and debt coverage is flagged as a key risk.
- **Asset sale / deleveraging (May 2026)**: sold **ISP Tek Services** (the US business acquired post-IPO) to Ivy Technology Holdings for **US$9M cash**, with proceeds plus cash used to retire **~$16M of outstanding debt** — a partial unwind of the acquisition-led US expansion described below.
- The board says it remains open to future change-of-control proposals.

**Trading view (as of June 2026):** micro-cap turnaround/special-situation. Catalysts are further asset sales, debt reduction milestones, and any renewed takeover interest; the main risks are the operating cash loss, impairment-prone acquired goodwill, and thin liquidity typical of ASX micro-caps. The 2025 bid-and-bust episode is also a case study in event-risk around indicative (non-binding) offers.

## Business Segments

Close The Loop's revenue is best understood as a roll-up of several distinct waste-stream and circular-economy services, rather than a single homogeneous recycling line. Broadly the company operates across:

| Segment | Activity | Geography | Revenue character |
|---------|----------|-----------|-------------------|
| **Resource recovery / recycling** | Collection and recycling of printer cartridges, batteries, soft plastics, glass, e-waste; "zero waste to landfill" service | Australia, Belgium, South Africa | Contract / volume-based, sticky corporate customers |
| **TonerPlas / asphalt additive** | Conversion of recovered toner and soft plastics into a road-asphalt additive — a flagship circular-economy product | Australia | Product sales, leveraged to infrastructure spend |
| **US services (legacy ISP Tek / Usource / Circular Services)** | US recycling, energy procurement (Usource) and circular-economy services acquired post-IPO | USA | Acquired contract revenue; partially divested May 2026 (ISP Tek sold) |
| **Print & integration (Pazazz)** | Print services and packaging integration | Australia | Lower-margin, ancillary |

The economics are characteristic of an **acquisitive industrial services roll-up**: revenue is largely contracted and recurring, but margins depend on integration discipline, freight/logistics costs, and the price of recovered commodity output. The May 2026 ISP Tek divestment marks a deliberate narrowing of the US footprint to reduce debt — a partial reversal of the 2021–2024 acquisition strategy.

## Competitive Positioning & Peers

CLG sits in the **Industrials > Environment & Facility Services** space. On the [[asx-limited|ASX]] its most-cited large-cap reference point is [[cleanaway-waste-management|Cleanaway (CWY)]], though the two are very different in scale and model: Cleanaway is a diversified, capital-intensive collections/landfill/liquid-waste major, whereas CLG is a micro-cap specialist in product-stewardship recycling and circular-economy additives.

| Company | Ticker | Model | Scale | Notes |
|---------|--------|-------|-------|-------|
| **Close The Loop** | CLG | Product-stewardship recycling, circular-economy additives, US services roll-up | Micro-cap | High insider ownership; turnaround/special-situation |
| [[cleanaway-waste-management\|Cleanaway]] | CWY | Diversified waste collection, landfill, liquid/industrial waste | Large-cap, [[asx-200]] | Infrastructure-heavy, regulated landfill assets, dividend payer |

CLG's structural differentiation is its niche in **producer-responsibility schemes** (cartridge/battery stewardship) and value-added recyclate products (TonerPlas) rather than commodity collections. The trade-off is that micro-cap scale leaves it without the route-density and balance-sheet depth of the majors, making it more exposed to integration missteps and financing pressure.

## Valuation Framework

For a small, acquisitive industrial-services roll-up like CLG, valuation hinges on a handful of qualitative levers rather than a single multiple:

- **Contract revenue quality** — how much of the book is recurring, contracted product-stewardship volume versus lumpier product/commodity sales. Sticky corporate recycling contracts deserve a higher multiple than commodity-exposed recyclate.
- **Roll-up math** — whether bolt-on acquisitions were bought below the group's own trading multiple and successfully integrated (accretive) or are now being impaired (value-destructive). The H1 FY26 impairment is the key warning that integration economics disappointed.
- **Net debt / deleveraging trajectory** — with negative operating cash flow flagged, the path of net debt (and the proceeds from divestments such as ISP Tek) is the dominant valuation swing factor; debt coverage is the central risk.
- **Underlying vs statutory earnings** — impairments and acquisition costs make statutory results noisy; the gap between underlying (~A$2.5M H1 FY26) and statutory (A$26.9M loss) must be scrutinised for one-offs that are actually recurring.
- **Takeover optionality** — as a sub-scale, controlled-register micro-cap, an M&A bid is a real (if unreliable) source of value, as the Adamantem episode showed.

This is a **special-situation / sum-of-the-parts** valuation, not a clean DCF — the preserved DCF figure below dates from FY2024 data and no longer holds.

## Trading & Options Playbook

- **Index membership:** ASX micro-cap; not an [[asx-200]] constituent, which limits passive/index buying and institutional sponsorship.
- **Liquidity:** Thin ASX micro-cap liquidity — wide bid-ask spreads, real difficulty building or exiting size. Use **limit orders**; market orders can move the stock.
- **Options:** No meaningful single-stock options market; the only practical expression is the underlying.
- **Franking / dividends:** No dividend is paid, so there are no [[franking-credits|franking credit]] benefits for income or [[smsf|SMSF]] investors; this is a pure capital-growth/special-situation holding.
- **Event-risk discipline:** The 2024–25 Adamantem bid-and-bust is a template for trading indicative offers — non-binding bids can collapse and gap the stock ~40% lower. Position size for binary event risk.
- **Catalyst watch:** further asset sales, debt-reduction milestones, renewed change-of-control approaches, and H1/FY results showing whether underlying cash generation is turning.

## Capital Return

CLG pays **no dividend** and is not a capital-return story. Cash generated (and divestment proceeds) is being directed to **debt reduction** — the May 2026 ISP Tek sale (US$9M cash) plus existing cash retired roughly A$16M of debt. With negative operating cash flow and an elevated debt load flagged as the key risk, any return of capital to shareholders is not on the agenda; the priority is balance-sheet repair.

## Risks

- **Operating cash loss / debt coverage** — negative operating cash flow against an elevated debt load is the central solvency-adjacent risk; debt covenants and refinancing terms matter.
- **Goodwill / impairment risk** — the acquisition-led model created intangible goodwill that is impairment-prone, as the A$23M H1 FY26 impairment demonstrated.
- **Integration execution** — seven acquisitions in a short window across multiple jurisdictions create execution and cultural-integration risk.
- **Event risk** — reliance on (non-binding) takeover interest as a value source; bids can and did collapse.
- **Liquidity / micro-cap risk** — thin float and controlled register make positions hard to exit and amplify volatility.
- **Commodity / freight exposure** — recyclate output prices and logistics costs swing margins.
- **Customer/contract concentration** — loss of major product-stewardship contracts would hit recurring revenue.
