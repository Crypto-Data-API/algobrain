Origin Energy Ltd (ASX: ORG) is one of Australia's largest integrated energy companies — a ~4.2 million-account electricity and gas retailer, owner of the Eraring power station and a growing battery fleet, holder of a 27.5% stake in the APLNG export project, and 20% owner of UK-based Octopus Energy. For traders it combines regulated-retail defensiveness with LNG commodity exposure and an energy-transition growth story, and is a perennial M&A candidate after the failed A$18 billion Brookfield/EIG takeover of 2023.

## Business Model & Segments

Origin reports across three primary operating segments: Energy Markets, Integrated Gas and Share of Octopus Energy.

The Energy Markets segment is one of Australia's largest retail energy businesses, serving approximately 4.2 million customer accounts across residential, small business and large industrial customers. The segment also includes Origin's electricity generation portfolio, which features the Eraring Power Station in New South Wales - one of Australia's largest coal-fired power stations - alongside gas peaking plants, contracted renewable generation and a growing fleet of grid-scale batteries.

The Integrated Gas segment houses Origin's 27.5% interest in Australia Pacific LNG (APLNG), a major coal seam gas-to-LNG project with an export facility on Curtis Island, Queensland, plus exploration interests in the Cooper-Eromanga and Browse basins and participation in the Hunter Valley Hydrogen Hub on Kooragang Island.

The Share of Octopus Energy segment reflects Origin's 20% holding in UK-based renewable energy retailer Octopus Energy Group, including exposure to Octopus's Kraken retail technology platform, which Origin licenses for its Australian retail operations.

## History

Origin Energy was formed on 18 February 2000 via demerger from Boral Limited, separating the energy businesses from Boral's building products and construction materials operations. The demerger, effective 1 January 2000, was implemented by scheme of arrangement under the Australian Corporations Law, with Boral shareholders receiving one Origin share for each Boral share. Origin listed on the ASX with an initial market capitalisation of approximately A$2.5 billion.

Over the following decades, Origin built a vertically integrated energy business, expanding upstream gas operations, acquiring and developing generation assets, and growing its retail customer base through a combination of organic growth and acquisitions. Origin became a foundation partner of APLNG, alongside ConocoPhillips and Sinopec, in a multi-billion-dollar coal seam gas-to-LNG development that commenced first LNG shipments in 2016.

In November 2022, a consortium comprising Brookfield Asset Management and EIG Global Energy Partners proposed an approximately A$18 billion scheme of arrangement to acquire Origin. The Board recommended the offer in March 2023 and it was approved by the ACCC in October 2023. However, the scheme was voted down in December 2023 after Origin's largest shareholder, AustralianSuper, opposed the deal and the resolution failed to reach the 75% threshold required for approval.

## Economic Moat & Competitive Position

Origin's [[economic-moat|economic moat]] is **moderate** and derives from integration, scale, and irreplaceable infrastructure rather than pricing power (retail tariffs are regulated and wholesale [[energy|energy]] prices are set by the market):

- **Vertical integration** — owning generation (Eraring, gas peakers, batteries) plus a ~4.2-million-account retail book lets Origin hedge wholesale-price volatility internally; the generation and retail legs partially offset each other through the price cycle.
- **Scale in retail** — one of Australia's largest energy retailers; customer scale, brand, and the licensed Kraken (Octopus) technology platform create efficiency and switching friction in a regulated, low-churn market.
- **Hard-to-replicate assets** — the 27.5% APLNG stake (a multi-billion-dollar coal-seam-gas-to-LNG export facility on Curtis Island) and the Eraring site are infrastructure that cannot be reproduced quickly; APLNG ties earnings to global LNG/oil-linked prices.
- **Octopus / Kraken optionality** — the 20% Octopus Energy holding gives exposure to a fast-growing UK retailer and a globally licensed retail-tech platform — a growth and technology angle most pure utilities lack.
- **Energy-transition positioning** — a 1.7 GW / 6.3 GWh battery-storage program and renewables PPAs position Origin to monetise grid firming as coal retires.

The moat is **regulated and cyclical**: retail margins are capped by Default/Victorian Market Offers (DMO/VDO), wholesale and LNG prices are exogenous and volatile, and the energy transition is simultaneously an opportunity (storage/firming) and a stranded-asset risk (Eraring). It is best read as an integrated utility with a commodity (LNG) overlay and a transition growth story.

## Growth Drivers & Catalysts

- **Battery & firming build-out** — the 1.7 GW / 6.3 GWh storage program (Eraring, Supernode, Mortlake) targeting 8-11% post-tax returns; grid firming as coal retires is the core transition growth driver.

## Bull Case vs Bear Case

### Bull case
- An integrated gentailer with a self-hedging generation-plus-retail structure, a high-cash-flow APLNG LNG stake, and a credible energy-transition growth pipeline (1.7 GW storage at 8-11% returns).
- Classic value/income: low P/E, fully franked yield, and strong adjusted free cash flow (up A$187M to A$705M in HY26) attractive to Australian income funds.
- Octopus/Kraken gives differentiated international and retail-technology upside versus a pure domestic utility.
- A standing takeover candidate — strategic scarcity value for infrastructure/private-capital buyers.

### Bear case
- Earnings are cyclical and commodity-linked: HY26 statutory profit roughly halved (A$557M vs A$1,017M) as LNG prices and energy-market conditions normalised from the post-2022 spike.
- Transition capex (batteries, firming) is heavy and lumpy; free cash flow has swung negative in some years, pressuring the dividend if commodity tailwinds fade.
- Regulatory caps (DMO/VDO) limit retail margin upside; political intervention in energy prices is a recurring risk.
- Eraring is both a firming asset and a stranded-asset/closure-cost risk; the transition is expensive and execution-dependent.

## Key Risks

- **Commodity price** — APLNG earnings track JKM/oil-linked LNG prices and wholesale electricity; both are volatile and exogenous.
- **Regulatory / political** — DMO/VDO retail-price resets each July and the risk of government intervention in energy markets cap margins.
- **Transition execution & stranded assets** — heavy battery/firming capex, Eraring closure economics (extended to April 2029), and coal-to-renewables timing risk.
- **Free-cash-flow volatility** — FCF has been negative in some years on growth capex; sustained weakness would threaten the franked dividend.
- **Leverage / cyclicality** — utility-level gearing combined with commodity-linked earnings amplifies the downside in a weak-price year.
- **Corporate-action overhang** — AustralianSuper's blocking stake shapes any future takeover; a failed or revived bid moves the register and the stock.
