**Contact Energy Limited** (ASX: CEN, NZX: CEN) is a renewable-energy "gentailer" — one of New Zealand's largest electricity generators and retailers and a major gas retailer — with a portfolio dominated by geothermal and hydroelectric generation. Dual-listed on the NZX (primary) and ASX and a constituent of the [[asx-200]], Contact serves close to 600,000 mass-market customers plus commercial and industrial offtakers; the July 2025 NZ$1.9B Manawa Energy acquisition made it a near-pure renewable generator and a key trans-Tasman utilities yield play.

## Business Overview & Segments

Contact is a renewable "gentailer" — it both **gen**erates electricity and re**tails** it, a vertically integrated model that is the dominant structure in the New Zealand electricity market. Owning generation (mostly geothermal and hydro) and a large retail book is a natural hedge: when wholesale prices spike, the generation arm captures the upside that the retail arm pays away, smoothing earnings through the cycle. Contact reports two segments:

| Segment | What it is | Key assets / scale |
|---|---|---|
| **Wholesale (Generation)** | Owns and operates generation; sells into the NZ wholesale market and to C&I offtakers | Five geothermal stations near Taupo (Wairakei, Poihipi, Te Huka, Te Mihi, Tauhara); Clutha River hydro (Clyde, Roxburgh); Stratford gas (Taranaki); Whirinaki diesel peaker; plus ~500 MW of Manawa hydro (25 schemes) |
| **Retail** | Sells electricity, natural gas, broadband and bottled LPG to mass-market and SME customers | ~600,000 customer connections under the Contact brand |

Following the July 2025 Manawa acquisition, renewable generation reached ~97% of output in H1 FY26, making Contact a near-pure renewable [[utilities]] play.

## Economic Moat

Contact's moat is the classic **regulated/quasi-regulated utility** combination: hard-to-replicate physical assets, vertical integration, and scale in a structurally concentrated market.

- **Irreplaceable generation assets** — long-life geothermal and hydro consented over decades; new consents (especially hydro and geothermal) are slow, contested and capital-intensive, which protects incumbents. Geothermal in particular offers low marginal cost, baseload output unaffected by weather or fuel prices.
- **Vertical integration (the gentailer hedge)** — owning generation *and* a retail book is the core defensive feature: it internalises wholesale-price volatility and stabilises margins versus a pure retailer or a pure merchant generator.
- **Scale & customer inertia** — ~600,000 connections, a recognised brand, and low retail switching in practice give a sticky, annuity-like revenue base.
- **Cost-curve position** — geothermal/hydro sit low on the NZ supply stack, so Contact is typically a price-maker at the margin in dry/peak periods rather than a pure price-taker.

The utility model means the moat is real but earns regulated-style returns: bond-proxy behaviour, rate sensitivity, and exposure to NZ market and hydrology rules are the trade-off for that stability.

## Recent Developments

For FY25 (year to 30 June 2025) Contact reported NPAT of NZ$331 million and EBITDAF of NZ$872 million. For H1 FY26 (six months to 31 December 2025) NPAT rose 44% to NZ$205 million and EBITDAF reached NZ$500 million, with the Manawa acquisition contributing from 11 July 2025. Renewable generation was 97% of output in the half, reflecting the Manawa hydro assets and contracted PPAs. Contact raised NZ$525 million in new equity to fund a 200 MW grid-scale battery, the Glorit solar project and Tauhara 2 drilling. Over 80% of announced Manawa cost synergies were secured within the first six months of ownership. Contact lifted its normalised FY26 EBITDAF guidance by NZ$15 million to NZ$995 million.

The most recent filing (annual) was lodged on 2023-06-30. See [[#Financial Reports]] below.

- **Manawa integration & synergies** — >80% of announced cost synergies secured within the first six months of ownership; the deal lifts renewable share and contracted PPAs.
- **New-build pipeline** — Tauhara 2 geothermal drilling, a 200 MW grid-scale battery, and the Glorit solar project (funded by a NZ$525M equity raise) add capacity and firming.
- **Electrification / decarbonisation demand** — NZ's transition raises long-run electricity demand (EVs, process heat, data centres), supporting wholesale prices and volumes.
- **Guidance momentum** — normalised FY26 EBITDAF guidance lifted by NZ$15M to NZ$995M.
- **Recurring catalysts** — monthly operating reports (generation/customer data), H1 results (February) and FY results (August, June year-end), NZ Electricity Authority market reviews, hydrology/lake-storage updates, and project milestones.

## Bull vs Bear

**Bull case**
- Near-pure renewable generation post-Manawa (~97% of output) with long-life, low-marginal-cost geothermal and hydro — a quality, defensive asset base.
- The gentailer hedge smooths earnings; bond-proxy stability plus a reliable dividend appeals to yield investors.
- Structural NZ electricity demand growth from electrification; constrained new supply supports pricing.
- Growth pipeline (Tauhara 2, battery, solar) and synergy delivery offer earnings upside on top of the annuity.

**Bear case**
- Hydrology risk — dry years cut hydro output, force reliance on thermal/spot purchases, and can compress margins despite higher wholesale prices.
- Bond-proxy sensitivity — rising NZ/AU rates de-rate the stock regardless of operating performance.
- Regulatory risk — NZ electricity market reviews could reshape the gentailer model or pricing.
- Capital intensity and execution risk on the build pipeline; the equity raise was dilutive.

## Key Risks

- **Hydrology / dry-year risk** — lake storage and inflows directly drive generation volume and the cost of meeting retail load.
- **Wholesale-price volatility** — the gentailer hedge dampens but does not eliminate exposure.
- **Interest-rate sensitivity** — bond-proxy behaviour means valuation moves with NZ/AU rate expectations.
- **Regulatory** — NZ Electricity Authority reviews, market-design changes, and consent processes.
- **Integration / execution** — delivering Manawa synergies and the new-build pipeline on budget.

## Valuation & How the Stock Trades

Contact trades as a **defensive renewable utility / bond proxy** — valued on stable EBITDAF, asset replacement value, and dividend yield rather than on growth multiples. Mechanically:

- **Commodity/hydrology beta** — earnings swing with NZ hydro inflows, lake storage and wholesale electricity prices; dry years lift wholesale prices but raise the cost of serving retail load, wet years compress spreads. The gentailer structure damps the net effect.
- **Rate sensitivity** — bond-proxy behaviour gives the shares meaningful sensitivity to NZ/AU rate expectations; it tends to de-rate when long yields rise.
- **Dividend / yield** — a core part of the total-return case; held broadly in trans-Tasman [[utilities]], infrastructure and income portfolios. The NZ$525M raise funds growth without breaking the payout.
- **Index torque** — an [[asx-200]] constituent via the ASX line and an NZX 50 heavyweight on its primary listing.
- **Correlated names** — NZ gentailers [[meridian-energy]], [[mercury-nz]], [[genesis-energy]]; AU comparables [[agl-energy]] and [[origin-energy]].
