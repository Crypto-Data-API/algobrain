**Mercury NZ Limited** (ASX: MCY) is a renewable utilities company listed on the [[asx-limited|Australian Securities Exchange]] and a constituent of the [[asx-200]] index. Mercury NZ Limited is one of New Zealand's largest electricity generators and energy retailers, operating a 100% renewable generation portfolio (hydro, geothermal, and wind). The company is listed on both the NZX (primary) and the ASX (foreign exempt listing).

## Business Overview & Segments

Mercury is a New Zealand **"gentailer"** — a vertically integrated generator-and-retailer typical of the NZ electricity market. It generates power from a **100% renewable** portfolio (hydro, geothermal, wind) and sells electricity, gas, broadband and mobile direct to customers, naturally hedging wholesale price volatility by matching generation against its own retail load. As a regulated-style, yield-oriented [[utilities|utility]], its earnings are dominated by one swing factor above all others: **hydrology** (Waikato River inflows).

| Segment | What it is | Role (qualitative) |
|---|---|---|
| **Generation / Wholesale** | Renewable generation traded into NZ's wholesale electricity market | The earnings driver; exposed to hydro inflows and wholesale price volatility |
| **Customer** | Retails electricity, gas, broadband and mobile (Mercury, GLOBUG; Trustpower retail integrated 2022-23) | Provides a natural hedge to generation and a stable mass-market cash flow |
| **Other** | Central services, capital development, minor revenue | Supporting functions and the growth-capex programme |

Mercury's generation portfolio totals approximately **2,155 MW**:

| Technology | Capacity | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Hydro** | ~1,096 MW | 9 stations on the Waikato River; ~10% of NZ's total electricity supply — the core asset and the source of hydrology risk |
| **Geothermal** | ~475 MW | 5 stations in the central North Island (Ngā Tamariki, Kawerau, Rotokawa, Nga Awa Purua, Mokai); baseload, weather-independent |
| **Wind** | ~584 MW | 5 plants (Tilt Renewables assets, Turitea); growth via new build |

The company is investing approximately **A$1 billion in new renewable capacity**, targeting 3.5 TWh of additional generation by 2030 — about 8% of NZ's annual energy demand.

## History

Mercury NZ originated from the 1999 break-up of New Zealand's state-owned generator ECNZ, which was split into Genesis Energy, Meridian Energy, and Mighty River Power on 1 April 1999. Mighty River Power took over the nine Waikato River hydro stations and additional thermal and geothermal assets.

Key milestones:

- **1999:** Mighty River Power formed from ECNZ split.
- **2000s-2010s:** Built out geothermal generation at Kawerau, Nga Awa Purua, Rotokawa, and Ngā Tamariki.
- **2013:** Partial privatisation and listing on the NZX and ASX.
- **July 2016:** Renamed Mercury NZ Limited.
- **August 2021:** Acquired Tilt Renewables' New Zealand operations (5 wind farms plus development options) for NZ$797 million (net cash cost NZ$634 million after Mercury's prior Tilt stake sale). First power from the new Turitea wind farm was also generated around this time.
- **May 2022:** Acquired Trustpower's retail business for NZ$467 million, bringing hundreds of thousands of customers and the Trustpower brand into Mercury. Trustpower's generation business was renamed Manawa Energy.
- **June 2023:** Consolidated Mercury and Trustpower retail brands under a single Mercury brand.
- **2024-2026:** A$1 billion renewable build programme: Kaiwera Downs Stage 2 wind farm, Kaiwaikawe wind farm, and Ngā Tamariki OEC5 geothermal unit.

## Economic Moat & Competitive Position

Mercury's [[economic-moat]] is **structural and asset-based**, characteristic of a hydro-rich gentailer:

- **Irreplaceable hydro assets** — the nine Waikato River hydro stations are a low-cost, long-life, effectively impossible-to-replicate generation base producing ~10% of NZ's electricity; new hydro consents at this scale are not obtainable today.
- **Low-cost renewable baseload** — geothermal provides weather-independent baseload, and the overall 100% renewable mix gives a structurally low marginal cost of generation versus thermal peers.
- **Vertical integration (gentailer)** — owning both generation and a large retail book naturally hedges wholesale price swings and creates [[switching-costs|customer]] stickiness across electricity, gas, broadband and mobile bundles.
- **Oligopoly market structure** — the NZ electricity market is concentrated among a handful of gentailers (Mercury, [[contact-energy]], [[meridian-energy]], [[genesis-energy]]), limiting new entry and supporting returns.

The offsetting weakness is **hydrology dependence**: the same hydro assets that anchor the moat also make earnings volatile year-to-year, and the business operates under NZ regulatory and political oversight of power prices.

## Growth Drivers & Catalysts

- **A$1bn renewable build** — Kaiwera Downs Stage 2 and Kaiwaikawe wind farms plus the Ngā Tamariki OEC5 geothermal unit, targeting 3.5 TWh of additional generation by 2030.
- **Electrification & demand growth** — NZ decarbonisation (EVs, process-heat electrification) lifting structural electricity demand.
- **Wholesale price environment** — high/volatile wholesale prices can boost trading margin in years of tight supply.
- **Retail integration** — extracting value from the integrated Trustpower retail base under one Mercury brand and bundling broadband/mobile.
- **Cost discipline** — operating-expenditure reductions (e.g. opex falling from NZ$207m to NZ$183m in 1H FY26).
- **Recurring catalysts** — half-year/full-year results, monthly operating data (hydro inflows, generation), commissioning of new capacity, wholesale price moves, and dividend declarations.

## Bull Case vs Bear Case

### Bull case
- 100% renewable, low-cost generation base anchored by irreplaceable Waikato hydro and geothermal baseload.
- Defensive, yield-oriented utility with **fully imputed** NZ dividends — popular with income investors.
- A$1bn growth pipeline adds ~8% of national demand and supports earnings growth into electrification.
- Vertical integration smooths wholesale volatility; cost discipline lifting margins (1H FY26 EBITDAF +28%).
- Oligopoly market structure supports stable through-cycle returns.

### Bear case
- **Hydrology risk** — dry years (like FY2025, ~17% below-normal inflows) compress EBITDAF and crater statutory profit (FY25 NPAT ~NZ$1m).
- Capital-intensive build programme (~NZ$600m growth capex in the period) pressures free cash flow and balance sheet.
- Regulatory/political risk on power pricing and market structure in NZ.
- Rate-sensitivity: as a yield utility, valuation de-rates when bond yields rise.
- Earnings and dividend cover are volatile, complicating valuation in dry years.

## Key Risks

- **Hydrology** — the single biggest swing factor; Waikato inflows and South Island lake levels drive earnings, and dry years sharply compress EBITDAF.
- **Wholesale price volatility** — spot/wholesale electricity prices swing with supply-demand and weather, affecting trading margin.
- **Capex / balance sheet** — the A$1bn renewable programme strains free cash flow; execution and cost-overrun risk.
- **Regulatory / political** — NZ oversight of power prices and electricity-market structure.
- **Interest-rate sensitivity** — a yield-oriented utility that de-rates when bond yields rise.
- **Liquidity (ASX line)** — the dual NZX/ASX listing splits liquidity; ASX volume is thinner than the primary NZX line.

## Recent Developments

For FY2025 (year ended 30 June 2025), Mercury reported:

- Net profit after tax of approximately NZ$1 million, down NZ$289 million year-on-year.
- EBITDAF of NZ$786 million, down NZ$91 million.
- Results were affected by hydro inflows approximately 17% lower than normal (3,410 GWh, the 4th lowest for the Waikato scheme since 1980), together with high wholesale electricity price volatility.

For 1H FY2026 (half year ended 31 December 2025), Mercury reported:

- EBITDAF up 28% to NZ$537 million.
- NPAT of NZ$20 million (up ~130%); interim dividend lifted to 10 cents per share (+4%).
- Trading margin rising from NZ$615 million to NZ$706 million.
- Operating expenditure declining from NZ$207 million to NZ$183 million.
- FY2026 EBITDAF guidance upgraded to approximately NZ$1.05 billion (from ~NZ$1.0 billion); approximately NZ$600 million of growth capex expected for the period. (Source URLs in Sources)

Development updates:

- **Ngā Tamariki OEC5 (geothermal):** A NZ$220 million expansion project. The fifth unit entered commissioning in January 2026 and the expansion (55 MW of new capacity; annual output ~1,120 GWh) was officially opened in March 2026.
- **Kaiwera Downs Stage 2 (wind):** Approximately NZ$486 million; first generation expected in FY26, with full generation expected by HY27.
- **Kaiwaikawe (wind):** Approximately NZ$287 million; first and full generation expected in HY27.

## Trading Relevance

- **Index membership**: [[asx-200]] (foreign-exempt ASX listing; primary listing on the NZX as MCY). Dual NZX/ASX listing means liquidity is split and ASX volume is thinner than the NZX line.
- **Catalysts**: half-year/full-year results, monthly operating data (hydro inflows, generation), wholesale electricity price volatility, commissioning of new wind/geothermal capacity, and dividend declarations.
- **Key swing factor**: hydrology — Waikato River inflows and South Island lake levels drive earnings; dry years (like FY2025) compress EBITDAF, wet years expand it.
- **Correlated names**: NZ gentailers [[contact-energy]], [[meridian-energy]], [[genesis-energy]]; broader AU/NZ [[utilities]] including [[agl-energy]] and [[origin-energy]]. A defensive, yield-oriented holding popular with income investors (fully imputed NZ dividends).

## Valuation & How the Stock Trades

Mercury trades as a **defensive, yield-oriented [[utilities|utility]]**, but its headline P/E is unusable in dry years (FY25 NPAT collapsed to ~NZ$1m, pushing the reported P/E to absurd levels). The market therefore values it on **EBITDAF, dividend yield and asset/replacement value**, looking through hydrology-driven earnings swings. Mechanically:

- **Dividend / yield profile** — a steady, growing, **fully imputed** NZ dividend (1H FY26 interim lifted to 10cps; historical yield ~3-3.5%) is the core attraction for income investors; imputation credits lift the effective after-tax yield for NZ residents.
- **EBITDAF lens** — because NPAT is hydrology-distorted, the market keys off EBITDAF guidance (FY26 upgraded to ~NZ$1.05bn) and dividend sustainability rather than reported EPS.
- **Beta** — low; a classic defensive utility that tends to hold up in risk-off markets, though it is rate-sensitive as a yield instrument.
- **How it moves** — half-year/full-year results, **monthly operating data (hydro inflows, generation)**, wholesale price volatility, commissioning of new wind/geothermal capacity, and dividend declarations.

See the data block in **Fundamentals** below for the figures as pulled (P/E distorted by near-zero FY25 earnings, D/E ~0.42x, ~3% historical yield).
