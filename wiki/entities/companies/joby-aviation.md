Joby Aviation Inc. (NYSE: JOBY) is the leading US developer of electric vertical take-off and landing (eVTOL) air taxis, and the company closest to FAA type certification in the sector. For traders it is a high-beta, pre-revenue-to-early-revenue story stock: price action is driven almost entirely by certification milestones, launch news, and capital raises, with heavy retail options speculation.

## Business Overview

Joby's piloted S4 aircraft carries four passengers ~100 miles at up to 200 mph, targeting quiet urban air-taxi service priced near premium ride-share. Key strategic pillars:

- **Toyota partnership** — Toyota is the largest outside investor (~$894M total commitment including a $500M investment completed across 2024-2025 tranches) and supports manufacturing scale-up; production facilities in Marina, CA and Dayton, OH.
- **Uber integration** — air-taxi bookings to be offered through the [[uber]] app.
- **Blade acquisition (Aug 2025)** — Joby acquired Blade Air Mobility's passenger business for up to $125M, giving it immediate helicopter route revenue (NYC airport transfers, Europe) and terminal infrastructure to seed eVTOL service. This made Joby revenue-generating ahead of certification: Q4 2025 revenue was ~$30.8M, and management guided 2026 revenue to $105-115M.
- **Defense/other** — DoD work via Agility Prime; hydrogen-electric demonstrator (H2FLY); autonomy ambitions via acquired Xwing technology.

## Business Segments

Joby is a pre-/early-revenue developer transitioning from R&D to commercialization. Its activities group into distinct lines, only one of which yet generates meaningful revenue:

| Segment / pillar | What it is | Revenue status |
|---|---|---|
| **eVTOL air taxi (core)** | The piloted S4 aircraft + planned urban air-mobility service | Pre-revenue; the entire long-term thesis, gated on FAA type certification |
| **Blade passenger business (acquired Aug 2025)** | Helicopter route revenue — NYC airport transfers, Europe — plus terminal infrastructure | Revenue-generating *today*; seeds eVTOL demand and routes |
| **Manufacturing** | Production facilities in Marina, CA and Dayton, OH; scale-up backed by [[toyota]] | Cost center; capacity build ahead of certification |
| **Defense** | DoD work via Agility Prime; hydrogen-electric (H2FLY) demonstrator | Small contract/grant revenue + strategic validation |
| **Autonomy** | Xwing-acquired autonomous-flight technology | Long-dated optionality; no near-term revenue |

The **Blade acquisition** is the structural pivot that turned Joby from a pure pre-revenue story into an early-revenue one: it provides immediate helicopter route revenue and physical terminals to seed eVTOL service, partially de-risking the demand side while the aircraft awaits certification.

## Bull vs Bear Case

**Bull case**
- Perceived **certification front-runner**: Stage 4 of the five-stage FAA type-certification process confirmed complete (late March 2026), closest to a type certificate.
- Deep-pocketed strategic backer in [[toyota]] (manufacturing + capital) and a built-in distribution channel via [[uber]].
- **Blade acquisition** delivers real revenue and routes *now*, de-risking demand and giving a commercial foothold ahead of US certification.
- **Dubai launch** (exclusive RTA agreement) enables revenue service in 2026 ahead of full US rollout.
- First-mover brand and a multi-billion-dollar cash position fund the path to launch.

**Bear case**
- **Pre-profit with heavy cash burn** (~$190-200M/quarter net), so continued **dilution** is near-certain until the model self-funds.
- **Certification timing risk**: any slip of the final FAA gate (Stage 5 / TIA flight testing) beyond 2026 resets the thesis.
- **Manufacturing-ramp execution** is unproven at scale; cost and timeline risk.
- **Sector safety/event risk**: an incident *anywhere* in eVTOL would hit the entire group's valuation and regulatory posture.
- Long, capital-intensive path to commercial-scale economics; air-taxi unit economics at scale remain unproven.

## Valuation Framework (qualitative — pre-revenue eVTOL)

Joby cannot be valued on conventional earnings multiples — it is pre-/early-revenue and loss-making. The market values it as a **real-option on a future air-mobility market**, gated on milestones and runway:

1. **Certification progress (the binary-ish driver):** each FAA stage cleared raises the probability-weighted value of eventual commercial operation; the final type certificate is the single largest value-unlock. Slippage is the largest de-rating risk.
2. **Cash runway vs. burn (the dilution clock):** with ~$190-200M/quarter burn, the market continuously prices *how long the cash lasts* and *how much dilution* is needed to reach self-funding. A strong cash position (post-raise) supports the multiple; a depleting one forces dilutive raises that cap upside.
3. **Total addressable market × probability of execution:** the long-term story is a large urban-air-mobility TAM discounted by execution and adoption risk — essentially a venture-style, scenario-weighted valuation rather than a multiple.
4. **Relative to [[archer-aviation]]:** the cleanest practical valuation anchor is *relative* — Joby's premium/discount to Archer on certification progress, cash, and partnerships.

The practical takeaway: Joby trades on **milestones and runway, not fundamentals** — catalysts (FAA stages, Dubai launch, capital raises) drive double-digit single-day moves, and the equity is best thought of as a long-dated, high-variance call on commercializing eVTOL.

## 2025-2026 Developments

- **FAA certification:** Stage 4 (airworthiness conformity/verification) of the five-stage type-certification process was confirmed complete in late March 2026; Stage 5 (final type certificate issuance, TIA flight testing with FAA pilots) is the last regulatory gate. Power-on testing of the first FAA-conforming aircraft has begun. Management targets a 2026 type certificate followed by commercial launch.
- **Dubai first:** commercial air-taxi service is planned to launch in Dubai in 2026 under an exclusive agreement with the RTA (Ras Al Khaimah to follow ~2027), allowing revenue service ahead of full US rollout. Demonstration flights in Dubai began in 2024-2025.
- **Capital:** ended Q3 2025 with ~$978M in cash, then raised a further ~$576M in equity. Q1 2026 net loss ~$110M; operating expenses ~$258M/quarter with net cash burn roughly $190-200M/quarter — runway and dilution remain the central bear points.

## Capital Return Policy

- **No dividend and no buybacks** — Joby is a pre-profit, cash-burning growth company that retains and raises capital to fund certification, manufacturing scale-up, and launch.
- The relevant capital dynamic is the **opposite of return**: continued equity issuance (and strategic capital from [[toyota]]) to extend runway against ~$190-200M/quarter burn. Dilution is the central bear point until the business self-funds.

## Risks

- **Certification slippage** beyond 2026 (the final FAA gate) — the largest single de-rating risk.
- **Dilution** from continued ~$190-200M/quarter cash burn until self-funding.
- **Manufacturing-ramp execution** at scale, unproven.
- **Sector safety events** — an incident anywhere in eVTOL would hit the whole group's valuation and regulatory posture.
- **Unproven unit economics** for air-taxi service at commercial scale; long, capital-intensive path to profitability.
