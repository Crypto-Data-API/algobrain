S&P Global Platts (now officially **S&P Global Commodity Insights** following the merger of S&P Global and IHS Markit in 2022) is the world's leading commodity price benchmarking and assessment agency. Platts price assessments serve as the basis for physical commodity contracts worldwide, with trillions of dollars in trade priced off their benchmarks.

## What Platts Does

Unlike exchanges that discover prices through order matching, Platts produces **price assessments** — editorial judgments of the market value of a commodity at a specific location, quality, and time. These assessments are produced by teams of specialist journalists and editors who gather market intelligence from buyers, sellers, and brokers throughout each trading day.

Platts assessments are embedded in physical commodity contracts as the reference price. When a refinery buys a cargo of crude oil "at Dated Brent plus $2.00," the "Dated Brent" component is a Platts assessment.

## Market on Close (MOC) Methodology

Platts employs a distinctive **Market on Close (MOC)** assessment methodology, particularly for oil markets:

1. **Window period**: A defined time window (typically 4:00-4:30 PM London time for European oil) during which market participants submit bids, offers, and trade confirmations to Platts editors
2. **Transparency**: All bids and offers are published in real-time on the Platts eWindow platform, visible to all market participants
3. **Assessment**: Platts editors assess the market value based on the bids, offers, and confirmed transactions within the window
4. **Publication**: The final assessment is published shortly after the window closes

The MOC process is both a price discovery mechanism and a price assessment — participants actively trade during the window knowing their activity will influence the published benchmark. This creates a form of auction-based price transparency for physical commodity markets.

## Key Benchmarks

### Energy

- **Platts Dated Brent**: THE physical crude oil benchmark for the Atlantic Basin and beyond. Based on a basket of North Sea crude oil grades (Brent, Forties, Oseberg, Ekofisk, Troll — collectively "BFOET"). The Dated Brent assessment underpins pricing for roughly two-thirds of the world's internationally traded crude oil.
- **Platts Dubai**: The key crude oil benchmark for Middle Eastern oil flowing to Asia.
- **Platts JKM (Japan-Korea Marker)**: The benchmark for spot LNG ([[natural-gas|liquefied natural gas]]) in Northeast Asia — increasingly important as the global LNG market grows.
- **European gas and power**: Multiple regional assessments for natural gas hubs (TTF, NBP) and electricity.

### Metals

- **Iron ore 62% Fe CFR China**: THE benchmark for seaborne [[iron-ore]] pricing. Virtually all seaborne iron ore trade references this Platts assessment. The shift from annual contract pricing to Platts spot-based pricing occurred in 2010, fundamentally changing how the iron ore market operates.
- **Steel assessments**: Hot-rolled coil, rebar, and other steel product benchmarks.
- **Minor metals and battery materials**: Growing coverage of lithium, cobalt, nickel sulfate, and other EV battery inputs.

### Agriculture and Shipping

- **Freight assessments**: Tanker and dry bulk freight rates.
- **Biofuels**: Ethanol, biodiesel, and sustainable aviation fuel assessments.
- **Petrochemicals**: Ethylene, propylene, and polymer pricing.

## Market Influence

Platts benchmarks are unique in the commodity world because they occupy a quasi-regulatory role:

- **Contract reference prices**: Trillions of dollars worth of physical commodity contracts reference Platts assessments
- **Government pricing**: Some national oil companies (e.g., Saudi Aramco) use Platts benchmarks as the basis for their official selling prices
- **Tax and royalty calculations**: Some governments use Platts prices to calculate resource royalties and taxes
- **Derivative settlement**: Some OTC derivatives settle against Platts assessments

## 2025-2026 Context

Platts is not separately listed — it is the price-reporting and benchmarks arm within the **Commodity Insights** segment of [[sp-global|S&P Global]] (NYSE: SPGI). Traders gain exposure to the Platts franchise via SPGI equity. Commodity Insights remains one of SPGI's steady subscription-and-licensing growth engines, with Dated Brent and the JKM LNG marker still the dominant physical-market benchmarks for crude and Asian spot LNG respectively (Verified via Perplexity, 2026-06-10). Methodology evolution continues in energy-transition commodities (battery metals, low-carbon hydrogen, carbon credits) as those markets institutionalise.

## Competitors

- **Argus Media**: Platts' primary competitor, particularly strong in European energy and niche commodity markets
- **IOSCO**: The International Organization of Securities Commissions has established principles for oil price reporting agencies (PRAs), and both Platts and Argus submit to IOSCO audits
- **Exchange prices**: CME (WTI), ICE (Brent futures) — these are derivatives prices, while Platts assesses physical market prices. The two interact closely.
