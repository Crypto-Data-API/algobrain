MARA Holdings Inc. (NASDAQ: MARA) is one of the largest publicly traded [[bitcoin]] mining companies in the world, headquartered in Fort Lauderdale, Florida. Originally incorporated in 2010 as Marathon Patent Group, the company pivoted to blockchain infrastructure and digital asset mining in the late 2010s, rebranding to Marathon Digital Holdings in 2021 and then to MARA Holdings in August 2024. MARA operates a massive fleet of bitcoin miners and is a core holding for investors seeking leveraged exposure to [[bitcoin]] through US equity markets.

## Business Overview

MARA is a vertically integrating digital energy and infrastructure company: it mines [[bitcoin]] at scale (one of the largest hashrate operators globally), historically pursued a "full HODL" treasury strategy that made it the second-largest corporate BTC holder after [[microstrategy|Strategy]], and is now redeploying capital into **AI/high-performance-computing (HPC) data centers** built on owned energy assets. The equity has long traded as a leveraged, high-beta proxy for bitcoin; the AI pivot is changing that correlation profile.

## Business Model

MARA's economics rest on three reinforcing layers, and the relative weight of each is what defines the current re-rating story:

- **Self-mining ([[bitcoin]] production).** MARA runs one of the largest ASIC fleets in the world, converting electricity into hashrate and hashrate into BTC. Unit economics are governed by three variables: **fleet efficiency** (measured in joules per terahash, J/TH — lower is better; modern air-cooled rigs sit around the high-teens-to-low-20s J/TH and immersion/hydro can push lower), **all-in power cost** ($/kWh), and **hash price** (the revenue per unit of hashrate per day, set by BTC price and network difficulty). Post-[[bitcoin-halving|halving]], block subsidy fell to 3.125 BTC, structurally halving hash price and forcing every miner to drive down J/TH and $/kWh or shrink margins. See [[bitcoin-mining]] and [[hash-rate]].
- **Energy ownership.** Rather than buying power on the open market, MARA has moved to **own generation and grid-interconnected sites** (the Long Ridge gas-fired plant being the flagship). Owning the megawatts converts a variable cost into a controllable, financeable asset and is the precondition for the AI pivot — hyperscalers buy *power-plus-shell*, not hashrate.
- **AI / HPC data-center colocation.** The strategic pivot mirrors the cohort ([[core-scientific]], [[terawulf]], [[hut-8]], [[iren]], applied-digital, [[hive-digital-technologies]]): convert energized, interconnected megawatts from variable-margin mining into **long-dated, take-or-pay HPC hosting leases**. Per-MW revenue from a creditworthy AI tenant is materially higher and far more predictable than mining, which is why the market re-rates capacity from a mining multiple to a data-center / infrastructure multiple as leases are signed. The scarce asset is **energized, interconnected power** — in a multi-year interconnection-queue backlog, existing capacity is the moat.

The simplest way to read the business: MARA is trying to migrate from a *price-taker on BTC* to a *landlord of compute*, using mining as the cash-flowing bridge that funds the conversion.

## 2025–2026 Developments

- **Energy ownership:** acquired **Long Ridge Energy & Power** (Ohio gas-fired generation) to anchor an energy-first infrastructure strategy.
- **Starwood alliance (Feb 2026):** partnership with Starwood Capital / Starwood Digital Ventures to convert and expand MARA sites into energy-backed AI/compute campuses — targeting **1 GW of IT capacity with a path beyond 2.5 GW**.
- **Treasury break (Q1 2026):** sold ~**20,880 BTC for ~$1.5B** — abandoning the full-HODL stance — to fund the AI buildout and **retire a large portion of its convertible debt**. Holdings fell to **35,303 BTC (~$2.4B)**.
- **Q1 2026 results:** revenue $174.6M (vs $213.9M y/y); **net loss $1.26B**, driven mostly by a ~$1.0B negative fair-value adjustment on bitcoin holdings (new FASB mark-to-market accounting cuts both ways). Stock nonetheless rallied ~40%+ into mid-2026 on the AI-pivot narrative.
- Management frames mining as the cash-flowing operational foundation, not a legacy to exit.

## Bull vs Bear Case

**Bull case**
- Owns/controls power and is converting it into AI/HPC capacity (Starwood 1 GW → 2.5+ GW path) — re-rating from a mining multiple to a data-center / infrastructure multiple.
- Largest miner by hashrate with lowest-incremental-cost expansion ability; benefits most from any BTC rally via both mining margin and treasury marks.
- Treasury optionality: can sell BTC to self-fund the buildout and retire converts, reducing reliance on dilution at the margin.
- High-beta liquidity magnet: the most liquid options in the cohort, so it leads basket rallies.

**Bear case**
- The AI pivot is still largely *targeted* capacity, not energizing contracted backlog — execution and timeline risk versus peers that already have signed, ramping leases.
- BTC drawdowns hit twice: mining margin and (under FASB mark-to-market) the treasury, producing large GAAP losses like Q1 2026's ~$1.0B fair-value swing.
- Chronic dilution: MARA has historically funded via large ATM equity programs and convertibles, capping per-share upside (see Capital Structure below).
- Post-halving hash price keeps mining margins thin unless BTC rises or J/TH improves faster than difficulty.

## Valuation Framework (qualitative)

There is no clean single multiple for MARA; the market triangulates several lenses (no specific multiples are asserted here — these are the *axes* analysts weigh):

- **Bitcoin-price beta.** Historically a ~2x-beta proxy for [[bitcoin]]; the equity's largest single value driver remains the BTC path, amplified by the treasury and mining margin. The AI pivot is intended to *lower* this beta over time.
- **BTC treasury (NAV) value.** The mark-to-market value of held BTC is a hard floor/anchor that moves daily with price (FASB fair-value accounting now flows it through earnings).
- **EV per EH/s.** The cohort's standard mining yardstick — enterprise value divided by exahashes of installed/effective hashrate — lets you compare MARA against [[riot-platforms]], [[cleanspark]] et al. on a fleet basis.
- **Power-cost per kWh.** All-in $/kWh is the durable mining margin determinant and the gating factor on HPC competitiveness; owned generation is meant to lock this in.
- **HPC-contract backlog / per-MW lease value.** As leases are signed, the market shifts weight toward contracted, recurring lease revenue valued like an infrastructure/REIT-style asset — the core of the re-rating (compare [[core-scientific]], applied-digital).
- **Dilution-adjusted per-share.** Because share count grows via ATMs and converts, EV-based and per-MW metrics matter more than headline market cap; always normalize for the share-count trajectory.

## Capital Structure & Dilution Risk

MARA's buildout is capital-intensive, and the financing mix is itself a core part of the thesis:

- **ATM equity programs.** MARA has repeatedly used at-the-market equity offerings to fund fleet and infrastructure expansion. ATMs are efficient but continuously increase share count, diluting per-share metrics — the single biggest structural headwind to the equity.
- **Convertible notes.** The company has issued large convertibles; these defer dilution but create overhang (and, for some, hedging flows that suppress upside). In Q1 2026 MARA used BTC sale proceeds to **retire a large portion of its convertible debt**, a deliberate de-risking of the cap structure.
- **BTC treasury as financing tool.** The 35,303 BTC reserve doubles as a war chest: selling coins (as in Q1 2026's ~20,880-BTC sale) is an alternative to issuing equity, trading treasury upside for reduced dilution.
- **Implication for traders:** watch the share-count trajectory and convertible calendar as closely as production. A capital raise into weakness is a recurring drawdown catalyst; debt retirement and self-funding via BTC sales are de-risking signals.

## Risks

- **BTC drawdowns hit twice** — compressing mining margin and (via FASB mark-to-market) the treasury, producing large GAAP losses.
- **Execution risk on the AI pivot** — converting/expanding sites to AI-grade capacity and signing creditworthy tenants on schedule; MARA is behind the late-stage peers on *contracted* backlog.
- **Dilution** — ongoing ATM and convertible issuance erodes per-share value.
- **Power and interconnection risk** — owned generation helps, but grid, fuel-price, and interconnection-queue dynamics still gate expansion.
- **Hash-price compression** — post-[[bitcoin-halving|halving]] subsidy plus rising network difficulty squeeze mining margins absent a BTC rally or efficiency gains.
- **Regulatory / policy risk** — energy-use scrutiny, crypto regulation, and accounting changes (FASB) all affect reported results and sentiment.
- **Crowding / basket risk** — as a cohort favorite, MARA participates in indiscriminate basket selloffs regardless of company-specific news.
