**Stanmore Resources Limited** (ASX: SMR) is an coking coal company listed on the [[asx-limited|Australian Securities Exchange]] and a constituent of the [[asx-200]] index. Stanmore Resources Limited (ASX: SMR) is a Brisbane-based metallurgical coal mining company operating wholly within Queensland's Bowen Basin. Since its transformational 2022 acquisition of BHP Mitsui Coal, Stanmore has become one of Australia's significant independent producers of hard coking and PCI coal, with saleable production capacity above 13 million tonnes per year.

## Business Model & Segments

Stanmore operates and controls three producing metallurgical coal complexes:

- **South Walker Creek** — an open-cut mine in the northern Bowen Basin producing PCI and weak coking coal; acquired from BHP Mitsui Coal in 2022.
- **Poitrel** — an open-cut hard coking coal mine adjacent to South Walker Creek; acquired in the same 2022 transaction.
- **Isaac Plains Complex** — Stanmore's original operating base near Moranbah, comprising multiple pits and associated coal handling and preparation plants.

The company also holds undeveloped and growth projects, including the Isaac Downs Extension, Eagle Downs, Lancewood and the Isaac Plains underground project. Stanmore holds around 2,000 square kilometres of exploration tenements in the Bowen and Surat basins.

The ultimate majority shareholder is Golden Investments (Australia) Pte. Ltd., a Singapore vehicle controlled by Indonesia's Widjaja family (Sinar Mas group).

## Business Overview & Assets

Stanmore is a single-basin, single-commodity producer: every operating tonne comes from [[coal|metallurgical (coking) coal]] mined in Queensland's Bowen Basin, the world's premier hard-coking-coal province. The portfolio splits between producing complexes (the cash engine) and an undeveloped growth pipeline (optionality on the next cycle). Saleable production runs at roughly 13–14 Mtpa.

| Asset | Coal type | Role |
|---|---|---|
| **South Walker Creek** | PCI / weak coking | Open-cut in the northern Bowen Basin; PCI and weak coking output. Acquired via the 2022 BMC deal; set record production in FY25 |
| **Poitrel** | Hard coking | Open-cut adjacent to South Walker Creek; premium hard-coking-coal product. Also from the 2022 BMC transaction; FY25 record production |
| **Isaac Plains Complex** | Hard / semi-soft coking | Stanmore's original operating base near Moranbah — multiple pits plus coal handling & preparation plants (CHPPs); the foundation asset since the 2015 turnaround |
| **Isaac Downs Extension** | Coking | Growth project; the planned FY26 production step-down precedes this extension. Permitting in progress |
| **Eagle Downs** | Hard coking | Undeveloped hard-coking-coal project; ownership being consolidated, development options under evaluation |
| **Lancewood / Isaac Plains underground** | Coking | Earlier-stage growth and exploration optionality across ~2,000 km² of Bowen and Surat basin tenements |

The product mix matters: hard coking coal (Poitrel, Eagle Downs) commands a premium to PCI/weak coking and a large premium to thermal coal, because it is a near-irreplaceable input to blast-furnace [[steel]] making. That product quality is one of the few durable edges available to a price-taker (see below).

## Economic Moat & Competitive Position

Stanmore has **no [[economic-moat|economic moat]] in the classic sense** — [[coal|metallurgical coal]] is a homogeneous global commodity and Stanmore is a pure **price-taker**. It sells into the seaborne hard-coking-coal benchmark at whatever the market clears; it has no pricing power, no brand premium, and no [[switching-costs|switching-cost]] lock-in over its steel-mill customers. What separates a survivor from a marginal producer in this industry is not moat but **position on the cost curve and asset quality**:

- **Cost-curve position** — the durable advantage. FOB cash costs held at ~US$87.8/t in FY25 keep Stanmore in the lower half of the seaborne met-coal cost curve, meaning it stays cash-generative at prices that push higher-cost producers into losses. In a commodity, being cheap to mine *is* the edge.
- **Hard-coking-coal product quality** — Poitrel/Eagle Downs hard coking coal sells at a premium to PCI, weak coking and thermal coal; premium product realises better margins through the cycle than thermal-heavy peers.
- **Bowen Basin scale & infrastructure** — established open-cut mines, CHPPs, and rail/port access in the world's leading hard-coking-coal province; large, contiguous tenement position (~2,000 km²) supports a long reserve/mine life and brownfield growth (Isaac Downs, Eagle Downs) at lower marginal cost than a greenfield entrant.
- **Reserve & mine life** — multi-decade resource base across the Bowen Basin underpins long-dated optionality.

The honest counterweights: there is **no pricing power**, so margins swing entirely with the realised met-coal price (FY25's swing to a net loss shows how fast). Two structural headwinds compound this — **ESG and financing constraints** (banks, insurers and index funds increasingly restrict coal exposure, raising the cost and reducing the availability of capital regardless of operating quality), and a **controlling-shareholder free-float constraint** (Golden Investments / Sinar Mas majority ownership limits the public float, dampens liquidity, and reduces minority-shareholder influence).

## Growth Drivers & Catalysts

- **Met-coal price recovery** — the single biggest swing factor; a rebound in the seaborne hard-coking-coal benchmark on stronger China/India [[steel]] demand flows almost directly to earnings given the fixed-ish cost base.
- **Isaac Downs Extension** — brownfield growth to backfill the planned FY26 production step-down; permitting progress is a watch item.
- **Eagle Downs development decision** — consolidated-ownership hard-coking-coal project; a sanction (or deferral) reshapes the medium-term volume and capex profile.
- **FOB cost control** — holding cash costs near ~US$87.8/t defends cost-curve position and through-cycle cash generation.
- **Dividends & capital returns** — Stanmore paid a final dividend (8.9 US cents, ~US$80M) *through* a loss year, signalling confidence in cash generation; capital-return policy is a recurring catalyst.
- **Production volumes** — quarterly saleable-production prints (record ~14.0 Mt in FY25; FY26 guidance 12.8–13.4 Mt) move the stock alongside price.

## Bull Case vs Bear Case

### Bull case
- **Resilient balance sheet** — modest net debt (~US$33M) and almost US$500M of liquidity provide room to invest and pay dividends through a downcycle.
- **Dividend through a loss year** — declaring a final dividend (~US$80M) despite a reported FY25 net loss signals management confidence in underlying cash generation (underlying EBITDA was still ~US$385M).
- **Hard-coking-coal leverage to a steel recovery** — premium met-coal product means outsized upside if China/India [[steel]] demand and the seaborne benchmark recover; low cost base amplifies the operating leverage.
- **Growth pipeline** — Isaac Downs, Eagle Downs and Bowen Basin tenements offer brownfield volume optionality at lower marginal cost than greenfield rivals.

### Bear case
- **FY25 net loss on price decline** — earnings swung to a ~US$47.2M net loss as realised met-coal prices fell (average realised price ~US$214/t in 2023 → ~US$168/t in 2024, softer in 2025); a pure price-taker with no offset.
- **ESG & financing constraints** — coal producers face rising capital costs and shrinking lender/insurer/index access regardless of operating quality.
- **Controlling-shareholder free float** — Golden Investments (Sinar Mas / Widjaja family) majority ownership limits float, liquidity, and minority influence.
- **Single-commodity, single-basin concentration** — 100% met coal, 100% Bowen Basin; no diversification against price, weather, regulatory or geotechnical shocks.

## Key Risks

- **Metallurgical coal price volatility** — earnings track the seaborne hard-coking-coal benchmark, which is driven by China/India [[steel]] demand and global blast-furnace output; the FY25 loss followed a multi-year price slide.
- **Bowen Basin weather** — wet-season flooding and rainfall regularly disrupt open-cut mining and rail haulage in Queensland.
- **Geotechnical / operational risk** — open-cut pit stability, equipment availability and CHPP throughput can move volumes and unit costs.
- **ESG & financing constraints** — restricted access to bank debt, insurance and index inclusion raises capital costs and limits strategic flexibility for coal producers.
- **Concentrated control / limited free float** — majority ownership by Golden Investments reduces liquidity and the weight of minority shareholders in governance and capital-allocation decisions.

## Valuation & How the Stock Trades

Stanmore trades as a **high-beta, leveraged play on the seaborne hard-coking-coal benchmark and Chinese steel margins** — a price-taker whose earnings swing directly with the realised metallurgical-coal price. Mechanically:

- **Earnings = price × volume − a fairly fixed cost base** — with FOB cash costs anchored near ~US$87.8/t, every dollar of benchmark price move drops largely to the bottom line. That makes the equity far more volatile than the underlying business and tightly correlated to met-coal price headlines.
- **Negative headline P/E** — the trailing P/E is negative because FY25 reported a net loss; the multiple is **uninformative at the trough** and must be read through-cycle (note the company still generated ~US$385M underlying EBITDA and ~US$270M free cash flow in FY25). Commodity producers are valued on through-cycle cash generation, EV/EBITDA and cost-curve position, not trough EPS.
- **Dividend signalling** — paying a dividend through a loss year is a deliberate signal of balance-sheet strength; capital-return decisions move sentiment.
- **Limited free float** — majority control by Golden Investments dampens trading liquidity and can amplify price moves on the available float; options liquidity is thin and positioning is typically via the equity.
- **Correlated names** — moves with [[coronado-global-resources]], [[whitehaven-coal]], [[bhp]] and the broader [[coal]] complex, and inversely with risk-off rotations away from carbon-exposed [[materials]].

## Recent Developments

Stanmore's 2025 financial year (calendar year) reflected record production but softer coal prices:

- **FY25 production**: Record saleable production of approximately 14.0 million tonnes, with 20.5 Mt of run-of-mine coal mined. Both South Walker Creek and Poitrel set record production despite weather-related challenges.
- **FY25 financials**: Revenue of approximately US$1.89 billion (down from US$2.50 billion in 2024), underlying EBITDA of approximately US$385 million, and a net loss of approximately US$47.2 million driven by lower realised metallurgical coal prices.
- **Cost performance**: FOB cash costs maintained at approximately US$87.8 per tonne.
- **Dividend**: A final dividend of 8.9 US cents per share was declared (approximately US$80 million), with the company signalling confidence in cash generation despite the reported loss.
- **Balance sheet**: Modest net debt of approximately US$33 million with almost US$500 million of liquidity following upsized revolving credit facilities.
- **FY26 guidance**: Saleable production of 12.8–13.4 Mt, reflecting a planned step-down as operations are optimised ahead of the Isaac Downs extension project.
- **Growth pipeline**: Continued progress on Isaac Downs extension permitting and ongoing evaluation of Eagle Downs development options.

## Trading Relevance

- ASX 200 constituent and one of the few liquid pure-play metallurgical (coking) coal exposures on the ASX. A leveraged play on steelmaking demand and the hard-coking-coal price.
- **Catalysts**: quarterly production reports (record FY25 saleable production ~14.0Mt; FY26 guidance 12.8-13.4Mt), realised met-coal price (the swing factor — average realised price fell from ~US$214/t in 2023 to ~US$168/t in 2024 and softened further in 2025), FOB cash costs (~US$87.8/t), dividend declarations, Isaac Downs extension and Eagle Downs development decisions.
- **Risks**: metallurgical coal price volatility (China/India steel demand), weather disruption in the Bowen Basin, geotechnical/operational risk, ESG and financing constraints on coal producers, concentrated control by majority shareholder Golden Investments (Sinar Mas / Widjaja family) which limits free float and minority influence.
- **Correlated names**: [[bhp]], [[whitehaven-coal]], [[coronado-global-resources]], [[new-hope-corporation]]; broadly tracks the seaborne hard-coking-coal benchmark and Chinese steel margins.
