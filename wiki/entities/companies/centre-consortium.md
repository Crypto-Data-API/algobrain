# Centre Consortium

**Centre Consortium** was the governance body that originally managed the [[usdc|USDC]] stablecoin standard. Co-founded in 2018 by [[circle|Circle]] and [[coinbase|Coinbase]], Centre established technical standards, compliance requirements, and governance rules for USDC issuance. The consortium was dissolved in August 2023, with [[circle|Circle]] assuming full governance and operational control of USDC.

## History

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 2018 | Co-founded by Circle and Coinbase to govern USDC |
| 2018 | Published the Centre USDC standard and launched USDC on Ethereum |
| 2020 | Expanded USDC to additional blockchains (Algorand, Solana, Stellar) |
| 2023 | Dissolved — Circle assumed full governance of USDC |

## Purpose and Structure

Centre served three functions:

1. **Technical standards**: Defined the smart contract specifications, minting/redemption processes, and multi-chain deployment rules for USDC
2. **Compliance framework**: Established KYC/AML requirements for USDC issuers and ensured [[stablecoin-regulation|regulatory]] alignment
3. **Governance**: Provided shared governance between Circle (issuer) and Coinbase (distribution partner), preventing either party from unilateral control

The consortium structure was designed to position USDC as an open, multi-issuer standard — not just a Circle product. In practice, Circle was always the sole issuer.

## Why It Was Dissolved

By 2023, the two-party governance model had become an unnecessary abstraction. Circle was the sole issuer, the sole operator of [[cctp|CCTP]], and the primary decision-maker on USDC policy. Dissolving Centre simplified governance and gave Circle full authority to pursue its IPO and regulatory strategy without coordination overhead.

The dissolution had no practical impact on USDC users — the token continued to function identically. However, it formalized what was already true: USDC is a Circle product, not a consortium product.

## Revenue Sharing

One important legacy of Centre was the revenue-sharing arrangement between Circle and [[coinbase|Coinbase]]. Coinbase earns a share of the interest income generated by USDC reserves — reportedly $468 million in 2023. This arrangement survived Centre's dissolution and remains a significant revenue stream for Coinbase, creating strong alignment between the two companies on USDC growth.

## Trading Relevance

Centre itself was never tradeable, but its legacy matters for two listed names: [[circle]] (NYSE: CRCL since its June 2025 IPO) books the reserve interest income on USDC, while [[coinbase]] (NASDAQ: COIN) retains the Centre-era revenue share on USDC reserves — a high-margin earnings stream that scales with USDC supply and US interest rates. Traders modelling either stock track USDC circulating supply and front-end yields as direct revenue drivers; the 2023 dissolution is the reason the economics now sit bilaterally between the two companies.
