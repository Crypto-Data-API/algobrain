> **Completed-acquisition (all-stock merger) case study.** EXAI no longer trades. Exscientia was absorbed into [[recursion-pharmaceuticals|Recursion Pharmaceuticals]] and delisted on 2024-11-20. This page is written in past tense and retained as a historical case study in AI-biotech narrative de-rating and stock-for-stock [[merger-arbitrage]]. Nothing here implies the stock still trades.

Exscientia plc (formerly NASDAQ: EXAI) was a UK-based pioneer in [[artificial-intelligence|AI]]-driven drug design — the first company to bring an AI-designed drug molecule into human clinical trials. **It no longer trades independently:** Exscientia was acquired by [[recursion-pharmaceuticals|Recursion Pharmaceuticals]] (NASDAQ: RXRX) in an all-stock merger that closed on 2024-11-20, and EXAI shares were delisted.

## What the Business Was

Founded in Oxford in 2012 by Andrew Hopkins, Exscientia built a generative-AI platform for small-molecule drug design and ran "pharmatech" partnerships with Sanofi, Bristol Myers Squibb and others. It IPO'd on Nasdaq in October 2021 near the peak of the AI-biotech bubble at a valuation around $3B, then suffered the sector-wide derating as clinical timelines and cash burn disappointed; its founder-CEO was dismissed in February 2024 over conduct issues, accelerating strategic-review pressure.

### Business model / segments

Exscientia's value proposition was **chemistry-first AI drug design**: using machine learning to design and prioritize small-molecule candidates faster and with fewer iterations than traditional medicinal chemistry. The business ran on two complementary engines:

| Engine | What it was | Monetization |
|--------|-------------|--------------|
| **Partnered / "pharmatech" programs** | AI-designed molecules developed for large-pharma partners (Sanofi, Bristol Myers Squibb, others) | Upfront fees, research funding, milestone payments, royalties |
| **Proprietary pipeline** | In-house clinical assets (e.g., oncology) advanced on Exscientia's own balance sheet | Equity value via clinical milestones; capital-intensive, slow |
| **Platform / precision-medicine tooling** | AI design plus patient-tissue ("precision medicine") capabilities | Differentiator vs. pure in-silico peers |

The headline claim — first AI-designed molecule into human trials — was a genuine technical milestone, but the company was valued on platform narrative rather than on near-term, de-risked clinical assets, which is the structural reason the multiple collapsed when sentiment turned.

## Competitive Positioning at the Time

Exscientia was one of three marquee "AI drug discovery" listed names, each pitching a different flavor of the same thesis: AI compresses the cost and time of finding drugs. The market initially paid platform multiples; it then demanded clinical proof, and the platform-only names de-rated hardest.

| Peer | Platform emphasis | Contrast with Exscientia |
|------|-------------------|--------------------------|
| [[recursion-pharmaceuticals]] (RXRX) | **Biology-first** — automated wet-lab phenomics screening at scale; NVIDIA-backed | The eventual acquirer; combining biology-first screening with Exscientia's chemistry-first design was the explicit merger logic |
| [[schrödinger]] (SDGR) | **Physics-based** computational chemistry plus a software-licensing business | Schrödinger had a real recurring software-license revenue line cushioning the drug pipeline; Exscientia leaned more on partnerships + pipeline |
| AbCellera, Relay Therapeutics, others | Antibody / dynamics-based discovery | Adjacent AI/comp-bio names that shared the 2021-2024 de-rating |

The competitive lesson: among the AI-biotech cohort, the survivors with the most durable economics had either recurring software revenue ([[schrödinger]]) or massive capital + a strategic backer ([[recursion-pharmaceuticals]]). Exscientia, valued on platform narrative without near-term clinical catalysts, became acquisition fodder rather than an acquirer.

## The Deal: Acquisition by Recursion (2024)

- **Deal:** all-stock merger announced August 2024, valued at roughly **$688 million**; each EXAI share converted into **0.7729 RXRX shares** (plus cash for fractional shares).
- **Timeline:** shareholders approved 2024-11-13; merger closed before the open on **2024-11-20**; EXAI delisted from Nasdaq.
- **Rationale:** combined Recursion's biology-first screening platform with Exscientia's chemistry-design platform into a single AI-drug-discovery company.
- **Why all-stock:** both companies were cash-burning, pre-profit biotechs; a stock-for-stock structure preserved cash, and Exscientia's balance-sheet cash plus partnerships were as much the prize as the platform.

## Merger-Arb / Trading Postmortem

Unlike the all-cash deals in this sector ([[splunk]], [[1life-healthcare|One Medical]]), this was a **stock-for-stock** merger, so the arb mechanics were different:

- **The hedge was the trade.** With a fixed exchange ratio of **0.7729 RXRX per EXAI**, the [[merger-arbitrage]] position was: long EXAI, short 0.7729 RXRX per share. The "spread" was the gap between EXAI's price and 0.7729 × RXRX, capturing the discount to the deal value while hedging out RXRX's own volatility.
- **Exchange-ratio (not financing) risk.** Because consideration was acquirer stock, EXAI holders' realized value floated with RXRX. The arb removed that by shorting RXRX in the ratio; an unhedged EXAI holder effectively took on RXRX directional risk into close.
- **Vol transfer.** All-stock rescue mergers *transfer the volatility to the acquirer's ticker.* On close, EXAI's idiosyncratic risk did not vanish — it migrated into [[recursion-pharmaceuticals]] (RXRX), which remains a high-beta, NVIDIA-backed AI-biotech trading on clinical milestones, partnership news, and AI sentiment.
- **Option adjustments.** Listed EXAI options were adjusted to the 0.7729 RXRX deliverable per OCC info memo 55579 (see Sources) — a standard mechanic in stock-for-stock closes that traders must model rather than assume cash settlement.

## Trading Relevance / Lessons

- **No direct exposure exists.** Exposure to the legacy Exscientia platform is now via [[recursion-pharmaceuticals]] (RXRX), itself a high-beta, NVIDIA-backed AI-biotech trading on clinical milestones, partnership news and AI sentiment.
- **Lesson for traders:** EXAI is a case study in AI-narrative derating — IPO near $3B, acquired for ~$688M three years later. AI platform stories without near-term clinical assets get valued on cash and partnerships when sentiment turns, and all-stock rescue mergers transfer the volatility to the acquirer's ticker.
- **Survivors had cushions.** Compared with [[schrödinger]]'s recurring software revenue and [[recursion-pharmaceuticals]]'s capital + strategic backing, Exscientia's narrative-only valuation left it exposed when the AI-biotech multiple compressed.

## Risks That Materialized (for an independent Exscientia thesis)

- **Narrative-multiple compression:** ~$3B IPO valuation to a ~$688M takeout in three years as platform stories repriced.
- **Clinical-timeline / cash-burn risk:** disappointing timelines and burn against a pre-revenue pipeline eroded the standalone case.
- **Governance shock:** the founder-CEO's February 2024 dismissal over conduct issues accelerated the strategic review that ended in the sale.
- **Loss of independence / dilution into acquirer:** EXAI holders became minority RXRX holders at a fixed 0.7729 ratio; the standalone vehicle ceased to exist.
