DraftKings Inc. (NASDAQ: DKNG) is an American digital sports entertainment and gaming company founded in 2012 and headquartered in Boston, Massachusetts. The company operates one of the largest legal online sportsbooks and daily fantasy sports platforms in the United States, and has expanded into iGaming (online casino), lottery courier services (Jackpocket, acquired 2024), and — since late 2025 — federally regulated prediction markets. DraftKings' stock is popular among options traders for earnings plays and as a proxy for the expanding US sports betting and event-contract regulatory landscape.

## Business Overview

DraftKings operates online sports betting in the large majority of legal US states and iGaming in the smaller set of states where it is permitted, in a near-duopoly with [[fanduel|FanDuel]] ([[flutter-entertainment|Flutter Entertainment]]). Revenue is driven by betting handle, structural sportsbook hold (net revenue margin reached 7.8% in Q1 2026), promotional intensity, and iGaming growth. The customer-acquisition-heavy model has crossed into GAAP profitability as marketing matures in older state cohorts.

## Business Segments

DraftKings' economics rest on a handful of operating drivers that traders should track separately from the headline revenue line.

### Online Sports Betting (OSB)

- **Handle** — total dollars wagered; the top-of-funnel volume metric. Grows with new-state launches, product engagement, and parlay/same-game-parlay mix.
- **Structural hold (net revenue margin)** — the share of handle DraftKings keeps after paying out winners. Higher-margin parlays lift structural hold; reported hold varies quarter to quarter with sport outcomes (bettor-friendly favorites/NFL results compress it). Q1 2026 net revenue margin reached 7.8%.
- **Promotional intensity** — free bets, deposit bonuses, and odds boosts. Promo spend is heaviest in newly launched states and during the NFL kickoff; declining promo as a % of handle in mature cohorts is the key profitability lever.

### iGaming (online casino)

- Higher-margin and less seasonal than OSB, but legal in fewer states. Slots and table games drive recurring, lower-variance revenue; state-by-state legalization is the principal growth gate.

### Adjacencies

- **Jackpocket (lottery courier)** — acquired 2024; cross-sells lottery players into OSB/iGaming.
- **DraftKings Predictions (prediction markets)** — CFTC-regulated event contracts launched late 2025; a federal-licensing path that sidesteps state-by-state gaming approval, both a hedge against and response to Kalshi/Polymarket.
- **Daily Fantasy Sports (DFS)** — the legacy product and original customer-acquisition funnel.

## Bull vs. Bear Case

**Bull case**
- Near-duopoly market structure with rational(izing) promotional spend as cohorts mature — operating leverage as marketing falls per dollar of handle.
- Inflection to sustained GAAP profitability and positive adjusted EBITDA; FY2026 guidance reaffirmed and a buyback authorized.
- Long legalization runway: more states adding OSB/iGaming; iGaming is the higher-margin prize.
- Prediction-markets optionality (DraftKings Predictions) opens a federally regulated, nationwide TAM.

**Bear case**
- **State tax hikes** — Illinois-style per-wager surcharges and rising gaming-tax rates directly compress contribution margin and can force promo cuts.
- **Prediction-market disintermediation** — Kalshi/Polymarket offer sports event contracts nationwide without state licenses, potentially undercutting the regulated-sportsbook model DraftKings invested billions to build.
- **Hold variance** — bettor-friendly sport outcomes (favorites winning, key NFL results) can compress a quarter's revenue regardless of operational execution.
- **Promo wars** with FanDuel can re-escalate, especially in new states.

## Valuation Framework

DKNG is valued on the path to mature-state profitability rather than trailing earnings. Qualitative drivers:

- **Net revenue growth** vs. **handle growth** — divergence reveals structural-hold and product-mix improvement.
- **Contribution margin / adjusted EBITDA trajectory** — the core de-risking metric as promo intensity falls in mature cohorts.
- **Cohort maturation** — older states' customers spend more and cost less to retain; the blended margin rises as the state mix matures.
- **iGaming mix** — higher iGaming share lifts blended margin and reduces seasonality.
- **TAM expansion** — incremental state legalizations and the prediction-markets optionality.
- Comparable lens: EV/revenue and EV/EBITDA against [[flutter-entertainment|Flutter]] (the cleanest peer), with prediction-markets treated as embedded optionality.

## Trading & Options Playbook

- **Catalysts calendar**: quarterly earnings (hold % vs. sport outcomes is the swing factor), state legalization votes/launches (e.g., Missouri went live Dec 2025), state tax-rate changes, prediction-market regulatory rulings, and **NFL season kickoff (September) seasonality** — the single biggest demand and promo event of the year.
- **Index/ETF membership**: NASDAQ-listed; not in the [[s-and-p-500]]. Held in consumer-discretionary and thematic gaming/betting ETFs (e.g., BETZ, ESPO-adjacent baskets) and growth mandates; a high-weight name in sports-betting thematics.
- **Volatility behavior**: liquid **weekly options** make DKNG a staple for earnings straddles and event-driven plays; IV ramps into prints and into the NFL kickoff. Regulatory headlines (tax hikes, CFTC rulings) produce sharp, gappy moves.
- **Common structures**: earnings straddles/strangles, event-driven directional plays around legalization/tax news, and longer-dated calls as a levered proxy on US betting TAM expansion.
- **Correlated names**: [[flutter-entertainment|Flutter]] (FLUT), MGM (BetMGM), Caesars (CZR), Rush Street (RSI), plus data-rights suppliers Genius Sports (GENI) and Sportradar (SRAD).

## 2025–2026 Developments

- **Q1 2026 (reported May 2026):** record Q1 revenue of US$1.65B (+17% YoY), adjusted EBITDA of US$168M (+64%), and GAAP net income of ~US$21M — the second consecutive profitable quarter. FY2026 guidance reaffirmed at US$6.5–6.9B revenue and US$700–900M adjusted EBITDA. A new share-buyback authorization accompanied the print.
- **Prediction markets pivot:** DraftKings moved aggressively into CFTC-regulated event contracts ("DraftKings Predictions"), with consumer volume hitting an annualized US$1B in April 2026 and total annualized volume of US$2.3B; management earmarked US$200–300M for exchange infrastructure and market-making in 2026. This is both a hedge against and response to competition from Kalshi and Polymarket, which offer sports event contracts nationwide without state gaming licenses.
- **Missouri mobile launch** (state went live December 2025) added a new growth state.

## Capital Return / Dilution

- DraftKings historically funded growth through losses and equity, but the 2026 inflection to profitability brought a **new share-buyback authorization** announced alongside the Q1 2026 print — a signal of confidence in free-cash-flow durability.
- **Stock-based compensation** has been a persistent source of dilution typical of growth tech; buybacks are partly aimed at offsetting SBC dilution. The company does not pay a dividend.
- Heavy planned spend on prediction-market infrastructure and market-making (US$200–300M earmarked for 2026) is a use of cash to watch against the buyback narrative.

## Risks

- **State tax / regulatory**: rising gaming-tax rates and per-wager surcharges (Illinois-style) directly hit margins; tax is set state by state, creating ongoing policy risk.
- **Prediction-market disruption**: Kalshi/Polymarket's nationwide CFTC-regulated sports event contracts could erode the state-licensed sportsbook moat.
- **Hold/outcome variance**: short-run revenue depends on sport results outside the company's control.
- **Competitive promo escalation**: a renewed promo war with [[fanduel|FanDuel]] would re-pressure contribution margin.
- **Responsible-gaming / problem-gambling regulation**: advertising restrictions and affordability checks could raise compliance costs and cap acquisition.
