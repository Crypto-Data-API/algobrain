Fox Corporation (NASDAQ: FOXA — Class A shares) is the Murdoch-controlled US media company built around live news and sports: Fox News, Fox Sports, the FOX broadcast network, Fox-owned TV stations, the Tubi free-streaming service, and (since August 2025) the Fox One direct-to-consumer service. The company carries a **dual-class structure** — **FOXA** (Class A) is economically equivalent but **non-voting**, while **[[fox-corporation-(class-b)|FOX]]** (Class B) carries the votes through which the Murdoch Family Trust controls the company; both lines are [[communication-services|Communication Services]] members of the [[s-and-p-500]]. Strategically, Fox is the **"leaned-in to live"** counter-bet against the cord-cutting decline that pressures more diversified peers like [[disney]] and [[comcast]]: rather than chase a sub-scale general-entertainment streaming war, it concentrates on must-watch live news and sports — the content most resistant to time-shifting and the glue that still holds the pay-TV bundle together.

## Business Overview & Segments

Fox was created in March 2019 as the remainder of 21st Century Fox after [[disney|Disney]] acquired the film/TV studio assets — the "New Fox," deliberately narrowed to live, can't-time-shift content (news and sports) plus the FOX broadcast network. Revenue splits roughly half-and-half between **affiliate fees** (recurring carriage payments from pay-TV distributors and, increasingly, virtual MVPDs) and **advertising**, the latter highly cyclical around NFL seasons, US elections and political ad spend. Affiliate fees give the model a quasi-subscription, contract-priced backbone; advertising adds operating leverage in big sports and election years.

| Segment / line | What it is | Revenue mix (qualitative) |
|---|---|---|
| **Cable Network Programming** | Fox News (the dominant US cable-news network and the company's profit engine), Fox Business, plus the FS1/FS2 sports cable channels | Largest profit contributor; affiliate-fee heavy with high-margin news; the engine that funds everything else |
| **Television** | FOX broadcast network (NFL, college football, MLB, entertainment), ~29 owned-and-operated local stations, and the **Tubi** free ad-supported streaming (AVOD) service | Large and advertising-heavy; most cyclical line; Tubi is the digital-growth and ad-inventory story within it |
| **Other** | Corporate, the FOX Studio Lot, and eliminations | Small / non-core |
| **Fox One (DTC)** | Standalone direct-to-consumer streaming bundle launched 2025-08-21 ($19.99/mo, $199.99/yr) carrying Fox News, Fox Sports, the FOX network, B1G, Fox Business and Fox Weather | Nascent; reported within the existing segments but the key forward optionality for reaching cordless households |

**Control structure.** The Murdoch Family Trust controls Fox through its ~40% block of **voting Class B (FOX)** shares; the publicly heavier **Class A (FOXA)** line is economically identical but **non-voting**. In September 2024 a Nevada probate court rejected Rupert Murdoch's attempt to amend the trust to entrench a single heir, and a 2025 family settlement left **Lachlan Murdoch** in long-term control. This dual-class, family-controlled governance is a permanent feature of the investment case — and the source of the FOXA/FOX trading spread (see [[fox-corporation-(class-b)]]).

## 2025–2026 Developments

- **FY2025 (ended 2025-06-30):** a record fiscal year — Q4 FY25 revenue $3.29B (+6% YoY), advertising +7% on Tubi digital growth; full-year EPS $4.91 (see fundamentals below), helped by Super Bowl LIX (February 2025), which set all-time ad-revenue records.
- **Fox One launched 2025-08-21** at $19.99/month ($199.99/yr) — Fox's first full direct-to-consumer bundle (Fox News, Fox Sports, FOX network, B1G, Fox Business, Fox Weather), aimed at cordless households; management reported building subscriber momentum through fiscal Q2 2026 (December quarter).
- **Tubi turned profitable** for the first time in fiscal Q1 2026 (September 2025 quarter) with revenue up 27%; Q1 FY26 total revenue was $3.74B on streaming and sports strength.
- **FY2026:** continued affiliate-renewal pricing power and NFL/college football ad strength; Q3 FY2026 results released April/May 2026 (8-K filed).

## Economic Moat & Competitive Position

Fox's [[economic-moat]] is **content- and contract-driven** rather than the SaaS-style [[switching-costs]] that protect software companies — there is no installed-base lock-in, and a viewer can drop Fox the moment a game ends. What protects the franchise is the scarcity and stickiness of *live* content:

- **Fox News audience loyalty & ratings leadership** — a durable, highly engaged audience that has held the top cable-news ratings for years. That loyalty translates into pricing power in affiliate negotiations: distributors that drop Fox News risk losing subscribers, which lets Fox push through carriage-fee increases even as the overall bundle shrinks.
- **Sports rights as appointment viewing** — NFL, college football, MLB and the Super Bowl are the few programs Americans still watch live and in volume. Live sports props up the affiliate bundle and commands the highest ad rates on television.
- **Brand & reach** — the FOX broadcast network and ~29 owned-and-operated stations give national plus local advertising reach that pure-streamers cannot replicate.

The moat is real but **bounded by structure**: it is rented through periodically re-bid sports-rights deals and renegotiated affiliate contracts, not owned in perpetuity. The "leaned-in to live" focus insulates Fox better than diversified peers from cord-cutting, but it does not exempt it — the addressable pay-TV base is still shrinking, and rights-cost inflation can outrun affiliate-fee growth. The bet is that live news and sports decline slowest, and that Fox can re-aggregate those audiences directly through Fox One and Tubi.

## Growth Drivers & Catalysts

- **Fox One DTC ramp** — converting cordless and cord-never households into a direct subscription relationship; subscriber disclosures are the key forward metric.
- **Tubi monetization** — now profitable, with fast ad-revenue growth (+27% in fiscal Q1 2026); a free, scaled AVOD audience that adds digital ad inventory.
- **Affiliate-renewal pricing power** — Fox News and sports let Fox raise carriage fees faster than the bundle shrinks, the core of the affiliate cash flow.
- **Sports-rights monetization** — NFL, college football and MLB drive the highest ad rates; Super Bowl years (Fox airs it roughly every 3 years) are step-change ad events.
- **Political-ad cycles** — even-year elections (2026 US midterms) are a recurring, high-margin advertising tailwind for Fox News and the owned stations.
- **Recurring catalysts** — quarterly earnings (Aug / Nov / Feb / May), Fox One subscriber updates, NFL and Super Bowl ad cycles, affiliate-renewal negotiations, and Murdoch family / governance headlines.

## Bull Case vs Bear Case

### Bull case
- A durable, ratings-leading live-news (Fox News) and live-sports franchise — the content most resistant to cord-cutting and streaming substitution.
- Affiliate-fee pricing power lets carriage revenue keep rising even as the pay-TV base shrinks.
- Tubi has turned profitable and is growing fast; Fox One adds direct-to-consumer optionality without the cost of a full general-entertainment streaming war.
- Strong free cash flow funds aggressive buybacks; the low-teens multiple and disciplined capital return give a value-plus-buyback story.
- Even-year political advertising and Super Bowl years provide recurring, high-margin earnings tailwinds.

### Bear case
- Structural pay-TV subscriber decline can outpace affiliate price increases, eventually shrinking the cash engine.
- Sports-rights cost inflation (each NFL/college renewal resets costs higher) can compress margins faster than ad/affiliate growth.
- Fox One risks cannibalizing the high-margin affiliate bundle if cord-cutters substitute the cheaper DTC product.
- Murdoch family control and the non-voting nature of FOXA mean minority holders have little governance say; succession remains an overhang.
- Defamation litigation tail risk persists after the $787.5M Dominion settlement (2023).

## Key Risks

- **Cord-cutting vs affiliate fees** — the central secular risk: if pay-TV subscriber losses outrun per-subscriber carriage-fee increases, the affiliate engine stalls.
- **Sports-rights cost inflation** — escalating NFL, college-football and MLB rights costs can erode the economics that make live sports valuable.
- **Fox One cannibalization** — the DTC bundle could pull margin out of the affiliate bundle rather than purely add new revenue.
- **Governance / control** — dual-class structure, ~40% Murdoch voting block, and the non-voting FOXA line leave minority investors with limited influence; Murdoch succession is a standing question.
- **Litigation tail** — defamation exposure after the $787.5M Dominion settlement (2023).
- **Advertising cyclicality** — heavy reliance on cyclical ad spend (NFL seasons, elections) makes quarterly and annual comparisons lumpy.

## Valuation & How the Stock Trades

Fox trades at a **low-teens earnings multiple** (FOXA ~13.9x per the fundamentals below) — a *discount* media valuation that prices in secular pay-TV decline, **not** the premium afforded to recurring-revenue SaaS names. The debate is whether live news and sports decay slowly enough, and Tubi/Fox One scale fast enough, to defend the affiliate cash engine. Mechanically:

- **Dual-class pair trade** — **FOXA (non-voting)** vs **[[fox-corporation-(class-b)|FOX (voting)]]** is a classic share-class spread; the voting Class B has historically traded at a small *discount* to FOXA (FOXA ~13.9x vs FOX ~12.4x per the fundamentals), an inversion of the usual voting-premium that reflects FOXA's superior liquidity and index/passive demand.
- **Liquidity** — FOXA is the more liquid line and carries the more active [[options]] market; it is the line most index and passive flows touch.
- **Earnings behavior** — results are advertising-cyclical, stepping up in NFL/Super Bowl and US election years and softening in off-years; affiliate fees provide the steadier baseline.
- **Capital return** — strong free cash flow is returned primarily through **buybacks**; the cash dividend is modest (a token contributor to total yield per the fundamentals). Repurchases, not the dividend, are the capital-return story — and buybacks also tighten the Murdoch family's relative voting grip over time.
- **Index torque** — both share classes are [[s-and-p-500]] / Communication Services members, so passive flows touch both lines.
