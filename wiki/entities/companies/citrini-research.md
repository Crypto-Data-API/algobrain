Citrini Research is a financial research firm best known for publishing the **"2028 Global Intelligence Crisis"** scenario in February 2026 — an explicit tail-risk framework that posits 10%+ unemployment and roughly 40% equity decline if superintelligent AI deployment outpaces labor reabsorption (Source: [[2026-04-22-gap-finder-possible-ai-fueled-global-job-loss-reces]]).

## Overview

Citrini Research is an independent thematic equity and global macro research shop, publishing since early 2023 under the pseudonymous analyst "Citrini" (@Citrini7 on X). It rose to prominence by identifying thematic winners early — highlighting names such as Nvidia (NVDA), Eli Lilly (LLY), Eaton (ETN) and Credo (CRDO) — and translating narratives into purpose-built equity baskets tracked in its subscriber model portfolio, the **Citrindex** (security-level diversification with theme-level concentration, updated weekly). Published theme baskets have included Artificial Intelligence, Fiscal Primacy, Robotics, Med-Tech & Healthcare, Natural Gas, GLP-1s, Electronic Warfare and Biotech; the firm continues to publish regular "State of the Themes" updates (most recently June 2026).

Its 2026 work focused specifically on how AI labor displacement translates into priceable scenarios for equity markets, becoming an early institutional reference for traders sizing AI-driven tail risk (Source: [[2026-04-22-gap-finder-possible-ai-fueled-global-job-loss-reces]]).

## The "2028 Global Intelligence Crisis" Scenario

Citrini's flagship 2026 thesis posits a specific cascade:
- Superintelligent AI deployment occurs faster than the labor market can reabsorb displaced workers.
- Unemployment climbs above 10%.
- Aggregate consumer demand collapses.
- Equity markets re-price ~40% lower as earnings and multiples both contract.

The scenario is unique among 2026 sell-side and independent research in that it is **named, quantified, and probability-weighted**, making it directly tradable as a tail-risk scenario rather than a generic narrative.

For the full scenario page, see [[citrini-2028-global-intelligence-crisis]].

## February 2026 Tech Selloff

The publication of the "2028 Global Intelligence Crisis" scenario triggered the early-February 2026 tech selloff. Service sectors bore the brunt — finance, real estate, media, medical services, and legal services all fell alongside software (Source: [[2026-04-22-gap-finder-possible-ai-fueled-global-job-loss-reces]]).

It was the first organized AI tail-risk scenario to gain institutional distribution, and several major sell-side firms followed with their own variants of the framework through 2026.

For the full event page, see [[2026-02-citrini-tech-selloff]].

## Why Traders Care

- **Tail-risk pricing reference**: Citrini's named scenario gives institutional desks a concrete distribution to plug into VaR and scenario PnL tools rather than handwaving "AI risk."
- **Sector signal**: the Feb 2026 selloff demonstrated which sectors the market believes are AI-displaceable (legal, finance, media, real estate, medical) — useful for short-basket construction.
- **Macro-thematic positioning**: the scenario directly supports the [[ai-sector-rotation-energy-hedge]] (energy +12% YTD as tech weakened) and other hedge structures.
- **Research-driven volatility**: Citrini is now on the watch-list of single-issuer research that can move price; treating its publications as event risk is prudent for short-vol books.
