Citadel Securities is the dominant [[market-maker]] in US equity markets, handling approximately 40% of all US retail equity order flow and roughly 25% of all US equity volume. It is a separate legal entity from the Citadel hedge fund, though both were founded by Ken Griffin. The firm is a critical piece of modern [[market-microstructure]] infrastructure.

## Overview

Founded in 2002 and headquartered in Miami (relocated from Chicago in 2022), Citadel Securities operates as a designated market maker on the [[nyse|NYSE]], [[nasdaq|Nasdaq]], and multiple US options exchanges. The firm executes trades in equities, options, ETFs, fixed income, and other asset classes. It has grown to become the single largest equity market maker globally, processing billions of dollars in trades daily.

## Business Model

Citadel Securities profits from the [[bid-ask-spread]] by continuously quoting prices on both sides of the market. The firm pays brokers like Robinhood, TD Ameritrade, and others for the right to execute their retail order flow -- a practice known as [[payment-for-order-flow]] (PFOF). Retail flow is attractive because it is generally "uninformed" (not driven by institutional information advantages), meaning it carries lower [[adverse-selection]] risk for the market maker.

## Trading Relevance

Understanding Citadel Securities' behavior is important for active traders:

- **Execution quality**: PFOF arrangements mean retail orders are routed to Citadel rather than lit exchanges. Citadel claims to provide price improvement over the national best bid/offer (NBBO), but the extent of this improvement is debated
- **Slippage patterns**: During high [[volatility]] events, market maker withdrawal can widen [[bid-ask-spread|spreads]] dramatically. Knowing that a single firm handles ~40% of retail flow reveals the concentration risk in execution infrastructure
- **Dark pool routing**: Citadel operates its own dark pool, and a significant portion of retail equity trading occurs off-exchange. This affects [[price-discovery]] and the information content of lit market volume
- **Options market making**: Citadel Securities is a major options market maker, influencing bid-ask spreads on equity options. Their hedging activity (particularly [[gamma]] hedging) contributes to intraday index movements

## 2025–2026 Developments

- **Record 2025**: trading revenue hit a record ~$12.2 billion in 2025, up roughly 25% on the prior full-year record, as elevated volatility and retail activity fattened market-making spreads.
- **Record Q1 2026**: ~$4.3 billion of trading revenue in Q1 2026 (vs ~$3.4 billion in Q1 2025) — the firm's best quarter ever.
- **Asset-class expansion**: continued build-out beyond US equities into rates (US Treasuries), credit, FX and commodities, plus a dedicated Digital Assets team positioning the firm as a crypto liquidity provider on venues including Coinbase, Binance and Crypto.com.
- **Ownership/valuation context**: minority investments by Sequoia and Paradigm in early 2022 valued the firm around $22 billion; no IPO has been announced as of June 2026.

## How Traders Get Exposure

Citadel Securities is private — there is no direct equity exposure. Indirect angles: its results are a proxy for market-making economics (read-across to listed peers [[virtu-financial]] (VIRT) and exchanges like [[cboe]]/CME); its PFOF relationships shape execution quality at retail brokers ([[robinhood]] HOOD); and any future IPO would be a landmark liquidity event for the sector.

## Regulatory Scrutiny

Citadel Securities has faced ongoing regulatory attention regarding PFOF practices, market concentration, and potential conflicts of interest. The SEC under Gary Gensler proposed rules to increase competition for retail order flow through order-by-order auctions, which would directly affect Citadel's core business model.
