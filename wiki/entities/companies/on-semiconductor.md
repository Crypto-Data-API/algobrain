ON Semiconductor — branded **onsemi** (NASDAQ: ON) — is a power and sensing semiconductor maker, the leading Western supplier of silicon-carbide (SiC) power devices for electric vehicles and a top automotive image-sensor vendor. It is a constituent of the [[s-and-p-500]] index and one of the highest-beta plays on the EV/auto-semis cycle.

## Business Overview & Segments

Spun out of Motorola in 1999, onsemi runs a power-and-sensing franchise. Roughly half of revenue is automotive and ~a quarter industrial. Under CEO Hassane El-Khoury (since 2020) the company shifted to a **fab-lighter, margin-first** model — exiting low-margin legacy parts, converting the East Fishkill fab, and building silicon-carbide (SiC) capacity (Bucheon, Korea; Czech expansion).

| Group | What it is | Role in the story |
|---|---|---|
| **Power Solutions (PSG)** | Discretes, modules, and **SiC** (EliteSiC) — major EV traction-inverter design wins incl. Tesla and VW Group | The growth and differentiation engine; SiC is the headline thesis and the new AI-rack-power vector |
| **Analog & Mixed-Signal (AMG)** | Power-management and signal-chain analog | Steady, broad-catalog analog content |
| **Intelligent Sensing (ISG)** | Automotive CMOS image sensors for ADAS | Leading auto image-sensor position; rides ADAS content growth |

The strategy is to be the leading **Western SiC supplier** for vehicle electrification while extending into a new demand pool: **48V/SiC power delivery for AI data-center racks**, where power-conversion content is exploding. The margin-first reset (fewer, higher-value parts; SiC vertical integration from substrate to module) is meant to lift through-cycle gross margins above the legacy commodity-power base.

## 2025-2026 Developments

- **FY2025 revenue ~$5.99B, down ~15% YoY** — a deep auto/industrial inventory correction year; gross margins compressed from the 2022-23 peak as utilization fell, and the company took restructuring actions (~2,400 jobs cut across 2025).
- **Allegro MicroSystems bid withdrawn (April 2025):** onsemi pulled its **$6.9B ($35.10/sh) hostile-leaning offer** for sensor maker Allegro after finding "no actionable path forward" — read positively for capital discipline.
- **Q4 2025 (reported Feb 2026):** in-line revenue but soft 2026 outlook initially sent the stock lower; **Q1 2026 then beat forecasts** and the stock rallied as auto inventory digestion ended and AI data-center power (48V/SiC for AI racks) emerged as a new demand vector.
- Strategic push into AI data-center power delivery and continued SiC share gains vs. STMicro/Infineon and Chinese entrants.

## Economic Moat & Competitive Position

onsemi's [[economic-moat|moat]] is narrower and more cyclical than a software or broad-analog franchise, and rests on process leadership plus design-in stickiness:

- **SiC vertical integration** — onsemi controls SiC from substrate to device to module, a scale and yield advantage few Western rivals match; SiC is harder to manufacture than silicon, raising the barrier.
- **Automotive design-in lock-in** — traction-inverter and image-sensor sockets are qualified years ahead and locked for a vehicle's production life; switching imposes requalification cost and program risk ([[switching-costs]]).
- **Margin-first portfolio reset** — exiting commodity parts and concentrating on differentiated power/sensing structurally lifts margins and reduces exposure to the most commoditized, price-competitive segments.
- **Image-sensor leadership** — a top position in automotive CMOS sensors for ADAS, a content-growth niche.

The moat is **real but contested and cyclical**: SiC faces aggressive **price competition from Chinese entrants** and from STMicro/Infineon; customer concentration (Tesla) is a swing factor; and gross-margin recovery depends on utilization, which depends on the auto/industrial cycle. onsemi is one of the highest-beta expressions of that cycle.

## Growth Drivers & Catalysts

- **Vehicle electrification / SiC** — EV traction inverters and onboard chargers drive SiC content; share gains vs STMicro/Infineon are the core thesis.
- **AI data-center power** — 48V/SiC power delivery for AI racks is the new, non-auto demand vector that re-rated the stock in early 2026.
- **ADAS image sensing** — rising sensor count per vehicle as autonomy levels increase.
- **Industrial recovery** — a cyclical rebound off the 2025 inventory trough lifts utilization and margins.
- **Margin reset** — the fab-lighter, exit-commodity strategy structurally raises through-cycle profitability.
- **Recurring catalysts** — quarterly guidance (utilization/gross-margin trajectory is the key metric), EV demand datapoints, SiC contract wins/losses, AI power-delivery announcements, and restructuring updates.

## Bull Case vs Bear Case

### Bull case
- Leading Western SiC supplier into a multi-year EV electrification wave, with vertical integration as a cost/yield edge.
- AI data-center power (48V/SiC) opens a second, non-auto growth pool and diversifies the cycle.
- Margin-first reset structurally lifts through-cycle gross margins above the legacy base.
- 2025 inventory correction appears to have bottomed; Q1 2026 beat suggests the upswing is starting; capital discipline shown by walking away from Allegro.

### Bear case
- One of the highest-beta names in the complex — deep cyclicality (the FY2020 trough in the trend below shows how far margins can fall).
- SiC price competition from China and from STMicro/Infineon can compress the marquee growth market's margins.
- Customer concentration (Tesla) and EV-demand air-pockets threaten the core thesis.
- Gross-margin recovery is utilization-dependent; a stalled cycle de-rates a high-beta name hard.

## Key Risks

- **EV / auto cyclicality** — demand air-pockets and inventory corrections drive large swings; utilization is the key margin lever.
- **SiC price competition** — Chinese entrants and STMicro/Infineon pressure SiC pricing in the core growth market.
- **Customer concentration** — Tesla and a few large auto programs are swing factors.
- **Margin recovery risk** — gross-margin rebound stalling if the cycle disappoints or pricing erodes.
- **Execution** — the fab-conversion and margin-first reset are multi-year and execution-dependent.

## Valuation & How the Stock Trades

onsemi trades as a **high-beta, cyclical power-semis name** at a mid-cyclical multiple (~32x trailing, lower on normalized earnings) — the market prices the EV/SiC and AI-power optionality against deep cyclicality. Mechanically:

- **EV/auto-semis beta** — the high-beta expression of the auto-semi cycle: moves on EV sales data, Tesla/legacy-OEM EV plans, and SiC pricing news; correlated with [[nxp-semiconductors]], [[analog-devices]], [[texas-instruments]], STMicro, Infineon, and Wolfspeed (the SiC distress watch).
- **Big earnings moves** — very liquid options chain with consistently high IV for a megacap-adjacent semi; historical earnings-day moves of ±8-15%, with utilization/gross-margin guidance driving the reaction.
- **Cycle re-rating** — the multiple expands at the trough (depressed earnings) and the AI-power vector added a fresh growth narrative in early 2026.
- **Capital return** — onsemi pays **no dividend**; capital return is via buybacks, and the franchise reinvests heavily in SiC capacity. Total-return-via-cyclical-growth, not yield.
- **Beta** — high; among the more volatile S&P 500 semiconductors.
