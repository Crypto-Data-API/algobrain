Allegro MicroSystems, Inc. (NASDAQ: ALGM) is a fabless designer of magnetic sensor ICs (Hall-effect and TMR current/position sensors) and analog power ICs, with roughly three-quarters of sales into automotive — especially xEV traction systems and ADAS — plus growing industrial, data-center power and robotics exposure. For traders it is a small/mid-cap auto-semis cyclical (~$3–5B market cap, mid-2026) that was the subject of a withdrawn 2025 takeover bid from ON Semiconductor, leaving persistent M&A optionality in the name.

## Business Overview

- **Products:** magnetic field sensors (market leader in automotive magnetic sensing), current sensors, motor-driver and power-management ICs. Content-per-vehicle rises with electrification (battery management, inverters) and ADAS.
- **History/ownership:** founded 1990 as a unit of Japan's Sanken Electric; IPO on Nasdaq in October 2020. Sanken was long the majority holder (~51%); Allegro repurchased ~38.8M shares from Sanken under a July 2024 agreement (Sanken also sold ~$232M of stock in a 2024 secondary), reducing Sanken to a large minority stake — its position remains a key overhang/catalyst for the float.
- **Footprint:** fabless/asset-light; HQ Manchester, New Hampshire; design centres in the US, Europe and Asia.

## Business Segments / Products

Allegro splits broadly between **magnetic sensing** and **power**, with automotive the dominant end market:

| Product family | What it is | Where it's used | Trading character |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Magnetic sensor ICs** | Hall-effect and **TMR** position/speed/current sensors | xEV motors, ADAS, steering, transmissions, industrial motion | Market leadership; content rises with electrification & ADAS |
| **Current sensors** | Isolated/in-line current measurement | EV inverters, battery management, data-center power | High-growth; tied to electrification and AI-server power |
| **Power ICs** | Motor drivers and power-management ICs | Automotive subsystems, industrial, data center | Cyclical analog; benefits from content growth |

By end market, **automotive is ~three-quarters of sales** (xEV traction + ADAS the growth drivers), with **industrial & other** (including a fast-growing **data-center power** slice) the diversifier. The fabless model keeps the business asset-light and capex-disciplined relative to integrated peers.

Allegro is the leader in automotive magnetic sensing but competes across analog/sensing/power against far larger broad-line players:

Allegro's moat is **decades of automotive magnetic-sensing IP, design-in incumbency, and qualified automotive supply relationships**; its risk is competing for share against [[texas-instruments]] and [[infineon]] scale, plus single-source customer cyclicality in autos.

## Bull vs Bear Case

**Bull case**

- Secular content-per-vehicle growth from xEV electrification and ADAS — more sensors and current/power ICs per car regardless of unit volumes.
- Market leadership in automotive magnetic sensing with sticky design-ins.
- Diversification into industrial and fast-growing **data-center power** (a record 14% of Q4 FY2026 sales).
- Persistent **M&A optionality** after the onsemi approach.
- Fabless, asset-light model.

**Bear case**

- ~3/4 automotive — high exposure to auto production rates and China auto demand.
- Cyclical analog/auto-semis: subject to inventory corrections (FY2025 was the trough).
- Sanken's residual stake is an **overhang** — secondary sales pressure the float.
- Competes against much larger [[texas-instruments]]/[[infineon]] in power and sensing.
- Takeover premium can deflate if M&A chatter fades.

## Valuation Framework (qualitative)

Allegro is valued as a **content-growth auto-semis cyclical with embedded M&A optionality**:

- **Design-win ramp / content per vehicle:** the structural driver is rising sensor and current/power-IC content per xEV and ADAS platform — content growth can outrun auto unit volumes.
- **End-market mix (auto / industrial / data-center):** the bull case is industrial and data-center power growing faster than the auto base and lifting the through-cycle multiple.
- **Semi-cycle / inventory beta:** as an analog/auto cyclical, ALGM amplifies the auto/industrial inventory cycle — FY2025 was the trough, FY2026 the recovery.
- **M&A optionality:** the withdrawn onsemi bid anchors a takeover-speculation premium; valuation carries an event component on top of fundamentals.
- **Ownership overhang:** Sanken stake reduction is both a clean-up catalyst and a float overhang that can cap upside near secondaries.

## 2025–2026 Developments

- **ON Semiconductor bid withdrawn (14 Apr 2025):** onsemi had proposed an all-cash acquisition at $35.10/share (~$6.9B; a 57% premium to the 28 Feb 2025 close) after media reports of interest surfaced in early March 2025. Allegro's board rebuffed the approach and onsemi withdrew, saying there was "no actionable path forward." The stock retains a takeover-speculation premium on auto-semis consolidation chatter.
- **FY2026 results (fiscal year ended March 2026):** sales $890.1M, +23% YoY, with non-GAAP EPS more than doubling to $0.54. Q4 FY2026 sales $243.2M, +26% YoY — a fifth consecutive quarter of sequential growth. Automotive +17%, industrial & other +38%; data-center products hit a record 14% of Q4 sales.
- **Cycle position:** FY2025 (ended Mar 2025) was the trough of the auto/industrial inventory correction; FY2026 marked a broad recovery led by xEV, ADAS and data-center power.
