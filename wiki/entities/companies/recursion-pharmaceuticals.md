Recursion Pharmaceuticals, Inc. (NASDAQ: RXRX) is a clinical-stage "TechBio" company that pairs large-scale automated biology experiments with [[artificial-intelligence|AI]] and machine learning to industrialise drug discovery. For traders it is one of the most liquid pure-play AI-drug-discovery names: a high-beta, narrative-driven small/mid cap whose share price swings on platform-validation milestones, pharma partnership payments, and cash-runway updates rather than on current earnings (it remains loss-making).

## AI Involvement

Recursion runs the "Recursion OS", an [[artificial-intelligence|AI]] operating system built on a proprietary dataset of trillions of biological and chemical relationships generated in its own wet-lab automation foundries. The platform maps cellular phenotypes, predicts drug-target interactions, and prioritises candidates. The company sits at the centre of the AI-in-biology theme alongside [[tempus-ai]] and [[schrödinger]] (and the Recursion-acquired Exscientia), and partners with NVIDIA on compute and foundation models.

## Business Overview

Recursion does not yet market an approved drug; revenue is principally collaboration and milestone payments from large-pharma partners (notably Sanofi, Roche/Genentech and Bayer) plus drug-discovery services. In 2024 it acquired UK AI-drug-design firm Exscientia, consolidating two leading AI-discovery platforms. Its valuation rests on (1) the breadth and quality of its proprietary biology dataset, (2) the productivity of its internal and partnered pipeline, and (3) its ability to convert AI predictions into validated clinical assets.

## 2025-2026 Developments

- **FY2025 results (reported 25 Feb 2026):** full-year revenue of about **$74.7 million**; year-end cash of roughly **$754 million**, with management guiding a runway into **early 2028** (Verified via Perplexity (sonar), 2026-06-10).
- **First clinical validation:** in the Feb 2026 update Recursion reported what it described as the first clinical validation of its AI operating system in **familial adenomatous polyposis (FAP)** — lead asset **REC-4881** showed a **43% median reduction in total polyp burden after 12 weeks** with **~75% of patients responding** in Phase 2 proof-of-concept work (Verified via Perplexity (sonar), 2026-06-10).
- **Sanofi milestone:** reached its **fifth program milestone with Sanofi**, taking cumulative Sanofi payments to about **$134 million**; cumulative partnership cash inflows have exceeded **$500 million**.
- **Pipeline reshaping (2025):** discontinued several legacy programs and acquired full rights to **REV102** (hypophosphatasia) from Rallybio, concentrating capital on higher-conviction assets (REC-4881, REC-617/CDK7, RBM39).
- Market capitalisation was around **$1.9 billion** in early 2026.

## Competitive Positioning / Peers

Recursion is among the most liquid pure-play "TechBio" / AI-drug-discovery names. The peer set spans dedicated AI-bio companies and the in-house AI efforts of big pharma.

| Company | Ticker | Approach | Differentiation | Relation to Recursion |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Recursion | RXRX | Phenomics + automated wet-lab + AI (Recursion OS) | Proprietary in-house experimental data at scale | — |
| [[schrödinger]] | SDGR | Physics-based computational chemistry + AI | Software licensing + internal pipeline | Closest listed peer; complementary physics-first approach |
| [[relay-therapeutics]] | RLAY | Motion-based structural drug design (dynamics) | Protein-motion simulation | Computational-discovery peer |
| [[tempus-ai]] | TEM | Clinical/genomic data + AI diagnostics | Real-world clinical data scale | Adjacent AI-healthcare peer |
| Big-pharma AI (e.g. Roche, Sanofi, AstraZeneca, plus [[alphabet]]'s Isomorphic Labs) | — | In-house / partnered AI discovery | Balance-sheet scale, proprietary clinical data | Both partners and competitors |

- **Versus [[schrödinger]]:** Schrödinger leads with physics-based simulation and a software-licensing revenue line; Recursion leads with massive proprietary experimental ("wet-lab") data. The two approaches are increasingly converging.
- **Versus [[relay-therapeutics]]:** Relay focuses on protein motion/dynamics; Recursion is broader and more data/throughput-driven.
- **Versus big-pharma AI:** large incumbents have data and balance-sheet scale; Recursion's edge is purpose-built throughput and an integrated platform, but it must keep proving the platform converts to clinical wins.

## Bull vs Bear Case

**Bull case**
- First reported clinical validation of an AI-discovery OS (REC-4881 in FAP, per page) supports the platform thesis.
- Deep-pocketed pharma partnerships (Sanofi, Roche/Genentech, Bayer) provide non-dilutive cash and external validation (>$500M cumulative inflows, per page).
- Strong balance sheet (year-end cash ~$754M, runway into early 2028, per page) buys time to mature the pipeline.
- Exscientia acquisition consolidated two leading AI-discovery platforms; NVIDIA compute relationship.
- Optionality on the secular AI-in-biology theme; high-beta upside in risk-on AI rotations.

**Bear case**
- Still loss-making with modest revenue (FY2025 ~$74.7M, per page) versus a multi-billion market cap — a narrative/platform multiple that can compress fast.
- AI-discovery remains unproven at scale; one early PoC does not establish a repeatable, productized edge.
- Burn-dependent: dilution and runway are the dominant fundamental risks.
- Big-pharma in-house AI and well-funded private rivals could erode the moat.
- High sentiment beta — sells off hard in AI risk-off rotations.

## Valuation Framework

Recursion sits between a software/platform story and a clinical biotech, so it is valued on a blend of qualitative levers:

- **Cash runway** — quarters of funding at current burn (per page: ~$754M cash, runway into early 2028); the gap to the next raise.
- **Platform/data multiple** — investors pay for the breadth and quality of the proprietary dataset and the OS, not current earnings; this multiple is sentiment-sensitive.
- **Partnership economics** — upfront, milestone, and royalty cash from Sanofi/Roche/Bayer offsets burn and validates the platform (cumulative >$500M inflows, per page).
- **Risk-adjusted pipeline value (rNPV)** — probability-weighted value of internal assets (REC-4881, REC-617, etc.).
- **Binary readout asymmetry** — clinical PoC and partnership milestones reprice the stock.
- **Dilution sensitivity** — share-count growth directly dilutes per-share value.

## Capital Structure & Dilution Risk

Recursion is pre-profit and cash-burn-dependent; despite a strong cash position (~$754M, per page) it funds a platform-plus-pipeline model that requires continued investment. Partnership inflows partly offset burn, but equity raises remain a recurring risk and new supply can cap rallies. The Exscientia acquisition was stock-funded, illustrating how M&A and financing both affect share count. Watch runway versus burn and milestone cadence for the timing of any future raise.

## Risks

- **Platform-proof risk:** the central debate is whether AI discovery produces a repeatable, durable clinical edge; failure to convert would compress the platform multiple.
- **Dilution/runway risk:** pre-profit cash burn; equity raises recur.
- **Partnership concentration:** reliance on a few large-pharma partners; renegotiation or termination would hurt.
- **Competitive risk:** big-pharma in-house AI and well-funded rivals.
- **Clinical risk:** internal pipeline assets face standard trial attrition.
- **Sentiment/beta risk:** high-beta proxy for AI-in-biology sentiment; severe drawdowns in AI risk-off.
