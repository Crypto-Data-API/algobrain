Revvity (NYSE: RVTY) is a life-sciences and diagnostics company formed in 2023 when [[perkinelmer|PerkinElmer]] divested its applied/food/enterprise services businesses and renamed the remaining health-science core. It is a constituent of the [[s-and-p-500]] index in the [[healthcare|Health Care]] sector, and matters to traders as a mid-cap proxy for biopharma R&D spending and diagnostics demand, with meaningful China exposure. It competes in the same "picks-and-shovels" life-science-tools arena as larger peers [[thermo-fisher-scientific]], [[danaher]] and [[agilent-technologies]].

## Business Overview & Segments

Revvity is a focused life-science-tools and diagnostics company — a smaller, higher-growth-aspiration cousin of [[thermo-fisher-scientific]] and [[danaher]] after shedding PerkinElmer's lower-margin applied/food/enterprise units. It reports two segments. The qualitative mix below is indicative, not a precise current breakdown — see the data disclaimer in Fundamentals.

| Segment | What it is | Mix (qualitative) | End markets |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Life Sciences** | Reagents, instruments and software for pharma/biotech discovery, incl. the **Signals** informatics platform | Roughly half of revenue | Pharma/biotech R&D, academia |
| **Diagnostics** | Newborn screening, immunodiagnostics (via **EUROIMMUN**), applied genomics | Roughly half of revenue | Clinical labs, hospitals, governments |

Customers span pharmaceutical and biotech companies, diagnostic labs, academia and governments. 2025 revenue was approximately $2.9 billion with roughly 11,000 employees. The company traces its corporate lineage to PerkinElmer, founded in 1937. The recurring **reagents + software** mix (versus one-time instrument sales) underpins margin quality and is central to the bull case.

## Economic Moat & Competitive Position

Revvity holds a **narrow** [[economic-moat|economic moat]] characteristic of the life-science-tools sector, though narrower than its larger scaled peers:

- **Razor-and-blade consumables** — placed instruments and assays (newborn-screening kits, EUROIMMUN immunoassays, reagents) drive recurring, sticky consumable revenue.
- **Switching costs in validated workflows** — once a clinical lab or pharma workflow is validated on a platform, regulatory and revalidation costs make switching painful.
- **Software lock-in** — the **Signals** informatics platform embeds Revvity into customer R&D data workflows.
- **Regulatory barriers in diagnostics** — newborn screening and immunodiagnostics carry clinical/regulatory approvals that deter entrants.

The moat is **real but sub-scale**: Revvity lacks the breadth and distribution flywheel of [[thermo-fisher-scientific]] or [[danaher]], so it is more exposed to single-end-market swings (academic funding, China VBP pricing). The thesis is that a focused, higher-margin portfolio can grow faster than the lumbering giants.

## Growth Drivers & Catalysts

- **Biopharma R&D recovery** — Life Sciences is a derivative of pharma/biotech discovery spending; a funding rebound is the key swing factor.
- **Recurring reagents + software mix** — shifting revenue toward consumables and the Signals platform supports margins and durability.
- **Diagnostics franchises** — newborn screening and EUROIMMUN immunodiagnostics provide steadier, regulated demand.
- **China immunodiagnostics divestiture** — exiting a value-based-procurement-pressured business removes a margin/geopolitical overhang.
- **Margin expansion** — post-PerkinElmer portfolio focus on higher-margin life-science tools.
- **Recurring catalysts** — quarterly earnings and guidance credibility, NIH/academic-funding headlines, biopharma capex commentary from peers, and progress on the China exit.

## 2025-2026 Developments

- **Q4 2025 (reported 2026-02-02):** revenue of $772M and adjusted EPS of $1.70, both above expectations; raised 2026 outlook to $2.96-2.99B revenue and $5.35-5.45 adjusted EPS.
- **Q1 2026 (reported 2026-05-05):** revenue $711M (pro forma $687M), adjusted EPS $1.06 (pro forma $1.04).
- **China divestiture (announced 2026-05-05):** strategic decision to divest the Immunodiagnostics business in China, which represented about 6% of 2025 revenue — reduces a key geopolitical/VBP-pricing overhang.

## Bull Case vs Bear Case

### Bull case
- A focused, higher-margin post-PerkinElmer portfolio can grow faster than the scaled giants.
- Recurring reagents + Signals software mix underpins durable, sticky revenue.
- The China immunodiagnostics exit removes a geopolitical and pricing overhang.
- A biopharma-R&D funding recovery is a direct top-line tailwind.

### Bear case
- A rich ~47x multiple leaves no margin for guidance misses.
- Sub-scale versus [[thermo-fisher-scientific]] and [[danaher]] — less breadth to absorb single-end-market shocks.
- Heavy exposure to academic/NIH funding and biopharma capex, both cyclical.
- Weak Fred-framework metrics (low ROE/ROA, sub-10% net margin) flag execution/return concerns.

## Key Risks

- **Academic & biopharma funding cyclicality** — Life Sciences demand tracks NIH/academic budgets and pharma R&D capex, both volatile.
- **Valuation risk** — a ~47x multiple amplifies the downside of any guidance miss.
- **China / geopolitical** — value-based procurement (VBP) pricing and trade tensions; the immunodiagnostics divestiture mitigates but does not eliminate exposure.
- **Clinical & regulatory** — diagnostics approvals and reimbursement are binary risk vectors. *(Clinical/diagnostic outcomes are inherently uncertain; nothing here predicts approval or reimbursement results.)*
- **Integration/portfolio execution** — delivering the higher-growth, higher-margin thesis post-PerkinElmer.
- **FX** — global revenue base.
