Eli Lilly (NYSE: LLY) is the world's most valuable pharmaceutical company and the dominant player in the [[glp-1]] incretin franchise via Mounjaro (tirzepatide, diabetes) and Zepbound (tirzepatide, obesity). It is the largest [[healthcare]] weight in the [[s-and-p-500]] and the bellwether for the entire obesity-drug trade — arguably the single most important non-tech megacap growth story of 2024-2026. Founded in Indianapolis in 1876, Lilly has transformed from a steady, dividend-paying legacy-diabetes maker into a high-growth name priced as a growth stock rather than a defensive pharma.

## Business Overview & Segments

Lilly reports as a single pharmaceutical segment, but its revenue is best understood as a set of therapeutic franchises. The cardiometabolic/incretin franchise — Mounjaro and Zepbound, both branded tirzepatide (a dual GIP/[[glp-1]] receptor agonist) — is the overwhelming growth engine, taking share from [[novo-nordisk]]'s Ozempic/Wegovy on superior weight-loss efficacy. Everything else is a diversified, cash-generative base that funds the incretin build-out.

| Franchise | Key products | Indication(s) | Revenue-driver role |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Cardiometabolic / incretins** | Mounjaro, Zepbound (tirzepatide) | Type-2 diabetes; obesity / weight management | Primary growth engine; the bulk of incremental revenue and the entire bull thesis |
| **Oncology** | Verzenio (abemaciclib) | HR+/HER2− breast cancer | Established blockbuster; steady contributor with broadening label/pipeline |
| **Neuroscience** | Kisunla ([[donanemab]]) | Early symptomatic Alzheimer's disease | Newer launch; optionality on a large under-treated population |
| **Immunology** | Taltz, Omvoh, Ebglyss | Psoriasis/PsA; ulcerative colitis & Crohn's; atopic dermatitis | Diversified specialty-care growth, less binary than incretins |
| **Legacy diabetes** | Trulicity, Humalog, Jardiance¹ | Type-2 diabetes | Mature, declining/flat base being cannibalised by tirzepatide internally |

¹ Jardiance is partnered with Boehringer Ingelheim. Supply build-out — multi-billion-dollar incretin API plants across the US (Indiana, North Carolina) and Europe — has been a core part of the thesis, since manufacturing capacity, not demand, has been the binding constraint on incretin revenue.

The pipeline centerpiece is **[[orforglipron]]**, a once-daily **oral** small-molecule [[glp-1]] — no injection, no cold chain, mass-manufacturable at pill scale — which would dramatically widen the addressable obesity market if approved, reaching primary-care prescribers and supply-constrained ex-US markets that injectables cannot. Behind it sits **retatrutide**, a triple GIP/GLP-1/glucagon agonist in Phase 3 that has shown even larger weight-loss in mid-stage trials.

## Economic Moat & Competitive Position

Lilly carries a wide [[economic-moat]] anchored in several reinforcing advantages:

- **Patent protection & IP** — Tirzepatide composition-of-matter and formulation patents protect Mounjaro/Zepbound well into the next decade, and orforglipron extends the franchise with fresh, distinct IP. The eventual [[patent-cliff]] on tirzepatide is the long-term moat-erosion risk, but it is years out.
- **Manufacturing scale as a real moat** — Incretin peptide/API capacity is genuinely scarce. Lilly's multi-year, multi-billion-dollar plant build-out is itself a competitive barrier: a rival with an approved drug but no fill-finish capacity cannot capture share. This is an unusually physical, capital-intensive moat for pharma.
- **R&D engine** — A productive incretin discovery platform (tirzepatide → orforglipron → retatrutide) demonstrates repeatable innovation rather than a single lucky asset.
- **Brand & physician relationships** — Deep endocrinology/primary-care detailing relationships and strong brand recognition for Mounjaro/Zepbound create [[switching-costs]] and prescriber inertia.
- **Regulatory barriers** — FDA approval, manufacturing qualification, and outcomes data are high entry hurdles that deter all but the largest, best-capitalised competitors.

The defining structural feature of the market is the **[[glp-1]] duopoly** with [[novo-nordisk]]: two incumbents control the overwhelming majority of incretin revenue, with Lilly currently holding the efficacy edge.

## Growth Drivers & Catalysts

- **[[orforglipron]] approval & launch** — The single largest catalyst on the calendar. An oral GLP-1 unlocks primary-care scripts and supply-constrained markets injectables can't serve.
- **Incretin volume + capacity ramp** — Revenue has been gated by manufacturing, not demand; each plant coming online converts directly into sales.
- **Label expansions** — Cardiovascular-outcomes data, obstructive sleep apnea, MASH (metabolic dysfunction-associated steatohepatitis), heart failure, and chronic kidney disease broaden the reimbursable population and strengthen payer coverage.
- **Retatrutide pipeline** — Phase 3 triple agonist with best-in-class weight-loss potential; a successful readout extends the franchise's efficacy lead.
- **Weekly script data** — IQVIA weekly prescription data for Mounjaro/Zepbound is watched as a near-real-time read on demand and is itself a recurring intra-quarter catalyst.

## Bull Case vs Bear Case

### Bull case

- Structural [[glp-1]] duopoly with an efficacy edge over [[novo-nordisk]]; obesity is one of the largest addressable markets in pharma history.
- [[orforglipron]] could expand the market by an order of magnitude by reaching pill-preferring and supply-constrained patients.
- Manufacturing capacity — the true bottleneck — is being solved, converting latent demand into revenue.
- Repeatable incretin R&D engine (tirzepatide → orforglipron → retatrutide) reduces single-asset risk.
- 2026 guidance implies ~30% growth at megacap scale — exceptionally rare.

### Bear case

- Enormous forward growth is already in the price; any disappointment triggers multiple compression from a near-100x trailing base.
- Competition intensifies — [[novo-nordisk]] oral semaglutide, [[amgen]] MariTide, Roche/Pfizer entrants, and low-cost Chinese GLP-1s.
- US drug-pricing policy (IRA negotiation, political pressure on GLP-1 list prices) could cap economics.
- Manufacturing or supply missteps directly cost revenue given the capacity-gated model.
- Trial-readout binary risk: a single failed or disappointing Phase 3 (orforglipron, retatrutide, label expansions) can re-rate the stock sharply.

## Key Risks

- **Valuation / multiple compression** — Forward growth is already priced in; LLY trades at a steep premium and a growth-rate miss compresses the multiple hard.
- **Competition** — [[novo-nordisk]] oral semaglutide, [[amgen]] MariTide, Roche/Pfizer obesity programs, and Chinese GLP-1 developers undercutting on price.
- **Manufacturing / supply** — Capacity-gated revenue makes any plant or fill-finish misstep an immediate top-line problem.
- **US drug-pricing policy** — IRA price negotiation and political scrutiny of GLP-1 list prices are a persistent overhang.
- **[[patent-cliff]]** — Tirzepatide loses exclusivity longer-term; orforglipron and retatrutide must carry the franchise across the cliff.
- **Trial-readout binary risk** — Major Phase 3 readouts are binary events that can move the stock double-digit percentages overnight.

## Valuation & How the Stock Trades

LLY trades at a steep premium to pharma peers (trailing P/E near 100x on the API's FY2024 base, far lower on forward 2026 EPS); the market prices it as a **growth stock, not a defensive pharma**. Key trading characteristics:

- **Index torque** — Largest [[healthcare]] component of the [[s-and-p-500]]; big enough that LLY earnings move the sector ETF (XLV) measurably.
- **Beta** — Moderate (well below a high-beta tech name); the stock is more idiosyncratic-catalyst-driven than market-beta-driven.
- **[[options]]** — Extremely liquid chain; persistently elevated implied [[volatility]] around data events. Popular structures include call spreads into binary readouts and IV-crush trades after FDA decisions.
- **Dividend** — A consistent dividend grower, a legacy of its blue-chip pharma heritage, though the yield is modest given the growth-stock multiple.
- **Pairs/relative** — Frequently traded against [[novo-nordisk]] as the two-name expression of the GLP-1 theme; readouts at either name move both.

## 2025-2026 Developments

- **Q4 2025 blowout (reported 2026-02-04)** — Revenue rose **43% y/y to $19.3 billion** on Mounjaro/Zepbound volume; non-GAAP EPS +42% to $7.54. Full-year 2025 revenue was approximately $63 billion (approximate, up roughly 40% y/y).
- **2026 guidance** — Revenue of **$80-83 billion** and non-GAAP EPS of **$33.50-35.00** — implying another ~30% growth year, exceptional at this scale.
- **Orforglipron filed** — Phase 3 ATTAIN/ACHIEVE programs read out in 2025 (double-digit weight loss with oral dosing); Lilly submitted orforglipron for obesity to the US FDA and Japanese regulators, with potential launch in 2026. Approval/launch is the biggest single catalyst on the calendar.
- **Valuation regime** — LLY trades at a steep premium to pharma peers (trailing P/E near 100x on the API's FY2024 base, far lower on forward 2026 EPS); the market prices it as a growth stock, not a defensive pharma.

## Trading Relevance

- **The obesity trade's anchor** — LLY and [[novo-nordisk]] move on each other's trial data; readouts ([[orforglipron]], retatrutide, CagriSema at Novo) move both names and ripple into medtech "GLP-1 victim" baskets (dialysis, bariatric surgery, snacks/food).
- **Catalysts** — FDA action on [[orforglipron]], quarterly Mounjaro/Zepbound scripts (weekly IQVIA data is watched), pricing/IRA and political drug-pricing headlines, supply/capacity updates, retatrutide Phase 3 data. See the Growth Drivers section above.
