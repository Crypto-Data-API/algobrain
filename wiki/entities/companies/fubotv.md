FuboTV Inc. (NYSE: FUBO) is a sports-first live-TV streaming (vMVPD) company that, in January 2026, completed a transformative business combination with [[disney|Disney's]] Hulu + Live TV — Disney now owns ~70% of the combined entity, which is the largest virtual pay-TV distributor in the US with 6.2 million North American subscribers. FUBO remains one of the most actively options-traded small/mid-caps, with very high [[implied-volatility]] and heavy retail directional flow.

## Business Overview

Fubo began in 2015 as a soccer-streaming startup and grew into a sports-led virtual MVPD (live TV over the internet, no cable box). The January 2026 combination folded Disney's Hulu + Live TV business into Fubo, creating a dual-brand operation (Fubo and Hulu + Live TV continue as separate consumer offerings) with combined scale to negotiate carriage economics. Fubo also resells ESPN's direct-to-consumer service, broadening its sports skew. Revenue comes from subscriptions plus a growing advertising business.

## Business Segments / Products

Fubo is a **virtual MVPD (vMVPD)** — internet-delivered live TV — distinguished from on-demand streamers by its live, channel-bundle, sports-first product. Post-merger it runs two consumer brands plus an ad business:

| Line | What it is | Role |
|---|---|---|
| **Fubo (brand)** | Sports-first live-TV bundle (no cable box) | The legacy core; sports skew drives subscriber seasonality |
| **Hulu + Live TV (brand)** | Disney's live-TV vMVPD, now operated by Fubo | Doubled scale; carriage-negotiation leverage |
| **ESPN DTC resale** | Reselling ESPN's direct-to-consumer service | Deepens sports positioning |
| **Advertising** | Ad-supported revenue across the platforms | Growing, higher-margin attach revenue |

The dual-brand structure keeps the two consumer offerings separate but pools scale for **carriage economics** — the cost of licensing channels/sports rights is the dominant cost line, so bargaining power is the strategic prize of the Disney combination.

## The Disney Transaction (deep dive)

The January 2026 combination is the defining fact of the FUBO investment case:

- **Structure:** Disney merged Hulu + Live TV **into** Fubo, taking **~70% ownership** of the combined entity. Fubo became the largest US virtual pay-TV distributor with **6.2M North American subscribers**.
- **Litigation settlement:** the deal simultaneously settled Fubo's antitrust litigation over the Venu Sports JV — [[disney]]/Fox/WBD paid Fubo **$220M**, and Venu was subsequently scrapped.
- **Market reaction:** FUBO shares more than tripled on the announcement day (January 2025).
- **What it changes:** scale and carriage leverage (a structural positive); but a controlled-company structure where Disney holds ~70% — minority public holders bear governance risk and any buy-in/buy-out of the remaining ~30% becomes a live speculation.

## Competitive Positioning / Peers

Fubo competes inside the fragmenting "streaming bundle" landscape against both other vMVPDs and the platform/DTC giants.

| Player | Model | Relation to FUBO |

Fubo's differentiation is the **live sports bundle** for cord-cutters; its existential cost pressure is **sports-rights inflation**, which can squeeze vMVPD margins regardless of subscriber growth. ## Bull vs Bear Case

**Bull case**
- Disney combination transformed scale: largest US vMVPD, 6.2M NA subscribers, far stronger carriage-negotiation leverage.
- Financial inflection: Q1 FY2026 revenue $1.54B (+39% YoY) with adjusted EBITDA ($37.75M) far above consensus; management guides to $80–100M adjusted EBITDA FY2026, positive FCF in 2027, and ≥$300M adjusted EBITDA by 2028.
- Litigation windfall + clean-up: $220M settlement and the end of the Venu overhang.
- Sports skew + ESPN DTC resale position it for the live-sports streaming shift.
- Growing, higher-margin advertising business.

**Bear case**
- Sports-rights cost inflation structurally squeezes vMVPD margins — the core threat to the whole model.
- Subscriber churn outside football season; subs dipped sequentially in the seasonally weak post-NFL quarter even as revenue surged.
- Governance/minority risk: Disney controls ~70%; public holders are minority owners subject to controlled-company dynamics and Disney's strategic priorities.
- Integration execution risk across two brands and Disney's systems.
- Constrained float (Disney's ~70% stake) amplifies volatility in both directions.

## Valuation Framework

Fubo is now a **scaled vMVPD inflecting toward profitability**, valued on subs/ARPU and the EBITDA path under Disney control:

- **Subscribers** — 6.2M NA total; the volume base. Seasonal (peaks in autumn with NFL/college football).
- **ARPU** — revenue per subscriber; the mix/pricing lever. Watch churn/ARPU together, especially in the post-NFL trough.
- **Content cost** — sports-rights and carriage costs are the dominant, inflating cost line; the swing factor for margins.
- **Adjusted EBITDA path** — guidance to $80–100M FY2026, ≥$300M by 2028; the metric the equity is now judged on.
- **Free cash flow** — positive FCF targeted in 2027; the credibility test of the turnaround.
- **The Disney structure** — ~70% Disney ownership constrains float and frames any buy-in/buy-out of the remaining ~30% as a discrete valuation event.

## Capital Structure & Dilution Risk

- The Disney combination was effected by merging Hulu + Live TV **into** Fubo for ~70% equity — i.e. Disney's stake came via issuance, which **diluted legacy holders** but brought scale and a controlling, deep-pocketed parent.
- **Controlled-company structure:** Disney's ~70% ownership concentrates control; public holders are a ~30% minority. This both constrains the tradable float (amplifying volatility) and creates governance/minority-protection risk.
- Improving EBITDA/FCF trajectory reduces the need for dilutive financing versus the pre-merger standalone story.
- Watch: any change in Disney's stake (buy-in of the minority, or secondary sales), and content-cost commitments that could pressure cash.
