Toast, Inc. (NYSE: TOST) is a cloud-based, all-in-one technology platform for restaurants, combining point-of-sale (POS) hardware, payments processing, and operations software (online ordering, payroll, loyalty, inventory, and lending). Founded in Boston in 2011, Toast is a high-growth restaurant-fintech name that traders watch for its rapid location additions, payment volume (GPV), and its 2025 turn to sustained GAAP profitability.

## Business Overview

Toast sells an integrated stack to restaurants of all sizes — from single locations to enterprise chains — earning revenue from three streams:

- **Subscription software** (SaaS modules: POS, payroll, online ordering, loyalty, marketing).
- **Financial technology solutions** — payment processing fees on gross payment volume (GPV), the largest revenue line, plus Toast Capital lending.
- **Hardware** — terminals, handhelds (Toast Go), and peripherals (low/negative margin, used to land accounts).

The business is measured on **ARR**, **GPV**, **total locations**, and recurring gross profit. Toast competes with [[block-inc|Block (Square)]], [[shopify]], [[fiserv|Fiserv/Clover]], and vertical POS players, differentiating on a restaurant-specific, end-to-end product. Its moat is the deep integration and switching costs once a restaurant runs its entire operation on Toast.

### Business Segments / Products

| Stream | What it is | Margin profile | Role |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Subscription software (SaaS)** | POS, payroll, online ordering, loyalty, marketing, inventory modules | High, recurring | The quality-of-revenue engine; ARR driver |
| **Financial technology (payments)** | Processing fees on gross payment volume (GPV) + Toast Capital lending | Spread / take-rate | Largest revenue line; scales with restaurant sales |
| **Hardware** | Terminals, Toast Go handhelds, kitchen display, peripherals | Low / negative | Customer-acquisition tool to land accounts |

The economic model is **land with hardware + POS, monetize via payments take-rate and attach more SaaS modules over time** — so the watched metrics are net new locations (the land), GPV (the monetize), and ARR / recurring gross profit (the compounding).

## 2025-2026 Developments

A clear inflection from growth-at-a-loss to profitable compounding (Source verified via Perplexity (sonar), 2026-06-10):

- **Q2 2025**: ARR ~$1.9B, GPV $49.9B, ~148,000 locations (8,500 net adds in the quarter), adjusted EBITDA $161M, GAAP operating income $80M, revenue ~$1.55B.
- **Q3 2025**: ARR crossed $2.0B, adjusted EBITDA $176M, ~156,000 locations (~7,500 net adds).
- **FY2025**: Record ~30,000 net new locations added; ARR up ~26% to over $2.0B; Q4 net income $101M with adjusted EBITDA $163M.
- **Q1 2026**: ARR ~$2.2B, GPV $51.3B, ~171,000 locations, net income $126M, adjusted EBITDA $179M; repurchased 14M shares for $378M year-to-date (through May 6, 2026).
- **FY2026 guidance**: Non-GAAP subscription + fintech gross profit of $2.290-$2.320B and adjusted EBITDA of $790-$810M.
- **Growth vectors**: American Express partnership, Toast Go 3 hardware, retail/grocery vertical expansion, enterprise accounts, and international expansion (including Australia).

## Competitive Positioning / Peers

Toast competes across restaurant-specific POS, broad commerce/payments platforms, and merchant acquirers. The competitive axis is **vertical depth (a restaurant-specific end-to-end OS) versus horizontal breadth (general-purpose commerce/payments).**

Toast's moat is **vertical integration and switching costs**: once a restaurant runs POS, payments, payroll, online ordering, and loyalty on one system, replacing it is operationally painful. The trade-off is concentration in a single, cyclical vertical (restaurants) versus the diversified end-markets of the horizontal players.

## Valuation Framework (qualitative)

Toast is a **fintech-plus-SaaS hybrid**, so it must be valued on a blend of usage (payments) and recurring (software) economics:

- **Locations:** the most-watched leading indicator — net new locations drive future GPV and ARR. Deceleration in net adds is the single biggest de-rating risk.
- **GPV (gross payment volume):** the payments base; fintech revenue is a take-rate on GPV, so GPV growth × take-rate = the largest revenue line. GPV is exposed to restaurant same-store sales and consumer discretionary spending.
- **Fintech take-rate / spread:** the net spread Toast keeps on payments after interchange and processing costs — compression here directly hits the model.
- **ARR & recurring gross profit:** the quality-of-earnings metric; management guides on **subscription + fintech gross profit** rather than gross revenue, which is the right unit for the business.
- **Rule of 40 & FCF:** Toast turned GAAP-profitable in 2025; FCF, adjusted EBITDA margin, and the growth+margin balance now anchor the multiple.
- **SBC:** stock-based compensation remains a dilution watch item for a recently-profitable growth fintech.

Because revenue is dominated by a payments take-rate on GPV, Toast carries gross-revenue figures far larger than its net/gross-profit base — analysts focus on **recurring gross profit**, not headline revenue, to avoid overstating scale.

- **Index / ETF membership:** not in the [[s-and-p-500]]; a widely-held mid/large-cap growth fintech. Appears in fintech and growth ETFs (e.g. IPAY, FINX, ARKK-style funds) rather than mega-cap tech funds.

## Bull vs Bear Case

**Bull case**
- Clear inflection to sustained GAAP profitability with strong adjusted EBITDA and a buyback underway.
- Record location adds and ARR past $2B; deep vertical integration and high switching costs.
- Multiple growth vectors: enterprise, retail/grocery, international (Australia), Amex partnership, fintech attach (Toast Capital).
- Best-in-class restaurant OS with a long runway of US and international restaurants still to convert.

**Bear case**
- Concentrated in a cyclical vertical: restaurant SMB spending and consumer discretionary demand drive GPV.
- Payments take-rate compression risk and competition from [[block-inc]], [[fiserv]] (Clover), and [[shift4]].
- Valuation sensitivity if location/GPV growth decelerates — the stock is priced for continued strong net adds.
- SBC dilution and the law of large numbers slowing percentage growth as the base scales.

## Capital Return / Dilution

Toast began **repurchasing shares** in 2026 (14M shares for $378M year-to-date through May 6, 2026), reflecting its turn to positive net income and free cash flow; it pays no dividend. As with most recently-profitable growth fintechs, the buyback is partly an offset to ongoing **stock-based compensation** dilution — the key shareholder question is whether net income and FCF growth let the diluted share count genuinely decline.

## Risks

- **Vertical/consumer cyclicality:** restaurant SMB spending and consumer discretionary demand directly drive GPV and net adds.
- **Take-rate compression:** competitive and interchange pressure on payments economics.
- **Competition:** [[block-inc]] (Square), [[fiserv]] (Clover), [[shift4]], and vertical POS players.
- **Valuation/decel risk:** priced for strong location/GPV growth; deceleration de-rates the stock.
- **Dilution:** ongoing SBC.
- **Lending/credit:** Toast Capital introduces some credit exposure tied to restaurant health.
