Occidental Petroleum (NYSE: OXY) is a large US [[oil]] and gas exploration-and-production company centered on the Permian Basin, with a chemicals arm (OxyChem, being divested to [[berkshire-hathaway]]) and a carbon-capture venture (1PointFive/STRATOS direct-air-capture). It is a constituent of the [[s-and-p-500]] index and a member of the [[energy]] sector, best known to traders as Berkshire Hathaway's flagship public-market energy bet (~28% common stake) and as a high-beta [[crude-oil|WTI]] proxy whose leveraged balance sheet amplifies oil moves in both directions.

## Business Overview & Segments

OXY's asset base is anchored in the **Permian** (scale built via the $38B Anadarko deal in 2019 and the ~$12B CrownRock acquisition closed Dec 2024), plus Rockies/Gulf of Mexico and Middle East (Oman, UAE, Algeria) production, a midstream segment, and OxyChem. The post-Anadarko story has been **deleveraging** — selling assets and using free cash flow to cut debt — while [[berkshire-hathaway]] accumulated common shares (~28%) plus preferred stock and warrants from the 2019 financing. Its **1PointFive** subsidiary operates the STRATOS direct-air-capture plant in Texas, the key call option on carbon markets.

| Segment | What it is | Qualitative revenue / role |
|---|---|---|
| **Oil & Gas (Upstream E&P)** | Exploration and production of [[crude-oil|crude]], natural gas and NGLs — anchored in the low-cost Permian Basin (Delaware + Midland), plus Rockies/DJ Basin, Gulf of Mexico, and international (Oman, UAE, Algeria) | The overwhelming earnings driver and the source of OXY's commodity beta; this is what makes the stock a [[crude-oil|WTI]] proxy |
| **OxyChem (Chemicals)** | One of the largest US producers of chlor-alkali, PVC and vinyls; a real, non-commodity-oil cash-flow engine | A stable, counter-cyclical profit center — *being divested to [[berkshire-hathaway]] (~$9.7B, agreed Oct 2025)* to accelerate debt reduction |
| **Midstream & Marketing** | Gathering, processing, transport, storage and trading/marketing of OXY's and third-party volumes; includes the WES (Western Midstream) interest | Smaller, more volatile; optimizes realized prices and logistics rather than driving the thesis |
| **1PointFive / STRATOS (Carbon capture)** | Direct-air-capture (DAC) venture; STRATOS is a flagship DAC plant in the Texas Permian, monetized via carbon-removal credits and 45Q tax credits | Tiny today — a long-dated **call option** on carbon markets and policy, not a current earnings contributor |

The franchise is best understood as a **Permian-levered upstream producer with two differentiators bolted on**: a genuine chemicals business (OxyChem) that smooths the oil cycle, and a first-mover DAC platform that gives optionality on a carbon-constrained future. The strategic spine since 2019 has been deleveraging — converting the Anadarko/CrownRock asset base and OxyChem proceeds into debt reduction while growing the dividend.

## 2025-2026 Developments

- **OxyChem divestiture:** in **October 2025** OXY agreed to sell its OxyChem chemicals business to **Berkshire Hathaway** (~$9.7B cash), accelerating debt reduction; proceeds applied to deleveraging into 2026.
- **Record 2025 operations (reported Feb 2026):** record production of ~**1.43 million BOEPD**, operating cash flow ~**$10.5B**, free cash flow before working capital ~**$4.3B**; principal debt cut by ~**$4B during 2025**.
- **Dividend:** quarterly dividend raised 8% in early 2026; total dividend increases of more than 18% since 1 January 2025.
- **CEO transition announced for the 2026 AGM:** long-time CEO **Vicki Hollub** to hand over (per 2026 proxy materials).
- Q4 2025: operational beat, though GAAP results included charges; market focus stayed on deleveraging pace and Berkshire's intentions.

## Economic Moat & Competitive Position

An honest framing matters here: at the commodity level OXY has **no durable [[economic-moat|economic moat]]**. An upstream [[oil]] and gas producer is a **price-taker** — it sells an undifferentiated commodity into a global market it cannot influence, has essentially no pricing power, and earns the same realized [[crude-oil|WTI]]/Brent strip as every rival. There is no [[switching-costs|switching cost]], no brand premium, no network effect on a barrel of oil. What separates winners from losers among E&Ps is therefore **cost position and capital discipline**, not a moat:

- **Low-cost acreage / breakevens** — OXY's Permian (Delaware + Midland) inventory has competitive per-barrel breakevens; the CrownRock bolt-on added contiguous, low-cost Midland Basin acreage. The lowest-cost producer survives the trough that bankrupts marginal operators — this is the closest thing to an edge.
- **Operational execution & scale** — drilling/completion efficiency, well productivity, and the scale to amortize infrastructure and secure services pricing.
- **Balance-sheet and reserve quality** — through-cycle survivability and reserve-replacement; the post-2019 deleveraging is precisely an attempt to convert a leverage *disadvantage* into resilience.

The genuine **non-commodity differentiators** are the two bolt-ons: **OxyChem**, a real chemicals franchise with its own (chlor-alkali/PVC) cyclicality that diversifies away from oil and is now being monetized to [[berkshire-hathaway]]; and **1PointFive/STRATOS direct-air-capture**, a first-mover platform that — if carbon-removal markets and the 45Q credit framework scale — could become a structurally advantaged, contract-backed business unlike anything a pure E&P owns. Neither is a moat today, but they are the only parts of the story not fully dictated by the oil price.

## Growth Drivers & Catalysts

- **Permian productivity** — well-cost deflation, longer laterals, and CrownRock-acreage integration drive low-cost barrel growth; production set a record ~1.43 MMBOEPD in 2025.
- **Deleveraging** — the central thesis: principal debt was cut by ~$4B during 2025, and OxyChem sale proceeds (~$9.7B, agreed Oct 2025) accelerate the path to OXY's debt targets, which de-risks the equity and frees cash flow for shareholders.
- **OxyChem sale proceeds** — earmarked for debt reduction; closing milestones are discrete catalysts.
- **Dividend growth & capital return** — quarterly dividend raised 8% in early 2026 (>18% of increases since Jan 2025); buybacks resume as leverage falls.
- **DAC / 45Q carbon optionality** — STRATOS ramp, carbon-removal offtake contracts, and 45Q tax-credit/policy developments are a long-dated upside lever.
- **Recurring catalysts** — quarterly earnings/production guidance, OPEC+ decisions, EIA/API [[crude-oil|WTI]] inventory data, OxyChem closing milestones, the CEO transition at the 2026 AGM, and DAC/45Q policy news.

## Trading Relevance

- **WTI beta:** OXY is among the highest-beta large-cap E&Ps to [[crude-oil|crude]]; the leveraged balance sheet amplifies oil moves both ways. Correlates with [[conocophillips]], Devon, [[apa]], [[chevron]], [[exxonmobil]].
- **Berkshire flow:** [[berkshire-hathaway]]'s buying historically created a soft floor near its accumulation levels; any change in Berkshire's stance (e.g., post-OxyChem deal) is a stock-specific catalyst.
- **Options:** very liquid chain, popular for covered calls and LEAPS given the Buffett narrative; the preferred/warrant history makes the cap structure worth checking before event trades (see [[options]]).
- **Risk framing:** oil price downside with above-average leverage; position-size around the commodity cycle, not static multiples (see [[risk-management]]).

## Bull Case vs Bear Case

### Bull case
- **Permian scale & low-cost barrels** — a large, contiguous, competitive-breakeven inventory that can grow production cheaply and survive the trough.
- **Berkshire backing** — [[berkshire-hathaway]]'s ~28% common stake (plus preferred + warrants) provides a credibility anchor and a historical soft floor near accumulation levels.
- **Deleveraging tailwind** — every dollar of debt retired (helped by OxyChem proceeds) transfers value from creditors to equity and lowers the bankruptcy tail.
- **DAC optionality** — STRATOS/1PointFive is a cheap, embedded call on carbon-removal markets and 45Q policy.
- **Capital return** — a growing dividend and resuming buybacks as leverage normalizes.

### Bear case
- **High-beta to WTI with leverage** — the same leverage that amplifies upside makes drawdowns vicious in an oil selloff; a sustained [[crude-oil|WTI]] decline hits cash flow and the multiple together.
- **Commodity price-taker** — no moat at the barrel level; OXY cannot escape the oil cycle, only manage cost position.
- **DAC capex returns unproven** — direct-air-capture economics depend on policy (45Q) and immature carbon markets; capital could earn sub-cost returns.
- **CEO transition** — long-time CEO Vicki Hollub's handover at the 2026 AGM introduces execution/strategy uncertainty.
- **Reserve replacement & capital discipline** — must replace produced reserves without overpaying at cycle peaks.

## Key Risks

- **Oil-price downside amplified by leverage** — the dominant risk; OXY's above-average debt magnifies the equity impact of a [[crude-oil|crude]] decline.
- **Execution on debt targets** — the deleveraging thesis depends on hitting debt-reduction milestones; a low-price year that stalls progress would weigh on sentiment.
- **Reserve replacement** — depletion is relentless; failure to replace reserves cost-effectively erodes the asset base.
- **Carbon-capture capex returns** — DAC/STRATOS capital may not earn its cost of capital if carbon prices or 45Q support disappoint.
- **Regulatory / policy** — emissions rules, federal-lands and permitting policy, and any change to 45Q/carbon incentives.
- **Berkshire-stance risk** — a shift in [[berkshire-hathaway]]'s posture (selling, or the OxyChem deal altering its involvement) is a stock-specific swing factor.

## Valuation & How the Stock Trades

OXY does not trade on a static earnings multiple — as a leveraged commodity producer its reported P/E swings violently with the oil cycle (and goes meaningless at the trough), so the market values it on **through-cycle reserves, free cash flow at a normalized strip, and net-debt trajectory** rather than a snapshot P/E. Mechanically:

- **Commodity beta dominates** — OXY is a high-beta [[crude-oil|WTI]] proxy; day-to-day the stock tracks crude and the leverage gears the move. It outperforms peers on oil rallies and underperforms on selloffs.
- **Berkshire flow as a soft floor** — historical accumulation by [[berkshire-hathaway]] near specific levels has anchored the downside in past selloffs; the stake is part of the valuation narrative.
- **Deleveraging re-rating** — as net debt falls, equity value rises mechanically and the perceived risk premium compresses — the core re-rating thesis.
- **Capital return** — a growing dividend plus resuming buybacks; the dividend yield is a meaningful part of total return, unlike a pure growth name.
- **Options & cap structure** — very liquid chain favored for covered calls and LEAPS on the Buffett narrative; preferred stock and warrant overhang are worth checking before event trades.
