# Dorsey Wright

**Dorsey Wright & Associates** is a systematic research and analytics firm specializing in [[relative-strength]] analysis, founded by Tom Dorsey in 1987 in Richmond, Virginia. The firm was acquired by [[nasdaq|Nasdaq]] in 2015 for approximately $225 million and now operates as part of Nasdaq's Investment Intelligence division. Dorsey Wright pioneered the application of [[point-and-figure]] charting combined with relative strength matrices for rules-based asset allocation and [[sector-rotation]] strategies.

## Core Methodology

Dorsey Wright's approach centers on **relative strength matrices** — systematic rankings that compare the price performance of assets, sectors, and factors against each other on a pairwise basis. Rather than using absolute momentum (is the price going up?), the firm measures relative momentum (is this asset outperforming that asset?). This distinction is critical: an asset can be declining in absolute terms but still rank highly if it is declining less than its peers.

The methodology uses [[point-and-figure]] charts as the underlying signal generator. Point-and-figure charts filter out noise by only recording price changes that exceed a specified box size, producing clean buy and sell signals based on column reversals. When applied across hundreds of asset pairs, this creates a matrix showing which assets are on point-and-figure buy signals relative to which other assets. The percentage of buy signals an asset holds against all others becomes its **relative strength score**.

## DALI System

The **Dynamic Asset Level Investing (DALI)** system is Dorsey Wright's flagship macro allocation model. DALI ranks six broad asset classes — US equities, international equities, fixed income, currencies, commodities, and cash equivalents — by [[relative-strength]] against each other. The system signals which asset classes deserve overweight, market weight, or underweight allocations based on the current relative strength regime.

DALI updates are published regularly and widely followed by financial advisors using Dorsey Wright models. When DALI shifts its favored asset class (e.g., from fixed income to equities), it signals a regime change that often persists for months or quarters, making it a useful tool for [[sector-rotation]] and tactical allocation.

## ETF Products

Dorsey Wright partners with **First Trust Advisors** to offer several ETFs based on their relative strength models:

| Ticker | Name | Strategy |
|--------|------|----------|
| **FV** | First Trust Dorsey Wright Focus Five | Holds the 5 strongest First Trust sector/industry ETFs by RS |
| **DWMO** | Invesco Dorsey Wright Momentum | Selects top-momentum US large-cap stocks |
| **PDP** | Invesco DWA Momentum | Large-cap momentum using DWA Technical Leaders Index |
| **PIZ** | Invesco DWA Developed Markets Momentum | International developed markets momentum |
| **PIE** | Invesco DWA Emerging Markets Momentum | Emerging markets momentum |

The **Focus Five ETF (FV)** is particularly notable as an "ETF of ETFs" — it selects the five strongest sector ETFs from the First Trust lineup and concentrates capital in them, rebalancing quarterly. This is a pure implementation of [[sector-rotation]] driven by [[relative-strength]] signals.

## Use by Financial Advisors

Dorsey Wright's models are widely used by registered investment advisors (RIAs) and broker-dealers for **tactical asset allocation**. The firm provides model portfolios that advisors can implement through separately managed accounts (SMAs) or ETF portfolios. Key advantages for advisors include fully systematic rules (removing emotional bias), documented methodology, and long track records. The models typically rebalance monthly or quarterly, making them suitable for advisory clients who prefer lower turnover than active day trading.

## Status as of 2026

Now branded **Nasdaq Dorsey Wright (NDW)**, the unit remains fully operational inside [[nasdaq|Nasdaq]]'s index and analytics business. The NDW Research Platform (daily reports, DALI tactical allocation tool, guided stock/ETF/mutual-fund models, and the "My Portfolios" watch-list service) is distributed directly and through custodians such as Schwab Advisor Services. The published 2025 DALI review noted that offensive asset classes — US and international equities — held the top two rankings for most of 2025, with the three offensive asset classes jockeying for position in H1 2025. Dorsey Wright-branded ETFs (PDP, PIZ, PIE, FV, DWMO and sector funds) continue to trade, making NDW signals indirectly market-moving when quarterly rebalances concentrate flows.
