# GE Vernova (GEV)

GE Vernova (NYSE: GEV) is the power-generation and grid-equipment company spun out of [[general-electric|General Electric]] in **April 2024** — gas turbines, grid/electrification equipment, and wind. A constituent of the [[s-and-p-500]] and one of the largest [[industrials]]-sector weights, it has been one of the market's premier **[[ai-data-center-power-demand|AI-electricity-demand]]** trades: a massive post-spin rerating driven by a sold-out gas-turbine order book and surging data-center power demand. The story is simple to state and hard to fault — building AI compute means building electricity, and the world's installed base of large gas turbines and grid equipment runs through a three-company oligopoly in which GE Vernova is the US champion.

## Business Overview & Segments

GE Vernova sits at the intersection of two structural tailwinds — electrification and the energy transition — selling the heavy equipment that generates power and the equipment that moves it across the grid. The franchise is unusually back-loaded: a large share of profit comes not from new-build but from **decades of high-margin services** on an enormous installed base of turbines, which competitors cannot easily contest. The bull case is that new-build pricing and grid-equipment scarcity have inflected upward at the same time; the bear case is that the headline P&L still carries low blended margins and a loss-making offshore-wind legacy.

| Segment | What it is | Profitability / growth (qualitative) |
|---|---|---|
| **Power** (gas, hydro, steam, nuclear) | Heavy-duty gas turbines (HA-class), plus hydro, steam and nuclear services. The HA-class fleet services roughly **a quarter of the world's electricity generation**; long-duration services on the installed base provide recurring revenue. | The profit engine — services annuity plus a sold-out new-build order book; the highest-quality earnings in the company |
| **Electrification** | Grid equipment — HVDC, switchgear, transformers — and solutions sold increasingly **direct to hyperscalers** for data centers. | Fastest-growing segment; revenue up ~25% in 2025, guided ~20% in 2026; capacity-constrained and pricing-rich |
| **Wind** (onshore + offshore) | Onshore wind turbines plus a loss-making offshore backlog of legacy projects, including blade-failure remediation at Vineyard Wind in 2024. | The drag on the group — being worked down toward breakeven; the swing factor on consolidated margins |

The shape of the business is what makes it interesting: a turbine sold today seeds **decades** of contracted service revenue, so the record new-build backlog is not only a current-revenue event but a multi-decade annuity being written at the same time.

## Economic Moat & Competitive Position

GE Vernova holds a wide [[economic-moat]] in its core Power franchise, rooted in industry structure and an installed base rather than in technology alone:

- **Heavy-duty gas-turbine oligopoly** — the global market for large gas turbines is effectively a **three-player oligopoly**: GE Vernova, **Siemens Energy**, and **Mitsubishi Heavy Industries**. There are no fringe entrants; designing, qualifying and supporting a multi-hundred-megawatt turbine is a multi-decade, capital- and reliability-intensive endeavour. That structure underpins the new-build pricing power now showing up in the backlog.
- **Installed-base services annuity & [[switching-costs]]** — turbines run for **decades**, and the OEM captures the long tail of parts, upgrades and maintenance. Once a customer's fleet is GE Vernova, re-sourcing service to a third party is rarely worth the reliability risk on equipment this critical — a durable services lock-in that throws off high-margin, recurring cash.
- **Grid-equipment scarcity** — HVDC links, large transformers and switchgear are **capacity-constrained**, with multi-year lead times. The Electrification segment benefits from the same scarcity that makes the equipment hard to substitute: hyperscalers are now contracting direct to lock up future delivery slots.
- **Roadmap and reference base** — a globally referenced HA-class fleet and a recognised brand give GE Vernova first call on the largest, most reliability-sensitive power projects.

The moat is real but the financials are honest about its limits: **blended margins are still low** (EBITDA margin in the low single digits, weighed down by mix and wind), the **wind segment remains a drag**, and the new-build half of the business is **cyclical** — heavy-equipment order cycles turn, and a multiple priced for a permanent supercycle is exposed if orders merely normalise.

## Growth Drivers & Catalysts

- **Gas-turbine supercycle** — gas equipment backlog plus slot-reservation agreements grew from **50 GW to 55 GW** during Q2 2025, on the way to roughly **80 GW exiting 2025** (stretching into 2029) and reported around **100 GW** by mid-2026 as prices rose; CEO Scott Strazik has said reservations could be **sold out through 2030 by end-2026**.
- **[[ai-data-center-power-demand|AI / data-center electricity scarcity]]** — the structural demand pull behind both gas new-build and grid equipment; GEV is a direct beneficiary of hyperscaler capex.
- **Electrification direct-to-hyperscaler orders** — record electrical-equipment orders sold direct to hyperscaler tech companies (data-center equipment across North America, Europe, Middle East, Australia); segment revenue +~25% in 2025, guided ~20% in 2026.
- **Capacity expansion** — production guided to **~20 GW annualized by mid-2026**, stretchable to **~24 GW by mid-2028** — converting backlog into deliverable revenue.
- **Services annuity on the installed base** — the highest-margin, most durable earnings stream, compounding as the new-build fleet grows.
- **Wind-loss reduction** — working down the loss-making offshore backlog toward breakeven is a self-help margin lever.
- **Nuclear / SMR optionality** — long-dated exposure to next-generation nuclear on the same electricity-scarcity theme.
- **US power policy** — grid-buildout incentives, permitting and power-market reform as macro catalysts.

## Bull Case vs Bear Case

### Bull case
- AI/data-center electricity demand is a multi-year structural pull; the gas-turbine backlog and slot reservations could be sold out through 2030 by end-2026.
- Three-player turbine oligopoly + capacity-constrained grid equipment = real pricing power now showing in the backlog.
- Every turbine sold seeds decades of high-margin services — a growing, durable annuity layered under the new-build cycle.
- Capacity expansion (20→24 GW) converts a record book into revenue; electrification orders direct from hyperscalers are setting records.
- Self-help on wind losses plus a clean balance sheet (near-zero D/E) provide margin and optionality upside.

### Bear case
- The stock is **priced for perfection** (~52x trailing PE) — any deceleration in order momentum, even a normalisation, can de-rate it hard.
- Blended margins remain low; the wind segment is still a drag and remediation can surprise.
- New-build is cyclical — heavy-equipment order cycles turn, and the current pace may not persist.
- Turbine supply-chain and capacity-expansion execution risk; missed ramps push out revenue.
- Demand is increasingly tied to hyperscaler capex — any pause in the AI build-out hits the whole thesis.

## Key Risks

- **Valuation / positioning** — a priced-for-perfection ~52x trailing multiple is vulnerable to order-momentum deceleration; the stock can de-rate sharply on any slowdown.
- **Wind-segment losses** — the offshore legacy backlog (incl. Vineyard Wind remediation) remains a margin and headline risk until worked down.
- **Turbine supply-chain & capacity slips** — capacity expansion to ~20–24 GW must execute on time; bottlenecks delay revenue recognition.
- **Data-center capex pause** — demand is increasingly a derivative of hyperscaler spending; a synchronized AI-capex pause would hit orders and the multiple together.
- **Cyclicality of heavy-equipment new-build** — order cycles for large turbines turn; a priced-for-supercycle name is exposed if the book merely normalises.
- **Execution on capacity expansion** — converting backlog to delivered units at the guided cadence is the gating risk on the growth story.

## Valuation & How the Stock Trades

GE Vernova trades on the strength of its order book and the AI-power narrative rather than on current margins — the headline P&L still carries low blended profitability, so the multiple reflects expected backlog conversion and services growth, not present earnings power. Mechanically:

- **High beta to the AI-power theme** — a very liquid mega-cap industrial with an active options market; it moves on [[nvidia]] / data-center capex headlines as much as on its own prints. It is the equipment-side expression of the [[ai-data-center-power-demand|electricity-scarcity]] trade.
- **Post-spin rerating** — among the strongest large-cap industrial reratings since the April 2024 spin (from ~$115 at spin to several hundred dollars), which leaves **valuation (~52x trailing PE per the fundamentals below) as the main bear point**.
- **Capital return** — initiated a dividend and buybacks from December 2024, but the **yield is token**; this is a capital-appreciation story, not an income one — reinvestment and the order book drive the total return.
- **Catalysts** — quarterly orders and slot-reservation disclosures, gas-turbine pricing commentary, hyperscaler power deals, wind charges, and US power-policy / nuclear-SMR news.
