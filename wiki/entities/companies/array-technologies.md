Array Technologies, Inc. (NASDAQ: ARRY) is one of the world's two dominant manufacturers of utility-scale single-axis solar trackers (alongside Nextracker), with its SmarTrack machine-learning software optimizing panel angles for energy yield. A small-cap, high-beta US solar name, it trades primarily on US renewable-energy policy, utility-scale solar installation volumes, and steel/logistics costs.

## Business Overview

Array sells ground-mounted tracker systems (DuraTrack, OmniTrack) plus the SmarTrack [[artificial-intelligence|AI]]/[[machine-learning-overview|machine-learning]] yield-optimization software to utility-scale solar developers and EPCs, mostly in the US with international exposure via its 2022 STI Norland acquisition (Spain/Brazil). Revenue is project-driven and lumpy; the orderbook (backlog) is the key forward indicator. Domestic-content manufacturing positions Array to help customers capture IRA 45X/domestic-content credits.

## Business Segments / Products

Array sells **electro-mechanical tracker systems plus optimization software**, with a recently broadened structural-infrastructure offering:

| Product / line | What it is | Role |
|---|---|---|
| **DuraTrack** | Flagship single-axis tracker for utility-scale ground mounts | Core volume product; durability/low-O&M positioning |
| **OmniTrack** | Terrain-following tracker for undulating sites | Expands addressable land/sites |
| **SmarTrack (software)** | [[machine-learning-overview|ML]] yield-optimization that adjusts panel angles for weather/diffuse light | Differentiator; lifts energy yield and ASP/stickiness |
| **STI Norland (intl.)** | Spain/Brazil tracker platform (2022 acquisition) | International diversification beyond the US |
| **APA Solar (foundations & fixed-tilt)** | Pile foundations and fixed-tilt racking (Aug 2025 acquisition) | Broadens Array into solar structural infrastructure; cross-sell |

Revenue is **project-driven and lumpy** — recognized as utility-scale projects ship — so the **orderbook/backlog** and its *conversion rate* are the key forward indicators, not any single quarter. **Domestic-content manufacturing** lets customers capture IRA 45X / domestic-content adders, a structural US advantage.

## Competitive Positioning / Peers

The utility-scale tracker market is effectively a duopoly at the top, inside the broader solar BoS (balance-of-system) and modules complex:

| Company | Relationship | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| [[nextracker]] (NXT) | **Primary direct competitor** | The other dominant single-axis tracker maker; larger scale; the head-to-head ARRY comp |
| [[shoals-technologies]] (SHLS) | **Adjacent BoS competitor/peer** | Electrical balance-of-system (combiners, EBOS) for utility-scale solar; same project cycle |
| [[first-solar]] (FSLR) | Adjacent — utility-scale modules | Different product (panels) but same utility-scale solar demand and policy drivers |
| GameChange Solar / FTC Solar | Smaller tracker competitors | Compete on price at the margin; pressure tracker ASPs |
| [[enphase-energy]] | Adjacent — solar electronics (resi-weighted) | Same TAN/solar complex sentiment, different end market |

Array's moat is **scale, an installed base, domestic-content manufacturing, and SmarTrack software**; its risk is a **direct duopoly with [[nextracker]]** plus ASP pressure from smaller tracker rivals and commoditization of the mechanical hardware.

## Bull vs Bear Case

**Bull case**

- Tracker duopoly with [[nextracker]] in a structurally growing utility-scale solar market.
- Record backlog ($2.2B at YE 2025) gives forward visibility.
- Domestic-content manufacturing aligns with IRA 45X / domestic-content adders — a US policy tailwind for now.
- APA Solar broadens the product set (foundations/fixed-tilt) and cross-sell.
- SmarTrack software differentiates and supports ASPs.

**Bear case**

- Project lumpiness: Q1 2026 revenue fell ~26% YoY — quarterly volatility and project pushouts are endemic.
- Heavy dependence on US clean-energy policy; reversal of credits would hit demand.
- Steel and logistics input-cost swings compress margins.
- ASP pressure from [[nextracker]] and smaller tracker rivals.
- Customer concentration among a handful of EPCs; rate-sensitivity of project financing.

## Valuation Framework (qualitative)

Array is valued as a **policy- and project-cycle-driven small-cap solar capital-equipment name**:

- **ITC / IRA / 45X policy sensitivity:** demand and domestic-content economics hinge on US clean-energy credits; policy headlines (45X, domestic-content adders, tariffs) are first-order drivers.
- **Backlog / orderbook:** the central forward metric — the market watches the **orderbook level *and* its conversion rate** to revenue, since quarterly revenue is lumpy.
- **Interest-rate sensitivity:** utility-scale project financing is rate-sensitive; higher rates slow project FIDs and pressure the solar complex broadly.
- **Cash generation vs. solar drawdowns:** like peers, ARRY is judged on margin and cash resilience through the deep solar-sector drawdowns rather than on smooth growth.
- **Input costs:** steel and logistics are direct margin levers — a cost spike can swamp volume gains.
- **Tracker-ASP / mix:** SmarTrack software and the APA structural products support ASP/mix against commoditizing mechanical hardware.
