Onto Innovation Inc. (NYSE: ONTO) makes process-control, metrology and inspection equipment for semiconductor fabrication — formed in 2019 from the merger of Rudolph Technologies and Nanometrics. For traders it is a mid/small-cap leveraged play on the [[artificial-intelligence|AI]] chip build-out, because its fastest-growing market is inspection and metrology for **advanced packaging** (2.5D/3D stacking and high-bandwidth memory) used in AI accelerators.

## AI Involvement

Process control and inspection for [[artificial-intelligence|AI]] chip fabrication: bump metrology (Dragonfly platform) for HBM and 2.5D/3D advanced packaging, plus optical metrology (Atlas platform) for leading-edge logic nodes including gate-all-around transistors.

## Business Overview

Onto sells into logic foundries, memory makers and OSATs. Its revenue splits across advanced packaging & specialty devices (the AI-driven growth engine, slightly more than half of revenue by late 2025), advanced nodes (leading-edge logic/memory metrology) and software/services. Customer concentration around the leading AI-supply-chain players ([[taiwan-semiconductor-manufacturing|TSMC]], HBM memory makers such as SK hynix/Samsung/Micron) is both the bull case and the key risk.

## Business Segments & Platforms

| Segment / platform | Function | AI relevance |
|---|---|---|
| **Advanced packaging & specialty** (Dragonfly) | Bump metrology and inspection for 2.5D/3D stacking and [[hbm-high-bandwidth-memory\|HBM]] | The growth engine; >50% of revenue by late 2025 |
| **Advanced nodes** (Atlas) | Optical metrology for leading-edge logic, including gate-all-around (GAA) transistors and HBM4 DRAM | Leverages the leading-edge logic and DRAM build-out |
| **Iris / films metrology** | Thin-film measurement | Process-control content across the fab |
| **Software & services** | Yield/analytics software, support | Recurring, higher-margin layer |

Onto's structural advantage is straddling both halves of the AI build-out: it has the bump-metrology franchise for advanced packaging (where AI accelerators are assembled with HBM) and optical metrology for the leading-edge logic/DRAM nodes those accelerators are fabbed on. That dual exposure makes it more diversified than a pure packaging-inspection peer.

## Competitive Positioning & Peers

Onto is a mid-cap process-control specialist positioned between a same-niche packaging pure-play and the dominant broad-line leader:

| Company | Focus | Relative scale |
|---|---|---|
| **Onto Innovation (ONTO)** | Bump metrology (Dragonfly) + optical metrology (Atlas); packaging + advanced nodes | Mid/small-cap; diversified across packaging and logic |
| [[kla\|KLA Corporation]] | Broad process control across the entire fab flow | Vastly larger; the dominant process-control franchise |
| [[camtek\|Camtek]] | Optical inspection/metrology concentrated in advanced packaging & HBM | Similar size; more packaging-concentrated pure-play |

Onto's edge versus [[camtek|Camtek]] is breadth (packaging plus leading-edge node metrology and software); its challenge versus [[kla|KLA]] is that KLA's scale, R&D budget and full-flow installed base set the competitive ceiling in process control.

## Bull vs Bear Case

**Bull case**
- Dual leverage: advanced-packaging/HBM metrology and leading-edge logic/DRAM metrology.
- Advanced-packaging revenue guided to grow strongly into 2026 on record backlog.
- Large multi-year HBM purchase agreements provide visibility through 2027.
- Atlas G6 adoption for gate-all-around and HBM4 DRAM opens a fresh node-driven leg.

**Bear case**
- Order lumpiness and customer concentration in HBM/2.5D buyers.
- High beta to any AI accelerator capex pause (see [[ai-capex-vs-cash-flow-divergence]]).
- Competes against the much larger [[kla|KLA]] in core process control.
- Export-control headlines can pressure the semicap equipment group broadly.

## Valuation Framework

Onto is valued qualitatively on the advanced-packaging cycle, node transitions and WFE mix:

- **Advanced-packaging mix** — the share and growth of the Dragonfly franchise, the AI-driven engine.
- **WFE cycle & memory-vs-logic mix** — Onto benefits from both leading-edge logic capex and HBM/DRAM (memory) capex, so the blend matters.
- **Node transitions** — gate-all-around (GAA) and HBM4 adoption drive Atlas pull-through.
- **Backlog & purchase agreements** — multi-quarter visibility (e.g., HBM volume purchase agreements) underpins forward revenue.
- **Customer concentration** — exposure to TSMC and the HBM memory makers is both upside and risk.

## Trading & Options Playbook

- **Index / ETF membership:** NYSE-listed (ticker ONTO); a holding in semiconductor-equipment and AI-themed baskets including the [[smh-vaneck-semiconductor-etf|SMH]] complex. Not an [[s-and-p-500]] member.
- **Semi-cycle sensitivity:** high-beta derivative of the AI capex cycle — correlated with [[nvidia]], the SOX index, [[asml-holding|ASML]], [[kla|KLA]], [[camtek|Camtek]] and other process-control names; HBM order news from memory makers moves the stock.
- **Catalysts calendar:** quarterly earnings and backlog commentary, HBM4 ramp timing, advanced-packaging capacity announcements from TSMC/OSATs, and export-control headlines affecting semicap equipment.
- **Volatility behavior:** moderately liquid options chain; IV spikes into earnings given a history of large post-print moves.

## Capital Structure & Dilution Risk

Onto runs a profitable, asset-light equipment model with a historically clean balance sheet — share dilution is not the central concern. The key capital-structure issues are order lumpiness and equipment-cycle cyclicality, which swing cash generation period to period; backlog and purchase agreements partly buffer this. Stock-based compensation modestly expands the share count over time.

## Risks

- **Order lumpiness** and **customer concentration** in HBM/2.5D and leading-edge logic buyers.
- **Cyclicality** of WFE; an AI accelerator capex pause hits utilization and backlog.
- **Competition** from much larger [[kla|KLA]] in process control.
- **Export controls** affecting the semicap equipment group and China demand.

## 2025-2026 Developments

- **FY2025**: record full-year revenue of $1.005 billion. Q4 2025 revenue of $267 million, up 22% sequentially; advanced packaging and specialty revenue ~$145 million in Q4 (just over half of total). 2.5D packaging orders for AI devices more than doubled quarter-over-quarter.
- **HBM win**: secured a $240 million volume purchase agreement with an HBM customer for Dragonfly 2D/3D bump metrology covering demand through 2027.
- **2026 outlook** (given Feb 2026): advanced packaging revenue expected to grow more than 30% in 2026 — a new record for that market — on a record backlog; advanced-nodes revenue also expected to grow on Atlas G6 adoption for gate-all-around and HBM4 DRAM.
