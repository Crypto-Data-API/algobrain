**Capricorn Metals Ltd** (ASX: CMM) is a gold producer listed on the [[asx-limited|Australian Securities Exchange]] and a constituent of the [[asx-200]] index. Capricorn Metals Ltd is a single-commodity Australian gold producer focused on Western Australia. Its sole producing asset is the Karlawinda Gold Project (KGP) in the Pilbara, and it is developing the Mt Gibson Gold Project in the Murchison region.

## Business Overview & Assets

Capricorn is a single-commodity Australian [[gold]] producer in the [[materials]] sector, with a low-cost producing mine and a fully permitted second development that gives it one of the clearest organic growth pipelines in the ASX mid-cap gold cohort. The pure-gold focus (no copper or by-product credits) makes it a clean, high-torque expression of the AUD gold price.

| Asset | Location | Status | Role / notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Karlawinda (KGP)** | Pilbara, WA (~70 km SE of Newman) | Producing | Sole producing asset; open-pit + CIL, ~4.5 Mtpa nameplate; expansion to 6.5 Mtpa lifts output toward ~150koz/yr |
| **Mt Gibson** | Murchison, WA | Development (fully permitted, 100%) | Targeted >155koz/yr over an 11-year-plus mine life; the second leg toward ~300koz/yr group output by 2027–28 |

Capricorn is unusually disciplined for a single-asset miner: it has historically run low gearing, self-funded development from operating cash flow, and built a large cash-and-gold balance (A$507.6M at the March 2026 quarter). Revenue is earned entirely from gold bullion sold to refiners.

## Economic Moat

Capricorn is a **price-taker** — it sells gold at the prevailing USD spot price and has no pricing power, so there is no franchise [[economic-moat]] of the kind a network business like [[brambles]] enjoys. The honest framing for a miner is **cost-curve position and asset quality**, and on that score Capricorn screens well: Karlawinda is a low-AISC operation (H1 FY26 AISC ~A$1,627/oz against a spot price well above A$4,000/oz), which keeps it deep in the money through most of the cycle and profitable even on a sharp gold pullback. Its relevant durable advantages are (1) a low-cost, simple open-pit/CIL operation, (2) a long-life, fully permitted growth asset (Mt Gibson) that lets it grow ounces organically rather than by dilutive acquisition, and (3) capital discipline that has avoided the [[capital-raise]] dilution that afflicts many peers. The limitation is single-asset concentration: the "moat" is really the quality and cost position of two WA orebodies, not an enduring competitive barrier.

## Bull Case vs Bear Case

### Bull case
- Low-cost producer (AISC ~A$1,627/oz) deep in the money; record margins at record gold prices.
- Clear, self-funded organic growth: Karlawinda expansion plus a fully permitted Mt Gibson toward ~300koz/yr.
- Capital-disciplined, low-gearing model that avoids dilutive raisings; A$507.6M cash and gold.
- Premium-quality single-commodity exposure — a clean, high-torque AUD gold play.

### Bear case
- Premium valuation (trailing PE ~53x) leaves little margin for error on a gold-price pullback or expansion overrun.
- Single-asset concentration — Karlawinda is the only producing mine until Mt Gibson is built.
- Execution risk on the Karlawinda expansion and Mt Gibson construction (cost/timeline).
- Pure price-taker with no by-product credits — fully exposed to a gold reversal and cost inflation.
- Reserve replacement at Karlawinda needed to sustain mine life.

## Key Risks

- **Gold-price reversal** — the largest risk; the premium multiple amplifies the de-rate on any pullback.
- **Single-asset concentration** — operational disruption at Karlawinda is outsized until Mt Gibson produces.
- **Execution / cost overrun** — Karlawinda expansion ramp and Mt Gibson construction.
- **Valuation** — premium PE vulnerable to multiple compression independent of the gold price.
- **Cost inflation** — labour, power, diesel in remote WA.
- **FX** — a stronger AUD compresses AUD-denominated margins.

## Valuation & How the Stock Trades

- **Gold and AUD beta** — CMM is a clean, high-torque AUD-denominated play on USD gold; as a pure-play, low-cost producer with no by-product offset, earnings amplify spot-gold moves, and a soft AUD compounds the margin expansion.
- **Premium multiple** — CMM has historically traded at a premium PE (~53x trailing per the fundamentals below) reflecting its low-cost status and growth pipeline; that premium is the swing factor — it can compress sharply if the gold price falls or the growth narrative stumbles, so the headline PE should be read against a cyclical-peak earnings base.
- **No meaningful dividend** to date — CMM is a capital-growth/leverage name, reinvesting cash into expansion rather than paying franked income (contrast [[evolution-mining]]).
- **Index/flows** — [[asx-200]] constituent in the ASX mid-cap gold cohort; held in gold and materials baskets/ETFs.
- **No meaningful single-stock options market**; exposure is taken in the shares or via ASX gold-sector ETFs.

## History

Capricorn Metals was formerly known as Malagasy Minerals Limited, having been incorporated in 2006 and changing its name to Capricorn Metals in February 2016 when the company's strategic focus shifted to the Karlawinda gold deposit. Karlawinda was developed through 2020 and poured first gold in mid-2021, reaching steady-state commercial production shortly after. In 2022 Capricorn acquired the Mt Gibson Gold Project from Central Australian Gold Exploration, establishing the company's second development pipeline.

## Recent Developments

For H1 FY26 (six months to 31 December 2025), Capricorn delivered record half-year results at Karlawinda, producing 62,794 ounces at an all-in sustaining cost (AISC) of A$1,627 per ounce and tracking toward the upper end of FY26 guidance of 115,000–125,000 ounces. In the December 2025 quarter, KGP produced 30,476 ounces. The Karlawinda Expansion Project, approved by Western Australia's Department of Energy, Mines, Industry Regulation and Safety in July 2025, has progressed materially, with bulk earthworks completed and process plant detailed design approximately 95% complete. Development of Mt Gibson is advancing, with A$2.9 million spent during the December 2025 quarter on detailed design, early procurement and contract preparation.

In the March 2026 quarter Karlawinda produced a preliminary 30,358 ounces, taking year-to-date FY26 output to 93,152 ounces and keeping the company on track for the upper end of FY26 guidance (115,000–125,000 oz). Cash and gold on hand rose to A$507.6 million at the end of the March quarter after a A$100.2 million cash build before ~A$50 million of capital expenditure across the Karlawinda expansion (~A$120 million project, taking processing to 6.5 Mtpa and annual output toward ~150,000 oz) and Mt Gibson development.
