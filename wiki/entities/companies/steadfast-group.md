**Steadfast Group Limited** (ASX: SDF) is the largest general insurance broker network and insurance underwriting agency group in Australasia, and a constituent of the [[asx-200]] index. For traders it is a structural-growth financial: a capital-light, fee-and-commission aggregator that compounds via bolt-on broker/agency acquisitions and rising premium rates, with brokers in its network placing roughly A$25 billion of gross written premium annually. The company is headquartered in Sydney, New South Wales.

## Business Model & Segments

Steadfast Group provides the Steadfast Network of general insurance brokers with centralised services including bulk product access, placement technology, licensing support, training, compliance, claims advocacy and marketing. It also operates a large portfolio of insurance underwriting agencies that develop and distribute niche and specialty insurance products through the broker channel.

Steadfast offers access to a wide range of business insurance lines, including aviation, business pack and interruption, contract works, corporate travel, cyber, events, farm, marine, management and product liability, professional indemnity, public liability, trade credit and workers' compensation insurance. Personal insurance lines include home and contents, landlord, motor, life and strata insurance.

Beyond brokerage and underwriting, Steadfast has built a technology ecosystem including placement platforms and automation tools (such as the July 2025 acquisition of Brisbane-based start-up Insurebot, which automates insurance quoting). The group also invests in equity interests in broker businesses and agency businesses, providing strategic capital to the network.

### Why the model compounds

Steadfast is a **capital-light fee aggregator**, not a risk-taker — it does not carry insurance balance-sheet risk the way an underwriter like [[qbe-insurance]] does. Its revenue rises on two largely independent levers: (1) **premium-rate inflation** in a hard insurance market (brokers earn a percentage of premium, so commission rises automatically when rates rise), and (2) **network expansion** via bolt-on broker/agency acquisitions and licence-fee growth. This gives it a structural-growth profile with relatively low cyclicality versus the underwriters whose products flow through it.

## Economic Moat & Competitive Position

Steadfast has built a **network-effects [[economic-moat|moat]]** — the largest general-insurance broker network in Australasia. The reinforcing advantages are:

- **Scale / buying power** — aggregating ~A$25bn of GWP gives the network bulk-purchasing leverage with insurers that no individual broker could obtain, the original reason the network was formed in 1996.
- **Network effects** — more brokers attract more insurer product and better terms, which attract more brokers; the placement technology and shared services deepen with scale.
- **[[switching-costs]]** — brokers embedded in Steadfast's placement platforms, licensing (AFSL) support, and compliance infrastructure face real friction leaving.
- **Consolidation flywheel** — Steadfast is the natural acquirer in a fragmented broker market; each bolt-on adds GWP, data, and cross-sell with minimal incremental capital.

The competitive position is strong but not unassailable: its closest rival [[aub-group]] runs a similar network/agency model, and a sustained soft insurance market would slow the commission-growth tailwind that flatters the model.

## Growth Drivers & Catalysts

- **Bolt-on M&A** — the core compounding engine: continuous acquisition of brokers and underwriting agencies (e.g. Novum Underwriting Partners, Rothbury, Insurebot in 2025).
- **Premium-rate inflation** — a hard insurance market lifts commission income automatically; ~2.4% average premium growth reported into FY26.
- **International expansion** — New Zealand, Singapore, London and US build-out; international net revenue stepped up sharply (+220% in the FY26 update) on acquisitions.
- **Licence-fee and technology revenue** — ~25% broker-licence-fee revenue growth for FY26; platform/automation monetisation.
- **Recurring catalysts** — half-year (Feb) and full-year (Aug) results, M&A announcements, organic premium-rate prints, CEO succession (founder Robert Kelly's planned retirement), and the broader insurance-pricing cycle.

## Bull Case vs Bear Case

### Bull case
- A proven, repeatable acquisitive-compounder with a capital-light, cash-generative, fully franked-dividend model.
- Network effects and scale leadership in the SME/independent-broker segment give a durable moat.
- Twin tailwinds: hard-market premium inflation plus network expansion; structural growth with low cyclicality.
- Long runway for consolidation in a still-fragmented Australasian and international broker market.

### Bear case
- A softening insurance-rate cycle removes the commission-growth tailwind and exposes how much of "growth" was rate-driven.
- Roll-up risk: integration missteps, paying up for acquisitions, or running out of attractive targets.
- Key-person/succession risk around founder-CEO Robert Kelly's retirement (and the October–November 2025 governance episode).
- Regulatory scrutiny of broker remuneration/commissions could pressure the fee model.

## Key Risks

- **Pricing-cycle reversal** — a soft insurance market slows commission and revenue growth.
- **Acquisition/integration risk** — the model depends on continuous, well-priced M&A; overpaying or poor integration erodes returns.
- **Succession / key-person** — founder Robert Kelly's planned retirement and CEO transition.
- **Regulatory** — reviews of broker commissions and conflicted remuneration in financial advice/insurance distribution.
- **Leverage** — debt taken on to fund acquisitions; covenant headroom matters if cash flows soften.

## Valuation & How the Stock Trades

Steadfast trades as a **quality structural-growth financial** — the market pays a premium-to-market earnings multiple for the recurring, capital-light, acquisitive-compounder model rather than valuing it on book value like an underwriter or REIT. Mechanically:

- **Valuation anchor** — a P/E re-rated above the broad financials sector reflects the predictability of fee/commission revenue and the M&A growth algorithm; the debate is whether premium-rate growth (cyclical) is being mistaken for structural growth.
- **Earnings behaviour** — the stock moves on organic premium-rate growth, the size/cadence of acquisitions, and underlying EBITDA/NPAT versus guidance at the half-year (Feb) and full-year (Aug) results.
- **Capital return** — a steady, fully franked dividend; the franking and consistency suit income-plus-growth investors.
- **Beta & flows** — a mid-cap ASX-200 financial held for its consistent acquisitive-growth model; lower cyclicality than the underwriters it distributes for.
- **Sensitivity** — less rate-sensitive than insurers or REITs; the dominant macro variable is the insurance-pricing cycle, not bond yields.

## History

Steadfast was established in April 1996 with 43 founding brokerages, under the vision of co-founder Robert B. Kelly, who had more than five decades of insurance sector experience. The company's formation addressed the challenge faced by independent general insurance brokers competing against larger multinational brokerages without collective buying power or scale.

The group expanded its broker network over the following decades and in 2005 made a key acquisition of a 50% stake in Miramar Underwriting Agency, marking strategic entry into the underwriting agency segment. By 2013, Steadfast operated a network of 278 brokers generating approximately A$3.9 billion in Gross Written Premium (GWP), with five underwriting agencies contributing A$114 million in GWP.

Steadfast listed on the ASX in August 2013 at A$1.15 per share. Since listing, Steadfast has pursued a consistent strategy of bolt-on acquisitions (of both brokers and underwriting agencies), international expansion into New Zealand, Singapore, London and the US, and investment in technology to automate placement and claims workflows.

Notable recent acquisitions include Novum Underwriting Partners (August 2025), contributing approximately US$140 million in GWP and 60% organic growth in its first period of ownership; Insurebot (July 2025); and Rothbury (announced 13 November 2025).

## Recent Developments

For FY25 (year ended 30 June 2025) and in a December 2025 FY26 investor update, Steadfast announced A$127.7 million of acquisitions and 2.4% average premium growth in FY26 to 30 November 2025. Group underlying revenue climbed 14.6% to A$1.01 billion, underlying EBITDA rose 12.6% to A$293.6 million and underlying NPAT increased 7.3% to A$137.5 million.

Steadfast's international businesses posted a sharp step-up in net revenue to A$56.4 million, up 220.5%, helped by acquisitions and strong performance in specialty lines, including Novum. The Australasian broker network grew from 402 to 421 brokers, with approximately a 25% increase in broker licence fee revenue for FY26.

On the leadership front, founder and long-serving CEO Robert Kelly announced his intention to retire as Managing Director and CEO, with a successor expected to be announced alongside FY26 results in August 2026. In October 2025, Kelly temporarily stepped aside from the CEO role while an external investigation into a workplace complaint was conducted; the investigation concluded in November 2025 on a confidential basis, and Kelly resumed his role.

The most recent filing (annual) was lodged on 2023-06-30. See [[#Financial Reports]] below.
