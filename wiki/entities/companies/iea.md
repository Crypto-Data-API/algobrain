The International Energy Agency (IEA) is an autonomous intergovernmental organization established in 1974 in response to the [[1973-oil-crisis]] to coordinate energy policy among oil-consuming nations. It has 31 member countries, mostly OECD members, and serves as the primary counterweight to [[opec]] in global energy governance.

## Role in Energy Markets

The IEA was originally created to help member countries respond to major disruptions in oil supply by coordinating emergency response measures, including strategic petroleum reserve (SPR) releases. Over the decades, its role has expanded to encompass comprehensive energy data collection, analysis, and policy recommendations.

For traders, the IEA is one of the most important sources of oil market intelligence — its supply, demand, and inventory estimates are closely watched and can move [[crude-oil]] prices on release.

## Key Publications

### Monthly Oil Market Report (OMR)
The IEA's flagship publication and the **second most watched energy report** in global markets after the [[eia|EIA]] weekly petroleum status report. The OMR provides:

- Global oil demand estimates and revisions (current quarter + forecasts)
- Non-OPEC supply estimates
- [[opec]] production tracking
- OECD commercial inventory data
- Refinery throughput and product market analysis
- "Call on OPEC" — the implied OPEC production needed to balance the market

Revisions to IEA demand forecasts are market-moving events. An upward revision to demand (or downward revision to supply) is bullish for [[crude-oil]]; the reverse is bearish.

### World Energy Outlook (Annual)
The IEA's annual long-term energy outlook, published every November. Includes multiple scenarios:

- **Stated Policies Scenario (STEPS)**: Based on existing policies and announced intentions
- **Announced Pledges Scenario (APS)**: Assumes all government climate pledges are met
- **Net Zero by 2050 (NZE)**: The pathway to net-zero emissions by 2050

The NZE scenario is a key reference for energy transition investment analysis — it implies peak oil demand before 2030 and a dramatic reshaping of the global energy mix.

### Medium-Term Oil Market Report
Annual 5-year forecast of oil supply and demand, used by industry and governments for medium-term planning.

## Strategic Petroleum Reserve Coordination

One of the IEA's original and most impactful functions is coordinating emergency petroleum reserve releases among member nations:

- Members are required to maintain oil stocks equivalent to at least 90 days of net imports
- The IEA has coordinated collective releases in 1991 (Gulf War), 2005 (Hurricane Katrina), 2011 (Libya conflict), and 2022 (Russia-Ukraine conflict)
- The 2022 coordinated release was the largest in history — 182 million barrels, combined with the US unilateral release of an additional ~180 million barrels from the SPR
- SPR release announcements can cause sharp short-term drops in [[crude-oil]] prices

## Clean Energy Transition Focus

Since the mid-2010s, the IEA has increasingly focused on clean energy and the energy transition:

- Tracks global renewable energy deployment, EV adoption, and energy efficiency
- Its annual "World Energy Investment" report is the most comprehensive survey of global energy investment flows
- The IEA's shift toward climate-focused analysis has occasionally created tension with some member states and oil-producing countries

## 2025-2026 Developments

The 2026 Middle East conflict involving Iran transformed the IEA's market outlook and triggered its emergency-response machinery:

- **May 2026 Oil Market Report:** The IEA forecast world oil demand to *contract* by 420 kb/d year-on-year in 2026, to 104 mb/d — roughly 1.3 mb/d below its pre-conflict forecast — with the steepest decline (-2.45 mb/d) in 2Q26. Petrochemicals and aviation were the most affected sectors.
- **Severe undersupply, not surplus:** Before the conflict, the IEA had been forecasting a 2026 supply surplus (it cut demand-growth forecasts while keeping a surplus view as late as early 2026). The May 2026 OMR reversed this: even assuming the conflict ended by early June, the market was projected to remain severely undersupplied through end-3Q26, with a cumulative liquids deficit reaching ~900 mb by September 2026 and inventories falling until a modest 4Q26 surplus begins rebuilding stocks.
- **Coordinated stock release:** The IEA coordinated a ~400 mb collective stock release — by far the largest in its history, dwarfing the 182 mb release of 2022 — reducing the drawdown burden on industry stocks to ~500 mb.

This episode is a live case study in how IEA report revisions and SPR coordination drive [[crude-oil]] price action: forecast swings of >1 mb/d between consecutive monthly reports produced sharp repricing across the crude curve.

## Trading Relevance

- **Monthly OMR release** (usually mid-month, ~09:00 London time) is a scheduled volatility event for Brent/WTI futures and energy equities; demand/supply revisions matter more than levels.
- The IEA's demand view is structurally lower than [[opec]]'s — the IEA-OPEC demand-forecast spread is itself a watched sentiment indicator.
- Coordinated stock-release headlines are immediate bearish shocks to flat price but typically steepen backwardation reversal trades; the 2022 and 2026 releases both saw initial sell-offs followed by recoveries as structural deficits reasserted.
