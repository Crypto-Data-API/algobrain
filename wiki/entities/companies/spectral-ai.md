Spectral AI, Inc. (NASDAQ: MDAI) is a Dallas-based medical-AI company developing **DeepView**, a predictive imaging device that uses multispectral imaging plus AI to assess burn-wound healing potential at the point of care — distinguishing tissue that will heal from tissue that will not, before clinical signs are visible. It is a micro-cap, pre-broad-commercialisation name whose stock is driven by binary regulatory and government-contract catalysts rather than recurring revenue.

> **Speculative micro-cap warning.** MDAI is pre-broad-commercialization, dependent on US government (BARDA) funding and a binary FDA clearance, and unprofitable — so equity raises and [[dilution]] are recurring stock-moving events. Nothing here is investment advice. Size for total loss.

## Business Overview

Spectral's value proposition is decision support for burn surgeons: DeepView aims to reduce the guesswork (and unnecessary surgery) in determining burn depth and healing trajectory. The business model is a mix of (1) device/diagnostic commercialisation pending FDA clearance, and (2) US government funding — Spectral has been substantially supported by **BARDA** (the Biomedical Advanced Research and Development Authority) under multi-year development contracts, which fund R&D and trials. As a micro-cap, the key fundamentals watch-items are FDA regulatory status, BARDA contract milestones/funding, trial readouts, and cash runway/dilution.

## Product & Technology (detail)

DeepView combines **multispectral imaging** (capturing tissue reflectance across multiple wavelengths beyond visible light) with an [[artificial-intelligence|AI]] classifier trained to predict healing potential. The clinical pain point it targets: burn-depth assessment is traditionally a subjective clinical judgement that is frequently wrong, leading to delayed or unnecessary excision/grafting. DeepView aims to give an objective, pre-symptomatic readout at the point of care.

| Dimension | Detail |
|---|---|
| Modality | Multispectral imaging + AI healing-potential prediction |
| Primary indication | Burn-wound depth / healing assessment (lead) |
| Customer | Burn surgeons, burn centres, military/mass-casualty triage |
| Revenue model (planned) | Device + diagnostic/recurring use, pending FDA clearance |
| Non-dilutive funder | **BARDA** multi-year development contracts (R&D + trials) |

The dual model is distinctive: BARDA funding both validates the technology (a sophisticated government buyer) and reduces dilution during development — but it also creates dependence on a single government program, and a non-renewal would force the company harder onto the equity market.

## Competitive Positioning

- **Standard of care** is clinical/visual burn-depth judgement and, secondarily, laser Doppler imaging — DeepView's pitch is faster, objective, pre-symptomatic prediction at the bedside.
- **Moat candidates:** proprietary multispectral hardware + trained AI model, accumulated BARDA-funded clinical datasets, and the regulatory head start if FDA clearance lands first in this niche.
- **Scale:** classic micro-cap; thematic AI-healthcare peers ([[tempus-ai]], [[recursion-pharmaceuticals]], [[schrödinger]]) are sympathy links, not competitors — MDAI's drivers are largely idiosyncratic (FDA, BARDA).

## Bull vs Bear Case

**Bull case**

- Differentiated, validated technology with a sophisticated non-dilutive backer (BARDA) — government funding is both cash and a credibility signal.
- FDA clearance is a discrete, potentially large re-rating catalyst that opens commercial burn-care and triage markets.
- Optionality beyond burns (other wound-care / tissue-viability applications) on the same multispectral-AI platform.
- Tiny enterprise value means commercial traction or a partnership could re-rate the stock several-fold.

**Bear case**

- Pre-broad-commercialization with no meaningful recurring revenue; the thesis is entirely binary regulatory + government-contract optionality.
- **Concentration on BARDA** — a single government program is the primary funding pillar; renewal/expansion risk is existential.
- Unprofitable → recurring equity raises and [[dilution]] while the company funds itself to commercialization.
- Slow medical-device commercial adoption and reimbursement uncertainty even after clearance.
- Micro-cap liquidity and NASDAQ continued-listing risk in a prolonged drawdown.

- **Regulatory pathway**: progressing DeepView toward US FDA clearance for burn indication, with pivotal-trial work supporting the submission; FDA clearance is the central binary catalyst (Verified via Perplexity, 2026-06-10).
- **BARDA funding**: continued reliance on US government (BARDA) contract funding as the primary non-dilutive source supporting development.
- **Cash and dilution**: as a pre-revenue/early-commercial micro-cap, the company's cash position and any equity raises are recurring stock-moving events.
