# TickerTags

TickerTags was a social-media listening and alt-data platform for institutional investors, co-founded in 2015 in Dallas, Texas by [[chris-camillo]] after roughly two years of development. Its core product mapped approximately 350,000 "tags" -- product names, brands, competitors, and industry terms -- to around 8,000 publicly-traded tickers, using mention-frequency spikes on social media as an alternative-data signal for equity price moves.

## Product

TickerTags ingested social-media streams (primarily Twitter/X, with other public sources) and maintained a curated taxonomy of tags connected to each covered ticker. Analysts and portfolio managers subscribed to real-time and historical dashboards showing mention-volume and sentiment changes per ticker.

The edge the platform sold was the systematized version of [[social-arbitrage]]: institutional users could screen thousands of tickers for unusual social-mention activity without having to observe consumer trends themselves. In practice this meant detecting product launches, viral marketing outcomes, competitor erosion, and consumer-preference shifts weeks before they showed up in earnings or sell-side research.

## Notable Outputs

- **Brexit referendum forecast, June 2016.** TickerTags' aggregated mention data correctly leaned toward the Leave outcome when political betting markets and sell-side polls favored Remain. The call was widely cited in the press and helped build TickerTags' institutional reputation.
- **Netflix subscription trend calls, 2016-2018.** Referenced in Camillo's interviews as a demonstration of the platform's ability to forecast streaming-service revenue inflections ahead of earnings.
- **Consumer product launches.** The platform was used to track the initial traction of a range of retail and CPG products ahead of the earnings that would reveal their revenue impact.

## Acquisition and Current Status

TickerTags was acquired by **[[m-science|M Science]]** -- the alternative-data subsidiary of [[jefferies-financial-group|Jefferies Financial Group]] -- around June 2018. The standalone retail/institutional product was wound down approximately 2020; the underlying data pipelines and taxonomy were absorbed into M Science's institutional research product line, where they continue to inform equity research delivered to Jefferies' buy-side clients.

## Significance

TickerTags is notable for three reasons:

1. It was one of the earliest attempts to productize [[social-arbitrage]] as an institutional alt-data feed, predating most of the major crypto-sentiment providers.
2. Its acquisition by M Science in 2018 is a concrete data point in the broader narrative of alt-data commoditization -- the movement of edge sources from retail-accessible platforms to institutional-only channels.
3. Its founding by Camillo gives it an unusual provenance: most alt-data firms are founded by data engineers; TickerTags was founded by a practicing trader whose own 15-year track record depended on the methodology he productized.

## Trading Relevance

TickerTags is a defunct standalone product but remains a relevant case study for traders:

- **Lesson on edge decay**: Its arc — retail-accessible social signal → institutional acquisition → product wind-down — is a textbook example of an alpha source migrating from public access to institutional-only channels as it gets commoditized (see [[alternative-data-providers]]).
- **Social-arbitrage proof of concept**: It demonstrated that systematized consumer-mention data could front-run earnings, validating [[social-arbitrage]] as practiced by co-founder [[chris-camillo]].
- **No direct exposure**: Privately held and absorbed into [[m-science]] (a [[jefferies-financial-group|Jefferies]] subsidiary); traders cannot access the product, only the lesson.
