VeVe is a mobile-first licensed digital collectibles app operated by parent company ECOMI (ECOMI Technology Pte. Ltd., registered in Singapore), which was co-founded in April 2017 by David Yu, Daniel Crothers, and Joseph Janik. The founding team has strong New Zealand roots — CEO David Yu is Auckland-based — while the legal/corporate entity sits in Singapore. VeVe sells officially licensed digital collectibles from major entertainment and consumer-brand IP holders — including Disney, Marvel, Star Wars, Lamborghini, and others — displayed via augmented reality and tradeable only inside VeVe's walled-garden marketplace, distinguishing it from open Ethereum-based NFT trading.

## History and founding

ECOMI was founded in April 2017 by David Yu, Daniel Crothers, and Joseph Janik, with its corporate home in Singapore (ECOMI Technology Pte. Ltd.) and much of its founding-team footprint in Auckland, New Zealand. The product was initially called "ECOMI Collect" before being rebranded to VeVe. The initial product thesis was that mainstream collectors of pop-culture memorabilia — action figures, comics, movie memorabilia — would adopt digital collectibles if the experience mirrored physical collecting (boxed, displayed, traded) rather than the more speculative, trait-driven framing of Ethereum NFT culture.

ECOMI issued the **OMI token**, originally a GO20 token on the **GoChain** network, as the internal value-transfer medium for VeVe's ecosystem. VeVe later built on **Immutable X** as the underlying infrastructure for collectible NFTs, giving the platform fast, gas-free minting and trading.

VeVe's growth was driven by signing a series of major licensing deals, giving it rights to issue digital collectibles based on popular film, TV, and comic franchises. Drops sold out quickly during the 2021 NFT bull cycle, and the secondary market inside the app saw significant activity. Volume contracted sharply in the subsequent bear market, consistent with broader licensed-IP NFT trends.

## What it does

- **Licensed digital figures**: NFTs representing 3D models of licensed characters (Marvel, DC, Disney, etc.) with editions ranging from common to ultra-rare.
- **AR display**: The VeVe mobile app renders collectibles in augmented reality, letting owners pose and photograph figures in real-world environments.
- **Daily drops**: New releases are scheduled on a near-daily cadence, often sold in randomized boxes or fixed-edition releases.
- **Internal secondary market**: Buyers and sellers transact within the app's marketplace. Listings are denominated in "gems" (the in-app currency) or USD.
- **Closed ecosystem**: Collectibles cannot be freely withdrawn to Ethereum and traded on [[opensea]], [[blur]], or [[magic-eden]]. Trading is confined to the VeVe app.

## Trading relevance

VeVe sits outside the open-NFT trading stack and should be evaluated on its own terms:

- **Walled-garden model**: Because collectibles cannot be moved to open marketplaces, price discovery happens only inside the app. This produces persistent information asymmetry versus open NFT markets and complicates portfolio accounting for traders used to on-chain transparency.
- **Not substitutable with open NFTs**: Traders should not treat VeVe figures as interchangeable with ERC-721 NFTs. Custody, redeemability, and liquidity are governed by VeVe's terms of service and its parent ECOMI, not by Ethereum smart contracts.
- **Licensing concentration risk**: VeVe's inventory value is tied to ongoing licensing relationships with Disney, Marvel, DC, and others. Loss of a major license would materially affect the tradeability of entire collections already sold to users.
- **OMI token exposure**: Speculation on OMI is distinct from speculation on VeVe collectibles; OMI price movements reflect ECOMI's ecosystem rather than a specific collectible's scarcity.
- **Comparison**: VeVe is closer in structure to [[nba-top-shot]] (another walled-garden licensed collectibles product from [[dapper-labs]]) than to open-trading NFT platforms.

## Current status

As of April 2026, VeVe continues to operate as a mobile app selling licensed collectibles with an internal secondary market. The company has not announced a migration to open-market trading of its collectibles, and the platform remains structurally distinct from Ethereum-based NFT venues.
