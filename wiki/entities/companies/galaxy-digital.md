Galaxy Digital (NASDAQ: GLXY) is the crypto-native financial services and AI-infrastructure firm founded by former Fortress/Goldman macro trader **Mike Novogratz**. It spans three businesses — global markets (trading/lending), asset management, and digital infrastructure — and has become a hybrid crypto-brokerage-plus-AI-data-center story, making it one of the more institutionally relevant listed crypto proxies.

## Business Overview

Galaxy is a vertically integrated crypto-financial-services firm that has bolted on a large AI/HPC data-center business, giving it a rare combination of trading-desk crypto beta and contracted infrastructure cash flow.

## Business Segments

- **Global Markets** — institutional crypto trading, derivatives, and lending; one of the largest OTC crypto counterparties (it executed bulk BTC sales for large holders, e.g. the widely reported ~$9B Satoshi-era whale sale in mid-2025).
- **Asset Management** — ETFs/funds and venture; roughly **$8B of assets on platform** as of Q1 2026.
- **Digital Infrastructure (Helios)** — the West Texas campus Galaxy converted from [[bitcoin]] mining to AI/HPC data centers. **CoreWeave has leased 526 MW for 15 years**, with expected average annual revenue **over $1B** and lease-level EBITDA margins around 90%; the first data hall was delivered and Phase I rent commences in **Q2 2026**. ERCOT approvals lift total approved Helios capacity to **over 1.6 GW**.

The dual identity — crypto financial services **plus** AI data-center landlord — is what differentiates GLXY from both pure crypto proxies and pure miner-to-AI converts.

## Bull vs Bear Case

**Bull case**
- Diversified: crypto trading/asset-management cash flow plus a long-dated, high-margin Helios lease book provides two distinct re-rating vectors.
- Helios/CoreWeave deal is large, contracted and high-margin (lease-level EBITDA ~90% per disclosures); further hyperscaler leases against >1.6 GW of approved capacity are pure optionality.
- Nasdaq uplisting (May 2025) broadened US institutional ownership and options access.
- Strong brand and counterparty status in institutional crypto under [[microstrategy|Mike Novogratz]]'s leadership.

**Bear case**
- Crypto drawdowns hit Global Markets revenue and any balance-sheet crypto/treasury marks simultaneously.
- Heavy counterparty concentration in CoreWeave for the Helios thesis.
- Data-center build-out is capex-intensive with execution and delivery risk.
- Earnings are lumpy — trading and venture marks add volatility that complicates valuation.

## Valuation Framework

Galaxy is a sum-of-the-parts story; the qualitative levers:

- **Book value / tangible equity** — a base anchor for the financial-services and balance-sheet crypto components, similar to how broker-dealers are valued.
- **Crypto beta** — Global Markets revenue and any treasury marks move with [[bitcoin]]/ETH; the equity carries a built-in option on crypto-cycle activity.
- **Helios contracted-revenue value** — discounted long-dated CoreWeave lease rent (>$1B/yr expected, ~90% lease EBITDA margin) valued on a data-center multiple, plus optionality on the unleased >1.6 GW.
- **Asset-management franchise value** — fee/carry stream scaling with assets on platform.
- **EV per MW** — the cross-peer infrastructure yardstick shared with [[cipher-mining]]/[[core-scientific]].
- **Execution/concentration discount** — applied until Helios delivery milestones convert to recurring rent and counterparty risk diversifies.

## Capital Structure & Dilution

- **Equity base** — the Nasdaq uplist and Cayman-to-Delaware reorganization broadened the float and institutional access; ongoing capex may require equity or debt funding.
- **Helios capex** — data-center build-out is capital-intensive; financing terms (project debt vs. equity) affect per-share value and leverage.
- **Balance-sheet crypto** — treasury and trading-book crypto holdings link equity value directly to spot [[bitcoin]]/ETH and add mark-to-market volatility.
- **Counterparty exposure** — lending and OTC activity carry credit/counterparty risk that can stress the balance sheet in crypto drawdowns.

## Risks

- Crypto price drawdowns hitting Global Markets revenue and balance-sheet/treasury marks simultaneously.
- Data-center execution and capex risk on Helios delivery.
- Counterparty concentration in **CoreWeave** for the infrastructure thesis.
- Lumpy, mark-driven trading and venture earnings that complicate valuation.
- Regulatory risk across crypto trading, lending and asset management.
