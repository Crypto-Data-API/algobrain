Blaize Holdings, Inc. (NASDAQ: BZAI) is a micro-cap fabless semiconductor company building programmable, low-power AI chips and software for **edge inference** — deploying AI outside the data center in industrial, defense, automotive, retail, and "physical AI" settings. It went public via SPAC in January 2025. For traders it is a highly speculative, pre-scale AI-hardware story with explosive percentage moves, large losses, and significant dilution risk.

## Business Overview

Blaize designs the Graph Streaming Processor (GSP) architecture and accompanying AI Studio software, targeting energy-efficient inference at the edge where power, latency, and cost constraints rule out big GPUs. Its go-to-market spans edge data centers, sovereign AI, rugged/defense, smart infrastructure, and embedded industrial systems, increasingly through partners (Nokia, Datacomm, Winmate) and a newer **Blaize AI Services** platform that adds recurring, API/per-query software revenue (e.g. face-recognition) on top of hardware. Revenue is still tiny and the company is deeply loss-making — this is an early commercialization, design-win-driven story.

## Business Segments / Products

Blaize sells a stack — silicon, software, and increasingly services — purpose-built for **inference at the edge**, the opposite end of the AI compute spectrum from data-center training GPUs.

| Product / layer | What it is | Differentiation | Revenue type |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Graph Streaming Processor (GSP)** | Programmable AI accelerator architecture | Streaming dataflow; energy-efficient, low-latency edge inference vs. big GPUs | Hardware / chips |
| **AI Studio** | Software toolchain to build/deploy edge AI without deep coding | Lowers integration friction; ties customers to the platform | Software / attach |
| **Blaize AI Services** | API/per-query application platform (e.g. face recognition) | Adds recurring software revenue on top of hardware | Recurring / per-query |
| **Modules & systems** | Boards/systems via partners (Nokia, Datacomm, Winmate) | Reach into rugged/defense/industrial markets | Hardware via partners |

**Why the GSP architecture.** Training runs in data centers on large [[nvidia]] GPUs; **inference at the edge** runs on devices where power, heat, latency, and cost are tightly constrained — factories, cameras, vehicles, defense gear. Blaize's GSP is designed for exactly that envelope: programmable enough to run varied models, but far lower power than a data-center GPU. AI Studio reduces the integration effort, and Blaize AI Services layers recurring per-query software revenue on top — the strategic move from a pure chip vendor toward a higher-margin platform. (See [[edge-ai]], [[model-inference-vs-training]], and [[model-deployment]].)

## Competitive Positioning / Peers

Blaize competes in the fragmented **edge-AI inference** silicon market — distinct from data-center training, where [[nvidia]] dominates.

| Company | Ticker | Focus | Blaize comparison |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Blaize** | BZAI | Programmable GSP edge-inference chips + software | Micro-cap, pre-scale, design-win-driven |
| [[nvidia]] | NVDA | Data-center training/inference GPUs; Jetson at edge | The 800-lb gorilla; Jetson competes at the high end of edge |
| [[ambarella]] | AMBA | Edge vision/AI SoCs (cameras, automotive) | Closest profile; established edge-vision peer |
| Lattice (LSCC) | LSCC | Low-power FPGAs at the edge | Low-power edge programmable-logic peer |

Blaize's relative position: a tiny, late-commercializing challenger competing on programmability and power-efficiency against both giants ([[nvidia]] Jetson, Qualcomm) and an established edge-vision peer ([[ambarella]]). Its survival depends on converting design wins and partnerships into volume before cash runs out.

## Bull vs Bear Case

**Bull case**
- Edge-AI inference is a large, growing market structurally distinct from data-center training — room for specialized players.
- Design-win pipeline (NeoTensr expanded to ~$70M potential value, Winmate, Nokia, Datacomm) could convert to a steep revenue ramp.
- Gross margin improved sharply (toward ~58%), and Blaize AI Services adds recurring software revenue.
- Programmable GSP + AI Studio is a differentiated, lower-power alternative to GPUs at the edge.

**Bear case**
- Pre-scale: trailing revenue is tiny, and FY2026 guidance (~$130M) implies a massive ramp that depends entirely on pipeline conversion.
- Deeply loss-making with ongoing adjusted-EBITDA losses; cash runway depends on continued equity raises (heavy [[dilution]] risk).
- Competes against [[nvidia]], Qualcomm, and [[ambarella]] — far better-capitalized and entrenched.
- SPAC-listed micro-cap: thin float, extreme volatility, weak institutional sponsorship.
- Design wins are not orders; potential contract values can shrink or slip.

## Valuation Framework

Blaize is pre-revenue-scale, so traditional multiples are meaningless; the analysis is pipeline-and-runway-driven:

- **Design-win pipeline and conversion** — backlog of potential contract value (e.g., NeoTensr ~$70M potential, Winmate, Nokia) and how much converts to firm orders/revenue.
- **Revenue ramp vs. guidance** — the gap between current run-rate and FY2026 guidance (~$130M) is the central credibility test.
- **Gross-margin trajectory** — the move toward ~58% signals product/mix improvement.
- **Cash runway and burn** — quarters of cash left at current adjusted-EBITDA loss; the survival clock.
- **Dilution / share count** — equity raises (e.g., ~$35M raised) and their effect on per-share value.
- **Recurring revenue mix (Blaize AI Services)** — the higher-multiple software component.

The dominant question for a pre-scale chip name: does the design-win pipeline convert into revenue before the cash runway forces dilutive raises.

## Capital Structure & Dilution Risk

As a pre-scale, deeply loss-making micro-cap, Blaize funds itself primarily through **equity issuance** — it raised ~$35M in the most recent quarter, and ongoing adjusted-EBITDA losses (FY2026 guidance implies a ~$45-50M loss) mean repeated raises are likely until revenue scales. This makes [[dilution]] the central financial risk: each raise at a depressed price erodes per-share value, and SPAC-era warrants/earnouts can add overhang. Watch quarterly cash burn, the implied runway, ATM/secondary activity, and warrant structures. (See [[dilution]].)
