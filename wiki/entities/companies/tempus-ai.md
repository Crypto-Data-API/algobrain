Tempus AI, Inc. (NASDAQ: TEM) is an AI-enabled precision-medicine company that combines one of the largest libraries of clinical and molecular oncology data with machine-learning tools to support diagnostics, therapy selection and pharma research. Founded by Eric Lefkofsky (Groupon co-founder) and IPO'd in June 2024, it sits at the intersection of healthcare and AI, making it a high-profile, high-volatility growth name. For traders it is a momentum/AI-narrative stock whose path to profitability and data-licensing economics are the central debate.

## Business Overview

Tempus operates three revenue lines:

- **Genomics** — its largest segment; sequencing and molecular diagnostics (oncology, hereditary, and increasingly cardiology/neuro), generating per-test revenue and building its proprietary data asset.
- **Data and Services** — licensing of de-identified multimodal clinical/molecular data and analytics to pharmaceutical and biotech partners — the higher-margin, strategically prized part of the model.
- **AI applications** — algorithmic tests and clinical-decision tools (e.g. cardiology AI from its Deep 6 / Mpirik-style and Ambry Genetics expansions).

The 2024-2025 acquisition of Ambry Genetics materially expanded its hereditary-testing footprint. The bull case is the flywheel: more tests generate more data, which improves AI models and feeds lucrative pharma data deals; the bear case is heavy cash burn and the difficulty of monetizing data at scale.

## Bull vs Bear Case

**Bull case**
- Data flywheel: scale of multimodal clinical+molecular data is hard to replicate and improves with volume.
- High-margin Data & Services line can re-rate the whole company if it compounds.
- Riding the AI-in-healthcare secular narrative with real proprietary data, not just a label.
- Ambry expands footprint beyond oncology into hereditary/other specialties.

**Bear case**
- Persistent net losses and meaningful cash burn; profitability path is the core debate.
- Data monetization at scale is unproven and lumpy (deal-timing dependent).
- Reimbursement/regulatory exposure on the diagnostics base.
- Founder-control governance; high short interest and sentiment-driven valuation.

## Valuation Framework (dual software + diagnostics)

Tempus must be valued on a sum-of-parts basis spanning two very different economic engines (qualitative):

- **Diagnostics base** — valued on test volume × reimbursement × gross margin; a healthcare-services multiple on the genomics line.
- **Data & Services / AI** — the higher-multiple, software-like asset; valued on revenue growth, gross margin and the durability of pharma data-deal bookings (closer to a SaaS/data-platform framing).
- **Path to profitability** — because losses persist, [[cash-runway]] and the trajectory of operating leverage matter more than any single multiple.
- **Sentiment premium** — a large, variable AI-narrative premium sits on top; valuation compresses fast if AI enthusiasm fades. See [[total-addressable-market]] for the TAM framing the bull case leans on.

## Capital Structure & Dilution Risk

Tempus is loss-making with meaningful cash burn (per file), so continued access to capital markets matters. As a relatively recent IPO (June 2024), it carries lockup/insider-selling dynamics that can pressure the share price, and acquisitions (e.g. Ambry) can be funded with cash, debt or equity. Founder Eric Lefkofsky's control structure concentrates governance. The key items to monitor are [[cash-runway]], operating-leverage trajectory, and share-count growth from any raises or stock-based compensation. No dividend.
