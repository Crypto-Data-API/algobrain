KLA Corporation (NASDAQ: KLAC) is the dominant supplier of **process control and yield management** equipment for semiconductor manufacturing — the inspection and metrology tools every leading-edge fab needs more of as chips get harder to make. One of the "Big 5" semicap names (with [[applied-materials]], [[lam-research]], [[asml]], Tokyo Electron), it is an [[s-and-p-500]] constituent and a core AI-infrastructure derivative with the highest margins in the equipment group. Formed from the 1997 merger of KLA Instruments and Tencor; renamed KLA in 2019.

## Business Overview & Segments

KLA holds **>50% share of the process-control market** — optical and e-beam wafer inspection, metrology, and reticle/mask inspection — a quasi-monopoly position analogous to [[asml]]'s in lithography. As process nodes shrink and device architectures go 3D (gate-all-around transistors, HBM stacking, advanced packaging), **process-control intensity rises faster than overall wafer-fab equipment (WFE) spend** — every new defect mode and every additional process step needs more inspection. That structural mix shift is the core bull case. A large installed base drives a high-margin, recurring **service** business (~$2.5B+/yr) that smooths the equipment cycle.

| Segment / line | What it is | Revenue mix (qualitative) |
|---|---|---|
| **Semiconductor Process Control** | Optical/e-beam wafer inspection, metrology, reticle inspection, defect review and yield analytics for leading-edge logic, memory and advanced packaging | The overwhelming majority (~90%) of revenue and the moat |
| **Specialty Semiconductor Process** | Etch, deposition and process tools for specialty/legacy device makers | Small but profitable adjacency |
| **PCB, Display & Component Inspection** | Inspection/test for printed circuit boards, displays and components | Smaller, more cyclical, lower-margin |
| **Services** (across segments) | Maintenance, spares, upgrades on the installed base | Recurring, high-margin, counter-cyclical — the cycle smoother |

The advantage is not one tool but a **defect-and-yield knowledge base** built over decades — KLA's inspectors generate the data fabs use to ramp new nodes, so designing it out risks yield. That makes process control the highest-margin corner of semicap (60%+ gross, ~40% operating).

## 2025–2026 Developments

- **FY2025 (ended 2025-06-30)**: record revenue **$12.16B**, GAAP net income **$4.06B**, GAAP diluted EPS **$30.37** (matching the fundamentals above). Q4 FY25 revenue $3.175B, GAAP EPS $9.06.
- **AI buildout**: management frames KLA as a direct enabler of the AI infrastructure ramp — leading-edge foundry/logic (TSMC N2), HBM/memory, and **advanced packaging**, where KLA raised its CY2025 advanced-packaging systems revenue target to **>$925M** (a business growing >30%/yr from inspection of chiplets/CoWoS-class integration).
- **China export controls**: BIS restrictions continue to bite — China fell from ~41-44% of revenue (2023-24 peak) toward an expected **15-30% in 2025-26**; analysts flag subcomponent-loophole tightening and annual license regimes (e.g., 2026 licenses for Samsung/SK Hynix China fabs) as ongoing policy risk in both directions.
- Dividend raised to ~$6.85-7.60/yr range with continued large buybacks; D/E has steadily fallen from the 2022 leveraged-buyback peak visible in the 5-period trend above.

## Economic Moat & Competitive Position

KLA has one of the **widest moats in semicap**, narrower in scope than [[asml]]'s lithography monopoly but similar in character — a near-monopoly in its niche:

- **Dominant share + technology lead** — >50% of process control; in optical patterned-wafer inspection the share is even higher. Rivals are a generation behind on sensitivity and throughput.
- **Data and yield-knowledge moat** — KLA's tools and the defect/yield data they generate are embedded in customers' node-ramp recipes. Switching inspection vendors mid-ramp risks yield, creating heavy [[switching-costs]].
- **Installed base + service flywheel** — a large fleet of tools feeds recurring, high-margin service and upgrade revenue, and keeps KLA close to every fab's roadmap.
- **R&D scale** — process-control complexity rises with every node; KLA's revenue base funds the R&D no smaller rival can match, widening the gap.

The result is the best [[economic-moat|economic moat]] economics in the equipment group. The watch items are China indigenization (domestic inspection startups, state-funded) and any disruptive metrology approach; neither is close today, but both are the long-term threats to the monopoly.

## Growth Drivers & Catalysts

- **Process-control intensity** — leading-edge logic ([[tsmc]] N2 and beyond), gate-all-around transistors and 3D scaling raise inspection content per wafer faster than overall WFE.
- **Advanced packaging** — CoWoS/chiplet integration for AI accelerators needs new inspection; KLA raised its CY2025 advanced-packaging systems target to **>$925M**, a >30%/yr line.
- **HBM/memory** — high-bandwidth-memory stacking for AI adds inspection steps; memory capex recovery is a swing factor.
- **AI buildout** — KLA frames itself as a direct enabler of the [[nvidia]]-driven AI infrastructure ramp.
- **Services** — recurring, growing with the installed base; a structural margin and stability driver.
- **Capital return** — rising dividend plus large buybacks against strong free cash flow.
- **Recurring catalysts** — [[tsmc]]/Samsung/Intel capex guidance, WFE forecasts, BIS export-control rule changes, HBM/advanced-packaging capacity announcements, quarterly guides (late Jan/Apr/Jul/Oct).

## Bull Case vs Bear Case

### Bull case
- Process-control intensity rises faster than WFE every node — KLA grows share of a growing pie structurally.
- Quasi-monopoly economics: highest margins in semicap, deep [[switching-costs]], a data/yield moat that is hard to dislodge.
- Advanced packaging and HBM open a new, fast-growing demand pool tied directly to AI.
- Recurring service revenue smooths the cycle; strong free cash flow funds dividend growth and buybacks.

### Bear case
- **Cyclicality** — WFE orders swing ±30% peak-to-trough; the stock looks cheapest at the top of the cycle and de-rates into downturns.
- **China policy** — BIS export controls are an unpredictable two-way swing factor on revenue and guidance.
- **China indigenization** — state-funded domestic inspection competitors are the long-term share threat.
- **Customer concentration** — a handful of leading-edge fabs ([[tsmc]], Samsung, Intel, top memory makers) drive most leading-edge demand.
- **Valuation** — a premium multiple leaves little room for a capex air-pocket or a missed guide.

## Key Risks

- **Cycle risk** — semicap is deeply cyclical; orders can fall sharply in a WFE downturn and the market prices the cycle 6-12 months ahead.
- **China export controls** — BIS restrictions cut China from ~41-44% of revenue (2023-24 peak) toward an expected 15-30% in 2025-26; rule changes move the stock multiple percent in a day, in both directions.
- **Geopolitical / indigenization** — long-run risk that China builds domestic process-control capability.
- **Customer concentration** — leading-edge demand rides on a few large fabs' capex decisions.
- **Technology risk** — a disruptive metrology/inspection approach, though none is close, would threaten the monopoly over time.
- **Valuation/positioning** — premium multiple and tight correlation to the SOX amplify drawdowns on AI-sentiment shocks.

## Trading Relevance

- **Semicap beta**: KLAC trades in a tight cluster with [[applied-materials]] (AMAT) and [[lam-research]] (LRCX) and the SOX index; it typically commands a premium multiple for its margin/moat profile (60%+ gross, ~40% operating).
- **Catalysts**: TSMC/Samsung/Intel capex guidance, [[nvidia]] data-center results (sentiment driver), WFE forecasts, BIS export-control rule changes (single biggest headline risk — multi-percent single-day moves), HBM/advanced-packaging capacity announcements, quarterly guides (late Jan/Apr/Jul/Oct).
- **Cyclicality**: orders can swing ±30% peak-to-trough; the market prices the cycle 6-12 months ahead — fundamentals look best at the top.
- Highly liquid options; a standard pair-trade leg against AMAT/LRCX and a frequent vehicle for China-policy event trades.

## Valuation & How the Stock Trades

KLAC trades on the **WFE/semicap cycle plus a quality premium** — the highest multiple in the equipment group, justified by the best margins and the deepest moat, but cyclical at heart:

- **Cycle-priced** — the multiple expands and contracts with the semicap cycle; the market discounts orders 6-12 months ahead, so the stock often peaks while reported fundamentals still look strong.
- **Premium to peers** — KLAC typically trades above [[applied-materials]] and [[lam-research]] on its monopoly-like process-control economics and recurring service base.
- **Beta** — moderately high, moving with the SOX index and AI-capex sentiment; sharp single-day moves on China export-control headlines.
- **Capital return** — a growing dividend (a modest yield) plus large buybacks; total return blends cyclical earnings growth with cash return.
- **Correlated trades** — a standard pair-trade leg against AMAT/LRCX and a frequent vehicle for China-policy event trades and AI-derivative baskets with [[nvidia]] and [[tsmc]].
