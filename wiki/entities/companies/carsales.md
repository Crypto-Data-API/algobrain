**CAR Group Limited** (ASX: CAR) is an internet content & information company listed on the [[asx-limited|Australian Securities Exchange]] and a constituent of the [[asx-200]] index. CAR Group is one of the largest online automotive marketplace groups in the world, headquartered in Melbourne. It owns and operates the leading digital vehicle marketplaces in Australia (carsales), South Korea (Encar), the United States (Trader Interactive) and Chile (chileautos), and is the majority owner of webmotors in Brazil.

## Business Model & Segments

CAR Group reports six segments: Australia - Online Advertising Services; Australia - Data, Research and Services; Investments; North America; Latin America; and Asia. It offers classified advertising for private and dealer customers, subscription and listing fees, priority placement and lead services, display advertising for automotive OEMs and finance companies, SaaS for dealer management and websites, research and valuation tools (including Redbook), photography and inspection services, and digital retailing tools. Non-automotive verticals include the sale of bikes, boats, trucks, caravans and other assets via dedicated marketplaces.

The model is a classic **digital-classifieds marketplace**: capital-light, ~95% gross margin, and highly cash-generative once a marketplace reaches dominant share. Roughly half of revenue is now offshore (Korea, US, Brazil, Chile), so the group is a portfolio of #1-or-#2 automotive marketplaces rather than a single-country story.

## Economic Moat & Competitive Position

CAR Group has a wide [[economic-moat|economic moat]] built principally on [[network-effects]] — the defining advantage of online classifieds:

- **Two-sided network effects** — the most car listings attract the most buyers, which attracts more sellers/dealers, which attracts more buyers. A marketplace that reaches dominant share (as carsales has in Australia, Encar in Korea) becomes the default destination and is extremely hard to dislodge.
- **Winner-take-most market structure** — automotive classifieds tend toward a single dominant player per country; carsales sits in the ASX "online classifieds oligopoly" trio with [[rea-group]] (property) and [[seek]] (jobs), all of which enjoy this dynamic.
- **High switching costs for dealers** — dealers embed carsales into their workflow via SaaS (dealer management, websites, digital retailing), data/valuation tools (Redbook) and lead flow, raising [[switching-costs]].
- **Pricing power / yield** — dominant share lets the marketplace raise depth/subscription pricing and add value-added services faster than inflation (the "price/volume/yield" growth algorithm).
- **Data advantage** — proprietary listing, pricing and lead data (Redbook valuations) compounds into research and retailing products rivals cannot easily match.

The moat's main threat is **horizontal free platforms** — chiefly Facebook Marketplace in private (C2C) listings — which can erode the low-value private segment even though the high-value dealer business is far more defensible.

## Growth Drivers & Catalysts

- **Price / yield growth** — dominant share enables above-inflation depth, subscription and premium-placement pricing in Australia.
- **Adjacency expansion** — data, valuations (Redbook), inspections, SaaS and digital-retailing tools cross-sold into the dealer base.
- **International ramp** — Trader Interactive (North America) and Encar (Korea) are the largest growth engines, both compounding double-digit revenue.
- **Latin America** — webmotors (Brazil) and chileautos provide emerging-market optionality.
- **Digital retailing / transactions** — moving beyond pure classifieds toward facilitating the transaction itself.
- **Recurring catalysts** — half-year (February) and full-year (August) results, dealer-lead volume and used-car pricing data, Encar regulatory/competitive news, webmotors stake-change headlines, FX (AUD vs USD/KRW/BRL), and CEO-transition execution under Will Elliott.

## Bull Case vs Bear Case

### Bull case
- Dominant, network-effect-protected marketplaces in Australia and Korea with durable pricing power.
- ~95% gross margin, capital-light, highly cash-generative — a quality compounder profile.
- A multi-country growth portfolio (US, Korea, Brazil, Chile) diversifies beyond the mature Australian core; international segments growing double digits.
- Adjacency expansion (data, SaaS, digital retailing) lengthens the runway and raises dealer switching costs.

### Bear case
- Premium ~37x P/E prices long-duration growth — acutely rate-sensitive and exposed to multiple compression in risk-off regimes.
- Facebook Marketplace erodes the private (C2C) listings segment.
- Cyclical dealer advertising spend falls in an auto-market or consumer downturn.
- Heavy offshore revenue exposes reported results to AUD/USD, AUD/KRW and AUD/BRL swings.
- Integration/execution and FX risk on the international acquisitions; leadership transition after McIntyre's departure.

## Key Risks

- **Competition from free platforms** — Facebook Marketplace in private listings is the structural threat to the low-value segment.
- **Cyclicality** — dealer advertising spend is tied to used-car volumes and business confidence.
- **Valuation / rate sensitivity** — a premium, long-duration multiple de-rates hard when rates rise or growth disappoints.
- **FX translation** — roughly half of revenue is offshore (KRW, USD, BRL).
- **Regulatory** — Encar faces periodic regulatory/competitive scrutiny in Korea; classifieds market-power questions can arise in any dominant market.
- **Execution / leadership** — integration of international assets and the CEO transition to Will Elliott (from August 2025).

## Trading Relevance

The company was founded in 1997 by Greg Roebuck and Wal Pisciotta in Melbourne as carsales.com.au. It listed on the ASX in 2009. International expansion began in 2013 with a 30% stake in Brazil's Webmotors and a strategic interest in Korea's SK Encar, with SK Encar becoming a controlled subsidiary (Encar) in 2017. In August 2021 carsales acquired 49% of U.S.-based Trader Interactive (RV, commercial truck, motorcycle, powersports marketplaces); it completed 100% ownership of Trader Interactive in 2022. In November 2023 carsales.com Ltd formally changed its name to CAR Group Limited to separate the listed parent from its operating marketplace brands globally. The company acquired a 41% ACP interest in 2005 (which was progressively unwound), and has expanded via M&A, partnerships and organic growth in adjacent digital automotive services.

## Recent Developments

For FY25 (year ended 30 June 2025) CAR Group recorded revenue of over A$1 billion, up 8%, and adjusted EBITDA up 10% year-on-year. Operating statistics included 2.3 million vehicles online, 19 billion page views, 49 million unique monthly visitors and 49,000 subscribed dealers across the group. In July 2025 CEO Cameron McIntyre announced his departure after 18 years with the company (nine as CEO). CFO William Elliott was promoted to Managing Director and CEO effective 15 August 2025. McIntyre subsequently joined REA Group as CEO effective 3 November 2025.

**H1 FY26 (six months to 31 December 2025, reported 9 February 2026):** reported revenue rose 8% to A$626M and NPAT rose 16% to A$143M; proforma revenue grew 13% in constant currency and proforma EBITDA of A$339M grew 12%. By segment, Australia (carsales) delivered 8% revenue and 8% adjusted EBITDA growth (Dealer +10%, Private +5%, Media +10%), while North America (Trader Interactive) grew revenue 13% and adjusted EBITDA 11% in constant currency. Management reaffirmed FY26 guidance of 12–14% proforma revenue growth and 10–13% EBITDA growth in constant currency.

## Valuation & How the Stock Trades

CAR Group trades as a [[technology]]-adjacent **online-classifieds compounder** on a premium, long-duration multiple (~37x trailing P/E per the Fundamentals block below) that prices durable network-effect-protected growth rather than a cheap valuation. Mechanically:

- **Rate sensitivity** — as a long-duration growth name, CAR is sensitive to bond yields; it tends to re-rate together with [[rea-group]] and [[seek]] (the ASX classifieds trio) on rate moves.
- **FX** — roughly half of revenue is offshore (Korea, US, Brazil, Chile), so AUD/USD, AUD/KRW and AUD/BRL moves affect reported numbers.
- **Quality economics** — ~95% gross margin and strong free-cash conversion underpin the rating; the debate is the multiple, not the business model.
- **Dividend** — pays a partially-franked dividend (see the Fundamentals block for the figures as pulled); the franking-credit gross-up matters for Australian residents, but the equity case is growth, not yield.
- **Catalysts that move it** — half-year (February) and full-year (August) results, Australian dealer-lead volumes and used-car pricing, Encar regulatory news, and FX.
