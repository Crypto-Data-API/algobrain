Cboe Global Markets (NYSE: CBOE) is the largest US options exchange operator and the home of the [[vix|VIX]] volatility complex and exclusively licensed [[s-and-p-500|S&P 500]] index (SPX) options. Founded in 1973 as the Chicago Board Options Exchange — the first listed options marketplace — it is a constituent of the [[s-and-p-500]] index, a member of the [[financials|Financials]] sector, and one of the few stocks whose earnings are a direct function of market [[volatility]] and trading activity itself.

## Business Overview & Segments

Cboe's crown jewels are **exclusive index licences**: SPX [[options]] (licensed from S&P Dow Jones Indices) and the [[vix]] options/futures complex trade only on Cboe. Proprietary-product transaction fees — led by SPX and the explosive growth of zero-days-to-expiry ([[0dte-options|0DTE]]) trading — are the dominant earnings driver, supplemented by recurring market-data and access fees.

| Business line | What it is | Mix / driver (qualitative) |
|---|---|---|
| **Options** | Four US options exchanges (Cboe Options, C2, BZX, EDGX); SPX + [[vix]] complex | Dominant earnings driver; led by exclusive SPX and surging [[0dte-options|0DTE]] flow |
| **Futures** | Cboe Futures Exchange — VIX futures | High-margin proprietary product tied to volatility demand |
| **North American Equities** | US (BZX/EDGX) and Canadian equities venues | Volume/transaction fees; lower-margin, competitive |
| **Europe & APAC** | European and Asia-Pacific equities/derivatives | Geographic diversification; growth optionality |
| **FX** | Institutional FX trading platform | Smaller; diversifies the transaction base |
| **Data Vantage** | Market data, analytics, access/connectivity services | Recurring, subscription-like revenue — the quality-of-earnings lever |

The strategic prize is the mix between **transaction revenue** (volatile, volume-driven, spikes with market stress) and the **recurring Data Vantage / access revenue** that smooths the cycle. The exclusive SPX/VIX franchise is what makes Cboe more than a commodity matching engine.

## Economic Moat & Competitive Position

Cboe holds a **wide [[economic-moat|economic moat]]** unusual for a transaction venue, built on:

- **Exclusive index licences** — SPX options and the [[vix]] complex trade only on Cboe; there is no legal substitute venue for the deepest US index-volatility products. This is the single strongest moat element.
- **Liquidity network effects** — order flow concentrates where liquidity is deepest; the SPX/VIX ecosystem is self-reinforcing and extremely hard to dislodge.
- **Recurring data / access revenue** — Data Vantage and connectivity fees create sticky, subscription-like revenue with high switching costs for market participants.
- **Regulatory and operational barriers** — running registered exchanges with the requisite technology, surveillance, and compliance is a high barrier to entry.

The moat is **wide but cyclical**: volumes mean-revert after volatility spikes, the licence could in theory be repriced or contested at renewal, and rivals compete fiercely for non-exclusive flow (especially in equities and for [[0dte-options|0DTE]] share).

## Growth Drivers & Catalysts

- **0DTE / SPX growth** — SPX options traded >970M contracts in 2025, ADV up 25% to a record 3.9M, driven by [[0dte-options|0DTE]] participation; the structural growth story.
- **Volatility events** — VIX-complex and options volumes spike with market stress; CBOE is effectively long realised trading activity.
- **Data Vantage** — recurring market-data and analytics revenue growth that raises quality of earnings.
- **Strategic realignment** — new CEO Craig Donohue's reorganisation (from May 2025) aimed at sharpening the proprietary-product and data focus.
- **Recurring catalysts** — monthly volume reports (published early each month), SPX/0DTE mix, VIX-complex activity, Data Vantage growth, quarterly earnings and 2026 growth targets.

## Bull Case vs Bear Case

### Bull case
- Exclusive SPX/VIX licences are a genuine monopoly on the deepest US index-volatility products.
- 0DTE/SPX volume growth is structural, not just a volatility spike — a sixth consecutive record options year (4.6B contracts, ADV 18.4M in 2025).
- Record 2025 (net revenue +17%, diluted EPS +45%) and a strong Q1 2026 (net revenue +29%, record options net revenue $467.6M, +33% YoY).
- A natural portfolio hedge: revenue rises when markets fall and volatility spikes.
- Recurring Data Vantage revenue smooths the transaction cycle and lifts the multiple.

### Bear case
- Transaction revenue is volume-driven and mean-reverts after volatility spikes — earnings can be lumpy.
- Premium valuation (P/E ~40x) prices continued record volumes.
- Competition for non-exclusive flow (especially 0DTE) from [[nasdaq-inc|Nasdaq]], NYSE, MIAX, BOX.
- Index-licence renewal/repricing risk on the SPX franchise.
- Regulatory changes to options market structure (e.g., 0DTE scrutiny) could dent the growth engine.

## Key Risks

- **Volume mean-reversion** — volumes fall back after volatility spikes; a calm-market stretch pressures transaction revenue.
- **0DTE-flow competition** — rivals contest the fastest-growing options segment.
- **Index-licence risk** — loss or repricing of the SPX licence would strike the core franchise.
- **Regulatory** — changes to options market structure, including any restriction on 0DTE trading.
- **Valuation** — a ~40x multiple leaves limited margin for a volume disappointment.

## Trading Relevance & How the Stock Trades

CBOE is effectively a **long-volatility equity**: revenue rises with trading volumes, which spike in volatile markets, making it a partial portfolio hedge among [[financials]]. Mechanically:

- **Recurring vs transaction revenue** — Data Vantage and access fees are the recurring annuity; SPX/[[vix]]/options transaction fees are the cyclical, volatility-geared torque.
- **Volume reports drive the stock** — monthly volume data (published early each month), SPX/0DTE mix, and VIX-complex activity move CBOE between earnings.
- **Counter-cyclical beta** — tends to outperform when markets sell off and volatility spikes — a rare positive-to-volatility name.
- **Options on CBOE** — liquid; used to express volatility-regime views.
- **Valuation** — premium exchange/data multiple (P/E ~40x) reflecting the exclusive franchise and recurring-revenue mix.
- **Dividend** — a modest, steadily growing dividend; the API "dividend yield" figure uses a non-standard definition (see source page).
- **Correlated names** — [[cme-group]], [[intercontinental-exchange]], [[nasdaq-inc|Nasdaq]].
