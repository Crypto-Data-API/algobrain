Riot Platforms Inc. (NASDAQ: RIOT) is a [[bitcoin]] mining and digital-infrastructure company headquartered in Castle Rock, Colorado. Originally incorporated in 2000 and formerly known as Riot Blockchain, the company rebranded to Riot Platforms in 2023 and operates large-scale mining and data-center campuses in Texas (Rockdale and Corsicana). It is a top-tier options name for traders seeking leveraged [[bitcoin]] exposure through equities, and is increasingly a play on the pivot of bitcoin miners into AI/high-performance-computing (HPC) data centers.

## Business Overview

Riot runs a vertically integrated model: self-mining bitcoin, large-scale power procurement (it earns meaningful power-credit revenue by curtailing during ERCOT grid stress), and an engineering arm. The Rockdale facility was one of the largest single bitcoin-mining sites in North America; the Corsicana campus provides the gigawatt-scale power footprint that underpins the company's expansion into AI/HPC colocation. Like all miners, Riot's economics are highly levered to the bitcoin price, network hash-rate/difficulty, power costs and fleet efficiency, and the company routinely sells mined BTC and issues equity to fund growth.

## Business Segments & Assets

Riot is in the middle of a strategic transition from a **pure bitcoin miner** to a **power-and-data-center operator** that monetises its gigawatt-scale grid footprint two ways: mining [[bitcoin]] and leasing capacity to AI/HPC tenants. The shared, scarce asset across both is **cheap, large-scale, interconnected power in [[ERCOT|ERCOT]] (Texas)**.

| Segment | Asset / activity | Driver | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Self-mining (Bitcoin)** | Mining fleet at Rockdale & Corsicana | BTC price, network hash-rate/difficulty, fleet efficiency, power cost | Legacy core; highly BTC-levered |
| **Power strategy** | Power procurement + ERCOT demand-response | Power credits from curtailing during grid stress | Real revenue offset to mining power cost; a structural Texas edge |
| **Data center / HPC (AI)** | Corsicana colocation capacity | Long-term tenant contracts (e.g. AMD) | The new bull thesis; converts power into recurring lease revenue |
| **Engineering** | In-house infrastructure/engineering arm | Build-out execution | Supports both mining and HPC build |

- **Hash rate (EH/s)** is the key mining-capacity metric: it measures Riot's share of total network mining power and, together with the BTC price and difficulty, determines how many BTC the company mines. Riot mined **1,473 BTC in Q1 2026** (down ~3.7% YoY on higher global hash rate and a lower average BTC price of ~$68,223 vs ~$82,535 a year earlier).
- **The HPC/AI pivot** is the central story: a portion of the **gigawatt-scale Corsicana power footprint** is being redirected from mining to AI/high-performance-computing colocation. **AMD doubled its contracted capacity at Riot to 50 MW** in Q1 2026 (from an initial 25 MW) — the key validation of the colocation strategy and the newly launched data-center segment, which contributed **$33.2M** of Q1 2026 revenue.
- **Power credits** from curtailing during ERCOT grid stress are a genuine differentiator: in Texas's deregulated grid, Riot can sell power back / earn demand-response revenue when prices spike, partially hedging its single largest cost.
- The strategic logic mirrors peers [[core-scientific]] and [[iren]]: the asset that matters is **secured power and interconnect**, and AI tenants will pay more per MW than mining earns at many BTC prices.

## Bull vs Bear

**Bull case**
- **Dual exposure** to two powerful themes: leveraged [[bitcoin]] upside *plus* a [[ai-data-center-power-demand|data-center / AI-power]] colocation pivot that re-rates the multiple from "miner" toward "power infrastructure."
- The **AMD 50 MW deal** (doubled from 25 MW in Q1 2026) validates the colocation strategy, with the data-center segment already contributing $33.2M in Q1 2026.
- **Gigawatt-scale ERCOT power** with demand-response revenue is a scarce, hard-to-replicate asset and a partial hedge on the largest cost line.
- AI/HPC lease revenue is recurring and contracted — far higher-quality cash flow than volatile mining, and it can exceed mining economics per MW.
- Tier-1 options liquidity and high beta make RIOT the cleanest equity vehicle for leveraged BTC and crypto-momentum exposure.

**Bear case**
- Still **highly levered to the bitcoin price**; a BTC drawdown hits both revenue and the multiple, and Q1 2026 mining was already pressured (lower avg BTC price ~$68,223 vs ~$82,535 YoY, higher global hash rate).
- **Rising network difficulty/hash rate** continually erodes per-rig BTC output, a structural headwind to the mining segment.
- The HPC pivot is **partly unproven** at scale; converting power into AI tenants requires capex, time, and execution against more-advanced peers like [[core-scientific|Core Scientific]] and [[iren|IREN]].
- **Chronic dilution:** Riot routinely issues equity and sells mined BTC to fund growth — Q1 2026 alone it sold 3,778 BTC for $289.5M net to fund the data-center build-out.
- Energy-policy and crypto-regulatory risk; ERCOT power-price spikes cut both ways.

## Valuation Framework

Riot is a hybrid that must be valued in **two parts**, because the bull thesis is precisely the re-rating from one to the other:

- **BTC-beta (mining segment).** The mining business is a leveraged play on the bitcoin price net of power cost and difficulty. Useful frames: BTC mined per quarter, **EH/s of installed hash rate**, the BTC holdings on the balance sheet (mark-to-market), and the all-in cost to mine a coin versus the BTC price. This segment trades at a low, volatile multiple — a commodity-producer profile.
- **HPC/data-center value (AI segment).** Contracted colocation capacity (MW under lease, e.g. the AMD 50 MW) earns recurring revenue that deserves a **data-center / infrastructure multiple** far above a miner's. The core of the bull case is that the market re-rates Riot's power footprint from "BTC-mining cost center" to "AI-hosting asset."
- **Power-asset optionality.** Secured gigawatt-scale ERCOT power and interconnect is itself a scarce, valuable option — convertible to whichever use (mining vs HPC) pays more at the margin.
- **Sum-of-the-parts.** The cleanest mental model: mining segment at a BTC-producer multiple + HPC segment at a data-center multiple + BTC holdings + power-asset optionality, net of dilution.

## Capital Structure & Dilution

Dilution is a structural feature of Riot's model, not a one-off. The company **routinely issues equity and sells mined BTC** to fund expansion — in Q1 2026 it sold **3,778 BTC for $289.5M net** (avg ~$76,626) specifically to fund the data-center build-out. This dual funding mechanism (equity issuance + BTC sales) means the share count and the BTC treasury are both in flux: BTC sales fund growth but reduce the balance-sheet crypto exposure, while equity issuance dilutes per-share BTC and HPC value. Traders should model **fully-diluted shares**, track the BTC-held versus BTC-sold balance, and treat capital raises as a recurring overhang against the HPC build-out's capital intensity.
