Fastly Inc (NYSE: FSLY) is an edge cloud platform in the [[technology|Information Technology]] sector providing content delivery network (CDN), edge compute, security, and observability services. Founded in 2011, the company differentiates through its programmable edge compute platform built on [[webassembly|WebAssembly]], allowing developers to run custom logic at the edge rather than relying on traditional static content caching. Fastly competes directly with [[cloudflare]], [[akamai-technologies|Akamai]], and Amazon CloudFront ([[amazon]]), though it is significantly smaller by revenue and market capitalisation than its two primary public competitors — making it a high-beta, turnaround-and-execution story rather than a scaled platform leader.

---

## Business Overview & Segments

Fastly's core differentiation is its programmable edge compute platform (Compute@Edge, now called "Fastly Compute") built on [[webassembly|WebAssembly]]. Unlike traditional CDNs that mainly cache static content, Fastly allows developers to run custom logic at the edge — closer to users — reducing latency for dynamic applications. Key customers include The New York Times, GitHub, Shopify, Pinterest, and Stripe.

Fastly does not break out granular product-level revenue, but the business is best understood across three lines: the legacy **network/CDN delivery** business (the largest revenue contributor and the most commoditised), the higher-growth **security** line (Next-Gen WAF, DDoS, bot management — the fastest-growing piece and the driver of recent RPO/backlog growth), and the strategic **edge compute** line (Fastly Compute), which is the long-term differentiator but a small share of revenue today.

| Segment / line | What it is | Revenue mix (qualitative) |
|---|---|---|
| **Network / CDN delivery** | Global content caching and acceleration — the historical core | Largest revenue share; most commoditised and price-competitive |
| **Security** | Next-Gen WAF (Signal Sciences), DDoS protection, bot detection | Fastest-growing line; the main driver of RPO/backlog growth |
| **Edge compute** | Fastly Compute (WebAssembly), the programmable-edge differentiator | Small today but strategically central to the bull case |

### Revenue Model

Fastly operates on usage-based pricing (bandwidth, requests, compute time) with enterprise contracts that include committed minimums. Approximately 80% of revenue comes from existing customers, indicating strong net dollar expansion but also raising questions about new customer acquisition velocity.

### Key Products

| Product | Description |
|---|---|
| **CDN / Content Delivery** | Core product — global content caching and acceleration |
| **Fastly Compute** | Edge compute platform, WebAssembly-based, successor to Compute@Edge |
| **Next-Gen WAF** | Web application firewall (acquired Signal Sciences in 2020 for $775M) |
| **DDoS Protection** | Distributed denial-of-service mitigation |
| **Image Optimization** | On-the-fly image transformation at the edge |
| **Bot Detection** | Automated bot identification and mitigation |

## Trading Relevance

Fastly is a textbook case study in several trading concepts:

### COVID-Era Bubble and Bust

Fastly was a quintessential COVID-era market darling. The stock went from ~$20 in early 2020 to $136 in October 2020 as internet traffic surged and investors paid extreme multiples for any company benefiting from the stay-at-home economy. The subsequent collapse to ~$8 by 2022 illustrates the danger of paying for growth that does not materialise — a classic [[mean-reversion]] pattern in momentum stocks that overshoot on narrative.

### Systemic CDN Risk Event (June 2021)

On June 8, 2021, a Fastly configuration error caused a global outage that took down major websites including Amazon, Reddit, the UK government, CNN, The New York Times, and Twitch for approximately one hour. This event demonstrated the systemic risk of CDN concentration — a single provider failure can cascade across the internet. Fastly stock dropped ~5% on the news, though it partially recovered as the company resolved the issue quickly and received positive attention for its transparent post-mortem.

### Customer Concentration Risk

At one point, TikTok represented approximately 12% of Fastly's revenue — a dangerous level of customer concentration. When TikTok's regulatory situation created uncertainty, it became a material risk factor for Fastly's stock. This is a common risk pattern in smaller enterprise software/infrastructure companies.

## Economic Moat & Competitive Position

Fastly's [[economic-moat|economic moat]] is **narrow and contestable** — a notably weaker position than the scaled platform leaders it competes with. Its advantages are real but thin:

- **Developer ergonomics + Wasm edge compute** — Fastly's programmable edge built on [[webassembly|WebAssembly]] is genuinely well-regarded by developers and offers a performance/flexibility edge over static caching. But "best-liked by developers" has not converted into share leadership, and the differentiation is replicable: [[cloudflare]]'s Workers (V8 isolates) compete directly for the same mindshare.
- **Modest [[switching-costs]]** — enterprise contracts with committed minimums, deep config/VCL integration, and the operational cost of re-pointing traffic create some stickiness (reflected in ~80% of revenue from existing customers). These switching costs are moderate, not deep — CDN is fundamentally multi-homeable, and large customers routinely split traffic across providers.
- **Weak [[network-effects]]** — unlike a marketplace or social platform, a CDN gains little from each additional customer; scale brings cost advantages (PoP density, peering) but not a self-reinforcing flywheel. Fastly's ~70 PoPs are an order of magnitude fewer than [[akamai-technologies|Akamai]]'s footprint, a structural scale disadvantage.
- **Scale disadvantage** — Fastly is materially smaller than [[cloudflare]] and [[akamai-technologies|Akamai]], with lower gross margins (~55-62% vs Cloudflare's ~75%), implying weaker unit economics and pricing power. Sub-scale players in an infrastructure business with high fixed costs face a structurally harder path to durable profitability.

Net: Fastly competes on product quality and developer experience from a sub-scale position. The moat narrows further as CDN commoditises; the security and edge-compute lines are where any durable differentiation must come from.

## Growth Drivers & Catalysts

- **Security line + RPO backlog growth** — the security products (WAF, DDoS, bot management) are the fastest-growing line, and remaining performance obligations (RPO ~$369M, +63% YoY as of the 2025-2026 prints) signal committed enterprise demand converting to future revenue. This is the most credible growth engine.
- **Edge-compute adoption** — broader uptake of Fastly Compute (WebAssembly) would shift the mix toward higher-value, stickier workloads and away from commoditised delivery.
- **Turnaround execution** — under CEO Todd Nightingale (ex-Cisco), the 2024-2026 strategy of profitability-over-growth-at-all-costs has begun to show: revenue re-accelerating (~20%+ YoY in recent quarters) alongside raised guidance.
- **Gross-margin inflection** — record GAAP gross margin (~62.5% in Q1 2026, up from the ~55% range), a key proof-point that the path to non-GAAP profitability is real.
- **Recurring catalysts** — quarterly earnings and guidance revisions, RPO/backlog disclosures, large enterprise customer wins, and any large-platform outage news (Fastly is systemically exposed both ways).

## Bull Case vs Bear Case

### Bull case
- The turnaround is working: revenue re-accelerated to ~20%+ YoY, gross margin hit a record ~62.5%, and FY2026 guidance was raised — the inflection bears doubted is visible in the prints.
- Security is a genuine, fast-growing line (RPO +63% YoY), diversifying away from commoditised CDN delivery and improving mix.
- Edge compute (Fastly Compute / [[webassembly|Wasm]]) is a real, developer-loved differentiator with long-term optionality if edge workloads scale.
- Small base + high beta means a credible path to profitability can re-rate the multiple sharply; an unprofitable name turning the corner has asymmetric upside.

### Bear case
- Sub-scale versus [[cloudflare]] and [[akamai-technologies|Akamai]], with weaker margins and a narrower product suite; Cloudflare is winning the developer-mindshare battle Fastly once led.
- CDN delivery is commoditising; edge-compute differentiation has not yet translated into share leadership.
- History of execution stumbles — management turnover, customer concentration (TikTok once ~12% of revenue), and the June 2021 outage — keeps the risk premium elevated.
- Still not durably profitable on a GAAP basis; any growth deceleration or guidance miss hits a high-beta, post-earnings-volatile stock hard. The recurring tension: a great product that has not been a great investment.

## Key Risks

Fastly has struggled with execution across multiple dimensions, and the risk profile remains elevated:

- **Customer concentration**: TikTok was ~12% of revenue at one point, creating single-customer risk; large enterprise customers can split or re-point traffic.
- **Management turnover**: Multiple CEO changes since IPO have signalled strategic uncertainty (stabilising under Nightingale, but the track record weighs on sentiment).
- **Competition from [[cloudflare]]**: Cloudflare moves faster, has a broader product suite, better margins, and more aggressive pricing — it is winning the developer mindshare battle.
- **Not yet durably profitable**: Negative GAAP operating margin; the path to sustained profitability depends on continued margin inflection and mix shift toward security/compute.
- **CDN commoditisation**: Pure CDN is becoming a commodity; Fastly's edge-compute differentiation has not yet translated into dominant market share.
- **Systemic outage / concentration risk**: As the June 2021 global outage showed, a single Fastly configuration error can cascade across the internet — a reputational and contractual risk for a CDN provider.

The stock is a case study in the difference between a great product and a great investment. Fastly's technology is well-regarded by developers, but that has historically not translated into the financial performance needed to justify growth-stage valuation multiples — the 2025-2026 turnaround is the test of whether that gap is finally closing.

## Valuation & How the Stock Trades

Fastly is an **unprofitable, growth-and-turnaround name**, so it is valued on **EV/revenue and the gross-margin trajectory** rather than on an earnings multiple — there is no meaningful P/E while GAAP operating margin is negative. Mechanically:

- **EV/revenue + margin inflection** — the bull/bear debate centres on whether re-accelerating revenue (~20%+ YoY) and a rising gross margin (~62.5% record, up from ~55%) justify a growth multiple, or whether sub-scale economics cap the re-rating. Non-GAAP operating-income guidance turning positive is the pivotal data point.
- **No dividend** — Fastly returns no capital; total return is entirely a function of growth, margin progress, and multiple re-rating.
- **High beta, post-earnings volatile** — a small-cap (market cap roughly ~$1.5-4B depending on the snapshot) that gaps sharply on earnings and guidance revisions; it trades as a high-beta proxy for risk appetite in unprofitable growth software.
- **Outage/headline sensitivity** — operational incidents (CDN outages) and large customer news can move the stock on top of the fundamental cadence.
