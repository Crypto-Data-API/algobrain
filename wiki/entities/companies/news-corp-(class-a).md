News Corp Class A (Nasdaq: NWSA) is the **non-voting** common stock of [[news-corp|News Corporation]], the global media and information services group (Dow Jones/WSJ, REA Group/Realtor.com, HarperCollins, News UK/Australia). It is a constituent of the [[s-and-p-500]] alongside the voting [[news-corp-(class-b)|Class B (NWS)]] shares. This page carries the Class-A-specific fundamentals; for the full business profile, history, FY2026 results and trading catalysts see [[news-corp]].

## Share Class Notes

- NWSA holders have **no voting rights**; the Murdoch family controls ~39% of the Class B vote, making governance effectively entrenched.
- NWSA is the more liquid line and typically trades at a modest discount to NWS; the A/B spread widens or narrows around governance and structure headlines, making it a recurring pairs-trade.
- Q3 FY2026 (reported 7 May 2026): group revenue US$2.19B (+9%), Total Segment EBITDA US$343M (+18%) — detail at [[news-corp]].

## Dual-Class Structure (NWSA vs NWS)

News Corp has a two-class common-equity structure, and **both classes are separately listed and both are [[s-and-p-500]] members** — an unusual situation that creates a structural pairs-trade. This page is the **Class A (NWSA)** line; the voting line is [[news-corp-(class-b)|Class B (NWS)]].

| Dimension | Class A — **NWSA** (this page) | Class B — [[news-corp-(class-b)\|NWS]] |
|---|---|---|
| **Voting rights** | **None** (non-voting) | **One vote per share** |
| **Economic rights** | Identical claim on dividends and earnings | Identical claim on dividends and earnings |
| **Typical pricing** | Usually trades at a **modest discount** to NWS | Usually trades at a **premium** (control/voting premium) |
| **Liquidity** | **More liquid** — larger float, the line index funds and most institutions hold | Less liquid — smaller float, concentrated holders |
| **Listing** | Nasdaq | Nasdaq; also ASX as CDIs |
| **Control** | The Murdoch family controls ~39% of the **Class B** vote, entrenching governance regardless of NWSA holdings | Same |

**Why the classes trade at different prices and liquidity:**

- **Voting premium.** NWS carries votes that matter for control of a family-influenced company, so it commands a control premium. NWSA gives up that vote and therefore trades cheaper — a recurring feature of dual-class media names (compare [[fox-corporation-(class-b)]]).
- **Economics are identical.** Dividends per share and earnings claims are the same for both classes; the price gap is *entirely* a function of votes, liquidity, and index/flow technicals — not cash-flow rights.
- **Liquidity-driven divergence.** Because NWSA has the larger float and is the line most index and passive funds track, its supply/demand technicals differ from NWS. Flows into broad-market or [[communication-services]] funds hit the two lines unevenly, which is what makes the A/B spread move.

**The A/B pairs-trade.** Traders monitor the NWSA-vs-NWS spread: it tends to *widen* (NWS richer) around governance/control headlines (Murdoch succession, any restructuring or consolidation chatter) and *narrow* when those fade. Because economic rights are identical, the spread is a relative-value bet on the voting premium rather than on the underlying business. Liquidity risk is real on the NWS leg, so size accordingly.

## Business Segments

NWSA and NWS represent the same underlying company; the segment economics are identical. News Corp is a diversified global media and information-services group. Its reportable areas (detail and any specific figures live on [[news-corp]]):

| Segment | What it is | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Digital Real Estate Services** | [[rea-group]] (Australia, majority-owned, separately listed) + Move/Realtor.com (US) | The highest-multiple, highest-growth profit pool; the market increasingly values News Corp on this |
| **Dow Jones** | The Wall Street Journal, Barron's, MarketWatch, Dow Jones professional information (Risk & Compliance, energy/Factiva) | High-margin subscription + B2B data; the strategic crown jewel |
| **Book Publishing** | HarperCollins | One of the "Big Five" trade publishers; scale + backlist |
| **News Media** | News UK (The Sun, The Times), News Corp Australia, the New York Post, Talk | Mature/declining print, cyclical advertising; the value-trap leg |
| **(Subscription Video — Australia)** | Foxtel was historically a segment | Foxtel was divested/restructured (sold to DAZN), simplifying the group |

The bull thesis is a **sum-of-the-parts** story: the durable, high-multiple Digital Real Estate + Dow Jones assets are partly masked by the legacy News Media print drag.

## Bull vs Bear Case

**Bull case**
- Sum-of-the-parts discount: high-growth, high-margin Digital Real Estate ([[rea-group]], Realtor.com) and Dow Jones professional information are worth more than the consolidated multiple implies.
- Dow Jones is shifting toward higher-margin B2B professional information (Risk & Compliance, energy) — recurring, subscription, AI-adjacent revenue.
- Portfolio simplification (Foxtel exit) sharpens the story and could invite further break-up/value-unlock catalysts.
- Strong balance sheet (low [[debt-to-equity|leverage]] — see Fundamentals) gives optionality for buybacks/M&A.

**Bear case**
- The News Media segment (print newspapers) is in structural decline and drags growth and sentiment.
- Family-controlled, non-voting structure (especially for NWSA) means public holders have no governance recourse; capital-allocation and succession risk are real.
- Cyclical advertising exposure in News Media and real-estate-cycle exposure in Digital Real Estate.
- Conglomerate discount may persist absent a forcing event.

## Valuation Framework (qualitative)

News Corp is best valued **sum-of-the-parts**, not on a single blended multiple:

- **Digital Real Estate** ([[rea-group]] stake — itself separately listed and visible — plus Realtor.com): the highest-multiple, highest-growth piece; the market increasingly anchors valuation here.
- **Dow Jones:** valued like an information-services/subscription business (higher multiple than legacy media) as the professional-information mix grows.
- **HarperCollins:** a scale trade-publishing asset valued on stable margins and backlist.
- **News Media:** valued at a low (sometimes negative-optionality) multiple given print decline.
- **Discount/holdco overhang:** a conglomerate/family-control discount applies; the qualitative debate is whether a break-up, simplification, or REA re-rating closes it.

For NWSA specifically, the cash-flow rights are identical to NWS, so any A/B *price* difference reflects the voting premium and liquidity, not a different intrinsic value.

## Trading & Options Playbook

- **Index/ETF membership:** NWSA is an [[s-and-p-500]] constituent and sits in [[communication-services]] funds including [[xlc]]; it is the more index-tracked of the two lines, so passive flows concentrate here.
- **Catalysts calendar:** quarterly earnings (News Corp reports on a June fiscal year — the Q3 print landed 7 May 2026); [[rea-group]] results and the Australian housing cycle; any portfolio-simplification or break-up news; Murdoch succession/governance headlines; advertising-cycle data points.
- **Volatility & liquidity:** NWSA is the more liquid line with tighter spreads and deeper options than NWS — the practical line for most directional and options positioning. Watch the **A/B spread** as its own signal.
- **Cross-reads:** [[rea-group]] (subsidiary), [[fox-corporation-(class-b)]] (sibling/governance comp), Zillow (real-estate read-through), and [[news-corp-(class-b)]] for the pairs-trade.

## Capital Return

- **Dividends:** News Corp pays a modest, regular dividend (identical per share for both NWSA and NWS); see the Fundamentals block for the per-share figure — do not infer figures not shown.
- **Buybacks:** the company has run share-repurchase programs across both classes; low leverage supports continued returns and optionality for M&A.
- **Class symmetry:** because economic rights are identical, both classes receive the same dividend; only the *price* (and hence yield on price) differs by the voting/liquidity premium.

## Risks

- **Structural print decline** in News Media weighing on group growth and sentiment.
- **Governance / control:** Murdoch-family control via Class B; NWSA holders are **non-voting** with no governance recourse.
- **Succession risk:** leadership-transition headlines drive volatility and the A/B spread.
- **Cyclicality:** advertising (News Media) and the housing cycle (Digital Real Estate) both expose the group to macro.
- **Conglomerate discount** may persist without a forcing catalyst.
- **FX translation:** large Australian and UK operations create currency exposure in reported results.
