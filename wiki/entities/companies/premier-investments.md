**Premier Investments Limited** (ASX: PMV) is a hybrid specialty-retailer and investment holding company, an [[asx-200]] [[consumer-discretionary]] constituent controlled by Solomon Lew. Post a 2024-2025 restructure it is now built around two high-growth retail brands — **Peter Alexander** (premium sleepwear) and **Smiggle** (children's stationery) — plus a strategic ~25-26% stake in [[breville|Breville Group]] and a portfolio of property/securities. For traders it is a cash-generative, low-debt, high-yield ASX retailer with embedded sum-of-the-parts optionality (the [[breville|Breville]] stake plus repeated talk of separating Peter Alexander and Smiggle), but also a name with a controlling-shareholder governance overhang and a holding-company discount.

## Business Model & Segments

Peter Alexander is a premium sleepwear and lifestyle brand operating across Australia, New Zealand and, following expansion into the United Kingdom, selected international markets. The brand has delivered sustained double-digit growth in recent years and sits at the premium end of the Australian sleepwear market.

Smiggle is a children's stationery and lifestyle brand with a network of physical stores and online channels across Australia, New Zealand, the UK, Ireland, Singapore, Malaysia and other markets, plus wholesale and indent arrangements in additional geographies. Smiggle's product range includes backpacks, lunchboxes, stationery, accessories and novelty items targeted at primary school-aged children.

Premier's investment portfolio includes a 25.5% stake in small kitchen electrical appliance group [[breville|Breville Group]] (valued at approximately A$1.17 billion), plus investments in property assets and securities.

## Economic Moat & Competitive Position

Premier's [[economic-moat|economic moat]] is brand-led and narrow rather than structural, resting on a small number of reinforcing advantages:

- **Brand strength of the two retained banners** — **Peter Alexander** owns the premium end of the Australian sleepwear/loungewear market, a defensible niche with genuine pricing power and high repeat purchase; **Smiggle** is a globally recognised kids' stationery and lifestyle brand with shelf-cut-through among primary-school children and their parents. Both are owned brands (not third-party distribution), so margin and merchandising are controlled in-house.
- **Vertically managed specialty retail** — design, sourcing, store-format and visual-merchandising are run centrally, giving Premier control over the full retail value chain and the ability to roll a proven format into new geographies (the UK/international rollout of Peter Alexander, Smiggle's multi-country store network).
- **The [[breville|Breville]] strategic stake** — a ~25-26% holding in a high-quality global appliance compounder is a balance-sheet asset that both anchors the sum-of-the-parts case and reflects management's franchise-investing approach.
- **Capital-allocation skill under Solomon Lew** — a decades-long record of buying assets cheaply (Just Group), scaling the winners, divesting the laggards, and returning surplus cash; the franchise's real edge is arguably allocation discipline rather than any single retail format.

The moat is genuinely **narrow and discretionary-cyclical**: fashion/lifestyle retail is fickle, Smiggle's international weakness shows brand equity is not permanent, and the controlled-ownership structure plus the conglomerate/holding-company mix means the market typically applies a holding-company discount to the sum of the parts.

## Growth Drivers & Catalysts

- **Peter Alexander UK / international rollout** — the single largest organic growth lever; the brand reached record FY25 sales of A$548M (+7.7%) and the offshore expansion is the multi-year runway.
- **Smiggle turnaround** — Smiggle sales fell 10.7% to A$264.2M in FY25 on soft international trading; a recovery in core international markets is the swing factor on group earnings.
- **Store rollout & format extension** — continued network growth and new-format stores across both retained banners.
- **Capital returns** — a A$100M on-market buyback was declared post-restructure; the low-debt, cash-generative balance sheet supports ongoing returns.
- **Sum-of-the-parts monetisation** — the central re-rating catalyst is any move to separately list or sell Peter Alexander or Smiggle, or to monetise the [[breville|Breville]] stake; the holding-company discount closes if the parts are crystallised.
- **Breville's own earnings** — as a ~25-26% associate, [[breville]]'s results feed Premier's NAV and reported profit.
- **Recurring catalysts** — half-year (March) and full-year (September) results, like-for-like sales momentum at Peter Alexander and Smiggle, and any structural-separation announcements.

## Bull Case vs Bear Case

### Bull case
- Embedded sum-of-the-parts optionality: the ~A$1.1B+ [[breville|Breville]] stake plus cash and property underpin a NAV that can exceed the traded market cap; crystallising any part re-rates the stock.
- Two genuinely strong owned brands (Peter Alexander at record sales; Smiggle a global kids' franchise) with in-house margin control.
- Cash-generative, low-debt balance sheet funding buybacks and dividends; high franked yield.
- A long Solomon Lew capital-allocation track record of buying low, scaling winners and divesting laggards (the Just Group → Myer exit being the latest example).

### Bear case
- Smiggle's international weakness (-10.7% in FY25) shows brand equity is not durable across all markets.
- Discretionary-spend softness and discounting pressure compress retail margins through a downturn.
- Concentrated, controlled ownership (Solomon Lew / associated entities) limits free float and invites a persistent holding-company discount plus related-party governance scrutiny.
- The CEO position was vacated during the restructure — leadership-transition risk with the Board overseeing brand leaders directly.
- FX on imported product and offshore sales adds earnings noise.

## Key Risks

- **Discretionary-spend & discounting** — apparel/lifestyle retail is highly sensitive to the consumer cycle; promotional intensity can erode margin quickly.
- **Smiggle international weakness** — the brand's soft trading in core offshore markets is the main earnings drag and the key turnaround risk.
- **Controlled ownership / governance** — Solomon Lew's controlling block and related-party arrangements draw governance scrutiny and cap the free float, sustaining a holding-company discount.
- **FX translation** — currency moves on imported product and offshore sales add headline-earnings volatility.
- **Leadership / CEO-vacancy transition** — the post-restructure executive model (Board overseeing brand leaders, no group CEO) carries succession and execution risk.
- **Fashion-cycle risk at Peter Alexander** — a premium lifestyle brand is exposed to taste shifts; sustaining double-digit growth off a record base is not guaranteed.

## History

Premier Investments was incorporated in 1987 and built an investment holding track record under the stewardship of Solomon Lew. In 2008, Premier completed an on-market takeover of Just Group Limited, making it a wholly owned subsidiary. Just Group at the time housed the Just Jeans, Jay Jays, Portmans, Dotti, Jacqui E, Smiggle and Peter Alexander brands.

Over the following 15 years, Premier invested heavily in scaling Peter Alexander and Smiggle internationally while progressively rationalising the legacy Apparel Brands portfolio. It also built a substantial minority holding in Breville Group. In March 2024, Premier announced plans to demerge Smiggle by early 2025 and consider a separate transaction for Peter Alexander. In September 2024, the Smiggle demerger was paused to enable Premier to explore a transaction involving its Apparel Brands division and Myer Holdings.

On 29 October 2024, Just Group entered a binding Share Sale and Implementation Agreement to be acquired by Myer in a reverse-merger transaction valued at A$580 million, in exchange for the issue of 890.5 million new Myer shares. The transaction closed on 26 January 2025. In February 2025, Premier distributed its newly issued Myer shares (7.2021 Myer shares per Premier share), valued at approximately A$1.03 billion, to Premier shareholders. The ATO ruled that the transaction did not qualify as a "[[demergers|demerger]]" under subsection 125-70(1) of the Income Tax Assessment Act 1997.

## Recent Developments

For the 52 weeks to 26 July 2025 (FY25), Premier delivered a statutory net profit after tax of A$338.2 million, up 31.1% year-on-year. On a continuing operations basis - focused on Peter Alexander, Smiggle and Breville - profit after tax fell 22.5% to A$144 million versus A$185.9 million in the prior year, reflecting divestment of the Apparel Brands. Peter Alexander delivered record sales of A$548 million, up 7.7%, while Smiggle sales fell 10.7% to A$264.2 million amid softer trading in core international markets.

Following the restructure, Premier declared a A$100 million on-market share buy-back. The company has signalled it retains flexibility to pursue further separation of Peter Alexander and Smiggle over time, depending on market conditions. The CEO position was vacated during the restructure, with the Board overseeing the transition and senior management continuing to run each retail brand.

## Leadership

Premier Investments is chaired by Solomon Lew, who has been the controlling shareholder and executive chairman for decades. Following the CEO departure announced during the strategic review, Premier is operating with a streamlined executive model, with individual brand leadership for Peter Alexander and Smiggle reporting through the Board.
