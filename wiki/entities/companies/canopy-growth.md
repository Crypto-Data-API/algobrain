Canopy Growth Corporation (NASDAQ: CGC; TSX: WEED) is one of the best-known Canadian licensed cannabis producers, founded in 2013 (originally as Tweed) and headquartered in Smiths Falls, Ontario. For traders it is less an earnings story than a regulatory-event vehicle: the stock moves violently on US federal cannabis-reform headlines, with chronically high implied volatility and a long history of dilution and restructuring. It trades on the [[nasdaq]] (and TSX) and is a core cannabis-ETF constituent.

## Business Overview

Canopy produces and sells cannabis for the Canadian adult-use and medical markets and international medical markets (notably Germany/Europe and Australia), plus vaporizer hardware (Storz & Bickel). Its **Canopy USA** structure holds non-controlled US assets — Wana Brands (edibles), Jetty Extracts and Acreage Holdings — pre-positioned to convert into a consolidated US business if federal restrictions ease. Constellation Brands, once the anchor strategic investor (~C$5B invested from 2017), converted its stake to non-voting exchangeable shares in 2024 as part of its retreat. Revenue is modest relative to the brand's fame — roughly C$270M in FY2025 (approximate, as of June 2026) — and the company has never been sustainably profitable.

The **Canopy USA** structure is the defining feature for traders: it lets a US-listed, federally-compliant company hold options/rights over plant-touching US businesses without consolidating their (federally illegal) revenue until reform permits. It is the most explicit "legalization call option" structure among the Canadian LPs. See [[regulation]].

## Bull vs Bear Case

**Bull case**
- The 2026 recapitalization extended liabilities to 2031, materially reducing near-term insolvency risk and buying time to reach the reform catalyst.
- Canopy USA is a pre-built, optioned path into the world's largest cannabis market the moment federal restrictions ease.
- Narrowing losses, growing Canadian adult-use and medical revenue, and a real, profitable hardware business (Storz & Bickel).
- High brand recognition and deep options liquidity make it a preferred reform-trade vehicle.

**Bear case**
- Chronic cash burn and a never-sustainably-profitable history; revenue is small relative to the brand and market cap.
- Heavy, repeated dilution and reverse-split risk; Constellation's retreat removed the marquee strategic backer.
- Reform timelines have slipped for years — the Canopy USA call option can stay out-of-the-money indefinitely.
- Canadian rec is oversupplied and price-compressed; structurally thin segment margins.

## Valuation Framework (qualitative)

- **US-federal rescheduling optionality** — Canopy USA makes this the purest "legalization call" among the LPs; valuation is dominated by the probability and timing the market assigns to US permissibility.
- **Gross margin & cash burn** — the survival variable; watch the path of operating losses and cash runway, especially post-recapitalization.
- **Dilution / share count** — the dominant per-share headwind; convertible and equity issuance history is severe.
- **Balance sheet / refinancing** — the January 2026 recap (liabilities pushed to 2031, term-loan reduction) reset the solvency clock; refinancing risk is the key downside scenario.
- **US-vs-Canada market structure** — Canadian rec is commoditized; the prize is consolidating US assets and growing EU medical.
- **Sum-of-the-parts** — Storz & Bickel hardware + medical can be valued as going concerns; Canopy USA is optionality on top.

## Capital Structure & Dilution Risk

- **Recapitalization (January 2026)**: completed a recapitalization extending all outstanding liabilities to January 2031, including a $50M term-loan reduction saving ~$6.5M/year in interest — materially reducing near-term insolvency risk.
- **Constellation exit**: the former anchor investor converted its ~C$5B stake to non-voting exchangeable shares in 2024, removing both the backstop and an overhang of potential supply.
- **Serial dilution**: equity and convertible issuance over the company's history has massively expanded the share count; reverse-split risk remains if the price deteriorates.
- For traders, dilution is the persistent counterweight to reform-driven rallies and the reason the long-term chart trends down despite periodic squeezes.
