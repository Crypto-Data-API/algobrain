Paramount Skydance Corporation (NASDAQ: PSKY) is the media and entertainment company created on August 7, 2025 by the merger of Skydance Media and Paramount Global, led by CEO David Ellison and backed by the Ellison family and RedBird Capital. For traders it is the most event-driven name in the [[communication-services|Communication Services]] media complex — a turnaround/consolidation story whose price is dominated by headlines around its pursuit of [[warner-bros-discovery|Warner Bros. Discovery]]. It is a constituent of the [[s-and-p-500]] index.

## Business Overview & Segments

PSKY combines a deep catalogue of film and TV intellectual property with the CBS broadcast network and a scaling streaming platform. The business reports across three pillars; the strategic story is the migration of the company's economics from declining linear [[television]] to streaming, while a parallel M&A campaign for [[warner-bros-discovery|Warner Bros. Discovery]] dominates the equity.

| Segment | What it is | Role in the story |
|---|---|---|
| **Studios** | Paramount Pictures, Skydance's film/animation operations, and Nickelodeon/MTV content production | Content engine; management has committed to releasing **15+ theatrical films annually starting in 2026** — a deliberate bet on theatrical scale and franchise IP |
| **Direct-to-Consumer (DTC)** | Paramount+, Pluto TV (free ad-supported / FAST), and BET+ | The swing factor in the P&L as streaming moves from losses toward profit. Secured **US media rights to UFC (~$7.7B over seven years beginning 2026)**, a flagship sports anchor for Paramount+ |
| **TV Media** | CBS network, CBS Sports / CBS News, and cable networks (MTV, Comedy Central, Nickelodeon, BET) | Cash-generative today but in **secular linear decline** — the legacy profit pool funding the streaming pivot |

Competitive position: PSKY is subscale versus [[netflix]] and [[disney|Disney]] in streaming, which is the central strategic rationale for consolidation. Management targets at least **$3B of run-rate cost efficiencies** from merger integration.

## Economic Moat & Competitive Position

Paramount Skydance has a **narrow and arguably eroding** [[economic-moat|economic moat]] — a real but contested position rather than a wide, durable one:

- **IP library & franchises** — a century-deep Paramount Pictures catalogue plus Nickelodeon/MTV/BET brands and Skydance's franchise capabilities. Library value is genuine but is a depreciating asset in an era of abundant content.
- **CBS broadcast & sports rights** — CBS is one of the few remaining national over-the-air broadcast networks, with NFL and other sports packages and the new UFC rights giving must-have live programming that travels into Paramount+. Sports and news are the stickiest piece of the linear bundle.
- **Pluto TV / FAST distribution** — a scaled free ad-supported streaming footprint that monetises the back catalogue without a subscription wall.

Against that, the moat is under pressure on every front: streaming scale economics favour the leaders ([[netflix]], [[disney|Disney]], [[amazon|Amazon]]), the legacy linear cash flows that fund the transition are **decaying** as cord-cutting accelerates, and content costs are structurally high. The subscale-in-streaming problem is precisely why management is pursuing [[warner-bros-discovery|Warner Bros. Discovery]] — consolidation is the bet that scale can manufacture a moat the standalone company lacks.

## Growth Drivers & Catalysts

- **Streaming-profitability pivot** — the DTC EBITDA inflection is the single most important operational metric; Q1 2026 showed DTC adjusted EBITDA swinging to $251M (from $4M a year earlier) on +11% revenue, +14% ARPU, and ~700K net adds.
- **UFC on Paramount+ (2026)** — the ~$7.7B/seven-year rights deal is a flagship live-sports subscriber/engagement driver.
- **Film slate scale-up** — the commitment to 15+ theatrical releases annually from 2026, leveraging franchise IP.
- **Cost-efficiency program** — target raised to at least **$3B run-rate**, with heavy headcount reductions across legacy Paramount through 2025-2026.
- **Warner Bros. Discovery pursuit** — the consolidation campaign; any resolution (success or break) is a step-change catalyst.
- **Recurring catalysts** — quarterly earnings (DTC EBITDA trajectory), WBD deal headlines, UFC launch, film opening weekends, and any asset sales of legacy cable networks.

## Bull Case vs Bear Case

### Bull case
- The streaming-profitability pivot is real and accelerating — DTC EBITDA has inflected and ARPU/subscribers are rising, narrowing the gap to the scale leaders.
- Deep IP library, CBS broadcast, and marquee sports rights (NFL, UFC) give must-have live and franchise content.
- $3B+ cost-efficiency program plus disciplined integration can lift margins independent of any deal.
- A successful [[warner-bros-discovery|WBD]] combination would add HBO/Warner IP and the scale to compete with [[netflix]] and [[disney|Disney]] — a re-rating catalyst.
- Ellison/RedBird backing brings capital and a long-horizon owner orientation.

### Bear case
- Subscale in streaming with negative trailing earnings; the path to durable profit is unproven at scale.
- Linear/cable decline may outpace streaming gains, so the funding base for the transition shrinks faster than DTC grows.
- The WBD pursuit risks **overpaying or over-leveraging** — or losing the asset after committing capital and management bandwidth.
- Ellison/RedBird control structure limits minority-holder influence over capital allocation.
- A pure event-driven name: the stock is hostage to deal headlines and antitrust/financing risk, not fundamentals.

## Key Risks

- **WBD deal risk** — overpaying for or losing [[warner-bros-discovery|Warner Bros. Discovery]] after capital and attention are committed; rival bidders ([[comcast]], others) and antitrust/financing hurdles can swing the outcome and the stock violently.
- **Linear decline** — CBS/cable cash flows decaying faster than streaming gains can replace them.
- **Leverage** — a mega-deal would materially raise debt (D/E already 1.11x per the feed); a strained balance sheet in a content-cost-heavy business is a real solvency-of-strategy risk.
- **Control structure** — Ellison family / RedBird control limits minority-shareholder influence on strategy and capital allocation.
- **Integration execution** — realizing $3B of synergies while pivoting the model and (potentially) absorbing WBD is operationally complex.
- **Cyclicality** — advertising and theatrical revenue are macro-sensitive; a downturn pressures both linear and streaming-ad economics.

## Valuation & How the Stock Trades

PSKY is a **pure event-driven / M&A name**: price action is dominated by the [[warner-bros-discovery|Warner Bros. Discovery]] saga — bid terms, financing, regulatory posture, and rival-bidder headlines — far more than by the quarterly print. Because trailing earnings are negative (the headline P/E of -1024x is not meaningful), the stock is better framed on **EV/EBITDA or sum-of-the-parts** (studios + DTC + TV media, net of debt) than on P/E. Mechanically:

- **Event-driven, high implied vol** — liquid single-name options carry persistently elevated implied volatility because of deal risk; the stock gaps on WBD developments more than on earnings.
- **No P/E anchor** — with negative net income, valuation runs off EV/EBITDA, segment sum-of-parts, and deal-scenario math rather than an earnings multiple.
- **Token dividend** — a nominal payout; capital return is not the story and is subordinate to deleveraging and deal financing.
- **Market cap** roughly **$17B as of mid-2026 (approximate)**; PSKY inherited Paramount Global's [[s-and-p-500]] membership.
- **Sector beta** — trades with the linear-media / streaming-consolidation theme; correlated to [[warner-bros-discovery]], [[disney]], [[netflix]], and [[comcast]].

> High-volatility, unresolved-situation caveat: the WBD outcome is **undetermined as of June 2026** — figures and the deal status below reflect that uncertainty and can move sharply on a single headline.

## 2025-2026 Developments

- **Merger closed August 7, 2025:** Skydance Media and Paramount Global completed their merger and PSKY began trading on Nasdaq the same day; David Ellison became chairman and CEO, with the deal following FCC approval of the CBS license transfers.
- **Warner Bros. Discovery pursuit:** through late 2025 and into 2026 PSKY waged an aggressive (at times hostile) campaign to acquire [[warner-bros-discovery|Warner Bros. Discovery]], including a tender-style bid reported around January 2026. As of June 2026 the outcome remains unresolved — financing, antitrust, and WBD board-process risk keep it the dominant driver of the stock.
- **FY2025 results:** revenue in the high-$28B to ~$29.5B range with a marginal net loss (net margin -0.3% per the fundamentals feed) as restructuring and integration costs offset improving streaming economics.
- **Q1 2026 (reported May 2026):** DTC segment adjusted EBITDA swung to $251M (from $4M a year earlier) on revenue of $2.4B (+11% YoY), ARPU up 14%, and ~700K net subscriber additions — evidence the streaming-profitability pivot is working.
- **Cost program:** efficiency target raised to at least $3B run-rate; heavy headcount reductions across legacy Paramount through 2025-2026.
