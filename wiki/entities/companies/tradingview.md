TradingView is the world's leading browser-based charting and social trading platform, serving over 60 million monthly active users across stocks, [[crypto|cryptocurrency]], [[forex]], [[futures]], and [[bonds]] markets. Founded in 2011 by Stan Bokov and a team of developers who previously built MultiCharts (a desktop trading platform), TradingView was built from the ground up as a web-native application, making professional-grade charting tools accessible to anyone with a browser — a significant departure from the desktop-only platforms that dominated the industry at the time.

## Platform and Features

TradingView's core product is its interactive charting platform, which supports hundreds of built-in [[indicators]] (including [[rsi|RSI]], [[macd|MACD]], [[bollinger-bands|Bollinger Bands]], and volume profile), drawing tools, and multi-timeframe analysis. The platform's proprietary scripting language, **Pine Script**, allows users to create custom indicators, strategies, and alerts, and has fostered a large community of user-generated scripts shared through TradingView's public library. The social component is a key differentiator: users publish trade ideas with annotated charts, follow other traders, and discuss markets in real time. Data coverage spans global equities (via exchanges and data partners), cryptocurrency (direct exchange feeds), forex, futures, and economic indicators.

## Business Model

TradingView operates on a freemium subscription model. The free tier provides basic charting with limited indicators and one chart per layout. Paid tiers (Essential, Plus, Premium, and the top-tier Ultimate at roughly $50-$60/month billed annually) unlock additional indicators per chart, multi-monitor layouts, more alerts, extended-hours data, second-based timeframes, and priority support. The platform also monetizes broker integrations — users can connect supported brokers and execute trades directly from TradingView charts — plus market-data add-ons. The company raised a **$298M round in 2021 led by Tiger Global at a ~$3 billion valuation**, was described as already profitable at the time, and remains privately held with no disclosed IPO or new round through mid-2026.

As of 2026 TradingView reports **30M+ monthly active users** and well over 100M visitors across 180+ countries (Semrush traffic estimates put it around ~200M+ monthly visits in April 2026), cementing its position as the dominant retail charting destination.

## Trading Relevance

TradingView has become the default charting tool for a generation of retail traders, particularly in cryptocurrency and forex markets where its free tier and browser-based access lower the barrier to entry. For [[technical-analysis]] practitioners, it serves as both an analytical workspace and a social network for sharing and discovering trading ideas. Pine Script has become one of the most widely used languages for retail strategy development and [[backtesting]], though its backtesting engine is simpler than dedicated platforms like QuantConnect or Backtrader. The platform's "ideas" feed and social features make it a useful gauge of retail sentiment and positioning.
