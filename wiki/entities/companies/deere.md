Deere & Company (NYSE: DE), known globally by the John Deere brand, is the world's largest manufacturer of agricultural machinery and a major producer of construction and forestry equipment. For traders it is the purest large-cap proxy for the global farm-income cycle, with a growing precision-agriculture technology layer (autonomous tractors, See & Spray) and a captive finance arm. It is a constituent of the [[s-and-p-500]] index and a core [[industrials|Industrials]] holding.

## Business Overview & Segments

Deere reports through four segments. **Production & Precision Agriculture** is the profit engine — large tractors, combines, sprayers and the embedded precision-ag technology layer that increasingly monetises iron after the sale. **Small Agriculture & Turf** covers compact/utility tractors and turf equipment, more exposed to consumer/residential demand. **Construction & Forestry** is the cyclical earth-moving and logging arm that puts Deere in direct competition with [[caterpillar]]. **Financial Services** (John Deere Financial) finances dealer and retail purchases and explains the elevated *consolidated* leverage relative to the industrial business alone — a captive-finance feature shared with [[paccar]] and [[caterpillar]].

Revenue is driven primarily by North American large-ag equipment demand, which tracks farm cash receipts, crop prices (corn/soybeans), and dealer inventory levels. Deere's strategic pivot is toward recurring, higher-margin revenue from precision-ag software and autonomy, targeting per-acre monetisation on top of equipment sales — a deliberate attempt to layer a software-like annuity onto a cyclical hardware base.

## Economic Moat & Competitive Position

Deere holds a wide [[economic-moat|economic moat]] resting on three reinforcing layers:

- **Dealer network & aftermarket** — an entrenched, exclusive dealer footprint provides parts, service, and used-equipment trade-in support that buyers depend on through the multi-decade life of a machine. High-margin parts and service revenue is the stickiest, least-cyclical profit stream, and the network is effectively impossible to replicate at scale. This is the classic equipment-OEM moat shared with [[caterpillar]] and [[paccar]].
- **Precision-ag / data lock-in** — the JDLink/Operations Center platform, guidance, and See & Spray create [[switching-costs]]: agronomic data, fleet telematics, and trained operators accumulate inside the Deere ecosystem, raising the cost of switching brands and opening a software-style per-acre monetisation path.
- **Scale, brand, and installed base** — Deere's manufacturing scale, R&D budget, and the strongest brand in agriculture amortise costs no rival can match and give it priority in a consolidating dealer channel.

The moat is wide but the *demand* is deeply cyclical: the strongest network in the world cannot offset a farm-income downturn. Right-to-repair pressure (farmers and regulators pushing for independent repair access) is the most-watched threat to the parts/service annuity.

## Growth Drivers & Catalysts

- **Farm-income recovery** — the dominant swing factor; rising crop prices and USDA farm cash-receipt forecasts drive large-ag replacement demand.
- **Precision-ag & autonomy monetisation** — recurring software/per-acre revenue (See & Spray, autonomous tillage) layered onto iron sales; the structural re-rating thesis.
- **Dealer inventory normalisation** — destocking that depressed FY2025 reversing as the cycle turns.
- **Aftermarket parts & service** — a durable, high-margin annuity that grows with the installed base.
- **Construction/infrastructure spend** — C&F demand tied to US construction and infrastructure cycles.
- **Recurring catalysts** — quarterly earnings (Feb/May/Aug/Nov), USDA farm-income forecasts, corn/soybean prices, tariff headlines, and full-year guidance revisions.

## Bull Case vs Bear Case

### Bull case
- Early-cycle ag recovery: farm income troughed and replacement demand on an aging fleet is set to rebound off a low FY2025 base.
- Precision-ag and autonomy convert a cyclical hardware company into a partly recurring-revenue compounder, supporting a higher through-cycle multiple.
- Best-in-class dealer network and aftermarket annuity defend margins even in down years.
- Disciplined capital return (buybacks + a growing dividend) compounds per-share value.

### Bear case
- Deep cyclicality: a prolonged farm-income downturn (low crop prices, high input costs) can crush volumes and operating leverage works violently in reverse.
- Cycle-trough earnings make the PE look deceptively high right when the stock may be cheapest.
- Right-to-repair legislation could erode the parts/service moat.
- Tariffs on inputs/steel and on ag exports squeeze both costs and end-demand.

## Key Risks

- **Ag-cycle / farm-income risk** — the central driver; crop-price and farm-income downturns hit volumes hard.
- **Dealer inventory & used-equipment values** — overstocked channels and falling used prices defer new-equipment orders and pressure Financial Services credit.
- **Right-to-repair / regulation** — legislation mandating independent repair access threatens high-margin parts and service.
- **Tariffs & trade** — input-cost inflation and retaliatory tariffs on US ag exports cut both ways.
- **Interest-rate sensitivity** — Financial Services and customer affordability are rate-exposed; higher rates raise financing costs for buyers.
- **Execution on autonomy** — the precision/autonomy monetisation thesis is not yet fully proven at scale.

## Valuation & How the Stock Trades

Deere is a *cyclical quality compounder*, and the most important valuation nuance is that its multiple must be read **through the cycle**, not on trailing earnings. At a cycle trough, earnings compress faster than the price, so the trailing PE optically expands — the stock can look "expensive" precisely when it is discounting recovery; conversely, at a cycle peak, a low PE on peak earnings can be a value trap. Mechanically:

- **Cyclical, market-beta name** — Deere trades on the farm-income cycle and broad industrial sentiment; it is not a defensive holding.
- **Through-cycle framing** — investors normalise earnings across the cycle rather than capitalising trough or peak EPS; the quality-compounder profile (see Fundamentals below) typically earns a premium to commodity-equipment peers.
- **Catalyst-driven moves** — large reactions to USDA farm-income data, crop prices, and guidance revisions, often more than to the headline print.
- **Capital return** — a steadily growing dividend plus consistent buybacks; the dividend is real but the equity story is cyclical recovery + precision-ag optionality, not yield.

## 2025–2026 Developments

- **FY2025 (ended 2 Nov 2025):** revenue declined versus 2024 amid a soft global ag downcycle and dealer inventory digestion, with tariff and input-cost pressure weighing on margins.
- **FQ1 2026 (Feb 2026):** net income of $656 million — trough-cycle earnings as the ag downturn bottomed.
- **FQ2 2026 (May 2026):** clear upside surprise — net sales and revenues of $13.369 billion, net income of $1.773 billion, EPS $6.55, with strength across major segments. Management **raised full-year FY2026 net income guidance to $4.5–5.0 billion**.
- The improving tone through 1H FY2026 has the market treating DE as an early-cycle ag-recovery play; key watch items remain US farm income, tariffs on inputs/exports, and used-equipment inventories.
