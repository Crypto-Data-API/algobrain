**NRW Holdings Limited** (ASX: NWH) is a diversified mining and civil contracting company listed on the [[asx-limited|Australian Securities Exchange]] and a constituent of the [[asx-200]] index. NRW Holdings Limited is a leading diversified Australian contract services provider to the resources and infrastructure sectors. Headquartered in Belmont, Perth, NRW provides civil construction, contract mining, and specialised minerals, energy and technology services, together with drill and blast, heavy equipment refurbishment, industrial engineering, and fabrication.

## Business Model & Segments

NRW reports three operating segments (with a fourth segment added in 2025):

- **Civil:** Delivers private and public civil infrastructure, mine development, bulk earthworks, and commercial/residential subdivision projects. Projects include roads, bridges, tailings storage facilities, rail formations, ports, renewable energy projects, water infrastructure, and concrete installations.
- **Mining:** Provides mine management, contract mining, load and haul, dragline, drill and blast, coal handling preparation plant services, maintenance services, and fabrication of water and service vehicles. Subsidiaries include Golding Contractors, Action Drill & Blast, and RCR Mining Technologies.
- **Minerals, Energy & Technologies (MET):** Designs, constructs, operates and maintains global resource projects. Offers materials handling design, fabrication, shutdown, onsite maintenance, industrial engineering, industrial electrical engineering, automation, switchboard design and manufacturing, instrumentation and electrical design and construction, refurbishment and rebuild of earthmoving equipment, heat treatment, and mining equipment solutions. Key brand is Primero.
- **Fredon (from 2025):** Industrial and building services contractor; acquisition announced September 2025 and completed October 2025.

## Economic Moat & Competitive Position

NRW is a **contractor / services** business, not a commodity producer — so, like [[orica|Orica]], it is **not a commodity price-taker**. Its earnings depend on mining and infrastructure *activity* (capex and production volumes), not on the price of what its clients dig up. Its competitive position is moderate — a scaled, diversified incumbent rather than a wide-moat franchise:

- **Volume / capex exposure, not price** — NRW is paid per contract and per tonne moved or earth shifted; it is a "picks-and-shovels" play on Australian mining **capex** (iron ore, gold, coal) and public infrastructure spend, more insulated from commodity-price swings than a miner.
- **Scale, diversification and order book** — across Civil, Mining and MET (plus Fredon), with a multi-billion-dollar Work-in-Hand order book (~A$7.25bn), NRW has the balance-sheet and equipment scale to bid large multi-year contracts that sub-scale contractors cannot — a modest barrier.
- **Switching / relationship lock-in** — long-life mine-services contracts (5-6 years), embedded operational integration with majors ([[bhp]], [[rio-tinto]], [[fortescue]]), and a fleet/equipment base create moderate [[switching-costs|switching costs]] and repeat-business stickiness.
- **The honest caveat** — mining/civil contracting is **competitive and low-margin** (net margins ~1-3%), with limited pricing power and recurring **fixed-price contract risk** (FY2025 impairments). There is no durable pricing moat; the edge is scale, execution and order-book visibility.

Honest verdict: a diversified, scaled mining-services contractor — steadier than a miner because it tracks capex/volumes, but thin-margin and competitive, so the "moat" is scale and order-book visibility rather than pricing power.

## Growth Drivers & Catalysts

- **Order-book / contract wins** — large multi-year awards (e.g. ~A$750m TEC Coal/Meandu, ~A$360m Evolution Castle Hill, ~A$167m Rio Tinto Brockman) refill and extend Work-in-Hand; the tender pipeline was cited at ~A$25.2bn.
- **Guidance upgrades** — NRW has a pattern of intra-year upgrades (two in two months around the Nov-2025 AGM); H1 FY2026 lifted FY26 guidance to ~A$4.1-4.2bn revenue / ~A$275-285m underlying EBITDA.
- **Fredon acquisition** — adds a new building-services segment (~A$1.15bn Work-in-Hand at close) and diversifies earnings.
- **Australian mining capex + infrastructure spend** — iron-ore sustaining capex, gold-project builds, and public infrastructure programs drive the addressable market.
- **Recurring catalysts** — half-year (Feb) and full-year (Aug) results, AGM guidance (Nov), and large contract-win announcements.

## Bull Case vs Bear Case

### Bull case
- Diversified services exposure to mining capex *and* public infrastructure — steadier than a single-commodity miner.
- Large, growing order book (~A$7.25bn WIH) and a huge tender pipeline give multi-year revenue visibility.
- Track record of guidance upgrades; record H1 FY2026 (revenue +19.5%, underlying EBITDA +36.5%).
- Fully franked, rising dividend plus accretive M&A (Fredon).

### Bear case
- Thin, competitive contractor margins (~1-3% net) with limited pricing power and fixed-price contract-blowout risk (FY2025 impairments).
- Cyclical: leveraged to the Australian mining-capex cycle — a downturn in iron ore/gold/coal capex would hit volumes.
- WA labour-cost inflation and skills shortages pressure margins.
- Order-book concentration in a handful of resources clients.

## Key Risks

- **Mining-capex cycle (demand)** — earnings ride Australian resources capex and production volumes; a capex downturn (iron ore, gold, coal) reduces work.
- **Contract / margin risk** — fixed-price civil and mining contracts can blow out (the FY2025 non-cash impairments); execution and pricing discipline are critical at thin margins.
- **Labour & input costs** — WA labour-cost inflation, equipment and fuel costs erode margins.
- **Client concentration** — order book skewed to a few large resources clients ([[bhp]], [[rio-tinto]], [[fortescue]] and gold/coal miners).
- **Acquisition integration** — Fredon, Primero and prior deals carry integration and impairment risk.
- **Rates / balance sheet** — moderate leverage; rising rates lift financing costs on equipment and acquisitions.

## Valuation & How the Stock Trades

The key valuation nuance for NRW is the **statutory-vs-underlying gap**: FY2025 statutory NPAT (~A$27.7m) was depressed by non-cash impairments, inflating the headline P/E (~118x) to a meaningless level — the cleaner read is **underlying EBITDA/EPS**, which were strong and rising:

- **Capex-cycle beta, not commodity-price beta** — NRW tracks mining *capex and volumes* rather than commodity spot prices, so it is steadier than a miner but still cyclical; it correlates with mining-services peers ([[monadelphous]], [[mineral-resources]]) and client capex.
- **Order-book-driven re-rating** — the stock moves on contract wins, Work-in-Hand growth and (frequent) guidance upgrades; the shares hit an all-time high after the H1 FY2026 result.
- **Dividend** — pays a rising, fully franked dividend — the franking gross-up materially lifts after-tax yield for Australian-resident investors.
- **Liquidity** — reasonable ASX liquidity for a small-to-mid-cap industrial; no meaningful listed options market, so exposure is via shares.

## History

NRW was established in 1994 in Kalgoorlie by Jeff McGlinn and John Silverthorne. Its first contract was at the Granny Smith Gold Mine. Key milestones:

- **1994-1998:** Expanded into additional gold and iron-ore mine contracts across WA; founded a transport division in 1998.
- **2000s:** Grew rapidly with multiple contracts in the Pilbara for BHP, Fortescue, and Rio Tinto.
- **5 September 2007:** IPO on the ASX, raising approximately A$46.6 million with a market capitalisation around A$605.6 million.
- **2010s:** Diversified by establishing Action Drill & Blast for specialist drill and blast services.
- **2019:** Acquired BGC Contracting, significantly scaling civil and contract mining.
- **March 2021:** Completed acquisition of Primero Group Limited, adding significant scale and capability in minerals, resources and technology.
- **2022:** Acquired DIAB Engineering.
- **September–October 2025:** Acquired Fredon Industries for up to A$200 million (announced 2 September 2025, completed October 2025); added as a new reporting segment.

## Recent Developments

For FY2025 (year ended 30 June 2025), NRW reported:

- Revenue of approximately A$3.27-3.30 billion, up approximately 12.2% year on year.
- Continued margin and earnings growth across Civil, Mining, and MET segments.

Outlook and trading:

- At the November 2025 AGM, NRW delivered its second earnings guidance upgrade in two months.
- FY2026 revenue expected in the range of approximately A$3.2-3.3 billion.
- Group order book (Work in Hand) of approximately A$7.25 billion (after Fredon acquisition), up from approximately A$7.1 billion at November 2025.

Major contract wins in 2025 and early 2026:

- A$750 million, 5.5-year mining services agreement with TEC Coal at Stanwell's Meandu Mine (via Golding Contractors).
- A$360 million, six-year mining services contract for Evolution Mining's Castle Hill gold project (WA).
- A$167 million civil contract with Rio Tinto for the Brockman Syncline 1 project in the Pilbara (July 2025).
- A$270 million of other WA mining and infrastructure contracts.

Fredon Industries acquisition (announced September 2025, completed October 2025):

- Added a new business segment with approximately A$1.15 billion of Work in Hand.
- Four secured contracts worth approximately A$150 million contributed to WIH at close of the acquisition.

**Update — H1 FY2026 results (reported February 2026):** NRW delivered a record half: revenue up 19.5% year-on-year to ~A$2.0 billion and underlying EBITDA up 36.5% to A$132.3 million. FY2026 guidance was upgraded to revenue of A$4.1–4.2 billion and underlying EBITDA of A$275–285 million (superseding the earlier ~A$3.2–3.3 billion revenue guidance noted above, reflecting the Fredon acquisition and organic growth). Management cited a ~A$25.2 billion tender pipeline. The interim dividend was lifted to 8.5 cents fully franked (from 7.0 cents), and the shares hit an all-time high after the result. Note that FY2025 statutory NPAT (A$27.7M) was depressed by non-cash impairments, so underlying metrics are the cleaner read.

The most recent filing (annual) was lodged on 2023-06-30. See [[#Financial Reports]] below.
