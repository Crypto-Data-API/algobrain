Aurora Cannabis Inc. (NASDAQ: ACB; TSX: ACB) is a Canadian cannabis producer that has repositioned itself as a **global medical cannabis company**, with operations in Canada, Europe (notably Germany), Australia and other international medical markets. For traders it is a high-recognition retail speculation vehicle whose story has shifted from cash-burning meme stock to a debt-free, free-cash-flow-positive medical-cannabis operator — though it remains a small cap with elevated volatility. It trades on the [[nasdaq]] (and TSX) and is a cannabis-ETF constituent.

## Business Overview

Aurora was founded in 2013 in Alberta and rode the 2017-2019 Canadian legalization bubble to a multi-billion-dollar valuation before massive dilution, write-downs and reverse splits. The current business is deliberately weighted to **international medical cannabis** (higher margin, prescription-driven, less competitive than Canadian recreational):

- **Global medical** — the growth engine; Germany (post-2024 partial legalization), Australia, UK and Poland. Medical net revenue hit a record C$76.2 million in the December 2025 quarter, up 12% YoY.
- **Canadian consumer/recreational** — de-emphasized; low-margin and hyper-competitive.
- **Plant propagation** — the Bevo Farms orchids/vegetable-seedlings business, a non-cannabis cash contributor under periodic strategic review.

The cannabis business is **debt-free**, a key differentiator from leveraged peers.

Aurora's distinguishing strategic choice is to lean into **international medical** rather than chase Canadian recreational volume. Medical cannabis is prescription-gated, higher-margin, and shielded from the commoditized Canadian rec price war, and the German 2024 partial legalization is a structural tailwind. This is a different bet from [[tilray]]'s CPG/beverage diversification or [[sndl]]'s retail/capital-allocation model. See [[healthcare]] and [[regulation]].

## Bull vs Bear Case

**Bull case**
- Genuine turnaround: debt-free cannabis business, positive adjusted EBITDA and free cash flow — a rarity in the sector.
- Medical-cannabis tilt (Germany/EU/Australia) is higher-margin and growing, insulated from Canadian rec price collapse.
- Record medical revenue (C$76.2M in the December 2025 quarter, +12%) and improving adjusted gross margin (62% in Q3 FY2026) show operating leverage.
- Optionality on US reform on top of an actually-improving base business.

**Bear case**
- Still a small cap with thin/negative GAAP profitability (GAAP net loss in the December 2025 quarter despite positive FCF).
- Small-cap liquidity and elevated volatility; sector beta dominates the price on policy news.
- Regulatory dependence in each medical market (Germany rule changes, Australia access).
- Historical willingness to dilute; legacy of write-downs and reverse splits weighs on sentiment.

## Capital Structure & Dilution Risk

- **Debt-free**: the cannabis business carries no debt, removing the refinancing/insolvency risk that defines leveraged peers like the pre-recap [[canopy-growth]].
- **Cash balance**: ~C$154-186 million during FY2025/FY2026, funding the medical-growth strategy and Bevo without forced raises.
- **Dilution history**: Aurora rode the 2017-2019 bubble to a multi-billion valuation, then went through massive dilution, write-downs and multiple reverse splits — the legacy that weighs on sentiment even as the current balance sheet is clean.
- For traders, the absence of near-term forced dilution is a genuine positive versus the rest of the group, but historical willingness to issue equity keeps it a watch item.
