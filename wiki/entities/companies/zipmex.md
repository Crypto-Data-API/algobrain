Zipmex was a Southeast Asian cryptocurrency exchange (Thailand, Indonesia, Singapore, Australia) that offered spot trading, an earn/yield product ("ZipUp+"), and a native Z Coin token. It is now defunct: the exchange froze customer withdrawals in **July 2022** during the crypto-credit crisis and, after a multi-year restructuring effort, effectively ceased to operate as a going concern by 2024. For traders it is a textbook case study in **exchange counterparty and custodial risk** — a smaller-scale parallel to the larger 2022 failures.

## What Happened

- **Founded 2018** in Singapore; grew quickly across Thailand and Indonesia, marketing high-yield "earn" products to retail users.
- **July 2022:** Zipmex **suspended withdrawals** as the contagion from [[celsius-network|Celsius]], [[voyager-digital|Voyager]] and Babel Finance spread. Its losses traced to exposure of its yield program to distressed lending counterparties — notably Babel Finance and Celsius — so customer funds advertised as "earn" deposits were impaired when those counterparties failed.
- **2022-2024:** Repeated restructuring and creditor-protection (Singapore moratorium) efforts, partial and staggered withdrawal re-openings, and search for rescue capital. None produced a durable recovery, and the platform's relevance collapsed.
- **Final status:** By 2024 the business had effectively wound down; the precise legal/liquidation endpoint is not cleanly documented, but Zipmex no longer functions as a meaningful exchange.

## Trading Lessons

- **Custodial risk is real risk:** assets on a centralised exchange — especially in a yield/"earn" product — are an unsecured claim on the exchange's balance sheet, not segregated client property. "Not your keys, not your coins."
- **Yield is a credit decision:** advertised exchange yields are funded by lending customer deposits to third parties; the headline APY hides the counterparty's solvency risk.
- **Contagion clusters:** Zipmex did not fail in isolation — it went down in the same 2022 wave as [[ftx|FTX]], [[blockfi|BlockFi]], [[voyager-digital|Voyager Digital]] and [[celsius-network|Celsius]], illustrating how interconnected crypto lenders transmit shocks.

Zipmex stands as an example of the exchange [[counterparty-risk|counterparty risk]] present in the cryptocurrency market.
