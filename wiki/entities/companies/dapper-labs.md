# Dapper Labs

**Dapper Labs** is a blockchain technology company founded in **2018** by Roham Gharegozlou (CEO), Dieter Shirley, and Mikhael Naayem. The company is best known for creating [[cryptokitties|CryptoKitties]] (2017, while still at Axiom Zen), the [[flow-blockchain|Flow blockchain]], and [[nba-top-shot|NBA Top Shot]]. Dapper Labs raised over **$600 million** in venture capital from investors including Andreessen Horowitz, Coatue Management, Google Ventures, and Samsung. The company was a defining force in the 2020-2021 NFT boom but has since struggled to maintain relevance as the NFT market contracted and competing platforms on [[solana|Solana]] and [[ethereum|Ethereum]] L2s gained dominance.

## History and Key Products

### CryptoKitties (2017)

CryptoKitties was a collectible game on [[ethereum|Ethereum]] where users bred, collected, and traded unique digital cats represented as [[nft|NFTs]]. Launched in November 2017, it was the first mainstream NFT application and famously **congested the Ethereum network** at its peak, with CryptoKitties transactions accounting for ~25% of all Ethereum traffic in December 2017.

- First project to use the ERC-721 standard (which Dieter Shirley co-authored)
- Some CryptoKitties sold for over $100,000
- The Ethereum congestion problem directly motivated Dapper Labs to build a purpose-built blockchain (Flow)

### Flow Blockchain (2020)

[[flow-blockchain|Flow]] is a layer-1 blockchain designed by Dapper Labs specifically for NFTs, games, and consumer applications. Key design decisions:

- **Multi-node architecture**: Separates consensus, verification, execution, and collection roles across different node types (unlike Ethereum's monolithic validator model)
- **Resource-oriented programming**: Uses Cadence, a purpose-built smart contract language
- **Consumer-friendly**: Designed for custodial wallets and credit card payments (lowering the barrier vs. MetaMask + ETH gas fees)

Flow launched in 2020 with exclusive partnerships for NBA Top Shot and other licensed sports collectibles.

### NBA Top Shot (2020-2021)

[[nba-top-shot|NBA Top Shot]] was Dapper Labs' breakout product — an officially licensed NBA digital collectibles platform where users buy, sell, and trade "Moments" (video highlights as NFTs).

| Metric | Peak (2021) | Status |
|---|---|---|
| **Monthly active users** | ~1.1 million | Declined significantly |
| **Total sales volume** | $800M+ (all-time by mid-2021) | Dramatically reduced |
| **Highest single sale** | $208,000 (LeBron James dunk) | Secondary market collapsed |
| **New user onboarding** | Credit card + custodial wallet | Maintained |

NBA Top Shot introduced millions of non-crypto users to NFTs and demonstrated that licensed IP could drive mainstream adoption. However, the platform experienced:

- **90%+ price declines** on most Moments from 2021 peaks to 2023
- SEC scrutiny — in 2023, a federal court ruled that NBA Top Shot Moments could be securities under the Howey test
- User base contraction as NFT hype faded

### Other Licensed Products

Dapper Labs expanded the Top Shot model to other sports leagues:

- **NFL All Day** (American football Moments)
- **UFC Strike** (MMA Moments)
- **LaLiga Golazos** (soccer highlights)

All followed similar trajectories: initial excitement, followed by declining volumes as the broader NFT market cooled.

## Current Status and Challenges (2024-2026)

Dapper Labs faces significant headwinds:

### Flow Adoption Struggles
- Flow's total value locked (TVL) and developer activity remain far behind [[ethereum|Ethereum]], [[solana|Solana]], and major L2s ([[arbitrum|Arbitrum]], [[optimism|Optimism]], [[base|Base]])
- The proprietary Cadence language creates a developer adoption barrier — most blockchain developers know Solidity (EVM) or Rust (Solana), not Cadence
- Flow attempted EVM compatibility in 2024 to attract Ethereum developers, but adoption has been slow
- DeFi ecosystem on Flow is minimal compared to competitors

### Market Position
- The licensed sports NFT vertical is a niche rather than the mainstream breakthrough Dapper Labs envisioned
- Competitors like Sorare (fantasy sports NFTs on Ethereum) captured similar markets
- The broader [[nft|NFT market]] contracted 90%+ from 2021-2022 peaks, with OpenSea (Ethereum), Magic Eden (Solana), and Blur dominating remaining volume — none on Flow
- Dapper Labs conducted multiple rounds of layoffs (2022-2023), reducing headcount significantly

### Regulatory Risk
- The 2023 court ruling that NBA Top Shot Moments could be securities creates ongoing legal uncertainty
- If Moments are securities, the entire Top Shot model may require SEC registration or restructuring

## Trading Relevance

- **FLOW token**: The native token of the Flow blockchain; trades on major exchanges but has declined ~95% from its ATH ($46.16 in Apr 2021) — a cautionary tale about L1 tokens tied to single-use-case ecosystems
- **NFT market barometer**: Dapper Labs' trajectory mirrors the broader NFT cycle — useful for understanding how narrative-driven crypto sectors rise and fall
- **Licensed IP model**: Dapper Labs proved that real-world brands can drive crypto adoption but also showed that brand partnerships alone do not create sustainable token value
- **Lesson for traders**: Projects with peak-cycle valuations ($7.6B) and strong brand partnerships can still lose 90%+ of value when the underlying narrative (NFTs, in this case) deflates
