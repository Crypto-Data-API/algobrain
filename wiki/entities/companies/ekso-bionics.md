Ekso Bionics Holdings, Inc. (NASDAQ: EKSO) is a micro-cap medical-device company that designs and sells wearable exoskeleton [[robotics-overview|robots]] for rehabilitation and industrial use. As a thinly traded nano/micro-cap, it is a speculative, high-volatility name whose stock reacts sharply to revenue trends, financing events, and reimbursement news.

## Business Overview

Ekso Bionics develops powered exoskeletons that help people walk and lift. Its two main product families are:

- **EksoHealth** — rehabilitation exoskeletons (e.g., EksoNR) used in clinics and hospitals to help patients recovering from stroke, spinal cord injury, and acquired brain injury relearn gait. The EksoNR has FDA clearances across several neurological indications.
- **EksoWorks / Industrial** — passive and powered exoskeletons (e.g., EVO vest) that reduce fatigue and injury for industrial workers performing overhead or repetitive tasks.

The devices use sensors and control algorithms to adapt assistance to the user's movement, which is the basis for the company's "AI-assisted robotics" positioning. Ekso remains pre-profit with modest revenue (roughly $15-20 million annually as of June 2026, approximate) and an ongoing reliance on capital markets to fund operations.
