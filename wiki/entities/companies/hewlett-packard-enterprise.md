Hewlett Packard Enterprise (NYSE: HPE) is an enterprise IT infrastructure company — servers (including AI systems), hybrid cloud/storage, networking, and the GreenLake as-a-service platform — created in the 2015 split of the original Hewlett-Packard. A constituent of the [[s-and-p-500]] index and the [[technology]] sector, after closing the $14B [[juniper-networks|Juniper Networks]] acquisition in July 2025 it has become one of the principal listed plays on AI data-centre networking and on [[ai-data-center-power-demand|AI data-centre buildout]] alongside [[cisco]] and [[arista-networks]].

## Business Overview & Segments

HPE sells enterprise infrastructure across compute, storage, and networking, increasingly wrapped in the GreenLake consumption-based (as-a-service) model that carries the recurring-revenue story. After the Juniper acquisition, **Networking became HPE's largest profit pool**, reshaping the company from a margin-thin server vendor toward higher-margin networking and services.

| Segment | What it is | Role |
|---|---|---|
| **Server** | Traditional enterprise servers + AI systems built around [[nvidia]] GPUs | Largest revenue line; lower margin; AI-server demand is the volume driver |
| **Hybrid Cloud** | Storage, private cloud, GreenLake platform | The recurring-revenue and as-a-service growth story |
| **Networking** (Aruba + Juniper) | Campus/branch wireless, routing, data-centre switching, Mist AI ops | Now the largest profit pool after the Juniper deal; the AI-networking franchise |
| **HPE Financial Services** | Financing for customers and channel | Earns a finance spread; supports hardware sales |

Two corporate developments reshaped the company: the **Juniper acquisition** (announced January 2024 at $40/share, ~$14B cash; cleared after a DOJ antitrust challenge via a June 28, 2025 settlement that required divesting HPE's Instant On campus/branch business and auctioning a non-exclusive licence to Juniper's Mist AIOps source code; closed July 2, 2025), and **activist involvement** — a July 2025 cooperation agreement with Elliott Investment Management added Robert Calderoni to the board and created a board Strategy Committee.

## Economic Moat & Competitive Position

HPE's [[economic-moat]] is **narrow** and uneven across segments. Commodity servers are a low-margin, competitive business where HPE has little durable pricing power against [[dell-technologies]] and ODMs — the AI-server boom drives volume but not necessarily rich economics. The moat is concentrated in **networking and services**: Aruba and Juniper bring installed bases, certified architectures, and management software (Mist AIOps) that create real [[switching-costs|switching costs]] in enterprise campus and data-centre networks, while **GreenLake** locks customers into multi-year consumption contracts that raise retention and recurring revenue. The Juniper deal materially widened the networking moat, turning HPE into a more credible challenger to [[cisco]] and [[arista-networks]] in AI data-centre switching.

The net position: a thin-moat hardware vendor transitioning toward a higher-margin networking-and-services model. The bull thesis is that Juniper plus GreenLake structurally lifts margins and stickiness; the bear thesis is that servers stay commoditized and AI-server margins disappoint.

## Growth Drivers & Catalysts

- **AI server demand** — enterprise and hyperscale demand for [[nvidia]]-GPU systems drives the Server segment and ties HPE to [[ai-data-center-power-demand|the AI data-centre buildout]].
- **Juniper integration & synergies** — networking is now the largest profit pool; synergy capture and AI-networking share gains are central to the margin story.
- **GreenLake / as-a-service** — growth in consumption-based contracts raises recurring revenue and retention.
- **AI-networking** — Mist AIOps and data-centre switching position HPE against [[cisco]]/[[arista-networks]].
- **Activist-driven discipline** — Elliott's board presence and Strategy Committee push capital allocation and margin focus.
- **Recurring catalysts** — quarterly earnings (off-cycle fiscal year ending October; reports Mar/Jun/Sep/Dec), AI-server order commentary, Juniper synergy updates, Elliott-driven strategic moves.

## Bull Case vs Bear Case

### Bull case
- Juniper makes networking the largest, highest-margin profit pool, structurally lifting mix and reducing reliance on commodity servers.
- AI-server demand drives strong top-line growth; FY2026 quarters have beaten and raised guidance.
- GreenLake builds recurring, sticky as-a-service revenue.
- A low-multiple, value/AI-infrastructure crossover name with activist (Elliott) pressure for discipline.

### Bear case
- Server economics stay commoditized; AI-server volume comes at thin margins.
- Juniper integration is large and complex; synergy and antitrust-remedy execution carry risk.
- FY2025 GAAP profitability collapsed to roughly breakeven in the deal year (see disclaimer below) — the turnaround is not yet proven on a clean GAAP basis.
- Leverage rose to fund Juniper (D/E ~1.07x); the balance-sheet cushion is thinner than peers.

## Key Risks

- **Margin / commoditization** — server hardware is structurally low-margin; AI-server demand may not translate to profit.
- **Integration risk** — absorbing Juniper (and its DOJ-mandated divestitures/licence) is a major undertaking.
- **Leverage** — debt taken on for Juniper raised D/E above 1.0x; interest costs weigh on GAAP earnings.
- **AI capex digestion** — a slowdown in datacenter buildouts would hit server demand.
- **GAAP distortion** — the FY2025 near-zero GAAP EPS makes screened multiples misleading (see disclaimer).

## Valuation & How the Stock Trades

HPE trades as a low-multiple value/AI-infrastructure crossover name; the headline GAAP PE (-1206x per the Fundamentals block) is **an artifact of near-zero FY2025 GAAP EPS in the Juniper deal year, not a structurally lossmaking business** — the market values HPE on forward non-GAAP earnings and free cash flow, which improved sharply into FY2026. Mechanically:

- **Profile** — S&P 500 member with a very liquid options chain and active weeklies; a popular value/AI-infrastructure crossover trade.
- **AI-supplier beta** — sensitive to the same AI capex cycle as [[nvidia]] suppliers but at a much lower multiple; correlates with [[dell-technologies]], [[cisco]], [[arista-networks]], [[super-micro-computer]].
- **Catalysts** — quarterly earnings (off-cycle October fiscal year, reporting Mar/Jun/Sep/Dec), AI-server order commentary, Juniper synergy updates, and Elliott-driven strategic moves.
- **Capital return** — pays a modest dividend; the equity story is the Juniper-driven margin transition and AI-server growth rather than buybacks.
- **Screening caveat** — Fred's framework tags HPE *Speculative / unprofitable*, reflecting FY2025 deal-year GAAP distortion; forward non-GAAP economics improved sharply in FY2026 — treat the screen with that context.

## 2025-2026 Developments

- **FY2025 (ended 2025-10-31)**: GAAP profitability collapsed to roughly breakeven (net margin 0.2%, diluted EPS -$0.04 per the Fundamentals section below) in the first year consolidating Juniper — note the headline PE of -1206x is an artifact of near-zero GAAP EPS, not of a structurally lossmaking business.
- **Q1 FY2026** (reported March 2026): Networking revenue surged 152% YoY on the Juniper consolidation, with strong wireless, routing, and data-centre switching demand.
- **Q2 FY2026** (reported June 2026): record quarter — revenue $10.7B (+40% YoY), non-GAAP EPS $0.79 (+108%), both above guidance; full-year FY2026 guidance raised for revenue, EPS, and free cash flow, with management citing AI, networking, and cloud demand and saying long-term targets will be hit two years early.
