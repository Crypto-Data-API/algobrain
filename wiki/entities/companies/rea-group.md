**REA Group Limited** (ASX: REA) is an internet content & information company listed on the [[asx-limited|Australian Securities Exchange]] and a constituent of the [[asx-200]] index. REA Group Limited (ASX: REA) is a digital real estate technology company headquartered at 511 Church Street, Richmond, Victoria. The company is majority-owned by News Corporation (approximately 61%) and is listed on the ASX as REA.

## Business Model & Segments

REA Group operates a portfolio of online property marketplaces across Australia, Asia and North America:

- realestate.com.au - Australia's largest residential property listings platform, at around four times the audience of its nearest competitor.
- realcommercial.com.au - the leading commercial property listings platform in Australia.
- flatmates.com.au - share and co-living residential marketplace.
- property.com.au - property research, analytics and consumer-facing property insights.
- REA India (78% owned) - Housing.com (consumer property search), Makaan.com, and PropTiger (the latter being divested in 2025).
- Move, Inc. (20% stake) - operator of the US real estate portal realtor.com, with News Corp owning the remaining 80%.

Beyond listings, REA offers mortgage brokerage and home financing solutions via REA Mortgage Choice; property data services; mortgage application and e-lodgement tools for brokers and lenders; commercial real estate information and technology; vendor-paid advertising; digital non-bank lending solutions; and home loans.

### Business overview & segments

REA is best understood as one dominant Australian marketplace surrounded by adjacent and offshore optionality. The core asset is realestate.com.au; everything else either feeds its [[network-effects|network-effect]] flywheel or extends the franchise into new verticals and geographies.

The structural point: realestate.com.au is a near-zero-marginal-cost digital platform with ~96% gross margins, while the offshore and adjacent businesses are where the *growth optionality* (and the execution risk) sits.

## History

REA Group, originally known as realestate.com.au, was established in 1995 by Karl Sabljak, his wife Carmel Sabljak, his brother Steve Sabljak, and business associate Martin Howell, in a Melbourne garage, with the vision of an accessible online property marketplace. The company listed on the ASX in 1999.

A turning point came in 2001 when News Corporation invested in the company, initially acquiring a 44% stake for A$2 million cash and A$8 million in advertising. News Corp subsequently increased its stake via on-market buying and placements, and today holds approximately 61% of REA Group. The company was renamed REA Group Limited in December 2008 to reflect its broader digital real estate strategy.

In October 2014, REA acquired a 20% stake in Move, Inc. - operator of US real estate portal realtor.com - for approximately US$200 million, as part of News Corp's US$950 million purchase of the remaining 80%. In 2018, REA invested in India's Elara Technologies, the parent company of Housing.com, Makaan.com and PropTiger, before acquiring a controlling stake in Elara (re-branded as REA India) in 2020.

## Recent Developments

For FY25 (year ended 30 June 2025), REA Group reported revenue up 15% to A$1.7 billion (with some sources reporting A$1.90 billion on an alternative basis), driven by a 14% increase in yield. Net profit after tax rose 23% to A$564 million, and the Board declared record dividends. REA India delivered 25% year-on-year revenue growth, with Housing.com revenue (H1 2025) up 46% to A$64 million.

In July 2025, REA India entered a binding agreement to divest its PropTiger business to sharpen focus on flagship consumer-facing portal Housing.com. Praveen Sharma was appointed CEO of REA India in July 2025, succeeding Dhruv Agarwala, who resigned on 3 April 2025 to pursue his own entrepreneurial ventures.

For Q1 FY26, REA Group reported group revenue of A$429 million, up 4% year-on-year, with REA India revenue down 20% (despite modest growth at Housing.com, reflecting divestment-related changes). REA Group also announced the retirement of group CEO Owen Wilson.

In February 2026, REA India appointed Ajay Sandhu as Chief Revenue Officer for Housing.com.

## Leadership

CEO Owen Wilson announced his retirement alongside the H1 FY25 results (Feb 2025), with the Board running an internal/external successor search; Wilson stated the failed 2024 bid for UK portal Rightmove was not a factor in the decision. REA India is led by CEO Praveen Sharma (appointed July 2025). The broader Board and executive team have deep experience across digital media, marketplaces, real estate technology and international operations, in many cases drawn from the News Corp group.

## Economic Moat & Competitive Position

REA holds one of the widest [[economic-moat|economic moats]] on the ASX, built on a classic two-sided **[[network-effects|network effect]]** in [[technology|digital]] classifieds:

- **The marketplace flywheel** — property buyers go to the portal with the most listings; agents and vendors list on the portal with the most buyers. Each side reinforces the other, and over two decades that flywheel has compounded realestate.com.au to roughly 4-5x the consumer audience of its nearest rival, [[domain-holdings|Domain]]. A challenger cannot buy its way to parity because it cannot acquire both sides at once.
- **Pricing power via yield, not volume** — because it is the must-have shop window, REA grows revenue primarily by raising price and selling deeper "premiere"/depth advertising products per listing, rather than relying on listing-volume growth. This is the hallmark of a pricing-power monopoly and is why revenue compounds even in flat or down listing markets.
- **Near-zero marginal cost** — ~96% gross margins and ~41% operating margins reflect a software platform where incremental listings cost almost nothing to serve, so price rises drop almost entirely to the bottom line.
- **High [[switching-costs]] for the supply side** — agents' workflows, marketing spend and vendor expectations are wired around realestate.com.au; defecting to a smaller portal means fewer eyeballs for the same dollar.

The moat has clear edges. It is **Australia-specific**: REA India (Housing.com) is a contested #2-type position in a fragmented market, and the Move/realtor.com stake is a minority interest in the US — neither enjoys the AU monopoly economics. And ~61% [[news-corp|News Corp]] ownership constrains free float and means minority holders do not control the register.

## Growth Drivers & Catalysts

- **Yield (price) growth on premiere listings** — annual price increases plus depth/premiere-product upsell is the primary revenue lever, largely decoupled from listing volumes.
- **Australian listing volumes & vendor-paid advertising depth** — turnover in the housing market sets the volume backdrop; how much vendors spend per listing sets the value.
- **REA India pivot to Housing.com** — sharpening focus on the flagship consumer portal after the 2025 PropTiger divestment.
- **Financial-services attach** — Mortgage Choice broking and home-loan monetisation layered onto the listings audience.
- **Data & insights products** — property.com.au and analytics extending monetisation beyond listings.
- **CEO succession clarity** — resolution of the group-CEO transition is a re-rating swing factor.
- **Renewed offshore M&A** — post-Rightmove, any further international expansion is an optionality/risk catalyst.
- **Recurring catalysts** — half-year/full-year results (Feb/Aug), monthly listings data, RBA rate decisions, and residential transaction-volume prints.

## Bull Case vs Bear Case

### Bull case
- Dominant two-sided [[network-effects|network-effect]] monopoly in Australian property classifieds — durable, hard to attack, and a generation ahead of [[domain-holdings|Domain]].
- Pricing power compounds revenue through **yield** rather than volume, so the model grows even in soft listing markets.
- ~96% gross / ~41% operating margins and a near-zero-marginal-cost platform make it a high-quality compounder.
- Optionality not fully in numbers: REA India scale-up, financial-services attach, and data products.
- Backed by [[news-corp|News Corp]], with a long record of disciplined yield-led monetisation.

### Bear case
- Premium ~30x P/E prices in continued execution; any growth wobble can de-rate it hard.
- Earnings volume is cyclically tied to housing listing volumes and rate-driven turnover.
- Agent/vendor pushback and regulatory/ACCC attention on a dominant platform's annual price rises could cap the yield lever.
- ~61% [[news-corp|News Corp]] control and low free float limit minority influence and liquidity.
- Offshore positions (REA India, Move/realtor.com) are weaker #2-type bets with execution risk, after a failed Rightmove tilt.

## Key Risks

- **Listing-volume cyclicality** — group volumes track Australian housing turnover, which is rate- and confidence-sensitive; a turnover slump pressures the volume side of revenue.
- **Pricing/regulatory pushback** — agent and vendor resistance to annual price increases, and potential ACCC/regulatory scrutiny of a dominant platform, threaten the core yield lever.
- **Valuation / multiple compression** — at a premium ~30x multiple, the stock is exposed to de-rating on any disappointment.
- **Control & free float** — ~61% [[news-corp|News Corp]] ownership constrains float and means strategic direction is not in minority hands.
- **Offshore execution** — REA India and the Move/realtor.com stake are contested positions, not monopolies, with weaker economics and execution risk.
- **Competitive resurgence** — a re-energised [[domain-holdings|Domain]] under new ownership is the main structural threat to AU dominance.

## Valuation & How the Stock Trades

REA trades as a premium-multiple [[network-effects|network-effect]] classifieds compounder rather than a cheap stock — the market pays roughly 30x earnings for monopoly economics, recurring high-margin revenue, and the fact that growth comes from **yield (price) rather than volume**. The ~96% gross margin underwrites the premium: incremental revenue is almost pure profit. Mechanically:

- **Yield-led, not volume-led** — the key fundamental swing factors are vendor-paid advertising depth and annual price increases on premiere products; listing *volume* (driven by housing turnover and RBA rate decisions) sets the cyclical backdrop but is the second-order driver.
- **Cyclical overlay** — because listing volumes track AU housing turnover, the stock is sensitive to interest-rate cycles and consumer-confidence-driven transaction activity, even though pricing power smooths the trend.
- **Low free float** — ~61% [[news-corp|News Corp]] ownership constrains the tradable float and the influence of minority holders; the register is dominated by long-only quality-growth managers.
- **Thin options market** — as an ASX single name, listed-options liquidity is limited; positioning is overwhelmingly via the equity.
- **Basket behaviour** — REA trades with the ASX classifieds/network-effect cohort ([[carsales|CAR Group]], [[seek]], [[domain-holdings|Domain]]) and re-rates on AU housing sentiment and rate expectations. See the data block and Fundamentals section above for the figures as pulled.
