Investor's Business Daily (IBD) is a financial media and stock research company founded by [[william-oneil|William O'Neil]] in 1984 (originally as *Investor's Daily*). Originally launched as a print newspaper designed to compete with The Wall Street Journal, IBD distinguished itself by providing data-driven stock ratings and proprietary screening tools rather than opinion-driven editorial. The publication essentially systematized O'Neil's [[canslim|CANSLIM]] methodology into a set of quantitative ratings accessible to retail investors. CANSLIM stands for: **C**urrent earnings, **A**nnual earnings, **N**ew products/highs, **S**upply and demand, **L**eader or laggard, **I**nstitutional sponsorship, **M**arket direction.

## Proprietary Rating System

IBD's core value proposition is its suite of proprietary stock ratings, each scored on a 1-99 scale (99 being the best):

- **Composite Rating** -- a combined score synthesizing earnings growth, [[relative-strength|relative price strength]], industry group rank, SMR, and accumulation/distribution. A stock with a Composite Rating of 95 ranks higher than 95% of all stocks in IBD's database
- **RS (Relative Strength) Rating** -- measures a stock's price performance over the trailing 12 months relative to all other stocks. O'Neil's research found that the best-performing stocks typically had RS Ratings above 80 before their major price advances
- **EPS Rating** -- evaluates a company's earnings-per-share growth, weighting recent quarters more heavily. Combines the latest two quarters' year-over-year growth with the 3-5 year annual growth rate
- **SMR Rating** -- grades a stock's Sales growth, profit Margins, and Return on equity on an A-to-E scale. Filters for fundamental quality alongside price momentum
- **Accumulation/Distribution Rating** -- measures institutional buying versus selling pressure over the trailing 13 weeks, graded A (heavy accumulation) to E (heavy distribution). Acts as a proxy for [[smart-money]] positioning

## Industry Group Rankings

IBD tracks 197 industry groups ranked by six-month price performance. O'Neil's research showed that approximately 37% of a stock's price movement is attributable to its industry group and 12% to its sector, meaning nearly half of a stock's trajectory is determined by where it sits in the market ecosystem. This insight directly informs the [[sector-rotation]] component of the [[sector-momentum-screen|sector momentum screen]] combination strategy.

## Stock Lists and Products

- **IBD 50** -- a weekly list of the 50 top-rated growth stocks, functioning as a momentum-screened watchlist
- **Big Cap 20** -- the 20 highest-rated large-cap stocks, for investors who want momentum exposure with lower volatility
- **IPO Leaders** -- newly public companies meeting IBD's growth and momentum criteria
- **MarketSmith** -- IBD's premium charting and screening platform, offering pattern recognition, institutional ownership data, and O'Neil's proprietary chart annotations. MarketSmith is the tool-of-choice for serious CANSLIM practitioners
- **Leaderboard** -- a curated real-time portfolio of IBD's highest-conviction stock ideas with specific entry points and position sizes

## Market Timing: Market Pulse

IBD publishes a daily "Market Pulse" column that categorizes the market environment as "Confirmed Uptrend," "Uptrend Under Pressure," or "Market in Correction." This follow-through day methodology -- looking for a strong-volume rally on day 4+ of a rally attempt -- provides a systematic framework for [[market-timing]] that complements the individual stock screening.

## Influence on Retail Investing

IBD played a pivotal role in popularizing [[momentum-screening]] and [[sector-rotation]] for retail investors. Before IBD, relative strength data and industry group rankings were largely the domain of institutional investors. By packaging these analytics into a daily newspaper (and later a digital platform), O'Neil made systematic growth investing accessible. The CANSLIM methodology taught through IBD has influenced a generation of growth-oriented traders and remains one of the most widely followed stock selection frameworks.

## Ownership and Current Status

News Corp acquired Investor's Business Daily from O'Neil Capital Management in 2021 for $275 million (announced March 2021, closed mid-2021), folding it into the Dow Jones division alongside The Wall Street Journal and Barron's. At the time of the sale IBD had ~130 employees and nearly 100,000 digital subscribers, with more than 90% of revenue from digital products. The daily print newspaper had already moved to a weekly print edition in 2016. Under News Corp, IBD has operated as a digital-first subscription business (Investors.com, IBD Digital, Leaderboard, SwingTrader, IBD Live, MarketSurge — the rebranded MarketSmith). Founder [[william-oneil|William O'Neil]] died on 28 May 2023, aged 90. As of 2026 the IBD ratings, IBD 50 list and Market Pulse remain actively published and are still the canonical retail implementation of [[canslim]]-style momentum screening.

IBD's educational content teaches specific chart patterns that O'Neil catalogued from studying every market winner dating back to the 1880s (cup-with-handle, flat bases, proper buy points), combining [[fundamental-analysis]] (earnings acceleration, revenue growth, ROE) with [[technical-analysis]].
