# Fidelity Select Funds

**Fidelity Select Funds** are a family of sector-specific mutual funds offered by Fidelity Investments, originally launched in 1981. They were among the first mutual fund products designed specifically for [[sector-rotation]] strategies, allowing individual investors to concentrate capital in individual industries and rotate between them as economic conditions change. At their peak, the Select fund family represented one of the most popular tools for retail sector rotators.

## Fund Lineup

The Fidelity Select family includes over 40 sector-focused funds spanning every major [[gics-sectors|GICS sector]] and many sub-industries:

Each fund is actively managed by a dedicated portfolio manager with deep sector expertise, distinguishing them from passive sector index funds. Managers have the flexibility to overweight or underweight specific sub-industries within their mandate.

## Historical Significance

The Select funds pioneered the concept of **sector-focused mutual funds for retail investors**. Before their launch, investors who wanted concentrated sector exposure had to pick individual stocks or use limited closed-end funds. The Select family democratized [[sector-rotation]] as a strategy by providing:

- **Broad sector coverage** — Enough funds to implement a full rotation across the [[business-cycle]]
- **Professional management** — Each fund run by a specialist portfolio manager
- **Low minimums** — Accessible to individual investors, not just institutions
- **Phone/online switching** — Originally, investors could switch between Select funds with a phone call, enabling rapid sector rotation

During the 1980s and 1990s, the Select funds attracted a dedicated following of sector rotators who tracked [[relative-strength]] across the fund family and systematically moved capital into the strongest-performing sectors monthly or quarterly.

## Sector Rotation Usage

Active sector rotators use the Select funds by:

1. **Ranking all Select funds by 3-month or 6-month [[relative-strength]]** against each other and a broad market benchmark (e.g., Fidelity's S&P 500 index fund)
2. **Concentrating in the top 2-4 sectors** — Allocating capital to the funds showing the strongest momentum
3. **Rebalancing monthly or quarterly** — Selling funds that drop out of the top tier and buying new leaders
4. **Overlaying [[business-cycle]] analysis** — Using [[economic-indicators]] and [[fed-policy]] signals to anticipate which sectors should lead in the current cycle phase

This approach maps directly to the [[sector-momentum-screen]] strategy, where sector selection is the first layer before individual stock screening.

## The 30-Day Holding Requirement

Fidelity imposes a **30-day short-term trading fee** (historically 0.75-1.50%) on Select fund shares held for fewer than 30 days. This policy was implemented to discourage excessive trading and protect long-term fund shareholders from the costs generated by rapid in-and-out rotation. For sector rotators, this effectively enforces a minimum monthly holding period, aligning with the typical monthly or quarterly rebalance cadence of most [[sector-rotation]] strategies.

## Comparison with Sector ETFs

The rise of sector ETFs — particularly the **SPDR Select Sector ETFs** (XLK, XLF, XLV, XLE, XLI, XLB, XLY, XLP, XLU, XLRE, XLC) — has largely supplanted the Fidelity Select funds for active sector rotation:

| Feature | Fidelity Select Funds | SPDR Sector ETFs |
|---------|----------------------|------------------|
| **Trading** | End-of-day NAV only | Intraday on exchange |
| **Expense ratio** | 0.60-1.00% | 0.09-0.13% |
| **Holding restriction** | 30-day fee | None |
| **Management** | Active (sector PM) | Passive (index tracking) |
| **Tax efficiency** | Lower (mutual fund structure) | Higher (ETF in-kind creation/redemption) |
| **Minimum investment** | Varies | Price of 1 share |

For most modern sector rotators, ETFs offer lower costs, intraday liquidity, and no holding restrictions. However, the Fidelity Select funds retain an edge for investors who value active management within sectors — a skilled PM can add alpha through sub-industry selection and timing within the sector mandate.

## Current Status (June 2026)

The Select lineup remains open and actively managed by Fidelity Investments (Boston, MA), though it is now a niche product: most retail sector rotation flows to sector ETFs (SPDR Select Sector funds and Fidelity's own MSCI sector ETF range, e.g. FTEC, FHLC, which carry ~0.08% expense ratios). FSELX (Select Semiconductors) is the standout of the family — its concentrated exposure to [[nvidia]], AMD and the AI-datacenter build-out made it one of the best-performing US mutual funds of the 2023-2026 period. The Select funds remain useful to traders mainly as (a) a long-history dataset for backtesting [[sector-rotation]] models (continuous NAV history since 1981, predating sector ETFs by 17 years) and (b) an active-management vehicle inside Fidelity-only retirement accounts.
