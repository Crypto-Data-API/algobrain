Keysight Technologies (NYSE: KEYS) is the world's largest electronic test-and-measurement company — the former Hewlett-Packard/Agilent instruments business, spun off from Agilent in 2014. It sells the design, emulation, and validation tooling behind AI data-center networking, 5G/6G wireless, semiconductors, aerospace/defense, and automotive electronics, making it a "picks-and-shovels" derivative of the AI capex cycle within the [[s-and-p-500]].

## Business Overview & Segments

Keysight reports two segments and is increasingly a software/services story layered on its instrument base. It sits in the [[technology|Information Technology]] sector (electronic test & measurement) and is a [[s-and-p-500]] constituent. Direct lineage: HP's original 1939 instruments business → Agilent (1999 spin) → Keysight (2014 spin from [[agilent-technologies|Agilent]]).

| Segment | What it is | Revenue mix (qualitative) |
|---|---|---|
| **Communications Solutions Group (CSG)** | Wireline/data-center test, wireless/5G-6G, aerospace & defense test & measurement | ~70% of revenue — the larger segment and the AI-data-center growth driver |
| **Electronic Industrial Solutions Group (EISG)** | Semiconductor measurement, automotive/EV, general electronics design & test | The remainder — semis, auto/EV electrification, general industrial |
| **Software & services (cross-segment)** | Design/emulation software, network/security test (Spirent), support and subscriptions | Rising recurring mix; the margin-quality and moat-deepening layer |

The franchise is a **"picks-and-shovels"** play: Keysight sells the design, emulation and validation tooling behind AI data-center networking, 5G/6G wireless, semiconductors, aerospace/defense and automotive electronics — a derivative of the AI capex cycle that captures test budgets regardless of which chip or network vendor wins.

## 2025–2026 Developments

- **FY2025 results (ended 2025-10-31)**: revenue **$5.375B, +8%**; adjusted EPS **$7.16, +14%**; record free cash flow ~$1.3B. Q4 was the strongest quarter in years — CSG Q4 revenue $990M (+11% YoY) driven by **AI data-center infrastructure investment**, non-terrestrial networks, and defense modernization.
- **Spirent Communications acquisition closed 2025-10-15** — a ~$1.5B deal first agreed in March 2024 that took ~19 months through UK and Chinese (SAMR) regulatory review; Keysight divested overlap assets to Viavi as a condition. Adds network/security test.
- **M&A spree**: FY2025 deployed **$1.7B** across Spirent, Synopsys' Optical Solutions Group, and Ansys' PowerArtist (the latter two were antitrust remedies from the Synopsys-Ansys merger). The three deals are guided to add **~$375M of FY2026 revenue**.
- The trailing-FY margin dip visible in the fundamentals (operating margin 16.3% vs ~25% in FY2022-23) reflects the 2024 demand trough plus acquisition integration costs; management targets recovery toward historical margins as AI/wireless test demand rebuilds.

## Economic Moat & Competitive Position

Keysight holds a wide [[economic-moat|economic moat]] for an industrial — narrower than a software platform but durable, built on installed base and standards entanglement:

- **Installed base + switching costs** — instruments are calibrated, validated and embedded into customers' engineering workflows and compliance/certification processes; re-tooling a lab around a rival vendor is costly and slow. See [[switching-costs]].
- **Standards entanglement** — Keysight participates in defining wireless (5G/6G), data-center and semiconductor test standards, so its tooling is often the reference for conformance — a self-reinforcing position.
- **Breadth + software layer** — the broadest test portfolio across RF/microwave, digital, optical and semiconductor, increasingly wrapped in design-emulation **software and services** that raise the recurring-revenue mix and margins.
- **Brand and lineage** — the HP/Agilent measurement heritage is a trusted default in aerospace/defense and high-end R&D.

The moat is **defensible but cyclical**: demand tracks customer R&D and capex budgets, which fall in downturns (visible in the FY2024 trough), so the franchise is high-quality but not immune to the electronics cycle.

## Growth Drivers & Catalysts

- **AI data-center test** — CSG demand from AI networking/optics validation is the current growth engine (Q4 FY25 CSG +11% YoY on AI infrastructure investment).
- **6G and wireless** — standardization milestones and the next wireless capex cycle.
- **Spirent + Synopsys/Ansys deals** — FY2025's $1.7B M&A (Spirent, Synopsys Optical Solutions, Ansys PowerArtist) guided to add ~$375M of FY2026 revenue.
- **Software/services mix** — rising recurring design-emulation software and services lift margins and smooth the cycle.
- **Defense modernization** — aerospace/defense test demand from rising defense budgets.
- **Recurring catalysts** — quarterly orders/backlog commentary (orders lead revenue), hyperscaler AI-capex guidance, 6G milestones, defense budgets, acquisition-synergy updates.

## Bull Case vs Bear Case

### Bull case
- A high-quality "picks-and-shovels" AI-capex derivative that captures test budgets regardless of which chip/network vendor wins.
- Rising software/services mix raises margins and recurring revenue, deserving a higher multiple.
- FY2025 M&A (Spirent + remedies) adds revenue and broadens the network/security and semiconductor test reach.
- Passes every Fred quality bar (8/8); strong balance sheet and record free cash flow.

### Bear case
- Cyclical: demand tracks customer R&D/capex and fell in the FY2024 trough; test budgets lag deployment.
- Margin dip (FY25 operating margin ~16% vs ~25% in FY22-23) from the demand trough plus acquisition integration costs.
- Integration risk across three 2025 acquisitions; synergy realization is unproven.
- Trailing GAAP PE (67x) optically rich; the re-rating depends on margin recovery.

## Key Risks

- **Cyclicality** — R&D/capex-driven demand contracts in downturns; orders lead revenue both up and down.
- **Margin recovery risk** — the FY24-25 margin dip must reverse as AI/wireless test demand rebuilds; integration costs weigh near-term.
- **Integration risk** — absorbing Spirent, Synopsys Optical Solutions and Ansys PowerArtist simultaneously.
- **Customer concentration cycles** — exposure to semiconductor, wireless and defense capex cycles.
- **Valuation/optics** — elevated GAAP PE; a stalled margin recovery de-rates the stock.

## Valuation & How the Stock Trades

Keysight trades as a quality industrial/[[technology|tech]] compounder, and the GAAP headline understates the quality of the business:

- **Adjusted EPS is the anchor** — trailing GAAP PE (67x in the snapshot) overstates richness; adjusted EPS ($7.16 in FY2025) is the Street's reference, and the GAAP/non-GAAP gap is mostly amortization from the 2025 acquisitions.
- **No dividend** — capital allocation favors reinvestment, M&A and buybacks over a payout; total return is growth- and margin-recovery-driven.
- **Lower-beta AI derivative** — moves with data-center networking names ([[arista-networks]], [[broadcom]], Marvell, the [[nvidia]] ecosystem) on AI-infrastructure headlines but with lower beta, because test budgets lag deployment.
- **Orders lead the print** — backlog/orders commentary moves the stock more than the revenue print; orders are the leading indicator.
- **Index/liquidity** — [[s-and-p-500]] member; moderately liquid options, busiest around the late-Nov/Feb/May/Aug earnings cycle.
- **Correlated names** — Teradyne, National Instruments (Emerson), Viavi, Fortive; semiconductors via EISG ([[kla|KLA]], [[analog-devices]]).
