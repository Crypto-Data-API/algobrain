Yuga Labs is the NFT company behind the [[bored-ape-yacht-club|Bored Ape Yacht Club]] (BAYC) and the acquirer of the [[cryptopunks|CryptoPunks]], [[autoglyphs|Autoglyphs]], and [[meebits|Meebits]] collections from [[larva-labs|Larva Labs]]. Founded in 2021 by a pseudonymous team later revealed as Greg Solano and Wylie Aronow, Yuga Labs raised $450 million from Andreessen Horowitz at a $4 billion valuation in March 2022, becoming the dominant force in the NFT industry by owning the three most iconic NFT collections in existence.

## History

Yuga Labs was founded in 2021 by a team using pseudonyms: Gordon Goner, Gargamel, No Sass, and Emperor Tomato Ketchup. In April 2021, the team launched the [[bored-ape-yacht-club|Bored Ape Yacht Club]] — a collection of 10,000 unique cartoon ape NFTs on [[ethereum|Ethereum]]. BAYC quickly became the defining PFP (profile picture) NFT collection, attracting celebrity holders and establishing a powerful brand identity.

A distinguishing feature of BAYC was that Yuga Labs granted full commercial IP rights to individual Ape holders. This enabled holders to create derivative businesses — most notably the Bored & Hungry restaurant in Long Beach, California, as well as music projects, merchandise lines, and other ventures built around individual Ape artwork.

In February 2022, BuzzFeed News doxxed the pseudonymous founders, revealing them as Greg Solano and Wylie Aronow — both based in Florida with backgrounds in creative writing and editing, not technology.

In March 2022, Yuga Labs made two transformative moves. First, the company raised $450 million in a seed round led by Andreessen Horowitz (a16z) at a $4 billion valuation. Second, it acquired the intellectual property rights to [[cryptopunks|CryptoPunks]], [[autoglyphs|Autoglyphs]], and [[meebits|Meebits]] from [[larva-labs|Larva Labs]], consolidating ownership of the NFT industry's most culturally significant collections under one roof. Yuga Labs subsequently extended the same commercial IP rights to CryptoPunks and Meebits holders that BAYC holders already enjoyed.

Also in March 2022, Yuga Labs launched ApeCoin ($APE), a governance and utility token for the broader Ape ecosystem. The token launched via airdrop to BAYC and Mutant Ape holders.

In April 2022, Yuga Labs conducted the Otherdeed land sale for its Otherside metaverse project. The mint generated over $320 million in revenue in a single event but infamously crashed [[ethereum|Ethereum]] gas prices, with individual transactions costing $100+ in gas fees. The chaotic mint was widely cited as an example of the scaling limitations of the Ethereum network.

In 2023, Yuga Labs faced an SEC investigation into whether ApeCoin and the Otherdeed sales constituted unregistered securities offerings. The company also experienced leadership changes and laid off staff as the broader NFT market contracted.

## Significance

Yuga Labs represents both the peak ambition and the inherent challenges of the NFT industry. At its height, the company controlled the cultural and financial center of the NFT market — owning BAYC, CryptoPunks, and Autoglyphs meant owning the history, the present, and (ambitiously) the metaverse future of NFTs.

The company's approach to IP rights was industry-defining. By granting holders full commercial rights to their NFT artwork, Yuga Labs established a model where NFT ownership meant more than digital collecting — it was a license to build businesses. This paradigm influenced hundreds of subsequent NFT projects.

The Otherdeed mint, while financially successful, became a cautionary tale about the limitations of blockchain infrastructure and the risks of hype-driven launches. The event demonstrated that even the most anticipated NFT drops could create negative user experiences when demand overwhelmed network capacity.

Yuga Labs' SEC investigation highlighted the regulatory uncertainty surrounding NFT-adjacent tokens and virtual land sales. The outcome of this scrutiny has implications for how future NFT companies structure their token economics and virtual asset offerings.

The company's trajectory — from pseudonymous founders to $4 billion valuation to market contraction and regulatory scrutiny — mirrors the broader arc of the NFT market itself.
