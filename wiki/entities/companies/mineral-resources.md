**Mineral Resources Limited** (ASX: MIN) is a diversified mining and mining services company listed on the [[asx-limited|Australian Securities Exchange]] and a constituent of the [[asx-200]] index. Mineral Resources Limited (MinRes) is a diversified Western Australian mining and mining services group. The company combines a large contracted mining services business (crushing, haulage, logistics, and pit-to-ship services) with its own iron ore and lithium mining operations, plus emerging energy (gas) and power interests.

## Business Model & Segments

MinRes reports four operating segments:

- **Mining Services:** Delivered primarily through CSI Mining Services. Services include pit-to-ship solutions (open pit mining, crushing and material handling, processing, haulage, port handling, marine services), design and construction of mineral processing facilities, transport and logistics (supply chain, road haulage, autonomous road trains, transhipping), equipment and parts manufacturing, site services and rehabilitation, and engineering and construction. CSI Mining Services delivered 15.9 million tonnes of crushed ore in a recent reporting period.
- **Iron Ore:** Flagship Onslow Iron project in the West Pilbara (Red Hill Iron Joint Venture - RHIJV - with Baowu, AMCI, and POSCO), with associated port, haulage, and transhipment infrastructure. MinRes also operates legacy iron ore production and mine-gate services at other Pilbara and Yilgarn sites.
- **Lithium:** Wodgina (Pilbara) and Mt Marion (Goldfields) world-class hard rock spodumene mines, producing spodumene concentrate and lithium battery chemicals. Lithium reserves across the two mines exceed 200 million tonnes. POSCO Holdings invested US$765 million for a 30% interest in the lithium joint venture covering Wodgina and Mt Marion.
- **Energy and Other:** Includes exploration and drilling services for the energy sector, manganese and garnet operations, gas production (Perth Basin exploration and development), and power generation via natural gas/LNG, solar, wind, and geothermal.

The mining services business generates stable fee-based revenues, while iron ore and lithium segments are exposed to commodity prices.

## History

Mineral Resources was founded in 1992, emerging from the combination of Crushing Services International (CSI), PIHA, and Process Minerals International (PMI). Founder and long-time Managing Director Chris Ellison has been central to the group since its establishment.

Key milestones:

- **1992:** MinRes formed; built an initial mining services platform.
- **2006:** Listed on the ASX.
- **2010s:** Expanded into iron ore mining in the Yilgarn and Pilbara and established flagship lithium operations at Mt Marion and Wodgina.
- **2021:** Acquired full control of Wodgina Lithium Operation (previously a JV with Albemarle) via the MARBL joint venture.
- **May 2024:** First ore on ship from Onslow Iron, ahead of schedule.
- **30 June 2025:** Onslow Iron achieved commercial production. Ramp to nameplate 35 mtpa capacity confirmed in Q1 FY2026.
- **2024-2025:** POSCO invested ~US$765 million for 30% in MinRes' lithium joint venture.

## Economic Moat & Competitive Position

MinRes is best understood as **two very different businesses bolted together**, and its [[economic-moat]] story lives almost entirely in one of them.

- **The mining segments are price-takers — no moat.** The [[iron-ore]] (Onslow Iron, plus legacy Yilgarn/Pilbara) and [[lithium]] (Wodgina, Mt Marion) operations sell undifferentiated commodities into global markets. They cannot set price; they can only control cost. For these, "quality" is not a brand or switching cost but **cost-curve position and asset life** — where each mine sits on the global cash-cost curve, its grade, strip ratio, mine life, and infrastructure (port/haul) advantage. Onslow Iron's value proposition is to ramp to ~35 mtpa as a structurally **low-cost** Pilbara tonne; the Wodgina/Mt Marion hard-rock spodumene mines are large, long-life resources (lithium reserves across the two exceed 200 Mt) that need to survive the bottom of the spodumene cycle. When prices fall, the only defence is being lower on the cost curve than the marginal producer.
- **Mining Services (CSI) is the differentiated, capital-light annuity — the closest thing to a moat.** CSI Mining Services delivers contracted pit-to-ship solutions (crushing, material handling, processing, haulage, port/marine, autonomous road trains) under long-dated, fee-based contracts. This earns **fee revenue rather than commodity margin**, embeds genuine [[switching-costs]] (the contractor is woven into a client's pit-to-port flow and capital plant), and smooths the violent cyclicality of the mining book. It is the stabiliser that lets MinRes carry more balance-sheet risk on the mines than a pure producer could.

Net characterisation: **a levered commodity play with a stabiliser**. The mines provide torque to [[iron-ore]] and [[lithium]] prices; CSI provides a base of recurring cash that partly offsets the cycle. The combination, plus a heavy Onslow build debt load, is what makes the equity behave like a levered option on commodity prices rather than a steady compounder.

## Growth Drivers & Catalysts

- **Onslow Iron ramp & unit costs** — the single biggest swing factor: reaching and holding nameplate ~35 mtpa and driving the **unit cost trajectory** down the curve. Every step of the ramp (and the carry-loan paydown tied to it) is a tradeable print.
- **Balance-sheet deleveraging** — the heavy debt taken on to build Onslow is the overhang; visible deleveraging (Onslow turning cash-flow positive, carry-loan reduction) is the central re-rating catalyst.
- **[[lithium]] / spodumene price recovery** — Wodgina and Mt Marion are high-torque to any recovery in spodumene/lithium-chemical prices off cycle lows.
- **Asset sales / JV sell-downs** — partial monetisations (e.g. POSCO's ~US$765m for 30% of the lithium JV) that recycle capital and cut debt; further sell-downs or infrastructure deals are recurring catalysts.
- **Mining Services (CSI) volume growth** — tonnes crushed/hauled under contract; the annuity base growing adds non-commodity earnings quality.
- **Energy & Other optionality** — Perth Basin gas and power interests as longer-dated, lower-weighting optionality.
- **Governance / board news** — any Ellison or board development (after the Nov 2024 probe and the Nov 2025 succession reversal) moves the stock.

## Bull Case vs Bear Case

### Bull case
- Onslow Iron ramps to nameplate ~35 mtpa as a **low-cost** Pilbara tonne, generating large free cash flow that rapidly de-gears the balance sheet.
- The equity is a **levered option** on iron-ore and lithium prices — in an up-cycle, MinRes outperforms lower-beta majors hard.
- A spodumene/lithium price recovery turns Wodgina and Mt Marion from cash drains into major earners.
- The CSI **Mining Services** annuity provides a recurring, fee-based cash base that partly offsets commodity cyclicality and arguably deserves a higher multiple than the mines.
- Tier-one, long-life resource base (200 Mt+ lithium reserves; large iron-ore footprint) gives decades of optionality and JV-monetisation capacity.

### Bear case
- The **debt load** built for Onslow makes the equity fragile: at low commodity prices the levered structure compresses equity value fast, and deleveraging stalls.
- **Lithium price weakness** can keep the lithium segment loss-making for an extended period.
- **Onslow ramp execution risk** — any slippage on volume or unit costs directly hits the deleveraging story.
- **Governance / headline risk** — the Ellison probe (fine and forfeiture) and the November 2025 reversal of his succession plan keep a discount and event-risk component in the price.
- High sensitivity to the **AUD** and **Chinese steel/EV demand**; through-cycle margins are well below current-period flattered reads (see [[#Fundamentals]]).

## Key Risks

- **Leverage / financing** — the heavy Onslow build debt makes the equity behave like a **levered option on commodity prices**; a commodity drawdown plus high debt is the core risk, and the Fred view flags D/E above the [[materials|Materials]] bar.
- **[[lithium]] price weakness** — prolonged spodumene/lithium-chemical softness keeps the lithium book under water and pressures group cash flow.
- **Onslow ramp execution** — missing the volume/unit-cost trajectory undermines both earnings and deleveraging.
- **Governance & headline risk** — the Chris Ellison saga (probe, A$8.8m fine, forfeitures) and the Nov 2025 succession reversal sustain a governance discount; board/leadership announcements are tradeable shocks.
- **[[iron-ore]] price & China demand** — Onslow economics ride on the iron-ore price, which trades off Chinese steel demand and Pilbara/Brazil supply; EV/battery demand drives the lithium side.
- **Currency** — AUD moves swing AUD-denominated commodity receipts and reported earnings.

## Valuation & How the Stock Trades

MinRes is **one of the most volatile large caps on the [[asx-200]]**, and it does not trade like a steady-state earnings compounder:

- **Levered option on commodities + deleveraging** — the equity is best framed as a geared call on [[iron-ore]] and [[lithium]] prices stacked on top of a balance-sheet-deleveraging story. Small moves in commodity prices or in the Onslow ramp produce outsized moves in equity value because of the debt load.
- **P/E is largely meaningless** at trough or heavily-levered earnings — the headline multiple (103.6x latest; see [[#Fundamentals]]) reflects depressed/transitional earnings, not expensiveness. The market trades it on commodity-price momentum, Onslow run-rates and deleveraging progress, not static multiples.
- **Sum-of-the-parts lens** — analysts increasingly value MinRes as the mines (cyclical, commodity-multiple) plus the **CSI Mining Services annuity**, which — being fee-based and recurring — arguably warrants a **higher, more services-like multiple** than the commodity segments. The market's willingness to credit that services value is part of the valuation debate.
- **Governance discount** — the Ellison overhang keeps a persistent discount/headline-risk premium embedded in the price.
- **Dividend & [[franking-credits]]** — distributions are cyclical and currently modest given the deleveraging priority; for Australian residents [[franking-credits]] add to the after-tax return when paid, but capital return is gated on getting debt down first.
