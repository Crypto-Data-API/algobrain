Healthpeak Properties (NYSE: DOC) is a healthcare [[reits|REIT]] focused on **outpatient medical buildings** and **life-science/lab campuses**, formed in its current shape by the March 2024 all-stock merger with Physicians Realty Trust (which gave it the DOC ticker, previously PEAK). It is a constituent of the [[s-and-p-500]] index and one of the "big three" healthcare [[reits|REITs]] alongside [[welltower]] and [[ventas]] — but with lab exposure that makes it the most biotech-funding-sensitive of the trio.

## Business Overview & Segments

- **Outpatient medical** — the largest segment post-merger: medical office buildings (MOBs) leased to physician groups and hospital systems (steady, defensive cash flows).
- **Lab/life science** — purpose-built campuses in the three core clusters (South San Francisco, San Diego, Boston); rents and occupancy track biotech venture funding and IPO cycles, which were soft in 2024–2025.
- **Senior housing / other** — legacy CCRC exposure, repositioned through 2025–2026 (see Janus Living below).

## Economic Moat & Competitive Position

Healthpeak's [[economic-moat|moat]] is a **location + specialized-asset** [[reits|REIT]] moat, strongest in lab and on-campus medical:

- **Lab cluster scarcity** — purpose-built lab space in South San Francisco, San Diego and Boston is genuinely scarce; the specialized fit-out (lab benches, HVAC, vibration control) and cluster network effects (proximity to talent, VCs, anchor institutions) create high [[switching-costs]] for biotech tenants — provided demand is there.
- **On-campus MOB stickiness** — medical office buildings physically attached to or near hospital systems enjoy very high retention; physician practices are loath to move away from referral networks and hospital affiliations.
- **Scale & cost of capital** — as a "big three" healthcare REIT with investment-grade access, Healthpeak funds and develops more cheaply than smaller landlords.
- **Defensive demand** — healthcare real estate demand is tied to demographics and care delivery rather than the economic cycle, giving the MOB book recession resistance.

The moat is **asset-specific rather than franchise-wide**: the lab segment's edge evaporates if biotech funding dries up and clusters oversupply (the 2024–25 risk), and Healthpeak competes directly with the lab pure-play [[alexandria-real-estate-equities]] for the same tenants.

## Growth Drivers & Catalysts

- **Lab recovery** — a rebound in biotech venture funding and IPOs would lift lab leasing demand and occupancy in the three core clusters.
- **MOB demand** — aging demographics and the shift of care to outpatient settings support steady medical-office absorption.
- **Capital redeployment** — Janus Living IPO proceeds earmarked for acquisitions through year-end 2026, expected accretive from 2027.
- **Portfolio simplification** — exiting legacy senior housing concentrates the story on higher-quality MOB + lab.
- **Rate relief** — lower discount rates compress cap rates and lift NAV for this long-duration income vehicle.
- **Recurring catalysts** — quarterly FFO results/guidance (Feb/May/Aug/Nov), biotech funding data, Fed/rate moves, acquisition-deployment announcements, and post-Janus senior-housing data.

## 2025–2026 Developments

- **Monthly dividend:** Healthpeak moved to monthly common dividends (declared in quarterly batches — Q2 2026 batch declared April 2026), a retail-shareholder-friendly differentiator among S&P 500 [[reits|REITs]].
- **Janus Living IPO (March 2026):** completed the IPO/separation of its Janus Living senior-housing platform; management guided the transaction as earnings-neutral in 2026 and accretive 2027+, with proceeds earmarked for acquisitions through year-end 2026.
- **Raised 2026 guidance (May 2026):** alongside Q1 2026 results, Healthpeak **raised 2026 earnings guidance** following the Janus Living IPO and related capital-allocation actions.
- Ongoing share buybacks — unusual for a REIT — reflecting management's view that the stock trades below NAV.

## Bull Case vs Bear Case

### Bull case
- Defensive MOB book with high retention and demographic tailwinds anchors the cash flows.
- Lab cluster scarcity offers upside if biotech funding recovers from the 2024–25 trough.
- Janus Living IPO simplifies the portfolio and arms management with acquisition capital.
- Trades below NAV (per management), supporting unusual REIT-level buybacks.
- Monthly dividend, covered on an AFFO basis, is a retail-friendly differentiator.

### Bear case
- Lab oversupply in core clusters plus soft biotech funding pressure the growth segment.
- Tenant credit risk — biotech failures can leave specialized lab space vacant and hard to re-let.
- Thin interest coverage (~1.9x) versus refinancing walls in a higher-for-longer rate world.
- Rate-sensitive, long-duration income vehicle — valuation moves inversely with the 10-year Treasury.
- Execution risk on post-Janus capital redeployment; accretion is a 2027 story, not 2026.

## Key Risks

- **Biotech funding cycle** — lab rents and occupancy track venture/IPO funding, which was soft in 2024–25; a prolonged drought hits the growth segment.
- **Lab oversupply** — new lab deliveries in San Francisco/San Diego/Boston clusters can outrun demand.
- **Tenant credit** — biotech tenants can fail, leaving specialized space vacant.
- **Interest coverage / refinancing** — ~1.9x coverage is thin for the refinancing schedule; rate moves matter.
- **Rate cycle** — long-duration income REIT; valuation is inversely tied to the 10-year Treasury.
- **Execution** — redeploying Janus IPO proceeds accretively is a management-execution risk.

## Valuation & How the Stock Trades

As a healthcare [[reits|REIT]], DOC is valued on **FFO/AFFO per share** and an implied **cap rate / NAV** basis, not GAAP EPS — the 56.9x P/E in the fundamentals below is meaningless because depreciation suppresses reported net income. Mechanically:

- **Price/FFO multiple** — the market pays a multiple of FFO/AFFO keyed to MOB stability plus lab-growth expectations; lab sentiment swings the multiple most.
- **Cap rate & NAV** — MOB and lab portfolios trade on capitalized NOI; management's view that DOC sits below NAV underpins the unusual REIT-level buybacks.
- **Rate sensitivity / duration** — a long-duration income vehicle that moves inversely to the 10-year Treasury; thin interest coverage makes refinancing terms matter more than for lower-levered peers.
- **Dividend** — ~US$1.22/yr paid **monthly** (since 2025), covered on an AFFO basis despite the >300% GAAP payout; the monthly cadence is a retail differentiator.
- **Beta** — moderate equity beta, high rate and biotech-funding sensitivity; the lab segment adds idiosyncratic volatility versus pure-MOB peers.
