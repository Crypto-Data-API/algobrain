fxhash is an open, permissionless generative art [[nft|NFT]] platform originally launched on the Tezos blockchain in November 2021, later expanding to [[ethereum|Ethereum]]. The platform enables artists to deploy generative art projects where each minted token produces a unique output from the artist's algorithm — similar to [[art-blocks|Art Blocks]] but with a lower barrier to entry, no curation gate, and initially much lower gas costs thanks to Tezos.

## History

fxhash was created by a developer known as ciphrd and launched in November 2021 on the Tezos blockchain. The platform arrived during the height of the generative art NFT boom, which had been catalyzed by the success of [[art-blocks|Art Blocks]] on [[ethereum|Ethereum]].

Art Blocks operated a curated model where artists applied and were selected for "Curated" drops, with additional "Playground" and "Factory" tiers. fxhash took a radically different approach: the platform was fully open and permissionless, meaning any artist could deploy a generative art project without approval. This democratized generative art minting and attracted a large community of artists who could not access Art Blocks' curated pipeline or who preferred the openness and lower costs of the Tezos ecosystem.

The choice of Tezos was strategic. At the time of fxhash's launch, minting an NFT on [[ethereum|Ethereum]] could cost tens or even hundreds of dollars in [[gas-fees|gas fees]], while Tezos transactions cost fractions of a cent. This made experimentation and smaller-scale projects economically viable, attracting artists who might not justify Ethereum gas costs.

fxhash quickly became the dominant generative art platform on Tezos, hosting thousands of projects and building a vibrant community of generative artists, many of whom used creative coding tools like p5.js, three.js, and GLSL shaders.

## Expansion to Ethereum

In 2023, fxhash expanded to [[ethereum|Ethereum]], launching fxhash 2.0 with multi-chain support. This move brought the platform's open model to Ethereum's larger collector base and higher-value market. The Ethereum expansion positioned fxhash as a direct alternative to [[art-blocks|Art Blocks]], offering:

- **No curation gate**: Any artist can launch a project (vs. Art Blocks' application process)
- **Multi-chain options**: Artists can choose Tezos for low costs or Ethereum for access to the larger market
- **Flexible parameters**: Artists can configure mint price, supply, and minting mechanics

## How Generative Art Minting Works

On fxhash, the process follows a pattern common to generative art NFT platforms:

1. **Artist uploads code**: The artist deploys a generative algorithm (typically JavaScript) to the platform
2. **Collector mints**: When a collector mints a token, the platform generates a unique random hash
3. **Hash determines output**: The artist's algorithm uses this hash as a seed to produce a unique visual output
4. **On-chain provenance**: The hash and metadata are stored on-chain, ensuring the artwork is deterministically reproducible

Each minted token is unique — the same algorithm produces different outputs for different hashes — but all outputs share the artist's aesthetic vision and algorithmic constraints.

## Platform Characteristics

- **Listing model**: Open/permissionless — any artist can deploy a generative project
- **Blockchains**: Tezos (original), [[ethereum|Ethereum]] (added 2023)
- **Art type**: Generative/algorithmic art exclusively
- **Minting mechanics**: Configurable by the artist (price, supply, scheduling)
- **Code storage**: Artist code stored on-chain or via decentralized storage (IPFS)
- **Royalties**: Configurable creator royalties on secondary sales

## Competitive Position

fxhash occupies a unique position in the generative art NFT landscape:

- **vs. [[art-blocks|Art Blocks]]**: Art Blocks is curated and Ethereum-only; fxhash is open/permissionless and multi-chain. Art Blocks commands higher average prices for curated projects; fxhash has a larger volume of projects and lower barrier to entry
- **vs. [[superrare|SuperRare]]** / **[[knownorigin|KnownOrigin]]**: These platforms focus on traditional digital art (hand-created pieces); fxhash is exclusively generative/algorithmic
- **vs. [[opensea|OpenSea]]**: OpenSea is a general secondary marketplace; fxhash is a primary minting platform specifically for generative art
- **vs. [[autoglyphs|Autoglyphs]]**: Autoglyphs was the first on-chain generative art project; fxhash is a platform that enables anyone to create similar generative art projects

## Community and Culture

fxhash fostered a distinctive community culture centered on creative coding and artistic experimentation:

- **Accessibility**: The low cost of Tezos minting encouraged experimentation with small runs and unconventional projects
- **Education**: The community developed tutorials, workshops, and collaborative events around generative art techniques
- **Events**: fxhash hosted community events and coordinated drops that became important moments in the generative art calendar
- **Open source ethos**: Many artists shared their techniques and code, contributing to a culture of learning and collaboration

## Status as of June 2026

fxhash remains an active but niche generative-art platform. Activity is tied to the broader [[nft|NFT]] market, which never recovered its 2021-2022 volume peak; generative art is one of the few NFT segments with a durable collector base, but secondary liquidity is thin compared to large profile-picture collections.

**Trader relevance is limited and indirect:**
- fxhash is a private platform with **no token and no public equity** — there is no direct way to trade it.
- Indirect exposure routes: Tezos (XTZ) and [[ethereum|Ethereum]] (ETH) activity, and the generative art segment generally as a sentiment gauge for high-end NFT demand (alongside [[art-blocks|Art Blocks]] floor prices).
- NFT market activity historically correlates with late-cycle crypto risk appetite — generative art mint volume can serve as a froth indicator.
