DRW is a Chicago-based proprietary trading firm founded in 1992 by Don R. Wilson, originally as a CBOT pit trader operating in fixed-income options and futures. DRW has since grown into one of the largest privately-held trading firms in the world, with diversified businesses spanning [[market-maker|market making]], proprietary trading, real estate, venture capital and crypto.

## Overview

DRW has approximately 2,000 employees across offices in Chicago, New York, London, Houston, Amsterdam, Montreal, Singapore, Tel Aviv and Tokyo. Like its Chicago peers ([[jump-trading]], citadel-securities), DRW is known for combining deep derivatives expertise with low-latency electronic execution.

The firm's name is the founder's initials. Don Wilson built the business around a pit-trading mindset (deep instrument-level understanding, aggressive position-taking, and tight risk discipline) and progressively layered electronic and quantitative methods on top. DRW operates several semi-autonomous business units, including:

- **DRW Trading** — the core derivatives market making and proprietary trading business
- **Cumberland** — DRW's institutional crypto and digital-asset trading arm, one of the largest US crypto OTC desks
- **DRW Investments** — venture capital and private equity
- **Convexity Properties** — Chicago-area commercial real estate

## Use of AI and Quantitative Methods

DRW's trading systems combine human discretionary trading with model-driven quoting and risk management. Public talks and job listings indicate the firm uses:

- Real-time [[volatility-surface]] fitting and arbitrage-free smoothing
- Machine-learning models for [[order-flow]] analysis and short-horizon price forecasting
- Custom low-latency C++ and FPGA-based pricing infrastructure
- Cross-product portfolio risk systems spanning rates, equities, energy and crypto
- Bayesian and ensemble approaches to model selection across regimes

DRW was an early institutional participant in crypto, with Cumberland trading [[bitcoin]] from 2014 onwards and now actively making markets in [[deribit|Deribit]] crypto options.

## Trading Activities

### Fixed Income, Equity and Energy Options

DRW markets-makes options on Treasury futures, equity indices, single stocks, and energy products (crude, natural gas) on CME, ICE, Eurex and Cboe.

### Crypto Market Making (Cumberland)

Cumberland is one of the few US-regulated institutional crypto liquidity providers and a major counterparty for institutional crypto options trades, particularly on [[deribit]] and CME crypto products.

### Real Estate and Venture

DRW's non-trading subsidiaries (Convexity Properties, DRW Investments) own meaningful real estate portfolios in Chicago and have venture exposure across financial technology and digital assets.

## 2025–2026 Developments

- **SEC case dropped (March 2025)** — In October 2024 the SEC sued Cumberland DRW, alleging it operated as an unregistered securities dealer and sold more than $2 billion of unregistered securities (naming tokens including SOL, POL, ATOM, ALGO and FIL). Following the change in SEC leadership under Acting Chair Mark Uyeda, the parties reached an agreement on 2025-02-20 and the SEC dismissed the suit, announced 2025-03-04 — explicitly on regulatory-policy grounds, "not on any assessment of the merits". The dismissal was part of the SEC's broader 2025 retreat from crypto enforcement (Coinbase, Kraken, Uniswap Labs cases also dropped).
- **Regulatory tailwind for Cumberland** — with the dealer-registration threat removed, Cumberland remained one of the largest US institutional crypto OTC liquidity providers through 2025–2026, benefiting from the friendlier US crypto-market-structure environment.
- DRW remains private; there is no ticker. Traders get no direct exposure — DRW matters as a counterparty and liquidity provider, not as an investment.

## Trading Relevance

For active options traders, DRW's behaviour matters in several ways:

- **Rate-options liquidity** — DRW is one of the dominant market makers in Treasury options and Eurodollar/SOFR options; its participation directly shapes execution for fixed-income vol traders.
- **Crypto options institutional flow** — Cumberland is a primary counterparty for large [[bitcoin]] and [[ethereum]] options trades; large blocks executed on Deribit often have a Cumberland leg.
- **Energy options dynamics** — DRW is a top-tier energy options market maker; its hedging flow contributes to observable patterns in WTI and natural gas option [[volatility-skew]].
