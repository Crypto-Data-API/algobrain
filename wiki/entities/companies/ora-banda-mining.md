**Ora Banda Mining Limited** (ASX: OBM) is a Western Australian [[gold]] producer and explorer listed on the [[asx-limited|Australian Securities Exchange]] and a constituent of the [[asx-200]] index, focused on the Davyhurst Gold Project in the Eastern Goldfields, approximately 125 kilometres from Kalgoorlie. Sitting in the [[materials]] sector, it is a mid-cap gold producer in a rapid production ramp (FY26 guidance ~60% above FY25) feeding a single central mill, which makes it a high-beta, single-hub play on the AUD gold price rather than a diversified miner. The company is headquartered in Subiaco, Western Australia and was incorporated in 2002.

## Business Overview, Segments & Assets

Ora Banda is a single-project gold producer: every tonne of ore is processed through one central carbon-in-pulp (CIP) plant at Davyhurst, fed by two producing underground mines with a development pipeline behind them. The company does not report multiple business segments — it is, in effect, the Davyhurst Gold Project. Riverina Underground achieved commercial production on 1 August 2024 following two consecutive months of steady-state output, and Sand King reached final investment decision in July 2024 and achieved steady-state production in the June 2025 quarter. Processing capacity is supplemented by third-party ore sales and processing arrangements to optimise mill utilisation.

| Asset | Type | Status | Role |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Riverina Underground** | Underground gold mine | Producing (commercial Aug 2024) | First foundation mine into the Davyhurst mill; ongoing mine-life extension drilling |
| **Sand King Underground** | Underground gold mine | Producing (FID Jul 2024; steady-state Jun-2025 qtr) | Second producing mine; full-year contribution underpins the FY26 ~60% production uplift |
| **Davyhurst CIP mill** | Central processing plant | Operating | Sole processing hub — the bottleneck and single point of concentration for the whole project |
| **Waihi** | Development deposit | Advancing | Targeted as a potential third underground mine; downdip extension testing in FY26 |
| **Round Dam** | Resource / growth project | Drilling | Largest recent exploration success — resource lifted to ~1.33 Moz, a near-tenfold increase |
| **Little Gem, Mulline** | Growth projects | Exploration | Regional pipeline supporting future mill feed and resource growth |

The most significant recent exploration success has been at Round Dam, where the latest resource update lifted the deposit to 1.33 million ounces of gold, a near tenfold increase on prior estimates. This helped drive total Davyhurst Gold Project resources to 3.3 million ounces, a 57% increase in the company's overall resource inventory — the single most important variable for a single-asset producer, since reserve/resource life is what converts a high-beta restart story into a durable mid-cap.

## History

Ora Banda's corporate lineage stretches back through several Western Australian gold mining entities. The company was originally known as Monarch Gold Mining Company, later Swan Gold Mining. On 1 December 2015, it changed its name to Eastern Goldfields Limited. Under the Eastern Goldfields banner, the company encountered operational and financial difficulties that ultimately led to a period of administration.

A successful recapitalisation in 2019 reset the business. On 28 June 2019, the recapitalised company returned to the ASX as Ora Banda Mining Limited with approximately A$15 million of cash to fund resource drill-out, detailed mine planning and regional exploration at Davyhurst. The rebrand marked the start of a new strategic phase focused on disciplined mine development, systematic exploration and restoration of the Davyhurst mill to profitable throughput.

Over subsequent years, Ora Banda progressed underground development at Riverina and Sand King, rebuilt its geological database and invested in a large-scale drilling program across the Davyhurst belt. This investment has driven sustained resource growth, underpinning the current multi-mine production profile.

## Recent Developments

For the 2025 financial year, Ora Banda reported revenue of A$404.3 million (up from A$214.2 million in FY24) on record gold sales of 91,687 ounces at an AISC of A$2,693/oz. EBITDA rose 243% to A$184.6 million and net profit after tax jumped 575% to A$186.1 million, assisted by a A$73.1 million tax benefit from recognising deferred tax assets linked to carry-forward losses. Operating cash flow rose 102% to A$184.0 million.

For the half year to 31 December 2025, the company delivered record half-year revenue of A$336.3 million, up 80% year-on-year. EBITDA increased 106% to A$173.2 million, EBIT rose 170% to A$140.7 million and profit before tax lifted 176% to A$140.1 million. Gold production for the December 2025 quarter was 32,036 ounces, up 5% on the prior quarter. Cash holdings finished at A$155.4 million at 31 December 2025.

FY26 production guidance is 140,000-155,000 ounces at an AISC of A$2,800-2,900/oz, representing approximately a 60% uplift on FY25, underpinned by a full year of Sand King production and third-party ore processing. The company has budgeted approximately A$73 million to complete around 329 kilometres of drilling in FY26 - more than the prior three years combined - targeting mine-life extensions at Riverina and Sand King, testing Waihi's downdip potential, and advancing Little Gem, Round Dam and Mulline.

The most recent filing (annual) was lodged on 2023-06-30. See [[#Financial Reports]] below.

## Economic Moat & Competitive Position

Like every primary gold miner, Ora Banda is a **price-taker with no [[economic-moat|economic moat]] in the conventional sense** — it sells an undifferentiated commodity at a global spot price it cannot influence, so there is no brand, no switching cost, and no pricing power. For producers, "competitive advantage" reduces to three honest, relative variables:

- **Position on the industry cost curve** — Ora Banda's edge or vulnerability is its AISC (guided ~A$2,800–2,900/oz for FY26) relative to peers and to the prevailing AUD gold price. At today's elevated bullion price the project generates strong margins, but a single-mill, underground-only operation has limited ability to cut unit costs in a downturn the way a large open-pit or multi-asset producer can.
- **Asset / grade quality and mine life** — the durable question is whether the orebodies are deep, high-grade and long-lived. The lift in Davyhurst resources to ~3.3 Moz (with Round Dam alone at ~1.33 Moz) is the company's most important "moat-like" attribute: it converts a fragile restart into a multi-year production base and is what the equity is really priced on.
- **Reserve replacement** — underground mines deplete fast; the FY26 ~329 km drill program (more than the prior three years combined) is the mechanism for replacing what is mined. Failure to replace reserves is the slow-motion way a single-asset gold producer erodes.

There is no structural barrier protecting Ora Banda from competition — its advantage is entirely operational and geological. Compared with diversified majors like [[bhp]] or large gold producers such as [[northern-star-resources]] and [[evolution-mining]], OBM has **no scale, no asset diversification and a single processing chokepoint**, which is why it trades as a higher-risk, higher-beta name rather than a quality compounder.

## Growth Drivers & Catalysts

- **FY26 production step-change** — guidance of 140,000–155,000 oz at AISC A$2,800–2,900/oz, roughly a **60% uplift on FY25**, driven by a full year of Sand King and third-party ore processing. Delivering this is the central near-term thesis.
- **Drilling-led resource & reserve growth** — a ~A$73 million, ~329 km FY26 drill program (more than the prior three years combined) targeting mine-life extensions at Riverina and Sand King, Waihi downdip potential, and Little Gem / Round Dam / Mulline.
- **Waihi as a third mine** — converting Waihi into a producing underground operation would lift mill feed and diversify the orebody base away from two mines.
- **Resource updates** — further reclassification of the 3.3 Moz Davyhurst resource (especially Round Dam) into reserves is a repeatable share-price catalyst.
- **The AUD [[gold]] price** — the dominant external driver; as an unhedged-leaning producer, every move in AUD-denominated bullion flows almost directly to margin and free cash flow.

## Bull Case vs Bear Case

### Bull case
- Rapid, funded production ramp (~60% FY26 uplift) with a full year of Sand King; earnings and cash flow step up sharply, normalising the elevated trailing P/E.
- Strong exploration momentum — 3.3 Moz resource, Round Dam at 1.33 Moz, and the largest drill program in company history — extends mine life and supports a credible path to a third mine at Waihi.
- High operating leverage to a strong AUD gold price; debt-free-leaning, growing cash balance gives optionality.
- Disciplined post-recapitalisation management has converted a failed predecessor (Eastern Goldfields) into a profitable multi-mine producer.

### Bear case
- **Single-asset, single-mill** business — any disruption at the Davyhurst CIP plant or at either underground mine hits the entire revenue base; there is no diversification buffer.
- Underground execution and grade-reconciliation risk; ramps frequently disappoint on tonnes, grade or cost.
- The trailing valuation (~82x FY24 earnings) only makes sense if the FY25–26 earnings step-change is sustained — any stumble re-rates the stock hard.
- Pure price-taker: a fall in the AUD gold price compresses margins quickly given a high, largely fixed underground cost base.
- Reserve replacement is a perpetual treadmill for underground producers; underwhelming drilling would shorten mine life and the implied valuation.

## Key Risks

- **Single processing-hub concentration** — the Davyhurst mill is the sole route to revenue; mechanical, permitting or feed-grade problems there are existential, not marginal.
- **Underground operational execution** — Riverina and Sand King carry development, dilution, ventilation and grade-reconciliation risks typical of WA underground gold.
- **Valuation normalisation** — a high trailing P/E (~82x on FY24) depends entirely on the earnings step-change holding; multiple compression is severe if production or AISC guidance slips.
- **Reserve replacement** — fast-depleting underground orebodies require continuous, successful drilling to sustain the production profile.
- **Gold-price and FX** — as a price-taker, OBM has full exposure to the AUD gold price; a stronger AUD or weaker USD gold both squeeze margins.

## Valuation & How the Stock Trades

Ora Banda is best understood as a **high-beta, single-asset call option on the AUD [[gold]] price layered on an operational ramp**, not a value or quality stock:

- **Gold-price beta** — an unhedged-leaning producer; the equity amplifies AUD gold moves in both directions, with margins and free cash flow geared to bullion.
- **Single-asset risk premium** — because all production flows through one mill from two mines, the market applies a discount/volatility premium versus diversified producers like [[northern-star-resources]] or [[evolution-mining]]; concentrated event risk is the trade-off for the growth leverage.
- **P/E normalisation** — the headline ~82x trailing multiple (on FY24) is misleading: it reflects a year before the production step-change. The bull thesis is that FY25–26 earnings collapse the forward multiple to a far lower number; the bear thesis is that the multiple is "right" only if every operational milestone is hit.
- **Liquidity & instruments** — an [[asx-200]] constituent since 2024–25, which brought passive index flows and improved liquidity, but there is **no meaningful listed-options market**, so exposure is taken via the ordinary shares. No dividend — this is a growth/reinvestment story, with cash directed to drilling and development rather than distributions.
