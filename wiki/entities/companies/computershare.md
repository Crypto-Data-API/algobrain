**Computershare Limited** (ASX: CPU) is a capital markets services company listed on the [[asx-limited|Australian Securities Exchange]] and a constituent of the [[asx-200]] index. Computershare Limited is one of the world's largest providers of share registry and related financial administration services, operating in more than 20 countries and administering the registers of thousands of listed companies and bond issuers. Founded in Melbourne in 1978, the company has grown through a long series of acquisitions to become a global leader in issuer services, employee share plan administration, corporate trust, and stakeholder communications.

## Business Overview & Segments

Computershare is a global financial-administration and registry-technology business — at its core a [[technology|technology]]-enabled outsourcer of mission-critical, recurring back-office functions for issuers, banks and bondholders. Its revenue base splits into two economically distinct streams: **fee/transaction revenue** from administering registers, plans and trusts, and **margin income** — interest earned on the large pools of client cash it holds on behalf of issuers and plan participants. The first is a steady, contract-driven annuity; the second is a high-incremental-margin, rate-leveraged kicker that makes Computershare one of the cleanest listed plays on global short-term interest rates.

| Segment / service line | What it is | Revenue / economics note |
|---|---|---|
| **Issuer Services** | Share registry maintenance, corporate actions, stakeholder management, governance, meeting/voting services for listed issuers | The historic core — sticky, recurring, contract-based annuity revenue; also the source of much of the client cash that generates margin income |
| **Corporate Trust** | Trust, agency, escrow and bond administration | Materially expanded by the 2021 acquisition of Wells Fargo's US corporate trust business; large, recurring, and a major driver of margin-income leverage on bondholder balances |
| **Employee Share Plans** | Administration of global employee share/option plans (Equate platform) | Recurring SaaS-like fees; secular growth as equity-based compensation spreads; sticky multi-year corporate mandates |
| **Communication Services** | Document composition, printing, inbound process automation, scanning, electronic delivery | Lower-margin, volume-driven; structurally shifting from print to digital delivery |
| **Mortgage & Property Services** | Tenancy bond protection (primarily UK) and related services | Niche, geographically concentrated; modest contributor |
| **Class Action Services** | Settlement administration and claims processing | Episodic / event-driven revenue tied to litigation cycles |

**Margin income** is the single most important swing factor in the earnings model: interest earned on tens of billions of dollars of client balances held across the issuer-services and corporate-trust businesses. Because the underlying balances are sticky and the income drops straight to the bottom line, the metric is highly geared to US/UK/AU short-term rates — a "higher for longer" rate environment is a powerful tailwind, while a rate-cut cycle is the key headwind (see [[financials]]).

## Economic Moat & Competitive Position

Computershare holds a wide, durable [[economic-moat|economic moat]] built on several reinforcing layers rather than a single advantage:

- **Global scale in registry** — one of the world's largest share-registry providers, operating in 20+ countries and administering the registers of thousands of listed companies and bond issuers. No rival matches its combined global footprint and multi-jurisdiction capability.
- **High [[switching-costs]] for issuers** — moving a register or a global employee-plan mandate is operationally risky, disruptive to shareholders/employees, and rarely worth the saving; contracts are multi-year and stickiness is high. Churn is low and pricing is defensible.
- **Regulatory / trust licensing** — corporate trust, escrow and bond administration require regulatory standing and trusted-intermediary status that take years to build, raising barriers to entry.
- **Network density & data** — running registers, plans and trusts at scale generates operating leverage and data/process advantages that smaller players cannot replicate.
- **Recurring, contracted revenue** — the bulk of fee revenue is annuity-like, insulating the franchise from cyclical swings (the volatility sits in margin income and episodic corporate-action/IPO volumes).

The moat is **wide but rate-exposed rather than rate-proof**: the durable defence is scale + switching costs on the fee base, while the most cyclical earnings line (margin income) rides the global rate cycle rather than any competitive dynamic.

## Growth Drivers & Catalysts

- **Margin income / "higher for longer" rates** — the most powerful near-term swing factor; sustained or rising short-term rates lift income on client balances straight to the bottom line.
- **Corporate Trust scale-up** — the 2021 Wells Fargo Corporate Trust acquisition materially expanded the US footprint and the client-balance pool that generates margin income; continued integration and cross-sell is a multi-year driver.
- **EBIT-ex-margin-income margin target** — management is targeting a ~20% EBIT-ex-margin-income margin by FY2028, i.e. growing the *underlying* fee business independent of the rate tailwind.
- **Buybacks** — ongoing share repurchases have steadily shrunk the register (shares outstanding down from ~604M in FY22 to ~578M in FY25), amplifying EPS.
- **Employee share plan growth** — secular expansion of equity-based compensation drives recurring plan-administration fees.
- **IPO / M&A and corporate-action cycle** — issuance, capital raisings and corporate actions drive transaction revenue; a more active market is a tailwind.

### 2025–2026 developments

- **H1 FY26 results (10 February 2026):** revenue of approximately US$1.6 billion; management EPS up 3.9%; EBIT excluding margin income up 12% year-on-year, with strong growth in issuer services, corporate trust and employee share plans. The interim dividend was increased 22%.
- **Guidance upgrade:** FY26 management EPS guidance lifted to around 144 US cents (up ~6% on FY25). The group is targeting a 20% EBIT-ex-margin-income margin by FY2028.
- **Margin income resilience:** despite global rate cuts through 2025, margin income on client balances held up better than feared — the key swing factor in the earnings model. CPU remains one of the ASX's cleanest plays on global short-term interest rates.
- Ongoing share buybacks continued to shrink the register (shares outstanding down from ~604M in FY22 to ~578M in FY25).

For fiscal year 2025, Computershare reported record earnings, with revenue of approximately A$3.12 billion (up 4.7% year-on-year) and earnings of A$607 million (up 72.1%). Group total revenue including sales and other revenue items was A$4.74 billion. The company maintained approximately 12,891 employees globally in 2025. CPU declared a cash dividend of A$0.55 per share with an ex-date of 17 February 2026. Management has flagged continued benefits from higher margin income in its corporate trust business and disciplined cost management.

## Bull Case vs Bear Case

### Bull case
- **Rates tailwind:** margin income on tens of billions of client balances is highly geared to short-term rates; a "higher for longer" environment supports earnings with little incremental cost.
- **Scale moat:** global registry leadership, high issuer [[switching-costs]] and trust licensing protect pricing and keep churn low.
- **Self-help on the fee base:** the FY2028 EBIT-ex-margin-income margin target shows underlying-business growth independent of rates.
- **Capital return:** ongoing buybacks have shrunk the register and amplify EPS; record FY25 earnings (A$607M, +72%) demonstrate operating leverage.
- **Recurring revenue:** annuity-like fee streams insulate most of the business from cyclical swings.

### Bear case
- **Rate-cut cycle:** a sustained decline in global short-term rates compresses margin income — the most cyclical and highest-margin earnings line.
- **Mature-market volume risk:** registry volumes and corporate-action activity are cyclical; print-based communications are in structural decline.
- **Integration / execution:** large acquisitions (Wells Fargo Corporate Trust) carry integration and cost-synergy execution risk.
- **FX translation:** earnings are reported in AUD but generated heavily in USD/GBP; currency moves distort headline growth.
- **Valuation:** the rate-leveraged earnings can flatter the multiple late in a rate cycle, leaving downside if margin income rolls over.

## Key Risks

- **Rate sensitivity** — the dominant risk: a falling-rate cycle directly compresses margin income.
- **Regulatory** — registry, trust and escrow businesses operate under regulatory and licensing regimes across many jurisdictions; rule changes can raise costs or alter the client-balance economics.
- **FX translation** — significant USD and GBP earnings translated into an AUD-reported result; currency swings move headline numbers.
- **Cyclicality of corporate actions** — IPO, capital-raising, M&A and corporate-action volumes ebb and flow with markets, driving transaction-revenue volatility.
- **Integration** — assimilating large acquisitions and realising synergies (notably Wells Fargo Corporate Trust) without service disruption.

## Valuation & How the Stock Trades

Computershare is best understood as a recurring-revenue [[technology|registry/financial-administration]] business with a rate-leveraged overlay — the fee base is annuity-like, while margin income on client balances behaves like a call option on global short-term rates. Mechanically:

- **Recurring-revenue characterisation** — the market values the sticky, contract-driven fee streams (issuer services, employee plans, corporate trust) at a software/services-like multiple, then layers on the rate-sensitive margin-income kicker.
- **Rate leverage via margin income** — earnings are leveraged to US/UK/AU short-term rates; CPU often trades as a rates-sensitivity pair against banks and insurers. Falling-rate cycles compress the earnings outlook; "higher for longer" is bullish (see [[financials]]).
- **Index membership** — [[asx-200]] constituent (top-50 by market cap); held widely in ASX financial-services and quality-factor baskets.
- **Catalysts** — half-year results (February) and full-year results (August), Fed/RBA rate-path repricing, corporate-action and IPO/M&A activity cycles, US corporate-trust flows.
- **Correlated names** — [[asx-limited]], [[hub24]], [[netwealth]], global peers Equiniti and Broadridge; rate-sensitivity overlaps with [[qbe-insurance]] and bank-margin trades.
- **Capital return** — steady, partly franked dividend plus ongoing buybacks; the dividend and a shrinking register are core to the total-return case for income-tilted holders.
