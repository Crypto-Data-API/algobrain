# E*TRADE / Power E*TRADE

Online discount broker founded in 1982 (as TradePlus, rebranded E*TRADE Securities in 1991), one of the original retail electronic brokerages and a flagship of the dotcom-era retail trading boom. Acquired by **Morgan Stanley in October 2020 for approximately $13 billion**, E*TRADE now operates as Morgan Stanley's self-directed retail brand. **Power E*TRADE** is its advanced trading platform -- a frequently overlooked, institutional-grade options interface that many E*TRADE customers do not realize they already have access to.

## Overview

E*TRADE serves two distinct user bases through one brand:

- **E*TRADE (standard web/app)**: simple investing-oriented interface for buy-and-hold investors, IRAs, and casual traders
- **Power E*TRADE (web and mobile)**: advanced trading platform with real-time [[options-greeks]], scenario analysis on options chains, automation features (quote triggers, one-click trades), pre-population of order tickets from screeners, and a layout closer to thinkorswim or [[tastytrade]] than to [[robinhood]]

Power E*TRADE is included free with any E*TRADE account -- no separate subscription, no minimum balance. It is generally considered legitimate competition to thinkorswim for options traders, and is frequently underrated because users default to the standard E*TRADE app and never enable the advanced platform.

Major events:

- **1982** -- founded as TradePlus in Palo Alto, CA
- **1991** -- rebranded E*TRADE Securities; one of the first online brokerages
- **1996** -- IPO on Nasdaq
- **October 2020** -- acquired by Morgan Stanley for ~$13 billion in an all-stock deal
- **Post-acquisition** -- continues to operate as a Morgan Stanley brand alongside Morgan Stanley Wealth Management; no longer separately listed (exposure only via Morgan Stanley, NYSE: MS)
- **2025-2026** -- Morgan Stanley announced crypto trading for E*TRADE clients (see below), the brand's biggest product expansion since the acquisition

## 2025–2026 Developments

- **Crypto trading launch (announced 2025, rollout first half of 2026):** Morgan Stanley is bringing spot cryptocurrency trading to E*TRADE through a partnership with infrastructure provider **Zerohash**, which handles custody, execution, and settlement. The initial offering covers **Bitcoin, Ethereum, and Solana**, making E*TRADE the first major wirehouse-owned retail platform to offer direct retail crypto — a competitive response to [[robinhood]] and Coinbase.
- Morgan Stanley's wealth-management arm (under Jed Finn) has framed the launch as phase one of a broader digital-asset strategy, including a planned client wallet solution; the bank also participated in Zerohash's $104M funding round at a ~$1B valuation (led by [[interactive-brokers]]).
- Morgan Stanley's asset-allocation guidance now contemplates 0–4% crypto sleeves depending on client risk tolerance — relevant context for potential retail crypto flows through the platform.

## Standalone vs Morgan Stanley Unit

E*TRADE is **not a separately listed company**. It was acquired by [[morgan-stanley|Morgan Stanley]] (NYSE: MS) in an all-stock deal worth ~$13B that closed in **October 2020**, and it now operates as Morgan Stanley's self-directed retail brokerage brand inside the Wealth Management division (alongside Morgan Stanley Wealth Management and the Shareworks/E*TRADE corporate stock-plan business). There is **no standalone E*TRADE ticker** — the only listed-equity exposure to the franchise is through [[morgan-stanley|Morgan Stanley]]. All of the business-quality, valuation, and "how it trades" discussion below therefore refers to E*TRADE as a *unit* and, where it trades, to the **parent, Morgan Stanley**.

## Business Overview & How It Makes Money

As a brokerage, E*TRADE monetizes self-directed retail accounts through several overlapping revenue lines — the same economic engine as any modern zero-commission broker:

| Revenue line | What it is | Driver |
|---|---|---|
| **Net interest income (NII)** | Spread earned on uninvested client cash (sweep deposits) and margin lending | The largest profit lever for online brokers; rises with interest rates and margin balances |
| **Payment for order flow (PFOF)** | Rebates from market makers for routing equity and (especially) options order flow | Scales with trade volume, particularly options ([[payment-for-order-flow|PFOF]]) |
| **Options & futures commissions** | $0.65/contract base (volume-discounted to $0.50) on options; $1.50/side futures | Active-trader monetization where $0-commission equities do not pay |
| **Asset-based fees** | Robo-advisor (Core Portfolios, 0.30%), managed accounts, mutual-fund fees | Recurring, fee-on-assets |
| **Corporate stock plans** | Shareworks/E*TRADE equity-compensation administration for corporates | A funnel of new retail accounts into the Morgan Stanley ecosystem |

Within Morgan Stanley, E*TRADE's strategic value is less about standalone profit than about **client acquisition and the deposit base**: it feeds self-directed customers and their cash (a cheap funding source) into the broader Wealth Management flywheel, and the corporate stock-plan business channels newly-vested employees into MS accounts.

## Economic Moat & Competitive Position

E*TRADE's individual [[economic-moat|moat]] is **narrow** — retail brokerage is a scale-and-trust commodity since the 2019 move to $0 equity commissions — but it is reinforced by its parent:

- **Brand and trust** — one of the original online brokers (since 1991) with a household name and a 40-year track record; trust matters enormously when customers park assets.
- **Switching costs / inertia** — account transfers, cost-basis history, and linked banking/IRA relationships create friction; assets tend to stay put.
- **Power E*TRADE platform** — institutional-grade options analytics bundled free is a genuine product differentiator versus simpler apps, retaining active options traders.
- **Parent scale ([[morgan-stanley|Morgan Stanley]])** — balance-sheet strength, research access (MS, Morningstar, S&P Capital IQ), banking integration, and the wealth-management cross-sell are advantages a standalone E*TRADE lacked.

The franchise lacks the lowest-cost moat of [[interactive-brokers]] (which competes on price, margin rates, and global access) and the viral, mobile-native distribution of [[robinhood]]; it occupies the "full-service-grade tooling at discount pricing, inside a wirehouse" niche.

## Growth Drivers & Catalysts

- **Crypto launch (2026)** — spot BTC/ETH/SOL via Zerohash makes E*TRADE the first major wirehouse-owned retail platform to offer direct retail crypto; a competitive answer to [[robinhood]]/Coinbase and a new flow/engagement driver.
- **Rate environment** — net interest income on sweep cash and margin balances is the dominant earnings lever; higher-for-longer rates are a tailwind.
- **Active-trader and options volume** — options/futures commissions and PFOF scale with engagement; elevated retail options activity benefits the franchise.
- **Wealth cross-sell** — migrating self-directed clients and corporate-stock-plan participants into Morgan Stanley advised and banking products.
- **Product modernization** — continued Power E*TRADE feature build-out and the planned digital-asset wallet.

## Bull vs Bear (as a Morgan Stanley franchise)

### Bull case
- A trusted, scaled retail-brokerage brand feeding cheap deposits and new clients into Morgan Stanley's high-margin Wealth Management flywheel.
- Power E*TRADE is a credible thinkorswim alternative that retains lucrative active options traders.
- 2026 crypto launch and digital-asset roadmap re-engage a younger, crypto-curious cohort the brand had been losing to [[robinhood]].
- Rate-driven net interest income provides a durable earnings base for the parent.

### Bear case
- $0 equity commissions commoditized the core product; E*TRADE competes on tooling and trust, not price, and is undercut on cost by [[robinhood]]/[[webull]] and on professional features/margin by [[interactive-brokers]].
- Brand awareness faded post-acquisition; Morgan Stanley has under-marketed Power E*TRADE.
- Crypto, PFOF, and retail-options revenue are politically and regulatorily exposed.
- No standalone investment thesis — exposure is diluted inside a large diversified bank.

## Key Risks

- **Regulatory** — scrutiny of [[payment-for-order-flow|PFOF]], best-execution rules, retail-options suitability, and crypto regulation all bear on brokerage economics.
- **Rate sensitivity** — a sharp fall in interest rates compresses the net-interest-income that drives broker profitability.
- **Competitive pricing** — the $0-commission race and margin-rate competition (vs [[interactive-brokers]]) cap pricing power.
- **Platform / operational risk** — outages or order-routing problems during volatile sessions damage trust, the brokerage's core asset.
- **Integration / brand drift** — continued under-investment in the E*TRADE brand inside Morgan Stanley could erode the franchise over time.

## Valuation & How the Stock Trades

There is no E*TRADE security to value or trade; the relevant instrument is the **parent, [[morgan-stanley|Morgan Stanley]] (NYSE: MS)**:

- **Bank/wealth-manager valuation** — Morgan Stanley trades on price-to-tangible-book and P/E like other banks, but at a **premium to balance-sheet-heavy money-center banks** (e.g. [[bank-of-america]]) because its earnings mix is tilted toward **capital-light, recurring Wealth and Investment Management fees** — the segment E*TRADE sits inside. The market pays up for fee-based ROE and stable wealth flows.
- **Where E*TRADE shows up** — in MS's results, E*TRADE contributes to Wealth Management net new assets, self-directed client cash/deposits (a funding and NII source), and transactional/PFOF revenue; analysts watch net-new-asset growth and the deposit base.
- **Drivers of the MS multiple** — wealth-management fee growth, net interest income on sweep deposits, trading/IB cyclicality, and capital return (dividend + buybacks gated by CCAR).
- **Beta** — Morgan Stanley is a financial with above-market beta and rate sensitivity; it trades with the bank group, the [[10-year-treasury|yield curve]], and equity-market activity levels.

## Pricing / Commissions

- **US stocks and ETFs**: $0 commission
- **Options**: $0.65/contract base; **discounted to $0.50/contract for clients trading 30+ options/futures contracts per quarter**
- **Futures**: $1.50/contract per side, plus exchange and regulatory fees
- **Mutual funds**: thousands of no-load, no-transaction-fee funds; $19.99 fee on transaction-fee mutual funds
- **Bonds**: $1/bond ($10 minimum, $250 maximum) on secondary trades; new-issue treasuries free
- **Margin rates**: tiered, generally higher than [[interactive-brokers]] but in line with full-service peers
- **No account minimums** on most account types
- **Robo-advisor (Core Portfolios)**: 0.30% annual fee, $500 minimum

Options pricing comparison: $0.65/contract sits at parity with [[charles-schwab]], above [[robinhood]] and [[webull]] (both $0/contract on equity options), and roughly comparable to [[tastytrade]] depending on trade size.

## What You Get / Key Features

### Power E*TRADE platform highlights

- **Real-time options Greeks** displayed inline on chains (delta, gamma, theta, vega, rho)
- **Options chains with scenario analysis**: model P/L under price, time, and IV scenarios before placing the trade
- **Quote triggers**: set conditions on quote/indicator/price events to fire orders -- a form of basic automation without coding
- **One-click trades**: pre-set order parameters and execute with a single action, useful for scalping and rapid options entries
- **Pre-population from screeners**: when you click a result in a stock or options screener, the order ticket fills with sensible defaults (strike, expiry, qty)
- **Spread builder**: drag-and-drop interface for verticals, iron condors, butterflies, calendars, and custom multi-leg strategies
- **Earnings move calculator**: implied move based on options pricing, surfaced on the chain
- **Probability of profit / breakeven analytics**: shown directly on multi-leg orders
- **Streaming charts**: 100+ studies, multiple chart styles, drawing tools
- **Paper trading**: available within Power E*TRADE
- **Mobile parity**: most of the above replicated in the Power E*TRADE mobile app

### Standard E*TRADE platform

- **Mutual funds, bonds, and IRAs**: deeper product range than [[robinhood]] or [[webull]]
- **Research**: Morgan Stanley research, Morningstar, S&P Capital IQ analyst reports included
- **Banking integration**: Morgan Stanley Private Bank checking, savings, and lending
- **Retirement planning tools**: traditional/Roth IRA, SEP IRA, Solo 401(k), rollover support

## Use Cases / Who Uses It

- Active options traders who want institutional-grade tooling without paying a platform fee
- Retail investors who want a single broker for taxable, IRA, and managed accounts
- Customers attracted by the post-acquisition Morgan Stanley research access
- Long-term investors who also dabble in options and want both UIs in one account
- Traders who specifically want quote triggers and one-click execution but do not want to write code (pre-built automation)
- Users priced out of professional platforms but needing more than [[robinhood]] offers
- Anyone evaluating thinkorswim alternatives -- Power E*TRADE is the most credible non-Schwab alternative for many workflows

## Strengths and Limitations

**Strengths**

- Power E*TRADE is genuinely competitive with thinkorswim on options analytics and is included free
- Strong product breadth (stocks, options, futures, mutual funds, bonds, IRAs, managed)
- Morgan Stanley research access post-2020 acquisition
- Established, regulated, well-capitalized parent company
- Quote triggers and one-click trades provide simple automation without code
- Volume discount on options ($0.50/contract at 30+ contracts/quarter) rewards active traders

**Limitations**

- Equity options at $0.65/contract are not competitive with $0/contract brokers ([[robinhood]], [[webull]]) for low-volume traders
- Standard E*TRADE app is dated; many customers never discover Power E*TRADE
- Margin rates are higher than [[interactive-brokers]] at most balance tiers
- Brand awareness has faded somewhat post-acquisition; Morgan Stanley has not heavily marketed Power E*TRADE
- Native cryptocurrency trading only arriving in 2026 (BTC/ETH/SOL via Zerohash) — long after crypto-native competitors
- Customer service experience varies; the Morgan Stanley integration introduced occasional friction during the transition years
- Order routing accepts [[payment-for-order-flow|PFOF]] like most retail brokers; execution quality is generally good on liquid names but worth monitoring on thin options
