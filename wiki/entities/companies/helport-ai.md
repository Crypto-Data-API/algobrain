Helport AI Limited (NASDAQ: HPAI) is a Singapore-headquartered micro-cap that sells SaaS software and AI tools for the **contact-center industry** (agent-assist, communication intelligence) plus AI-enabled BPO services, operating across Asia and North America. For traders it is a speculative sub-$50M AI-label micro cap: very low float-adjusted liquidity, a collapsed share price, and dilution risk dominate any fundamental story.

## AI Involvement

[[artificial-intelligence|AI]]-powered customer communication and workforce platform (flagship product Helport AI Assist; HyprX "digital twin" platform launched January 2026)

## Business & Product (detail)

Helport sells AI software and AI-enabled services into the **contact-centre / BPO** value chain, operating across Asia and North America:

| Product / line | Function |
|---|---|
| **Helport AI Assist** | Flagship real-time agent-assist: live guidance, scripting, compliance prompts and communication intelligence during customer calls |
| **HyprX** | "Digital twin" platform (launched 13 January 2026) for enterprise sales, training, customer engagement and embedded devices |
| **AI-enabled BPO services** | Outsourced contact-centre operations augmented by Helport's AI tooling (a services revenue line, not pure SaaS) |
| **Vertical initiatives** | e.g. AI-powered debt-collections (launched 7 April 2026 with a leading Asian consumer-finance company) |

The model blends SaaS software (higher margin, more durable) with AI-enabled BPO services (lower margin, people-heavy). For a contact-centre AI vendor, the strategic question is whether agent-assist remains a standalone product or gets absorbed into the large CCaaS platforms (Five9, NICE, Genesys, Salesforce, Microsoft) that already own the contact-centre seat. Revenue durability — whether press-released "wins" become recurring, multi-year contracts — is the central uncertainty.

## Bull vs Bear Case

**Bull case**
- Focused exposure to a real, large market (contact-centre AI / agent-assist) with genuine enterprise demand.
- Steady product cadence (HyprX, vertical debt-collections) keeps a flow of potential catalysts.
- SaaS component, if it scales into multi-year contracts, could deliver durable recurring revenue and operating leverage.
- A collapsed share price means modest good news can move the stock sharply.

**Bear case**
- ~80% decline from its 52-week high, sub-$1 share price, and live Nasdaq minimum-bid-price compliance risk.
- Unproven revenue durability — press-release "wins" often fully retrace; the BPO services mix dilutes SaaS-like margins.
- SPAC-listing provenance with the dilution/reverse-split cycle typical of micro-cap AI names.
- China-linkage adds governance, related-party and ADR/PCAOB/delisting overhang (below); competes against far larger CCaaS incumbents embedding the same AI.

## China-Linked / Governance & Listing Risk

Helport is a Singapore-headquartered but China-linked Nasdaq micro-cap and carries the elevated risks of that cohort:

- **Governance / related-party risk:** small China-linked issuers often have concentrated insider control and related-party arrangements; scrutinise filings (related-party transactions, customer concentration, revenue recognition).
- **Audit / PCAOB exposure:** China-adjacent issuers have been the focus of [[pcaob]] inspection-access disputes (HFCAA). Confirm the auditor is PCAOB-inspectable; an audit-access lapse is a delisting trigger.
- **Delisting risk (two layers):** the China-linkage delisting overhang *plus* an immediate, mechanical [[nasdaq]] [[listing standards|minimum-bid-price]] problem — the sub-$1 price makes a [[reverse-stock-split]] a live watch item.
- **Short-report history:** sub-$50M China-linked AI-labelled names are frequent short-seller targets; treat aggressive disclosures and unverifiable contract claims sceptically.
