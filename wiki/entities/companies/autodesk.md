Autodesk (NASDAQ: ADSK) is the dominant design-software vendor for architecture, engineering, construction (AEC) and manufacturing, selling AutoCAD, Revit, Fusion and the Construction Cloud on a subscription model. For traders it is a high-margin [[technology|Information Technology]] SaaS compounder in the [[s-and-p-500]] whose stock moves on billings/ARR growth, margin-expansion progress and construction/manufacturing end-market sentiment.

## Business Overview & Segments

Autodesk's revenue is ~90%+ recurring subscriptions across two big franchises plus a small media business, with gross margin running near 90%:

| Segment | What it is | Revenue mix (qualitative) | Key products |
|---|---|---|---|
| **AEC** (Architecture, Engineering & Construction) | Design and build software for buildings and infrastructure; the BIM standard | The largest and fastest-growing segment, levered to global construction digitization and BIM mandates | Revit, Civil 3D, Autodesk Construction Cloud |
| **Design & Manufacturing** (AutoCAD + M&D) | General-purpose drafting plus product design / CAD-CAM | Large, mature; AutoCAD is the cash cow, Fusion the cloud growth bet | AutoCAD, AutoCAD LT, Fusion, Inventor |
| **Media & Entertainment** | 3D animation and VFX for film and games | Small | Maya, 3ds Max |

The activist-driven (Starboard Value, 2024) margin story — moving to direct billing, cutting go-to-market cost — is a key part of the bull case.

## Economic Moat & Competitive Position

Autodesk's [[economic-moat]] is built on very high [[switching-costs]] and entrenched industry-standard status, reinforced by a recurring-subscription model:

- **Industry-standard formats & workflows** — Revit/BIM is effectively mandated or specified on large construction projects, and AutoCAD's DWG is the default 2D drafting format; entire AEC and manufacturing supply chains exchange files in Autodesk formats, so a single firm cannot leave unilaterally without breaking interoperability.
- **Training & ecosystem network effects** — universities, trade programs and CAD-certification tracks teach Autodesk tools; firms hire for AutoCAD/Revit fluency, which keeps the next project on Autodesk — a self-reinforcing flywheel.
- **Multi-year project lock-in** — buildings and infrastructure run on years-long timelines tied to a chosen toolset; switching mid-project is impractical, giving Autodesk durable pricing power.
- **Subscription model** — the perpetual-to-subscription transition (and the newer named-user/transaction model) smoothed revenue and lifted recurring revenue above 90%.

The moat is wide in AEC; it is more contested in manufacturing (where Dassault and PTC are strong) and at the cloud-CAD frontier.

## Growth Drivers & Catalysts

- **Construction digitization / BIM mandates** — secular shift of the build phase onto Autodesk Construction Cloud is the largest growth vector.
- **New transaction model** — the shift to direct billing improves economics and is driving reported revenue acceleration.
- **Margin expansion** — the Starboard-driven cost discipline and go-to-market efficiency program lifting free cash flow.
- **Fusion / cloud CAD** — converging design, manufacturing and data on one cloud platform.
- **AI features** — generative design and AI assistants as monetisation and retention levers.
- **TAM expansion down-market** — "Autodesk for Small Business" extends reach to small teams.
- **Recurring catalysts** — quarterly billings/free-cash-flow guidance, margin-target updates, restructuring progress, activist headlines, and non-residential construction-spend macro data.

## Bull Case vs Bear Case

### Bull case
- AEC/BIM is a multi-decade digitization story where Autodesk is the entrenched standard with deep [[switching-costs]].
- The new transaction model plus Starboard-driven cost cuts drive simultaneous revenue acceleration and margin expansion — a powerful free-cash-flow combination.
- ~90% gross margins and 90%+ recurring revenue make per-share compounding durable.
- TAM expansion (small business, cloud, AI features) adds optionality not fully in numbers.

### Bear case
- Construction is cyclical; non-residential softness or a macro slowdown hits the fastest-growing segment.
- Premium ~40x+ P/E leaves little room for execution missteps or AI-monetisation disappointment.
- Manufacturing share is contested by Dassault and PTC; cloud-CAD competition is intensifying.
- Restructuring charges and a January fiscal year-end make the reporting cadence and margin path harder to read.

## Key Risks

- **Construction cycle** — non-residential construction softness directly pressures AEC growth.
- **Valuation** — a rich multiple demands sustained billings growth and visible margin expansion.
- **Competitive** — Dassault/PTC in manufacturing, Bentley/Nemetschek in AEC, Procore in construction management.
- **Execution** — delivering the activist margin targets and managing the transaction-model transition cleanly.
- **AI monetisation** — converting generative-design and AI features into revenue rather than just retention.

## Valuation & How the Stock Trades

Autodesk trades as a premium SaaS compounder priced on billings/ARR growth and the credibility of its margin-expansion plan rather than on near-term earnings (it carries one of the higher software multiples — see the Fundamentals block below for figures as pulled). Mechanically:

- **No dividend** — capital return is via buybacks; the equity story is growth-plus-margin compounding, not yield.
- **Off-cycle reporting** — an end-of-January fiscal calendar means prints land in Feb/May/Aug/Nov, off-cycle from most software peers, so ADSK earnings are read for an early sector tone.
- **Options** — liquid chains with active weeklies; IV spikes into quarterly earnings.
- **Catalysts** — quarterly billings/free-cash-flow guidance, margin-target updates, restructuring progress, activist headlines, construction-spend macro data.
- **Correlated names** — [[adobe]] (subscription-software multiple proxy), PTC, Dassault, [[salesforce]]; construction exposure links it to the non-residential construction cycle.

## 2025-2026 Developments

- **FQ2 2026 (reported 28 August 2025)**: revenue up **17% YoY to $1.76 billion**, ahead of expectations, driven by AEC and the transition to the new transaction model.
- **22 January 2026 restructuring**: announced a worldwide plan cutting **~7% of the workforce (~1,000 roles)**, with **$135-160 million** of pre-tax charges, to fund AI investment and margin targets; completion targeted by end of FY2027 Q4.
- **4 May 2026**: launched **Autodesk for Small Business**, a global initiative targeting small teams/entrepreneurs — a TAM-expansion move down-market.
- **Stock action**: as of June 2026 ADSK traded near **$237**, roughly **-17% YTD** and ~28% below its 52-week high of $329, as investors questioned the pace of AI monetization and macro softness in construction.
