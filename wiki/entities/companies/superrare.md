SuperRare is a curated [[nft|NFT]] marketplace specializing in single-edition (1/1) digital artworks on the [[ethereum|Ethereum]] blockchain. Launched in April 2018, SuperRare pioneered the artist-centric model for crypto art by combining curation, on-chain provenance, and creator royalties — establishing itself as the premier destination for high-end digital art NFTs.

## History

SuperRare launched in April 2018 as one of the earliest curated NFT art platforms, alongside [[knownorigin|KnownOrigin]]. The platform was founded by John Crain, Charles Crain, and Jonathan Perkins. Unlike open marketplaces such as [[opensea|OpenSea]] and [[rarible|Rarible]], SuperRare required artists to apply and be approved before listing work, creating an exclusive gallery-like environment that attracted serious digital artists and collectors.

The platform's curation model helped establish legitimacy for NFT art by ensuring a baseline quality standard. Early adopters included prominent digital artists such as XCOPY, Pak, Hackatao, and Coldie, whose works on SuperRare achieved significant prices during the NFT boom of 2020-2021.

In August 2021, SuperRare launched the $RARE governance token, transitioning toward a decentralized autonomous organization (DAO) model. The token allowed holders to participate in governance decisions including curation through community-run "SuperRare Spaces" — independent storefronts curated by $RARE token holders rather than the core team alone.

## Business Model

SuperRare charges a 15% commission on primary sales, which was a pioneering structure in the NFT marketplace space. The platform also enforces a 10% royalty on secondary sales, with the artist receiving the majority of this royalty. This artist-first economics model influenced how subsequent NFT platforms structured their fee systems.

The $RARE token introduced additional economic dynamics. A portion of marketplace fees flows to the SuperRare DAO treasury, and $RARE holders can stake tokens to participate in curation and governance decisions.

## Platform Characteristics

- **Curation model**: Artists must apply and be approved; curated by both the core team and community-run SuperRare Spaces
- **Edition type**: Primarily 1/1 unique artworks, distinguishing it from edition-based platforms
- **Blockchain**: [[ethereum|Ethereum]] (ERC-721 standard)
- **Smart contracts**: On-chain provenance and royalty enforcement
- **Social features**: Artist profiles, collector galleries, activity feeds, and a built-in social layer for discovering art
- **Governance**: $RARE token holders vote on platform decisions and curate Spaces

## Notable Sales and Artists

SuperRare became known for hosting sales by some of the most recognized names in crypto art:

- **XCOPY**: One of the highest-selling artists on the platform, known for glitch art and themes of death and dystopia
- **Pak**: Prolific digital artist whose works on SuperRare contributed to a career total of hundreds of millions in NFT sales
- **Hackatao**: Duo known for layered, symbolic digital art
- **Coldie**: 3D stereoscopic artist and early crypto art pioneer

During the 2021 NFT bull market, individual 1/1 works on SuperRare routinely sold for tens to hundreds of ETH, with top pieces reaching six- and seven-figure USD valuations.

## Competitive Position

SuperRare's curated approach positioned it as the "gallery" tier of the NFT marketplace hierarchy, in contrast to:

- **[[opensea|OpenSea]]** and **[[rarible|Rarible]]**: Open, permissionless marketplaces with broader reach but less curation
- **[[knownorigin|KnownOrigin]]**: Similar curated model, but smaller scale (acquired by eBay in 2022)
- **[[makersplace|MakersPlace]]**: Another curated platform, though with a slightly different collector base
- **[[blur|Blur]]**: Trader-focused marketplace optimized for speed and volume rather than art discovery

The platform faced challenges during the 2022-2023 NFT market downturn, with trading volumes declining significantly across all NFT marketplaces. The shift toward royalty-optional marketplaces like [[blur|Blur]] also threatened SuperRare's royalty-enforcing model.

## 2024-2026 Status and Trader Exposure

SuperRare is a privately held company; there is no equity to trade. Trader-relevant exposure runs through two channels:

- **$RARE token** — an ERC-20 governance token whose price tracks NFT-market sentiment and SuperRare-specific catalysts (treasury actions, fee-switch/utility proposals, Spaces activity). Like most NFT-ecosystem tokens it suffered a deep drawdown through the 2022-2024 NFT bear market and remains thinly traded and highly volatile.
- **Secondary art liquidity** — collectors gain/lose exposure by holding 1/1 works; floor prices for blue-chip crypto artists (XCOPY, Pak) are the practical "market."

As of 2024-2026 the broader NFT market remains far below its 2021 peak, with marketplace volumes consolidated and royalty enforcement structurally weakened by trader-focused venues like [[blur|Blur]]. SuperRare continues to position as the high-end "gallery" tier rather than a volume venue, leaning on curation, Spaces, and artist relationships rather than competing on fees. Any durable recovery in $RARE and platform volume depends on a renewed art-NFT cycle.
