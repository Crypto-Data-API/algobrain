**IGO Limited** (ASX: IGO) is an other industrial metals & mining company listed on the [[asx-limited|Australian Securities Exchange]] and a constituent of the [[asx-200]] index. IGO Limited is an Australian mining and battery minerals company headquartered in Perth, Western Australia. The group discovers, develops, and delivers minerals that it identifies as critical to clean energy transition technologies, including lithium, nickel, copper, and cobalt.

## Business Model & Segments

IGO operates through the following segments:

- **Nova Operation:** 100% owned nickel-copper-cobalt underground mine and concentrator in the Fraser Range, Western Australia. Nova has been IGO's foundational base metals operation in recent years, with a planned mine closure in 2026.
- **Forrestania Operation:** Nickel sulphide mining and processing in Western Australia, acquired with Western Areas. Mining ceased and transitioned to care and maintenance in October 2024.
- **Cosmos Project:** A nickel development asset acquired with Western Areas in 2022, centred on the Odysseus Underground Mine.
- **Lithium Business:** IGO's 49% interest in Tianqi Lithium Energy Australia (TLEA), which holds a 51% interest in the Greenbushes Lithium Mine (the remaining 49% is held by Albemarle) and the Kwinana Lithium Hydroxide Refinery in Western Australia.
- **Growth:** Exploration and project evaluation across Australia (WA, NT, SA) and internationally (Greenland).

Revenue comes from the sale of nickel, copper, and cobalt concentrates from Nova, and equity-accounted share of profits from the TLEA joint venture (Greenbushes spodumene and Kwinana lithium hydroxide).

## Economic Moat & Cost Position

IGO is a commodity [[economic-moat|price-taker]] across both [[lithium]] and [[nickel]] — it cannot set prices for fungible battery minerals. Its competitive edge is therefore **cost-curve position and asset quality**, where it has an unusually strong hand on the lithium side and a weak one on nickel:

- **Greenbushes is the moat** — via TLEA, IGO owns 49% of the **world's lowest-cost hard-rock lithium mine**. First-quartile (often first-decile) cost position means Greenbushes stays cash-generative even when higher-cost spodumene mines (e.g. parts of [[liontown]]'s ramp) are underwater. This is genuine, durable asset-quality advantage — the strongest cost moat among ASX lithium names.
- **Nickel is the opposite** — IGO's Australian nickel-sulphide assets sit **high on the global cost curve**, undercut by the flood of low-cost Indonesian laterite/RKEF supply (the same supply that drove [[bhp|BHP]] to shutter Nickel West and underpins [[nickel-industries]]). Hence Forrestania to care-and-maintenance and Nova's scheduled closure.
- **Operational risk is real** — the Apr-2026 Greenbushes guidance cut (head-grade, recovery and reliability issues analysts called "systemic") showed that even a tier-one asset carries execution risk on top of price beta.
- **No control of the JV** — IGO holds a non-operating 49% economic interest; Albemarle/Tianqi influence operating decisions, limiting IGO's strategic control over its best asset.

Honest verdict: IGO's edge is owning a slice of the best lithium asset on earth; its weakness is legacy high-cost nickel it is exiting.

## Growth Drivers & Catalysts

- **Lithium price recovery** — IGO's earnings increasingly track Greenbushes spodumene realisations; a rising price flows through TLEA distributions.
- **Greenbushes CGP3 ramp** — the third chemical-grade plant adds ~500 ktpa concentrate capacity, lifting IGO's attributable volume.
- **Kwinana refinery** — any path to reliable Train 1 (and a Train 2 decision) would add downstream hydroxide value; conversely, further impairments are a risk.
- **Nova closure (~Dec 2026)** — simplifies the story into a near pure-play lithium holding company; removes a declining cash flow but also a diversifier.
- **Recurring catalysts** — quarterly activity reports (Jan/Apr/Jul/Oct), half-year (Feb) and full-year (Aug) results, spodumene/lithium-hydroxide price prints, Greenbushes operational updates.

## Bull Case vs Bear Case

### Bull case
- Owns 49% of Greenbushes — the lowest-cost hard-rock lithium mine globally, profitable through the cycle.
- Leveraged, lower-cost expression of a [[lithium]]-price recovery versus higher-cost single-asset peers.
- Net-cash balance sheet provides resilience through the trough.
- Lithium distributions fund the business while loss-making nickel is wound down.

### Bear case
- Becomes a near single-asset, single-commodity (lithium) holding company after Nova closes — concentration risk rises sharply.
- Non-operating 49% stake: no control over its best asset, and Greenbushes showed operational fragility in 2026.
- Heavy FY25 statutory loss (~A$955m) from impairments; nickel assets value-destructive at current prices.
- Fully exposed to the spodumene price with no diversifying commodity left.

## Key Risks

- **Commodity price** — both lithium and nickel are depressed/volatile; lithium is now the dominant earnings driver and risk.
- **Demand** — EV/battery demand (especially China) sets the lithium balance; a slowdown hits price and volume.
- **Operational (JV)** — Greenbushes grade/recovery/reliability issues (the "systemic" Apr-2026 guidance cut) and lack of operating control.
- **Concentration** — post-Nova single-asset, single-commodity exposure.
- **Permitting & closure** — Nova rehabilitation/closure costs; Kwinana impairment risk; WA approvals.
- **Capex / capital allocation** — Kwinana Train 2 and exploration spend during a low-price cycle.

## Valuation & How the Stock Trades

IGO's trailing P/E is **negative** (FY25 impairment-driven loss), so it is not an earnings-multiple story; it trades on the **through-cycle value of its lithium stake**:

- **Commodity beta** — primarily a [[lithium]]/spodumene-price proxy (with residual nickel sensitivity until Nova closes); high beta to battery-metals sentiment.
- **Distribution-driven** — equity-accounted TLEA profits and cash distributions, not consolidated revenue, drive the value — a sum-of-the-parts / look-through-NPV name rather than a P/E name.
- **Dividend** — historically a dividend payer (5y avg yield meaningfully positive), with payouts geared to lithium-JV distributions; expect variability with the cycle.
- **Single-stock risk** — the Apr-2026 ~14-18% one-day drop on the Greenbushes guidance cut (even as lithium prices roughly doubled off lows) shows IGO carries idiosyncratic operational risk on top of commodity beta.

## History

IGO was incorporated in 2000 as Independence Group NL, listing in Perth as a gold exploration and mining company. Over the subsequent two decades the company diversified and repositioned towards battery metals:

- **September 2015:** Acquired Sirius Resources NL in a transformational deal, gaining the Nova-Bollinger nickel-copper-cobalt project in the Fraser Range.
- **January 2020:** Renamed from Independence Group NL to IGO Limited, reflecting its broader strategy and brand.
- **December 2020:** Announced a binding agreement with Tianqi Lithium Corporation to acquire a 49% non-controlling interest in a new joint venture vehicle, providing indirect interests in Greenbushes and Kwinana. The transaction (~A$1.9 billion) completed in 2021 and reset the company as a battery minerals producer.
- **June 2022:** Completed acquisition of Western Areas, adding the Forrestania nickel operation and Cosmos development project.
- **2024-2025:** Faced a weaker nickel and lithium price environment; Forrestania placed into care and maintenance in October 2024; impairments recorded against Kwinana.

## Recent Developments

For the financial year ended 30 June 2025 (FY25), IGO reported a statutory net loss after tax of approximately A$955 million, a sharp reversal from a A$3 million net profit in FY24. The loss reflected impairments (including Kwinana) and depressed lithium and nickel prices. Notwithstanding, Greenbushes continued to perform strongly, maintaining an EBITDA margin of around 66% and retaining its position as the world's lowest-cost hard-rock lithium producer.

Operational highlights:

- **Greenbushes CGP3:** The third chemical grade processing plant began spodumene concentrate production, targeting approximately 500,000 tonnes per annum of additional capacity.
- **Nova:** In Q2 FY26, nickel production increased approximately 22% to ~5,107 tonnes and copper production rose ~21% to ~2,318 tonnes quarter-on-quarter, with cash costs reduced ~23% to A$3.97/lb through optimisation. Nova remains on track for planned closure in 2026.
- **Financial position:** Group cash at recent quarter end reported around A$299 million, with strong cash generation from the lithium joint venture distributions.

### FY2026 updates (to June 2026)

- **1H FY26 results (19 February 2026):** Strong Nova performance and improving Greenbushes margins as lithium prices recovered. Q2 FY26 (December quarter) slides showed Greenbushes achieving a ~64% EBITDA margin on realised spodumene prices of ~US$850/t, up 16% quarter-on-quarter.
- **24 April 2026 — Greenbushes guidance cut:** IGO slashed FY26 spodumene production guidance to 1,375-1,425 kt (from 1,500-1,650 kt), citing lower head grades, recovery issues, maintenance and plant-reliability problems described by analysts as "systemic". The shares fell ~14-18% on the day despite lithium prices roughly doubling off their lows — a reminder that IGO carries operational risk on top of commodity-price beta.
- **Balance sheet:** Net cash of ~A$327 million at 31 March 2026, supported by Nova free cash flow (~A$52 million in the March quarter) and Greenbushes distributions.
- **Nova closure:** On track to deliver unchanged FY26 guidance, with mine closure expected around December 2026 — after which IGO becomes an almost pure-play lithium (Greenbushes/TLEA) holding company.
