American Tower (NYSE: AMT) is the world's largest telecom-tower [[reits|REIT]], owning ~150,000 communications sites across the US, Latin America, Africa, Asia and Europe, plus the CoreSite US data-center business. An [[s-and-p-500]] constituent and one of the largest [[reits|REITs]] by market cap, it is a core vehicle for both the 5G-infrastructure and (via CoreSite) AI-interconnection themes in [[real-estate|Real Estate]].

## Business Overview & Segments

AMT leases vertical space on towers to wireless carriers under long-term, escalator-linked contracts — a high-margin, recurring-revenue model where each additional tenant on a tower is nearly pure incremental margin (hence the extreme reported EBITDA/operating margins in the fundamentals below, which are also inflated by the [[reits|REIT]]'s pass-through revenue accounting). US carriers ([[verizon]], [[at-and-t]], T-Mobile) anchor domestic revenue; international markets add growth but bring FX and carrier-consolidation churn. CoreSite (acquired 2021, ~US$10B) contributes US interconnection-dense data centers that have become the standout growth asset in the AI cycle.

| Segment / line | What it is | Role in the model (qualitative) |
|---|---|---|
| **US & Canada towers** | Macro towers leased to [[verizon]], [[at-and-t]], T-Mobile and others under 5–10yr non-cancellable leases with ~3% fixed escalators | Cash-flow anchor; the highest-margin, lowest-churn revenue and the bulk of NOI |
| **International towers** | Sites across Latin America, Africa, Europe and (formerly) India | Higher organic growth and tenancy upside, but FX translation and carrier-consolidation churn are the offset |
| **CoreSite (data centers)** | US interconnection-dense colocation campuses in major metros | Fastest-growing piece; the AI/interconnection optionality and the main destination for success-based capex |
| **Services / other** | Site development, construction, tower-management services | Small; supports the leasing flywheel rather than driving it |

The economic unit is the *co-location*: a tower built for one tenant becomes far more profitable as the second and third tenant are added, since the incremental cost of an extra carrier's antennas is minimal. Tower economics therefore reward density and tenancy ratios more than raw site count.

## Economic Moat & Competitive Position

American Tower's [[economic-moat|moat]] is a classic **location + contract** [[reits|REIT]] moat rather than a brand or technology edge:

- **Irreplaceable locations** — zoning, permitting and community opposition make new macro-tower sites genuinely hard to build, so the existing portfolio enjoys quasi-scarcity in dense corridors. Carriers need *that* tower, not a tower nearby.
- **Long-dated, escalating contracts** — multi-year leases with built-in ~3% annual escalators and high renewal rates create bond-like, inflation-linked cash flows and very high [[switching-costs]] (relocating a radio network is costly and disruptive).
- **Co-location operating leverage** — adding tenants to an existing tower is nearly pure incremental margin, so the most-tenanted portfolio compounds returns fastest. AMT's scale gives it the densest portfolio to lease against.
- **Scale & cost of capital** — as the largest global tower owner with investment-grade access to debt, AMT can fund builds and M&A more cheaply than sub-scale rivals.
- **CoreSite interconnection** — data-center "network effects" (the more networks and clouds present, the more valuable the campus) add a second, AI-levered moat layer.

The moat is **wide on the asset but narrow on pricing power against three giant customers**: US revenue concentrates in three carriers whose consolidation (Sprint/T-Mobile, DISH) directly removes tenants. International markets dilute customer concentration but add FX and credit risk.

## Growth Drivers & Catalysts

- **5G densification** — carrier network upgrades drive new amendments and co-locations on existing towers (high-margin organic growth).
- **CoreSite / AI interconnection** — data-center demand from AI and cloud is the fastest-growing line; success-based capex is being funnelled here.
- **Contractual escalators** — ~3% built-in annual rent escalators compound organically with no new capex.
- **Developed-market builds** — 700+ new European site builds planned for 2026; capital is being concentrated in lower-risk developed markets and CoreSite.
- **Rate relief** — as a long-duration, levered cash-flow vehicle, AMT is among the largest beneficiaries of a falling-rate regime (both lower refi costs and multiple expansion).
- **Recurring catalysts** — Q4/February guidance day (the most important print), carrier capex commentary, DISH churn milestones, FX moves (Africa/LatAm), and CoreSite/AI datacenter news.

## 2025–2026 Developments

- **FY2025 results (Feb 24, 2026):** revenue US$10,645M (+5.1%); Q4 revenue US$2,738M (+7.5%); FY attributable AFFO US$5,042M, with AFFO/share growth of 6.6% (as adjusted). Dividend growth resumed at mid-single digits.
- **CoreSite:** data-center revenue grew ~14% in 2025 on AI and interconnection demand — the fastest-growing piece of the portfolio, with success-based capex being funnelled to it.
- **2026 guidance ("the reset"):** headline AFFO/share growth of only ~1% — depressed by one-time DISH Network churn, FX and refinancing costs; normalized growth ~5%. Dividend growth guided ~5% (~US$3.3B of distributions).
- **Capital plan 2026:** US$1.9B capex (85% to developed markets and CoreSite, including 700+ new European site builds); continued exit/de-emphasis of higher-risk emerging markets after the 2023 India sale.
- **Stock context:** AMT traded down roughly 40%+ from its 2021 all-time high into 2026 — a long de-rating driven by higher rates and DISH/Sprint churn, leaving it a popular candidate in "quality REIT recovery" screens.

## Bull Case vs Bear Case

### Bull case
- Irreplaceable, contractually escalating tower cash flows with very high renewal rates — bond-like and inflation-linked.
- CoreSite gives AI/interconnection optionality on top of the tower base, growing double-digits.
- Among the most rate-sensitive large caps — a falling-rate regime drives both cheaper refinancing and multiple re-rating.
- Down ~40%+ from highs into 2026; a "quality REIT recovery" candidate if the DISH/FX drags wash through.
- Global scale and investment-grade balance sheet give a cost-of-capital edge over sub-scale tower owners.

### Bear case
- Extreme leverage (D/E ~9.7x, $40B+ debt) makes the equity a levered bet on rates — higher-for-longer is a direct headwind.
- US revenue concentrates in three carriers; consolidation (DISH, Sprint/T-Mobile) removes tenants and caps domestic growth.
- International growth carries FX translation risk and weaker carrier credit (Africa/LatAm).
- 2026's ~1% headline AFFO/share guide shows how quickly churn, FX and refi can swamp organic growth.
- Capex must keep flowing to CoreSite to sustain the growth story, pressuring free cash flow.

## Key Risks

- **Rate cycle** — long-duration, heavily levered cash flows make AMT trade like a levered bond; higher-for-longer pressures both refinancing cost and valuation.
- **Customer concentration & churn** — three US carriers dominate domestic revenue; carrier consolidation (DISH/Sprint) directly removes tenants.
- **FX & emerging-market credit** — Africa/LatAm currencies and weaker carriers add translation and collection risk.
- **Refinancing wall** — $40B+ of debt must be rolled across the rate cycle; covenant headroom matters in a downturn.
- **CoreSite competition** — must keep winning interconnection demand against [[equinix]] and [[digital-realty]] to justify the growth premium.

## Valuation & How the Stock Trades

As a [[reits|REIT]], AMT is valued on **AFFO per share** (adjusted funds from operations) and an implied **cap rate / NTA** basis, not GAAP EPS — the 72.8x P/E in the fundamentals below is meaningless because depreciation crushes reported net income while leaving cash flow intact. Mechanically:

- **AFFO multiple & growth** — the market pays a price/AFFO multiple keyed to AFFO/share growth; the 2026 "reset" to ~1% headline (vs ~5% normalized) growth is exactly why the stock de-rated.
- **Cap rate & NTA** — tower portfolios trade on the implied capitalization rate of their lease cash flows; falling rates compress cap rates and lift net tangible asset value, and vice versa.
- **Rate sensitivity / duration** — long-dated, escalating leases plus heavy leverage make AMT a long-duration instrument that moves inversely to the 10-year Treasury; it is a standard equity vehicle for Fed-cut views.
- **Dividend** — a growing, mid-single-digit dividend (~US$3.3B of distributions guided for 2026) covered on an AFFO basis; the dividend, not buybacks, is the capital-return story.
- **Beta** — moderate equity beta but extreme *rates* beta; the stock behaves like a levered bond with a growth kicker.
