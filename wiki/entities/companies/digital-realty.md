Digital Realty (NYSE: DLR) is one of the two dominant global data-center [[reits|REITs]] (alongside [[equinix]]), owning and operating 300+ data centers across ~25 countries. For traders it is a core [[real-estate|real-estate]] / REIT-format vehicle for the AI-infrastructure buildout — leasing colocation and wholesale capacity to hyperscalers and enterprises — whose stock trades on bookings/backlog momentum, core-FFO guidance, and the rate environment that governs all REITs. It is a constituent of the [[s-and-p-500]] index and a primary public-equity expression of the [[ai-data-center-power-demand]] theme.

## Business Overview & Segments

Digital Realty's portfolio spans two demand pools sold through its PlatformDIGITAL ecosystem: **0–1 MW colocation and interconnection** (enterprise, higher-margin, [[equinix]]-like, with stickier multi-tenant economics) and **>1 MW hyperscale/wholesale** capacity leased to cloud and AI players. Revenue is contractual rent with built-in escalators; growth comes from development (land + secured-power banks converted into leased megawatts), re-leasing spreads (cash mark-to-market), and capital recycling, funded by a mix of debt, asset/development JVs (notably with sovereign and infrastructure capital, e.g. partnerships involving Blackstone, GI Partners, and a hyperscale fund), and equity. The AI wave has shifted the binding constraint from demand to **power availability and grid interconnection**, making Digital Realty's secured-power land bank one of its most valuable assets.

| Segment / line | What it is | Role in the story (qualitative) |
|---|---|---|
| **Hyperscale / wholesale (>1 MW)** | Large turn-key capacity leased to cloud and AI players on long-term contracts | The growth engine — where AI/cloud bookings, the record backlog, and pre-leased construction sit |
| **Colocation & interconnection (0–1 MW)** | Multi-tenant enterprise capacity plus the ServiceFabric/PlatformDIGITAL interconnection layer | Higher-margin, stickier, lower-churn; the closest analogue to the [[equinix]] business |
| **Development pipeline / land bank** | Land + secured power + capacity under construction (a large share pre-leased) | The forward earnings stream; power-securing capability is the differentiating scarce input |
| **JV / capital-recycling platform** | Stabilised assets sold into JVs with infrastructure/sovereign capital | Funds development without over-issuing equity; recycles cap-rate gains into new builds |

As a REIT, Digital Realty pays out most taxable income, so the headline GAAP P/E (114.9x below) is **not** the right valuation lens; the market prices DLR on **core FFO per share** (2026 guidance $8.00–8.10) and on the visibility of the leasing backlog.

## Economic Moat & Competitive Position

Digital Realty's [[economic-moat|moat]] for the data-centre REIT model is built on **scale, location/power, and switching costs** rather than a single durable advantage:

- **Secured power and land** — in an era where the constraint is megawatts and grid interconnection, an entitled, power-secured land bank in key metros is a scarce, slow-to-replicate asset. This is the central moat input for any data-centre REIT.
- **Colocation switching costs and interconnection density** — the 0–1 MW/interconnection business carries genuine [[economic-moat|stickiness]]: once an enterprise's network and partners are cross-connected inside a campus, migrating is costly and risky (the same network-effect logic that protects [[equinix]]).
- **Hyperscale contract structure** — long-dated leases with creditworthy cloud counterparties and built-in escalators provide bond-like, inflation-linked cash flows; the watch item is the credit quality of newer "neocloud"/AI counterparties versus blue-chip hyperscalers.
- **Capital-markets access and scale** — global scale lowers cost of capital and supports a JV model that recycles capital into development, a self-reinforcing advantage over sub-scale operators.

The moat is real but **capital-intensive and not impregnable**: hyperscalers self-build, neoclouds and private capital are flooding into the sector, and the economics are sensitive to construction cost, power price, and the cost of capital.

## 2025–2026 Developments

- **FY2025:** record leasing driven by AI/hyperscale demand; full-year 2025 core FFO around $7.32/share with 2026 initially guided to $7.90–8.00.
- **Q1 2026 (reported April 2026):** core FFO **$2.04/share, up 15% y/y**; second-highest bookings quarter in company history — **$707 million of bookings at 100% share** ($423M at DLR share), including its **largest single lease ever**, record 0–1 MW + interconnection bookings, and 116 new logos.
- **Backlog at record $1.8 billion** ($1.0B at DLR share); **1.2 GW under construction, 61% pre-leased**.
- **Guidance raised:** 2026 core FFO lifted to **$8.00–8.10/share** (from $7.90–8.00).
- Key debates: AI lease durability/credit quality (neocloud counterparties), power constraints slowing delivery, and rate sensitivity of the REIT capital model.

## Growth Drivers & Catalysts

- **AI/cloud data-center super-cycle** — DLR revenue is effectively a derivative of hyperscaler capex ([[microsoft]], [[alphabet]], [[amazon]], [[meta]]) and the broader [[ai-data-center-power-demand]] theme.
- **Record backlog conversion** — a large pre-leased construction pipeline (1.2 GW under construction, ~61% pre-leased) converting to in-service rent over coming years gives unusual forward visibility.
- **Re-leasing / mark-to-market** — older leases re-pricing to current (tighter) market rents as power scarcity lifts pricing power.
- **Secured power as the scarce input** — ability to deliver megawatts on schedule is the binding growth lever; power-procurement headlines are share-price catalysts.
- **Capital recycling / JVs** — selling stabilised assets into infrastructure-capital JVs to fund development without diluting equity.
- **Recurring catalysts** — quarterly earnings (bookings, backlog, core-FFO guide), hyperscaler capex guidance, Fed/rates moves, JV announcements.

## Bull Case vs Bear Case

### Bull case
- Structural AI/cloud demand and power scarcity give DLR durable pricing power and a multi-year, highly visible backlog.
- Global scale, secured-power land bank, and interconnection density are hard to replicate; cost of capital advantage funds growth.
- Core-FFO growth (mid-teens recent quarters) plus re-leasing spreads compound NAV; rate cuts would re-rate the whole REIT complex.
- JV/capital-recycling model funds development while limiting equity dilution.

### Bear case
- Rate sensitivity: rising long-end yields raise the discount rate on long-duration cash flows and the cost of development debt — DLR can fall on good AI news if real yields spike.
- Supply/return compression: private capital and neoclouds flooding in could over-build and erode development yields.
- Counterparty credit: newer "neocloud"/AI tenants are less creditworthy than blue-chip hyperscalers; an AI-capex digestion phase would hit bookings and the multiple together.
- Capital intensity: the model needs continuous access to debt and equity; in risk-off regimes that access tightens.

## Key Risks

- **Rate / duration risk** — long-duration REIT cash flows are highly sensitive to long-end [[treasuries]] yields; see [[interest-rate-risk]].
- **Demand concentration & AI-capex cyclicality** — most growth rides on a handful of hyperscalers' capex; a synchronized pause would hit revenue and valuation simultaneously.
- **Counterparty / credit quality** — durability and credit of neocloud/AI lease counterparties.
- **Supply / cap-rate compression** — over-building and private-capital competition compressing development returns.
- **Power and construction execution** — grid-interconnection delays, power cost, and construction-cost inflation cap deliverable megawatts.
- **Capital-markets dependence** — reliance on debt/equity issuance to fund a capital-intensive development pipeline.

## Valuation & How the Stock Trades

Digital Realty is **not** valued on GAAP earnings — the 114.9x headline P/E reflects heavy REIT depreciation, not economics. Traders and investors price DLR on:

- **Core FFO / AFFO per share** — the REIT cash-earnings metric; 2026 core-FFO guidance of $8.00–8.10 is the number the stock trades against, with growth driven by backlog conversion and re-leasing.
- **NAV and implied cap rate** — the value of in-place leases plus the development pipeline, discounted at a market cap rate; falling cap rates (lower rates) lift NAV, rising cap rates compress it.
- **Rate sensitivity / duration** — as a long-duration cash-flow stream, DLR trades partly as a bond proxy: it can sell off with rising real yields even when AI demand news is positive, and re-rates hard on a "Fed cuts → REIT re-rate" trade.
- **Dividend** — pays a meaningful (if modest-yield) growing dividend, as REIT distribution rules require; total return is dividend plus FFO/NAV growth.
- **Behavior** — a liquid S&P 500 REIT with active options; trades as a hybrid of AI-infrastructure beta and rate-sensitive REIT duration. **Pair-trade candidate vs [[equinix]]** (DLR = more hyperscale/AI exposure; EQIX = more interconnection/enterprise). Correlated AI-capex names: [[vertiv]], [[super-micro-computer]]; inverse sensitivity to long-end Treasury yields.
