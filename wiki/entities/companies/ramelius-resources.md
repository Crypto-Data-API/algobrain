**Ramelius Resources Limited** (ASX: RMS) is an gold company listed on the [[asx-limited|Australian Securities Exchange]] and a constituent of the [[asx-200]] index. Ramelius Resources Limited (ASX: RMS) is a Western Australian gold producer that has been listed on the ASX since 2003 and in production since 2006. The company is headquartered at 58 Mounts Bay Road, Perth, Western Australia and has a market capitalisation of approximately A$7.6 billion.

For traders, Ramelius is a mid-tier, [[materials|Materials]]-sector pure-play [[gold]] miner — a high-beta, leveraged expression of the **Australian-dollar gold price**, operating a hub-and-spoke model in Western Australia and reshaped by the 2025 [[#Notable history & milestones|Spartan Resources acquisition]] that added the high-grade Dalgaranga/Never Never deposit and set a path toward a ~500koz/yr FY30 production target.

## Business Overview & Assets

Ramelius runs a **hub-and-spoke** operating model: a small number of central processing plants ("hubs") fed by ore from the host deposit plus nearby satellite pits, supplemented by a development and exploration pipeline that is meant to backfill mill capacity and extend mine life. The portfolio spans producing hubs, an asset in care-and-maintenance, a transformational growth deposit, and earlier-stage development projects.

| Asset / hub | Type / status | Role |
|---|---|---|
| **Mt Magnet hub** (+ Penny, Marda satellites) | Producing — core processing hub, Murchison Goldfield | Group cornerstone; mill fed by Mt Magnet plus satellite ore; planned expansion targeted to contribute ~350koz/yr toward the ~500koz FY30 goal |
| **Edna May** | Care-and-maintenance (from FY26) | Mothballed to redirect capital to higher-return projects; optionality to restart if grade/price warrant |
| **Dalgaranga / Never Never** (ex-Spartan) | Growth — high-grade development | Acquired via the ~A$2.4B Spartan Resources deal (2025); high-grade Never Never ore scheduled to enter the mill from the June-2026 quarter — the key near-term grade and volume uplift |
| **Rebecca / Roe** (Eastern Goldfields) | Development | Consolidated via the Apollo (2022) and Breaker (2023) takeovers; future hub/feed optionality, ~50 km apart |
| **Cue Gold Project** | Development | Earlier-stage growth feed in the Murchison |

The economics of a hub-and-spoke gold producer turn on keeping the central mills full with the **highest-grade ore available** — which is precisely why the Never Never deposit and the Mt Magnet expansion matter more to the medium-term story than the headline trailing earnings multiple.

## Economic Moat & Competitive Position

Ramelius has **no pricing power**: [[gold]] is a globally fungible commodity priced in deep, liquid markets, so every producer is a **price-taker** and sells at the prevailing spot price regardless of its own cost base. A gold miner therefore cannot build the kind of [[economic-moat]] that a branded or network-effect business enjoys. What *can* be durable is operational and balance-sheet advantage — the things that determine how much margin the company keeps at any given gold price:

- **Cost-curve position (AISC)** — the single most important competitive variable. A lower all-in sustaining cost means wider margins in good times and survival when the gold price falls; sitting low on the industry cost curve is the closest thing a price-taker has to a moat.
- **Grade & asset quality** — higher-grade ore (notably **Never Never** from the Spartan acquisition) lowers unit costs and lifts mill output per tonne processed; grade is the lever that most directly moves AISC.
- **Mill / processing capacity in a hub-and-spoke model** — owning central processing plants lets Ramelius toll and blend ore from multiple deposits, maximising plant utilisation and capturing satellite ounces that a single-mine operator could not.
- **Reserve base & mine life** — replacing depleted ounces (via exploration and M&A) is existential for a miner; a longer, well-defined reserve life de-risks the equity and supports the ~500koz FY30 ambition.
- **Balance-sheet strength** — low leverage and a strong cash position let Ramelius invest through the cycle, fund growth capital, and weather price downturns without distressed equity raises.

The competitive reality is that these advantages are **relative and erodable** — a sustained gold-price downturn compresses margins for everyone, and grade naturally declines as the best ore is mined first. The moat such as it exists is "stay low on the cost curve and keep replacing high-grade ounces," not any structural barrier to entry.

## Growth Drivers & Catalysts

- **Never Never (Dalgaranga) high-grade ore** — introduction of higher-grade Spartan ore into the mill from the **June-2026 quarter** is the key near-term driver of production and grade (and thus AISC).
- **Spartan integration synergies** — extracting operational and corporate synergies from the ~A$2.4B Spartan combination; integration execution is the swing factor on whether the deal delivers.
- **Path to ~500koz/yr by FY30** — the Mt Magnet expansion (targeted ~350koz/yr contribution) plus Never Never underpin the long-term production-growth target.
- **Exploration** — a lifted FY26 exploration budget (A$80–100M) aimed at reserve replacement and new feed for the hubs.
- **AUD gold price** — the dominant external driver; record AUD gold lifts margins directly given a largely AUD cost base.
- **Recurring catalysts** — quarterly production / AISC reports, half-year (February) and full-year (August) results, FY guidance, and exploration/reserve updates.

## Bull Case vs Bear Case

### Bull case
- The **Spartan / Never Never** acquisition delivers a genuine grade uplift, lowering AISC and lifting mill output from the June-2026 quarter.
- A credible **growth pipeline to ~500koz/yr by FY30** (Mt Magnet expansion + Never Never) gives the equity a production-growth story on top of gold-price beta.
- Strong **leverage to a record AUD gold price** — with a largely AUD cost base and USD-priced gold revenue, margins amplify favourable moves in both spot gold and AUD/USD; FY25 delivered record NPAT of A$474.2M (+119%) on revenue of A$1,203.4M from 302,882oz.
- A historically conservative balance sheet lets the company fund growth capital and exploration through the cycle.

### Bear case
- **Mine-sequencing and grade volatility** can swing earnings hard: H1 FY26 saw a swing to a ~A$11.7M loss as gold production fell 32%, largely on Edna May entering care-and-maintenance and lower mined grades — a reminder that even a strong gold price doesn't guarantee profits.
- **Edna May care-and-maintenance** removes a production source and concentrates output on fewer hubs near-term (FY26 guidance ~<200koz).
- **Integration execution** risk on the large Spartan deal — synergies and the Never Never ramp must be delivered to justify the scrip-and-cash consideration.
- **Single-commodity concentration** — 100% gold, so there is no diversification cushion if the gold price reverses.

## Key Risks

- **Gold-price reversal** — as a price-taker, a sustained fall in AUD gold compresses margins directly with no offset.
- **Grade & mine-sequencing volatility** — production and unit costs swing with which ore is being mined; grade naturally declines over a mine's life.
- **AISC / cost inflation** — labour, energy, consumables and contractor cost pressure can erode margins even at a flat gold price.
- **Spartan integration risk** — failure to capture synergies or a delayed Never Never ramp would undercut the acquisition thesis.
- **Reserve replacement** — every ounce mined must be replaced via exploration or M&A; failure shortens mine life and de-rates the equity.
- **Single-commodity concentration** — no by-product credits or commodity diversification.
- **Thin options liquidity** — limited single-name derivatives, so risk must be expressed through the equity itself.

## Valuation & How the Stock Trades

Ramelius trades as a **leveraged play on the AUD gold price**. With costs incurred largely in Australian dollars and revenue earned on USD-priced gold, the company's margins amplify moves in *both* spot gold and the AUD/USD exchange rate — the stock is a high-beta vehicle for expressing a bullish gold view on the ASX. Mechanically:

- **Highly cyclical earnings** — results are extremely sensitive to mine-sequencing and grade, not just the gold price. The FY25 record (NPAT A$474.2M, +119%) versus the H1 FY26 swing to a ~A$11.7M loss (production −32% as Edna May went to care-and-maintenance) shows how quickly the P&L can turn even with gold elevated; trailing earnings multiples are therefore a poor guide and through-cycle production, AISC and reserves matter more.
- **Thin single-name options liquidity** — derivatives on RMS are limited, so positioning is generally taken through the **equity** rather than options.
- **Modest dividend** — Ramelius pays a dividend (maintained through the H1 FY26 weakness) but the equity is owned for growth and gold leverage, not income; capital is being directed toward growth projects.
- **What moves it** — the AUD gold price, AUD/USD, quarterly production and AISC prints, exploration/reserve updates, and the Never Never ramp; it trades in sympathy with [[northern-star-resources]], [[evolution-mining]], [[regis-resources]] and [[bellevue-gold]] and with [[gold|gold spot]].

## Business Model & Segments

Ramelius owns and operates two production and processing hubs in Western Australia:

- Mt Magnet - located adjacent to the town of Mt Magnet, approximately 500 kilometres north-east of Perth in the Murchison Goldfield of the Western Australian Yilgarn Craton. The company reports three operating segments: Mt Magnet, Edna May and Exploration. The Mt Magnet hub processes ore from Mt Magnet itself and from satellite operations at Penny and Marda. A planned expansion of Mt Magnet is expected to contribute approximately 350,000 ounces per annum of the group's 500,000 oz FY30 target.
- Edna May - located within the Westonia Greenstone Belt in the Southern Cross Province of the Archaean Yilgarn Craton. Edna May's processing plant has historically processed ore from the Edna May mine and satellite operations. Edna May entered care and maintenance during FY26 to focus capital on higher-return projects.

Ramelius is also developing the Rebecca/Roe gold projects in the Eastern Goldfields, and the Cue Gold Project, and following the Spartan Resources acquisition in 2025 owns the Dalgaranga (Never Never) gold project.

## History

Ramelius Resources was incorporated in 1979, with the company's modern incarnation formed through early-2000s exploration and asset consolidation. It was publicly listed on the ASX in 2003. Ramelius's first major success was the Wattle Dam Gold Mine, a high-grade operation that established the company's operational credentials.

In 2010, Ramelius acquired Mt Magnet Gold Pty Ltd from Harmony Gold and recommenced open-pit mining in late 2011 with a reserve base of approximately 545,000 ounces. Over subsequent years the company consolidated its position in the Murchison region, acquired the Edna May project and progressively added satellite deposits such as Penny and Marda to its portfolio.

In January 2022, Ramelius completed the takeover of Apollo Consolidated Limited, gaining 100% ownership of the Lake Rebecca Gold Project (now the Rebecca Gold Project). In June 2023, the company completed a takeover of Breaker Resources NL, taking 100% ownership of the Lake Roe Gold Project (located only 50 km from Rebecca).

On 1 August 2025, Ramelius completed the scrip-and-cash acquisition of Spartan Resources for approximately A$2.4 billion. Spartan shareholders received 0.6957 Ramelius shares plus A$0.25 in cash per Spartan share. The transaction secured ownership of Spartan's Dalgaranga (Never Never) gold project in Western Australia, and Simon Lawson was appointed non-executive director and Deputy Chair of Ramelius, with Deanna Carpenter also joining the Board.

## Recent Developments

For FY25 (year ended 30 June 2025), Ramelius reported record revenue of A$1,203.4 million from the sale of 302,882 ounces of gold. Net profit after tax was a record A$474.2 million, up 119% from A$216.6 million in FY24.

For H1 FY26 (six months to 31 December 2025), Ramelius reported revenue down 4% to A$485.6 million. EBITDA halved and net profit swung to an A$11.7 million loss from a A$170.4 million profit in the prior comparable period. Gold production fell 32% to approximately 100,623-101,000 ounces, mainly due to Edna May entering care and maintenance and lower mined grades at operating sites. Ramelius maintained its dividend.

FY26 production guidance remains just below 200,000 ounces, with higher-grade ore from the Never Never deposit to be introduced in the June 2026 quarter. The company has lifted its FY26 exploration budget to A$80-100 million.

The most recent filing (annual) was lodged on 2023-06-30. See [[#Financial Reports]] below.

## Leadership

The wider executive team comprises leaders with backgrounds in gold mining, geology, processing and capital markets.
