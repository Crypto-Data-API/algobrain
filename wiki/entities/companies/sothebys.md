Sotheby's is one of the world's oldest and largest auction houses, founded in 1744 in London (now headquartered in New York). Sotheby's entered the [[nft|NFT]] market in 2021, notably selling CryptoPunk #7523 — the only "Alien" Punk with a mask — for $11.8 million in June 2021. The auction house subsequently launched Sotheby's Metaverse, a dedicated digital art and NFT platform, and has conducted ongoing NFT auctions alongside its traditional fine art, jewelry, and collectibles business.

## History

Sotheby's was founded in 1744 by Samuel Baker as a book auctioneer in London, making it the oldest auction house in the world still in operation. Over nearly three centuries, the company expanded into fine art, jewelry, wine, real estate, and virtually every category of high-value collectibles. Sotheby's is currently owned by BidFair USA, an entity controlled by French-Israeli telecommunications billionaire Patrick Drahi, who acquired the company in 2019 for $3.7 billion.

Sotheby's and [[christies|Christie's]] constitute the two dominant global auction houses, collectively handling the majority of the world's high-value art and collectibles transactions. Sotheby's annual sales typically range from $5-7 billion.

## Ownership and Trading Relevance

Sotheby's is **private** — it is owned by BidFair USA, controlled by French-Israeli telecoms billionaire Patrick Drahi, who took it private in 2019 for $3.7B. There is no public equity to trade; for market participants its relevance is as (a) a bellwether and price-discovery venue for **art and collectibles as an alternative asset class** ([[alternative-investments]]), and (b) a node in the [[nft|NFT]] / digital-art market.

### ADQ ($1B) investment — 2024

In August 2024, Abu Dhabi's sovereign wealth fund **ADQ agreed to acquire a minority stake in Sotheby's**, anchoring a total ~$1B capital injection (the bulk from ADQ, with Drahi adding capital and remaining majority owner). The deal, via newly issued shares, was designed to **deleverage** Sotheby's and fund growth/innovation — relevant context given the debt strain across Drahi's wider empire (Altice, reportedly carrying ~$60B of debt). The investment closed by end-2024 and signalled Gulf sovereign-capital appetite for the high-end art market.

### Art-market signal

Sotheby's annual sales typically run ~$5-7B. The high-end auction market softened through 2023-2025 alongside weaker top-end art prices and reduced discretionary spending, and the NFT segment contracted sharply from its 2021 peak. For traders, Sotheby's headline results and major-evening-sale outcomes serve as a sentiment read on ultra-high-net-worth risk appetite and on the luxury/collectibles cycle.

## Entry into NFTs

Sotheby's entered the NFT market in spring 2021, shortly after [[christies|Christie's]] made global headlines with the Beeple "Everydays" sale in March 2021. Sotheby's approach combined traditional auction prestige with crypto-native sensibilities.

### CryptoPunk #7523 Sale (June 2021)

The most significant early NFT sale at Sotheby's was CryptoPunk #7523, sold on June 10, 2021, for $11,754,000. Key details:

- **Collection**: [[cryptopunks|CryptoPunks]] by [[larva-labs|Larva Labs]]
- **Attributes**: One of only nine "Alien" Punks (rarest type) and the only Alien wearing a medical mask — a detail that gained cultural resonance during the COVID-19 pandemic
- **Sale context**: Part of Sotheby's "Natively Digital: A Curated NFT Sale"
- **Buyer**: Shalom Meckenzie, the largest shareholder of DraftKings
- **Significance**: Established CryptoPunks as investment-grade collectibles recognized by traditional auction institutions

### Bored Ape Yacht Club Auction (September 2021)

Sotheby's also conducted a high-profile auction of 101 [[bored-ape-yacht-club|Bored Ape Yacht Club]] NFTs in September 2021, selling the lot for $24.4 million. This sale further cemented the relationship between traditional auction houses and the NFT market.

## Sotheby's Metaverse

In October 2021, Sotheby's launched **Sotheby's Metaverse**, a dedicated platform for digital art and NFT sales. The platform offered:

- **Curated NFT auctions**: Dedicated NFT sales curated by Sotheby's specialists
- **Primary and secondary sales**: Both new drops and resales of existing NFTs
- **Cryptocurrency payments**: Accepted [[ethereum|ETH]], Bitcoin, and USDC for NFT purchases
- **Cross-category**: Generative art, PFP collections, digital photography, and other NFT categories
- **Traditional auction integration**: NFT lots alongside physical art in major evening sales

Sotheby's also created a notable presence in the metaverse itself, opening a virtual gallery in Decentraland — a blockchain-based virtual world.

## Digital Art Specialist Team

Sotheby's invested in building internal expertise around NFTs and digital art. Michael Bouhanna was appointed as the auction house's head of digital art sales, leading the curation and strategy for NFT offerings. The team worked to bridge the knowledge gap between traditional art collectors and the crypto-native NFT community.

## Ongoing NFT Activity

Following the initial high-profile sales, Sotheby's continued to auction NFTs across multiple categories:

- **Generative art**: Works from [[art-blocks|Art Blocks]] and other generative platforms
- **CryptoPunks and Bored Apes**: Continued sales of blue-chip NFT collections
- **Photography NFTs**: Digital photography and mixed-media works
- **Grails series**: Recurring curated NFT auction series featuring prominent crypto artists

The 2022-2023 NFT market downturn led to reduced sale volumes and some unsold lots, but Sotheby's maintained its NFT program as a long-term category commitment rather than a temporary offering.

## Significance for the NFT Market

Sotheby's NFT involvement had complementary effects to [[christies|Christie's]] entry:

1. **Dual validation**: Both major auction houses embracing NFTs provided stronger institutional legitimacy than either alone
2. **Collector access**: Sotheby's client base of ultra-high-net-worth traditional art collectors gained exposure to NFTs through a trusted intermediary
3. **Price benchmarks**: Auction house sales provided transparent price discovery for blue-chip NFTs
4. **Cultural integration**: Placing NFTs alongside Old Masters and contemporary art in major sales signaled NFTs' acceptance as a legitimate art form
5. **Infrastructure**: Sotheby's Metaverse created dedicated institutional-grade infrastructure for NFT sales
