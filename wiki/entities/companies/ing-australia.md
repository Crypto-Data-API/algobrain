ING Australia is the largest digital-only bank in Australia, with approximately 2 million customers. A subsidiary of ING Group (headquartered in Amsterdam, listed on Euronext Amsterdam), ING Australia pioneered fee-free, branchless banking in the Australian market — forcing the Big Four banks to reduce or eliminate many retail banking fees.

## Overview

ING launched in Australia in 1999 as "ING Direct" and disrupted the market by offering consistently high savings interest rates, no monthly account fees, and fee-free access to any ATM in Australia. The ING Orange Everyday transaction account and Savings Maximiser high-interest savings account became flagship products that attracted cost-conscious consumers away from the Big Four banks.

ING does not offer any share trading, brokerage, or margin lending services. It is a pure banking institution focused on savings, transactions, home loans, and personal loans. However, ING is highly relevant to investors as a cash management tool — its consistently high savings rates make it an ideal vehicle for holding the cash component of an investment portfolio, and its competitive home loan offset accounts are valuable for [[negative-gearing]] strategies.

## History

- **1999**: Launched in Australia as ING Direct — one of the first digital-only banks in the country
- **2000s**: Rapid customer growth driven by superior savings rates and zero-fee positioning. Ran memorable advertising campaigns ("Save your money!")
- **2008**: GFC — ING Direct benefited from flight to safety as customers moved deposits to ADIs covered by the government guarantee. Parent company ING Group received Dutch government bailout, but ING Direct Australia was unaffected
- **2013**: Launched the Orange Everyday transaction account — competed directly with Big Four transaction accounts
- **2017**: Rebranded from "ING Direct" to "ING" in Australia
- **2019**: Introduced fee-free international ATM withdrawals and no foreign transaction fees — made ING the default travel card for Australian travellers
- **2020**: Introduced tighter conditions on Savings Maximiser bonus interest (deposit $1,000+ per month, make 5+ card transactions) — generated customer frustration but still competitive
- **2024**: ~2 million Australian customers, consistently ranked among top savings rates in the market

## Products and Services

### Orange Everyday (Transaction Account)

ING's flagship transaction account:

- **No monthly fees**: $0 account keeping
- **Fee-free ATMs**: Use any ATM in Australia without fees (ING rebates the ATM owner's fee) — requires meeting monthly deposit and transaction conditions
- **No international transaction fees**: No currency conversion markup on overseas card transactions — one of the best travel cards in Australia
- **Apple Pay, Google Pay**: Full digital wallet support
- **Conditions for benefits**: Must deposit $1,000+ per month and make 5+ eligible transactions to unlock fee-free ATMs, international fee-free transactions, and Savings Maximiser bonus interest

### Savings Maximiser (High-Interest Savings)

ING's flagship savings product:

- **Among the highest savings rates in Australia**: Consistently competitive with the top savings rates in the market (typically 5%+ during high-rate environments)
- **Bonus interest conditions**: Must also hold an Orange Everyday account, deposit $1,000+ per month, make 5+ card transactions, and grow savings balance each month
- **No minimum balance**: Interest paid from $0
- **Interest calculated daily, paid monthly**
- **Variable rate**: Moves with the RBA cash rate

### Home Loans

Competitive home loans with features attractive to investors:

- **Offset account**: 100% offset — every dollar in the offset reduces interest on the home loan. Critical for [[negative-gearing]] strategies where maintaining maximum deductible interest is important
- **Competitive rates**: Consistently below Big Four standard variable rates
- **No ongoing fees on some products**
- **Online application**: Fully digital — no branch visits required

### Living Super

ING's [[superannuation]] product:

- **MySuper compliant**: Default investment option available
- **Multiple investment options**: Conservative to high-growth
- **Low fees**: Competitive with industry super funds
- **Integration**: Linked with ING banking for contribution management

### Other Products

- **Personal loans**: Competitive rates, fully online application
- **Insurance**: Home and contents insurance
- **Term deposits**: Available but rates are typically lower than the Savings Maximiser for short terms
- **Credit cards**: Orange One card — low rate or rewards options

## For Investors and Traders

ING is not an investable stock on the ASX — it is a subsidiary of ING Group, which is listed on Euronext Amsterdam (ticker: INGA). However, ING is highly relevant to Australian investors as a banking partner:

**Cash management**: ING's high savings rates make it one of the best options for holding the cash component of an investment portfolio. Rather than leaving cash in a low-interest Big Four savings account or a broker's cash management account, investors can park cash in an ING Savings Maximiser and earn a competitive return while maintaining liquidity.

**Offset accounts for property investors**: ING's 100% offset home loans are particularly valuable for [[negative-gearing]] strategies. By maintaining savings in an offset account rather than paying down the investment loan, investors preserve the full tax deductibility of their investment loan interest while effectively "earning" the home loan interest rate on their offset savings (tax-free, since offset interest is not income — it is a reduction in expense).

**Travel and international**: For investors who travel frequently or make international investments, ING's zero foreign transaction fees and fee-free international ATM withdrawals are a significant cost advantage over Big Four cards.

**Limitations**: No share trading, no brokerage, no margin lending. ING is complementary to, not a replacement for, an investment platform like [[commsec]], [[selfwealth]], or [[nabtrade]].
