Bit Digital Inc. (NASDAQ: BTBT) is a New York-based digital-asset and AI-infrastructure company that has transformed from a Bitcoin miner into an **Ethereum treasury and staking** company plus an owner of **AI/HPC data-center capacity** (via its majority stake in WhiteFiber). It now brands itself a "Strategic Asset Company." For traders it is a small-cap that offers leveraged exposure to both ETH price/staking yields and the AI data-center buildout — a dual-narrative, high-volatility name.

## Business Overview

Bit Digital pivoted away from pure Bitcoin mining: it converted its remaining BTC into ETH and built a large **Ethereum treasury** that it natively stakes for yield, while building/operating **high-performance computing (HPC) and AI data centers** through **WhiteFiber, Inc. (NASDAQ: WYFI)**, in which it holds a majority equity interest. Revenue therefore comes from (a) ETH staking rewards and treasury value, and (b) AI/HPC colocation and cloud infrastructure via WhiteFiber. This makes BTBT effectively a hybrid ETH-treasury / AI-infrastructure vehicle rather than a conventional miner.

## Business Segments / Products

Bit Digital is unusual in the cohort: instead of pivoting *bitcoin-mining* power to AI, it pivoted its *balance sheet* to ETH and houses its AI infrastructure in a separately listed subsidiary.

**The ETH-treasury model.** Rather than holding cash or BTC, Bit Digital holds ETH and natively stakes the majority of it, earning protocol rewards (a yield-bearing crypto treasury). This makes the equity a leveraged, yield-bearing proxy on [[ethereum]] and on [[ethereum-staking]] economics — distinct from the BTC-mining cohort. (See [[ethereum-staking]].)

**WhiteFiber and AI/HPC.** Bit Digital's AI exposure is held through **[[whitefiber]] (WYFI)**, a majority-owned subsidiary that IPO'd in August 2025. WhiteFiber operates HPC/AI data-center capacity (colocation and cloud). Because WYFI trades separately, Bit Digital's AI value is partly mark-to-market visible, and Bit Digital can finance WhiteFiber growth (e.g., the delayed-draw term loan facility) while retaining control. (See [[ai-data-centers]].)

## Bull vs Bear Case

**Bull case**
- ETH treasury + native staking = yield-bearing, leveraged [[ethereum]] exposure rather than idle cash/BTC.
- WhiteFiber (WYFI) crystallizes AI-infrastructure value in a separately traded vehicle Bit Digital controls.
- Dual narrative (ETH + AI data centers) gives two independent re-rate vectors.
- Can finance WhiteFiber growth (e.g., delayed-draw term loan) while keeping control.

**Bear case**
- High ETH price dependence — an ether drawdown hits the treasury and sentiment hard.
- Staking carries slashing, validator, and protocol/regulatory risk (staking has drawn regulatory scrutiny in the US).
- AI value depends on WhiteFiber execution and WYFI's own equity performance.
- Small-cap, volatile; dilution risk from capital raises.
- "Strategic Asset Company" framing means the equity is partly a crypto-and-subsidiary holding company — valuation can disconnect from operations.

## Valuation Framework

Bit Digital is best valued sum-of-the-parts rather than on earnings:

- **ETH treasury value and % staked** — the dominant asset; mark-to-market on ETH holdings.
- **Staking yield / annualized staking revenue** — recurring crypto-native income (see [[ethereum-staking]]).
- **Stake in [[whitefiber]] (WYFI)** — market value of the majority equity interest; the AI-infrastructure component.
- **NAV vs. market cap** — does BTBT trade at a premium/discount to (ETH treasury + WYFI stake)?
- **Share count and dilution** — equity-raise cadence.
- **WhiteFiber energized MW / contract backlog** — AI growth runway.

The key analytical move is decomposing BTBT into its ETH treasury plus its WYFI stake, then judging whether the blended equity is cheap or rich versus that sum.

## Capital Structure & Dilution Risk

Bit Digital's balance sheet is dominated by its ETH treasury, which doubles as a financing buffer and a source of volatility. The company has financed WhiteFiber growth via debt (e.g., a ~$100M delayed-draw term loan facility for a WhiteFiber subsidiary), which funds AI expansion without immediately diluting parent shareholders. Like peers, however, it has historically used equity issuance (ATM, secondary offerings) to grow the ETH treasury and operations. Watch share count, ETH-treasury monetization/staking, WhiteFiber financing structure, and any parent-level raises. (See dilution.)

## Risks

- **ETH price risk** — treasury value and sentiment are tied to [[ethereum]].
- **Staking risk** — slashing, validator failure, protocol changes, and US regulatory scrutiny of staking.
- **WhiteFiber execution risk** — AI value depends on WYFI delivering capacity and revenue.
- **Holding-company discount** — blended equity can trade below NAV.
- **Dilution / financing risk** — capital raises to grow treasury and AI (see dilution).
- **Regulatory risk** — digital-asset and staking regulation in the US and abroad.
