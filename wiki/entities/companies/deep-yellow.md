**Deep Yellow Limited** (ASX: DYL) is an Australian-listed [[uranium]] development and exploration company and a constituent of the [[asx-200]] index. Its strategy is to build a multi-asset uranium production platform centred on the **Tumas Project** in Namibia, supported by the fully-permitted **Mulga Rock Project** in Western Australia and ongoing exploration. Deep Yellow is **pre-revenue**: it generates no uranium income today, and a Final Investment Decision (FID) on Tumas has been held back as explicitly **price-contingent**. For a trader, the equity is best understood as a leveraged **option on Tumas being sanctioned** — a high-beta proxy on the long-term [[uranium]] contract price plus single-project execution risk, run by an experienced uranium team with [[paladin-energy|Paladin]] heritage.

## Business Overview & Assets

Deep Yellow's portfolio is organised into four reporting segments, all oriented around bringing a multi-asset [[uranium]] platform into production rather than around current cash flow:

- **Tumas Project (Namibia):** The company's flagship, a 95%-owned palaeochannel-style uranium deposit in Namibia's Erongo Region, covering approximately 125 square kilometres. Tumas is a Definitive Feasibility Study (DFS)-stage project targeting a steady-state output of approximately 3.6 million pounds U3O8 per annum over a mine life in excess of 30 years. It is effectively construction-ready, with FID gated on the long-term contract price (see [[#Tumas status (as of June 2026)]]).
- **Mulga Rock Project (Western Australia):** A fully permitted uranium project located 240 km east-north-east of Kalgoorlie (added via the 2022 Vimy Resources acquisition), with development timing dependent on uranium market conditions. It is the second pillar of the dual-asset strategy and a source of growth optionality once Tumas is sanctioned.
- **Exploration:** Greenfield and brownfield uranium exploration activity, principally across Namibia and Australia — including the S Bend prospect near Tumas, which expanded the resource footprint in early 2026.
- **Other Activities:** Property investment and trustee of share-trust activities.

The company does not yet generate uranium revenue; its financial position is supported by equity capital, partner contributions and project investment.

## Economic Moat & Competitive Position

Deep Yellow is — and once in production will remain — a **price-taker** on the [[uranium]] term price. It has **no moat in the pricing sense**: its revenue, when it arrives, will be set by global uranium contract pricing, not by anything the company controls. This is made explicit by management's stance that it will sanction Tumas only when the **long-term contract price** supports the economics (company modelling assumes ~US$82.50/lb U3O8 — see [[#Tumas status (as of June 2026)]]), not on spot-price strength. Compared with the very widest-moat businesses (see [[economic-moat]]), the only durable edges Deep Yellow can claim are:

- **A multi-asset development platform** — the "dual-pillar" strategy pairs Tumas (Namibia) with the fully-permitted Mulga Rock (WA), giving more than one shot at production and growth optionality once the cycle turns. Most peers are single-asset.
- **An experienced uranium team** — the business was rebuilt by John Borshoff (founder of [[paladin-energy|Paladin]]) and his Africa/Australia uranium specialists; project delivery is now led by Greg Field (ex-[[rio-tinto|Rio Tinto]], delivered the US$7bn Oyu Tolgoi underground build).
- **A relatively favourable jurisdiction** — Namibia is an established, Tier-1-ish uranium-mining country (home to large operating mines), reducing political and permitting risk versus frontier locations.

Crucially, Tumas sits **mid-cost-curve**, not bottom: company modelling implies an AISC near **US$45/lb**, well above the lowest-cost incumbents such as Kazatomprom or [[cameco]]'s Athabasca tier-1 mines. That means Deep Yellow needs a structurally healthier uranium price to clear its hurdle rate than the cheapest producers do — its competitive position is one of leverage to a rising price, not of low-cost resilience in a downturn.

## Growth Drivers & Catalysts

- **The Tumas FID** — the single defining, binary event for the equity: sanction de-risks the story and unlocks construction; further deferral keeps it an option.
- **The long-term [[uranium]] contract price** — the dominant external driver; Tumas economics are gated on term pricing reaching/holding management's ~US$82.50/lb assumption.
- **Offtake agreements** — utility contracts at supportive prices both fund and de-risk FID.
- **Construction funding** — a ~US$474M initial capex implies a substantial equity/debt raise around or after FID (a dilution catalyst, see Risks).
- **Greg Field's delivery roadmap** — the new MD/CEO's plan and any sequencing of Tumas → Mulga Rock.
- **Mulga Rock advancing** — second-pillar progress adds growth optionality and reduces single-asset concentration.
- **Namibian permitting / fiscal news** — royalty, water and approvals updates in-country.
- **Uranium demand backdrop** — nuclear-fleet restarts and life extensions, new-build and small modular reactor (SMR) programmes, and a widely-flagged structural supply deficit underpin the long-run thesis that lifts term prices.

## History

Deep Yellow was incorporated in 1985 and has long been focused on uranium exploration and development. The company was transformed following the 2018/2019 arrival of John Borshoff (former founder of [[paladin-energy|Paladin Resources]]) and his Australia/Namibia-focused uranium team, who reorganised the business around a "dual-pillar" strategy: the Namibian assets and, subsequently, the acquisition of Vimy Resources in 2022, which added the Mulga Rock Project and other Western Australian uranium assets.

## Recent Developments

In early 2026, Deep Yellow confirmed uranium mineralisation at the S Bend prospect near Tumas, expanding the project's resource footprint. Managing Director and CEO John Borshoff stepped down immediately, and Acting CEO Craig Barnes resumed his role as CFO. Deep Yellow appointed Greg Field – a qualified mining engineer and former Managing Director – Project Development at Rio Tinto – as the new Managing Director and CEO, with a start date no later than 1 May 2026. Field's project delivery experience includes Rio Tinto's US$7 billion Oyu Tolgoi underground copper-gold project. The Tumas Final Investment Decision (FID), previously targeted for March 2025, was deferred in April 2025 pending improved market conditions.

The most recent filing (annual) was lodged on 2023-06-30. See [[#Financial Reports]] below.

### Tumas status (as of June 2026)

Tumas is effectively construction-ready while the Final Investment Decision remains **price-contingent**: by end-March 2026 bulk earthworks were 91% complete, detailed engineering 68% advanced, and tendering finalized for 79% of major process-plant equipment packages. Company modelling at a long-term contract price of US$82.50/lb U3O8 shows post-tax NPV8 of ~US$577M, IRR ~19%, initial capex ~US$474M and AISC near US$45/lb. Management has stated it will sanction construction only when long-term contract pricing supports the economics — not on spot-price strength. First production remains targeted roughly two years after FID.

## Bull Case vs Bear Case

### Bull case
- Tumas is **construction-ready** (bulk earthworks 91% complete, detailed engineering well advanced, most major equipment packages tendered) — the project is de-risked on everything except the price-gated FID.
- Robust modelled economics at the assumed long-term price (post-tax NPV8 ~US$577M, IRR ~19%, >30-yr mine life) give meaningful upside if the [[uranium]] term price holds or rises.
- A genuine **multi-asset platform** — Mulga Rock adds a second, fully-permitted production pillar and growth optionality few single-asset developers have.
- An **experienced delivery team** (Borshoff/[[paladin-energy|Paladin]] heritage, now Greg Field ex-[[rio-tinto|Rio Tinto]]) lowers first-build execution risk relative to a typical junior.
- Leverage to a widely-flagged structural [[uranium]] supply deficit and rising nuclear/SMR demand.

### Bear case
- The equity is an **option on a deferred FID** — already pushed back from March 2025 — with no revenue and ongoing cash burn until then.
- **Mid-cost-curve** economics (AISC ~US$45/lb) mean Tumas needs a healthier price than the lowest-cost incumbents ([[cameco]], Kazatomprom) to clear its hurdle; a weak uranium regime keeps it un-sanctioned indefinitely.
- **Dilution risk** — funding a ~US$474M build implies a substantial equity/debt raise at or after FID.
- **Single-project concentration** until Mulga Rock advances.
- **Leadership transition** risk after founder John Borshoff's early-2026 exit, despite Field's strong CV.
- Pure first-time-builder execution and capital-cost-inflation risk on a multi-hundred-million-dollar greenfield in Namibia.

## Key Risks

- **FID slippage** — the defining risk; already deferred once (from March 2025) and explicitly tied to the long-term contract price.
- **Uranium-price regime turn** — a soft term price keeps Tumas un-economic and the stock as a wasting option.
- **Dilution at FID** — construction funding of ~US$474M implies equity issuance that can pressure the share price.
- **Single-asset concentration** — value is dominated by Tumas until Mulga Rock is developed.
- **Execution / capital-cost inflation** — first-time builder risk on a greenfield project; cost overruns erode the modelled NPV/IRR.
- **Leadership transition** — founder Borshoff's exit and the handover to Greg Field (started by 1 May 2026).
- **Jurisdiction / fiscal** — Namibian royalty, water-supply and permitting developments.

## Valuation & How the Stock Trades

Deep Yellow is **pre-revenue**, so conventional earnings multiples are meaningless — the ~199x trailing P/E in the data block reflects a near-zero/erratic accounting result, not a valuation signal, and should be ignored. The stock instead trades as a **leveraged proxy on the long-term [[uranium]] price**, with the share price functioning as an **option on the Tumas FID**: it re-rates on anything that raises the probability and value of sanction (term-price strength, offtake, funding clarity) and de-rates on deferral or dilution fear. Mechanically:

- **High beta to the uranium tape** — multi-percent daily moves with the uranium spot/term complex are routine; size positions for volatility.
- **No meaningful single-stock options market** on the ASX line, so exposure/hedging is share-only (contrast with [[nexgen-energy]]'s US-listed options).
- **No dividend** — all capital is directed at advancing Tumas; total return is pure price appreciation (or loss).
- **Valuation anchors are project NPV, not multiples** — the market discounts the modelled post-tax NPV8 (~US$577M at ~US$82.50/lb) by the probability and timing of FID, less the dilution required to fund the ~US$474M build (see [[#Tumas status (as of June 2026)]]).
