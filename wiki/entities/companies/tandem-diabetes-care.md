Tandem Diabetes Care, Inc. (NASDAQ: TNDM) is a San Diego-based medical-device maker specialising in automated insulin delivery (AID) systems for people with diabetes. Its core products — the t:slim X2 and the compact tubeless-capable Tandem Mobi pumps, paired with the Control-IQ+ algorithm that automatically adjusts insulin using continuous-glucose-monitor (CGM) data — put it in direct competition with Insulet (Omnipod) and Medtronic. For traders it is a small/mid-cap medtech growth name whose stock is highly sensitive to pump-shipment volumes, new-product launches, and reimbursement-model shifts.

## Business Overview

Tandem's revenue comes from insulin-pump hardware sales plus a recurring stream of disposable supplies (cartridges, infusion sets) and software. The competitive moat is its Control-IQ algorithm and integration with multiple CGMs (Dexcom, Abbott), giving patients an automated "hybrid closed-loop" system. The growth thesis rests on (1) expanding the addressable market by converting multiple-daily-injection users to pumps, (2) the smaller, more discreet Mobi platform broadening appeal, and (3) international expansion. The 2026 transition to a US pharmacy-channel "pay-as-you-go" reimbursement model is a major structural change — better for long-run access but a near-term revenue headwind.

## AI Involvement

Tandem's Control-IQ+ technology is an algorithm-driven automated insulin-delivery system that ingests real-time CGM data and predicts/adjusts insulin dosing to keep glucose in range — a clinically validated example of closed-loop AI/ML in regulated medical devices, central to the AI-MedTech investment theme.

## 2025-2026 Developments

- **FY2025 results (reported 19 Feb 2026):** annual sales surpassed $1 billion, up ~12% worldwide; Tandem said it plans to launch Mobi Tubeless in 2H 2026. (Verified via Perplexity, sonar)
- **Q1 2026 results (reported 7 May 2026):** sales of $247.2 million, worldwide pump shipments above 29,000 (US ~19,000), gross margin ~55%; the company launched a pay-as-you-go reimbursement model in the US pharmacy channel. (Verified via Perplexity, sonar)
- **2026 guidance:** sales of $1.065-$1.085 billion with adjusted EBITDA margin of 5-6%; management flagged ~$70-80 million of near-term 2026 revenue headwind from the reimbursement-model shift plus ~$15 million from international transition/destocking. (Verified via Perplexity, sonar)

## Competitive Positioning / Peers

Tandem competes in automated insulin delivery and the broader diabetes-tech ecosystem, where pump makers and CGM makers are partly competitors, partly partners.

- **Versus [[insulet]]:** Insulet's tubeless Omnipod and earlier pharmacy-channel adoption are the main competitive pressure; Tandem's pay-as-you-go shift is partly a response to that distribution model.
- **Versus [[medtronic]]:** Medtronic is vertically integrated (own pump + CGM) and far larger, but historically slower on UX; Tandem differentiates on multi-CGM openness and form factor.
- **Versus [[dexcom]]:** Dexcom is an enabling partner (Control-IQ+ ingests CGM data); CGM-partner integration is both a strength and a dependency.

## Bull vs Bear Case

**Bull case**
- Crossed $1B in annual sales (per page) with a recurring disposables annuity behind a growing pump install base.
- Control-IQ+ is a clinically validated closed-loop algorithm — a genuine software moat in a regulated category.
- Mobi (and Mobi Tubeless, 2H 2026 per page) addresses the form-factor gap versus Omnipod and expands the addressable user base.
- Large structural tailwind: converting injection users to pumps; international expansion.
- Pharmacy-channel/pay-as-you-go shift improves long-run access and recurring revenue mix.

**Bear case**
- Near-term revenue headwinds from the reimbursement transition (~$70-80M) and international destocking (~$15M) (per page) muddy growth optics.
- Intense competition from [[insulet]] (tubeless/pharmacy lead) and [[medtronic]] (integration/scale).
- Thin adjusted-EBITDA margin (5-6% guide, per page) — execution-sensitive profitability.
- Dependency on CGM partners (Dexcom, Abbott) for the closed-loop value proposition.
- Small/mid-cap volatility; large earnings gaps on guidance.

## Valuation Framework

As a commercial medtech, Tandem is valued on growth, margins, and device-cycle dynamics rather than pipeline rNPV:

- **Revenue growth & pump shipments** — pump-shipment counts are the leading indicator; install-base growth drives the disposables annuity.
- **Margin trajectory** — gross margin (~55% per page) and the path to higher adjusted-EBITDA margin (5-6% guide, per page) determine operating leverage.
- **TAM / penetration** — share of diabetes patients on AID pumps; injection-to-pump conversion is the long-run expansion lever.
- **Device/product cycle** — new-platform launches (Mobi, Mobi Tubeless) drive replacement and new-patient demand; mid-cycle lulls pressure shipments.
- **Recurring mix** — disposables/software provide visibility and support a higher-quality multiple than pure hardware.
- **EV/Sales & EV/EBITDA** — the relevant multiples for a growth medtech approaching scaled profitability.
