Matterport, Inc. (formerly NASDAQ: MTTR) was a 3D spatial-data and "digital twin" platform for real estate, using [[artificial-intelligence|AI]] to turn camera/LiDAR scans into navigable 3D property models. **It no longer trades: CoStar Group completed its acquisition of Matterport on 2025-02-28 and the MTTR ticker was retired.** Exposure to the business is now only available via [[costar-group]] (NASDAQ: CSGP). This is a **completed-acquisition case study**, written in the past tense; MTTR is delisted and deregistered.

## What the Business Was

Matterport was a spatial-data and "digital twin" software/hardware company: it captured physical spaces via cameras and LiDAR and used [[artificial-intelligence|AI]] to convert scans into navigable, measurable 3D models for real estate, AEC (architecture/engineering/construction), insurance, and facilities use cases.

| Business segment / product | What it was |
|---|---|
| Digital-twin capture | Camera/LiDAR scanning that generated navigable 3D models of properties |
| Spatial AI / Cortex | AI that auto-generated floor plans, measurements, and spatial analytics from scans |
| SaaS subscriptions | Recurring hosting/management of spaces — the core revenue model |
| Capture hardware & app | First-party Pro cameras plus a smartphone capture app broadening the funnel |

## Acquisition by CoStar Group (2024–2025) — The Deal

- **2024-04**: CoStar Group announced an agreement to acquire Matterport at an enterprise value of roughly **US$1.6B** — a large premium to the then-distressed SPAC-era share price.
- **2025-02-28**: Deal closed. Each Matterport Class A share converted into **0.03552 CoStar shares + US$2.75 cash**; total consideration ~**US$901M cash plus ~11.64M CoStar shares**.
- Trading in MTTR was halted on Nasdaq at close; the company was delisted and deregistered.
- Strategic rationale: combining Matterport's digital-twin capture technology with CoStar's real-estate data and marketplaces (Homes.com, Apartments.com, LoopNet).

## Competitive Positioning at the Time

Matterport was the category leader in real-estate digital twins but a post-SPAC small cap with heavy losses, competing against proptech and spatial-data peers and increasingly against generic 3D/AI capture.

## Merger-Arb / Trading Postmortem

The 2024 takeover was a classic **cash-and-stock merger-arb** setup (see [[merger-arbitrage]]):

- Mixed consideration meant the arb hedge was **long MTTR / short 0.03552 CSGP**, with the cash component ($2.75) providing a fixed floor and the stock leg carrying CSGP beta.
- With a ~10-month pendency, the dominant spread drivers were **regulatory-timeline risk** and short-rate carry on the position, plus CSGP price moves on the stock portion.
- Trading was halted on Nasdaq at close (2025-02-28) and the company deregistered — a hard, dateable terminal event for the arb.

## Trading Lessons / History

- Matterport went public via SPAC (Gores Holdings VI) in July 2021 near the SPAC-mania peak, briefly trading above $30; it subsequently fell >90% as growth slowed and SPAC-era projections proved unattainable — a case study in [[spac]] de-rating.
- The 2024 takeover bid produced a classic **merger-arbitrage** setup (part cash, part stock, with regulatory timeline risk — the deal took ~10 months to close).

## Lessons / Why It Was Acquired

- **SPAC de-rating made it cheap**: a >90% drawdown from the SPAC peak left a category-leading asset available far below its de-SPAC valuation — a strategic buyer bought the technology, not the hype.
- **Capture layer for a data giant**: CoStar wanted the immersive-capture front end to defend and differentiate its marketplaces; the value was strategic fit, not standalone profitability.
- **Small-cap, unprofitable, post-hype**: the combination of leadership-in-category + chronic losses + depressed multiple is a recurring profile for proptech/SPAC takeouts.

## Risks That Applied (historical)

- **SPAC-era projection risk**: the original de-SPAC growth assumptions proved unattainable, a primary cause of the de-rate.
- **Persistent operating losses / cash burn**: the standalone business was not self-funding, raising going-concern-style risk absent a deal.
- **Acquirer-beta risk (during pendency)**: the stock portion of consideration exposed holders to CSGP's price into the 2025-02-28 close.
- **Regulatory-timeline risk**: the ~10-month close window left an extended period of carry and break exposure for arbs.

## AI Involvement

AI-powered 3D spatial data and digital twins platform — property capture, automated floor plans, and spatial analytics; now being integrated into CoStar's products.
