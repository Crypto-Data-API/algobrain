Prologis (NYSE: PLD) is the world's largest industrial/logistics [[reits|REIT]], owning and developing roughly 1.2+ billion square feet of warehouses leased to e-commerce, retail and 3PL tenants ([[amazon]], FedEx, DHL among the largest). For traders it is the bellwether for global logistics [[real-estate|real estate]] — a rate-sensitive large cap that doubles as an e-commerce volume proxy, and increasingly a data-center development story. A [[s-and-p-500]] constituent and the single largest holding in most REIT ETFs, PLD effectively *is* the industrial-real-estate trade.

## Business Overview & Segments

Prologis owns logistics facilities across the Americas, Europe and Asia, concentrated in high-barrier consumption markets near ports and population centres. The franchise is more than a rent-collector: it pairs an enormous owned portfolio with one of the largest private development platforms in real estate, a fee-earning third-party capital business, and a growing energy/services layer. Revenue is driven by long leases carrying embedded mark-to-market upside (in-place rents sit well below market), development gains, promote fees from co-investment vehicles, and Essentials services. Since 2024–2025 it has been redeploying its land bank and secured power capacity into **data-center development**, selling or stabilising projects into AI-driven demand.

| Segment / line | What it is | Revenue mix (qualitative) |
|---|---|---|
| **Real Estate / Rental** | Core in-place rent from the owned logistics portfolio; long leases with embedded mark-to-market upside as in-place rents trail market | Dominant — the recurring engine of net operating income and FFO |
| **Development** | Build-to-suit and speculative ground-up logistics (and now data-center) projects, monetised by stabilising or selling at a development margin | Large and cyclical; the swing factor on FFO growth and value creation |
| **Strategic Capital** | Co-investment vehicles (open-end funds, JVs) where Prologis manages third-party capital for asset-management and **promote fees** | High-margin, capital-light, fee-based — smooths and amplifies returns |
| **Essentials** | Solar, EV charging, energy storage and operations services layered onto rooftops/sites | Small today but a strategic, growing ancillary revenue pool |
| **Data Center development** | Emerging build-to-suit data-center projects using the existing land bank and secured power capacity | Small but the highest-optionality growth vector; AI-driven demand |

The durable engine is the **land bank + entitlements + secured power** feeding development, fed back through the strategic-capital flywheel — Prologis develops, then sells or seeds assets into managed vehicles, recycling capital and earning fees on the way.

> **Note on REIT accounting:** REITs are conventionally valued on **FFO**, not EPS — the headline 107.7x PE reflects depreciation-heavy REIT accounting and the API's margin definitions (net margin >100% reflects JV/promote and gain-on-sale income), not operating economics. See [[#Valuation & How the Stock Trades]] and the [[#Fundamentals]] caveats.

## Economic Moat & Competitive Position

Prologis holds one of the widest [[economic-moat|economic moats]] among [[reits|REITs]], built on reinforcing layers rather than a single advantage:

- **Irreplaceable infill land** — its portfolio is concentrated on supply-constrained land near ports and dense population centres (the "last touch" of the supply chain), where high barriers to entry mean new competing supply is hard to build. This scarcity underpins pricing power.
- **Global scale & network** — the largest logistics footprint on earth gives data, relationships and operating leverage no peer can match; a single tenant can be served across continents.
- **Below-market in-place rents** — years of leasing below current market mean a large embedded **mark-to-market** that converts to organic rent growth as leases roll, largely independent of new demand.
- **Development + strategic-capital flywheel** — an in-house development machine plus fee-earning co-investment vehicles lets Prologis create value, recycle capital, and earn promotes — capital-light returns layered on top of the rent base.
- **Customer relationships** — deep, recurring relationships with the world's largest occupiers ([[amazon]], FedEx, DHL, major 3PLs) drive repeat build-to-suit demand and retention.
- **Land bank + power capacity** — an entitled land bank and secured electrical capacity now feed the **data-center pivot**, a scarce resource that few real-estate competitors can replicate.

The moat is wide but cyclical: it does not insulate Prologis from the rate cycle or from logistics oversupply, but it does mean Prologis takes share and sets the marginal rent in its markets.

## Growth Drivers & Catalysts

- **Embedded rent mark-to-market** — in-place rents below market convert to organic NOI growth as leases roll, a multi-year tailwind largely independent of new demand.
- **Development starts** — build-to-suit and speculative logistics plus the new **data-center** pipeline (Q1 2026 brought $1.3B of data-center build-to-suit starts); FY2026 development starts guided to $3.5–4.5B.
- **Strategic-capital promotes** — co-investment vehicles generate fee income and promote crystallisations that lift FFO in strong vintages.
- **E-commerce & 3PL demand** — secular growth in online retail and supply-chain reshoring/nearshoring drives warehouse absorption.
- **Port volumes & freight** — import/throughput data is a leading read on logistics demand and PLD leasing.
- **Energy / Essentials** — solar, storage and EV-charging attach as a growing ancillary, and secured power capacity is the enabler of the data-center pivot.
- **FFO-guidance revisions** — quarterly **core FFO** updates (FY2026 core FFO guidance raised to $6.07–$6.23) are the most-watched catalyst for the stock.
- **Rate-cut repricing** — as a leveraged duration asset, PLD re-rates on falling 10-year Treasury yields and Fed-cut expectations.

## Bull Case vs Bear Case

### Bull case
- Scarce infill land near ports and population centres means structurally constrained competing supply and durable pricing power.
- Large embedded **mark-to-market** on below-market in-place rents converts to organic FFO growth as leases roll.
- **Data-center optionality** — entitled land plus secured power capacity is a scarce, high-margin growth vector few real-estate peers can match.
- Unmatched global scale and the development + strategic-capital flywheel generate capital-light, fee-based returns on top of the rent base.
- Rising core-FFO guidance and a well-covered, growing dividend support a quality-compounder thesis.

### Bear case
- **Rate sensitivity** — a leveraged duration asset; sustained high rates pressure cap rates, NAV and the multiple.
- **Post-COVID absorption hangover** — the warehouse demand surge normalised; some US submarkets (notably Southern California) saw market-rent corrections, capping mark-to-market.
- **Trade/tariff slowdown** — tariffs and slower goods trade reduce import volumes and warehouse demand.
- **Execution & capital intensity** of the data-center pivot — a new, power- and capex-heavy business with execution and competitive risk.
- Valuation embeds continued FFO growth; any logistics digestion phase or rate-up shock can de-rate the sector leader hard.

## Key Risks

- **Interest-rate / cap-rate risk** — sustained high rates pressure cap rates, NAV and AFFO, and raise refinancing costs; PLD is among the highest-beta large REITs to the rates complex.
- **Oversupply** — speculative development across the industry can outrun demand in key logistics markets, softening rents (the recent SoCal correction is the template).
- **Trade / tariff slowdown** — reduced goods trade and import volumes cut warehouse demand.
- **Tenant concentration & credit** — large 3PL/e-commerce occupiers concentrate exposure; a major-tenant default or pullback would hit occupancy.
- **Data-center execution** — the capital-intensive pivot carries development, power-procurement and competitive-supply risk; returns are unproven at scale.
- **Refinancing & leverage** — maturing debt rolled at higher coupons compresses spreads and AFFO if rates stay elevated.

## Valuation & How the Stock Trades

Prologis is valued like a REIT, not an industrial company — and the data block's headline figures must be read with that in mind:

- **FFO / core FFO is the yardstick**, not GAAP EPS. Depreciation-heavy REIT accounting and gain-on-sale/promote income distort GAAP earnings (hence the ~107.7x reported P/E and >100% net margins); the market values PLD on **core FFO** and **AFFO** multiples and on **NAV** versus price.
- **NAV & implied cap rate** — the stock trades around an estimate of net asset value; the **implied cap rate** the share price puts on the portfolio is the key relative-value gauge versus private-market logistics transactions.
- **Rates / duration** — PLD behaves as a leveraged duration asset: it rallies on falling 10-year Treasury yields and Fed-cut repricing, and sells off on hawkish surprises — the dominant short-term driver.
- **Dividend** — the yield is a meaningful part of total return; what matters is **dividend coverage out of FFO/AFFO** (the API "dividend yield" figure in the data block is a non-market definition and should not be read literally). Prologis has a long record of dividend growth funded by FFO, not balance-sheet stretch.
- **Sector torque** — as the largest weight in VNQ/XLRE, PLD moves the whole real-estate sector; flows into/out of REIT ETFs amplify its moves.
