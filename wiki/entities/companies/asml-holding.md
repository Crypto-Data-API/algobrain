ASML Holding N.V. (NASDAQ: ASML) is a Dutch multinational and the world's sole supplier of extreme ultraviolet (EUV) lithography machines, which are essential for manufacturing the most advanced semiconductor chips. Founded in 1984 as a joint venture between Philips and ASM International and headquartered in Veldhoven, Netherlands, ASML is Europe's largest technology company by market capitalization. Its monopoly on EUV makes it an irreplaceable link in the [[artificial-intelligence|AI]] chip supply chain — no cutting-edge AI processor can be produced without ASML's machines.

## Business Overview

ASML sells lithography systems (DUV, EUV, and next-gen High-NA EUV) plus a large and growing Installed Base Management (service/upgrade) business. Its customers are the leading-edge foundries and memory makers — [[taiwan-semiconductor-manufacturing|TSMC]], Samsung, Intel, SK Hynix, Micron. A single High-NA EUV tool can cost ~€350M+. Revenue is cyclical and lumpy (tool recognition timing), but the service base provides a recurring floor. Geopolitics matters: export controls restrict sales of advanced tools to China.

## Business Segments

ASML reports revenue across three economically distinct buckets. Understanding the mix is essential because each behaves differently across the [[semiconductor-cycle|semiconductor cycle]] and carries different margin and moat characteristics.

| Segment | What it is | Moat / economics | Cyclicality |
|---|---|---|---|
| **EUV systems** | Extreme ultraviolet lithography tools for leading-edge logic and DRAM (≤7nm-class and below); includes High-NA (0.55 NA) next generation | Absolute monopoly — ASML is the only company on earth that can build EUV. Highest unit price (€150M+ standard EUV; ~€350M+ High-NA) | High — tied to leading-edge foundry capex decisions |
| **DUV systems** | Deep ultraviolet (immersion ArF, dry ArF, KrF, i-line) for mature and trailing nodes | Dominant but not monopoly — competes with [[nikon|Nikon]] and Canon in older segments; still ~80–90% share of immersion DUV | Cyclical; large China demand for mature-node capacity |
| **Installed Base Management** | Service contracts, field options, and performance/productivity upgrades on the ~6,000+ tools already in the field | Recurring, high-margin, sticky — grows with the cumulative installed base regardless of new-tool demand | Low — the recurring "floor" that smooths the cycle |

Key structural points for traders:

- **The installed base is the hidden annuity.** Every tool ASML ships becomes a multi-decade service relationship. As the base grows, Installed Base Management revenue becomes a larger, more predictable share — this is the quality-of-earnings story that justifies a premium multiple versus other [[semiconductor-equipment|semi-cap equipment]] names.
- **EUV is the monopoly; DUV is the cash cow.** The headline narrative is EUV, but DUV (immersion in particular) carries enormous volume and was the swing factor in the China demand surge as Chinese fabs stockpiled mature-node capacity ahead of tightening export rules.
- **High-NA EUV is the next leg.** The 0.55-NA generation extends the roadmap below 2nm. Adoption pace by [[intel|Intel]], [[taiwan-semiconductor-manufacturing|TSMC]], and Samsung is the key multi-year watch item — early High-NA orders signal the next capex super-cycle.
- **Lithography is the chokepoint of the entire [[artificial-intelligence|AI]] supply chain.** No leading-edge [[nvidia|Nvidia]], AMD, or hyperscaler accelerator exists without ASML tools upstream — making ASML the purest "picks-and-shovels" exposure to AI compute buildout.

## Competitive Positioning / Peers

ASML occupies a unique position: it is not really comparable to any single peer because it is a monopoly in its core product. The relevant peer set is the broader [[semiconductor-equipment|wafer-fab-equipment (WFE)]] complex, where ASML owns lithography while others own deposition, etch, and metrology.

| Company | Primary domain | Overlap with ASML | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **ASML** | Lithography (EUV monopoly + DUV leader) | — | Sole EUV supplier; the indispensable chokepoint |
| [[applied-materials]] | Deposition, etch, ion implant, CMP (broadest WFE portfolio) | Complementary, not direct | Largest WFE vendor by revenue; sells alongside ASML in the same fabs |
| [[lam-research]] | Etch and deposition (memory-heavy) | Complementary | Key beneficiary of NAND/DRAM cycles |
| [[tokyo-electron]] | Coat/develop track, etch, deposition (Japan WFE leader) | Adjacent + track tools paired with ASML scanners | The track tool that feeds ASML's lithography step |
| Nikon / Canon | Lithography (DUV and legacy only) | Direct in DUV, **zero** in EUV | The only nominal litho competitors; shut out of leading edge |
| KLA | Process control / inspection / metrology | Complementary | Yield-management layer atop lithography |

The takeaway: ASML's competitive moat is not "better than a rival" — there is no EUV rival. The risk is not competition but **demand cyclicality, customer concentration, and geopolitics**. ASML, [[applied-materials]], [[lam-research]], and [[tokyo-electron]] together rise and fall on the same WFE capex cycle, so they trade as a correlated basket via the [[smh-vaneck-semiconductor-etf|SMH]] and [[soxx|SOXX]] ETFs.

## Bull vs Bear Case

**Bull case**
- **Unbreakable monopoly.** EUV cannot be replicated without decades of R&D, a global supplier ecosystem (Zeiss optics, Cymer light sources), and tens of billions in cumulative investment. The moat is arguably the widest in technology hardware.
- **AI capex super-cycle.** Leading-edge demand for AI accelerators and HBM memory drives sustained foundry/memory capex, the direct demand driver for EUV.
- **Installed-base annuity** grows mechanically each year, improving earnings quality and downside support.
- **High-NA optionality** opens a fresh decade-long product cycle below 2nm.

**Bear case**
- **Lumpy revenue recognition** makes any single quarter's bookings and shipments highly volatile — the stock can swing violently on timing alone.
- **China export controls** structurally cap the addressable market; further tightening (or Chinese retaliation/indigenization) is an open-ended risk.
- **Extreme customer concentration** — a handful of foundries/memory makers decide ASML's order book; a capex pause at [[taiwan-semiconductor-manufacturing|TSMC]], Samsung, or [[intel|Intel]] hits hard.
- **Cyclical downturns** in semis can compress new-tool demand even as the service base holds.

## Valuation Framework

ASML is valued less on trailing earnings and more on the **forward order book and the durability of the monopoly**. Qualitative levers an analyst watches:

- **Bookings / backlog** — the single most-watched figure. Quarterly net bookings (especially the EUV split) guide future revenue better than any income-statement line. A backlog measured in tens of billions of euros provides multi-year visibility.
- **EUV mix and ASP** — rising EUV (and especially High-NA) share lifts both growth and gross margin.
- **Installed Base Management share** — a larger recurring-service mix supports a higher, more defensible multiple.
- **China revenue normalization** — after a stretch of elevated China DUV demand, the pace of normalization (and any further export-control restriction) is a key swing factor for near-term revenue.
- **Gross margin trajectory** — management's long-run margin targets (driven by High-NA ramp economics and service) anchor the bull case.

Because ASML is a monopoly compounder with cyclical inputs, it typically trades at a premium multiple that expands on AI-capex optimism and compresses on capex-pause or China-restriction fears.

## Trading & Options Playbook

- **Listings & liquidity:** dual-listed — primary on Euronext Amsterdam (AMS:ASML) and a NASDAQ-listed ADR (ASML). The US line has a deep, liquid options chain; mega-cap status keeps spreads tight.
- **Index/ETF membership:** core holding in semiconductor ETFs — heavily weighted in [[smh-vaneck-semiconductor-etf|SMH]] and [[soxx|SOXX]]; also in broad [[technology]] and European indices (AEX heavyweight). Trades as a high-beta proxy for the whole AI/semi-capex theme.
- **Catalyst calendar:** quarterly results (bookings are the key print), capital-markets/Investor Day updates, China export-control headlines, customer (TSMC/Intel/Samsung) capex guidance, and High-NA milestone news.
- **Volatility behavior:** IV builds into earnings given the binary nature of bookings surprises; the ADR can gap on overnight Amsterdam moves and on macro semiconductor sentiment. Correlated tightly with [[nvidia]] and [[taiwan-semiconductor-manufacturing|TSMC]] on AI-capex days.

## Capital Return

ASML returns capital through a combination of a growing dividend and large share buybacks — a multi-billion-euro repurchase program was in place (~€12B), reflecting strong free-cash-flow generation and management confidence in the long-run demand outlook. The buyback provides a floor of demand for the shares and, alongside the dividend, signals that even a heavy-R&D monopoly can be shareholder-friendly through the cycle.

## Risks

- **China export controls** — the dominant geopolitical risk; tightening restrictions structurally limit the addressable market.
- **Semiconductor capex downcycles** — new-tool demand is cyclical even though service revenue is sticky.
- **Customer concentration** — a few foundries/memory makers drive the order book.
- **High-NA adoption pace** — slower-than-expected ramp delays the next growth leg.
- **Lumpy tool-recognition timing** — quarterly results can swing sharply on shipment/recognition timing independent of underlying demand.
- **Supply-chain dependency** — reliance on a narrow set of critical suppliers (Zeiss optics, light-source components) for the most advanced tools.

## 2025–2026 Developments

- **FY2025:** total net sales €32.7B with net income €9.6B.
- **Q1 2026 (reported late Jan 2026):** net sales €8.8B (+~13% y/y), net income €2.8B, EPS €7.15 — a beat. EUV contributed €4.1B (~65% of sales, +28% y/y).
- **2026 guidance raised:** lower end of full-year revenue lifted to €36B (from €35B), upper band held at €40B; Q2 2026 guided €8.4–9.0B at 51–53% gross margin. Plans to build ≥60 standard EUV systems in 2026 (~36% more than the prior year).
- **Mistral AI investment:** ASML invested ~€1.3B in French AI startup Mistral as a strategic partner — an unusual move tying the equipment monopoly to the AI application layer.
- Year-end 2025 backlog €38.8B (incl. €7.4B EUV bookings). High-NA EUV ramp timing remains a watch item; large (~€12B) buyback in place.
