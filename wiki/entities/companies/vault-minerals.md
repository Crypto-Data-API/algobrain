**Vault Minerals Limited** (ASX: VAU) is an gold company listed on the [[asx-limited|Australian Securities Exchange]] and a constituent of the [[asx-200]] index. Vault Minerals Limited (ASX: VAU) is an Australian mid-tier gold producer formed in June 2024 by the merger of equals between Red 5 Limited and Silver Lake Resources Limited. Headquartered at the South Shore Centre in South Perth, the company produces gold and gold/copper concentrate primarily from three operating hubs in Western Australia, plus a gold project in Ontario, Canada.

## Business Overview & Segments

Vault is a mid-tier [[gold]] producer in the [[materials]] sector, formed by the 2024 merger of equals between Red 5 and Silver Lake Resources. It reports through four operating segments, anchored by the flagship King of the Hills (KoTH) mine, with the Deflector gold-copper operation providing modest by-product diversification.

| Segment | Location | Type | Role / notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Leonora Operations** | WA (Goldfields) | Gold, open-pit + underground | Flagship King of the Hills (KoTH) + Darlot satellite; centralised KoTH processing plant; the growth engine via the plant expansion |
| **Mount Monger** | WA (near Kalgoorlie) | Gold | Inherited from Silver Lake; mature production hub |
| **Deflector Region** | WA (near Yalgoo) | Gold–copper | Deflector + Rothsay satellite; highest segment EBITDA margin (~58%) via gold-copper concentrate |
| **Sugar Zone** | Ontario, Canada | Gold | In care-and-maintenance / restart planning; offshore optionality |

The company sells gold and gold/copper concentrate into spot and term markets and runs a low-debt, cash-and-bullion-rich balance sheet, which maximises operational leverage to the AUD gold price.

## Economic Moat

Like all gold miners, Vault is a **price-taker** — it has no pricing power over its output and earns the prevailing USD spot price. There is no franchise moat here in the [[economic-moat]] sense; what matters is **cost-curve position, asset quality and mine life**. Vault's relevant advantages are honest and asset-specific: a single large centralised processing plant at KoTH (scale economics on a long-life orebody), a high-margin gold-copper concentrate stream at Deflector, and a clean, low-debt balance sheet that lets it run unhedged and capture full upside. The flip side is that as a relatively young merged entity it is mid-cost and mid-scale versus the senior producers, with no by-product credits at the group level large enough to push it decisively down the cost curve. The KoTH plant expansion is the lever to improve unit costs and effectively "buy" a better cost-curve position through volume.

## History

- **1995**: Red 5 Limited incorporated; focused initially on gold exploration in the Philippines.
- **2010s**: Red 5 pivoted to Western Australia and built the King of the Hills open-pit and underground gold mine at Leonora.
- **2022**: King of the Hills commissioned and ramped to commercial production.
- **Silver Lake Resources** (formed 2007) built a portfolio of WA gold assets centred on Mount Monger and later acquired Deflector, Rothsay and Sugar Zone (Canada).
- **February 2024**: Red 5 and Silver Lake announced a binding Scheme Implementation Deed for a merger of equals (valued at approximately A$2.2 billion combined).
- **19 June 2024**: Merger implemented; Silver Lake delisted shortly afterwards.
- **September 2024**: Red 5 renamed Vault Minerals Limited.

## Recent Developments

Under managing director and CEO Luke Tonkin (Silver Lake founder), Vault has focused on integrating the merger, expanding King of the Hills capacity and optimising the wider portfolio:

- **FY25 full-year results (year to 30 June 2025)**: Net profit after tax of A$237.0 million; adjusted group EBITDA of A$619.4 million (43% margin). Closed the year with A$685.9 million in cash and bullion and no debt.
- **Segment margins**: Leonora 44% EBITDA margin, Mount Monger 28%, Deflector 58% — highlighting the strong performance of Deflector's gold-copper concentrate operation.
- **H1 FY25 (to 31 December 2024)**: Produced 195,417 oz and sold 199,428 oz, generating A$678.8 million revenue and A$267.4 million EBITDA; net profit after tax of A$119.3 million.
- **King of the Hills plant upgrade**: Stage 1 commissioning completed in early 2026, targeting throughput of approximately 6.0 Mtpa (a ~50% uplift). Stage 2 on track for Q2 FY27, targeting 7.5–8.0 Mtpa in 2H FY27.
- **Capital management**: Announced share buyback programme alongside FY25 results.
- **Safety incident**: Light-vehicle fatality involving a DeltaFM employee at the King of the Hills site in early 2026 resulted in voluntary suspension of operations pending investigations.
- **Leadership transition**: CEO Luke Tonkin announced he will step down by mid-2026; a succession process is underway.

## Bull Case vs Bear Case

### Bull case
- High operational leverage to record AUD gold via a cash-rich, no-debt balance sheet (A$685.9M cash and bullion at FY25).
- KoTH plant expansion lifts volume ~50%+ and should lower unit costs, improving cost-curve position.
- Deflector's gold-copper concentrate adds a high-margin, partly diversified earnings stream.
- Merged scale supports [[asx-200]] index inclusion and broader institutional/ETF ownership.

### Bear case
- Pure price-taker with mid-tier costs — a gold-price reversal compresses margins hard.
- Integration and execution risk on a young (2024) merged entity; expansion delivery is not guaranteed.
- Leadership transition risk — founder-CEO Luke Tonkin stepping down by mid-2026.
- Operational/safety overhang — the early-2026 KoTH fatality and voluntary suspension highlight single-site concentration risk.
- Thin reported financial history and low data completeness make through-cycle quality hard to assess.

## Key Risks

- **Gold-price reversal** — the dominant risk from record levels; high beta amplifies the downside.
- **Execution / expansion** — KoTH Stage 1/2 delivery, ramp and cost outcomes.
- **Operational & safety** — single-site concentration at KoTH; the early-2026 fatality and operations suspension.
- **Leadership** — CEO succession after Luke Tonkin's mid-2026 retirement.
- **Cost inflation** — labour, power, consumables in the WA Goldfields.
- **FX** — a stronger AUD compresses AUD-denominated margins.

## Valuation & How the Stock Trades

- **Gold and AUD beta** — VAU is a higher-beta, AUD-denominated proxy on USD gold; unhedged-to-lightly-hedged production with a low-debt balance sheet means earnings and free cash flow amplify spot-gold moves, and a softer AUD compounds the effect.
- **No dividend of note** — capital return to date is via a buyback rather than a meaningful franked dividend, so VAU is a capital-growth/leverage play rather than an income name (contrast [[evolution-mining]]).
