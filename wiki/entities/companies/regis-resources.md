**Regis Resources Limited** (ASX: RRL) is a mid-tier Australian gold producer with operating interests across Western Australia (Duketon, plus a 30% stake in Tropicana) and New South Wales (the undeveloped McPhillamys project). It is listed on the [[asx-limited|Australian Securities Exchange]] and is a constituent of the [[asx-200]] index. For traders, RRL is a high-beta, unhedged play on the **Australian-dollar gold price**: now debt-free with a large cash/bullion balance, it offers strong operating leverage to bullion while carrying single-commodity, grade and permitting risk (notably the stalled McPhillamys project). It is headquartered in Subiaco, Western Australia.

## Business Model & Segments

Regis Resources's operating assets comprise:

- Duketon Gold Project (100% owned) - located in the North Eastern Goldfields of Western Australia, Duketon comprises two centres with three processing plants totalling approximately 10 million tonnes per annum capacity. The Duketon operations encompass multiple open-pit and underground deposits, including Garden Well, Rosemont and Moolart Well.
- Tropicana Gold Mine (30% JV interest) - located northeast of Kalgoorlie in Western Australia, operated by AngloGold Ashanti Australia Limited (70%). Tropicana is a large-scale operation producing approximately 450,000-500,000 ounces per annum at a group level.
- McPhillamys Gold Project (100% owned) - located in the Central Western region of New South Wales near Blayney, McPhillamys is one of Australia's largest undeveloped open-pit gold projects, with a resource of approximately 2.7 million ounces and potential for a mine life of 10+ years with expansion potential.

The company's FY26 guidance is 350,000-380,000 ounces at an AISC of A$2,610-A$2,990 per ounce.

## History

Regis Resources was founded on 12 May 1986 as Johnson's Well Mining NL and officially listed on the ASX on 19 February 1987. The company was incorporated in Perth by Western Australian prospectors and geologists aiming to transform grassroots greenstone exploration into a producing gold company. Joseph Gutnick was a key early figure, serving as director and at various times managing director and chairman until 2004.

A significant shift came in 1994 with the acquisition of tenements in the Duketon Belt. Exploration drilling in 1997 revealed a significant extension to the known Christmas Well gold mineralisation within the Duketon Belt, leading to the discovery of the Rosemont deposit. The Garden Well Gold Mine commenced operations in 2011, followed by the Rosemont Gold Mine in 2013, establishing the Duketon Gold Project as Regis's core asset. By the end of 2013, Regis had developed approximately 10 Mtpa of processing capacity across Duketon.

In May 2021, Regis acquired a 30% stake in the Tropicana Gold Mine from IGO Limited for A$903 million, adding a high-quality cash flow asset to the portfolio. Regis also progressed the McPhillamys gold project in NSW through permitting, with a construction-ready resource of approximately 2.7 million ounces. On 16 August 2024, the Federal Environment Minister issued a Section 10 declaration under the Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander Heritage Protection Act 1984 covering the approved tailings storage facility site, rendering the project currently unviable in its approved form. Regis has commenced judicial review proceedings, with a hearing held on 10-12 December 2025.

## Business Overview & Assets

Regis is a mid-tier ASX-listed [[gold]] producer with a concentrated three-asset portfolio: a 100%-owned operating hub in WA (Duketon), a minority stake in a large, high-quality JV mine (Tropicana), and a large undeveloped NSW deposit (McPhillamys) currently blocked by a federal heritage declaration. Group production is in the ~350-380koz range (FY26 guidance), with revenue almost entirely a function of ounces sold times the AUD gold price.

Because Regis is **unhedged**, its earnings are direct operating leverage on the AUD gold price: roughly fixed AISC against a fully variable revenue line means margins (and therefore profit) move far more than the gold price itself.

## Economic Moat & Competitive Position

A gold miner has **no [[economic-moat]] in the conventional sense**. [[gold]] is a globally fungible commodity and every producer is a pure **price-taker** — Regis cannot set its selling price, differentiate its product, or build switching costs or network effects. There is no pricing power. The only durable, defensible edges in this business are operational and balance-sheet, not commercial:

- **Cost-curve position (AISC)** — the single most important competitive variable. A miner sitting low on the all-in-sustaining-cost curve survives and generates cash through price troughs that push higher-cost peers into losses. Regis's FY25 AISC of ~A$2,531/oz (bottom of guidance) and FY26 guidance of A$2,610-2,990/oz place it as a competitive but not lowest-cost mid-cap; cost discipline is the moat-substitute.
- **Asset quality & grade** — higher grade means more ounces per tonne milled and lower unit costs. Tropicana is the higher-quality asset (large, long-life, well-run by AngloGold); Duketon is a mature, multi-deposit hub where grade and reserve sequencing drive cost outcomes.
- **Reserve life & replacement** — gold mining is liquidation-by-nature; every ounce sold must be replaced through exploration or acquisition or the business runs down. The ~2.7 Moz McPhillamys resource and Duketon exploration are the reserve-life story — but McPhillamys is currently stranded by permitting.
- **Balance-sheet strength** — being **debt-free** with a large cash/bullion balance (~A$930M at 31 Dec 2025) is a genuine competitive advantage in a cyclical, capital-hungry industry: it lets Regis fund growth, weather price downturns, and act counter-cyclically without dilution or distress.

Net: Regis competes on cost, grade, reserve life and balance-sheet resilience — not on any structural pricing advantage. In a falling gold market it has no defence beyond its cost position.

## Growth Drivers & Catalysts

- **AUD gold price** — the dominant driver; as an unhedged producer Regis's margins amplify every move in spot gold and the AUD/USD cross.
- **McPhillamys judicial-review outcome** — the binary catalyst: the Dec 2025 hearing on the Section 10 heritage declaration could unlock (or permanently strand) a ~2.7 Moz, 10+ year project. Regis has stated it is "not giving up."
- **Exploration & reserve updates** — Duketon and broader portfolio drilling to extend reserve life; FY26 exploration budget of A$50-60M (plus growth capital of A$180-195M).
- **Quarterly production / AISC reports** — the regular operational scorecard; delivery against FY26 guidance (350-380koz at AISC A$2,610-2,990/oz) drives the stock between results.
- **Capital returns** — with a debt-free balance sheet and ~A$930M cash/bullion (Dec 2025), the market watches for dividends, buybacks or accretive M&A as deployment options.

## Bull Case vs Bear Case

### Bull case
- **Debt-free with a large cash/bullion balance** (~A$930M at Dec 2025) — fortress balance sheet that funds growth and survives downturns without dilution.
- **Unhedged, high operating leverage to a record AUD gold price** — margins expand sharply when bullion is strong; FY25 delivered 373koz at AISC ~A$2,531/oz and H1 FY26 set record profit and cash flow.
- **McPhillamys optionality** — a ~2.7 Moz, 10+ year project carried at little market value while blocked; a favourable judicial-review outcome is a large, low-cost call option.
- **Cash deployment optionality** — the strong balance sheet allows counter-cyclical acquisitions or capital returns.

### Bear case
- **Single-commodity concentration** — 100% exposed to gold; no diversifying by-products or commodities to cushion a gold-price reversal.
- **Grade & permitting risk** — mine sequencing, grade variability and recovery can move the P&L quickly even with a firm gold price.
- **McPhillamys block** — the project is stranded in its approved form; an adverse judicial-review outcome removes a major piece of the growth story.
- **Cost inflation (AISC)** — rising labour, energy and consumables can compress margins; FY26 AISC guidance steps up to A$2,610-2,990/oz.
- **JV dependence** — Tropicana's output and costs are controlled by AngloGold Ashanti, not Regis.

## Key Risks

- **Gold-price reversal** — the dominant risk; as an unhedged price-taker, a sustained fall in the AUD gold price compresses margins and earnings disproportionately.
- **Grade & recovery variability** — lower mined grades or recovery shortfalls raise unit costs and cut ounces (as seen sector-wide in mine-sequencing swings).
- **AISC / cost inflation** — labour, energy and consumables inflation erodes margins; FY26 guidance already steps costs up.
- **Single-commodity concentration** — no commodity diversification to offset a gold downturn.
- **Tropicana JV dependence** — 30% non-operating interest means reliance on AngloGold Ashanti's operating performance and decisions.
- **McPhillamys heritage / regulatory block** — the Section 10 declaration and ongoing judicial review create binary regulatory risk around a major growth asset.

## Valuation & How the Stock Trades

Regis trades as a **high-beta, unhedged play on the AUD gold price** rather than on conventional earnings multiples — a trailing P/E is of limited use for a commodity producer whose profit swings violently with the cycle (note the FY24 net loss versus the FY25 record profit). Mechanically:

- **Operating leverage to bullion** — fixed-ish AISC against a fully variable, unhedged revenue line means profit moves far more than the gold price; the stock is a geared expression of a bullish [[gold]] view on the ASX.
- **AUD/USD overlay** — gold is priced in USD while most costs are in AUD, so the AUD/USD cross is a second lever on realised margins.
- **Balance sheet** — debt-free with a large cash/bullion balance (~A$930M at Dec 2025) lowers financial risk and supports the equity through price troughs; it also creates capital-return and M&A optionality.
- **Dividend** — modest/thin and not the reason to own the stock; the investment case is operating leverage and growth optionality, not yield.
- **What it trades on** — spot gold, AUD/USD, quarterly production and AISC delivery, and the McPhillamys judicial-review outcome — far more than on reported headline earnings.

## Recent Developments

For FY25 (year ended 30 June 2025), Regis delivered strong operational results, with production of 373,000 ounces at the top end of guidance and AISC of A$2,531 per ounce at the bottom end of guidance. The company repaid A$300 million in debt during the year, ending FY25 debt-free. Regis built approximately A$662 million in cash since December 2023.

For H1 FY26 (six months to 31 December 2025), Regis reported record profit and cash flow, with Managing Director and CEO Jim Beyer citing a continuing step up in financial performance, record net profit after tax and record cash flow. Cash and bullion were A$930 million at 31 December 2025, after building approximately A$413 million over six months; Regis remains debt-free. Kalkine commentary later in FY26 noted share price volatility in line with sector weakness.

FY26 guidance is 350,000-380,000 ounces of gold at an AISC of A$2,610-2,990/oz, with planned investment of A$180-195 million in growth capital and A$50-60 million in exploration. Regis is continuing to pursue development and permitting options for McPhillamys and has consistently stated it is "not giving up" on the project.

The most recent filing (annual) was lodged on 2023-06-30. See [[#Financial Reports]] below.
