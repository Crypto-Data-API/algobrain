Courtyard is a phygital tokenization platform that issues NFTs on Polygon representing professionally graded physical trading cards — Pokémon, basketball, baseball, and football — with the physical card vaulted with global security logistics firm Brink's and a burn-to-redeem mechanism that lets holders take delivery of the underlying card. Courtyard's co-founder and CEO is Nicolas le Jeune. The platform became one of the largest NFT volume categories during the 2024-2025 graded-card NFT boom.

## History and founding

Courtyard launched on Polygon as a tokenization layer for graded collectibles, co-founded by CEO Nicolas le Jeune. A defining early decision was partnering with **Brink's** — the global security/logistics firm with existing expertise storing fine art and precious metals — to vault the physical cards. The model targeted a specific pain point in traditional trading card markets: liquidity for high-value graded cards is thin, price discovery is slow, shipping is risky, and the cost of moving physical cards between buyers is high. By vaulting cards with Brink's and representing them as NFTs on Polygon, Courtyard aimed to let collectors trade these assets with the execution mechanics of NFT marketplaces.

Adoption built slowly through 2023 and accelerated sharply in 2024-2025 as Pokémon-card-backed NFTs in particular became one of the dominant NFT volume categories, at times outpacing traditional PFP collections in on-chain trading activity.

## What it does

- **Custodied graded cards**: Cards graded by **PSA, CGC, or BGS** are received, authenticated, and stored in a **Brink's vault**. Supported categories include Pokémon, basketball, baseball, and football cards.
- **1:1 NFT representation**: Each vaulted card has a corresponding NFT on Polygon. Ownership of the NFT is equivalent to claim on the physical card.
- **Burn-to-redeem**: The holder can burn the NFT to take physical delivery of the graded card, with shipping and insurance handled through Courtyard.
- **Pack openings and drops**: Courtyard also sells randomized packs whose contents are revealed on-chain, blending collectible pack mechanics with NFT minting.
- **Secondary trading**: NFTs trade on Courtyard's own interface and on aggregators that index Polygon collections.

## Trading relevance

- **New asset class bridge**: Courtyard brings a real-world collectibles market (estimated in the billions annually for graded trading cards) onto a blockchain execution layer, giving crypto-native traders exposure to a category that previously lived on eBay, PWCC, and Goldin.
- **Arbitrage against traditional auction venues**: Traders monitor pricing differences between Courtyard-vaulted NFTs and comparable cards selling on eBay, PWCC auctions, and direct-sale platforms. Spreads can persist because redemption friction and audience segmentation keep the markets partially separate.
- **Pack economics**: Randomized pack drops have an expected value determined by the distribution of vaulted inventory. Sophisticated traders model EV vs. pack price similarly to how TCG breakers model sealed product expected value.
- **Volume category**: Graded-card NFTs have been one of the most active NFT categories by transaction count and volume in 2024-2025.

## Key risks

- **Custodian insolvency**: The entire model depends on the vault operator remaining solvent and honoring redemptions. If the custodian fails, NFT holders become unsecured creditors unless insurance and legal structure protect them.
- **Authentication and grading risk**: If a card was misgraded, counterfeit, or degraded in storage, the NFT represents a less-valuable or worthless claim.
- **Insurance scope**: Traders should understand whether insurance covers theft, fire, water damage, and misgrading — and what the claim process looks like in practice.
- **Redemption friction**: Shipping costs, customs (for international redemption), and the risk of damage in transit are borne by the redeemer.
- **Regulatory exposure**: Tokenized physical collectibles sit at the intersection of securities, commodities, and collectibles law depending on jurisdiction and marketing.

## Current status

As of April 2026, Courtyard is an operational phygital tokenization platform on Polygon, continuing to vault and tokenize graded cards and run pack drops.
