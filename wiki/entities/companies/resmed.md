ResMed (NYSE: RMD; ASX: RMD via CDIs) is the global leader in **sleep-apnea and respiratory-care devices** — cloud-connected CPAP/bilevel machines, masks and a growing out-of-hospital software portfolio. It is an S&P 500 (and effectively S&P/ASX 200) constituent and a high-quality compounder: dominant share of a duopoly with Philips, recurring mask/consumable resupply revenue, and expanding margins. For traders the defining swing factor since 2023 has been the **GLP-1 debate** — whether weight-loss drugs shrink the sleep-apnea market — which has repeatedly whipsawed the stock; ResMed's own data argues GLP-1s are net-additive to CPAP demand.

## Business Overview & Segments

ResMed reports across two segments. The bulk of revenue and profit comes from **Sleep and Breathing Health** — the connected-device franchise — while **Residential Care Software** adds a recurring, out-of-hospital software annuity. The combination of connected hardware, cloud-based care platforms and out-of-hospital software creates a digital-health ecosystem spanning diagnosis, therapy initiation, adherence, ongoing supply management and post-acute care.

| Segment / line | What it is | Revenue mix (qualitative) |
|---|---|---|
| **Sleep & Breathing Health — devices** | Cloud-connected CPAP/bilevel flow generators (AirSense 11, AirCurve 11 platforms) for obstructive sleep apnea and respiratory care | Large; the install base that seeds the high-margin resupply annuity |
| **Sleep & Breathing Health — masks & consumables** | Full mask range plus accessories sold on an ongoing resupply cadence | The durable, recurring profit engine — sticky reorder revenue with attractive margins |
| **Sleep & Breathing Health — diagnostics & digital** | ApneaLink Air, NightOwl home diagnostics, EasyCare Tx; AirView (clinician monitoring) and myAir (patient app) | Small revenue but central to the adherence/data moat and resupply attach |
| **Residential Care Software (SaaS)** | Brightree (home medical equipment / HME), HEALTHCAREfirst, MatrixCare (skilled nursing, senior living, home health/hospice), MEDIFOX DAN (Europe) | A recurring software annuity; lower-growth than devices but diversifying and high-retention |

ResMed is dual-listed: primary on the NYSE, with [[asx-200]]-eligible CHESS Depositary Interests (CDIs) on the ASX giving Australian investors local exposure.

## Economic Moat & Competitive Position

ResMed holds a wide [[economic-moat|economic moat]] in [[healthcare]] devices, built on several reinforcing layers:

- **Duopoly structure** — the global CPAP/PAP market is effectively a two-player field (ResMed and [[philips]]). After Philips' 2021 recall of millions of sleep/respiratory devices, ResMed captured displaced patients and consolidated share, hardening the duopoly in its favour.
- **Resupply annuity & switching costs** — once a patient is set up on a device and mask, resupply of consumables is recurring and sticky. Clinicians, durable-medical-equipment suppliers and patients face real [[switching-costs]] tied to fitting, comfort and reimbursement workflows.
- **Data & software lock-in** — AirView/myAir and the Residential Care Software stack embed ResMed into clinician and provider workflows, raising retention and supporting adherence (which payers reward).
- **Scale & manufacturing** — global scale in masks/devices amortises R&D and supports steady gross-margin expansion; vertical reach from diagnosis to post-acute care is hard to replicate.
- **Regulatory & clinical evidence** — large real-world datasets and regulatory approvals across markets are a barrier to new entrants.

The moat is **wide but exposed to two debates**: the long-running [[glp-1]] question (whether weight-loss drugs shrink the addressable OSA pool) and a potential Philips manufacturing recovery restoring competitive intensity.

## Growth Drivers & Catalysts

- **Under-diagnosis tailwind** — the large majority of OSA sufferers globally remain undiagnosed; home diagnostics and awareness expand the funnel for years.
- **Resupply & install-base compounding** — every device placed deepens the recurring mask/consumable annuity.
- **Gross-margin expansion** — component-cost normalisation and mix have driven multi-quarter margin gains (Q3 FY26 GAAP gross margin ~62.2%).
- **GLP-1 as a funnel** — ResMed's real-world data shows GLP-1 patients are ~11% more likely to initiate PAP and resupply at higher rates when on combined therapy — reframing the bear thesis as a tailwind.
- **Software/SaaS attach** — Brightree/MatrixCare cross-sell and out-of-hospital digitisation.
- **Recurring catalysts** — quarterly earnings (revenue growth, gross-margin expansion, device/mask mix), new GLP-1/sleep real-world data, Philips recall/recovery news, and device launches (AirSense/AirCurve).

## Bull Case vs Bear Case

### Bull case
- Wide-moat duopoly with a sticky, high-margin resupply annuity and multi-year gross-margin expansion.
- GLP-1 weight-loss drugs are net-additive to PAP demand, not a substitute — turning the headline bear fear into a tailwind.
- Huge under-diagnosed OSA population leaves a long runway; home diagnostics widen the funnel.
- Fortress balance sheet (D/E 0.11x, strong free cash flow) funding R&D, buybacks and a growing dividend.
- Optionality in software (Brightree/MatrixCare) and connected-care data.

### Bear case
- GLP-1 substitution fear is a persistent sentiment overhang that can de-rate the stock even if fundamentals hold.
- A Philips return to full production restores competitive intensity and could compress share/pricing.
- Premium multiple (~29x PE) is vulnerable to de-rating on any growth or margin disappointment.
- Reimbursement/pricing pressure in key markets; FX (AUD/USD) translation for ASX holders.

## Key Risks

- **GLP-1 substitution narrative** — sentiment risk even where ResMed's own data argues GLP-1s expand the funnel.
- **Competitive resurgence** — a Philips manufacturing recovery restoring lost share.
- **Reimbursement / pricing** — payer and regulatory pressure on device and resupply economics.
- **Valuation / positioning** — a premium multiple leaves little room for execution slips.
- **FX translation** — AUD/USD moves affect reported results and ASX-CDI returns.

## History

ResMed was founded in June 1989 by Dr Peter Farrell following a technology transfer from the Baxter Centre for Medical Research. The company was built on Professor Colin Sullivan's 1980 CPAP patent and prototypes. When the Baxter Centre decided not to enter the sleep apnea market, Dr Farrell acquired the technology and established ResMed (short for Respiratory Medicine) to bring CPAP to the world.

ResMed opened an office in San Diego in 1992 and incorporated in the US two years later, establishing the city as its corporate headquarters. The company completed its NASDAQ IPO in 1995 to access growth capital and the larger US healthcare market. ASX-listed CHESS Depositary Interests (CDIs) provide exposure for Australian investors under the ticker RMD.

Over subsequent decades ResMed expanded its portfolio through organic R&D and significant acquisitions. In April 2016 it acquired out-of-hospital software provider Brightree for US$800 million. In late 2018 it acquired US aged-care and home-care software providers HEALTHCAREfirst (US$126 million) and MatrixCare (US$750 million). These acquisitions created the Residential Care Software segment and established ResMed as a leading out-of-hospital digital health platform.

On 1 March 2013, Mick Farrell - Peter Farrell's son - became Chief Executive Officer. He was appointed Chairman of the Board in January 2023.

## Recent Developments

In FY25 Q4, ResMed delivered headline revenue growth of 10%, non-GAAP gross margin expansion of 230 basis points and 23% non-GAAP EPS growth. In Q1 FY26, the company delivered headline revenue growth of 9% year-on-year, 280bps of non-GAAP gross margin expansion and 16% non-GAAP EPS growth. Q2 FY26 produced 11% headline revenue growth, 310bps of non-GAAP gross margin expansion and mid-teens non-GAAP EPS growth.

In **Q3 FY26 (quarter ended 31 March 2026)**, revenue grew **11% to $1.43 billion**, GAAP gross margin improved to **62.2%** (from 59.3%), net income was **$398.7 million**, GAAP diluted EPS rose to **$2.74** (non-GAAP $2.86), and operating cash flow was **$554.1 million** with $1.66 billion cash on hand; the company declared a **$0.60 quarterly dividend** (Verified via Perplexity (sonar) / WebSearch, 2026-06-10).

ResMed has continued to invest in its digital health ecosystem. Real-world data published by the company across a three-year analysis shows that patients on GLP-1 medications (such as tirzepatide) are 11% more likely to initiate PAP therapy, and patients on combined GLP-1 and PAP therapy have a higher PAP re-supply rate. The company positions GLP-1-based obesity therapy together with CPAP as a complementary standard of care for many patients with obstructive sleep apnea.

CEO Mick Farrell was named Chair of the AdvaMed Board of Directors for a two-year term beginning January 2026, reinforcing ResMed's profile in the global medical device policy landscape.

## Leadership

Mick Farrell is Chairman and Chief Executive Officer, having held the CEO role since 1 March 2013. Dr Peter Farrell is the company's founder and remains Chairman Emeritus. The Board and executive team comprise leaders with deep experience in medical devices, software, cloud-based health platforms and global healthcare operations. Mick Farrell was also named Chair of the AdvaMed Board for a two-year term beginning January 2026.
