**Pro Medicus Limited** (ASX: PME) is an Australian [[healthcare]]-informatics company and a global leader in enterprise medical imaging software, sitting at the intersection of [[healthcare]] and [[technology]], listed on the [[asx-limited|Australian Securities Exchange]] and a constituent of the [[asx-200]] index. Headquartered in Richmond, Victoria, it supplies its Visage 7 platform and related radiology information system (RIS) software to hospitals, imaging centres and health systems across Australia, North America and Europe. For traders it is one of the ASX's premier high-growth, ultra-high-margin (~99% gross) software compounders — a debt-free, founder-led name protected by a wide [[switching-costs|switching-cost]] [[economic-moat|moat]], whose extreme valuation makes it highly sensitive to contract-win cadence.

## Business Model & Segments

Pro Medicus's flagship product is the Visage 7 Enterprise Imaging Platform, a healthcare imaging software that provides radiologists, physicians and clinicians with access and visualisation capability for 2D, 3D and 4D medical images, including picture archive and communication system (PACS) and digital imaging functionality. The company's product suite also includes:

- Visage RIS - a proprietary medical practice management system including training, installation, professional services, and after-sale support and service.
- Promedicus.net - an e-health platform supporting secure email and integration products.
- Visage Ease - a mobile application providing access to medical imaging results.
- Visage Ease Pro - a mobile application enabling interpretation of diagnostic imaging studies stored on a Visage 7 server.

Pro Medicus operates a software-as-a-service model for many of its North American clients, with long-term (typically 5-10 year) minimum-volume contracts. Its US subsidiary, Visage Imaging Inc., is the principal vehicle for North American deployments. The company counts 11 of the top 20 "sandstone" US teaching hospitals among its customers, including Mayo Clinic, Johns Hopkins, NYU Langone, Duke Health, Massachusetts General Hospital (Harvard), Trinity Health and Northwestern Medicine.

The economic shape of the business is unusual: a tiny headcount supports a global installed base, so incremental revenue drops through at near-100% gross margin. The recurring SaaS contract structure (minimum-volume, multi-year) makes the revenue base highly visible and deeply embedded in customers' clinical workflows.

## History

Pro Medicus was founded in Melbourne in 1983 by Dr Sam Hupert and Anthony Hall, with the aim of applying computer technology to medicine at a time when rising X-ray film costs were driving demand for digital alternatives. The company built an early reputation in Australia for radiology practice management and pioneered secure email and digital systems for medical professionals, including securing one of Australia's first internet domain names.

Pro Medicus listed on the ASX in 2000. A pivotal moment came in 2009 when, during the global financial crisis, the company acquired Visage Imaging from a distressed seller for US$5 million. This acquisition introduced the Visage 7 platform and transformed Pro Medicus from a domestically focused radiology software company into an internationally competitive enterprise imaging vendor.

Over the following decade, Pro Medicus progressively won contracts with major US health systems. The company leveraged Visage 7's thin-client streaming architecture - capable of serving large imaging datasets from a central server - to compete with established PACS vendors on performance, scalability and total cost of ownership. Contract wins cumulatively built a deep recurring revenue base across leading academic and integrated delivery networks.

## Economic Moat & Competitive Position

Pro Medicus holds one of the **widest [[economic-moat|economic moats]]** on the ASX, and it is built almost entirely on **[[switching-costs|switching costs]]** rather than scale or cost advantage:

- **Mission-critical clinical-workflow lock-in** — Visage 7 sits at the centre of a hospital's radiology read workflow. Once radiologists are trained on it and it is integrated with the EMR, RIS, modalities and reporting tools, ripping it out means re-validating a life-safety-critical system, retraining staff and risking diagnostic continuity. The [[switching-costs]] are extreme; displacement is rare once a system is embedded.
- **Multi-year minimum-volume SaaS contracts** — typical 5-10 year terms with contractual minimums lock in revenue and make the installed base sticky and highly visible. Renewals historically come at higher value (e.g. the Northwestern uplift), evidencing pricing power.
- **~99% gross margin / thin-client performance lead** — the server-side streaming architecture lets a single central server serve very large 3D/4D datasets faster than incumbents' fat-client PACS, winning on performance, scalability and total cost of ownership. The near-100% gross margin is itself a moat: it funds R&D and undercuts on TCO while still printing >50% net margins.
- **Reference-customer flywheel** — winning marquee "sandstone" academic systems (Mayo Clinic, Johns Hopkins, NYU Langone, Duke Health, Massachusetts General/Harvard, Trinity Health, Northwestern Medicine) creates a powerful reference effect: the most prestigious US institutions de-risk the buying decision for the next health system, compounding wins.
- **Founder-led focus** — co-founders Dr Sam Hupert and Anthony Hall remain at the helm and as major shareholders, sustaining a singular product focus that the diversified imaging conglomerates struggle to match.

This is a **wide but valuation-exposed** moat: the durability of the franchise is rarely the debate — the debate is whether the win/renewal cadence keeps pace with a multiple that prices years of continued share-gain.

## Growth Drivers & Catalysts

- **US health-system win/renewal cadence** — the dominant driver. Each large win materially moves the forward revenue base: the 10-year, ~A$330M Trinity Health deal (the largest in company history), NYU Langone and Duke upgrades, the Northwestern uplift (A$22M → A$37M) and the ~A$31M MedStar renewal illustrate both new wins and rising renewal economics.
- **Forward contractual-minimum backlog** — five-year revenue based on contractual minimums passed **A$1 billion for the first time in HY2026**, giving unusual visibility for a growth name.
- **AI / algorithm products** — Pro Medicus is layering AI offerings (e.g. cardiac and breast algorithms and an AI marketplace) on top of the Visage platform, opening a new high-margin revenue line; see [[artificial-intelligence]] and [[ai-healthcare]].
- **TAM expansion beyond radiology** — extending the enterprise-imaging platform into adjacent specialties such as cardiology and ophthalmology widens the addressable market well beyond the core radiology read.
- **USD revenue tailwind** — the large and growing North American base means most incremental revenue is USD-denominated; a softer AUD flatters reported (AUD) results.
- **Recurring catalysts** — half-year (Feb) and full-year (Aug) results, new large US hospital-system wins and renewals, five-year forward contractual-minimum disclosures, and AI product/regulatory milestones.

## Bull Case vs Bear Case

### Bull case
- Wide [[switching-costs|switching-cost]] moat plus a >A$1bn contractual-minimum backlog gives rare revenue visibility for a high-growth name.
- ~99% gross margin and >50% net margin with a tiny headcount — incremental wins drop almost entirely to the bottom line; debt-free, net-cash balance sheet.
- Long runway: only a fraction of US (let alone European) health systems converted; AI products and adjacent specialties (cardiology, ophthalmology) expand the TAM.
- Rising renewal economics (Northwestern uplift) evidence durable pricing power, not just unit growth.
- Founder alignment and a reference-customer flywheel among the most prestigious US academic systems.

### Bear case
- **Extreme valuation** — a PE well above 100x prices years of flawless execution; any slowdown in win cadence can de-rate the multiple hard.
- **Customer concentration** — revenue rides on a relatively small number of large US health systems; the loss or delay of a marquee contract is a material event.
- **Lumpy, deal-dependent growth** — quarters without a large win can disappoint a market that has priced continuous share-gain.
- **Key-person risk** — heavy reliance on founders Hupert and Hall.
- **FX** — USD revenue translated to AUD adds volatility, and a stronger AUD is a headwind.

## Key Risks

- **Valuation / positioning** — at a PE >100x, the stock is priced for sustained execution; a missed win or a soft results print can trigger an outsized de-rating.
- **Customer concentration** — a few large US academic/integrated-delivery systems drive much of the revenue; concentration cuts both ways.
- **Contract-win-cadence dependence** — the equity story requires a steady drumbeat of new wins and uplifting renewals to justify the multiple; the pipeline is inherently lumpy.
- **FX translation** — most revenue is USD while the company reports in AUD; AUD/USD swings move reported results.
- **Key-person risk** — founders Sam Hupert and Anthony Hall are central to product and commercial direction.
- **Competitive response** — aggressive bundling or architectural catch-up by GE HealthCare, Philips, Siemens Healthineers, or share gains by Sectra, could slow the win cadence.

## Valuation & How the Stock Trades

Pro Medicus trades at an **ultra-premium multiple** (PE >100x) that reflects its ~99% gross margin, recurring SaaS economics, debt-free net-cash balance sheet and long growth runway rather than any conventional cheapness. Mechanically:

- **Trades on win cadence and backlog** — the share price is driven by new large US health-system wins, uplifting renewals and the disclosed five-year contractual-minimum backlog (which passed A$1bn in HY2026) far more than by the headline results line.
- **High realized volatility around results** — given the premium multiple, half-year (Feb) and full-year (Aug) results and contract announcements regularly produce large single-day moves; revenue growth versus an already-high base is the key print.
- **Thin ASX options** — single-stock options liquidity is thin relative to US large caps; most trading exposure is via the ordinary shares.
- **Token dividend / franking** — the company pays a small, partly franked dividend; the franking-credit gross-up modestly improves after-tax yield for Australian residents, but capital return is incidental to the growth story.
- **AUD/USD sensitivity** — as a USD-revenue, AUD-reporting business, the stock has FX sensitivity baked into reported results.

## Recent Developments

In FY2025 (year ended 30 June 2025), Pro Medicus reported revenue of A$213.0 million (up 31.9%) and net profit after tax of A$115.2 million (up 39.2%). Cash and other financial assets finished the year at A$210.7 million, up 35.5%. During the year, the company announced approximately A$520 million in new contracts, including a 10-year, A$330 million contract with Trinity Health - the largest in Pro Medicus's history - plus upgrades to existing contracts with NYU Langone (A$24 million over 5 years) and Duke Health (A$15 million over 5 years).

In HY2026 (half year ended 31 December 2025), Pro Medicus reported record revenue of A$124.8 million (up 28.4% year-on-year), underlying EBIT of A$90.7 million (up 29.7%), and profit after tax of A$171.2 million (up 230.9%). Pro Medicus also disclosed that its five-year forward revenue based on contractual minimums had exceeded A$1 billion for the first time.

More recently, Visage Imaging signed a five-year contract extension with Northwestern Medicine, upgrading the contract value from A$22 million to A$37 million over five years. In March 2026 the company announced two further five-year contract renewals with combined minimum value of approximately A$40 million (including a reported MedStar Health renewal worth ~A$31 million over five years). PME remains debt-free with a large net-cash balance.

The most recent filing (annual) was lodged on 2023-06-30. See [[#Financial Reports]] below.
