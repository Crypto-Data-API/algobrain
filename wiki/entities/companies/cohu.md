Cohu, Inc. (NASDAQ: COHU) is a small-cap supplier of **back-end semiconductor test equipment** — test handlers, contactors/interface products, inspection metrology and (since acquiring Equiptest/DiCon-era assets and tester lines) automated test equipment — used by chipmakers and OSATs to test and sort packaged chips. For traders it is a high-beta, deep-cyclical play on semiconductor unit volumes, especially automotive, industrial and mobile chips, with AI compute test demand an emerging kicker.

## Business Overview

Cohu's revenue splits between **systems** (test handlers, testers, inspection) and a steadier **recurring** stream (~50% in downturns) of contactors, interface boards, spares and services tied to the installed base. Customer exposure skews to automotive and industrial semis (NXP, Infineon, Texas Instruments, STMicroelectronics end markets) plus mobile RF — which is why the 2023–2025 auto/industrial chip inventory correction hit Cohu harder and longer than front-end equipment names. The leading indicator management publishes each quarter is **test-cell utilization** at customers; recoveries in utilization precede new handler orders by roughly two quarters. Competitors include Advantest, Teradyne (testers) and ASMPT (handlers).

## Business Segments / Products

Cohu sits in the **back-end** of semiconductor manufacturing — after wafers are fabricated and packaged, Cohu's gear tests, sorts, handles and inspects the finished chips. Its model deliberately pairs lumpy capital-equipment sales with a sticky recurring stream.

| Segment | What it is | Cyclicality |
|---|---|---|
| **Test handlers** | Mechanical systems that present packaged chips to a tester at temperature, then sort by result | Highly cyclical (capex-driven) |
| **Automated test equipment (testers)** | Electrical test platforms (analog/mixed-signal, RF) acquired/built over time | Cyclical |
| **Contactors & interface products** | Consumable sockets/interface boards that wear out and are replaced | **Recurring**, installed-base tied |
| **Inspection & metrology** | Optical/visual inspection of packaged devices | Mixed |
| **Spares & services** | Aftermarket support on the installed base | **Recurring** |

The key business-model insight: the **recurring** lines (contactors, interface, spares, services) hold up to roughly half of revenue in a downturn, cushioning the deep cyclicality of handler/tester sales. That recurring base is tied to the *installed base* and to *test-cell utilization* — the metric management publishes as a leading indicator. End-market mix is weighted to **automotive and industrial** semis plus **mobile RF**, which is why Cohu's cycle is governed by NXP/Infineon/TI/STMicro inventory behavior rather than by AI-compute demand alone.

## AI Involvement

[[artificial-intelligence|AI]] chip testing and handling equipment: rising test intensity for HBM, advanced packaging and AI accelerators expands back-end test demand per chip, and Cohu has been winning contactor/inspection content tied to AI compute devices.

## Competitive Positioning / Peers

Cohu plays in **back-end test** alongside two much larger pure-play test names and a handler rival. It is the small-cap, handler-and-consumables-weighted option in a field led by tester giants.

| Company | Overlap with Cohu | Scale vs Cohu | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| [[teradyne]] | ATE (testers); broad semi + robotics | Vastly larger | The bellwether tester name; sets sentiment for the whole back-end |
| [[advantest]] | ATE (testers), esp. SoC/memory/HBM | Vastly larger | Big AI/HBM test beneficiary; reports often move Cohu by read-through |
| ASMPT | Handlers, packaging/assembly | Larger | Direct handler competitor on the assembly side |
| FormFactor | Probe cards / interface | Comparable / larger | Adjacent interface-test content |
| Onto Innovation | Inspection / metrology | Comparable / larger | Adjacent inspection overlap |

**How to frame it:** Cohu is leveraged to **chip unit volumes** (especially auto/industrial/mobile), not to AI bit-growth the way Advantest is. That makes it a higher-beta, later-cycle recovery play: when [[teradyne]] and [[advantest]] inflect, Cohu typically follows with a lag tied to the auto/industrial inventory turn. Watching the larger peers' bookings is a leading indicator for Cohu.

## Bull vs Bear Case

**Bull case**
- Deep-cyclical recovery leverage: test-cell utilization is climbing toward the levels that historically trigger handler capacity orders — operating leverage is steep off the trough.
- Recurring revenue (~50% in downturns) cushions the cycle and is growing on services/interface/spares.
- AI/HBM/advanced-packaging test intensity is a secular per-chip content tailwind layered on the cyclical recovery.
- Small cap with high beta — outsized upside if the auto/industrial chip cycle turns convincingly.

**Bear case**
- Still loss-making through the trough; profitability lags the order recovery.
- Auto/industrial inventory correction has been deeper and longer than front-end equipment cycles; a stalled recovery hits Cohu hardest.
- Smaller and less diversified than [[teradyne]] / [[advantest]]; less exposed to the strongest (AI) part of the back-end.
- Thin small-cap liquidity amplifies drawdowns and gap risk.

## Valuation Framework (qualitative)

Cohu is a **deep-cyclical** and cannot be valued on trough earnings — at the bottom it is loss-making, so trailing P/E is meaningless or negative. The honest lens is mid-cycle / normalized earnings power and the inventory-cycle position:

- **Semi-cycle / inventory beta (primary):** Cohu's results are governed by the auto/industrial/mobile chip inventory cycle. Value is highest when bought *into* the trough (utilization rising, orders not yet recovered) and the multiple compresses near peak earnings — the classic "cheap on peak P/E, expensive on trough P/E" cyclical trap.
- **Recurring-revenue floor:** the ~50% recurring base sets a downside on revenue and gross margin, which supports a higher trough valuation than a pure equipment name.
- **Operating leverage:** as systems revenue recovers on top of a fixed cost base, incremental margins are high — small revenue changes swing earnings disproportionately, so the market pays forward for the recovery before it shows in EPS.
- **AI/HBM content kicker:** rising test intensity per chip is a secular adder to normalized earnings power that can justify a structurally higher mid-cycle multiple than the last cycle.

Net: value Cohu on *where it sits in the cycle* and *normalized* earnings, using test-cell utilization and peer bookings as the timing signal — not on the reported (often negative) trailing number.

## Trading & Options Playbook

- **Deep cyclical, leads its own earnings:** COHU historically inflects on the utilization/orders turn, not on reported profits — the stock bottoms before EPS does. Watch test-cell utilization, auto-semi inventory commentary from NXP/Infineon/TI, and book-to-bill.
- **Catalysts calendar:** quarterly earnings and guidance, semi-equipment peer reports ([[teradyne]], [[advantest]] set the tone and front-run Cohu by read-through), auto/industrial chip-cycle data points, and AI/HBM test-intensity wins.
- **Semi-cycle sensitivity:** very high beta to the SOX / [[smh-vaneck-semiconductor-etf|SMH]] tape and to the back-end-test sub-cycle specifically; amplitude is larger than large-cap semis because of the small float and trough losses.
- **Index / ETF membership:** not in the [[s-and-p-500|S&P 500]]; listed on [[nasdaq|NASDAQ]] and held in semiconductor and small-cap baskets, with semicap exposure via the [[smh-vaneck-semiconductor-etf|VanEck Semiconductor ETF (SMH)]] and broad-tech ([[xlk]]) sleeves.
- **Volatility behavior:** small-cap liquidity means options exist but are **thin** — spreads widen and slippage is real; position sizing and defined-risk structures matter more than in large-cap semis.
- **Correlated names:** [[teradyne]], [[advantest]], FormFactor, Onto Innovation; [[amd]]/[[nvidia]] complex sentiment for the AI angle.

## Capital Structure & Dilution

- Operating company with a real installed base and recurring revenue; the cyclical risk is **earnings**, not solvency — Cohu is not a cash-runway story.
- Through-cycle the main equity considerations are stock-based compensation and the occasional bolt-on acquisition (Cohu has grown its tester/handler lines partly via M&A), which can be funded with cash, debt or stock.
- Because trough quarters are loss-making, watch the balance sheet and cash burn at the *bottom* of the cycle, but the recurring revenue floor materially limits downside vs a pure equipment name.

## Risks

- **Cyclicality (primary):** a deeper or longer auto/industrial/mobile inventory correction extends losses; the recovery timing is outside Cohu's control.
- **Customer/end-market concentration:** heavy auto + industrial + mobile-RF exposure (NXP/Infineon/TI/STMicro end demand).
- **Competitive scale gap:** smaller and less AI-levered than [[teradyne]] / [[advantest]].
- **Liquidity:** thin small-cap shares and options amplify drawdowns and gap risk.
- **Profitability lag:** even on a real recovery, GAAP profitability trails the order turn.
