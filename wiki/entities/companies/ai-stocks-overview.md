A curated catalog of 150 publicly traded companies with significant AI involvement that are **not** constituents of the [[s-and-p-500]] index. These span semiconductors, AI software, robotics, healthcare AI, cybersecurity, quantum computing, and more.

For S&P 500 constituents with AI exposure (NVDA, GOOGL, MSFT, etc.), see [[companies-overview]].

## Key Themes Driving AI Stocks

The companies in this catalog are not a monolith — they cluster around a handful of structural theses that drive their share prices:

- **AI infrastructure / data-center capex** — the picks-and-shovels layer: foundries ([[taiwan-semiconductor-manufacturing|TSMC]]), lithography ([[asml-holding|ASML]]), connectivity ([[astera-labs|Astera Labs]]), GPU cloud ([[coreweave|CoreWeave]]), servers ([[supermicro|Super Micro]]), power and cooling. The clearest beneficiaries of the hyperscaler spending wave — but exposed to the [[ai-capex-vs-cash-flow-divergence|capex-vs-cash-flow divergence]] risk if spend outruns returns.
- **AI software / application layer** — companies embedding AI into existing SaaS products ([[snowflake|Snowflake]], [[hubspot|HubSpot]], [[atlassian|Atlassian]]). Thesis: monetising AI features as a premium tier. Risk: AI commoditises their own moat.
- **AI-native cybersecurity** — security that uses and defends against AI ([[sentinelone|SentinelOne]], [[zscaler|Zscaler]], [[crowdstrike|CrowdStrike]]); the cleanest basket expression is [[cibr-first-trust-nasdaq-cybersecurity-etf|CIBR]]. See [[ai-cybersecurity]].
- **AI in healthcare/biotech** — drug discovery and diagnostics ([[recursion-pharmaceuticals|Recursion]], [[tempus-ai|Tempus AI]], [[schrödinger|Schrödinger]]); high optionality, long timelines, binary catalysts.
- **Frontier / speculative compute** — quantum ([[ionq|IonQ]], [[d-wave-quantum|D-Wave]]) and robotics/autonomy ([[symbotic|Symbotic]], [[mobileye-global|Mobileye]]); narrative-driven, pre-revenue or early-revenue, extreme volatility.

## How to Trade the AI Theme

Different vehicles express the AI thesis with different risk profiles:

| Approach | Vehicle | Tradeoff |
|----------|---------|----------|
| **Single-name conviction** | Direct equity in a specific layer (e.g. [[coreweave\|CRWV]], [[snowflake\|SNOW]]) | Highest upside and idiosyncratic risk; requires a thesis on which company wins |
| **Thematic basket** | Sector ETFs (e.g. [[cibr-first-trust-nasdaq-cybersecurity-etf\|CIBR]] for AI-cyber) | Captures the theme, diluted single-name risk, small expense drag |
| **Index / mega-cap proxy** | [[nasdaq\|Nasdaq 100]] / [[s-and-p-500\|S&P 500]] (NVDA, MSFT, GOOGL) | Lowest risk, but mega-cap AI exposure is partly diluted by non-AI holdings |
| **Options expression** | Liquid single-name options on the most active names | Defined risk / leverage; see [[options-stocks-overview]] and [[options]] |
| **Speculative torque** | Small/micro-cap quantum, robotics, AI-meme names | Lottery-ticket payoff profile; size accordingly |

Practical considerations: many of these names carry **high [[implied-volatility]]** and move 10-20% on earnings, so position sizing and the [[ai-capex-vs-cash-flow-divergence|capex-vs-cash-flow]] macro overlay matter more than for broad-market holdings. For Australian/[[smsf]] investors, US-listed AI names and thematic ETFs are accessed via international brokerage ([[interactive-brokers]], [[stake]], [[morgans]]) — see [[australian-investing]].

## Risks

- **Capex overbuild** — the central bear case: hyperscaler AI spend may outrun monetisable demand, hitting the infrastructure layer first (see [[ai-capex-vs-cash-flow-divergence]])
- **Valuation / multiple compression** — many names trade on high revenue multiples vulnerable to rising real rates (see [[uup]] / [[us-dollar]] for the rate-and-dollar macro backdrop)
- **Commoditisation** — falling model and inference costs can erode application-layer pricing power
- **Concentration & correlation** — AI names are highly correlated; a basket of 20 AI stocks is closer to one bet than twenty
- **Narrative reflexivity** — frontier/speculative names are flow- and story-driven and unwind violently when sentiment turns
- **Single-name blow-up risk** — outages, security incidents, and missed guidance produce sudden 20%+ drawdowns
- **Survivorship** — this catalog is a snapshot; small/micro-caps are subject to dilution, delisting, and acquisition
