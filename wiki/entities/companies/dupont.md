DuPont (NYSE: DD) is a US specialty-chemicals and multi-industrial company in the [[materials|Materials]] sector and a constituent of the [[s-and-p-500]] index. For traders, the defining event is the **November 2025 spin-off of its electronics business as Qnity Electronics (NYSE: Q)**, which left "new DuPont" as a slimmer healthcare, water and diversified-industrials franchise — so pre-spin financials (including the Fundamentals below, period end 2024-12-31) are not directly comparable to the post-spin company.

## Business Overview & Segments

The modern DuPont was created in 2017 via the Dow–DuPont merger and 2019 three-way split (Dow → commodity chemicals, [[corteva|Corteva]] → agriculture, DuPont → specialty products); the heritage E. I. du Pont de Nemours dates to 1802. Post the **Qnity Electronics spin (completed 2025-11-01)**, "new DuPont" is a slimmer healthcare, water and diversified-industrials franchise — the high-margin electronics/semiconductor-materials business that historically dominated the growth narrative now trades separately as Qnity (NYSE: Q). **Critical data caveat: the pre-spin FY2024 financials in the Fundamentals block below still include the electronics business and are therefore *not* directly comparable to the continuing post-spin company** — screen the legacy metrics with that discontinuity in mind.

| Segment / line | What it is | Role in the post-spin portfolio (qualitative) |
|---|---|---|
| **Healthcare & Water Technologies** | Tyvek medical/sterile packaging, Liveo biopharma silicone consumables; reverse-osmosis membranes and ion-exchange resins for water filtration | The growth and margin anchor — secular, less-cyclical demand from biopharma, medical and water-treatment end markets |
| **Diversified Industrials** | Tyvek building-envelope wrap, shelter solutions, adhesives, and safety/protection lines | More construction- and housing-cycle exposed; the cyclical sleeve of continuing operations |
| **Aramids (Kevlar, Nomex)** | High-strength/heat-resistant fibers for protective gear, aerospace and industrial uses | Being **divested** as part of portfolio simplification — a pending catalyst, not a continuing core |
| **Qnity Electronics (NYSE: Q)** | Semiconductor materials and electronics solutions | **Spun off 2025-11-01** — no longer part of DuPont; listed here only to mark the exposure DD shed |

DuPont's durable franchises are spec-in materials sold into qualified, regulated applications (medical, water, aerospace) where the customer designs the material into the product. Legacy PFAS liabilities (shared with Chemours and Corteva under the 2021 cost-sharing agreement, including the ~$1.2B water-utility settlement of 2023) remain a long-tail legal overhang across the whole complex.

## 2025–2026 Developments

- **Qnity Electronics spin-off completed 2025-11-01** — shareholders of record 2025-10-22 received 1 Qnity share per 2 DD shares; Qnity (semiconductor materials and electronics solutions) began regular-way NYSE trading under ticker "Q" on 2025-11-03.
- **Water retained** — management abandoned the earlier plan to also separate Water & Protection, keeping water as core for cash-flow stability.
- **Q4 2025 / FY2026 outlook (reported 2026-02)** — earnings beat with FY2026 adjusted EPS guidance of $2.25–$2.30 (above ~$2.17 consensus); Q1 2026 adjusted EPS target ~$0.48. Healthcare & Water organic growth (~3%) is offsetting soft construction-linked Diversified Industrials demand.
- **Aramids divestiture** — sale process for Kevlar/Nomex progressing; completion is the next major portfolio catalyst.

## Economic Moat & Competitive Position

DuPont's [[economic-moat|economic moat]] is narrower than a software or platform franchise but real, and it rests on materials science rather than scale alone:

- **Brand and spec-in advantage** — Tyvek (medical/sterile packaging and building envelope) and the heritage Kevlar/Nomex brands are designed into customers' products and specifications; once a material is qualified, it is sticky.
- **Formulation IP and regulatory qualification** — medical packaging, biopharma consumables (Liveo) and water-treatment membranes require lengthy customer validation and, in healthcare, regulatory sign-off. That qualification process creates real [[switching-costs]]: requalifying an alternate supplier is slow, costly and risk-laden, which protects pricing and share in the Healthcare & Water franchise.
- **Application engineering / systems** — DuPont sells solutions (filtration systems, building-envelope systems) rather than bulk commodity, supporting higher and more stable margins than commodity chemicals.

The moat is **uneven across the portfolio**, and the company's `fred_profile` is candidly *Cyclical / commodity*. Healthcare & Water is the genuinely defensible, secular sleeve; Diversified Industrials is more exposed to the construction/housing cycle and competes closer to commodity economics. Net of the Qnity spin, the bull thesis is that the residual mix tilts the whole company toward the higher-quality, less-cyclical end — but materials remain partly cyclical and price-takers on input costs.

## Growth Drivers & Catalysts

- **Healthcare & Water organic growth** — biopharma and medical demand for Tyvek/Liveo consumables, plus secular water-filtration demand (scarcity, reuse, industrial water treatment) underpin the franchise's ~mid-single-digit organic growth ambition.
- **Water-treatment secular tailwind** — desalination, municipal reuse and industrial water needs are a long-duration demand pool less tied to the macro cycle.
- **Aramids divestiture completion** — a discrete portfolio catalyst that crystallizes value and further simplifies the company.
- **Post-spin re-rating** — as a slimmer, higher-quality multi-industrial, DuPont can earn a higher multiple if it demonstrates more stable, less-cyclical earnings.
- **FY2026 guidance execution** — delivery against the FY2026 adjusted EPS guide of **$2.25–$2.30** (above ~$2.17 consensus) is the near-term proof point; Healthcare & Water organic growth (~3%) offsetting soft construction-linked Diversified Industrials demand is the swing.
- **Recurring catalysts** — quarterly guidance prints, the Aramids sale close, index/ETF rebalancing flows around the spin, and PFAS litigation headlines.

## Bull Case vs Bear Case

### Bull case
- Post-Qnity, DuPont is a higher-quality, less-cyclical multi-industrial — the mix tilts toward defensible Healthcare & Water franchises.
- Water filtration and biopharma/medical packaging are secular, sticky, regulation-gated demand pools with real [[switching-costs]].
- Portfolio simplification (Aramids sale, electronics spin) sharpens the story and can support a re-rating toward water/healthcare-specialist multiples.
- Conservative balance sheet (low D/E in the legacy metrics; interest coverage above Fred's bar) gives room to invest and return cash through the cycle.
- FY2026 EPS guidance above consensus signals operating momentum despite soft construction demand.

### Bear case
- Diversified Industrials is construction/housing-cycle exposed; a building downturn pressures a meaningful sleeve of earnings.
- PFAS legacy litigation (shared with Chemours/Corteva) is a long-tail, hard-to-size legal overhang that can resurface in headlines and settlements.
- Materials are partly cyclical and price-takers on input costs — the `fred_profile` *Cyclical / commodity* tag is earned, and through-cycle margins can disappoint.
- Separation execution risk: stranded costs and dis-synergies from carving out Qnity, plus deal/closing risk on the Aramids divestiture.
- The re-rating thesis is not guaranteed — if earnings prove as cyclical as before, the multiple stays anchored to the chemicals group.

## Key Risks

- **PFAS legacy liabilities** — shared with Chemours and [[corteva]] under the 2021 cost-sharing agreement, including the ~$1.2B water-utility settlement of 2023; the ultimate liability is uncertain and a long-tail overhang.
- **Construction / housing cycle** — Diversified Industrials (Tyvek building envelope, shelter, adhesives) is levered to building activity; a slowdown directly hits volumes and mix.
- **Commodity input costs & cyclicality** — feedstock and energy costs, plus broad chemicals-cycle demand, can compress through-cycle margins below current-period reads.
- **Separation execution / dis-synergies** — stranded costs, dis-synergies and operational disruption from the Qnity carve-out and the pending Aramids sale.
- **FX** — a multinational with material non-US revenue; currency swings move reported results.
- **Data discontinuity for screens** — pre-spin FY2024 financials include the divested electronics business, so static-metric screens (see Fundamentals) misstate the continuing company.

## Valuation & How the Stock Trades

DuPont trades as a re-rating story: post the Qnity spin the market is asked to price a slimmer, higher-quality, less-cyclical multi-industrial rather than a diversified chemicals conglomerate. Mechanically (qualitative — no precise current figures beyond the data block below):

- **Re-rating potential** — the bull case is multiple expansion toward water/healthcare-specialist peers if earnings prove more stable; the bear case anchors the multiple to the cyclical chemicals group.
- **Dividend** — DuPont pays a meaningful dividend (see the per-share figure in Fundamentals); the 5-year average yield sits in the ~2–3% range, making it a modest income plus capital-return name rather than a pure growth stock.
- **Beta / cyclicality** — a moderately cyclical [[materials]] name; it trades with the chemicals group and the ISM/manufacturing and housing cycles rather than as a defensive.
- **Index / ETF flows** — [[s-and-p-500]] membership means passive ownership; the Qnity spin and the pending Aramids divestiture drive index/ETF rebalancing flows worth watching around the events.
- **Options** — liquid single-name [[options]]; implied volatility tends to lift into quarterly guidance prints and around the discrete portfolio catalysts.
