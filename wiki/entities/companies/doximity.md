Doximity Inc. (NYSE: DOCS) operates the largest verified professional network for U.S. healthcare professionals — often called "LinkedIn for doctors" — monetized chiefly through pharmaceutical marketing and hospital recruiting. For traders it is a rare profitable health-tech name (adjusted EBITDA margins above 50%) whose stock makes outsized moves on quarterly guidance, making it a popular earnings-options underlying.

## Business Overview

Doximity's network reaches over 80% of U.S. physicians, who use it for medical news, secure messaging, telehealth dialing, scheduling (Amion), and AI documentation tools (Doximity GPT). Revenue comes mostly from pharmaceutical manufacturers and health systems buying targeted marketing and hiring solutions — making the stock sensitive to pharma digital-ad budgets rather than patient volumes. The model is asset-light with very high incremental margins and an active buyback.

## 2025–2026 Developments

- **FQ3 FY2026 (quarter ended 2025-12-31):** revenue US$185.1M, up 10% YoY from $168.6M; FY2026 guidance raised to US$642.5–643.5M revenue and US$355.5–356.5M adjusted EBITDA (~55% margin).
- **FY2026 (ended 2026-03-31):** revenue came in at US$644.9M, up 13% from US$570.4M in FY2025.
- Management called 2026 an "AI investment year," pushing AI workflow tools (Doximity GPT, AI scribing) to deepen physician engagement; announced a partnership with Aledade and a new prescribing feature.

## Business Segments & Products

Doximity is a two-sided network: physicians on one side (the audience), pharma manufacturers and health systems on the other (the payers). Revenue is overwhelmingly subscription-based from the latter, with the product suite designed to deepen physician engagement so the audience stays sticky and valuable.

| Product / module | Users | Monetization |
|---|---|---|
| **Professional network / newsfeed** | Physicians (80%+ of U.S. doctors per file) | Engagement engine; not directly monetized |
| **Pharma marketing** | Pharmaceutical manufacturers | Largest revenue line — targeted digital marketing |
| **Hiring / recruiting** | Health systems, hospitals | Subscription recruiting solutions |
| **Telehealth & workflow** | Physicians | Secure messaging, telehealth dialer, e-fax |
| **Amion (scheduling)** | Hospitals/physician groups | Scheduling software (acquired) |
| **Doximity GPT / AI tools** | Physicians | AI documentation/scribing — 2026 engagement investment |

The model is asset-light with very high incremental margins (per file, adjusted EBITDA margin ~55%) and an active buyback. The strategic risk is that revenue concentrates in the top-20 pharma advertisers, so growth tracks pharma digital-ad budgets rather than patient or physician volumes.

## Competitive Positioning & Peers

Doximity is unusual among health-tech: it is profitable and high-margin, in contrast to cash-burning telehealth and care-delivery peers. Its true competition is less other physician networks (it dominates that) and more the broader pool of pharma marketing channels.

| Company | Ticker | Model | Contrast with Doximity |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Doximity** | DOCS | Physician network + pharma marketing SaaS | Profitable, ~55% EBITDA margin, asset-light |
| [[teladoc]] | TDOC | Virtual care delivery | Capital-intensive care delivery; profitability-challenged |
| Veeva ([[veeva-systems]]) | VEEV | Life-sciences CRM/cloud | Sells software *to* pharma; adjacent, not direct |
| Phreesia (PHR) | PHR | Patient intake + pharma "point-of-care" marketing | Competes for pharma marketing dollars |
| LinkedIn (private/MSFT) | — | General professional network | Generic analog ("LinkedIn for doctors") |

Versus [[teladoc]], the contrast is stark: Doximity monetizes attention with software-like margins, while Teladoc carries the cost of delivering care. Read-throughs for Doximity come more from pharma marketing-budget commentary than from telehealth peers.

## Bull vs Bear Case

**Bull case**
- Rare *profitable* health-tech name with ~55% adjusted-EBITDA margins (per file) and strong free cash flow.
- Near-monopoly verified physician network (80%+ of U.S. doctors) — a defensible, hard-to-replicate moat.
- Asset-light, high incremental margins, active buyback returning capital.
- AI tools (Doximity GPT, scribing) deepen engagement and add new monetization surfaces.

**Bear case**
- Revenue concentration in top-20 pharma advertisers — a digital-ad pullback hits growth directly.
- Growth has moderated to low-double-digits (per file: FY2026 +13%); decelerating growth pressures a premium multiple.
- "2026 AI investment year" (per file) trades near-term margin for engagement — execution risk.
- Cyclically exposed to pharma marketing budgets, which flex with macro and drug-launch cycles.

## Capital Structure & Dilution Risk

In contrast to the cash-burning biotechs in its wiki neighborhood, Doximity is profitable and free-cash-flow generative (per file), with no need to tap capital markets for survival. The capital-structure story is the *reverse* of dilution: an active buyback shrinks the share count over time. The offsets to monitor are stock-based compensation (common in software) and founder/insider ownership (CEO Jeff Tangney is a co-founder), plus any dual-class governance. No dividend; capital is returned via repurchases.

## Risks

- **Customer concentration** — dependence on top-20 pharma advertisers; a budget pullback hits growth directly.
- **Growth deceleration** — a maturing growth rate (per file, +13% FY2026) pressures a premium valuation.
- **Cyclicality** — pharma marketing budgets flex with macro conditions and drug-launch cycles.
- **AI-investment execution** — the "2026 AI investment year" trades near-term margin for engagement.
- **Competition for ad dollars** — pharma marketing spend can shift to other channels (point-of-care, search, social).
- **Valuation/multiple** — a high-multiple SaaS name is sensitive to rate and sentiment shifts.
