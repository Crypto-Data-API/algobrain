Archer Aviation Inc. (NYSE: ACHR) is a pre-revenue eVTOL (electric vertical take-off and landing) aircraft developer building the four-passenger **Midnight** air taxi. For traders it is a high-volatility, milestone-driven story stock: the equity trades almost entirely on FAA certification progress, launch-market announcements (UAE, LA28 Olympics), defense optionality, and dilution risk — not on financials.

## Business Overview

Founded in 2018 and listed via SPAC in 2021, Archer designs and manufactures Midnight, targeting short urban hops (~20-50 miles) as a helicopter replacement. Manufacturing is at its high-volume facility in Covington, Georgia, built with strategic partner [[stellantis]] (also an investor and contract-manufacturing collaborator). United Airlines holds a conditional order book, and Archer has launch agreements across the UAE, Japan, Korea, and Indonesia. A defense vertical (including work with Anduril on hybrid VTOL aircraft) gives the company a second, militarized revenue path. Principal competitor: [[joby-aviation]] (NYSE: JOBY); both are racing to be first to certified US commercial eVTOL service.

## 2025-2026 Developments

- **FAA Phase 3 closed (April 2026)**: Archer became the first eVTOL company to close Phase 3 of the FAA's four-phase Type Certification process for Midnight, unlocking **Type Inspection Authorization (TIA)** — FAA personnel can now fly aboard during "for-credit" certification testing. Initial US operations are expected in 2026. (Archer Q1 2026 results, May 2026)
- **White House eIPP**: Archer was selected as the air-taxi partner in 3 winning applications spanning 8 states (including Florida, Texas, New York) under the federal eVTOL Integration Pilot Program, accelerating pre-certification US operations.
- **LA28**: named the **Official Air Taxi Provider of the 2028 LA Olympic Games**, anchoring California operations at Hawthorne Airport near LAX.
- **UAE commercialization first**: Midnight entered the UAE GCAA's Restricted Type Certificate program in May 2026 with **Abu Dhabi Aviation** as operating partner; paid demonstration flights have begun in Abu Dhabi and the GCAA is on track to certify Midnight as early as Q3 2026 — likely the world's first commercial Midnight market.
- **Funding**: repeated large equity raises through 2024-2025 (including an ~$850M raise in June 2025) keep liquidity strong by eVTOL standards but make dilution the recurring cost of the story.

## Business Segments / Products

Archer is best understood as one product platform (the Midnight aircraft) wrapped in three go-to-market verticals: a civil **Urban Air Mobility (UAM)** network, a **manufacturing** engine, and a nascent **defense** vertical. None yet generates meaningful revenue, so each is valued as optionality rather than a cash stream.

| Segment / product | What it is | Status & key facts | Strategic role |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Midnight** (the aircraft) | Four-passenger + pilot eVTOL air taxi; twelve electric tilt-rotor propellers, battery-electric powertrain, designed for short ~20-50 mile urban hops with rapid back-to-back recharging | The single certification and manufacturing focus; helicopter replacement on noise, cost, and emissions | The whole equity thesis rests on this airframe achieving FAA Type Certification |
| **Urban Air Mobility (UAM) network** | Planned ride-by-app air-taxi service flying between vertiports near airports, stadiums, and city cores | United Airlines holds a conditional order book; launch markets across UAE, Japan, Korea, Indonesia; LA28 Olympics anchor for California ops | Converts the aircraft into a recurring-revenue mobility service — the long-term bull case |
| **Manufacturing** | High-volume final-assembly plant in Covington, Georgia | Built with strategic partner [[stellantis]], which provides contract-manufacturing scale, capital, and automotive-grade production expertise | De-risks scale-up; a manufacturing moat vs peers that must build a supply chain from scratch |
| **Defense vertical** | Hybrid-electric VTOL aircraft for military use cases | Partnership with Anduril on a hybrid VTOL platform; a second, dual-use revenue path | Adds non-civil optionality and a potential earlier-revenue channel less dependent on FAA timelines |

The vertical integration with [[stellantis]] (manufacturing + capital) and the [[autonomous-vehicles|autonomy]]-adjacent flight-control software stack are the two structural differentiators most often cited by bulls.

## Competitive Positioning / Peers

The certified-US-eVTOL race is effectively a two-horse contest between Archer and [[joby-aviation]], with a long tail of domestic and foreign challengers at varying stages of distress or commercialization. The variables that matter: position in the FAA Type Certification queue, balance-sheet depth, manufacturing readiness, and the strength of strategic backers.

Archer's competitive case is built on (1) a deep-pocketed automotive manufacturing partner in [[stellantis]], (2) one of the strongest cash positions in the sector, and (3) the parallel UAE Restricted Type Certificate path that could deliver first commercial revenue ahead of full US certification. The chief risk is that [[joby-aviation]] — backed by Toyota — reaches certified US service first, capturing first-mover network and order-book advantages. The broader sector lesson, written by [[lilium]]'s troubles, is that certification timelines routinely outrun cash runways.

## Bull vs Bear Case

**Bull case**
- First-mover optionality: leading or co-leading the FAA Type Certification race, with the UAE RTC path offering earlier commercial revenue.
- Manufacturing moat via the [[stellantis]] partnership and the Covington, GA plant — scale-up is partly de-risked vs peers.
- Multiple revenue legs: civil UAM network, defense (Anduril hybrid VTOL), and international launch markets.
- Sector-leading liquidity buys time that under-capitalized rivals (see [[lilium]]) lack.
- Marquee anchors — United order book, LA28 Olympics, eIPP city selections — create a steady catalyst calendar.

**Bear case**
- Pre-revenue: the entire valuation is forward optionality; any certification slip resets the thesis.
- eVTOL history is one of repeated timeline pushes; final FAA Type Certification and safe scaled operations remain unproven at commercial scale.
- Chronic cash burn funded by serial dilution — the recurring cost of staying in the race.
- Order book is largely conditional/non-binding and can shrink.
- A well-funded [[joby-aviation]] could win the certification race; new-category safety events anywhere in eVTOL could freeze the whole sector.

## Valuation Framework

Archer is a **pre-revenue story stock**; conventional earnings or cash-flow multiples do not apply. Value is assigned to:
- **Certification milestones** — each closed FAA phase, TIA flight-test progress, and the eventual Type Certificate re-rates the equity.
- **Launch-market optionality** — the UAE RTC path, eIPP city selections, LA28, and international agreements are priced as call options on future networks.
- **Order backlog** — the United order book and partner agreements are largely *conditional/non-binding*, so they support narrative more than guaranteed revenue.
- **Defense optionality** — the Anduril hybrid-VTOL work adds a dual-use call option.

The practical valuation discipline is **cash runway vs burn**: with quarterly burn in the low hundreds of millions (per the Key Financials above) and a multi-billion-dollar cash position, the market judges whether liquidity outlasts the road to certified revenue. The stock effectively trades on an extreme implied price-to-future-sales narrative, and **dilution is a persistent drag** — each raise that extends runway also lowers per-share claims on the eventual prize. (No earnings multiple is meaningful here; figures beyond those in Key Financials are not asserted.)

## Capital Structure & Dilution Risk

Serial equity issuance is the defining feature of Archer's capital structure and the recurring cost of funding a pre-revenue program. The company has tapped at-the-market (ATM) programs and follow-on offerings repeatedly — including the **~$850M raise in June 2025** noted above — to keep one of the sector's strongest cash balances. Strategic and high-profile investors ([[stellantis]], United Airlines, and Stanley Druckenmiller among them) lend balance-sheet credibility and validation, but the through-line for shareholders is that runway is bought with dilution: share count grows over time and each raise dilutes the eventual per-share value of certified operations. Convertible or warrant overhang and any future raises into strength remain the key capital-structure watch items.

- Extremely high-beta retail-favorite; large options market relative to its size, elevated IV, frequent short squeezes; often trades as a pair/basket with [[joby-aviation]].

## Risks

- **Certification slippage:** the dominant risk — eVTOL's track record is repeated timeline pushes, and any delay to final FAA Type Certification directly resets the equity thesis.
- **Cash burn & dilution:** chronic operating burn funded by serial equity raises; runway depends on continued market access, and each raise dilutes shareholders.
- **Manufacturing scale-up:** translating prototype assembly into reliable high-volume production at Covington is unproven at commercial scale.
- **Early-operations safety events:** a new aircraft category means any accident — to Archer or a peer — could freeze regulatory progress sector-wide.
- **Competition:** a Toyota-backed [[joby-aviation]] reaching certified US service first; EHang already operating in China; broader peer pressure.
- **Order-book risk:** the United and partner order books are largely conditional/non-binding and can be reduced or cancelled.
