Algorhythm Holdings, Inc. (NASDAQ: RIME) is a nano-cap holding company — formerly The Singing Machine Company (consumer karaoke) — that sold its legacy karaoke business in 2025 and pivoted entirely to **SemiCab, an AI-enabled freight logistics optimization platform**. For traders it is a speculative, heavily diluted shell-turnaround stock with repeated reverse splits and Nasdaq-compliance risk, not a fundamentals investment.

## What the Company Is / Product (Detail)

After a multi-decade history as a consumer-electronics karaoke maker, Algorhythm is now a **pure-play AI freight-logistics company built around SemiCab**:

- **SemiCab** applies [[artificial-intelligence|AI]]/[[machine-learning|ML]] to **truckload freight orchestration** — predicting and matching loads to reduce empty miles and improve truck utilization for retailers, brands and carriers (operations in the US and India).
- The economic model is **brokered freight**: it carries the low gross margins typical of freight brokerage, with the AI thesis being that better load-matching can lift utilisation and, eventually, margin at scale.
- The legacy **Singing Machine** karaoke hardware business was divested in 2025, completing the pivot.

## Business Overview & Pivot History

- Decades-long history as The Singing Machine Company, a Fort Lauderdale consumer-electronics karaoke maker; renamed **Algorhythm Holdings** in 2024 after acquiring the SemiCab logistics-AI platform.
- **Feb 2025: 1-for-200 reverse stock split** (effective 2025-02-10, approved at the 2025-01-13 annual meeting) to regain Nasdaq minimum-bid compliance.
- **Aug 2025: sold the Singing Machine karaoke business to Stingray for $4.5M**, completing the pivot to pure-play AI freight logistics.
- Also announced a six-month suspension of new capital-raising activities alongside the reverse-split plan — an acknowledgment of dilution fatigue among holders.
- FY2025 10-K: posted a loss, with the thesis resting on scaling SemiCab's contracted freight volume.

## Competitive Positioning / Peers

SemiCab competes in **AI-enabled freight brokerage / digital freight matching**, a crowded space dominated by far larger, better-capitalised players. As a nano-cap it is a marginal participant.

| Company | Type | Positioning vs. RIME/SemiCab |
|---|---|---|
| **Algorhythm / SemiCab (RIME)** | Nano-cap AI freight broker | Sub-scale, loss-making, heavily diluted; marginal participant |
| **Uber Freight** | Digital freight platform | Massive scale and capital; direct functional competitor |
| **C.H. Robinson** | Freight brokerage incumbent | Industry-leading scale, profitable, deep carrier network |
| **Convoy (defunct)** | Digital freight broker | Well-funded digital broker that nonetheless failed — a cautionary peer for the model's economics |
| **RXO / Echo Global** | Tech-enabled brokers | Scaled tech-enabled brokerages with real volume |

The competitive reality: digital freight matching is capital-intensive and low-margin even for well-funded players (Convoy's collapse is the cautionary tale); a nano-cap turnaround has little structural advantage.
