Cipher Mining Inc. (NASDAQ: CIFR) began as a Texas-focused [[bitcoin]] miner and has transformed into one of the clearest "miner-to-AI-data-center" conversion stories, with roughly $9 billion of contracted AI/HPC hosting revenue signed with Amazon Web Services and Google-backstopped Fluidstack across 2025-2026. It remains a popular, high-beta [[options]] vehicle that now trades on AI-infrastructure news as much as BTC price.

## Business Overview

Cipher (founded 2021, listed via SPAC, backed by Bitfury) controls a pipeline of large low-cost power sites in Texas — Odessa (its original mining flagship), Barber Lake, Black Pearl and others. Its edge is cheap contracted power and shovel-ready acreage in ERCOT, the asset hyperscalers are short of. Pro-forma business mix after the 2025-2026 deals is roughly one-third bitcoin mining, two-thirds AI/HPC hosting.

## Business Segments

Cipher is mid-transformation from a single-segment miner to a contracted AI/HPC landlord with a mining cash-cow attached:

| Segment | What it is | Trading lens |
|---|---|---|
| **Bitcoin Mining (Odessa)** | Self-mining of [[bitcoin]] at the original ~300 MW-class Odessa flagship, providing interim operating cash flow while AI campuses are built. | The shrinking-but-real BTC torque leg; hashprice- and difficulty-sensitive like the rest of the miner basket. |
| **AI / HPC Hosting (the growth engine)** | Turnkey data-center capacity leased to hyperscalers/neoclouds — AWS (Black Pearl, Barber Lake-class campuses) and Fluidstack/Google (Barber Lake). Long-dated, take-or-pay-style rent. | The dominant valuation driver — ~$9B contracted backlog over 10-15 year terms reframes the stock as a data-center landlord, not a miner. |
| **Power & Land Portfolio** | Contracted ERCOT power and shovel-ready Texas acreage (Odessa, Barber Lake, Black Pearl). | The scarce input hyperscalers cannot self-source quickly; each MW is convertible to high-margin AI rent. |

Key metrics: **contracted MW and revenue backlog**, **energized vs. under-construction capacity**, **delivery-milestone dates**, **lease counterparty credit (Amazon, Google backstop)**, and residual **mining hashrate/hashprice**.

## Bull vs Bear Case

**Bull case**
- Roughly $9B of contracted, long-dated AI/HPC revenue with blue-chip backstops (Amazon direct; Google behind Fluidstack) transforms the cash-flow profile and lowers counterparty risk.
- Shovel-ready, low-cost ERCOT power is exactly the scarce input hyperscalers need — pricing power on incremental MW.
- Optionality on further hyperscaler leases against the remaining power pipeline.
- Retained mining provides interim cash flow and a BTC kicker.

**Bear case**
- Aggressive 2026 delivery schedule (AWS phases, Fluidstack Phase I) carries real construction/execution risk; slippage hits the re-rating thesis.
- Funded with multi-billion-dollar, often high-yield/junk-rated debt — leverage and refinancing risk if rates rise or delivery slips.
- Equity issuance (including Google's stake) and convertible/debt structures dilute or encumber holders.
- Residual mining exposes the stock to halving-compressed hashprice and BTC drawdowns.

## Valuation Framework

Cipher is best framed as a **contracted-cash-flow data-center developer with a mining stub**:

- **Contracted AI/HPC backlog** — discounted value of long-dated lease rent; the dominant input. Quality depends on counterparty credit and delivery certainty.
- **EV per MW (energized and contracted)** — the cross-peer yardstick as the market prices power-into-compute conversion; increasingly the metric that matters more than EV per EH/s.
- **Lease-level economics** — rent per MW, term, escalators and EBITDA margin (data-center leases carry far higher and more stable margins than mining).
- **Net debt and cost of capital** — heavy reliance on senior secured/high-yield notes makes interest cost and leverage central to equity value.
- **Residual mining value** — Odessa's hashrate × hashprice, valued on a miner multiple, plus any BTC treasury.
- **Execution discount** — until milestones are delivered, the market applies a haircut to backlog; each on-time delivery narrows it.

## 2025–2026 Developments

- **AWS mega-lease (2025)**: ~$5.5 billion, 15-year lease with Amazon Web Services for 300 MW of turnkey AI capacity, delivered in two phases beginning July 2026 with rent commencing August 2026.
- **Fluidstack/Google deals (2025)**: 168 MW, 10-year AI hosting agreement at Barber Lake worth ~$3.0 billion, with Google backstopping $1.4 billion of Fluidstack's obligations and taking a ~5.4% equity stake in Cipher; expanded in November 2025 by a further 56 MW (~$830 million), lifting Google's backstop to $1.73 billion. Phase I delivery to Fluidstack targeted September 2026.
- **Black Pearl financing (Feb 2026)**: subsidiary Black Pearl Compute priced a $2 billion senior secured note offering for a second Amazon-leased Texas campus backed by a 15-year, 300 MW AWS lease; a further $810 million high-yield raise followed for additional Amazon capacity.
- **Key financials, as of June 2026 (approximate)**: contracted AI/HPC revenue backlog of roughly $9+ billion over 10-15 year terms; multi-billion-dollar debt raises fund construction; bitcoin mining (Odessa, ~300 MW class) continues to provide interim cash flow.

## Capital Structure & Dilution

Cipher funds its data-center build with a debt-heavy structure layered on equity:

- **Senior secured / high-yield notes** — multi-billion-dollar raises at the project level (e.g. the Black Pearl Compute notes for AWS-leased capacity) fund construction; introduces leverage and refinancing risk.
- **Strategic equity** — Google's ~5.4% stake (taken alongside the Fluidstack backstop) aligns a hyperscaler but adds shares.
- **Lease backstops as credit enhancement** — Google's $1.4-1.73B backstop of Fluidstack obligations materially de-risks that contracted stream and improves financing terms.
- **Leverage to delivery** — debt service is fixed while contracted rent only switches on at delivery, so the equity is sensitive to milestone timing; delays compress the window between cash out and cash in.

## Risks

- Construction and execution risk on aggressive 2026 delivery schedules (AWS phases, Fluidstack Phase I).
- High leverage and refinancing exposure from junk-rated/senior-secured debt raises.
- Counterparty/concentration risk on a small number of large hyperscaler tenants.
- BTC price and post-halving hashprice compression on the residual mining segment.
- ERCOT grid/power-curtailment and Texas regulatory risk.
- Dilution from equity and convertible/strategic-stake issuance.
