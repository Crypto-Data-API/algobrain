Rekor Systems, Inc. (NASDAQ: REKR) is a small/micro-cap technology company that applies [[artificial-intelligence|AI]] and [[computer-vision-overview|computer vision]] to roadway and traffic intelligence — vehicle recognition, license-plate reading, traffic analytics and public-safety alerts. For traders it is a speculative, high-volatility AI-theme small cap: pre-profit and cash-burn-dependent, its share price is driven by contract wins with state DOTs and public-safety agencies, revenue-growth prints and financing/dilution events rather than earnings.

## Business / Product (Detail)

Rekor's platform ingests live camera and sensor feeds and uses computer-vision models to identify vehicles (make, model, colour, plate), detect incidents, and generate real-time traffic and safety analytics. Core products serve three markets:

- **Transportation management** — DOT traffic data, congestion monitoring, and incident detection for state and local departments of transportation.
- **Public safety** — vehicle-of-interest alerts, AMBER/wanted-vehicle notifications for police and public-safety agencies.
- **Urban mobility** — tolling-adjacent and curb/parking analytics.

It positions itself as infrastructure for "AI roadway intelligence," adjacent to the broader smart-infrastructure and [[autonomous-vehicles|autonomous-vehicle]] data ecosystem.

### AI involvement

Rekor's platform ingests live camera and sensor feeds and uses computer-vision models to identify vehicles (make, model, colour, plate), detect incidents, and generate real-time traffic and safety analytics. The technical moat — to the extent one exists — is the combination of edge/camera ingestion, vehicle-recognition models, and a recurring-revenue SaaS layer on top of government data feeds.

## Business Model & Customer Mix

Revenue comes from software subscriptions, hardware/sensor deployments and professional services, increasingly skewed toward recurring SaaS as the model matures. Customers are predominantly government — state and local DOTs, police and public-safety agencies — which means **long sales cycles, lumpy contract timing and budget/procurement dependence**. As a sub-scale company it has historically run operating losses and consumed cash, relying on periodic equity/convertible financing.

The government-customer concentration is double-edged: contracts are sticky and credit-worthy once won, but **sales cycles are long, timing is lumpy, and revenue is hostage to government budget and procurement cycles**.
