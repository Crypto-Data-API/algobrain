Rarible is a multi-chain [[nft|NFT]] marketplace launched in 2020 that pioneered the concept of marketplace mining through its $RARI governance token. Founded by Alexei Falin and Alex Salnikov, Rarible operates an open, permissionless listing model where any creator can mint and sell NFTs without an application or approval process, in contrast to curated platforms like [[superrare|SuperRare]] and [[knownorigin|KnownOrigin]].

## History

Rarible was founded in 2020 by Alexei Falin and Alex Salnikov, both from Moscow, Russia. The platform launched on [[ethereum|Ethereum]] as an open marketplace where anyone could create, buy, and sell NFTs without requiring approval from the platform.

In July 2020, Rarible introduced the $RARI governance token and pioneered the concept of "marketplace mining" — a mechanism that distributed $RARI tokens to active buyers and sellers on the platform as a reward for trading activity. This was one of the earliest applications of token incentives to bootstrap a marketplace, drawing parallels to DeFi yield farming strategies that were popular at the time (see [[yield-farming]]).

The marketplace mining approach successfully drove early adoption, briefly making Rarible one of the highest-volume NFT marketplaces. However, the mechanism also attracted wash trading — users buying and selling NFTs to themselves to earn $RARI tokens — which inflated volume metrics and ultimately led to adjustments in the token distribution formula.

In 2021, Rarible raised $14.2 million in a Series A funding round led by Venrock and CoinFund, with participation from 01 Advisors (founded by former Twitter CEO Dick Costolo).

## Multi-Chain Expansion

Rarible differentiated itself from competitors by expanding beyond Ethereum to support multiple blockchains:

- **Ethereum**: The original and primary chain, supporting ERC-721 and ERC-1155 token standards
- **Flow**: Dapper Labs' blockchain, enabling access to the Flow NFT ecosystem
- **Tezos**: Low-cost blockchain popular with digital artists seeking lower gas fees than Ethereum
- **Polygon**: Ethereum Layer 2 scaling solution, offering near-zero gas fees for minting and trading

This multi-chain strategy gave Rarible access to creator and collector communities across different blockchain ecosystems, whereas competitors like [[opensea|OpenSea]] and [[superrare|SuperRare]] were initially Ethereum-only.

## Rarible Protocol

Beyond the consumer marketplace, Rarible developed the Rarible Protocol — an open-source, blockchain-agnostic protocol for building NFT applications. The protocol provides:

- Indexing of NFT data across supported chains
- Order book functionality for NFT trading
- Lazy minting (creating NFTs without upfront gas costs until the first purchase)
- Royalty enforcement mechanisms

The protocol was designed to allow third-party developers to build their own NFT marketplaces and applications, positioning Rarible as infrastructure rather than just a consumer-facing marketplace.

## Business Model and Platform Design

- **Listing model**: Open/permissionless — any creator can mint and list without approval
- **Fees**: 2.5% service fee on sales (at launch; adjusted over time)
- **Governance**: $RARI token holders vote on protocol upgrades and fee structures
- **Minting**: Supports both standard minting and lazy minting
- **Collections**: Users can create custom smart contract collections or use Rarible's shared contracts

## Competitive Position

Rarible occupies the open/permissionless tier of the NFT marketplace landscape:

- vs. **[[superrare|SuperRare]]** / **[[knownorigin|KnownOrigin]]**: Rarible allows anyone to list; curated platforms require approval
- vs. **[[opensea|OpenSea]]**: Both are open marketplaces; OpenSea has historically had larger volume, but Rarible differentiated with $RARI governance
- vs. **[[blur|Blur]]**: Blur targeted pro traders with speed and aggregation features; Rarible focused more broadly on creators and collectors

The 2022-2023 NFT market downturn significantly impacted Rarible's trading volumes alongside all NFT platforms. The rise of [[blur|Blur]] and its aggressive incentive programs shifted market share in the trader-focused segment.

Through 2024-2026 the broader NFT market remained a fraction of its 2021-2022 peak, and Rarible — like most marketplaces — saw structurally lower trading volumes. The competitive landscape consolidated around [[opensea|OpenSea]], [[blur|Blur]] and aggregators, with [[magic-eden|Magic Eden]] gaining share via multi-chain (notably Solana) expansion. Rarible has continued to lean on the **Rarible Protocol** (infrastructure / white-label marketplaces) and the **$RARI** governance token as its differentiators rather than competing head-on for raw spot volume.

## Trading Relevance

Rarible is not a publicly listed company; traders gain exposure only indirectly via the **$RARI** governance token (a low-liquidity, high-volatility crypto asset) or by trading the NFTs and collections listed on the platform. Its main relevance to traders is as a barometer of NFT-market activity and as an early case study in **marketplace-mining tokenomics** — the wash-trading distortions $RARI incentives produced are a recurring lesson in how token rewards can inflate volume metrics ([[wash-trading]] risk). Catalysts for $RARI and platform activity track the overall crypto/NFT risk cycle, [[ethereum|Ethereum]] gas costs, and competitive moves by [[opensea]] and [[blur]].
