KnownOrigin was a curated digital art [[nft|NFT]] marketplace built on [[ethereum|Ethereum]]. Launched in 2018 in Manchester, UK, KnownOrigin was one of the earliest platforms dedicated to NFT art alongside [[superrare|SuperRare]]. The platform operated a curated, application-based model for artists and became known for supporting emerging digital artists. In June 2022, KnownOrigin was acquired by eBay — one of the first major acquisitions of an NFT platform by a traditional e-commerce company — but eBay wound the marketplace down in 2024, making it a case study in Web2 buying at the top of a speculative cycle.

## History

KnownOrigin was co-founded in 2018 by David Moore, Andy Gray, and James Morgan in Manchester, UK. The platform launched as a curated marketplace where digital artists could tokenize and sell their work as NFTs on the [[ethereum|Ethereum]] blockchain. Like [[superrare|SuperRare]], KnownOrigin required artists to apply and be approved before listing, creating a gallery-like curation standard.

The platform was built during the very early days of the NFT art market, before the term "NFT" had entered mainstream awareness. KnownOrigin, SuperRare, and [[makersplace|MakersPlace]] collectively formed the first wave of dedicated crypto art marketplaces, establishing the infrastructure and community norms for what would later become a multi-billion-dollar market.

KnownOrigin distinguished itself through a focus on supporting emerging and mid-career digital artists, maintaining relatively accessible price points compared to the high-end positioning of SuperRare. The platform also featured edition-based works (limited editions of a piece) in addition to 1/1 unique artworks, giving artists more flexibility in how they released and priced their creations.

## eBay Acquisition

In June 2022, eBay acquired KnownOrigin for an undisclosed sum. The acquisition was notable as one of the first instances of a major traditional e-commerce company purchasing an NFT-native platform. eBay had previously begun allowing NFT listings on its main marketplace and signaled a broader interest in digital collectibles.

Following the acquisition, eBay stated its intention to integrate KnownOrigin's technology and expertise to expand its NFT and digital collectibles offerings. The deal gave eBay access to KnownOrigin's smart contract infrastructure, curated artist community, and operational experience in the NFT marketplace space.

The timing of the acquisition — during the early stages of the 2022 NFT market downturn — raised questions about whether eBay was making a strategic long-term bet on digital art or buying at the top of a speculative cycle.

## Shutdown (2024)

The acquisition did not survive the NFT bear market:

- **January 2024:** eBay laid off roughly 30% of the KnownOrigin/Web3 team, signaling retreat from its NFT strategy.
- **July 2024:** KnownOrigin announced it would wind down its on-chain marketplace and minting contracts over the following weeks ([nft now](https://nftnow.com/news/knownorigin-ebay-nft-marketplace-announces-shutdown/)).
- **End of 2024:** the on-chain marketplace was fully shut. KnownOrigin emphasized that existing NFTs, minted as ERC-721 tokens on [[ethereum|Ethereum]], remain provable and tradable on secondary marketplaces such as [[opensea|OpenSea]].
- **2025:** eBay laid off the remaining KnownOrigin/Web3 staff in Manchester as it exited the NFT bet and pivoted toward AI and live shopping ([Shopifreaks](https://www.shopifreaks.com/ebay-is-laying-off-its-remaining-web3-and-knownorigin-team-in-manchester-as-it-exits-its-nft-bet-and-pivots-to-ai-and-live-shopping/)).

The shutdown placed KnownOrigin among a wave of early NFT marketplaces (e.g., Async Art, October 2023) that closed in the 2023-2024 bear market as volume consolidated onto [[opensea|OpenSea]], [[blur|Blur]], and Magic Eden.

## Platform Characteristics

- **Curation model**: Application-based artist approval
- **Edition types**: Both 1/1 unique works and limited editions
- **Blockchain**: [[ethereum|Ethereum]] (ERC-721 standard)
- **Royalties**: Enforced creator royalties on secondary sales
- **Focus**: Emerging and mid-career digital artists
- **Social features**: Artist profiles, collector galleries, activity feeds

## Competitive Position

KnownOrigin occupied a similar market niche to [[superrare|SuperRare]] and [[makersplace|MakersPlace]] as a curated digital art platform, but with some distinctions:

- **vs. [[superrare|SuperRare]]**: Both curated, but KnownOrigin supported limited editions alongside 1/1s and had a stronger focus on emerging artists at more accessible price points
- **vs. [[makersplace|MakersPlace]]**: Similar curated approach; KnownOrigin had a UK/European community emphasis while MakersPlace leaned more US-centric
- **vs. [[opensea|OpenSea]]** / **[[rarible|Rarible]]**: KnownOrigin's curation stood in contrast to these open, permissionless marketplaces
- **vs. [[blur|Blur]]**: Entirely different focus — Blur targeted high-volume traders while KnownOrigin served the art collector community

## Legacy and Impact

KnownOrigin holds an important place in NFT art history as one of the platforms that proved the viability of blockchain-based art marketplaces before the 2021 mainstream NFT explosion. The platform's early years (2018-2020) helped establish fundamental marketplace conventions — artist profiles, edition management, provenance tracking, and creator royalties — that later became standard across the NFT ecosystem.

The eBay acquisition is now a closed case study in the intersection of Web2 and Web3 commerce: eBay bought near the top of the 2021-2022 NFT cycle and shut the platform down roughly two years later without ever integrating NFT art into its mainstream marketplace. For traders, the episode is a useful marker of cycle timing — corporate acquisitions of crypto-native platforms clustering at the top of a speculative cycle (June 2022) and capitulation/shutdowns clustering at the bottom (2024) — and a reminder of platform risk for NFT collectors: liquidity and discovery infrastructure can disappear even when the tokens themselves persist on-chain.

KnownOrigin's Manchester origins also highlighted that the NFT art movement was not exclusively a Silicon Valley or New York phenomenon — significant infrastructure was built by teams distributed across the UK and Europe.
