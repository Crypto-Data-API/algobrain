Mobileye Global Inc. (NASDAQ: MBLY) is the Jerusalem-based leader in camera-first advanced driver-assistance systems (ADAS) and [[autonomous-vehicles|autonomous driving]] technology, built around its EyeQ system-on-chip family shipped in hundreds of millions of vehicles. Majority-owned by [[intel]] (which bought it for $15.3 billion in 2017 and re-IPO'd it in October 2022), MBLY is the main listed pure-play on ADAS/AV adoption — and trades with a persistent Intel-ownership overhang.

> **Note:** an earlier version of this page listed the ticker as "MOBE"; the correct listing is **NASDAQ: MBLY** (confirmed via Perplexity, 2026-06-10).

## AI Involvement

[[artificial-intelligence|AI]]-powered advanced driver assistance and [[autonomous-vehicles|autonomous driving]] — computer vision (EyeQ chips), REM crowd-sourced mapping, and full self-driving stacks (SuperVision, Chauffeur, Drive).

## Business Overview

Mobileye sells EyeQ vision processors and software for base ADAS (the high-volume, lower-margin core business with most global automakers), and is ramping higher-content products: **Surround ADAS** (multi-camera, higher ASP for mass-market vehicles), **SuperVision** (hands-off, eyes-on), **Chauffeur** (eyes-off), and **Mobileye Drive** (driverless/robotaxi). Volkswagen Group is the anchor customer for the advanced portfolio, with four advanced products in simultaneous execution across VW brands, including the first Surround ADAS design win.

## Business Segments (Detail)

Mobileye is best understood as a **content-per-vehicle ladder**: the same computer-vision stack sold at progressively higher price points and autonomy levels. The thesis hinges on customers climbing that ladder.

| Product tier | Autonomy level | Economics | Status |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Base ADAS (EyeQ)** | L1–L2 driver assist | High volume, low ASP, the cash-generating core | Mature; shipped in hundreds of millions of vehicles |
| **Surround ADAS** | Enhanced L2, multi-camera | Higher ASP for mass-market cars | First design win (VW); ramping |
| **SuperVision** | L2+ hands-off / eyes-on | Materially higher ASP, software-rich | In production; expanding OEM base |
| **Chauffeur** | L3 eyes-off (conditional) | Premium ASP | Launching |
| **Mobileye Drive** | L4 driverless / robotaxi | Highest value, service/recurring optionality | Commercializing 2026 |

Two cross-cutting assets underpin the stack:
- **EyeQ system-on-chip family** — the purpose-built vision processor; design wins lock in multi-year royalty/ASP streams.
- **REM (Road Experience Management)** — crowd-sourced HD mapping built from data harvested across the EyeQ installed base, a data-network-effect moat for higher autonomy levels.

The key operating variable is **content per vehicle / ASP mix**: revenue can grow even with flat unit volumes as customers move from base ADAS up to Surround/SuperVision/Chauffeur. Management points to advanced-product launches driving a growth inflection around 2027.

## Competitive Positioning / Peers

Mobileye is the listed pure-play on camera-first ADAS/AV, competing on compute, software, and OEM relationships.

| Company | Relationship to MBLY | Axis |
|---|---|---|
| [[nvidia]] | Rival AV-compute platform (DRIVE) | High-end autonomy compute, OEM design wins |
| [[qualcomm]] | Rival ADAS/cockpit platform (Snapdragon Ride) | Mass-market ADAS SoCs |
| [[tesla]] | Camera-first FSD competitor (in-house) | Vision-only autonomy narrative; OEM in-housing threat |
| [[intel]] | **Majority owner** (ownership overhang, not a product rival) | Control + secondary-offering supply |
| [[luminar-technologies]] / [[ouster]] | Sensor suppliers (lidar) — partners and a thesis contrast (camera-first vs lidar) | Sensor architecture |
| Chinese domestic ADAS (Horizon Robotics, etc.) | Regional competitors | China auto market share |

Mobileye's moat is the **EyeQ installed base + REM mapping data + deep OEM integration**, which create switching costs measured in vehicle-program cycles. The core threats are OEMs **in-housing** ADAS software (the [[tesla]] model), [[nvidia]]/[[qualcomm]] winning high-end compute sockets, and intensifying Chinese domestic competition.

## Bull vs Bear Case

**Bull case**
- **Content-per-vehicle ladder** — customers climbing from base ADAS to Surround/SuperVision/Chauffeur lifts ASP and revenue without needing unit growth.
- **Robotaxi commercialization** in 2026 (VW autonomous ID.Buzz; Benteler/Holon shuttle with [[lyft]]) opens an L4 optionality leg.
- **REM mapping** data-network effect strengthens with the installed base.
- 2027 growth inflection as Drive and SuperVision become material revenue drivers.
- Strong balance sheet (~$1.8B cash, no need to raise) plus a new $250M buyback signals confidence.

**Bear case**
- **GAAP losses persist** (heavy R&D plus Intel-acquisition intangibles amortization), keeping the valuation guidance-sensitive.
- **OEM in-housing** of ADAS software (the [[tesla]] path) is the existential threat.
- **Auto-production cyclicality** and the 2024 China inventory glut showed how quickly demand can normalize.
- **Intel ownership overhang** — recurring secondary-offering supply.
- **Chinese domestic competition** eroding share in a key market.
- Robotaxi/AV timelines have a long history of slipping industry-wide.

## Valuation Framework (Qualitative)

Mobileye is valued on **ADAS/EyeQ design wins and content-per-vehicle expansion**, with robotaxi as embedded optionality — not on near-term GAAP earnings (which are negative). The multiple is driven by:

1. **Design-win pipeline & backlog** — future revenue locked in by OEM program awards; the leading indicator of the model.
2. **Content per vehicle / ASP mix** — the shift up the ladder (base → Surround → SuperVision → Chauffeur) is the core growth lever.
3. **Advanced-product ramp timing** — the 2027 inflection narrative; slippage compresses the multiple.
4. **Robotaxi / Mobileye Drive optionality** — an L4 service leg that, if realized, is valued separately from the chip business.
5. **Intel-overhang discount** — controlled-company status and secondary-offering risk weigh on the multiple regardless of fundamentals.

Because GAAP results are loss-making, the stock trades on forward design-win and revenue-trajectory expectations, making it highly **guidance-sensitive**. See [[price-to-sales-ratio]] and [[discounted-cash-flow]].

## Capital Return

Mobileye initiated its first capital return since listing with a **$250 million share-repurchase program** announced alongside Q1 2026 results — a signal of balance-sheet confidence (backed by ~$1.8B cash) rather than a large-scale return program. No dividend. Capital is otherwise reinvested in R&D for the advanced-product portfolio. The dominant capital-markets variable on the *supply* side remains [[intel]] secondary offerings of its majority stake.

- **2025:** Continued EV/ADAS demand normalization after the 2024 China inventory glut; management points to advanced-product launches driving a growth inflection in 2027, when Drive and SuperVision become material revenue drivers.
- **Intel secondary offerings (2025):** Intel sold down part of its stake via registered offerings while retaining majority ownership — a recurring supply overhang on the stock.
