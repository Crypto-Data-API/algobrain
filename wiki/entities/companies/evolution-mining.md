Evolution Mining Limited (ASX: EVN) is a senior Australian gold and copper producer operating mines in New South Wales, Queensland, Western Australia and Ontario, Canada, with meaningful copper by-product exposure through Ernest Henry and Cadia. Evolution is one of the ASX's most actively traded gold stocks — a top-three ASX gold name alongside [[northern-star|Northern Star Resources]] — and rode the 2024–2026 gold bull market to record profits and a market capitalisation near A$29B by April 2026.

Fred McNaught uses Evolution Mining as a case study in [[capital-raise]] risk detection. He identified a capital raise event where the market had not yet priced in the dilution risk, demonstrating his approach to detecting unpriced risks in resource companies. Fred's analysis of EVN illustrates the importance of monitoring gold miners not just for commodity price exposure but also for balance sheet events — capital raises, debt refinancing, and acquisition-driven dilution — that can destroy shareholder value independently of the underlying gold price.

## Business Overview & Assets

Evolution is a senior [[gold]] and [[copper]] producer in the [[materials]] sector, operating a geographically diversified portfolio across eastern Australia, Western Australia and Ontario. The defining feature of the business is its **copper by-product credits** (chiefly from Ernest Henry and Cadia): copper revenue is netted against gold production costs, structurally lowering Evolution's all-in sustaining cost (AISC) below that of a pure-play gold miner and giving it a measure of cost-curve insulation that most ASX gold peers lack.

| Asset | Location | Type | Role / notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Cadia** | NSW | Gold–copper, block-cave underground | Cornerstone, long-life, low-cost; acquired from Newmont in 2024 — the largest gold mine in Australia |
| **Ernest Henry** | Qld | Copper–gold underground | Long-life; copper by-product materially lowers group AISC; 100% owned after the 2024 buy-out |
| **Cowal** | NSW | Gold, open-pit + underground | Steady mid-tier producer |
| **Mungari** | WA | Gold | WA operation; mill-expansion growth optionality |
| **Mt Rawdon** | Qld | Gold, open-pit | Near end of mine life; transitioning to closure/pumped-hydro repurposing |
| **Red Lake** | Ontario, Canada | Gold underground | Turnaround asset; has historically underdelivered versus the acquisition case |

Revenue is earned primarily from gold bullion, supplemented by copper concentrate from Cadia, Ernest Henry and Red Lake. The business hedges a minority of production to underwrite project economics.

## Economic Moat

Gold producers are fundamentally **price-takers**: Evolution sells into a deep global market at the prevailing USD spot price and has no pricing power over its output. There is therefore no brand, network or switching-cost moat in the [[economic-moat]] sense that applies to a franchise business like [[brambles]]. What stands in for a moat in mining is **position on the industry cost curve** and **asset quality / mine life** — durable, low-cost, long-life ounces that stay profitable when the gold price falls and the marginal producer shuts.

On that honest framing, Evolution screens better than most ASX peers for two structural reasons: (1) **copper by-product credits** push its net AISC down the cost curve, and (2) **Cadia** is a genuine tier-one, multi-decade orebody. The flip side is that this is a quality-of-assets advantage, not an enduring competitive moat — reserves deplete, grades decline, and the "moat" must be continually rebought through exploration and acquisition (the very [[capital-raise]] / dilution risk Fred flags). The right way to underwrite EVN is cost-curve position and balance-sheet discipline, not pricing power.

## Growth Drivers & Catalysts

- **Gold price** — the dominant driver; record AUD gold in 2024–2026 drove record margins and free cash flow.
- **Copper price** — Cadia and Ernest Henry credits mean a strong copper tape is a tailwind to reported AISC and earnings.
- **AUD/USD** — a weaker AUD amplifies AUD-denominated margins on USD-priced metal (see Valuation below).
- **Cadia and Mungari expansions / mine-life extensions** — organic volume growth from the cornerstone assets.
- **Red Lake turnaround** — operational improvement here is upside optionality (and a key execution risk).
- **Deleveraging and capital management** — gearing fell to ~6% by 1H FY26; record fully franked dividends and potential buybacks become the capital-return story.
- **Recurring catalysts** — half-year results (February), full-year results (August), quarterly activity reports, FY guidance, and gold-macro events (Fed cuts, central-bank buying, geopolitical risk).

## Bull Case vs Bear Case

### Bull case
- Diversified, long-life portfolio anchored by tier-one Cadia; copper credits keep net AISC low on the cost curve.
- Record gold (and firm copper) prices drive record cash flow; gearing down to ~6% gives balance-sheet optionality.
- Leverage to gold is amplified by a soft AUD — AUD-denominated margins expand faster than spot.
- Capital discipline post-deleveraging: rising fully franked dividends attractive to domestic [[australian-investing|Australian]] holders.

### Bear case
- Pure price-taker: a gold-price reversal from record levels compresses margins fast (high beta, ~1.5–2x to [[gold]]).
- Acquisition-and-dilution history — the [[capital-raise]] risk Fred flags; growth has repeatedly been bought, not built.
- Red Lake (and integration/operational execution) has historically underdelivered versus the acquisition case.
- Cost inflation (labour, power, diesel) and reserve-replacement pressure erode through-cycle returns.
- Copper exposure cuts both ways — a copper downturn lifts reported AISC just as gold may also be falling.

## Key Risks

- **Gold-price reversal** — the single largest risk; record-level prices mean the asymmetry is now to the downside.
- **Capital-raise / acquisition dilution** — Fred's case study; balance-sheet events can destroy value independently of the gold price (mitigated for now by the ~6% gearing and ~A$1B cash position).
- **Operational / single-asset** — Red Lake underdelivery; any disruption at Cadia (the cornerstone) is outsized.
- **Cost inflation** — labour, power and consumables compress margins through the cycle.
- **Copper-price risk** — weak copper lifts reported AISC and hits the credit that defines EVN's cost-curve edge.
- **FX** — a stronger AUD compresses AUD margins on USD-priced metal.

## Valuation & How the Stock Trades

- **Gold and AUD beta** — EVN is a leveraged, AUD-denominated play on USD gold. The two drivers stack: a rising USD gold price *and* a falling AUD both expand AUD margins, so the stock tends to move 1.5–2x the move in gold in both directions.
- **Copper overlay** — a second commodity driver via Cadia/Ernest Henry; the stock has partial copper beta on top of gold.
- **Dividend** — fully franked; the franking credit is a real after-tax uplift for Australian residents and supports a domestic-income holder base now that gearing is low.
- **Index/flows** — [[asx-200]] (and ASX-50-scale) constituent; one of the most liquid AUD gold exposures, held by domestic super funds and global gold-sector funds (GDX includes EVN).
- **No meaningful single-stock options market** relative to US megacaps; exposure is taken in the shares or via ASX gold-sector ETFs.

## Business Model & Segments

Evolution operates a diversified suite of gold and gold–copper assets:

- **Cadia** (NSW): Bulk low-cost gold–copper mine acquired from Newcrest/Newmont in 2024 – the cornerstone asset.
- **Ernest Henry** (Qld): Long-life copper-gold mine providing a valuable by-product credit that materially lowers all-in sustaining costs.
- **Cowal** (NSW): Open-pit + underground gold mine.
- **Mungari** (WA): Gold operation.
- **Mt Rawdon** (Qld): Near mine-life end, transitioning to closure/repurposing.
- **Red Lake** (Ontario, Canada): Underground gold mining operations.

Revenue is earned primarily from gold bullion sales, supplemented by copper concentrate sales from Cadia, Ernest Henry and parts of Red Lake. The business hedges a minority of production to lock in project economics.

## History

Evolution was incorporated in 1998 and was formerly known as Catalpa Resources Limited before changing its name to Evolution Mining Limited in November 2011, following the merger of Catalpa with Conquest Mining and the injection of Newcrest's Cracow and Pajingo assets. The company has grown materially through disciplined acquisitions, including Mt Carlton (2013), La Mancha's Cowal stake (2015), Mungari (2015), the 29.9% economic interest in Ernest Henry (2016, later upsized), Red Lake (2019), and the transformational A$2.4 billion acquisition of the 80% it didn't already own of Ernest Henry plus an interest in Cadia from Newmont in 2024.

## Trading Relevance

- **Index/flows:** [[asx-200]] (and ASX 50-scale by 2026) constituent; one of the most liquid AUD gold exposures, heavily traded by both domestic super funds and global gold-sector funds (GDX includes EVN).
- **What moves it:** USD gold price first, AUD/USD second (a weaker AUD amplifies AUD-denominated margins), then operational news — quarterly production reports, FY guidance (FY26: 710–780koz gold, 70–80kt copper at AISC A$1,640–1,760/oz), and copper prices for Ernest Henry/Cadia credits.
- **Catalysts:** half-year results (February) and full-year results (August), quarterly activity reports, gold-price macro events (Fed cuts, central-bank buying, geopolitical risk), capital-management announcements now that gearing is down to 6%.
- **Correlated names:** [[northern-star]], [[barrick-gold]], Newmont, Westgold, Genesis Minerals; beta to [[gold]] typically 1.5–2x in both directions.
- **Key risks:** gold price reversal from record levels, cost inflation (labour, power), operational incidents (Red Lake has historically underdelivered vs acquisition case), and the [[capital-raise]] / acquisition-dilution risk highlighted in Fred's case study above — though the 1H FY26 balance sheet (A$967M cash, 6% gearing) has materially reduced that risk for now.
