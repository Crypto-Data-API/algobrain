Corpay (NYSE: CPAY), formerly FLEETCOR Technologies (renamed March 2024), is a global corporate-payments company spanning cross-border FX, accounts-payable automation, and vehicle/fuel-card payments. A [[s-and-p-500]] constituent in the [[financials|Financials]] sector, it matters to traders as a high-margin (54% EBITDA), acquisitive payments compounder repositioning from legacy fuel cards toward faster-growing B2B cross-border payments — punctuated in 2025 by a Mastercard strategic investment and the $2.2B Alpha Group acquisition.

## Business Overview & Segments

Corpay is a global corporate-payments company that monetizes spend across three reportable areas. Historically the business was a fuel-card pure play (as **FLEETCOR**); the strategic arc of the past decade has been a deliberate repositioning of the revenue mix away from mature [[#Vehicle-Payments|Vehicle Payments]] and toward faster-growing **Corporate Payments** (cross-border FX and accounts-payable automation). Across all three areas the model monetizes *payment volume* rather than balance-sheet lending: it earns currency spreads, card interchange, transaction and SaaS fees, and a meaningful stream of **float income** on customer balances held between transaction and settlement (which rises with short-term rates). Growth has historically been **M&A-led** — a serial acquirer of fuel-card and payments assets — so the leverage shown in the [[#Fundamentals|fundamentals]] (D/E ~2.56x) reflects debt-funded acquisitions and aggressive buybacks against a thin book-equity base, a pattern typical of the high-margin [[financials|payments]] peer group rather than operating distress.

| Segment | What it is | Revenue mix (qualitative) |
|---|---|---|
| **Corporate Payments** | Cross-border FX and hedging for corporates and investment funds, accounts-payable (AP) automation, and virtual-card issuance | The growth engine — fastest-growing area (~17% organic in 2025); the strategic centre of gravity post-Alpha and Mastercard |
| **Vehicle Payments** | Legacy fleet/fuel cards across North America, Brazil and Europe — closed-loop fuel networks plus merchant acceptance, telematics-adjacent controls | Mature and highly cash-generative; sensitive to fuel prices and trucking activity; the historical core, now shrinking as a share of the mix |
| **Lodging / Other Payments** | Workforce/crew lodging, gift and other niche payment programs | Small; supplementary, cyclical with travel and project activity |

The repositioning thesis is that high-margin Corporate Payments out-grows the secularly challenged Vehicle Payments fast enough to lift both the consolidated growth rate and the multiple. Reported EBITDA margins sit around the mid-50s%, among the richest in [[financials|payments]] — see the [[#Fundamentals|fundamentals]] block.

## Economic Moat & Competitive Position

Corpay's [[economic-moat|economic moat]] is genuine but narrower and more contested than a pure card network's:

- **Closed-loop fuel-card networks** — in Vehicle Payments, Corpay operates proprietary fuel-acceptance networks with deep merchant relationships and purchase controls (SKU-level data, fraud limits, per-vehicle policies) that are sticky inside fleet operations. This is the most durable part of the moat.
- **Switching costs & embedded workflow** — once a fleet's fuel policy or a corporate's AP/cross-border workflow is wired into Corpay's controls and reporting, ripping it out is operationally painful — classic [[switching-costs]] that support retention and pricing.
- **Scale & float** — large processed volume amortizes compliance, licensing and tech spend, and the float on customer balances is a structurally profitable, rate-geared asset few small competitors can match.
- **Mastercard cross-border channel** — the 2025 [[mastercard]] partnership (minority stake plus an exclusive commercial cross-border provider arrangement for bank FX flows) embeds Corpay into a global distribution rail it could not build alone — a new, distribution-led moat extension in the growth segment.

The bound on the moat is the **secular question over fuel**: EV adoption and improving fleet efficiency slowly erode fuel-card transaction volume, and cross-border FX/AP is a more competitive arena (banks, fintechs, networks) than legacy fuel cards. The moat is real where Corpay is incumbent; it is *being rebuilt* in the higher-growth, more-contested places it is migrating toward.

## Growth Drivers & Catalysts

- **Corporate Payments / cross-border organic growth** — the structural driver; cross-border FX, hedging and AP automation grew far faster than the consolidated rate (Corporate Payments ~+17% organic in 2025) and is the mix-shift engine.
- **Mastercard channel ramp** — management flagged the [[mastercard]] cross-border collaboration could add **2–3pp to 2026 organic growth** as bank FX flows route through Corpay.
- **Alpha Group integration & synergies** — the ~$2.2B (£1.6B) Alpha Group acquisition (completed 5 Nov 2025) extends UK/Europe corporate and fund cross-border reach and was guided as meaningfully EPS-accretive in 2026; realising synergies is the swing factor.
- **Further B2B payments M&A** — a serial acquirer; the $500M AvidXchange co-investment (with TPG) is optionality on US middle-market AP automation, and bolt-ons remain part of the algorithm.
- **FX volatility** — cross-border revenue benefits from currency volatility, so volatile-FX regimes are a tailwind.
- **Float income with rates** — interest earned on customer balances scales with short-term rates; a higher-for-longer rate path supports the float line.
- **Capital return** — consistent buybacks against strong free cash flow.

## Bull Case vs Bear Case

### Bull case
- High-margin, cash-generative compounder (mid-50s% EBITDA margins) with a long runway of bolt-on M&A.
- Mix shift toward growthier **cross-border / Corporate Payments** lifts both the growth rate and the warranted multiple over time.
- The [[mastercard]] partnership embeds Corpay into a global cross-border distribution rail (2–3pp 2026 organic tailwind flagged) it could not build alone.
- Accretive M&A (Alpha Group, AvidXchange optionality) plus buybacks compound EPS even when organic growth is moderate.
- Float income provides a rate-geared, low-cost earnings cushion.

### Bear case
- **Secular fuel-card decline** — EV adoption and fleet efficiency slowly erode the legacy Vehicle Payments volume that still funds the model.
- **Leverage & acquisition dependence** — D/E ~2.56x and an M&A-led growth algorithm mean the story leans on continued debt-funded deals and a thin book-equity base.
- **Integration risk** — Alpha and other deals must deliver promised synergies; mis-execution would dent the accretion narrative.
- **Fuel-price & FX sensitivity** — Vehicle Payments revenue flexes with fuel prices; cross-border revenue with FX volatility — both add earnings variability.
- **Thin organic visibility** — headline growth blends organic and acquired, making clean organic momentum harder to underwrite.

## Key Risks

- **Fuel / EV secular decline** — the structural shrinkage of fuel-card transaction volume as fleets electrify and improve efficiency.
- **M&A integration & leverage** — debt-funded acquisitions, buybacks against thin book equity, and reliance on serial dealmaking for growth.
- **Fuel-price & FX swings** — earnings sensitivity to fuel prices (Vehicle Payments) and to currency volatility/levels (cross-border).
- **Rate-driven float income** — a meaningful, rate-geared earnings component that falls as short-term rates decline.
- **Regulatory / interchange** — exposure to interchange regulation, cross-border money-transmission licensing, and FX-conduct rules across many jurisdictions.
- **Customer credit** — short-dated receivables/credit risk on card balances if a fleet or corporate customer fails, amplified in a downturn.

## Valuation & How the Stock Trades

Corpay trades as a **payments/card-network-adjacent compounder**, valued on organic revenue growth, EBITDA margin and the EPS accretion its M&A and buybacks deliver rather than on book value. It is a **GARP-style** name that periodically de-rates toward "value" whenever fuel-card / EV secular-decline fears resurface, then re-rates as Corporate Payments growth and accretive deals reassert the compounder narrative — the central tension is whether [[#Vehicle-Payments|Vehicle Payments]] maturity is more than offset by [[#Business-Overview-Segments|Corporate Payments]] growth. The watched value drivers are payment **volume**, FX **spreads**, fee take-rates, and **float** income (rate-sensitive). Per the data block, it screens around a mid-20s P/E (~26x) on rich EBITDA margins (~54%) with elevated leverage (D/E ~2.56x) — all read qualitatively, not as live quotes. Capital return is buyback-led (little or no dividend), and options liquidity is moderate — adequate for earnings event trades around the quarterly prints (Feb/May/Aug/Nov), Mastercard channel updates, and Alpha integration milestones. As an [[s-and-p-500]] member it sits in payments/fintech baskets (IPAY) and broad financials ETFs.
