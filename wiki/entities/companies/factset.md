FactSet Research Systems (NYSE: FDS) is a financial data and analytics platform serving buy-side and sell-side investment professionals — the number-three workstation/data terminal franchise behind [[bloomberg]] and LSEG/Refinitiv. Classified in [[financials|Financials]] but a [[technology]]-style subscription-software business at heart, it is a subscription compounder in the [[s-and-p-500]] whose 2025-2026 setup is a CEO transition plus a market debate over whether generative AI disrupts or accelerates financial-data vendors.

## Business Overview & Segments

FactSet sells subscriptions to its workstation, data feeds, analytics, and identifier services. The key operating metric is **organic ASV (Annual Subscription Value)** — annualized committed subscription revenue. Retention runs above 90% of clients and ~95%+ of ASV, making revenue highly predictable. Clients span asset managers, hedge funds, bankers, wealth platforms and corporates; the firm has grown EPS for decades on ASV growth plus margin discipline and buybacks (see Fundamentals: 39% EBITDA margin, 27% ROE, FY2025 diluted EPS $15.55).

| Segment / line | What it is | Revenue mix (qualitative) |
|---|---|---|
| **Workstation** | The FactSet desktop terminal for analysts, PMs and bankers | The core seat-based subscription; the franchise product |
| **Data feeds & content** | Fundamentals, estimates, ownership, supply-chain, ESG and other datasets delivered via APIs/feeds | Large and growing; the "data" pivot beyond the terminal |
| **Analytics & wealth** | Portfolio analytics, performance/risk, and wealth-management tools | High-value, sticky workflow software |
| **CUSIP Global Services** | The CUSIP/CINS securities-identifier registry (acquired 2022, ~$1.9B) | Quasi-monopoly identifier business; high-margin, also a regulatory-scrutiny item |

The business is sold by ASV and region (Americas, EMEA, Asia-Pacific) rather than as wide product segments, but the lines above capture how the revenue and moat are built.

## 2025–2026 Developments

- **CEO succession:** announced 2025-06-03 — **Sanoke Viswanathan** (ex-JPMorgan, head of International Consumer Banking) succeeded long-time CEO Phil Snow effective early September 2025. **Bob Stolte** became CTO on 2026-03-02.
- **AI-disruption derating:** through 2025 the stock derated alongside other data/research vendors (Morningstar, MSCI moves on similar fears) as investors questioned whether LLMs erode the value of curated financial data and research workstations; the PE in the Fundamentals (15.8x) is well below FactSet's historical 25-30x range, reflecting that fear.
- **AI product push:** FactSet has been shipping AI-assisted workflows (transcript summarization, code generation in its Portfolio Analytics, conversational search) to defend seats — execution under the new CEO is the central 2026 question.

## Economic Moat & Competitive Position

FactSet's [[economic-moat|moat]] is a classic **workflow-software moat** built on data plus integration:

- **High [[switching-costs]]** — analysts and PMs build models, screens, and workflows inside FactSet; ripping out the terminal and feeds means retraining, re-plumbing data pipelines, and disrupting reporting. Retention above 90% of clients and ~95%+ of ASV reflects this stickiness.
- **Recurring, predictable revenue** — seat- and feed-based subscriptions with multi-year inertia make revenue among the most predictable in [[financials]].
- **Integrated data + analytics** — owning both the content (fundamentals, estimates, ownership, supply chain) and the analytics that sit on top deepens lock-in versus a pure data vendor.
- **CUSIP registry** — the CUSIP Global Services identifier business is effectively a monopoly utility embedded in market plumbing (also the source of antitrust attention).

The moat is **narrower than [[bloomberg]]'s**: Bloomberg's terminal + chat network effect is the dominant front-office standard, and LSEG/Refinitiv is larger in data. FactSet wins on price/value and deep buy-side analytics rather than ubiquity. The live debate is whether generative AI erodes the workstation premium (cheaper AI-native tools synthesizing data) or *deepens* the moat (FactSet's proprietary, permissioned data feeding AI workflows clients can trust).

## Growth Drivers & Catalysts

- **Organic ASV growth** — the master variable; re-acceleration toward high-single digits would re-rate the stock.
- **Data-feed and analytics expansion** — selling content and APIs beyond the seat-based terminal broadens the base.
- **Wealth-management** — advisor platforms are a growing, sticky distribution channel.
- **AI-assisted workflows** — conversational search, transcript summarization and code generation to defend and grow seats.
- **Banking/sell-side hiring cycle** — recovery in banker headcount lifts seat counts.
- **Buybacks and dividend** — a 25+ year dividend-growth record plus repurchases against strong free cash flow.
- **Recurring catalysts**: quarterly earnings with organic ASV growth, annual guidance each September quarter, new-CEO strategy updates, banking hiring cycles.

## Bull Case vs Bear Case

### Bull case
- Sticky, recurring, ~95%+-retained ASV makes FactSet one of the most predictable compounders in [[financials]].
- A depressed multiple (well below the historical 25-30x) versus a still-growing, high-margin subscription franchise.
- Proprietary, permissioned data plus AI workflows could *deepen* the moat as clients want trustworthy data feeding their models.
- Long dividend-growth record and consistent buybacks compound EPS.

### Bear case
- **AI disruption** — cheaper AI-native tools could erode the workstation premium and slow ASV growth.
- **ASV deceleration** — growth below ~4% would undermine the compounder thesis and the multiple.
- **Sell-side cost cuts** — banking seat reductions directly pressure ASV.
- **CEO transition risk** — a new CEO must execute the AI and growth pivot.
- Smaller and less defensible than [[bloomberg]]; squeezed between the terminal leader and larger data players.

## Key Risks

- **Generative-AI substitution** — the central debate; cheaper AI tools could displace seats or compress pricing.
- **ASV growth deceleration** below ~4% would break the compounder narrative.
- **Banking/buy-side seat losses** — sell-side cost cuts and consolidation reduce subscriber counts.
- **CUSIP regulatory/antitrust scrutiny** — the identifier monopoly draws periodic challenge.
- **Execution under new leadership** — the CEO transition adds strategy-execution risk.
- **Competitive pressure** — squeezed by [[bloomberg]]'s dominance and [[s-and-p-global]]/LSEG scale.

## Valuation & How the Stock Trades

FDS trades as a **recurring-revenue subscription compounder** — valued on ASV durability rather than cyclical earnings — currently de-rated on AI-disruption fears:

- **SaaS-like lens** — the market values FactSet on organic ASV growth, retention, and margin expansion, much like a software compounder; the historical multiple was 25-30x.
- **Current de-rating** — the ~15.8x snapshot PE sits well below that range, reflecting AI-disruption fear and slower ASV growth, not a change in business model.
- **Low beta / quality factor** — historically a low-volatility "quality" name; modest options liquidity; not a high-beta trading vehicle.
- **Earnings behavior** — moves on the ASV growth print and guidance more than on headline EPS.
- **Capital return** — a long dividend-growth record (25+ years) plus buybacks; a dividend-growth-plus-repurchase total-return profile rather than a high yield.
- **Sentiment correlation** — trades inversely to AI-disruption headlines about research/data vendors and alongside [[msci]], [[s-and-p-global]], [[moodys]].
