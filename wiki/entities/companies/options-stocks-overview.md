## What Makes a Stock Options-Liquid

Not every stock has a tradeable options market. The names in this catalog share characteristics that produce deep, liquid chains:

- **High retail and institutional interest** — meme stocks ([[gamestop|GME]], [[amc-entertainment|AMC]]), crypto proxies ([[microstrategy|MSTR]], [[mara|MARA]]), and high-growth names attract two-sided flow.
- **High [[implied-volatility]]** — elevated IV means richer premium, which draws both buyers (leverage/speculation) and sellers (premium harvest). High-IV names are where [[straddle-strangle|strangles]], [[iron-condor|iron condors]], and [[covered-call|covered calls]] earn the most.
- **Tight bid-ask spreads** — liquidity begets liquidity; market makers quote tight spreads where flow is heavy, lowering transaction cost.
- **Catalyst density** — earnings, regulatory events, product launches, and crypto-price swings give traders reasons to position, sustaining volume.
- **Strike and expiration depth** — liquid names offer weeklies, many strikes, and far-dated [[leaps-strategy|LEAPS]], enabling complex spreads.

Liquidity is the trader's edge: it determines whether you can enter and exit at fair prices, how much slippage you eat, and which strategies are even feasible. The tiers below rank these names by that liquidity.

## How to Use This Catalog

Match the liquidity tier to your strategy and holding period:

- **Tier 1 (ultra liquid)** — the only tier where short-dated, fast-turnover strategies ([[gamma-scalping]], [[gamma-squeeze|gamma plays]], 0-DTE-style speculation) are viable, because spreads are tight enough to trade in and out repeatedly.
- **Tier 2 (high liquidity)** — the sweet spot for **defined-risk earnings and income strategies**: [[iron-condor|iron condors]], [[straddle-strangle|strangles]] around earnings, [[covered-call|covered calls]], and [[leaps-strategy|LEAPS]]. Liquid enough for multi-leg spreads without punitive slippage.
- **Tier 3 (popular)** — best for **directional, lower-frequency** positions: long calls/puts, [[vertical-spread|debit spreads]], and covered calls held to expiry. Wider spreads make scalping unprofitable, so trade with limit orders and longer horizons.

General rules that apply across the catalog: check [[implied-volatility]] (IV rank/percentile) before selling premium, size for the fact that these names routinely gap 10-20% on catalysts, and prefer defined-risk structures on the most volatile meme and crypto-proxy names.

## Risks of Trading These Names

- **Gap risk** — these are the highest-volatility names in the market; overnight 10-30% gaps can blow through stop levels and short-option strikes
- **IV crush** — buying options into earnings often loses even on a correct directional call, as IV collapses after the event
- **Assignment risk** — short options (covered calls, condors, [[short-squeeze|squeeze]] situations) can be assigned early, especially around dividends and hard-to-borrow names
- **Liquidity illusion** — Tier 3 chains can look liquid in calm markets but spreads blow out during stress, trapping positions
- **Crowding / reflexivity** — meme and gamma-squeeze dynamics reverse violently; the same retail flow that lifts a name can vanish overnight
- **Pin and expiration risk** — heavy open interest at round strikes can pin price near expiry, distorting payoff
- **Survivorship** — this is a snapshot; liquidity migrates, names get acquired or delisted, and tiers change over time
