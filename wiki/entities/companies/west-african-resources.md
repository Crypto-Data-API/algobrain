**West African Resources Limited** (ASX: WAF) is a [[gold]]-mining company listed on the [[asx-limited|Australian Securities Exchange]] and a constituent of the [[asx-200]] index. It is a Perth-based mid-tier [[gold]] producer focused exclusively on Burkina Faso in West Africa, and for traders it is a high-margin, fast-growing [[commodities|price-taker]] whose two dominant swing factors are the [[gold]] price and Burkina Faso sovereign/security risk. Headquartered at 1 Alvan Street in Subiaco within the [[materials]] sector, WAF owns and operates two large-scale gold mines — Sanbrado and Kiaka — and is developing the satellite Toega deposit. Unusually for a gold miner it trades on a low single-digit P/E, reflecting the market's heavy discount for jurisdiction risk.

## Business Model & Segments

WAF's asset portfolio sits in the Birimian greenstone belt of Burkina Faso:

- **Sanbrado Gold Operations** (90% WAF / 10% Government of Burkina Faso, free-carried): approximately 90 km east-southeast of the capital Ouagadougou. Combines the high-grade M1 South underground mine with multiple open pits feeding a centralised processing plant. First gold poured March 2020, six months ahead of schedule and US$20 million under budget.

- **Kiaka Gold Project** (85% WAF / 15% Government of Burkina Faso): approximately 45 km south of Sanbrado and 110 km south-east of Ouagadougou. Kiaka is a large-scale open-pit gold project with a nearly two-decade reserve life and an updated feasibility study that supported the ~US$760 million development. First gold poured June 2025, ahead of schedule and under budget.

- **Toega Gold Deposit** (satellite to Sanbrado): approximately 13 km south-west of Sanbrado, within trucking distance of the Sanbrado processing plant. First gold scheduled for Q3 2026. Toega provides approximately seven years of additional ore feed to Sanbrado.

Gold doré is shipped and refined offshore, with sales through international bullion markets.

## Economic Moat & Competitive Position

WAF is a [[gold]] **price-taker** — it sells doré into global bullion markets at the international gold price it cannot influence — so there is no [[economic-moat|economic moat]] in the franchise sense. The honest competitive frame is **asset quality, cost-curve position and jurisdiction**:

- **Genuinely high-margin assets.** WAF's ~63% gross and ~53% operating margins, FY25 AISC ~US$1,488/oz, and ROE ~27% put it among the more profitable mid-tier producers — a first-class position on the gold cost curve while the gold price is high.
- **Long reserve life.** Kiaka's ~two-decade reserve and a 10-year outlook averaging ~533koz/yr give WAF rare visibility for a mid-tier — most gold juniors fight constant reserve depletion.
- **The offset: jurisdiction.** Everything sits in Burkina Faso, a Sahel state with elevated security and sovereign risk. The market caps WAF's multiple precisely because the asset quality is high but the location is not — the low P/E is the price of single-country, high-risk-jurisdiction concentration.
- **Execution track record.** Both Sanbrado and Kiaka were delivered ahead of schedule and under budget — operationally a genuine differentiator versus serial mine-builders.

## Growth Drivers & Catalysts

- **Record gold price** — the AUD and USD gold price set successive highs through 2024–2026; as a high-margin producer WAF's earnings are highly geared to it.
- **Production ramp** — 2026 guidance of 430,000–490,000 oz (Kiaka 240–280koz, Sanbrado 190–210koz); the 10-year outlook averages ~533koz/yr, peaking ~596koz in 2030, >5.3 Moz total over 2026–2035.
- **Toega first gold** (Q3 2026) extends Sanbrado feed by ~7 years.
- **Free-cash-flow inflection** — Kiaka's completion turns FCF strongly positive, opening the door to debt reduction and shareholder returns.
- **Recurring catalysts** — quarterly activities reports (Jan/Apr/Jul/Oct), [[gold]] price, AISC trend, reserve/resource updates, and — critically — Burkina Faso mining-code and stake-acquisition news.

## Bull Case vs Bear Case

### Bull case
- High-margin, low-cost, fast-growing mid-tier with two long-life mines and a clear 10-year growth profile.
- Strong leverage to a record [[gold]] price; profitability metrics (ROE ~27%, ROA ~17%) are best-in-class for the peer set.
- Demonstrated ability to build mines ahead of schedule and under budget.
- Very low headline multiple (P/E ~7x) — cheap if the jurisdiction discount proves overdone.

### Bear case
- **Single-country concentration in Burkina Faso** — a Sahel state with active security threats and a government actively seeking larger stakes in mines (proposed +35% of Kiaka, taking the state to 50%).
- Sovereign/fiscal risk: mining-code reform, royalty/tax changes, and forced equity dilution can transfer value from shareholders to the state.
- Periodic ASX trading suspensions in 2025 tied to stake-acquisition negotiations signal real, recurring policy overhang.
- [[commodities|Price-taker]] — a gold-price reversal would compress the high margins quickly.

## Key Risks

- **Sovereign / jurisdiction risk (dominant)** — Burkina Faso government's pursuit of larger mine stakes, mining-code changes, royalties/taxes; this is the single largest reason for the depressed multiple.
- **Security risk** — Sahel insurgency and regional instability threaten operations, logistics and personnel.
- **Gold-price reversal** — high margins are gold-price dependent; a sharp fall hits earnings and valuation together.
- **Concentration** — all assets in one country and one commodity; no diversification cushion.
- **Reserve/resource conversion & execution** — delivering the 10-year plan depends on continued drilling success and on-budget development.

## Valuation & How the Stock Trades

WAF trades as a **high-margin [[gold]] producer with a heavy jurisdiction discount**. Unusually for a profitable miner, the trailing P/E sits around 7x — not because earnings are inflated (unlike many commodity names, WAF is genuinely and durably profitable here) but because the market discounts Burkina Faso sovereign and security risk. Mechanically:

- **Gold beta** — the equity is leveraged to the [[gold]] price and trades with the gold complex, AUD/USD and gold-equity sentiment.
- **Jurisdiction premium/discount** — Burkina Faso headlines (stake proposals, mining-code reform, security) move the stock independently of gold; trading suspensions have occurred around stake negotiations.
- **Dividend** — historically minimal as cash funded Kiaka construction; the post-Kiaka FCF inflection is what could enable distributions.
- **How to size it** — value on gold-price scenarios and a jurisdiction-risk discount; position-size for binary sovereign-risk outcomes rather than treating it as a steady compounder.
- **Liquidity** — liquid [[asx-200]] gold name; exposure is typically taken in shares (listed-options coverage is limited).

## Recent Developments

2025 was a transformative production year, with Kiaka's ramp-up driving record results and a substantial 10-year outlook upgrade:

- **Calendar 2025 production**: Record 300,383 oz of gold — 205,228 oz from Sanbrado plus 95,155 oz from Kiaka during ramp-up.
- **2025 financials**: Revenue of approximately A$1.54 billion, net profit after tax of approximately A$567 million, operating cash flow of approximately A$790 million, all-in sustaining costs (AISC) of approximately US$1,488/oz.
- **2026 guidance**: Group production of 430,000–490,000 oz (Kiaka 240,000–280,000 oz; Sanbrado 190,000–210,000 oz). Group AISC targeted below US$1,900/oz.
- **10-year outlook (March 2026 update)**: Group production forecast to average 533,000 oz per annum from 2026 to 2035, peaking at approximately 596,000 oz in 2030. Total forecast output of more than 5.3 Moz of gold over 2026–2035. Sanbrado mine plan extended to 2036.
- **Sovereign development**: The Burkina Faso government has proposed increasing its stake in the Kiaka project by an additional 35% (which would take its interest to 50%), as part of broader mining sector reforms. WAF has engaged with the government on the proposal; market commentary has highlighted the resulting sovereign risk.
- **ASX trading suspension episodes**: Periodic suspensions have occurred in 2025 relating to Burkina Faso mining stake acquisition negotiations.
