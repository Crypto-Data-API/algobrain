Aurora Mobile Limited (NASDAQ: JG), known as **Jiguang** in China, is a Shenzhen-based customer-engagement and marketing-technology provider that has pivoted into enterprise [[artificial-intelligence|AI]] via its GPTBots.ai agent platform and its overseas EngageLab push-notification/messaging business. For traders it is a micro-cap US-listed China ADR — extremely volatile, headline-driven, and a frequent mover on AI news flow; it achieved its **first-ever full-year GAAP profit in 2025**.

## Business Overview

Aurora Mobile began as China's leading third-party push-notification SDK (JPush), embedded in a large share of Chinese mobile apps, and monetizes through:

- **Developer services** — push notification, SMS/messaging APIs and analytics for app developers in China (the stable legacy base).
- **EngageLab** — the international (ex-China) omnichannel customer-engagement platform; the company's primary growth driver, credited with the 2025 revenue acceleration.
- **GPTBots.ai** — an enterprise AI-agent/chatbot platform positioned as the "second growth curve," integrating frontier Chinese models (DeepSeek-V4 preview, GLM-5) for customer-service and workflow automation.
- **Market Intelligence / vertical data products** — app-economy data services.

## Bull vs Bear Case

**Bull case**

- EngageLab provides genuine international (ex-China) revenue diversification and growth (FY2025 acceleration; FY2026 guidance RMB450–480M, ~20–28% growth).
- GPTBots.ai gives an AI-agent "second growth curve" option integrating frontier Chinese models (DeepSeek-V4, GLM-5).

**Bear case**

- VIE/ADR structure and US-China delisting overhang (PCAOB/HFCAA) sit on top of all China ADRs.
- AI revenue (GPTBots.ai) is early; the gap between narrative and contribution is large.

## China VIE / Governance & Listing Risk

As a Shenzhen-based US-listed ADR, JG carries the structural risks of the China-ADR cohort:

- **VIE structure:** US shareholders typically hold an interest in an offshore holding company with contractual ("variable interest entity") claims over the China operating entities, *not* direct equity — a structure that has never been fully tested in Chinese courts and is an inherent governance risk.
- **PCAOB / HFCAA delisting overhang:** China ADRs are subject to [[pcaob]] audit-inspection requirements; an inspection-access lapse can force delisting under the Holding Foreign Companies Accountable Act. This is a live, exogenous risk for all such names.
- **China regulatory exposure:** data-security, cybersecurity and cross-border-data rules directly affect the push-notification/SDK business — a regulatory action could impair the legacy base.
- **Governance / related-party:** review related-party transactions and ADS-issuance (dilution) in the 20-F.
