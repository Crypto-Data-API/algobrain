**Alkane Resources Ltd** (ASX: ALK) is a gold and antimony producer listed on the [[asx-limited|Australian Securities Exchange]] and a constituent of the [[asx-200]] index. Alkane Resources is an Australia-headquartered gold and antimony producer listed on the ASX (primary) and TSX. After its 2025 merger with Canadian-listed Mandalay Resources, Alkane became a mid-tier multi-asset precious-metals and critical-minerals producer with operations spanning two continents.

## Business Overview & Assets

Alkane is a mid-tier, multi-jurisdictional [[gold]] producer in the [[materials]] sector with a meaningful **antimony** (critical-minerals) revenue stream and a long-dated **[[copper]]-gold porphyry** growth option. The 2025 merger with TSX-listed Mandalay Resources transformed it from a single-mine NSW gold producer into a three-mine, two-continent producer. The combined company targets ~160,000–175,000 gold-equivalent ounces (GEOs) in FY2026, rising toward ~180,000 GEOs the following year.

| Asset | Location | Type | Role / notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Tomingley** | NSW, Australia (~25 km SW of Dubbo) | Gold, open-pit + underground | Historic flagship; mine plan extends beyond 2030 after southern-extension development |
| **Costerfield** | Victoria, Australia | Gold + antimony, underground | From the Mandalay merger; antimony adds critical-minerals exposure and a second priced product |
| **Björkdal** | Sweden | Gold, underground | From the Mandalay merger; provides geographic and currency diversification |
| **Boda-Kaiser** | NSW, Australia | Copper–gold porphyry (exploration) | Long-dated organic growth option; a potential large-scale future development |

Revenue is earned from gold (the majority), antimony concentrate at Costerfield, and gold-equivalent output across the portfolio. Boda-Kaiser is pre-development optionality, not current production.

## Economic Moat

As a gold (and antimony) miner, Alkane is largely a **price-taker** — it earns prevailing spot prices and has no franchise [[economic-moat]] of the kind a network business like [[brambles]] has. The honest framing is **cost-curve position, asset quality and mine life**. Alkane's relevant, asset-specific advantages are: (1) **diversification** — three mines across NSW, Victoria and Sweden reduce single-asset risk that hurts peers like [[capricorn-metals]]; (2) **antimony by-product / co-product economics at Costerfield**, which is the one place Alkane is *not* a pure price-taker on a single metal — antimony is a thin, concentrated, strategically sensitive market where supply (especially after China's 2024–2025 export restrictions) drives outsized price moves; and (3) **Boda-Kaiser optionality**, a genuine large-scale orebody that could re-rate the company if developed. The limitation is that Alkane runs at a relatively high group AISC (~A$2,739/GEO in H1 FY26), so it sits mid-to-high on the cost curve and is more exposed than low-cost peers to a gold pullback.

## Growth Drivers & Catalysts

- **Gold price (AUD/USD/SEK)** — the dominant driver across the three operations.
- **Antimony price** — Costerfield antimony spiked after China's 2024–2025 export restrictions; a thin market with outsized price sensitivity and a genuine swing factor on earnings.
- **AUD/USD** — a weaker AUD amplifies AUD margins on USD-priced metal.
- **Merger integration & synergies** — bedding down the Mandalay assets and lifting GEO output toward ~180koz.
- **Boda-Kaiser copper-gold porphyry** — drilling and study milestones on a long-dated, potentially company-making growth option.
- **Tomingley southern extension** — extends flagship mine life beyond 2030.
- **Recurring catalysts** — quarterly production/cost reports, FY guidance (FY2026: 160,000–175,000 GEOs), Boda-Kaiser results, and gold/antimony macro.

## Bull Case vs Bear Case

### Bull case
- Diversified three-mine, two-continent producer — lower single-asset risk than peers.
- Antimony exposure is a high-leverage, supply-constrained critical-minerals kicker on top of gold.
- Boda-Kaiser copper-gold porphyry is a large, long-dated optionality not in current numbers.
- Strong H1 FY26 cash generation (A$374M cash/bullion/investments after the March quarter) and record results at record gold prices.

### Bear case
- Higher group AISC (~A$2,739/GEO) means mid-to-high cost-curve position and more downside on a gold pullback.
- Pure price-taker on gold; the merger added scale but not a structural cost advantage.
- Integration/execution risk on a young (Aug 2025) merger across three jurisdictions.
- Antimony is a thin, volatile, policy-driven market — the same leverage cuts both ways.
- Boda-Kaiser is pre-development and capital-intensive; value is contingent and years away.

## Key Risks

- **Gold-price reversal** — the largest risk; high cost base amplifies the margin hit.
- **Antimony price / policy** — thin market and China export-control swings cut both ways.
- **Integration / multi-jurisdiction execution** — three mines across NSW, Victoria and Sweden.
- **Cost inflation** — labour, power, consumables across operations.
- **FX** — AUD, USD and SEK translation; a stronger AUD compresses AUD margins.
- **Boda-Kaiser development risk** — capital intensity and timeline on the growth option.

## Valuation & How the Stock Trades

- **Gold (and antimony) plus AUD beta** — ALK is an AUD-denominated play on USD gold with an added, idiosyncratic antimony driver; earnings amplify spot-gold moves and a soft AUD compounds the effect, while antimony price swings add a second, less-correlated lever.
- **Dual listing** — primary on the [[asx-200]] (ASX: ALK) with a secondary TSX line since 7 August 2025; the register has broadened from the Mandalay deal.
- **No meaningful dividend** — ALK is a capital-growth/leverage name reinvesting into Boda-Kaiser and growth, not a franked-income play (contrast [[evolution-mining]]).
- **Liquidity** — mid-tier ASX gold name; no listed options market of note — exposure is taken in the shares or via ASX gold-sector ETFs.

## History

Alkane Resources was incorporated in 1969 and is headquartered in West Perth, Western Australia. For much of its history it operated as a junior explorer-producer focused on gold, copper, zirconium and rare-earth opportunities in central New South Wales, including the Dubbo Project (rare earths/zirconium), which was later spun out as Australian Strategic Materials in 2020. The Tomingley gold mine entered production in 2014 and celebrated 10 years of operation in 2024. On 5 August 2025 Alkane closed an all-share merger with TSX-listed Mandalay Resources valued at approximately A$559 million, creating a diversified multi-jurisdictional producer; Alkane began trading on the TSX under ticker ALK on 7 August 2025, with the ASX remaining its primary listing.

## Recent Developments

Alkane reported record first-half FY2026 results (six months to 31 December 2025): revenue of A$404 million, adjusted EBITDA of A$185 million and net profit of A$65 million. H1 production was 74,000+ gold-equivalent ounces, including 72,732 oz of gold and 391 tonnes of antimony; the December quarter alone delivered 43,600 GEOs. All-in sustaining costs averaged A$2,739/GEO. Cash, bullion and listed investments totalled A$246 million at 31 December 2025, rising to A$374 million after the strong March quarter. FY2026 production guidance of 160,000-175,000 GEOs was maintained, with A$78-88 million earmarked for growth capital and exploration, including Boda-Kaiser.

The most recent filing (annual) was lodged on 2023-06-30. See [[#Financial Reports]] below.
