Equifax (NYSE: EFX) is one of the three major US consumer credit bureaus (alongside Experian and TransUnion) and the owner of The Work Number, the dominant US income-and-employment verification database. A constituent of the [[s-and-p-500]], it is classified under [[industrials|Industrials]] (Research & Consulting Services) but trades as a [[financials|financial-data]] name — a leveraged play on US mortgage origination volumes plus a secular data-analytics growth story.

## Business Overview & Segments

Equifax monetises **proprietary data assets** — consumer credit files and the income/employment records in The Work Number — sold as reports, scores, and analytics to lenders, employers, and government agencies. Roughly a fifth of revenue is mortgage-linked, which makes EFX one of the most rate-sensitive names in the data/analytics group.

| Segment | What it is | Mix / margin (qualitative) |
|---|---|---|
| **Workforce Solutions** | The Work Number — income/employment verification for lenders, employers, government | Highest-margin and fastest-growing; the crown jewel |
| **US Information Solutions (USIS)** | Consumer credit reports and scores sold to lenders | Core bureau business; highly mortgage-sensitive |
| **International** | Credit/data operations outside the US | Diversifying; FX-exposed, lower-margin |

Since the **2017 data breach** (147M consumers affected, ~$700M in settlements) the company has spent heavily migrating to a single cloud-native data fabric, which now underpins its **"EFX.AI"** product pipeline — the engine of new-product (Vitality Index) revenue.

## Economic Moat & Competitive Position

Equifax sits inside a **credit-bureau oligopoly** and owns a near-unique data asset — a textbook wide [[economic-moat|economic moat]] rooted in intangibles and data network effects:

- **Oligopoly structure** — the US consumer-credit-bureau market is effectively a three-firm oligopoly (Equifax, Experian, TransUnion); lenders pull from all three, so each bureau's data is non-substitutable.
- **The Work Number data moat** — a payroll-fed income/employment database with millions of employer contributors that competitors cannot easily replicate; the more records it ingests, the more valuable and harder to challenge it becomes (data network effect).
- **Switching costs / regulatory entrenchment** — credit data is woven into lending workflows, underwriting models, and regulation; rip-and-replace is impractical.
- **Cloud + EFX.AI** — the completed cloud transformation lets Equifax layer higher-margin analytics and AI products on top of the raw data, widening the moat.

The moat is **wide but mortgage-cyclical and regulation-exposed**: a large slice of revenue swings with origination volumes, and credit-report pricing/usage sits under CFPB and FICO-pricing scrutiny.

## Growth Drivers & Catalysts

- **Mortgage-rate cycle** — early-2026 rate declines spurred origination; Q1 2026 US Mortgage revenue grew 38% (USIS mortgage +60%). The dominant near-term swing factor.
- **Workforce Solutions / The Work Number** — Verification Services +14% in Q1 2026 on strong government and diversified-markets demand; structurally the highest-margin growth engine.
- **EFX.AI / Vitality Index** — new-product revenue hit 17% of total in Q1 2026, built on the completed cloud transformation.
- **Government verification** — contract wins for income/employment verification across agencies.
- **Recurring catalysts** — quarterly earnings, MBA mortgage-application trends, FICO/CFPB credit-report pricing and regulatory headlines, government-verification contract news.

## Bull Case vs Bear Case

### Bull case
- Wide-moat data franchise inside a credit-bureau oligopoly with a near-unique asset (The Work Number).
- Workforce Solutions is a high-margin secular grower less tied to the mortgage cycle.
- Completed cloud transformation drives EFX.AI and a rising new-product (Vitality Index) mix.
- Operating leverage when mortgage volumes recover: Q1 2026 set a record $1.649B revenue (+14% YoY, $37M above guidance midpoint).
- Data network effects deepen the moat as more records and analytics accrue.

### Bear case
- Mortgage cyclicality: ~a fifth of revenue swings with origination volumes; high beta to rate-cut expectations.
- Regulatory overhang: CFPB and FICO score-pricing scrutiny on credit-report pricing and usage.
- Balance-sheet leverage (D/E ~1.19x, sub-1 current ratio) above the [[industrials|Industrials]] Fred bars.
- Breach-tail risk: the 2017 breach showed the reputational and financial cost of a data failure.
- Premium valuation (P/E ~35x) prices a mortgage recovery and continued Workforce Solutions growth.

## Key Risks

- **Mortgage-rate sensitivity** — origination volumes drive a large revenue slice; a rate back-up hits USIS and the mortgage line directly.
- **Regulatory** — CFPB oversight of credit reporting and FICO/credit-report pricing headlines.
- **Data security / breach tail** — a repeat of a 2017-style breach would be reputationally and financially severe.
- **Leverage** — D/E ~1.19x and a sub-1 current ratio leave less balance-sheet cushion than a net-cash data peer.
- **Valuation** — a ~35x multiple is vulnerable to a mortgage-recovery disappointment.

## Trading Relevance & How the Stock Trades

Equifax trades as a **rate-sensitive financial-data name** — a recurring-data franchise with a cyclical mortgage overlay. Mechanically:

- **Recurring data + mortgage beta** — the bureau/Workforce data is recurring and subscription-like; the mortgage-linked piece is the cyclical torque that drives the stock's high beta to rate-cut expectations.
- **MBA data as a nowcast** — mortgage-inquiry/application data gives traders a partial read on the print, so surprises cluster in guidance, not the headline.
- **FICO/CFPB headlines** — credit-report and score-pricing news ([[fair-isaac|FICO]], CFPB) frequently move EFX and peers in sympathy.
- **Options** — liquid single-name options with elevated implied moves around quarterly earnings.
- **Valuation** — a premium data-franchise multiple (P/E ~35x) reflecting the oligopoly moat and Workforce Solutions growth.
- **Dividend** — a modest dividend; the API "dividend yield" figure uses a non-standard definition (see source page).
- **Correlated names** — TransUnion (TRU), Experian (EXPN.L), [[fair-isaac|FICO]], [[moodys|Moody's]], [[verisk]].
