**CRISPR Therapeutics AG** (NASDAQ: CRSP) is a Swiss-headquartered, NASDAQ-listed gene-editing biotechnology company co-founded in 2013 by Nobel laureate Emmanuelle Charpentier, and the commercial partner (with [[vertex-pharmaceuticals|Vertex Pharmaceuticals]]) behind Casgevy, the first CRISPR/Cas9 gene-editing therapy ever approved by regulators. For traders it is a high-beta, high-implied-volatility biotech whose price is driven by clinical-trial readouts, Casgevy commercial ramp, and binary regulatory events rather than near-term earnings — making it a classic options name around catalysts.

## Business Overview

CRISPR Therapeutics develops therapies using CRISPR/Cas9 gene editing across four areas: hemoglobinopathies (its lead, commercialised), immuno-oncology (allogeneic CAR-T), in vivo editing (cardiovascular, liver), and regenerative medicine (diabetes). Its flagship product is **Casgevy (exagamglogene autotemcel / exa-cel)**, a one-time ex vivo edited cell therapy for severe sickle cell disease (SCD) and transfusion-dependent beta thalassemia (TDT), co-developed and commercialised with Vertex (Vertex leads commercialisation; CRISPR shares in profits). Casgevy is a landmark scientific achievement but a complex, high-priced (~US$2.2 million per patient) autologous therapy with a long, capacity-constrained treatment journey, so the commercial ramp is gradual.

## 2025-2026 developments

- **Casgevy commercial ramp.** Casgevy generated full-year 2025 revenue of ~US$116 million (Q4 2025 ~US$54 million), and ~US$43 million in Q1 2026. By early 2026 more than 500 patients globally had initiated the Casgevy treatment journey, against an eligible base of 60,000+ patients across approved markets.
- **Broad approvals.** Casgevy is approved in the US, Canada, UK, EU, Switzerland, Saudi Arabia, Bahrain, Qatar, UAE and Kuwait for patients aged 12+ with severe SCD or TDT. Pediatric (younger-patient) regulatory submissions were targeted for H1 2026.
- **Strong balance sheet.** CRISPR held approximately US$2.44 billion in cash as of 31 March 2026, bolstered by a ~US$585 million convertible-notes issuance — a large runway that de-risks pipeline funding.
- **Pipeline catalysts (2026).** Key readouts and trial events include CTX310 and the Lp(a)-lowering program updates in H2 2026, topline Phase 2 CTX611 data in H2 2026, and planned trial starts for CTX460 (mid-2026) and CTX340 (H1 2026). The allogeneic CAR-T franchise (CTX112) remains a watched immuno-oncology program.

## Pipeline & Programs

CRISPR Therapeutics is best understood as one commercial asset plus a multi-modal clinical and preclinical pipeline. The modalities matter for risk: *ex vivo* (cells edited outside the body, as in Casgevy) is de-risked relative to *in vivo* (editing delivered inside the body via LNP/AAV), which is the frontier the platform is now pushing into.

| Program | Modality / target | Indication area | Partner | Status / catalyst |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Casgevy (exa-cel)** | *Ex vivo* CRISPR/Cas9 (BCL11A) | Sickle cell disease, beta thalassemia | [[vertex-pharmaceuticals]] (commercial lead) | Approved & commercial; ramp ongoing |
| **CTX112** | Allogeneic ("off-the-shelf") CAR-T | Immuno-oncology / autoimmune | Wholly owned | Clinical; immuno-oncology franchise watch item |
| **CTX310** | *In vivo* LNP gene editing (ANGPTL3) | Cardiovascular (lipids) | Wholly owned | Clinical readouts referenced for 2026 |
| **Lp(a) program** | *In vivo* gene editing | Cardiovascular (Lp(a) lowering) | Wholly owned | Updates referenced H2 2026 |
| **CTX611 / CTX340 / CTX460** | Cell therapy / *in vivo* | Oncology, cardiovascular, rare disease | Wholly owned | Early-stage; trial starts/readouts referenced 2026 |
| **Regenerative (diabetes)** | Edited stem-cell-derived islets | Type 1 diabetes | Wholly owned | Preclinical / early |

The strategic significance is the platform pivot from a single ex vivo cell-therapy success toward scalable *in vivo* editing in large cardiovascular populations — where a one-time gene edit competing against chronic [[pcsk9]]-style lipid drugs would be a far larger TAM than rare hemoglobinopathies. (Programs and indications per file sources; no new trial figures asserted.)

## Competitive Positioning & Peers

The gene-editing cohort is the natural comparison set. CRISPR is differentiated by being the *only* one with an approved, marketed CRISPR medicine, but peers are arguably ahead in the harder *in vivo* race.

| Company | Ticker | Edge / platform | Lead status |
|---|---|---|---|
| **CRISPR Therapeutics** | CRSP | CRISPR/Cas9; Casgevy commercial; in vivo CV push | Only approved CRISPR therapy |
| [[intellia-therapeutics]] | NTLA | CRISPR/Cas9, *in vivo* LNP leader (ATTR, HAE) | Late-stage in vivo programs |
| [[editas-medicine]] | EDIT | CRISPR/Cas9 & Cas12a | Earlier-stage / repositioning |
| [[beam-therapeutics]] | BEAM | Base editing (no double-strand break) | Clinical base-editing programs |
| [[vertex-pharmaceuticals]] | VRTX | Casgevy commercial partner; large-cap profit base | Diversified commercial biopharma |

Versus [[intellia-therapeutics]], CRISPR trades the security of an approved product against Intellia's perceived in-vivo lead; versus [[beam-therapeutics]], the contrast is classic Cas9 cutting vs. base editing precision. All trade together as an XBI/gene-editing beta basket.

## Bull vs Bear Case

**Bull case**
- First and only approved CRISPR therapy (Casgevy) — durable scientific/regulatory moat and a one-time-cure value proposition.
- Fortress balance sheet (~US$2.44B cash per file) funds the pipeline without near-term dilution pressure.
- Optionality: *in vivo* cardiovascular editing addresses far larger markets than rare blood disorders.
- Profit-share with deep-pocketed [[vertex-pharmaceuticals]] de-risks commercialization.

**Bear case**
- Casgevy ramp is slow and capacity-constrained (autologous, ~US$2.2M list, multi-month patient journey).
- Years of operating losses; equity value rests on unproven *in vivo* programs.
- Crowded, well-funded competition ([[intellia-therapeutics]], [[beam-therapeutics]], [[editas-medicine]]).
- Single binary readout disappointment can re-rate the whole sector.

## Valuation Framework

As a clinical-stage-plus-one-product biotech, CRISPR is valued on pipeline expectation, not earnings multiples. Useful lenses (qualitative):

- **Cash runway** — the ~US$2.44B balance (per file) against annual R&D burn determines how many shots-on-goal can be funded before returning to capital markets; see [[cash-runway]].
- **Risk-adjusted pipeline NPV (rNPV)** — each program's probability-of-success × peak-sales × discount, summed; gene-editing rNPV swings hard on phase transitions.
- **Binary readout repricing** — the stock gaps on data; option-implied moves often dwarf the realized fundamental change.
- **Partnership economics** — Casgevy profit-share with [[vertex-pharmaceuticals]] is a quasi-annuity that anchors a sum-of-the-parts floor under the speculative pipeline.

## Trading & Options Playbook

- **Binary catalyst calendar:** Casgevy quarterly revenue/patient-initiation prints, pipeline data readouts (CTX310/Lp(a), CTX611, CTX112), pediatric regulatory submissions, and cash-runway updates. Each is a discrete event around which [[implied-volatility]] inflates then crushes — the classic biotech [[iv-crush]] dynamic.
- **Index / ETF membership:** Not in the [[s-and-p-500]]; a meaningful component of biotech/gene-editing thematic funds — most notably the SPDR S&P Biotech ETF (XBI, equal-weighted, so high-beta names move it) and the iShares Biotechnology ETF (IBB). Healthcare-sector exposure via [[xlv]] is minimal given CRISPR's size.
- **Event-vol behavior:** high realized and implied vol; sharp overnight gaps on data. Common structures are long [[straddle]]/[[strangle]] into binary readouts and premium selling into the post-event IV collapse.
- **Sympathy moves:** trades with [[intellia-therapeutics]], [[beam-therapeutics]] and [[editas-medicine]] — a disappointment at one peer can drag the basket regardless of CRISPR's own data.

## Capital Structure & Dilution Risk

CRISPR funds a cash-burning pipeline from its balance sheet. Per file, the ~US$2.44B cash position was bolstered by a ~US$585M convertible-notes issuance — meaning future dilution can arrive either via equity raises (common for biotech runway extension) or via convertible conversion if the stock appreciates. No dividend; the share count is the key thing to monitor alongside cash runway. The Vertex profit-share partially offsets reliance on capital markets but does not eliminate it.

## Risks

- **Binary clinical risk** — any pipeline readout can fail; in vivo editing carries unproven safety (off-target edits, delivery) questions.
- **Commercial-ramp risk** — Casgevy uptake is gated by treatment-center capacity and the complex autologous journey.
- **Competition** — well-funded rivals ([[intellia-therapeutics]], [[beam-therapeutics]], [[editas-medicine]]) racing for the same indications.
- **Financing/dilution** — sustained losses imply continued capital-markets dependence.
- **Sector beta** — high correlation to XBI; biotech risk-off (rates, regulation, drug-pricing policy) hits regardless of company news.
- **Regulatory/reimbursement** — gene-therapy pricing and payer coverage remain contested.
