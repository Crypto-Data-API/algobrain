**L1 Group Limited** (ASX: L1G) is an asset management company listed on the [[asx-limited|Australian Securities Exchange]] and a constituent of the [[asx-200]] index. L1 Group Limited is an Australian-listed global investment manager formed through the 2025 merger of L1 Capital and Platinum Asset Management. Based in Melbourne, L1 Group operates a diversified platform of listed equities and alternative investment strategies, serving institutional and retail clients.

## Business Overview & Segments

L1 Group is a diversified active [[financials|Financials]]-sector asset manager: it earns **management fees** on funds under management (FUM) plus **performance fees** on the alternative/long-short strategies, so the income statement is a geared function of both market levels and relative fund performance. The platform spans the founding L1 Capital alternatives/long-short franchise and Platinum's long-running global-equities business, distributed through both ASX-listed investment vehicles (LICs) and unlisted/open-end funds.

| Strategy | Vehicle(s) | Description |
|---|---|---|
| **Long-Short Australian Equities** | LSF (ASX: LSF, L1 Long Short Fund Limited) + unlisted | Flagship L1 Capital strategy; Australia's best-known long-short fund and the brand cornerstone |
| **Global Long-Short Equities** | GLS (ASX: GLS, L1 Global Long Short Fund Limited) + unlisted | International long-short extension of the L1 Capital style |
| **Catalyst (event-driven)** | L1 Capital Catalyst Fund | Concentrated, event-driven long strategy |
| **Activist / Engagement** | L1 Capital activist mandates | Engages with listed companies to drive value/performance |
| **International / Global Equities (Platinum)** | Platinum International, Asia, European, Japan Funds + LICs | Platinum's long-standing contrarian global-equities franchise, integrated post-merger |
| **Multi-Strategy Hedge Fund** | Unlisted alternatives vehicle | Diversified alternatives product |
| **UK Residential Property** | Private real-estate fund | Illiquid private real-estate strategy |
| **Gold equities** | L1 Gold Fund Limited (ASX-listed, from 24 Apr 2026) | Newest listed vehicle; thematic gold-equity exposure |

Client types include large superannuation funds, pension funds, asset consultants, financial planning groups, family offices, private wealth firms, high-net-worth individuals, and retail investors. Revenue is primarily from management fees on funds under management and performance fees on certain strategies.

## Economic Moat & Competitive Position

As an active asset manager, L1 Group holds a **narrow [[economic-moat|moat]]** built on switching friction rather than structural lock-in:

- **Sticky FUM and [[switching-costs]]** — institutional mandates carry inertia (consultant due-diligence cycles, board sign-off, redemption notice periods), so capital is slow to move in *and* slow to move out. This stickiness is the primary moat for any asset manager.
- **Brand and track record** — the L1 Capital Long Short Fund's decade-plus record (~19.5% p.a. since inception, gross, per the manager) is a genuine reputational asset that attracts and retains FUM and supports performance-fee economics.
- **Franking-supported distributions** — for Australian-resident holders, franking credits add to the effective yield, a structural pull on the domestic shareholder base (see [[australian-investing]]).

There are **weak-to-no [[network-effects]]**: unlike a platform business, one client's fund does not get better as others join, and outperformance does not compound a competitive flywheel. The moat erodes quickly if performance lapses — fees and FUM follow returns. The central competitive risk post-merger is the **integration of Platinum's outflow-prone legacy global-equities book**: Platinum had been losing FUM for years, and stemming those redemptions while retaining the L1 Capital base is the key determinant of whether the combined entity grows or merely consolidates.

## Growth Drivers & Catalysts

- **FUM growth** — the master variable: rising markets and net inflows lift management-fee revenue directly; quarterly FUM updates are the key recurring catalyst.
- **Performance-fee optionality** — strong years in the long-short strategies can produce outsized performance-fee accruals, giving the stock upside torque in up markets.
- **Flow stabilisation** — arresting legacy Platinum outflows (which moderated materially in the March 2026 quarter) would re-rate the combined group from "consolidation" toward "growth."
- **New listed vehicles** — product launches such as the **L1 Gold Fund Limited** (ASX trading from 24 April 2026) add FUM and fee streams.
- **Merger synergies** — cost rationalisation and product simplification from integrating Platinum.
- **Franking-credit-supported dividends** — a structural attraction for the Australian-resident shareholder base (see [[australian-investing]]).

## Bull Case vs Bear Case

### Bull case
- **Proven flagship track record** — the L1 Long Short Fund's ~19.5% p.a. since-inception record (gross) is a durable FUM magnet and performance-fee engine.
- **Performance-fee torque** — in strong markets, performance fees can drive earnings well above the management-fee base, giving asymmetric upside.
- **Franking + merger synergies** — franked distributions attract domestic holders; cost rationalisation from the Platinum integration supports margins.
- **Flow inflection** — net inflows into L1 Long Short and L1 Affiliates, with legacy Platinum outflows moderating, point toward stabilising-to-growing FUM.

### Bear case
- **Platinum legacy outflows** — the inherited global-equities book has a multi-year redemption history; if outflows reaccelerate, combined FUM shrinks regardless of L1 Capital's success.
- **Market-geared FUM** — fees ride on FUM, which is itself market-beta; drawdowns (e.g., the March 2026 war-in-Iran selloff that cut FUM 3.8% in a quarter) hit revenue with leverage.
- **Key-person risk** — the franchise is tightly identified with co-founders Raphael Lamm and Mark Landau; their departure or underperformance would impair the brand moat.
- **Integration execution** — merging two cultures, product sets, and back offices carries operational and client-retention risk.
- **Performance-fee volatility** — the same torque that lifts up-market earnings makes earnings lumpy and hard to value; a weak performance year can zero out the performance-fee line.
- **Headline-PE optics** — the ~93.6x trailing PE (a merger-year accounting artifact, see below) can deter screening-driven buyers.

## Key Risks

- **Market-beta FUM exposure** — management fees track FUM, which tracks markets; equity drawdowns compress revenue and (via performance fees) earnings disproportionately.
- **Outflow trajectory** — continued or reaccelerating redemptions from the legacy Platinum strategies are the single biggest swing factor on combined FUM.
- **Key-person dependence** — concentration of investment authority and brand in Lamm/Landau (and historically Clifford on the Platinum side).
- **Integration risk** — the Platinum merger must retain clients and realise synergies without disruption.
- **Performance-fee volatility** — lumpy, performance-contingent fees make earnings and valuation noisy year to year.

## History

**L1 Capital** was founded in 2007 in Melbourne by Raphael Lamm and Mark Landau. The firm launched with no outside backers, securing its first institutional mandate from Catholic Super (A$50 million). Over its first seven years, L1 Capital refined its quality-and-value style with a contrarian bias in long-only Australian equities. In 2014 it launched the L1 Capital Long Short Fund, which went on to become Australia's best-known long-short strategy. The firm subsequently expanded into global equities (L1 Capital Global Long Short Fund), activist strategies, a catalyst fund, and UK residential property.

**Platinum Asset Management** was founded in 1994 in Sydney by Kerr Neilson and Andrew Clifford, focused on global equities with a contrarian style. Platinum listed on the ASX in 2007 under the ticker PTM.

**Merger (2025):**
- **8 July 2025:** Platinum Asset Management and L1 Capital announced binding merger terms, combining to create an Australian-listed global investment manager with funds under management of approximately A$16.5 billion.
- **21 August 2025 / 27 August 2025:** ASX releases confirmed the terms and implementation path.
- **October 2025:** Scheme of arrangement implemented; Platinum renamed L1 Group Limited, ticker changed from PTM to L1G.
- **Ownership:** Existing L1 Capital shareholders hold 74.0% of the issued capital, and former PTM shareholders hold 26.0%.

As at December 2025, the combined group reported funds under management of approximately A$18 billion.

## Recent Developments

Since completion, L1 Group has focused on integrating Platinum's established global equities franchise with L1 Capital's alternatives, long-short, and activist strategies. Key initiatives include:

- Brand consolidation under the L1 Group name on the ASX.
- Rationalisation of overlapping products and fund administration.
- Continued operation of listed investment vehicles LSF (L1 Long Short Fund Limited) and GLS (L1 Global Long Short Fund Limited).
- Maintenance of Platinum's existing listed investment companies and open-end global strategies under the combined platform.

The L1 Capital Long Short Fund itself celebrated more than a decade of operation in 2024-2025, with a long-term since-inception return of approximately 19.5% p.a. gross of fees per the manager's disclosures.

**2026 updates:**

- **FUM:** A$16.96B at 31 March 2026, down 3.8% over the quarter as global markets sold off sharply on the outbreak of war in Iran; FUM rebounded to ~A$17.9B by 10 April 2026 ([FUM update](https://www.listcorp.com/asx/l1g/l1-group-limited/news/funds-under-management-update-march-2026-3339565.html)).
- **Flows:** net client flows positive for the L1 Long Short and L1 Affiliates strategies; outflows from legacy Platinum strategies moderated materially versus the prior quarter.
- **New listings:** L1 Gold Fund Limited scheduled to begin ASX trading on 24 April 2026.
- **Leadership:** trade press reported the group CEO would step down in 2026, with co-founders Raphael Lamm and Mark Landau continuing as joint managing directors/CIOs ([Money Management](https://www.moneymanagement.com.au/news/funds-management/l1-group-ceo-peters-step-down)).

## Trading Relevance

- **FUM-leveraged equity:** as a listed asset manager, L1G earnings are a geared play on markets (management fees on FUM) plus optionality on performance fees from the long-short strategies. FUM updates and fund performance disclosures are the key recurring catalysts.
- **Watch:** quarterly FUM updates, Platinum-legacy outflow trajectory (the central integration risk), performance-fee accruals in strong markets, new listed-vehicle launches (e.g., L1 Gold Fund), and franking-credit-supported dividends.
- **Comparables:** [[magellan-financial-group]], GQG Partners, Perpetual, Pinnacle Investment Management on the ASX-listed asset-manager factor; the Platinum merger mirrors prior consolidation in Australian active management.
- **Note:** the high headline PE (93.6x in the Fundamentals section) reflects merger-year accounting on the renamed Platinum entity rather than steady-state earnings of the combined group.

## Valuation & How the Stock Trades

L1G trades as a **FUM-geared (leveraged) play on markets**: because revenue is management fees on FUM plus performance fees, the equity behaves like an amplified bet on Australian and global equity levels and on the firm's relative performance. Mechanically:

- **Headline PE is misleading.** The ~93.6x trailing PE (see the Fundamentals section) is a **merger-year accounting artifact** on the renamed Platinum (PTM) entity — purchase accounting, integration charges, and a depressed merger-year net income — *not* steady-state earnings of the combined group. The stock should be read on FUM-based, fee-margin, and price-to-FUM logic rather than a trailing earnings multiple until a clean post-merger year prints.
- **FUM beta.** Drawdowns hit fees with leverage (the March 2026 war-in-Iran selloff cut FUM 3.8% in a quarter); strong markets do the reverse and can trigger performance fees.
- **Franking-supported dividend.** Distributions carry franking credits, materially lifting the after-tax yield for Australian residents — a key part of the total-return case (see [[australian-investing]]).
- **Reporting and access.** Reports in **AUD**; trades in **ASX hours**. The flagship listed vehicles (LSF, GLS) and the L1 Gold Fund offer separate listed exposures within the group's orbit.

> *Point-in-time figures (market cap A$2.72B, FUM levels, the 93.6x PE) are as disclosed/pulled on the dates noted and are indicative; asset-manager metrics move with markets and the merger-accounting normalises over time.*
