Adisyn Ltd (ASX: AI1) is an Australian micro-cap that pivoted from managed IT/cybersecurity services for SMEs into **semiconductor materials technology** via its January 2025 acquisition of Israel-based **2D Generation**, which is developing graphene as a replacement for copper interconnects in sub-2nm chips. For traders it is a high-risk, announcement-driven ASX speculative stock that re-rated repeatedly through 2025-2026 on graphene milestones.

## Business Overview

Two legs: the legacy **managed technology and [[artificial-intelligence|AI]] services** business for Australian SMEs (modest recurring revenue, includes defence-adjacent clients), and **2D Generation**, the growth story. 2D Generation's ADIS technology aims to deposit continuous graphene at temperatures below the ~450°C ceiling commercial fabs can tolerate — addressing copper interconnect resistance/electromigration limits as TSMC, Samsung and Intel push below 2nm. The company collaborates with imec-linked networks and Tel Aviv University researchers. Commercialisation is pre-revenue and milestone-based.

## 2025-2026 Developments

- **January 2025** — completed acquisition of 2D Generation, repositioning the company as a semiconductor-technology play.
- **Mid-2025** — published a three-milestone research roadmap (sub-300°C graphene-like carbon layer → full-coverage coupon → wafer-scale repeatability).
- **January 2026** — first milestone: graphene-like carbon layer deposited below 300°C, independently verified by Prof. Yoram Selzer (Tel Aviv University).
- **April 2026** — breakthrough: **full-coverage graphene on a 1cm x 1cm coupon** using an industrial ALD system well below 450°C; shares spiked ~20%+ on the announcement.
- **April-May 2026** — five material announcements in three weeks: a **A$14M institutional placement** cornerstoned by Regal Funds Management and Meitav, two technology milestones, a **US patent allowance**, and a manufacturing MOU with a €201M-revenue Israeli industrial group.
- **Next target** — wafer-scale repeatability and a demonstration prototype by end-2026.

## Product & Technology

Adisyn is a two-leg business, but the equity story is almost entirely the **2D Generation** semiconductor-materials leg.

| Leg | What it is | Role in the thesis |
|---|---|---|
| **Managed IT / [[artificial-intelligence|AI]] services** | Legacy managed-technology and cyber business for Australian SMEs (incl. defence-adjacent clients) | Modest recurring revenue; provides some cashflow and a corporate vehicle, but not the re-rating driver |
| **2D Generation (ADIS)** | Israel-based graphene-deposition technology aimed at replacing copper interconnects in sub-2nm chips | The growth/option story; pre-revenue, milestone-driven |

The technical thesis: as leading-edge logic shrinks below 2nm, copper interconnects suffer rising resistance and electromigration, becoming a bottleneck for [[tsmc]], [[intel]] and Samsung. Graphene is a candidate replacement, but commercial fabs cannot tolerate the high temperatures traditional graphene growth requires. 2D Generation's **ADIS** approach aims to deposit continuous, fab-compatible graphene **below the ~450°C ceiling** using atomic-layer-deposition (ALD) tooling. The roadmap is explicitly staged — sub-300°C graphene-like carbon → full-coverage coupon → wafer-scale repeatability → demonstration prototype — and each stage is a discrete, binary catalyst.

The critical caveat (already noted in [[Trading Relevance]] and [[Risks]]): **the gap between a 1cm × 1cm lab coupon and a qualified production wafer is exactly where most semiconductor-materials startups fail.** Qualification cycles with Tier-1 fabs are measured in years, and adoption is gated by integration, yield, and reliability — not by a single lab demonstration.

## Capital Structure & Dilution Risk

- AI1 is **pre-profit** on its growth leg and funds operations through **equity placements** — the most recent being the **A$14M institutional placement** (April–May 2026) cornerstoned by Regal Funds Management and Meitav.
- **Dilution is the base case.** Expect recurring raises (placements, SPPs, options exercises) at discounts to market; each expands the share count and dilutes existing holders.
- **Register concentration:** a small register dominated by founders, the new cornerstone institutions, and a large retail base. Low free float amplifies both up-moves and down-moves; a single large holder selling into thin volume can drive an outsized move.
- **Latent supply:** watch for placement shares coming free, options/performance-rights overhang, and any escrowed consideration stock from the 2D Generation acquisition — all add potential supply.
- A raise is itself a catalyst: often discounted (a near-term drag) but sometimes welcomed if it funds the next milestone. Always re-check the **current** share count after any raise before sizing.
