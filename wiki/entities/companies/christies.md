Christie's is one of the world's two dominant auction houses, founded in 1766 by James Christie in London and privately owned by Groupe Artémis (the Pinault family holding company that also controls Kering). For traders it matters as a real-time barometer of the high-end art and luxury market — its sale totals are a sentiment read on ultra-high-net-worth risk appetite — and as the institution that legitimized [[nft|NFTs]] with the $69.3 million Beeple sale in March 2021.

## History

James Christie founded Christie's auction house in London in 1766. Over more than 250 years, Christie's became one of the two dominant global auction houses (alongside [[sothebys|Sotheby's]]), conducting sales of fine art, decorative arts, jewelry, real estate, and collectibles. Christie's is privately owned by Groupe Artémis, the holding company of French billionaire François-Henri Pinault.

Christie's annual sales typically range from $5-8 billion, with flagship auction rooms in London, New York, Hong Kong, and Geneva. The house has sold some of the most expensive artworks in history, including Leonardo da Vinci's "Salvator Mundi" ($450.3 million, 2017).

## Entry into NFTs

Christie's entry into the NFT space was catalyzed by the explosive growth of crypto art in late 2020 and early 2021. The auction house recognized that digital art and blockchain-based provenance represented a significant new category for the art market.

### The Beeple Sale (March 2021)

The defining moment came on March 11, 2021, when Christie's sold Beeple's (Mike Winkelmann) "Everydays: The First 5000 Days" for $69,346,250. Key details of this historic sale:

- **Artwork**: A collage of 5,000 digital images Beeple had created daily since May 2007
- **Format**: Purely digital — the buyer received an [[nft|NFT]] on [[ethereum|Ethereum]], with no physical component
- **Opening bid**: $100
- **Final price**: $69,346,250 (including buyer's premium)
- **Buyer**: Vignesh Sundaresan (known as MetaKovan), founder of Metapurse, a Singapore-based NFT fund
- **Payment**: Accepted in [[ethereum|Ether (ETH)]]
- **Significance**: First purely digital NFT artwork sold by a major auction house; third-highest price for a living artist at the time

The sale generated global media coverage and is widely credited with bringing NFTs to mainstream public consciousness. It demonstrated that major institutional players in the art world were willing to legitimize digital art and blockchain-based ownership.

Beeple had previously sold NFTs on [[makersplace|MakersPlace]], where his works had already fetched hundreds of thousands of dollars. The Christie's sale represented a dramatic escalation — from crypto-native marketplace prices to traditional auction house record-breaking territory.

### Subsequent NFT Activity

Following the Beeple sale, Christie's conducted numerous additional NFT auctions:

- **CryptoPunks**: Christie's auctioned a lot of nine [[cryptopunks|CryptoPunks]] for $16.9 million (May 2021)
- **Art Blocks curated works**: Generative art from [[art-blocks|Art Blocks]] featured in Christie's sales
- **Various digital artists**: Ongoing auctions featuring prominent crypto artists

## Christie's 3.0

In late 2022, Christie's launched **Christie's 3.0**, an on-chain auction platform built on [[ethereum|Ethereum]]. This represented a significant step beyond simply listing NFTs in traditional auction formats:

- **On-chain bidding**: Bids placed directly on the blockchain via smart contracts
- **Trustless settlement**: Auction settlement handled by smart contracts rather than traditional post-auction processes
- **Wallet-based participation**: Bidders connect crypto wallets directly, without requiring traditional Christie's account registration
- **Curated drops**: Christie's 3.0 hosts curated NFT drops alongside traditional auction formats

The launch of Christie's 3.0 signaled the auction house's commitment to integrating blockchain technology into its core operations rather than treating NFTs as a temporary trend.

## Significance for the NFT Market

Christie's involvement in NFTs had several major effects on the broader market:

1. **Institutional legitimacy**: A 250-year-old auction house validating NFTs as art gave the technology credibility with traditional art collectors and institutions
2. **Price discovery**: The Beeple sale established that digital art could command prices comparable to major physical artworks
3. **Media attention**: The $69.3 million sale generated more mainstream coverage of NFTs than any previous event
4. **Collector bridge**: Christie's served as a bridge between traditional art collectors and the crypto-native NFT ecosystem
5. **Market infrastructure**: Christie's 3.0 demonstrated that traditional art market institutions could adopt blockchain-native tools

## 2025–2026 Developments

- **2025 turnover**: Christie's reported total 2025 sales of approximately $6.2 billion, up 6% year-on-year, with public auctions contributing $4.7 billion (up 8%). 2025 was the art market's first growth year since 2022 — combined global auction totals at Christie's, Sotheby's and Phillips rose about 11% — driven largely by renewed supply of works priced above $10 million.
- **Luxury offsets soft fine art**: luxury sales (handbags, watches, cars, jewelry) rose 17% in 2025 to roughly $795 million; in H1 2025 luxury generated $468 million, about 22% of the $2.1 billion half-year total, plugging the gap left by a weaker mid-market for fine art.
- **Private sales growing**: private deals accounted for roughly 24% of Christie's 2025 revenue, and the house's three largest 2025 transactions were all private — a structural shift away from headline evening auctions.
- **2026**: early 2026 sales (American art, Old Masters — the latter up 24% in 2025 to $182 million) suggest momentum carried into the spring season.

## Trading Relevance

Christie's is private — there is no ticker. Traders use it indirectly:

- **Luxury-cycle signal**: auction results lead/confirm trends in listed luxury names (Kering — same Pinault ownership via Artémis — LVMH, Richemont, Sotheby's-adjacent credit).
- **Wealth-effect barometer**: trophy-lot sell-through rates track UHNW confidence, useful colour for risk-on/risk-off macro reads.
- **Crypto/NFT sentiment**: Christie's NFT activity (Beeple, CryptoPunks, Christie's 3.0) historically coincided with [[nft|NFT]] market cycle peaks — institutional engagement is a late-cycle marker.
